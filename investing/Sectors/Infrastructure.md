# Infrastructure

Private infrastructure investment — the fastest-growing segment of [[Private markets]], driven by AI data center buildout and energy transition. Record $206.6B raised in 2025, surpassing the prior high by $30B. Infrastructure is displacing PE as the dominant fundraising category: of the 17 funds that closed on $10B+ in 2025, only 5 were primary PE.

## Dynamics

Infrastructure has structural tailwinds that other private market segments lack:

1. **AI capex**: hyperscalers need $100Bs+ in off-balance-sheet DC financing — infrastructure funds are the natural vehicle
2. **Energy transition**: policy-driven (IRA, EU Green Deal) capital needs exceed public market capacity
3. **Inflation linkage**: infrastructure revenues (tolls, tariffs, regulated returns) often index to inflation — natural hedge
4. **Long duration match**: 20-30 year asset lives match pension/insurance liability profiles
5. **Regulatory moats**: permits, grid connections, water rights create natural barriers

Risks:
- AI DC overbuild if demand doesn't materialize — I Squared Capital's Wahba (Dec 2025): *"Momentum is strong, but if this is irrational exuberance, investors will lose when the music stops"*
- Interest rate sensitivity — infrastructure valuations use DCF; higher discount rates compress values
- Political risk — [[Data center land competition]] creating backlash (Loudoun County, Virginia legislature)
- Consolidation of managers into mega-platforms reduces competition for assets
