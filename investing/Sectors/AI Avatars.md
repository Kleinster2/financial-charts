# AI Avatars

AI-generated human presenters for enterprise video — corporate training, marketing, sales, localization, and customer service. Not creative filmmaking (see [[AI-Native Studios]]) but scalable, personalized business communication.

AI avatar market: **~$800M** (2025), projected **$5.93B** by 2032.

## Deep dives

### Synthesia

Founded by **Victor Riparbelli** (CEO). The clear category leader.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Series E | $200M at $4B valuation (Jan 2026) |
| Investors | GV (Alphabet, led Series E), Accel, NEA, NVentures ([[NVIDIA]]), Hedosophia |
| Revenue | $150M ARR, expects to pass $200M in 2026 |
| Customers | 60,000+ companies, 90% of Fortune 100 |
| M&A interest | [[Adobe]] explored $3B acquisition (2025), couldn't agree on price; made strategic investment instead |

**Product evolution:** Moving from static AI avatars to **conversational AI Agents** — interactive video with real-time user interaction for organizational learning and upskilling. This shifts the TAM from "video creation tool" to "enterprise training platform."

230+ stock avatars, 140+ languages. Enterprise-grade security.

### HeyGen

Founded 2020 by **Joshua Xu** (CEO) and **Wayne Liang** (originally Surreal → Movio → HeyGen).

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Series A | $60M (June 2024, led by Benchmark) |
| Revenue | ~$95M ARR (Sep 2025), up from $57.5M at end-2024 |
| Profitability | **Profitable since Q2 2023** |
| Team | ~157 people |
| Efficiency | ~$600K revenue per employee |

230+ digital avatars, 140 languages. Photo-realistic avatars from user photos/videos. Remarkable capital efficiency — near $100M revenue with <$70M raised and 157 people.

### D-ID

Founded in Tel Aviv. 200M+ videos produced, 280K+ developers on API.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Series B | $25M (March 2022, led by Macquarie Group) |
| Total funding | $48M over 7 rounds |
| Clients | PepsiCo, Fidelity, J.P. Morgan, [[SoftBank]] |
| Acquisition | Bought Simpleshow (September 2025) |

Smaller scale than Synthesia/HeyGen but strong developer ecosystem.

## Competitive dynamics

**Synthesia vs HeyGen** is the core rivalry:

| Dimension | Synthesia | HeyGen |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Scale | Larger (60K customers) | Growing fast (85K) |
| Revenue | $150M ARR | ~$95M ARR |
| Funding | $530M+ | ~$69M |
| Profitability | Not disclosed | **Profitable** since Q2 2023 |
| Moat | Enterprise relationships, GV/NVIDIA backing | Capital efficiency, speed |
| Direction | Conversational AI agents | Video translation, personalization |

**D-ID** is the developer-first alternative — API ecosystem rather than enterprise sales.
