# Private Real Estate

Private real estate investment — developers, operators, and fund managers who own properties outside of public REIT structures. Dominated by mega-managers ([[Blackstone]], [[Brookfield]]) but includes large private developers like Lightstone and Related Companies.

## Why this sector matters

**Scale of capital.** Blackstone and Brookfield alone manage ~$600B in real estate. Private capital now rivals or exceeds public REIT market cap in many sectors.

**Market-moving.** Major transactions (data centers, logistics, multifamily portfolios) often involve private buyers. Their deployment decisions signal sector conviction.

**Public-private arbitrage.** Private managers buy public REITs (Blackstone/BREIT acquiring REIT portfolios), creating valuation linkages.

## Business models

### Fund managers (Blackstone, Brookfield, Starwood)

| Strategy | Description | Return Target |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Opportunistic** | Value-add, distressed, development | 15-20% IRR |
| **Core-plus** | Stabilized with upside | 10-14% IRR |
| **Core** | Trophy assets, income-focused | 7-10% IRR |
| **Debt** | Real estate lending, mezzanine | 8-12% |

Revenue model: Management fees (1-1.5% of AUM) + carried interest (20% of profits above hurdle).

**BREIT model:** Blackstone's non-traded REIT (~$60B) offers retail investors access to institutional real estate. Semi-liquid with quarterly redemption limits.

### Developers/operators (Related, Lightstone)

| Revenue Source | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Development profits** | Build and sell/stabilize |
| **Operating income** | NOI from owned properties |
| **Fee income** | Property management, development fees |
| **Capital recycling** | Sell stabilized assets, redeploy |

More merchant builder than perpetual owner — though Lightstone and Related hold significant long-term portfolios.

## Current themes (2025-2026)

### Data center buildout

Private capital is the dominant force in data center development. See [[Data Centers]] for infrastructure detail.

| Owner | Platform | Capacity | Investment |
|-------|----------|----------|------------|
| **[[Blackstone]]** | [[QTS Data Centers]] | 70+ facilities, 2+ GW | $25B+ pipeline |
| [[KKR]] | [[CyrusOne]] | 50+ facilities | Growing |
| [[Brookfield]] | Multiple | Expanding | Significant |
| [[Related Companies]] | Related Digital | New (2025) | Emerging |

**Blackstone/[[QTS Data Centers|QTS]] dominance:**
- Acquired QTS for $10B (2021) — now 8x larger footprint
- Pipeline grew from $1B to $25B+ by mid-2025
- $25B Pennsylvania investment (Jul 2025) — digital + energy infrastructure, 6,000 construction jobs
- $3.5B CMBS refinancing (2025) — largest data center securitization of the year
- UK expansion: Cambois facility (Phase 1: 2026, complete: 2035)
- BREIT: Data centers = 21% of portfolio (end 2025), just behind industrial (22%)

**Scale required:** $150B in permanent financing expected for US data center construction in 2026-2027. Only mega-managers can deploy capital at this pace.

### Multifamily consolidation

Large portfolios trading between private owners:
- Lightstone: 25,000 units
- [[Blackstone]] BREIT: major multifamily holder
- Cap rate compression vs single-family

### Office distress

Post-COVID office valuations creating opportunities:
- Brookfield: significant office exposure, selective defaults
- Tishman Speyer, Hines: repositioning assets
- Related: office component of Hudson Yards

### Retail renaissance

Select retail formats recovering:
- Experiential, grocery-anchored outperforming
- Related: Hudson Yards retail
- Starwood: selective retail investments
