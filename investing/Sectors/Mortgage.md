**Mortgage** — $17T+ US market spanning residential ($12.5T) and commercial ($4.7T) lending. Two distinct sub-sectors with different players, economics, and cycles.

---

## Residential vs commercial

| Factor | [[Residential Mortgage]] | [[Commercial Mortgage]] |
|--------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| Borrower | Individuals | Businesses, investors |
| Collateral | 1-4 unit homes | Office, retail, industrial, MF |
| Underwriting | Income, credit, DTI | Property cash flow (DSCR) |
| Term | 30-year fixed | 5-10yr balloon |
| Prepayment | Usually free | Yield maintenance |
| GSE role | **Dominant** (70%) | Multifamily only |
| Non-bank share | **66%** | ~15% |
| Current stress | Rate sensitivity | Office distress |

---

## Insights

- **Residential:** Non-banks won (66% share), GSEs enable 30-year fixed, extremely rate-sensitive
- **Commercial:** Banks dominate but pulling back, office distress driving defaults, $1T+ maturity wall
- **Multifamily bridges both:** GSE-eligible commercial, best liquidity
