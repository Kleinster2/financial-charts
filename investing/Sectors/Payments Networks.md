- Fundamentally different business model

**The difference:**
| Factor | Payments Networks | Digital Fintechs |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| Model | Toll booth on all transactions | Compete for transactions |
| Margins | 60%+ operating | 10-30% |
| Growth | GDP+ | Hyper-growth target |
| Risk | Regulatory, real-time payments | Execution, credit |
| Valuation | 25-30x P/E | Revenue multiples |

---

## Business model

**Toll booth economics:**
- Small fee (~0.2%) on every card transaction globally
- Network effects: more merchants → more cardholders → more merchants
- Near-zero marginal cost per transaction
- 60%+ operating margins

**Moat sources:**
1. Network effects (two-sided platform)
2. Regulatory relationships (150+ countries)
3. Fraud/security infrastructure
4. Brand trust

## Competitive dynamics

| Competitor | Threat Level | Detail |
|------------|--------------|--------|
| Real-time payments (Pix, FedNow) | Medium | Account-to-account bypasses cards |
| Big Tech (Apple Pay, Google Pay) | Low | Rides on V/MA rails |
| BNPL (Klarna, Affirm) | Low | Often uses V/MA for funding |
| [[Stablecoins]] | Low | Not competing for consumer checkout. Gaining traction in segments cards don't serve well: cross-border remittances, creator payouts, EM dollar access. [[Meta]]/[[Stripe]] integration (H2 2026) is the first Big Tech deployment but targets creator payouts (~$100 transfers), not card swipes. The $150B interchange pool is protected by credit, chargebacks, and consumer habit |

## Investment angle

**Bull case:**
- Secular cash-to-digital shift (15%+ cash globally)
- Cross-border growth (higher take rate)
- Value-added services expansion
- Duopoly pricing power

**Bear case:**
- Real-time payments adoption
- Interchange regulation (EU caps, US pressure)
- Antitrust scrutiny
- Merchant pushback on fees
