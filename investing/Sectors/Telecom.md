## Cluster validation

> [!success] Cluster status: validated — a distinct, market-neutral US-wireless factor (Jun 2026)
> The big-3 US wireless carriers ([[AT&T|T]]/[[Verizon|VZ]]/[[T-Mobile|TMUS]]) trade as one factor. Intra-corr 0.632 (weekly 0.753), PC1 75.5%, all three cluster, random-basket null rejected at p 0.0008, threshold ROBUST (stable [0.40–0.60], width 0.20), holdout STRENGTHENING (1.19). Distinct from cable (+0.277) and — the standout — essentially uncorrelated with the market (−0.045 vs XLC/SPY, a +0.677 intra-advantage): a genuinely market-neutral telecom factor, the US mirror of the validated [[China Telecom|China big-3 telecoms]] (0.638). No value/growth split — TMUS joins the T+VZ pair inside the cut. The cohort has been tightening (loose ~0.40 in 2020–24, 0.63 in 2025–26).

The US wireless oligopoly is one of the cleaner validated cohorts in the campaign and a rare market-neutral one. The three national carriers share a single dominant driver — the 3-player subscriber/spectrum/capex competition and rate-sensitive, dividend-heavy balance sheets — that binds them regardless of the value/growth distinction between the legacy carriers ([[AT&T]], [[Verizon]]) and the un-carrier challenger ([[T-Mobile]]). The defining feature is the near-zero market correlation: this is a defensive factor that moves on telecom-specific dynamics, not the broad tape — like tobacco, a place to express a view with little index beta.

### Distinctness — market-neutral, distinct from cable

![[us-telecom-cluster-correlation-1y.png]]
*1Y pairwise correlation heatmap. The wireless block is uniformly warm, cooler against cable, and strikingly flat — near zero — against the market (XLC/SPY).*

The +0.677 intra-advantage versus the market is the campaign's largest market-distinctness number outside tobacco, and the −0.045 raw correlation confirms it: US wireless is a near-perfectly market-neutral factor. It is also distinct from cable (+0.277) — the wireless carriers and the cable/broadband operators trade on different drivers (subscriber/spectrum vs broadband/content) despite both being "telecom." A broad communication-services ETF (XLC, dominated by [[Meta]]/[[Alphabet]]) does not capture it; the wireless big-3 is its own thing, and the three-name basket — or a wireless-specific telecom ETF — is the expression.

The strengthening holdout (1.19) and the tightening rolling series both point to a factor becoming more, not less, coherent — the opposite of the day's fragmenting restaurants.
