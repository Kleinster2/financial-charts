#sector #commodities #agriculture

**Fertilizer** — The industry that produces and distributes crop nutrients. Three segments: nitrogen (~58% of nutrient consumption), phosphate (~24%), potash (~18%). ~$182B global market (2024). Structurally tied to food security and population growth. See [[Ammonia]], [[urea]], [[Phosphoric Acid]] for the key input products. See [[Nitrogen]], [[Phosphate]], [[Potash]] for segment-level concepts.

## Global product mix

![[global-fertilizer-market-by-product-aurelion.png]]
*Product mix by nutrient segment, % of global consumption. Nitrogen (109M mt N) is dominated by [[urea]] (50%) with NPK blends (17%), AN/CAN (8%), DAP/MAP overlap (6%), UAN, ammonia direct (3%), and other (10%). Phosphate (44M mt P₂O₅) is DAP/MAP (49%), NPK (26%), SSP (7%), TSP (4%), and other (15%). Potash (35M mt K₂O) is MOP/SOP (60%), NPK (36%), and other (5%). NPK blends appear in every segment, meaning constraints in any one nutrient ripple into blend production. Source: Nutrien, IFA via Aurelion Research.*

The hidden link across segments: NPK blends account for 36% of potash, 17% of nitrogen, and 26% of phosphate consumption. They require inputs from all three nutrients. Supply constraints in any nutrient ripple into the others through blend production — no segment is truly insulated.

## Market structure

| Segment | Share | Global production (2024) | Key feedstock | Dominant producer |
|---------|-------|--------------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Nitrogen | ~60% | ~150M mt nutrient | Natural gas (or coal in China) | [[China]] (28.9M mt nutrient) |
| Phosphate | ~25% | 444M mt rock | Phosphate rock + [[Sulfuric Acid]] | [[China]] (303M mt rock, 68%) |
| Potash | ~15% | 76.1M mt | Mined potassium salts | [[Canada]] (~25M mt, 33%) |

Global market ~$182B (2024), projected ~$282B by 2030 (4.1% CAGR). Asia-Pacific accounts for 52.5% of consumption. Latin America and South Asia are the main growth engines, expected to add 3-4M mt of nutrients each through 2028.

### Nitrogen

China dominates at 28.9M mt of nutrient, followed by the US (13.6M mt) and India (13.3M mt). Production economics are overwhelmingly driven by natural gas prices — gas represents 60-80% of nitrogen fertilizer production costs. North American producers benefit from cheap Henry Hub gas vs European TTF. China uses coal-based synthesis (~29% of global ammonia), making it structurally higher-cost but domestically insulated from gas markets.

Global urea capacity: ~240M mt vs ~185M mt demand. Overcapacity is structural — operating rates matter more than nameplate. 6.6M mt of new/replacement urea capacity added in 2025, another 5M mt expected in 2026.

### Phosphate

China produced 303M mt of phosphate rock in 2024 (~68% of global output). [[Morocco]] is second at 30M mt (~7%) but controls ~70% of global reserves through [[OCP Group]]. Russia produced 14M mt, Saudi Arabia 9.5M mt. The gap between China's production dominance and Morocco's reserve dominance is a key geopolitical dynamic.

Emerging demand pull from [[Phosphoric Acid]] for LFP battery cathodes — LFP now >60% of Chinese battery market. This diverts phosphate supply from agriculture to energy storage, tightening fertilizer-grade supply. China has imposed phosphate export restrictions partly for this reason.

### Potash

Most concentrated segment. Canada (~25M mt, 33%), Russia (~9M mt), and Belarus (7M mt, up 56% from 4.5M mt in 2023) together account for ~70% of global supply. Western sanctions on Russia/Belarus created supply disruption in 2022-2023, but Belarusian production has largely recovered.

## Geopolitical dynamics

### China export restrictions

China slashed urea exports 86% in H1 2024 to stabilize domestic prices. Limited exports resumed May 2025 under a 3.5-4M mt quota cap through September. Phosphate exports also constrained — Beijing is diverting phosphate toward LFP battery production. The dual effect: tightens global fertilizer supply while accelerating China's battery supply chain. Re-entry risk is permanent — Beijing could resume full exports at any time.

### Russia / Belarus sanctions

EU placed import quotas on Russian potash; US sanctioned specific companies. Both eased restrictions in late 2022 due to food security concerns. Uralkali itself remains unsanctioned. Belarusian production recovered sharply (7M mt in 2024 vs 4.5M mt in 2023, +56%). The sanctions created a brief supply scarcity premium in potash, now mostly unwound as volumes reroute through non-sanctioned channels.

### European gas vulnerability

European ammonia capacity was cut ~50% during the 2022 energy crisis. Partial recovery since, but European nitrogen producers remain structurally disadvantaged vs North American (Henry Hub ~$2-3/MMBtu) and Middle Eastern producers. [[OCI]] European Nitrogen achieves 32 MMBtu/ton ammonia efficiency vs EU average of 37 MMBtu/ton — cost discipline matters more in Europe. Cold winters or LNG disruptions could spike European gas prices, flowing through to global ammonia/urea prices.

### Morocco phosphate leverage

[[OCP Group]] controls ~70% of global phosphate reserves despite producing only 7% of current output. This is a long-duration strategic asset — as Chinese reserves deplete (China has ~5% of reserves but produces 68% of rock), Morocco's dominance will compound. OCP is investing $14.06B (2025-2027) to expand. The [[Western Sahara]] territorial dispute adds a political dimension — much of OCP's reserves are in disputed territory.

## Recent developments (2024-2026)

OCI strategic dismantlement: Effectively dismantled itself through four major divestitures totaling ~$11.2B — Methanex deal ($1.6B, closed Jun 2025), Fertiglobe to ADNOC ($3.62B), Iowa Fertilizer to Koch/KAES ($3.6B), Clean Ammonia to Woodside ($2.35B). Remaining entity is primarily European nitrogen assets.

Corteva split (announced Oct 2025): Separating into two public companies by H2 2026 — New Corteva (Crop Protection, ~$7.8B) and SpinCo/Pioneer (Seed, ~$9.9B). Tax-free spin-off.

Nutrien restructuring: Targeting ~$200M annual savings by 2025 (ahead of original 2026 target). Mine automation at 49%. Reviewing strategic alternatives for Brazilian retail operations.

Price trends (late 2025): Urea ~$567/ton (+16% YoY), potash ~$484/ton (+9% YoY), DAP ~$866/ton (+17% YoY). For 2026: urea expected to decline ~7% and DAP ~8% as new capacity comes online.

Capacity additions: Global ammonia capacity projected to grow from 166.6M mt (2024) to 184.9M mt by 2029 (+9%). Blue/green ammonia projects in development: CF Blue Point Complex (with [[JERA]] and Mitsui), [[OCI]] Beaumont 1.1M mt blue ammonia, [[Yara]] Porsgrunn 500 kt/year green ammonia (with [[Orsted]]).

## Africa growth story

Africa is the structural growth market. Current application rates of <20 kg/hectare are far below global averages. The Nairobi Declaration (2024) set a binding AU target of 50 kg/hectare.

| Metric | 2024 | 2033 |
|--------|------|------|
| Market size | $13.4B | $22.2B |
| CAGR | — | 5.7% |

Nigeria (12.4% share), South Africa, and Egypt lead consumption. [[OCP Group]] is the dominant supplier, controlling ~54% of the African fertilizer market. Morocco's geographic proximity and phosphate reserves position it as Africa's structural fertilizer supplier.

## Iran conflict fertiliser shock (March 2026)

The [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure triggered a fertiliser supply crisis — ~50% of 2-week urea exports disrupted, [[QAFCO]] shut, South Asian plants curtailed. Urea +40%. Full analysis in [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade#Fertiliser supply crisis (March 14)]].

See [[Nitrogen Trap]] for the full 14-channel cascading framework — the crisis propagates far beyond fertilizer pricing into sulfur/phosphate, petrochemicals, pharmaceuticals, sovereign debt, insurance architecture fracture, and the nonlinear quadratic yield response that concentrates damage in the Global South. The market is pricing ~45-day disruption; structural indicators point to 120+ days. USDA Prospective Plantings (March 31) is the first hard data point for acreage shift quantification.

## Demand drivers

![[fertilizer-demand-caloric-intake-aurelion.png]]
*Global caloric intake per capita has maintained a steady upward trajectory for over three decades, creating a persistent baseline for fertilizer demand. Driven by population growth + shifts toward protein-rich diets and higher fruit/vegetable consumption in developing economies. Source: Aurelion Research.*

![[fertilizer-industrial-nox-demand-aurelion.png]]
*Secondary demand vector: industrial nitrogen demand from tightening NOx emissions regulations (SCR/AdBlue in diesel, power plant emissions controls) reinforces the agricultural floor. Source: Aurelion Research.*

The dual exposure — agricultural food-security demand plus industrial emissions-control demand — insulates overall nutrient consumption from isolated sector shocks and reinforces the case for sustained global capacity utilization.

## Investment considerations

### Bull case

- Food security is non-discretionary — population growth to ~10B by 2050 drives structural baseline demand
- Africa is a generational growth story (<20 kg/ha vs 50 kg/ha AU target)
- China export restrictions remove most Chinese urea from global markets, supporting non-Chinese producer pricing
- Russia/Belarus sanctions create structural supply uncertainty in potash
- Natural gas disruptions benefit NA producers with cheap Henry Hub gas (CF, Koch, Nutrien) vs European competitors
- LFP battery demand is diverting phosphate from fertilizer, tightening phosphate supply
- Latin America and South Asia adding 3-4M mt nutrient demand each through 2028

### Bear case

- Nitrogen overcapacity: global urea capacity ~240M mt vs ~185M mt demand. 11.6M mt new capacity in 2025-2026
- China re-entry risk: Beijing could resume full exports at any time (3.5-4M mt quota already approved for mid-2025)
- Natural gas volatility cuts both ways — low gas benefits producer costs but crushes nitrogen product pricing
- ESG pressure: agricultural N2O emissions up 40% since 1980. EU nitrogen application limits. Regulatory tightening could structurally reduce demand in developed markets
- Precision agriculture reduces per-acre application rates (only 27% of US farms use precision ag as of 2023 — adoption is early but directionally negative for volumes)
- Russian/Belarusian production recovery: Belarus potash +56% in 2024, Uralkali sales +28% — sanctions are leaking, removing scarcity premium

## Energy transition angle

Ammonia is emerging as an energy carrier beyond agriculture — shipping fuel, power plant co-firing (Japan), hydrogen transport medium. Blue/green ammonia projects are being developed by fertilizer incumbents ([[CF Industries]], [[Yara]], [[OCI]]) and energy majors. This could diversify fertilizer producers' revenue streams but also divert ammonia supply from agriculture. See [[Ammonia]], [[Green hydrogen]].
