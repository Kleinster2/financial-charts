**Commercial real estate** encompasses office, retail, industrial, multifamily, and hospitality properties — a ~$20T asset class in the US alone. The sector faces a structural reset: remote work permanently reduced office demand, e-commerce reshaped retail, and the 2022-2024 rate cycle crushed leveraged valuations across all sub-sectors. Over $1.5T in CRE debt matures by 2026, creating forced refinancing at higher rates and distressed sales.

## Key dynamics

- Rising rates crushed valuations 2022-2024; regional banks heavily exposed to CRE loans
- Office sector distressed — remote work permanent for many white-collar roles, vacancy rates ~20% nationally
- Industrial/logistics benefiting from e-commerce and reshoring trends
- Massive debt maturities creating forced sales, refinancing stress, and bank loss recognition
- Data center demand from AI buildout creating a new high-growth CRE sub-sector
- [[CMBS]] delinquency rates rising, especially in office-backed securities
