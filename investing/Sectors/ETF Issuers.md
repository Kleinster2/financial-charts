# ETF Issuers

Hub note mapping the US exchange-traded fund industry by issuer — from the Big Three passive giants to the niche alternative shops pushing the product frontier.

---

## Industry overview

US ETF assets hit **$13.4T** at year-end 2025 (global: $19.5T), after a record **$1.48T** in net inflows — up 34% from the prior annual record. The market added 1,110 new ETFs in 2025 alone (50% YoY increase), bringing the total to ~4,800 US-listed ETFs across 460+ issuers.

**Structural dynamics:**
- **Concentration:** Top 4 issuers control 80% of AUM and captured 65% of 2025 flows
- **Passive dominance:** 89% of AUM is passive, but 80%+ of new launches are active
- **Mutual fund migration:** Mutual funds saw $551B in outflows (Jan–Nov 2025) while ETFs took in $1.24T — the gap widened to $1.79T
- **Active ETF surge:** $475B in active ETF flows (32% of total), despite active being only 11% of AUM. See [[Active ETFs]]
- **Regulatory shift:** SEC approved ETF share class for mutual funds; capped leverage at 2x for new filings (Dec 2025), blocking 3x/4x/5x products

## Competitive dynamics

**The fee war is over at the top.** Vanguard won — average 0.08%. The Big Three compete on distribution, indexing rights (MSCI vs FTSE vs S&P), and retirement plan shelf space. Margins are thin.

**The fee war never started at the bottom.** Niche issuers charge 0.65%–1.50% and justify it through:
- First-mover on product types (2x single-stock leverage, 0DTE covered call)
- Exposure that can't be replicated cheaply (altcoin ETFs, drone pureplays)
- Yield packaging (covered call income funds marketed on distribution rate)

**Key battlegrounds (2025–2026):**

| Front | What's happening |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Active conversions** | Mutual fund → ETF share class (Capital Group, Dimensional, Fidelity) |
| **Single-stock leverage** | REX/Tuttle pioneered 2x; SEC capped at 2x, blocking 3x+ entrants |
| **Covered call income** | Explosive growth; JPM (JEPI $40B+), YieldMax, Defiance, REX, Roundhill all competing |
| **Crypto** | Post-SEC approval: spot BTC/ETH done, altcoins (XRP, SOL, DOGE) next wave |
| **Pre-IPO ETFs** | REX filed for leveraged SpaceX/Anthropic ETFs before IPO — new frontier |
| **International expansion** | REX launched FEPI UCITS via HANetF; others following |
| **0DTE options** | Roundhill's daily expiry covered call funds (XDTE, QDTE) growing fast |

## Revenue economics

ETF issuers earn management fees (expense ratio × AUM). Rough economics:

| Segment | Avg ER | AUM needed for $100M revenue | Margin |
|---------|--------|------------------------------|--------|
| Passive index | 0.10% | $100B | Thin — scale game |
| Active equity | 0.35% | $29B | Moderate |
| Leveraged/inverse | 1.00% | $10B | High — but regulatory risk |
| Covered call income | 0.80% | $12.5B | High — distribution rates attract retail |
| Crypto | 0.80% | $12.5B | High — but volatile AUM |

The niche issuers punch above their weight on revenue per dollar of AUM. REX's MicroSectors (1.34% ER on $11.8B) generates more fee revenue than many firms 3x its AUM.

## Insights

- The ETF industry is a **barbell**: massive passive oligopoly at the top (Big Three = 72% of AUM), and a Cambrian explosion of niche product factories at the bottom (460+ issuers competing for the remaining 28%)
- The real innovation is happening in the long tail — single-stock leverage, 0DTE income, pre-IPO bets, drone/defense thematics — all from firms with <$15B in AUM
- **Distribution rate marketing** is the covered call income sector's dirty secret: YieldMax, REX (FEPI/AIPI), and others advertise 25–35% yields that consist primarily of return of capital, not actual income
- SEC's 2x leverage cap (Dec 2025) is a moat for REX/Tuttle — they got there first, and no one can now launch 3x+ single-stock products to outcompete them
- The mutual fund → ETF migration is structural and accelerating: $551B out of mutual funds vs $1.48T into ETFs in 2025. Every traditional AM firm is now an ETF issuer or dying
