## Origins & how it works

Traces to 1980s insurance companies lending directly to corporates. Post-2008 regulations clamped down on risky bank lending â†’ private credit filled the gap. Low rates drove a global hunt for yield; PE firms bought 20,000+ companies from 2009-2021 (~$2.5T market value), all needing debt. Rising rates from 2022 turbocharged appetite further â€” floating-rate private credit suddenly yielded 12-13%. Stephen Schwarzman (2023): "If you are living in a no-growth economy and somebody can give you 12%, 13% with almost no prospect of loss, that's about the best thing you can do."

### Lending strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Direct lending** | Loans held to maturity, solo or small group |
| **Distressed debt** | Buy debt trading below par, profit on restructuring |
| **Venture debt** | Loans to pre-profit startups (revenue multiples) |
| **Mezzanine** | Higher-risk debt between senior loans and equity |
| **Special situations** | Event-driven (M&A, bankruptcy, spin-off) |

### Why borrowers choose private credit

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Credit flexibility** | Accommodates borrowers ineligible for bank loans |
| **Pricing certainty** | Set upfront (vs fluctuating syndicated loan pricing) |
| **Execution speed** | Easier than traded leveraged loans |
| **PIK option** | Payment-in-kind = defer interest payments |

### Traditional advantages over bank lending (eroding)

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Maintenance covenants** | Limits on leverage tested regularly |
| **Information access** | Non-public financials from borrowers |
| **Relationship lending** | Direct line to management |
| **Early warning** | Covenant breach = intervention trigger |

If borrower breaches covenants: demand equity injection from PE sponsor, force asset sales, require more collateral, or initiate restructuring. **These tools are being lost** â€” see covenant erosion below.

### Retail expansion: how we got here

Private credit was historically institutional-only. From 2020-2025, PE firms aggressively pushed semi-liquid vehicles to retail/HNW investors â€” an $80T capital pool.

| Firm | Product | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|

**The fundamental contradiction:**

- **What exists:** Illiquid loans to middle-market companies that can't be sold quickly without losses
- **What happens under stress:** Honoring redemptions benefits early redeemers at remaining investors' expense â†’ classic bank run dynamics â†’ gate

## Recession resilience â€” unknown

**Fed (May 2023):** Not worried, but lack of transparency makes systemic risk hard to assess.

**Private credit argument:** Capital locked in longer than bank deposits. Stronger underwriting. Floating rates adjust.

**Reality:** No one knows. Industry untested through major recession since its 2008+ growth. Covenant-lite trend removes early warning tools.
