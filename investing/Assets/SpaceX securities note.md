# SpaceX securities

SPCX is [[SpaceX]]'s [[Nasdaq]]-listed Class A common — the public instrument for the integrated launch / [[Starlink]] / AI stack that began trading Jun. 12, 2026 in the largest IPO ever. Two structural features separate the instrument from the operating company. First, it is a controlled, [[Dual-class shares|dual-class]] security: [[Elon Musk]]'s 10x-voting Class B leaves him with about 82.4% of the vote after the offering, so public Class A holders get economic exposure without control, and removal of Musk requires Class B approval. Second, the economic value and the investable instrument diverge by more than an order of magnitude — a roughly $2.1T full-company market value sits on top of only about $70-75B of public float (~4% of the company), because the base offering was 100% primary and lock-ups hold the rest off-market. That float-vs-full-cap wedge governs how SPCX gets weighted in indices, how it trades against its former public proxies, and how the price record evolves as lock-ups roll. The business, segments, and strategy live in [[SpaceX]]; the deal structure lives in [[SpaceX IPO 2026]]; this note carries the instrument, share structure, float schedule, and price record.

## Share structure

SPCX is the public sleeve of a two-class common structure built to lock in founder control before the listing rather than retrofit it afterward:

- Class A — the public, [[Nasdaq]]-listed line (SPCX), one vote per share, full economic interest. The base IPO sold 555,555,555 primary Class A shares (100% primary; no selling shareholders in the base deal).
- Class B — 10 votes per share, held by [[Elon Musk]]. After the offering Musk holds about 82.4% of total voting power (82.3% if the over-allotment is fully exercised) on roughly 40% of economic shares.

Three governance terms harden the control bargain beyond a normal founder-friendly IPO. Removal of Musk requires Class B approval, so public Class A holders cannot effect a removal under the bylaws as drafted. Mandatory arbitration governs all shareholder disputes, closing the Delaware-court path that voided Musk's [[Tesla]] pay package (the company re-incorporated in [[Texas]] after that ruling). And two [[Mars]]- and [[Space data centers|orbital-DC]]-contingent supervoting pay grants (up to ~1,000mn and ~302mn post-split Class B shares, keyed to $6.565T-$7.5T market-cap milestones) can further strengthen control if they ever vest — the S-1 recognized no expense for either because the milestones were deemed improbable. The board is nine directors; Musk is CEO and chair, with [[Gwynne Shotwell]] as president and COO. Full governance treatment in [[SpaceX IPO 2026]] and [[Dual-class shares]].

## Float and lock-up schedule

The instrument's defining number is not the ~$2.1T headline — it is the thin public float and the schedule on which it grows. The base deal floated ~$75B of stock (~4% of the company); [[FTSE Russell]] put investable market cap near $70B. Everything else is locked:

| Holder | Lock-up | Release |
|---|---|---|
| [[Elon Musk]] | 366 days | One year and one day after the IPO |
| Select investors / officers / directors | Staggered | Windows after Q4 2026 through Q2 2027 earnings |
| All other shares | Staggered | From after Q2 2026 earnings through 180 days post-IPO |

The schedule matters for trading because float growth is the variable that lets proxy substitution deepen. With only ~4% public float at debut, the new listing cannot fully absorb the demand that had been routed through public substitutes, so the scarcity premium migrates only partially (see trading character below). As the staged windows roll from late 2026 into 2027, tradable supply rises and the substitution can extend — which is why float growth, not just price, is the signal to watch on SPCX.

## Index inclusion mechanics

A pure [[Float-adjusted market capitalization|float-adjusted]] index would treat SPCX as roughly a $75B stock at entry despite the ~$2.1T economic value. The major index providers rewrote or reviewed their rules specifically to admit it without forcing buyers to chase thin float:

| Index family | Mechanic | SPCX read |
|---|---|---|
| [[Nasdaq 100]] | Fast Entry: full market cap ranks top-40; modified-cap weighting with low-float cap = lesser of full eligible listed cap or 3x free float | ~$225B modified weighting cap → roughly order-of-1% NDX weight, not a $2.1T megacap weight; inclusion ~15 trading days |
| [[FTSE Russell]] | Fast entry for Russell U.S. + FTSE GEIS; ~$70B investable cap clears the $17.5B Russell Top 500 and $13.5B GEIS thresholds | Entry possible after the 5th trading day; pulls in global and U.S. passive flow |
| [[S&P 500]] | 12-month seasoning rule under review; 50% public-float minimum unchanged | The float gate, not seasoning, is the binding constraint at ~4% float |

The practical effect: SPCX can be far larger in [[QQQ]] than in a pure total-market float product, but nowhere near a full-market-cap weight in either, and the [[S&P 500]] is gated by float rather than by the headline valuation. Full rule detail and source filings in [[SpaceX IPO 2026]].

## Brazil access — SPCX34 BDR

A Brazil-resident investor does not need an offshore account to hold SPCX. [[B3]] admitted an unsponsored Level I [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts]] receipt, ticker SPCX34, on Jun. 12, 2026 — the same session as the [[Nasdaq]] debut. The 34 suffix marks it não patrocinado: a Brazilian depositary created the receipt off the US listing without [[SpaceX]]'s participation, so it carries lighter disclosure and no foreign-issuer registration.

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Ticker | SPCX34 (on [[B3]]) |
| Type | Unsponsored Level I [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts]] |
| Paridade | 1:15 — one [[Nasdaq]] SPCX share = 15 SPCX34 BDRs |
| Listing | Jun. 12, 2026 (same day as the IPO) |
| Early quote | ~R$54.74 vs ~R$675 for a full share at the $135 IPO price; B3 cited an R$50–70 band |
| Settlement | Reais, through a local home broker — no câmbio |

The receipt tracks SPCX one-for-fifteen, adjusted by USD/BRL, so a SPCX34 holder is long the company and short the real in one position. Three frictions matter. The BDR carries no vote — moot here, since public Class A already has almost none against [[Elon Musk]]'s ~82.4% Class B control. Local liquidity in a one-session-old receipt sitting on a ~4% float will be thin, with wider spreads than the [[Nasdaq]] line. And the Brazilian tax treatment is worse than for local shares: 15% on any gain with no R$20k/month isenção (detail in [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts#Taxation for a Brazil resident|BDR tax]]). The alternative is to buy SPCX directly offshore through an international broker ([[Interactive Brokers]], [[BTG Pactual]] international, [[Avenue Securities|Avenue]], [[Banco Inter|Inter]], [[Nomad]]) in USD — [[BTG Pactual]] was itself an international co-manager on the offering. Full instrument mechanics in [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts]].

## Trading character — proxy unwind and the float wedge

SPCX debuted as the new primary instrument for a theme that had been traded for years through substitutes, and the first session showed the substitution mechanically. SpaceX closed up 19% while every public space proxy fell on an up-tape (+0.54% [[SPY]]): [[EchoStar]] (SATS) -10.97% / -3.38σ, [[AST SpaceMobile]] (ASTS) -15.53% / -2.70σ, [[Intuitive Machines]] (LUNR) -13.12%, [[Rocket Lab]] (RKLB) -10.79%, and [[Redwire]] (RDW) -11.53%. The scarcity premium those names carried for being "the only way in" migrated into SPCX itself. See [[SpaceX debut proxy unwind]].

![[spacex-debut-proxy-unwind.png]]
*Five public space proxies normalized to 100 on Apr. 15, 2026 (SPCX is one session old and not shown). The synchronized Jun. 12 cliff is the cohort SPCX now sits atop: with the clean instrument finally listed, holders rotated out of the substitutes. The thin SPCX float means the substitution is incomplete — the proxy bid can keep draining as lock-ups roll, or stabilize if float stays scarce.*

As a trading object, SPCX is the consolidated [[SpaceX]] / [[xAI]] / [[Twitter|X]] issuer, not a clean launch-and-[[Starlink]] play: 2025 revenue of $18.674B arrived with a $4.937B net loss, [[Connectivity]] is the profit engine ($11.387B revenue, $4.423B operating income) while the AI segment is the loss-and-optionality layer ($3.201B revenue, $6.355B operating loss). At the debut valuation the instrument trades near 91-100x trailing sales versus [[NVIDIA]] around 21-24x, so the public buyer is underwriting [[Starship]] cadence, orbital-compute optionality, and Musk capital allocation, not a current-earnings multiple. The [[Reuters]] base-rate caution applies directly to this instrument: high-valuation IPOs beat the [[S&P 500]] only about a quarter of the time over the prior five years, and Musk's >20% retail allocation widens participation at exactly that hurdle.
