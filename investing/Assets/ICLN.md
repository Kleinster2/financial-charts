# ICLN

The [[BlackRock]] (iShares) Global Clean Energy ETF — the most-referenced broad clean-energy benchmark. Tracks the S&P Global Clean Energy Index: a global basket of ~130 solar, wind, fuel-cell, and clean-utility names across the US, Europe, China, India, and Latin America. Inception June 2008; expense ratio 0.41%; ~$3.1B AUM.

Its character matters for the vault's cluster work: ICLN is solar-led (it correlates 0.86 with solar TAN) but globally diversified, and crucially it *holds the listed hydrogen names* — [[Bloom Energy|BE]] is its single largest position (~13.8%) and [[Plug Power|PLUG]] is a top-10 holding. That is why the [[Hydrogen economy#Cluster validation|hydrogen pure-play cohort]] resolves to ICLN (a negative intra-advantage — the names correlate with the ETF more than with each other): the hydrogen names ARE a chunk of ICLN. ICLN is the clean-energy ETF that the [[Hydrogen economy|hydrogen]], [[Solar]], and (loosely) [[Nuclear renaissance|nuclear]] cohorts all sit inside — a clean-energy-speculative beta that has had a brutal half-decade since the 2020–21 bubble.
