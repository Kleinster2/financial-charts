## April 2026 read

March's Iran-war selloff briefly pushed SK Hynix back into the bucket of energy-sensitive Korean cyclicals. By late April the stock was again being priced as AI infrastructure. The rebound was helped by two linked signals: the Korea tape stabilized as the oil shock faded, and SK Hynix's SOCAMM2 rollout for [[NVIDIA]]'s Vera Rubin platform reinforced the view that the company can widen from HBM into adjacent server-memory modules.

That leaves the stock trading on a simple question. Is SK Hynix just in the best part of a richer memory cycle, or has the company earned a more durable valuation because customers are now buying qualified AI-memory supply rather than commodity bits? The Korea discount narrows when scarcity and qualification wins dominate. It widens whenever investors slip back into treating the name as pure cyclical beta.
