# Aura Minerals securities

AUGO is [[Aura Minerals]]'s [[Nasdaq]]-listed common stock, the US trading line for the Americas gold-copper producer since its July 2025 IPO. The same economic interest also trades in reais on [[B3]] as the sponsored Level III [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts|BDR]] AURA33, which has been Aura's primary listing since 2020. The business, operations, treasury strategy, and financials live in [[Aura Minerals]]; this note carries the instrument — the dual listing, share structure and control, price record, and the peer comparison.

## Share structure and control

AUGO/AURA33 is a controlled company: founder-Chairman Paulo Carlos de Brito holds the majority economic and voting stake through Northwestern Enterprises Ltd., so public holders take economic exposure without control. Unlike a [[Dual-class shares|dual-class]] structure, control rests on a straightforward majority holding rather than supervoting shares. As a British-Virgin-Islands issuer, Aura is a foreign private issuer with the [[SEC]] (6-K interim, 20-F annual, F-1 for the IPO) and reports under IFRS — which is why the [[Aura Minerals#Financials|net line]] carries large non-cash swap/FX remeasurement.

## Dual listing — AUGO and AURA33

Aura is one of the cleaner cases of the Brazilian-listing-plus-US-listing path. AURA33 — a sponsored Level III [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts|BDR]] — was its primary trading line and a genuine fundraising vehicle on [[B3]] from 2020; the July 2025 [[Nasdaq]] IPO (AUGO) added a US and global institutional base, index eligibility, and the public-equity currency for the MSG/Serra Grande acquisition. The two lines track the same economic interest, bridged by USD/BRL. Instrument mechanics for the Brazilian line are in [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts]].
