## Price context

## June 2026 equity raise and market reaction

[[Alphabet]]'s Jun 1 2026 plan to raise up to $80B in equity adds a new market-facing variable to GOOGL / GOOG: AI capex is no longer being funded only through operating cash flow, debt, leases, buyback deferral, and externalized project vehicles. The announced stack is a $15B Class A / Class C common-stock offering, $15B of depositary shares representing mandatory convertible preferred stock, a $40B at-the-market Class A / Class C program, and a $10B [[Berkshire Hathaway]] private placement.

Market-reaction status: Jun 2 regular-session close was not available as of 12:31 ET during ingestion. FT reported a more-than-2% after-hours decline after the announcement, but that is not a verified closing move and should not be used as the final tape. The daily note logs an after-close follow-up for GOOGL / GOOG, BRK.A / BRK.B, QQQ, XLC, and XLK.

The pricing question is whether investors treat the issuance as capacity-positive and valuation-dilutive at the same time. The bull reading is that Google Cloud demand and TPU capacity need are large enough to justify every funding source. The bear reading is that the old "ad cash funds AI without dilution" frame is broken; share count now competes with AI duration.

*Sources: [FT - Alphabet to sell $80bn in stock to fund AI spending spree](https://www.ft.com/content/341f151b-f472-4530-8579-d4b803519257), Jun 1 2026; [[Cleary Gottlieb]] (Jun 2 2026); Alphabet [[SEC]] 424B5 supplements, Jun 2 2026.*
