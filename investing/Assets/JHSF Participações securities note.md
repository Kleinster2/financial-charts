# JHSF Participações securities note

Tradeable instrument for [[JHSF Participações]] — common shares (ON) listed on the [[B3|São Paulo Bovespa]] under ticker JHSF3, the sole listed security of the company. No ADR program; US institutional access via [[B3]] direct trading only. The share has been one of the strongest performers in the Brazilian real-estate cluster over the 12 months to May 2026 (+122% YoY), but corrected ~24% from its April 22, 2026 all-time high through the May 13 [[Brazil Week NYC 2026]] sell-off window.

## Synthesis

The +122% YoY move with +41% YTD makes JHSF3 a structural-rerating case rather than a cyclical bounce (inference — the move size is too large for pure rate-cycle compression). Three load-bearing factors (inference):

1. Q3 2025 operational inflection (net profit more than doubled; +77% EBITDA growth) plus visible recurring-income trajectory toward the R$1B 2026 target
2. The asset-management arm (JHSF Capital) creating a path to US institutional capital that would be re-rate-worthy if it lands
3. Affluent-Brazilian-consumer-as-defensive thesis — the customer base is less rate-sensitive than broader Brazilian retail, and luxury demand has held up better than mass-market through the Selic peak cycle

The April-May 2026 correction (-24% from ATH) is the cleanest near-term risk gauge. Drivers of the pullback (inference):

- Election-cycle volatility opening 6-9 months ahead of October 2026 first round
- The [[2026 Brazil presidential election|Flávio/Vorcaro Dark Horse]] scandal week (May 13-15) and the BRL weakness that came with it
- BRL/USD pressure from the Iran-war global bond rout — the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|May 15 long-end repricing]] hit EM credit and currency channels alongside the property/macro thread
- Profit-taking after the April peak — the move was substantial, valuation extension visible

What to watch: BrazilCham gala recognition + JHSF Capital US-fundraising flow as the upside catalyst; election-cycle BRL/Selic dynamics as the downside catalyst; the recurring-income hit-rate vs the R$1B 2026 target as the operational tell.
