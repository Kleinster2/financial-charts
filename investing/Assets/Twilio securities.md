# Twilio securities

TWLO is [[Twilio]]'s NYSE-listed common equity. The stock is the public-market expression of the CPaaS / customer-engagement infrastructure thesis: whether usage-based communications APIs become a durable AI-agent traffic beneficiary or get compressed by AI-native customer-service platforms.

## Earnings reactions

| Event | Date | Key numbers | Stock reaction |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------|
| Q1 2026 | Apr 30 / May 1 2026 | Revenue $1.41B (+20% reported, +16% organic), non-GAAP EPS $1.50 vs $1.27 consensus, non-GAAP operating income $279M (+31%), raised FY2026 revenue / op income / FCF guides | TWLO closed $183.34 on May 1, +23.8% from Apr 30 |

The May 2026 move matters because it was not just an earnings beat. It gave the market a clean answer to the [[AI SaaS Disruption]] question: AI agents can cannibalize seat-based software, but they can also drive more API calls through communications infrastructure. [[Sierra]], [[Bland.ai]], and [[KPN]] were the customer-win proof points that Twilio's platform can sit underneath the new voice-agent layer.
