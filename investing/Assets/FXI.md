# FXI

iShares China Large-Cap ETF — tracks the FTSE China 50 Index, providing exposure to the 50 largest Chinese stocks trading on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (H-shares and Red Chips). Includes mainland tech giants like [[Alibaba]], [[Tencent]], and [[Meituan]] alongside the big state banks and energy majors — internet-heavy but broader than [[KWEB]] (the two correlate 0.94).

In the vault's cluster-validation work FXI is the factor anchor for two Chinese-ADR cohorts. It is the ETF that [[Chinese EV startups]] actually track — China-country beta dominates the EV-sector beta, so [[NIO]]/[[Xpeng|XPEV]]/[[Li Auto|LI]] correlate with FXI as much as with each other — and one of the China ETFs that absorbs the [[Chinese internet]] ADR cohort. Both are cases of a Chinese ADR cohort resolving to its China ETF rather than to its US business-model twins.
