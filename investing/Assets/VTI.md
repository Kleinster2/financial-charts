# VTI

**VTI** is the [[Vanguard]] Total Stock Market ETF — broad US equity exposure across large, mid, small, and micro caps. Tracks the CRSP US Total Market Index. One of the largest ETFs by AUM globally.

VTI is the clean broad-US-equity baseline for the vault: it answers what a float-adjusted total-market investor can actually own, making it useful when comparing low-float mega-IPO mechanics, S&P 500 exposure, and Nasdaq 100 rule changes. The main signal is not stock selection but whether broad investable-market weighting diverges from full-market-cap narratives in founder-controlled listings.

## SpaceX low-float IPO read-through (Jun. 2026)

The [[SpaceX IPO 2026]] is a clean example of why [[Float-adjusted market capitalization]] matters for total-market products. At the Jun. 4 roadshow terms, [[SpaceX]] is offering 555,555,555 Class A shares at an expected $135/share, or about $75B of public float against a roughly $1.75T-$1.77T company value. For a broad float-adjusted total-market [[index]], the initial investable weight is therefore in the low-tenths-of-a-percent zone, not the multi-percent weight implied by full company value.

This differs from the [[Nasdaq 100]], which now uses a hybrid low-float cap. [[Nasdaq]] can weight a low-float security off up to three times free-floating shares, so the same [[SpaceX]] base float could become about $225B of modified Nasdaq 100 weighting market cap before ordinary NDX constraints. VTI-style broad-market exposure is the cleaner "what can public investors actually buy?" lens; [[QQQ]] is the "large enough to represent, but capped by float" lens.
