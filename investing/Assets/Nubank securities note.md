# Nubank securities

NU is [[Nubank]]'s (Nu Holdings Ltd.) NYSE-listed Class A common stock, the public instrument for Latin America's largest digital bank. The same economic interest trades in reais on [[B3]] as the unsponsored Level I [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts|BDR]] ROXO34 (one BDR equals one-sixth of a Class A share). Two structural features define it. First, it is a controlled, [[Dual-class shares|dual-class]] security: founder [[David Vélez]]'s 20-votes-per-share Class B leaves him with roughly 74% of the vote on about 20% of the economics, so public Class A holders get economic exposure without control. Second, it is a Cayman-incorporated [[Foreign private issuer]] that files 6-K interims under IFRS, so it carries the same reporting cadence as the other foreign issuers in the vault. The business, customers, and strategy live in [[Nubank]]; this note carries the instrument, share structure, and price record.

## Share structure and control

NU is a controlled company built on a two-class common structure. Class A (the public, NYSE line) carries one vote; Class B, held by founder and CEO [[David Vélez]], carries 20 votes — leaving Vélez with roughly 74% of the voting power on about 20% of the economic shares. Public holders therefore take economic exposure to Latin America's largest neobank without meaningful control, the same controller-risk frame as the vault's other [[Dual-class shares|dual-class]] names.

As a Grand Cayman issuer, Nu Holdings reports to the [[SEC]] as a [[Foreign private issuer]] — 6-K interims, 20-F annual, IFRS — so the standardized quarterly vendor stream is absent and the cleanest figures come from the filings. [[Berkshire Hathaway]] anchored the 2021 IPO with a $500M cheque (it has since trimmed to ~1.8%), and the company approved a $1B buyback in 2026, an early capital-return signal for a still-compounding grower.
