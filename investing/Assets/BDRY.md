# BDRY

BDRY (Breakwave Dry Bulk Shipping ETF) is a US commodity-pool ETF launched March 2018 that holds near-dated freight futures (FFAs) on dry bulk shipping routes published by the [[Baltic Exchange]] — Capesize, Panamax, and Supramax. It was the first pure-play dry bulk shipping ETF and served as the template for the later [[BWET]] tanker fund. Sub-advised by [[Breakwave Advisors]] ([[John Kartsonas]]) and sponsored by [[Amplify ETFs]] since December 28, 2023 (previously ETF Managers Capital).

BDRY gives retail and smaller institutional investors direct exposure to the cost of moving iron ore, coal, grain, and fertilizers by sea — commodities that together define the physical tonnage underlying the global industrial cycle.

---

## Synthesis

The 2021-22 dry bulk boom was BDRY's defining moment and remains the most important precedent for interpreting the later [[BWET]] run. Starting from $8.47 on January 4, 2021, BDRY rose 390% to $41.51 on October 6, 2021 as the Baltic Dry Index (BDI) hit multi-year highs on pandemic-era supply chain disruption, Chinese infrastructure demand, and port congestion. By the end of 2022, the fund closed at $9.19 — having given back essentially all of the 2021 gain in 14 months as rates normalized, congestion cleared, and the commodity boom faded.

The lesson embedded in that round-trip: shipping rates are cyclical, not structural. The 2021 BDRY holder who bought at $15 in early 2021 and sold at $40 in October was correct on the trade. The holder who held from $15 through to late 2022 gave back everything. [[John Kartsonas]] has cited this history explicitly during the [[BWET]] 2026 run to caution against treating an 8x move as a durable regime change rather than a cyclical spike.

## Holdings and structure

| Allocation | Class | Routes |
|------------|-------|--------|
| ~50% | Capesize | Iron ore, coal long-haul (Brazil/Australia → China) |
| ~40% | Panamax | Grain, coal, minor bulks |
| ~10% | Supramax | Smaller cargoes, minor bulks |

Contract roll: near-dated FFAs, rolled monthly. Like [[BWET]], BDRY is subject to contango drag in normal markets and backwardation boost when spot rates spike above the forward curve.

Pool structure: commodity pool (not '40 Act fund). Expense ratio 3.50%.

## The 2021-22 cycle — BWET's playbook

| Date | BDRY close | Return from Jan 2021 |
|------|------------|----------------------|
| Jan 4, 2021 | $8.47 | — |
| Oct 6, 2021 (peak) | $41.51 | +390% |
| Dec 30, 2022 | $9.19 | +8% |

Drivers up: post-pandemic demand rebound, iron ore at ~$230/tonne peaks, Chinese steel production, port congestion tying up [[Capesize]] capacity, fuel spike under IMO 2020 rules.

Drivers down: Chinese property slowdown (Evergrande), steel output cuts, port congestion normalizing, commodity prices rolling over, forward curve flattening.

The full round trip took less than two years and erased every gain for holders who did not sell. This is the base case Kartsonas warns against for [[BWET]] in 2026.
