## May 2026 short-thesis trigger

[[Jason's Chips]] argues the original small-cap setup was defensible but broke after the stock's roughly 20x rerating. The source's central tape point: Sivers is being priced as if it already controls a CPO bottleneck, while the disclosures still show development work, opportunity-pipeline language, and no public data-center photonics volume order.

Three separations matter for the securities note:

| Market narrative | Reality check |
|------------------|--------------|
| "Talks with every optics startup" | Conversations and JDAs are not backlog |
| "CPO bottleneck" | Bottlenecks require qualified supply, hard assets, and share |
| "Photonics scarcity" | Scarcity may accrue to proven InP manufacturers and UHP laser suppliers such as [[Lumentum]] |

## What to watch

- Q1 2026 report and any reset of revenue quality after the May 2026 stock move.
- Whether opportunity pipeline converts into signed production orders.
- Any disclosure of Sivers' role, volume, price, and timing in an actual CPO deployment.
- Insider selling and lock-up expiries after the Apr/May 2026 capital raise.
- Whether U.S. access through Nasdaq listing changes liquidity without changing fundamentals.
