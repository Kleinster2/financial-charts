# AST SpaceMobile securities

ASTS is [[AST SpaceMobile]]'s Nasdaq-listed Class A common — the clean public instrument for the direct-to-cell satellite thesis. Two structural features separate the instrument from the operating company: it is a Nasdaq "controlled company" under founder-CEO [[Abel Avellan]]'s super-voting Class C stock, and it sits atop an Up-C structure whose operating-company (OpCo) units put a large non-controlling interest between consolidated results and per-Class-A-share economics. The stock trades as a high-beta space pure-play and one of the cleanest public proxies for [[SpaceX]] / space-sector sentiment, so its tape is frequently driven by cohort and IPO-cycle flows rather than name-specific fundamentals.

## Share structure

ASTS carries three classes of common stock plus the Up-C operating-company units:

- Class A — the public, Nasdaq-listed line (ASTS), one vote per share, full economic interest in the public company.
- Class B — one vote per share, held by certain pre-merger strategic holders; paired with OpCo units (no separate economic interest in the public company).
- Class C — super-voting stock held by founder-CEO [[Abel Avellan]]. The "Class C Share Voting Amount" formula targets up to 88.31% of total voting power (net of Avellan's other controlled stock, divided across Class C shares outstanding), giving Avellan voting control until a sunset date.

Avellan and his permitted transferees control roughly 71.6% of voting power, so AST SpaceMobile qualifies as a Nasdaq controlled company and relies on the controlled-company governance exemption. The Up-C structure means consolidated net loss is split between the public company and a large non-controlling interest (the OpCo units): Q1 2026 allocated $58.6M of the $249.6M pre-NCI net loss to NCI, leaving $191.0M attributable to Class A common. Per-share economics therefore track the Class A slice, not the whole consolidated entity.

## Capital structure and trading character

The balance sheet is convertible-heavy: AST SpaceMobile funds the constellation build with convertible notes (including a 2032 series), and Q1 2026 carried a ~$100.5M "other expense" line driven largely by induced-conversion expense on 2032-note repurchases plus FX. Pro forma cash was ~$3.5B at the Q1 print, against a ~$700M/yr burn — a roughly 7–8 quarter runway that frames recurring capital raises (and dilution) as part of the instrument's forward profile.

As a trading object, ASTS is a high-vol, high-beta (~3.3) member of the [[Space pure-plays]] cohort and the only scaled public direct-to-cell pure-play, which makes it the cleanest single-name proxy for the [[SpaceX IPO 2026|SpaceX IPO]] / public-space scarcity premium. That proxy status cuts both ways: it rallied on SpaceX-IPO anticipation (May 27, +8.3%) and sold off when the primary listing arrived (the Jun. 12 [[SpaceX debut proxy unwind]], -15.5% / -2.7σ) even as SpaceX itself closed up 19%. The business and strategy live in [[AST SpaceMobile]]; this note carries the instrument, share structure, and price record.
