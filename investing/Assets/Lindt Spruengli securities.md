#asset #equity #swiss

Lindt & Sprüngli has two listed equity instruments on the SIX Swiss Exchange:

- LISN (registered share) — full voting rights, CHF par value, one of the highest nominal share prices in global markets (~CHF 106,600 as of Apr 15, 2026)
- LISP (participation certificate) — no voting rights, dividend rights equivalent to registered share, at substantially lower per-unit price; higher free float

See [[Lindt Spruengli]] for company profile. See [[Cocoa]] for the commodity dynamics that drive LISN earnings volatility through cocoa butter input cost.

## Why the high share price

Lindt has never executed a traditional stock split on its registered shares. Management's historical rationale: maintaining a high share price targets long-term holders and limits short-term speculative trading; the LISP participation certificate provides a lower price point and broader float for income-oriented investors.
