## Day-1 read

The print resolved the demand side of the underwriting tension that [[Cerebras IPO revival April 2026]] laid out, but did so on the most aggressive AI-infrastructure mark in the public market today. Three structural features of the session:

1. Volume exceeded 100% of float — 32.68M shares traded against 30M offered. Every IPO share changed hands at least once before the close. This is the opposite of a tight-float squeeze; the demand-supply imbalance the $185 → $350 open captured was real, not mechanical scarcity.
2. Intraday range $86.27 = 27.7% from the low. CBRS traded a full 28% range from $300.07 to $386.34, meaning intraday volatility printed at levels normally associated with single-name event-driven names, not Day-1 IPO equity. That is the cleanest read on how unsettled the valuation question is — even after pricing cleared.
3. The close landed in the lower third of the range. Open $350, high $386.34, close $311.07. The close-vs-high gap of $75.27 = 19.5% means the price-discovery process moved the marginal-buyer estimate lower as the session went on. The aftermarket question for May 15 is whether that drift continues.

The post-market $329.57 print muddles the close-of-day fade narrative: buyers absorbed back to +78% vs offer once daytime trading ended. The first independent confirmation comes Friday May 15 — whether the print holds in regular hours with the IPO-allocation cohort no longer the only relevant buyer.
