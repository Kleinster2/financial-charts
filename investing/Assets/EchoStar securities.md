#securities #satellite #telecom

**EchoStar securities** — SATS (Nasdaq) went from $12.14 (Feb 2024 low, post-Dish re-merger disgust) to $128.57 (Jan 2026 ATH) — a 10.6x move in 23 months, driven entirely by spectrum-for-equity deals that transformed a debt-laden satellite TV company into the highest-leverage public [[SpaceX]] proxy. The stock doesn't trade on operating fundamentals (adjusted net loss ~$1B/year, CCC+ credit) — it trades on the implied value of ~51.9M SpaceX Class A shares and the probability those shares actually arrive. Two dynamics to track: the SpaceX implied value per SATS share (currently ~$170 at $1.75T IPO vs $112 stock price) and the deal-closing risk (FCC/DOJ approval, targeted ~Nov 2027).

The price chain traces SATS through five distinct regimes: boring satellite infrastructure company → Dish re-merger → debt crisis → spectrum monetization → SpaceX proxy.

The inflection was Aug 26, 2025. Before the AT&T deal, SATS was a $30 stock with $26B in debt that nobody wanted. The AT&T cash solved the near-term debt wall, and the SpaceX equity deals solved the valuation story. Within four months (Aug-Dec 2025), SATS went from $30 to $104 — a 3.5x move — on deal announcements alone, not operating improvements.

The Jan 2026 ATH at $128.57 marked peak optimism: SpaceX secondaries at $588/share, IPO reports swirling, S&P 500 inclusion announced. Everything since has been the market digesting execution risk — FCC approval timeline, debt maturity mismatch, and whether SpaceX actually IPOs at $1.75T.

## Relative value — SpaceX implied value

SATS trades as a SpaceX proxy. The key metric is the implied SpaceX value embedded in the SATS stock price vs the standalone value of legacy operations.

### Implied value framework

| Component | Value per SATS share | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| SpaceX stake (~51.9M shares at $1.75T) | ~$170 | $49B / 288.9M SATS shares |
| SpaceX stake (~51.9M shares at $1.25T) | ~$121 | $35B / 288.9M shares |
| AT&T cash (net of debt) | ~($10) | $23B cash - ~$26B debt + SpaceX $8.5B cash = residual |
| Legacy operations | ~($5-10) | Negative value: $1B/year adjusted loss, declining subs |
| Current SATS price | $112 | |

At $112, the market is pricing SpaceX at roughly $1.2-1.4T implied — a ~20-30% discount to the $1.75T IPO target. The discount reflects:
1. Deal-closing risk (FCC/DOJ, ~Nov 2027)
2. SpaceX IPO valuation uncertainty
3. Holding-company discount (debt, legacy losses)
4. Time value — shares don't arrive for ~18 months

## Short interest

21.5% of float sold short at the time of S&P 500 inclusion (Mar 23, 2026) — one of the most-shorted names in the S&P 500. The short thesis:
- SpaceX deal hasn't closed; FCC/DOJ risk is real
- Legacy business generates ~$1B adjusted loss/year
- Forward EPS -$52.93 (deeply unprofitable)
- Stock up 10.6x from lows on deal announcements, not earnings
- If SpaceX IPO is delayed or values below expectations, the entire thesis collapses

The long vs short is a pure bet on deal-closing probability and SpaceX valuation. There is no fundamental equity value story beyond the SpaceX stake.
