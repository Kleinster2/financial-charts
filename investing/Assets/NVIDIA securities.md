#securities #semiconductors #ai

**NVIDIA securities** — NVDA (NASDAQ) went from $11.21 (Oct 2022 trough) to $207.03 (Oct 2025 ATH) — an 18.5x move in 36 months, the largest market cap expansion in stock market history. Now the world's most valuable company at ~$4.0T (Mar 2026), down ~20% from peak. The defining AI trade: NVDA *is* the benchmark against which every AI investment is measured. Two dynamics to track: the absolute price (what NVDA does on its own) and the relative price (NVDA against SMH, the hyperscaler complex, and the inference challengers).

The key inflection was May 25, 2023. Before that earnings call, NVDA was a $750B gaming/data center company. After it, NVDA became the AI infrastructure monopolist. The stock went from $30 to $207 in 29 months — a 7x move on a company that was already $750B. Nothing comparable exists in large-cap history.

The October 2025 peak at $207 marked the moment when every possible tailwind was priced in simultaneously: Blackwell at scale, China H200 approved, Groq acquired, $500B+ backlog, hyperscaler capex guides rising. Everything after has been the market digesting what could go wrong — inference competition, tariffs, deployment vs shipment gap.

## Relative value

### NVDA / SMH ratio

NVDA's beta to SMH has historically been ~1.2-1.4x (the dominant semiconductor name but not the highest-beta). The ratio tracks whether NVDA is leading or lagging the sector.

| Date | NVDA | SMH | Raw NVDA/SMH | Context |
|------|------|-----|-------------|---------|
| Oct 2022 | $11.21 | ~$81 | 0.14 | Crypto trough — NVDA lagging sector on gaming collapse |
| May 2023 | $37.95 | ~$131 | 0.29 | Post-earnings explosion — NVDA re-rates vs sector |
| Jun 2024 | $135.52 | ~$261 | 0.52 | Peak ratio — NVDA pulling SMH up |
| Jan 2025 | $149.39 | ~$271 | 0.55 | Slight widening — NVDA still dominant |
| Oct 2025 | $207.03 | ~$335 | 0.62 | ATH ratio — maximum NVDA premium |
| Mar 2026 | $165.17 | ~$343 | 0.48 | Compressing — sector holding up, NVDA falling |

The ratio went from 0.14 to 0.62 — a 4.4x relative move. NVDA went from ~14% of SMH's price to ~62%. The compression from 0.62 to 0.48 since Oct 2025 reflects the inference competition narrative: the sector (SMH) is holding up because memory and packaging names are rallying, but NVDA specifically is derating on margin compression fears.

### NVDA vs hyperscaler customers

The relative pricing against the hyperscaler complex (META, MSFT, GOOG, AMZN) tracks a different dynamic: whether the market values the picks-and-shovels supplier or the application layer more.

In 2023-2024, NVDA dramatically outperformed all four hyperscalers. In 2025-2026, the relationship is inverting: hyperscalers are holding up better because they have diversified revenue and visible AI monetization paths, while NVDA faces the cyclicality question — what happens when the capex cycle peaks?

| Event | Date | Key numbers | Stock reaction |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------|
| Q1 FY24 | May 25, 2023 | $7.2B DC guide vs $5.2B est | +24% — the day AI investing became real |

| Q4 FY26 | Feb 25, 2026 | $68.1B rev, $78B Q1 guide (+7.4% beat) | +3.5% AH, settled +0.7%. Beat-and-fade |

Pattern: earnings reactions have compressed as the stock got larger. The +24% day in May 2023 was NVDA at $750B. By Feb 2026 at $4.8T, a 7% guidance beat barely moved the stock. The market has shifted from pricing discovery (what is AI worth?) to pricing sustainability (how long does this last?).
