## April 2026 read

Goldman's Q1 2026 print exposed the main pricing tension in the stock. EPS and revenue beat, equities hit a record, advisory rebounded sharply, and capital return remained large. But investors focused on the 10% FICC decline because peers posted double-digit FICC growth in the same quarter. The market is effectively saying Goldman deserves a premium only if its most risk-sensitive franchise wins in the volatile quarters that should be its natural habitat.

That leaves GS trading on two linked questions. First, was the April underperformance just a one-quarter positioning miss tied to the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran shock]] and the rates desk, or evidence that the franchise is less repeatable than the market assumed? Second, can Goldman compound book value and buybacks fast enough that episodic trading misses stop mattering? The stock will keep reflecting that debate.
