# Fisher-Price

[[Mattel]]'s infant, toddler, and preschool brand, one of the most recognized toy names globally but in structural decline as screen time captures ever-younger children.

## Synopsis

Fisher-Price has been a cornerstone of [[Mattel]]'s portfolio since Mattel acquired the company in 1993, but the brand faces headwinds that no product refresh can fully address. Birth rates are declining across developed markets, and the 0-3 age segment -- Fisher-Price's sweet spot -- is increasingly occupied by tablets and streaming content rather than physical toys. [[Mattel]] reported persistent declines in the Infant, Toddler, Preschool segment through 2023-2024, with Fisher-Price underperforming the broader toy category.

The brand still carries enormous recognition and trust with parents, which gives it optionality for licensing, content partnerships, and premium product lines. [[Mattel]]'s broader strategy under CEO Ynon Kreiz has been to treat its brands as IP franchises (the [[Barbie]] movie model), and Fisher-Price has content plays including a Netflix show and YouTube presence. But the core economics are challenging: Fisher-Price products are low-ASP, high-SKU, and compete against both private-label alternatives and digital substitutes. For [[Mattel]] investors, Fisher-Price is a drag on margins that is partially offset by its contribution to the company's preschool distribution network.
