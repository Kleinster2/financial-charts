# B300

[[NVIDIA]]'s [[Blackwell]] Ultra refresh of the [[B200]], targeted at AI training and inference at hyperscaler scale. The reference server packages eight B300 GPUs in a single chassis sold by [[NVIDIA]] partners including [[Super Micro Computer|Supermicro]], [[Foxconn]], and [[Dell]]. B300 is restricted from sale to [[China]] under U.S. [[Export controls]].

## China pricing dynamic (Apr 2026)

[[Reuters]] reported on Apr 30, 2026 that B300 server prices in [[China]] doubled to roughly $1M as the [[Yih-Shyan Liaw]] / [[Super Micro Computer|Supermicro]] indictment (Mar 19, 2026) and a broader smuggling crackdown dried up grey-market supply. The U.S. list price for the same eight-GPU server is about $550,000, putting the [[China]] premium near 80%. Some Chinese buyers shifted to renting at up to 190,000 yuan/month on one-year contracts.

The pricing signal is the cleanest live read on:

- The reach of [[Export controls]] enforcement after the [[SEA chip diversion]] route narrowed
- Chinese AI demand intensity — Chinese models lifted global token-usage share from 5% to 32% between March 2025 and March 2026
- The [[Law of one price]] retreat already documented for [[B200]] (~50% China premium) extending to the next-generation chip at a wider gap

See [[NVIDIA#April 30, 2026: B300 server prices near $1M in China as smuggling crackdown bites]] for full context.
