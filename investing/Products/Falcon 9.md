**Falcon 9** — [[SpaceX]]'s reusable medium/heavy-lift launch vehicle. The most-flown rocket in history. ~700 cumulative flights with 99% success rate as of Q1 2026, averaging approximately 1 launch every 2 days through 2025. List price raised to $74M in Q1 2026 (the fourth increase since commercial launches began at $61M); rideshare moved $6,000 → $7,000/kg. The pricing increases function as confidence signals that SpaceX is capacity-constrained by *internal* demand from [[Starlink]], [[Direct to Cell]], and orbital data center constellation deployment.

## Pricing history

| Increase | Period | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Initial commercial pricing | ~2009 | $61M |
| 1st increase | 2018 | $62M |
| 2nd increase | 2022 | $67M |
| 3rd increase | 2024 | $69.75M |
| 4th increase | Q1 2026 | $74M |

Per [[Space Capital]] (Chad Anderson, Apr 26, 2026): "These are confidence signals. SpaceX is capacity constrained by internal demand. Starlink, Direct to Cell, and now orbital data centers. That constraint creates real opportunity for [[Blue Origin]], [[Rocket Lab]], [[Firefly Aerospace]], and other new entrants to absorb commercial and institutional demand."

The pricing trajectory reflects two structural shifts: SpaceX's internal demand crowding out commercial launch slots, and the absence of cost-competitive alternatives (Falcon 9's price is still 50-70% below ULA / Arianespace alternatives despite the increases).

## Reuse statistics

Falcon 9 first-stage boosters are reused. Some boosters have flown 25+ times. The current reusability record is held by booster B1067, which had achieved 25+ landings (refurbishment cadence: ~2-3 weeks between flights at peak).

---

## Why Falcon 9 matters

Falcon 9's combination of reliability, cadence, and price has structurally changed the launch market. The Q1 2026 manifest highlights:

- [[Starlink]] satellite deployment (5/day cadence, 10,000 simultaneous in orbit by mid-March 2026)
- [[Direct to Cell]] satellite ramp
- Government/Department of Defense launches via Starshield
- Commercial launches (compressed by Starlink internal demand)
- [[Vast Space]] [[Haven-1]] commercial space station launch (NET May 2026)

Falcon 9's competitive moat is being challenged by:
- [[Rocket Lab]] Neutron (medium-lift, in development)
- [[Blue Origin]] [[New Glenn]] (heavy-lift, 2 orbital flights as of Q1 2026)
- [[Arianespace]] [[Ariane 64]] (came online Q1 2026)
- [[Firefly Aerospace]] [[Firefly Alpha]] (return-to-flight Mar 2026)
