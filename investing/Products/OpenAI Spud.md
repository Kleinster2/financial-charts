# OpenAI Spud

[[OpenAI]]'s codename for its next major model, confirmed in internal communications in March 2026. Pretraining is complete. CEO [[Sam Altman]] told employees it is a "very strong model" that can "really accelerate the economy." Expected release within weeks — potentially early-to-mid April 2026. Whether it ships as GPT-5.5 or GPT-6 remained unclear at the time. Reported to be natively multimodal.

Resolved: Spud shipped as [[GPT-5.5]] on April 23, 2026 — [[OpenAI]]'s most capable model, released in [[ChatGPT]] and [[Codex]] for paid subscribers (API access following once cyber guardrails were added). [[Greg Brockman]] framed it as the product of nearly two years of research and the new base system for ChatGPT — the direct counter to [[Anthropic]]'s [[Claude Mythos|Mythos]] / [[Claude Fable 5|Fable 5]], landing in the same Q2 window.

## Strategic context

Spud represents a major strategic pivot for [[OpenAI]]. The company discontinued [[Sora]] (video generation) and wound down its $1B [[Disney]] partnership to reallocate compute. [[OpenAI]] had been in an internal "Code Red" since December 2025 as [[Anthropic]] and [[Google]] closed the capability gap. [[Fidji Simo]] called Anthropic's success a "wake-up call."

The decision to kill a flagship creative product to prioritize a language model signals where [[OpenAI]] sees its competitive moat: enterprise productivity and agentic AI, not content generation.
