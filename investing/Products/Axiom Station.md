**Axiom Station** — [[Axiom Space]]'s commercial space station, designed to attach to [[ISS]] as a precursor and detach to operate as a free-flying station post-ISS-retirement. [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] candidate — the only candidate with the ISS-attached precursor architecture (vs. [[Vast Space|Vast]] [[Haven-1]] / [[Haven-2]] free-flying, [[Voyager]] [[Star Lab]] single-Starship-deployment, [[Orbital Reef]] modular).

## Why Axiom Station matters

The ISS-attached precursor strategy reduces technical risk (Axiom modules can be checked out while still attached to ISS, with ISS power/comms/life-support backstops). The trade-off: schedule depends on ISS lifetime, and any delay in ISS deorbit cascades into Axiom's free-flying transition.

Under [[Jared Isaacman]]'s Q1 2026 alternative [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] proposal — a NASA-designed core module attached to ISS — Axiom's architecture overlaps with the proposed NASA-owned module concept. The interplay is consequential: if NASA opts to design and own a core module attached to ISS, Axiom's distinguishing strategic positioning narrows materially.
