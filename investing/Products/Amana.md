[[Amana]] — [[Whirlpool]]'s value / multifamily-positioned appliance brand. Originally founded 1934 in Amana, Iowa as the Amana Refrigeration. Spent decades under [[Raytheon]] ownership (acquired 1965) and was the brand under which Raytheon commercialized the [[Microwave oven]] for consumer use ("Radarange"). Spun out of Raytheon as part of [[Goodman Manufacturing]] in 1997, sold to [[Maytag]] in 2001, then to [[Whirlpool]] via the 2006 [[Maytag]] acquisition.

Within the [[Whirlpool]] portfolio, Amana is the lowest-priced national brand — it sits below [[Whirlpool brand]] in price/quality positioning. Its primary customers are property managers and real-estate developers buying appliances in volume for multifamily / rental housing, and price-sensitive home buyers shopping at the discount end of the [[Home Depot]] / [[Lowe's]] channel. The brand is also the strongest in the Whirlpool portfolio in the apartment / condo new-construction segment.

The cyclical exposure for Amana is structurally different from [[Whirlpool brand]] (mass-market replacement) or [[KitchenAid]] (gift / premium kitchen). Amana volume tracks: (1) multifamily new construction, which is itself driven by interest rates / cap rates; (2) rental-property turnover-driven appliance replacement; (3) entry-level home purchases. Q1 2026 [[Whirlpool]] guide-cut commentary on housing-cycle weakness has the most direct exposure here — multifamily starts have decelerated through 2025-2026, making Amana volume the cleanest negative-leverage line within the portfolio.
