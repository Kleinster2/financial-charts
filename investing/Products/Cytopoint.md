#product #pharma #animal-health #dermatology #monoclonal-antibody

[[Cytopoint]] (lokivetmab) — [[Zoetis]] anti-IL-31 caninized monoclonal antibody. Indicated for the treatment of clinical manifestations of atopic dermatitis in dogs. Approved EU 2017, US 2017. The complementary mechanism alongside [[Apoquel]] (JAK inhibitor) within the [[Zoetis]] companion-animal dermatology franchise — antibody-blocking IL-31 cytokine vs JAK inhibition of cytokine signaling.

The drug is a once-monthly subcutaneous injection administered in-clinic, which makes it operationally distinct from oral [[Apoquel]] — Cytopoint binds to revenue through veterinary visits, not pharmacy refills. The Q1 2026 ZTS commentary on declining veterinary visits and pet-owner price sensitivity puts more pressure on Cytopoint than on Apoquel.

---

## Mechanism

Caninized monoclonal antibody targeting interleukin-31 (IL-31) — the primary itch-signaling cytokine in canine atopic dermatitis. Binds and neutralizes circulating IL-31, preventing receptor binding and downstream JAK signaling. Onset: 24 hours; duration: 4-8 weeks per injection.

Distinct from [[Apoquel]] (oclacitinib): Cytopoint blocks the cytokine; Apoquel blocks signaling downstream of the cytokine receptor. The two are sometimes used in combination, but more commonly as substitutes within the same patient's lifetime.
