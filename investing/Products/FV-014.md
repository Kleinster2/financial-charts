**FV-014** — [[Rheinmetall]]'s kamikaze drone / loitering munition, also referred to as "Raider" in some press accounts. 100 km range, up to 70-minute flight duration, with autonomy software from [[Auterion]]. Selected by the [[Bundeswehr]] in April 2026 as the third supplier in [[Germany]]'s three-way drone procurement (alongside [[Helsing]]'s HX-2 and [[Stark]]'s OWE-V Virtus).

The FV-014 is [[Rheinmetall]]'s re-entry ticket into [[Germany]]'s drone procurement after losing the first round. The delay cost the company time but did not cost it the program — the three-supplier model was explicitly designed to keep the incumbent land-systems prime in the mix.

---

## Role in the three-supplier model

The [[Bundeswehr]] chose three suppliers to hedge technology, production, and industrial-base risk. FV-014 is the incumbent-prime's entry; HX-2 ([[Helsing]]) is the AI-native startup's entry; OWE-V Virtus ([[Stark]]) is the purpose-built weapons-startup entry. All three will run in parallel with field trials, training, and iterative improvement. See [[European defense spending]].

## Technical positioning

- **Auterion software inside** — same autonomy stack [[Rheinmetall]] has been partnered on since December 2024, now embedded in a flagship product. Swarm coordination and GPS-denied operation are central to the pitch.
- **Longer flight / range than FPV analogs** — 70 minutes and 100 km puts it above the commercial-FPV class and into the strategic-strike end of loitering munitions.
- **Incumbent-prime scale** — Rheinmetall's production base differentiates FV-014 from the startup entries on mass-manufacture credibility. [[Helsing]] had to acquire [[Grob Aircraft]] to get there; Rheinmetall already had it.
