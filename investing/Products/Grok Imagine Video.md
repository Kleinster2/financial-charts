# Grok Imagine Video

[[xAI]]'s AI video generation product within the broader [[Grok]] family. Per the Arena user-voted blind-test leaderboard (FT, May 17 2026), the **only non-Chinese model in the top 3** of either image-to-video or video edit — beats [[Google]]'s [[Veo]] 3.1 audio in image-to-video, and ranks ahead of [[Kuaishou]]'s [[Kling]] O3 Pro in video edit.

## Arena leaderboard — May 17 2026

The FT chart (Arena user-voted rankings, blind test) tells a different story than the FT article narrative. The article framing is "Chinese ahead, US behind." The chart shows xAI's video product is the strongest non-Chinese option in two of three categories:

| Category | Grok rank | Score | Notable |
|---|---|---|---|
| Text-to-Video | Not top-4 | — | [[Veo]] 3.1 audio/fast take #3/#4 |
| Image-to-Video | **#3** | 1,421 | **Beats [[Veo]] 3.1 audio at 1,402** |
| Video edit | **#3** | 1,259 | Ahead of [[Kling]] O3 Pro at 1,244 |

The pattern: Grok Imagine Video competes most strongly where reference inputs (a starting image, a base video clip) anchor the generation — image-to-video and editing. In pure text-to-video, where the model has only the prompt to work from, Chinese models with vast short-video training corpora ([[TikTok]], [[Douyin]], [[Kuaishou]]) win. The asymmetry maps to data: anchored generation needs less raw training video to produce a good output than pure-text-prompt generation.

The strategic implication for [[xAI]]: video generation is producing returns commensurate with the [[Colossus]] capex narrative. [[Sora]]'s discontinuation in March 2026 left [[Veo]] as the assumed "US incumbent," but the leaderboard shows Grok Imagine Video has effectively become the strongest US-built video model in at least one category. Relevant for the [[SpaceX IPO 2026]] valuation narrative.
