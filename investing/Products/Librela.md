[[Librela]] (bedinvetmab) — [[Zoetis]] caninized monoclonal antibody targeting Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) for the control of pain associated with canine osteoarthritis. The first monoclonal antibody approved by the FDA for canine osteoarthritis pain. Once-monthly subcutaneous injection administered in-clinic.

Librela is one of the highest-growth franchises in [[Zoetis]]'s companion-animal portfolio post-2024 launch and is also one of the names where the [[Zoetis]] Q1 2026 commentary on "softer demand for premium innovative products" lands directly — Librela is the textbook premium-innovative product launching into a softening consumer environment.

---

## Mechanism

Caninized monoclonal antibody targeting Nerve Growth Factor (NGF). Binds circulating NGF and prevents activation of the TrkA receptor on pain-sensing neurons, blocking the chronic-pain signaling pathway. Onset: 7-14 days; duration: ~28 days per injection.

The anti-NGF mechanism is structurally distinct from prior canine OA pain treatments (NSAIDs like carprofen, gabapentin for neuropathic pain). It addresses the underlying pain-signaling pathway rather than treating downstream inflammation or sensitization.
