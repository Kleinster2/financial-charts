# Google Search

Google Search — [[Google]]'s core search product and the economic center of [[Alphabet]]'s advertising business. The I/O 2026 update pushed Search further into agentic AI through [[Search AI Mode]], Gemini 3.5 Flash, information agents, booking/calling agents, generated UI, and persistent trackers.

---

## Why it matters

Search is where Google's AI strategy has the highest stakes. Gemini adoption in the app proves reach; Gemini inside Search determines whether that reach protects the profit pool. The product must answer two questions at once: can Google defend the user habit against standalone AI assistants, and can it rebuild monetization when answers and agents compress the click path?

The I/O 2026 direction is clear. Search is not just becoming a better answer box. It is becoming a task surface: ask, monitor, compare, book, call, and track. That is the right strategic response to AI assistants, but it changes the economics of publisher traffic, ad placement, merchant discovery, and user trust.

## I/O 2026 reset

| Change | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Gemini 3.5 Flash in AI Mode | Makes frontier reasoning cheap and fast enough for broad Search use |
| Information agents | Search can continue the work after the first query |
| Booking/calling agents | Search moves closer to executing transactions |
| Generated UI | Results pages become temporary apps for specific tasks |
| Persistent trackers | Search becomes a monitoring surface, not only a retrieval surface |
