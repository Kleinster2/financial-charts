# Silicon One

[[Cisco]]'s merchant-silicon networking chip family. Originally introduced 2019 as a unified architecture spanning routing and switching workloads. The G-series (G100, G200, G300) is the line targeting hyperscaler / AI cluster networking, competing with [[Broadcom]]'s Tomahawk and [[NVIDIA]]'s networking silicon in the [[Vera Rubin]] platform.

---

## G300 (announced Feb 10, 2026)

Unveiled at Cisco Live EMEA (Amsterdam). Targets gigawatt-scale AI clusters.

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Throughput | 102.4 Tbps |
| Process | [[TSMC]] 3nm |
| Cooling | 100% liquid option (~70% energy improvement vs air) |
| GPU utilization claim | +28% job completion time vs prior generation |
| Network utilization claim | +33% |
| Availability | H2 2026 |

The G300 is the chip that anchors [[Cisco]]'s AI-networking story — the $9B FY26 AI-orders guide raised at the Q3 FY26 print (May 13 2026) flows through Silicon One-equipped systems plus partner products.
