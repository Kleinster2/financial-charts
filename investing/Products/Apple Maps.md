# Apple Maps

[[Apple]]'s built-in mapping and navigation service, launched September 2012 with iOS 6 to replace [[Google]] Maps as the iPhone default. Its debut was one of Apple's most public product failures — distorted geography and bad directions drew a rare apology from CEO [[Tim Cook]], who briefly recommended rivals while Apple rebuilt the product. A decade of reconstruction (first-party map data, Look Around, detailed city maps) followed.

In 2026 Maps is being turned into an advertising surface: per the [[Financial Times]], [[Apple]] is adding search ads that let local businesses bid on queries (a coffee shop bidding on "coffee" in its locale), mirroring [[Google]] Maps and [[Yelp]]. It is one prong of Apple's broader Services ad push — alongside the App Store and [[Apple News]] — estimated at ~$8bn of iOS ad revenue in 2026, a figure Maps could roughly double. See [[Apple#Innovation gap and the reinvestment question (FT, May 2026)]] for why Services monetization matters to the equity story.
