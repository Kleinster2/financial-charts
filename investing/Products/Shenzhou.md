#product #china #space

Shenzhou — [[China]]'s crewed spacecraft series for transporting astronauts to [[Tiangong]]. The program has carried regular three-person crews to the station since 2021 and is now being used by the [[China Manned Space Agency]] to extend China's long-duration human-spaceflight experience ahead of the [[Chinese crewed lunar program]].

## Shenzhou-23 (May 2026)

[[Shenzhou]]-23 launched from [[Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center]] on May 24, 2026 with commander Zhu Yangzhu, pilot Zhang Yuanzhi / Zhang Zhiyuan, and payload specialist Li Jiaying / Lai Ka-ying. Reuters and AP reported that one of the three is expected to stay aboard [[Tiangong]] for a year, a record-duration mission for China, while the other two return through the normal station-rotation cadence.

The mission matters because it changes Shenzhou from routine station transport into an explicit lunar-readiness test. Long-duration physiology, crew performance, and autonomous rapid docking all feed the future [[Mengzhou]] / [[Lanyue]] rendezvous architecture for a crewed moon landing before 2030.
