#product #hardware #wearables

Fitbit Air — [[Google]]'s $99 screenless fitness tracker, announced May 7, 2026 and shipping May 26, 2026. The smallest, cheapest device in the [[Fitbit]] lineup since the Inspire 3, positioned as a [[WHOOP]] competitor and as the customer-acquisition wedge for [[Google Health Premium]] and ultimately [[Google AI Pro]]. Builds on a [[Fitbit Charge 6]]-class sensor stack with [[Pixel Watch 4]]-derived algorithms.

Launch event coverage: [[Fitbit Air launch May 2026]].

Air does not replace any existing SKU — it expands downward into the screenless / always-on segment that [[WHOOP]] and [[Oura]] proved out.

---

## What Air gives up vs Charge 6

The Air sensor stack is essentially the [[Fitbit Charge 6]] minus the screen. The single substantive feature loss is the on-demand ECG app — Air uses background AFib detection via PPG, not user-initiated ECG capture. For wellness use, this is a tradeoff buyers will accept. For anyone managing a known arrhythmia, the [[Fitbit Charge 6]] (or a [[Apple Watch]] / [[WHOOP]] with FDA-cleared ECG) is the correct tier.

## Subscription mechanics

The post-trial decision tree drives the [[Subscription economy|subscription stickiness]] math:

- Buyer takes [[Google Health Premium]] standalone: $99/yr → Air owner ARPU is roughly hardware + Premium.
- Buyer is already a [[Google AI Pro]] subscriber ($19.99/mo): [[Google Health Premium]] is included free → Air owner ARPU rises into the AI sub.
- Buyer upgrades from Premium-only to AI Pro to consolidate: net incremental +$140/yr ARPU vs Premium-only.

The funnel terminal is [[Gemini]] usage, not Fitbit hardware revenue.
