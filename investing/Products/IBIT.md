**IBIT** — [[BlackRock]]'s spot [[Bitcoin]] ETF, the largest in the world by AUM. Launched January 11, 2024, as part of the first wave of SEC-approved spot Bitcoin ETPs. Custodied by [[Coinbase]]. Trades on NASDAQ.

---

## Synopsis

IBIT is the dominant vehicle for institutional and retail Bitcoin exposure through traditional brokerage accounts. It reached $70B in AUM within its first year — the fastest ETF to hit that milestone in history — and generated an estimated $245M in annual fees for [[BlackRock]]. The fund holds ~768,000 BTC as of March 2026, making it one of the largest single pools of Bitcoin in existence.

The product's edge is distribution: [[BlackRock]]'s brand, iShares infrastructure, and availability across every major brokerage platform gave it an insurmountable head start over competitors. Options launched in November 2024, adding a derivatives layer that further entrenched IBIT as the default Bitcoin access point for TradFi. The risk is straightforward — it's a single-asset fund that tracks Bitcoin's price with a 0.25% annual drag.

## Evolution

IBIT's story is the story of [[BlackRock]] legitimizing Bitcoin for institutional finance.

- June 2023: [[BlackRock]] filed its S-1 for a spot Bitcoin ETF, shocking the market. The world's largest asset manager ($10T+ AUM) entering crypto was a watershed — previous spot ETF applicants (Grayscale, VanEck, WisdomTree) had been rejected for years. Larry Fink, who had called Bitcoin "an index of money laundering" in 2017, now called it "digital gold." The filing triggered a wave of copycat applications from [[Fidelity]], Invesco, and others within weeks.

- January 10, 2024: SEC approved 11 spot Bitcoin ETFs simultaneously, ending a decade-long regulatory battle. The approval came one day after a fake tweet from the SEC's hacked X account prematurely announced approval, briefly spiking Bitcoin before being retracted.

- January 11, 2024: IBIT commenced trading. First-day volume across all spot Bitcoin ETFs hit $4.6B. IBIT quickly separated from the pack — [[BlackRock]]'s distribution network (wirehouse access, model portfolio inclusion, iShares brand recognition) gave it structural advantages no crypto-native issuer could match.

- 2024 (first year): IBIT reached $10B AUM in 49 days — the fastest any ETF had reached that milestone. By year-end it held $70B, eclipsing SPDR's Gold Trust (GLD), which took years to reach comparable scale. The fund absorbed the majority of net inflows into the spot Bitcoin ETF category while GBTC hemorrhaged assets due to its 1.50% fee.

- November 2024: IBIT options launched on NASDAQ, adding leveraged and hedging capabilities. Options volume quickly became among the highest of any ETF, giving institutional traders the derivatives toolkit they needed to size Bitcoin positions within traditional risk frameworks.

- 2025: Bitcoin rallied to all-time highs above $100K, pushing IBIT past $70B AUM. The fund became [[BlackRock]]'s top revenue-generating ETF product. IBIT was included in the [[Strategic Bitcoin Reserve]] discussion as the U.S. government explored building a national Bitcoin position.

- Early 2026: Bitcoin corrected sharply from highs, pulling IBIT from ~$71.82 to the $38 range (-46% from peak). Despite the drawdown, net flows remained positive at +$22B over the trailing year — institutional holders largely held through the correction. On Feb 6, 2026, IBIT hit a single-day volume record of $10B, suggesting capitulation-style trading. Daily flow volatility increased (e.g., +$322M on Mar 5 followed by -$143M on Mar 6).

## Structure

- Grantor trust: IBIT holds physical Bitcoin, not futures. NAV tracks spot BTC price minus expenses.
- Creation/redemption: Authorized participants create/redeem shares in cash (not in-kind BTC), per SEC requirement for all spot Bitcoin ETFs.
- No yield: Bitcoin generates no income. Total return = price appreciation minus 0.25% annual fee.
- Tax treatment: Treated as a grantor trust for tax purposes. Gains taxed at collectibles rate (28% max federal) for long-term holds, not the standard 20% LTCG rate.

## Bull / Bear

Bull:
- Dominant market share with widening moat (liquidity begets liquidity)
- [[BlackRock]]'s distribution ensures inclusion in model portfolios and retirement platforms
- Options market adds institutional utility no competitor matches
- Bitcoin as macro hedge / digital gold narrative still intact long-term
- 0.25% fee is competitive for the access and liquidity provided

Bear:
- Single-asset exposure to the most volatile major asset class
- 28% collectibles tax rate vs 20% LTCG for direct BTC holders who hold >1yr
- [[Coinbase]] custodial concentration risk (also custodian for GBTC, ARKB)
- Fee compression pressure — Bitwise at 0.20%, others may go lower
- Bitcoin correlation to risk assets has increased, reducing diversification benefit
