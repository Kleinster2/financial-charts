[[KitchenAid]] — [[Whirlpool]]'s premium kitchen-focused brand. Founded 1919 by [[Hobart Manufacturing Company]] (originally as a stand-mixer brand). [[Whirlpool]] acquired the brand in 1986 from [[Hobart Manufacturing Company]] for $150M. The brand is anchored in the iconic stand mixer (stand-mixer market position is near-monopolistic in the US premium segment) and extends across kitchen appliances: refrigeration, dishwashers, ranges, microwaves, and small countertop appliances.

The cross-channel dynamic is unusual: KitchenAid stand mixers are sold heavily through department stores, [[Williams-Sonoma]], [[Sur La Table]], and direct-to-consumer at premium price points ($300-$700+ retail), while major appliances (refrigeration / dishwashers) sell through the [[Home Depot]] / [[Lowe's]] big-box channel that drives [[Whirlpool brand|Whirlpool]] sales. The dual channel makes KitchenAid relatively insulated from the home-improvement-channel cyclicality that hits [[Whirlpool brand|Whirlpool]] and [[Maytag]] hardest.

Q1 2026 [[Whirlpool]] guide cut on housing-cycle weakness has uneven brand-level impact: KitchenAid is structurally the most insulated within the portfolio because (1) stand-mixer demand is gift-occasion-driven rather than home-improvement-driven, and (2) the premium positioning supports price holding through demand softness.
