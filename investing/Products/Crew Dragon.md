**Crew Dragon** — [[SpaceX]]'s crew capsule for [[NASA]]'s [[Commercial Crew Program]]. Operational since May 30, 2020 (Demo-2 with astronauts Hurley and Behnken). The first private spacecraft to launch humans to orbit. Backbone of US ISS crew rotation since 2020 and the platform that returned Wilmore and Williams home in September 2024 after [[Boeing Starliner]]'s failed CFT — context for the Q1 2026 Starliner Type A mishap reclassification.

## Strategic context

Crew Dragon's operational dominance is the structural backdrop for [[Boeing]]'s [[Boeing Starliner|Starliner]] crisis. With Crew Dragon operational, profitable, and rotational, Starliner's certification path under [[Jared Isaacman]]'s [[Type A mishap]] reclassification becomes economically harder to justify. The NASA Commercial Crew Program was originally designed for two providers; one provider (SpaceX) is sufficient for ISS crew rotation, and the second (Boeing) is now under regulatory pressure that may not resolve before ISS retirement (~2030).

Crew Dragon is also the platform for commercial astronaut missions outside NASA contracts — [[Inspiration4]] (Sep 2021, all-civilian), [[Polaris Dawn]] (Sep 2024, commercial spacewalk), [[Axiom Space]] missions to ISS, plus emerging commercial-station missions ([[Haven-1]] in May 2026).
