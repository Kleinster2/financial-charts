Confluence is [[Atlassian]]'s enterprise wiki and knowledge management platform, used by over 60,000 organizations for internal documentation, project planning, and team collaboration. Launched in 2004, it became the de facto standard for technical documentation in software teams alongside [[Jira]] (Atlassian's project tracker), benefiting from deep integration between the two products. The combination of Jira for task tracking and Confluence for documentation creates significant switching costs — migrating one means migrating both.

Confluence generates revenue through Atlassian's cloud subscription model. The product's cloud migration (from on-premise Server, which Atlassian sunset in February 2024) was a major revenue driver, converting one-time license buyers into recurring subscribers. Competition has intensified from [[Notion]], Coda, and Slite in the modern wiki space, but Confluence retains its enterprise installed base advantage, particularly with large engineering organizations that value its Jira integration and compliance features (SOC 2, HIPAA-eligible).
