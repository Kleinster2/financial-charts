# Tavneos

Tavneos (generic name avacopan) is an oral C5a-receptor antagonist drug developed for ANCA-associated vasculitis (AAV) — a rare autoimmune disease in which antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies attack small blood vessels, causing organ damage in the kidneys, lungs, and skin. Tavneos was originally developed by [[ChemoCentryx]] and acquired by [[Amgen]] in 2022 ($3.7B acquisition). FDA-approved 2021 (under [[ChemoCentryx]]) for adjunctive treatment of severe ANCA-associated vasculitis.

## FDA withdrawal proposal (Apr 27 / Apr 30, 2026)

On April 27, 2026, FDA's Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER) issued a proposal to withdraw approval of Tavneos, asserting that there is *"new information indicating lack of substantial evidence of effectiveness"* for the drug. On April 30, 2026, the FDA posted notice in the Federal Register formally proposing to withdraw approval. A withdrawal is the strongest post-marketing regulatory action the FDA can take short of safety-driven recall — and is rare for a drug already on the market three-plus years.

The withdrawal proposal is the first major post-acquisition regulatory hit on the [[ChemoCentryx]] integration into [[Amgen]]'s portfolio. Tavneos was acquired in 2022 specifically for its differentiation in the ANCA-vasculitis space — replacing high-dose corticosteroid burden with a more targeted C5a-receptor antagonist mechanism. A formal withdrawal would zero the ~$300M annualized revenue franchise.

The market reaction on May 1, 2026 — [[Amgen]] -5.6% to $326.87 (-3.6σ on a low-vol name) — combined the Tavneos hit with two other negative surprises ([[Prolia]] biosimilar erosion -34% YoY accelerating, and an IRS draft Notice of Proposed Adjustment for tax years 2016-2018).
