DriX is an autonomous surface drone (USV) series manufactured by [[Exail Technologies]] for hydrography, ocean survey, and naval defense applications. The platform combines high-endurance autonomous navigation with multi-sensor payloads for seabed mapping, mine countermeasures, and maritime surveillance. DriX is one of the most commercially successful USVs in the European defense market, with sales growing 75% in 2025.

The DriX H-8 is the established model — a fleet of five was sold to a European navy for maritime surveillance. The DriX H-9, launched in 2025, doubles the H-8's autonomy to 20 days at sea and extends range for long-duration ocean survey missions. First H-9 orders include a global hydrographic authority and the [[France|French Navy]]'s SHOM (Service Hydrographique et Oceanographique de la Marine). The H-9's extended endurance positions it for the growing demand for persistent maritime domain awareness, particularly in the context of undersea cable protection and [[Drone warfare|autonomous naval operations]].
