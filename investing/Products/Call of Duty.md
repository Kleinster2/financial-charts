**Call of Duty** is the highest-grossing video game franchise in history — $35B+ lifetime revenue, 500M+ copies sold since [[Activision Blizzard]] launched the original in 2003. Now owned by [[Microsoft]] after the $68.7B [[Microsoft Activision acquisition|acquisition]] (October 2023). The franchise generates an estimated $3–4B annually across premium game sales, [[Warzone]] free-to-play, [[Call of Duty Mobile]], and in-game microtransactions. The economics have fundamentally shifted: in 2022, 78% of [[Activision Blizzard]]'s revenue came from in-game purchases and subscriptions, not product sales — and CoD drives the bulk of that recurring stream.

The franchise sits at a structural inflection point. [[Microsoft]] made [[Black Ops 6]] the first CoD available day one on [[Game Pass]] (October 2024), and the results were revealing: 82% of unit sales landed on [[PlayStation]] (where users pay $70) while [[Xbox]] users played through subscriptions. [[Microsoft]] internally estimated $300M in lost direct sales from Game Pass inclusion. The follow-up, [[Black Ops 7]] (2025), saw sales crash 60%+ — first time since 2008 that CoD wasn't top 3 in US annual sales, with [[Battlefield 6]] outselling it for the first time ever. The open question: is [[Microsoft]] trading direct franchise revenue for subscription ecosystem value, and does the Game Pass math work at 35–37M subscribers?

## Market discovery timeline

CoD's market-moving moments have been inseparable from the [[Microsoft Activision acquisition]] saga and the Game Pass experiment.

| Date | Event | Market reaction |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Jan 18, 2022 | [[Microsoft]] announces $68.7B [[Activision Blizzard]] acquisition at $95/share | ATVI jumps from $65.39 to $82.31 (+26%). CoD's ~$3B/yr revenue is the strategic centerpiece |
| Dec 8, 2022 | FTC files suit to block acquisition | ATVI spread widens; trades ~$73–77 |
| Apr 26, 2023 | UK CMA blocks deal | ATVI drops; spread widens to ~$20 vs offer price |
| Jul 11, 2023 | Judge denies FTC injunction | ATVI surges; spread collapses toward $95 |
| Jul 16, 2023 | [[Sony]] signs 10-year deal to keep CoD on [[PlayStation]] | Key regulatory concession — preserves CoD's largest revenue platform |
| Aug 22, 2023 | Cloud gaming rights sold to [[Ubisoft]]; CMA agrees to reevaluate | Deal restructure clears final hurdle |
| Oct 13, 2023 | Deal closes at $95/share ($75.4B total including debt) | ATVI delisted. MSFT at ~$327 |
| Oct 28, 2022 | [[Modern Warfare II]] crosses $1B sell-through in 10 days | Fastest in franchise history — validates CoD's commercial durability pre-acquisition |
| Oct 25, 2024 | [[Black Ops 6]] launches day one on Game Pass. "Biggest CoD launch ever" — 33.7M players in first month | MSFT gaming revenue up 43% YoY in FY25 Q1. But 82% of unit sales on PS5 |
| Jan 2026 | Former CEO Bobby Kotick claims CoD sales down 60% in court filing; [[Black Ops 7]] finishes #5 in 2025 US sales | Negative narrative cycle for Microsoft Gaming. [[Battlefield 6]] outsells CoD for first time since 2008 |

The timeline reveals a pattern: CoD's commercial strength justified a $69B acquisition, but the Game Pass inclusion is systematically shifting revenue from direct sales to subscription subsidization. The franchise's value to [[Microsoft]] increasingly depends on whether it drives Game Pass retention, not whether it sells units.

## Franchise economics

### Revenue model shift

CoD evolved from a pure premium franchise (~$60 annual release) to a live-service ecosystem with three revenue streams:

| Stream | Model | Estimated annual contribution |
|--------|-------|-------------------------------|
| Premium releases | $70 upfront (annual cycle) | ~$1–1.5B in launch year |
| [[Warzone]] | Free-to-play, cosmetics/battle pass | ~$1.5–2B |
| [[Call of Duty Mobile]] | Free-to-play, microtransactions | ~$250–300M (declining) |
| In-game purchases (across all) | Skins, operators, blueprints, battle pass | Majority of total revenue |

[[Activision Blizzard]] 2022 breakdown: $1.64B product sales (22%) vs $5.89B in-game/subscription revenue (78%) across all titles. CoD drives the bulk of the Activision Publishing segment's in-game bookings.

## Warzone

[[Warzone]] launched March 10, 2020 as a free-to-play battle royale tied to [[Modern Warfare]] (2019). It transformed CoD's economics from annual resets to persistent year-round engagement.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Launch | March 10, 2020 |
| First 24 hours | 6M downloads |
| First month | 50M downloads |
| By April 2021 | 100M downloads |
| Revenue model | Free-to-play with cosmetics, battle pass |
| Daily revenue (est.) | ~$5.2M from in-game purchases |
| Peak monthly revenue | ~$175M (April 2025, Verdansk nostalgia return) |
| Steam all-time peak concurrent | 488,897 (November 2022) |
| Recent Steam peak | 138,335 (April 2025) |
| Average concurrent (Feb 2026) | ~43,750 (Steam only) |

Before Warzone, CoD's audience reset each October with a new $60 release (~30M annual premium buyers). After Warzone, a persistent free-to-play layer maintained year-round engagement and cosmetic spending between premium releases. The result: franchise net bookings roughly doubled YoY in 2020, hitting $3B in a 12-month period.

## Platform dynamics

| Platform | Share of unit sales | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| [[PlayStation]] | ~82% (BO6 2024) | Historically ~55–60%. Game Pass distorted this dramatically |
| [[Xbox]] | Minimal direct sales | Game Pass subscribers don't buy — they stream |
| PC (Steam/Battle.net) | Growing | Warzone F2P drives PC engagement. PC revenue exceeded console for first time in 2023 across Activision portfolio |
| Mobile | Declining | CoD Mobile mature/declining. Warzone Mobile failed |

The 82% [[PlayStation]] skew in [[Black Ops 6]] is the clearest evidence of Game Pass cannibalization. [[Microsoft]] estimated $300M in lost direct sales. Analysts estimated 6M fewer copies sold than a typical CoD release. The cannibalization is concentrated on [[Xbox]], where subscribers have no reason to pay $70.

## The Game Pass question

This is the central economic question for CoD's future value within [[Microsoft]]:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Game Pass subscribers | 35–37M (mid-2025) |
| Game Pass annual revenue | ~$5B |
| Game Pass Ultimate price | $29.99/mo (raised from $19.99, Oct 2024) |
| PC Game Pass BO6 spike | +30% subscribers around launch |
| Estimated lost CoD sales from GP | $300M (BO6), much higher for BO7 |
| CoD's pre-acquisition annual premium sales | ~$2B+ |

The math: CoD historically generated $2B+ in annual premium sales as a standalone franchise. Under Game Pass, those sales shift to [[PlayStation]] (where users still pay) while [[Xbox]] users access CoD through subscriptions. [[Microsoft]] is betting that CoD drives enough Game Pass retention and growth to offset the lost direct revenue. At $30/month and 35–37M subscribers, Game Pass generates ~$5B annually from all content — CoD's share of that is unquantified but clearly less than $2B.

The BO7 disaster (60%+ sales decline, #5 annual ranking) suggests franchise fatigue compounding the Game Pass effect. [[Activision Blizzard]] acknowledged "fatigue" from consecutive Black Ops releases.
