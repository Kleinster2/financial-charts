**Ozempic** ([[Novo Nordisk]]) — Once-weekly injectable peptide GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide) for Type 2 diabetes. FDA-approved December 2017. The largest single-product franchise in the [[Novo Nordisk]] portfolio — DKK 127.1bn (~$18.9bn) in 2025 sales, more than the combined revenue of any other Novo drug.

Ozempic is the diabetes twin of [[Wegovy]] (same molecule, semaglutide, marketed for obesity at higher dose) and the older sibling of [[Rybelsus]] (oral semaglutide for diabetes). Ozempic carried Novo's GLP-1 franchise for the four years between its launch and the 2021 Wegovy obesity approval, building the manufacturing capacity, prescriber relationships, and cultural recognition that the obesity franchise would later inherit.

The cultural side of Ozempic — the social-media-driven off-label use for weight loss before Wegovy was widely available, "Ozempic face," and the celebrity association — created the demand precondition for the entire 2021–2025 GLP-1 boom.

## Mechanism

Same molecule as [[Wegovy]] — semaglutide is a 31-amino-acid peptide engineered from native human GLP-1 with three modifications enabling once-weekly dosing (DPP-4 resistance via alanine→glycine substitution, albumin-binding fatty-acid acylation). See [[Wegovy]] for engineering detail or [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the broader class context.

The diabetes dose range (0.5–2.0mg) is lower than the Wegovy obesity dose (2.4mg). Diabetes glycemic control efficacy plateaus at lower receptor activation than obesity weight-loss efficacy.

## Efficacy

| Trial | Endpoint | Result |
|-------|----------|--------|
| SUSTAIN-6 (cardiovascular outcomes) | MACE in T2D + CV risk | 26% reduction — first GLP-1 to demonstrate CV benefit in diabetes |
| SUSTAIN-2 to -7 series | HbA1c reduction vs comparators | -1.5 to -1.8% — superior to most oral antidiabetics |
| Real-world weight effect | Weight loss in T2D | ~6–10% — driving off-label demand for obesity |

The cardiovascular outcomes win in SUSTAIN-6 (2016) was strategically important — it positioned Ozempic as not just a glycemic-control drug but a CV-protective drug, expanding its prescribing rationale beyond traditional diabetes targets.

## Growth deceleration (2025)

Ozempic's growth rate collapsed during 2025:

| Quarter | YoY Growth |
|---------|-----------|
| Q1 2025 | +28% |
| Q2 2025 | +15% |
| Q3 2025 | +3% |
| Q4 2025 | +1% |

The drivers: (1) US market saturation in Type 2 diabetes; (2) Lilly's [[Mounjaro]] capturing new-patient share via SURPASS-2 head-to-head superiority; (3) coverage shifts as PBMs ([[CVS Caremark]]) restrict Ozempic in favor of preferred GLP-1s; (4) Trump MFN drug-pricing pressure.

The deceleration was a major factor in [[Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen]]'s ouster (May 2025) and [[Mike Doustdar]]'s appointment (August 2025). See [[Novo Nordisk]] for the broader strategic pivot.

## Patent cliff

Ozempic's semaglutide compound patent in major emerging markets ([[Canada]], [[China]], [[India]], [[Brazil]]) expires in 2026 — opening the door to generic semaglutide in markets accounting for ~1/3 of the world's adults with obesity (IQVIA). Novo lost lawsuits attempting to block Indian generics. Expected generic semaglutide pricing: $15–40/month in India vs ~$97 branded.

US semaglutide patent extends to December 2031 (with patent term extension). Settled litigation with multiple generic challengers in fall 2024 — terms confidential. See [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the full patent and biosimilar timeline.

## Competitive position

| Drug | Manufacturer | Mechanism | Notes |
|------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| **Ozempic** | [[Novo Nordisk]] | GLP-1 monotherapy peptide | Market leader by revenue but losing share |
| [[Mounjaro]] | [[Eli Lilly]] | Dual GIP/GLP-1 peptide | Superior efficacy in SURPASS-2; gaining share |
| [[Rybelsus]] | [[Novo Nordisk]] | Oral semaglutide (peptide + SNAC) | Diabetes oral version |
| Trulicity | [[Eli Lilly]] | GLP-1 monotherapy peptide | Older once-weekly; tirzepatide is replacing |
| [[Foundayo]] | [[Eli Lilly]] | Oral small-molecule GLP-1 | Approved for obesity April 2026; diabetes filing pending |

The structural problem for Ozempic: dual-receptor agonists (Mounjaro) and small-molecule orals (orforglipron's pending diabetes indication) are both better positioned to take new-patient share. Ozempic's 2025 revenue is still record-high but growth is gone. Sustaining revenue depends on the price cuts driving volume gains rather than continued share growth.
