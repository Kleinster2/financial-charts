[[Solensia]] (frunevetmab) — [[Zoetis]] felinized monoclonal antibody targeting Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) for the control of pain associated with feline osteoarthritis. The first monoclonal antibody therapy approved by the FDA for feline OA pain. Sister product to [[Librela]] (canine analog), with the same anti-NGF mechanism in a felinized antibody backbone.

Solensia is the smaller sibling in the [[Zoetis]] anti-NGF franchise — the feline OA market is structurally smaller than the canine OA market (fewer cats than dogs in companion-animal homes; cats also less likely to receive monthly injections due to behavioral handling challenges). But Solensia carries a structural advantage: feline NSAIDs have an unusually narrow safety window in cats compared to dogs, which makes anti-NGF a particularly attractive substitute for the indication.

---

## Mechanism

Felinized monoclonal antibody targeting Nerve Growth Factor (NGF). Same target and mechanism as [[Librela]] but with a feline-compatible antibody backbone to minimize immunogenicity in cats. Once-monthly subcutaneous injection administered in-clinic. Onset: 7-14 days; duration ~28 days.
