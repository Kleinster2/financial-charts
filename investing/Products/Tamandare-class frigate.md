**Tamandaré-class frigate** — Brazilian Navy multi-mission surface combatant program, four ships being built at Itajaí ([[Santa Catarina]]) by the Águas Azuis consortium ([[thyssenkrupp Marine Systems]] + [[Embraer]] Defense + Atech). Based on the MEKO A-100 design family, modified for Brazilian Navy requirements. Lead ship Tamandaré (F200) was launched in 2024; commissioning expected 2026, with subsequent ships at approximately 18-24 month intervals.

## Program structure

The Tamandaré program is the lead Brazilian Navy surface-combatant modernization following the 1990s Niterói-class refits. Four ships will be built at Itajaí — the entire program is sourced from Brazilian industry under TKMS technical direction. The Águas Azuis consortium structure differs from PROSUB: where [[PROSUB]] uses a Naval Group + Brazilian Navy joint venture ([[Itaguaí Construções Navais]]), Tamandaré uses a consortium where TKMS provides design and construction expertise and [[Embraer]] Defense (via subsidiary Atech) provides combat-system integration, with the local industrial structure organized as a private-sector consortium rather than a state-owned JV.

The MEKO A-100 base design is a TKMS export-standard hull family with modular weapons and sensor architecture, suited to small navies needing multi-role capability without the cost of a full-displacement frigate. Brazilian modifications include adaptation to Brazilian Navy combat-system standards, weapons (some indigenous integration including [[MANSUP]] anti-ship missiles), and crew habitability for tropical-ocean operations.

## Strategic significance

Tamandaré is structurally important as the first Brazilian Navy surface combatant program to be built entirely on Brazilian soil since the Niterói-class of the 1970s, and the first to integrate indigenous Brazilian combat-system technology (via [[Embraer]] Defense / Atech) at scale. The consortium structure with TKMS validates a complementary model to PROSUB: where PROSUB uses a state-owned JV, Tamandaré uses a private consortium with the foreign prime in the lead role and Brazilian industrial partners providing local construction and system integration.

The program also slots [[Embraer]] Defense into surface combatants alongside its aerospace position, consolidating Embraer's role as the central Brazilian defense systems integrator across air, land (via aerospace heritage), and now naval domains. This is a meaningful expansion of Embraer's defense addressable market and provides a longer-term industrial logic for Embraer's defense segment beyond the [[C-390 Millennium]] and [[A-29 Super Tucano]] product lines.
