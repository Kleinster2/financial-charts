[[Whirlpool brand]] — The mass-market core brand within [[Whirlpool]]'s multi-brand portfolio. The eponymous brand carries the largest unit-volume share of Whirlpool's North American business and is positioned at the price/volume center of the appliance market — between the value [[Amana]] brand and the heritage / durability [[Maytag]] brand within the Whirlpool stable.

This stub note exists primarily to disambiguate "Whirlpool" the brand within the portfolio from [[Whirlpool]] the parent corporation. The portfolio relationship is: Whirlpool Corporation owns a portfolio of brands including the eponymous Whirlpool brand, [[Maytag]], [[KitchenAid]], [[JennAir]], [[Amana]] (plus international brands [[Bauknecht]], [[Indesit]], [[Hotpoint]] historically). Most reference to "Whirlpool" in the vault refers to the corporation; references to brand-level dynamics use [[Whirlpool brand]].

Within the portfolio:
- [[Whirlpool brand]] — mass-market core
- [[Maytag]] — heritage / durability mid-premium
- [[KitchenAid]] — premium kitchen-focused
- [[JennAir]] — luxury / built-in
- [[Amana]] — value / multifamily

The May 7 [[Whirlpool]] Q1 2026 guide cut affects all five brands but with different cyclical exposure — Whirlpool brand is the closest large-volume read on the broader consumer-discretionary big-ticket-appliance pulse.
