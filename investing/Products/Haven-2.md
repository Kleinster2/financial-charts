**Haven-2** — [[Vast Space]]'s proposed permanent successor to ISS. Multi-module station designed for indefinite presence, with [[NASA]] as anchor customer under [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] Phase 2. Selection targeted mid-2026 (Phase 2 procurement strategy under review Q1 2026 per [[Jared Isaacman]]'s alternative framework — see [[Commercial LEO Destinations]]).

## Strategy

Vast's two-station path: [[Haven-1]] launches NET May 2026 as a 3-year pathfinder station, demonstrating operational competence ahead of the Haven-2 Phase 2 bid. Successful Haven-1 operation (life support, station-keeping, crew operations, customer revenue) is the differentiator that distinguishes credible commercial-station providers from concept-stage entrants.

[[Max Haot]] (CEO) advocates for two CLD winners under the original 2018 commercialization framework, arguing the existing CLD budget can support both Vast and a competitor (likely [[Voyager]] [[Star Lab]] or [[Axiom Space]]) with crew utilization shared across the two stations.
