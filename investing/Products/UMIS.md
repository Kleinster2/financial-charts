# UMIS

Unmanned Mine Countermeasure Integrated System manufactured by [[Exail Technologies]]. A full MCM toolbox combining aerial, surface ([[Inspector USV]]), and underwater ([[A18-M]], [[K-STER C]]) unmanned vehicles with towed sonars and sweeps, all unified through UmiSoft C2 (command and control) software. UMIS represents the leading edge of the naval mine warfare transition from manned minesweepers to autonomous systems.

Selected by [[Belgium]] and the [[Netherlands]] under the rMCM (replacement Mine Counter Measures) program, and by [[Indonesia]]. Won a ~EUR 400M contract from an undisclosed navy in Q1 2025 -- the largest in [[Exail Technologies]]' history. The system's modularity is its core selling point: navies can deploy different combinations of sensors and effectors depending on the threat, from deep-water survey to shallow-water mine neutralization, all controlled from a single shore or ship-based station.
