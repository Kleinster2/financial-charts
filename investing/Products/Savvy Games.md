Savvy Games Group — [[Saudi Arabia]]'s [[Saudi PIF|Public Investment Fund]] gaming arm, building the world's largest gaming and esports portfolio through cross-border M&A. Founded January 2021, HQ Riyadh, led by CEO [[Brian Ward]]. Has deployed ~$16B across five major transactions: [[Moonton]] ($6B, agreed Mar 2026), [[Scopely]] ($4.9B, 2023), [[Niantic]] games business ($3.5B, 2025), ESL Gaming ($1.05B, 2022), and [[VSPO]] ($265M, 2023). Portfolio spans mobile ([[Mobile Legends]], [[Monopoly Go!]], [[Pokémon Go]]), esports (ESL, FACEIT, [[VSPO]]), and live services. Main vehicle for Crown Prince [[Mohammed bin Salman]]'s plan to make [[Saudi Arabia]] a global gaming hub under [[Saudi Vision 2030]]. Targeting EBITDA positive 2026, cash flow positive 2027.

---

## Synopsis

[[Savvy Games Group]] is the execution vehicle for [[Saudi Arabia]]'s bid to dominate global gaming — one of the largest entertainment sectors at ~$200B annually. Armed with a ~$40B PIF mandate and [[Brian Ward]] as CEO, Savvy has assembled a portfolio that few gaming conglomerates can match: [[Monopoly Go!]] (~$2.2B player spending in 2024, second-highest-grossing mobile game per AppMagic), [[Pokémon Go]] (one of the highest-grossing mobile games ever, via [[Niantic]]), [[Mobile Legends]] (110M MAU, dominant in Southeast Asian esports, via [[Moonton]]), and the world's largest esports tournament infrastructure (ESL/FACEIT). Total deployed capital: ~$16B across five deals in four years.

The strategic logic is geographic and demographic: Savvy is buying where the players are. [[Moonton]] gives it Southeast Asia and Latin America. [[Scopely]] and [[Niantic]] give it North America and Europe. [[VSPO]] gives it Chinese esports audiences. [[Brian Ward]] has explicitly flagged that US-China geopolitical tensions create dealmaking opportunities for a Saudi buyer — [[ByteDance]] will sell to Riyadh what it cannot sell to Washington. The [[Moonton]] deal illustrates this: [[ByteDance]] retreating from gaming after failing to dislodge [[Tencent]], and Savvy stepping in as the only buyer with both the capital and the geopolitical positioning to do a $6B cross-border Chinese acquisition.

The risk is execution. Savvy is assembling a portfolio, not yet running a unified business. Ward says EBITDA positive in 2026 and cash flow positive in 2027, but that requires integrating studios across five countries and multiple gaming genres. The [[Electronic Arts]] acquisition ($55B, Sep 2025) was done by [[Saudi PIF]] directly — Savvy was not involved — raising questions about how the broader Saudi gaming strategy is coordinated between the subsidiary and the parent fund.

## Saudi gaming strategy

National gaming and esports strategy unveiled 2022. Key milestones:
- Esports World Cup hosted in Riyadh (summer 2024/2025) — 500+ teams, $60M+ prize pool
- Olympic Esports Games to be hosted in [[Saudi Arabia]] in 2027 (12-year deal with [[IOC]])
- Target: 250 gaming companies, 39,000 jobs by 2030

Ward confirmed board approval for a five-year plan with strategy and KPIs. Savvy spent 2025 revamping the business from sales pipelines to product operations. Ward (GamesBeat, late 2025): "one hundred percent optimistic" about 2026.

## Relationship to PIF gaming strategy

Savvy is PIF's gaming subsidiary but does not control all PIF gaming investments:
- [[Electronic Arts]] take-private ($55B, Sep 2025) — done by PIF directly with [[Silver Lake]] and [[Affinity Partners]], assembled by [[Jared Kushner]]; Savvy "not directly involved" (FT)
- PIF also holds stakes in [[Nintendo]], [[Take-Two]], and [[Activision Blizzard]] separately
- Savvy focuses on operational gaming (acquiring studios, running games); PIF's direct stakes are financial/portfolio positions
