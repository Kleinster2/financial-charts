Seascan ROV is a retrievable high-performance remotely operated vehicle manufactured by [[Exail Technologies]] for underwater mine identification and classification. Deployed from [[Inspector USV]] unmanned surface vessel platforms, Seascan uses high-definition sonar and cameras to locate, identify, and classify sea mines at depths up to 300 meters. Unlike the expendable [[K-STER C]] mine neutralizer (also made by Exail), Seascan is designed for retrieval and reuse across multiple missions, making it the identification component in a two-phase MCM (mine countermeasures) workflow: Seascan finds and classifies the mine, then [[K-STER C]] is deployed to destroy it.

The system is part of a broader shift in naval mine warfare away from manned minesweepers toward unmanned, modular MCM packages. [[Belgium]] and the [[Netherlands]] selected Exail's MCM suite (Inspector + Seascan + K-STER C) for their joint mine countermeasures program, one of NATO's largest MCM modernization efforts.
