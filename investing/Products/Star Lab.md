#product #space #commercial-station

**Star Lab** — [[Voyager]]-led commercial space station joint venture targeting 2029 launch on a single [[SpaceX]] [[Starship]] flight. Voyager owns 67% of the JV; [[Airbus]] (30.5%), [[Palantir]] (1%), [[Mitsubishi]] (0.8%), and [[MDA]] (0.8%) round out ownership. [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] candidate. Voyager's 10K disclosure of the Starship launch contract — $90M / $900/kg — is the cleanest publicly disclosed primary source on Starship economics for a real customer contract.

## Single-launch deployment economics

Star Lab is designed to deploy in a single Starship flight — distinguishing it from Vast's [[Haven-1]] (single Falcon 9, much smaller station) and Axiom's modular ISS-attached architecture. The economics only work because Starship enables single-launch deployment of a full multi-module station; under legacy Falcon 9 / Falcon Heavy economics, the same station would require 8-10 launches.

The contracted price disclosed in Voyager's 10K — $90M / $900/kg — is what made the math viable at $2.8-3.3B development cost. Per [[Space Capital]] (Apr 2026): "That's a generational step change in cost structure, even if that pricing will rise as demand scales."

The 10K disclosure has implications beyond Star Lab itself. It's the cleanest publicly available primary source on Starship pricing for a real customer contract, and it's the price point [[NASA]] used to anchor the SLS-vs-Starship comparison driving Q1 2026's lunar architecture restructure (see [[Artemis]]). Every CLD candidate, every commercial lunar program, and every orbital data center constellation builds on the assumption that Starship reaches that price point at scale; Star Lab made it a contracted figure.

## Why Star Lab matters

Three threads converge on Star Lab:

1. **Voyager equity story** — VOYG's post-IPO valuation hinges on Star Lab's success. The CLD Phase 2 selection (mid-2026) is the single biggest equity catalyst.
2. **Starship economics validation** — The $90M / $900/kg disclosure is the single most-cited figure in Q1 2026 for the structural cost shift that's restructuring NASA's contracting posture.
3. **Multi-national customer base** — Airbus stake brings ESA; Mitsubishi brings Japan; MDA brings Canada. Star Lab is structurally pre-positioned for international agency demand that Vast must compete for case-by-case.
