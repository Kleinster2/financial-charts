Tesla Semi — [[Tesla]]'s Class 8 battery-electric truck. Centered driving position, 500-mile range, 4x faster charging than competing BEV trucks. Mass production starting summer 2026 from Nevada Gigafactory after years of delays. Under $300K — about double a diesel rig, ~$100K less than competing BEV trucks. California carriers secured $195M in grants for 1,002 units.

## Key features

Centered driving position eliminates the right-side blind spot that plagues conventional trucks. Driver is flanked by screens showing every object around the tractor-trailer. Dakota Shearer ([[IMC Logistics]], pilot tester): reversed a stuck 40-foot trailer out of a tight bend without exiting the cab — "That right there showed me that the technology the Tesla has makes a big difference."

500-mile range is the headline spec. Competing BEV trucks from [[Volvo Cars|Volvo]] and [[Nikola]] top out at ~225 miles. For [[King Fio Trucking]] (Long Beach), that difference means two or three round-trips/day to the Inland Empire or a single round-trip to Las Vegas vs short port shuttles only. King Fio CEO Jennie Abarca: "The Teslas change everything. It opens up a whole different type of delivery that I can make." She has 20 Semis on order (already operates 11 Volvo/Nikola BEV trucks).

Angel Rodriguez ([[Hight Logistics]], Long Beach), 56-year-old driver who swapped a 13-gear diesel for a Semi in a one-month pilot: "It's just easier on your body. It's less stressful because you're not really having to engage the clutch and the stick shift."

Fewer moving parts than diesel = reduced maintenance. [[Big F Transport]] (Wilmington, CA) employs 5 mechanics for 40+ diesel rigs — expects to need only 1 mechanic for an all-EV fleet (for trailer chassis only). Counterpoint: Robert Braswell ([[ATA]] Technology & Maintenance Council) warns BEV high-voltage systems require journeyman-electrician-grade technicians — hidden cost.

## Market context

The Semi arrives into a hostile BEV trucking environment:

- [[California]] dropped its clean truck mandate (Jan 2025) anticipating Trump opposition
- [[Nikola]] filed for bankruptcy (Feb 2025), stranding carriers with hard-to-maintain vehicles and expensive hydrogen refueling
- Prolonged freight downturn + rising labor costs + tariff uncertainty = carriers delaying all vehicle purchases
- BEV trucks generally cost 3x diesel, take hours to charge, only travel ~200 miles
- Trump administration ended EV subsidies, eased fuel economy regulations

The Semi's value proposition is that it closes the performance gap enough to justify the premium: 500 miles vs 200, fast charging vs hours, lower TCO via reduced maintenance.

[[PepsiCo]] was one of the first companies to pilot the Semi.
