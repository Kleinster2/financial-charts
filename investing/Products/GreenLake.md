# GreenLake

[[HPE]]'s hybrid cloud platform offering an as-a-service consumption model for on-premises and edge infrastructure. ARR reached $3.2B (+63% YoY) in Q1 FY2026, approaching 50,000 customers, and is on track for $3.5B by end of FY2026. GreenLake is the centerpiece of [[HPE]]'s strategic pivot from hardware sales to recurring revenue.

## Synopsis

GreenLake delivers infrastructure-as-a-service for compute, storage, networking, and AI workloads in the customer's own data center or at the edge — combining the economic flexibility of cloud (pay-per-use, operational expense) with the data sovereignty and latency advantages of on-premises deployment. The platform manages the full lifecycle: provisioning, monitoring, optimization, and billing. For enterprises that cannot or will not move workloads to [[AWS]], [[Azure]], or [[Google]] Cloud due to regulatory, latency, or cost concerns, GreenLake offers a middle path.

The 63% YoY ARR growth is driven by two forces: the general enterprise shift toward consumption-based IT spending and the specific demand surge for on-premises AI infrastructure (GPU clusters, AI training/inference nodes). [[HPE]]'s $14B acquisition of Juniper Networks (closed 2025) strengthens GreenLake's networking layer. The risk is competitive: [[Dell|Dell Technologies]]' APEX platform targets the same hybrid cloud market, and the hyperscalers themselves are extending their services to on-premises environments ([[AWS]] Outposts, [[Azure]] Stack). GreenLake's 63% growth rate suggests it is winning share in the current cycle, but the long-term question is whether hybrid cloud remains a durable category or a transitional waypoint as enterprises eventually migrate to public cloud.
