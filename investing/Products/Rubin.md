**Rubin** — [[NVIDIA]]'s next-generation GPU architecture after [[Blackwell]]. 10x inference token cost reduction vs Blackwell, 10x performance per watt. Six new chips in the platform. Named after astronomer Vera Rubin.

First samples shipped Feb 2026 (confirmed by [[Jensen Huang]] on Dwarkesh Apr 15 as "back a few weeks ago, fully healthy"). Production H2 2026. First deployers: [[AWS]], [[Google]], [[Microsoft]], [[Oracle]], [[CoreWeave]], [[Lambda Labs]], [[Nebius]].

## Roadmap cadence — annual releases through 2029

Jensen confirmed the full forward roadmap on Dwarkesh Apr 15, 2026:

| Architecture | Year | Status |
|---|---|---|
| [[Blackwell]] | 2024-2025 | In production |
| Vera Rubin | 2026 | First samples Feb 2026, production H2 2026 |
| Vera Rubin Ultra | 2027 | Announced |
| Feynman | 2028 | Announced, [[TSMC]] A16 (1.6nm) first partner |
| Unnamed | 2029 | Confirmed on roadmap |

**Why annual cadence matters:** Jensen's argument against "Moore's Law is dead" is that transistor-level progress (~25%/yr) composes with algorithmic, architectural, and co-design improvements across generations. [[Blackwell]] delivered 50x [[Hopper]] energy efficiency — impossible from transistor scaling alone. The annual-release cadence is how NVIDIA compounds each layer of the stack.

**Feynman link to A16:** [[TSMC]]'s 1.6nm-class A16 process (backside power delivery) enters mass production late 2026 with Feynman as first confirmed partner. The 30-year no-contract [[TSMC]] relationship enabled this advance roadmap coordination.

See [[Jensen Huang]] for the roadmap framing and [[TSMC]] for the A16 process context.
