# A19

[[Apple]]'s 2025 mobile system-on-chip, the engine of the iPhone 17 family, fabricated on [[TSMC]]'s third-generation 3nm process (N3P). It ships in two tiers: the A19 (iPhone 17, iPhone 17e) and the higher-binned A19 Pro (iPhone 17 Pro, Pro Max, and the thin iPhone Air). Both carry a 6-core CPU (2 performance + 4 efficiency); the A19 Pro adds GPU "Neural Accelerators" — per-core tensor units in the new Apple10 GPU architecture that lift on-device matrix-multiply throughput to roughly 4x the [[A18 Pro]]'s peak GPU compute.

The A19 is where [[Apple]]'s design-as-moat argument is clearest: the ~10% generation-on-generation gain came mostly from architecture, not the node — N3P delivered only ~4% of it, design the rest. With the frontier model layer now rented from [[Google]] ([[Apple-Google AI deal]]), the on-device tensor capacity in the A19 Pro is Apple's lever for running [[Apple Intelligence]] locally rather than in the cloud. See [[Apple Silicon]] for the in-housing strategy and [[Apple#Innovation gap and the reinvestment question (FT, May 2026)]] for the market frame.
