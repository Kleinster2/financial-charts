**VBTP-MR Guarani** — 6x6 amphibious wheeled armored personnel carrier developed by [[Iveco Defence Vehicles]] (Italy) jointly with the [[Brazilian Army]]. Lead vehicle of the Brazilian Army's "Urutu-III" modernization program, intended to replace the entire EE-11 Urutu APC fleet. Contracted in December 2009 for 2,044 vehicles over an 18-year procurement window (2012-2030), total contract value R$6B (~$3.37B). First export delivery to the Philippine Army in 2025 — the first major export of a Brazilian-built modern armored vehicle.

## Program structure

The Guarani is the lead vehicle of the Brazilian Army's "Urutu-III" modernization program. The EE-11 Urutu, an indigenous 6x6 APC developed by ENGESA in the 1970s-80s, was the standard Brazilian Army medium APC until its progressive retirement starting in the 2000s. The Urutu-III program was structured as a co-development with [[Iveco Defence Vehicles]] under the Brazilian Army's mandatory tech-transfer regime; the resulting vehicle is owned and operated as a Brazilian Army system, manufactured at the Iveco Sete Lagoas plant, with industrial property rights jointly held.

The Brazilian Army modular family doctrine envisions multiple variants on the Guarani platform: armored personnel carrier (baseline), reconnaissance, command, ambulance, mortar carrier, anti-tank guided missile carrier, and engineering support. As of 2025, several of these variants have entered service or are in advanced development.

The Philippine export is structurally significant: it makes Brazil a meaningful armored-vehicle exporter for the first time since the ENGESA EE-11 export run of the 1980s. The Guarani export pitch combines competitive price, amphibious capability uncommon in this class, and modular variants — features that compete favorably against the Patria AMV, Mowag Piranha, Otokar Arma, and Chinese VN-1 family in markets where political alignment supports a Brazilian buy.

---

## Strategic significance

The Guarani program is the structural anchor of [[Iveco Defence Vehicles]]'s Brazilian operation and a meaningful element of the [[Brazilian defense industrial base|Channel 1 — full foreign subsidiary]] case. Without the 2,044-unit Guarani order, Iveco would not have built the Sete Lagoas production plant; with it, Iveco has an embedded Brazilian footprint that supports both the long-running Brazilian Army order and the emerging export program. The vehicle is also the most visible counter-example to the assumption that Brazilian defense procurement only flows to aerospace and naval primes — the Brazilian Army is a meaningful customer, and the Guarani is its flagship.
