**Habshan-Fujairah Pipeline (ADCOP)** — 360 km crude oil pipeline from Abu Dhabi's Habshan field to the port of [[Fujairah]] on the Gulf of Oman, bypassing the [[Strait of Hormuz]]. Capacity: 1.5 mb/d — over half of the UAE's daily crude exports. Operated by Abu Dhabi Crude Oil Pipeline LLC, a subsidiary of [[ADNOC]]. Commissioned in 2012.

## Route

The pipeline passes east of Abu Dhabi city, through Sweihan, west of Al Ain, and exits on the Fujairah coast on the Gulf of Oman. The 14 km offshore section connects to the loading terminal. Crude reaches tankers at the Fujairah Oil Industry Zone (FOIZ), which also serves as a major global bunkering hub.

---

## Strategic significance

ADCOP was built specifically to give the UAE an export route independent of the Strait of Hormuz. At 1.5 mb/d, it can handle more than half of UAE's crude production (~3.2 mb/d). Unlike the Saudi [[East-West Pipeline]], the Fujairah terminus is on the Gulf of Oman — no Red Sea/Bab el-Mandeb chokepoint risk.

However, during the [[Operation Epic Fury|March 2026 Iran conflict]], this strategic advantage was negated. Iranian strikes targeted Fujairah directly, and tankers became reluctant to call at the port despite it being geographically outside Hormuz. The pipeline's capacity was rendered moot when ships wouldn't dock. This exposed a critical flaw: bypass infrastructure only works if the loading port is safe enough for commercial shipping.

## March 2026 crisis status

- Pipeline itself: operational (no reported direct hits on the pipeline)
- [[Fujairah]] port: Iranian target, tankers reluctant to load
- Effective throughput: well below 1.5 mb/d capacity due to shipping risk
- The previous sub-agent task incorrectly stated capacity as "400k bpd" — the pipeline's actual design capacity is 1.5 mb/d per IEA, EIA, and ADNOC data

## MEI panel assessment (March 20, 2026 — Connelly)

[[Colby Connelly]] ([[Middle East Institute|MEI]] Senior Fellow, energy expert) confirmed the ADCO pipeline carries just under 2 million bbl/d to [[Fujairah]], but Fujairah is being bombarded. [[Iran]] targets it specifically because it's an alternate export route for the [[UAE]]. Iran is "very aware" of bypass routes and "sees them as leverage points." Both the Fujairah route and the [[East-West Pipeline|Saudi East-West pipeline]] to [[Yanbu]] are under some Iranian attack or disruption attempts.

ADCO pipeline expansion is considered likely as a post-war structural response, but the current reality is that [[Fujairah]]'s port operations are severely compromised by sustained Iranian bombardment — making the pipeline's capacity functionally useless regardless of the pipeline's physical integrity.

Source: MEI panel, March 20 2026 — Connelly
