# B200

[[NVIDIA]]'s flagship data center GPU for AI training and inference. Blackwell architecture. Successor to [[H100]]. Volume production 2024-2025.

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Architecture | Blackwell |
| Process | TSMC 4NP |
| Transistors | 208B |
| HBM3e memory | 192GB |
| Memory bandwidth | 8 TB/s |
| TDP | 1000W (SXM) |
| FP8 performance | 9 PFLOPS (2x H100) |
| FP4 performance | 18 PFLOPS |
| List price | ~$30,000-40,000 |
| Announced | March 2024 (GTC) |
| Volume | H2 2024 onwards |

## GB200 superchip

Two B200 GPUs connected to one Grace CPU via NVLink-C2C:

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| GPUs | 2x B200 |
| CPU | 1x Grace (72 Arm cores) |
| Combined memory | 384GB HBM3e + 480GB LPDDR5X |
| Interconnect | NVLink-C2C (900 GB/s) |
| Form factor | Single board |

**GB200 NVL72:** 36 GB200 superchips (72 GPUs) in a single rack with liquid cooling. 720 PFLOPS FP8.

## China grey-market premium

The B200 is restricted for export to [[China]] under the late-2024 expansion of US semiconductor export controls. A grey-market channel emerged in 2025-2026 that moved billion-dollar quantities of B200 hardware into China through Anhui-based "Gate of the Era" and similar operators. Reporting from Tom's Hardware and Digitimes documented:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| China grey-market price premium vs US list | ~50% above comparable US pricing |
| Per-rack China grey-market price | RMB 3.0-3.5M (~US$489K) |
| B200 hardware that reached China post-controls | Over $1B in three months |
| "Gate of the Era" estimated channel volume | ~$400M direct + secondary |

[[Patrick Foulis]] cited this 50% premium in his April 24 2026 [[Financial Times|FT]] essay as evidence of the [[Law of one price]] retreat — a chip with identical specifications and supplier sells at materially different prices in the two largest semiconductor markets, sustained by export-control architecture rather than transport or production costs. The premium is the cleanest live measure of how much the [[US-China decoupling]] regime is segmenting the AI-accelerator market.
