# Trello

[[Atlassian]]'s kanban-style project management tool, acquired in January 2017 for $425M. The simplest product in [[Atlassian]]'s portfolio and a top-of-funnel driver for the broader platform.

## Synopsis

Trello was founded by [[Fog Creek Software]] (Joel Spolsky's company, later renamed Glitch) and launched in 2011 as a visual, card-based project management tool. Its simplicity was the product: where [[Jira]] required configuration and admin overhead, Trello worked out of the box for non-technical teams. By the time [[Atlassian]] acquired it in 2017, Trello had ~19M registered users and was growing virally through freemium adoption. The $425M price tag looked expensive for a tool with limited monetization, but [[Atlassian]] saw Trello as a gateway drug -- teams start on Trello, outgrow it, and graduate to [[Jira]] or [[Confluence]].

The strategic logic has partially played out. Trello reached 50M+ registered users by 2019, but monetization remains the challenge -- the free tier is so functional that conversion to paid plans is low relative to [[Atlassian]]'s other products. The competitive landscape also intensified: [[Asana]], [[Monday.com]], and [[Notion]] all target the same "lightweight project management" space. [[Atlassian]] has responded by integrating Trello more deeply into its platform (Trello cards linking to [[Jira]] tickets, [[Confluence]] pages) and adding premium features like automation (Butler), timeline views, and admin controls. Trello is unlikely to be a standalone growth engine, but as a user acquisition funnel for the [[Atlassian]] ecosystem, the $425M was reasonable.
