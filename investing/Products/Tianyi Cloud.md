**Tianyi Cloud** (天翼云) — [[China Telecom]]'s public-cloud platform and the largest of the three Chinese telecom-operator clouds. FY2025 revenue exceeded RMB120bn, which China Telecom cites as #2 in China public-cloud IaaS behind [[Alibaba Cloud]]. The vehicle that makes China Telecom the cloud/AI leader of the three state telecoms, built on the [[东数西算]] national computing buildout and anchored in the government and SOE segment.

---

## Scale and the two-leaderboard problem

Tianyi Cloud's >RMB120bn revenue makes it comparable to or larger than [[Huawei Cloud]] and [[Tencent Cloud]], and China Telecom markets it as the #2 cloud in China. But the ranking depends entirely on the cut:

| Measure | Where Tianyi ranks | Why |
|---|---|---|
| China Telecom reported cloud revenue | #2 (~RMB120bn) behind Alibaba | includes public + private + government + integration |
| Narrow public-cloud IaaS (Omdia/Canalys) | leaderboard is [[Alibaba Cloud]] ~33%, [[Huawei Cloud]] ~16–19%, [[Tencent Cloud]] ~9–15% | telecom clouds counted separately / under-weighted |

The reconciliation: a large share of Tianyi's revenue is government, state-enterprise, and private/hybrid cloud plus systems integration — not the pure public-cloud IaaS that the Western trackers measure. So Tianyi is simultaneously a top-tier cloud by revenue and a lower entry on the public-IaaS leaderboard. The honest read is that it is one of China's largest clouds by money, dominant in the state segment, and lighter in the developer/MaaS layer where the [[China token economy]] is growing fastest.

---

## Where it wins

- Government, SOE, and provincial/municipal cloud — where China Telecom's state ownership, security posture, and national footprint are decisive. This is the same buyer [[Huawei Cloud]] targets, and the telecom clouds taking the state segment is part of why Huawei's external cloud growth stalled.
- The [[东数西算]] buildout — China Telecom converts its land, power, and fiber into intelligent-computing capacity (91 EFLOPS, roughly double [[China Unicom]]'s 45 EFLOPS), the supply side of the state cloud.
- AI-related revenue grew ~89% (H1 2025), the fastest-growing line.

## Where it lags

Tianyi is weak in the consumer/developer MaaS layer — it has no flagship foundation model or token platform competing with [[Qwen]], [[Doubao]], or [[Hunyuan]]. Its strength is infrastructure and government integration, which is lower-margin and policy-dependent. In the [[China AI cloud war]] framing it is a state-infrastructure player, not a model/token competitor.

---

## Why it matters for China Telecom

Tianyi is the single biggest reason [[China Telecom]] has a credible non-utility growth story and led the China-telecom bloc's re-rating. But the vault's cluster work shows the stock still trades as a telecom, not a cloud name (telecom-vs-cloud correlation 0.253) — the cloud value is in the income statement, not yet in the equity's return behavior. If Tianyi ever grows large enough to re-rate China Telecom toward the cloud complex, this is the line that would do it.
