# Urea

The world's most-produced nitrogen fertilizer and the single largest derivative of [[Ammonia]], accounting for ~55% of global ammonia consumption. ~180 Mt/year global production. Produced by reacting ammonia with CO2 at high pressure. [[China]] is the largest producer. Key price benchmarks: Yuzhny FOB (Black Sea), Middle East FOB, US Gulf NOLA.

## Iran conflict crisis pricing (March 2026)

The [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure disrupted ~49% of globally traded urea (TFI estimate). Prices surged across all benchmarks:

| Benchmark | Price (Mar 12) | Change |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| NOLA barges | $600-620/st | +30% from $475 pre-conflict |
| NOLA spot peaks | $683/st | Retail highs |
| North America FPI | $810/st (Mar 9) | New cycle high |
| SE Asian granular | >$700/t | Apr-May delivery |
| Australian | >A$1,200/t | +45% from A$830-840 pre-war |

The crisis simultaneously disrupts Gulf urea production (QAFCO shut, Iran offline), Gulf-dependent LNG feedstock to South Asian plants (India at 60% capacity, Bangladesh 4-5 factories shut, Pakistan halted), and Chinese export availability (phosphate ban through August). See [[Nitrogen Trap]] for the full cascading framework.

Non-agricultural urea demand also at risk: AdBlue/diesel exhaust fluid (critical for Australian heavy freight), industrial process inputs. [[Australia]] imports virtually all urea, ~2/3 from the Gulf.
