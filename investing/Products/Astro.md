#product #robotics #amazon

**Astro** — [[Amazon]]'s wheeled home robot, unveiled fall 2021. Alexa-powered, designed for home monitoring and security patrols. Never reached general availability after 4+ years. Business version bricked after 10 months. Effectively a failure that led Amazon to pivot toward acquired humanoid robotics ([[Sprout]]).

## What went wrong

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| No compelling use case | "Luxury item lacking compelling use case" (CNET) |
| Tiny adoption | "A few hundred units at most" shipped (Bloomberg, Mark Gurman) |
| Massive team | 800+ people worked on it |
| Price | $1,000 early access, planned $1,450 retail |
| Business version killed | Bricked after 10 months, full refunds |
| Never GA | Invite-only for 4+ years and counting |

Ring Home Trial integration and Alexa voice control weren't enough to justify a $1,000+ wheeled device that duplicated functionality available from cheaper cameras and speakers.
