[[Google]]'s AI "world model" that generates video game-like virtual worlds from text prompts. Described by Google as an "experimental research prototype" as of early 2026, but its release triggered sharp sell-offs across gaming stocks — [[Take-Two]], [[Roblox]], [[Ubisoft]], [[CD Projekt]], [[Nexon]], [[Capcom]] all declined.

---

## Market impact (Feb 2026)

Genie's release prompted investors to sell gaming stocks on fears that consumers would soon generate their own personalized worlds, making traditional studios obsolete. FT (Feb 12) argued this misread gaming as a pure tech business — it is equally a media business. Consumers prefer professional entertainment for the same reason they watch movies despite having cameras and editing software: creating an innovative gameplay loop or a 100-hour story takes more than an app.

Genie co-lead (Feb 2026): *"[The goal is to] empower creators and developers"* rather than *"replace the existing experience."*

[[Unity Software]] identified as most directly threatened — its engine-as-a-service model faces substitution risk from AI world generators. [[Roblox]] somewhat insulated by its user-generated content repository and in-game currency.

The [[Electronic Arts]] $55B take-private consortium may have benefited from timing — if they had waited, AI-driven valuation compression might have lowered the price.
