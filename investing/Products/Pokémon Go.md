**Pokémon Go** — augmented reality mobile game launched in 2016 by [[Niantic]], placing [[Nintendo]]'s Pokémon characters over real-world camera views. Became an overnight global sensation, pioneering location-based gaming at scale. 30M monthly active players, $1B+ annual revenue (2024). Sold to [[Scopely]] ([[Savvy Games|Savvy Games Group]]) for $3.5B in March 2025 as [[Niantic]] pivoted to geospatial AI.

## Significance

One of the highest-grossing mobile games ever, with durability rare in mobile gaming — still generating $1B+ annually eight years after launch. Pioneered real-world augmented reality gaming, requiring players to physically move through their environment. The mapping data collected from players became the foundation for [[Niantic|Niantic Spatial]]'s geospatial AI pivot.

The $3.5B sale price reflects both the game's revenue durability and the strategic value to [[Scopely]]/[[Savvy Games|Savvy Games Group]], which combined it with [[Monopoly Go!]] to create a 500M+ player mobile gaming portfolio under [[Saudi PIF]] ownership.
