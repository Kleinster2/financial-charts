#product #edtech #lms #k12

**Schoology** is the K-12 learning management system owned by [[PowerSchool]] (acquired 2019 for $300 million). Competes primarily in the K-12 segment alongside [[Google Classroom]] and [[Canvas LMS]] for K-12. Marginal presence in higher education — its strategic positioning is K-12 SIS-LMS integration, leveraging PowerSchool's dominant K-12 SIS distribution.

## Synopsis

Schoology was founded in 2009 by NYU students Jeremy Friedman, Ryan Hwang, and Tim Trinidad as a social-network-style classroom tool. PowerSchool acquired it in November 2019 for $300 million in cash, integrating it into the PowerSchool product line as the K-12 LMS layer that complements PowerSchool's SIS dominance. The combined PowerSchool stack — SIS, gradebook, LMS, analytics — is sold to K-12 districts as an integrated platform.

In the K-12 LMS market specifically, Schoology competes against [[Google Classroom]] (which is essentially free at the per-seat level for schools using Google Workspace for Education, making it the default for many districts) and [[Canvas LMS]] (Instructure's K-12 push). [[PowerSchool]]'s 2024 take-private by [[Bain Capital]] for ~$5.6B is the most recent major capital event in this segment.
