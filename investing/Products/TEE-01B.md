# TEE-01B

[[China|Chinese]]-built high-resolution imaging satellite operated by [[Iran]]'s [[IRGC]] Aerospace Force since late 2024 under an "in-orbit delivery" contract from [[Earth Eye Co]]. TEE-01B captures imagery at approximately 0.5-metre resolution — roughly an order of magnitude better than Iran's indigenous military satellites and comparable to high-end Western commercial imagery. The satellite was launched from [[Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center]] in June 2024 and handed over to IRGC control in late 2024; ground-segment operation runs through [[Emposat]]'s global station network. During the March 2026 war, the IRGC tasked TEE-01B to surveil at least ten US military sites across [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Jordan]], [[Bahrain]], [[Iraq]], [[Kuwait]], [[Djibouti]], and [[Oman]], plus civilian Gulf infrastructure in [[UAE]] and [[Bahrain]]. See [[IRGC Chinese satellite targeting March 2026]] for the targeting chronology.

## Capability comparison

| Satellite | Operator | Resolution | Order-of-magnitude difference |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| TEE-01B | [[IRGC]] (via [[Earth Eye Co]]) | ~0.5 m | Baseline |
| [[Noor-3]] | IRGC Aerospace (domestic) | ~5 m (claimed) | ~10× worse |
| [[Noor-2]] | IRGC Aerospace (domestic) | 12-15 m | ~25× worse |

The order-of-magnitude improvement is decisive for identifying aircraft type, vehicle movement, and changes to base infrastructure — the data needed to direct drone and missile strikes with meaningful accuracy. Neither of Iran's domestic systems would allow analysts to count parked aircraft or verify strike outcomes.

## Commercial and operational architecture

TEE-01B is the anchor case for the [[Earth Eye Co]] "in-orbit delivery" export model: satellite launched and commissioned inside China, then transferred to the foreign customer by changing operational control rather than physical possession. Ground operations are enabled by [[Emposat]]'s commercial ground-station network in third countries, which keeps the capability functioning even when Iranian domestic satellite infrastructure is struck by [[Israel]] (multiple times in 2025-2026).

See [[Chinese dual-use commercial satellite exports]] for the framework and [[Earth Eye Co]]/[[Emposat]] for the commercial counterparties.

## Methodology note (FT verification)

FT analysed TEE-01B's orbit using public US Space Force tracking data. Documented timestamps, coordinates, and sensor angles on the leaked Iranian tasking logs were consistent with the satellite's position. Damage assessment used [[European Space Agency]] [[Sentinel-1]] radar imagery and [[Sentinel-2]] medium-resolution optical imagery.
