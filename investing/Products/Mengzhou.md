Mengzhou — [[China]]'s next-generation crew spacecraft for the [[Chinese crewed lunar program]]. It is being developed alongside the [[Long March 10]] rocket family and the [[Lanyue]] lunar lander, with the lunar mission architecture relying on automated rendezvous between the Mengzhou capsule and Lanyue lander in lunar orbit.

## Program role

CMSA and Chinese state media describe Mengzhou as part of a shared development path for near-Earth and lunar missions. China Daily reported that the Long March 10 rocket family and Mengzhou spacecraft are being designed for both near-Earth transport tasks and future crewed lunar exploration. Reuters reported that the 2030 mission hinges on automated lunar-orbit rendezvous between Mengzhou and [[Lanyue]].

The market read-through is indirect: if Mengzhou and Long March 10 progress on schedule, China keeps pressure on [[NASA]] and the US commercial lunar stack. If either slips, the public 2030 target becomes less credible.
