# K-STER C

K-STER C is an expendable underwater mine disposal ROV manufactured by [[Exail Technologies]]. The system is guided to seafloor mines and improvised explosive devices, then neutralizes them with a shaped charge. Originally developed by [[ECA Group]] in 2008, K-STER C has become the backbone of [[NATO]]'s mine countermeasure modernization, with a backlog exceeding 1,000 units. It is deployed as part of the [[UMIS]] integrated mine countermeasure system, which combines K-STER with autonomous underwater vehicles for detection.

## Significance

K-STER C represents the expendable-drone model applied to naval mine warfare — a domain traditionally handled by expensive, reusable mine-hunting ships. The economics favor expendables: a K-STER unit costs a fraction of risking a ship and crew over a minefield. The growing order book reflects [[NATO]]'s pivot toward distributed, unmanned mine countermeasures as the Baltic and Mediterranean face heightened mine threats. Production at the Ostend facility positions [[Belgium]] as a hub for European naval drone manufacturing.
