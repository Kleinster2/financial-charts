**Mounjaro** ([[Eli Lilly]]) — Once-weekly injectable peptide for Type 2 diabetes; the first FDA-approved dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist (May 2022). Same molecule (tirzepatide) is sold as **Zepbound** for obesity (Nov 2023). The combined franchise generated $36.5bn in 2025 — the world's best-selling drug, surpassing [[Merck]]'s [[Keytruda]] ($31.7bn).

The drug's strategic significance: it was the first incretin therapy to engineer a single peptide that activates two receptors (GIP and GLP-1) rather than one, which explains its consistent superiority over [[Wegovy]] (semaglutide) on weight loss in head-to-head trials. The dual-receptor design is the structural reason [[Eli Lilly]] gained 58% US GLP-1 share by 2025 against [[Novo Nordisk]]'s 42%.

## Mechanism

Tirzepatide is a 39-amino-acid peptide engineered to activate both the GLP-1 receptor and the glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) receptor. GIP, the other major incretin hormone, was characterized in 1973 as the first incretin identified — but its therapeutic potential was unclear until tirzepatide demonstrated that dual activation was both feasible and additive.

The molecule is built on a GIP backbone modified to also bind GLP-1R, with an albumin-binding fatty acid chain (similar engineering principle to Novo's semaglutide) for once-weekly dosing. See [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the broader class context.

| Trial | Tirzepatide weight loss | Comparator |

The SURMOUNT-5 head-to-head against [[Wegovy]] (published late 2024) was decisive in shifting US prescribing share toward Lilly.

## Sister brand: Zepbound

Same molecule, separate brand, separate marketing. Zepbound carries the obesity indication and is the franchise's growth driver — Q3 2025 Zepbound sales of $3.6bn (+184% YoY) matched the entire diabetes-only revenue of [[Wegovy]] in early years. Combined with Mounjaro's diabetes revenue, the tirzepatide franchise overtook Keytruda as the world's largest drug in late 2025.

Zepbound was also FDA-approved (Dec 2024) for moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea in patients with obesity — the first drug approved for OSA and a template for cross-indication expansion of GLP-1 class drugs.

## Manufacturing

Tirzepatide is a peptide produced via solid-phase peptide synthesis. [[Eli Lilly]] has invested several billion dollars in dedicated tirzepatide manufacturing facilities in Indianapolis, Ireland, and North Carolina since 2022. Capacity expansion has tracked demand more successfully than [[Novo Nordisk]]'s Wegovy build-out, which is part of why Lilly avoided the worst of the 2022–2025 compounding-pharmacy gray market that hurt Novo. See [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the compounding context.
