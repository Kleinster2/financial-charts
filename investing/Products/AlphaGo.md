**AlphaGo** — [[Google DeepMind]]'s Go-playing AI system. Defeated [[Lee Sedol]] 4-1 in March 2016, the first program to beat a professional Go player without handicap. Move 37 in Game 2 became an iconic moment of AI creativity -- an unconventional shoulder hit that no human would play, which turned out to be a winning move. Succeeded by AlphaZero (2017), which generalized the approach to chess and shogi with zero human game knowledge.

[[Jakub Pachocki]] cites AlphaGo as a precedent for AI models discovering novel strategies, comparing [[FrontierMath]] solutions to watching AlphaGo play "interesting games indefinitely."
