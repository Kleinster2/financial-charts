**Wegovy** ([[Novo Nordisk]]) — Once-weekly injectable peptide GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide 2.4mg) for chronic weight management. FDA-approved June 4, 2021. The drug that catalyzed the modern obesity-pharmaceutical era — going from FDA approval to viral cultural phenomenon within 12 months and pulling [[Novo Nordisk]] to a peak market cap of ~$650bn (2024).

A peptide-pill version of Wegovy (oral semaglutide 25mg, with SNAC absorption enhancer) was FDA-approved December 2025 and launched in January 2026 — 600,000 US prescriptions in its first three months, "one of the most successful drug launches in pharmaceutical history" (Novo). The injectable remains the higher-revenue form.

Wegovy is the obesity twin of [[Ozempic]] (same molecule, semaglutide, marketed for diabetes). The two together plus the [[Rybelsus]] oral diabetes pill made up Novo's $34.6bn 2025 semaglutide franchise.

## Mechanism

Semaglutide is a 31-amino-acid peptide engineered from native human GLP-1 with three modifications: an alanine-to-glycine substitution at position 8 (resists [[Dipeptidyl Peptidase 4|DPP-4]] cleavage), a fatty-acid side chain at position 26 (binds plasma albumin for long half-life), and a small further modification at position 34. The albumin-binding chemistry stretches half-life from native GLP-1's ~2 minutes to ~7 days — enabling once-weekly dosing.

The Wegovy dose (2.4mg) is roughly twice the maximum [[Ozempic]] dose (2.0mg), reflecting that obesity efficacy improves with higher GLP-1 receptor activation than diabetes glycemic control requires. See [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the full class context and the [Technologies vault GLP-1 note](obsidian://open?vault=technologies&file=GLP-1%20Receptor%20Agonists) for the underlying medicinal chemistry.

## Efficacy

| Trial | Endpoint | Result |
|-------|----------|--------|
| STEP-1 (68 weeks) | Weight loss vs placebo | 14.9% (semaglutide) vs 2.4% (placebo) |
| STEP-3 (with intensive lifestyle) | Weight loss | 16.0% |
| SELECT (cardiovascular outcomes) | Major adverse cardiovascular events | 20% reduction in MACE — first GLP-1 cardiovascular outcomes win in non-diabetics |
| SURMOUNT-5 (head-to-head vs tirzepatide) | Weight loss | **13.7%** (Wegovy) vs **20.2%** ([[Mounjaro|tirzepatide]]) — head-to-head loss |

The SURMOUNT-5 head-to-head loss to tirzepatide (published late 2024) was a structural setback. Lilly's dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist consistently outperforms semaglutide monotherapy on weight loss; this is the medicinal-chemistry basis for Lilly's 58% US GLP-1 share against Novo's 42%.

## Compounding and distribution

Wegovy was on the FDA semaglutide shortage list from 2022 through February 2025. During the shortage window, compounding pharmacies and telehealth platforms ([[Hims & Hers]], [[Ro]], LifeMD) supplied compounded semaglutide to ~2 million US patients (year ending Nov 2024). After shortage resolution and FDA enforcement, the compounded gray market collapsed — culminating in [[Novo Nordisk]]'s February 2026 lawsuit against Hims & Hers, which was settled March 9, 2026 with a branded distribution agreement under [[Mike Doustdar]]. Wegovy is now distributed through Novo's NovoCare ecommerce platform, telehealth partners ([[Hims & Hers]], [[Ro]], Weight Watchers, LifeMD), and traditional pharmacy.

See [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the full enforcement timeline and [[Pharma consumerization]] for the channel strategy.

## Competitive position

| Drug | Manufacturer | Mechanism | Form |
|------|-------------|-----------|------|
| **Wegovy injectable** | [[Novo Nordisk]] | GLP-1 monotherapy peptide | Once-weekly injection |
| **Wegovy oral pill** | [[Novo Nordisk]] | GLP-1 monotherapy peptide + SNAC | Daily pill |
| Zepbound | [[Eli Lilly]] | Dual GIP/GLP-1 peptide | Once-weekly injection — superior weight loss |
| [[Foundayo]] | [[Eli Lilly]] | Small-molecule oral GLP-1 | Daily pill — no SNAC, anytime dosing |
| [[CagriSema]] | [[Novo Nordisk]] | GLP-1 + amylin combo | Once-weekly injection — Novo's late-stage response |

Wegovy's competitive trajectory: structurally outflanked on efficacy (Zepbound, [[Retatrutide]]), challenged on convenience (Foundayo's once-daily oral with no food restrictions). Novo's bull case rests on volume gains from the price cuts, the oral-pill rollout, and [[CagriSema]] approval (filed Dec 2025).
