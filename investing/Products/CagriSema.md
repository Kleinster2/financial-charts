**CagriSema** ([[Novo Nordisk]]) — Investigational once-weekly fixed-dose injectable combining a novel amylin analog ([[cagrilintide]]) with [[Wegovy|semaglutide]] in a single pen. Designed as Novo's next-generation obesity drug — the first amylin/GLP-1 combination, intended to combat [[Eli Lilly]]'s tirzepatide ([[Mounjaro]]/Zepbound) and [[Retatrutide|retatrutide]] efficacy lead. NDA submitted to FDA December 18, 2025; review expected through 2026; approval projected late 2026 or early 2027 with launch in 2027.

CagriSema is the centerpiece of Novo's defense of its obesity franchise as semaglutide approaches the end of its competitive lifecycle. The drug's REDEFINE 1 Phase 3 result of 22.7% weight loss is meaningfully above [[Wegovy]]'s ~17% but materially below [[Retatrutide|retatrutide]]'s 28.7% in TRIUMPH-4 — and below the 25% target Novo had publicly set, a disappointment that contributed to the December 2024 stock drop and was one of the proximate causes of CEO [[Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen]]'s May 2025 ouster.

## Mechanism — adding a second metabolic axis

CagriSema combines two distinct hormone-mimetic peptides in one injection:

| Component | Hormone class | Mechanism contribution |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------|
| Semaglutide | GLP-1 receptor agonist | Insulin secretion + appetite suppression + delayed gastric emptying |
| Cagrilintide | Amylin analog | Satiety signaling via the AP/area postrema; complementary appetite suppression |

Amylin is a 37-amino-acid peptide hormone co-secreted with insulin from pancreatic beta cells. It acts on the hindbrain to slow gastric emptying and increase satiety — partially overlapping with GLP-1's effects but through a different receptor system. The combination hypothesis: dual receptor activation produces additive appetite suppression beyond what GLP-1 alone achieves, with the cagrilintide component carrying weight-loss effect at lower semaglutide doses (potentially better GI tolerability).

The strategic difference vs [[Mounjaro|tirzepatide]]: tirzepatide is a *single* peptide engineered to bind two receptors (GIP + GLP-1); CagriSema is *two* peptides delivered together. Manufacturing is more complex but the molecular flexibility is higher — Novo can independently tune each component's dose without re-engineering the molecule.

Cagrilintide alone is also being developed by Novo as a standalone drug; the molecule was the basis for the 2017 acquisition of [[Zealand Pharma]]'s amylin program intellectual property.

## Efficacy

| Trial | Endpoint | Result |
|-------|----------|--------|
| REDEFINE 1 (Phase 3, obesity without diabetes) | Weight loss at week 68 | **22.7%** mean weight loss; 60% achieved ≥20%; 23% achieved ≥30% |
| REDEFINE 1 | Proportion ≥5% weight loss | 91.9% (vs 31.5% placebo) |
| REDEFINE 2 (Phase 3, obesity + Type 2 diabetes) | Weight loss at week 68 | **15.7%** (vs 3.1% placebo) |

The 22.7% REDEFINE 1 result was Novo's most-watched Phase 3 readout of 2024 and disappointed against the 25% target Novo had publicly emphasized. The shortfall, combined with the Lilly head-to-head losses, was an explicit driver of the May 2025 [[Novo Nordisk Foundation]] decision to replace [[Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen]] with [[Mike Doustdar]]. See [[Novo Nordisk]] for the full strategic context.

## Strategic significance

For [[Novo Nordisk]]:

1. **Bridge to next-gen.** CagriSema is the bridge between current semaglutide franchise and longer-term programs (amycretin, oral CagriSema, future polyagonists). If approved, it stabilizes obesity revenue through the 2027–2030 window when emerging-market generic semaglutide and Lilly competition would otherwise compress the franchise.

2. **Channel-aware design.** As a fixed-dose combination delivered once weekly through a single pen, CagriSema fits the Novo telehealth and direct-to-consumer channel strategy under [[Mike Doustdar]]'s [[Pharma consumerization]] pivot. Compounding pharmacies cannot easily replicate a fixed-dose combination of two peptides.

3. **Manufacturing leverage.** Both components use Novo's existing peptide manufacturing infrastructure ($4bn+ US capacity expansion 2022–2025). No new platform investment required.

For competitors:

- [[Eli Lilly]]'s response is [[Retatrutide|retatrutide]] (triple agonist, 28.7% weight loss in TRIUMPH-4) — clearly superior on the headline efficacy number but later to market and with tolerability concerns
- [[Foundayo]]'s differentiation is convenience (oral) rather than maximum efficacy

CagriSema is positioned in the efficacy-tolerability middle, betting that a meaningfully-better-than-Wegovy injectable with a clean tolerability profile will hold a commercial position even as triple-agonist competition emerges.

## Adjacent Novo programs

| Program | Status | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Cagrilintide monotherapy | Phase 2 / 3 | Amylin analog standalone |
| Amycretin | Phase 3 launching early 2026 | Single-molecule dual GLP-1/amylin (the "two-in-one" version of CagriSema) — 22% weight loss in 36 weeks; potential best-in-class |
| Oral CagriSema | Earlier-stage | Pill formulation of the combination |

Amycretin, if it reads out cleanly, would supersede CagriSema's strategic role — a single peptide engineered to do what CagriSema requires two peptides to deliver. The two-program parallel structure (CagriSema for near-term commercialization, amycretin for medium-term replacement) mirrors Lilly's tirzepatide → retatrutide internal succession.
