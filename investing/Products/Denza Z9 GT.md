# Denza Z9 GT

Premium electric grand tourer by Denza, [[BYD]]'s wholly-owned premium sub-brand, positioned to compete in the performance luxury EV segment against [[Tesla]] Model S Plaid and [[Porsche]] Taycan. European launch scheduled for April 8, 2026 at Paris's Palais Garnier, marking the first [[BYD]]-family vehicle to bring Flash Charging 2.0 to Europe.

## Synopsis

The Z9 GT sits at the apex of Denza's lineup: a 122 kWh Blade Battery 2.0, triple-motor powertrain producing 960+ PS, sub-3-second 0-100 km/h, and up to 800 km WLTP range on the rear-wheel-drive variant. The headline spec is Flash Charging 2.0 compatibility — 1,500 kW peak charging — which dramatically reduces charge times and serves as [[BYD]]'s answer to [[Tesla]]'s Supercharger network advantage. The choice of a grand tourer form factor rather than an SUV signals Denza's intent to compete on driving experience and design, not just range and price.

The European launch is strategically significant. [[BYD]] has been steadily expanding in Europe through its more affordable models (Atto 3, Seal, Dolphin), but the Z9 GT represents a brand-elevation play — launching a flagship at a Paris opera house rather than a motor show is designed to position Denza alongside European luxury marques. The timing coincides with EU tariff uncertainty on Chinese EVs (provisional duties of 17-38% imposed in 2024, under review), which makes the pricing equation for the Z9 GT in Europe uncertain. If [[BYD]] can absorb the tariffs and still undercut [[Porsche]]/[[Mercedes-Benz]] on a spec-for-spec basis, the Z9 GT becomes a proof point for Chinese premium EVs in the European market.
