#product #space #commercial-station

**Haven-1** — [[Vast Space]]'s first commercial space station. NET May 2026 launch on [[SpaceX]] [[Falcon 9]]. Targeted as the first standalone (non-ISS-attached) commercial LEO platform. Single-module, free-flying, ~3-year operational life supporting 4 short-duration crewed missions averaging 10 days each. Successful operation positions Vast as a credible Phase 2 winner in the [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] competition (NASA selection mid-2026).

## Why Haven-1 matters

Haven-1 is Vast's pathfinder — proving operational competence ahead of the [[Haven-2]] [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] Phase 2 bid. Per Vast leadership (cited in industry coverage): "If we stick to our plan, we will be the first standalone commercial LEO platform ever in space with Haven-1."

The CLD context (see [[Commercial LEO Destinations]]): NASA Phase 2 selection is targeted mid-2026. ~$4B private capital deployed across CLD candidates ([[Vast Space]], [[Voyager]] [[Star Lab]], [[Axiom Space]], [[Orbital Reef]]). Haven-1's successful launch and operations is the demonstration of capability that distinguishes the credible commercial-station providers from concept-stage entrants.

## Mission profile

| Phase | Description |
|---|---|
| Launch | NET May 2026, Falcon 9, free-flying deployment (no ISS rendezvous) |
| Operations | 4 crewed missions over 3-year lifespan |
| Crew composition | 4 astronauts per mission, ~10 days |
| Total astronaut-days | 160 |
| Reentry | End-of-life deorbit |

The mission profile is deliberately conservative — Haven-1 is not the long-duration habitat that [[Haven-2]] would be. It's a demonstration of station-keeping, life support, crew operations, and revenue generation (sovereign space agency missions, private astronaut missions).

## Customer profile

Vast's business model leans on existing customers rather than speculative future markets:

| Customer tier | Description |
|---|---|
| NASA | Anchor — flights through CLD framework |
| Sovereign space agencies | Japan, Europe (ESA), UAE, Saudi Arabia — follow NASA |
| Private astronaut missions | Fee-paying private crews (e.g., Axiom-style) |
| Speculative new markets | In-space pharma manufacturing, media, sponsorship — *not* attributed material revenue in Vast's model |

[[Max Haot]]'s Apr 2026 framing: "We look at the existing market of sovereign space agencies and our low-cost approach can get us to profitability there. Now... new markets — manufacturing drugs in space, cure disease, media and entertainment sponsorship, private individual buying mission — that's the new market that everyone with a space station will get to develop. But in our business model, we are conservative. We are not attributing any material revenue to that."
