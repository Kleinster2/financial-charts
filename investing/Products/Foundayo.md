**Foundayo** ([[Eli Lilly]]) — Oral GLP-1 receptor agonist for obesity and weight management. Brand name for orforglipron, a once-daily, small-molecule (non-peptide) GLP-1RA that can be taken any time of day without food or water restrictions. FDA-approved April 1, 2026; commercial launch followed within days through [[LillyDirect]] and telehealth providers, with retail pharmacy expansion thereafter.

Foundayo is the second oral weight-loss GLP-1 to launch in 2026 — [[Wegovy]]'s pill version preceded it by three months — but the first true small-molecule oral GLP-1, which is the more strategically significant fact. As a small molecule rather than a peptide, orforglipron is materially cheaper to manufacture, not subject to peptide compounding dynamics, and not exposed to the biosimilar pathway that will determine semaglutide's eventual genericization.

The product was discovered by [[Chugai Pharmaceutical]] (Japan) and licensed to Lilly in 2018.

## Strategic significance

Three reasons Foundayo's launch matters more than the FT framing of "Lilly's rival oral pill":

1. **Manufacturing economics.** Small-molecule oral synthesis is ~order-of-magnitude cheaper than injectable peptide manufacturing. Lilly does not face the same capacity-build problem that constrained Novo's Wegovy launch
2. **Compounding immunity.** Compounding pharmacies cannot replicate small-molecule patented chemistry the way they could replicate semaglutide peptide. The regulatory loophole that hurt Novo from 2022–2025 cannot apply to Foundayo
3. **TAM expansion.** Oral dosing without food/water restrictions removes the largest patient adherence barrier in injectable GLP-1s. Lilly's bull case is that small-molecule orals expand the addressable market beyond the current ~12% US adult penetration

## Competitive position

| Drug | Company | Form | Mechanism |
|------|---------|------|-----------|
| **Foundayo** | [[Eli Lilly]] | Oral, small-molecule | GLP-1 monotherapy |
| [[Wegovy]] pill | [[Novo Nordisk]] | Oral, peptide | GLP-1 monotherapy |
| Zepbound | [[Eli Lilly]] | Injectable | GIP/GLP-1 dual agonist |
| Wegovy injectable | [[Novo Nordisk]] | Injectable | GLP-1 monotherapy |

Wegovy oral was approved December 2025 and launched January 2026 — 600,000 US prescriptions in its first three months, "one of the most successful drug launches in pharmaceutical history" (Novo). Foundayo erodes the first-mover advantage at the four-month mark.
