#product #edtech #lms #opensource

**Moodle** is the dominant open-source learning management system globally, founded in 2002 by Australian academic Martin Dougiamas and developed by Moodle HQ Pty Ltd in Perth, Australia. Free to install and use under the GPL license, Moodle has the largest international LMS user base — particularly outside North America — though its share of US higher-ed enrollment is meaningfully smaller than [[Canvas LMS]] or [[D2L Brightspace]]. Self-hosting institutions value the source-available model and the absence of per-seat license fees; the trade-off is the need for in-house technical maintenance or a paid Moodle Partner integrator.

## Synopsis

Moodle's strategic position is the open-source alternative to commercial LMS vendors. The community model creates a different competitive dynamic from commercial vendors: there is no commercial sales force pushing Moodle into greenfield deals at US universities, but there is a global community of self-hosting institutions, particularly across Europe, Latin America, Asia, and Africa, that value not paying license fees and being able to modify the platform. In the US higher-ed market, Moodle's institutional share is small relative to [[Canvas LMS]] but remains meaningful at smaller institutions and community colleges where IT cost discipline matters more than the breadth of vendor support.

Moodle Workplace is the corporate L&D variant, competing with [[Cornerstone OnDemand]] and others in workplace training rather than against academic-LMS peers.
