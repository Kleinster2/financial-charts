# Hot Wheels

Hot Wheels is [[Mattel]]'s die-cast vehicle brand, launched in 1968 as a direct competitor to Matchbox cars. It has since grown into one of the best-selling toy brands in the world, with over 8 billion cars produced. Hot Wheels is the #1 selling toy in the world by unit volume and a key growth driver for [[Mattel]], generating ~$1.1B in annual revenue (2023) — making it larger than [[Barbie]] was before the movie-driven resurgence.

The brand's durability comes from its multi-generational collector base: adult collectors now account for a meaningful share of revenue through premium lines (Hot Wheels Legends, Red Line Club) that sell for $20-200+ per car versus $1 for basic models. [[Mattel]] has expanded the franchise into digital (Hot Wheels Unleashed video games), live events (Monster Trucks Live), and content (Netflix series). The brand's licensing and experiential revenue stream makes it a hedge against the secular decline in physical toy demand.
