#product #pharma #animal-health #dermatology #jak-inhibitor

[[Apoquel]] (oclacitinib tablet) — [[Zoetis]] flagship companion-animal dermatology drug. First Janus kinase ([[JAK]]) inhibitor approved for veterinary use. Indicated for the control of pruritus associated with allergic dermatitis and the control of atopic dermatitis in dogs at least 12 months of age. FDA approved May 2013.

The drug is the cornerstone of [[Zoetis]]'s companion-animal derm franchise (paired with [[Cytopoint]] for the immunology-side of the same indication). Apoquel + Cytopoint together are one of two pillars of the May 7, 2026 Q1 commentary on competitive pressure in dermatology — see [[Zoetis]] Q1 2026 section on [[2026 recession risk]] consumer-pulse fade in pet care, with intensifying competition from [[Merck Animal Health|Merck]]'s Numelvi (atinvicitinib) JAK inhibitor.

---

## Mechanism

JAK inhibitor selective for JAK1 and JAK3. Inhibits pro-inflammatory and pruritogenic (itch-causing) cytokines that depend on those pathways. Onset of action: relief from pruritus within 4 hours; effective control within 24 hours. Oral once-daily then twice-daily maintenance.

---

## Clinical efficacy

In allergic-dermatitis trials: 67% treatment success after one week vs 29% on placebo. Cumulative use across [[Zoetis]] companion-animal label is now in the millions of dogs since 2013.

## Q1 2026 read

[[Zoetis]] Q1 2026 commentary attributed part of the revenue miss to "intensifying competition" specifically in dermatology — Apoquel + [[Cytopoint]] franchise is the named pressure point. The competitive backdrop is structurally different from prior years because [[Numelvi]] launched into a market where pet-owners are showing increased price sensitivity (per CEO commentary), making switching dynamics more elastic.
