#product #china #space

Tiangong — [[China]]'s modular space station and the low-Earth-orbit testbed for the [[Chinese crewed lunar program]]. First hosted crew in 2021 and is now the platform where the [[China Manned Space Agency]] is testing long-duration human physiology, docking procedures, space-station logistics, and lunar-mission hardware specifications.

## May 2026 lunar-readiness role

CMSA told state media that Tiangong supports the 2030 lunar target in three ways: it builds an experienced taikonaut pool, validates technologies in orbit, and provides a platform for testing spacecraft specifications. Reuters then tied the [[Shenzhou]]-23 mission directly to that program logic: one astronaut is expected to remain aboard Tiangong for a year, and the mission will study radiation exposure, bone-density loss, psychological stress, and autonomous docking procedures.

The station is also the platform for China's more speculative human-space-habitation science. Reuters reported that Chinese state media described a human artificial-embryo experiment using stem-cell samples sent to Tiangong in May 2026, intended to study long-term human residence, survival, and reproduction in space.
