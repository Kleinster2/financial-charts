[[JennAir]] — [[Whirlpool]]'s luxury / built-in kitchen appliance brand. Originally founded 1947 by Louis Jenn (initially as Jenn-Air Products Company in Indianapolis), known for inventing the first downdraft cooktop (1961). Acquired by [[Maytag]] 1982; came to [[Whirlpool]] via the 2006 [[Maytag]] acquisition. Repositioned as Whirlpool's luxury built-in brand in the 2010s.

Within the [[Whirlpool]] portfolio, JennAir competes head-to-head with [[Sub-Zero]] / [[Wolf]] (privately held), [[Miele]], [[Thermador]] (Bosch / [[BSH Home Appliances|BSH]]), and [[Viking]] / [[Middleby Residential]] for the luxury segment of the kitchen appliance market — typically integrated into custom-cabinetry kitchens at the $4,000-$15,000+ per appliance price point.

The luxury segment is structurally less cyclical than mass-market appliances because the customer is less price-sensitive and the purchase is typically tied to a renovation or new-construction project where the appliance is a small line item in a large project budget. That said, Q1 2026 [[Whirlpool]] guide-cut commentary suggests even the luxury / built-in segment is showing demand softness — likely the new-construction / multifamily-luxury sub-segment specifically.
