# Aito (问界 / Wenjie)

[[Huawei]]-branded EV line co-developed with [[Seres]] as the manufacturing partner. Aito sits inside [[Huawei]]'s broader automotive HarmonyOS / ADrive software-defined vehicle (SDV) ecosystem and represents the company's most direct go-to-market in passenger EVs.

## Product family

| Model | Segment | Notable |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Aito M5 | Mid-size SUV | First Aito; HarmonyOS cabin |
| Aito M7 | Larger SUV | Volume seller |
| Aito M9 | Full-size luxury SUV | Top of China's ultra-luxury segment as of 2026 — onboard fridge, karaoke system, color-projector headlights, optional onboard toilet, Huawei ADrive autonomy |

The M9's distinguishing feature set is the cleanest illustration of [[Reverse tech transfer in China auto|how domestic Chinese OEMs are leapfrogging foreign luxury brands]] on feature-richness rather than competing on traditional brand prestige or engine refinement.

## Position in Huawei's auto strategy

Aito is one of three primary "smart-vehicle" brand investments for Huawei (alongside [[Luxeed]] (Chery JV) and [[Stelato]] (BAIC JV)). Across all three, Huawei contributes:

- HarmonyOS in-vehicle operating system
- ADrive ADAS / autonomous-driving stack
- Cabin AI and voice assistant
- Smart-driving compute (running on [[Ascend]] automotive variants)

The OEM partner contributes the chassis, manufacturing, and homologation. The reversed JV structure — where the *technology* comes from the Chinese tech company and the *manufacturing* comes from the Chinese OEM — is structurally distinct from both the legacy foreign-JV pattern and pure-play startups like [[Nio]] / [[Xpeng]] / [[Li Auto]].

## Why it matters

Aito is the visible competitive pressure on foreign luxury brands inside [[China]] — the M9 has displaced a meaningful share of [[Mercedes-Benz]] and [[BMW]] full-size SUV demand in the domestic market through 2025-2026. It also represents the first credible test case for whether Huawei's cross-licensing strategy can scale into a global automotive software franchise akin to what [[Google|Android Auto]] and [[Apple|CarPlay]] are for connectivity.
