# LPG

Liquefied petroleum gas — a mixture of propane and butane produced as a byproduct of oil refining and natural gas processing. Used globally for heating, cooking, petrochemical feedstock, and vehicle fuel. Critical household fuel in developing economies where piped natural gas networks don't exist.

## Why LPG matters

LPG occupies a unique position in the energy stack: it's simultaneously a petrochemical feedstock (competing with [[Naphtha]] in crackers), a household fuel (cooking and heating in India, SE Asia, Africa), and a transport fuel (autogas in parts of Europe and Asia). This triple demand base means supply disruptions create cascading effects across sectors that don't obviously connect — a Gulf LPG shortage can simultaneously raise the cost of plastic production in China and cooking fuel in Indian households.

---

## March 2026 — Iran conflict

The [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure disrupted LPG flows from the Gulf's largest exporters. Two critical developments:

**India** suspended LPG shipments to commercial operators (hotels, restaurants, industry) to prioritize household consumers. This reflects the political sensitivity of cooking fuel in India — LPG subsidies are a cornerstone of government welfare programs. Hotels and restaurants warned of forced closures.

**[[Wanhua Chemical]]** (China) declared force majeure for Middle Eastern customers, citing LPG feedstock disruptions. Wanhua is the world's largest MDI (methylene diphenyl diisocyanate) producer — the feedstock shortage cascades into polyurethane, insulation, and automotive supply chains.
