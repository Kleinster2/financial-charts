# Purple AI

[[SentinelOne]]'s autonomous security operations platform, using generative AI for threat hunting, investigation, and response. Hit a 50%+ attach rate on new licenses in Q4 FY2026 (January 2026) — a record that signals Purple AI is transitioning from a feature add-on to a core purchasing driver for [[SentinelOne]]'s endpoint security business.

## Synopsis

Purple AI launched in 2023 as [[SentinelOne]]'s answer to the growing demand for AI-powered security operations. The platform enables security analysts to query their environment using natural language, automate investigation workflows, and receive AI-generated threat assessments — collapsing what traditionally took hours of manual log analysis into minutes. The 50%+ attach rate on new deals means more than half of new [[SentinelOne]] customers are buying Purple AI alongside the core endpoint protection platform, which validates the thesis that AI-native security tools command premium pricing rather than cannibalizing existing revenue.

The competitive context matters: [[CrowdStrike]]'s Charlotte AI, [[Microsoft]]'s Security Copilot, and [[Palo Alto Networks]]' AI-powered features all target the same opportunity — using LLMs to democratize security operations and address the chronic analyst shortage (estimated 3.5M unfilled cybersecurity positions globally). For [[SentinelOne]], Purple AI is an existential differentiator: as a smaller player ($800M+ ARR vs. [[CrowdStrike]]'s $4B+), it needs a compelling reason for enterprises to choose its platform over the market leader. The attach rate trajectory — from launch to 50%+ in under two years — suggests Purple AI is providing that reason, but the durability depends on whether generative AI security features become a commodity or remain a genuine switching cost.
