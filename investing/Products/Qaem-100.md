**Qaem-100** -- [[Iran]]'s three-stage solid-fuel space launch vehicle, developed by the [[IRGC]] Aerospace Force. Successfully placed the 60 kg Chamran-1 satellite into 550 km low Earth orbit in 2024. Its military significance lies in potential conversion to an intermediate-range ballistic missile: analysts estimate it could deliver ~1,000 kg to 3,000-4,000 km, making it the leading candidate for the missiles fired at [[Diego Garcia]] on March 21, 2026. The solid-fuel design gives it immediate military utility -- rapid launch, mobile basing, no field fueling -- that [[Iran]]'s liquid-fueled Simorgh SLV lacks.
