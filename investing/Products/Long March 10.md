Long March 10 — [[China]]'s heavy-lift rocket family under development for the [[Chinese crewed lunar program]]. The [[China Manned Space Agency]] frames the rocket family as dual-use across near-Earth transport and future crewed lunar exploration, with flight data from near-Earth variants expected to improve the reliability of the lunar mission stack.

## Program role

Reuters reported that China has been carrying out safety tests on the hardware needed for the 2030 mission, including Long March 10 rockets, [[Mengzhou]], and [[Lanyue]]. China Daily added that Long March 10 family development is being integrated across near-Earth space-station transportation and the future lunar stack, with multiple flights over the next two years meant to build technical maturity.

That makes Long March 10 the Chinese equivalent of the US heavy-lift bottleneck in the [[Artemis]] system. The US has flown SLS and is now waiting on lander readiness; China has a regular [[Shenzhou]] / [[Tiangong]] cadence but still needs to prove the new heavy-lift and lunar-transfer stack.
