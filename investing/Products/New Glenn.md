**New Glenn** — [[Blue Origin]] heavy-lift launch vehicle. First-stage reusable, methane-LOX (BE-4 engines). 45,000 kg payload to LEO. First orbital launch 2024. By late May 2026, the program had reached three orbital launches but then suffered a launch-pad hotfire explosion, making flight cadence the central question.

## Why New Glenn matters

New Glenn is the access-to-orbit foundation under Blue Origin's full vertical-integration play: [[Project Sunrise]] (51,600 orbital DC satellites), Blue Moon ([[Artemis]] V lunar lander), and [[Orbital Reef]] (commercial space station with [[Sierra Space]]). All three programs depend on New Glenn cadence reaching production scale. Per [[Space Capital]] (Apr 2026), [[SpaceX]] [[Falcon 9]] is capacity-constrained by internal demand, opening commercial launch slots that New Glenn can absorb if the rocket reaches the cadence Blue Origin is targeting.

## May 2026 - factory scale versus launch reliability

The May 2026 upper-stage facility announcement and May 28 pad explosion define the New Glenn constraint. The facility is a production-scale answer: 830,000 square feet at Cape Canaveral, roughly $600M of investment, and a stated focus on upper-stage manufacturing. The explosion is an execution answer: before the new factory matters, Blue Origin still has to diagnose the vehicle/pad anomaly, repair any launch complex damage, and demonstrate repeatable launches.

The program should therefore be read as capacity in waiting, not capacity already earned. [[Project Sunrise]], Blue Moon, and Orbital Reef all become more credible if New Glenn reaches cadence; they remain option-like while each pad or vehicle failure can reset the schedule.

*Sources: Space Florida / Florida governor announcement, May 22 2026; AP, May 29 2026; Reuters via GMA News, May 29 2026.*
