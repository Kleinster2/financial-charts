Barbie is [[Mattel]]'s flagship fashion doll brand and its largest revenue contributor, launched in 1959 by Ruth Handler and named after her daughter Barbara. The brand generated ~$1.5B in annual gross billings pre-movie, making it the world's best-selling fashion doll franchise. Barbie spans dolls, accessories, media content, licensing, and experiential (Barbie Dreamhouse experiences).

The July 2023 *Barbie* film directed by Greta Gerwig and starring Margot Robbie grossed $1.4B at the global box office, making it the highest-grossing film of 2023 and triggering a massive brand halo effect. Mattel saw Barbie gross billings jump 16% in Q3 2023 and the film validated CEO Ynon Kreiz's strategy of transforming Mattel from a toy company into an "IP-driven entertainment company." The film's success catalyzed [[Mattel]]'s broader film slate (including planned [[Hot Wheels]], Polly Pocket, and other franchise adaptations) and a [[Mattel]] stock re-rating. Post-film normalization saw billings decline in 2024 as the one-time boost faded.
