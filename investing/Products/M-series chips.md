# M-series chips

[[Apple]]'s system-on-chip line for the Mac and iPad — the desktop/laptop-class counterpart to the iPhone's A-series. The line opened with the M1 in November 2020, the chip that began Apple's two-year transition off [[Intel]] processors, and ran through M2 (2022), M3 (2023, [[TSMC]] 3nm) and M4 (2024). It shares the unified-memory, big-core architecture of Apple's phone silicon, scaled up with more CPU/GPU cores and memory bandwidth across the Pro / Max / Ultra variants.

The open question now is foundry, not design: [[Apple]] is reportedly evaluating [[Intel]]'s 18A node ([[Intel 18A]]) for future M-class production around 2027. If validated, it would make Apple's design organization — not any single foundry's process — the durable moat, loosening its dependence on [[TSMC]]. [[Apple Silicon]] covers the umbrella in-housing strategy; this note tracks the Mac/iPad chip line itself. See [[Apple#Innovation gap and the reinvestment question (FT, May 2026)]] for the strategic frame.
