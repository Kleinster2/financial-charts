Lanyue — [[China]]'s crewed lunar lander under development for the [[Chinese crewed lunar program]]. It is the surface-transfer vehicle paired with the [[Mengzhou]] crew spacecraft, with the mission architecture depending on automated rendezvous between the two vehicles in lunar orbit.

## Program role

CMSA has identified Lanyue as one of the major lunar-landing flight products undergoing prototype R&D and testing. Reuters' May 2026 Shenzhou-23 report made the operational link clearer: [[Shenzhou]]-23 will practice rapid autonomous rendezvous and docking with [[Tiangong]]'s core module ahead of the 2030 lunar architecture, which depends on automated lunar-orbit rendezvous between [[Mengzhou]] and Lanyue.

That means Lanyue is one of the hard readiness gates for China's 2030 target. The hardware can be state-funded, but it still has to prove human-rated landing, ascent, docking, and abort reliability on a compressed calendar.
