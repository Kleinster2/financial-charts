[[Maytag]] — [[Whirlpool]]'s heritage / durability-positioned brand within the multi-brand portfolio. Founded 1893 by F.L. Maytag in Newton, Iowa; acquired by [[Whirlpool]] in March 2006 for $1.7B. The brand carries the strongest durability / "Maytag Repairman" ad-equity in the Whirlpool portfolio and historically commands a price premium in laundry (washers / dryers) and dishwashers above the mass-market Whirlpool brand.

Within the [[Whirlpool]] portfolio:
- [[Whirlpool brand|Whirlpool]] — mass-market core (price / volume)
- Maytag — durability / heritage (mid-premium)
- [[KitchenAid]] — premium kitchen-focused
- [[JennAir]] — luxury / built-in
- [[Amana]] — value / multifamily

Q1 2026 Whirlpool guide cut on housing-cycle weakness affects all five brands but has the cleanest pass-through in the mass-market and value segments ([[Whirlpool brand]] and [[Amana]]); Maytag and [[KitchenAid]] are partially insulated by the durability / premium positioning that lets them hold prices through demand softness.
