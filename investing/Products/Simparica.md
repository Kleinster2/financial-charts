#product #pharma #animal-health #parasiticide #isoxazoline

[[Simparica]] (sarolaner) — [[Zoetis]] companion-animal parasiticide franchise. Isoxazoline-class flea and tick treatment for dogs. Available as Simparica (sarolaner monotherapy) and Simparica Trio (sarolaner + moxidectin + pyrantel) — the latter combines flea/tick coverage with heartworm prevention and intestinal parasite control in a single chewable tablet.

One of three pillars of [[Zoetis]]'s companion-animal portfolio (with [[Apoquel]] / [[Cytopoint]] derm + [[Librela]] / [[Solensia]] OA pain). Q1 2026 commentary explicitly named parasiticides as a category facing "intensifying competition" — Simparica's primary head-to-head is [[Boehringer Ingelheim]]'s NexGard (afoxolaner, also isoxazoline), with [[Merck Animal Health|Merck]]'s Bravecto (fluralaner) the secondary competitor.

---

## Mechanism

Isoxazoline-class GABA / glutamate-gated chloride channel inhibitor. Selectively binds insect / acarine GABA receptors, causing uncontrolled neurological excitation and death of fleas and ticks.

Simparica Trio combines:
- Sarolaner (isoxazoline) — flea/tick
- Moxidectin (macrocyclic lactone) — heartworm prevention
- Pyrantel (anthelmintic) — intestinal parasites (roundworm, hookworm)

The Simparica Trio launch in 2020 was strategic — combining three categories into one chewable pulled the parasiticide market toward a single-product dispensing model that's harder for single-category competitors to match. NexGard countered with NexGard Plus (similar three-in-one combination) in 2024, eroding part of Simparica Trio's structural advantage.
