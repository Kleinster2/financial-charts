**Retatrutide** ([[Eli Lilly]]) — Investigational once-weekly injectable peptide designed as the first triple agonist of the GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors in a single molecule. Currently in Phase 3 (TRIUMPH program); Lilly has guided to NDA filing in 2026 with potential FDA approval in 2027. The most-watched late-stage obesity asset in the industry — phase 2 and phase 3 trials have produced the highest weight-loss numbers of any incretin-class drug to date.

If approved, retatrutide replaces tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) as Lilly's flagship metabolic compound — a planned internal succession that mirrors the [[Wegovy]] → CagriSema (Novo) succession but with a more aggressive efficacy bar.

## Mechanism — why three targets

Retatrutide is a single peptide engineered to activate three different receptors simultaneously. The hypothesis behind triple agonism: each hormone contributes a distinct metabolic effect, and combining all three exceeds what dual or single agonists achieve.

| Receptor | Effect contributed |
|----------|-------------------|
| GLP-1 | Insulin secretion in response to glucose; appetite suppression; gastric emptying delay |
| GIP | Insulin secretion (additive to GLP-1); appears to reduce GI side effects of GLP-1; modulates fat metabolism |
| Glucagon | Increases energy expenditure (raises metabolic rate); promotes lipolysis (fat breakdown) |

The glucagon-receptor inclusion is the novel piece. Glucagon is traditionally thought of as the *opposing* hormone to insulin (it raises blood glucose), so adding it to a diabetes drug seems counterintuitive. The mechanism: at therapeutic doses, glucagon receptor activation drives energy expenditure and fat oxidation more than hepatic glucose production, producing net weight loss with manageable glucose effects when balanced by GLP-1/GIP activity.

The molecule was discovered and disclosed (LY3437943) in a Lancet 2022 Phase 1b paper.

## Efficacy

### Obesity

| Trial | Endpoint | Result |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Phase 2 (NEJM 2023, 48 weeks) | Weight loss vs placebo | Up to 24.2% weight loss at highest dose |
| TRIUMPH-4 (Phase 3, 68 weeks, obesity + knee OA) | Weight loss + osteoarthritis pain | **28.7% weight loss** (~71.2 lbs); substantial OA pain relief |
| TRIUMPH-1 (Phase 3, obesity) | Weight loss | Readout expected Q2–Q3 2026 |
| TRIUMPH-2 (Phase 3, obesity + comorbidity) | Weight loss | Readout expected Q2–Q3 2026 |

The 28.7% TRIUMPH-4 figure is the highest weight-loss result reported for any drug class — substantially above tirzepatide's ~22.5% (SURMOUNT-1) and semaglutide's ~17% (STEP-1). It also exceeds the average post-bariatric-surgery weight loss for sleeve gastrectomy.

### Diabetes (Phase 3)

At 40 weeks in Type 2 diabetes patients, retatrutide produced average HbA1c reduction up to 2.0% and weight loss up to 36.6 lbs (16.8%) — superior to placebo on all primary and key secondary endpoints. The diabetes program supports the planned 2026 NDA filing.

## TRIUMPH program (Phase 3)

The TRIUMPH program is one of the most extensive Phase 3 obesity programs ever conducted — eight trials across multiple indications and populations:

| Trial | Indication | Status |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| TRIUMPH-1 | Obesity (chronic weight management) | Q2–Q3 2026 readout |
| TRIUMPH-2 | Obesity + comorbidity | Q2–Q3 2026 readout |
| TRIUMPH-3 | Obesity + cardiovascular outcomes | Long duration |
| TRIUMPH-4 | Obesity + knee osteoarthritis | Reported (28.7% weight loss) |
| TRIUMPH-5 | Type 2 diabetes | Reported (2.0% HbA1c, 16.8% weight loss) |
| TRIUMPH-6 | Obstructive sleep apnea | In progress |
| TRIUMPH-7 | Specific population | In progress |
| TRIUMPH-8 | Specific population | In progress |

Lilly's 2026 NDA filing will anchor on TRIUMPH-1 and TRIUMPH-2 (obesity); TRIUMPH-4 may support a knee-osteoarthritis comorbidity indication.

## Tolerability concern

The Phase 2 trial reported an 18% discontinuation rate at the highest dose (12mg) — meaningfully above tirzepatide's typical ~5–10% Phase 3 discontinuation rate. The dropout was largely GI side effects (nausea, vomiting). This is the most-watched safety signal in the program. If TRIUMPH-1/-2 reproduce the high-discontinuation pattern, Lilly may need to launch with a lower-dose strategy or rely on uptitration protocols, both of which compress real-world efficacy below the Phase 2/3 headline numbers.

## Strategic significance

For [[Eli Lilly]]:

1. **Internal succession.** Retatrutide is positioned to succeed [[Mounjaro]]/Zepbound (tirzepatide) as the flagship injectable. Lilly cannibalizes itself rather than ceding share.
2. **Efficacy ceiling.** ~28.7% weight loss, if it holds in TRIUMPH-1/-2, redefines the category benchmark. Competitors will struggle to match without a comparable triple-agonist or super-polyagonist program.
3. **Cross-indication breadth.** TRIUMPH explicitly tests OA, sleep apnea, and CVD — Lilly is treating obesity as the central node from which multiple comorbidity indications are commercializable.

For [[Novo Nordisk]]:
- Retatrutide is the more pressing competitive threat than [[Foundayo]]. Foundayo competes on convenience (oral); retatrutide competes on efficacy (highest weight loss). Novo's response candidates ([[CagriSema]], amycretin) deliver ~22% weight loss, materially below retatrutide's bar.

For the [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] class:
- Validates the multi-receptor agonism approach beyond dual (tirzepatide). Opens the platform thesis for quadruple, quintuple, and beyond — see academic preclinical work on quintuple agonists at Helmholtz Zentrum München (EASD 2025), discussed in [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]].
