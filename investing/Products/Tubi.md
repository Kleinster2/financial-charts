**Tubi** — [[Fox Corporation]]'s free, ad-supported streaming TV (FAST) service. Acquired by Fox in 2020 for ~$440M. Reached 100M+ monthly active users and its first profitable quarter in Fox's FQ1 2026 (calendar Q3 2025), with revenue up 27% YoY. The most-used free streaming service in US broadband homes by some measures, and the centerpiece of Fox's digital/streaming strategy ahead of the [[Roku]] acquisition.

## Role in the Fox–Roku deal

Tubi is why the [[Roku]] acquisition is strategically coherent for Fox: it gives Fox an owned FAST service to pair with Roku's CTV distribution layer and The Roku Channel. Notably, Fox's stated expectation is to keep The Roku Channel and Tubi as separate brands rather than merge them — two distinct FAST brands under one roof, with Roku contributing the OS/distribution and first-party data. Combined, Tubi (~2.2% of US TV viewing) and The Roku Channel (~3%) give the merged entity two of the largest FAST properties in the US.
