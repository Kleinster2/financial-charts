## Synthesis

The link closes a loop. The Risk Parity vault has tracked AOR as its lone static benchmark for a while but treated "60/40" abstractly in its comparison theory. Tying it to the investing-vault suite note supplies the real instrument behind the abstraction, the three sibling rungs that show 60/40 is just one point on a capital-weighted equity-risk gradient, and the empirical equity-bull premium (AOR 8.4% vs AOA 10.6% CAGR over 17 years) that the vault's regime tables predict only qualitatively. The reusable point is structural: the entire iShares ladder lives on the capital-weighted axis that risk parity deliberately steps off — so AOR is not a midpoint between AOK and a risk-parity fund, it is a point on a different line. No new investing-vault notes today; this is a graph-completion pass plus the commit of the 2026-06-26 ladder work.

The sleeve charts are the first concrete layer of the risk-parity merge scoped earlier: rather than fold 463 machine-generated tracking notes into the investing vault, the integration moves the data into market_data.db and the analysis into the hub. The three charts already earn their place — managed futures show why risk parity carries them (uncorrelated crisis alpha, ~+6.5% CAGR while lagging a +15% S&P), capital-efficient shows the 2022 bond-overlay drag (US NTSX trailing plain SPY) alongside GDE's gold-leverage outlier (+170%), and tail/defensive shows the raw cost of convexity (TAIL −39% over 7.5 years). Each makes a structural risk-parity claim legible on live tickers rather than backtest tables.

The CXMT deepdive sharpens the memory squeeze read-through. The old binary was wrong in both directions: CXMT is not a fake supplier that can be ignored, and it is not a clean Chinese flood of cheap DRAM that breaks the global oligopoly in 2026. The better read is policy-gated validation. CXMT has enough conventional DRAM capability for Apple and PC OEMs to test China-local sourcing, but buyer approvals, 1260H status, possible Entity List escalation, yield/die-size gaps, and HBM qualification keep it from being frictionless global relief. That makes it a wedge supplier: bullish for China self-reliance and a medium-term risk to incumbents, but not yet a cycle-ending force for [[Samsung]], [[SK Hynix]], or [[Micron]].

Continuation cleanup tightened the graph around the same conclusion. [[Zhu Yiming]] no longer carries an unsupported direct Big Fund funding-amount claim; the financing evidence is now framed as state-backed ecosystem support plus the related Changxin Xinqiao financing. The geopolitics-vault trade-war note now treats CXMT/YMTC as part of the same 1260H retaliation architecture as Alibaba/Baidu/BYD and rare-earth countermeasures: capital-markets and procurement labels are functioning as supply-chain statecraft, not just blacklist bookkeeping.

The HBM follow-through moves the same story one technical layer up. The right read is not that China has no path, and not that CXMT breaks the HBM oligopoly. It is that China has demand pull, HBM2/HBM3 roadmap evidence, and reported domestic packaging work, but the value-capture breakpoint is still customer-qualified stacked memory. Until a Huawei/Cambricon/Biren-class accelerator publicly qualifies domestic HBM3/HBM3E at volume, [[SK Hynix]], [[Samsung]], and [[Micron]] retain the pricing umbrella from validated supply, take-or-pay customer agreements, and the HBM4 transition.


The research-migration tranche tests the merge thesis in practice: the lift is selective, not wholesale. Of 127 RP-vault research notes, the three that moved are general portfolio-construction concepts the hub genuinely lacked and can reuse beyond risk parity; the 25-note `05 Implementation` folder, the code, and the case studies stayed put because they are practitioner how-to, not durable concepts. The cleanest weave was [[Hierarchical Risk Parity]]: its correlation-distance clustering ($d=\sqrt{0.5(1-\rho)}$, dendrograms, join distances) is the same machinery the vault's cluster-validation framework already runs — so the migrated note doesn't just sit in the hub, it explains a tool the hub was already using. That is the test for whether a note belongs: does it connect to something already here, or is it vault-tourism.

Tranche 2 lifts the macro half of the risk-parity foundation. The flagship is [[Economic Environment Framework]]: the growth×inflation quadrants are a general regime lens the whole hub can use, not a risk-parity-only device, and its per-quadrant stock-bond correlation table is the conceptual parent of the empirical [[Stock-Bond Correlation Regime]] note — the framework predicts the sign of the correlation, the regime note measures it. That pairing is the migration working as intended: a lifted concept that explains a chart the hub already had rather than arriving context-free. Six of the ~15 lift candidates are now in; the seminal papers and the comparison frameworks remain.

Tranche 3 closes the strategy-comparison layer and produced the migration's first net-new empirical artifact. The factor-zoo chart resolves the Factor-investing note's standing chart gap with on-topic data — and the data makes the note's own argument: over 2013–26 only momentum (MTUM, +16.2% CAGR) beat cap-weighted SPY, with value, size, quality, and low-vol all lagging through the mega-cap growth era. That is single-factor decay rendered in prices, the empirical case for multi-factor diversification the note had asserted in the abstract. Nine of ~15 lift candidates are in; the seminal papers (as one literature note) and the Asset-Classes / Funds dedup pass remain.
