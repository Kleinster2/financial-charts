# 2026-04-05

## Synthesis

The Hormuz crisis entered a new phase this weekend. Trump's Easter ultimatum — bomb Iran's power plants by Tuesday 8 PM if the strait doesn't reopen — is the sharpest escalation yet, and Iran's response (rejecting it, threatening to close [[Bab al-Mandab]] as a second chokepoint) suggests no near-term resolution. The analytical consensus is converging on a grim timeline: [[Natasha Kaneva]]/JPM calculates OECD inventories hit operational minimum (~842M bbl) by late April, after which prices — not stockpiles — become the market-clearing mechanism. [[Francisco Blanch]]/BofA revised up to $92.50 avg for 2026 and introduced $160-200 escalation scenarios. Meanwhile [[IEA]]'s Birol warned "April will be much worse than March" and is weighing a second SPR release.

The demand-side picture is sharpening. [[Robin J. Brooks]]'s Comtrade analysis quantifies what was previously vague: [[Pakistan]] and [[Philippines]] source ~99% and ~97% of crude from the Gulf, [[Japan]] ~93%, [[South Korea]] ~72%. These aren't gradual adjustments — they're economies facing sudden-stop energy access. On the supply side, the alternatives are also under pressure: [[Sergey Vakulenko]] documents how Ukrainian drones destroyed 20 storage tanks across [[Primorsk]] and [[Ust-Luga]], reducing Russia's Baltic export buffer. The "40% halted" headlines overstate throughput impact but understate the strategic vulnerability — if attack tempo exceeds repair cycle, forced production shutdowns follow within weeks.

The recession probability trackers are approaching a decision point. [[Moody's]] AI model sits at 49% — one tick below the 50% threshold that has preceded every US recession in 80 years. [[Goldman Sachs]] raised to 30%, [[EY]]-Parthenon to 40%. The March NFP (+178K vs 59K consensus) superficially reassures, but the household survey showed 64K fewer employed and the labor force shrank by 396K. CPI is heading toward 4% (Cleveland Fed Nowcast already at 3.16% for March). The bulls ([[Ed Yardeni]]: market bottom at S&P 6,316, year-end 7,700; [[Mike Wilson]]: correction "nears end stage") are betting on a quick Hormuz resolution and resilient consumer. The question is whether they get that resolution before inventories hit operational minimum in late April.

Separately: the [[Spire Global]] note (SPIR +18.9%, 3.4σ) revealed a ticker alias collision with [[Sapir Organization]] — fixed. Spire's positioning in [[Golden Dome]] via the SHIELD IDIQ and its March sensor breakthroughs (single-sat RF geolocation, HyMS, quantum magnetometer) make it a defense/ISR name worth tracking, especially with Hormuz driving demand for maritime domain awareness.

Earnings season starts this week: [[Delta Air Lines]] (Apr 8) is the first real corporate test of the oil shock (~$400M Q1 jet fuel headwind). Bank earnings (Apr 14) and [[TSMC]] (Apr 16) follow. Forward guidance is the variable — not headline beats.
