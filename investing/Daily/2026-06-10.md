## Synthesis

The cluster framework's published p-values are now real. Yesterday's audit flagged every registry p-value as measured against a polluted null; today the pool was cleaned, the math fixed, and all 34 cohorts re-graded — and the re-run vindicated the vault's qualitative layer: every cohort that failed the honest test had already been called falsified on intra-correlation, holdout, or threshold grounds, and every validated cohort survived. The causal read-through: the framework's redundancy (four independent diagnostics) is what protected it — when one diagnostic (the permutation p) was silently broken, the others carried the verdicts. The interesting structural finding is that the pollution was size-dependent, not uniformly flattering: tiny cohorts faced an artificially high bar because the dirty pool occasionally dealt all-crypto hands, while weak mid-size cohorts slid under an artificially low one because FX and macro series dragged the null down. Remaining exposure: everything still validates in-sample relative to cohort discovery — the post-definition out-of-sample pass (audit open item 5) is the next structural upgrade.
