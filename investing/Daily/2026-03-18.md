## Key ingestion: The Nitrogen Trap (Shanaka Anslem Perera)

Framework piece arguing the Hormuz closure exposed the most consequential structural asymmetry in modern statecraft: the world built strategic petroleum reserves after the 1973 oil shock, but no equivalent exists for fertilizer — despite ~48% of the global population depending on synthetic nitrogen for food. The crisis propagates through 14 interacting channels (nitrogen → sulfur → petrochemicals → pharmaceuticals → desalination → AdBlue → protein → ethanol → cotton → sovereign debt → monetary policy → aquaculture → packaging → feedback loops). Core thesis: market is pricing ~45 days of disruption, structural indicators (insurance recovery timeline, mine clearance, Red Sea 26-month precedent) suggest 120+ days. The quadratic yield response curve means damage concentrates in the Global South where farming systems operate on the steep ascending portion.
