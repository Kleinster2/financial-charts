## Markets

### JPMorgan marks down private credit software loan collateral

**The stress escalates from fund-level to bank-funding layer.** [[JPMorgan Chase|JPMorgan]]'s trading division marked down the value of software loans used as collateral by private credit funds in its back-leverage financing business. This restricts how much private credit firms can borrow against their portfolios and in some cases could force firms to post more collateral. See [[JPMorgan private credit collateral markdown]] for full event note.

Key details:
- **First major bank** to remark private credit collateral (FT)
- Markdowns apply specifically to **software company loans**
- Pre-emptive — no margin calls triggered. JPM source: "financial discipline rather than waiting until a crisis comes"
- JPM did the same thing during early COVID
- Dimon flagged at JPM leveraged finance conference (early March): being "more prudent" on software assets

**Market reaction:**
- ARES -4.5%
- OWL, KKR, CG ~-2.6%
- JPM -0.5%

**Why this is qualitatively different from prior events:**

| Prior events (fund-level) | Mar 11 (bank-funding level) |
|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| OBDC II gate (Feb 19) — retail redemptions | JPM marks down collateral — restricts back-leverage |
| BCRED record 7.9% redemption (Mar 3) | Reduces private credit funds' borrowing capacity |
| BlackRock/HPS gate (Mar 6) | If other banks follow → systemic leverage squeeze |

Private credit funds now face a **two-sided squeeze**: retail investors pulling capital out (redemptions) + banks reducing the leverage available on remaining assets (collateral markdowns). This is the mechanism that turns fund-level stress into industry-wide deleveraging.

**The progression of the crisis (updated):**
1. ✅ **Equity repricing** (Jan-Feb) — SaaS stocks -25% to -50%
2. ✅ **Primary market freeze** (Feb-Mar) — no major software debt deal since Oracle $25B (Feb 2)
3. ✅ **Retail BDC gates** (Feb 19 →) — Blue Owl, BlackRock/HPS
4. ✅ **Record redemptions** (Mar 3) — Blackstone BCRED 7.9%
5. ✅ **Derivative instruments** (Mar 9) — Goldman TRS to short software loans
6. ✅ **Bank collateral markdowns** (Mar 11) — JPMorgan restricts back-leverage ← **NEW**
7. ⏳ **Other banks follow?** — competitive dynamics make it likely
8. ⏳ **March 31 quarter-end** — >$100B in funds face simultaneous redemption windows

*Sources: FT, Reuters, Bloomberg, CNBC (Mar 11, 2026)*

## Hims & Hers — Novo Nordisk deal resolves GLP-1 overhang

**The single largest risk to HIMS is gone.** [[Novo Nordisk]] dropped its copyright lawsuit and signed a distribution deal: [[Hims & Hers]] will sell branded Ozempic and Wegovy (injectable + oral) at Novo's self-pay prices, and stops advertising compounded GLP-1s except rare clinical cases. [[Novo Nordisk|Novo]] CEO [[Mike Doustdar]] (Mar 9): *"They will no longer advertise, promote, market compounded products to the masses."* FDA Commissioner [[Marty Makary]] endorsed the deal.

**Market reaction:**
- HIMS **+41%** (Mar 9), **+14.8%** (Mar 11) to ~$26
- [[Barclays]] raised PT to $29 (from $25), maintains Overweight
- [[Bank of America]], [[Citigroup|Citi]]: upgraded Hold → Buy

**Why this deal differs from the Apr 2025 collapse:** Hims explicitly agrees to stop compounded GLP-1 marketing (not just add branded alongside). Novo has more leverage now (DOJ referral, FDA crackdown). The investment case pivots from survival to growth.

*Sources: CNBC (Mar 9), Bloomberg (Mar 7), Yahoo Finance*

## IEA record 400M barrel strategic reserve release

**Largest emergency stock release in IEA history** — 400M barrels, dwarfing the 182M barrel Ukraine-era release. [[IEA]] Executive Director [[Fatih Birol]] from Paris: unanimous decision by all 32 member countries. US contributing 172M bbl from SPR (starting next week), [[South Korea]] 22.46M, [[UK]] 13.5M, [[Japan]] and [[Germany]] also contributing.

IEA holds 1.2B bbl public + 600M bbl industry-obligated stocks. But as [[Jeff Currie]] noted earlier today — 400M bbl at 2M bbl/day max flow is "minuscule" against the ~18M bbl/day disruption. The release is a confidence signal, not a supply fix.

**Birol's assessment:** Middle East production cuts, refinery operations disrupted (diesel/jet fuel particularly affected), LNG supply reduced 20% — Asian and European economies competing for cargoes. Brent moved from ~$120 to ~$90 on the week. Structural tightness persists until [[Strait of Hormuz]] tanker traffic resumes.

*Source: CNBC, Al Jazeera, Axios, NPR (Mar 11, 2026)*

## Pentagon Anthropic exemption memo — 180-day ramp-down softened

Pentagon CIO [[Kirsten Davies]] (Mar 6 memo): [[Anthropic]]'s Claude may continue beyond 180-day phase-out in "rare and extraordinary circumstances" where use is "mission-critical" and "no viable alternative exists." Exemptions require risk mitigation plans.

**Key details:** Ban still extends to contractors (30-day notification, then full compliance by deadline). Priority removal from nuclear weapons and ballistic missile defense systems. Supply-chain risk designation remains in force.
