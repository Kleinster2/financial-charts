## Synthesis

Day 4 of [[Operation Epic Fury]]. The defining pattern: US equities pricing a short war while commodities hedge the tail. S&P recovered from -2.2% to -0.88% — second consecutive aggressive dip-buy. Gold reversed -4.48% (profit-taking after $5,400 after +23% in a month). Oil (USO +3.39%) remains the only conflict trade holding. Defense primes gave back all Day 3 gains. AVAV bounced +9.59% off yesterday's SCAR panic. [[Target]] beat earnings ($2.44 vs $2.16 est, +13%), stock +6.74%. [[CrowdStrike]] reports AMC.

Beyond the conflict, today's big theme was AI infrastructure broadening beyond GPUs: [[Ayar Labs]] raised $500M for optical interconnects, [[ASML]] revealed a beyond-EUV roadmap (packaging tools, Hyper-NA, 1000W light source), [[Nebius]]/NVIDIA proved data centers can cut power 40% on demand, and [[Apple]] raised MacBook prices $100-$400 because HBM is eating consumer memory wafers. The infrastructure stack is deepening — optics, packaging, power flexibility, memory allocation — and the investment opportunity set is widening with it. [[Elliott Management|Elliott]] deployed $1B into [[Pinterest]] (AI advertising thesis). [[Anthropic]]'s Pentagon ban entered its second week — [[OpenAI]] took the contract.

Earnings day was heavy: [[Target]] beat on cost control (+6.74%), [[Best Buy]] same story (+5.5%), [[On Running]] beat but guided 23% cc growth vs Street's 30%+ (-9.7%), [[AutoZone]] margin compression (-6.32%), [[Sea Limited]] growth machine ($23B rev, +36%) but EPS miss, [[CrowdStrike]] record everything (ARR $5.25B, 79% GM, $376M FCF). Pattern: the market is punishing deceleration and margin compression even when headline numbers beat, while rewarding cost discipline in consumer. Cybersecurity looks resilient — outage fully healed, Iran conflict adds secular tailwind.
