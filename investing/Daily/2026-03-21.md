## Synthesis

Three streams today, and the tension between them is the story.

The Iran conflict is crossing from disruption into structural damage. Al-Kaabi saying five-year force majeure on some [[LNG]] contracts is not a shipping delay — it is supply destruction. Tooze's "Wile E. Coyote" framing sharpens it: Brent hit $119 intraday and no hedge fund at his Hong Kong conference has a thesis. No one buying the dip, no one panic-selling. That is the Feb 2020 pattern — financial markets running on momentum weeks after the physical economy has already broken. The insurance mechanics we restructured today make the asymmetry precise: closure is instant (JWC lists the area, P&I clubs cancel, charter parties fail, ships halt), recovery requires 30-60 incident-free days before standard policies even begin reinstating. A ceasefire is not a resolution. The [[Hormuz Permanent Risk Premium]] thesis is hardening.

Meanwhile Karpathy hasn't typed code since December. Three months. The 80/20 → 2/98 ratio is not a productivity improvement — it is a phase change. [[AutoResearch]] running overnight experiments against objective metrics, finding optimizations he missed in two decades of manual work. The "claws" paradigm — persistent agents controlling physical systems through WhatsApp — is already running his house. His own framework (digital → interface → physical) puts robotics years out, but the digital layer is already autonomous.

The gap between these two realities is widening. The physical world is getting harder — insurance markets, pipeline bottlenecks, diesel at +56% in Vietnam, five-year force majeure on gas contracts. The digital world is accelerating — agents writing code, running research, eliminating the human bottleneck entirely. Tesla Semi entering mass production into a diesel price spike is the one place the two curves intersect: electrification as energy crisis hedge. But [[Nikola]]'s bankruptcy is a reminder that atoms are harder than bits. The companies that bridge the gap — or profit from the widening — are the positioning question.
