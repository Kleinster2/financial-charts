### Hormuz Transit Regime escalation

[[Lloyd's List]] published detailed analysis calling the Hormuz passage system a "de facto toll booth regime." At least 2 vessels have paid tolls settled in [[yuan]]. Iranian parliament drafting legislation to codify transit fees — lawmaker Rezaei Kouchi: "We provide its security, and it is natural that ships and oil tankers should pay such fees." Only 4 ships crossed the strait on Mar 25 (vs 80-105/day pre-crisis). 10 large vessels staging at Larak Island waiting for IRGC clearance. Two cargo vessels hugged Omani coast without AIS to avoid Iranian system. Legal exposure severe: IRGC is a designated FTO under US law, any payments risk criminal prosecution.

### Google TurboQuant

[[Alphabet|Google]] Research unveiled [[TurboQuant]] — KV-cache compression suite achieving 6x memory reduction and 8x attention speedup on [[NVIDIA]] H100, zero accuracy loss, no retraining. Two-stage: PolarQuant (polar coordinate conversion eliminates normalization overhead) + QJL (1-bit error correction). Papers existed since Apr 2025; formal unveiling timed to ICLR 2026. Open-source, free for commercial use. Community ported to MLX and llama.cpp within 24 hours. Memory stocks sold off but [[Jevons' Paradox]] suggests total memory demand likely increases as inference costs drop.

### Meta Hyperion boomtown dynamics (Fortune deep dive)

Fortune published a detailed ground-level report on [[Meta]]'s Hyperion data center in Richland Parish, Louisiana — the most granular account yet of what happens when a $27B AI infrastructure project lands in a community where 25% live below the poverty line.

Key data: 3,650 total acres (2,250 original + 1,400 acquired Feb 2026), 3 GCs (Turner/DPR/Mortenson), ~3,700 workers currently with peak expected at 5,000-8,000, but only ~500 permanent jobs. $875M in Louisiana contracts in year one, $300M+ in local infrastructure. Construction materials and catering largely sourced from out-of-state. Local food truck operators who bet savings on the boom are being undercut by national catering companies brought in by DPR. Rents tripling; residents receiving eviction notices to "make room" for Meta workers.

Macro context: AI data center capex projected at $630-700B in 2026 (+62% YoY), $5.2T cumulative by 2030 (McKinsey). Amazon also announced $12B in NW Louisiana (Feb 2026). Louisiana positioning as "Silicon Bayou."
