## Synthesis

The dominant story is the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran conflict]] — Operation Epic Fury entered day 3 with the war widening (Hezbollah-Israel strikes, Kuwait friendly fire). Markets are in full risk-off: S&P futures -1.4%, oil +7-9% (spiked 13% intraday), gold at $5,400, VIX +10%, European gas +23%. The most interesting signal is the defense subsector dispersion — drones and primes up 5-10%, defense IT flat. The market is pricing munitions burn, not consulting contracts. This matches the Feb 18 pattern but amplified: US primes moved 4-5x more on actual kinetic conflict than on threat, suggesting the budget-growth thesis was already priced but active weapons consumption was not. The [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure is no longer a tail risk — it's happening: ~150 ships stalled, [[Maersk]] blocking all transit, only Iranian/Chinese vessels going eastbound. [[FGE]] estimates 40M+ barrels disrupted over 2 days. Three supply risk layers: Iranian production (2.5M bbl/day), Hormuz transit (20M+ bbl/day), and now regional infrastructure attacks (Ras Tanura, Qatar LNG, Dukhan, Jebel Ali). The LNG/petrochemical angle is underappreciated — European gas +23% and Qatar LNG production halted means this isn't just an oil story.

Separately, NVIDIA's $4B photonics investment in [[Lumentum]] and [[Coherent]] signals the next bottleneck shift: from compute to interconnect. Co-packaged optics replaces copper links with integrated photonics, and NVIDIA is locking in supply for its Spectrum-X/Quantum-X switches. The nonexclusive structure is smart — NVIDIA gets capacity rights and purchase commitments without paying an exclusivity premium, while both suppliers retain other customers. CPO market at 37% CAGR to $20B by 2036.

[[Palantir]]'s UBS upgrade (Buy, $180 PT) comes at an interesting intersection — sitting at the crossroads of AI spend and defense spend during active conflict. But the stock moved on the upgrade, not on Iran, which tells you the market doesn't treat Palantir as a kinetic-conflict play. [[Apple]] launches 5+ products this week including the iPhone 17e — the AI-to-mass-market play.
