## L'Oréal FY 2025 — €44.1B revenue (+4.0% LFL), 20.2% margin (record), Galderma stake to 20%

[[L'Oréal]] reported 2025 annual results (Feb 12 release, Feb 13 earnings call):

| Metric | FY 2025 | YoY |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Revenue | €44.1B | +4.0% LFL, +1.3% reported |
| Operating profit | €8.9B | 20.2% margin (+20bps, record) |
| Net income ex NR | €6.8B | +0.3% |
| EPS | €12.71 | +0.4% |
| Net cash flow | €7.2B | +7.8% |
| Dividend | €7.20/share | +2.9% |

Division highlights: Professional Products fastest-growing (+7.5% LFL, 22.9% margin). North Asia barely grew (+0.5%). Emerging markets led — SAPMENA-SSA +10.9%, Latin America +8.3%. E-commerce surpassed 30% of sales.

Key strategic moves:
- **[[Galderma]] stake increased to 20%** (from 10%), closed Feb 10 — two board seats, deeper aesthetics push
- **Beauty Tech hub in [[India]]** — AI-powered beauty solutions (generative/agentic AI)
- **Governance:** Jean-Paul Agon and Patrice Caine renewals at April 24 AGM

Updated [[L'Oréal]] actor note with full FY 2025 financials, divisional and geographic breakdown, Galderma deal.
