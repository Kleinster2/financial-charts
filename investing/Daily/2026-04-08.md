### Intel + Terafab ($25B foundry deal)

[[Intel]] +11.4% to $58.95 after signing on as the primary foundry partner for [[TERAFAB]], the $25B joint fab between [[Tesla]], [[SpaceX]], and [[xAI]]. Intel contributes 18A process node and advanced packaging ([[EMIB]], [[Foveros]]). Two fabs on Giga Texas campus: (1) automotive/robotics chips for FSD, [[Cybercab]], [[Tesla Optimus]], (2) AI datacenter + orbital processors for xAI/SpaceX.

Initial: 100K wafer starts/month; full scale: 1M wafer starts/month. This is the anchor customer [[Intel Foundry Services]] has pursued since the IDM 2.0 strategy. Comes weeks after Lip-Bu Tan's March 4 reversal on 18A external foundry and days after Intel reacquired Fab 34 from [[Apollo]] for $14.2B.

### Gartner: semiconductor revenue $1.3T in 2026

[[Gartner]] forecast global semiconductor revenue at $1,320B in 2026, up 64% from $805B in 2025 — the highest growth in two decades. Memory revenue nearly triples to $633B from $216B, driven by "memflation": DRAM prices +125%, NAND +234%. Relief not expected until late 2027.

AI semiconductors ~30% of total revenue. Third consecutive year of double-digit growth. Revenue to reach $1,555B in 2027.
