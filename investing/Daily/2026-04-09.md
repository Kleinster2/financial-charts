### The interesting angle
The video surfaced a genuinely novel concept: benchmark freight indices continuing to publish daily assessments for routes that are physically closed. Lloyd's List called it "imaginary rates." This is not a data error — the Baltic Exchange chose to publish through the vacuum rather than suspend, because suspension would break downstream instruments (TD3C FFAs on ICE, BWET NAV marking, charter contract negotiation). The result: BWET's 836% YTD move is partly real (disrupted physical market + rerouting ton-miles) and partly a mechanical artifact of the index continuing to print above actual-fixture reality. Benchmark mean-reversion on the way down may therefore outpace physical mean-reversion, creating asymmetric risk for holders. Kartsonas is publicly cautionary precisely because of BDRY's 2021-22 round trip, which is the clearest precedent for how this kind of cycle resolves.
