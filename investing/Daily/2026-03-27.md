# 2026-03-27

## Synthesis

Overnight recovery attempt after Thursday's ugly session. Asian shares -0.4%, US/Europe futures rising after [[Trump II|Trump]] extended Iran ceasefire deadline by 10 days (to April 6). Oil edging lower. Nasdaq entered correction territory on March 26 — biggest monthly global stock selloff since 2022.

The dominant new story from the March 26 session was the social media "Tobacco Moment": two jury verdicts in one week found [[Meta]] and [[Alphabet|Google]] liable for harms to minors ($375M NM penalty + $6M LA verdict), while the EU opened its first [[Digital Services Act]] probe into [[Snap|Snapchat]]. Senator [[Dick Durbin]] called to sunset [[Section 230]]. META -7.96%, GOOGL -3.45%, SNAP -10.69%. Over 2,400 cases consolidated in California federal court. See [[2026 social media liability crisis]].

[[Robin J. Brooks]] published a data-driven analysis showing [[Saudi Arabia]] benefits from continued conflict: pre-war revenues of ~$150B/yr (7 mb/d at $60 Brent) vs ~$200B/yr now (~5 mb/d at $110 Brent via [[East-West Pipeline]]/[[Yanbu]]). WSJ reports Saudis unhappy with US peace overtures. The windfall holds even at realistic port-bottlenecked volumes of 4 mb/d. See [[Iran conflict war duration]].

Three key analysts converging on the same thesis: this is longer and deeper than the market prices. [[Helima Croft]] (RBC, Mar 26): "brace for a potentially extended military operation" — production restoration will take months-to-years even after a ceasefire. [[Jeff Currie]] (Carlyle): paper-physical disconnect widening — Oman crude $162/bbl vs Brent futures $110, jet fuel $200+. Goldman and Citi predict futures could surpass the 2008 peak ($147.50). [[Lyn Alden]] (Mar 22 newsletter, "A Flywheel of Chaos"): her "gradual print" thesis holds for now (Fed balance sheet up $120B) but probability of a "big print" ($2T+) is rising — the toxic combo of sustained energy prices → Treasury stress → stagflation → tax receipt collapse could cascade later in 2026. "Nothing stops this train, and war can potentially accelerate it."
