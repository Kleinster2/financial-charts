## Synthesis

Today's vault work cleaned up the dangling Brazil-neobank salvage from the old C6 / Brazil fintech PRs. The story is that [[Brazil fintech]] is not just a list of fast-growing apps; it is a market architecture built around [[Banco Central do Brasil]] and [[Pix]]. Payments became public infrastructure, so the durable moat moved to underwriting, funding, product density, and trust.

[[C6 Bank]] is the useful proof point because it is not public like [[Nubank]] or [[Banco Inter|Inter]]. It is a private full-service digital bank with a 46% [[JPMorgan Chase]] stake, 40M+ clients, and R$2.5B of 2025 net income. That makes C6 a read-through on what global-bank capital wants from Brazilian digital banking: not just app growth, but secured credit, affluent products, business banking, and governance strong enough to scale.

[[Redwire]] is the useful proof point on the defense side because the headline catalyst is real but easy to overread. The Jan. 27, 2026 [[MDA]] SHIELD selection ties RDW to [[Golden Dome]], but it is vendor-pool eligibility under a $151B ceiling, not guaranteed revenue. The actual story is whether Redwire can turn the [[Edge Autonomy]] acquisition into repeatable Defense Tech production while fixing the losses and cash burn visible in FY2025.

[[BWET]] is the useful proof point in shipping because the best trade was not where the ETF money went. The tanker-rate ETF captured the [[Strait of Hormuz]] freight shock, but the capital flowed to [[BNO]], the familiar Brent oil wrapper. That says the bottleneck is not just market access; it is investor legibility. The purer instrument can win the performance table and still lose the allocation battle.
