The SpaceX index-mechanics update tightens the passive-flow story without turning it into hype. The base roadshow float is about $75B, so pure float-adjusted products such as total-market funds start from the investable slice rather than the roughly $1.75T-$1.77T headline valuation. The Nasdaq 100 is different after its May 1 methodology change: it can weight low-float securities at up to three times free-floating shares, which makes SpaceX look more like a roughly $225B modified-market-cap constituent than a $75B constituent, but still nowhere near a full-trillion-dollar index weight. The causal read-through is that QQQ can absorb a bigger SpaceX weight than VTI-style products while still respecting supply limits; as lockups expire or insiders sell, both float-adjusted and Nasdaq modified weights can rise even if the stock price does not.

The Jun. 5 semiconductor event-note refinement matters because it prevents the tape from being overclassified as either generic risk-off or memory-specific idiosyncrasy.
