## Synthesis

Refreshed [[Loyal]] (canine longevity / [[Longevity]] thesis) from a stale Feb-2026 snapshot to current. Verified against fresh sources that the FDA picture is unchanged since January: LOY-002 has two of three conditional-approval sections accepted (RXE Feb 2025, Target Animal Safety Jan 2026), with only the manufacturing (CMC) section outstanding — conditional approval is reported for late 2026 / 2027, so the next catalyst is the CMC submission, not anything that has already happened. Added the full three-drug pipeline (LOY-001 large-breed injection, LOY-002 senior-dog pill, LOY-003 oral large-breed), the complete five-round funding history ($11M seed → $27M Series A led by [[Khosla Ventures]] → $45M Series B led by [[Bain Capital Ventures]] → $22M B-2 → $100M Series C led by [[age1]] with [[Baillie Gifford]]; $250M+ total), ~$40–90/mo pricing, and the ~1-added-year lifespan goal. De-bolded the body and fixed compliance (Funding history heading, Leadership table, Evolution timeline).

Why it matters: Loyal is less a pet-pharma bet than a regulatory-category play — getting the FDA to accept "extend healthy lifespan" as an approvable indication, which does not exist today. The read-through tell is the Series C investor mix: [[Baillie Gifford]], a public-markets growth house that rarely touches pre-revenue private biotech, underwriting alongside [[Longevity]] specialist [[age1]] ([[Laura Deming]]'s successor fund) — the category is being priced as a future market, not a science project. Dogs age several times faster than humans, so a canine result reads out in years and de-risks the human-geroscience translation. The natural incumbent/competitor/acquirer is [[Zoetis]], which owns the veterinary channel.

Longevity hub buildout (follow-on): expanded [[Longevity]] from today's stub into a full concept hub and seeded the private-company universe behind it. The organizing insight is that longevity is a category that does not legally exist — the FDA recognizes no "aging" indication — so every serious program routes around the gap: a disease-of-aging proxy ([[Life Biosciences]] chose optic neuropathy for its ER-100 partial-reprogramming therapy, the first FDA-cleared human reprogramming trial, Jan 28 2026; [[Retro Biosciences]] chose Alzheimer's, data ~Aug 2026), an animal-first lifespan claim ([[Loyal]] in dogs), or a metformin proof of concept that aging itself is treatable (the [[TAME trial]]). The capital tell mirrors Loyal's — generalist tech and crossover money ([[Sam Altman]], Bezos, Milner, [[Khosla Ventures]], [[Baillie Gifford]]) pricing the category as a future market, not a science project; best-funded [[Altos Labs]] (~$5.56B) has been the slowest to the clinic, so capital has not bought speed. The cautionary public precedent is [[Unity Biotechnology]] (senolytics, ceased operations 2025); the lone listed proxy is [[BioAge Labs]] (BIOA), which is why the hub deliberately carries no basket chart yet.
