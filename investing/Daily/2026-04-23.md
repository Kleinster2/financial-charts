Main takeaway:
Reuters got one layer deeper into the confidential [[SpaceX IPO 2026|SpaceX S-1]]. The important new fact is not simply that [[SpaceX]] wants to manufacture its own GPUs. It is that the filing now frames chip supply as a disclosed bottleneck: SpaceX says it lacks long-term contracts with many direct chip suppliers, expects to keep buying a significant share of compute hardware from third parties, and offers no assurance that [[TERAFAB]] will hit schedule, or happen at all. That turns the silicon story from flashy vertical-integration upside into a real financing and execution risk factor inside the IPO itself.

The second important shift is interpretive. Earlier shorthand around [[Intel]] had drifted toward "18A solved the fab layer" after the Apr. 7 X-post announcement. Reuters pushed that back toward uncertainty. The report said it remained unclear whether Intel or the venture partners would control the fabrication technologies inside the plant, and Musk pointed to Intel's 14A as the process that "seems like the right move" by the time Terafab scales. The cleaner read now is Intel-linked manufacturing path, yes, but node choice, scope, and timeline still open.
