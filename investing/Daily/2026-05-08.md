### Nasdaq "Enhanced IPO" deepdive — SPAC IPO listing standards reform (May 8, 2026)

`/deepdive Enhanced IPO` triggered after the Instructure work. Disambiguated to the [[Nasdaq Enhanced SPAC IPO listing standards 2026]] rule (SR-NASDAQ-2026-033, filed April 15, 2026, operative May 15, 2026 — one week from today). Pulled primary-source detail from the Goodwin alert, the Sichenzia Ross Ference Carmel client alert, and the Mondaq summary. Federal Register direct fetch hit a Cloudflare-style access wall; secondary-source legal alerts converged cleanly on the same rule mechanics.

The substantive lift falls hardest on the Capital Market, where Nasdaq created an entirely new SPAC-specific listing standard (Rule 5505(b)(4)) raising MVLS from $50M (general standard) to $75M, unrestricted publicly held shares from $15M to $20M, market makers from 3 to 4, and public shareholders from 300 to 400. The Global Market change is a single threshold lift on Rule 5405(b)(3)(A) raising MVLS from $75M to $100M. Notably, the rule does NOT change post-combination de-SPAC requirements and cites NO statistical data. The framing language — "thinly capitalized SPACs" prone to "market manipulation by larger stockholders or through social media driven momentum" — describes the exact profile of small-cap SPAC IPOs underwritten by [[Dominari Securities]] and [[D Boral Capital]] in the [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]] ecosystem we documented this morning.

The 30-day grandfathering window means SPACs that complete IPO listings between April 15 and May 15, 2026 still qualify under prior rules; pending SPACs not yet priced as of May 15 must clear the new thresholds. The comment period runs through ~May 18, 2026 (21 days after April 27 Federal Register publication).
