## Synthesis

Silver equity beta closes the precious-metals corner of the commodity-beta family with the cleanest "one complex" result yet. Where copper miners = the copper price (+0.033 over CPER) and gold miners = gold (+0.070 over GLD), the silver miners go a step further: they are not even distinct from the GOLD miners (−0.041 vs [[GDX]] — they correlate with gold miners more than with each other). So gold and silver mining are ONE precious-metals-mining factor, captured equally by [[GDX]], [[SIL]], or SLV; the silver names are simply the higher-beta (56-75% annualized vol) expression of it. This extends the commodity-beta law from "miners = their own metal" to "precious-metals miners = one metals-mining complex" — the monetary-metal cohorts ([[Gold equity beta|gold]], silver, and the [[Precious metals royalties|royalties]]) all collapse into the same trade, distinguished only by beta. It is the mirror image of the campaign's distinct factors, which earn separation through a driver no liquid ETF prices; here the driver — the gold/silver price — is the most liquid, ETF-saturated trade in markets, so nothing separates.

Alcohol & spirits is the day's opposite failure mode, and the contrast is the lesson. Silver fails by collapsing — too tight, fully inside an ETF (everything is the metal price). Alcohol fails by fragmenting — real co-movement (all three nulls reject, so it is not noise) that nonetheless never resolves into one factor: intra 0.483 below the floor, a beer pair and a spirits pair that only fuse when you loosen the cut past 0.50, and a factor structure that inverts out-of-sample (PC1 loadings corr −0.24). Both are Tier 3, but for inverse reasons — one too coherent to be distinct from its ETF, the other too incoherent to be a factor at all. The campaign's distinct names live in the narrow middle: tight enough to cohere (intra above ~0.55, a separable threshold band) yet driven by something no liquid ETF already prices. Alcohol misses on the first test the way silver misses on the second. The consumer-category lesson recurs — like apparel, the market does not trade "drinks" as a monolith; it trades the GLP-1/moderation worry on spirits, the volume-decline story on mass beer, and the premiumization/FX story on the global names as separate trades.
