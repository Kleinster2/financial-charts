## Synthesis

The stagflation print arrived. February NFP at -92K (vs +50K expected) collided with oil >$90 on Iran conflict Day 8, and the market finally stopped dip-buying. The Russell 2000 -2.39% is the cleanest expression: small caps are most exposed to domestic labor weakness and least hedged on energy costs. This is the first session since the conflict started where equities sold off and *stayed* down — no intraday recovery. The Dow hit -945 intraday before recovering to -453, but the pattern of aggressive dip-buying that characterized Days 1-5 broke.

The only refuge was idiosyncratic AI earnings: Marvell +22.1% (custom ASIC revenue from ~0 to $1.5B in a year) and Samsara +17.38% (GAAP profitability inflection). These moves confirm that the AI infrastructure buildout is decoupled from the macro cycle — hyperscaler capex commitments ($650B+) don't respond to a single NFP print. Gap -13.49% on a one-cent EPS miss and Athleta -10% comp tells the other story: consumer discretionary is fragile, and any excuse to sell gets taken in a risk-off tape.

The SoftBank $40B loan story and Stargate Abilene expansion collapse are two sides of the same coin. The AI infrastructure buildout is real but messy — demand forecasts shift, financing structures evolve, and the gap between headline commitments ($500B) and actual deployment is widening. Meta stepping into the Abilene expansion slot (with NVIDIA putting down $150M to broker the deal) shows that the capacity will get built — the question is who occupies it, not whether it exists.

## February NFP: -92K (stagflation catalyst)

Nonfarm payrolls dropped **-92,000** — massive miss vs +50K expected. Unemployment ticked to **4.4%** from 4.3%. Third time in five months the economy lost jobs.

Healthcare lost 28K due to [[Kaiser Permanente]] strike (30,000+ workers in Hawaii and California). But stripping out the strike effect, the print is still deeply negative.

The combination: negative payrolls + oil >$90 + Iran conflict entering second week = **stagflation narrative**. Fed rate cut expectations already compressed (June cut only ~55% probability per Mar 4 Reuters poll). A hot oil shock on a weak labor market is the worst-case macro configuration for the Fed.

*Source: [BLS Employment Situation Summary](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm) (Mar 6, 2026); [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/06/g-s1-112788/oil-prices-stocks-jobs-report)*

## Oracle/OpenAI drop Stargate Texas expansion

[[Oracle]] and [[OpenAI]] abandoned plans to expand the flagship [[Project Stargate|Stargate]] Abilene campus from 1.2 GW to 2 GW. Negotiations stalled over financing and OpenAI's "frequently changing demand forecasting." Developer is [[Crusoe Energy]].

[[Meta]] in talks to lease the expansion capacity. [[NVIDIA]] put down a **$150M deposit** on the capacity before approaching Meta — acting as GPU-placement broker. Core Oracle-OpenAI contract (4.5 GW, $300B lifetime value) remains intact.

First concrete downsizing of the $500B Stargate headline. See [[Project Stargate]].

*Source: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/oracle-and-openai-end-plans-to-expand-flagship-data-center) (Mar 6, 2026)*

## SoftBank seeks $40B bridge loan for OpenAI

[[SoftBank]] seeking record $40B dollar-denominated bridge loan (~12-month tenor) to fund its $30B commitment to [[OpenAI]]'s $110B round ($730B pre-money valuation). [[JPMorgan Chase]] leading a 4-bank underwriting syndicate.

This would push SoftBank's total OpenAI investment to ~$71B (~13% stake). S&P revised SoftBank outlook to **negative** (Mar 3), warning that OpenAI now rivals [[Arm Holdings]] in portfolio weight but without the liquidity — unlisted assets projected >50% of portfolio. LTV pushed to ~37%, above the 35% downgrade threshold.

The funding chain: sold [[NVIDIA]] ($5.8B), [[T-Mobile]] ($12.7B), expanded Arm margin loan ($13.5B→$20B). The balance sheet is now fully tethered to AI adoption pace and OpenAI IPO timing. See [[SoftBank]].

*Source: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/softbank-seeks-record-loan-of-up-to-40-billion-for-openai-stake) (Mar 6, 2026); [S&P](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/softbank-s-30-billion-openai-bet-spurs-s-p-credit-outlook-cut) (Mar 3, 2026)*

## Foxconn Jan-Feb revenue +21.6%

[[Foxconn]] (Hon Hai) reported Jan-Feb 2026 revenue of **NT$1.33T** ($41.9B), +21.6% YoY. January surged +35.5% (AI server demand); February +8.1% (Lunar New Year drag). Chairman Young Liu: 2026 will be "a very good year" — full-year double-digit growth, AI server market share rising above current 40%.

Cloud/networking revenue overtook consumer electronics for the first time in 2025 (41% vs 35%). [[Foxconn]] is the largest [[NVIDIA]] server assembler globally, qualified as NVIDIA PBR (Pilot Build Request) partner for earliest-access builds of GB200/GB300 platforms. See [[Foxconn]].

### Matt Levine "Money Stuff" ingestion (Mar 5 column)

Four threads from the column, each touching existing vault coverage:

**1. Saba/Cox SREIT tender offer** — [[Saba Capital]] and [[Cox Capital Partners]] expanded their illiquidity arbitrage from Blue Owl BDCs to [[Starwood Capital Group|Starwood]]'s $8.2B non-traded REIT (SREIT). Offering to buy 5.4% of Class S and I shares at 24-29% discounts to NAV. Confirms this is a **scalable strategy** — Weinstein is treating the illiquidity premium as a market-wide factor, targeting any semi-liquid vehicle where retail is trapped.

**2. Retail dip-buying** — Feb 2026 was the **5th-biggest month for retail stock buying on record** ([[Citadel Securities]]), one of the strongest since the 2021 meme frenzy. On Monday (Mar 3, Iran conflict selloff), retail poured **$2.2B into stocks and ETFs** ([[JPMorgan Chase]]), helping indexes finish nearly flat. The contradiction: retail stock investors have nerves of steel, but retail private credit/REIT investors are fleeing.

**3. Dispersion trade under stress** — The dispersion trade (sell index options, buy single-stock options — a bet against correlation) has been popular as AI repricing drives huge individual stock moves while the index stays flat. 2026 YTD through Feb 13 saw **the largest dispersion since at least 1994** (WSJ). But the Iran conflict spiked implied one-month correlation to its **highest level since November**. Amy Wu Silverman ([[RBC]]): *"We could be at an important inflection point."*

**4. Bolivia nonfungible cash** — A military cargo plane carrying 423M bolivianos ($62M) in new banknotes crashed near El Alto. ~30% of bills stolen from crash site. Central bank voided all serial numbers, released an app for vendors to manually verify each bill. Result: vendors refusing all Series B bills regardless of validity. Commerce grinding to a halt — a real-world illustration of what happens when you make money nonfungible.
