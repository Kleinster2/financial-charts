### [[Hindustan Zinc]] — Vedanta zinc subsidiary (NOT in May 2026 demerger)

Created actor stub for Hindustan Zinc Limited (HZL; NSE: HINDZINC, BSE: 500188). [[Vedanta]] holds 63.4% (acquired in 2002 privatisation under [[Anil Agarwal]]); [[Government of India]] retains ~29.54% as the second-largest holder. Critical clarification surfaced during research: HZL is *not* part of the May 1, 2026 [[Vedanta]] 5-way demerger — it remains a separately-listed Vedanta subsidiary, with the residual Vedanta Ltd inheriting the 63.4% stake. Operationally HZL is the world's #2 integrated zinc-lead-silver producer, anchored at the Rampura Agucha, Sindesar Khurd, and Zawar mines in [[Rajasthan]]. FY 2026 (year ending March 2026) just released: revenue ₹39,192 cr (+18% YoY), net profit ₹13,832 cr (+33.6% YoY), Q4 net profit ₹5,033 cr (+68% YoY) — all-time records. Market cap ₹2.68T (~$32B) up 46.2% YoY; share price ₹636.75 May 7 close. Structural differentiator flagged for future research: silver expansion to 1,500 TPA by FY 2030 makes HZL one of the world's top silver producers — a thesis-distinct capability vs generic zinc miners that the vault has not yet validated as a standalone thread.

### [[Cairn India]] — Vedanta oil & gas vertical demerging into separately-listed entity

Created actor stub for Cairn India / Cairn Oil & Gas — the oil & gas vertical that *is* being spun out in the May 1, 2026 [[Vedanta]] demerger (1:1 ratio; new entity uses the existing Malco Energy Limited (MEL) shell, renamed subject to RoC approval; listing expected within 1-2 months of the May 1 record date). Original asset is the Rajasthan oil block (Mangala field, 2004 discovery), the largest onshore oil discovery in India in two decades. FY 2025 production 84,276 BOEPD; cumulative 781 MMBOE; ~25% of India's domestic crude. Capex programme: $4-5B targeting an intermediate 300,000 BOEPD and a long-term 1 million BPD ambition (~4× ramp). Cairn was the legacy name from the 2007 IPO of Cairn India Ltd as a Cairn Energy subsidiary; merged into [[Vedanta]] in 2017 (became Vedanta's oil & gas division). The May 2026 demerger restores it to standalone-listed status, with a new ticker awaiting issuance. Cross-vault relevance: ties into [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] / [[US Energy Independence Six Countries]] threads as one of the few large non-OPEC, non-Western producers ramping into the structural-deficit window.

### [[Bill Ford]] — Ford Executive Chair, great-grandson of Henry Ford

Created actor stub for William Clay Ford Jr., Executive Chair of [[Ford]] (Chair since 1999; was CEO 2001-2006, handed to Alan Mulally Sept 2006). Born May 3, 1957; [[Princeton]] AB 1979; [[MIT]] Sloan SM 1984. Director since 1988. Long history at Ford in operations / marketing / finance, then governance. Notable as an early environmental-sustainability advocate inside the company (positioned the company on climate well before the industry consensus shifted). Family-control governance: the Ford family's Class B shares control 40% of voting power despite holding ~2% of total economic shares — Bill Ford is the senior steward of that voting block. Investing-vault relevance is the dual-overlay with [[Jim Farley]] (CEO) and [[Sherry House]] (CFO) on the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] commodity-shock thread: Bill Ford's continuity as Executive Chair anchors the long-horizon family-stewardship frame around the cyclical Trump-II / Iran-war stress test now running through Detroit-OEM earnings.

### [[Jim Farley]] — Ford CEO since October 2020

Created actor stub for James D. Farley Jr., CEO of [[Ford]] effective October 1, 2020 (succeeded Jim Hackett). Born June 10, 1962; [[Georgetown]] BS, UCLA Anderson MBA. Career arc: [[Toyota]] / Lexus North America (1990-2007, ran Lexus marketing then Toyota US marketing) → [[Ford]] 2007 (Group VP Global Marketing) → CEO Ford Europe / Ford South America → COO 2020 → CEO Oct 2020. Investing-vault relevance: Farley's Q1 2026 earnings-call commentary alongside [[Sherry House]] on the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] commodity-shock — *"We were already seeing global industry shortages. Then you had the Middle East"* — anchors the FT-quoted CEO-CFO duet on the Detroit-OEM transmission of the Iran-war commodity shock. Working signal flagged: succession-cycle posture as Farley's tenure extends into year 6+ and Ford's strategic-pivot effectiveness becomes the binding read.

### [[Anthony Bamford]] — JCB chairman since 1975

Chair of [[JCB Group]] since 1975, succeeding his father Joseph Cyril Bamford (born same day father founded JCB, 23 October 1945). Education path verified during research and corrected from the prompt's tentative claim: Ampleforth College → [[Massey Ferguson]] apprenticeship in France → University of Grenoble (languages). Knighted 1990; peerage 3 October 2013 (Baron Bamford of Daylesford in the County of Gloucestershire); retired from House of Lords March 2024. Investing-vault relevance is the dual-vertical Bamford-family hydrogen strategy: JCB's £100M, 150-engineer hydrogen-ICE programme (130+ evaluation engines built; January 2025 RDW Netherlands first national type-approval; 10 other European licensing bodies followed; full EU type-approval for non-road mobile machinery May 2025) running parallel to Jo Bamford's [[Wrightbus]] / [[Ballard]] hydrogen fuel-cell push. The Bamford family operates two distinct but mutually supportive industrial portfolios: traditional diesel-and-now-hydrogen construction equipment (JCB) and zero-emission buses (Wrightbus). Conservative Party donations: £10M+ cumulative since 2001; £3.94M in the 2019 GE.

### [[Salvador Caetano]] — Portuguese parent group of CaetanoBus

Created actor stub for Salvador Caetano Group / Grupo Salvador Caetano. Portuguese family-controlled industrial group founded 1946 by Salvador Fernandes Caetano (1926-2011) in Vila Nova de Gaia as Martins, Caetano & Irmão, Lda — initially a coachbuilder. Today operates ~50-100 operating companies across 6 countries: aggregated sales ~€2.0bn, ~6,600+ employees. Group leadership: Maria Angelina Caetano Ramos (founder's daughter) as group president; José Reis da Silva Ramos as chair of TCAP / [[CaetanoBus]] / Saltano holding (TCAP since 2010). [[Toyota]] partnership since 1968 (Portuguese distribution); Ovar industrial assembly from 1971. The investing-vault relevance is the [[CaetanoBus]] subsidiary's role in the European hydrogen-bus thread alongside [[Wrightbus]] / [[Solaris]] — Salvador Caetano carries the Portuguese end of the EU hydrogen-bus market, differentiated by the [[Toyota]] fuel-cell platform (vs [[Ballard]] for Wrightbus + Solaris). [[Mitsui]]'s direct investment into CaetanoBus (December 2017); Toyota Caetano Portugal became direct shareholder of CaetanoBus December 2020. Porto BRT consortium leadership (CaetanoBus as prime contractor for the world's first OEM-led hydrogen BRT) is the working example of vertically integrated mobility-system play under the Salvador Caetano umbrella.
