## Synthesis

The VOYG promotion is the cohort's first new member by the discipline the note itself set: not a backfit, but a pre-registered criterion (catalyst-cycle co-movement) that the June 12 event then satisfied — VOYG fell -14% with the basket while correlating more with the cohort (0.565) than the market (0.438). That makes the space pure-plays an eight-name factor and shows the framework working forward, not just cataloguing. HawkEye 360 is the counter-case on data discipline: a compliance gate wanted three charts, but two would have to be drawn from an Alpha Vantage FY2025 figure that is verifiably wrong (it contradicts the S-1 by ~$60M of revenue and flips breakeven into a loss), so the right move was to generate the one honest chart (price) and document the deferral rather than satisfy the gate with bad data — the gate exists to enforce quality, and forcing wrong charts would invert its purpose.

Completing the space pure-plays cohort means all seven validated members now carry full Financials, charts, and securities notes — the cohort that anchors the SpaceX-proxy-unwind thread is uniformly deep. The backfills reinforced two recurring de-SPAC data hazards: warrant/earnout remeasurement makes GAAP net income a poor read on these names (every one shows large non-operating swings — BlackSky's -$245.6M 2021, Spire's +$51.3M 2025 divestiture gain, Intuitive Machines' +$62.8M 2023), so the operating loss and FCF carry the signal; and the Alpha Vantage feed occasionally injects a corrupt figure (Spire's -$25B Q4 2025 net income), which is why verifying against filings is not optional for de-SPAC tickers.

The space-proxy work closes the SpaceX-proxy-unwind thread analytically. The proxy-unwind note had flagged an open question — do the five June-12 fallers form a durable cluster, or did they just co-move on the catalyst? The answer is both, split by name: four of them (ASTS, LUNR, RKLB, RDW) are members of the already-validated [[Space pure-plays]] factor (intra-corr ~0.61, PC1 ~69%, strengthening since the Nov 2025 regime shift), so they genuinely co-move as a factor; the fifth, [[EchoStar]] (SATS), is not — at 0.205 average correlation to the cohort it is indistinguishable from market beta, and its tie to ASTS (0.109) is the weakest pair in the test. That is the clean statistical statement of the proxy-unwind note's qualitative "two kinds of proxy" split: the thematic pure-plays share a factor, the ownership proxy does not, and the June-12 event collapsed the distinction only because the SpaceX direct listing was a catalyst common to both. The LUNR backfill that surfaced alongside it took the cohort's most-central PC1 name (loading 0.468) from a thin stub to full deepdive depth, with the de-SPAC accounting (remeasurement-driven net income) made explicit so the operating trajectory reads through the GAAP noise.

The financial-data run is the constructive bookend to yesterday's exchanges falsification, and the two together are the cleanest matched pair the cluster framework has produced. "Exchanges" failed to cluster (intra 0.360, zero threshold width) because two of its four members had quietly changed business — [[Intercontinental Exchange|ICE]] and [[Nasdaq]] shifted enough revenue to indices, ratings, and analytics SaaS that they stopped trading on transaction volume. This run validates where they went: the seven data providers form a real factor (intra 0.607, weekly 0.701, all nulls rejected at the floor), and Nasdaq joins it as a full member from the lowest threshold while ICE sits on the boundary. The signature is symmetric — in the exchanges run NDAQ/ICE were the contaminants pulling the exchange cohort apart; here they are the contaminants the data cohort cannot be separated from, because they belong to it. The widest number in the batch makes the point: the data cohort's correlation to the [[CME Group]]/[[CBOE]] derivatives pair is 0.052 (+0.554 intra-advantage), so two sets of companies that share a regulator, a customer base, and the word "exchange" have essentially no common return factor. The investable read is to stop trading sector labels: a "market-infrastructure" sleeve is really two factors — a transaction/volatility factor (CME/CBOE) and a recurring-data toll-road factor (SPGI/MCO/MSCI/FactSet/Thomson Reuters/RELX/Morningstar + NDAQ/ICE) — and only the second is the durable, subscription-priced compounder.

Theatrical exhibition is the day's cleanest falsification and sharpens what the framework measures one more turn. Yesterday's exchanges run taught that a shared moat is not a cluster; this one teaches that a shared end-market is not either. [[AMC Entertainment]], [[Cinemark]], and [[IMAX]] all live or die on box-office revenue, yet they do not trade as one factor (intra 0.328, PC1 55%, random-basket null not rejected at p 0.099) because they monetize that same revenue through structurally different models with different dominant risks: AMC is a levered meme stock driven by retail flow and dilution risk (71% annualized vol, a 0.17 correlation with IMAX), IMAX is an asset-light premium-format licensor earning a royalty stream, and Cinemark is the only clean operating exhibitor. The statistical fingerprint is precise — the independence null rejects (they do co-move a little) while the random-basket null does not (no more than any three comparable stocks) — exactly the shape of "a label without a factor." It differs from the exchanges case in one way worth recording: exchanges fragmented into a constructive sub-cluster (the data toll roads), whereas theatrical exhibition leaves nothing underneath — three idiosyncratic names, no hidden structure. The practical read collapses to a single name: for box-office exposure, Cinemark is the clean operating expression; the basket is three unrelated risks. Across the session the framework has now produced every verdict shape on the same question — validated-distinct, validated-but-ETF-replicable, real-but-inseparable ([[Brazil fintech]]), and falsified ([[Medtech]], theatrical exhibition) — each turning on whether a plausible label corresponds to a real return factor.

The four interconnect names reject all three nulls — including the vol-matched null at 0.0002 — so unlike [[Medtech]] or theatrical exhibition they genuinely co-move; there is real shared AI-datacenter-capex beta. Same shape as [[Brazil fintech]] (real cohort, not a separable factor); the durable tradeable structure inside 中特估 is the telecom sub-bloc, not the ten-name theme. Copper closes the audit's reachable backlog and adds the one axis the taxonomy was missing: commodity beta. The result is the mirror image of the AI-optics finding, and together they fix what null tests can and cannot establish. AI optics passed the nulls yet was two pairs averaged, not one factor; copper passes the nulls at the 0.0001 floor and IS one factor — but a trivial, non-distinct one. A commodity cohort is guaranteed to crush the random-basket and vol-matched nulls because every member is levered to one exogenous price, so cohesion is necessary but uninformative; the only diagnostics that carry information are the threshold scan (the miners never separate from COPX, CPER, GDX, or SPY) and the intra-advantage vs the metal itself (+0.033 — a sliver of operating leverage and nothing more). The investable read collapses to the ETF: own COPX for levered copper-equity beta or CPER for the unlevered metal; picking [[Southern Copper]] over [[First Quantum Minerals]] is a bet on cost curve and jurisdiction (Grasberg, Cobre Panamá, Kamoa-Kakula), not on a separate factor. It is the commodity twin of [[Nuclear renaissance|nuclear/SMR]]'s validated-but-ETF-replicable verdict. With copper done, every cluster-validation candidate that had reachable data — across the whole multi-day audit — now has a verdict; the only items left (DTC telehealth, title insurance) await their tickers.

Beyond the audit, a second arc of seven fresh cohorts mapped two general laws of when listed equities cohere into a tradeable factor. The narrative law: a theme becomes a factor in proportion to the ABSENCE of fundamentals among its members. [[Quantum computing]] — four revenue-less pure-plays — is the tightest cohort in the set (intra 0.837, weekly 0.833, PC1 88%); [[GLP-1 receptor agonists|GLP-1 / obesity]] — four names that each carry a dominant real business — is among the loosest (weekly 0.004, falsified). The fundamental anchor is what decouples; its absence is what binds, because there is nothing idiosyncratic left to separate the names. The value-chain-split law, refined: a macro theme contains multiple distinct equity factors split not by business MODEL but by dominant DRIVER. Housing splits into construction ([[Homebuilders]]) vs transaction ([[Title Insurance]]), +0.397; AI electricity into equipment ([[Datacenter power and cooling]]) vs generation ([[AI-power IPPs]]), +0.384; [[Ad-tech]] shatters in real time as [[AppLovin]] finds a new driver (its AXON AI ad engine) and exits the cohort. [[Airlines]] is the clean counterexample that fixes the predicate as shared-driver, not shared-model: legacy and low-cost carriers are different models but one factor (intra 0.763, no split), because both live on jet fuel and travel demand — the −0.494 inverse-oil correlation is that single shared driver made visible. The remaining beyond-audit results slot into the established families: [[Crypto equities]] is real-but-it-IS-bitcoin-beta (the copper analogue for BTC, replicable by IBIT, +0.326 distinct from financials yet −0.032 from spot bitcoin). Seven cohorts, every verdict shape represented, and two laws that now predict — before running the math — whether a plausible label will turn out to be a factor.

Separately, a Brazil-access question on SPCX exposed and closed a standing vault gap. The cluster campaign has deepened US coverage relentlessly, but the vault had no model of how a Brazil-resident — the reader these notes are written for — actually buys any of it: "BDR" existed only as two stray ticker suffixes, with no instrument note and nothing on the SpaceX listing's Brazilian line. The new [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts]] concept fixes that at the structural level, and the read that matters is the friction, not the convenience: a BDR is an access wrapper whose economics track the underlying one-for-one, so the choice between SPCX34 and the offshore SPCX line turns entirely on second-order costs — the missing R$20k isenção makes the BDR the worse wrapper for an active trader, while no-câmbio local settlement makes it the better one for a buy-and-hold position. The same logic now applies to every US name in the vault, since each carries (or can mechanically acquire) a 34-suffix receipt — which is why this belongs as a concept, not a SpaceX footnote.
