## Synthesis

Completing the space pure-plays cohort means all seven validated members now carry full Financials, charts, and securities notes — the cohort that anchors the SpaceX-proxy-unwind thread is uniformly deep. The backfills reinforced two recurring de-SPAC data hazards: warrant/earnout remeasurement makes GAAP net income a poor read on these names (every one shows large non-operating swings — BlackSky's -$245.6M 2021, Spire's +$51.3M 2025 divestiture gain, Intuitive Machines' +$62.8M 2023), so the operating loss and FCF carry the signal; and the Alpha Vantage feed occasionally injects a corrupt figure (Spire's -$25B Q4 2025 net income), which is why verifying against filings is not optional for de-SPAC tickers.

The space-proxy work closes the SpaceX-proxy-unwind thread analytically. The proxy-unwind note had flagged an open question — do the five June-12 fallers form a durable cluster, or did they just co-move on the catalyst? The answer is both, split by name: four of them (ASTS, LUNR, RKLB, RDW) are members of the already-validated [[Space pure-plays]] factor (intra-corr ~0.61, PC1 ~69%, strengthening since the Nov 2025 regime shift), so they genuinely co-move as a factor; the fifth, [[EchoStar]] (SATS), is not — at 0.205 average correlation to the cohort it is indistinguishable from market beta, and its tie to ASTS (0.109) is the weakest pair in the test. That is the clean statistical statement of the proxy-unwind note's qualitative "two kinds of proxy" split: the thematic pure-plays share a factor, the ownership proxy does not, and the June-12 event collapsed the distinction only because the SpaceX direct listing was a catalyst common to both. The LUNR backfill that surfaced alongside it took the cohort's most-central PC1 name (loading 0.468) from a thin stub to full deepdive depth, with the de-SPAC accounting (remeasurement-driven net income) made explicit so the operating trajectory reads through the GAAP noise.

The financial-data run is the constructive bookend to yesterday's exchanges falsification, and the two together are the cleanest matched pair the cluster framework has produced. "Exchanges" failed to cluster (intra 0.360, zero threshold width) because two of its four members had quietly changed business — [[Intercontinental Exchange|ICE]] and [[Nasdaq]] shifted enough revenue to indices, ratings, and analytics SaaS that they stopped trading on transaction volume. This run validates where they went: the seven data providers form a real factor (intra 0.607, weekly 0.701, all nulls rejected at the floor), and Nasdaq joins it as a full member from the lowest threshold while ICE sits on the boundary. The signature is symmetric — in the exchanges run NDAQ/ICE were the contaminants pulling the exchange cohort apart; here they are the contaminants the data cohort cannot be separated from, because they belong to it. The widest number in the batch makes the point: the data cohort's correlation to the [[CME Group]]/[[CBOE]] derivatives pair is 0.052 (+0.554 intra-advantage), so two sets of companies that share a regulator, a customer base, and the word "exchange" have essentially no common return factor. The investable read is to stop trading sector labels: a "market-infrastructure" sleeve is really two factors — a transaction/volatility factor (CME/CBOE) and a recurring-data toll-road factor (SPGI/MCO/MSCI/FactSet/Thomson Reuters/RELX/Morningstar + NDAQ/ICE) — and only the second is the durable, subscription-priced compounder.

Theatrical exhibition is the day's cleanest falsification and sharpens what the framework measures one more turn. Yesterday's exchanges run taught that a shared moat is not a cluster; this one teaches that a shared end-market is not either. [[AMC Entertainment]], [[Cinemark]], and [[IMAX]] all live or die on box-office revenue, yet they do not trade as one factor (intra 0.328, PC1 55%, random-basket null not rejected at p 0.099) because they monetize that same revenue through structurally different models with different dominant risks: AMC is a levered meme stock driven by retail flow and dilution risk (71% annualized vol, a 0.17 correlation with IMAX), IMAX is an asset-light premium-format licensor earning a royalty stream, and Cinemark is the only clean operating exhibitor. The statistical fingerprint is precise — the independence null rejects (they do co-move a little) while the random-basket null does not (no more than any three comparable stocks) — exactly the shape of "a label without a factor." It differs from the exchanges case in one way worth recording: exchanges fragmented into a constructive sub-cluster (the data toll roads), whereas theatrical exhibition leaves nothing underneath — three idiosyncratic names, no hidden structure. The practical read collapses to a single name: for box-office exposure, Cinemark is the clean operating expression; the basket is three unrelated risks. Across the session the framework has now produced every verdict shape on the same question — validated-distinct, validated-but-ETF-replicable, real-but-inseparable ([[Brazil fintech]]), and falsified ([[Medtech]], theatrical exhibition) — each turning on whether a plausible label corresponds to a real return factor.

AI optics/networking is the session's subtlest result and adds a verdict shape the batch had not cleanly isolated: a label that passes the cohesion tests yet is still not one factor. The four interconnect names reject all three nulls — including the vol-matched null at 0.0002 — so unlike [[Medtech]] or theatrical exhibition they genuinely co-move; there is real shared AI-datacenter-capex beta. But the cohesion is two tight pairs averaged, not a single structure: an optical pair (COHR/CIEN) and an electrical-interconnect pair (CRDO/ALAB) that do not join until distance 0.589, with the optical half indistinguishable from the broad optical complex ([[Lumentum]]/[[Fabrinet]], −0.054 intra-advantage). A four-name basket can clear a random-basket test purely by being two cohesive pairs — which is why the threshold scan and the holdout, not the null rejection, carry the verdict here. The constructive output is the cleanest part: the test maps the four names onto the vault's existing architecture — the optical sleeve is the listed expression of the [[AI fiber supercycle]], and the [[Credo Technology Group]]/[[Astera Labs]] interconnect-silicon pair is a genuinely new micro-cohort under [[Connectivity]]. With the optics candidate resolved, the 2026-06-12 audit backlog is down to data-blocked items only (Japan semi materials, DTC telehealth, title insurance, copper, the IoT-module micro-pair).

Japan semi materials closes the audit's addable backlog and lands as the batch's cleanest "geography is not a factor" result. [[Japan]]'s dominance of photoresists (~90%) and wafers (~55%) is real and strategic, but dominance is a market-share fact, not a co-movement one: the five Tokyo names intra-correlate at 0.381 (0.145 weekly) and shatter into singletons, because "materials" is three different demand curves — wafer area, EUV resist layers, and two conglomerates whose chip revenue is a rounding error against petrochemicals and healthcare. The result carries two constructive edges. First it adjusts the standing theses precisely: the [[Long Japan wafers]] Shin-Etsu+Sumco pairing is the one materials structure that holds (0.53 pairwise), while the [[Long Japan photoresists]] "diversified alternatives" ([[Sumitomo Chemical]], [[Fujifilm]]) are diversified away from the thesis. Second, and sharper, it inverts the chokepoint framing: within the Japan chip supply chain it is equipment ([[Tokyo Electron]]+[[Lasertec]], 0.623), not materials (0.381), that forms the tight cohort — equipment shares one [[WFE]] capex cycle, materials sell into different markets. The async/FX caveat is itself a methodological keeper: for a Tokyo cohort the only honest distinctness test is against another Tokyo cohort, because the headline "+0.31 vs US peers" is just the 14-hour close gap and the yen. With this done, the audit is cleared of every DB-addable candidate; what remains needs either harder-to-source listings (the IoT-module micro-pair) or fresh ticker coverage (DTC telehealth, title insurance, copper).

The China-bloc closeout is the audit's bookend and a clean matched pair on the "what is a factor?" question. The big-3 telecoms are a textbook validated cluster — they reject all three nulls, hold STABLE out of sample, and survive the hardest test for a cross-listed cohort: the context run shows they cluster apart from both the SOE-energy majors and the [[Alibaba]]/[[Tencent]]/[[Baidu]] cloud complex, so [[China Telecom]]'s cloud story is real in the fundamentals but invisible in daily co-movement. [[中特估]], the parent SOE-value-up theme, is the instructive contrast: it passes the random-basket null (the ten SOEs genuinely co-move) but fails the vol-matched null — the precise statistical signature of a duration basket, where the names move together because they are all low-vol, high-dividend bond proxies bid up by the same yield-starved domestic capital, not because there is a distinct "中特估 factor." Same shape as [[Brazil fintech]] (real cohort, not a separable factor); the durable tradeable structure inside 中特估 is the telecom sub-bloc, not the ten-name theme. The process keeper is a Windows bug worth fixing at the source: a CJK cohort name crashes the permutation script's stdout under cp1252 after the math finishes but before the registry write, so the row silently fails to land — a failure that reads as "it ran fine" (exit 0) but leaves no trace.

Copper closes the audit's reachable backlog and adds the one axis the taxonomy was missing: commodity beta. The result is the mirror image of the AI-optics finding, and together they fix what null tests can and cannot establish. AI optics passed the nulls yet was two pairs averaged, not one factor; copper passes the nulls at the 0.0001 floor and IS one factor — but a trivial, non-distinct one. A commodity cohort is guaranteed to crush the random-basket and vol-matched nulls because every member is levered to one exogenous price, so cohesion is necessary but uninformative; the only diagnostics that carry information are the threshold scan (the miners never separate from COPX, CPER, GDX, or SPY) and the intra-advantage vs the metal itself (+0.033 — a sliver of operating leverage and nothing more). The investable read collapses to the ETF: own COPX for levered copper-equity beta or CPER for the unlevered metal; picking [[Southern Copper]] over [[First Quantum Minerals]] is a bet on cost curve and jurisdiction (Grasberg, Cobre Panamá, Kamoa-Kakula), not on a separate factor. It is the commodity twin of [[Nuclear renaissance|nuclear/SMR]]'s validated-but-ETF-replicable verdict. With copper done, every cluster-validation candidate that had reachable data — across the whole multi-day audit — now has a verdict; the only items left (DTC telehealth, title insurance) await their tickers.
