## Synthesis

Today's scan says the market is still paying for AI infrastructure, but it is separating business models more aggressively. Chip-tool makers and data-center equipment providers are getting the upside from capacity expansion and power/cooling bottlenecks, while data-center REIT owners are showing the downside of duration, capital intensity and financing burden. The same discipline lens explains [[Air Products]]: the stock rose because management abandoned a low-return hydrogen project, not because the energy-transition theme improved.

The APD follow-up confirms the scan's first read: the final-close event tape was +8.04% raw and +8.02% abnormal versus a 63-session [[XLB]] model, or +8.08 residual sigma. The market was not paying up for hydrogen growth; it was paying up for the deletion of a low-return project. That keeps [[Hydrogen economy]] as an offtake-and-return-threshold story rather than a thematic capex story.

The other big structural signal is that regulated crypto infrastructure may not be a simple stablecoin rent monopoly. [[Open USD]] turns stablecoins into a distribution fight among payment networks, banks, exchanges and cloud/platform companies; [[Circle]]'s move is a warning that USDC's value depends on network control, not just regulatory legitimacy. The CRCL follow-up made that measurable: CRCL fell -17.55% close-to-close, and even after giving it credit for [[Coinbase]]'s -3.60% crypto-equity selloff, the 63-session COIN beta model left a -13.43% abnormal move and -3.23 residual-sigma shock. That says the tape was repricing Circle's issuer-rent durability, not merely marking down crypto beta.

[[AeroVironment]] is the defense-demand counterweight. The Q4 follow-up shows AVAV was not just riding a green defense tape: the stock rose +18.76% close-to-close, and a 63-session [[ITA]]+[[SPY]] model expected only +1.16%, leaving a +17.59% abnormal move and +4.17 residual-sigma shock. The market paid for proof that precision-strike drones, backlog conversion and BlueHalo scale can offset the SCAR overhang, even though FY2027 adjusted EPS guidance below consensus keeps integration/margin risk alive.

[[Nike]] remains a turnaround with messy earnings quality.
