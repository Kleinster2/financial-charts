## Synthesis

### Data as an asset (FT/Kuiken)

The durable concept under this op-ed is the accounting recognition of data, and the analytically live fact is the asymmetry between the two systems. China built a top-down stack — doctrine (data = 5th factor of production, 2020), then auditable rule (MOF provisions, 2024, coordinated by the [[National Data Administration]]), then market plumbing (data exchanges) — and wired it into [[LGFV|municipal balance sheets]] as fresh collateral against a debt overhang. The mechanism is real but the take-up is thin (~2% of A-shares), so the policy is still mostly statement-of-intent, and the [[US-China Economic and Security Review Commission]] itself called the asset values "difficult to determine." The [[United States]] is the mirror image: deeper de facto markets for data ($200bn brokers; data as the prize asset in the [[FTC]] Toysmart fight and RadioShack's bankruptcy) but no standard-setting engagement, because [[GAAP]] expenses internally generated intangibles and [[FASB]] has no project. The market read-through, per [[Mike Kuiken]]: as data becomes the feedstock for AI, the side that learns to measure it shapes how AI-era enterprise value gets valued, secured, and disclosed — and the US blind spot is a choice, not a technical limit. Cold research corrected the FT's "intangible asset" simplification (the rule is dual inventory/intangible) and surfaced the adoption gap the op-ed left out. Primary lens is investing (balance sheets, intangibles); geopolitics (strategic competition) and technologies (data economy / AI feedstock) flagged as sibling-vault glosses below.

## Synthesis (Pátria deepdive)

The Pátria build closed the most interesting open thread from the Cleam network: the cluster work falsified the equity story. Pátria markets itself as the "mini-Blackstone of Latin America," and on fundamentals the analogy holds — a fee-earning AUM machine compounding 38% with a Blackstone pedigree. But the statistical test says the stock does not trade with the US alt complex at all: it is a hierarchical singleton whose pairwise correlations to BX/KKR/APO are no higher than to the broad market or to Brazil, and whose own variance dominates. The causal read is the EM wrapper — BRL, Brazil rates, and EM risk appetite swamp the alt-manager beta, which is exactly why PAX sits 33% below its IPO price despite five years of AUM growth. Business comparables and trading comparables are different objects; the cluster framework is what separates them.
