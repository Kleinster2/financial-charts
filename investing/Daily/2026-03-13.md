### Section 301 concept note

- [[Section 301]] — new concept note. Trade Act of 1974 provision (19 U.S.C. §§2411-2420) — the primary US tariff authority post-SCOTUS. Legal mechanics (no rate cap, no duration limit, 12-month investigation process), full history (130 cases since 1974, Reagan-era Japan campaigns → WTO dormancy → Trump China trade war → Biden four-year review → post-SCOTUS replacement vehicle). Current China rates: EVs 100%, semis 50%, solar 50%, steel/aluminum 25%. Comparison table of all five tariff authorities (Section 301 vs 232 vs IEEPA vs 122 vs 338). March 2026 expansion: [[USTR]] launched investigations into 76 economies (16 excess capacity, 60 forced labor) — unprecedented multi-country use. Legal durability: [[HMTX Industries v United States]] (Federal Circuit, Sep 2025) and [[SCOTUS IEEPA ruling]] (Feb 2026) both distinguish Section 301 from IEEPA. Replacement tariffs ~early 2027 earliest due to procedural timeline.
