This lifts the count of genuinely distinct, non-ETF-replicable factors in the campaign to five — [[Railroads]], [[Tobacco majors basket|tobacco]], [[Solid waste|waste]], [[Life science tools|life-science tools]], [[Trucking and LTL|trucking]]. The pattern is now sharp: the distinct factors are defensive/oligopolistic (waste, rail, tobacco) or single-driver picks-and-shovels (tools), where a liquid ETF either does not exist or is too diluted to capture the names; the ETF-replicable ones are broad cyclical complexes (distributors, building products, apparel) a sector ETF already prices. Distinctness is earned where the basket out-coheres every tradeable ETF — and that happens at concentrated oligopolies and specialized supply chains, not at diffuse cyclical labels.

[[Auto parts retail]] adds a new shape: distinctness concentrated in a pair. The five-name aftermarket label is loose (intra 0.471) and never clusters cleanly, yet it is genuinely distinct from cyclical retail (+0.157 vs [[XRT]]/[[XLY]]) — and that distinctness is overwhelmingly the premium [[O'Reilly Automotive|ORLY]]+[[AutoZone|AZO]] duopoly, which carries a +0.599 intra-advantage over the retail ETFs (one of the campaign's largest) and the widest stable threshold band in the sub-cohort sweep (0.45). The ownable factor is a robust two-name duopoly, not a sector basket: the high-quality DIY/DIFM model decouples from cyclical retail, while the laggards (turnaround [[Advance Auto Parts|AAP]], industrial-mixed [[Genuine Parts|GPC]], salvage/Europe [[LKQ Corporation|LKQ]]) each run their own story. It refines the campaign law — distinctness is earned by a shared driver no liquid ETF prices — to note that the unit of distinctness can be a pair, not a whole sector.

[[Drug distributors]] caps the distribution arc (industrial, auto-parts, healthcare) with the campaign's most ETF-distinct result. The big-3 oligopoly ([[McKesson|MCK]]/[[Cencora|COR]]/[[Cardinal Health|CAH]]) carries a +0.502 intra-advantage over the healthcare/medtech ETFs — the largest margin recorded — the widest separable threshold band (0.30), and a STABLE holdout. It is the cleanest illustration of the campaign's law: a low-margin, regulated, fee-based logistics oligopoly whose drivers (generic deflation, distribution-fee contracts, opioid-settlement cash, new-modality volume) are orthogonal to the drug-development cycle [[XLV]] prices, so no ETF can replicate it. The distribution arc itself splits cleanly by structure — the concentrated oligopolies (drug big-3; the ORLY/AZO auto duopoly) are distinct factors, the diffuse one (industrial MRO distributors) is sector-ETF beta. Distinctness tracks concentration, not the "distribution" label. The campaign now stands at six genuinely distinct non-ETF factors — [[Railroads]], [[Tobacco majors basket|tobacco]], [[Solid waste]], [[Life science tools]], [[Trucking and LTL]], [[Drug distributors]] — every one a concentrated oligopoly or specialized supply chain.
