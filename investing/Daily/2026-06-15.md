## Synthesis

Lithium is the third commodity-beta cohort, and it is the one that maps the law's boundary. Copper and gold both showed that a commodity's listed miners cohere into a real factor that a single ETF replicates; lithium confirms the pattern a third time — the four names clear the vol-matched null at p 0.0003, so the cohesion is genuinely lithium-specific and not an artifact of their enormous shared volatility — but it does so at the lowest cohesion in the family (weekly 0.62 against gold's 0.86 and copper's 0.71), and the reasons why are the lesson. Three taxes hold it down: the cohort spans development stages, so the pre-revenue Thacker Pass developer [[Lithium Americas]] (118% annualized vol) trades on GM-and-DOE financing news rather than spot lithium and splits off entirely; the names are cross-region ADRs whose asynchronous closes depress the daily correlation below the weekly; and two of the four run above 115% vol, so they are idiosyncratic-catalyst machines. What survives all of that is still a factor, but a loose one, and the sub-cohort sweep makes the ETF-replicability total: even stripping the cohort down to its three producers leaves LIT sitting on top from threshold 0.30 — there is no clean lithium sub-cluster to own, only LIT or the [[Albemarle]]/[[SQM]] producer pair. The structural fact underneath distinguishes lithium from its commodity siblings: there is no investable lithium spot instrument, no GLD or CPER, so unlike gold or copper the equity is not a levered proxy for a metal you could otherwise hold — it is the only lithium exposure that exists, which is exactly why the equity-specific noise matters more here than anywhere else in the family. The regime reading refines the gold finding rather than confirming it cleanly: lithium is tightening as it rebounds (0.53 to 0.60 since 2024), but its holdout still reads weakened, because a recent rebound off a choppy bottom is not the clean sustained move that gold's bull was — the rule is that strong, sustained trends tighten commodity-equity cohorts, and lithium's rebound is too young and too noisy to have done so yet. With copper, gold, and lithium all validated, the commodity-beta family is now three-deep and internally ordered: the same law holds for each (a real cohort that an ETF replicates, where single-name selection is idiosyncrasy not factor), but cohesion ranges from gold's 0.86 to lithium's 0.62, set entirely by how clean the price regime is and how much idiosyncratic noise the members carry — the cleanest forward test the family offers is that a sideways chop in any of the three metals should visibly loosen its cohort.

Uranium closes the commodity-beta family at four and extends it on the one axis the first three left implicit: how far the equity sits from the metal. The uranium miners validate exactly like gold and copper — tight (0.765), durable (STABLE holdout 1.01), all nulls at the 0.0001 floor, replicated by their ETF (URNM, -0.088 advantage) — and they confirm the regime rule more cleanly than any sibling, because uranium's price path is legible: cohesion ran 0.78-0.80 through the 2022-24 spot bull, slackened to 0.73 in the 2025 consolidation, and re-tightened in 2026, so the cohort visibly breathes with the metal's trend. But the new lesson is the +0.180 wedge against physical uranium (SRUUF, the Sprott Physical Uranium Trust), more than double gold's, and the reason is structural: half the cohort is pre-production developers — [[NexGen Energy]]'s Arrow, [[Denison Mines]]'s Phoenix — that own no spot exposure at all and trade as options on the next decade of supply. So uranium is the family's proof that the miners-vs-metal distance is not noise but a measurable function of how much of a cohort is building future production rather than selling current output: copper +0.033 (mature majors), gold +0.070 (established producers), uranium +0.180 (half developers). The year made it concrete — the uranium equities returned +45-156% while the metal returned +8.5%, the widest equity-over-metal gap the family has recorded. With four metals validated, the family now reads as one law with two dials: cohesion set by the cleanliness of the price regime (gold and uranium tight in sustained bulls, copper and lithium loose in chop), and distance-from-metal set by the cohort's developer content — both forward-testable, a chop in uranium should loosen this 0.77 cohort toward copper's 0.71, and a wave of uranium developers reaching production should pull the equity back toward spot.

Sports betting is the cleaner half of the question TKO answered earlier today: where TKO had no peers at all (negative intra-advantage, a true sector orphan), the online sportsbooks do form a real, distinct factor — positive intra-advantage versus casinos, B2B suppliers, and the market, and a random-basket null it clears — but only as a duopoly. The market structure and the factor structure are the same fact: [[FanDuel]] and [[DraftKings]], ~70–75% of US GGR between them, are the one robust cluster (0.681, threshold-stable), while the small operator [[Rush Street Interactive|Rush Street]] trades as a satellite and the picks-and-shovels data suppliers ([[Genius Sports]], [[Sportradar]]) don't cluster at all — data licensing is a different business than betting margin, and the tape knows it. The casino control settling into its own separate cluster is the other clean read: omni-channel land-based operators carry digital arms but still trade on a different factor than the online-first books, so "gaming" is at least two equity factors, not one. What pulls the verdict from validated down to partial is the same scale axis that defines the industry — the third name is a minnow next to the duopoly — plus an eroding tightness (holdout 0.83) that lines up with the [[Prediction markets]] threat introducing a new, unevenly-distributed shared risk just as the operators' state-mix and product economics diverge. The campaign's split-axis catalogue gains a fourth entry: after value-chain (housing, AI-power), driver-divergence (ad-tech), and geography (casinos), sports betting splits by scale — the duopoly is the factor, the small operator the satellite.

The BDR thread closed out into a small but complete cross-border map. Two concept notes now bracket it: [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts]] is the onshore (B3, reais) wrapper and [[Offshore investing for Brazilians]] the offshore (USD, abroad) route, with [[Foreign private issuer]] explaining the far end — that many of the US names a Brazilian buys either way are themselves foreign issuers filing 6-K/IFRS, which is the same fact that made [[Aura Minerals]] and [[Equinox Gold]] report GAAP-look losses on real operating profits. The [[Equinox Gold]] deepdive was the cleanest demonstration of that lesson and of its limit: its operating line reconciles and tells a doubling-revenue, Greenstone-and-Calibre growth story, while its reported net line is merger-and-NCI noise that the honest move documents and defers rather than charts. The rule it reinforces: a deepdive is only as fresh as its last verification pass, and corporate actions move faster than vault notes.
