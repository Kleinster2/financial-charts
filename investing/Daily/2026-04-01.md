## Synthesis

Eyck Freymann (Hoover/Stanford) argues in the FT that Iran's Hormuz closure is a directly replicable playbook for China against Taiwan — and the semiconductor version would be far more damaging than the energy version. The critical mechanism: standard war-risk insurance contains a "Five Powers Clause" that auto-terminates coverage in any US-China conflict. For Hormuz, Iran had to *prove* it could threaten shipping; for Taiwan, insurance evaporates at the mere *perception* of confrontation. No strategic semiconductor reserve exists (unlike oil SPR), TSMC Arizona covers ~5% of capacity, and the supply doesn't just get expensive — it disappears. Strengthens the structural bear case for TSMC-as-Taiwan-entity and the bull case for geographic diversification (Samsung Taylor, IFS, GFS).
