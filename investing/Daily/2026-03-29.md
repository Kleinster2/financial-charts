# 2026-03-29

## Synthesis

Markets closed Friday March 28 with the S&P 500 at 6,368.85 (-1.67%), Nasdaq at 20,948.36 (-2.15%), Dow at 45,166.64 (-1.73%, now in correction territory). Fifth consecutive weekly loss — longest streak in 4 years. VIX at 31.05 (+13.16%). The Iran war oil shock is now dominating everything.

Brent closed at $112.57 — up 51% in March, the largest monthly gain on record, beating September 1990's Kuwait invasion (+46%). [[BloombergNEF]]: 9M bpd knocked off global supply. The critical new data is the paper-physical disconnect: Brent futures +36% from pre-war vs Dubai physical +76%. [[Marko Papic]] ([[BCA Research]]) published the most concrete supply-cliff timeline: current 4.5-5M bpd loss doubles by mid-April as SPR releases and sanctions waivers exhaust. [[Kuwait Petroleum]] CEO: 3-4 months to restart shut-in wells even after a ceasefire.

Gold collapsed ~15% in March — worst month since 2008. [[Turkey]] CB sold 50 tonnes ($3B) to stabilize lira. UK gilts heading for worst month since Truss mini-budget (10Y near 5%). Modupe Adegbembo ([[Jefferies]]): European governments at "much weaker fiscal starting point than 2022."

Two mega-IPO stories dominated corporate news: [[SpaceX]] IPO filing imminent ($75B raise, $1.75T valuation, June target, banks listed alphabetically, no standard lockup, >20% retail allocation) and [[Anthropic]] Claude Mythos/Capybara model revealed via data leak (new tier above Opus, "dramatically higher" than Opus 4.6, unprecedented cyber risks) plus Q4 2026 IPO discussions ($60B raise). [[OpenAI]] raising $10B more (total round $120B+), sweetening PE deals with 17.5% guaranteed preferred returns.
