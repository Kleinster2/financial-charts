## Synthesis

April 9 session: S&P 500 +0.62%, Dow +0.58%, Nasdaq +0.83%. Ceasefire day-two reality check — [[Strait of Hormuz]] remains effectively closed despite Apr 8 truce. Iran charging crypto tolls >$1M/vessel, allowing only 4-9 ships/day. WTI +3% to $97.87, Brent +1% to $95.92 as markets repriced the logistics gap [[Helima Croft]] warned about within hours of the deal. Israel-Lebanon direct talks announced, providing some broader de-escalation signal.

Dispersion was the story: [[Constellation Brands]] +8.5% (4.1σ) on Q4 earnings beat with $900M buyback (though FY2028 guidance withdrawal on [[GLP-1]] headwinds is the more interesting signal). [[Amazon]] +5.6% (3.1σ) on Jassy letter — AWS AI at $15B+ annualized run rate, custom chip business valued at ~$50B standalone. Meanwhile the cybersecurity cluster ([[CrowdStrike]] -7.5%, [[Zscaler]] -11.3%, [[Okta]] -10.9%, [[Rubrik]] -10.6%) continued its post-Claude Code Security de-rating on a risk-on day. [[Snowflake]] -11.8% (3.6σ, highest sigma) on leadership transitions and class action overhang. [[Banco Inter]] -8.0% (2.9σ) repeating its Apr 2 selloff — thin-float EM amplifier pattern.

[[Robert Pape]]'s "The New Balance of Power After the Ceasefire" is the analytical frame to watch: the ceasefire is not a pause but a structural shift. Iran demonstrated that persistent uncertainty over Hormuz = a form of power. The US lost the ability to guarantee critical energy flow stability, and allies are hedging incrementally. [[Adam Tooze]]'s March Chartbook made the complementary macro argument: 2026's oil shock removes the central bank easing option that rescued 2025 from tariff shock.
