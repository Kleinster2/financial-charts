## Synthesis

The user asked me to fix the compliance debt I'd flagged-but-deferred a day earlier, and doing it properly turned up a substantive correction rather than just chart plumbing. Yesterday's chat answer and note edits had asserted Salesforce's SBC wedge "narrowed from ~43 points to ~16" — but ~43 points is Snowflake's gap (non-GAAP +9% vs GAAP −31%); Salesforce's is ~14, of which SBC is ~8.5 points and acquired-intangible amortization most of the rest. The number got cross-contaminated between the two cases in the same paragraph that was drawing the contrast. Pulling the FY2026 10-K to make the charts current is what surfaced it — the SBC figures ($2.80B → $3.52B, steady at ~8-8.5% of revenue) let me decompose the wedge precisely instead of asserting a round number. The lesson is the recurring one: verification isn't a finishing step you can defer, because the act of grounding the claim in the filing is what catches the claim being wrong. The new `sbc-operating-margin-wedge.png` now carries the whole thread in one image — two SBC-heavy software companies a decade apart on the same curve, one across the zero line and one not — which is the durable artifact the Tang-chart question was reaching for all along.

The China-telecom deepdives complete the bloc the Jun-12 China Unicom work opened, and the three notes now read as one structural story: a single state-driven factor (PC1 ~78%, the three carriers a clean cluster separable from both US telecoms and China-ETF beta) sliced into three risk profiles. China Mobile is the defensive core — lowest vol, highest margin (~13% vs ~6% and ~5%), biggest dividend, the natural index anchor — but its 2025 net-income dip (−0.9%, the first of the cycle) is the warning that a dividend story resting on a saturated ~RMB1tn base eventually needs earnings that hold. China Telecom is the growth tilt: Tianyi Cloud at >RMB120bn and #2 in China IaaS is a genuine cloud platform, which is why it led the bloc's re-rating (~+250%) — but on daily returns it still trades as the highest-beta expression of the same policy factor, not as an independent cloud name. China Unicom is the laggard and the data-asset pioneer. The investable insight is that all three are expressions of one trade (the 中特估 SOE value-up re-rating funded by post-5G capex discipline), and the only thing that would break the cluster is Tianyi Cloud growing large enough to pull China Telecom into the [[Alibaba Cloud]] complex — which the cohort data says has not happened. The EO 13959 cross-vault note closes the other half: the 2021 NYSE delistings that pushed all three telecoms' primary listings home are not just a price footnote but a capital-markets-decoupling precedent, the financial-channel analogue of semiconductor export controls, now documented in the geopolitics vault where that statecraft lens belongs. Tooling: Alpha Vantage's no-HK-fundamentals gap held for both names (financials sourced from company reporting, Sankey/waterfall skipped with reason), the same constraint logged on the Unicom deepdive.

The four follow-ons turned the telecom bloc into a properly bounded factor and gave it its macro home. The cloud-cluster test did real work: it falsified the soft version of the cloud thesis (China Telecom does not yet trade with [[Alibaba]]/[[Tencent]]/[[Baidu]] — telecom-vs-cloud correlation 0.253 against 0.635 intra-bloc) while confirming the bloc is a genuine telecom factor rather than generic state-champion beta (the SOE-energy majors cluster apart at 0.219). The most useful structural detail is that the telecom and cloud branches are statistical siblings — so if Tianyi Cloud ever does pull China Telecom out of the bloc, the dendrogram shows exactly which neighbor it would migrate toward, and the telecom-vs-cloud correlation is the dial to watch. The 中特估 note then supplies the why for the whole bloc: the re-rating is fundamentally a yield trade (sub-2% CGBs and a dead property market forcing income-starved domestic capital into 4–7%-yielding SOEs), with CSRC policy as permission rather than cause — which is the honest, falsifiable framing (it lives or dies on the yield gap and payout durability, not on a slogan). PetroChina's ~+330% since the November 2022 announcement is the same trade in its highest-octane form (dividend bid plus an oil tailwind), China Mobile's steadier ~+115% the cleaner read. HFCAA completes the decoupling picture EO 13959 opened: two tracks, one keyed to ownership and one to audit transparency, the second defused in 2022 but never repealed — a dormant lever, not a closed chapter, and the force behind the listing-repatriation that brought all three telecoms home. The cross-vault graph is now coherent end to end: investing holds the markets/accounting detail, geopolitics holds the statecraft, and they point at each other.

The Anthropic export-control ban is the day's most consequential ingest and the cleanest case yet of the vault's "full-stack AI containment" thread turning from forecast into fact. In April the [[Export controls#White House distillation memo (Apr 23, 2026)|White House distillation memo]] argued model access was itself a technology-transfer vector and predicted controls on model weights or frontier-model exports; on June 12 [[Commerce Department|Commerce]] placed [[Anthropic]]'s [[Claude Fable 5|Fable 5]] and [[Claude Mythos|Mythos 5]] under export controls outright, and Anthropic took both fully offline because the rule's reach (foreign nationals inside and outside the US, including its own foreign-born staff) left no way to comply selectively. Two things make this more than a one-day headline. First, it inverts the logic of the entire chip regime: export controls were built to deny adversaries while the [[American AI Export Program]] proliferated US models to allies — this action instead hard-bans a US model to allies (UK, EU) over a disputed jailbreak, even as [[NVIDIA]] H200 / [[Blackwell]] access to [[China]] has been loosening; Jimmy Goodrich's "puzzling" quote names the contradiction directly. Second, it is the second government front against Anthropic — stacked on the unresolved [[Pentagon AI access dispute 2026|Pentagon supply-chain designation]] and lawsuit — landing 11 days after the [[Anthropic IPO 2026|confidential S-1]] and converting the "Mythos rolling out to allies" capability story the filing leaned on into a paused, litigated regulatory overhang. The read for the IPO is that Anthropic's safety-and-capability posture, which had been a commercial asset (the #1-app surge after the Pentagon standoff), is now a recurring source of binary government risk priced into a near-$1tn listing.
