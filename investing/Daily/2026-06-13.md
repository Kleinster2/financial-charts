## Synthesis

The user asked me to fix the compliance debt I'd flagged-but-deferred a day earlier, and doing it properly turned up a substantive correction rather than just chart plumbing. Yesterday's chat answer and note edits had asserted Salesforce's SBC wedge "narrowed from ~43 points to ~16" — but ~43 points is Snowflake's gap (non-GAAP +9% vs GAAP −31%); Salesforce's is ~14, of which SBC is ~8.5 points and acquired-intangible amortization most of the rest. The number got cross-contaminated between the two cases in the same paragraph that was drawing the contrast. Pulling the FY2026 10-K to make the charts current is what surfaced it — the SBC figures ($2.80B → $3.52B, steady at ~8-8.5% of revenue) let me decompose the wedge precisely instead of asserting a round number. The lesson is the recurring one: verification isn't a finishing step you can defer, because the act of grounding the claim in the filing is what catches the claim being wrong. The new `sbc-operating-margin-wedge.png` now carries the whole thread in one image — two SBC-heavy software companies a decade apart on the same curve, one across the zero line and one not — which is the durable artifact the Tang-chart question was reaching for all along.

The China-telecom deepdives complete the bloc the Jun-12 China Unicom work opened, and the three notes now read as one structural story: a single state-driven factor (PC1 ~78%, the three carriers a clean cluster separable from both US telecoms and China-ETF beta) sliced into three risk profiles. China Mobile is the defensive core — lowest vol, highest margin (~13% vs ~6% and ~5%), biggest dividend, the natural index anchor — but its 2025 net-income dip (−0.9%, the first of the cycle) is the warning that a dividend story resting on a saturated ~RMB1tn base eventually needs earnings that hold. China Telecom is the growth tilt: Tianyi Cloud at >RMB120bn and #2 in China IaaS is a genuine cloud platform, which is why it led the bloc's re-rating (~+250%) — but on daily returns it still trades as the highest-beta expression of the same policy factor, not as an independent cloud name. China Unicom is the laggard and the data-asset pioneer. The investable insight is that all three are expressions of one trade (the 中特估 SOE value-up re-rating funded by post-5G capex discipline), and the only thing that would break the cluster is Tianyi Cloud growing large enough to pull China Telecom into the [[Alibaba Cloud]] complex — which the cohort data says has not happened. The EO 13959 cross-vault note closes the other half: the 2021 NYSE delistings that pushed all three telecoms' primary listings home are not just a price footnote but a capital-markets-decoupling precedent, the financial-channel analogue of semiconductor export controls, now documented in the geopolitics vault where that statecraft lens belongs.
