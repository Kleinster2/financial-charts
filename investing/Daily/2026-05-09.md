### Sector internal correlation diagnostic — vault structure check

Question raised in conversation: should `Sectors/` hubs have meaningful internal correlation, i.e. should member actors share a price driver, or are they purely organisational buckets? Ran a whole-vault diagnostic to find out.

Method: parsed every `Sectors/*.md` for `[[Actor]]` wikilinks, mapped actors to tickers via `Actors/*.md` frontmatter aliases plus `ticker_metadata.name` fallback, pulled daily closes from `stock_prices_daily` over the trailing 252 trading days through 2026-05-03, and computed pairwise Pearson correlation on simple returns. 96 sector hubs total; 68 had at least three mappable tickers.

Findings: 17 sectors at avg pairwise ≥ 0.50 (coherent thematic hubs doing analytical work — [[WFE]] 0.83, [[Private Real Estate]] 0.77, [[US Memory]] 0.70, [[Copper Mining]] 0.66 etc). 23 in mid-band 0.30–0.50. Below 0.30 sit two distinct cases: genuinely diluted thematic sectors (Healthcare 0.17, Defense 0.15, Insurance 0.26, Media 0.16, Consumer Internet 0.23) and organisationally defined hubs (Sovereign Wealth Funds 0.13, Activist Investors 0.13, Corporate Venture Capital 0.10) where members are categorised by what they do, not what they sell, so correlation is correctly absent.

Structural read: parents like [[Semiconductors]] (0.31, 23 actors) get out-coherence by their own children — [[AI Compute]] 0.58, [[US Memory]] 0.70, [[Semiconductor Materials]] 0.65, [[Semiconductor Test]] 0.46 are all tighter than the parent. Same shape for [[AI Infrastructure]] 0.27. The structural pattern is to demote some parent hubs to indices and let the sub-sectors do analytical work.
