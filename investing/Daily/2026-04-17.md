### Post-v4-report gap-fill — demand-side chemistry concepts + Bolivia Paz transition

Filling the four gaps surfaced in yesterday's `/report lithium-4`. The report's cold-research pass flagged that the lithium-deficit narrative was missing the demand-substitution side (sodium-ion going commercial 2026, recycling reaching ~20% of demand by 2030, solid-state's mixed lithium-demand effect) and that the [[Bolivia]] country-level context was absent beyond the [[YLB]] stub. Ran cold research on each before writing per `docs/research-workflow.md#cold-research-pass` Phase 1.8.

- [[Sodium-ion batteries]] — added ## Synthesis section. Central insight: CATL's Naxtra mass production in 2026 at 175 Wh/kg, passing GB 38031-2025 EV traction standard, is 2026 commercial reality competing for demand growth that Wood Mac 2028 deficit forecasts assume lithium captures. CATL has 38-42% global SIB share; shipments 9 GWh 2025 (+150% YoY). Cost parity with LFP at $40-50/kWh by 2027. Western commercial scale is essentially non-existent — pattern matches the 2010s LFP and solar-panel industrial-policy failures.
- [[Battery recycling]] — added ## Synthesis section. Central insight: recycling supplies ~20% of lithium demand by 2030 (~500 ktpa LCE) — structural backstop against deficit forecasts. Tesla 92% 4680 recovery; Redwood 95% lithium recovery. Hydrometallurgy 33-53% cheaper than mining. But Western freestanding recyclers struggle — [[Li-Cycle]] paused Rochester construction Oct 2023; stock -90%. Chinese vertical integration ([[CATL]] Brunp 120 ktpa Foshan) is the benchmark. Timing mismatch: 2018-2023 EV wave doesn't reach EOL until 2030-2040, so recycling capacity must build ahead of feedstock.
- [[Solid-state batteries]] — added ## Synthesis section. Inverse framing from sodium-ion: sulfide-electrolyte solid-state (Toyota, Samsung SDI, Idemitsu Kosan Li-sulfide facility) uses metallic lithium anodes, INCREASING lithium content per cell. Net-positive for lithium demand if mass-scale commercialization happens. But consensus timelines keep slipping — high-volume now positioned for early 2030s, not 2026-2028. The risk is a Chinese entrant reaches scaled production before Western pure-plays (CATL/BYD have quiet programs).
- [[Bolivia]] — new actor note. 21% of global lithium reserves (~23M tonnes M&I, the largest share of any country) but 2024 production was a few hundred tonnes. Political transition is the story: [[Rodrigo Paz]] succeeded Luis Arce **November 8, 2025** — first non-MAS president in two decades. Paz framework: "modernization without privatization" — reviewing CBC (Chinese) and Rosatom (Russian) DLE contracts blocked by Congress July 2025. Expected outcome: renegotiation not cancellation, along Chilean Nova Andino Litio lines. Three scenarios (status quo / partial unlock / full unlock) bracket 2028-2030 deficit forecasts — Bolivia is the single largest forecast-uncertainty variable in global lithium supply.
- [[Rodrigo Paz]] — new actor stub. President of Bolivia from Nov 8 2025. Son of former President Jaime Paz Zamora. Center-right PDC coalition (no majority). Lithium policy is the market lever being tracked.
- [[YLB]] — expanded with Paz transition section. Added structural implication: future YLB operational model likely resembles [[Codelco]]'s — state entity holds operational mandate but partners with foreign technology operators on specific projects. $800M+ of Arce-era DLE investment under audit.
- [[Lithium Triangle]] — Bolivia section updated with Paz transition and contract-review context. The political regime has now shifted across all three countries in under 18 months: Milei (Dec 2023), Kast (Mar 2026), Paz (Nov 2025).
- [[Lithium]] — Related section updated to include [[Sodium-ion batteries]], [[Battery recycling]], [[Solid-state batteries]], [[Bolivia]], [[Rodrigo Paz]] as peer references.

### Epistemic-scaffolding synthesis pattern — codified and applied to four hub concepts

User-curated exemplar from the v4 lithium report reframe conversation: the synthesis should move beyond "central insight → constraint → counter-example → one-line" and add three epistemic moves — (1) testable-claim opening naming what the price implicitly answers, (2) bull/bear symmetry requiring both sides to be named with what-must-be-true conditions, (3) forecast-spread anchoring with analyst-camp attribution and a defensible reframe that honestly weakens the stronger claim.

Applied the pattern to four hub concept notes:

- [[Lithium]] — rewrote synthesis around user-edited exemplar. Core claim: "the rally has prices embedded in it." Three things priced accurately (tightening, response, demand acceleration); fourth thing partially priced (substitution). Two forces (sodium-ion at cell level, recycling at mine level) with specific figures. Bull/bear symmetry (both substitution vectors have to underperform). Forecast spread (Fastmarkets 2026 / Wood Mac 2028 / IEA 2030s). Defensible reframe: "priced closer to the low-substitution end of the forecast spread," not "not pricing substitution at all."
- [[CUDA moat]] — "NVIDIA's valuation has an answer embedded — how durable is CUDA lock-in 2026-2030?" Three things priced (training dominance 85%+, 4M developers, supply chain). Fourth thing (erosion rate at edges). Two forces (compiler abstraction + hyperscaler ASIC). Three camps (moat holds / erodes at edges / breaks). Reframe: priced closer to the high-durability end.
- [[Rare earth leverage]] — "Dysprosium +180% prices the monopoly-durability question." Three things priced (refining dominance, weaponization precedent, strategic-foresight premium). Two forces (replication: Lynas, MP, Iluka, REEtec + technology unlock: phosphogypsum, chromatography). Three camps (monopoly entrenched / chokepoint erodes gradually / technology breaks model). Reframe: priced closer to "monopoly entrenched."
- [[AI consolidation]] — "Big Tech valuations embed an answer to how much AI gets captured by regulatory-arbitrage consolidation." Three things priced (acqui-hire mechanics, startup thinning, frontier-lab pricing). Two forces (regulatory convergence: DMA/SMS/DOJ + market dispersion: open-weight/ASICs/China/enterprise TAM). Three camps (consolidation wins / regulators catch up slowly / converge and dispersion bites). Reframe: priced closer to "consolidation wins, dispersion incremental."

Pattern codified in `docs/research-workflow.md` as "Hub-Concept Synthesis Pattern" — seven moves in order (testable-claim opening, what's priced accurately, the fourth thing, two mechanism forces, bull/bear symmetry, forecast spread, defensible reframe), with scope (when to use, when to skip) and explicit listing of failure modes it prevents (recency bias, source anchoring, overclaim).

### Bundeswehr three-supplier drone procurement + Papperger "Legos" gaffe

April 15 2026 Bundestag vote: [[Rheinmetall]] added as third supplier to the German kamikaze-drone procurement (€298M upfront for [[FV-014]]), joining [[Helsing]] (€269M, HX-2) and [[Stark]] (€269M, OWE-V Virtus). A €1B per-supplier cap is imposed absent further parliament approval — so the program is bounded at ~€3B total near-term vs. original framework of up to ~€4.3B cut to €2B combined in February. Three-supplier structure is an explicit hedge: [[Germany]] did not want single-vendor dependency on either an incumbent prime or a startup. Contrast with the US, where [[Anduril]] has a single Army contract up to ~€20B over 10 years — per-supplier ceiling ~20× the German one.

- [[Stark]] — new actor stub. Berlin/Munich defense startup founded 2024 by [[Florian Seibel]] (prior [[Quantum Systems]]) after Quantum investors resisted weaponization. ~$100M raised, $500M valuation (Aug 2025, [[Sequoia Capital]] lead). Backed by [[Peter Thiel]] — Feb 2026 political pushback from Green-party defense specialist. Flagship OWE-V Virtus loitering munition. Oct 2025 trial miss (reportedly 0/4 strikes) underlines the "award-precedes-performance" risk that the three-supplier model is partly insurance against.
- [[Auterion]] — new actor stub. Zurich/Munich autonomy OS company founded 2018 by [[Lorenz Meier]] (co-creator of [[PX4]]). ~€100M 2025 revenue, profitable — rare for a defense-software startup. [[Rheinmetall]] took a "significant" minority stake Nov 2025 after Dec 2024 MOU; AuterionOS powers [[FV-014]]. Hardware-agnostic open-OS model positioned as European analog to [[Anduril]]'s closed Lattice OS — "Windows, not iPhone" bet on long-run defense software.
- [[FV-014]] — new product note. [[Rheinmetall]]'s kamikaze drone, also referred to as "Raider." 100 km range, 70-minute flight duration, AuterionOS inside. Rheinmetall's re-entry ticket after missing the February first round (couldn't demonstrate in time). €298M upfront, framework up to €2.39B over 7 years capped at €1B absent further approval.
- [[Armin Papperger]] — new actor note. [[Rheinmetall]] CEO since 2013. Share price +400% since Feb 2022. Led drone pivot via [[Auterion]] partnership. April 2026 "Legos" controversy: said Ukrainian drones were made by "Ukrainian housewives" with "3D printers in the kitchen" — "this is not innovation." Comments landed days before the Bundestag FV-014 vote. Rheinmetall walked the comments back. Revealing cultural position: legacy prime competing against the Ukrainian cottage industry whose output is actually destroying Russian armor.
- [[Oleksandr Kamyshin]] — new actor stub. Adviser on strategic industries to [[Volodymyr Zelenskyy]]; former Minister of Strategic Industries (2023–2024) and CEO Ukrainian Railways. Responded to Papperger: Ukraine's "Lego drones" had already burned more than 11,000 Russian tanks. Framed the tension — legacy primes with slow delivery cycles vs. battlefield-tested attritable munitions produced at scale.
- [[Rheinmetall]] — expanded with "Drone pivot (2024–2026)" section covering MOU → stake → Feb miss → Apr win, plus Papperger "Legos" subsection. Related updated with drone-procurement cluster. Quick-stats table adds drones row.
- [[Helsing]] — expanded with "Bundeswehr kamikaze-drone contract (Feb–Apr 2026)" section. Three-supplier structure described as double shield: caps Helsing's share but insulates its contract from political blowback at either [[Peter Thiel]]'s [[Stark]] stake or Rheinmetall's Apr 2026 Legos gaffe. Related updated with Stark, Rheinmetall, FV-014, Auterion.
- [[European defense spending]] — flagship-numbers table adds Bundeswehr three-supplier procurement row (€269M + €269M + €298M, €1B per-supplier cap) and US contrast row ([[Anduril]] €20B / 10-yr ceiling ~20× the German ceiling) — quantifying the structural-capacity gap that Europe's multi-supplier hedge creates.
- [[Drone warfare]] — European-players table rebuilt with [[Helsing]] / [[Stark]] / [[Rheinmetall]] / [[Auterion]] rows plus a Bundeswehr three-supplier paragraph contrasting the €1B/supplier European model against [[Anduril]]'s US single-vendor model (€20B / 10 yrs). Related section adds FV-014, Stark, Auterion, Papperger, Kamyshin.
- [[Grob Aircraft]] — new actor stub. Southern German airframe manufacturer acquired by [[Helsing]] in 2025 as the production base for the CA-1 Europa autonomous combat aircraft. The acquisition converted Helsing from software-first defense AI into a software-plus-hardware vendor — material for [[Bundeswehr]] procurement, which leans on domestic industrial-base arguments.

The structural insight: [[Germany]]'s three-supplier hedge protects against single-point failure (Stark trial miss, Rheinmetall delivery lag, Thiel political heat) but also bounds per-supplier revenue at €1B vs. Anduril-scale US procurement. For the three German entries, the program is materially accretive but not company-transforming — the real scaling question is whether the cap lifts before Lithuania deployment in 2027.
