### Epistemic-scaffolding synthesis pattern — codified and applied to four hub concepts

User-curated exemplar from the v4 lithium report reframe conversation: the synthesis should move beyond "central insight → constraint → counter-example → one-line" and add three epistemic moves — (1) testable-claim opening naming what the price implicitly answers, (2) bull/bear symmetry requiring both sides to be named with what-must-be-true conditions, (3) forecast-spread anchoring with analyst-camp attribution and a defensible reframe that honestly weakens the stronger claim.

Applied the pattern to four hub concept notes:

- [[Lithium]] — rewrote synthesis around user-edited exemplar. Core claim: "the rally has prices embedded in it." Three things priced accurately (tightening, response, demand acceleration); fourth thing partially priced (substitution). Two forces (sodium-ion at cell level, recycling at mine level) with specific figures. Bull/bear symmetry (both substitution vectors have to underperform). Forecast spread (Fastmarkets 2026 / Wood Mac 2028 / IEA 2030s). Defensible reframe: "priced closer to the low-substitution end of the forecast spread," not "not pricing substitution at all."
- [[CUDA moat]] — "NVIDIA's valuation has an answer embedded — how durable is CUDA lock-in 2026-2030?" Three things priced (training dominance 85%+, 4M developers, supply chain). Fourth thing (erosion rate at edges). Two forces (compiler abstraction + hyperscaler ASIC). Three camps (moat holds / erodes at edges / breaks). Reframe: priced closer to the high-durability end.
- [[Rare earth leverage]] — "Dysprosium +180% prices the monopoly-durability question." Three things priced (refining dominance, weaponization precedent, strategic-foresight premium). Two forces (replication: Lynas, MP, Iluka, REEtec + technology unlock: phosphogypsum, chromatography). Three camps (monopoly entrenched / chokepoint erodes gradually / technology breaks model). Reframe: priced closer to "monopoly entrenched."
- [[AI consolidation]] — "Big Tech valuations embed an answer to how much AI gets captured by regulatory-arbitrage consolidation." Three things priced (acqui-hire mechanics, startup thinning, frontier-lab pricing). Two forces (regulatory convergence: DMA/SMS/DOJ + market dispersion: open-weight/ASICs/China/enterprise TAM). Three camps (consolidation wins / regulators catch up slowly / converge and dispersion bites). Reframe: priced closer to "consolidation wins, dispersion incremental."

### Bundeswehr three-supplier drone procurement + Papperger "Legos" gaffe

April 15 2026 Bundestag vote: [[Rheinmetall]] added as third supplier to the German kamikaze-drone procurement (€298M upfront for [[FV-014]]), joining [[Helsing]] (€269M, HX-2) and [[Stark]] (€269M, OWE-V Virtus). A €1B per-supplier cap is imposed absent further parliament approval — so the program is bounded at ~€3B total near-term vs. original framework of up to ~€4.3B cut to €2B combined in February. Three-supplier structure is an explicit hedge: [[Germany]] did not want single-vendor dependency on either an incumbent prime or a startup. Contrast with the US, where [[Anduril]] has a single Army contract up to ~€20B over 10 years — per-supplier ceiling ~20× the German one.
