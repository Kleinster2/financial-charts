- **[[Sulfuric Acid]]** — New comprehensive product note. World's most-produced industrial chemical (~261 Mt, ~$15-17B market, 2024). Supply: ~55-60% elemental sulfur burning (Claus process byproduct), ~30-35% smelter acid (copper/zinc/lead smelting byproduct), remainder pyrites and SAR. ~90% of supply is involuntary byproduct. [[China]] dominates: 120 Mt output (46% global), 141 Mt capacity. Demand: fertilizers ~55%, mining/chemicals ~35%, energy transition <3% but growing. Key nexus: Chinese smelter revenue inverted from TC/RC fees (0-5%) to acid sales (60-70% of revenue). Energy transition thesis: lithium spodumene ~7-8t acid/t LCE, nickel HPAL 250-1,400 kg/t ore, rare earth 3.4-4.0t/t REO. Long-arc: if oil refining declines, sulfur supply tightens while acid demand grows -- inflection from involuntary oversupply to deliberate undersupply.

- **[[Caustic Soda]]** — New comprehensive product note. World's most widely used industrial alkali (~83 Mt, ~$46B, 2024). Co-produced with [[Chlorine]] via chlor-alkali electrolysis — every tonne NaOH yields ~0.89t Cl2. The co-product lock is the thesis: caustic prices move 180° out of phase with chlorine/PVC cycles. Demand: alumina refining (Bayer process) ~20-25%, chemicals ~20%, pulp/paper ~12-15%, water treatment. [[Olin]] permanently shut >1 Mt US capacity since 2021. Energy intensity ~2,200-2,400 kWh/t makes it a proxy for electricity costs. New demand vectors: lithium hydroxide, sodium-ion batteries, hydrogen byproduct.

- **[[Chlorine]]** — New comprehensive product note. Foundational disinfection chemical and chemical building block (~75-80 Mt, ~$35-40B, 2024). Co-produced with [[Caustic Soda]] in fixed 1:1.1 ratio (ECU). ~34% to PVC (construction cycles), ~15-20% water treatment (inelastic — 98% of US utilities use chlorine-based disinfection), ~10-12% isocyanates (polyurethane). [[China]] ~40% of production, uses coal-based calcium carbide VCM route (mercury contamination issue). Semiconductor angle: electronic-grade HCl for wafer cleaning. The co-product trap: in weak PVC markets, chlorine has negative netback — producers pay to dispose of it.

- **[[Ammonia]]** — New comprehensive product note. World's largest-volume synthetic chemical (~150 Mt, $80-160B, 2024). Haber-Bosch process consumes ~1-2% of global energy, ~1.4% of global CO2. Feedstock: ~72% natural gas (SMR), ~22% coal ([[China]]), ~6% other. ~70% to fertilizers ([[urea]] is #1 derivative). Green ammonia thesis: shipping decarbonization (IMO 2050 targets) could add 150-200 Mt demand; NEOM project ($8.4B, 1.2 Mt/year, ~2027) is first commercial scale. Green costs 3-4x grey ($885-1,050/t vs $250-400/t). Highly traded unlike H2SO4. Russia-Ukraine war shut Togliatti-Odessa pipeline, shifted trade flows to US Gulf/Middle East.

- **[[Soda Ash]]** — New comprehensive product note. Essential industrial chemical (~68 Mt, ~$20-21B, 2024). Bifurcated supply: natural (trona mining, Wyoming/Turkey, $50-80/Mt cash cost) vs synthetic (Solvay process, $120-180/Mt). Natural holds 30-50% cost advantage + lower carbon. [[China]] ~53% output (mostly synthetic), US ~18% (all natural), [[Turkey]] ~16% (all natural). ~48% to glass. Energy transition angles: solar PV glass drove 18% China demand growth in 2024; lithium carbonate precipitation needs ~2.5t soda ash/t Li2CO3. Bear case: China expanded 5.5 Mt synthetic in 2024, exports surged 127% YoY. [[WE Soda]] acquired Genesis Alkali for $1.425B (Feb 2025).

- **[[Phosphoric Acid]]** — New comprehensive product note. Second-most-produced industrial acid (~50 Mt P2O5 eq, $40-55B, 2024). Made via wet process: phosphate rock + [[Sulfuric Acid]] (~2.8t H2SO4/t P2O5). [[Morocco]] holds ~70% of phosphate reserves; [[OCP Group]] has ~31% market share ($9.76B revenue 2024). ~85% to fertilizers (DAP/MAP/TSP). LFP battery cathode chemistry is energy transition angle — LFP now 60%+ market share in China. Phosphogypsum byproduct: ~5t per t P2O5, weakly radioactive, 100-280 Mt/year accumulating globally. Peak phosphorus debate: phosphorus is non-substitutable in agriculture and must be mined.

- **[[Hydrofluoric Acid]]** — New comprehensive product note. Gateway chemical for all fluorine chemistry (~2.2 Mt, ~$2.5-3B, 2024). Made from fluorspar (CaF2) + [[Sulfuric Acid]]. [[China]] controls ~60% of fluorspar supply — critical mineral chokepoint. Demand: fluorochemicals/refrigerants ~40-45%, aluminum smelting, petroleum alkylation, semiconductors (electronic-grade HF for wafer etching), uranium processing (UF6). Japan's 2019 HF export restriction to South Korea (forced labor dispute) cut exports 87.9% — dry run for semiconductor supply chain weaponization. [[Stella Chemifa]] and [[Soulbrain]] dominate electronic-grade. Energy transition demand: [[PVDF]] for battery binders, uranium conversion for nuclear renaissance, Kigali Amendment driving HFC→HFO refrigerant transition.

- **[[Elemental Sulfur]]** — New comprehensive product note. World's most-produced industrial commodity that almost nobody intends to make (~85 Mt, ~$12.8-13B, 2024). ~95% involuntary byproduct of oil/gas refining (Claus process). ~90% converted to [[Sulfuric Acid]]. Upstream chokepoint for fertilizers, mining, energy transition chemistry. Long-arc thesis: if oil refining declines, sulfur supply declines — but acid demand (fertilizers, lithium, nickel) grows. UCL study estimated 100-320 Mt annual acid shortfall by 2040. [[China]] 47% sulfur import-dependent. Alberta oil sands produce massive stockpiles (literal yellow sulfur blocks). Historical Frasch mining driven out by 1980s — gap between byproduct and primary-production economics is the structural break point.
