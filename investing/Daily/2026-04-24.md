The six-note set also sharpens the typology the [[Electrolyzers]] note only gestured at. There are now three clearly distinguishable Western strategies in hydrogen hardware:

1. Public pure-play, no parent shield: [[Nel ASA]], [[ITM Power]], [[Plug Power]] — most exposed to the Chinese alkaline cost gap; most stressed financially; survival dependent on IRA 45V persistence and niche-win execution.
2. Conglomerate-embedded, cross-subsidy possible: [[Siemens Energy]] Silyzer — the largest incumbent Western PEM producer, survives because the grid-equipment parent funds the hydrogen unit through the demand gap.
3. Private patient capital + technology differentiation: [[Topsoe]] ([[Temasek]]-backed SOEC), [[Sunfire]] (KfW / Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners-backed dual-track). The model best-positioned to ride through 2026-2028 demand compression and emerge on the other side.

On the Chinese side, the pattern is scale plus verticalization: [[Longi Green Energy]] from solar, [[Sungrow]] from inverters — both applying the same industrial-policy-plus-process-engineering playbook that flattened Western solar and is now doing the same to alkaline electrolyzer pricing.
