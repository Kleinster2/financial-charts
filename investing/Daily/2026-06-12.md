## Synthesis

The Lionsgate note was wrong on its single most important fact — the corporate structure. The vault still described a pre-2025 conglomerate (LGF.A/LGF.B dual class, Starz inside, ~$2B cap), when in reality the studio has been a standalone single-class NYSE company (LION, ~$4.0B cap) since the May 2025 separation. The deeper read is that Lionsgate is now an optionality play. The entire two-step separation — de-SPAC, then Starz split — was engineered to produce a clean, separately valued, acquirable pure-play studio. FY2026 shows why that matters: a $181.8M full-year net loss alongside the best studio quarter ($165.4M OIBDA) in twelve years, all on a ~$1.6B net-debt balance sheet. The investment case is the asymmetry — a ~$1B/yr library annuity plus a single hit (Q4's $190.4M FCF) can flip the cash picture fast, and that same profile makes Lionsgate a standing takeout target (Legendary reportedly circled in Aug 2025). Feltheimer's extension to 2031 is the counterweight signaling near-term independence.
