## Synthesis

The "Roku" prompt surfaced a factual error propagated into two notes: a "28% premium to June 10 close" that the primary press release never states and that the arithmetic contradicts ($160 over Roku's $116.94 June-10 close is +36.8%, not 28%). The 28% maps to a longer-VWAP unaffected reference (~$125), conflated during the original ingestion with June 10 — the date that actually anchors the *Fox* stock-leg VWAP ($66.03), not the Roku premium.

The more important correction is analytical. Both notes framed Roku as having "traded roughly flat into the $160 terms." It hadn't: in a fixed-exchange-ratio cash-and-stock deal (60% cash / 40% FOXA at 0.9693 shares), the value Roku holders receive floats with Fox's share price. Fox fell ~16% on the $8.3B of new debt and 152M-share dilution, which mechanically cut the implied package from $160 to ~$149. Roku at $140.90 sits ~12% below the headline and ~5.5% below the devalued package — a genuine [[Merger Arbitrage|merger-arb]] spread pricing completion risk into a 1H-2027 close, not a stock "trading flat." That distinction is the signal: the spread is the market's probability-weighted discount on regulatory clearance, the floating stock leg, and Fox's financing.
