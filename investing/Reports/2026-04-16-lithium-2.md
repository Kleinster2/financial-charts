# Lithium — four uncoordinated supply regimes and the Brazilian cost undercut

The +16.3% [[Albemarle|ALB]] session (+4.9σ beta-adjusted on 60-day vol) and the parallel +8.8% [[SQM]] move today were read as a deficit-arriving rally. The surface case supports it: Chinese lithium carbonate spot at ~167,500 CNY/T (~$23,000/t) is up 134% year-over-year, the pricing-agency Fastmarkets' 2026 deficit forecast of 1,500t is pricing through, and [[UBS]] raised the [[Albemarle|ALB]] target to $230 on April 9. But the actual driver is not a single demand acceleration — it is four uncoordinated national supply regimes all tightening simultaneously. [[China]] has cancelled 27 lithium-mining permits in Yichun and framed closures as "anti-involution" — Beijing's campaign against overcapacity-driven price collapses in strategic industries. Zimbabwe suspended concentrate exports in April 2026 to force domestic refining. [[CATL]]'s Jiangxi mine went down in August 2025 on permit expiry (~4% of global supply per [[Morgan Stanley]]). And [[Albemarle]]'s Kemerton suspension removed 50,000+ tonnes of its own announced capacity. None of this is coordinated. There is no OPEC of lithium. The Apr 16 move is the market pricing the near-term implication of four producers acting unilaterally with no mechanism to align on price or volume — exactly the dynamic the [[Lithium Triangle]] concept note documents is structurally impossible to negotiate into existence.

The [[Lithium Triangle]] — the resource basin shared by [[Chile]], [[Argentina]], and [[Bolivia]] around the Puna Plateau salt flats — demonstrates the incoherence in concentrated form. [[Bolivia]] holds 21% of global reserves, the largest share of any country, but produced only a few hundred tonnes in 2024; a $1B Chinese-investor deal and a parallel Russian agreement both stalled in July 2025 when Bolivian congress descended into shouting matches over foreign dependency. [[Chile]] now runs a partial-state-control model: the December 2025 [[Codelco]]-[[SQM]] **Nova Andino Litio** JV secures the Salar de Atacama brine operation through 2060 but captures 70% of operating margin for the state through 2030, rising to 85% from 2031. [[Argentina]] went the opposite direction under Milei — installed capacity tripled from 35,500 to 186,000 tonnes per year of lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE, the standard industry unit) through market-open concessions. And then in March 2026, [[José Antonio Kast]] took power in Chile pledging to make lithium "concessionable" — a model that would unwind the state-anchored National Lithium Strategy Boric built in 2023, though Nova Andino's 2060 contract has protection. The regional regime has moved state-led → state-JV → potentially-market-led in under three years. The commodity cycle crashed and rallied on the same timeline. They are connected: resource-nationalism peaks when prices crater and unwinds when the price signal returns.

The Nova Andino Litio JV is analytically underweighted. [[SQM]] accepting a 70% state-margin capture through 2030 and 85% from 2031 is not an ordinary royalty hike — it is a multi-decade rent allocation in which the state monetizes the concession value itself while the private partner keeps operational control through the intermediate phase. The implied haircut explains why [[SQM]] ran +8.8% today against [[Albemarle|ALB]] at +16.3% despite identical commodity exposure — roughly half of ALB's move is the equity value that would accrue to shareholders under an unconstrained concession but accrues to the Chilean state at [[SQM]]. This is the priced-in cost of political legibility: [[SQM]] now has 2060 concession certainty, the kind of decades-visibility [[Albemarle|ALB]]'s Australian and Nevada operations don't carry, but at the price of giving up most of the upside. Kast's "concessionable" policy, if it materializes, would apply to *new* Chilean lithium entrants — creating an asymmetric two-tier regime in which Nova Andino operates under 85%-state-margin while new entrants operate under market terms. That is the structural bull case for capacity coming online outside the Atacama within a Kast term.

Brazil is the answer the market is not yet fully pricing. [[Sigma Lithium]]'s Grota do Cirilo mine in Minas Gerais runs at $440/t CIF China and is ramping from 270 ktpa toward 520 through 2026. [[Atlas Lithium]]'s Neves project has cleared a 145% IRR at $489/t production cost — among the lowest anywhere — and was named in the April 2026 Japan-US Critical Minerals Joint Fact Sheet as the only Brazil-based lithium project eligible for allied-government financing, with [[Mitsui]] already committing $30M against off-take. [[AMG]]'s Mibra mine has been running since 1945 and is building the only scaled European LiOH conversion plant at Bitterfeld-Wolfen, bypassing Chinese refining entirely. Brazil produced 4,900 MT in 2023 and is forecast at 57,500 MT LCE in 2025 — a 10x ramp in five years, at cost structures that undercut most of the incumbent cost curve. And yet [[Brazil]] is still at the concentrate-export stage, missing processing margin — the [Brazil: Mining Sector](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=Analysis%2FMining%20Sector) analysis documents this as a pattern across minerals, not just lithium: high-grade extraction followed by Chinese-routed intermediation. The [Brazil: Critical Minerals](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=Analysis%2FCritical%20Minerals) note names the trap directly — "same commodity trap as other minerals." The [Geopolitics: Critical Minerals](obsidian://open?vault=geopolitics&file=Concepts%2FCritical%20Minerals) analysis layers in the diplomatic sequencing: Roberto Goulart Menezes at the University of Brasília frames the February 2026 [[India]]-Brazil MOU as "a pilot before negotiating with a country with which Brazil has an asymmetrical relationship" — Brasília testing the terms with a peer before sitting down with the US or EU. Brazil has the cost curve to dominate; it does not yet have the diplomatic sequencing — or the downstream processing — to capture the margin those costs deserve.

The synthesis: the Apr 16 rally is four uncoordinated supply regimes converging. The next wave of supply discipline comes from Chile's new political regime (if Kast follows through), the Nova Andino Litio JV ramping at 85%-state-margin from 2031, and the diplomatic window closing on Brazil's peer-pilot framework. The next wave of supply *undiscipline* comes from Argentine capacity expansion, Brazilian cost-curve disruption, and Smackover DLE reaching commercial scale. At $23,000/t spot, both waves are being funded simultaneously. That is what today's tape is pricing — and what the [[Sigma Lithium]], [[Atlas Lithium]], [[AMG]] cost curves make concrete.
