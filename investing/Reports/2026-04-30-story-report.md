The first is the screenless companion device from the io acquisition, associated with designer [[Jony Ive]], acquired for $6.4B in May 2025 and targeted for late 2026 or early 2027. The second is an AI-agent smartphone reported by [[Ming-Chi Kuo]] on Apr 27, 2026, with [[Qualcomm]] and [[MediaTek]] jointly designing the processor, [[Luxshare Precision]] as exclusive co-design and assembly partner, specifications and suppliers to be finalized in late 2026 or early 2027, mass production targeted for 2028, and annual shipments framed at 300-400M units. For reference, global smartphone shipments were about 1.2B in 2025 and iPhone shipments about 230M.

Why it matters: the Apple-Google phone duopoly is the layer above OpenAI's consumer product. If ChatGPT remains an app, operating-system owners can replace, throttle, or tax distribution. The hardware program is OpenAI's answer to that constraint: an agent-first device where the agent is the interface, the app concept weakens, and on-device context gathering plus [[Edge inference]] become part of the product architecture.

The tension: the supplier report is still a supply-chain leak rather than official confirmation, and the 2028 mass-production timeline is distant. The shipment target is enormous, the market reaction in [[Qualcomm]] faded intraday, and the program competes for capital at the same time OpenAI's compute commitments are under scrutiny.

Watch: official supplier confirmation, end-2026 or early-2027 specifications, whether the screenless device actually ships before the smartphone, Qualcomm/MediaTek economics, and any sign that OpenAI builds or licenses an operating system rather than relying on modified Android.
