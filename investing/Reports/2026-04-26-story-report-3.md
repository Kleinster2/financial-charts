The tension: Borna's Heydaripour, Expana's Moss, and the Iran Pistachio Association's Agah all describe the disruption as severe but "manageable for now" — leaving open whether the price tape reverts if a durable ceasefire holds and [[Bandar Abbas]] reopens. The deeper [[Hormuz Permanent Risk Premium]] frame says the war-risk repricing is permanent even after the conflict ends; the pistachio frame says specific harvest-and-route arithmetic still matters and can mean-revert. Both are right at different time horizons. The 2025 below-expectations harvest in all three major producers also means that even with a clean Bandar Abbas reopen, 2026 inventory may not be sufficient to clear the elevated demand cleanly — particularly into the summer ice-cream demand pinch Heydaripour flagged.

Watch: [[Expana]] pistachio benchmark prints into Q2/Q3 2026; Iranian customs export numbers; [[Bandar Abbas]] reopening status (currently still constrained by the partial blockade and mine-clearance); [[Strait of Hormuz]] mine-clearance progress (mines were responsible for ~80% of US warships sunk or disabled since 1945, and clearance estimates run weeks to months); the 2026 [[US]] + [[Turkey]] harvest, which will determine whether non-Iranian supply can close the gap; and whether other low-substitutability Iranian agricultural exports (saffron especially) exhibit the same pattern, which would corroborate the "small-commodity substitution-failure tell" template for tracking the cascade going forward.
