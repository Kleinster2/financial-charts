The story is that [[Electrolyzers]] moved from a concept note into a functioning actor graph for the cleanest hardware case of the [[Second China shock]].

What changed: six dead-link candidates became entity notes: [[Nel ASA]], [[ITM Power]], [[Longi Green Energy]], [[Sungrow]], [[Topsoe]], and [[Sunfire]]. Together they sharpen the typology: exposed Western pure plays, conglomerate-backed Western survivors, private patient-capital SOEC specialists, and Chinese solar/inverter giants applying scale and verticalization to hydrogen equipment.

Intel turns AI capex from GPU scarcity into a systems trade

Touched: [[Intel]], [[AI capex arms race]], [[Semiconductors]], [[AMD]], [[Arm Holdings]], [[AMD securities]]

The story is that Apr. 24 confirmed the Apr. 20 CPU-broadening thesis: AI capex scarcity is expanding from GPUs into CPUs, analog/power, memory, packaging, and manufacturing capacity.

What changed: [[Intel]] beat Q1 revenue consensus with $13.58B versus $12.42B, Data Center and AI revenue of $5.1B versus $4.41B, Q2 revenue guide of $13.8B-$14.8B, and a +23.6% verified Apr. 24 close. [[AMD]] rose +13.9%, [[Arm Holdings]] +14.8%, [[NVIDIA]] +4.3%, SMH +5.1%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index +4.3% to a record close.

Why it matters: inference and [[Agentic AI]] workloads make the control plane valuable, not just the accelerator.

The tension: Intel sold some previously shelved / de-spec inventory, so one quarter does not prove durable supply or margin recovery.

Watch: Q2 follow-through, server CPU supply, and whether Intel's foundry optionality becomes signed volume.
