# Memory Taxonomy Card - two sleeves, one bottleneck

The clean formulation is that [[Memory]] is a parent industry, not a single tradeable basket. The market data supports two investable sleeves under it: [[US Memory]] and [[Korea Memory]]. Both are real, but they answer different questions. [[US Memory]] is the memory-cycle expression inside US semiconductor beta. [[Korea Memory]] is the tighter HBM-leadership pair. Treating them as one "global memory" basket erases the most useful signal in the cluster work.

[[US Memory]] passes the statistical reality test but not the clean-independence test. [[Micron]], [[SanDisk]], and [[Western Digital]] have 0.696 intra-correlation, 79.72% PC1 explained variance, and random-basket p < 0.001, so the sleeve is not arbitrary. The caveat is topology: at the loose dendrogram threshold, the cohort pulls in broad semis / ETF beta. That makes the sleeve useful for high-beta US memory-cycle exposure, especially around [[Micron]], NAND tightness, and [[CHIPS Act]] support, but it is not a pure standalone memory factor.

[[Korea Memory]] is the cleaner micro-cluster. [[Samsung]] and [[SK Hynix]] have 0.756 pair correlation, 87.82% PC1 explained variance, random-basket p = 0.006, and threshold-stable width 0.45. At N=2, the math is mechanically simpler than a full cohort, but the boundary result is stronger: the pair is weakly tied to [[US Memory]], [[EWY]], [[SOXX]], [[SMH]], and [[SPY]]. The investment meaning is not just "Korea has memory companies." It is that HBM leadership has become its own tradable micro-structure, with [[SK Hynix]] as the execution leader and [[Samsung]] as the scale/catch-up leg.

[[HBM]] is the bridge that makes the split matter. HBM is not just a better DRAM product; it is the bandwidth constraint inside AI accelerators, and the supply chain is concentrated in the Korea sleeve. That is why [[Memory shortage 2025-2026]] should read as more than a generic commodity shortage. AI demand reallocates wafer starts and packaging capacity toward HBM, which tightens commodity DRAM/NAND in parallel, while [[Export controls]] and China domestic memory challengers add the policy layer. The shortage note is the cycle mechanism; HBM is the causal bottleneck.

The upstream semi layer tells us how the shock transmits. [[Semiconductors]] remains the parent industry hub, while [[PHLX Semiconductor]] / SOXX / SMH are the headline proxy tape. [[Hyperscaler capex]] sets the demand envelope, and [[AI infrastructure financing]] describes the funding window that can reprice when rates or credit spreads move. That explains the Jun. 5 selloff framing: US memory looked ugly because it is high-beta semi exposure with memory-cycle torque; Korea memory transmitted the same AI/HBM stress but remains statistically distinct.

The reusable card: memory is a parent industry with two sleeves, one HBM bottleneck, and separate policy/financing transmission channels. Use [[US Memory]] for US memory-cycle and CHIPS-supported semi beta. Use [[Korea Memory]] for cleaner HBM leadership. Use [[HBM]] and [[Memory shortage 2025-2026]] to explain why the sleeves are connected by fundamentals but not interchangeable in the tape.
