# Lithium — the 2028 deficit forecast depends on which technology curve moves first

The tape says deficit. [[Albemarle|ALB]] ran +16.3% today on +4.9σ beta-adjusted, [[SQM]] ran +8.8% on +2.6σ, and Chinese lithium carbonate spot at ~167,500 CNY/T (~$23,000/t) is up 134% year-over-year — the highest print since mid-2023. Fastmarkets (the industry pricing agency) pegs the 2026 balance at a 1,500t deficit. [[UBS]] raised the [[Albemarle|ALB]] target to $230 on April 9. But the structural deficit story is not a simple supply-shortage narrative — it is a three-way race between supply response, demand acceleration, and demand substitution, and only the first two are being priced. [[Wood Mackenzie]]'s March 2026 Lithium Energy Transition Outlook pushes the deficit to 2028; the IEA's 2025 Global Critical Minerals Outlook sees near-term markets well-supplied with deficits in the 2030s. The agencies agree on direction, disagree on timing by 2-5 years — the rally is priced against the early-arrival scenario, which is not the consensus.

The supply-tightening is real and unilateral: China's "anti-involution" permit cancellations in Yichun, Zimbabwe suspending concentrate exports, [[CATL]]'s Jiangxi mine expiry (~4% of global supply per [[Morgan Stanley]]), and [[Albemarle]]'s own Kemerton suspension. There is no OPEC of lithium — four producers act unilaterally, exactly the coordination failure [[Lithium Triangle]] documents as structurally impossible. And the supply *response* is also scaling: Brazilian producers ([[Sigma Lithium]] at $440/t CIF China ramping to 520 ktpa, [[Atlas Lithium]]'s Neves at 145% IRR / $489/t production cost, [[AMG]]'s integrated Mibra-Bitterfeld route), [[Lithium Americas]]' Thacker Pass Nevada with $2.23B DOE loan and 40 ktpa Phase 1 in late 2027, [[Lithium Argentina]]'s Caucharí-Olaroz at sub-$6,000/t ramping toward a 150 ktpa Pastos Grandes joint venture with [[Ganfeng Lithium]], and 36 DLE projects globally including [[Rio Tinto]]'s $2.5B Rincon and [[Standard Lithium]]'s Smackover operation. Wood Mackenzie puts the capex required at $104-276B through 2050 across scenarios — capital is arriving whether the deficit is 2026 or 2028.

The piece the rally is not pricing is demand substitution accelerating on the same timeline. [[CATL]]'s Naxtra sodium-ion brand launched April 2025; by December 2025 CATL committed to large-scale 2026 deployment across passenger vehicles, commercial vehicles, battery swapping, and grid storage, with Naxtra having passed China's new GB 38031-2025 EV traction-battery standard (effective mid-2026) at 175 Wh/kg energy density supporting 500km range. CATL's "dual-star" strategy runs sodium-ion and lithium-ion in parallel — not a replacement thesis but a parallel-platform thesis that takes meaningful share of the lower-energy-density EV segment and most of grid storage. The Fulin sodium-ion battery energy-storage power station is already operational at commercial scale. This is not 2030 hypothetical technology — it is 2026 commercial reality competing for exactly the demand growth the [[Wood Mackenzie]] deficit forecast assumes lithium captures. Parallel: battery recycling is now modeled to supply ~20% of lithium demand by 2030 (Tesla reports 92% critical-mineral recovery from 4680 cells; [[Li-Cycle]] commissioned a 35 ktpa hub; CATL's Brunp facility at Foshan expanded to 120 ktpa). Secondary supply is secondary supply — every tonne recycled does not need to be mined. Solid-state batteries cut the other way: Toyota's 2027-2028 mass-production timeline uses lithium sulfide (Idemitsu Kosan building a dedicated 1,000 tpa facility for Toyota), which increases lithium-per-vehicle demand, but consensus sees high-volume solid-state commercialization in the early 2030s rather than 2027.

The political-regime layer that v2 and v3 emphasized — [[Codelco]]-[[SQM]] Nova Andino Litio at 70%/85% state capture, [[José Antonio Kast]]'s "concessionable" framework coming in, Milei's open RIGI regime in [[Argentina]], [Brazil: Mining Sector](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=Analysis%2FMining%20Sector)'s concentrate-trap pattern, [Geopolitics: Critical Minerals](obsidian://open?vault=geopolitics&file=Concepts%2FCritical%20Minerals)' diplomatic-sequencing frame with the February 2026 [[India]]-Brazil MOU — is real and underweighted, but it operates entirely on the supply-side. A first-order [[IEA]] observation the supply-side story misses: 2024 lithium demand grew ~30% versus the ~10% CAGR baseline through the 2010s. That is a three-fold acceleration of the demand curve, which is what makes the 2026-vs-2028-vs-2030s deficit debate live. If sodium-ion and recycling together cover the lower end of that demand growth, and hard-rock Brazil plus DLE plus brine expansion cover the upper end of the supply response, the 2028 deficit forecast becomes optional. If the demand acceleration is sticky and sodium-ion ramps slower than CATL's roadmap, Fastmarkets' 2026 deficit prices through.

One line: the rally is real, the supply response is real, the demand substitution is real — the 2028 deficit forecast assumes only one of them moves, and all three are now in motion.
