When an asset class falls 8 to 10% twice in three weeks on different news, the news is not the cause; the positioning is. The beta stack the event note taxonomizes for Jun 5 repeats almost exactly on Jun 23, which is the constraint that makes the read durable. The one structural difference matters: Jun 5's trigger was exogenous (macro data), while Jun 23's was endogenous (a sector bellwether's own demand commentary, on an earnings eve). That is the more concerning of the two, because it means the doubt is now coming from inside the sector, not only from the discount rate.

The clean disconfirmation would be a defensives-down session — if the next leg sees staples and healthcare sell alongside semis, the story flips from rotation within a risk-on market to generalized de-risking.
