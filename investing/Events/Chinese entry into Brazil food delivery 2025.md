#event #brazil #china #tech #delivery

**Chinese entry into Brazil food delivery 2025** — [[Meituan]]'s [[Keeta]] and [[Didi]]'s [[99]] launched food delivery services in [[Brazil]] in 2025, challenging [[iFood]]'s ~80% market share in a market worth R$100B in annual orders. The entry triggered corporate espionage allegations, police investigations, and billions of reais in investment commitments on both sides.

---

## Synthesis

The real story isn't delivery apps — it's the Chinese tech internationalization playbook running into a fortified local incumbent. [[iFood]] controls ~80% of a R$100B market and has survived previous challengers ([[Uber Eats]] withdrew 2022, [[99]] originally exited 2023). The Chinese entrants are betting that subsidy-fueled blitzscaling — a combined R$7.6B pledge — can crack what two prior attempts couldn't. The espionage allegations add a layer of geopolitical friction: [[Lula]]'s government initially celebrated the Chinese investment, but corporate espionage via foreign consultancies targeting 500 iFood employees, police raids on ex-staff who moved to 99, and sealed court documents describing data theft create an awkward dynamic for a government courting Beijing. [[Prosus]] (iFood's parent) has the most at stake — iFood is its crown jewel in LatAm, and its ability to defend market share against deep-pocketed Chinese competitors will test whether local network effects hold against subsidy warfare. For [[Meituan]], this is a test of whether Keeta can be the international growth leg that China's saturating domestic market demands, especially as [[Alibaba]]'s Ele.me squeezes margins at home.

## Corporate espionage allegations

### iFood's claims

[[iFood]] CEO Diego Barreto told FT (Mar 15, 2026) he identified a "co-ordinated effort" to steal business secrets. Key allegations:

- ~500 approaches to [[iFood]] employees identified via [[LinkedIn]]
- Overseas consultancies using "suspicious and aggressive tactics"
- Employees offered hundreds of dollars for interviews
- Questions focused on profit margins, revenues, new business areas, technology, pricing strategies
- Activity began around the time Chinese contenders announced Brazil entry

Barreto (Mar 2026): *"I see attempted sabotage by someone. I can't tell you who that someone is, but I can say that foreign consulting firms are acting as intermediaries."*

Barreto (Mar 2026): *"This is not a common market practice. It is clearly an attempt to extract sensitive data and information from iFood, such as our pricing strategies. This is corporate espionage — theft of intellectual property developed here at iFood, in Brazil."*

### Police investigations

At least 4 former [[iFood]] staff investigated by police for suspected data theft / unauthorized sharing. Details from sealed court documents seen by FT:

| Case | Detail | Status |
|------|--------|--------|
| Ex-employee 1 | Downloaded data on 4,900 restaurants, sent to private email | Warrant executed Oct 2025 |
| Ex-employee 2 | Contacted by consultancy in China, did video interview, received ~$1,400 payment. Believed unidentified participant was a [[Meituan]] executive. | Warrant executed Oct 2025 |
| Ex-employee 3 | One of three investigated who went to work for [[99]] | Investigation ongoing |
| Case 4 | — | Dropped |

3 of the 4 investigated ex-employees went to work for [[99]].

Judge-ordered search-and-seizure warrants executed October 2025 at addresses linked to two of the people. Officers took electronic devices; forensic examinations under way.

### Keeta's counter-claims

[[Keeta]] CEO Tony Qiu told FT the company did not carry out the practices at the heart of [[iFood]]'s complaints.

Qiu (Mar 2026): *"We hold a very high standard for ethical and legal behaviours, so we are not engaging in this type of activities that they claim. And in fact, so far we have not received any accusations or lawsuit from any company."*

Qiu claimed [[Keeta]] itself may have been targeted: the day after its Santos launch (Oct 2025), 8-10 people visited restaurants "pretending to be Keeta employees," asking owners to show Keeta systems, taking photos/videos, and shutting down some accounts. Police investigating.

Some [[Keeta]] workers also approached by consultancies seeking views on the industry — but company said no employees accepted.

### Meituan's response

[[Meituan]] said it had engaged the consultancy in question "for several years on sector studies, a common practice that follows all market standards and complies with legal requirements." Both [[Keeta]] and [[Meituan]] stated "their full commitment to best market practices" and willingness to co-operate with authorities.

### 99's response

[[99]] said it "did not tolerate or endorse any form of misconduct involving the use of external data obtained through illegal means." Referenced Code of Conduct, training, monitoring, and accountability policies.

## Political context

The Lula government trumpeted the [[Keeta]] and [[99]] investments as part of Brazil's closer relationship with [[China]]. The R$7.6B combined pledge came as Lula has been courting Chinese investment across sectors. However, the corporate espionage allegations create friction — [[iFood]] framing the attacks as foreign entities stealing Brazilian IP adds a nationalist dimension to what was presented as a win-win.

## Structural dynamics

Why [[Brazil]] is attractive: 200M+ highly online population, mass adoption of digital payments ([[Pix]]), armies of motorcycle messengers, largest consumer market in Latin America.

Why challengers fail: [[iFood]]'s ~80% market share creates a three-sided network effect — more restaurants attract more consumers, which attracts more couriers, which attracts more restaurants. [[Uber Eats]] and [[99]] (first time) both failed to crack it. The question is whether [[China|Chinese]]-backed capital (R$7.6B) can subsidize long enough to overcome the incumbency advantage.

Restaurant and courier discontent: Morgado noted some restaurants and motorcyclists welcomed competition, unhappy with [[iFood]]'s near-monopoly position. This is the wedge the Chinese entrants are exploiting.
