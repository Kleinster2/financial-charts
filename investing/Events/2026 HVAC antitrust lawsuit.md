# 2026 HVAC antitrust lawsuit

Class-action antitrust lawsuit filed March 20, 2026 alleging a cartel of HVAC manufacturers controlling over 90% of the US market conspired to inflate prices beginning January 2020, using COVID disruptions and new environmental regulations as cover. Market reaction concentrated March 26 when analyst/media pickup hit. *Berg v. Robert Bosch LLC et al.*, U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Michigan (Case No. 26-cv-10949-SKD).

## Key allegations

The complaint alleges HVAC equipment prices rose 68% above the 2019 baseline by 2025, while raw material costs remained near baseline — a gap the plaintiff argues is inexplicable absent coordination. Specific mechanisms cited:

- Executives allegedly used investor calls and industry conferences to broadcast price-increase intentions as coordination signals
- The Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute ([[AHRI]]) identified as the hub for sharing sensitive competitive data
- ACHR News alleged to have served as a platform for synchronized price announcements
- Manufacturers allegedly reduced factory production intentionally to keep inventory low and prevent natural price declines
- A [[Lennox International]] executive quoted in the complaint: the industry "realized" it could coordinate pricing

## Amplifying factors (March 24-26)

The antitrust filing on March 20 was not the sole driver. Two additional catalysts compressed into the same week:

### Watsco demand signal (March 24)

[[Watsco]] (WSO), the dominant HVAC distributor, slid 5.4% after management disclosed January-February 2026 sales were down ~5% YoY. This was the first hard demand datapoint for 2026 and signaled soft residential HVAC conditions heading into the spring selling season. Primed the sector for further selling.

### USMCA tariff overhang

~40% of commercial HVAC systems sold by [[Trane Technologies]] and [[Carrier Global]] are manufactured in Mexico under [[USMCA]] tariff protection. The ongoing USMCA review threatens to eliminate those protections, forcing either production relocation or consumer price increases. Construction input prices (steel, aluminum, copper) already up 3.4% YoY.

## Implications

The pricing power that made HVAC/DC cooling names attractive as AI infrastructure plays is exactly what the lawsuit targets. If the cartel allegations prove out, the margin expansion from 2020-2025 was partly artificial — and vulnerable to treble damages under antitrust law. Even if the case ultimately fails, discovery will expose internal pricing communications, creating headline risk through 2026-2027.

The timing is notable: these companies had already been de-rated from CES 2026 highs after [[Jensen Huang]]'s Rubin "chiller-free" comment, and now face a second overhang from the same product category. The stacking of technology risk (chiller-free chips) + legal risk (price-fixing) + demand softness ([[Watsco]] signal) creates a triple headwind that didn't exist three months ago.

[[Trane Technologies|TT]]-specific amplifiers: management had already guided for ~20% Q1 2026 revenue decline from channel inventory normalization + residential weakness, with flat adjusted operating margins (missing consensus). CFO and Chief Accounting Officer both executed share sales in February-March. ~40% of commercial HVAC manufactured in Mexico under [[USMCA]] tariff protection — review threatens those economics.
