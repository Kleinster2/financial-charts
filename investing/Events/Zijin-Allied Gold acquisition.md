# Zijin-Allied Gold acquisition

China's [[Zijin Mining]] acquires Canadian miner [[Allied Gold]] for C$5.5B (~$4B) — first major China cross-border mining deal since Canada-China diplomatic thaw.

---

## What happened

**January 26, 2026:** Zijin Gold International (HKG: 2259), a Hong Kong-listed subsidiary of Zijin Mining, announced a definitive agreement to acquire Allied Gold Corp (TSX/NYSE: AAUC) in an all-cash transaction.

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Buyer | [[Zijin Mining]] (via Zijin Gold International) |
| [[Target]] | [[Allied Gold]] |
| Deal value | **C$5.5B (~US$4B)** |
| Price/share | C$44 (5.4% premium to Jan 24 close) |
| Payment | All cash |
| Break fee | C$220M |
| Expected close | Late April 2026 |
| Approvals needed | Shareholder, court, Canada/China regulatory |

**Market reaction:**
- Zijin Gold (HKG): +11.6% to HK$233
- Zijin Mining (HKG): +2.9% to HK$43.40
- Zijin Mining (SHA): +2.8% to ¥40.61
- Allied Gold (pre-market): +4%

## Why it matters

### 1. Zijin's largest acquisition ever

The C$5.5B deal surpasses Zijin's previous largest — the C$1.3B purchase of Continental Gold (Buriticá) in 2019. Signals aggressive expansion despite US sanctions pressure.

### 2. Canada-China thaw

First major cross-border deal since PM Mark Carney's visit to China (January 2026) — the first Canadian PM visit since 2017. Carney met Xi Jinping and signaled desire to stabilize relations.

**Context:** Canada and China reached preliminary agreements on:
- EV tariff reductions
- Canola trade barriers
- Strategic cooperation framework

Carney explicitly said Canada seeks reduced US economic dependence — China deal flow is part of that pivot.

### 3. Record gold prices

Gold crossed **$5,100/oz** for the first time the same day (Jan 26). Record prices are:
- Boosting miner margins and cash flows
- Fueling M&A as majors chase reserves
- Making African assets more attractive

### 4. [[Africa]] gold expansion

Allied's portfolio gives Zijin:

| Asset | Country | Status |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Bonikro | Côte d'Ivoire | Producing |
| Agbaou | Côte d'Ivoire | Producing |
| Sadiola | Mali | Producing |
| Kurmuk | Ethiopia | Development |

Zijin already has African exposure via Akyem (Ghana, acquired 2024 from [[Newmont]]) — Allied adds West African scale.

### 5. Chinese mining consolidation

Zijin is building a global gold/copper empire:
- 2024: Akyem (Ghana) from Newmont
- 2026: Allied Gold ([[Africa]] ops)
- Plus: Kamoa-Kakula (copper), Serbia, Colombia, etc.

State backing + record commodity prices = aggressive deal flow.

## Strategic implications

**For Zijin:**
- Significantly expands gold reserves and production
- Diversifies beyond China amid domestic depletion concerns
- [[Africa]] exposure balances US/Western-sanctioned operations

**For Canada:**
- Signals openness to Chinese resource investment post-thaw
- May face domestic political criticism (China security concerns)
- Tests new Carney government's China policy

**For gold sector:**
- Validates African gold assets at premium valuations
- May trigger more M&A from Chinese majors
- Western miners may face competition for African targets
