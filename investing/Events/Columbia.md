**Columbia** — February 1, 2003. Space Shuttle Columbia broke apart on reentry over Texas, killing all 7 crew. [[Type A mishap]]. Caused by foam strike during launch that damaged thermal protection on the leading edge of the left wing. STS retired permanently in 2011 in part due to the post-Columbia safety review.

## Why Columbia matters

One of three [[Type A mishap]] precedents (alongside [[Apollo 1]] and [[Challenger]]) that frame NASA's most severe accident classification. Cited by [[Jared Isaacman]] in his Feb 2026 reclassification of [[Boeing Starliner]] CFT as a Type A mishap — placing Starliner in the same regulatory tier as Columbia.

The post-Columbia retirement of STS was the catalyst for [[NASA]]'s Commercial Resupply Services (CRS, 2008) and Commercial Crew Program (2014) — the framework that produced [[SpaceX]] [[Crew Dragon]] and [[Boeing Starliner]] as the post-Shuttle crew vehicles.
