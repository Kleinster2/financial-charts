# Brazil Week NYC 2026

**Brazil Week NYC 2026** — annual multi-producer business and finance week, May 11-14, 2026, anchored by the 25th [[FT Brazil Summit]], the 15th [[LIDE]] Brazil Investment Forum, the 7th [[Money Report]] Brazilian Week, and the [[BrazilCham]] Person of the Year Awards Gala. Honorees this year: José Auriemo Neto (chairman, [[JHSF Participações]]) as Brazilian Person of the Year and [[Cristiano Amon]] (president and CEO, [[Qualcomm]]) as American Person of the Year. Coincided with the May 13 [[Brazil]] ADR cluster sell-off (-3 to -5σ across PAGS, XP, BSBR, CSN, PBR).

*Sources: BrazilCham 2026 events page; Money Report programming for Brazilian Week 2026 NY; LIDE NY agenda (15th Brazil Investment Forum); Financial Times Brazil Summit 2026 announcement; Portal IN coverage of attendees.*

---

## Synthesis (last verified 2026-05-16)

The central insight: New York's Brazilian business calendar staged its most prestigious convening week — the BrazilCham gala at the [American Museum of Natural History](obsidian://open?vault=nyc&file=American%20Museum%20of%20Natural%20History), the FT Brazil Summit at Convene One Liberty, the LIDE forum at the Harvard Club — at exactly the moment the Brazilian equity complex and political risk priced new information *(see Schedule + #Market backdrop)*. The constraint: the same May 13 day that delivered the Person of the Year gala also delivered (i) the largest single-day coordinated Brazil ADR cluster sell-off of the year so far, driven by April [[IPCA]] at 4.39% YoY brushing the upper tolerance band and [[Petrobras]]' May 11 Q1 miss *(see [[Brazil#Macro (May 2026)|Brazil macro table]]; #Market backdrop)*, and (ii) The Intercept Brasil's WhatsApp-audio scoop on Flávio Bolsonaro's R$134M ask from [[Daniel Vorcaro|Vorcaro]] for the *Dark Horse* biopic — the same day BrazilCham was awarding its Person of the Year prize uptown *(per The Intercept Brasil, May 13; see #Market backdrop)*. The one-line: the event functioned as a dealmakers' window in which Brazilian political risk priced *into* US institutional capital's afternoon rather than abstractly six months out *(inference)*.

## Person of the Year gala (May 13)

The BrazilCham flagship recognizes one Brazilian and one American executive each year. The 2026 selection lines up against the chamber's structural framing of the Brazil-US axis:

| Award | Recipient | Affiliation | Why selected |
|-------|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Brazilian Person of the Year | José Auriemo Neto | Chairman, [[JHSF Participações]] | Brazilian luxury real estate, hospitality, and aviation; expanding the JHSF Capital arm into US asset management |
| American Person of the Year | [[Cristiano Amon]] | President and CEO, [[Qualcomm]] | Brazilian-born (Campinas, UNICAMP electrical engineering); led Qualcomm 5G rollout and diversification beyond mobile into auto, PC, edge AI |
| Social Responsibility — Community Impact in Healthcare | Américas Amigas | — | Breast cancer screening access |
| Social Responsibility — Women's Leadership Development | Rede Mulher Empreendedora (RME) | — | Women entrepreneur support |
| Social Responsibility — Leadership Development | Civics Educação | — | Civic education programs |

The Amon selection threads the needle: he is the only Brazilian-born CEO running an S&P 500 semiconductor company, and his presence at the AMNH gala signals the chamber's bet that the Brazil-US technology corridor — historically thin — has a viable connector in [[Qualcomm]]'s automotive, PC, and 5G ambitions. Auriemo Neto's recognition is the reverse direction: a Brazilian operator building out into US markets via JHSF Capital.

## Market backdrop — the May 13 coincidence

The week's market context is the durable analytical hook. May 13 was simultaneously the gala day and the day the Brazilian equity complex sold off on the cumulative weight of three repricings landing in the same week (see [[Brazil#Macro (May 2026)|Brazil macro table]]):

| Date | Event | Channel |
|------|-------|---------|
| Apr 29 | COPOM cuts 25bp to 14.50% with hawkish framing (inflation "moving further away from target") | [[Selic]] |
| May 9 | April [[IPCA]] accelerates to 4.39% YoY (vs 3.81% Mar); food, housing, transport all up | Inflation |
| May 11 | [[Petrobras]] Q1 2026 NI R$32.66B (-7.2% YoY) on production +16% — realized prices and refining margins compressed | Earnings |
| May 13 | Ibovespa -1.8%, [[EWZ]] -3.8%, [[PagSeguro\|PAGS]] -5.4σ, [[XP Inc\|XP]] -3.3σ, [[Santander Brasil\|BSBR]] -3.2σ, [[CSN]] (SID) -3.1σ — same trading session as the AMNH gala | Equity beta |

The optical contrast — Brazilian executives celebrating partnerships and award speeches at the AMNH while ADRs sold off two miles south on the NYSE floor — is the year's clearest single-day demonstration of how compressed the markets-versus-soft-power timeline has become for Brazil. The political dimension landed the same day: on May 13 The Intercept Brasil published WhatsApp audio of Flávio Bolsonaro negotiating R$134M from jailed [[Banco Master]] owner [[Daniel Vorcaro]] (recorded September 8, 2025, a day before Vorcaro's arrest) for *Dark Horse*, the Bolsonaro biopic. International coverage followed (UPI, ABC News); opposition filed a criminal representation. The scandal landed *while* Brazilian-American business leaders were assembled at the AMNH gala, gave the BrazilCham event audience a real-time political-risk demonstration on the same trading session as the ADR sell-off, and is one of the candidate explanations for the subsequent Polymarket reprice (Flávio 40 → 28 between Apr 16 and May 15). The structural reading: the [[2026 Brazil presidential election]] is six months out, the political volatility window is already open, and US capital can no longer treat the Brazilian business calendar as separable from the political risk cycle. The week was the last reasonably calm setting before the 60-90 day pre-vote BRL vol regime that has produced 8-15% realized volatility around every election since 2002 (per [[Brazil]]).
