# 2026 Two Sessions

Annual meetings of [[China]]'s National People's Congress (NPC) and Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), March 2026. The 2026 session is pivotal: it previews the **15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030)** and sets the tone for China's industrial policy in a period of escalating US tech sanctions and geopolitical tension.

---

## Synthesis

The 2026 Two Sessions is framing China's next five years around a single thesis: **every export control becomes a mandate for domestic replacement**. The language from CPPCC delegates is remarkably consistent — "original innovation," "independent innovation," "bottlenecks into springboards." This isn't new rhetoric, but the 15th Five-Year Plan puts it into a structured framework with "extraordinary measures" specifically for integrated circuits.

The investment signal: China's tech self-sufficiency push is accelerating, not plateauing. [[CATL]] spending $2.9B/year on R&D with 21,000 researchers. Pan Jianwei's quantum team built domestic dilution refrigerators after the US export ban. [[Phytium]] calling for "full-chain systematic approach" to chips — meaning vertical integration across the entire semiconductor stack. For investors, this means: (1) Chinese tech champions with R&D scale will widen their moats, (2) Western equipment/component makers face growing domestic Chinese alternatives, (3) the decoupling timeline is compressing.

### The dilution refrigerator precedent

Pan Jianwei's quantum team's response to the US dilution refrigerator ban is the clearest example of the "bottleneck → springboard" dynamic:

1. US bans export of dilution refrigerators (critical cooling equipment for quantum processors)
2. Pan's team reverse-engineers and builds domestic versions at international standards
3. Domestic units now power the Zuchongzhi series — China's leading quantum processors
4. China no longer dependent on Western supply for this critical component

**Pattern recognition:** This is the same playbook that produced [[Huawei]]'s Kirin 9020 after the chip ban, [[SMIC]]'s 7nm process, and domestic lithography equipment. Each sanction creates a protected market for domestic alternatives with state backing.

## Investment implications

| Theme | Winners | Losers |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **China tech self-sufficiency** | CATL, [[SMIC]], [[Huawei]], [[Phytium]], domestic equipment makers | [[ASML]] (long-term demand erosion in China), [[Applied Materials]], [[Lam Research]] |
| **Quantum** | Chinese quantum labs, domestic cryogenics | Western quantum equipment exporters |
| **Batteries/EV** | CATL, [[BYD]], supply chain | Western battery startups competing against Chinese scale |
| **Semiconductors** | Chinese foundries, EDA, equipment | Equipment makers losing China revenue |

**Key risk for Western companies:** China's domestic alternatives may be inferior initially, but state-backed demand guarantees a market. Over 5-10 years, quality converges while cost advantages persist. The 15th FYP is the framework for this convergence.
