**NSPM-11** (National Security Presidential Memorandum 11), "AI in the National Security Enterprise," is a [[Donald Trump|Trump]] presidential memorandum signed June 5, 2026 that hard-wires the administration's "all lawful use" stance toward military AI into national-security policy — and gives agencies a contract-termination trigger against AI vendors that restrict how the government uses their models. It is the codification of the [[Pentagon AI access dispute 2026|Pentagon's fight with Anthropic]].

It is a presidential memorandum, not an executive order, and sits alongside a sibling cyber memorandum (NSPM-12) issued the same week.

## What it does

- Directs the Secretary of War ([[Pete Hegseth|Hegseth]]), the Director of National Intelligence, and other agency heads to accelerate AI adoption across intelligence and warfighting, streamline procurement, and revoke Biden-era guardrails.
- Contract-termination clause: agencies must pursue "termination for default or for convenience" of contracts with AI companies that have "repeatedly demonstrated a pattern of conduct" inconsistent with the memo — vendors that restrict government use of their models. It does not name [[Anthropic]], but is universally read as aimed at it.
- Waiver: pairs the termination order with a "strictly limited waiver" to keep a contract where "necessary to responsibly steward United States national security" — reportedly the mechanism keeping [[Claude]] at the [[NSA]].
- Orders the Secretary of War to revise DoDD 3000.09 (Autonomy in Weapons Systems) within 90 days — the directive at the center of Anthropic's autonomous-weapons red line.

Signing-statement framing: "The United States can and will responsibly accelerate the use of AI across intelligence and warfighting domains in line with American values."

## Why it matters

NSPM-11 converts an ad hoc procurement fight into standing executive policy: "all lawful use" is now a written instruction with a termination trigger, not a one-off Pentagon position. It is one of three distinct 2026 instruments pointed at [[Anthropic]] — alongside a separate executive order requiring labs to hand models to the government 30 days before release (see [[AI regulation]] and [[CAISI]]), and the [[Export controls#First deployed-model export control — Anthropic Fable 5 / Mythos 5 (Jun 12, 2026)|June 12 Commerce export ban]] on [[Claude Fable 5]] / [[Claude Mythos|Mythos 5]]. None of the three is a negotiated agreement; the government acts unilaterally, and Anthropic responds through litigation and negotiation. [[CAISI]] is the cooperative counterpart — the evaluation lane that does run on a mutual MOU.
