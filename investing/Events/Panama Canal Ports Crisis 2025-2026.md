#event #geopolitics #infrastructure #shipping

US-China proxy battle over two strategic ports at either end of the [[Panama Canal]] — Balboa (Pacific) and Cristóbal (Atlantic) — operated by [[CK Hutchison]]'s subsidiary Panama Ports Company (PPC) since 1997. What started as Trump rhetoric about China "running the canal" escalated into a $23B asset sale, Beijing intervention, a Supreme Court ruling, and a legal standoff involving [[Maersk]], [[BlackRock]], and [[MSC]].

## The ports

| Port | Side | Operator (since 1997) |
|------|------|----------------------|
| Balboa | Pacific | Panama Ports Co. ([[CK Hutchison]]) |
| Cristóbal | Atlantic | Panama Ports Co. ([[CK Hutchison]]) |

- Original concession: 1997 (25 years, awarded to Hutchison Whampoa)
- 2005 addendum: PPC committed $1B+ additional investment, paid $102M for inherited infrastructure
- 2021 renewal: Extended to 2047
- PPC is the largest port investor in [[Panama]] — 5,000 direct jobs, ~25,000 indirect

## Why it matters

**Strategic chokepoint:** The Panama Canal handles ~6% of global trade. Controlling ports at both ends gives operational leverage over transit logistics, scheduling, and intelligence.

**Precedent risk:** If the US successfully forces Chinese-linked operators out of Panama, it creates a template for similar actions at other ports globally. Beijing is fighting hardest to prevent this — particularly to protect [[COSCO]]'s Chancay port in Peru.

**Cybersecurity dimension:** ZPMC cranes at both ports (6 at Balboa, 7 at Cristóbal, acquired 2023) create potential backdoor access. US House committees flagged these as national security vulnerabilities. Even under new ownership, the physical/digital infrastructure remains Chinese-made.

**CKH trapped:** Cannot sell (Beijing blocks), cannot keep (Panama revokes). The $22.8B BlackRock deal valued Hutchison Ports well above market (~$13B est. prior valuation) — a financial win that may never close.

**Rhodium Group analysis:** US retains treaty leverage with Panama on national security grounds. Beijing has claimed a partial victory by derailing the outright US acquisition. Both sides have incentives to escalate.
