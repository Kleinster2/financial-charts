# Richard Caring hospitality sale April 2026

**April 11-12, 2026** — [[Diafa]], an Abu Dhabi luxury hospitality platform affiliated with [[IHC]], completed a majority investment in the hospitality portfolio built by [[Richard Caring]]. The deal shifts control of a cluster of London restaurants and private members' clubs into the [[Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan]] capital network while leaving Caring in place as executive chairman.

## Synthesis

The important change is not simply that a British hospitality entrepreneur sold a stake. It is that a locally assembled portfolio of high-status London venues now has a capital sponsor whose strategy is platform-building, not just ownership. That shifts the frame from trophy-asset monetization to global brand export.

For the vault, the event matters because it extends the existing [[UAE]] capital story into luxury hospitality. The same Abu Dhabi network already tracked through [[Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan]] and [[IHC]] is now using a dedicated vehicle, [[Diafa]], to aggregate experience-led consumer brands that can travel across cities and geographies. The transaction is a clean example of Gulf capital moving from passive trophy ownership toward operating-platform ambition.

## What changed

Company statements described the deal as a majority investment and emphasized continuity: [[Richard Caring]] stays executive chairman while [[Diafa]] supplies the scale capital. Outside reporting valued the broader transaction at about £1.4bn and framed it as the culmination of a sale process that had been under discussion since 2025.

The strategic point is the combination of asset quality and exportability. The portfolio already had brand density in London's high-end dining and club scene; under [[Diafa]], the pitch becomes wider international rollout rather than a collection of venue-level cash flows.
