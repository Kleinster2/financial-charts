# US-Japan Investment Framework

$550B Japanese investment commitment in the US, exchanged for tariff relief under [[Donald Trump]]. Conceived by [[Masayoshi Son]] (who floated the idea of a joint US-Japan sovereign wealth fund to [[Howard Lutnick]]) and structured as a 50/50 US-Japan partnership via special-purpose vehicles. First announced 2025; governance terms grant Trump ultimate decision-making authority over project selection.

---

## Synthesis

This deal is structured to extract maximum US benefit from Japanese capital while giving [[Japan]] minimal governance. The terms read like a sovereign investment fund run by the US with Japanese money: Tokyo provides 100% of the funding, gets 50% ownership, splits profits 50/50 only until it recoups principal, then drops to 10%. [[Donald Trump]] picks the projects. Japan has 45 business days to fund each one — or presumably violate the deal's terms. [[Masayoshi Son]] sits at the nexus as the only Japanese figure who can credibly propose deals at this scale and who has direct access to Trump, making him simultaneously indispensable and a governance risk for Tokyo. The fee dispute on the Portsmouth plant — ¥1tn proposed, cut 90%+ — illustrates the tension: Son's companies execute the projects, but Japanese officials increasingly question why public funds subsidize a private developer with no equity exposure.

## Deal structure

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Total commitment | $550B Japanese investment in the US |
| Japan gets | Tariff relief |
| Ownership | 50/50 US-Japan via SPVs |
| Profit split (phase 1) | 50/50 until Japan recoups invested capital |
| Profit split (phase 2) | 90% US / 10% Japan (after recoupment) |
| Project selection | Trump has ultimate decision-making power |
| Funding timeline | Japan must fund within 45 business days of project announcement |
| Funding source (Japan) | [[JBIC]] + commercial lenders |
| Credit guarantee | [[Nexi]] guarantees 90%+ of commercial loan portion |
| Architect | [[Masayoshi Son]] (proposed sovereign wealth fund concept to [[Howard Lutnick]]) |

## Governance concerns (Mar 2026)

Japanese officials alarmed on multiple fronts:

- Japan is getting "edged out" of project selection — [[SoftBank]]/Trump select, Japan funds
- Projects backed by companies lacking necessary experience (implied: SoftBank has no power plant operating history)
- Son's direct line to Trump is an asset but also a liability — bypasses normal Japanese government channels
- Commercial banks remain nervous about lending despite generous [[Nexi]] guarantees
- Senior official quote (anonymous, Mar 2026): "Why do we have to pay a fee? They don't have to put up any of the money."

The [[Sanae Takaichi]] summit with Trump on March 19, 2026, is described by senior officials as a "make or break" moment — Tokyo wants more governance input before committing further tranches.

## Portsmouth fee dispute

[[SoftBank]] was set to earn ¥1tn ($6.3B) as developer/operator fee for the $33B gas plant in Portsmouth, Ohio. The fee existed because SoftBank holds no equity in the project — it is financed entirely by [[Japan]] (via [[JBIC]] and commercial lenders) and owned 50/50 by the US and Japan through an SPV. Without the fee, SoftBank earns nothing for its developer role.

Japanese officials intervened. The fee was cut by more than 90% from the original ¥1tn proposal. Final payments will be earned over 15-20 years if SoftBank reaches the target capacity of 9.2 GW.

SoftBank has placed $10B in orders for ~170 turbines from [[GE Vernova]] and plans to sell the electricity to data centers it will also operate, serving customers including [[OpenAI]].

Japanese officials still pushing for plant operation to be put out to tender, given SoftBank's limited power sector experience.

*Source: Financial Times (Mar 18, 2026) — David Keohane, Harry Dempsey, Leo Lewis*
