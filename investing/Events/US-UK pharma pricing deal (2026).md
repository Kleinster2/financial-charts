# US-UK pharma pricing deal (2026)

On April 2, 2026, the [[United States|US]] and the [[United Kingdom|UK]] announced a bilateral pharmaceutical-pricing arrangement under which the UK's National Health Service pays higher prices for new innovative drugs, and in exchange the US applies no tariffs to UK-origin patented and non-patented pharmaceutical products from January 1, 2026 through January 19, 2029. The deal followed a US [[Section 232 semiconductor tariff|Section 232]] investigation into the pharmaceutical sector and is the template the [[USTR]] is offering other reference-pricing regimes — most explicitly [[Germany]] in the [[US Section 301 investigation of Germany drug pricing (2026)|June 2026 Section 301 probe]].

---

## Synopsis

The deal is the negotiated-exit model for the [[Pharma R&D burden-sharing|burden-sharing]] doctrine. Rather than impose the [[Liberation Day tariffs|100% Section 232 pharma tariffs]] on UK drug exports, the US accepted higher NHS prices as the burden-sharing contribution and granted a tariff carve-out. [[USTR]] [[Jamieson Greer|Greer]] explicitly cited the deal when opening the Germany investigation: "Germany should follow suit with constructive negotiations to address this imbalance."

The structural significance is that the deal treats a foreign country's domestic drug-reimbursement price as a trade-policy variable — the same premise the Germany Section 301 probe extends to an ally that has so far declined to negotiate ([[Friedrich Merz|Merz]]: reimbursement is "a matter of national jurisdiction").
