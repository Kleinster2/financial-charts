# Plaza Accord

September 22, 1985 agreement among the [[G5]] ([[United States]], [[Japan]], [[West Germany]], [[France]], [[United Kingdom]]) at the Plaza Hotel in New York to depreciate the [[US dollar]] against the [[Japanese yen]] and [[Deutsche Mark]]. The yen surged from ~237/USD to ~120/USD by 1988. The resulting yen appreciation devastated [[Japan]]'s export sector and prompted the [[BOJ]] to slash the discount rate from 5.0% to 2.5%, flooding the economy with cheap money that fueled the 1986-1991 asset price bubble. The bubble's collapse triggered the [[Japan lost decades]]. A key early example of coordinated FX intervention with massive unintended consequences.
