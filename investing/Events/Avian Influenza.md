**Avian Influenza** refers to the highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) H5N1 outbreak cycle that has devastated U.S. and global poultry flocks since 2022, driving egg prices to historic levels and reshaping the economics of industrial poultry production.

## Synopsis

The current HPAI outbreak, which began in early 2022, has killed or required the culling of 100M+ birds in the United States alone — roughly 12% of the national flock. The virus has been unusually persistent, spreading through migratory bird populations and infecting commercial flocks across multiple seasons rather than burning out in a single wave as previous outbreaks did. The economic impact concentrates in eggs: U.S. egg prices hit $5.29/dozen in Jan 2025, more than doubling from pre-outbreak levels, because egg-laying flocks take 5-6 months to replace (vs. 6-8 weeks for broiler chickens). The price spike functions as a regressive tax on consumers — eggs are a staple protein, particularly for lower-income households.

The investment implications split along business model lines. [[Cal-Maine Foods]] (CALM), the largest U.S. egg producer, is heavily exposed to HPAI-driven flock losses — its concentrated, industrial-scale operations are exactly the type of facility where a single outbreak can destroy millions of birds. In contrast, [[Vital Farms]] (VITL), which uses a decentralized network of small pasture-raised farms, has reported minimal HPAI impact because the free-range model naturally limits flock density and cross-contamination. The outbreak has also accelerated investment in biosecurity (sealed housing, air filtration, restricted access protocols) and revived interest in mRNA poultry vaccines, where both [[Moderna]] and startups are developing candidates. The [[USDA]] manages the federal response — flock depopulation orders, indemnity payments to affected producers, and import restrictions — making its policy decisions a direct determinant of the supply recovery timeline. For commodities traders, HPAI has introduced a persistent risk premium in egg futures and shifted the seasonal pattern of poultry prices.
