# Cable broadband subscriber meltdown April 2026

Q1 2026 cable earnings (Apr 22 [[Comcast]] / Apr 24 [[Charter Communications]]) confirmed that residential broadband subscriber attrition accelerated, not stabilized, into the spring. The market priced the downside aggressively in a single session — [[Charter Communications|CHTR]] -25.5% on Apr 24, [[Comcast|CMCSA]] -12.9% on contagion — wiping out the prior week's relief rally and pushing both names through 52-week lows.

What changed is not the existence of fixed-wireless and fiber competition (both have been visible since 2023) but the realization that the cable footprint is structurally past peak broadband subscribers, and that Q1 2026 was the first quarter in which neither operator's mobile-line growth was large enough to offset broadband-pressure narratives in price action.

## Cox synergies

Charter's most operationally bullish disclosure: Cox synergy target raised to "at least $800 million" run-rate operating-cost savings, up from a prior $500M. This is the strategic cushion against subscriber attrition. Pending California approval; expected close summer 2026. After close, the combined entity would have ~70M residential and SMB customers across 46 states — nearly level with Comcast.

The market read: synergies are real, but they take 18-24 months to fully run-rate while broadband subscriber pressure is now. The selloff suggests the discount rate on synergies just went up.

## Mobile economics — converging slowly

| Operator | Q1 2026 mobile net adds | Total mobile lines | YoY line growth |
|----------|------------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| [[Charter Communications]] | +368,000 | 12.0M | +17% |
| [[Comcast]] | +435,000 (record) | 9.7M | +16% |

Both are MVNOs. Charter on [[Verizon]], Comcast on [[Verizon]]. The economics inflect toward profitability somewhere around 2027 at current run-rate growth, sooner if the operators move customers off of the host network onto their own WiFi-first traffic.

Mobile is the durable thesis-saver if the broadband book stabilizes anywhere. It is not yet a thesis-replacer.

## Versant spinoff — the Comcast-specific overhang

[[Comcast]]'s pending [[Versant]] spinoff (cable networks: USA, MS NOW, CNBC, Oxygen, E!, Golf, Syfy) is on track for 2026 completion. The Q1 print said Versant exit will mechanically increase Comcast net leverage slightly. The structural argument for separating the linear-decline networks from the Connectivity + Content + Theme parks growth bundle is intact, but the spin happens into a weaker cable-equity environment than originally framed.

## Synthesis

The Q1 2026 cable prints surface a structural transition rather than a cyclical earnings miss. For a decade the cable thesis was "broadband is a utility — losses on video are mechanical, broadband cash flow is durable." Apr 24 undercuts the durability premise: residential broadband net adds are now sufficiently negative across both major operators to make broadband behave like a fading legacy product rather than a defensive utility. Mobile and convergence — the second leg of the thesis — are growing fast but not yet large enough to fill the broadband gap on the income statement.

The $800M Cox synergy raise is the cleanest concrete bullish disclosure of the cycle, but it is structurally backward-looking — synergies offset stable-revenue cost; they do not solve a top-line attrition problem. The market correctly reads this asymmetrically.

The longer the broadband attrition continues, the more cable economics depend on the unlikely scenario in which fixed wireless and fiber both run out of new-subscriber demand and cable's installed base stabilizes by exhaustion. That outcome is consistent with neither carrier's nor fiber-builder's current growth trajectory.

The structural argument flips the cable thesis from "essential utility under pressure" to "two-stock duopoly managing a long fade." Apr 24 priced that flip in a single session.

## Investment readthrough

The cable thesis has narrowed to two questions, with mobile increasingly the answer to both:

1. Can Charter execute Cox synergies fast enough to offset broadband attrition before the equity hits a forced-deleveraging trigger?
2. Can Comcast convert its mobile + Peacock + theme-parks story into a multiple re-rating that pulls the stock out of the cable-comp trade?

The Apr 24 price action says the market is willing to mark cable equities at deep-distress multiples — Charter trading at 5x EBITDA, [[Comcast]] at 7x — even though both are still cash-generative.

Pair-trade implications: long FWA proxies ([[T-Mobile]], [[Verizon]] mobile segment) versus short cable; long fiber overbuilders ([[AT&T]], Frontier) versus short cable. Both have largely played out year-to-date but the Apr 24 print refreshes the gap.
