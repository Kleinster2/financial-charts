**Howard Hughes Vantage Risk acquisition** — [[Howard Hughes Holdings]] announced the $2.1B acquisition of Bermuda-based P&C insurer [[Vantage Risk]] on Dec 18, 2025. The deal is [[Bill Ackman]]'s first major step toward transforming HHH from a real estate company into a diversified holding company modeled on [[Berkshire Hathaway]].

---

## Deal terms

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Acquirer | [[Howard Hughes Holdings]] (NYSE: HHH) |
| Target | [[Vantage Risk]] (Bermuda) |
| Price | $2.1B |
| Financing | Cash + up to $1B in HHH stock from [[Pershing Square]] |
| Asset management | [[Pershing Square]] manages Vantage's investment portfolio |
| Announced | Dec 18, 2025 |

Ackman: "The acquisition of Vantage is a milestone event in the transformation of Howard Hughes into a diversified holding company."

---

## Strategic logic

The deal replicates the [[Berkshire Hathaway]] playbook: acquire an insurer → use [[Insurance float]] as low-cost permanent capital → invest the float through a centralized investment operation. Pershing Square managing Vantage's assets is the direct analog to Buffett managing Berkshire's insurance float.

Vantage specializes in P&C lines: lawsuits, political violence, and cyber risk. This is shorter-duration float than the life/annuity model used by [[Apollo]]/[[Athene]] and [[KKR]]/[[Global Atlantic]], but closer to Berkshire's original approach (GEICO auto, National Indemnity specialty).

---

## Broader trend

Using cheap insurance capital to finance acquisitions and private lending has become a crowded strategy. The article (FT, Dec 18 2025) noted:

| Actor | Vehicle | Insurance type |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| [[Bill Ackman]] / HHH | [[Vantage Risk]] | P&C (Bermuda) |
| [[Apollo]] | [[Athene]] | Life/annuities ($430B) |
| [[KKR]] | [[Global Atlantic]] | Life/annuities (>$200B) |
| [[Brookfield]] | [[American Equity]] | Fixed indexed annuities ($130B) |
| [[Third Point]] (Dan Loeb) | [[Malibu Life Reinsurance]] | Life reinsurance (Cayman, US fixed annuities) |

Loeb's conversion of his London-listed vehicle into Malibu Life Reinsurance — a Cayman-domiciled reinsurer targeting US fixed annuity market share — was a parallel move in the same period.
