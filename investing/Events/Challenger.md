#event #space #disaster

**Challenger** — January 28, 1986. Space Shuttle Challenger broke apart 73 seconds after launch, killing all 7 crew (Christa McAuliffe, the first teacher in space, among them). [[Type A mishap]]. Caused by O-ring failure on the right solid rocket booster in cold conditions. Resulted in a ~32-month STS grounding and broad reform of NASA's safety culture and decision-making processes (Rogers Commission).

## Why Challenger matters

One of three [[Type A mishap]] precedents (alongside [[Apollo 1]] and [[Columbia]]) that frame NASA's most severe accident classification. Cited by [[Jared Isaacman]] in his Feb 2026 reclassification of [[Boeing Starliner]] CFT as a Type A mishap — placing Starliner in the same regulatory tier as Challenger.
