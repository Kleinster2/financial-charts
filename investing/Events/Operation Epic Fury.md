**Operation Epic Fury** — joint [[United States|US]]-[[Israel|Israeli]] military strikes on [[Iran]] beginning February 28, 2026, the largest American combat operation since the 2003 invasion of Iraq. The strikes killed Supreme Leader [[Ali Khamenei]], triggered Iranian retaliation across the [[Persian Gulf]], and effectively closed the [[Strait of Hormuz]] to tanker traffic. The operation marked the first peer-level conflict to feature autonomous drone swarms on both sides and reshaped energy, defense, and shipping markets within hours.

On March 21, [[Iran]] fired two intermediate-range ballistic missiles at [[Diego Garcia]] (~3,700 km), the farthest-range strike Iran had ever attempted. One broke up mid-flight; the other was intercepted by an [[SM-3]]. Neither reached the target. The attack demonstrated Iran's attempt to break its ~2,000 km operational range ceiling but confirmed the capability remains unreliable. See [[Diego Garcia attack 2026]] and [[Iranian missile arsenal]].

First confirmed combat use of [[LUCAS]] one-way attack drone — Task Force Scorpion Strike (SOCCENT) launched ground-based LUCAS against IRGC command/control, air defense, and [[Shahed-136]] manufacturing sites. The $35,000 drone, reverse-engineered from Iran's own Shahed by [[SpektreWorks]], went from public debut to combat in 43 weeks.

## Economic pressure campaign — Operation Economic Fury

Running alongside the military campaign is the economic pressure campaign, publicly branded as Operation Economic Fury by [[Scott Bessent]] at the WSJ CEO Council on April 14, 2026. The campaign was ordered in March 2025 (a year before the military strikes) and described by Bessent as having already produced a December 2025 Iranian bank failure, [[Central Bank of Iran]] money printing, and subsequent street riots (see [[Iran#2025-2026 protests and internet blackout]]). April 14 marked formal branding and escalation — Gulf partners ([[UAE]], [[Qatar]], [[Saudi Arabia]]) now willing to share Iranian regime bank accounts after ~40 days of missile fire (Qatar lost 14% of GDP per Bessent; UAE absorbed 1,200+ hits). Treasury also pressing Chinese banks on [[Secondary sanctions]] exposure.
