#event #cloud #dma #eu #antitrust

On Jun 25 2026, the [[European Commission]] reached a preliminary position that [[Amazon]] and [[Microsoft]] should be designated as [[Digital Markets Act]] gatekeepers for [[AWS]] and [[Microsoft Azure]], respectively.

---

## Synthesis

This is the EU turning cloud from enabling infrastructure into a platform-gatekeeper market. The Commission's theory is not only size; it is lock-in, switching costs, entrenched ecosystems, and AI procurement gravity. That makes the cloud oligopoly a direct regulatory surface for the [[AI consolidation]] thesis: the same hyperscaler advantages that help absorb incremental AI demand can become the reason Brussels imposes conduct rules.

---

## What happened

The Commission preliminarily found that AWS and Azure are the largest and second-largest cloud computing services in the [[EU]] and act as important gateways between businesses and customers in the bloc, even though they did not meet the DMA's normal quantitative thresholds.

The case is still preliminary. [[Amazon]] and [[Microsoft]] can inspect the file, reply in writing, and exercise rights of defence. If the Commission confirms the findings, each company would receive a cloud gatekeeper designation and then have six months to bring the cloud service into DMA compliance.

---

## Why cloud now

The official rationale maps directly onto the cloud-moat debate:

| Commission factor | Investing read-through |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Entrenched user bases | Enterprise workloads are sticky once identity, data, security, and developer tooling sit on one cloud |
| Lock-in and high switching costs | Multi-cloud rhetoric does not eliminate migration and refactoring costs |
| Large ecosystems | Marketplaces, partner tools, and managed services compound the default-platform advantage |
| AI tools and partnerships | AI procurement increases the strategic value of hyperscaler compute, model access, and data gravity |

The Commission also opened related market investigations on Nov 18 2025 into cloud gatekeeper designations and whether existing DMA obligations are sufficient for cloud-sector competitiveness. Dutch regulator ACM is supporting the investigations through a joint investigative team.
