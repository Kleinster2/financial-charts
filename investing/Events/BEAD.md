BEAD (Broadband Equity Access and Deployment) -- $42.5B federal program to expand high-speed internet access, primarily targeting underserved and unserved areas. Enacted as part of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) of 2021.

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total funding | $42.5B |
| Administered by | NTIA (National Telecommunications and Information Administration) |
| Enacted | 2021 (IIJA) |
| Status (2025) | State allocation plans approved, but low operator participation raises execution concerns |

## Relevance

[[CoBank]]'s 2025 year-ahead report flagged that small rural operators lack resources to meet BEAD's complicated requirements. Low participation could blunt the program's intended impact on closing the rural digital divide. Electric cooperatives deploying fiber broadband are key potential beneficiaries.
