## Why it matters

The conference signals the industry's pivot from capability demonstration to enterprise deployment. Per [[Patrick Moorhead]] (Moor Insights & Strategy): "The conversation has shifted from AI capability to enterprise deployment and ROI. Watch for directional shifts on infrastructure pricing, capacity, and agentic AI roadmaps from hyperscalers."

This is the conference where hyperscaler infrastructure commitments, pricing signals, and agentic AI roadmaps get publicly stress-tested in front of enterprise buyers. The timing — same week as [[TSMC]] March revenue — makes it a concentrated signal window for AI infrastructure demand.

## Opening night (Apr 6)

[[NVIDIA]]'s [[Bryan Catanzaro]] opened the conference with a talk framing AI as a **five-layer cake**: energy, chips, infrastructure, models, and applications — powering "one of the largest infrastructure buildouts in history." CEO [[Stefan Weitz]] welcomed attendees and set the tone alongside [[Fei-Fei Li]] and [[Al Gore]].

The five-layer framework is itself an investable thesis map:

| Layer | Key players (vault) |

| Energy | [[Nuclear power for AI]], [[BYOP]], oil/gas |
| Chips | [[TSMC]], [[NVIDIA]], [[Intel]], [[Broadcom]] |
| Infrastructure | [[Oracle]], [[Amazon|AWS]], [[Microsoft|Azure]], [[Google|GCP]], data centers |
| Models | [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], [[Google]], [[Meta]] |
| Applications | Enterprise SaaS, agentic AI, vertical solutions |

## Context

SF Mayor [[Daniel Lurie]] announced the conference as part of the city's strategy to cement San Francisco as the global AI hub. [[OpenAI]] expanding in Mission Bay, [[Databricks]] investing $1B in the city. Moscone Center conferences projected to generate $600M in economic impact for SF in 2025.

The move from Las Vegas to San Francisco is itself a signal — the AI industry gravitating toward proximity to its builder ecosystem rather than convention-center neutrality.

April 2026 is the densest month in the AI conference calendar: 16+ events across four continents. HumanX sits at the top tier alongside [[Google Cloud Next 2026]] (Apr 22–24, Las Vegas) and [[ICLR 2026]] (Apr 23–27, Rio de Janeiro). The clustering reflects an inflection point in the enterprise planning cycle — Q1 results in hand, budgets confirmed, runway to act before year-end.

## Panels

### The State of Modern War (All-In Podcast, Apr 6)

[[Shyam Sankar]] ([[Palantir]] CTO) and [[Trae Stephens]] ([[Anduril]] co-founder) interviewed at the conference. Key claims:

- [[Anduril]] raising Series H at ~$60B, landed $20B Army contract. Arsenal-1 (5M sq ft, Columbus OH) opened Apr 5 — producing Furies.
- [[Palantir]] at ~$400B market cap. Sankar writing a book on defense re-industrialization.
- Defense industrial base: 51 primes → 5 post-Cold War. Only 6% of weapons spending to defense specialists in 1989 → 86% today.
- US has 8 days of munitions for a China conflict vs ~800 needed. 10,000:1 drone production gap.
- Anthropic/Maven: Stephens — "Abstention from national security is not a morally neutral decision."
- [[Office of Strategic Capital]]: [[Emil Michael]] deploying $200B ("Deal Team Six") for supply chain bottlenecks.
- Only at-scale manufacturer founded this century in the US: [[Tesla]].
