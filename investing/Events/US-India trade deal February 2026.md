#event #trade #policy

**US-India trade deal** — Trump administration announces bilateral tariff reduction, February 2026. First major trade deal of 2026.

---

## Deal terms

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| US reciprocal tariff on India | 25% | **18%** |
| Effective | — | Immediate |

---

## India commitments

Per Trump announcement:
- Buy American products "at a much higher level"
- **Stop buying Russian oil**, buy more from US
- General commitment to reduce trade imbalance

---

## Strategic significance

**For US:**
- First major bilateral deal of 2026
- Demonstrates "reciprocal tariff" framework can yield deals
- Reduces India tariff from 25% baseline

**For India:**
- Access to US market at lower tariff rate
- Closer alignment with US vs Russia
- Modi-Trump relationship deepening

**Geopolitical:**
- Part of broader effort to pull [[India]] away from Russia/China orbit
- Energy security implications (Russia oil → US oil)
- [[Quad]] alliance strengthening

---

## Context

Trump tariff strategy:
1. Impose high baseline tariffs (25% "reciprocal")
2. Negotiate bilateral deals for reductions
3. Use deals as political wins

See also:
- Taiwan deal (Jan 2026): 20% → 15%
- China tariff truce (extended through Nov 2026)
