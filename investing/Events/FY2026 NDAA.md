#event #defense #legislation #2025

**FY2026 National Defense Authorization Act** — $900.6B defense authorization signed by Trump on December 18, 2025 (S. 1071). Passed 312-112 (House), 77-20 (Senate). Beyond the annual defense budget, the bill carries two provisions with outsized investment implications: the COINS Act codifying outbound investment restrictions on [[China]] (AI, semiconductors, quantum), and the [[US Development Finance Corporation|DFC]] reauthorization tripling its portfolio cap to $205B — the latter enabling the March 2026 Gulf maritime reinsurance facility.

## Shipbuilding ($26B)

The largest shipbuilding authorization in years, reflecting bipartisan alarm at fleet size vs. [[China]]'s naval expansion:

- 3rd [[Columbia]]-class ballistic missile submarine + advanced procurement for future boats. Navy could contract five Columbia-class starting FY2026
- 1 [[Virginia]]-class attack submarine + advance procurement
- Advance procurement for future DDG-51 [[Arleigh Burke]]-class destroyers
- Ford-class aircraft carrier — flexibility to purchase next two in "one or more contracts" (block buy option)
- 1 anti-submarine warfare auxiliary ship
- 2 Ship-to-Shore Connector landing craft
- 3-year pilot program for automated shipbuilding technologies to cut construction time
- Maritime industrial base strengthening: expanded hiring/workforce authorities, robotic automation

---

## Munitions ($25B+)

More than $25B authorized to rebuild depleted US munitions stockpiles. Army directed to expand robotic automation in ammunition manufacturing. This reflects lessons from Ukraine aid drawdowns that exposed critically low inventories across multiple munition types.

---

## COINS Act (outbound investment restrictions)

The Comprehensive Outbound Investment National Security Act of 2025, embedded in the NDAA, codifies and expands restrictions on US investment in adversary-nation advanced technology. Amends the [[Defense Production Act]] to create mandatory notification obligations.

Prohibited technology categories:
- Integrated circuits and semiconductors
- Artificial intelligence systems
- Quantum information systems
- High-performance computing and supercomputing (new)
- Hypersonic systems (new)

Countries of concern (expanded from China-only):
- [[China]] (including Hong Kong, Macau)
- [[Cuba]], [[Iran]], [[North Korea]], [[Russia]], [[Venezuela]] (Maduro regime)

Treasury authorized to add additional technology categories via rulemaking. Mandatory notification for "notifiable technologies" short of prohibition. The Act codifies the executive order-based Outbound Investment Security Program that Treasury had been building — giving it statutory permanence that survives future administrations.

Notable exclusion: the GAIN AI Act was dropped from the final bill. It would have required US chipmakers ([[NVIDIA]], [[AMD]], [[Intel]]) to prioritize American customers before selling advanced AI chips to China — a provision the semiconductor industry lobbied hard against.

## DFC reauthorization

The DFC Modernization and Reauthorization Act of 2025, attached to the NDAA:

- Portfolio cap tripled: $60B → $205B
- Authorization extended through December 31, 2031
- Can now lend to wealthier countries (previously restricted to low/middle-income), with spending caps and national security certification
- ICT-related deals explicitly called out as priority (countering China's digital infrastructure lending)
- New chief strategic officer role
- CEO must produce five-year Strategic Priorities Plan

See [[US Development Finance Corporation]] for full details and the March 2026 Gulf reinsurance deployment enabled by this expansion.

## AI and cybersecurity

- DoD must develop a comprehensive, risk-based cybersecurity framework for AI/ML systems covering workforce risks, AI-specific threats, supply chain risks, adversarial tampering, and data theft
- Higher security levels required for AI systems of greatest national security concern
- Does not address the [[Pentagon AI access dispute 2026]] — the Anthropic/Pentagon standoff occurred weeks after signing

---

## Acquisition reform

- Section 812: shifts procurement from "lowest overall cost" to "best value" — significant for defense contractors competing on capability rather than price
- Section 811: amends or repeals 86 provisions in prior NDAAs to streamline acquisition
- Section 824: expands acceptable past performance to include commercial/non-government projects — benefits non-traditional defense contractors and tech companies entering the defense market

---

## Nuclear

- Domestic Uranium Enrichment Program funding increased for labor, materials, and design activities
- Broader nuclear strategy to support US nuclear industry leadership
