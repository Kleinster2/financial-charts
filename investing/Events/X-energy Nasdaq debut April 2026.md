# X-energy Nasdaq debut April 2026

[[X-energy]] became the first sizable advanced-reactor company to list via traditional IPO on Apr 24, 2026, opening on Nasdaq under ticker XE. Pricing came in at $23 — above the $16-$19 marketed range — on 44.3M Class A shares for ~$1.02B in gross proceeds. Day-1 close: $29.20 (+27%), implying ~$11.9B equity value.

The event matters because it converts the AI-driven nuclear-revival thesis from a 2030s policy story into a public-market data-center procurement story. The two prior US SMR listings — [[Oklo]] and [[NuScale]] — both took the SPAC route. X-energy chose a book-built IPO and got upsized terms, suggesting institutional demand for advanced-reactor exposure ahead of any commercial reactor coming online.

## Strategic read-through

### [[X-energy]]

The IPO converts X-energy's $600M+ pre-IPO private capital and $1.2B+ in DOE ARDP funding into a public-market currency for the next phase: building first-of-kind reactors and proving the replication math. The risk is that day-1 pricing reflects strong AI-demand sentiment more than execution conviction. With $0 revenue and 2030s commercial timing, multi-year drawdown risk on the equity is high if either NRC milestones slip or a customer reschedules.

### [[Nuclear power for AI]]

A traditional-IPO advanced-reactor listing closing +27% above range materially validates the thesis. It is the cleanest public-market vote so far that AI power demand is willing to underwrite advanced-reactor capex before any reactor is operating. The follow-through question is whether [[Amazon]]'s commitment translates into a binding offtake structure rather than a non-binding LOI.

### [[Amazon]]

Amazon's role as the second project in the Xe-100 deployment sequence — paired with its [[Amazon Climate Pledge Fund]] pre-IPO investment — is the clearest signal yet that the company is treating long-dated nuclear capacity as part of its AI-power strategy. The economic structure resembles its [[Anthropic]] approach: the same company is the strategic investor and the demand anchor.

### [[Oklo]] / [[NuScale]] / [[TerraPower]] / [[Kairos Power]]

XE's listing gives the broader SMR cohort a fresh public-market comp. Watch whether OKLO and SMR re-rate against XE's day-1 valuation or whether the market keeps differentiating on technology (HTGR vs LWR vs molten salt) and customer base. [[TerraPower]] and [[Kairos Power]] remain private but now have a clearer IPO benchmark.

### [[NRC]]

X-energy's regulatory progress — fuel facility license granted, first construction license targeted by end-2026 — is the case study the new NRC leadership will be evaluated against. If those timelines hold for X-energy, they become the template for [[TerraPower]], [[Kairos Power]], and follow-on Xe-100 deployments. If they slip, the post-IPO equity will absorb the cost.
