# Nasdaq semiconductor selloff June 2026

## Synthesis

On Jun. 5, 2026, a stronger-than-expected US payrolls report repriced the Fed path higher and hit rate-sensitive tech/growth. [[Nasdaq]] Composite fell 4.18% in local price data, while FT reported a 4.2% fall and the largest Nasdaq drop since the Apr. 10, 2025 tariff shock. The sharper move was in chips: FT reported the [[PHLX Semiconductor]] Index down 10.3%, and the vault's SOXX proxy closed down 10.44%.

The event matters because it was the first large downside tape after the late-May SOX melt-up and the Jun. 1-5 AI-infrastructure equity-financing sequence. The issue was not a single company miss. It was the whole AI capital-markets stack getting tested at once: higher front-end yields, mega-cap AI capex funded with equity, a live [[SpaceX IPO 2026]] roadshow, and [[Memory|memory]]/chip stocks priced for uninterrupted demand.

The final taxonomy is layered. The trigger was a marketwide rates shock, because SPY fell 2.58%. The core abnormal expression was QQQ/XLK tech-growth, because QQQ's 90-session beta to SPY was only about 1.29 and SPY therefore explained roughly -3.3% of the -4.8% QQQ move. The sector read-through was SOXX/SMH-led semiconductors, while memory was the leading damage pocket inside that semi beta rather than a separate memory-specific break.

A second wave followed on Jun 23, covered in the dated sections below. It was a more semiconductor-concentrated selloff (SPY only -1.45% against SOXX -7.88%) led by memory de-risking into [[Micron]]'s Jun 24 fiscal Q3 print. The print then beat by roughly 20% on both lines and guided Q4 to $50B, and MU round-tripped most of the drop in after-hours. The corrected trigger for Jun 23 is memory-glut fear plus Micron-eve de-risk against a hawkish-rates backdrop — not a [[Broadcom]] AI-demand comment; the tape shows AVGO as the most resilient semi that day.

Against QQQ beta, the selloff was less idiosyncratic than the headline SOX-vs-Nasdaq spread suggests. SOXX's 90-session beta to QQQ was 1.93, so QQQ's -4.80% implied -9.27% before alpha; SOXX's actual -10.44% left a -1.17 percentage-point beta-adjusted shortfall, or about -1.0x residual sigma when the intercept is included. SMH's QQQ-beta-adjusted shortfall was smaller, about -0.64 percentage points without an intercept and -0.78x residual sigma with one.

Against SOXX, memory was a high-beta pocket rather than a separate break. MU's 90-session beta to SOXX was about 1.43, WDC's was about 1.23, and an equal-weight MU/WDC basket was about 1.33. With SOXX down 10.44%, that beta implied roughly -13.66% for the basket versus the actual -12.16%. The FT-reported SanDisk and SK Hynix declines confirm memory was a leading damage channel, but the listed US memory beta check argues against calling the event memory-led in a risk-adjusted sense.

The clean read: this was primarily a rates-led QQQ/XLK tech-growth derating, expressed through a broad SOXX/SMH semiconductor drawdown. Memory was the highest-beta damage pocket inside that drawdown. The raw tape still matters because financing windows care about headline drawdowns, not only regression residuals.

## Insights

This is the downside companion to the late-May SOX melt-up already logged in [[Semiconductors]]. The sector was priced for supply scarcity, AI capex durability, [[Memory|memory]] pricing power, and broadening leadership beyond [[NVIDIA]]. Jun. 5 showed the same basket is also rate-sensitive when the front end reprices higher.

For [[AI infrastructure financing risk]], the event connects valuation to funding. Alphabet's $85B deal showed equity markets could still absorb enormous AI-infrastructure issuance. Meta's FT-reported exploration showed a second Mag 7 issuer was considering the same channel. The Jun. 5 selloff says that channel is sensitive to rates and market-window risk even before any second issuer formally launches.

For [[SpaceX IPO 2026]], the event is a roadshow-window warning. FT explicitly placed the selloff against [[SpaceX]]'s planned IPO the following week, plus expected [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]] listings. The IPO can still price if demand is deep, but the benchmark backdrop moved from euphoric AI-scarcity bid to capital-markets absorption test.

## Jun 23, 2026 — second wave: memory-glut de-risk into the Micron print

Nineteen days after the Jun 5 rates shock, the same stack sold off again — but with a different trigger and a more semiconductor-concentrated signature. On Jun 23 [[SPY]] fell only 1.45% (versus 2.58% on Jun 5), yet [[SOXX]] dropped 7.88% and memory led the damage: MU -13.18%, [[SanDisk]] (SNDK) -13.64%, [[Western Digital]] (WDC) -8.45%. Semiconductor equipment was hit nearly as hard ([[Lam Research]] LRCX -9.33%, [[KLA]] KLAC -9.17%, [[Applied Materials]] AMAT -8.48%). This was not a marketwide risk-off; it was a semis-and-memory derating on a quiet broad-market day.

The trigger was memory-glut fear plus de-risking ahead of Micron's Jun 24 print, against the hawkish-rates backdrop the [[Rate expectations|Warsh Fed]] set the week before. It was not a [[Broadcom]] AI-demand comment: AVGO fell 3.06% on Jun 23, the smallest decline in the semis group — if a Broadcom remark had triggered the move, AVGO would have led the drop, not resisted it. The tape refutes the Broadcom-trigger attribution.

The broad market barely moved; this was a sector-and-memory event, not a marketwide one.

## Jun 24, 2026 — the Micron print resolves the second wave

MU closed Jun 24 at $1,048.51 (-0.31%), then reported fiscal Q3 2026 after the close: revenue $41.46B (vs ~$35.1B Street, $33.5B guide), non-GAAP EPS $25.11 (vs ~$20.39 Street), 84.9% gross margin, and a Q4 guide of $50.0B at ~86% margin — plus the multi-year [[Strategic Customer Agreements]] framing. In after-hours trading MU rose roughly 14% (sources ranged +12.6% to +15%) toward ~$1,195, round-tripping most of the Jun 23 crash.

The resolution wrong-footed the Jun 23 de-risk: the memory-specific selling ahead of the print reversed almost completely within hours of the result. The second wave's memory-specific tilt — the part not explained by semi beta — was a head-fake. The broad semi/rates derating (the QQQ/SOXX layer) is a separate question that the Jun 25 session and the [[Rate expectations|rates path]] will continue to price.

*After-hours figures are extended-session, secondary-source ([[CNBC]], [[Yahoo Finance]]) as of ~8:00 PM ET Jun 24; the canonical beta-adjusted abnormal move uses the Jun 25 regular-session close and is a pending follow-up. Full print detail in [[Micron]] and [[Memory squeeze thesis]].*
