**H2CAST** — German pilot project to convert existing salt caverns at Etzel into hydrogen storage, testing whether underground storage can support a future regional hydrogen network.

---

## What it is

- Project started in Q1 2022 and is scheduled to run through Oct 31, 2026.
- Goal: adapt existing gas caverns and related above-ground facilities for hydrogen use in [[Germany]] and northwest [[Europe]].
- Strategic logic: hydrogen demand and supply will not line up perfectly in time or location, so cavern [[Storage]] is meant to become the balancing layer.

---

## [[Plug Power]]'s role

- Oct 21, 2025: Plug said it completed the first phase of hydrogen deliveries to H2CAST, supplying 44.5 metric tons between April and August 2025, then won a second contract for another 35 metric tons.
- Apr 15, 2026: Plug said those deliveries had contributed to full cavern fill, with roughly 90 metric tons of hydrogen, equivalent to about 1 million standard cubic meters, transferred into the caverns overall.
- Readthrough: H2CAST is one of Plug's clearest current proof points that its European business includes logistics and storage-adjacent execution, not just electrolyzer sales.

---

## Why it matters

Hydrogen infrastructure does not scale if every molecule has to be produced and consumed in the same moment. H2CAST matters because it is testing whether existing salt caverns can become the long-duration [[Storage]] layer for industrial hydrogen, grid balancing, and future regional network build-out.
