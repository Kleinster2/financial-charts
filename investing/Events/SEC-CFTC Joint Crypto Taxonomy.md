# SEC-CFTC Joint Crypto Taxonomy

On March 17, 2026, the [[SEC]] and [[CFTC]] published a joint 68-page interpretive release — "Application of the Federal Securities Laws to Certain Types of Crypto Assets and Certain Transactions Involving Crypto Assets" — the most significant US crypto regulatory action in a decade. Entered the Federal Register on March 23.

The release ends years of jurisdictional ambiguity between the two agencies by establishing a five-category taxonomy for crypto assets and explicitly naming 16 tokens as digital commodities not subject to securities law.

---

## Five-category taxonomy

| Category | Definition | Securities status | Jurisdiction |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Digital commodities** | Tokens deriving value from functional crypto system operation and supply/demand, not from others' managerial efforts. Must be functional. May carry governance rights | Not securities | [[CFTC]] |
| **Digital collectibles** | NFTs, artwork, in-game items, memes. Value from artistic/cultural significance and demand. Creator royalties alone don't make it a security; fractionalization may | Not securities (generally) | Case-by-case |
| **Digital tools** | Utility tokens — memberships, tickets, credentials, identity badges. Value from utility. May be "soul-bound" (non-transferable) | Not securities | Case-by-case |
| **Stablecoins** | Pegged to fiat/assets. GENIUS Act-compliant and "Covered Stablecoins" (per April 2025 staff statement) addressed. Other arrangements may still be securities | Partially resolved | Split — see [[CLARITY Act]] |
| **Digital securities** | Financial instruments (equity, debt, fund shares) represented on-chain. A security remains a security regardless of format | Securities | [[SEC]] |

## 16 named digital commodities

The release explicitly identified 16 tokens as digital commodities — effectively ending any future SEC enforcement action against them as securities:

Bitcoin (BTC), Ether (ETH), Solana (SOL), XRP, and 12 others (compiled based on tokens underlying futures contracts on designated contract markets).

This list is not exhaustive — other tokens may qualify under the framework's criteria.

## Key innovations

### Asset vs. investment contract separation

The most novel component. The SEC now formally distinguishes the crypto *asset* from the *investment contract*:

- A non-security token sold via an investment contract can **"separate"** from it once the issuer fulfills its promised essential managerial efforts
- Once separated, subsequent transactions in that asset are no longer subject to securities law
- Separation depends on the issuer's representations and promises — marketing and promotional statements are critical
- Separation can occur at any time, including immediately upon delivery

This is a practical roadmap for token issuers: make specific promises, fulfill them, and the token becomes a freely tradeable non-security commodity.

### Transactions NOT involving securities

The release confirms that the following are NOT securities transactions:

- **Proof-of-work mining** (including mining pools) — rewards are compensation for validation services
- **Proof-of-stake staking** — same logic
- **Redeemable wrapped-token arrangements**
- **Airdrops**

### New defined terms

| Term | Significance |
|------|-------------|
| Crypto system | The network/protocol a token operates on |
| Onchain vs. offchain | Format doesn't alter securities status |
| Functional and decentralized | Key criteria for digital commodity status |
| Whitepaper | Channel through which issuer statements create profit expectations under Howey |
| Central party | Presence/absence affects classification |

## Legal status and limitations

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Authority | Commission-level interpretive release — the SEC's most authoritative crypto guidance ever issued |
| Binding? | **No** — not binding on federal courts. Private litigation risks remain |
| Supersedes | All prior SEC staff statements on covered topics |
| Howey test | Still binding legal precedent — analysis remains fact-specific |
| Enforcement | Both SEC and CFTC committed to administer their statutes consistent with the release, including in enforcement |
| Permanence | Only Congress can make it binding law — hence the [[CLARITY Act]] |

[[Paul Atkins]] previewed possible exemptive rulemaking building on the taxonomy in a speech the same day.

## Market significance

The taxonomy is the regulatory unlock behind the Q1 2026 tokenization surge:
- **March 18** (next day): SEC approved [[Nasdaq]] tokenized securities rule
- **March 24**: NYSE tapped [[Securitize]] for its Digital Trading Platform
- **March 25**: House Financial Services Committee tokenization hearing
- **April 2026**: [[CLARITY Act]] Senate Banking markup targeted

Before this release, every crypto project operated under "is this a security?" ambiguity. The taxonomy replaces case-by-case enforcement with a classification framework. The 16 named commodities getting explicit clearance removes the single biggest overhang on institutional crypto adoption.
