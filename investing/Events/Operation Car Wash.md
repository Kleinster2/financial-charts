# Operation Car Wash

Operation Car Wash (Operação Lava Jato) was the sprawling Brazilian anti-corruption investigation that ran from 2014 to roughly 2021, centered on a kickback scheme at [[Petrobras]] and spreading across construction firms, politicians, and major corporates. It reshaped Brazilian politics and markets for a decade.

## What happened

- Began as a money-laundering probe in Curitiba (the name comes from a car wash used to launder funds) and uncovered a cartel of construction firms (Odebrecht, Camargo Corrêa, and others) paying bribes for inflated Petrobras contracts, with kickbacks routed to politicians and parties.
- Led by judge Sergio Moro and a Curitiba task force using aggressive plea bargains (delação premiada).
- Implicated three presidents (Lula — convicted, later annulled by the STF; Dilma Rousseff; Michel Temer) and reached into the corporate elite, including the [[JBS]] / Batista plea of 2017, in which Joesley Batista's recording of President Temer became a national scandal.

## Legacy

- Massive convictions and settlements (Odebrecht's global plea, J&F's R$10.3B agreement), a deep recession-era hit to the construction and [[Petrobras]] complexes, and a realignment of Brazilian politics that helped carry Jair Bolsonaro to the presidency in 2018.
- Later discredited in part: the "Vaza Jato" leaks (2019) exposed collusion between Moro and prosecutors, the STF ruled Moro biased and annulled Lula's convictions (2021), and the task force was wound down.
