**Avant Gardner bankruptcy 2025** — Chapter 11 filing by Brooklyn's largest nightclub complex (Brooklyn Mirage, Great Hall, Kings Hall) on August 4, 2025 with ~$155M in secured debt owed to [[Axar Capital]]. [[Axar Capital|Axar]] credit-bid $110M+ for the assets, then transferred operations to [[FIVE Holdings]] ([[Pacha]] brand). Plan confirmed February 12, 2026. Case No. 25-11446 (D. Del.), Judge Mary F. Walrath.

---

## Synthesis

This bankruptcy is a case study in Axar's distressed-to-control playbook — and in how an innocuous seller-financed acquisition can metastasize into a $155M secured lending relationship. Axar didn't set out to own a nightclub. The chain was: SFX bankruptcy (2016) → LiveStyle (Axar-controlled) → sold Electric Zoo to [[Avant Gardner]] for $15M (May 2022) → Avant Gardner couldn't pay after 2023 E-Zoo disaster → unpaid seller note converted Axar into senior creditor → escalating tranched lending through 2023-2025 → $155M total secured debt → classic loan-to-own. The UCC's allegations of incremental control seizure (board stacking, management installation) track the same pattern seen in StoneMor. Axar absorbed an estimated $30-40M loss on the overall deal — not every distressed play works, and nightclub assets with permit problems and political entanglements are harder to monetize than deathcare or insurance. The FIVE Holdings/Pacha exit was the best available outcome, not a triumph.

## Origins and the Axar lending chain

The story of how [[Axar Capital]] ended up with $155M in secured claims against a nightclub starts with SFX Entertainment.

- 2013 — Jurgen "Billy" Bildstein (Austrian-born, formerly ran Alte Borse in Switzerland) founded Reynard Productions, an events production company in NYC. The name ("Reynard" = medieval French for "fox") referenced his earlier label/promotion company Cityfox.

- 2015 — Cityfox Halloween party shut down at a former NuHart Plastics factory (a Superfund toxic site) at 99 Scott Ave. Cost ~$500K in refunds. NYPD issued summonses for selling alcohol without licensing.

- Spring 2016 — Philipp Wiederkehr (Swiss banker, Wiederkehr Real Estate) purchased a block-sized lot in East Williamsburg — buildings that formerly housed a steel plant and waste management facility. Construction begins. Wiederkehr is the property owner/landlord; Bildstein is the operator. [[Avant Gardner]] LLC rents from WRE under a ground lease. WRE provided Tenant Improvement Allowance capital.

- Summer 2016 — Brooklyn Mirage opens as a pop-up/seasonal venue (~2,800 capacity initially). City authorities shut it down within one month for safety violations.

- April 2017 — NY State Liquor Authority grants full licensure. Brooklyn Mirage reopens as permanent venue. Bildstein holds 100% equity.

- May 2022 — [[Avant Gardner]] acquires Made Event LLC and EZ Festivals LLC (Electric Zoo festival) from LiveStyle for $15M. The critical detail: [[Axar Capital]] owned LiveStyle (having taken it over from SFX Entertainment's 2016 bankruptcy). [[Andrew Axelrod]] structured the deal so Avant Gardner could pay using Electric Zoo festival proceeds — effectively seller-financed.

- 2023 — Electric Zoo disaster. First day canceled (stages unbuilt, no permits), oversold by ~7,000 tickets, gate-crashing. Led to unpaid vendor claims (~$1M+), a consolidated class action ($24M+ claimed, settled for $4M general unsecured claim in bankruptcy), and 2024 edition canceled. Avant Gardner couldn't pay Axar the $15M purchase price. The unpaid seller note converted Axar into a senior secured creditor.

- Late 2023 onward — Axar extends roughly $120M in additional loans over several tranches. Capital arrived staged: initial $5M from Axar, then $7.5M contingent on performance. TVT Capital provided ~$6.25M then another $2M. Each tranche required continued confidence management could stabilize. Total funded secured debt at filing: $121M senior secured term loan + $21M in protective advance bridge loans + $11.8M junior secured note (LiveStyle, later transferred to Bradford Capital Holdings LP) = ~$155M.

- 2024-2025 — $30M renovation underway: 270-degree virtual immersion system (100M pixels, 3,000 tiles), L-Acoustics sound system (100+ speakers), heavy timber construction (197 x 164 ft, 65 ft high).

## Permit revocation and collapse

- May 1, 2025 — NYC Department of Buildings revokes Temporary Place of Assembly Certificate of Occupancy hours before scheduled reopening (Sara Landry headlining). Three construction permits placed "On Hold" April 30-May 1.

DOB Commissioner James Oddo: *"From [Mirage's] questionable footing to the large truss at its zenith, from its cantilevered mezzanines to its exterior walls, it was potentially unsteady"* and unsafe for 6,000 people.

Specific safety failures found by DOB:
- Steel truss over stage designed with heavy-duty bracing but constructed unbraced
- No engineering for wind or seismic activity
- Engineered strand board instead of heavy timber (wrong fire rating — "combustible")
- Insufficient sprinkler systems
- Not enough exit stairwells for emergency evacuation
- Missing accessible routes and bathrooms
- Structure filed as "temporary" but failed to meet temporary or permanent standards

- May 22, 2025 — CEO Josh Wyatt fired (had served since Oct 2024). Gary Richards — Axar's non-executive board chairman, known as "Destructo" in the EDM world — steps in as interim CEO.

- June 2025 — [[Andrew Axelrod]] personally enlisted to oversee permitting.

- July 2025 — Independent Restructuring Committee formed, chaired by Hooman Yazhari (Axar's other board appointee).

- August 4, 2025 — Chapter 11 filing.

## Board control and UCC allegations

Axar placed Gary Richards and Hooman Yazhari on the [[Avant Gardner]] board, creating a 2-1 majority over Bildstein (sole equity holder). The UCC alleged Axar "took control of Avant Gardner incrementally by installing associates into management and board positions." Specific claims:

1. Classified the Mirage stage as a "temporary structure" to circumvent more stringent permanent construction safety standards
2. Submitted designs without a roof to evade maximum height restrictions
3. Incorrectly stated floor space calculations to "skirt requirements concerning square footage limits for mezzanines and balconies"
4. Board-controlled management (Richards, Yazhari) directed these decisions while holding 2-1 board majority

The UCC sought a trustee takeover on September 7, 2025 (sealed omnibus objection) citing "fraud." The argument: Axar's management decisions caused the DOB permit revocation that triggered the bankruptcy.

## FIVE Holdings / Pacha transaction

[[FIVE Holdings]] entered "a long-term agreement assuming full operational management" of Brooklyn Mirage and Great Hall. Axar retains ownership initially; FIVE operates. Includes a purchase option — this structure is what triggered creditor outrage, as it was potentially designed to avoid triggering the CVR payout threshold.

FIVE Holdings background: founded 2011 as SKAI Holdings, renamed FIVE in 2017. Chairman/CEO Kabir Mulchandani (Indian-born, Phillips Exeter/Stanford). $2.2B net worth (Nov 2025). Revenue $589M (2024, +28% YoY). EBITDA $208M (2024). $460M revolving credit facility (Sept 2025). 1,700+ hotel keys, 3,000+ employees. Acquired 100% of The Pacha Group in October 2023 for €302.5M (~$330M) — includes Pacha Ibiza (opened 1973), hotels, global trademarks.

Pacha NYC history: Original Pacha NYC operated at 618 W 46th St, Hell's Kitchen (2005-2016, 30,000 sq ft). Closed January 2016 due to rising costs and death of Rob Fernandez (promotions director, 2015).

Timeline of the deal revelation:
- Jan 1, 2026 — Brooklyn Magazine (reporter Scott Enman) breaks the story exclusively
- Post-publication — creditors ask Axar for details. Axar initially tells creditors the report is "incorrect"
- Jan 9 — Axar revises position, says no "definitive agreement" exists
- Jan 12 — UCC learns deal is "all but done"
- Jan 30 — Creditors formally yank support, allege deal arranged "under the cover of darkness"
- Feb 2 — Agreement in Principle filed resolving all disputes

## Settlement terms

Original settlement (Oct 2025) — valued at $20M+:
- $3.3M cash
- Axar funds administrative expenses, priority unsecured claims, estate wind-down
- Ground lease defaults cured
- Governance reform: ouster of incumbent director; appointment of independent director Vikram Jindal (selected by UCC)
- Litigation claims against certain insiders preserved
- CVR: 15% of enterprise value above ~$175M threshold to unsecured creditors

Modified settlement (Feb 2026) after FIVE Holdings revelation:
- CVR threshold reduced from ~$175M to $90M (much more likely to pay out)
- $3.5M guaranteed CVR minimum added (regardless of monetization)
- Total minimum creditor recovery: $6.8M+ ($3.3M cash + $3.5M CVR floor)

Judge Walrath (Feb 12, 2026): *"There are no outstanding remaining objections. The debtor has met the requirements for confirmation under the bankruptcy code."* Called it "a remarkable settlement and in the best interests of the estate."

Orrick (UCC counsel): Axar shifted "from foe to friend."

## Political connections

Frank Carone — Brooklyn attorney, former NYC Mayor Eric Adams' chief of staff (left end of 2022). Was Avant Gardner's attorney before joining City Hall. Sued NY SLA twice on Bildstein's behalf (2018). His firm Oaktree Solutions Ltd. filed a $90K proof of claim for unpaid "monthly retainers" (no description of services). Brooklyn federal prosecutors investigating Carone's business dealings (grand jury, reported Jan 30, 2026).

Mayor Adams connection: As Brooklyn Borough President, wrote in support of Avant Gardner's liquor license. Adams' office set meetings between Avant Gardner and governor's aides regarding licensing. Adams administration separately under federal indictment.

NY State Liquor Authority (2020 filing): described venue as "a place for teenagers and young adults to get high on Ecstasy, Ketamine and other drugs." Multiple patron overdoses, at least two deaths (found in nearby Newtown Creek, 2023).
