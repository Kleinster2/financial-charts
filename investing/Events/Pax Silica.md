## What it covers

Six cooperation pillars spanning the full technology stack:

1. **Critical minerals** â€” mining, refining, processing (reducing China dependence)
2. **Energy** â€” power for data centers, clean energy for AI compute
3. **Advanced manufacturing** â€” chip fabrication, equipment, materials
4. **Semiconductors** â€” design, fabrication, packaging, testing
5. **AI infrastructure** â€” compute, data centers, models
6. **Logistics** â€” shipping, supply chain mapping, protection

Pacific Forum analyst Akhil Ramesh: *"Economic security today is inseparable from control over energy, critical minerals, high-end manufacturing, and advanced models."*

## Strategic context

### Why it matters

Pax Silica formalizes the US strategy of building a **"trusted" parallel supply chain** that excludes China:

- **China produces** ~60% of rare earths, dominates mineral processing, and is building indigenous chip capacity
- **US export controls** (Oct 2022, Jan 2025) restricted China's access to advanced chips and equipment
- **CHIPS Act** ($52B) funds domestic US fabrication but can't cover the full global supply chain
- Pax Silica extends the logic: the **entire stack** (minerals â†’ chips â†’ AI) needs allied coordination

### Notably absent
- **China** â€” the implicit adversary
- **Taiwan** â€” conspicuously absent despite [[TSMC]]'s foundry dominance; likely due to diplomatic sensitivity (One China policy)
- **EU as a bloc** â€” individual European countries (Greece, UK, Netherlands) involved, not the EU itself
