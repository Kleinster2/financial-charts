# OFC 2026

**OFC 2026** (Los Angeles, March 17-20) — ~18,000 attendees, 706 exhibitors, sold-out floor. The optical networking industry's flagship conference became, by unanimous consensus, an AI infrastructure conference. An analyst at a media roundtable: "This has already changed from a telecom show to an AI show."

---

## Three new MSAs — standardizing AI optical interconnect

The biggest structural development: three multi-source agreements launched in the same week, collectively defining open standards for every layer of AI optical connectivity.

| MSA | Founded by | Purpose |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| OCI (Optical Compute Interconnect) | [[AMD]], [[Broadcom]], [[Meta]], [[Microsoft]], [[NVIDIA]], [[OpenAI]] | Open optical scale-up interconnect for AI clusters |
| XPO | [[Arista Networks]], others (40+ members) | External laser source architectures for CPO |
| Open CPX | [[Ciena]], [[Coherent]], [[Marvell]], Molex, Samtec, TeraHop | Optical engine specs for co-packaged/near-package interconnect |

The OCI MSA is the headline — six of the largest AI companies (including the two leading GPU designers, three hyperscalers, and one frontier lab) aligning on open optical scale-up interconnect. This signals the industry recognizes that electrical interconnect cannot scale to next-generation AI cluster sizes.

## Key vendor developments

### [[Marvell]] — full-stack AI connectivity

20+ demos spanning the entire AI connectivity stack. The message: Marvell owns the silicon layer from chip-to-chip through rack-to-rack.

| Product | Spec | Significance |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Aquila | 1.6T coherent-lite DSP | Data center interconnect |
| Ara | 1.6T PAM4 optical DSP | Industry-first 8×200G TRO |
| Die-to-die interconnect | 3nm | Chiplet connectivity |
| SerDes | PCIe 8.0 at 256GT/s | Next-gen I/O |
| CXL memory expansion | — | Memory disaggregation |
| COLORZ 800 | ZR/ZR+ DCI | Joint demo with [[Lumentum]] R300 Optical Circuit Switch |

### [[Coherent]] — all-in on CPO, $2B NVIDIA backing

Three distinct CPO architectures demonstrated — covering the full spectrum of integration approaches:

| Demo | Architecture |
|------|-------------|
| 6.4T (32×200G) socketed silicon photonics CPO | External laser source |
| Multimode VCSEL socketed CPO | Short-reach |
| 400G InP modulator on silicon | III-V on silicon |

Also showed 400mW CW lasers for CPO, 200G EMLs for 1.6T pluggables, and differential EMLs targeting 400G/lane for future 3.2T. Added to S&P 500 during the week (stock ~$247). The [[NVIDIA]] $2B investment is the anchor — NVIDIA betting on Coherent as the optical subsystem supplier for next-gen GPU clusters.

### [[Arista Networks]] — XPO launch

eXtra-dense Pluggable Optics (XPO) transceivers: 4× per-rack bandwidth vs OSFP, 75% smaller footprint. Mass production 2027 with 40+ MSA members. Targeting hyperscaler AI training networks specifically.

### [[Ciena]] — Vesta CPO + Hyper-Rail

Hyper-Rail Reconfigurable Line System: 32× density increase, 75% power reduction per rack. Vesta CPO pluggable connects directly to switch ASICs — bypassing the traditional transceiver cage entirely.

### [[Nokia]] — modular coherent transport, 2027 roadmap

Application-optimized suite: 1.6T and 2.4T pluggables, 3.2T "coherent lite" for campus, double-sided pluggables for CPO. Multi-rail in-line amplifier supporting 160 fiber pairs per rack (40× density improvement), shipping H2 2026. Targeting 70% TCO reduction. DSP + optical front-end vertical integration is the differentiator.

## Investor signal

The networking bottleneck thesis is now fully mainstream. Every major vendor is building specifically for AI cluster connectivity. The CPO/XPO standardization push — with hyperscaler backing from [[NVIDIA]], [[AMD]], [[Meta]], [[Microsoft]], [[OpenAI]], and [[Broadcom]] — de-risks the transition timeline from pluggable to co-packaged optics.
