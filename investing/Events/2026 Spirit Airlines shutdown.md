# 2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown

[[Spirit Airlines]] (Spirit Aviation Holdings, OTC: FLYYQ) ceased all operations effective Saturday May 2, 2026 after a $500M White House bailout collapsed in late-April negotiations. The collapse is the first major US-airline failure attributable to the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran war]]-driven jet fuel price shock, on top of structural distress that pre-dated the conflict.

## Why the bailout failed

The terms required: in exchange for a $500M federal bridge, the government would have received the overwhelming majority of Spirit's equity. A key creditor group rejected the package on the grounds that government nationalization would dilute existing creditors and impose operating constraints that defeated the recovery economics. The White House did not increase the package size, and Spirit had insufficient runway to negotiate alternative private financing once jet fuel costs continued to compound through April.

---

## Iran-war causal chain

Spirit's distress was structural — unprofitable since post-Covid travel collapse, two bankruptcies in less than a year. But the Feb 2026 emergence plan was financially modeled against pre-war jet fuel assumptions. Jet fuel surged from ~$80-90/bbl pre-war to $150-230/bbl through March 2026 (see [[Iran conflict airline disruption#Fuel cost shock|fuel cost shock]] and [[Jet fuel]]).

For an unhedged ultra-low-cost carrier with thin operating margins by design, fuel-cost flow-through is the binary risk:

- [[Bernstein]] estimate: fuel + related expenses ~35% of [[Ryanair]] revenue in 2025 (a hedged peer)
- ULCC business model relies on fuel cost being roughly stable — fare structures cannot rapidly absorb 2-3x cost shocks
- Spirit had minimal hedge coverage entering the war (recent emergence from bankruptcy → stripped balance sheet, no instruments)

The shutdown is the first US-airline-specific consequence of the Iran war, three months in.

| Consumer confidence | First "war made my flight disappear" headline of the conflict; reinforces [[Iran conflict airline disruption#Demand destruction risk|demand-destruction]] feedback loop |
