**Pershing Square UMG takeover bid** — On Apr 7, 2026, [[Bill Ackman]]'s [[Pershing Square]] submitted a non-binding takeover proposal for [[Universal Music Group]], valuing the company at ~€55.8B ($64.4B). The cash-and-stock offer would merge UMG with Pershing Square SPARC Holdings, creating a NYSE-listed entity eligible for S&P 500 inclusion.

---

## Deal terms

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Offer price | €30.40/share (~78% premium to Apr 2 close) |
| Enterprise value | ~€55.8B ($64.4B) |
| Cash component | €5.05/share (€9.4B total) |
| Stock component | 0.77 shares of New UMG per UMG share |
| Share cancellation | 17% of outstanding UMG shares |
| New UMG shares outstanding | 1.541B |
| Listing | NYSE (Nevada corporation) |
| Reporting | US GAAP |
| Expected close | Year-end 2026 |

**Structure:** UMG merges with Pershing Square SPARC Holdings. Cash funded by SPARC rights holders, debt, and proceeds from Pershing Square's [[Spotify]] stake.

---

## Ackman's thesis

1. UMG trades at a deep discount to intrinsic value
2. Overhang from [[Bolloré Group]]'s ~18% stake and associated regulatory disputes
3. Postponement of planned US listing depresses valuation
4. "Suboptimal" shareholder communication
5. US listing would unlock liquidity, broaden investor base, enable index inclusion

## Analyst commentary

**ING:** Called the bid non-binding and possibly likely to fail, but said it "has the merit of raising valid questions and making the case for dramatic changes." Flagged risk that UMG management could leave, given management's plans for ~€1B/year in emerging-market M&A.
