# Minamata Convention

The **Minamata Convention** on Mercury is an international treaty adopted January 19, 2013 (entered into force August 16, 2017) designed to protect human health and the environment from anthropogenic mercury emissions. Named after Minamata, Japan, where industrial mercury discharge caused decades of severe poisoning. Ratified by 140+ countries.
