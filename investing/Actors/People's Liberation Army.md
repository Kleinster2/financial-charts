# People's Liberation Army

**People's Liberation Army** — [[China]]'s armed forces, under the direct command of the Chinese Communist Party's Central Military Commission. Relevant to markets through [[Export controls]], tech transfer allegations, and sanctions risk for companies with alleged PLA ties.

---

## Synopsis

The PLA matters to the investing vault because Chinese military doctrine turns civilian technology markets into security-screening objects. Semiconductors, commercial satellites, AI, telecom modules, ports, drones, cyber tools, and cloud infrastructure are evaluated by US and allied policymakers through their potential PLA end use, not only through their stated commercial product category.

## May 2026: total war and systems confrontation

Howard Wang's 2026 RAND / Journal of Contemporary China paper, discussed with Manoj Kewalramani on Tracking People's Daily, defines China's "total war" concept as peacetime mobilization of civilian institutional advantages that can become warfighting capabilities during conflict. This is not the classic unlimited-war meaning. It is a systems-confrontation framework: the adversary's finance, sanctions, space services, information operations, industrial base, food, energy, and alliance cohesion are all part of the battlespace.

For markets, the useful distinction is between [[Military-civil fusion]] and the broader total-war frame. MCF brings civilian technology and talent into PLA modernization. Total war goes further: civilian systems themselves can be directed by the party center in parallel with PLA operations. That is the conceptual bridge from a "commercial" satellite, port, telecom module, software system, or crypto/payment rail to a sanctions or procurement problem.

Wang identified China SatNet as the cleanest practical case. The state created China SatNet in 2021 to build a proliferated LEO communications constellation with civilian broadband use cases, while PLA writing discusses the military utility of such a system against Starlink and inside a resilient ISR architecture. That supports the logic already visible in [[Chinese dual-use commercial satellite exports]]: commercial space is no longer just a market; it is a wartime infrastructure layer.

## Purges and readiness

Wang reads the 2024-2026 PLA purges as most plausibly rooted in real corruption, especially around Rocket Force and equipment development, rather than simply factional infighting. If the corruption touched missile quality, silo reliability, or nuclear force credibility, the market-relevant signal is not only internal CCP instability. It is uncertainty around whether China's strategic-deterrent and force-modernization claims are as operationally reliable as the procurement budget implies.

Short term, purges can damage readiness and expertise. Long term, a less corrupt PLA is a more capable military. The open question is whether Xi's repeated anti-corruption campaigns change incentives or simply punish successive cohorts after the same procurement structure reproduces graft.
