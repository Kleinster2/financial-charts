# Bill Gross

Co-founder of [[PIMCO]] and the most influential fixed-income investor of the late 20th century, known as the "Bond King." Ran the world's largest bond fund for over four decades before an acrimonious departure in 2014.

## Synopsis

Bill Gross co-founded Pacific Investment Management Company ([[PIMCO]]) in 1971 as a subsidiary of Pacific Mutual Life Insurance, and over 43 years built it into the world's dominant fixed-income franchise. At its peak, PIMCO managed ~$2T in assets, with Gross's flagship Total Return Fund alone reaching ~$270B -- the largest bond fund ever. His investment letters and "secular forum" outlook calls moved markets; a Gross call on Treasury yields or credit spreads was treated as gospel by institutional allocators.

The downfall was equally dramatic. Co-CEO [[Mohamed El-Erian]] departed in January 2014 amid reports of a toxic working relationship with Gross, followed by Gross's own abrupt exit in September 2014. He left for Janus Capital (now [[Janus Henderson]]), where he managed a small unconstrained bond fund that never regained significant AUM. The PIMCO Total Return Fund suffered ~$100B in outflows in the year surrounding his departure, proving that the franchise was partly a Gross personality cult but also that PIMCO's institutional infrastructure was robust enough to survive -- assets eventually stabilized under new management. Gross retired from Janus in 2019 and has since been known primarily for a bitter divorce (from Sue Gross), art collecting, and stamp collecting. For the vault, Gross matters as a cautionary tale about key-person risk in asset management and as the architect of the institutional bond market's modern structure.
