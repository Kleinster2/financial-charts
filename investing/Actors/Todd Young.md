Todd Young is a US Senator (R-Indiana, since 2017) and co-author of the CHIPS and Science Act (2022) alongside [[Chuck Schumer]] — the landmark $52B+ semiconductor subsidy bill designed to reshore chip manufacturing to the [[United States]]. The bipartisan partnership with Schumer on CHIPS was unusual in a polarized Senate and reflected Young's positioning as one of the most tech-fluent Republican senators. A former Marine intelligence officer with an MBA from the University of Chicago, Young has focused his legislative agenda on AI governance, semiconductor supply chain security, and US competitiveness vis-a-vis [[China]].

Active on AI policy through the Senate AI Working Group (co-chaired with Schumer, Martin Heinrich, and Mike Rounds), which produced the "Driving US Innovation in AI" roadmap in 2024. Spoke at the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] in May 2024. His policy influence extends beyond legislation: the CHIPS Act directly enabled [[TSMC]]'s Arizona fab construction, [[Intel]]'s Ohio expansion, and [[Samsung]]'s Taylor, Texas buildout.
