**[[AMI Labs]] (Jan 2026-):** Building [[World Models]] based on JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture), which LeCun proposed in 2022 at [[Meta]]'s FAIR lab. Focus on physics-aware AI trained on video, audio, and sensor data rather than text tokens. Raised **$1.03B** at **$3.5B pre-money valuation**. Investors include [[Jeff Bezos]], [[NVIDIA]], [[Samsung]], [[Temasek]], [[Eric Schmidt]].

**Why he left Meta:** [[Mark Zuckerberg]] brought in [[Alexandr Wang]] (28, ex-[[Scale AI]] CEO) to lead Meta's Superintelligence Labs. LeCun was forced to report to Wang after 600 FAIR-adjacent layoffs. LeCun publicly called Wang "young and inexperienced" and said: *"You certainly don't tell a researcher like me what to do."* Open-source vs. closed approach was the structural friction.

## Key contributions

**Convolutional Neural Networks (1989):**
- LeNet architecture for handwritten digit recognition
- Revolutionized computer vision
- Foundation for modern image AI

**Backpropagation:**
- Early form proposed during PhD (1987)
- Core algorithm for training neural networks

**Other:**
- DjVu image compression
- Lush programming language

## Views on AI

Vocal critic of AGI hype. Advocates for "world models" — AI that understands physics and causality rather than just pattern matching. Skeptical of LLM-only approaches to human-level AI.
