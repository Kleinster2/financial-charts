# Zhipu AI

Zhipu AI is a Chinese AI lab spun out of [[Tsinghua University]]'s KEG (Knowledge Engineering Group) lab, best known for the GLM (General Language Model) series. GLM 5 reached #3 on the OpenRouter leaderboard by token volume (1.03T tokens, +462% month-over-month, Feb 2026), demonstrating rapid commercial adoption. The company was founded in 2019 by Tang Jie and other Tsinghua researchers, making it one of the earliest Chinese LLM startups — predating the [[ChatGPT]]-triggered wave by several years.

## Position in Chinese AI

Zhipu occupies a distinct niche among Chinese AI labs. While [[DeepSeek]] focuses on reasoning efficiency and [[MiniMax]] on multimodal generation, Zhipu has prioritized enterprise deployment and API accessibility — its pricing is among the lowest in the Chinese market, driving the token-volume growth visible on OpenRouter. The Tsinghua pedigree gives it privileged access to China's top AI talent pipeline. Zhipu's open-source ChatGLM models have been widely adopted for Chinese-language fine-tuning, creating an ecosystem effect similar to Meta's Llama in the West.
