**iFood** — [[Brazil]]'s dominant food delivery platform (~80% market share), subsidiary of Netherlands-listed [[Prosus]]. ~R$100B annual order volume (2025) across food delivery and grocery. Facing first serious challenge from Chinese-backed rivals [[Keeta]] ([[Meituan]]) and [[99]] ([[Didi]]) after surviving [[Uber Eats]] (withdrew 2022) and [[99]]'s first attempt (withdrew 2023).

## Competitive position

With 200M+ users in a highly online market with mass [[Pix]] adoption and extensive motorcycle courier networks, iFood built a near-monopoly that two prior international challengers couldn't crack. The three-sided network effect (restaurants-consumers-couriers) is the moat. Restaurant and courier discontent with monopoly pricing is the vulnerability that Chinese entrants are exploiting with subsidized offers.

---

## Corporate espionage allegations (2025-2026)

iFood alleges a "co-ordinated effort" to steal business secrets coinciding with Chinese competitors' entry. Identified ~500 approaches to employees via [[LinkedIn]] by overseas consultancies offering hundreds of dollars for interviews on pricing strategies, margins, revenues, and technology. Police investigating at least 4 former employees for suspected data theft. See [[Chinese entry into Brazil food delivery 2025]].
