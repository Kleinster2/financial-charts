Cat Group — private Lebanese construction company founded in 1941. Built the western section of [[Saudi Arabia]]'s [[East-West Pipeline]] in the 1980s as the Cat Houston joint venture (with Houston Contracting Company). Projects span North and West Africa, [[Pakistan]], Somalia, and the Middle East: airports, roads, power stations, cement plants, hospitals, pipelines. Subsidiary [[Mothercat Limited]] handles mechanical, electrical, and pipeline work.

## April 2026 — pipeline enquiries

Christopher Bush told the FT (April 2, 2026) the company has received enquiries about new Gulf bypass pipelines even before the war. *"I have multiple different presentations on my desk."* Bush estimated replicating the East-West Pipeline today would cost at least $5B. Multi-country routes from [[Iraq]] through [[Jordan]], [[Syria]], or [[Turkey]] would cost $15-20B. Front-end engineering studies already exist for Iraq routes.
