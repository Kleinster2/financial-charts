**Rockwell Group** is a New York-based architecture and design firm founded in 1984 by David Rockwell, known for immersive hospitality, theater, and restaurant design. The firm has shaped some of the most recognizable dining and entertainment spaces in [[New York City]] and globally.

## Synopsis

Rockwell Group occupies a niche at the intersection of architecture, theater, and experiential design — the firm's signature is creating spaces where the physical environment tells a story. David Rockwell's background in theater (he designed the set for the 2002 Academy Awards ceremony and multiple Broadway productions) permeates the hospitality work: restaurants, hotels, and retail spaces are treated as stage sets with lighting, materials, and spatial flow designed to create emotional responses. The client list reads like a who's who of high-end hospitality — Nobu, TAO Group, W Hotels, and [[Mercer Street Hospitality]]'s Seahorse at the W Union Square (a 125-seat dining room).

For the vault, Rockwell Group matters as a signal of where capital flows in luxury hospitality. When a new restaurant or hotel project hires Rockwell, it signals serious investment in experiential positioning rather than cost-driven commodity dining. The firm's project pipeline is a leading indicator of confidence in high-end commercial real estate and hospitality markets, particularly in [[New York City]] and Las Vegas.
