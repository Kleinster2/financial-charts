#actor #payments #france

**Worldline** (Euronext: WLN) is a [[France|French]] payments and transactional services company, the largest payment processor in continental [[Europe]] by transaction volume. Formerly a subsidiary of [[Atos]], Worldline was spun off in 2014 and has since grown through acquisitions into a full-stack merchant acquiring, payment processing, and financial services platform.

## Synopsis

Worldline processes over 20 billion payment transactions annually across 30+ countries, serving merchants, banks, and public-sector clients. The company's growth was built on M&A — the transformative deals were Ingenico's terminals business (2020, ~EUR5.3B) and SIX Payment Services (2018, ~EUR2.3B), which gave Worldline scale in Southern Europe and Switzerland. But the acquisition spree left the company with integration debt, margin compression, and a stock that has been in freefall — down roughly 75% from its 2021 peak as organic growth disappointed and the Ingenico integration proved harder than modeled.

[[Credit Agricole|Credit Agricole]] acquired a 7% stake in January 2024, providing a strategic anchor but also signaling that Worldline needed a credibility boost with investors. The company competes with [[Adyen]] (Netherlands, premium positioning) and [[Nexi]] (Italy, similar mid-market profile) in European payments, but trades at a steep discount to both on revenue multiples. The bear case is that Worldline is stuck in the "legacy processor" bucket — too focused on physical terminals and bank partnerships while the market shifts to omnichannel and embedded payments. The bull case is that European payments consolidation is secular, the discount is excessive, and a strategic acquirer (private equity or a US processor like [[Fiserv]]) takes the company out.
