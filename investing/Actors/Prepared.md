**Prepared** was an AI-powered 911 communications platform acquired by [[Axon]] in October 2025. The technology reduced emergency call operator workload by 33% in pilot deployments by handling non-critical calls via AI, representing one of the first meaningful AI applications in public safety dispatch.

## Synopsis

Prepared built a platform that applied AI to the 911 dispatch workflow — one of the most high-stakes, high-volume, and chronically understaffed functions in US public services. The core technology used natural language processing to triage incoming emergency calls, routing non-critical calls (noise complaints, minor incidents, information requests) to AI-powered handling while flagging true emergencies for human operators. The 33% workload reduction in pilot programs addressed a real crisis: US 911 call centers face 20-30% vacancy rates and burnout-driven turnover that creates dangerous response time gaps.

[[Axon]]'s acquisition of Prepared in October 2025 — alongside its purchase of [[Carbyne]], another emergency communications platform — signals a strategic push to own the entire public safety technology stack from dispatch through body cameras to evidence management. For Axon investors, the acquisitions extend the company's TAM beyond hardware (Tasers, body cameras) into software-driven recurring revenue from the same municipal and law enforcement customer base. The AI dispatch layer is particularly interesting because it creates a data funnel: every 911 call processed generates training data that improves the model, and every municipality that adopts the platform becomes locked into Axon's ecosystem for interoperability reasons.
