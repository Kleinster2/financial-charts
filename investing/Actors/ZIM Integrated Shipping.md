## Business model

ZIM operates a global container shipping network with an asset-light strategy — chartering most of its fleet rather than owning vessels outright. This gives it flexibility to scale capacity up/down with freight rate cycles but means it captures less of the upside in a sustained rate spike (charter costs rise too).

Key trade lanes: Asia–US, Asia–Europe, intra-Asia, Mediterranean. ZIM has historically been a niche operator (smaller than Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM) but punches above its weight in specific routes.

## Iran conflict exposure

ZIM sits at the intersection of multiple conflict dynamics:

1. **Israeli identity** — as Israel's national shipping line, ZIM has been a Houthi target since 2023. The Iran conflict intensifies this risk.
2. **Red Sea / Bab el-Mandeb** — Houthi attacks forced ZIM to reroute around Cape of Good Hope, adding cost and transit time.
3. **Strait of Hormuz closure** — doesn't directly block ZIM's main trade lanes (Asia–US, Asia–Europe go through Suez/Cape, not Hormuz), but the broader shipping disruption and rate environment affects all carriers.
4. **Rate tailwind** — global shipping disruption generally benefits container lines through higher freight rates. ZIM rallied sharply during the 2024 Houthi crisis for this reason.
5. **Tanker vs container** — the Hormuz closure most directly affects oil/LNG tankers, not container ships. But the knock-on effects (insurance, crew risk, port congestion) cascade across all shipping.

Mar 3 close: **-3.2%** despite tanker rate spike — container lines are getting the risk-off treatment rather than the rate-tailwind bid, possibly because the Hapag-Lloyd acquisition caps upside.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Hapag-Lloyd acquisition at premium (~$35 implied vs $28 market)
- Rate environment strong amid multiple shipping disruptions
- Asset-light model means lower capex burden
- Massive dividend yield (~15%) while independent

Bear:
- Acquisition caps equity upside at deal price
- Israeli identity = persistent security/insurance risk in current conflict
- Container shipping is deeply cyclical; post-disruption rate normalization inevitable
- Deal may face regulatory scrutiny (competition review)
