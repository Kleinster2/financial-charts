# Jeanne Shaheen

**Jeanne Shaheen** is a Democratic US senator from New Hampshire, serving since 2009 and holding senior seats on the Armed Services and Foreign Relations committees.

## Synopsis

Shaheen is one of the Senate's key defense and foreign policy voices, with particular focus on [[NATO]] alliance cohesion, defense-industrial competitiveness, and sanctions enforcement. Her committee positions give her direct influence over defense authorization and foreign aid, making her a recurring presence in debates around [[Taiwan]] contingency planning, European security assistance, and the [[CHIPS Act]] implementation. She spoke at the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] in May 2024, reflecting the forum's bipartisan defense-tech orientation.

Before the Senate, Shaheen served as governor of New Hampshire (1997-2003), making her the first woman in US history elected both governor and senator. Her policy lane sits at the intersection of defense and economic competitiveness — she has pushed for reshoring defense supply chains, strengthening allied semiconductor capacity, and maintaining US technology export controls against [[China]]. For the investing vault, she matters as an indicator of bipartisan consensus on defense spending priorities and tech decoupling policy.
