# Lacoste

French sportswear and lifestyle brand founded 1933 by tennis champion René Lacoste and André Gillier. Iconic crocodile logo — one of the most recognized in fashion. ~€3B revenue (~60% of parent MF Brands Group's ~€5B total). 1,100+ stores in 98 countries, 8,200+ employees. Wholly owned by [[MF Brands Group]] (formerly [[Maus Frères]]), a Swiss family-held company that acquired full control in 2012 for ~€1B from the Lacoste family. MF Brands also owns [[Aigle]], [[Gant]], and [[Tecnifibre]]. CEO Thierry Guibert. Targeting €4B revenue. Debt-free with acquisition firepower.

## Ownership

Wholly owned by MF Brands Group (formerly Maus Frères SA), a Swiss family holding company founded 1892 by Ernest and Henri Maus. The Lacoste family (including Sophie and Philippe Lacoste, grandchildren of founder René) sold their remaining stakes by 2012. Sophie and Philippe Lacoste went on to acquire [[Fusalp]].

### Funding rounds

No external funding. Privately held by the Maus family since 2012 acquisition (~€1B). Debt-free.
