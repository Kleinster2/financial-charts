#actor #aluminum #materials #private

**Novelis** — World's largest producer of rolled [[Aluminum|aluminium]] products and the largest recycler of aluminium globally. Wholly-owned subsidiary of [[Hindalco Industries]] (Aditya Birla Group, [[India]]) since 2007. HQ Atlanta, Georgia. A central supplier to the North American auto industry — supplies aluminium body sheet for [[Ford]]'s F-Series pick-up trucks and other OEM platforms. A pair of fires at a Novelis plant in early 2026 — pre-dating the [[Operation Epic Fury|2026 Iran war]] — forced [[Ford]] to source replacement aluminium from overseas, exposing the company to 50% [[Section 232]] tariffs and adding ~$1B of cost.

## 2026 plant fires — Ford supply disruption

A pair of fires at a Novelis aluminium plant disrupted production of body sheet supplied to [[Ford]] in early 2026 — before the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]] began. Ford was forced to source aluminium from overseas while Novelis recovers, adding $1B of cost — including the hit from 50% [[Section 232]] tariffs on imported metal. Ford CFO [[Sherry House]] (Q1 2026 earnings, FT May 3 2026): "we were already seeing global industry shortages. Then you had the Middle East."

The Novelis disruption is one of the rare cases where a US producer outage — not a tariff or a geopolitical event — directly forced reliance on tariffed imports. The combination magnified the pass-through.

*Source: FT (Inagaki/Davies/Ash), May 3, 2026.*
