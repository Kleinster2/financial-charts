## Apr 24, 2026 — BDC asset quality and 2028-29 maturity wall

On Bloomberg Real Yield (Apr 24, 2026):

### Software concentration and the maturity wall

- Average 25% software concentration across BDCs.
- Software borrowers have largely refinanced — refinanced maturities now stack into 2028-29.
- Pinto expects the credit cycle to play out *slower* in fixed income than what equity markets have already priced.
- Watching billings and customer-retention numbers; no deterioration yet, but Q2/Q3 is where stress could surface.

### Asset quality bifurcation between BDC structures

| BDC structure | Troubled-borrower share (Apr 24) |
|---|---|
| Non-traded perpetual BDCs | 0.5% |
| Traded BDCs | 2.0% (down from 2.5%) |

Pinto's read: numbers are "asset-quality good" today and likely to *normalize / mean-revert* over time rather than spike massively. Q3-Q4 2026 is the window for a more meaningful move.

### Refinancing fork ahead

When the 2028-29 software-loan maturity wall hits, lenders face a binary: refinance the credits where the investment thesis still holds, or exit and let the company restructure. Companies that lose lender support will be the ones that exit.

### On retail in private credit

- Last five years have seen an estimated $200B–$400B of retail money come into private credit.
- Retail brings additional risk into the ecosystem — not just gathering, but care, education, and disclosure obligations.
- Expects more transparency, discipline, and direction post-crisis.

### On the Jon Gray ([[Blackstone]]) panel exchange

At a Moody's conference, Pinto described pulling Gray back from the upbeat private-credit pitch with the asset-quality deterioration angle. Gray acknowledged it but maintained the long-run return thesis.

*Source: Bloomberg Real Yield, Apr 24, 2026 — Scarlet Fu / Olivia Raimonde interview*
