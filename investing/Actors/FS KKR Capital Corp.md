**FS KKR Capital Corp** is a publicly traded business development company (BDC) jointly managed by FS Investments and [[KKR]]. Cut its quarterly dividend from $0.70 to $0.48 in February 2026 as non-accruals rose to 3.4%.

## Synopsis

FS KKR is one of the largest publicly traded BDCs, providing direct lending to middle-market companies -- the same space where private credit has experienced explosive growth. The joint management structure pairs FS Investments' origination platform with [[KKR]]'s credit underwriting, a combination that was supposed to deliver institutional-quality lending to a publicly traded vehicle. For several years the formula worked: FSK paid a generous dividend and traded near NAV, offering retail investors BDC yields of 12-14% with the perceived safety of KKR's brand.

The February 2026 dividend cut (31% reduction from $0.70 to $0.48) and rising non-accruals (3.4%) signal that the credit cycle has arrived for middle-market lending. The [[Medallia]] loan writedown -- a software company that was a PE-backed leveraged buyout -- is a specific example of how sponsor-backed loans that looked safe at origination can deteriorate when growth stalls and leverage bites. FSK's stress matters as a canary for the broader [[Private Credit]] market: if a KKR-managed BDC is cutting distributions, smaller and less well-managed private credit vehicles are likely in worse shape. The stock trades at a persistent discount to NAV, reflecting the market's view that reported book value overstates the true recovery on the loan book. For investors in [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]], FSK is exhibit A.
