# Seemann Composites

**Seemann Composites** and its sister company Materials Sciences Corporation (MSC) manufacture advanced composite structures for maritime defense applications — submarines, unmanned underwater vehicles (UUVs), unmanned surface vehicles (USVs), and strategic naval surface platforms. Based in Gulfport, Mississippi, the companies specialize in VARTM (vacuum-assisted resin transfer molding) processes for large-scale defense structures.

## Synopsis

Seemann/MSC represents the specialized composites layer of the defense supply chain that rarely gets investor attention but is structurally critical. Composite hulls and structures are lighter, corrosion-resistant, and have lower magnetic signatures than steel — essential properties for next-generation naval platforms, particularly in mine countermeasures vessels and autonomous underwater systems. The company's technology traces to the Seemann Composites Resin Infusion Molding Process (SCRIMP), a patented VARTM technique that became an industry standard for large marine structures.

[[Karman Holdings]] acquired Seemann and MSC in February 2026, expanding Karman's portfolio from aerospace/missile subsystems into maritime defense. The acquisition signaled Karman's strategy of rolling up niche defense component makers across domains — air, space, and now sea. Sid Charbonnet, president of both Seemann and MSC, joined Karman's leadership team. The deal extends Karman's reach into the growing autonomous maritime segment, where composite hull fabrication is a bottleneck for programs like the Navy's Large Unmanned Surface Vehicle (LUSV).
