FIA is the governing body of world motorsport, regulating safety standards, technical rules, and sporting codes for [[Formula 1]], the [[World Rally Championship]], and other series. Based in Paris, founded in 1904.

## Synopsis

The FIA's investment relevance lies in its regulatory power over [[Formula 1]], a sport that has become a major media and commercial property since [[Liberty Media]] acquired the commercial rights in 2017 for $4.4B. The FIA controls the technical regulations that determine competitive balance — cost caps, engine formulas, aerodynamic rules — which in turn drive constructor spending, sponsor allocations, and team valuations. The 2026 engine regulation change (introducing new power unit rules with increased electrical component) is restructuring the competitive order and creating entry points for new manufacturers like [[Audi]] (via Sauber) and [[Ford]] (partnership with [[Red Bull]]).

Under president Mohammed Ben Sulayem (elected 2021), the FIA has faced governance controversies including clashes with [[Liberty Media]] over commercial revenue sharing and allegations of interference in race direction. The tension between the FIA as a sporting regulator and Liberty Media as commercial operator is structural — the FIA controls the rules but Liberty controls the money. For investors in F1-adjacent assets (Liberty Media, team sponsors, circuit operators), FIA regulatory decisions on cost caps and calendar expansion are material inputs.
