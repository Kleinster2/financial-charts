Pipedream was an AI agent builder and workflow automation platform enabling developers to connect APIs, automate processes, and build integrations. Acquired by [[Workday]] to bolster its agentic AI capabilities.

## Synopsis

Pipedream built a developer-focused platform for API integration and workflow automation — essentially a programmable middleware layer where users could connect hundreds of SaaS applications, trigger event-driven workflows, and deploy serverless functions without managing infrastructure. The product resonated with developers who needed to automate repetitive tasks across tools like [[Slack]], [[GitHub]], [[Salesforce]], and databases. The platform processed millions of workflows monthly and built a community of developers who shared and remixed integration templates.

[[Workday]]'s acquisition of Pipedream fits the enterprise software industry's broader push toward agentic AI — the idea that AI agents will autonomously execute multi-step business processes rather than simply answering questions. Workday's core HCM and financial management platforms generate enormous volumes of structured data and repetitive workflows (payroll processing, expense approvals, workforce planning) that are natural targets for agent-based automation. Pipedream's API integration layer and workflow engine provide the plumbing for Workday agents to interact with the broader enterprise software stack. The acquisition signals that the agentic AI buildout requires not just model capability but integration infrastructure — the ability to reliably trigger actions across systems.
