# Jessica Alba

Jessica Alba is an actress and entrepreneur who founded [[Honest Company]] in 2011, building it into one of the first celebrity-to-consumer brand successes before its troubled 2021 IPO. She took [[Honest Company]] public at a $1.4B valuation in May 2021, but the stock collapsed to ~$200M market cap by 2025, making it a cautionary tale in the [[Influencer-to-brand playbook]]. Alba stepped back from day-to-day operations as CEO was handed to professional management, though she remains a board member and the brand's public face.

Alba's career arc illustrates both the potential and limits of celebrity brand-building. She identified a genuine market gap (clean/non-toxic baby products) early, raised venture capital from institutional investors, and scaled to $346M in revenue — a real business, not a vanity project. But the transition from founder-led startup to public company exposed weaknesses in product quality, supply chain management, and the lack of durable pricing power that brand affinity alone cannot provide. Her experience is frequently cited alongside [[Kim Kardashian]]'s [[SKIMS]] ($5B valuation, still private) as a contrast in celebrity brand execution.
