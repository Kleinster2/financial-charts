# Speechify

AI text-to-speech platform founded in 2017 by brothers Cliff and Erix Weitzman. Revenue ~$17.6M (Jul 2025), ~160 employees. The app converts text to natural-sounding speech across 1,000+ voices in 60+ languages, used by 20M+ users. Won Apple Design Award at WWDC 2025. Celebrity partnerships include Gwyneth Paltrow (official voice + Goop AI) and Snoop Dogg (official partner). Competes with [[ElevenLabs]], Resemble.AI, and others in the AI voice/TTS market.

## Synopsis

Speechify built a consumer-facing TTS product that Apple called "a critical resource that helps people live their lives." Founded by Cliff Weitzman — who has dyslexia and built the app to help himself read — the company grew to 20M+ users and ~$17.6M revenue by mid-2025. The celebrity partnership strategy is distinctive: Gwyneth Paltrow as the official voice positions Speechify in the lifestyle/wellness space (Goop AI integration), while Snoop Dogg brings mainstream cultural reach. In a market where [[ElevenLabs]] dominates the developer/enterprise segment (voice cloning, API), Speechify focuses on the consumer accessibility use case — turning any text (articles, PDFs, emails, books) into natural-sounding audio. The Apple Design Award validates the product quality. The question is whether a consumer TTS app can compete long-term against ElevenLabs and native TTS features increasingly built into iOS, Android, and browsers.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- 20M+ users with consumer-grade UX; Apple Design Award validation
- Celebrity partnerships drive brand awareness at low cost
- Accessibility use case is defensible and growing (neurodiversity awareness)
- Revenue growing; ~$17.6M run rate on ~160 person team = efficient

**Bear:**
- TTS is becoming a commodity — Apple, Google, OpenAI all building native TTS
- [[ElevenLabs]] dominates the enterprise/developer segment with superior voice quality
- Consumer willingness to pay for TTS may decline as free alternatives improve
- No disclosed funding rounds or valuation — financial trajectory unclear
