# Nathan Lambert

AI researcher and post-training lead at the [[Allen Institute for AI]] (Ai2). Founder of the [[ATOM Project]] (American Truly Open Models), an initiative to establish US leadership in open-weight AI models. Writer of the [Interconnects](https://www.interconnects.ai) newsletter on Substack — one of the most widely read technical AI substacks — and host of the *Interconnects Interviews* podcast covering technical AI trends.

Previously a research scientist at [[Hugging Face]], and before that worked at [[Google|DeepMind]] and Facebook AI Research. His team at Ai2 coined the term "RLVR" (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards) in the Tulu 3 work before [[DeepSeek]] popularized the approach. On ATOM's motivation: "July 2025, we had four or five DeepSeek-caliber Chinese open-weight models and zero from the US. That was the moment when I was like, I guess I have to spend energy on this because nobody else is gonna do it."

Vault-relevant collaboration / guest network: regular podcast appearances on Latent Space (RLHF 201, with [[Swyx]] / [[Alessio Fanelli]]), [[SuperDataScience]] / Jon Krohn (Episode 791 on RLHF, June 2024), the AI Summer podcast (January 2025), and Tim Lee's [Understanding AI](https://www.understandingai.org/). Educational publishing footprint: long-running RLHF Resources page at [interconnects.ai/p/rlhf-resources](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/rlhf-resources) is a canonical learning index for the field — frequently cited as the field's reference compilation rather than a single bound book.
