# FinecoBank

Italian digital bank and brokerage platform, listed on Borsa Italiana (FBK.MI). One of Europe's largest direct banks by assets under administration, combining current accounts, trading, and wealth advisory on a single integrated platform.

---

## Synopsis

FinecoBank built its competitive position by being early to digital banking in Italy — a market where traditional banks (UniCredit, Intesa Sanpaolo) were slow to modernize. The platform offers a fully integrated experience: banking (deposits, payments), self-directed trading (equities, ETFs, bonds, options), and managed wealth advisory through a network of ~2,800 financial advisors. This three-legged model is the moat — customers who bank, trade, and get advice through Fineco have much higher switching costs than those using a standalone brokerage or neobank. AUA has grown past EUR 120B, with net inflows consistently positive even in volatile markets.

The investment angle is Fineco as a European fintech compounder. Operating margins run 50%+ because the digital platform scales without branch costs, and the advisory network is commission-based (advisors are independent contractors, not employees). The stock trades at a significant premium to European bank peers, reflecting the market's recognition that Fineco is really a tech platform with a banking license rather than a bank with an app. The risk is [[AI disintermediation]] — if AI-powered financial advice commoditizes the advisory layer, Fineco's high-margin advisory fees (the bulk of revenue) come under pressure. UniCredit's 2023 sale of its remaining Fineco stake removed the overhang of a controlling shareholder, leaving the company fully independent.
