# Shenyang Institute of Automation

Chinese Academy of Sciences institute in Shenyang that became one of the foundational institutions for Chinese robotics and automation. Its role in the investing vault is not as a standalone commercial security, but as the research and industrialization base behind China's robotics lineage.

## Role in Chinese robotics

SIA is the institutional node behind China's first industrial robot prototype in 1980 and the SZJ-1 teach-and-playback industrial manipulator in 1982, according to the Chinese Academy of Sciences. Under Jiang Xinsong, the institute pushed robotics into national science planning and then into the 863 Program's intelligent-robot agenda.

The institute's work went beyond manipulator prototypes. Its programs included autonomous mobile robots, undersea vehicles including HR-01 and CR-01, arc-welding robots, precision instrumentation, computer control, and factory automation systems. That breadth is why SIA belongs in the robotics sector map: it shows Chinese robotics as an accumulated controls / automation / integration capability, not just a humanoid startup boom.

---

## Commercialization path

People's Daily reported in July 2000 that Siasun Robot & Automation was set up in Shenyang with SIA as the largest shareholder, transferring the institute's industrial robot R&D department, prototype factory, and more than 200 researchers into a market-facing company. That makes Siasun the commercialization leg of the SIA / CAS robotics stack.

This is the important pattern for investing work: the commercial surface can appear later, while the real capability formation happened inside a state institute over multiple decades.
