# LoongUAV

Private Chinese tactical drone and loitering munition manufacturer. Founded 2022, incorporated in [[Hong Kong]], mainland operations. The company represents [[China]]'s push into the mass-producible expendable strike drone category — the same class as [[Iran]]'s [[Shahed-136]] — but with significantly better specs on paper.

---

## Synopsis

LoongUAV is a private, opaquely structured Chinese drone maker that burst onto the scene at AirShow China 2022, claiming $500M+ in orders from 12 countries at its debut. The company makes everything from FPV bombers to a full MALE UAV (LOONG 15), but its strategic significance centers on the LOONG M9 loitering munition — a 200 kg, 1,620 km-range expendable strike drone that slots into the same category as [[Iran]]'s [[Shahed-136]] / [[Russia]]'s Geran-2 but with substantially better guidance (dual-seeker, frequency-hopping, anti-jamming GNSS) and 3x the warhead (50 kg vs ~30-40 kg). Testing began December 2025; publicly unveiled at World Defense Show 2026 in Riyadh.

The company is not investable — private, no known VC/PE funding, no listing, no ADR. Key executives are undisclosed. The $500M orders claim from a PR Newswire release has not been independently verified. But the product line is real and the M9 fills a gap in the global arms market: a Chinese-origin, mass-producible loitering munition with better specs than the Shahed at a likely competitive price point. If even a fraction of the claimed export interest materializes, LoongUAV becomes a meaningful vector for Chinese drone proliferation — separate from the state-owned [[AVIC]] / Wing Loong ecosystem.

The key risk is that "private" Chinese defense companies often have opaque state connections, and LoongUAV's sudden emergence with a full product line and claimed $500M in orders raises questions about whether this is genuinely private or a front for state-directed arms exports with plausible deniability.

## Evolution

The story of LoongUAV is the story of China's drone proliferation going private — or at least appearing to.

- 2022 — LoongUAV appeared at AirShow China 2022 in Zhuhai with a full product line, claiming to have secured $500M+ in orders from 12 countries including [[Russia]], [[Ukraine]], [[UAE]], [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Brazil]], South Africa, Armenia, Yemen, Uzbekistan, Zambia, and Jordan. The claim came via PR Newswire and has never been independently verified. For a company founded the same year to debut with this scale of orders and product breadth raises obvious questions about how long the actual R&D had been underway and under whose auspices.

- 2022-2025 — The company claims to have scaled to 500+ R&D staff, 20 factories, and 200,000+ drones/year production capacity. No revenue figures, no funding disclosures, no named executives. The opacity is notable — even by Chinese private defense company standards, the lack of any named leadership is unusual.

- December 2025 — The LOONG M9 loitering munition entered testing. At 200 kg / 1,620 km range / 50 kg warhead, this is a materially more capable system than the [[Shahed-136]] (which weighs ~200 kg with a smaller ~30-40 kg warhead and ~2,500 km range but inferior guidance). The M9's dual-seeker and anti-jamming GNSS represent a generation beyond the Shahed's simple INS + GNSS.

- February 2026 — LoongUAV unveiled the M9 and broader product line at WDS 2026 in Riyadh. The venue is strategic — [[Saudi Arabia]] is the largest Middle Eastern arms buyer and has been diversifying away from exclusive US/UK dependence. The Saudi market was already open to [[China|Chinese]] MALE drones via [[AVIC]]'s Wing Loong.

## Distinction from AVIC / Wing Loong

LoongUAV is not connected to [[AVIC]] or the Wing Loong program. The name overlap is coincidental — "Loong" (龙) means dragon in Chinese. Key differences:

| | LoongUAV | AVIC Chengdu UAS (688297.SS) |
|---|---------|------|
| Ownership | Private (HK) | State-owned (AVIC subsidiary) |
| Founded | 2022 | 2007 |
| Products | Tactical, FPV, loitering munitions | MALE/HALE (Wing Loong 1/2/3) |
| Listed | No | Shanghai STAR Market |
| Market cap | Unknown | ~CNY 29.5B (~$4B) |
| Export model | Direct commercial | Government-to-government |

The distinction matters for proliferation: state-owned AVIC exports are subject to Chinese government approval and tend to go to allied governments. A "private" company can theoretically sell to anyone willing to buy — including non-state actors and countries that Beijing wants to arm without official fingerprints.

## Sector context

The broader Chinese military drone market is projected at ~$4.68B (2025) growing to $8.18B (2030), with ~70,000 drone companies in [[China]]. LoongUAV occupies the tactical/expendable segment alongside ZIYAN, while [[AVIC]] dominates MALE/HALE. The market is bifurcating: expensive, reusable platforms for major militaries vs. cheap, expendable systems for asymmetric warfare — and [[China]] is now competing in both tiers.

The M9's significance is strategic: it gives [[China]] a competitive offering in the expendable strike drone category that [[Iran]]'s Shahed currently dominates. If [[China]] enters this market at scale, it could dramatically expand the number of actors with access to precision-guided, long-range loitering munitions — with implications for regional conflicts from the Middle East to Africa to Southeast Asia.
