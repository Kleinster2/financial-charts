**PieceMakers Technology** is a Taiwanese semiconductor company focused on customized HBM/DRAM for edge AI applications, with [[Nanya Technology]] as its strategic investor holding approximately 38%.

## Synopsis

PieceMakers represents a niche bet within the [[HBM]] supply chain — rather than competing head-on with [[SK Hynix]], [[Samsung]], and [[Micron]] in the datacenter HBM market (where capacity is pre-sold to [[NVIDIA]] and hyperscalers), PieceMakers is targeting customized ultra-high-bandwidth memory for edge AI devices. The thesis is that as AI inference moves from the cloud to devices (phones, cars, robots, industrial equipment), memory architectures will need to be optimized for different power/bandwidth tradeoffs than datacenter HBM, creating an opening for specialized players.

[[Nanya Technology]]'s NT$660M investment for ~38% in December 2024 is the anchor of PieceMakers' credibility. Nanya is [[Taiwan]]'s third-largest DRAM maker (after [[TSMC]]-affiliated companies) and has struggled to compete in commodity DRAM against Korean giants. The PieceMakers investment represents Nanya's attempt to leapfrog into a higher-value segment by backing a startup with customized designs rather than trying to match Samsung and SK Hynix at scale. The risk is execution: edge AI memory is a nascent market with uncertain demand curves, and PieceMakers has no production revenue yet — this is pre-revenue R&D backed by a strategic investor's bet on where memory demand migrates.
