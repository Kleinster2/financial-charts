**Al Jazeera** is a [[Qatar]]-funded global news network founded in 1996. Operates Arabic and English-language channels plus the AJ+ digital platform. A perennial diplomatic flashpoint -- shutting Al Jazeera was among the 13 demands in the 2017 [[GCC]] blockade of Qatar.

## Synopsis

Al Jazeera functions simultaneously as a news organization, a soft-power instrument for [[Qatar]]'s foreign policy, and a regular irritant to regional governments that would prefer less coverage. Founded with a $137M grant from Qatar's emir and staffed initially by journalists from a cancelled BBC Arabic TV venture, it became the dominant Arabic-language news network within a decade. The network's coverage of the 2003 Iraq War and the Arab Spring uprisings cemented its global reputation -- and its status as a target. [[Saudi Arabia]], [[UAE]], Bahrain, and [[Egypt]] made Al Jazeera's closure one of 13 demands during the 2017 Qatar blockade.

The investment lens is indirect but important. Al Jazeera's editorial decisions move markets in the Gulf: its coverage of dissent in [[Saudi Arabia]], its reporting on the [[Abraham Accords]], and its Gaza war coverage have all triggered diplomatic responses that affected sovereign credit spreads, capital flows, and the operating environment for Gulf-listed companies. [[Israel]]'s ban on Al Jazeera's operations (2024) during the Gaza war was a new escalation. For investors in Gulf equities or sovereign debt, Al Jazeera coverage is a leading indicator of intra-GCC tension. The network itself is not a commercial entity -- it runs on Qatari state funding with no advertising revenue model to speak of -- but its editorial posture is a real-time readout of Doha's diplomatic priorities.
