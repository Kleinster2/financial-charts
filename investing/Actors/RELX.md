## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Subscription/recurring revenue model
- Data moats in legal and scientific
- High margins, cash generative
- Essential infrastructure for professions

**Bear:**
- AI disruption of legal research workflows
- Open access pressure on Elsevier
- High valuation historically

---

## February 2026: Claude Cowork selloff

**Feb 3-4, 2026:** Stock fell **-14%** — worst single-day move since 1988 — on [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|Claude Cowork launch]]. Stock nearly halved from 2025 peak.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Two-day decline | -14% |
| Historical context | Worst since 1988 |
| Distance from peak | Nearly -50% |
| Catalyst | Claude Cowork legal automation |

**Why RELX is exposed:**
- LexisNexis competes directly with AI legal research tools
- Core value prop: helping lawyers find relevant cases faster
- AI agents can potentially replicate this workflow
- High multiple left significant compression room

**Bull case:** Proprietary legal databases are the moat. AI needs authoritative data sources — RELX owns them.
