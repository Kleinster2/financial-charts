**NetJets** is the world's largest private jet company, a wholly owned subsidiary of [[Berkshire Hathaway]] since 1998. It operates a fleet of ~800 aircraft through fractional ownership and jet card programs, serving ultra-high-net-worth individuals and corporations who buy shares of specific aircraft rather than owning outright.

## Synopsis

The fractional ownership model that NetJets pioneered (founded as Executive Jet Aviation in 1964, renamed NetJets in 2002) solved a specific problem: private jet access without the capital commitment, crew management, and maintenance overhead of whole aircraft ownership. Customers purchase a 1/16th to 1/2 share of a specific aircraft type and receive a guaranteed number of flight hours annually, with NetJets managing scheduling, pilots, maintenance, and logistics. The model works because fleet utilization across hundreds of owners is far more efficient than individual ownership — when one owner's plane would sit idle, another's need fills the gap. [[Warren Buffett]] bought NetJets for [[Berkshire Hathaway]] in 1998 for $725M after being a customer himself; he famously quipped that the purchase was the "Tiffany's of aviation."

NetJets' financial performance within Berkshire is not separately reported in detail, but the subsidiary benefited enormously from the post-COVID demand surge for private aviation — charter and fractional demand spiked as wealthy individuals avoided commercial flying, and wait lists for fractional shares extended to months. The 2024 order for 250 [[Embraer]] Praetor 500 midsize jets (with options for 250 more) is the largest single business jet order in history and signals confidence in sustained demand. The competitive landscape includes [[Flexjet]] (owned by [[Directional Aviation]]), [[Wheels Up]] (public, SPAC-era casualty), and charter brokerages like VistaJet and Sentient Jet. NetJets' scale advantage — largest fleet, most airports served, guaranteed availability within hours — creates a network effect that smaller competitors struggle to match.
