**Ding Xuexiang** (丁薛祥, born September 13, 1962) — First-ranked Vice Premier of China and sixth-ranked member of the Politburo Standing Committee. Xi Jinping's former chief of staff who rose from a few months working together in Shanghai to the inner circle of Chinese power.

## Background

**Education:**
- BE, Northeast Heavy Machinery Institute (now Yanshan University), 1982
- MS, Fudan University School of Management

**Early career:**
- Born in Nantong, Jiangsu
- Started at Shanghai Research Institute of Materials (1982)
- Rose through Shanghai municipal government

**Xi relationship:** Worked for Xi in 2007 during Xi's brief tenure as Shanghai Party Secretary. Despite only months together, impressed Xi with administrative skills and low-profile personality.

## Assessment

VOA on his nomination: "Loyal and strong in coordination, but lacking experience in macroeconomic management and other people's livelihood affairs."

**Strength:** Administrative/organizational capacity, Xi's trust
**Weakness:** No prior economic policy experience before vice premiership
