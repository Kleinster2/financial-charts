# Hindalco

## Synopsis

[[Hindalco]] (Hindalco Industries Limited; NSE: HINDALCO; BSE: 500440) is the [[Aditya Birla Group]] flagship metals company — India's largest aluminium producer, largest single-location copper smelter operator (the Birla Copper Dahej complex, Gujarat, 500,000 tpa), and parent of [[Novelis]], the world's largest flat-rolled aluminium producer (acquired 2007). The integrated structure runs from Indian bauxite mining through alumina refining, primary aluminium smelting, and downstream rolling, along with a parallel copper-anode-and-rod franchise that anchors the broader Indian non-ferrous metals sector. FY 2025 (year ending March 2025) was a record year on every consolidated metric — revenue ₹2,38,496 cr (~$28.7B, +10% YoY), PAT ₹16,002 cr (~$1.93B, +58%), Q4 PAT ₹5,284 cr (+67%), Novelis FY 2025 revenue $17.1B (+5.6%) — reflecting both pricing leverage on the LME aluminium uptick and operational pull-through from the Aditya Birla \$10B 5-year capex plan that the firm announced in August 2025.

The investing-vault relevance runs through three threads. First, [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]]: Hindalco's Novelis subsidiary is the upstream-supply choke point that Ford's Q1 2026 commodity guidance cited as the trigger for the firm's ~$1bn aluminium-tariff substitution cost. Second, [[Aluminum]]: Hindalco is the largest direct beneficiary of the +16% LME re-rating documented in the Iran-war commodity-shock thread, with FY 2025 aluminium upstream EBITDA +78% YoY (₹16,262 cr) and downstream EBITDA +16% (₹633 cr). Third, [[Big Law transformation|Cluster]]: HINDALCO.NS clusters tightest with [[Vedanta|VEDL.NS]] in the Indian non-ferrous pair (1Y r = 0.73), a sub-cluster within the broader Indian metals cohort that splits at threshold 0.4 from the steel sub-cluster (TATASTEEL + JSWSTEEL).

## Corporate structure and operating segments

Hindalco operates four reportable segments through FY 2025:

| Segment | Geographic focus | Products | FY 2025 EBITDA contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aluminium upstream | India | Bauxite mining, alumina refining, primary aluminium smelting | ₹16,262 cr (+78% YoY) — record |
| Aluminium downstream | India | Aluminium rolled products (sheets, foils), extrusions | ₹633 cr (+16% YoY) — record |
| Copper | India (Dahej) | Copper anode and rod; CC rod | ₹3,025 cr (+16% YoY) — record |
| Novelis (consolidated) | US, Canada, EU, Brazil, Asia | Flat-rolled aluminium for cans, autos, building, specialty | Disclosed at Novelis level — $17.1B FY 2025 revenue |

The four-segment structure means Hindalco's revenue mix is roughly 80% aluminium (upstream + downstream + Novelis combined), 20% copper. Novelis itself contributes >70% of consolidated revenue in USD-converted terms — meaning Hindalco's reported INR-denominated revenue carries substantial USD/EUR/CAD currency exposure passed through Novelis's downstream regional pricing.

## Iran-war commodity transmission (2026)

Hindalco's structural position in [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] runs in two directions. As an upstream beneficiary, Hindalco captures the +16% LME aluminium re-rating in its primary aluminium pricing pass-through — visible in FY 2025 upstream EBITDA +78% YoY. As a downstream supplier through Novelis, Hindalco is the choke point that Ford's Q1 2026 commodity guidance cited: 2026 plant fires at Novelis-operated facilities forced [[Ford]] (and other Big Three OEMs) into overseas-sourced aluminium that triggered Section 232 50% tariff exposure, contributing ~$1bn of Ford's $2bn 2026 commodity hit.

The dual-direction exposure means Hindalco's FY 2026 (year ending March 2026) and FY 2027 results will reflect both the upside (continued aluminium pricing leverage) and the downside (Novelis customer compensation discussions, supply-chain remediation capex, and any pass-through pricing pressure from US tariff exposure). Q1 FY 2026 results (June 2025 quarter, reported ~August 2025) and onward are the working signal for whether the Iran-war commodity-shock thread sustains as a tailwind or whether Novelis-side supply-chain costs offset the upstream benefit.

## August 2025 capex plan

In August 2025 Hindalco announced a $10B five-year investment program, with the majority allocated to upstream aluminium and copper smelting expansion. The headline project is the Birla Copper Dahej smelter expansion: a 300,000-tpa addition on top of the existing 500,000-tpa unit, which on completion will make Dahej the largest copper smelting complex in the world outside China. Other components include further aluminium-smelting capacity and downstream rolled-products investments.

The capex plan positions Hindalco as the consolidator of Indian non-ferrous capacity at a moment when Western downstream supply chains are seeking [[China]]-alternative sourcing for aluminium and copper. The Dahej expansion specifically is an Indian-sovereign play on the post-2024 US-China copper-refining decoupling thread, paralleling [[Indonesia]]'s nickel-refining position in the EV battery supply chain.
