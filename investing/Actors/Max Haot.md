## Q1 2026 — CLD restructuring debate

In the Apr 2026 [[Space Capital]] interview, Haot articulated Vast's industry-side response to NASA's CLD restructuring proposal: the original 2018 commercialization framework (NASA as customer, not owner) is what unlocked ~$4B in private capital across the candidate field, and reverting to a NASA-owned core module risks both the deployed capital and NASA's standing as a reliable anchor customer for future commercial space programs. Vast's argument: the existing CLD budget can support two commercial winners under the original framework, with crew utilization shared across both stations. Once NASA commits as a customer, Japan, Europe, UAE, and Saudi Arabia follow as additional customers — "and so it's a growing market, and we need that market to be able to successfully transition to fully commercial."

See [[Vast Space]] for the company-level position and [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] for the program structure.
