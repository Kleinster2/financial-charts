**A&O Shearman** is a global law firm formed from the May 2024 merger of Allen & Overy (founded 1930, London) and Shearman & Sterling (founded 1873, New York), creating one of the largest legal practices in the world with over 3,500 lawyers across 40+ offices.

## Synopsis

The merger combined Allen & Overy's strength in European capital markets, banking, and regulatory work with Shearman & Sterling's deep roots in US M&A, project finance, and antitrust — producing a transatlantic firm with credible top-tier practices on both sides of the Atlantic. The deal was driven by the same consolidation pressures reshaping professional services: global clients demand seamless cross-border coverage, and mid-tier firms without US scale were losing share to the "Magic Circle + US elite" convergence. A&O Shearman now competes directly with [[Freshfields]], [[Clifford Chance]], [[Linklaters]], and the US white-shoe firms ([[Sullivan & Cromwell]], [[Cravath]]) for the largest cross-border mandates.

For the vault, A&O Shearman matters as an early and aggressive adopter of legal AI. The firm was the first major law firm client of [[Harvey]], the legal AI startup (December 2022), deploying it for document review, contract analysis, and legal research. This positioned A&O Shearman as a case study in how AI transforms professional services economics — if AI tools can handle 30-40% of associate-level work, the leverage model that drives law firm profitability (partners billing at $1,500+/hour atop associate teams at $500-800/hour) gets restructured. The firm's willingness to move early on AI suggests management sees it as a competitive advantage rather than a margin threat.
