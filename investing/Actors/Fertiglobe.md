# Fertiglobe

Joint venture between [[ADNOC]] and [[OCI]] (pre-2024), now ADNOC-controlled, producing nitrogen fertilizer across the MENA region. Listed on Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange (ticker FERTIGLB). Operates across the [[United Arab Emirates|UAE]], [[Egypt]], and [[Algeria]]. ADNOC acquired [[OCI]]'s 50% stake in 2024 as part of OCI's $11.2B strategic dismantlement, making Fertiglobe a core piece of ADNOC's downstream chemical strategy.

## Iran conflict exposure

Fertiglobe's UAE and Egyptian assets are partially [[Strait of Hormuz]]-exposed. UAE-origin cargoes exit the Gulf through Hormuz; Egyptian EBIC output exits via the Mediterranean and is less directly affected by the blockade. The 2026 [[Nitrogen Trap]] crisis has lifted nitrogen prices sharply — Fertiglobe is a beneficiary on realized pricing to the extent its cargoes can still ship, but a loser on volumes where Hormuz blocks access. Algerian production routes via Mediterranean ports and is unaffected by the chokepoint directly.

---

## Strategic context

The 2024 acquisition by [[ADNOC]] fits Abu Dhabi's broader downstream strategy — controlling value-added chemical output from its hydrocarbon base rather than selling raw gas or crude. Fertiglobe gives ADNOC a platform for blue and green ammonia pilots, positioning it for the emerging ammonia-as-energy-carrier market (shipping fuel, hydrogen transport, power co-firing). The MENA production base is competitively positioned on natural gas cost vs. European or Chinese producers, though Hormuz routing risk is the structural vulnerability.
