#actor #ai #crypto #private #usa

**Venice AI** — Privacy-focused [[ChatGPT]]-style chat and image-generation platform. Founded by [[Erik Voorhees]] (ShapeShift founder) in 2024; Teana Baker-Taylor (ex-[[Circle]] VP of Policy) as COO. Does not retain user data; inference runs through encrypted proxies to the [[Morpheus]] decentralized GPU network on [[Arbitrum]]. Launched VVV token on [[Base]] (Coinbase L2) January 27, 2025 — now at ~$417M market cap (April 14, 2026). Introduced DIEM secondary token in August 2025 as tokenized perpetual AI compute credit. Positioning: "private and uncensored" — structural alternative to [[OpenAI]]/[[Anthropic]] models that pairs [[Crypto]] payments + web3 infrastructure with open-weight LLMs.

Venice and [[Ridges AI]] are two different go-to-market expressions of the same "decentralized AI" thesis. Venice is a consumer chat product; Ridges is an autonomous coding-agent market. Venice's token does revenue-funded buybacks and burns; Ridges' SN62 alpha token is pure emissions. Both are betting that centralized AI's cost structure and data practices create a durable opening for a token-incentivized alternative.

## How it works

User input is sent through an encrypted proxy server to decentralized GPU compute on the [[Morpheus]] network. The AI model returns output through the same encrypted channel. Venice itself has no server-side record of the conversation, no account-linked history, no training-data harvesting. Web3 infrastructure is used for:

- Local browser storage for chat history (never on Venice's servers)
- End-to-end encryption for inference routing
- [[Crypto]] payments and token-gated tiers
- Permissionless access (no account required for base tier)

Model library includes open-weight LLMs ([[Llama]], [[Qwen]], [[DeepSeek]], and others) plus image-generation models like [[Flux]] and [[Stable Diffusion]]. Model selection is user-controlled — Venice does not route to a single in-house model.

Staking 100 VVV unlocks free Venice Pro access. Stakers can also mint DIEM (see below). Stakers earn yield from emissions + revenue-funded buybacks that partially redirect to stakers rather than burning.

### DIEM (secondary token)

Launched August 2025. Each DIEM = $1 of Venice AI credit per day, in perpetuity. Only VVV stakers can mint DIEM. Tradeable on secondary markets, so DIEM prices reflect discounted perpetual AI-credit cash flows — a rare on-chain "productive asset" for an AI app.

DIEM has posted triple-digit monthly gains since launch on rising utility demand (per Bitcoin.com, early 2026 coverage).

## Competitive positioning

| Competitor | Approach | Key axis of differentiation |
|------------|----------|-----------------------------|
| [[ChatGPT]] | Centralized, account-required | Venice = no data retention, uncensored |
| [[Claude]] | Centralized, safety-filtered | Venice = open-weight models, user-controlled |
| [[Gemini]] | Centralized, [[Google]]-integrated | Venice = anti-surveillance, wallet-based |
| [[Perplexity]] | Centralized, search-focused | Venice = chat + image, privacy-first |

Venice is not competing on frontier model quality — it uses open-weight models that are typically 6-18 months behind frontier closed models. The bet: a material minority of users will accept that capability gap in exchange for privacy, censorship resistance, and crypto-native payment. Shared investor/customer base with other "sovereign stack" projects — OpenClaw referrals drove a reported 500%+ VVV surge over one month in early 2026.

## OpenClaw cross-reference

Venice was repeatedly cited by [[OpenClaw]] community as a "sovereign-stack" partner — OpenClaw users (who rebranded from "Clawdbot" under Anthropic C&D) seek anti-surveillance LLM workflows and default to Venice for any inference they can't self-host. WEEX Crypto News attributed a 500%+ VVV rally in one month (early 2026) largely to OpenClaw's publicity of Venice as the privacy-native chat frontend. This is a crypto-AI community network effect that centralized incumbents cannot replicate without abandoning their product model.

## Strategic framing

Venice is the first decentralized AI app to reach meaningful consumer scale with a coherent tokenomics story. Where [[Ridges AI]] bets on cost disruption of B2B software engineering, Venice bets on privacy + censorship resistance as a consumer product category. Central constraint: open-weight models are demonstrably worse than frontier closed models on most benchmarks, and the "privacy premium" market is narrower than the cost-disruption market in enterprise software. Counter-example: [[Signal]] has ~100M users on a privacy-first messaging model that never tried to match [[WhatsApp]] on features — suggesting there is a real market for "good enough + private" vs "best + surveilled." One-line: if the Signal analog works for AI chat, Venice is early enough to own it; if users will not pay up (in tokens) for privacy they do not feel immediately threatened by, Venice remains a crypto-native niche.
