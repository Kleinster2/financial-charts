## Star Lab (commercial space station)

[[Star Lab]] is Voyager's CLD-program candidate — a joint venture targeting 2029 launch on a single [[SpaceX]] [[Starship]] flight. The full station deploys in one launch, leveraging Starship payload capacity. Distinguishes Star Lab from competitors like [[Vast Space|Vast]] (multiple Falcon 9 launches) and [[Axiom Space]] (modular ISS-derived).

| Star Lab JV stake | Owner |
|---|---|
| 67% | Voyager |
| 30.5% | [[Airbus]] |
| 1% | [[Palantir]] |
| 0.8% | [[Mitsubishi]] |
| 0.8% | [[MDA]] |

The Starship dependency is structural: Star Lab's economics only work because Starship enables single-launch deployment. The 10K disclosure of $90M / $900/kg pricing is what made the math viable at $2.8-3.3B development cost.

See [[Star Lab]] for module spec, mission profile, and JV structure.

## Q1 2026 — 10K Starship economics disclosure

Per [[Space Capital]] (Chad Anderson, Apr 2026): Voyager's 10K is the cleanest public-domain source on Starship pricing for a real customer contract. The $90M / $900/kg figure ([[NASA]] reference: SLS at >$10K/kg) is what's doing the structural work behind the Q1 2026 NASA architecture pivot away from SLS/[[Lunar Gateway]] toward Starship-native lunar surface deployment.

The disclosure matters beyond Voyager itself — every CLD candidate, every commercial lunar program, and every orbital data center constellation builds on the assumption that Starship reaches that price point at scale. Voyager's 10K turned the assumption into a contracted figure.

### Q1 2026 fundamentals snapshot

![[voyager-fundamentals-chart.png]]
*Quarterly revenue and net income for VOYG with price overlay. The structural shape: revenue is government services / defense contracting (~30-50M per quarter, 12-15% gross margin), with [[Star Lab]] development costs flowing through OpEx and producing widening net losses. Pure go-public-and-fund-the-station structure.*

![[voyager-sankey.png]]
*FY2025 income statement flow. $166M revenue → $145M COGS (87%, gross margin 13%) → $21M gross profit → $124M OpEx → -$103M operating income → -$105M net income. The 13% gross margin is the gating issue for path-to-profitability — Voyager's existing services business does not throw off cash to fund the Star Lab buildout, so the IPO proceeds and follow-on equity issuance are doing the heavy lifting.*
