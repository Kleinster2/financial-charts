**InterPositive** — AI post-production startup founded by [[Ben Affleck]] in 2022. Acquired by [[Netflix]] on Mar 5, 2026 for up to $600M (with earnouts). Positioned as non-generative filmmaker tools. One of Netflix's largest acquisitions ever.

---

## Synopsis

InterPositive operated in stealth from 2022 until its acquisition. The 16-person team (engineers, researchers, creatives) built AI tools for post-production workflows: the system trains a model on a production's own dailies, then lets filmmakers relight shots, fix continuity issues, adjust environments, mix and color, and add visual effects. The company positions the tools as non-generative — not creating new content, not using footage without permission, not text-to-video (not a [[Sora]]/[[Runway]] competitor). See Analysis below for why this framing matters more as labor politics than as a technical description.

Netflix paid up to $600M structured with earnouts (upfront cash was less; remainder contingent on performance targets). The team was integrated into Netflix's workforce. Affleck became a Netflix senior adviser. The strategic logic: at Netflix's production scale ($20B/year content spend), even modest post-production cost reduction or quality improvement compounds significantly. The tools are filmmaker-built and filmmaker-oriented — a talent attraction play as much as a cost play.

## Analysis

The "not generative AI" framing is positioning, not a hard technical boundary. Relighting a scene, adjusting environments, adding VFX, fixing continuity — these operations almost certainly use generative techniques under the hood (diffusion models, neural radiance fields, inpainting). You cannot relight a shot or modify an environment without generating new pixel values. The underlying models are generative by any reasonable technical definition.

The distinction InterPositive draws is about inputs and intent:
- Input: trained on the production's own dailies, not scraped internet data
- Output: enhanced/modified existing footage, not wholly new content from a text prompt
- Use case: filmmaker-controlled post-production tools, not autonomous content creation

This framing is a labor politics play. [[SAG-AFTRA]] and the [[WGA]] fought generative AI in the 2023 strikes and won contract protections against AI-generated performances and scripts. Any tool that calls itself "generative AI" in Hollywood immediately triggers guild scrutiny and talent resistance. By positioning InterPositive as "practical filmmaker tools for post" — trained only on footage you already own, controlled by the filmmaker, enhancing rather than replacing — Affleck sidesteps the political minefield entirely. The fact that he's a working actor and SAG-AFTRA member himself makes the positioning credible in a way a tech startup couldn't manage.

For [[Netflix]], the framing is equally useful. They can deploy generative-class AI across their $20B/year content pipeline while avoiding the "Netflix replaces artists with AI" headline. The acquisition announcement's careful language — "not generative," "filmmaker-built," "does not use footage without permission" — reads like it was drafted with labor counsel in the room.

The risk: if the tools become powerful enough that they visibly displace post-production labor (VFX artists, colorists, editors), the "not generative" distinction won't hold. The guilds will reclassify it based on outcomes (jobs lost) rather than inputs (what data it trained on). The 2023 strike contracts will likely need renegotiation in the 2026-2028 cycle, and tools like InterPositive will be central to that fight — regardless of what the company calls them.
