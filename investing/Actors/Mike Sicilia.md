# Mike Sicilia

Co-CEO of [[Oracle]] alongside [[Safra Catz]], appointed in September 2024 as part of a succession planning shift by founder [[Larry Ellison]]. Previously served as EVP of Oracle Industries, where he built Oracle's vertical SaaS business — industry-specific cloud applications for healthcare, financial services, construction, and utilities. The co-CEO structure pairs Sicilia's applications and industry expertise with [[Clay Magouyrk]]'s infrastructure focus (OCI). Before Oracle, Sicilia had a background in healthcare IT, which informed Oracle's $28.3B acquisition of [[Cerner]] (2022) — the largest deal in Oracle's history, giving it a dominant position in electronic health records. The co-CEO model is a bet by Ellison that Oracle's cloud transition requires both a strong infrastructure layer (Magouyrk/OCI) and a strong applications layer (Sicilia/Industries) operating with CEO-level authority.
