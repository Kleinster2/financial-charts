# Nel ASA

Nel ASA is a [[Norway]]-headquartered pure-play electrolyzer manufacturer and one of the oldest names in commercial water electrolysis — the company traces its lineage to Norsk Hydro's hydrogen unit founded in 1927. Listed on the [[Oslo Stock Exchange]] (ticker NEL) since 2014. Makes both alkaline and PEM [[Electrolyzers|electrolyzer]] systems. The most prominent Western electrolyzer pure-play and therefore the cleanest public-market proxy for the [[Second China shock]] in hydrogen manufacturing.

## Why Nel matters

Nel is the reference public-market name in the [[Electrolyzers]] space — the pure-play status (no parent balance sheet subsidy, no diversified revenue base) means its share price and operating results read almost directly as a signal on Western electrolyzer economics. When the [[Green hydrogen]] thesis is working, Nel moves with it; when Chinese alkaline cost compression hits the EU Hydrogen Bank auction outcomes, Nel's order book is where it shows up first.

The company has been through multiple strategic pivots:
- 2017-2020: acquisition spree (Proton OnSite, H2 Logic) to build full product range
- 2021-2022: peak bubble valuations on IRA/EU hydrogen announcements
- 2023-2025: repeated guide cuts, order cancellations, CEO changes
- 2024: announced 500 MW US alkaline plant in Michigan, later delayed
- 2025-2026: focus narrowing to core US + Nordic markets as Chinese competition squeezes European pipeline

---

## The second-shock pressure

Nel sits at the intersection of most structural headwinds the [[Second China shock]] framework identifies:

1. Chinese alkaline cost advantage of 60-70% vs Nel's price points
2. EU Hydrogen Bank auction clearing prices below Nel's cost base
3. Chinese competitors (Longi Hydrogen, Sungrow) scaling to GW-per-year capacity while Nel is still in tens to low hundreds of MW
4. US IRA 45V as the single surviving demand channel, with Section 45V domestic-content rules partially favorable but not sufficient alone to clear Nel's order book

Whether Nel survives as an independent entity through 2028-2030 is a genuine open question. The bull case requires a combination of (a) IRA demand holding post-Trump 2.0 rollbacks, (b) European state-aid response to the Chinese import pressure, and (c) Nel-specific execution on the US Michigan plant and cost-out program.
