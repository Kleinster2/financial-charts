**iShares** is [[BlackRock]]'s ETF platform and the world's largest ETF issuer by assets under management (~$4T+ across 1,400+ products globally). Originally launched by Barclays Global Investors (BGI) in 2000, the platform was acquired by BlackRock in 2009 as part of the $13.5B BGI acquisition — a deal that transformed BlackRock from a fixed-income manager into the world's dominant asset manager. iShares products span equities, fixed income, commodities, and alternatives, with flagship funds including IVV (S&P 500), AGG (US aggregate bonds), EFA (developed ex-US), EEM (emerging markets), and [[IBIT]] (spot Bitcoin). The platform's scale creates a self-reinforcing liquidity advantage: tighter spreads attract more flow, which deepens liquidity, which tightens spreads further. iShares competes primarily with [[Vanguard]] (second-largest ETF issuer) and [[State Street]] (third-largest, home of [[SPY]]).
