## Q1 2026 13F — Mag 7 reshuffle (Reuters May 15, 2026)

Third Point's Q1 13F shows a deliberate divergence from [[Pershing Square]] on the Microsoft-vs-Alphabet split, with both funds converging on a new Meta position:

| Action | Stock | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Sold | [[Microsoft]] | 925,000 shares, liquidating position held since late 2022 |
| Bought | [[Alphabet]] | 175,000 shares (new) |
| New position | [[Meta]] | Established in Q1 alongside [[Pershing Square|Ackman]]'s parallel new META position |

The Q1 13F lines up against [[Bill Ackman|Ackman]]'s opposite trades over the same window — Ackman bought MSFT in February and exited GOOGL (most in Q1, rest in Q2). Two former voluble activists now both running "be selective in Mag 7" theses but disagreeing on which names. Both converged on META as AI-benefit candidate. Per Reuters, both Ackman and Loeb have moved to quieter, stock-picking-and-riding posture vs the 2010s public-letter activist style.

*Source: [Ackman, Loeb take different routes on tech bets in early 2026](https://www.reuters.com/), Reuters, May 15 2026, by Svea Herbst-Bayliss.*

## Malibu Life Reinsurance (2025)

Loeb converted his London-listed vehicle into Malibu Life Reinsurance, a Cayman Islands-domiciled reinsurer focused on winning US fixed annuity market share. The move was contested by some shareholders but ultimately approved. The strategy mirrors the broader [[PE-insurance convergence]] — using annuity float as [[Permanent capital]] to fund investments. Part of a wave alongside [[Bill Ackman]]'s [[Howard Hughes Vantage Risk acquisition|Howard Hughes/Vantage Risk deal]] and the established PE-insurer platforms ([[Apollo]]/[[Athene]], [[KKR]]/[[Global Atlantic]]).
