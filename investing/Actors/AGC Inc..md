**AGC Inc.** — Japanese multinational and one of the world's largest glass manufacturers, with leading positions in flat glass, automotive glass, display glass, and specialty chemicals. Formerly known as Asahi Glass Co., the company rebranded to AGC Inc. in 2018 to reflect its diversification beyond glass into electronics materials, life sciences, and high-performance chemicals. Founded in 1907 by Toshiya Iwasaki as part of the [[Mitsubishi]] zaibatsu network.

## Synopsis

AGC's investment relevance lies at the intersection of several structural themes: EV adoption (lighter, larger automotive glass with embedded electronics), display technology (glass substrates for OLED and next-gen displays), and semiconductor materials (high-purity synthetic quartz for [[EUV lithography]] photomasks). The company's chemicals segment — including fluorochemicals and specialty gases — connects it to semiconductor manufacturing supply chains. Revenue is roughly split: building/industrial glass (~30%), automotive (~25%), electronics (~25%), chemicals (~20%).

The competitive landscape in flat and automotive glass is an oligopoly: AGC, [[Saint-Gobain]], [[NSG Group]], and [[Fuyao Glass Industry Group]] dominate globally. [[Fuyao Glass Industry Group|Fuyao]] has been gaining share in automotive glass on cost advantages, pressuring AGC and [[Saint-Gobain]] in that segment.
