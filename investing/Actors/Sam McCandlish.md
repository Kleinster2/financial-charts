# Sam McCandlish

**Sam McCandlish** is a co-founder of [[Anthropic]] and co-author of the seminal scaling laws research that formalized the relationship between model size, data, compute, and performance.

## Synopsis

McCandlish's primary contribution to AI research — and the reason he matters for the investing vault — is the 2020 paper "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models," co-authored with [[Jared Kaplan]] and others at [[OpenAI]]. This paper provided the empirical and theoretical framework showing that language model performance scales predictably with compute, parameters, and data, following power-law relationships. The implications were enormous: it gave labs a quantitative basis for planning multi-billion-dollar training runs, and it gave investors a framework for understanding why AI infrastructure spending would grow on a predictable trajectory. Every [[NVIDIA]] GPU purchase by a hyperscaler implicitly references the scaling paradigm that McCandlish helped establish.

McCandlish left [[OpenAI]] in early 2021 as part of the six-person departure led by [[Dario Amodei]] and [[Daniela Amodei]] that founded [[Anthropic]]. The departure was driven by disagreements over OpenAI's safety practices and governance direction — the group believed a new organization could pursue frontier AI development with more rigorous safety alignment from the ground up. McCandlish's continued presence at Anthropic (confirmed as of February 2026) places him among the small group of researchers whose technical contributions to scaling laws and whose institutional choices (leaving OpenAI for safety-focused Anthropic) have shaped the competitive structure of the frontier AI industry.
