## Why Foulis matters

Foulis sits in a small set of writers who treat the 2020s globalisation retreat as an *active market structure shift* rather than a political-economy backdrop. He frames geopolitical fragmentation in terms readers can trade: chokepoint exposure of trade flows, divergence between regional commodity grades, China-US tech-product price gaps, and which business models — commodity desks, sogo shosha, macro hedge funds, neoclouds — capture the resulting basis. The framing connects the [[Weaponized interdependence]] / [[US-China decoupling]] policy literature to live cross-asset positioning.

---

## Key recent argument — "The golden age of arbitrage" (April 24 2026)

Central thesis: the [[Law of one price]] is in retreat. The post-1990 era of free trade, supply chains, and global IP rules made the law a daily reality; sanctions, economic nationalism, and ~50% of trade flows passing through militarily tense zones are dismantling it.

Three implications:

| Implication | Foulis evidence |
|---|---|
| Golden age of arbitrage | [[JPMorgan Chase]] markets arm best result in history; commodity firms + Wall Street trading desks earn ~$140bn/yr (2× 2019); [[Buffett Japan sogo shosha trade\|Japanese trading houses]] on a tear; rush of macro hedge fund openings 14 years after [[Soros Fund Management\|Soros]] returned outside capital; [[Neocloud financing\|neoclouds]] doing cross-border AI-chip rental "without too many questions asked" |
| Inflation divergence | US +25%, Russia +51%, China +4% cumulative over five years → eventual rate and FX dispersion |
| Innovation catalyst | 1970s analog: [[1973 oil crisis\|Middle East oil shock]] → North Sea deepwater, Alaska pipelines, larger tankers. 2020s: energy/materials science, manufacturing automation, AI agents masking nationality |

Concrete price gaps Foulis cites:
- Crude oil grades $97-$147 across [[WTI\|Texas]], Guyana, [[Brent crude\|North Sea]], [[Russia]] (some of the biggest dispersions ever)
- [[Gold]] in [[Europe]] vs New York divorced in 2025; [[Copper]], silver, nickel similarly dislocated
- AI tokens ~80% cheaper in [[China]] than California (in practice 10-100× depending on tier — see [[Law of one price]])
- [[NVIDIA]] [[B200]] chips ~50% more expensive in China grey market
- [[EU-China EV tariffs|Chinese EVs]] in Dalian ≥30% cheaper than Detroit equivalents
