## Q1 2026 results (released Apr 24)

HCA delivered a narrow earnings beat on a print where the volume mix was the story. Same-facility admissions grew, but well below the seasonal trajectory that the market was expecting. Reaffirmed (rather than raised) full-year guidance plus respiratory-season weakness drove the -8.8% selloff.

CEO Sam Hazen: "the start of the year presented a dynamic environment for HCA Healthcare," with the company "did not experience a typical seasonal volume increase" due to mild respiratory activity and January winter storms in certain markets.

The reaffirmation, combined with the volume miss versus prior-year seasonality, was read as the company protecting downside on the [[ACA]] enhanced-tax-credit expiration and Medicaid supplemental-payment changes already baked into the guide.

## Why HCA matters

Hospital bellwether. HCA's scale makes it the best proxy for US hospital industry health. Volume trends, payer mix shifts, and labor costs show up in HCA earnings before anywhere else.

Policy sensitivity. ACA exchanges and Medicaid supplemental payments flow directly to HCA's bottom line. The company is a direct read on healthcare policy implementation.

## 2026 challenges

HCA flagged ~$1B headwind from policy changes:

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| ACA enhanced tax credits expiring | $600M-$900M |
| Medicaid supplemental payment changes | $250M-$450M |

The company's "resiliency program" targeting $400M in cost savings aims to offset these headwinds.

2026 guidance:
- Revenue: $76.5B-$80B
- Net income: $6.5B-$7B
- Adjusted EBITDA: $15.55B-$16.4B
- CapEx: $5B-$5.5B
