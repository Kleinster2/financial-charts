Labour Party (Norway) (Arbeiderpartiet) is Norway's centre-left party and the dominant governing force in postwar Norwegian politics. Led by [[Jonas Gahr Støre]], who served as PM 2021-2025 and won re-election in September 2025.

## Synopsis

The Arbeiderpartiet built the Norwegian welfare state and presided over the establishment of the [[Government Pension Fund Global]] (the world's largest sovereign wealth fund at $1.7T+). The party's economic model combines high taxes, generous public services, and an oil-funded fiscal buffer — a social democratic framework that depends on continued petroleum revenues even as the party rhetorically supports climate transition. This tension between oil wealth and green policy is the central contradiction of Norwegian politics and directly affects energy sector investment flows.

The 2022 [[Wealth tax]] increase under Støre's first government triggered a measurable wave of [[Capital flight]] — wealthy Norwegians (including [[Kjell Inge Røkke]] of [[Aker ASA]]) relocated to Switzerland and other low-tax jurisdictions, taking taxable assets with them. Norway's experience became a case study in wealth tax elasticity: the revenue raised was partially offset by the departure of the tax base itself. Støre's September 2025 re-election, despite this backlash, suggests the Norwegian electorate prioritizes redistribution over capital retention. For investors, Labour's tax policies affect the listed Norwegian equity market (OSE), particularly family-controlled companies and the broader Nordic wealth management ecosystem.
