**UK Fusion Energy** — the [[UK]]'s government-backed fusion development company, formerly UK Industrial Fusion Solutions (UKIFS). Wholly owned subsidiary of the [[UK Atomic Energy Authority]] (UKAEA), based in Oxfordshire. Responsible for developing the Spherical Tokamak for Energy Production (STEP) reactor at a former coal-fired power station site in Nottinghamshire. The UK government has pledged £1.3bn in backing and is restructuring the entity to attract private investment, signaling a shift from pure public funding to commercial orientation.

## STEP reactor

The Spherical Tokamak for Energy Production aims to be the first fusion reactor to deliver electricity to the grid — a milestone no fusion reactor has achieved anywhere in the world. Planned for a former coal-fired power station site in Nottinghamshire. The project aims to demonstrate commercial viability by 2040.

According to a 2024 government tender notice, the total cost could reach £20bn. Progress on the programme is expected to reduce engineering risks and generate technologies the company can exploit commercially.

## Commercial restructuring (March 2026)

Under a new national fusion strategy announced March 2026, the UK government is:

- Renaming UKIFS to UK Fusion Energy
- Restructuring to operate "more fully as a commercially oriented entity"
- Positioning the company to raise external investment in future
- Developing capabilities as a "fusion power plant integrator" for global deployment

The move reflects surging private investor interest in fusion — [[Artificial intelligence|AI]]-driven power demand is a key driver. The timing positions the UK against intensifying competition:

| Region | Fusion investment context |
|--------|--------------------------|
| US | $1.6bn private funding in 2025 ([[PitchBook]]) |
| [[China]] | $6.5-10bn since 2022 (Special Competitive Studies Project, [[Eric Schmidt]]-chaired) |
| UK | £1.3bn government-backed, now seeking private capital |

## Competitive landscape

UK Fusion Energy sits in a different category from the private fusion startups — it is a government-backed integrator, not a venture-backed technology developer. The private players include:

- [[Commonwealth Fusion Systems]] — tokamak, $2B+ raised, US
- [[Helion Energy]] — pulsed FRC, $1B+ raised, US
- [[Inertia Enterprises]] — ICF, $450M Series A (Feb 2026), US
- [[Tokamak Energy]] — spherical tokamak with HTS magnets, >$250M, UK (private sector peer)
- [[TAE Technologies]] — $1.2B+ raised, US
