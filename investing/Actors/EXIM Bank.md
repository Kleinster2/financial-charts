**EXIM Bank** (Export–Import Bank of the United States) — Official US export credit agency, established 1934. Provides financing for US exports when private lenders are unable or unwilling to accept risk.

## Function

EXIM provides:
- **Buyer financing** — loans to foreign purchasers of US goods
- **Export credit insurance** — protection against buyer default
- **Working capital guarantees** — help exporters secure bank financing

Key principle: Does not compete with private sector — fills gaps where commercial lenders won't go.

---

## [[Project Vault]] role

In February 2026, EXIM approved a **$10B loan** for Project Vault — the largest single transaction in agency history:
- Finances strategic critical minerals stockpile
- Counter [[China]] supply manipulation
- Announced same day as Trump's initiative

This represents a significant expansion of EXIM's traditional export financing role into strategic resource security.
