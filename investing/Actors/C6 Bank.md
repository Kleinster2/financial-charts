#actor #company #fintech #neobank #brazil #private

**C6 Bank** — Brazilian full-service digital bank launched in 2019, backed by [[JPMorgan Chase]] at a 46% indirect stake, with 40M+ clients and R$2.5B 2025 net income. It sits between [[Nubank]]'s mass-market scale, [[Banco Inter|Inter]]'s super-app model, and [[BTG Pactual]]'s wealth-led digital push inside [[Brazil fintech]].

C6 is the most institutionalized version of the Brazilian neobank story: venture-speed onboarding and product breadth, but with a Wall Street balance-sheet partner. The strategic question is whether the bank can keep growing credit without giving back the 2025 profitability inflection. Its official 2025 numbers show the operating leverage worked: revenue rose, expenses fell, ROE hit 45%, and the expanded credit book reached R$89.3B.

The open question is valuation and control. C6 is private, the 2021 and 2023 JPMorgan transactions did not disclose deal value, and the company still has a controlling founder/management side beside [[JPMorgan Chase]]. That makes C6 more of a read-through on Brazilian digital banking economics than a directly tradeable equity, but the bank matters because it shows what a large global bank thinks is worth owning in [[Brazil]].

## Evolution

- 2018-2019 — C6 was assembled by an experienced Brazilian banking team and launched after receiving a multiple-bank license from [[Banco Central do Brasil]]. The early pitch was not just "free digital account"; it was a full-service bank in an app, with cards, investments, international accounts, toll tags, and business banking.

- 2020 — The bank raised R$1.3B at the holding level and was valued at R$11.3B, according to C6's own timeline. That was the classic [[Fintech boom (2020-2021)]] moment: cheap growth capital, huge digital-banking TAM, and investors willing to underwrite losses for customer acquisition.

- 2021 — [[JPMorgan Chase]] agreed to take 40% of C6. The deal mattered because it gave a fast-growing Brazilian digital bank global-bank credibility and gave JPMorgan a consumer-banking laboratory in one of the world's most advanced instant-payment markets. C6 ended that year at 14M clients and 100% city coverage in [[Brazil]].

- 2023 — JPMorgan agreed to increase its stake to 46% after C6's client base had grown from 8M at the initial investment announcement to 25M and the credit portfolio had moved from R$9.5B to R$40B. C6 also reached monthly breakeven in November, shifting the story from pure land grab to proof that the credit-and-product stack could cover costs.

- 2025 — C6 crossed 40M clients and reported R$2.5B net income, R$9.2B net revenue, 45% ROE, and an R$89.3B expanded credit book. The bank's own disclosure says 80% of the credit portfolio is secured, mainly payroll loans and auto finance, which is the critical detail: the profitability turn is credit-led, but it is not the same risk mix as unsecured card-led neobank growth.

## Business model

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Retail banking | Checking, savings, cards, [[Pix]], investments, toll tag, rewards, insurance |
| Credit | Payroll lending, auto finance, home equity, business credit |
| Wealth / affluent | C6 Invest, C6 Graphene, global accounts, international investments |
| Business banking | C6 Empresas, C6 Pay acquiring, business credit, cash management |
| International | C6 Conta Global in dollars/euros; global card and investment access |

C6's differentiation is breadth. [[Nubank]] started with a credit card and scaled into banking; [[Banco Inter|Inter]] emphasizes a super-app with marketplace and global-account features; C6 tries to look more like a complete bank from day one. That is why [[JPMorgan Chase]] matters: the value of the partnership is less brand halo than product, governance, risk, and balance-sheet discipline.

The 2025 quality test is whether the credit book remains clean as it scales. C6 reported 90+ day NPLs of 2.9%, or 2.0% excluding the accounting change from CMN Resolution 4.966, and said 80% of the portfolio is secured. That makes the bank's profitability less fragile than a pure unsecured-credit expansion, though still exposed to [[Selic]], payroll-lending regulation, auto-cycle risk, and [[Brazilian real]] funding conditions.
