**Janus Henderson** — Anglo-American asset manager (HQ London/Denver, NYSE: JHG) caught in a three-way bidding war that epitomizes the asset management consolidation wave. The industry is consolidating as firms face higher costs and investors shift from actively managed funds to cheaper index-tracking alternatives.

---

## Synopsis

[[Trian Partners|Trian Fund Management]] and a group led by [[General Catalyst]] agreed to buy Janus Henderson for $49/share in an all-cash deal valued at $7.4B. The board recommended the Trian offer. Trian already held a >20% stake in Janus Henderson before launching the bid.

Texas-based [[Victory Capital]] gatecrashed with two competing bids. The first (February 2026): $30 cash + 0.25 Victory shares per JHG share, total value $8.6B — rejected by the board as "not in the best interests of Janus Henderson and its shareholders." The second (~Mar 17-21, 2026): $40 cash + 0.25 Victory shares, same $8.6B total value but with more cash certainty. Under the revised bid, JHG shareholders would own 31% of the combined entity (down from 38% under the first offer). Victory claims a 16% premium to the Trian proposal and targets $500M/year in cost synergies.

Victory Capital attacked the Trian deal's structure: JHG would be owned by a "newly created acquisition vehicle with no operating experience." The board has not yet responded to the revised bid.
