# Zealand Pharma

Danish biotech specializing in peptide-based medicines, partnered with [[Boehringer Ingelheim]] on survodutide, a dual GLP-1/glucagon receptor agonist in Phase 3 trials for obesity and MASH. One of the few mid-cap biotechs with a serious shot at the GLP-1 market currently dominated by [[Novo Nordisk]] and [[Eli Lilly]].

## Synopsis

Zealand Pharma has built a 25-year peptide engineering platform that enables the design of novel peptide therapeutics targeting metabolic diseases. The crown jewel is survodutide (BI 456906), partnered with [[Boehringer Ingelheim]], which showed 18-19% body weight loss in Phase 2 obesity trials — competitive with [[Eli Lilly]]'s tirzepatide and [[Novo Nordisk]]'s semaglutide. The dual GLP-1/glucagon mechanism offers potential differentiation: the glucagon component may drive greater fat loss and liver fat reduction, making survodutide particularly relevant for MASH (metabolic-associated steatohepatitis), a liver disease with no approved treatments in most markets.

The partnership with [[Boehringer Ingelheim]] is critical — BI funds development and holds global commercial rights, paying Zealand milestones and royalties. This de-risks Zealand's balance sheet but also caps the upside: if survodutide becomes a blockbuster ($5B+ peak sales potential in the GLP-1/obesity class), Zealand captures a fraction of the economics. The company also has wholly-owned pipeline assets including petrelintide (amylin analog for obesity) and glepaglutide (for short bowel syndrome, approved in the EU as Rybelsus). Zealand trades in Copenhagen (ZEAL) with a market cap that fluctuates heavily around clinical data readouts — a typical biotech dynamic amplified by the enormous TAM of the [[GLP-1 receptor agonists|GLP-1]] obesity market.
