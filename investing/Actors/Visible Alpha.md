**Visible Alpha** — Consensus estimates and financial data platform that aggregates individual analyst models rather than just headline numbers. [[S&P Global]] bought it in 2024, and it is now part of S&P Global Market Intelligence / Capital IQ Pro.

---

## Synopsis

Visible Alpha's differentiation is granularity. Traditional consensus providers ([[FactSet]], [[Bloomberg LP]], [[Refinitiv]]) aggregate analyst estimates at the headline level — revenue, EPS, EBITDA. Visible Alpha goes deeper, collecting the full analyst model: segment-level revenue, margin assumptions, capex estimates, working capital projections. This gives institutional investors the ability to see not just where consensus sits but why — which assumptions are driving the bull and bear cases, where analysts disagree at the line-item level, and how individual models have changed over time.

The company was founded in 2015 by a consortium of major banks, launched commercially in 2017, and later added investors including [[Goldman Sachs]], [[Morgan Stanley]], and [[JPMorgan Chase|JPMorgan]]. [[S&P Global]] completed its acquisition of Visible Alpha on May 1, 2024, folding the product into Capital IQ Pro. For the vault, Visible Alpha represents the broader unbundling and rebundling of financial data infrastructure: specialist line-item consensus was valuable enough to become part of the incumbent market-data stack rather than remain a standalone challenger. The risk is that AI-powered analysis tools make granular consensus data less valuable if models can derive similar insights from earnings call transcripts and filings directly.

## Hyperscaler FCF dataset (May 2026)

FT used [[Visible Alpha]] consensus estimates in May 2026 to show the [[Amazon]] / [[Alphabet]] / [[Microsoft]] / [[Meta]] free-cash-flow trough caused by AI infrastructure capex. The useful vault point is methodological: Visible Alpha's value is not a headline EPS number, but line-item consensus for capex, cash flow, debt, and buybacks across comparable companies. That is exactly the dataset needed for [[Hyperscaler capex]], [[Hyperscaler bond issuance]], and [[AI infrastructure financing risk]].

*Source: [FT article](https://www.ft.com/content/b3dfaba9-17a2-4fac-90fe-4ab3ca7c9494), May 8 2026.*
