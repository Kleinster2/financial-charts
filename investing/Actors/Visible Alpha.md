**Visible Alpha** — Consensus estimates and financial data platform that aggregates individual analyst models rather than just headline numbers. Competes with [[FactSet]], [[Bloomberg LP]], and [[Refinitiv]] in the financial data infrastructure market.

---

## Synopsis

Visible Alpha's differentiation is granularity. Traditional consensus providers (FactSet, Bloomberg, Refinitiv) aggregate analyst estimates at the headline level — revenue, EPS, EBITDA. Visible Alpha goes deeper, collecting the full analyst model: segment-level revenue, margin assumptions, capex estimates, working capital projections. This gives institutional investors the ability to see not just where consensus sits but why — which assumptions are driving the bull and bear cases, where analysts disagree at the line-item level, and how individual models have changed over time.

The company was founded in 2015 and has raised capital from a consortium of major banks and asset managers, including [[Goldman Sachs]], [[Morgan Stanley]], and [[JPMorgan]]. This investor base is also its customer base, which creates both a commercial advantage (built-in distribution) and a potential conflict (incumbents funding a product that could disrupt their own research businesses). For the vault, Visible Alpha represents the broader unbundling of financial data infrastructure — the same trend that created [[FactSet]]'s dominance over legacy terminals is now creating room for more specialized, higher-resolution data products. The risk is that AI-powered analysis tools make granular consensus data less valuable if models can derive the same insights from earnings call transcripts and filings directly.
