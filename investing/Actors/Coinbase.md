**Coinbase** — Largest US crypto exchange. Infrastructure layer for tokenization. Regulatory bellwether for crypto industry.

---

## Why Coinbase matters

Coinbase is crypto's on-ramp and infrastructure provider:

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Exchange | Buy/sell crypto (retail + institutional) |
| Custody | Store crypto for institutions |
| Staking | Earn yield on proof-of-stake coins |
| Base | Layer 2 blockchain for apps |
| USDC | Co-creator with Circle |

---

## Tokenization relevance

**Infrastructure for tokenized assets:**
- Custody solutions for token issuers
- Trading infrastructure
- Regulatory expertise
- Base blockchain for token settlement

If tokenization scales, Coinbase benefits as infrastructure.

## Regulatory position

**The most compliant US crypto company:**
- Registered with SEC (public company)
- State money transmitter licenses
- Custody licenses
- Working with regulators

**Risk:** SEC has sued Coinbase over unregistered securities. Ongoing litigation.

**Tailwind:** SEC chair Atkins more crypto-friendly than Gensler.

---

## USDC stablecoin

**Co-created with Circle:**
- Dollar-backed stablecoin
- ~$30B+ market cap
- Used for trading, payments, DeFi
- Coinbase earns interest on reserves

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Crypto infrastructure leader
- Regulatory moat (compliance)
- Subscription revenue growing
- Base blockchain growth
- Tokenization tailwind
- SEC chair change positive

**Bear:**
- SEC litigation ongoing
- Crypto volatility affects revenue
- Competition (Binance, Kraken)
- Fee compression
- Regulatory uncertainty
