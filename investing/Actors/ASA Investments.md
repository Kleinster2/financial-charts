ASA Investments is a [[Brazil|Brazilian]] financial firm founded by Alberto Safra, son of [[Joseph Safra]], after he recruited the CEO and senior executives from [[Banco Safra]] — a move his father viewed as betrayal, leading to Alberto's reduced inheritance allocation. The launch of ASA was the catalyst for the [[Safra family]] inheritance dispute (2020-2024). Under the July 2024 settlement, Alberto divests from [[J. Safra Group]] and pursues business independently through ASA.

The firm operates across asset management, proprietary trading, and advisory in Brazilian capital markets. Alberto built ASA into a credible standalone platform by leveraging the talent pipeline he pulled from the family bank, positioning it as an independent Brazilian financial house competing against the family empire he was forced out of.
