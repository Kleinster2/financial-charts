**Philip Jefferson** — Vice Chair of the [[Federal Reserve]] Board of Governors since September 2023. Economist (Vassar BA, [[Virginia]] PhD) who spent most of his pre-Fed career in academia (Davidson College, Swarthmore). Named by [[Scott Bessent]] (April 14, 2026) as a possible chair pro tem if [[Kevin Warsh]] is not confirmed before [[Jerome Powell|Powell]]'s chair term expires mid-May 2026.

## Fed chair transition context

At the WSJ CEO Council on April 14, 2026, [[Scott Bessent]] floated Jefferson as a possible chair pro tem if [[Kevin Warsh]]'s Senate confirmation slips past the May 15, 2026 expiration of [[Jerome Powell|Powell]]'s chair term. [[Tom Tillis]] is conditioning Warsh confirmation on resolution of the DOJ investigation of Powell. Bessent: "Vice Chair Philip Jefferson could be. Chris Waller could be. Lot of people could be. I think the president of the United States probably has a preference."
