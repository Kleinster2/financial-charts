# Southern Transitional Council

Yemeni separatist political organization established May 2017, advocating for the restoration of South Yemen as an independent state (the pre-1990 [[People's Democratic Republic of Yemen]]). Headquartered in Aden. Backed primarily by the [[UAE]] and aligned with the broader anti-[[Houthis|Houthi]] coalition led by [[Saudi Arabia]] — but with persistent UAE-Saudi disagreement over the STC's separatist agenda, which Riyadh has historically opposed in favor of unified Yemeni sovereignty.

## December 2025 Saudi-UAE rupture

The Saudi-UAE Yemen coalition fractured in late December 2025 over Saudi airstrikes on what Riyadh said were Iranian weapons shipments to STC-backed positions in southern Yemen. Per Al Jazeera and Washington Post reporting (April 2026), the strike provoked an Emirati response and effectively ended joint operational coordination on the southern Yemen file. The rupture is now part of the broader Saudi-UAE realignment context surrounding the [[2026 UAE OPEC exit]].
