**Qiming Venture Partners** is a [[China]]-focused venture capital firm with $9B+ in assets under management, investing across technology, healthcare, and consumer sectors. Founded in 2006, it ranks among the top-tier VC firms in the Chinese tech ecosystem.

## Synopsis

Qiming is one of the handful of Chinese VC firms that institutional LPs consider top-decile — alongside [[Sequoia China]] (now HongShan), [[Hillhouse Capital]], and [[GGV Capital]]. Founded in 2006 by Duane Kuang (formerly of [[Intel Capital]]) and Gary Rieschel, the firm was purpose-built to bridge US-style venture investing with China's rapid tech growth. Its portfolio reads like a map of Chinese tech's last two decades: early investments in [[Xiaomi]], [[Meituan]], [[Bilibili]], and dozens of enterprise and healthcare companies that rode China's digitization wave.

The firm's $9B+ AUM makes it substantial by Chinese VC standards, though the investment climate has shifted dramatically since 2022. The combination of China's tech regulatory crackdown, US-China decoupling (particularly CFIUS scrutiny of Chinese investments and LP pressure to avoid China exposure), and a domestic IPO bottleneck has compressed China VC returns and fundraising. Qiming has adapted by expanding healthcare and deep-tech allocations (robotics, semiconductors, AI) that align with Chinese government industrial policy, while its earlier consumer and fintech bets face a tougher exit environment. The firm's investments in companies like [[ACE Robotics]] (embodied AI) reflect this strategic pivot toward sectors with both policy tailwinds and global applicability.
