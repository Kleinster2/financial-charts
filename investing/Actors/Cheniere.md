## Iran conflict beneficiary (Mar 2026)

[[QatarEnergy]]'s force majeure and [[Ras Laffan]] shutdown removed ~20% of global LNG supply. Cheniere is the primary US beneficiary — stock rose 5%+ in the first days of the conflict. Already running near capacity, but flexible destination contracts allow rerouting cargoes to highest-paying buyers (CNBC, Mar 3). [[NYT]] (Mar 4): Cheniere, along with [[Shell]], [[TotalEnergies]], and [[ExxonMobil]], stands to earn significant profits even if Qatar quickly restarts.

### US-Iran ceasefire impact (Apr 8, 2026)

Shares fell -5.9% on Trump's announcement of a two-week conditional ceasefire with [[Iran]], including [[Strait of Hormuz]] reopening. As a key beneficiary of the Qatar LNG disruption, Cheniere faces headwinds from potential conflict de-escalation that could normalize global gas markets.

**Market dynamics:**
- Conflict-driven LNG demand premium begins to unwind as geopolitical risk recedes
- Still benefits from structural Qatar supply disruption (multi-year [[Ras Laffan]] repairs) regardless of ceasefire
- US LNG export capacity remains limited — structural advantage continues

**Structural implications:**
- **If ceasefire holds:** Reduced geopolitical risk premium in LNG pricing, but Qatar's damaged infrastructure keeps structural supply shortage intact
- **If ceasefire collapses:** Rapid re-escalation could drive LNG prices even higher than conflict peaks, given demonstrated vulnerability of Gulf infrastructure

See [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] and [[Iran conflict economic disruption]] for broader context.
