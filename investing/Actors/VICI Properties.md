# VICI Properties

S&P 500 experiential REIT. Owns 93 properties (54 gaming, 39 other experiential) across the US and Canada -- ~127M sqft, ~60,300 hotel rooms, 500+ restaurants/bars. Marquee assets include Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, and The Venetian on the Las Vegas Strip. Triple-net lease model: tenants bear operating expenses while VICI collects long-duration contractual rent. Expanding beyond gaming into wellness ([[Canyon Ranch]]), entertainment, hospitality, and partner financing.

The core question is whether VICI's partner-driven capital deployment remains REIT-like enough for public-market investors. Q1 2026 was financially solid, with AFFO per share up 4.5% and guidance raised, but the stock sold off as the company leaned further into mezzanine loans, Canadian casino sale-leasebacks, and large relationship-driven transactions.

## Q1 2026 results and capital deployment (May 2026)

VICI reported Q1 2026 on Apr 29. AFFO grew 5.7% to $650.9M and AFFO/share grew 4.5% to $0.61. Management raised 2026 AFFO guidance to $2.665-2.695B, or $2.44-2.47/share. On the surface this was a good REIT quarter: higher guidance, stable dividend capacity, and a low-end-of-target leverage position.

The market reaction was negative. VICI closed at $28.36 on May 1, down 2.9% from $29.20 on Apr 30 (-2.5 sigma in the vault mover scan). The selloff looks less like earnings disappointment and more like digestion of the capital-allocation mix. Q1 and subsequent updates included a $1.5B mezzanine loan commitment with [[Cain International]] and [[Eldridge Industries]] for the One Beverly Hills development, a pending CAD$200.6M / USD$144.4M [[Pure Casino Entertainment]] / [[Gamehost]] casino real-estate transaction in Alberta at an 8.0% cap rate, a new lease tied to [[Clairvest]]'s acquisition of MGM Northfield Park operations, and progress toward the $1.16B [[Golden Entertainment]] real estate acquisition.

The thesis implication: VICI is still compounding, but the public equity is asking for a discount when growth comes through increasingly complex credit and relationship structures rather than simple gaming sale-leasebacks. The raised guidance says the machine works; the stock reaction says investors are debating how much mezzanine / partner-finance exposure a REIT multiple should absorb.

*Sources: [VICI Q1 2026 results](https://investors.viciproperties.com/news-releases/news-release-details/vici-properties-inc-announces-first-quarter-2026-results), local DB prices, Apr 29-May 1 2026.*

## Evolution

- 2017 - Created from Caesars' real estate spinout as a gaming-focused triple-net REIT.
- 2022 - Acquired MGM Growth Properties, making VICI the dominant Las Vegas Strip landlord.
- 2023 - Expanded non-gaming experiential strategy through [[Canyon Ranch]] preferred equity and loan investment.
- 2026 - Raised AFFO guidance while adding larger partner-finance and Canadian casino transactions, increasing the complexity of the growth model.
