# University of Chicago

Private research university in Chicago that shaped modern economics, finance, and public policy through the "Chicago school" tradition of free-market economics, efficient market hypothesis, and rigorous empirical methodology.

## Synopsis

The University of Chicago's intellectual output directly shaped the infrastructure of modern capital markets. Eugene Fama's efficient market hypothesis, the Black-Scholes options pricing model (Fischer Black was a UChicago PhD), Milton Friedman's monetarism, and the law-and-economics movement all emerged from or were incubated at UChicago. The Booth School of Business (renamed after a $300M gift from David Booth in 2008) produces research that moves markets -- its faculty have won more Nobel Prizes in Economics than any other institution. The Harris School of Public Policy and the Energy Policy Institute at the University of Chicago (EPIC) are increasingly relevant for climate/energy policy research.

For the vault, UChicago matters as a talent and ideas pipeline. [[Jeff Currie]] (PhD Economics) chairs the EPIC advisory group, connecting the university to commodities and energy markets research. The Chicago school's influence on antitrust policy, monetary policy, and financial regulation means UChicago alumni and faculty populate the Fed, SEC, CFTC, and major trading firms in outsized numbers. The university's endowment (~$10B+) is itself a significant institutional investor, managed in a model that influenced the "endowment model" of alternative-heavy asset allocation pioneered by Yale's David Swensen but rooted in Chicago's own investment office innovations.
