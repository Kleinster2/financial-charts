Hengli Petrochem (Hengli Petrochemical Co., Ltd.) is one of [[China]]'s largest private integrated refining and petrochemical companies, headquartered in Dalian, [[Liaoning]]. Listed in [[Shanghai Stock Exchange|Shanghai]] under 600346.SS. Hengli operates a ~400 kb/d refinery in Changxing Island, Dalian — among the largest single-site refineries globally — paired with a downstream PTA / polyester / aromatics complex that anchors the parent group's textile-fiber value chain.

## Apr 25 2026 — first major Chinese private refiner sanctioned

On Apr 25, 2026, the [[US Treasury]] sanctioned a Hengli Petrochem unit alleging Iranian crude purchases, the first time the current enforcement wave has reached a private Chinese refiner of Hengli's scale (i.e., not a Shandong [[Chinese teapot refineries|teapot]]). Hengli denied importing any Iranian oil. The stock fell limit-down 10% on the Apr 27 Shanghai open.

The designation matters for three reasons:

1. Hengli sits much higher up the integrated-refining stack than the small Shandong teapots that have absorbed prior sanctions waves. A Hengli sanction puts dollar-system access at risk for a publicly listed group whose textile-fiber operations have global customers.
2. The action came in the same week as renewed [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|US-Iran negotiations]] via Pakistani mediators, indicating Washington is tightening shadow-flow enforcement while keeping diplomatic optionality open.
3. Combined with [[CENTCOM]] disclosures that 36+ Iranian-flagged tankers have been turned back into the [[Persian Gulf]] during the dual-blockade phase, the [[Shadow fleet]] supply lever supporting Asian buyers is materially smaller than it was three weeks ago — which is one of the buffers [[Stephen Stapczynski]] flags as decaying in his Apr 27 supply-balance map (see [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] Apr 27 entry).
