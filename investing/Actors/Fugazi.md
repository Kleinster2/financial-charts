# Fugazi

**Fugazi** is a Los Angeles-founded streetwear brand positioning itself between traditional streetwear and upscale fashion, known for subversive takes on Americana and a deliberately ironic name (slang for "fake"). Flagship store on Canal and Orchard in Manhattan's Lower East Side, opened April 2024.

## Synopsis

Founded by Trevor Gorji, Fugazi is a self-funded, bootstrapped streetwear label that's been growing steadily since its first product launch in 2019. The brand's breakout moment was the "One in the Chamber" sneaker — a bootleg of the Chicago Air Jordan 1 with revolvers replacing Nike branding — which went viral. Gorji describes the brand as "a post-structuralist commentary on streetwear and the modern-day consumerist phenomenon," but the actual product is more accessible than that sounds: thoughtful flips on classic American garments (chain-stitched denim, western jackets, layered zip-ups) with minimal logo reliance.

The LES flagship at Canal and Orchard — one of NYC's hottest streetwear blocks alongside Awake NY and Colbo — includes an attached cafe (le Gaz). The brand is entirely self-funded with no outside investment, doesn't wholesale, and runs DTC only. WWD named it one of the top 5 emerging streetwear brands to watch in 2025. Gorji moved from LA to NYC in August 2023 to be closer to the fashion ecosystem, and is eyeing a future LA flagship and first official footwear collaboration.

## Evolution

- **2017:** Trevor Gorji conceives the brand while attending USC. He'd previously run Paradox, a skate-oriented label from his teenage years in Sarasota, Florida that sold at local skate shops and became his college essay subject.

- **2019:** First official product launch. The "One in the Chamber" sneaker — a bootleg Chicago AJ1 with revolvers replacing Swooshes and Wings logos — goes viral and establishes Fugazi's identity as subversive streetwear with design substance.

- **2020:** Also briefly runs "North Korea Skate Team" project (short-lived).

- **April 2023:** Hosts temporary pop-up space in West Hollywood, testing brick-and-mortar appetite.

- **August 2023:** Gorji relocates from LA to NYC to embed in the fashion capital.

- **December 2023:** Self-funded runway show at Tweed Courthouse, off-schedule. Instead of filling front rows with press and buyers, Gorji uses a lottery system to invite brand supporters — a deliberate anti-industry statement.

- **April 2024:** Opens first flagship store at Canal & Orchard on the LES, with le Gaz cafe. The block is a who's-who of NYC streetwear (Awake NY, Colbo, Scarr's Pizza).

- **January 2025:** WWD names Fugazi one of the top 5 emerging streetwear brands to watch. The brand is still entirely self-funded and doesn't use a publicist.

## Investment case

### Bull
- Strong brand identity and cult following without outside capital — rare in an era of VC-funded hype brands
- Canal & Orchard location puts it in NYC's streetwear epicenter
- DTC-only model preserves margins and brand control
- "Streetwear-to-upscale" positioning occupies a gap between Supreme-style graphics brands and luxury houses

### Bear
- Self-funded means slow scaling — no war chest for inventory, marketing, or expansion
- Single flagship + DTC is a narrow distribution moat
- No financials publicly available — unclear if the economics work at scale
- The name is a genuine barrier for new customers who don't get the irony
- Streetwear is trend-cyclical — today's "it" brand can be tomorrow's clearance rack
