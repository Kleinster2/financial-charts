**Allen Media Group** is the privately held media company founded by [[Byron Allen]] in 1993 (as Entertainment Studios), built around free ad-supported (AVOD) and broadcast assets — The Weather Channel, ~28 local TV stations, theGrio, free streaming, and the pending BuzzFeed acquisition — and valued at over $4.5 billion in 2022.

No priced venture equity rounds; growth has been funded through debt and asset acquisitions rather than outside equity. The debt-financed broadcasting roll-up has been the source of recurring balance-sheet strain, and AMG has at times explored selling stations to deleverage.

## Strategy

Allen Media Group is the AVOD ecosystem [[Byron Allen]] aims to pair with [[Starz]]'s subscription streaming (SVOD), framing the combination as "a left hook and a right hook." The BuzzFeed acquisition is positioned to seed a free global streaming "super app," and Allen has separately moved into the broadcast late-night slot vacated by Stephen Colbert at CBS. The throughline is aggregating distribution and content to build a free, ad-supported scale platform — with [[Starz]] as the paid-subscription complement he is pursuing. See [[Starz]] → The takeover situation.
