**Subaru** is a [[Japan]]ese automaker specializing in all-wheel-drive vehicles and horizontally opposed boxer engines, with disproportionate dependence on the North American market.

## Synopsis

Subaru occupies an unusual niche among global automakers: a mid-size manufacturer (~930K units/yr) that derives over 70% of revenue from the [[United States]], making it effectively a single-market bet disguised as a Japanese OEM. [[Toyota]] owns ~20% of the company and provides hybrid powertrain technology, but Subaru operates independently. The product lineup is narrow — Outback, Forester, Crosstrek, and Impreza account for the vast majority of sales — but brand loyalty metrics rank among the highest in the industry, particularly in the U.S. Northeast and Pacific Northwest.

The EV transition is the central strategic risk. Subaru's first dedicated EV, the Solterra (co-developed with Toyota as the bZ4X), launched in 2022 to underwhelming reception. The company plans 8 battery EVs by 2028 under a next-gen platform, but it is spending far less on electrification than peers — roughly $1.9B through 2028, a fraction of what [[Volkswagen]] or [[Hyundai]] are committing. The bull case is that Subaru's loyal customer base in cold-weather markets (where AWD remains genuinely useful) buys time. The bear case is that the EV gap widens until Toyota either absorbs Subaru or the brand fades into irrelevance as U.S. fuel economy and emissions standards tighten.
