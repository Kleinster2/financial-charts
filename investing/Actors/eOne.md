**eOne** (Entertainment One) is the film and TV production/distribution business acquired by [[Lionsgate]] from [[Hasbro]] in December 2023 for $500M — a deal that brought a 6,500-title library and scripted/unscripted TV (*The Rookie*, *Yellowjackets*, *Naked & Afraid*) into Lionsgate, now operating partly as Lionsgate Canada.

## History

eOne began in 1973 as the Canadian music distributor Records on Wheels, became ROW Entertainment, and by 2007 was Entertainment One, rolling up film/TV distributors (Alliance Films, Les Films Séville). In 2015 it bought 70% of Astley Baker Davies (producer of Peppa Pig) and became an investor in Amblin Partners alongside [[Steven Spielberg]]. [[Hasbro]] acquired the whole company for $4B in 2019 to build a media arm.

---

## The Lionsgate acquisition (and the Hasbro split)

[[Hasbro]] reversed course, exiting media to refocus on branded toy IP, and sold the eOne film/TV assets to [[Lionsgate]] for $500M (closed Dec 27, 2023) — an ~87% markdown from its purchase price two years earlier. The split of assets matters:

| Went to [[Lionsgate]] | Kept by [[Hasbro]] |
|----------------------|--------------------|
| 6,500-title film/TV library | eOne family brands |
| Scripted TV (The Rookie, Yellowjackets) | Astley Baker Davies / Peppa Pig stake |
| Unscripted TV (Naked & Afraid) | Folded into Hasbro Entertainment |

For [[Lionsgate]], eOne is a library-and-TV bolt-on that feeds the Television Production segment and the library annuity; for [[Hasbro]] it was a costly media detour. See [[Lionsgate]] → eOne acquisition.
