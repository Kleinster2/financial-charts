# Linda McMahon

Co-founder and former CEO of [[WWE]] who pivoted from professional wrestling into Republican politics as one of [[Donald Trump]]'s earliest and most durable allies. Now serves as U.S. Secretary of Education.

## Synopsis

Linda McMahon built [[WWE]] alongside husband Vince McMahon from a regional wrestling promotion into a publicly traded entertainment empire (market cap ~$9B at time of [[Endeavor]] merger). She spent ~$100M of personal wealth on two failed Connecticut Senate bids (2010, 2012), but the political investment paid off through access to Trump's inner circle. She served as SBA Administrator in the first Trump term (2017-2019), then chaired the America First Policy Institute, a Trump-aligned think tank, before co-chairing the 2024 presidential transition alongside [[Howard Lutnick]].

Her appointment as Education Secretary in January 2025 is a political signal more than a credentialing exercise -- she has no education policy background, and the role aligns with Trump's stated goal of shrinking or eliminating the Department of Education. For markets, McMahon matters as a transition gatekeeper: her co-chair role with [[Howard Lutnick]] shaped cabinet picks and regulatory appointments across agencies. The wrestling-to-politics pipeline she represents (along with the Trump-McMahon personal relationship stretching back to WWE storylines) illustrates how Trump's orbit rewards loyalty over domain expertise.
