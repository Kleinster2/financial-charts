**Project Prometheus** — AI startup building models that simulate physical-world behavior (aerodynamics, material stress, engineering design). Co-CEOs [[Jeff Bezos]] and [[Vik Bajaj]]. Raised $6.2B in 2025. Separately, Bezos is raising a $100B "manufacturing transformation vehicle" fund to acquire industrial companies and apply Prometheus technology to accelerate automation.

---

## Synopsis

Project Prometheus sits at the intersection of two capital cycles: the AI model race and the emerging physical-AI-for-industry push. The company is building AI systems that simulate how the physical world behaves — predicting airflow around a wing, where a metal part will crack under pressure, how materials perform under stress. The initial product is software tools for engineering simulation and design, competing in the $10B+ CAE (computer-aided engineering) market dominated by [[Ansys]], [[Dassault Systèmes]], and [[Siemens]]. The differentiation claim is that foundation-model approaches can make simulations orders of magnitude faster than traditional finite-element methods.

The real story is the $100B fund. Bezos is in early talks with sovereign wealth funds (Middle East, Singapore) and major asset managers to raise a "manufacturing transformation vehicle" that would acquire companies in chipmaking, defense, and aerospace, then apply Prometheus AI to boost efficiency and profitability. At $100B, it would rival [[SoftBank]]'s Vision Fund. [[JPMorgan Chase]] is in preliminary talks to back the project through its Security and Resiliency Initiative, a $10B fund launched Dec 2025 and led by former [[Berkshire Hathaway]] investment manager [[Todd Combs]]. The fund represents a new playbook: PE-style acquisition of industrial companies with AI-native operational transformation — similar to what firms are already doing in accounting and property management, but at manufacturing scale.

The key risk is whether physics-simulation AI actually delivers ROI at the level that would justify a $100B fund's return targets. Engineering simulation is a notoriously sticky market with deep domain expertise requirements. The bull case is that Bezos brings the operational credibility (Amazon's 750K+ warehouse robots), the capital network, and a team poached from [[OpenAI]] and [[Google DeepMind]].

## Technology

Prometheus builds AI systems that simulate physical-world behavior. Examples from investor documents:

- Simulating how air flows around an airplane wing
- Predicting where a metal part will crack under pressure
- Engineering simulation and design tools (initial product)

This targets the CAE market — traditional tools use finite-element analysis and computational fluid dynamics, which are computationally expensive and slow. The pitch is that AI foundation models can approximate these simulations at a fraction of the compute cost, enabling rapid iteration in product design.

## Context — physical AI convergence

Prometheus enters a crowded but early field of "physical AI" companies:

| Player | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| [[Atoms]] ([[Travis Kalanick]]) | Manufacturing/mining/food robotics — acquiring and operating physical businesses |
| [[Tesla]] ([[Elon Musk]]) | Humanoid robots (Optimus), factory automation |
| Prometheus ([[Jeff Bezos]]) | AI simulation for engineering/manufacturing — software layer for industrial transformation |

The distinction matters: Atoms is an operator (owns kitchens, mines, robots), Tesla is a manufacturer, and Prometheus is a simulation/software layer that would sit on top of acquired manufacturing companies via the $100B fund. Different bets on where the value accrues in AI-for-industry.
