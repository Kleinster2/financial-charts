# Leonid Radvinsky

Ukrainian-American billionaire — 100% owner of [[OnlyFans]]. Died March 23, 2026, aged 43, after a long battle with cancer. Net worth $7.8B (Forbes, Oct 2025). Extracted $1.8B+ in dividends from the platform since 2021; received a record $701M dividend in 2025. Since 2024, his [[OnlyFans]] shares have been held in a trust, leaving the fate of the platform's ownership in flux.

## OnlyFans acquisition

Acquired 75% stake in [[Fenix International]] Ltd ([[OnlyFans]] parent) from founders Tim and Guy Stokely in 2018. Later acquired remaining 25% — sole owner until death.

Price paid not disclosed, but platform was much smaller then. Extraordinary ROI given $1.8B+ already extracted in dividends while retaining 100% ownership of a $7-8B asset.

## Background

Born in Odesa, [[Ukraine]], moved to the US as a child and grew up in Chicago. Studied economics at [[Northwestern University]]. Started up and invested in various tech businesses before [[OnlyFans]], including the adult webcam business MyFreeCams — that experience gave him insight into creator economics and payment processing challenges that informed the [[OnlyFans]] strategy.

Low public profile — rarely gave interviews, no social media presence. Operated from behind the scenes throughout his ownership of [[OnlyFans]].

## Death and succession

Radvinsky died March 23, 2026, after a long battle with cancer. [[OnlyFans]] (via [[Fenix International]]) confirmed the death. His shares had been transferred to a trust in 2024 as his illness progressed. The fate of those shares — and the multibillion-dollar sale process — is now unclear.

## Potential exit

Bloomberg reported (May 2025) discussions to sell majority stake to consortium led by Forest Road Company at $7-8B valuation. David and Simon Reuben (British billionaires) may participate.

At the time of his death, Radvinsky had been in talks over a potential multibillion-dollar sale of a majority stake. Negotiations with various venture capital groups had been complicated by his illness (FT, Mar 23 2026). Whether those talks will proceed is unclear — the decision now rests with the trust.
