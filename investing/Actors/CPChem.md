# CPChem

50/50 petrochemicals joint venture between [[Chevron]] and [[Phillips 66]]. One of the world's largest producers of olefins and polyolefins, operating across the full ethylene-to-polyethylene value chain.

---

## Synopsis

CPChem represents one of the most significant petrochemical joint ventures globally, combining [[Chevron]]'s feedstock access with [[Phillips 66]]'s downstream processing expertise. The company operates primarily along the US Gulf Coast, with major facilities in Texas and Louisiana, producing ethylene, polyethylene, polypropylene, and aromatics that feed into packaging, automotive, construction, and consumer goods supply chains. The 50/50 structure means neither parent consolidates CPChem, making it an off-balance-sheet earnings contributor that investors often undervalue when analyzing either parent.

The strategic relevance is twofold. First, CPChem has been investing in major capacity expansions — its Golden Triangle Polymers project near Orange, Texas, is a multi-billion-dollar ethylene cracker and polyethylene facility targeting completion in the mid-2020s. This expansion positions CPChem to capture growing demand from US petrochemical exports, particularly to Asia. Second, CPChem is a key variable in any analysis of a [[Phillips 66]] or [[Chevron]] breakup or restructuring — the JV structure locks both parents into a partnership that neither can easily exit, and the asset's value (likely $15-25B on a standalone basis) is material to both companies' sum-of-parts valuations.
