#actor #defense #naval #france #state-owned

**Naval Group** — French state-controlled naval defense conglomerate, designer and builder of surface combatants, submarines, and naval combat systems. The world's leading designer of conventionally-powered submarines and one of the few global firms with sovereign nuclear submarine design capability. Lead foreign partner on Brazil's [[PROSUB]] program, building four Scorpène-class diesel-electric submarines plus the Álvaro Alberto nuclear-powered submarine in joint operation with [[Itaguaí Construções Navais]] at the Itaguaí naval complex.

---

## Synopsis

Naval Group is the most strategically embedded foreign defense supplier in Brazil. The [[PROSUB]] partnership signed in 2008 traded full submarine design and construction tech transfer for a guaranteed 4-conventional + 1-nuclear program build — the single largest defense investment in Brazilian history. As of November 2025, the third Scorpène (Tonelero / S42) was commissioned having been built entirely by [[Itaguaí Construções Navais]] in Brazil, validating that the tech transfer has achieved sovereign Brazilian submarine construction capability. The first steel for the nuclear-powered Álvaro Alberto was cut at Itaguaí in October 2023, and a new Naval Group contract supporting the program was signed in September 2025.

The Brazilian relationship is unusual in Naval Group's portfolio because it is the only export instance of meaningful nuclear-submarine technology cooperation outside the AUKUS framework. France's willingness to transfer nuclear-submarine architecture to a non-NATO partner reflects both the strategic value of the long-term industrial commitment (Itaguaí is a multi-decade operation) and a counter-positioning move against US-UK-Australia naval-cooperation dominance. For Brazil, the program represents a "Blue Amazon" sovereignty doctrine in operational form. For Naval Group, it is both a profitable build program and the institutional benchmark for non-AUKUS submarine cooperation worldwide.

## Brazilian program — PROSUB

The Brazilian government signed PROSUB with then-Direction des Constructions Navales Services (DCNS, now Naval Group) in 2008 under President [[Lula]]'s first term. The structure:

- 4 conventionally-powered Scorpène SBR submarines (S-BR variant), Brazilian-built
- 1 nuclear-powered submarine (Álvaro Alberto / SN-BR / SNBR), Brazilian-built with French-supported design but Brazilian-developed reactor
- Itaguaí naval complex construction (shipyard, base, and tunnel) — 100+ buildings, 700m hull-section transport tunnel
- Comprehensive tech transfer covering hull construction, propulsion (excluding nuclear reactor), combat systems, and personnel training

| Submarine | Commissioned | Status |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Riachuelo (S40) | Sept 2022 | In service |
| Humaitá (S41) | Jan 2024 | In service |
| Tonelero (S42) | Nov 2025 | In service — fully Brazilian-built |
| Angostura (S43) | Forthcoming | Under construction at Itaguaí |
| Álvaro Alberto (SN-BR) | Forthcoming (target 2035+) | First steel cut Oct 4, 2023 |

September 2025: new Naval Group contract signed in support of the nuclear submarine phase. The Brazilian nuclear reactor itself is developed by [[Marinha do Brasil]] in cooperation with [[CTMSP]] (Centro Tecnológico da Marinha em São Paulo) and is not part of the French technology package.
