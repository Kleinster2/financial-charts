## Public commentary themes

### Compute supply / demand

Patel's recurring frame: AI infrastructure is supply-constrained at every layer of the stack, with implications cascading across hyperscaler capex, model-lab pricing power, and incumbent data-services valuations. Specific calls and frameworks ingested into the vault:

### The Dario-conservatism thesis (Mar 2026)

Patel's sharpest framing on Anthropic's compute strategy: [[Dario Amodei]] explicitly undershot compute commitments out of bankruptcy concern, while [[OpenAI]] signed aggressive multi-year deals (with [[CoreWeave]], [[Oracle]], [[SoftBank]], [[NScale]]) before having the cash to pay. Result: by end of 2026 OpenAI has materially more compute than Anthropic, and Anthropic is now contracting at premium rates with lower-quality providers to catch up. The Patel framing was that the conservative posture was the strategic mistake. See [[Anthropic#Compute capacity and the cost of conservatism (Mar 2026)]] for the full argument.

### Token economics framework (Apr 2026)

The Apr 23, 2026 [[Invest Like the Best]] interview was structured around the supply-and-demand-of-tokens lens, with three pieces that landed as separate vault concepts:

- [[Idea-execution inversion]] — the structural reordering of innovation economics under AI; ideas are now scarce, execution is cheap
- [[Phantom GDP]] — Malcolm-at-SemiAnalysis framework: AI-driven output rises while measured GDP can fall because nominal value-added collapses
- [[Inference economics#Permanent-underclass thesis (Patel, Apr 2026)|Permanent-underclass thesis]] — "If you don't use more tokens, you'll never escape the permanent underclass"; concentration of frontier-tier access among well-capitalized buyers

### GPU useful-life extension

Patel's running counter to the [[Michael Burry]] et al. [[GPU depreciation cycle|2-3-year-depreciation thesis]]: in a chip-constrained world with rapidly improving models, GPU economic value rises, not falls, because the same hardware produces more tokens of better models over time. The Apr 23 interview added concrete: [[Hopper]] clusters re-signing for 3-4 more years (total ~7-8 years useful life), [[A100]] clusters re-signing for 2 more years, [[H100]] prices rising, cluster gross margins beyond the 35% headline. See [[GPU depreciation cycle]].

### Memory supercycle

Patel called DRAM prices to "double or triple from here" on Apr 23, 2026 — extending the existing [[Memory shortage 2025-2026|memory supercycle]] thesis. Mechanism: capacity grows ~20-30%/year max for DRAM (less for NAND), true incremental supply doesn't land until late-27/2028, demand reallocation continues. The smartphone volume halving call (1.4B → ~800M in 2026, 500-600M in 2027) from March stays in place.

### TSMC capex trajectory

Patel projection on Apr 23, 2026: [[TSMC]] 2028 capex "may be $100B." Comparison: 2025 capex disclosed at $56B; [[SemiAnalysis]] tracker at $57.4B since January. Implication propagates downstream to [[Lam Research]], [[Applied Materials]], [[ASML]], [[Carl Zeiss SMT]], [[MKS Instruments]] — wafer-fab equipment chain still "very underappreciated" at current multiples, per Patel.
