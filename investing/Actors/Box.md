**Box** (NYSE: BOX) — cloud content management and file sharing platform founded in 2005, IPO'd in 2015. CEO [[Aaron Levie]] has steered the company through a transition from growth-stage cloud storage competitor to a mature, profitable enterprise content platform. Box competes with [[Microsoft]] (SharePoint/OneDrive), [[Dropbox]], and [[Google]] (Drive) but differentiates on enterprise security, compliance, and workflow features. Market cap ~$5B (early 2026).

## Synopsis

Box's investment case is a classic "boring SaaS" story: mid-single-digit revenue growth, expanding margins, and aggressive share buybacks funded by strong free cash flow generation (~20% FCF margin). The stock underperformed growth peers during the 2020-2021 bull market but held up better in the 2022-2023 correction. Activist pressure from [[Starboard Value]] (2021) accelerated the margin expansion playbook. Box's AI strategy centers on Box AI — enterprise content intelligence layered on top of its content repository, powered by partnerships with [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]] models. Aaron Levie is also an active angel investor in AI entertainment (including [[Promise]]).
