[[Marc Bitzer]] — CEO of [[Whirlpool]] since October 2017, succeeding Jeff Fettig. Swiss-born; joined Whirlpool 1999 via the company's acquisition of [[Bauknecht]] (German appliance maker, where Bitzer had been since 1995). Career arc within Whirlpool: ran Europe, Middle East and Africa region (2011-2015), then North America (2015-2017), then named COO and CEO-designate before formal CEO appointment. Also chair of the Whirlpool board since January 2019.

Education: University of St. Gallen (Switzerland) Ph.D. in business administration, 1991.

---

## Why Marc Bitzer matters

Bitzer is the named decision-maker on the May 7, 2026 [[Whirlpool]] FY26 GAAP EPS guide cut from prior ~$6.25 (his January 2026 commentary) to $2.45-2.95 — a 50%+ cut in four months on an already-cyclical name. This is the largest discrete-period guide reset of his eight-year tenure as CEO.

The January 2026 framing he provided ("based on our recent successful product launches, reduced promotional intensity and a gradual recovery of the housing market") implicitly priced a housing recovery starting in H1 2026. The May 7 commentary characterizing Q1 demand as "weak appliance demand reminiscent of recession levels" is the cleanest CEO-level admission to date that the housing recovery is not arriving on the prior assumed timeline.

The strategic response Bitzer has chosen — accelerated cost and pricing actions to defend margins rather than chase volume — is consistent with his prior cycle behavior. His 2018-2019 European-restructuring playbook involved similar volume-defensive cost actions that ultimately restored margins ahead of revenue. The investing-vault question for [[2026 recession risk]] is whether the 2026 housing-cycle cut is a deeper version of that same playbook or a structural reset.
