# Air Lease Corp

Air Lease Corporation (NYSE: AL) is the second-largest US-listed aircraft lessor (behind [[AerCap]]), headquartered in Los Angeles, California. Founded by Steven F. Udvar-Hazy (the lessor industry's founding figure, previously founded International Lease Finance Corporation / ILFC). Air Lease's business model is operating-lease structures on new commercial aircraft delivered direct from [[Boeing]] / [[Airbus]] to airline operators globally.

## Post-Spirit shutdown role (May 2026)

[[Spirit Airlines]]'s May 2 2026 shutdown ([[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]]) returns ~200 [[Airbus A320]]-family aircraft to the secondary lessor market. Air Lease Corp, alongside [[AerCap]], is the most likely absorber by virtue of US-based operations, scale, and counterparty relationships with [[Frontier Airlines]] / [[Allegiant Travel]] / [[JetBlue]]. Air Lease's new-delivery focus historically meant less exposure to mid-life secondary aircraft, but the Spirit fleet redistribution creates an opportunistic acquisition window — particularly for newer A320neo family deliveries that were on Spirit's order book pre-bankruptcy.

The [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran-war]] jet-fuel cost regime favors fuel-efficient newer-generation aircraft, which is Air Lease's structural niche. The dynamic is mixed: ULCC counterparty distress reduces lessor revenue from that customer segment, but global airline demand for fuel-efficient lift increases lease-rate factor on newer A320neo / A321neo / 737 MAX aircraft.
