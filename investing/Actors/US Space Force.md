## May 2026 - Rocket Lab GEO space-domain-awareness award

[[US Space Force|Space Systems Command]] awarded [[Rocket Lab]] a $90M contract to design, manufacture, integrate, and operate two geostationary satellites carrying the Heimdall space-domain-awareness optical payload. The program matters because it puts a listed commercial-space company into the GEO custody layer: not launch, not communications capacity, but the ability to maintain awareness of objects in the geostationary belt.

The award uses Rocket Lab's Lightning bus and the Heimdall payload from Rocket Lab Optical Systems, the division created from [[GEOST]]. For USSF procurement, this is a commercial-prime test: the same company is providing the bus, payload integration, launch integration onto a government-furnished vehicle, and up to five years of on-orbit operations.

*Sources: [Rocket Lab official release, May 21 2026](https://investors.rocketlabcorp.com/news-releases/news-release-details/rocket-lab-awarded-90m-contract-build-geo-satellites-hosting); [[Journal of Space Commerce]], May 27 2026.*
