**Genting Group** — Malaysian conglomerate controlled by the [[Lim family]], operating the world's largest casino chain under the Resorts World brand. Spans [[Genting Berhad]] (KLSE: 3182), [[Genting Singapore]] (52% owned, operates [[Resorts World Sentosa]]), [[Genting Malaysia]] (49.3% owned, operates Resorts World Genting, [[Resorts World Las Vegas]], [[Resorts World NYC]]). Won one of three New York downstate casino licenses (December 2025). Genting Hong Kong went bankrupt in 2022.

---

## Synopsis

The Lim family built Asia's first modern casino empire by securing [[Malaysia]]'s only legal gaming license in the 1960s — a regulatory monopoly that has generated cash flow for six decades. From that foundation, the group expanded globally under the Resorts World brand: Resorts World Sentosa in [[Singapore]] (52% via Genting Singapore, the most valuable subsidiary), Resorts World Genting in Malaysia, Resorts World Las Vegas ($4.3B, opened 2021), Resorts World NYC (won full casino license December 2025, $5.5B expansion planned), and smaller operations in the UK, Bahamas, and Egypt.

The reality is mixed. Genting Singapore is the crown jewel: SGD 2.45B revenue and SGD 390M net profit in FY2025, ~$10B market cap, and a SGD 6.8B ($5B) expansion underway (RWS 2.0, targeting 2030). But Resorts World Las Vegas has been a disappointment — Q4 2024 EBITDA collapsed to $1M from $58M, revenue has been declining 18-21% YoY through 2025, and the property became the first Strip resort to temporarily eliminate resort fees due to low occupancy. It also drew a $10.5M fine (second-largest in Nevada history) for anti-money laundering failures. And Genting Hong Kong — the cruise line arm — went bankrupt in January 2022 with $2.78B in debt, triggered by the insolvency of its MV Werften shipyard in Germany. The group's batting average on expansion outside its Asian strongholds is poor.

The NYC license (December 2025) is a second chance at US success. Resorts World NYC already operates a slot parlor at Aqueduct that will add table games by March 2026, with a $5.5B expansion plan including 6,000 slots, 800 tables, 2,000 hotel rooms, and a 7,000-seat arena. But the license came with conditions: an independent third-party monitor for at least 5 years, related to the AML problems at RWLV — which Genting had omitted from its NY application.

## Evolution

The story of Genting is the story of a regulatory moat in the Malaysian highlands that funded a global casino empire — brilliant in Asia, costly elsewhere.

- **1965**: Lim Goh Tong — born in Fujian province, China, emigrated to Malaysia — conceived the idea of a hilltop resort while dining at Cameron Highlands. He realized a mountain resort closer to Kuala Lumpur would have massive potential. Founded Genting Highlands Berhad on April 27, 1965. Secured Malaysia's first and only legal casino license from PM Tunku Abdul Rahman — reportedly approved six hours after application. The regulatory monopoly has held for 60 years.

- **1971**: Resorts World Genting (originally Genting Highlands Resort) completed atop a mountain ~50km from KL. Malaysia's sole legal casino. This single property has been the cash engine behind every subsequent expansion.

- **1980s-2000s**: Expanded into plantations (palm oil), power generation, and cruise lines. The diversification into non-gaming reflected both the family's ambition and the reality that Malaysia's casino license, while valuable, constrained domestic gaming growth.

- **2007**: Lim Goh Tong died. His son Lim Kok Thay (second generation) took full control, pursuing aggressive international expansion.

- **2010**: Resorts World Sentosa opened in Singapore. The economics proved transformative — until [[Marina Bay Sands]] outcompeted it. RWS holds ~28% of Singapore's casino market vs. MBS's ~72%. The regulator punished the gap: MBS received a 3-year license renewal (maximum) in 2025; RWS got only 2 years for "unsatisfactory" tourism performance.

- **2021**: Resorts World Las Vegas opened June 24, 2021 — the first new resort on the Strip since the Cosmopolitan in 2010. Cost: $4.3B (most expensive resort property ever in Las Vegas). 59-story tower, 3,506 rooms across three [[Hilton]] brands (Las Vegas Hilton, Conrad, Crockfords). Performance has been disappointing: declining revenue, collapsed EBITDA, eliminated resort fees to drive occupancy.

- **2022**: Genting Hong Kong filed for winding-up in Bermuda (January 19, 2022). Liquidated by court order October 2022. Had $2.78B in triggered debt from the bankruptcy of its MV Werften shipyard in Germany. Losses wiped out the cruise line division (Crystal Cruises, Dream Cruises, Star Cruises).

- **March 2025**: Tan Kong Han became CEO of Genting Berhad — the first non-Lim family member to hold the role. Lim Kok Thay remains executive chairman. Third-generation Lim Keong Hui became CEO of Genting Plantations, positioning the succession but signaling the family needs outside management for the core gaming business.

- **March 2025**: RWLV fined $10.5M (second-largest in Nevada history) for anti-money laundering failures — allowed known illegal bookmakers to gamble millions for over a year.

- **December 2025**: Resorts World NYC won one of three downstate casino licenses. $5.5B expansion plan: 6,000 slots, 800 tables, 2,000 hotel rooms, 7,000-seat arena, 30+ F&B outlets, 3,000 housing units. License conditioned on independent monitor for 5+ years. First phase (table games at existing Aqueduct facility) expected by March 2026.

## Key properties

### Singapore — Resorts World Sentosa (Genting Singapore, 52% owned)
FY2025: revenue SGD 2.45B, net profit SGD 390M (-32.6% YoY). Gaming revenue SGD 1.60B (-6%). RWS 2.0 expansion: SGD 6.8B ($5B), groundbreaking November 2024, progressive rollout over 8 years targeting 2030. Includes: Universal Studios Singapore Minion Land, tripled Singapore Oceanarium, ~700 new hotel rooms.

### Malaysia — Resorts World Genting (Genting Malaysia, 49.3% owned)
Malaysia's only legal casino. Genting Malaysia FY2025: revenue MYR 11.88B (+8.9%), EBITDA MYR 3.30B (+13.3%), net profit MYR 670M.

### Las Vegas — Resorts World Las Vegas (Genting Malaysia)
Opened June 24, 2021. $4.3B. 3,506 rooms (three Hilton brands). Struggling: Q3 2025 revenue $175M, EBITDA loss of $12M. First Strip property to temporarily eliminate resort fees (summer 2025). $10.5M AML fine (March 2025). Civil RICO lawsuit from high roller RJ Cipriani (December 2025).

### New York — Resorts World NYC (Genting Malaysia)
Current: slot parlor (racino) at Aqueduct Racetrack, Queens. Full casino license won December 2025 (15-year). $5.5B expansion: 500,000 sq ft gaming, 2,000 hotel rooms, 7,000-seat arena, 3,000 housing units. 5-year independent monitor required. Projects $2.5B to MTA in next 4 years.

### Catskills — Resorts World Catskills (Empire Resorts, Genting Malaysia)
Genting Malaysia acquired remaining 51% of Empire Resorts from Kien Huat Realty (May 2025). Challenging: EBITDA positive since 2021 but negative free cash flow. $300M bond maturing November 2026, with less than $20M unrestricted cash. S&P downgraded to 'ccc'.

### UK — Genting UK
30+ provincial casinos plus exclusive London casinos. Resorts World Birmingham: GBP 150M, largest casino in UK.
