**Huawei Cloud** (华为云) — China's #2-3 cloud provider. The only major Chinese cloud built entirely on domestic hardware ([[Ascend]], Kunpeng). Should be the biggest beneficiary of China's AI push — instead, external revenue is declining while competitors surge.

## Why Huawei Cloud matters

Huawei Cloud is the paradox of China's AI buildout. It makes the chips ([[Ascend]] 910C), runs the cloud, and has the deepest government/SOE relationships. Yet external cloud revenue *fell* 3.5% in 2025 while [[Alibaba Cloud]] grew 36% and the global cloud market grew 29%. Huawei is winning the hardware layer but losing the platform layer.

## The revenue decline problem

The central tension: Huawei Cloud is the sovereignty play — entirely domestic hardware, no US dependency — yet it's losing market share during the biggest AI infrastructure buildout in Chinese history.

| Provider | 2025 growth | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| [[Alibaba Cloud]] | **+36%** | AI demand driving acceleration |
| Global cloud market | **+29%** | Omdia Q4 2025 |
| [[Tencent Cloud]] | **+22%** | Business services segment |
| **Huawei Cloud** | **-3.5%** external | Only major cloud declining |

**Why Huawei is losing ground:**

1. **Token economy favors speed over sovereignty.** The MaaS explosion benefits platforms optimized for developer velocity and low-latency inference. [[Volcano Engine]] and [[Alibaba Cloud]] are faster to iterate. Huawei's corporate culture prioritizes reliability and security — virtues in telecom, liabilities in the token race.

2. **Chip supplier, not platform operator.** [[ByteDance]] and [[Alibaba]] are ordering Huawei's new AI chips (Reuters, Mar 2026) — but running their *own* cloud platforms on top. Huawei is becoming the Intel of China AI: selling the hardware layer while others capture the platform margin.

3. **Developer ecosystem gap.** The MaaS market is driven by developers and startups building AI-native applications. Huawei's strengths (government, SOE, telecom) don't map to the developer community driving token growth. [[Volcano Engine]] captured 49.2% of MaaS tokens by making onboarding frictionless for developers.

4. **Ascend training gap.** Ascend 910C works for inference but frontier model training is still problematic. [[DeepSeek]]'s R2 delays were partly attributed to training difficulties on Ascend hardware. Developers who hit training walls on Ascend switch to NVIDIA-based clouds ([[Volcano Engine]], [[Alibaba Cloud]]).

## Strategy: sovereignty + telecom DNA

**"Shared Intelligence, Shared Success"** — Huawei Cloud's 2026 theme, centered on partner ecosystem for AI enterprise solutions.

**Telecom-centric distribution:** Positioning telecom carriers as AI distribution channels. This is Huawei's unique angle — decades of carrier relationships globally, and carriers are a natural channel for enterprise AI services.

**Government/SOE lock-in:** Where security and domestic hardware mandates override price competition. State Grid, Chinese Academy of Sciences, military applications — Huawei Cloud is the default where sovereignty is non-negotiable.

**International expansion:** Leveraging telecom incumbency in markets where US clouds face political resistance:
- **APAC:** 50+ financial, 200+ government/enterprise, 500+ internet customers across 10+ countries
- **Middle East:** Data sovereignty requirements favor non-US clouds
- **Africa:** Existing Huawei telecom infrastructure creates natural upsell

**Infrastructure requirements:** Data center partners must support 30kW/rack (2x industry standard) and liquid cooling for GPU clusters — signaling serious AI infrastructure buildout despite the revenue decline.

## Competitive position

| Metric | Huawei Cloud | [[Alibaba Cloud]] | [[Volcano Engine]] | [[Tencent Cloud]] |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Revenue trend | **Declining** (-3.5%) | **Growing** (+36%) | Explosive | Growing (+22%) |
| Hardware | **Own chips** (Ascend) | Own + NVIDIA | NVIDIA-dependent | NVIDIA-dependent |
| Customer base | Govt/SOE/telecom | Enterprise/retail | Developers/startups | SMB/gaming/social |
| Strategy | Sovereignty | Full stack | Commodity tokens | Aggregator |
| International | **Strong** (telecom base) | Limited | Minimal | Growing |

**Key advantage:** Only major cloud with zero US hardware dependency. Where sovereignty mandates exist (government, military, sensitive infrastructure), Huawei Cloud has no competitor.

**Key vulnerability:** The fastest-growing segment of China's cloud market (MaaS, developer tools, AI-native apps) is exactly where Huawei Cloud is weakest.
