**Nuvia** — Chip design startup founded by ex-[[Apple]] architects. Acquired by [[Qualcomm]] in 2021 for $1.4B. Became the foundation of Qualcomm's Oryon custom CPU cores.

---

## Why it matters

Nuvia gave [[Qualcomm]] the CPU design talent to compete with [[Apple]] silicon. The Oryon cores derived from Nuvia's work now power Snapdragon X Elite (PCs) and Snapdragon 8 Elite (phones).

## Lawsuits

**Apple vs Williams (2019-2023):** [[Apple]] sued Gerard Williams for breach of contract, alleging he plotted Nuvia while still employed. Apple dropped the case Apr 2023.

**[[Arm Holdings|Arm]] vs Qualcomm (2022-2025):** [[Arm Holdings]] claimed Nuvia's licenses couldn't transfer to [[Qualcomm]], demanded destruction of all Nuvia-derived designs. Jury ruled Dec 2024 that Qualcomm did not breach the license. Court entered final judgment for Qualcomm Sep/Oct 2025. Arm appealing; Qualcomm countersuit set for trial Mar 2026.

---

## Legacy in Qualcomm products

- **Oryon** custom CPU core — direct descendant of Nuvia's work
- **Snapdragon X Elite** (Jun 2024): 12 Oryon cores, laptops competing with [[Apple]] M-series
- **Snapdragon 8 Elite**: Second-gen Oryon cores for smartphones
- Third-gen Oryon cores now span both mobile and PC product lines
