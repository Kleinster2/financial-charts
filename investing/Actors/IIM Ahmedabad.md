# IIM Ahmedabad

Top business school in [[India]], consistently ranked #1 domestically. Founded in 1961 with support from the [[Harvard Business School]], known for its rigorous case-method Post-Graduate Programme in Management (PGDM, equivalent to MBA).

---

## Synopsis

IIM Ahmedabad (IIMA) occupies a role in Indian business and finance similar to what [[Harvard Business School]] and Wharton represent in the US — the default credential for anyone aiming at the top of Indian corporate life, consulting, or finance. The school's alumni network permeates Indian industry: CEOs of major conglomerates, founders of fintech and e-commerce companies, senior officials in the [[RBI]] and finance ministry, and increasingly, the Indian diaspora running global firms and funds. The acceptance rate for the PGDM is under 1%, making it one of the most selective graduate programs globally.

For the investing vault, IIMA matters as a talent pipeline indicator. When analyzing Indian companies, Indian AI startups, or the India macro thesis, IIMA alumni density in a company's leadership team is a positive signal — it indicates the company can attract top domestic talent, which in Indian tech and finance is a genuine competitive advantage given the war for talent among [[Infosys]], [[TCS]], global banks, and startups. Notable alumni appearing in the vault include [[Mixo Das]] (JPMorgan Asia-Pacific equity strategist, PGDM 2005-2007), illustrating the school's reach into global capital markets. The campus in Ahmedabad, Gujarat — designed by Louis Kahn — is itself architecturally significant, reflecting the institution's founding-era ambition.
