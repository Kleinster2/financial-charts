Austria is a landlocked Central European republic with a population of ~9.1 million and GDP of ~$500B. A founding member of the [[IEA]] (1974) and [[EU]] member state since 1995 (joined alongside [[Sweden]] and [[Finland]]). The economy is export-oriented, heavily integrated with [[Germany]] (largest trading partner), and anchored by industrials, tourism, and financial services.

Vienna serves as headquarters for several international organizations including [[OPEC]], the [[OSCE]], and [[IAEA]], giving Austria outsized diplomatic relevance relative to its economic size. The financial sector features notable names like [[Erste Group]] and [[Raiffeisen Bank International]] (RBI), the latter with significant exposure to Central and Eastern Europe including [[Russia]] — a source of sanctions complications since 2022. Austria's energy policy is distinctive in Europe for its complete nuclear power ban (constitutional since 1999) while maintaining above-average hydroelectric generation (~60% of electricity).
