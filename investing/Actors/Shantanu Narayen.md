**Shantanu Narayen** is [[Adobe]]'s CEO from 2007 to 2026, departing once a successor is named and transitioning to Chairman. The longest-serving CEO of a major software company, he led Adobe's transformation from perpetual-license to subscription model.

## Synopsis

Narayen's defining achievement was the 2013 Creative Cloud transition -- moving Adobe from one-time license sales to recurring subscriptions, a bet that initially cratered revenue and drew analyst fury but ultimately tripled the company's market cap. He followed with acquisitions of [[Marketo]] ($4.75B, 2018) and [[Magento]] ($1.68B, 2018) to build out Adobe's Experience Cloud, making the company a three-platform business: Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, and Experience Cloud. Under his tenure Adobe grew from ~$3B revenue to ~$22B, with operating margins consistently above 35%.

The exit timing (announced March 12, 2026) is notable. Adobe faces its most serious strategic challenge since the Creative Cloud transition: generative AI threatens to commoditize the creative tools that underpin the entire business. [[Firefly]], Adobe's generative AI platform, has been adopted quickly (~13B images generated in its first 18 months) but the market remains skeptical that a subscription-SaaS company can defend pricing when open-source alternatives proliferate. Narayen is leaving at the moment when the next CEO's job is hardest -- the classic "retiring at the top" move that either looks prescient or cowardly in hindsight.
