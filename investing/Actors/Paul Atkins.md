## Crypto involvement

**Pre-SEC:**
- Co-chair, Token Alliance (Chamber of Digital Commerce)
- Advisory board, Securitize (crypto securities firm)
- Helped develop digital asset industry best practices

**As Chairman:**
- Created SEC Crypto Task Force (hired [[Michael Selig]] as Chief Counsel)
- Ended "regulation by enforcement" approach
- Working on "commonsense crypto asset taxonomy"

---

## Regulatory philosophy

**Free-market, innovation-friendly:**
- Known for advocating reduced regulatory burdens
- Believes clear rules better than enforcement actions
- Skeptical of aggressive SEC expansion

**Contrast with Gensler:**
| Gensler (2021-2025) | Atkins (2025-) |
|---------------------|----------------|
| 100+ crypto enforcement actions | Rulemaking first |
| "Most tokens are securities" | Taxonomy to clarify |
| Hostile to industry | Collaborative |

Atkins is serving remainder of Gensler's term, which expires Jun 5, 2026. Trump could renominate him for full 5-year term or appoint someone else.

---

## Key actions (2026)

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| Mar 17 | **[[SEC-CFTC Joint Crypto Taxonomy]]** — co-published 68-page interpretive release with [[CFTC]]. Five-category token taxonomy, 16 named digital commodities. Most significant US crypto regulatory action in a decade |
| Mar 18 | Approved [[Nasdaq]] tokenized securities rule (Release 34-105047) — first US exchange approved for tokenized trading |
| Mar 25 | House Financial Services Committee hearing: "Tokenization and the Future of Securities" |
| Pending | Previewed possible exemptive rulemaking building on the taxonomy |

**Term clock:** Atkins is serving the remainder of Gensler's term, expiring **June 5, 2026**. The [[CLARITY Act]] push and tokenization approvals are racing against this deadline. If Trump does not renominate him (or if the CLARITY Act doesn't pass before his term ends), the regulatory clarity could be reversed by a successor.
