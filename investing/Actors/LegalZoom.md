## Why LegalZoom exists

**Problem:** Legal services are expensive. Small businesses can't afford attorneys for routine matters.

**Solution:** Automated document generation, guided questionnaires, flat-fee attorney access.

**Moat (pre-AI):** Brand recognition, SEO dominance, customer base.

---

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Subscription model = recurring revenue
- Small business formation is steady
- Legal services underpenetrated for SMBs
- Could integrate AI to cut costs

**Bear:**
- **AI disruption is existential** — Claude Cowork does exactly what LegalZoom does
- Low switching costs
- Commoditized offering
- Free AI alternatives emerging

---

## February 2026: Claude Cowork selloff

**Feb 3-4, 2026:** Stock fell **-20%** — largest percentage decliner among legal services — on [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|Claude Cowork launch]].

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Two-day decline | -20% |
| Catalyst | Claude Cowork legal automation |

**Why LegalZoom is most exposed:**

1. **Direct competition:** Claude Cowork Legal can generate the same documents LegalZoom sells
2. **No data moat:** LegalZoom doesn't own proprietary legal databases
3. **Price compression:** AI-generated docs will be free or near-free
4. **Customer overlap:** SMBs and consumers — exactly who will adopt AI fastest

**Bear case in full:** LegalZoom's entire value proposition is automating routine legal work. AI does this better and cheaper. The business model may not survive.

**Bull case:** Brand trust matters for legal documents. Some customers will pay for human review and guarantees.
