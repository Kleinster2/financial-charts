# Pernod Ricard

French spirits group, the world's second-largest by revenue (after [[Diageo]]), formed 1975 from the merger of Pernod (founded 1805) and Ricard (founded 1932). Listed on Euronext Paris (RI.PA). Brand portfolio includes Absolut Vodka, Jameson Irish Whiskey, Chivas Regal, Martell Cognac, Beefeater, Havana Club, Mumm/Perrier-Jouët Champagne, and Glenlivet. The group's substantial Chinese luxury-spirits exposure (Martell Cognac is the second-largest Cognac brand in China after [[Hennessy]]) makes it a sensitive case for the [[Common Prosperity]] anti-ostentation framework.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. [[Common Prosperity]] anti-ostentation exposure — Martell Cognac is one of the highest-profile Western luxury brands consumed in elite Chinese networking and gift-giving contexts. The 2021-onward anti-ostentation rhetoric, combined with the property-crisis-driven wealth effects, has produced sustained Cognac-category weakness affecting Pernod, [[LVMH]] (Hennessy), and [[Rémy Cointreau]]
2. China anti-dumping investigation 2024-2025 → 2025 ruling with exemption — Cognac imports were the target of a Chinese anti-dumping investigation launched January 2024 in retaliation for [[EU]] EV tariffs on Chinese carmakers. Final ruling July 5, 2025 imposed 27.7-34.9% duties on EU brandy for 5 years, but Pernod, [[Rémy Cointreau]], and Möet Hennessy ([[LVMH]]) secured exemptions via minimum-price commitments (Pernod stressed the deal "does not constitute an acknowledgment of dumping practices"). The asymmetry of who got exempted is the cleanest investing read — the major branded cognac houses cleared the policy filter while smaller / unbranded brandy exporters absorbed the duties
3. Premium-spirits cyclical exposure — alongside [[Diageo]], the cleanest comp for the global premium-spirits cycle. Cognac and whisky pricing power dynamics in EM (China + India) are the swing factors
