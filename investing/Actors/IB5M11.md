## What it tracks

The IMA-B 5+ sub-index holds only the longer NTN-Bs (over five years to maturity), so IB5M11 is the highest-duration, most rate-sensitive member of the B3 fixed-income ETF family — the cleanest expression of a "Brazilian real-rates falling" view.

## Use case

The long-duration IPCA bet: maximum convexity to a Selic-cutting cycle and to falling long real yields, at the cost of the most mark-to-market volatility. Used to lengthen duration deliberately versus the broad [[IMAB11]] or the short [[B5P211]].

## Correlation

Its long duration gives it the highest equity overlap in the family — 0.47 daily-return correlation to [[EWZ]] over two years. Long Brazilian bonds and Brazilian equities are nearly the same Selic-cycle trade at the margin. See [[Brazilian fixed-income ETFs]].
