**Melius** (Melius Research) is an independent equity research firm founded by Scott Davis, formerly a senior analyst at [[JPMorgan]] and [[Morgan Stanley]]. The firm focuses on multi-industry, capital goods, and industrial technology coverage.

## Synopsis

Melius occupies a niche in the post-MiFID II research landscape where independent shops can thrive by offering differentiated, high-conviction coverage that bulge-bracket banks have deprioritized. Scott Davis built his reputation covering industrials and multi-industry conglomerates at [[Morgan Stanley]] and [[JPMorgan]], and launched Melius to operate with the freedom that sell-side economics increasingly constrain at large banks. The firm's coverage universe includes names like [[Honeywell]], [[3M]], [[General Electric]], [[Emerson Electric]], and other industrial bellwethers.

For vault purposes, Melius matters as a frequently cited source on industrial and capital goods names. Their calls tend to carry weight with institutional investors because the team's deep sector expertise and willingness to take non-consensus positions. The firm operates on a subscription model, selling research directly to buy-side clients rather than bundling it with brokerage commissions — a structure that aligns analyst incentives with research quality rather than trading volume.
