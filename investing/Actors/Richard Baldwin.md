**Richard Baldwin** — professor of international economics at IMD in Lausanne. Previously at the Graduate Institute Geneva (1991-2023) and founder/editor of VoxEU (now CEPR.org). Leading trade economist known for work on global value chains, "the great convergence," and trade fragmentation. Author of *The Great Convergence: Information Technology and the New Globalization* (2016) and *The Globotics Upheaval* (2019).

## Views

### Why tariffs didn't wreck the system (cited Apr 1 2026)

Two Substack posts cited by [[Martin Wolf]] (FT, Apr 1 2026):

- *"Why Didn't Trumpian Tariffs Wreck the World Trade System"* — argues Trump did not do all that he threatened. His bark was worse than his bite.
- *"How Did the 75-percenters Save the System?"* — the US (14% of world goods imports) and [[China]] (together 25% of world trade) do not dominate global trade enough to destroy it. The remaining 75% of the system continued trading because it depends on it. No retaliation cycle, no imitation of US WTO repudiation.

Core insight: the world trading system is more decentralized than it appears from the US-China lens. It survived 2025 because most countries chose continued trade over alignment with either side.
