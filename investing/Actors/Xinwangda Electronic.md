**Xinwangda Electronic** (Sunwoda Electronics, SHE: 300207) is a [[Shenzhen]]-based lithium battery manufacturer supplying consumer electronics (smartphones, laptops) and EV battery packs. Listed on [[Shenzhen]] Stock Exchange (ChiNext).

## Synopsis

Xinwangda occupies the second tier of [[China]]'s battery hierarchy -- below [[CATL]] and [[BYD]] in EV batteries but a significant player in consumer electronics power packs, where it supplies major smartphone OEMs including [[Apple]] and [[Samsung]]. The company has been aggressively expanding into EV batteries and energy storage systems, investing in production capacity across multiple Chinese cities. Founded in 1997, it rode the smartphone boom of the 2010s to build scale, then pivoted toward the higher-margin, higher-growth EV opportunity.

The investment case hinges on whether Xinwangda can break into the EV battery top tier or remains trapped as a second-string supplier. [[CATL]]'s dominance (37% global share) and [[BYD]]'s vertical integration make this difficult, but Xinwangda's consumer electronics relationships give it a cash flow base to fund the transition. The company also invests in tech startups through venture activities, including an A-round investment in [[DeepMirror]]. Revenue has grown rapidly but margins remain compressed by competition and the commodity nature of lithium-ion cells. Listed on ChiNext, it trades at typical Chinese battery-sector valuations -- high revenue growth, low profitability, policy-dependent.
