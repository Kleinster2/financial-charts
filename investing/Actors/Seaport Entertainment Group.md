**Seaport Entertainment Group** — NYC entertainment/real estate company spun off from [[Howard Hughes Holdings]] in July 2024. Operates the South Street Seaport district in lower Manhattan: Pier 17, rooftop concert venue, retail, dining. Inherited ~$1.5B in cumulative HHH investment and a history of losses. Publicly traded on NYSE American (SEG).

## Tin Building failure

The Tin Building by Jean-Georges Vongerichten was the district's flagship food hall — 54,000 sq ft, ~$200-250M to build and outfit. It never came close to breaking even.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Opened | Sep 2022 |
| Closed | Feb 24, 2026 |
| 2023 gross revenue | $32.4M |
| Daily losses (early 2025) | ~$100K |
| Cumulative losses | $100M+ |
| Employees at closure | 132 |
| HHH equity stake | 25% ($45M) + $10M in warrants (written off 2024) |

Reasons: remote location lacking year-round foot traffic, high prices ($7 croissants, $25 cocktails), seasonal tourism dependency, confusing layout. Now leased to Balloon Museum.

## 250 Water Street (sold)

Full-block development site. Former mercury-contaminated brownfield (19th-century thermometer factory). HHH bought it in June 2018 for $180M, invested $40M+ in air rights and remediation. Eight prior proposals failed Landmarks Preservation Commission approval over ~25 years.

HHH won LPC approval in May 2021 (26-story, 324 feet, 550,000 sq ft, SOM architects). The Seaport Coalition sued, alleging a $40M quid pro quo (museum funding for approval). Justice Arthur Engoron halted construction twice (2022-2023). Appellate Division and NY Court of Appeals overturned (May 2024).

SEG sold the site to Tavros Capital for $143M (closed Feb 2026). Net proceeds ~$76.1M after debt repayment. A significant loss on $220M+ invested.
