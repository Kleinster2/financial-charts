# Cravath, Swaine & Moore

Founding institution of the modern American corporate law firm — the source of the "Cravath system" of lockstep partnership, hire-from-within, and one-firm-one-vote governance that defined elite NY firms through the 20th century. The 2016 [[Scott Barshay]] departure to [[Paul, Weiss]] (which the FT and academic commentators date as the start of the [[Big Law transformation]]) triggered a five-year arc of formal lockstep modifications that culminated in Cravath publicly abandoning pure lockstep in December 2021 and adding a non-equity partner tier in late 2021 / publicly disclosed November 2023. As of 2026, Cravath is no longer a strict-lockstep firm; with [[Debevoise]]'s June 2025 addition of a non-equity tier, [[Wachtell Lipton]] is the only major US holdout still operating an all-equity-partner lockstep partnership.

## The 2016 Barshay departure

In 2016, M&A partner [[Scott Barshay]] left Cravath for [[Paul, Weiss]] — the move conventionally cited as the inflection point of the [[Big Law transformation]]. Paul Weiss's compensation package was reportedly multiples of what Cravath's lockstep system could pay; Cravath had been the archetype of lockstep, and the departure proved that even there, a top originator could be moved with money.

## Compensation modifications timeline

| Date | Change | Source |
|---|---|---|
| December 2021 | Presiding partner Faiza Saeed announces "modified lockstep" in internal memo. Retains seniority-based pay bands but allows partners making "extraordinary" contributions ("franchise builders") to be paid above their band. Formal abandonment of strict lockstep in place at Cravath since 1976. | American Lawyer, Law360, ABA Journal (Dec 2021) |
| Late 2021 | Non-equity (salary) partner tier introduced quietly. Salaried partners remain eligible for promotion to equity. | Bloomberg Law / American Lawyer (Nov 2023 retrospective disclosure) |
| November 7, 2023 | Saeed memo publicly disclosing the non-equity tier (5-10 partners as of late 2023). | Bloomberg Law, American Lawyer |

Saeed framed the December 2021 change to the *Wall Street Journal* as restoring the discretionary model Cravath used pre-1976.

Outflows accelerated after the lockstep modifications, suggesting modifications-without-scale produced the worst-of-both outcome the [[Big Law transformation]] frame predicts:

*Above the Law* ran "No Longer A Rarity: More Partners Leave Cravath" (January 2024) cataloguing the pattern; *Bloomberg Law* later ran "Cravath Partner Exits Show No Firm Is 'Untouchable'." The 2026 pace of 9 partner departures by May represents an *acceleration* of the post-modification trajectory, not stabilisation — consistent with the warning-sign argument that modified lockstep does not stop the slide toward scale-plus-pay.

## Vault read

The Cravath case is the empirical evidence for the warning-sign argument in [[Big Law transformation]]: lockstep modification, once initiated under defensive pressure, does not stabilise into a new equilibrium — it slides toward the scale-plus-pay model over time. Cravath nine years post-Barshay sits closer in compensation structure to [[Paul, Weiss]] than to [[Wachtell Lipton]], and the per-partner economics are below both lockstep elites and scale-plus-pay leaders. That is the trajectory question Wachtell's 2025-26 modifications now face.
