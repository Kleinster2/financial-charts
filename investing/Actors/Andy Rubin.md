# Andy Rubin

Creator of [[Android]] and founder of Playground Global, a venture capital firm focused on AI and robotics. One of the most consequential mobile technology figures of the 21st century.

## Synopsis

Andy Rubin co-founded Android Inc. in 2003 and sold it to [[Google]] in 2005 for ~$50M -- arguably the most value-destructive acquisition (for the seller) in tech history, given that Android now powers ~72% of global smartphones and generates tens of billions in annual revenue for Google's ecosystem. Rubin ran Android at Google until 2013, when he was moved to head Google's robotics division before departing in 2014. His exit was later complicated by sexual harassment allegations reported by the New York Times in 2018, which revealed Google had paid him a $90M exit package despite the misconduct findings -- a scandal that triggered employee walkouts at Google.

Post-Google, Rubin launched Essential Products (the Essential Phone in 2017, which flopped) and Playground Global, a venture capital firm investing in AI, robotics, and deep tech startups. Playground has backed companies including [[Kindred AI]] (warehouse robots), Nuro (autonomous delivery), and various AI infrastructure plays. Rubin's pattern is consistent: he spots platform shifts early (mobile OS, robotics, AI) but execution beyond Android has been uneven. For the vault, Rubin matters as a venture investor in the AI/robotics pipeline and as a case study in how Silicon Valley's tolerance for founder misconduct has shifted over the past decade.
