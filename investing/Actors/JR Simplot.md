# JR Simplot

Private Idaho-based agribusiness, one of the largest US producers of phosphate fertilizer and the dominant US frozen potato processor. Founded 1929 by John Richard "J.R." Simplot. Headquartered in Boise, Idaho. Remains family-controlled through subsequent generations.

## Business segments

### AgriBusiness

US phosphate fertilizer production — mines phosphate rock at Smoky Canyon, Wyoming, processes at Pocatello, Idaho. Also nitrogen fertilizer distribution via retail network. One of two major US phosphate fertilizer producers alongside [[Mosaic]].

### Food Group

Frozen potato processor — supplies french fries to McDonald's, Burger King, Yum Brands, and global QSR chains. Also avocado, vegetable, and fruit processing.

### Land & Livestock

Cattle feedlots, ranching operations.

## Phosphate tariff dispute

[[JR Simplot]] and [[Mosaic]] jointly petitioned for countervailing and anti-dumping duties on Moroccan phosphate imports (2020-2021), winning tariffs that significantly reduced [[OCP Group]] shipments to the US. US phosphate imports fell from above 3M tonnes in 2018 to ~2M tonnes by 2025, squeezing supply for US farmers during a period of rising global phosphate costs.

In March 2026, 64 US agricultural groups published a joint letter urging JR Simplot and [[Mosaic]] to drop their support for the duties, citing severe supply tightness driven by [[China]] export restrictions, the 2026 [[Strait of Hormuz]] disruption, and ongoing [[Russia]] sanctions. The letter became a focal point in the broader [[Nitrogen Trap]] crisis debate — domestic fertilizer protectionism vs. farm-level supply needs.
