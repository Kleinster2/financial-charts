# Hindustan Zinc

## Synopsis

[[Hindustan Zinc]] (Hindustan Zinc Limited; NSE: HINDZINC; BSE: 500188) is the world's #2 zinc producer and India's only integrated zinc-lead-silver miner, headquartered in Udaipur, [[Rajasthan]]. The company runs an end-to-end value chain from underground zinc-lead mining at Rampura Agucha (the world's largest underground zinc operation, 5.4 Mtpa ore capacity), Sindesar Khurd (5.6 Mtpa), Rajpura Dariba, Zawar, and Kayad — through hydrometallurgical zinc smelters and lead smelters — to refined output of 827 kt of zinc, 219 kt of lead, and 687 metric tonnes of silver in FY 2025. Founded as a government-owned enterprise on January 10, 1966 from the Metal Corporation of India, the firm was privatised starting in 2002 when [[Vedanta]] founder [[Anil Agarwal]]'s Sterlite Opportunities and Ventures Limited took an open-offer 64.92% stake; today [[Vedanta]] Limited holds approximately 63.4% (the controlling stake), the [[Government of India]] retains 29.54%, and the remainder is publicly traded.

The investing-vault relevance runs through three threads. First, [[Vedanta]] May 2026 demerger: Hindustan Zinc is the most valuable single asset across the Vedanta complex and is *not* itself part of the demerger spin-out — it stays as a separately listed Vedanta subsidiary, with the residual Vedanta Ltd entity inheriting the 63.4% ownership stake as its primary value driver post-demerger. Second, silver as monetary metal: HZL's 687 MT FY 2025 silver production positions it as a pass-through beneficiary of any silver re-rating, and management has guided to expanding silver capacity to 1,500 TPA by FY 2030 — more than doubling the current footprint. Third, India non-ferrous metals cohort: HINDZINC sits adjacent to but structurally distinct from the four-name [[Hindalco]] cluster validation cohort (HINDALCO + VEDL + TATASTEEL + JSWSTEEL), with the differentiator being HZL's near-pure single-commodity zinc/silver exposure versus the multi-metal conglomerate structure of [[Vedanta]] and [[Hindalco]].

## Position in Vedanta demerger

The May 2026 [[Vedanta]] demerger does not directly involve [[Hindustan Zinc]] as a corporate action. HZL remains a separately listed company with its own NSE/BSE quotation and continues operating under its existing structure. What changes is the ownership chain at the Vedanta Ltd level: the residual Vedanta entity post-demerger (the one that retains the original NSE: VEDL listing) carries the 63.4% HZL stake as its core asset, alongside the international zinc-mining business, copper, ferro-chrome, nickel, and emerging ventures. Per the ICICI Direct demerger research note, the residual Vedanta entity "derives bulk of value from Hindustan Zinc stake" — meaning the zinc-silver leg of the multi-commodity Vedanta complex effectively trades through both VEDL (the pass-through holding company) and HINDZINC (the direct subsidiary).

For the [[Hindalco]] cluster validation framework, HINDZINC is a candidate for an extension cluster: a single-commodity zinc/silver name that can be tested against the multi-metal Indian conglomerates (HINDALCO, VEDL) and global zinc peers (e.g., Korea Zinc, Boliden, Teck Resources). The existing four-name Indian metals cluster does not include HINDZINC.

## Position in silver complex

Hindustan Zinc's 687 MT of FY 2025 silver output makes it the largest single-asset silver producer outside of the Mexican-American silver complex (Fresnillo, Pan American Silver, Hecla, Coeur). The expansion path to 1,500 TPA by FY 2030 implies more than a doubling of silver volumes over the next five years — at LBMA silver pricing ranges of $30–35/oz, this represents incremental annual revenue of roughly $750M–$900M from silver alone, on top of the zinc/lead base.

The silver narrative is not yet validated as a standalone investment thread in the vault; HZL is currently held in the vault as a Vedanta-cluster name, but the silver capacity expansion is the structural thesis that differentiates HZL from a generic zinc miner.
