**SBB** (Samhallsbyggnadsbolaget i Norden) — Swedish landlord specializing in social infrastructure properties (schools, government offices, elderly care facilities) across the Nordics. Once valued at SEK 100B+, the company became a poster child for European real estate distress when rising rates exposed an overleveraged balance sheet.

---

## Synopsis

SBB's story is a cautionary tale about what happens when a yield-starved market meets a leveraged acquirer in a zero-rate environment. Founded in 2016 by CEO Ilija Batljan, a former Social Democrat politician, SBB grew explosively by acquiring public-sector properties — schools, courthouses, care homes — financed with cheap debt and hybrid instruments. The thesis was simple: government-tenanted properties with inflation-linked rents are the safest cash flows in real estate. By 2022, SBB had amassed a portfolio valued at over SEK 150B and a market cap exceeding SEK 100B. Then the Riksbank started hiking.

The rate cycle exposed everything. SBB's debt stack — roughly SEK 80B in bonds and hybrids — had been priced for perpetual low rates. As refinancing costs spiked, the company's investment-grade rating was stripped (Fitch downgraded to junk in 2023), the stock fell over 90% from its peak, and CEO Batljan stepped down in 2024. The company has been in triage mode since: selling assets (often at discounts to book value), restructuring debt, and attempting to spin off its education property portfolio. SBB is relevant not as a recovery play but as a reference case for European real estate leverage risk — the same dynamics threaten other Nordic and German landlords carrying pre-2022 debt loads. The autonomous driving partnership with [[WeRide]] and Canton of Zurich is a separate Swiss Federal Railways entity (also abbreviated SBB) and is unrelated.
