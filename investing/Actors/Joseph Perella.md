# Joseph Perella

Joseph Perella is a US investment banker, co-founder of [[Perella Weinberg Partners]] (2006), and one of the architects of the modern hostile M&A advisory playbook. Built his career at [[First Boston]] (1972–1988), where he led the M&A department alongside [[Bruce Wasserstein]] and helped shape the 1980s wave of takeover practice. Co-founded Wasserstein Perella & Co. in 1988, which was later sold to Dresdner Bank in 2001. Joined [[Morgan Stanley]] in 1993 (after Wasserstein Perella was sold), served as chairman of institutional securities and investment banking until 2005, when he left following the boardroom fight that forced out CEO [[Philip Purcell]].

After leaving Morgan Stanley with [[Terry Meguid]], Perella partnered with [[Peter Weinberg]] (ex-[[Goldman Sachs]]) to found [[Perella Weinberg Partners]] in 2006. The firm's mandate mix — sell-side M&A, fairness opinions, restructuring, shareholder defence — directly reflects the practice Perella built at First Boston and Wasserstein Perella.
