**José Antonio Kast** — President of [[Chile]] from March 2026. Leader of the right-wing Partido Republicano, won the November 2025 Chilean presidential election, replacing [[Gabriel Boric]]. Market-relevant for his pledged overhaul of Chilean [[Lithium|lithium]] policy — moving from Boric-era state-anchored joint ventures toward market-led concessions.

---

## Why Kast matters for markets

| Lever | Stated position | Policy implication |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| [[Lithium]] concessions | Pledged to make lithium "concessionable" (remove nuclear-material classification, streamline private access) | Potential new private capacity at Atacama and beyond, outside [[Codelco]] state oversight |
| Mining royalties | Pro-investor positioning | Likely reversal of Boric-era royalty increases |
| Copper / [[Codelco]] | Supportive of private competition | Possible increase in private participation in copper sector |
| Fiscal policy | Orthodox, pro-investment | Peso / IPSA tailwind expected |

**Nova Andino Litio contract:** The Boric-era [[SQM]]-[[Codelco]] joint venture (Nova Andino Litio SpA, December 2025) has contractual protection through 2060 and cannot be simply unwound. Kast's "concessionable" framework applies to *new* lithium concessions, not the existing JV. This creates a competitive asymmetry: Nova Andino operates under the 70%/85% state-margin framework while new entrants could operate under a more market-friendly regime.
