## History

**1910–1980: Building the franchise**
Founded as bond brokerage. Became powerhouse in fixed income trading.

**1980s: The golden era**
- CEO John Gutfreund nicknamed "King of Wall Street"
- Pioneered **mortgage-backed securities** — bought home mortgages from thrifts, packaged and sold to investors
- Created the first MBS (Ginnie Mae securities)
- Culture immortalized in Michael Lewis's *[[Liar's Poker]]*

**1981: Bloomberg's exit**
[[Michael Bloomberg]], a general partner running IT and block trading, was pushed out when Phibro acquired Salomon. His **$10M severance** funded the creation of [[Bloomberg LP]].

**1991: Treasury bond scandal**
Trader Paul Mozer submitted false bids to buy more Treasury bonds than permitted (35% limit). CEO Gutfreund knew but failed to act.
- Fine: **$190M** + $100M restitution fund
- Gutfreund forced out
- Warren Buffett (Berkshire owned $700M stake) became interim chairman to save the firm

**1997–1998: Absorption**
- [[Travelers]] Group acquired Salomon for **$9B** (1997)
- [[Travelers]] merged with Citicorp (1998) → Salomon became part of Citigroup
- Renamed Salomon Smith Barney, then Citigroup Global Markets (2003)

**2009: Final dismantling**
Citigroup CEO Vikram Pandit dismantled remaining Salomon operations.

**2022: Revival attempt**
Former employees announced plans to revive the Salomon Brothers brand as independent investment bank.

## Legacy

**Innovations:**
- Mortgage-backed securities market
- Quantitative trading culture
- Bond arbitrage strategies

**Alumni:**
- [[Michael Bloomberg]] — founded Bloomberg LP
- John Meriwether — founded Long-Term Capital Management (LTCM)
- Lewis Ranieri — "father of mortgage securities"

**LTCM connection:** Salomon alumni created LTCM, which took $1T+ in arbitrage positions before its 1998 collapse required Fed-coordinated bailout.
