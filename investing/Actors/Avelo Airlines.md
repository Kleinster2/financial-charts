**Avelo Airlines** is a US ultra-low-cost carrier launched in April 2021, the first new mainline US airline in nearly 15 years. Founded by Andrew Levy, former CFO of [[Allegiant Air]], Avelo targets underserved secondary airports with point-to-point service — the same playbook Levy helped build at Allegiant but with a fleet mix that now includes next-generation narrowbodies.

## Synopsis

Avelo entered the market during the post-COVID travel rebound, initially operating from [[Hollywood Burbank Airport]] with a fleet of used [[Boeing]] 737-800s before expanding to a second base at [[New Haven]]'s Tweed Airport. The airline's differentiation is airport selection: it flies where legacy carriers and even other ULCCs do not, keeping landing fees low and load factors high. In 2024, Avelo became the first US customer for the [[Embraer]] E195-E2, ordering up to 20 aircraft — a strategic bet on the geared turbofan ([[Pratt & Whitney]] GTF) economics of the E2 family for routes too thin for a 737.

The E195-E2 order is the interesting signal. At ~136 seats and ~20% lower fuel burn than a 737-800, the E2 lets Avelo serve markets that cannot fill a 189-seat 737 profitably. If Avelo proves the model, it validates Embraer's push into the US mainline market beyond regional flying. As a privately held startup, financial visibility is limited — profitability has not been publicly confirmed, and the airline remains dependent on continued private funding for fleet growth.
