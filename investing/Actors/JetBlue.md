# JetBlue

JetBlue Airways Corporation (NASDAQ: JBLU) is a Long Island City, NY-based low-cost legacy carrier operating an [[Airbus A320]]-family and [[Embraer E190]]-family fleet across the US, Caribbean, Latin America, and select transatlantic routes. JetBlue sits between pure ULCCs ([[Frontier Airlines]] / [[Allegiant Travel]] / formerly [[Spirit Airlines]]) and full-service legacy carriers ([[Delta Air Lines]] / [[United Airlines]] / [[American Airlines]]) on the cost-vs-amenity spectrum — known for in-flight Wi-Fi, leather seats, and Mint premium-cabin product on transcon and Caribbean routes.

## Failed Spirit acquisition (Jan 2024)

JetBlue's $3.8B agreement to acquire [[Spirit Airlines]] was blocked in January 2024 by US District Court Judge William Young on antitrust grounds — the merger would have eliminated the largest pure-play US ULCC. The block was upheld and JetBlue terminated the deal. Spirit subsequently filed for two bankruptcies in less than 12 months and ceased operations May 2 2026 ([[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]]).

---

## Post-Spirit shutdown context (May 2026)

The May 2 2026 Spirit shutdown is a complicated dynamic for JetBlue. On the strategic side, the antitrust block on the 2024 merger is now moot — there is no Spirit to merge with. On the operational side, JetBlue can absorb select Spirit-vacated routes and gate slots opportunistically without the M&A overhead. JetBlue's transatlantic and Caribbean route franchise faces the same [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran-war]] jet-fuel cost regime that broke Spirit, but with a more diversified revenue base (amenity-premium product, Mint seats, partner alliances) that gives more pricing flexibility.
