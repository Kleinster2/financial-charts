# Deutsche Lufthansa

**Deutsche Lufthansa** — Germany's flag carrier and Europe's largest airline group by revenue. Operates Lufthansa, Swiss, Austrian Airlines, Brussels Airlines, Eurowings. Hub at Frankfurt Airport (FRA).

## Iran conflict impact (March-April 2026)

### March: schedule-share opportunity

Adding flights to Asia and Africa amid increased long-haul demand as passengers sought alternatives to Gulf carrier transfers. Suspended several Middle East routes (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Dammam, Amman, Erbil, Beirut, Tel Aviv, Tehran) on rolling timelines through Apr 30. See [[Iran conflict airline disruption]].

### April 27: cut wave (direction reversal)

Per [[Financial Times|FT]], Lufthansa joined [[Delta Air Lines|Delta]], [[Cathay Pacific]], and [[Qantas]] in cutting flights as [[Jet fuel|jet fuel]] doubled to ~$200/bbl ([[Argus Media|Argus]] NW Europe). The schedule-share opportunity from disrupted Gulf hubs has been overwhelmed by the fuel-cost shock.

This is the first-mover demand-destruction step in the [[Refining slate rigidity]] framework — airline cuts reduce jet demand but do not by themselves rebalance [[Oil|crude]], because refiners produce roughly fixed product slates and the rest of the slate (diesel, gasoline, naphtha, fuel oil) has to ration in turn. See [[Refining slate rigidity]] and [[Crack spread]] for the cross-product mechanics.

*Source: FT, "Why flying less won't do much to ease the oil crisis" (Apr 27, 2026).*
