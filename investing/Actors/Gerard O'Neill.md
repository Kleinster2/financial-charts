#actor #person #space #usa

Gerard O'Neill — Princeton physicist who in 1974 proposed using electromagnetic mass drivers to launch lunar material to orbital construction sites at the L5 Lagrange point. Founded the Space Studies Institute (1977). Built the first working mass driver prototype at [[MIT]]'s Francis Bitter National Magnet Laboratory with Henry Kolm (1977). His work laid the intellectual foundation for [[SpaceX]]'s 2026 [[Lunar Mass Driver]] concept, though Musk's application (AI satellite manufacturing) differs from O'Neill's (space colony construction).
