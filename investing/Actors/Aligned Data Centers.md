# Aligned Data Centers

> [!info] One-line read
> Dallas-based hyperscale/colocation operator — 50+ campuses and 5+ GW across the Americas (North America plus South America via [[ODATA]]) — and the subject of the largest-ever data-center acquisition: a [[BlackRock]]/[[Global Infrastructure Partners]]-led consortium with [[MGX]] buying it from [[Macquarie]] at ~$40B enterprise value, expected to close H1 2026.

Aligned Data Centers is a privately held data-center platform headquartered in Dallas, Texas, supplying hyperscale and enterprise colocation across North and South America. It is known for sustainable, adaptive cooling (adiabatic) and rapid-deployment design. It expanded into Latin America by acquiring [[ODATA]] in 2023, building a Pan-American footprint that now spans 50+ campuses and over 5 GW of capacity.

## The $40B sale (2025–2026)

[[Macquarie]] Asset Management agreed to lead the sale of Aligned at roughly $40B enterprise value to a consortium led by [[BlackRock]]'s [[Global Infrastructure Partners]], joined by Abu Dhabi's [[MGX]] and the AI Infrastructure Partnership. It is the biggest acquisition of a data-center company on record, surpassing the ~$16.6B [[Blackstone]]-led purchase of APAC operator AirTrunk in 2024. The transaction is expected to close in the first half of 2026, subject to regulatory approvals. The size of the deal is a marker of how much institutional capital is chasing AI data-center capacity. See [[Private Real Estate]] and [[AI Infrastructure]].

## Funding history

Aligned is an infrastructure-private-equity platform, not a venture-funded startup — its capital history runs through ownership changes and large debt/ABS financings rather than priced equity rounds.

| Period | Event | Counterparty | Ownership / value |
|--------|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Since 2017 | Build-out under CEO Andrew Schaap — scaled from 2 to 85 data centers | [[Macquarie]] Asset Management and co-investors | Majority owner |
| 2023 | Acquired [[ODATA]] for LatAm expansion | — | Pan-American: 50+ campuses, 5+ GW |
| Oct 2025 | All-equity sale agreed (close H1 2026) | [[BlackRock]]/[[Global Infrastructure Partners]] + [[MGX]] + AI Infrastructure Partnership | ~$40B enterprise value; ~100% |

The ~$40B price is the largest data-center acquisition on record, surpassing the ~$16.6B [[Blackstone]]-led purchase of AirTrunk (APAC) in 2024.
