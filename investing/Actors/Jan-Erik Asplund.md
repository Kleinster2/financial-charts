# Jan-Erik Asplund

Co-founder of [[Sacra]], the private-company research platform providing independent financial analysis, revenue estimates, and business model breakdowns for venture-backed startups.

## Synopsis

Jan-Erik Asplund co-founded [[Sacra]] alongside [[Walter Chen]] and [[Conor Gleeson]] to build a research layer for private markets. Sacra's core product is detailed financial analysis of private companies — the kind of bottom-up revenue modeling, competitive positioning, and business model decomposition that public-market analysts produce for listed companies but that barely exists for private ones. Asplund's work at Sacra includes authoring deep-dive reports on companies across SaaS, fintech, logistics, and AI, using alternative data sources (job postings, web traffic, app downloads, supplier data) to triangulate revenue and growth figures that companies themselves don't disclose.

The value proposition Asplund and the Sacra team address is structural: as companies stay private longer (median time to IPO has stretched from ~4 years in the 2000s to 10+ years), the pool of economically significant companies with zero public financial data keeps growing. VCs doing diligence, LPs evaluating fund performance, and corporate development teams scoping acquisitions all need this analysis. Sacra's subscriber base includes venture firms, institutional LPs, and strategic acquirers. The platform was backed by [[Hustle Fund]] and operates as a bootstrapped-plus-modest-venture business rather than a heavily capitalized startup.
