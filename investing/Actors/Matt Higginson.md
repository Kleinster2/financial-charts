# Matt Higginson

Matt Higginson is a partner in the Boston office of [[McKinsey]] and the firm's Global Head of Digital Assets. He is the lead author of McKinsey's most-cited recent stablecoin payments research, including the February 2026 study "Stablecoins in payments: What the raw transaction numbers miss" (co-produced with [[Artemis Analytics]]) and the July 2025 paper "The stable door opens: How tokenized cash enables next-gen payments." His public framing of the stablecoin market is the dominant counterweight to the "trillions in transactions" headline narrative — Higginson's core finding is that ~99% of cited stablecoin transaction volume is crypto-internal (exchange settlement, DeFi rotation, market-making) rather than real payments, putting the genuine payments-volume figure at roughly $390B annually on $1-2B/day of real flow.

## Key research framings

### Real payments vs raw transaction volume (Feb 2026, with Artemis)

The transaction-volume figures cited in industry reporting (trillions per year) measure all on-chain stablecoin transfers — including crypto exchange settlement, DeFi protocol activity, and market-making rotation. Higginson's measurement filters for transfers that look like real payments (B2B settlement, remittances, payroll, treasury) and finds about $1-2B/day, or roughly $390B/year. The implication is that stablecoins as a payment rail are about 1,000-2,000x smaller than the headlines suggest — and 25-50x smaller than even modest comparisons to global card or wire volumes.

Stablecoins in circulation grew from ~$150B to ~$300B over the prior year — a real doubling — and real payment transaction volume also approximately doubled, so the directional thesis remains intact even at the more modest absolute figure.

### Geographic origination (with Artemis)

About 60% of stablecoin payments originate from Asia. This contradicts the dominant North America/Europe framing in most coverage. The implication is that policy decisions in [[MAS]], the [[HKMA]], and [[JFSA]] are now load-bearing for global stablecoin market structure regardless of what the US does post-[[GENIUS Act]].

### "Scramble" framing on GENIUS Act activation (Bloomberg TV, May 2026)

Higginson's near-term forward call: when the [[GENIUS Act]] fully comes into force by end-2026, there will be a scramble among dozens of US banks to position themselves between stablecoin issuance and customer-facing distribution. The bottleneck is interface and trust, not technology. Banks that build the right interface layer will compress the issuer-to-user value chain, similar to how [[Stripe]] compressed merchant-to-card-network distribution a decade ago.

### "Friction is a feature" framing on transaction size

One of the cleanest framings on stablecoin sweet spot: nobody wants a $5M transfer to be instant — friction in between is what prevents costly mistakes. That risk-versus-speed calibration is reversed on $200 cross-border remit, where friction is pure cost. Stablecoins' wedge is small/SMB cross-border, not enterprise treasury.
