[[Kristin Peck]] — CEO of [[Zoetis]] since January 2020, succeeding founding CEO Juan Ramón Alaix. Joined Zoetis at the 2013 IPO as Executive Vice President, Group President U.S. Operations, Business Development & Strategy. Prior career at [[Pfizer]] (2004-2013), where she ran corporate strategy and the spin-out of the animal health business that became Zoetis. Earlier at [[Apollo Real Estate]], [[Goldman Sachs]] real estate group.

Education: Georgetown BS (foreign service), [[Columbia Business School]] MBA, Columbia M.A. (international affairs). Board memberships include [[Pfizer]] (former).

## Why Kristin Peck matters

Peck is the named operational and financial decision-maker on the May 7, 2026 [[Zoetis]] Q1 print and the FY26 guide reset. The Q1 commentary attributing the miss to "more challenging operating environment" with three named consumer-pulse pressures (price sensitivity / declining vet visits / softer premium demand) is the most explicit recession-language Peck has used since taking over as CEO in 2020. She is also the architect of the [[Neogen]] genomics acquisition (announced earlier, H2 2026 close target) — the largest M&A deal of her tenure.

The investing-vault relevance is as a cross-reference for [[Zoetis]] strategic-pivot tracking. Peck inherited a high-multiple growth name from Alaix and is now navigating it through what looks like a structural premium-pet consumer reset. The next 2-3 quarters will test whether the [[Neogen]] integration + the [[Lenivia]] (next-gen anti-NGF) launch + the 12+ blockbuster pipeline materializes fast enough to bridge the 2026 organic-growth flattening.
