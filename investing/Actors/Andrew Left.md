**Andrew Left** — Founder of Citron Research, one of the earliest and most prominent activist short sellers. Ran Citron from 2001, making his name with short reports on Valeant (2015), Evergrande, and numerous Chinese reverse mergers. Indicted by the DOJ in July 2024 on 19 counts alleging he traded ahead of his own reports. Pleaded not guilty; case pending.

## Career arc

- 2001 — Founded Citron Research
- 2015 — Short report on [[Valeant Pharmaceuticals]] (stock -90%+ over following years)
- 2016-2020 — Series of short campaigns on Chinese reverse mergers
- January 2021 — Publicly short [[GameStop]] when [[Reddit]]/WallStreetBets organized the squeeze. Took heavy losses. Shortly after announced he was done with short selling. [[Muddy Waters|Carson Block]] cites this as the inflection point for the post-2021 activist short landscape
- July 2024 — [[DOJ]] indictment on 19 counts: allegedly traded short-dated options ahead of his reports, closed positions at prices different from public recommendations, and fabricated invoices to hide hedge fund relationships

---

## Why he matters

Left is one of the few named defendants in the DOJ's 2021-2024 investigation into short sellers. The outcome of his case may set precedent for whether publishing research while holding positions — a practice common across Wall Street — crosses into manipulation. [[Muddy Waters|Carson Block]] of [[Muddy Waters]] received a no-action close-out letter from the SEC in the same sweep.

Left's post-GameStop exit also marks the practical death of meme-era activist short selling: the cost of being on the wrong side of a coordinated retail squeeze is career-ending even when the underlying short thesis is correct.
