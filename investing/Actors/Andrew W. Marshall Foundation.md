# Andrew W. Marshall Foundation

US foundation continuing the strategic-analytical lineage of [[Andrew W. Marshall]] (1921-2019), the long-running director of the Pentagon's Office of Net Assessment (1973-2015 — a record 42-year tenure). The foundation supports research and fellowships in the net-assessment tradition: long-horizon strategic competition analysis, focused on geopolitical / military / technological balance assessments. [[Jordan Schneider]] holds a fellowship at the foundation, the institutional link between his ChinaTalk publication and the defense / net-assessment community.

## Why it matters in the vault

The Marshall Foundation is the connective tissue between [[Jordan Schneider]]'s ChinaTalk publication and the defense / net-assessment community that does long-horizon strategic-competition analysis. Marshall himself was the architect of US net-assessment doctrine — the framework for comparing US and adversary military capabilities over multi-decade time horizons rather than year-to-year. The foundation's fellowships extend that tradition; Schneider's affiliation is one of the channels through which ChinaTalk's analysis reaches Pentagon and Congressional readers.
