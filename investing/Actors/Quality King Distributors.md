**Quality King Distributors** is a Queens-based gray-market distributor of health, beauty, and pharmaceutical products. Founded 1961 by Bernard and Ruth Nussdorf. Family net worth ~$1.6B (Forbes, 2014). Source of [[Meadow Lane]] founder Sammy Nussdorf's family wealth.

## Synopsis

Quality King built a $4B+ revenue business on a simple arbitrage: buy branded consumer products (shampoo, soap, OTC drugs, fragrances) at volume discounts or through international channels, then resell to US retailers at prices undercutting authorized distributors. The model is legal -- the Supreme Court affirmed this in the landmark 1998 case Quality King v. L'Anza (first-sale doctrine), which established that goods legally sold abroad can be imported and resold without copyright holder permission. That ruling effectively legalized gray-market distribution of copyrighted goods in the US.

The Nussdorf family has run the business across three generations from a warehouse complex in Bellport, Long Island. Glenn Nussdorf serves as CEO/Chairman. The family's wealth, estimated at $1.6B by Forbes in 2014, has been deployed into real estate and other ventures. Sammy Nussdorf, part of the family, used that capital base to launch [[Meadow Lane]], a high-end grocery concept. Quality King itself remains stubbornly private -- no IPO rumors, no PE involvement -- making it one of the larger family-controlled distribution businesses in the US.
