Local Government Pension Scheme — the [[UK]]'s funded defined-benefit pension scheme for local-government and related public-sector employees in England, Wales and Scotland. With roughly £400bn in assets and ~6.5M members, the LGPS is one of the largest funded pension schemes in the world and the largest pool of [[UK]] public-sector investment capital. Unlike most UK public-sector pensions (unfunded, pay-as-you-go), the LGPS holds real invested assets — which makes it a live target for the [[Pension reform]] agenda.

The LGPS is structured as ~86 individual funds run by local authorities, a fragmentation the [[UK]] government has been consolidating since 2015 into a small number of asset "pools." The [[Rachel Reeves]] government accelerated this via the 2024–2025 "Fit for the Future" consultation, targeting consolidation into eight pools and pushing higher allocation to [[UK]] private markets and infrastructure. LGPS pooling is the parallel public-sector track to the private-sector DC reform driven by the Mansion House Accord — same goal of mobilising [[UK]]-allocated institutional capital, different scheme architecture.
