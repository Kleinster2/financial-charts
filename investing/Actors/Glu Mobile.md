Glu Mobile was a mobile game developer best known for casual and lifestyle titles, acquired by [[Electronic Arts|EA]] for $2.4B in April 2021. The deal exemplified the pandemic-era premium paid for mobile gaming assets.

## Synopsis

Founded in 2001, Glu Mobile carved out a niche in casual mobile games with titles like Design Home, Covet Fashion, and Kim Kardashian: Hollywood — the latter being a surprise 2014 hit that generated over $200M in lifetime revenue and demonstrated the power of celebrity IP in free-to-play mobile. The company's strength was in lifestyle and fashion simulation games that attracted a predominantly female audience underserved by the broader gaming industry. At the time of the [[Electronic Arts|EA]] acquisition, Glu had roughly $540M in annual bookings.

[[Electronic Arts|EA]] paid $2.4B (approximately 4.4x trailing bookings) in an all-cash deal, motivated by the desire to fill a gap in its mobile portfolio outside sports. The acquisition came during a wave of gaming M&A driven by COVID-era engagement spikes and mobile growth optimism. In retrospect, the deal's value proposition depended on sustaining engagement in titles with aging player bases — Design Home peaked around 2019, and the broader mobile casual market faced headwinds from [[Apple]]'s ATT privacy changes that disrupted user acquisition economics across the industry.
