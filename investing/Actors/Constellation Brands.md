# Constellation Brands

**Major US beverage company** — NYSE: STZ. Dominant in US beer through its exclusive rights to import, market, and distribute [[Grupo Modelo]] brands (Corona, Modelo Especial, Pacifico) in the US. Also holds wine and spirits portfolio (Kim Crawford, Meiomi, High West, Casa Noble). Acquired Tipsy Scoop (alcohol-infused ice cream), validating the category where [[Aubi & Ramsa]] competes.

## Q4 FY2026 earnings (Apr 9, 2026)

Adjusted EPS $1.90 (beat $1.74 consensus), GAAP $1.16 (vs -$2.09 YoY). Revenue $1.92B (-11% reported, ~flat organic) vs $1.896B est. Stock +8.5% to $163.07 (4.1σ move).

Beer: $1.73B (+1% YoY). Q4 depletions +0.6%, shipments +1.1%. [[Modelo Especial]] -1% (still #1 US beer by dollar sales), Pacifico +21%, Victoria +17%, Corona Extra -6%. Full-year beer depletions -2.1%, shipments -3.8%. Beer operating margin 33.2% (-340 bps YoY); FY2027 guided 37-38%.

Wine & Spirits: $194.2M (-58% YoY on divestitures; shipment volumes -72.9%). Remaining portfolio outpaced category with +8% depletion growth.

Full-year FCF $1.8B, operating cash flow $2.7B, $1.6B+ returned to shareholders. New $900M buyback authorized. Balance sheet: $9.7B long-term debt.

FY2028 guidance withdrawn — "limited near-term visibility" due to consumer volatility, [[GLP-1]] medication impact on high-calorie beverage demand, and input cost unpredictability. FY2027 guided: organic net sales -1% to +1%; EPS $11.20-$11.90 (below $12.36 consensus). CEO transition underway: Nicholas Fink participated alongside outgoing CEO Bill Newlands.

Hedging: fuel ~100%, aluminum ~90%, natural gas ~80%, FX ~80%. FIFA World Cup marketing investment planned (beer marketing ~9.5% of sales).

*Source: [Constellation Brands Q4 FY2026 press release](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/STZ/constellation-brands-reports-full-fiscal-year-and-fourth-quarter-s69zfubez5hm.html), April 9, 2026.*
