**Condor Non-Lethal Technologies** — Rio de Janeiro-based Brazilian manufacturer of non-lethal weapons, riot control munitions, tear gas, pyrotechnics, and law-enforcement training systems. One of the largest non-lethal weapons producers in Latin America and a major exporter into Africa, the Middle East, and Asia. 51% acquired by [[EDGE Group]] (UAE state-owned defense conglomerate) in May 2024 — EDGE's second Brazil acquisition after [[SIATT]].

---

## Synopsis

Condor's value to [[EDGE Group]] is product-line and market access rather than missile-grade technology. Non-lethal weapons are a separate procurement category from lethal munitions and run through ministries of interior and public-security agencies rather than defense ministries — a customer set where EDGE had no native footprint before the acquisition. Condor's existing export network across African and Asian law-enforcement buyers plugs directly into EDGE's regional ambitions and broadens the conglomerate's addressable market beyond pure-defense procurement.

The May 2024 deal closed eight months after EDGE took its 50% stake in [[SIATT]] and establishes the pattern: EDGE acquires majority or near-majority stakes in mid-size Brazilian defense and security firms with strong domestic positions but limited export capacity, then uses UAE-side logistics, distribution, and political access to scale exports into Gulf-aligned and non-aligned markets. The Brazilian government has not pushed back — the Lula administration views inbound BRICS-aligned defense capital as consistent with its industrial-policy posture and with broader BRICS strategic alignment.

## EDGE Group transaction

[[EDGE Group]] acquired 51% in May 2024. Terms not publicly disclosed. The transaction was the second leg of EDGE's Brazil entry strategy following the September 2023 [[SIATT]] 50% acquisition. Condor sits within EDGE's Platforms & Systems-adjacent product line and operates as a wholly separate brand and management team from the missile-focused SIATT operation.

## Strategic positioning

Non-lethal weapons procurement runs largely outside the defense-budget cycle and is less exposed to the geopolitical export-control frictions that constrain missile sales. That makes Condor an income-and-margin asset for the EDGE portfolio rather than a strategic-capability play. The Brazilian domestic market — federal police, military police of 27 states, prison systems — provides a stable baseline; Latin American, African, and Middle Eastern exports provide growth.
