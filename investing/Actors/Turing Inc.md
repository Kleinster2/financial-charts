**Turing Inc** — Japanese autonomous driving startup founded August 2021, pursuing an end-to-end generative AI approach to self-driving where a single neural network handles everything from camera input to driving commands. Headquartered in Shinagawa, Tokyo. Not to be confused with [[Turing]] (Turing.com, the US AI engineering company).

## Technology

Turing takes the end-to-end approach — structurally similar to [[Tesla]]'s FSD and [[UK]]-based [[Wayve]]. A single multimodal neural network processes camera and sensor inputs for real-time driving decisions, rather than the modular pipeline used by [[Waymo]] and most traditional ADAS suppliers.

| Technology | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Heron | Proprietary multimodal generative AI model, up to 70B parameters, processes camera/sensor input for driving decisions |
| Terra | Japan's first generative world model for autonomous driving (unveiled Aug 2024) |
| Custom AI chips | In-vehicle inference chips targeting mass production by 2028 |

This positions Turing against [[Tier IV]] (Japan's other major AV startup), which pursues the opposite approach: an open-source modular platform based on Autoware using traditional rule-based + sensor-fusion stacks.

## Partnerships and strategy

Turing is in talks with "several major automakers" to license Heron for joint autonomous vehicle development. The business model is shifting toward supplying autonomous driving software and custom chips to OEMs rather than manufacturing vehicles themselves — a platform play.

[[Denso]] partnership is the most concrete: joint AV development leveraging Denso's manufacturing and integration capabilities with Turing's end-to-end AI stack.

## Japan AV landscape

Japan has ~24 AV startups (13 funded, 7 at Series A+). Turing is the most prominent pure-play end-to-end AI entrant. The field splits on philosophy:

| Company | Approach | Status |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Turing Inc | End-to-end generative AI | Series A, demo vehicle 2026 |
| [[Tier IV]] | Open-source modular (Autoware) | Est. 2015, ~400 employees |
