## Iran war response

- ~March 20, 2026 ([[Middle East Institute|MEI]] panel): Declared Wednesday a public holiday every week as a demand-destruction measure — the most quoted early example of forced energy conservation. See [[Iran conflict country responses#Reference — demand destruction measures]].
- May 17, 2026 ([[Financial Times|FT]]): Escalated to a formal four-day working week, alongside [[Pakistan]] and [[Philippines]]. The [[IEA]] framed the step as "evoking memories" of the 2022 post-[[Russia]]-[[Ukraine]] energy crisis that triggered emerging-market debt crises. Sri Lanka — which defaulted in 2022 during that prior shock — is one of the analytical reference points for what an oil-import shock looks like at the sovereign-credit margin.

The 2022 default is the binding-constraint backdrop: Sri Lanka entered the Iran war without an SPR buffer, without fiscal space, and with [[IMF]] restructuring still in progress. The Wednesday-only conservation measure (March) was a first response from a sovereign with the least room to absorb the shock; the four-day expansion (May) is the predictable next step as buffers depleted.
