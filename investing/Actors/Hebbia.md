# Hebbia

AI document intelligence startup building enterprise-grade knowledge work tools. Uses retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) and proprietary models to analyze, extract, and synthesize insights from massive document sets — contracts, SEC filings, legal discovery, due diligence materials.

## Synopsis

Hebbia targets the highest-value knowledge work in finance, law, and consulting — the kind of document-heavy analysis where a single analyst might spend 40 hours reading through a data room or parsing hundreds of contracts. The platform ingests unstructured documents (PDFs, spreadsheets, presentations), indexes them into a queryable knowledge base, and lets users ask complex analytical questions across the entire corpus. This is distinct from chatbot-style AI: Hebbia's product is designed for structured, auditable output with source citations, which matters when the work product goes into an investment committee memo or a legal brief. The company raised a $130M Series B in mid-2024 at a $700M valuation, led by [[Andreessen Horowitz]], with participation from [[Index Ventures]] and [[Peter Thiel]].

The market Hebbia is attacking — enterprise document intelligence — is large and underserved because incumbents like Relativity (legal) and [[Kensho]] (finance) built their products pre-LLM. The AI-native approach lets Hebbia handle ambiguity and cross-document reasoning that traditional NLP tools cannot. Key customers include major investment banks, law firms, and consulting firms using Hebbia for deal diligence, contract review, and regulatory analysis. The risk is that this is a crowded space — [[OpenAI]], [[Google]], and every major LLM provider is building document analysis capabilities. Hebbia's moat depends on enterprise trust, compliance features, and workflow integration rather than model superiority.
