# Zhongguancun

Technology hub in Beijing's Haidian District, often called "China's Silicon Valley." The district serves as the epicenter of China's tech industry and AI development.

## Overview

- **Location**: Haidian District, northwest Beijing
- **Area**: ~488 km² (Zhongguancun Science Park total)
- **Established**: 1988 as first national high-tech zone
- **Companies**: 20,000+ tech firms as of 2023
- **Revenue**: ¥8.7 trillion (~$1.2T) in 2023

## Strategic Significance

1. **Talent pipeline**: Proximity to Tsinghua/PKU provides direct access to China's best CS graduates
2. **Government support**: Designated national innovation zone with preferential policies
3. **AI cluster effects**: Concentration of AI labs creates talent/knowledge spillovers
4. **Compute access**: Priority allocation of domestic GPUs and cloud resources

## Investment Lens

Zhongguancun's density of AI activity makes it a proxy for China's AI competitiveness. Watch for:
- Startup formation rates and funding rounds
- Talent flows (returns from US, departures to other hubs)
- Policy announcements specific to the zone
- Real estate/office demand as leading indicator
