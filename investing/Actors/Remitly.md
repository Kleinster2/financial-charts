# Remitly

Remitly Global (NASDAQ: RELY) is a Seattle-based digital cross-border payments and remittance platform, public since September 2021. The business turned profitable in 2025 with $1.635B FY revenue (+29% YoY), $67.9M net income, and $272M adjusted EBITDA, and continued to accelerate into 2026 — Q1 2026 revenue $452.8M (+25% YoY) drove a guidance raise to $1.96-1.975B for FY26 (~20% growth) and adjusted EBITDA of $370-385M. The CEO transition from co-founder [[Matt Oppenheimer]] to [[Sebastian Gunningham]] (former [[Amazon]] S-team SVP, ex-[[Santander]] Consumer Finance chairman) took effect February 19, 2026, repositioning the company around product scaling (Flex, Remitly Business, multi-currency wallets) and a measured stablecoin integration. Remitly's transaction engine selectively routes flows over stablecoin rails — co-positioned with [[Coins.ph]] on the Philippine corridor — where stablecoins beat traditional FX on price and speed; Gunningham frames sender demand for stablecoins as still low because receivers want dollar-denominated accounts, not stablecoin balances.

## Business model

Remitly is a digital-first remitter — most flows originate via mobile app from migrant workers in the [[USA]], UK, EU, GCC, and Australia, with delivery into ~170 corridors via bank deposit, mobile money, cash pickup, or home delivery in select markets. Revenue is primarily transaction fees and FX spread. The unit economics improve with sender retention because customer acquisition cost is amortized over years of recurring sends.

### Stablecoin rail integration

Remitly's transaction engine routes each send across the lowest-cost / fastest available rail. Stablecoins enter the routing decision as a third path alongside correspondent banking and local real-time payment networks. Where stablecoins win — typically smaller-ticket, time-sensitive, high-FX-friction corridors — Remitly uses a [[Stablecoin sandwich]]: USD on Remitly's side, stablecoin in transit across blockchain, local currency on the receiving end via partners like [[Coins.ph]] (Philippines).

Gunningham's framing on stablecoin posture (Q1 2026 earnings call, Bloomberg TV May 2026): adopt where it makes sense, where it beats price and speed; regulatory picture for direct customer-held stablecoin balances is still too murky to push fast; sender demand is low because receivers want dollar-denominated accounts at home, not stablecoin balances.

## Leadership transition (Feb 19, 2026)

Co-founder Matt Oppenheimer stepped down as CEO and assumed executive chairman role; Sebastian Gunningham succeeded him. The handoff frames Remitly's pivot from growth-at-all-costs founder-CEO mode to scaled-operator mode in a now-profitable business pushing into new products. Gunningham's playbook is built around three decades of marketplace and payments scaling — [[Apple]] enterprise, [[Oracle]] LatAm, [[Amazon]] S-team SVP Marketplace (~30,000 staff under him), [[WeWork]] co-CEO 2018-2020, then transformation roles at [[Santander]] Consumer Finance and Openbank 2021-2025, and CEO of Material Bank 2023-2025.

## Investment case

Bull:
- First profitable year (2025) at scale, FY26 trajectory implies ~22-23% EBITDA margin path
- Stablecoin rail is an upside option, not a threat — Remitly routes selectively and captures the spread on rails it wouldn't have without crypto
- New product surface (Flex, Remitly Business, multi-currency wallets) builds revenue per active user
- New CEO with scaled-operator pedigree and clean track record

Bear:
- $5B mkt cap on $1.96B revenue = 2.6x sales, but operates in a structurally compressing-take-rate space (Wei Zhou's 8% → 2-4% → 50 bps → 20-30 bps path is a Remitly take-rate compression chart)
- [[Wise]] head-to-head pressure on transparency-marketed corridors
- Receiver-side fintechs ([[Coins.ph]], regional super-apps) capture more of the value chain as they scale own user bases
- Stablecoin disruption risk if a non-remitter ([[Stripe]]/Bridge, [[Circle]] direct, [[Coinbase]]) builds end-to-end corridor offerings
