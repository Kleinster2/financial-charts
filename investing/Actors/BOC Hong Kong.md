# BOC Hong Kong

Commercial banking subsidiary of [[Bank of China]] and one of the three note-issuing banks in [[Hong Kong]], alongside [[HSBC]] and [[Standard Chartered]]. [[EWH]] holding (~4.4%).

---

## Synopsis

BOC Hong Kong operates as the principal commercial banking vehicle for [[Bank of China]]'s Hong Kong operations, serving both retail and corporate clients across the territory. As a note-issuing bank — one of only three authorized to print Hong Kong dollar banknotes — BOCHK occupies a quasi-sovereign role in the city's financial infrastructure, a position that survived the 1997 handover intact and gives the bank a structural deposit advantage that pure commercial competitors cannot replicate.

The investment angle is primarily as a proxy for Hong Kong's banking sector health and, by extension, China-Hong Kong capital flows. BOCHK benefits from Hong Kong's role as the primary offshore RMB clearing center, processing the majority of cross-border RMB settlements. The stock trades at a material discount to book value during periods of Hong Kong political or property market stress, and at a premium when capital flows normalize. The ~4.4% weight in [[EWH]] makes it one of the largest single-name exposures for anyone holding the Hong Kong ETF, meaning BOCHK's performance significantly influences any passive Hong Kong bet.
