# Anthony Bamford

## Synopsis

[[Anthony Bamford]] (Anthony Paul Bamford, Baron Bamford of Daylesford) has been chairman of [[JCB Group]] since 1975, succeeding his father Joseph Cyril Bamford, the founder. Born 23 October 1945 (the same day his father set up the firm), educated at Ampleforth College, apprenticed at [[Massey Ferguson]] in France, then read languages at the University of Grenoble before joining the family business. He was knighted in 1990, made a life peer on 3 October 2013 as Baron Bamford of Daylesford in the County of Gloucestershire and of Wootton in the County of Staffordshire, and retired from the House of Lords in March 2024. Bamford-family wealth was £9.45B in 2024 (+60% YoY per Bloomberg Billionaires Index), built on a [[JCB Group]] turnover trajectory from £43M in 1975 to £5.8B / ~$7.7B in 2024. He is among the largest individual donors to the UK [[Conservative Party]] of the past two decades, with cumulative donations and gifts above £10M since 2001 and £3.94M during the 2019 general election alone, and was a vocal Brexit advocate.

The investing-vault relevance is the dual-vertical Bamford-family hydrogen strategy: hydrogen-combustion engines for construction equipment under his direction at [[JCB Group]] (a £100M, 150-engineer programme that secured first national type-approvals across Europe in January 2025 and full EU type-approval in May 2025) running in parallel to son Jo Bamford's hydrogen-fuel-cell push at [[Wrightbus]] via the [[Ballard]] partnership. The two bets are split deliberately across drivetrains — combustion vs fuel cell — and across product categories — off-highway construction and agriculture machinery vs on-road buses — but both sit under one family balance sheet and the same UK hydrogen-economy policy backdrop. Anthony Bamford is the party who chose hydrogen ICE over electrification for [[JCB Group]] and personally championed the programme through to commercial certification.

## Position in Bamford-family hydrogen strategy

The Bamford family has placed two distinct hydrogen bets across two industrial verticals, with Anthony and Jo each owning one. Anthony Bamford has personally spearheaded [[JCB Group]]'s hydrogen-combustion engine programme — a £100M, 150-engineer effort that as of early 2025 had produced more than 130 evaluation engines powering backhoe loaders, [[Loadall]] telescopic handlers, and generator sets. The Netherlands' Vehicle Authority RDW issued the first national certification in January 2025, with Great Britain, Northern Ireland, Germany, France, Spain, Belgium, Poland, Finland, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein following. In May 2025, [[JCB Group]] secured the first full EU type-approval for a hydrogen engine in non-road mobile machinery. Bamford framed the January 2025 milestone as "a very significant moment for JCB" and a vote of confidence in hydrogen combustion as a viable off-highway decarbonisation pathway against the dominant battery-electric narrative.

Son Jo Bamford runs the parallel hydrogen-fuel-cell vertical via [[Wrightbus]], acquired in 2019 through his personal vehicle Bamford Bus Company. Wrightbus selected [[Ballard]]'s FCmove-HD engine for the StreetDeck Hydroliner Generation 2.0 (the world's first hydrogen-led double-decker, launched May 2025) and a 70-fuel-cell-engine 2024 order. The split is deliberate: hydrogen ICE keeps [[JCB Group]]'s diesel-era manufacturing footprint and supplier base intact while decarbonising the combustion cycle, suitable for off-highway machinery where refuelling infrastructure and duty cycles favour an engine-block solution; fuel cells suit on-road buses with predictable depot refuelling and stricter zero-tailpipe-emission tendering. Both legs depend on the same upstream UK hydrogen-supply build-out, and the family is positioned to benefit whether construction-equipment OEMs converge on combustion or buses on fuel cells. Anthony Bamford's Conservative Party financial weight and his pre-2024 House of Lords platform gave the [[JCB Group]] hydrogen programme privileged access to UK industrial-policy framing during its development phase.
