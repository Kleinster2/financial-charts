# Karen Young

Senior Research Scholar at the [[Columbia University]] Center on Global Energy Policy at SIPA. Political economist focused on the Gulf, the broader MENA region, and the intersection of energy, finance, and security. Founding director of the Program on Economics and Energy at the [[Middle East Institute]] (and remains a senior fellow there). Previously a Resident Scholar at the [[American Enterprise Institute]] and Senior Resident Scholar at the [[Arab Gulf States Institute]]; was earlier a Research Fellow at the London School of Economics Middle East Centre. Teaches at Columbia SIPA and at the US Department of State Foreign Service Institute.

In the investing vault, Young's distinctive contribution is the gas-with-oil and diversification-agenda framing of UAE energy strategy — the argument that the UAE's offshore oil ramp is also a gas ramp (associated gas from offshore wells), driven by domestic electricity demand for [[AI infrastructure deals|AI data centers]] and the broader Vision-2030-style economic-diversification agenda, with strategic implications for [[China]] supply geography. Her [[2026 UAE OPEC exit|April 28 PBS NewsHour interview]] is the canonical reference for this framing in the vault.
