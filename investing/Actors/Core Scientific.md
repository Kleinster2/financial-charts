**Core Scientific** — Post-bankruptcy crypto-to-AI infrastructure convert. $3.5B [[CoreWeave]] anchor contract, 4.5 GW gross power pipeline, and a debt-funded push to become an independent high-density colocation platform.

The live question is whether Core Scientific can turn power access into contracted AI load quickly enough to justify the capital stack. The May 2026 Q1 release reframes the company from a rescued bitcoin miner into a high-density colocation developer: colocation is now the majority revenue line, capex is running ahead of contracts, and the new 7.75% secured notes make conversion speed the equity's binding variable.

## Q1 2026 — debt-funded AI colocation scale-up

*Source: Core Scientific Q1 2026 release, May 6, 2026.*

The Q1 print confirms Core Scientific has crossed from "bitcoin miner with an AI hosting option" into a debt-funded power-and-colocation platform. The company closed a $3.3B offering of 7.75% senior secured notes due 2031 and expanded its gross power capacity pipeline to 4.5 GW, including planned 1.5 GW expansions at both Muskogee, Oklahoma and Pecos, Texas.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Read |
|--------|---------|------|
| Total revenue | $115.2M vs $79.5M Q1 2025 | AI colocation now offsets mining decline |
| Colocation revenue | $77.5M vs $8.6M | Segment has become the business |
| Digital asset self-mining revenue | $30.1M vs $67.2M | Legacy mining revenue shrinking |
| Gross profit | $30.1M vs $8.2M | Mix shift improving unit economics |
| Net loss | -$347.2M | Includes $266.5M non-cash impairment |
| Adjusted EBITDA | $4.4M vs -$6.1M | Still early, but positive |
| Capex | $389.2M; $129.9M funded by [[CoreWeave]] | Customer-funded buildout is central to cash math |
| Liquidity | $1.04B | Cash cushion after financing |
| Billable capacity | 243 MW | ~$350M average annualized colocation GAAP revenue |
| Hunt County, TX | ~$233M land/power acquisition | Expected ~430 MW gross power capacity with approved [[ERCOT]] ramp |

The investment read is two-sided. Bull case: Core Scientific now has the capital readiness and power pipeline to negotiate with hyperscalers from strength, not as a distressed miner. Bear case: the $3.3B secured-note raise pulls financing risk forward; 7.75% debt only works if the company converts pipeline MW into contracted, billable AI load quickly enough. The 4.5 GW number is strategic-option value, not current revenue.
