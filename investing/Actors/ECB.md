#actor #centralbank #europe

ECB (European Central Bank) -- central bank for the euro area, responsible for monetary policy and banking supervision (Single Supervisory Mechanism). Sets key interest rates for the Eurozone. HQ in Frankfurt, Germany. President: Christine Lagarde (since Nov 2019).

## Iran war rate shift (Apr 2026)

The [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran conflict]] oil shock has reversed the ECB's easing trajectory. FT/FactSet futures curves (Apr 8, 2026) show two rate hikes now priced in — a sharp reversal from start-of-year expectations of continued cuts. ECB officials have broadly supported this repricing, unlike the [[Bank of England]] where governor [[Andrew Bailey]] pushed back.

![[ft-rate-expectations-apr-2026.png]]
*FT: Market expectations of rate moves — policy rates and futures curves for Fed, ECB, BoE, BoJ. Start of year (cyan dashed), last month (green dashed), today (pink). Source: FT calculations, Federal Reserve, ECB, BoE, BoJ, FactSet. Graphic: Chris Giles & Joel Suss.*

The ECB panel shows the "today" line diverging upward from prior expectations — rates expected to reach ~2.5% by 2028, versus ~2.0% at the start of the year. The gap reflects the inflation pass-through from the energy shock: European gas prices doubled since early 2026 (see [[Iran conflict European impact]]) and the ECB's mandate prioritizes price stability over growth.

[[Rick Rieder]] ([[BlackRock]], Apr 7) cited the ECB among central banks "all moving rates higher" — a claim the FT data supports for the ECB specifically. The ECB is starting from a lower level (~2.5% deposit rate) than the [[Bank of England]] (~4.50%) or [[Federal Reserve]] (~3.50-3.75%), giving it more room to hike without crushing growth.

### Wunsch May 20 signal

[[Pierre Wunsch]] sharpened the June decision tree in a May 20 [[Bloomberg]] interview. His frame: [[Europe]] is not in recession, but it is moving toward lower growth and higher inflation because the Gulf shock is geopolitical and binary. He said he had been slightly in favor of hiking in April, could live with waiting, and would view a still-unresolved conflict by June as raising the probability of action.

That is important because it keeps the ECB in a hike-or-hold regime rather than a normal easing-cycle pause. The policy question is not whether the energy shock is "temporary" in the abstract; it is whether second-round inflation behavior appears before growth has weakened enough to dominate the reaction function.

*Sources: [[Bloomberg]] Television / [[YouTube]] transcript, May 20 2026; [[Bloomberg]], "ECB's Wunsch Says Rate Hike Likely If War Isn't Over by June," Mar 27 2026; Le Monde, "European Central Bank moves toward a June interest rate hike," May 1 2026.*
