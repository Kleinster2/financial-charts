## The $500M proprietary AI build (May 2026)

Ballis announced Kirkland's $500M commitment to build its own AI platform — more than $100M in 2026 and hundreds of millions more over three to four years — rather than rely solely on third-party tools available to rivals. He framed widely available AI as raising the industry floor while Kirkland aims above it ("not hired for the floor"), and tied the build to a shift from the billable hour toward value-based, outcome-linked pricing.
