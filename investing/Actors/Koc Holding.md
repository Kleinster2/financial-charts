# Koc Holding

[[Turkey]]'s largest industrial conglomerate and a proxy for the country's entire investable economy. Listed on [[Borsa Istanbul]] (KCHOL.IS).

## Synopsis

Koc Holding controls ~8% of [[Turkey]]'s GDP through stakes in banking ([[Yapi Kredi]], 50/50 JV with [[UniCredit]]), energy (Tupras, Turkey's sole refiner at ~28Mt/year capacity), automotive (Tofas, a [[Stellantis]] JV producing Fiat/Peugeot vehicles), and consumer durables (Arcelik, Europe's second-largest white goods maker after [[BSH]]). The conglomerate structure means KCHOL trades at a persistent holding company discount to NAV, typically 20-30%, which compresses or widens based on Turkey's macro cycle and lira stability.

For foreign investors, Koc is the de facto gateway into Turkish equities without taking single-sector risk. The Jan 2026 foreign inflow surge ($218M net) signals renewed interest after years of outflows driven by unorthodox monetary policy under Erdogan. With the [[CBRT]] returning to rate orthodoxy under Governor Erkan and inflation decelerating from 75%+ peaks, the question is whether Turkey's equity re-rating has further to run or whether KCHOL's discount to NAV already prices in the normalization. The Vehbi Koc family retains control through a foundation structure, which provides governance stability rare in Turkish corporates.
