# Acciona

Spanish multinational conglomerate combining infrastructure construction with one of [[Europe]]'s largest renewable energy operators. Madrid-listed (ANA.MC), HQ Alcobendas. Majority shareholder of [[Nordex]] since the 2016 merger of Acciona Windpower into Nordex Group. Direct expression of the [[Energy security as investment thesis|European energy-security capex cycle]] through both the Energía operating segment and the Nordex stake.

## Why Acciona matters

Acciona is the rare publicly listed name that combines a major infrastructure construction franchise (highways, water, services) with a top-tier renewable energy operator. The Energía subsidiary (Acciona Energía, separately listed in Madrid as ANE.MC) is one of [[Europe]]'s largest pure-play renewable developer-operators by capacity. The conglomerate structure means a single equity provides exposure to both the [[Energy security as investment thesis|European sovereignty]] renewable capex cycle and the [[Industrial policy|infrastructure-buildout]] cycle that the same political moment funds.

The [[Nordex]] majority stake adds upstream exposure — Acciona is both Nordex's parent (capital provider, project-pipeline customer) and benefits from the Nordex equity rally on the same energy-security thesis. Per FT, May 3 2026: when the renewables cohort re-rated on the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran war]]-driven flows, Acciona participated in two channels simultaneously — direct (own renewable assets and infrastructure) and indirect (Nordex stake mark-to-market).

## 2026 performance — dual-track exposure

Per FT, May 3 2026: Acciona was up 33% YTD as of that date. The DB confirms +32.9% YTD May 3, with subsequent extension to +39.3% YTD as of May 18 (€259.20). Acciona is one of the few names in the FT cohort still extending its YTD gains through mid-May — likely because the dual exposure (operating renewable assets benefit from higher merchant power prices in [[Spain]] and across the [[European Union|EU]]) acts as a partial hedge to the renewable-developer pullback that affected pure-play names like [[Nordex]] and [[Siemens Energy]] over the same window.

The 2026 EBITDA guide (€2.8-3.1B) implies a step down from 2025's €3.21B record, reflecting expected normalization in Spanish power prices and infrastructure cycle moderation. The equity's continued rally despite the slight guide-down suggests the market is repricing the renewable capex thesis through-cycle rather than valuing it on near-term EBITDA.

## Relationship with Nordex

In 2016, Acciona Windpower (the Spanish conglomerate's wind turbine manufacturing arm) merged into Nordex Group, creating the listed [[Nordex]] entity in which Acciona holds majority control. The structure preserves Nordex as an independent listed equity while letting Acciona consolidate the wind-equipment economics into its conglomerate accounts.

The relationship has practical implications:
- Nordex receives capital backing and a built-in customer (Acciona Energía installs Nordex turbines in many projects)
- Acciona gains a turbine-manufacturer earnings stream upstream of its own renewable-developer arm
- Both companies benefit from the same [[Energy security as investment thesis|sovereignty capex cycle]] but at different points in the value chain

## Cross-listings within the Acciona group

- Acciona S.A. (ANA.MC) — parent conglomerate
- Acciona Energía (ANE.MC) — renewable subsidiary, separately listed
- [[Nordex]] (NDX1.DE, Frankfurt) — majority-owned wind turbine manufacturer

For energy-security exposure, ANA.MC provides the most concentrated combined play; ANE.MC isolates the renewable operator economics; NDX1.DE isolates the turbine manufacturing.
