#actor #analyst #commodities

**Francisco Blanch** — Head of Global Commodities, Equity Derivatives and Cross-Asset Quantitative Investment Strategies at [[Bank of America]] Global Research. One of the most cited commodity strategists on Wall Street.

---

## Pre-war views (Feb 2026)

Oil: Bearish. Expected ~2M bpd surplus in 2026, Brent avg $60 (down from $68 in 2025). Structural floor from low inventories outside [[China]] and strategic reserve buying. Global consumption projected to hit 108M bpd by 2031 (from 104M in 2025).

[[Natural gas]]: Neutral. $4/MMBtu Henry Hub forecast.

Electricity: Bullish. Key theme: "electrons over molecules." Sees AI exposure in [[Commodities]] primarily via electricity, particularly [[PJM]] market.

Gold: $4,000 target (set mid-2025).

Industrial metals: Bullish into 2026+.

Geopolitical risk: [[Russia]]-[[Ukraine]] peace deal with sanctions relief would be "pretty bearish for commodity markets broadly, particularly for oil and gas."

## Iran war revision (March 2026)

Blanch's pre-war $60 Brent forecast became instantly stale after the [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure (Feb 27). He published the most aggressive revision among major bank commodity strategists through March.

### March 27, 2026 — "biggest gap in energy supplies ever" (CNBC)

CNBC Squawk on the Street (Mar 27): Called the Iran war *"the biggest gap in energy supplies the world's ever seen."* Said the market is *"pretty anxious."*

Revised forecasts:

| Scenario | Brent forecast |
|----------|---------------|
| Base case | $77.50 avg 2026 (Q2 avg $80) |
| Pre-war surplus returns (2027) | $65 |
| Severe disruption | $100 avg |
| Extended Hormuz closure (late 2026) | Up to $130 |

Recession risk: sees odds growing *"by the week"* if war continues into April/May.

Blanch: *"The war is gonna transform the way we think about commodities, more fundamentally."*

### Earlier March calls

March 16: Laid out "the path to $200 oil" — extreme tail scenario if conflict broadens.
March 11: $170/bbl if war reaches Q3.

### Analytical shift

Blanch entered the conflict as one of the most bearish commodity voices on the Street ($60 Brent, 2M bpd surplus thesis). The Hormuz closure forced a complete framework revision. His March 27 $77.50 base is still well below [[Jeff Currie]] (who says $100 is mispriced low) and the physical market (Dubai at $126), reflecting residual pre-war surplus assumptions that may need further revision. Contrast with [[Helima Croft]]'s view that the disruption is structurally longer than the market prices.
