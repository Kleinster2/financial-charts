## March 2026 — Iran conflict analysis

### Three-tier crude pricing (CNBC, March 23)

Sen framed the current oil market as a three-tier system reflecting regional tightness:

| Tier | Benchmark | Level | Rationale |
|------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| 1 (tightest) | [[Dubai crude]] / [[Murban]] | $150-160/bbl | Reflects genuine physical tightness — 90% of Gulf crude goes to Asia |
| 2 | [[Brent crude]] | "Most mispriced" — should be closer to Dubai | Global benchmark lagging physical market |
| 3 (most shielded) | [[WTI]] | Disconnecting from global benchmarks | US production + [[Strategic Petroleum Reserve]] release + market expectations of US intervention |

Sen's core claim: Brent is the most mispriced benchmark — it should be trading much closer to Dubai. The WTI-Brent spread will widen further, not narrow.

### Physical vs paper disconnect

The physical market — not just in Asia but even in Europe (Angolan crude, North Sea crude) — is trading at significant premiums to futures. Sen attributed the paper market lag to "fatigue in the trading community" — traders not taking positions after a very difficult year. Physical shortages in [[Jet fuel|jet fuel]] (parts of Asia), [[LPG]], and [[Naphtha]] will eventually force paper to catch up.

### Hormuz reopening timeline

Energy Aspects pushed their [[Strait of Hormuz]] reopening estimate from end of March to end of April. Sen stressed that even a ceasefire wouldn't be sufficient — "every company CEO I'm speaking with is saying that I don't want to be the first one to send my ship through." Guarantees beyond a ceasefire are needed for commercial traffic to resume.

On [[Kharg Island]] seizure scenario: even if US troops take Kharg, it doesn't mean Iranian oil stops flowing — and it doesn't mean other oil can flow through [[Strait of Hormuz|Hormuz]] safely. A coalition escort operation is required, not just a military seizure.

### "Reverse COVID" analogy

Sen and [[Jeff Currie]] frame the current crisis as a reverse COVID — similar magnitude (~11M bbl/day shut in on the supply side, ~15-16M bbl/day including refined products) but inverted. COVID was a demand shock with supply intact; this is a supply shock with demand intact. ~4x the scale of the 2022 [[Russia]]-[[Ukraine]] supply fear (~3M bbl/day).

### Central bank dilemma

The supply shock is "extremely inflationary." If central banks cut rates (prioritizing growth over inflation), that's "even more bullish for oil." If they raise rates, economic growth suffers further. No good option.

### Other positions

- Energy Secretary [[Chris Wright]] confirmed export bans are "not going to happen" — aligning with Sen's prior assessment
- [[Iran]] reaching out to US through third parties for ceasefire talks (Axios reporting)
- Up to 5,000 US Marines arriving in region within days (second Marine amphibious unit)
- [[Robin J. Brooks]] ([[Brookings Institution]]) calling for "literally an oil embargo" on Iran
