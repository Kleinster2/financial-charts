# Wei Zhou

Wei Zhou is the CEO of [[Coins.ph]], the largest crypto-native fintech in the [[Philippines]], a role he stepped into in April 2022 after leading a [[Joffre Capital]]-backed consortium that acquired the company from [[Gojek]] for roughly $190M. Zhou previously served as the first chief financial officer of [[Binance]] (2018-2021), having joined from [[Goldman Sachs]]'s Hong Kong investment banking and Asian Special Situations Group. He is also an Operating Partner at Joffre Capital, the technology buyout firm that co-led the Coins.ph deal.

## Career arc

Zhou's path runs from US investment banking through China-listed CFO seats (Zhaopin, Charm Communications) and an LBO-style operating chair at Grindr, into the first finance leadership at Binance during its hyper-growth phase, and now into operating control of a regulated Philippine fintech. The thread is recurring exposure to Asia cross-border financial plumbing — IB advisory, SPAC-era public company finance, crypto exchange treasury, and now a regulated emerging-market remit corridor. Joffre Capital provided the buyout capital structure for the Coins.ph deal and Zhou retains an operating partner role there.

His departure from Binance in mid-2021 preceded the most intense phase of US regulatory pressure on the exchange. Binance founder [[CZ]]'s memoir mentions Zhou as one of the most-discussed figures in the firm's history, though Zhou has publicly disputed CZ's characterization of certain events (notably an alleged blocking of Binance Philippine market entry).

## Strategic frame at Coins.ph

Zhou's public framing centers on three points:

1. Stablecoins as the "conduit" — not a destination — for delivering financial services to overseas Filipino workers in their home corridor. Workers earn dollars abroad and want their families' financial services denominated in dollars at home.
2. Remittance cost compression as a generational opportunity. From 8% a decade ago to today's 2-4%, with stablecoins enabling sub-50 bps. On $40B of Philippine annual remit flow each 1% saved is ~$400M staying in worker pockets.
3. Trust and convenience still set the speed of adoption — Zhou's view is that customers want stablecoin rails working behind familiar app interfaces, not direct exposure to crypto UX.
