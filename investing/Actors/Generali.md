Generali is [[Italy]]'s largest insurance company and one of the top five insurers in [[Europe]] by gross written premiums. Founded in Trieste in 1831 — when the city was part of the Habsburg Empire — it is among the oldest continuously operating financial institutions in the world. The company operates across life insurance, property & casualty, and asset management in over 50 countries, with particular strength in Italy, Germany, France, and Central/Eastern Europe. Generali Asset Management oversees ~600B EUR in AUM.

The company's modern history is shaped by Italian corporate governance dynamics. The Mediobanca-led shareholder pact historically controlled board composition, but CEO Philippe Donnet (reappointed 2022 despite a rival slate backed by billionaire Leonardo Del Vecchio and CRT Foundation) has pursued an independence strategy. Generali competed with [[Allianz]] for control of AGF ([[France]]) in 1997 — a bid Allianz won. More recently, Generali agreed to merge its asset management operations with Natixis Investment Managers (a [[BPCE]] subsidiary) in early 2025, creating a combined ~1.9T EUR AUM platform that would rank among Europe's largest.
