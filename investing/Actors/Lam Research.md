## Core thesis

Lam benefits from increasing process complexity at advanced nodes. More deposition and etch steps per wafer = more Lam tools. Memory exposure ([[HBM]] buildout) adds cyclical upside.

---

## What they do

| Segment | Description | Share |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Deposition | Deposits thin films on wafers | Leading |
| Etch | Removes material to create structures | Leading |
| Clean | Wafer cleaning between steps | Strong |

These steps multiply with node complexity — more layers, more steps, more tools.

### Product portfolio detail

**Building Up (Deposition):**
- ALTUS — CVD/ALD for tungsten and molybdenum contacts/wires. ALD Moly tools leading the W→Mo transition (3× metallization SAM per wafer). Only company with ALD Moly in production in foundry/logic
- SABRE — Electrochemical deposition for copper interconnects + advanced packaging. 6,000 installed plating cells (largest base in industry). SABRE 3D critical for [[HBM]] stacking
- SPEED, VECTOR, Striker — Dielectric (insulating) layer deposition

Carving Down (Etch):
- Flex, Vantex — Dielectric etch. Vantex with cryogenic tech for high-aspect-ratio 3D NAND (200+ layers). Won key multi-generation decision at major NAND customer
- Kiyo, Versys Metal — Conductor etch. Akara (new) with plasma pulsing for DRAM — multiple wins at top DRAM maker
- Syndion — Deep silicon etch for CMOS image sensors and TSVs

Keeping it Clean:
- Coronus — Plasma bevel clean
- DV-Prime, Da Vinci, EOS, SP Series — Wet clean systems

### The "Complexity is Cash Flow" moat

As chips go vertical (200+ layer 3D NAND, GAA transistors, HBM stacks), the number of deposition and etch steps grows non-linearly. Lam's market can expand even if total WFE is flat. GAA transition triples metallization steps per wafer. Advanced packaging share expected to jump ~5pp in CY2025. Management goal: win >50% of incremental SAM at each technology inflection.

## Q3 FY2026 earnings — record quarter, $140B WFE guide, NAND conversions accelerating (Apr 22, 2026)

Record revenue quarter with a meaningful WFE industry upgrade behind it. LRCX shares added 2% in after-hours trade as the forward guide, not the print, is the centerpiece: management raised its 2026 wafer fab equipment forecast to $140B (up from implied $130B range exiting Q2) and accelerated the timing of major [[NAND flash|NAND]] conversion investments. This is the first sell-side call that directly supports the bull thesis that the AI-driven logic refresh is finally dragging memory capex with it.

| Metric | Q3 FY2026 | Q3 FY2025 | Prior guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $5.84B | $4.72B (+23.7%) | $5.25B |
| EPS (adj) | $1.47 | — | $1.17 |
| EPS vs consensus | $1.47 | — | Beat $1.36 |
| Gross margin | 49.9% | — | — |
| Sequential growth | +9%+ | — | — |

FQ4 (June 28, 2026 quarter) guidance:

| Metric | Guide midpoint | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $6.60B | ± $0.40B |
| Gross margin | 50.5% | ± 1% |
| Operating margin | 36.5% | ± 1% |
| EPS | $1.65 | ± $0.15 |

At the midpoint, the FQ4 guide implies continued sequential acceleration — the third quarterly beat-and-raise in a row, which has not happened at this magnitude since the pre-2022 memory upcycle. Gross margin stepping to 50.5% is structurally important: it is the highest margin LRCX has guided to since 2022 peaks.

The $140B WFE number. Lam's industry spend forecast is the most-watched single data point in semi-cap. The step-up from ~$130B to $140B encodes three things: (1) the [[Iran conflict semiconductor energy risk|Iran-war energy risk]] has not derailed fab capex timing; (2) the AI build — 3nm/2nm logic at [[TSMC]] and [[Samsung]] — is pulling higher etch/deposition intensity than prior nodes; (3) NAND conversion (planar → 3D, higher stacks) is moving forward rather than deferring. The NAND acceleration point is the cleanest bullish read-across for [[Micron]] and [[SK Hynix]], whose capex commitments are the variable feeding Lam's order book into FY2027.

Advanced packaging tailwind. The Customer Support Business Group and advanced packaging were called out as primary sequential drivers. Advanced packaging is the structural win for Lam — [[CoWoS]], [[HBM]] back-end tooling, and 3D chiplet integration all lean on etch/deposition steps where Lam has share leadership. This is the "complexity is cash flow" moat compounding.

China still capped. The "50% affiliate rule" revenue cap estimated at ~$600M for CY2026 was unchanged in the call; management did not flag incremental Entity List expansions in the quarter. If export controls tighten in Q2/Q3, that $140B number comes back into question.

Read-across to [[TEL|Tokyo Electron]] (ADR TEL -9% same day). Lam's beat + raise diverges sharply from the TE Connectivity / TOELY (Tokyo Electron) weakness on Apr 22. LRCX and [[Applied Materials]] (AMAT) are now the semi-cap names with the cleanest beat-and-raise prints — the divergence will either resolve with AMAT confirming in early-May earnings, or Lam-specific advantages (NAND exposure, advanced packaging share) will become the explanation.
