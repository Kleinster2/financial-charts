# Majorel

**Majorel** was a BPO company acquired by [[Teleperformance]] in November 2023 for approximately EUR 3B, creating the world's largest customer experience outsourcer.

## Synopsis

Majorel was formed as a joint venture between [[Bertelsmann]] and Saham Group, IPO'd on Euronext Amsterdam in 2021, and was absorbed by [[Teleperformance]] barely two years later in a deal valued at ~EUR 3B. The acquisition was Teleperformance's response to the structural challenge facing the BPO industry: AI-driven automation threatening to commoditize traditional voice and chat support. By acquiring Majorel, TEP gained scale (combined ~500K employees across 170+ countries) and Majorel CEO Thomas Mackenbrock, who became TEP's group CEO post-close — an unusual move signaling that the acquirer valued Majorel's leadership and digital transformation capabilities over its own incumbent management.

The Majorel deal is a reference point for BPO sector consolidation economics. TEP paid ~EUR 3B for a company that added geographic diversification (Majorel was strong in DACH and Africa) and digital CX capabilities, but the timing coincided with [[ChatGPT]]'s emergence and the rapid market repricing of every BPO stock on AI substitution fears. TEP's share price fell roughly 50% from its 2022 highs to the Majorel close, meaning the market was effectively saying the acquirer was destroying value. The strategic bet — that scale and digital services would protect margins as AI handles basic queries — remains unproven.
