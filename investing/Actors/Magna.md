## St. Clair EV factory — stranded asset

Magna built a 1 million-sq-ft factory in St. Clair, Michigan to produce battery enclosures for [[General Motors|GM]]'s Hummer EV and Chevrolet Silverado EV. The contract was large enough to justify a greenfield build; Magna invested ~$575 million in the facility and equipment. St. Clair won the site competition partly through a $44 million incentive package (state, county, city) plus a new water tower.

GM originally pressed Magna for more capacity than Magna wanted. The companies compromised: Magna boosted planned capacity; GM absorbed more setup costs and agreed to pay higher per-unit rates if demand fell short.

Plant opened early 2022. Demand never materialized.

| Metric | Planned | Actual (Mar 2026) |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Headcount | 900 | A few hundred |
| Utilization | Full production | Mostly empty |
| Status | Multi-shift | Single shrunken shift |

Production supervisor Daniel Martin: *"We had rows and rows of battery trays. We were just boxing them up and throwing them in warehouses."* Workers were laid off in waves, sometimes rehired, then laid off again.

Magna is now touring automakers and suppliers through the facility, hoping to attract new business. GM lifted exclusivity restrictions that previously barred Magna from dealing with rival OEMs. Kotagiri estimates 18-24 months to find new customers and reach profitability again. New customers would likely build different parts than what the plant was originally configured for.

*Source: WSJ (Sharon Terlep), March 31, 2026*

## EV battery enclosure ambitions (shelved)

Magna planned an entirely new business unit around EV battery enclosures. Kotagiri projected $2.5 billion/year in revenue from the unit. The thesis:

| EV vs ICE part | ICE cost | EV cost |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| All-wheel drive system parts | ~$450 | $800-$1,200 (e-drive system) |

Higher content per vehicle made EVs attractive for Tier 1 suppliers. Kotagiri told investors EV-related sales would reach $4.5 billion within a few years.

Kotagiri still believes EVs will take hold in the US as they have elsewhere, but is making no guess on timing.

*Source: WSJ (Sharon Terlep), March 31, 2026*

## GM contract economics

[[General Motors|GM]] contracts were struck expecting GM would build 1 million EVs/year. By December 2025, GM was selling ~8,000 EVs/month. GM paid ~$400 million total to suppliers whose contracts were modified or eliminated — but payoffs only partially cover losses and do not cover factory downtime while suppliers seek new business and retool.

GM idled the Detroit factory where it builds the electric trucks Magna supplies (announced late March 2026).
