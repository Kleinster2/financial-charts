# PACCAR

**Third-largest heavy truck manufacturer globally** — parent company of Kenworth and Peterbilt truck brands. Ticker PCAR. Develops proprietary MX-13 diesel engine (45%+ uptake in its trucks, reducing dependence on [[Cummins]]). Historically [[Cummins]]' single largest customer for heavy-duty engines. Also manufactures DAF trucks in Europe.

## Q1 2026 results (Apr 28, 2026) — truck cycle trough confirmed

PACCAR reported Q1 2026 results that missed Wall Street revenue estimates and confirmed the Class 8 truck cycle is at a low — stock closed -6.0% to $119.61 on the print (4.1σ idiosyncratic move). The deliveries figure (33K) is well below the Q2 guide (37-38K), implying Q1 was the trough; the FY 2026 US/Canada market guidance of 230,000-270,000 Class 8 units sits in the bottom third of the historical 200K-330K range.

### 2026 market guidance — cyclical low

| Region | 2026 Class 8 forecast | Historical context |
|---|---|---|
| US/Canada | 230K-270K | Low end of cycle (recent peak ~310K, trough ~200K) |
| Europe (above 16-ton) | 280K-320K | Steady |
| South America (above 16-ton) | 100K-110K | Brazil-led recovery from 2024 low |

Management framing: "the market is strengthening as driver and fleet capacity becomes limited and customers begin to realize higher freight rates." The freight-rate signal is the standard leading indicator for truck order recovery — when spot rates rise to where carriers can absorb new equipment costs, orders follow with a 3-6 month lag.

### Why the cycle matters for the broader thesis

PACCAR is a clean read on the freight cycle, which feeds two distinct macro signals: (1) the [[Recession risk 2026|2026 recession-risk indicator set]] — Class 8 orders are a real-time freight demand proxy, and a 230-270K guidance range below mid-cycle is consistent with weak goods-flow data; (2) the trade-flow / tariff-policy mix — Q1 includes early-2026 tariff re-pricing, which compresses the goods-imports / domestic-trucking demand chain.

The PACCAR Parts $1.71B revenue (counter-cyclical aftermarket business) provides margin support during the trough. Margin expansion to 13.1% on weaker volume reflects pricing discipline and engine-mix benefit (MX-13 attach rate above 45%).
