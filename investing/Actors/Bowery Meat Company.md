**Bowery Meat Company** — Upscale steakhouse at 9 East 1st Street in the East Village, New York City. Operated by [[Mercer Street Hospitality]], the restaurant group founded by [[John McDonald (restaurateur)|John McDonald]].

---

## Synopsis

Bowery Meat Company occupies a distinctive niche in the NYC dining scene — a serious dry-aged beef program in a neighborhood better known for ramen and dive bars. The restaurant specializes in large-format dry-aged cuts, chops, and tableside preparations, competing with midtown steakhouse institutions like [[Peter Luger]] and Keens while drawing a younger, downtown crowd. The East Village location on 1st Street between 1st and 2nd Avenues puts it in the heart of one of Manhattan's densest restaurant corridors.

As a [[Mercer Street Hospitality]] property, Bowery Meat sits within a portfolio that includes Lure Fishbar and The Mercer Kitchen, all targeting the high-end-but-not-stuffy segment of NYC dining. The restaurant has maintained strong reviews and reservation demand since opening, serving as a proof point for the downtown premium dining model — the thesis that diners will pay steakhouse prices ($60-100+ entrees) outside the traditional Midtown/FiDi steakhouse belt if the product and atmosphere deliver.
