# FCC

The Federal Communications Commission — independent U.S. agency regulating interstate and international communications (radio, TV, wire, satellite, cable).

---

## Why it matters

The FCC controls spectrum allocation and satellite constellation licensing, making it a key gatekeeper for [[Starlink]], [[Amazon]] Leo, [[AST SpaceMobile]], and other LEO broadband players. Approval timelines and deployment deadlines directly impact satellite company valuations.

## Nexstar-Tegna approval (March 2026)

On March 19, 2026, the FCC approved [[Nexstar Media Group]]'s $3.54B acquisition of [[Tegna]], creating the largest local TV operator in the U.S. (265 stations, 44 states + D.C.). The FCC waived its rule capping any single owner from broadcasting to more than 39% of U.S. households — a significant precedent for broadcast consolidation. [[Nexstar Media Group|Nexstar]] agreed to divest six stations as a condition. Eight Democratic-led states and [[DirecTV]] filed lawsuits to block the deal the day before approval.

See [[Nexstar-Tegna Merger]] for full details.
