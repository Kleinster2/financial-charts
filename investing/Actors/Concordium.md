Concordium is a compliance-focused layer-1 blockchain founded by [[Lars Seier Christensen]] in 2018 and headquartered in [[Switzerland]] (Zug). Its core differentiator is a built-in identity layer — every wallet holder must pass an identity verification through a trusted third party, but on-chain transactions remain pseudonymous unless a court order compels de-anonymization. This "ID at the protocol level" design targets regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, supply chain) that cannot use fully anonymous chains for compliance reasons.

The project raised ~$36M in token sales and private rounds. Christensen, who co-founded [[Saxo Bank]] and built it into a $1B+ online trading platform, brings credibility with institutional finance but faces the classic enterprise blockchain challenge: convincing regulated firms to adopt a new chain rather than using permissioned layers on [[Ethereum]] or private chains. Concordium uses a proof-of-stake consensus mechanism and claims near-zero transaction costs and sub-10-second finality.
