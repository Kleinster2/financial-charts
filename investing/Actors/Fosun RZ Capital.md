Fosun RZ Capital is the venture capital arm of [[Fosun International]], one of [[China]]'s largest private conglomerates (founded by Guo Guangchang in 1992). The VC unit focuses on technology and innovation investments across AI, robotics, enterprise software, and advanced manufacturing, typically writing checks at Series A through C stages. Based in [[Shanghai]], it leverages the broader Fosun ecosystem — which spans insurance (Fosun United Health Insurance), pharma (Fosun Pharma, including the BioNTech COVID vaccine distribution deal for China), tourism (Club Med), and steel (Nanjing Iron & Steel) — to provide portfolio companies with distribution partnerships and industrial expertise.

Fosun RZ Capital is part of a larger trend of Chinese conglomerate CVC arms investing in deep tech, alongside peers like [[Alibaba]]'s strategic investments unit and [[Tencent]]'s investment group. The Fosun parent has faced headwinds from China's property downturn and regulatory pressure on private conglomerates, with credit concerns periodically surfacing around Fosun International's leverage ratios. These parent-level concerns can affect the VC unit's ability to fund follow-on rounds for portfolio companies.
