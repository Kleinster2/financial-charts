# Satisfy

Paris-based premium running brand founded in 2015 by [[Brice Partouche]]. Satisfy reimagined running gear through a high-fashion, punk-DIY lens — the tagline is "running apparel developed to unlock the High." Revenue doubled from €5.5M (FY2023) to ~€11M (FY2024), and a €11.3M Series B (Jan 2025, led by [[Bpifrance]]) funds a push toward €100M revenue within five years. The brand is a [[World Building as CPG Moat]] case study: Satisfy doesn't sell running clothes — it sells a subversive identity for what a runner can look and feel like, backed by its own magazine (Possessed), footwear (TheROCKER), and collaborations with [[Sonic Youth]], [[Hoka]], [[Oakley]], [[Norda]], and [[Crocs]].

## Synopsis

Satisfy is the most culturally influential running brand to emerge in the last decade. Founded in Paris by Brice Partouche, who came from the fashion world rather than athletic apparel, the brand launched with MothTech™ — body-mapped ventilation inspired by moth-eaten vintage band tees, a $140 cotton shirt with intentional holes that became iconic and polarizing. From there, Satisfy built an entire cultural universe: a Sonic Youth collaboration that felt like alignment rather than marketing, footwear collabs with Hoka (the Mafate Speed 4 Lite sold out instantly), Norda, and Crocs, its own soft drink (Floda), a print magazine (Possessed), Oakley eyewear partnerships, and now its first proprietary trail shoe (TheROCKER, launching summer 2025). Revenue doubled to ~€11M in FY2024, and a €11.3M Series B from Bpifrance and French Touch targets 10x growth to €100M in five years. The bull case: Satisfy has done what On and Hoka did for mainstream running but for the cultural/fashion segment, and it has category expansion permission across footwear, eyewear, media, and lifestyle. The bear case: premium pricing ($140 tees, $200+ shorts) limits TAM, and scaling from €11M to €100M requires wholesale execution that could dilute the cult brand.

## Evolution

The story of Satisfy is the story of running culture getting a punk makeover. Partouche founded the brand in Paris in 2015 with a manifesto and a moth-eaten t-shirt. MothTech™ — body-mapped ventilation that looked like vintage band merch pulled from a thrift bin — became the brand's defining product and ethos statement: running gear could be creative, subversive, and beautiful without sacrificing performance. Manufacturing was set up in Portugal with premium fabrics from Italy and Japan, a "Designed in Paris, Made in Europe" model unusual in sportswear.

The 2018 "Run! Punk Run!" campaign — a short film following a tattooed runner and his motorcycle crew through LA suburbs — set an aesthetic blueprint that the running world is still copying. But the cultural inflection came in 2021 with the Sonic Youth collaboration, which put album art on tech garments and positioned Satisfy at the intersection of music, art, and athletics. From 2022–2024, footwear collaborations escalated: first Norda (trail shoe), then Crocs (a neoprene-gaitered clog that defied categorization), then two Hoka collabs — the Clifton LS and the Mafate Speed 4 Lite, which sold out immediately and influenced Hoka's own design language.

Satisfy launched its own magazine (Possessed, 2023), its own soft drink (Floda, 2023), and an Oakley eyewear partnership (2023, with pairs reselling for up to $700). In January 2025, the brand closed an €11.3M Series B led by Bpifrance and French Touch, with revenue having doubled from €5.5M to ~€11M in a single year. The capital funds the brand's first proprietary trail shoe — TheROCKER (summer 2025), featuring in-house Euforia foam and Vibram soles inspired by RC car tires — and a push toward €100M revenue within five years. A new COO from Sézane and Celine was hired to build omnichannel infrastructure. As of 2025, Satisfy has effectively graduated from apparel brand to cultural platform.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Cultural brand in a structural growth market (global running apparel ~$15B, growing 6-8% annually)
- Revenue doubling (€5.5M → €11M) demonstrates product-market fit
- Category expansion already proven: apparel → footwear → eyewear → media → beverages
- Premium positioning = high gross margins; European manufacturing = quality narrative
- Collaborations with Hoka, Oakley, Sonic Youth build cultural capital without diluting brand
- €100M target is ambitious but comparable to On's early trajectory

**Bear:**
- $140 tees and $200+ shorts limit addressable market
- Scaling from €11M to €100M requires wholesale/retail buildout that could dilute exclusivity
- Running fashion is trend-sensitive — Satisfy's punk aesthetic could age out
- Competition from On, Hoka, and emerging brands is intense
- Profitability not disclosed; high-quality European manufacturing is expensive
