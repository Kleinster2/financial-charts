**Armin Papperger** — CEO of [[Rheinmetall]] since 2013. Presides over the company's transformation from a cyclical German defense supplier into [[Europe]]'s defense consolidator through the [[Ukraine]]-war and Zeitenwende rearmament cycle. Rheinmetall's share price is up roughly 400% under his tenure since [[Russia]]'s February 2022 invasion.

Position: the most visible CEO in [[European defense spending]]. Papperger has pushed [[Rheinmetall]] out of its tanks-and-ammunition comfort zone into drones (via the [[Auterion]] stake and [[FV-014]]), naval shipbuilding, and space. He has personally led the [[Schuldenbremse reform]] win: Rheinmetall is the largest listed beneficiary of [[Germany]]'s unlimited defense-spending exemption.

## Apr 2026 "Legos" controversy

In March and April 2026 Papperger drew public anger from [[Ukraine]] after comparing the Ukrainian drone industry to playing with "Legos." He said:

- Ukrainian drones were made by "Ukrainian housewives"
- "They have 3D printers in the kitchen, and they produce parts for drones"
- "This is not innovation"

The comments landed in the days before the [[Bundestag]] vote on [[Rheinmetall]]'s [[FV-014]] contract. Responses:

- [[Oleksandr Kamyshin]] (adviser on strategic industries to [[Volodymyr Zelenskyy]]): Ukraine's "Lego drones" had already burned more than 11,000 Russian tanks.
- [[Volodymyr Zelenskyy]]: "If every Ukrainian housewife can produce drones, then every Ukrainian housewife can be the CEO of Rheinmetall."
- Rheinmetall subsequently walked back the comment, stressing "the utmost respect for the Ukrainian people's immense efforts in defending themselves" and praising [[Ukraine]]'s "innovative strength and fighting spirit."

The gaffe is revealing about [[Rheinmetall]]'s cultural position: a legacy prime eyeing the same drone procurement opportunity as the Ukrainian cottage industry whose output is actually burning Russian armor. The €298M [[FV-014]] contract passed the [[Bundestag]] days later.
