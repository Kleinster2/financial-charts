**Lula (Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva)** — President of [[Brazil]] (2003-2010, 2023-present). Founded the Workers' Party ([[PT]]). Launched the [[Brazil Biodiesel Program|PNPB biodiesel program]] (2004), [[Bolsa Familia]] welfare program, and presided over Brazil's commodity boom era. Third term focused on energy transition (Fuels of the Future Law), reindustrialization, and social spending. Seeking an unprecedented fourth term in the [[2026 Brazil presidential election]] — as of April 16, 2026, [[Polymarket]] prices him at 39% to win, trailing [Flávio Bolsonaro](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=People%2FFl%C3%A1vio%20Bolsonaro) at 40% for the first time. Datafolha April 11 poll showed Flávio 46 vs Lula 45 in a runoff — a four-point swing from March's 46-43 Lula lead.

## 2026 re-election campaign

Polymarket flipped April 2026. Lula was a clear favorite three months ago; as of April 16 the contract prices Flávio Bolsonaro at 40% versus Lula at 39%. First-round polling structure: Lula 39-40, [Flávio Bolsonaro](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=People%2FFl%C3%A1vio%20Bolsonaro) 35-37, [Ronaldo Caiado](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=People%2FRonaldo%20Caiado) 5-6, [Romeu Zema](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=People%2FRomeu%20Zema) 4-5. Runoff priced as near-certain (first-round outright winner at 14-15%).

Path bounded by 48% of voters who reject Lula under any circumstances. Campaign depends on holding the Northeast supermajority from 2022 and winning back center voters who backed Tebet in first round last cycle.

Continuation policy implications:
- [[Fernando Haddad]] or successor at Finance; [[Gabriel Galípolo]] retained at BCB (term through December 2028 under independence law)
- [[Arcabouço Fiscal]] framework continues under pressure; primary surplus elusive; debt/GDP toward 95%
- Tax reform implementation on 2026-2033 schedule; 10% dividend withholding tax retained; Bill 1,087/2025 expanding income taxation continues
- Industrial policy emphasis on biofuels, renewable energy, sustainable agribusiness, "Nova Indústria Brasil"
- Foreign policy: BRICS deepens, [[China]] remains primary partner, EU-Mercosul proceeds May 1, 2026
- Tension with [[Trump]] administration continues despite institutional cooperation ([[DESARMA]] April 10 security partnership signed during open Lula-Trump tension)
