Clay Magouyrk is [[Oracle]] EVP running [[Oracle Cloud Infrastructure|OCI]], the company's hyperscaler cloud division. He joined Oracle in 2017 specifically to build OCI from the ground up, bringing experience from [[Amazon Web Services|AWS]] where he worked on core infrastructure. Under Magouyrk, OCI grew from a negligible player to a legitimate hyperscaler contender, winning major AI training contracts including a $10B+ deal with [[OpenAI]] (2024) and becoming a key partner in the Stargate project alongside [[SoftBank]] and [[NVIDIA]].

Magouyrk's technical credibility — he architected OCI's second-generation cloud from scratch rather than iterating on Oracle's legacy infrastructure — is central to [[Larry Ellison]]'s pitch that Oracle can compete with [[Amazon Web Services|AWS]], [[Microsoft Azure|Azure]], and [[Google Cloud]] on AI workloads. OCI's differentiation is its bare-metal GPU clusters with RDMA networking, which delivers better price-performance on large-scale AI training.
