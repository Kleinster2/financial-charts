## "Brent is the most mispriced benchmark" — Sen (CNBC, March 23)

Founder [[Amrita Sen]] on CNBC (Mar 23) framed the market as a three-tier system: [[Dubai crude]] at $150-160/bbl (genuine physical tightness, 90% of Gulf crude goes to Asia), then [[Brent crude]] ("should be much higher, closer to Dubai"), then [[WTI]] (disconnecting from global benchmarks due to US production + [[Strategic Petroleum Reserve|SPR]] release).

On physical vs paper disconnect: differentials on Angolan and North Sea crude trading at significant premiums to futures. "Fatigue in the trading community" — traders not taking positions. Physical shortages in [[Jet fuel|jet fuel]], [[LPG]], [[Naphtha]] will force paper markets to catch up.

Energy Aspects pushed their [[Strait of Hormuz]] reopening estimate from end of March to end of April. CEO conversations universally say no one wants to send the first ship through even after a ceasefire.

On the magnitude: Sen and [[Jeff Currie]] frame this as a "reverse COVID" — ~11M bbl/day supply shut-in (~15-16M including refined products), ~4x the [[Russia]]-[[Ukraine]] 2022 supply fear (~3M bbl/day). If central banks cut rates to protect growth, "even more bullish for oil."

*Source: CNBC International, March 23, 2026*
