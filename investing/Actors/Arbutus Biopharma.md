**Arbutus Biopharma** is a biotech company that holds foundational patents on lipid nanoparticle (LNP) delivery technology — the same technology used in [[Moderna]]'s and [[BioNTech]]/[[Pfizer]]'s COVID-19 mRNA vaccines. The March 2026 settlement with Moderna for up to $2.25B transformed Arbutus from a small-cap drug developer into one of the largest patent monetization stories in biotech history.

## Synopsis

LNP technology is the delivery mechanism that makes mRNA therapeutics work. Naked mRNA degrades within seconds in the bloodstream; encapsulating it in lipid nanoparticles protects it long enough to reach cells and trigger protein production. Arbutus (formerly Tekmira Pharmaceuticals) developed and patented key LNP formulations in the early 2010s, originally for siRNA drug delivery. When the COVID-19 pandemic created a multi-billion-dollar mRNA vaccine market overnight, Arbutus found itself holding patents that arguably covered core technology used by [[Moderna]] in its Spikevax vaccine, which generated over $18B in revenue in 2022 alone.

The patent dispute with Moderna was protracted. Arbutus filed in the Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB) and federal court, while Moderna simultaneously challenged the patents' validity. In March 2026, the parties settled for up to $2.25B — a figure that dwarfs Arbutus's pre-settlement market cap and validates the LNP IP as genuinely foundational rather than peripheral. Separately, Arbutus maintains a pipeline of hepatitis B (HBV) therapeutics, including imdusiran (an RNAi agent), though the clinical-stage drug portfolio is early and the company's near-term value is almost entirely the Moderna settlement cash and residual IP licensing potential. For biotech investors, ABUS is now a cash-rich micro-cap with optionality on the HBV pipeline and any further LNP licensing claims against other mRNA developers.
