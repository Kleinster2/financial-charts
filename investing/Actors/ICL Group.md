# ICL Group

Israeli specialty minerals and chemicals company, operating phosphate mines in the Negev Desert and potash extraction from the Dead Sea. Produces phosphoric acid (white and green), specialty phosphates, bromine compounds, and potash fertilizers. Dual-listed on NYSE (ICL) and TASE.

## Semiconductor exposure — bromine

The [[Dead Sea]] is the world's largest source of bromine, a chemical used to score (lithographic patterning) on silicon wafers. ICL is the global #1 bromine producer. [[South Korea]] imports virtually all of its bromine from Israel, making ICL's Dead Sea operations a single-source dependency for [[Samsung Electronics|Samsung]] and [[SK Hynix]] memory chip manufacturing. As a conflict-zone producer, ICL faces both direct risk (attacks on Israeli facilities) and indirect supply chain risk (shipping disruption, insurance withdrawal on Israel-origin cargo). See [[Iran conflict semiconductor energy risk]].

*Source: FT, Tej Parikh, March 22 2026*
