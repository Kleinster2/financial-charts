**Jeffrey Hirsch** is the president and CEO of [[Starz]], in the role since September 2019, who led the network's profitable transition from linear cable to streaming and now steers it through its post-[[Lionsgate]] independence and the [[Byron Allen]] takeover campaign.

## Career

Hirsch spent the first part of his career at Time Warner Cable (from 1999), rising to EVP and Chief Marketing Officer for Residential Services and serving as president of the NYC, LA, and Rochester regions, with a hand in early digital products like the TWC iPad app. He joined [[Starz]] in July 2015 as president of global marketing and product development, became COO in 2016, and has led the company as president and CEO since September 2019.

Under Hirsch, [[Starz]] became — in its own framing — the only linear network to make a profitable transition to digital, with roughly 70% of revenue now from OTT, positioned as a premium destination for women and underrepresented audiences (the *Power* universe, *Outlander*).

---

## At Starz today

Hirsch has guided [[Starz]] through the May 2025 separation from [[Lionsgate]] and a deleveraging turnaround, telling investors the company beat all 2025 financial guidance and framing 2026 as a positive inflection point. He now manages the company through an unsolicited takeover campaign by [[Byron Allen]] and the board's poison-pill defense. See [[Starz]] → Streaming business and Financials.
