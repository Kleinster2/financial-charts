**UniCredit** — Italy's second-largest bank and a pan-European banking group headquartered in Milan. Under CEO [[Andrea Orcel]] (since April 2021), UniCredit has undergone a radical transformation: aggressive cost-cutting, €16B+ in buybacks, and a return to acquisitive growth. The bank operates across 13 countries with significant presences in Germany (via HypoVereinsbank), Austria (Bank Austria), and Central/Eastern Europe. Market cap ~€65B (early 2026).

## Synopsis

UniCredit's current significance centers on two M&A stories. First, the unsolicited €10.1B bid for [[Banco BPM]] (launched November 2024), which Banco BPM's board rejected as undervaluing the bank — creating a three-way dynamic with [[Crédit Agricole]] (19.8% Banco BPM shareholder) that remains unresolved. Second, Orcel's surprise accumulation of a ~28% stake in [[Commerzbank]], Germany's second-largest bank, which triggered political backlash from the German government (a 12% shareholder) and [[ECB]] regulatory scrutiny. Both deals reflect Orcel's vision of building a true pan-European banking champion, but face political resistance in both Italy and Germany.

Orcel's track record justifies investor attention: UniCredit shares have roughly quadrupled since he took over, driven by returns on capital that rival the best US banks. The question is whether M&A ambition derails the capital return story.
