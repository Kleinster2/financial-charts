## May 2026 inventory warning

In the May 23 2026 [[Financial Times]] article on US producers responding to the Iran-war oil-price surge, Sheffield argued that private producers would probably add rigs and crews first. His caveat was the durable one: across the [[Permian Basin]], high-quality inventory is already scarce, so accelerating the remaining Tier 1 wells can simply pull future production forward and shorten the runway.

That comment refines [[Shale supply discipline]] by separating two constraints that are easy to conflate: capital discipline can loosen if the forward curve rises, but inventory exhaustion remains binding even after management teams decide to drill.
