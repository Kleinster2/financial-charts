Cha Cha Tang is a Chinese restaurant in New York City, a collaboration between [[John McDonald (restaurateur)|John McDonald]] of [[Mercer Street Hospitality]] and [[Wilson Tang]], who also runs [[Nom Wah Tea Parlor]] — Chinatown's oldest dim sum restaurant (est. 1920). The concept focuses on elevated Chinese-American comfort food and tea-based cocktails, blending Wilson Tang's Cantonese culinary heritage with McDonald's restaurant group infrastructure.

The restaurant reflects a broader NYC dining trend of established operators partnering with culturally rooted chefs to build crossover concepts that appeal beyond ethnic enclaves. Wilson Tang has become one of NYC's most visible Chinese-American restaurateurs through Nom Wah's expansion (multiple locations plus a frozen dumpling line) and media appearances.
