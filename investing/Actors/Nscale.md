**Nscale** is a [[UK]]-headquartered AI infrastructure company valued at $14.6B (Series C, March 2026), born from [[Australia|Australian]] crypto miner Arkon Energy. [[NVIDIA]] equity investor (~$667M), [[Microsoft]] anchor customer ($23B+ in contracts), [[OpenAI]] offtaker via Stargate Norway. Preparing for IPO in H2 2026 with [[Goldman Sachs]] and [[JPMorgan]] as advisers.

Nscale is the most aggressive crypto-to-AI pivot in the neocloud cohort — from bitcoin mining defaults to a $14.6B valuation in under 18 months. Three dynamics determine whether the valuation holds:

1. Execution on a massive physical buildout. Nscale has 1.3GW+ of capacity in development across [[Norway]], the [[UK]], [[Texas]], [[Iceland]], [[Portugal]], and [[West Virginia]], with the Monarch acquisition adding an 8GW runway. The pipeline includes ~630,000 [[NVIDIA]] GPUs (200K GB300s for [[Microsoft]], 430K Vera Rubins under LOI). The gap between contracted capacity and operational capacity is enormous — Glomfjord (60MW) is the only site generating revenue today. Everything else is under development with phased delivery from Q3 2026 through 2028+.

2. [[NVIDIA]] as kingmaker. [[Jensen Huang]] personally endorsed Nscale as a "national champion for AI infrastructure in the UK," projected £50B in revenue over six years, and invested ~£500M in equity. NVIDIA participated in the Series B, pre-Series C SAFE, and Series C. This is not just capital — it is chip allocation priority, credibility with hyperscalers, and political cover for UK/EU sovereign AI ambitions. The question is whether NVIDIA's endorsement reflects genuine conviction or [[Neocloud financing|demand orchestration]].

3. Credit and governance history. The FT Alphaville investigation (March 23 2026) revealed that Nscale's parent Arkon Energy defaulted on two [[Sandton Capital Partners]] loans in 2024, required multiple waivers, and posted a $102M loss on $19.2M in revenue. Sandton — a distressed credit specialist — lent ~$110M on terms as aggressive as 17.5% interest with steep OID discounts, then converted its position into equity warrants representing ~14%+ of ordinary shares. The defaults were resolved via Series B proceeds that Sandton itself participated in. Textbook pivot from distressed lender to equity holder — it worked, but the credit history is real.

Open questions: Can Nscale execute simultaneous buildouts across six countries while managing a $1.4B term loan and preparing for IPO? The Monarch site (8GW, natural gas behind-the-meter via [[Caterpillar]]) changes the company's environmental narrative from "100% renewable hydro" to "natural gas microgrid." [[Microsoft]] concentration as anchor customer mirrors [[CoreWeave]]'s vulnerability (65% Microsoft revenue). The IPO timing — H2 2026 — depends on whether capital markets stay receptive to AI infrastructure stories after buildout costs hit.

## Evolution

The story of Nscale is the story of a coal miner's son from [[Australia]] who built a crypto mining company, defaulted on his loans, and convinced [[NVIDIA]]'s CEO he could be Britain's AI infrastructure champion — all within four years.

2021: Josh Payne founded Arkon Energy in [[Melbourne]] as a [[Bitcoin]] mining operation. Payne's background was unconventional — he had worked in Australian coal mines, reading entrepreneurship books during downtime, then built a construction recruitment platform before pivoting to renewable energy and crypto. The initial thesis was simple: cheap stranded energy (particularly Nordic hydro) could power [[Bitcoin]] mining profitably.

2022-2023: Arkon expanded to [[Norway]], acquiring the Glomfjord data center near the Svartisen glacier and developing a 60MW facility powered by hydroelectric energy. Norway's power costs were among Europe's lowest, and the Arctic climate reduced cooling costs. Arkon also developed a facility in Hannibal, [[Ohio]], diversifying geographically. To fund these buildouts, Arkon turned to [[Sandton Capital Partners]], a [[New York City|NYC]]-based distressed credit investor co-founded by Rael Nurick. Sandton lent ~$110M in cumulative principal across multiple tranches: the Hannibal development loans (three tranches totaling $37.2M committed / $43.6M principal balance, at 15-17.5% interest with steep OID discounts), $30M + €5M for Glomfjord development, ~$10M for the Glomfjord acquisition, and ~$19M for debt refinancing and equipment. Not every loan was used for its stated purpose — Sandton was apparently comfortable with the flexibility, given the terms.

December 2023: Arkon defaulted on a Dutch subsidiary's loan conditions, triggering a waiver from Sandton. Shortly after, it was again granted relief — delayed interest payments, pushed-back principal, and another waiver to avoid a second default. A $30M seed round also closed around this time. The Sandton relationship was symbiotic: Sandton had been issued warrant shares (326,307 in June 2023, 278,473 more in December) giving it a fixed claim on ~14%+ of Arkon's ordinary shares, exercisable on an exit event. Rocking the boat meant sinking its own equity position.

2024: The pivot year. [[Bitcoin]] mining economics were deteriorating post-halving, and the AI infrastructure boom offered a more valuable use case for the same physical assets — GPUs and power. Arkon rebranded as Nscale, emerging from "stealth mode" with a $155M Series A (December 2024) led by Sandton itself — the distressed lender becoming the lead equity investor. The round was oversubscribed, with Kestrel 0x1, Blue Sky Capital Managers, and Florence Capital participating. Arkon's 2024 financials told the story of transition: $19.2M in revenue (almost entirely from Norwegian operations), a $102M net loss, $19.7M in staff costs (roughly equal to total revenue), and $40.9M in administrative expenses including $9.4M in professional fees. A $40.8M warrant liability reflected the fair value of equity promises to Sandton. Bitcoin mining operations were being wound down.

2024 (continued): Arkon also defaulted on two Sandton term loans (Tranche A and Tranche C) by failing to make required principal repayments, constituting an event of default under the Credit and Security Agreement. Both tranches were reclassified as current liabilities. Sandton's Nurick later called these an "accounting technicality" — the parties had agreed to extend and roll over, but paperwork was not signed because "everyone was focused on closing the Series A."

April-May 2025: Arkon Energy was formally acquired, completing the corporate restructuring into Nscale. The Hannibal, Ohio facility (originally funded by Sandton's loans) was sold. Nscale's focus shifted entirely to AI infrastructure.

July 2025: Nscale announced Stargate Norway — a 50/50 joint venture with [[Aker ASA|Aker]] to build a 230MW AI data center campus near Narvik, targeting 100,000 [[NVIDIA]] GPUs by end of 2026. [[OpenAI]] was the initial offtaker, making this its first European data center, part of the "OpenAI for Countries" program. Payne had cold-messaged [[Aker ASA|Aker]] CEO Øyvind Eriksen on LinkedIn to propose the partnership — a detail that captures both the audacity and the informality of Nscale's dealmaking. The initial investment was ~$1B for a 20MW first phase, split equally.

September 2025: The breakthrough. [[NVIDIA]] CEO [[Jensen Huang]] announced a £500M (~$667M) equity investment in Nscale at a London press conference, declaring "the UK is going to be an AI superpower" and "we convinced ourselves that Nscale could be a national champion for AI infrastructure in the UK." [[Jensen Huang|Huang]] projected £50B in Nscale revenue over six years. This was part of a broader announcement: NVIDIA and partners (Nscale + [[CoreWeave]]) committed up to £11B (~$15B) to deploy 120,000 Blackwell Ultra GPUs in UK data centers. Nscale simultaneously closed a $1.1B Series B led by [[Aker ASA|Aker]], with [[Dell]], [[Fidelity]], [[Nokia]], NVIDIA, [[Point72]], G Squared, [[Blue Owl Capital|Blue Owl]], and Sandton participating. Implied valuation: ~$3.1B.

October 2025: The [[Microsoft]] anchor deal materialized — contracts covering ~200,000 GB300 GPUs across [[Texas]] (104K, leased from [[Ionic Digital]]), Norway (52K, via Aker-Nscale JV), UK Loughton (23K), and Portugal Sines (12.6K). The cumulative value was reported at $14B initially, later revised to $23B+. A $433M pre-Series C SAFE followed, with Blue Owl, [[Dell]], NVIDIA, and [[Nokia]] participating.

December 2025: Nscale hired Nidhi Chappell as Global President of AI Infrastructure — formerly CVP for Azure AI and HPC Infrastructure at [[Microsoft]], where she led four generations of supercomputers powering [[ChatGPT]]. Hiring from the customer demonstrated both ambition and recruiting pull.

February 2026: A $1.4B delayed-draw term loan closed, led by [[PIMCO]], Blue Owl, and LuminArx Capital Management, with [[Goldman Sachs]] as sole structuring and placement agent. Goldman and [[JPMorgan]] were hired to prepare the IPO.

March 9, 2026: The $2B Series C closed at a $14.6B post-money valuation — more than quadrupling from the Series B six months earlier. [[Aker ASA|Aker]] and 8090 Industries co-led, with [[Citadel]], [[Jane Street]], [[Lenovo]], Linden Advisors, and returning investors. Aker's 50% Stargate Norway JV stake was rolled into Nscale, raising Aker's ownership to ~27.3% of share capital (~23.9% fully diluted). Three board members joined: [[Sheryl Sandberg]] (former [[Meta]] COO), Susan Decker (former [[Yahoo]] president), and [[Nick Clegg]] (former UK Deputy PM, former [[Meta]] global affairs president). The board composition signaled IPO readiness and political connectivity across the US and UK.

March 16, 2026 (GTC): Nscale acquired American Intelligence & Power Corporation's Monarch site in [[West Virginia]] — up to 8GW of power capacity, the largest single-site expansion in the neocloud sector. An LOI with [[Microsoft]] covered 430,000 [[NVIDIA]] Vera Rubin NVL72 GPUs at 1.35GW, with deliveries starting late 2027. A partnership with [[Caterpillar]] provided 2GW of natural gas generation by H1 2028 via behind-the-meter microgrids. Nscale established a new "Energy & Power" division headquartered in [[Houston]]. The Monarch deal fundamentally changed Nscale's energy story — from 100% renewable Nordic hydro to US natural gas at industrial scale.

## Sandton Capital Partners relationship

The FT Alphaville investigation (Louis Ashworth and Euan Healy, March 23 2026) documented the full history of [[Sandton Capital Partners]]' involvement with Nscale, from distressed lender to top-five investor.

Sandton is a NYC-based firm that "specializes in providing solutions for situations that are too difficult for more traditional sources of capital to navigate" — distressed credit. Co-founder Rael Nurick (Harvard Business School 2003, ex-[[Credit Suisse]] leveraged finance, ex-Vestar Capital, ex-GoldenTree, ex-Knighthead Capital) sits on Nscale's board.

Sandton's lending to Arkon Energy totaled ~$110M in cumulative principal across multiple facilities. The Hannibal, Ohio development loans (three tranches) illustrate the terms:

![[nscale-sandton-loan-terms.png]]
*Source: FT Alphaville, from Arkon Energy accounts. Sandton loan terms for the Hannibal development facility.*

| | 2023 Initial Loan | 2023 Amendment | 2024 Tranche |
|---|---|---|---|
| Term loan commitment | $23,700,000 | $4,500,000 | $9,000,000 |
| Principal balance | $26,808,081 | $5,454,545 | $11,340,000 |
| Interest rate | 15.0% | 17.5% | 15% |
| Upfront fee | $237,000 | $45,000 | $90,000 |
| Exit fee | $237,000 | — | $1,575,000 |
| Legal fee | $50,000 | — | — |

The OID structure: across the three Hannibal tranches, Sandton committed $37.2M but the principal balance totaled $43.6M — Arkon owed 17% more than it received, before interest. At 15-17.5% coupon rates plus upfront and exit fees, this was distressed pricing.

Additional Sandton facilities: $30M + €5M for Glomfjord development, ~$10M for the Glomfjord acquisition, ~$19M for earlier debt paydown and equipment.

Loan agreements included warrant issuances: 326,307 warrants in June 2023, 278,473 additional warrants in December 2023, plus a $10M greenshoe option on convertible notes at Series A prices (exercised July 2025). As of March 2024, Sandton held 430,834 warrants — equivalent to ~14%+ of ordinary shares after an anti-dilution adjustment triggered by a June 2024 equity raise. The warrants are exercisable "in connection with an exit event" — the planned IPO.

![[nscale-sandton-waivers.png]]
*Source: FT Alphaville, from Arkon Energy accounts. Loan amendment and waiver details.*

Default and waiver timeline:
- December 2023: Default on Dutch subsidiary loan conditions; Sandton granted waiver
- Shortly after: Interest payments delayed, principal pushed back, additional waiver
- 2024: Failed to make required principal repayments on Tranche A and Tranche C; event of default under Credit and Security Agreement; loans reclassified as current liabilities
- 2025: All loans repaid with Series B proceeds (Sandton participated in the Series B)

Rael Nurick to FT Alphaville: "Calling these defaults simply isn't an accurate reflection of what happened... We had agreed to extend and roll over the loans — that was clear between both parties — but the paperwork just wasn't signed until early 2025 because everyone was focused on closing the Series A. The disclosure in the accounts is really an accounting technicality, nothing more."

The transformation from ~$110M in distressed loans to a top-five equity position in a $14.6B company is the Sandton trade. The Series A converted Sandton from creditor to equity lead; the warrants preserved upside through dilution. Whether this represents sharp investing or a lender who had no choice but to double down is a question the IPO prospectus will need to address.

## NVIDIA relationship

[[NVIDIA]]'s involvement operates on three levels:

Capital: ~£500M ($667M) equity investment announced by Jensen Huang personally (September 2025). NVIDIA participated in the Series B, pre-Series C SAFE, and Series C.

Chip allocation: Nscale has contracted for ~200,000 GB300 GPUs (Microsoft deal) and 430,000 Vera Rubin NVL72 GPUs (Monarch LOI) — 630,000 total, implying significant priority status. Nscale also hosts 4,600 GB300s for NVIDIA's [[DGX Cloud]] marketplace in [[Iceland]].

Credibility: [[Jensen Huang|Huang]]'s personal endorsement ("national champion for AI infrastructure in the UK"), the £50B revenue projection, and the joint UK announcement with [[CoreWeave]] framed Nscale as NVIDIA's preferred European partner. This carries political weight — UK government sovereign AI ambitions, [[Project Stargate|Stargate]] branding, board appointments with political figures.

The [[Neocloud financing]] pattern applies: NVIDIA invests in GPU buyers who then place orders that flow back as NVIDIA revenue. Whether Nscale's contracted capacity represents genuine end-customer demand or NVIDIA-orchestrated demand absorption is the same question that hangs over [[CoreWeave]], [[IREN]], and the broader neocloud cohort.

## IPO prospects

Josh Payne to FT (October 2025): "We have public market ambitions, and execution is an enormous focus of mine." Target: H2 2026. [[Goldman Sachs]] and [[JPMorgan]] hired as advisers (February 2026). Listing venue not confirmed (London vs New York).

The board additions (Sandberg, Decker, Clegg) and C-suite hires (Chappell from Microsoft, Hurwitz from Palantir, Takhtajan from JPMorgan) are classic IPO-readiness signals. [[Sandton Capital Partners|Sandton]]'s warrant shares are exercisable "in connection with an exit event," aligning the earliest financial backer with the IPO timeline.

The prospectus will need to address: the Arkon defaults, customer concentration on [[Microsoft]], the gap between operational and contracted capacity, the shift from renewable to natural gas energy sourcing, and [[NVIDIA]]'s dual role as investor and supplier.
