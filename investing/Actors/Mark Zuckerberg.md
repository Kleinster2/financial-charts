## Zuckerberg discount

[[Robert Armstrong]]'s May 28 2026 [[Financial Times|FT]] column uses Zuckerberg as the case study in founder-control valuation risk. The claim is not that Zuckerberg is a bad CEO; it is that [[Meta]]'s [[Dual-class shares]] leave public shareholders unable to overrule or replace him if capital allocation shifts from high-return ads into lower-confidence metaverse or AI spending.

The article ties the market memory to the [[Meta 2022 collapse]]: Reality Labs / metaverse spending helped turn a core ad compounder into a trust problem, while the [[Year of Efficiency]] repaired the stock but did not remove the governance issue. This becomes the analogy for [[SpaceX IPO 2026]], where [[Elon Musk]] keeps supervoting control over a platform whose public-market story depends on cross-subsidizing launch, satellites, AI compute, and [[Starship]].

*Source: [[Financial Times|FT]], [[Robert Armstrong]], May 28 2026: https://www.ft.com/content/e0485f5a-e50a-4dea-9e42-3dc38d82111b.*
