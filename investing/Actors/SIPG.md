## Yangshan Deep Water Port

SIPG's flagship asset, 30 km offshore on reclaimed islands connected by the 32.5 km Donghai Bridge. Total investment: $20B+.

| Period | Yangshan | Shanghai total | Notes |
|--------|----------|----------------|-------|
| H1 2025 | 14.026M TEU | 27.065M TEU | +7.3% YoY |
| 2024 | 50M+ TEU | — | First port globally |

Yangshan handles >50% of Shanghai's total throughput.

**Expansion:** Xiao Yang Shan (north side) under construction — 11.6M TEU capacity, opens 2026.

Phase IV uses [[ZPMC]] equipment: 28 automated quay cranes, 121 automated RMG cranes, 145 AGVs.

**Aug 2025:** US sanctioned Yang Shan Shengong fuel storage facility (Iranian oil allegations) — caused China fuel futures volatility.

## Competitive Position

[[China]] controls 9 of the world's 10 busiest ports, handling 17B+ tonnes and 330M TEUs in 2024.

Shanghai port's automation gives it efficiency advantages over manual terminals — dual-trolley quay cranes increase loading efficiency by 50%.

## Investment Angle

**Bull case:**
- Dominant position in global shipping chokepoint
- State backing ensures capital access
- Automation leadership drives margin expansion
- 3.4% yield with growth

**Bear case:**
- Geopolitical risk — Western supply chain diversification
- CNY exposure for non-mainland investors
- State control limits shareholder returns
- Tariff wars could reduce volume
