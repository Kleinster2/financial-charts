# IHS CERA

Energy research and consulting firm originally founded as Cambridge Energy Research Associates (CERA) in 1983 by Daniel Yergin and James Rosenfield. Acquired by IHS in 2004, becoming IHS CERA. Subsequent integration through the 2016 IHS Markit merger and then the 2022 S&P Global / IHS Markit combination put CERA's energy-research franchise inside S&P Global, where it continues as the CERAWeek conference brand and the underlying energy research practice. [[Sergey Vakulenko]] worked at IHS CERA as part of his 25-year oil-and-gas consulting career, alongside his roles at [[Gazprom Neft]] and [[Royal Dutch Shell]].

## Why it matters in the vault

CERA is the institutional home of much of the Western energy-research tradition — Yergin's *The Prize* (1991) is the canonical narrative history of the global oil industry, and CERA's analytical frameworks anchor much of the contemporary energy-consulting world. For vault purposes, the IHS CERA reference matters mainly as an analyst-affiliation marker — Vakulenko's tenure there is part of why his Russian-energy analysis carries the credibility it does, sitting alongside his operating-side roles at Gazprom Neft and Shell.

CERAWeek (the annual Houston conference) is also one of the venues where Jeff Currie and other vault-tracked energy analysts publicly stake positions — the March 26, 2026 Currie "turbocharges the energy transition" framing was delivered at CERAWeek.
