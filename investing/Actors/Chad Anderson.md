## Q1 2026 framings

Key analytical positions Anderson advanced in the Apr 26, 2026 Space IQ briefing:

1. **SpaceX as platform owner**: At $1.25T private valuation (~3x cumulative space economy investment since 2009), the market is paying for the platform that owns the next 50 years of orbital infrastructure, not today's business
2. **Capital efficiency contrast**: SpaceX self-funded post-2021 (Starlink revenue) while Blue Origin's ~$27B Bezos founder investment over 25 years produced 39 cumulative flights and 2 New Glenn orbital launches
3. **Orbital DC platform war**: Question shifted from "will it work" to "who owns the rails" within Q1 — [[SpaceX]] (Terrafab), [[Blue Origin]] ([[Project Sunrise]]), [[NVIDIA]] ([[Vera Rubin Space 1]]), [[Google]] ([[Project Suncatcher]])
4. **NASA contractor renegotiation under Isaacman**: [[Boeing Starliner|Starliner]] Type A reclassification + $20B lunar base + [[Lunar Gateway]] pause + [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] restructuring
5. **GeoInt as sleeper**: $3.8B Q1 2026 = 86% of all 2025 GeoInt in a single quarter
6. **Anchor customer thesis**: Project Sunrise architecture points toward "a single institutional anchor customer (a hyperscaler or a government) that makes the whole system viable — same playbook as [[Starshield]]"

The Space Capital lens is bullish — Anderson is a VC raising money to deploy into the space economy, and the framings are designed to bring institutional allocators off the sidelines. Use the data points as authoritative; cross-check the framings against more skeptical sources ([[SemiAnalysis]], [[Deepwater Asset Management]]) before adopting them as load-bearing in your own analysis.
