**Samsonite** — The world's largest luggage company by revenue, with a brand portfolio spanning premium (Tumi), mid-market (Samsonite), and value (American Tourister). Listed in Hong Kong (1910.HK) despite being operationally headquartered in Luxembourg.

---

## Synopsis

Samsonite is a pure play on global travel recovery and premiumization. The company's revenue profile is roughly 40% Americas, 35% Asia, and 25% EMEA, making it one of the few consumer brands with genuine tri-regional exposure. The 2016 acquisition of Tumi for $1.8B was transformative — it shifted the portfolio upmarket and added a high-margin brand that now accounts for roughly a quarter of group revenue. Post-COVID travel recovery lifted the stock from its 2020 lows, but shares remain well below the 2018 peak, reflecting both the Hong Kong listing discount and concerns about Chinese consumer spending.

The Hong Kong listing is itself an analytical puzzle. Samsonite is a Western brand with a Western management team, listed in Asia because that is where the growth was supposed to come from. The listing discount — Hong Kong-listed stocks trade at lower multiples than US or European peers — has prompted periodic speculation about a dual listing or re-domiciling. The company's participation in [[Google]]'s Direct Offers agentic commerce initiative suggests it is exploring AI-driven direct-to-consumer channels, potentially to reduce dependence on wholesale partners and airport retail. For investors, the thesis is simple: a global travel recovery name trading at a geographic discount, with Tumi as the margin engine.
