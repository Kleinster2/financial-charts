**Jim Chanos** — Founder of Kynikos Associates (1985), the most famous dedicated short-selling firm of the modern era. Made his name shorting Enron in 2001 before the collapse. Long-time bear on Chinese real estate and the broader China growth model. Announced in late 2023 that he was closing Kynikos' main short-only funds and converting the firm to an advisory business, citing the difficulty of sustaining a dedicated short strategy in the post-GFC bull market. Cited by [[Muddy Waters|Carson Block]] alongside [[Nate Anderson]] as examples of prominent short-sellers who "threw in the towel."

## Why he matters

Chanos set the template for research-driven institutional short selling before [[Muddy Waters]] or [[Hindenburg Research]] existed. His conversion away from dedicated short-only funds in 2023 — alongside Hindenburg's disbanding (Jan 2025) and [[Andrew Left]]'s July 2024 indictment — maps out the closing of a generation of activist short firms. [[Muddy Waters|Carson Block]] ([[Muddy Waters]], April 2026) frames this as partly structural (short-only is a hard business during sustained vol suppression) and partly cyclical (the passive-flow era makes fundamental shorts less potent).
