# Sinocism

Sinocism is Bill Bishop's China-focused newsletter and source-curation operation, read by investors, policymakers, diplomats, executives, journalists, and academics tracking [[China]]. Its market value is not trade calls; it is early signal on Chinese official-language framing, elite-policy cues, and English/Chinese source threads that often precede broader Western coverage.

## Vault relevance

Sinocism matters because it sits between daily China news flow and policy interpretation. Bishop curates Chinese- and English-language items, translates or points to official material, and flags when [[Beijing]]'s language changes. For the investing vault that is useful when a story is less about one company and more about the ruleset surrounding China exposure: capital controls, tech talent, sanctions, state media, [[Taiwan]], the United Nations, and US-China crisis management.
