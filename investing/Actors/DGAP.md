# DGAP

The German Council on Foreign Relations (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Auswärtige Politik / DGAP) is Germany's principal foreign-policy think tank, founded 1955 in Bonn (now Berlin-headquartered). Independent and nonpartisan; focused on analysis of German foreign policy, European integration, transatlantic relations, and Germany's positioning toward emerging powers. [[Noah Barkin]] (Watching China in Europe substack + Rhodium Group + GMF) is among DGAP's affiliated experts on Germany-China policy.

## Why it matters in the vault

DGAP is the most-cited German foreign-policy institution and the venue where serious German China-policy analysis surfaces ahead of mainstream press coverage. For vault threads on Germany-China economic relations, the EU industrial-policy response to Chinese subsidies, or transatlantic alliance dynamics under [[Friedrich Merz]], DGAP commentary is a high-signal source. Barkin's affiliation is part of why his Watching China in Europe substack carries the analytical depth it does on the Germany-side dimension specifically.
