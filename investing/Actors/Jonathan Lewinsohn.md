# Jonathan Lewinsohn

Co-founder and managing partner of [[Diameter Capital]], a New York-based credit hedge fund. With Scott Goodwin, runs Diameter's liquid and illiquid credit strategies. Frequent Bloomberg commentator on credit market dislocations.

## Apr 22, 2026 — "Lehman-style" private credit redemption framing

Speaking on Bloomberg the week of April 20, 2026, Lewinsohn compared the private-credit redemption pressure to the Lehman-era bank runs:

> *"There is a reckoning threatening. People are trying to withdraw money where they can from private credit and that has created something. If you look at what has gone on in the last three or four months as people have tried to get money out of private credit funds — private credit doesn't have a liability mismatch. We have the opportunity to buy loans being sold."*

The framing matters because it positions Diameter as a *buyer* of forced sales — the liquid-credit side of the trade where gated BDCs and stressed insurers have to sell at distressed prices. This is consistent with [[Diameter Capital]]'s strategy and aligns with the broader [[Distressed assets|Distressed debt]] funds positioning ([[Marblegate Asset Management|Marblegate]], [[Strategic Value Partners|SVP]], [[Davidson Kempner]]) that emerged in early 2026 around the [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]].

*Source: Bloomberg, week of Apr 20, 2026; replayed on Bloomberg Real Yield Apr 24*
