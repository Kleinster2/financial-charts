[[Chime]] — Largest US neobank. NASDAQ: CHYM since June 17, 2025 IPO. ~$7.4B market cap (May 7, 2026 close $20.10). Headquartered San Francisco. Founded 2012 by Chris Britt and Ryan King. Q1 2026 print delivered first GAAP-profitable quarter in company history; the May 7 stock reaction was paradoxically negative (-7.5%) despite the operational milestone — a sell-the-news pattern that pairs with the same-day [[Tapestry]] -8.7% (beat-and-raise) and [[Arm Holdings]] -8.1% (record beat) reactions.

The earlier note framed Chime as still private at $8B; the IPO repriced the company at $11B initial market cap, and the stock has subsequently re-rated downward to a ~$7.4B market cap on member-growth pace concerns — making the post-IPO fade roughly -33% from IPO levels through May 7, 2026.

## IPO and post-IPO trajectory

[[Chime]] priced its IPO June 17, 2025 at an $11B initial market cap. The deal was anchored by the fintech-IPO reopening narrative that ran through 2025 alongside [[Klarna]] / [[StubHub]] / [[Reddit]] / [[Instacart]] precedents. The post-IPO trajectory has been a steady de-rating — partly the typical lock-up / supply pressure that hits all newly-public names, partly the broader fintech / consumer-discretionary multiple compression through Q4 2025 - Q1 2026, partly company-specific concerns around active-member growth pace.

The IPO-to-May-7 chart shape: peaked early at ~$31 within first weeks of trading, drifted lower through H2 2025, sat in the low $20s through Q1 2026, and reset down again on the May 7 Q1-print reaction.

## Q1 2026 earnings (May 6-7, 2026) — first GAAP profit, but stock fell -7.5%

[[Chime]] reported Q1 2026 May 6 after close. Despite hitting the company's first GAAP-profitable quarter as a public entity, CHYM closed -7.5% on May 7 to $20.10 from $21.73 — the largest single-day move in its short public history. The reaction is the cleanest fintech example of the May 7 "beat-and-fade" pattern that also caught [[Tapestry]] -8.7% (Q3 beat + raised guide) and [[Arm Holdings]] -8.1% (record Q4 beat).

FY 2026 revenue guide raised to $2.66-2.69B.

Why the stock fell despite the beat / raise / first profit milestone — three plausible reads (without a definitive single explanation from the call):

1. *Sell-the-news after pre-print run-up.* CHYM had been near its post-IPO trough heading into the print, and the rebound from $20.79 (May 5) to $21.73 (May 6) priced in some of the beat. The May 7 -7.5% gives back the rally with extra.
2. *Member-growth pace.* 700K net adds is solid but below buy-side whisper expectations that had reportedly been closer to 800K-1M based on the [[Chime Prime]] launch tailwind. The 19% YoY growth rate is impressive but decelerating from 2024 levels.
3. *Forward-guide ARPU concerns.* The raised FY revenue guide ($2.66-2.69B) implies a roughly flat ARPU trajectory rather than the upward inflection some analysts had modeled post-Chime-Prime. Margin expansion is real but ARPU compression caps the upside.

The pattern matches the broader May 7 mover-tape paradox: strong fundamentals + sell-the-news multi-week setup + weak forward signal on a sub-headline metric. See [[Tapestry]] and [[Arm Holdings]] same-day for parallel reactions.

For the [[2026 IPO pipeline]] thread, the CHYM trajectory is now the clearest data-point that fintech IPO post-debut performance has been weak — even on a strong fundamental print, the public market is repricing the multiple downward rather than rewarding operational milestones.
