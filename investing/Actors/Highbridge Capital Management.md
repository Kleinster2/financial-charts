Highbridge Capital Management — global alternative investment firm co-founded in 1992 by [[Glenn Dubin]] (CEO) and [[Henry Swieca]] (CIO), seeded with $35M. Named after the 19th-century High Bridge aqueduct connecting Washington Heights to the Bronx. Built from a convertible-arbitrage shop into a multi-strategy platform that peaked near $38B in 2007. [[JPMorgan Chase]] acquired a 55% stake in 2004 and bought the remainder in 2009, fully integrating the firm into J.P. Morgan Asset Management's alternatives division. Today it operates as a JPMorgan unit focused on credit and volatility strategies, with ~$7.5B AUM (April 2025).

## Evolution

- 1992 — founded by [[Glenn Dubin]] and [[Henry Swieca]], $35M seed; began in convertible arbitrage.
- After 1994 losses, diversified into seven discrete strategies; delivered +6.04% during the 1998 [[LTCM]] crisis.
- 2004 — AUM $7.4B; [[JPMorgan Chase]] Asset Management acquired 55% for $1.3B.
- 2007 — peak AUM ~$38B.
- 2009 — JPMorgan bought the remaining stake; both founders fully exited (Swieca later launched [[Talpion Fund Management]]).
- Now a JPMorgan unit, ~$7.5B AUM (April 2025), credit/volatility focused across hedge funds, drawdown vehicles, and co-investments.

## HPS spin-out lineage

The firm's credit arm, Highbridge Principal Strategies, was established in 2007 and became independent in 2016 through a principal/employee buyout led by Scott Kapnick, renaming itself [[HPS Investment Partners]]. [[BlackRock]] acquired HPS for ~$12B, completing the deal July 1, 2025 — creating a combined private-credit franchise of roughly $220B in client assets.
