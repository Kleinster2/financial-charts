**Fanjul Corp** is a private holding company controlled by five Cuban-exile siblings — Alfonso "Alfy," Lillian, José "Pepe," Alexander, and Andres Fanjul — that operates the largest cane sugar empire in the Western Hemisphere through its subsidiaries [[Florida Crystals]] (farming/production) and ASR Group (refining/brands including Domino, C&H, Redpath, Tate & Lyle Sugars). The family is worth ~$4B combined and is the most politically connected agricultural dynasty in the US, running a deliberate dual-party donation strategy (Alfy covers Democrats, Pepe covers Republicans) that has delivered decades of sugar subsidy protection worth an estimated $50-125M/year to the family.

---

## Synopsis

The Fanjul empire is built on three interlocking advantages: scale (190,000+ acres in Palm Beach County, 6.5M tons/year refining capacity globally), political protection (the US sugar program keeps domestic prices ~2x world levels, costing consumers $2.4-4B/year but delivering ~$1.4-2.7B/year to producers), and vertical integration from field to branded consumer product. Florida Crystals handles the upstream — sugarcane, rice, renewable energy from bagasse — while ASR Group handles the downstream, refining and marketing under brands that dominate every major English-speaking market. Central Romana (35% Fanjul-owned) adds Dominican Republic sugar, beef, and the ultra-luxury Casa de Campo resort.

The political machine is the real moat. The family has spent $20M+ in lobbying since 1999 and tens of millions more in campaign contributions. The split-party strategy — Pepe fundraising for Bush, Rubio, and Trump while Alfy bundled for Clinton and Obama — ensures access regardless of who holds power. The payoff is concrete: the 2025 "One Big Beautiful Bill" delivered the first meaningful increase to sugar loan rates in 40 years (raw cane to 24¢/lb). Trump personally brokered the [[Coca-Cola]] cane sugar deal after calling Pepe during his second inauguration. And in March 2025, the Trump administration quietly rescinded a forced-labor import ban on Central Romana sugar that CBP had imposed in 2022.

The risks are reputational and regulatory: Everglades pollution from sugar runoff is a perennial political flashpoint, the Central Romana forced-labor findings are documented by the DOL (16 of 25 workers interviewed experienced forced labor), and the sugar program's consumer cost is a target for free-trade advocates. But the family has survived every challenge for six decades by outspending and out-networking opponents.

## Evolution

The story of Fanjul Corp is the story of how a dispossessed Cuban sugar dynasty rebuilt its empire on American soil — and then fortified it with the most effective political lobbying operation in US agriculture.

- Pre-1959 — Cuban empire: The Fanjul fortune traces to Andres Gomez-Mena, a Spanish immigrant who built a Cuban sugar empire before his death in 1910. His daughter Lillian Rosa married Alfonso Fanjul Sr., who expanded the holdings to 10 sugar mills, 3 distilleries, and extensive Havana real estate. Alfonso Sr. also controlled Czarnikow-Rionda Company in New York and Cuban Trading Company. By the late 1950s, the Fanjul-Gomez-Mena clan was among the wealthiest families in [[Cuba]].

- 1959-1960 — Exile and restart: Castro seized everything. The family fled to New York. Within a year, Alfonso Sr. and other Cuban exiles pooled $640,000 to buy 4,000 acres in Pahokee, Florida, in the muck soil south of Lake Okeechobee — the same subtropical growing conditions they'd left behind. They barged sections of three small sugar mills from Louisiana and reassembled them into the Osceola mill. Production began in 1961 under Osceola Farms Company. The speed of the restart — growing sugar within two years of losing everything — set the template for the family's relentless execution.

- 1960s-1980 — Quiet growth: For two decades the family expanded acreage in the Everglades Agricultural Area while Alfonso Sr. ran the operation. The US sugar program — import quotas and price supports dating to the 1930s — provided a protected market that made Florida cane farming reliably profitable. When Alfonso Sr. died in 1982, eldest son Alfy (then 45) took over as chairman of the renamed Flo-Sun Land Corporation.

- 1984 — The transformative acquisition: Alfy engineered the deal that made the Fanjuls a sugar superpower: purchasing Gulf & Western's sugar businesses in both Florida and the Dominican Republic. This brought Okeelanta Sugar Refinery, 90,000 additional acres of Florida farmland, multiple sugar mills, and — crucially — Central Romana Corporation in the Dominican Republic, the country's largest private employer and producer of ~70% of its sugar. The Gulf & Western deal also included Casa de Campo, the 7,000-acre luxury resort in La Romana that now hosts 2,300+ private villas ($500K-$40M). In one stroke, the Fanjuls went from successful Florida farmers to the largest sugarcane land owners in the state with a Caribbean production base and a trophy resort.

- 1992-2000 — The political machine activates: The brothers formalized what became their signature move: Alfy became co-chairman of Bill Clinton's Florida campaign (1992) while Pepe aligned with the Republican establishment. This wasn't casual giving — it was strategic market access. Alfy hosted both Clintons at his Dominican Republic mansion. Pepe became one of George W. Bush's leading patrons. The family entered an awkward spotlight when phone logs confirmed Clinton spoke to Alfonso Fanjul while Monica Lewinsky was in the Oval Office on President's Day 1996 — the call appeared in the Starr Report. Separately, the family's FAIC Securities subsidiary was caught in the SEC's first Rule G-37 pay-to-play case in municipal bonds ($88K in donations to Dade County politicians, $379M in subsequent bond underwriting), settling for $434,959 in 1996.

- 2001-2010 — Building ASR Group: The family pivoted from pure production to branded refining. ASR (American Sugar Refining) acquired Domino Sugar and its North American refineries from Tate & Lyle in 2001, added Redpath (Canada's largest refinery) in 2007, and took over Tate & Lyle's EU refining operations (UK, Portugal) in 2010. By the end of this run, ASR had 6+ million tons/year refining capacity and dominant brands in the US, Canada, and UK. The Fanjuls now controlled the full value chain: field → mill → refinery → branded consumer product.

- 2020s — Trump era acceleration: The relationship between Pepe and Trump deepened into genuine friendship. Fanjul Corp gave $1M to MAGA PAC plus $413K to the RNC in 2024; total family giving to Trump committees exceeds $7M since 2016. The returns were tangible: the "One Big Beautiful Bill" (signed July 4, 2025) raised sugar loan rates for the first time in 40 years. Trump personally called Pepe from his second inauguration to discuss the [[Coca-Cola]] cane sugar deal. The Trump administration quietly rescinded the CBP forced-labor ban on Central Romana imports in March 2025. And the family donated to the Trump White House Ballroom renovation. On the business side, ASR closed the 130-year-old Yonkers Domino refinery (end 2025, 300 jobs) and restructured around Chalmette and Baltimore with continuous operations.

- March 16, 2026 — White House: Pepe Fanjul attended a White House meeting during the Iran/Strait of Hormuz crisis. Trump mentioned the Fanjul family in a press conference about [[Cuba]]'s future, saying they had expressed a desire to return to the island after 50+ years, calling them "very enterprising and very intelligent people." The Hormuz disruption is incidentally bullish for domestic sugar — Brazilian mills divert cane to ethanol when energy prices spike, and Gulf-routed refined sugar (via Al Khaleej refinery in Dubai) faces transit disruption, both tightening supply in a market where the Fanjuls' domestic production is insulated.

## Controversies

### Everglades
Florida Crystals controls ~200,000 acres in the Everglades Agricultural Area below Lake Okeechobee. Phosphorous runoff from sugar farming is the primary obstacle to Everglades restoration. Lake O is held artificially high to irrigate cane; when levels spike, polluted water is dumped into the St. Lucie and Caloosahatchee estuaries, fueling toxic algae blooms. The sugar lobby initially claimed to support the reservoir plan but fought implementation for two decades.

### Central Romana forced labor
In November 2022, US Customs and Border Protection issued a Withhold Release Order against Central Romana citing forced labor evidence. A 2024 DOL study found 16 of 25 workers interviewed were experiencing forced labor — withheld wages, excessive overtime, abusive conditions. The Trump administration quietly rescinded the ban in March 2025.

### FAIC Securities (1994-1996)
SEC's first Rule G-37 pay-to-play case. $88K in political donations → $379M in bond underwriting. Settled for $434,959 without admitting guilt.
