The U.S. Department of Justice is the federal executive department responsible for enforcement of federal law and administration of justice. In financial markets, the DOJ matters as the lead prosecutor for corporate fraud, antitrust enforcement, and sanctions violations. Its Antitrust Division reviews mergers (alongside the [[FTC]]), while the Criminal Division pursues securities fraud, FCPA violations, and money laundering. The DOJ's ability to bring criminal charges — not just civil penalties like the [[SEC]] — gives it outsized leverage in corporate settlements.

Notable DOJ actions with market impact include the $6B+ [[Allianz]] Structured Alpha fraud settlement (2022), the [[Google]] antitrust rulings (search monopoly found 2024, ad tech trial 2025), the [[Boeing]] 737 MAX criminal fraud agreement, and ongoing enforcement of Russian sanctions. Under AG Merrick Garland (2021-2025), the DOJ prioritized corporate crime accountability, including requiring admission of wrongdoing in settlements. The department's Antitrust Division has been particularly active, challenging major tech and healthcare mergers.
