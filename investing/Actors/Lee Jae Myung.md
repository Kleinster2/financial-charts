Lee Jae Myung is the President of [[South Korea]] (inaugurated 2025), the leader of the Democratic Party of Korea. He pledged to lift the KOSPI to 5,000 during his five-year term by tackling the Korea discount through corporate governance reform.

## Synopsis

Lee's presidency comes after one of the most turbulent periods in South Korean politics — the brief martial law crisis of December 2024, which triggered a constitutional crisis and accelerated the election timeline. His KOSPI 5,000 pledge is an unusually specific market target for a head of state, and the mechanism is the "Korea Discount" reform agenda: improving corporate governance at family-controlled chaebols, increasing dividend payouts, reducing cross-shareholdings, and forcing transparency in related-party transactions. The Korea discount — South Korean equities trading at roughly 0.9x book versus 1.5x+ for Japanese peers — has been a structural feature for decades, driven by chaebol governance opacity.

The investment case for Lee's presidency mirrors the early stages of Japan's "Abenomics" corporate governance push: a political catalyst forcing entrenched industrial groups to return capital. [[Samsung]], [[Hyundai]], [[SK Group]], and [[LG]] all trade at discounts that reflect governance risk rather than business fundamentals. [[Mixo Das]] at [[JP Morgan]] has been tracking the governance reform impact on Korean equities. The risk is execution — previous Korean presidents have promised chaebol reform without delivering, and Lee faces opposition from the chaebol-aligned business establishment. His own legal history (narrowly acquitted of corruption charges in 2024) adds political fragility to the reform agenda.

## Industrial policy — the $576 billion chip drive (Jun 29, 2026)

Lee's signature industrial-policy move is the [[South Korea AI chip investment drive June 2026|$576 billion AI-chip national drive]] unveiled Jun 29, 2026. Framed as a "great leap forward" on a "triple axis" of semiconductors, [[Physical AI|physical AI]], and data centres, it commits [[Samsung]] and [[SK Hynix]] to 800 trillion won ($518.3 billion) for new fabs in the southwest, with a goal of doubling [[DRAM]] output within five years and AI data-centre investment targets of 550 trillion won by 2029 and >1,000 trillion won by 2035.

The plan does double duty as regional-development policy. The southwest (Gwangju, South Jeolla) is Lee's electoral stronghold (85% vote share), and the announcement came as his approval had slid for six weeks to 46.5% ([[Realmeter]]). Lee defended the geography against opposition charges of patronage, pitching the region's abundant, underused power as the answer to the Yongin/Pyeongtaek capacity ceiling. The plan is the industrial-policy companion to his [[Korea discount]] governance-reform agenda — it does not close the governance discount directly, but it reframes Korea as an AI-infrastructure sovereign committing capital at [[CHIPS Act]] scale.

The market read it as oversupply risk: [[Samsung]] −4.86% and [[SK Hynix]] −1.68% on the day ([[Reuters]]-reported), extending the [[Nasdaq semiconductor selloff June 2026|Jun 23 memory-glut de-risk]] thread.
