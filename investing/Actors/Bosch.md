**Bosch** (Robert Bosch GmbH) — World's largest automotive supplier and a diversified German technology conglomerate. Privately held through a unique ownership structure: [[Robert Bosch Stiftung]] (foundation) owns 94% of equity but holds no voting rights; [[Robert Bosch Industrietreuhand KG]] (industrial trust) controls ~93% of voting rights with minimal equity. The Bosch family retains ~6% of capital and ~7% of votes. Headquartered in Gerlingen (near Stuttgart), Germany. Revenue €90.5B (2024). ~417,900 employees across 400+ subsidiaries in 60+ countries.

## Ownership structure

Bosch's ownership is deliberately designed to preserve independence and fund charitable work:

| Entity | Equity stake | Voting rights | Role |
|--------|-------------|---------------|------|
| [[Robert Bosch Stiftung]] (Foundation) | 94% | 0% | Receives dividends; funds charitable programs |
| [[Robert Bosch Industrietreuhand KG]] (Industrial Trust) | ~1% | ~93% | Controls company governance; managed by former executives and eminent public figures |
| Bosch family | ~5% | ~7% | Minority stake; family spokesperson Dr. Christof Bosch sits on supervisory board |

This structure means: the foundation gets the profits, the trust makes the decisions, and the family has symbolic representation. It insulates Bosch from capital market pressures — no quarterly earnings calls, no activist investors, no hostile takeovers. But it also means no external equity capital for large transformations.

## Business segments

### Mobility (~€55.9B, ~62% of revenue)

The dominant segment and Bosch's core identity. Products include:
- Powertrain: fuel injection systems (diesel and gasoline), electric motors, power electronics, eAxles
- Chassis systems: ESP, ABS, brake-by-wire, Vehicle Motion Management
- ADAS / automated driving: radar, cameras, ultrasonic sensors, domain controllers
- Cockpit electronics: displays, connectivity, infotainment
- Software and services: vehicle software platform; targeting >€6B in software/services revenue by ~2030

2024 performance: roughly flat YoY despite declining global auto production. Bosch is the world's #1 Tier 1 auto supplier by revenue, ahead of [[Denso]] (~$47B), [[Continental]] (~€41B), and [[ZF Friedrichshafen]] (~€40B).

### Industrial Technology (~€6.5B, ~7% of revenue)

- Bosch Rexroth: hydraulics, linear motion, assembly technology, factory automation
- Bosch Connected Industry: Industry 4.0 software, IoT platforms

2024: down 13% YoY. Hit hard by weak mechanical engineering markets in Europe, China, and the US. Highly cyclical — tied to capital equipment spending.

### Consumer Goods (~€20.3B, ~22% of revenue)

- BSH Hausgeräte (joint venture with [[Siemens]] — Bosch bought out Siemens' 50% in 2015, now 100% owned): Bosch, Siemens, Gaggenau, Neff, Thermador brands
- Power Tools: Bosch Professional (green/blue line), Dremel

2024: +2% nominal, +3% fx-adjusted. First growth since post-COVID demand pullback. BSH is a global top-3 home appliance maker.

### Energy and Building Technology (~€7.5B, ~8% of revenue)

- Bosch Thermotechnology: heat pumps, boilers, HVAC
- Bosch Building Technologies: fire/security systems (product business sold in 2024; retaining systems integration)
- Planned acquisition: [[Johnson Controls]]-[[Hitachi]] HVAC business for ~$8B — expands US and Asia presence

2024: down 3% YoY. Weak European heating market.

## Funding rounds

Bosch has never raised external funding. Founded in 1886, it has been self-funded throughout its 140-year history. The current ownership structure was established by founder Robert Bosch's will (1942) and formalized in 1964 when the Robert Bosch Stiftung became the majority equity holder.

| Event | Year | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| Founding | 1886 | Robert Bosch opens "Workshop for Precision Mechanics and Electrical Engineering" in Stuttgart |
| Foundation transfer | 1964 | Robert Bosch Stiftung receives 94% equity; Industrietreuhand KG receives voting rights |
| Siemens BSH buyout | 2015 | Bosch acquires [[Siemens]]' 50% stake in BSH Hausgeräte for €3.25B |
| JCI-Hitachi HVAC acquisition | 2024 (announced) | ~$8B acquisition of [[Johnson Controls]]-[[Hitachi]] HVAC business |

## Restructuring and headcount reduction

Bosch announced major layoffs in 2024-2025, primarily in the Mobility division:

| Announcement | Jobs affected | Context |
|-------------|--------------|---------|
| Oct 2024 | 7,000 | Initial announcement; financial targets missed |
| Dec 2024 | +8,000 | Expanded cuts, mostly automotive |
| Sep 2025 | +13,000 | Largest round; €2.5B cost gap identified |

Cumulative: ~22,000 jobs to be cut in Germany, most in automotive. Driven by: slower-than-expected EV transition, underutilized EV production capacity, intense Chinese competitor pricing pressure, and weak European macro. Bosch is the latest casualty of Germany's industrial slowdown alongside [[Volkswagen]], [[Continental]], and [[ZF Friedrichshafen]].

## EV and autonomous driving strategy

### Electromobility

Bosch bet heavily on EVs with multi-billion-euro investments in:
- eAxles: integrated electric drive systems (motor + inverter + transmission)
- Power electronics: silicon carbide (SiC) chips manufactured in-house at Reutlingen fab
- Hydrogen fuel cells: SOFC stacks for commercial vehicles; cryogenic hydrogen pump (testing in US — 600kg/hour, 10-minute truck refueling for 1,000km range)

The EV transition has been slower and more painful than expected. Bosch built capacity for an EV ramp that hasn't fully materialized, leading to underutilization and the restructuring above. Despite this, Hartung maintains electromobility, hydrogen, and sustainable tech remain core growth pillars.

### Autonomous driving and ADAS

Bosch is one of the world's largest ADAS suppliers. Key developments:

- [[WeRide]] partnership (WePilot AiDrive): Joint development of a one-stage end-to-end ADAS solution. WeRide provides the AI/software stack; Bosch provides hardware integration, manufacturing scale, and Tier 1 supply chain access. Targeted for mass production in 2025. This is an L2++ system designed for OEM deployment — Bosch's manufacturing expertise makes it scalable.
- Vehicle Motion Management: Software-defined vehicle dynamics platform enabling brake-by-wire and advanced chassis control
- Radar and sensors: Bosch is a top-3 global automotive radar supplier
- 5,000 AI specialists working across ADAS, manufacturing AI, and product AI

### QuantumScape connection

Bosch had earlier collaboration with [[QuantumScape]] on solid-state battery technology, though the partnership is less prominent than QuantumScape's primary relationship with [[Volkswagen]]/[[PowerCo]]. Bosch's own solid-state battery R&D was scaled back — the company exited its solid-state battery joint venture in 2018, concluding the technology was too capital-intensive for its risk appetite.

## Competitive positioning

Bosch is the undisputed #1 Tier 1 auto supplier by revenue. Competitive landscape:

| Company | Revenue | Key strength | Bosch advantage |
|---------|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Bosch | €90.5B | Breadth + scale | Diversified beyond auto; software + hardware |
| [[Denso]] | ~$47B | Toyota relationship | Bosch is 2x larger; more geographically diversified |
| [[Continental]] | ~€41B | Tires + auto tech | Bosch has no tire business but broader tech portfolio |
| [[ZF Friedrichshafen]] | ~€40B | Transmissions, chassis | ZF is more leveraged (Wabco acquisition debt) |

Bosch's moat: unmatched breadth across powertrain, chassis, electronics, sensors, software, and consumer products. No other Tier 1 spans all these domains. The private ownership structure enables long-term R&D bets that public competitors can't match — but it also means no access to public equity markets for large-scale capital raises.

Vulnerability: Chinese suppliers (especially [[CATL]], [[BYD]]'s vertical integration, and emerging Tier 1s) are increasingly competitive on cost. Bosch's European cost base is a structural disadvantage in a price war.

## Investment relevance

Bosch is not publicly traded, but it matters enormously for investors because:

1. Supply chain bellwether: Bosch's results signal the health of global auto production. Its 2024 weakness confirms the European auto downturn thesis.
2. ADAS/EV capex indicator: Bosch's investment decisions (and retreats) reveal where the smart money in automotive technology is flowing.
3. OEM dependency: Public companies like [[WeRide]], [[QuantumScape]], and sensor makers depend on Bosch as a Tier 1 integration partner. Bosch's partnership validates their technology.
4. Competitive pressure: Bosch's restructuring and cost-cutting affect competitors like [[Continental]] (public: CON.DE) and [[ZF Friedrichshafen]] (private). If Bosch is struggling, the whole Tier 1 sector is struggling.
5. BSH/appliance market: Bosch's Consumer Goods recovery or decline signals trends for [[Whirlpool]], [[Electrolux]], and other appliance makers.

Listed subsidiary: Bosch Limited (BSE: 500530) — Indian listed entity, but represents a small fraction of the group.
