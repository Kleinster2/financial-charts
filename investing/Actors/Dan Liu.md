Dan Liu is the founder and CEO of [[Lovense]], the teledildonics company he started in 2010 after a long-distance relationship ended. Liu studied MS Information Systems at Cardiff University (2002-03) and worked at Ericsson and other telecom firms before pivoting to consumer hardware. Based in Guangzhou, he built Lovense into the dominant brand in app-controlled intimacy devices, with products sold in 100+ countries and a reported user base in the millions.

## Origin story

In 2009, Liu was in a long-distance relationship. It did not last. While lack of sexual intimacy was only one factor, the experience sparked his interest in teledildonics — remote-controlled intimacy devices that could bridge physical distance. He founded Lovense the following year, initially crowdfunding on Indiegogo. The company's early products focused on Bluetooth-enabled devices controllable via smartphone app, a concept that was ahead of its time but found a receptive market as long-distance relationships became more common.
