**GfK** is a German market research firm — founded in 1934, historically Germany's largest consumer analytics provider — acquired by [[NIQ]] (formerly NielsenIQ) in 2023 to create the world's largest consumer intelligence company.

## Synopsis

GfK built its franchise on point-of-sale tracking and consumer panel data across Europe and Asia, becoming the dominant source of retail analytics in markets where [[Nielsen]] (the original Nielsen, pre-split) had weaker coverage. The company tracked what consumers actually bought — not what they said they bought — through electronic POS data from retailers and in-home scanning panels. This data was essential for CPG companies, electronics manufacturers, and retailers making pricing, assortment, and distribution decisions. GfK was publicly listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange until 2023, when [[NIQ]] acquired it for ~EUR 4.2B (including debt) in a deal designed to merge NIQ's Americas dominance with GfK's European and Asian strength.

The merger created a genuine global consumer intelligence monopoly in certain categories: the combined entity covers 90+ markets and claims full-funnel measurement from brand awareness through purchase behavior. For CPG companies, this means one vendor can now tell them how their ads performed, how consumers feel about their brand, and what actually sold — across most of the world. The antitrust risk was minimal because the two firms had complementary geographic footprints rather than overlapping ones. The integration is ongoing as of 2026, with the combined entity operating under the [[NIQ]] brand. The strategic context is that both traditional research firms face pressure from alternative data providers (credit card panels, social media sentiment, retail media networks) that offer faster, cheaper consumer signals without the survey overhead.
