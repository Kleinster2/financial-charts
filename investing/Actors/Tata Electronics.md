**Tata Electronics** — [[Tata Group]] subsidiary and [[Apple]]'s newest major manufacturing partner outside [[China]]. Operates two distinct businesses: a contract iPhone assembler (the India diversification anchor for Apple) and a greenfield advanced-logic fab in [[Gujarat]] ([[Dholera]]) under the India Semiconductor Mission.

Tata Electronics matters because it sits at the intersection of three structural shifts: Apple's China-plus-one diversification into India, India's bid to build a domestic semiconductor stack, and the supply-chain-security question of whether a newly-scaled Indian contract manufacturer can protect the trade secrets of the most secrecy-obsessed company in consumer electronics. The June 2026 [[World Leaks]] ransomware breach — which exposed iPhone 18 Pro supplier lists, component maps, and drop-test photos — made the third question acute rather than theoretical.

## The Gujarat fab

Tata Electronics is building India's first advanced-logic semiconductor fabrication plant in Dholera, [[Gujarat]], a ~$14B project under the [[India Semiconductor Mission]] with technology partner [[PSMC]] (Taiwan's Powerchip Semiconductor Manufacturing Corporation). It is the flagship of India's push to stand up domestic wafer capacity and is the most-cited test case for whether [[India execution gap|India can convert semiconductor policy ambition into operating fabs]]. See [[India execution gap]] and the [[India]] actor note.

## Apple assembly and the India shift

Tata Electronics both supplies parts and assembles iPhones as a contract manufacturer, making it a cornerstone of [[Apple]]'s diversification beyond China and of Prime Minister [[Narendra Modi]]'s push to make India an electronics-manufacturing powerhouse. India is on track to make 26% of the world's iPhones in 2026, up from 6% four years ago ([[Counterpoint]]). The bet has scaled fast: Tata is now described as one of Apple's most important manufacturing partners outside China.

The commercial sensitivity is the backdrop for the breach below. Apple's supplier arrangements — who makes what, where parts are dual-sourced versus sole-sourced — are fiercely protected, and Apple does not disclose them in its public supplier database.

## World Leaks ransomware breach (Jun 2026)

A ransomware group calling itself [[World Leaks]] posted more than 200,000 files stolen from Tata Electronics to the dark web, including sensitive Apple intellectual property. [[Reuters]] reviewed the files; the sequence:

| Date | Development |
|---|---|
| ~Jun 22, 2026 | [[Reuters]] first reported the Tata Electronics cyberbreach, with claims of exposed Apple and [[Tesla]] trade secrets |
| Jun 26, 2026 | Tata restricted internal access to sensitive systems and hired a global consultant for a forensic audit; Apple reported investigating and working with Tata on long-term measures |
| Jun 29, 2026 | [[Reuters]] detailed the iPhone 18 Pro–specific contents: supplier lists, component maps, drop-test photos |

### What the leak contained

At least six files map many components in the unreleased iPhone 18 Pro models to the specific company that supplies them — including chips on the main circuit board and parts of the battery and cameras. In total, the documents detail hundreds of parts on the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro. Several files carried Apple "confidential" watermarks and internal Apple code-names consistent with the iPhone 18 Pro generation. The folder includes photographs of iPhones undergoing drop tests at a Tata plant, dated early 2026, depicting a conventional grey slab handset with a three-rear-camera setup.

The cache also included component design papers for older iPhones, some [[Tesla]] parts (Tesla is another Tata client), and documents from [[TSMC]] and [[Qualcomm]] — both of which make parts used in iPhones.

### Why it matters

The breach exposes both Apple's bargaining leverage and its vulnerabilities: the records show where Apple draws a part from several suppliers and where it relies on just a few. That mapping is precisely the detail Apple does not disclose publicly, and its release could hand rivals, counterfeiters, and Apple's own vendors a view of who makes what. It also cuts at the trust underpinning the Apple–Tata partnership at the exact moment Apple's India expansion depends on Tata as its newest major assembler.

[[AppleInsider]] first reported that iPhone 18 Pro documents were part of the Tata leak. [[Reuters]] has not independently verified the authenticity of the data and could not immediately reach World Leaks for comment; World Leaks has previously claimed responsibility for a [[Nike]] break-in (Jan 2026).

### Timing pressure on Apple

The leak lands at a difficult moment for Apple. The same week, Apple raised iPad and MacBook prices due to soaring memory and storage chip costs (the [[Memory shortage 2025-2026]] running through the supply chain), with analysts expecting iPhone price increases to follow. The iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max are reportedly on track for a September release, so the breach exposes pre-launch product and supplier detail during the final stretch of the cycle.
