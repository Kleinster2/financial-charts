# HKMA

Hong Kong Monetary Authority — de facto central bank of [[Hong Kong]]. Manages the HKD peg to USD (7.75–7.85 band), supervises banks, and since August 2025 regulates fiat-referenced stablecoin issuers under the Stablecoins Ordinance. Also operates the Exchange Fund, Hong Kong's reserve vehicle.

## Stablecoin regime

HKMA is the licensor for fiat-referenced stablecoin issuers in Hong Kong and for any HKD-pegged tokens issued globally. Under the Stablecoins Ordinance effective Aug 1, 2025, license applicants must demonstrate 1:1 high-quality-liquid-asset reserves, segregated custody, par-value redemption, and fit-and-proper senior management. Application window closed Sept 30, 2025; first license batch expected early 2026. HKMA has publicly denied approving any CNH-pegged stablecoin as of April 2026 and has warned against promoters marketing unlicensed products.

HKMA also participates in mBridge, the multi-CBDC cross-border settlement pilot alongside [[PBOC]], Bank of Thailand, and Central Bank of UAE (BIS exited in 2024). See [[Digital Yuan]].
