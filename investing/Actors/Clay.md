---

Clay is a go-to-market (GTM) software company whose platform lets revenue teams build automated prospecting and enrichment workflows by orchestrating dozens of data providers and AI agents in a spreadsheet-like interface. Founded in 2017 by Kareem Amin (with Varun Anand joining as co-founder in 2021), the New York company popularized the idea of a "GTM engineer" — a technical growth role that builds data pipelines rather than manually researching leads. Its AI agent, Claygent, runs open-ended web research (monitoring competitor mentions, even reading satellite imagery to estimate a prospect's scale) to score and personalize outreach. Clay reached a $3.1B valuation (Series C, August 2025) after 6x growth in 2024, with over 10,000 customers including [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], Cursor, Canva, [[Intercom]], and Rippling. It is a customer of [[Parallel Web Systems]], using web-research APIs inside its enrichment pipelines — making Clay a useful read on both AI-GTM adoption and downstream demand for [[Agentic search infrastructure]].

## Product

Clay's interface is a programmable spreadsheet: each row is a company or person, each column an enrichment step that can call one of 150+ data sources (ZoomInfo-style providers, web data, social, financial) or an AI agent. Key elements:

- Waterfall enrichment — query multiple data providers in sequence until a field is filled, paying only for hits, which raises match rates above any single vendor.
- Claygent — an AI research agent that runs open-ended web tasks (competitor monitoring, qualification, custom research) and writes the result back into the table; this is where Clay consumes [[Agentic search infrastructure]] APIs like [[Parallel Web Systems]].
- Credit model — usage-based pricing on enrichment/AI credits, with unlimited seats; revenue scales with workflow volume rather than headcount.

## What to watch

- Category durability — whether "GTM engineer" persists as a role and software category, or [[Salesforce]]/HubSpot absorb the workflow. The core bull/bear hinge.
- Credit-model retention — usage-based revenue is powerful when workflows expand and fragile if customers rationalize spend; net revenue retention is the tell.
- AI-agent depth — whether Claygent stays differentiated as foundation models and [[Agentic search infrastructure]] vendors commoditize web research.
- Data-dependency economics — Clay's margins and reliability ride on 150+ third-party sources; consolidation or price changes upstream flow through.
