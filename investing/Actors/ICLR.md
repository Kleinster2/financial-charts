**ICLR (International Conference on Learning Representations)** — annual academic conference, one of the top three venues for machine learning research alongside NeurIPS and ICML. Founded 2013 by Yoshua Bengio and Yann LeCun. Focuses on representation learning and deep learning.

---

## Why it matters

ICLR is where foundational algorithmic techniques — transformer variants, attention mechanisms, optimization methods, quantization approaches — get formally presented and scrutinized. Pre-prints often circulate on arXiv first, but ICLR presentation is where results get peer-reviewed and productized.

Recent examples directly shaping memory and inference cost structures:
- [[TurboQuant]] (Apr 2026, Rio de Janeiro) — [[Google]] Research's KV-cache compression suite. The public ICLR presentation in late April 2026 is the first opportunity for testing outside [[Google]], and the inflection point at which the near-term memory-demand debate moves from theoretical to empirical.
