# Jeff Dean

Jeff Dean is Chief Scientist at [[Google]], overseeing [[Google DeepMind]] and the company's broader AI research direction. He is one of the most influential engineers in computing history, having co-designed several of the foundational systems that made Google's scale possible: MapReduce (distributed computation), BigTable (distributed storage), and the infrastructure that underpins TensorFlow. He joined Google in 1999 as employee ~20 and has been central to every major technical era of the company.

Dean led Google Brain before its merger with [[DeepMind]] into [[Google DeepMind]] in 2023, and previously served as SVP of Google AI. His role as Chief Scientist makes him the technical counterpart to [[Demis Hassabis]] (CEO of Google DeepMind) — Dean focuses on infrastructure and scaling while Hassabis drives research. Dean is also an active angel investor, with seed-stage investments in AI companies including [[Harvey]] (legal AI). His presence at Google is one reason the company retains top AI talent despite aggressive recruiting from [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], and startups.
