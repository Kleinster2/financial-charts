Paul Sankey is an independent energy analyst and founder of [[Sankey Research]], a subscription-based oil and gas research firm launched in 2020. Senior Advisor to [[Oliver Wyman]]'s Energy and Natural Resources practice since March 2026. Ranked #1 Oil & Gas Analyst by Institutional Investor three times during his sell-side career. Known for high-conviction, contrarian calls including predicting negative oil prices ahead of the April 2020 WTI collapse and the $50/bbl scenario note in 2024. Regular presence on [[Bloomberg]] and [[CNBC]], particularly during the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]].

---

## Core thesis

Bearish on oil prices for 2026 (target $40/bbl once geopolitical premium unwinds), but bullish on oil equities as "severely undervalued." The divergence thesis: the [[Hormuz Permanent Risk Premium|Hormuz crisis]] creates temporary supply disruption, but structural oversupply returns once resolved. Every 100 million barrels returning to market takes ~$4 off the oil price on a short-term basis. The Trump administration's preference for lower energy costs adds political tailwinds to the bear case on crude.

On the Hormuz crisis itself: describes it as the worst supply disruption "possibly since 1973," a "crisis of shipping" with no obvious solution, and warns that any resolution "has to be on Iranian terms."
