**PVH** (NYSE: PVH) is a global apparel company that owns [[Tommy Hilfiger]] and Calvin Klein, two of the most recognized lifestyle brands in fashion. Formerly Phillips-Van Heusen, the company traces its roots to 1881 but was transformed by the acquisitions of Calvin Klein ($430M, 2003) and Tommy Hilfiger ($3B, 2010).

## Synopsis

PVH generates ~$8.65B in annual revenue (FY2024), with over 70% coming from outside the [[United States]] — making it one of the most internationally exposed US-listed apparel names. [[Europe]] is the growth engine, particularly through Tommy Hilfiger's strength in Northern Europe and Calvin Klein's recovery play in premium positioning. The company runs a $5B share repurchase authorization and has been aggressively buying back stock as margins recover under CEO Stefan Larsson's PVH+ Plan, which targets mid-teens operating margins by 2025 through direct-to-consumer channel shift and SKU rationalization.

The risk is structural dependence on two brands in a market where consumer preferences shift fast and promotional pressure from off-price channels compresses margins. Calvin Klein has been the perennial underperformer — cycling through creative directors without finding a stable identity since the Raf Simons exit. European wholesale exposure also means FX headwinds are a recurring drag on reported results. The stock trades at ~10x forward earnings, reflecting skepticism that margin targets are sustainable.
