# Mario Draghi

Italian economist and central banker, one of the most consequential European policymakers of the 2010s-2020s. His vault edge is the bridge between monetary credibility and the EU competitiveness agenda: he stabilised the euro as ECB President and later authored the EU's defining competitiveness diagnosis.

## Why he matters

Two structural reasons. First, "whatever it takes" remains the canonical example of central-bank communication substituting for balance-sheet action — a recurring reference in [[ECB]]/[[Federal Reserve]] credibility discussions. Second, the 2024 [[Mario Draghi report]] made him the author of record for the European-decline-and-revival narrative that drives EU [[Industrial policy]] and that [[Paul Krugman]]/[[Brad DeLong]] contest on measurement grounds (see [[US-Europe productivity paradox]]).
