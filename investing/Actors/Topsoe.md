# Topsoe

Topsoe (formerly Haldor Topsøe) is a [[Denmark]]-based industrial catalysis and engineering company, founded in 1940. Majority-owned by the Topsøe family with Temasek ([[Singapore]]) holding a minority stake since 2022. One of the world's leading catalyst manufacturers for ammonia synthesis, methanol, hydrogen production, and emissions control — and since the late 2010s, the developer of a differentiated [[SOEC]] (solid oxide electrolysis cell) [[Electrolyzers|electrolyzer]] technology that is one of the Western industry's cleanest shots at a technology-led rather than cost-led answer to Chinese alkaline dominance.

## Why Topsoe matters

Topsoe represents a different strategic answer to the [[Second China shock]] than the pure-play Western electrolyzer makers ([[Nel ASA]], [[ITM Power]]) or the conglomerate-embedded businesses ([[Siemens Energy]] Silyzer). Three distinguishing features:

1. **SOEC technology differentiation.** Solid oxide electrolyzers operate at 700-850°C, which (a) makes them structurally more efficient than alkaline or PEM at the cell level (can approach 85-90% system efficiency versus ~70% for alkaline), and (b) lets them co-electrolyze water and CO₂ to produce syngas directly — relevant for synthetic aviation fuels (e-SAF). Chinese competitors have minimal SOEC presence as of 2026.

2. Catalysis-driven systems integration. Topsoe's hydrogen electrolyzer offering is not a standalone box — it plugs into Topsoe's existing ammonia-synthesis and methanol-synthesis catalyst business, so a customer building a green-ammonia or e-SAF plant gets the electrolyzer and the downstream synthesis catalyst stack from the same vendor. This bundled technical-integration position is hard for pure-play electrolyzer competitors to match.

3. Private ownership / patient capital. The Topsøe family + Temasek ownership structure allows for longer-horizon investment through the current subsidy-demand mismatch than public-market pure-plays can sustain. Topsoe is not going to be forced into a distressed capital raise the way [[Plug Power]], [[Nel ASA]], or [[ITM Power]] could be.

## The Herning gigafactory

Topsoe's 500 MW-per-year SOEC gigafactory in Herning, [[Denmark]], has been ramping since 2023 with full nameplate planned by 2026-2027. The facility is one of the largest dedicated SOEC manufacturing sites globally. It is the concrete bet that SOEC cost curves will follow a similar industrialization path to alkaline — which industry analysts view as an open question, because SOEC cell manufacturing involves ceramic processing rather than stamped-metal assembly, and the learning rate for ceramics at volume is historically slower.

The Herning facility has received material EU and Danish-state support; it is emblematic of what European state-capacity response to the [[Second China shock]] looks like when it actually manifests. Whether the bet produces competitive cell pricing at scale will be visible by 2027-2028.
