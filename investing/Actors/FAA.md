The FAA is the US federal agency regulating all aspects of civil aviation, from aircraft certification and airworthiness to air traffic control and pilot licensing. Part of the Department of Transportation, it employs roughly 45,000 people and oversees the world's busiest airspace. For investors, the FAA matters as the gatekeeper for aircraft certification (which directly affects delivery timelines for [[Boeing]], [[Airbus]], and emerging eVTOL manufacturers), drone/UAV integration into national airspace, and the pace of commercial space launch approvals.

The agency's certification process became a focal point after the Boeing 737 MAX crashes (2018-2019), which exposed the FAA's reliance on manufacturer self-certification (the Organization Designation Authorization program). Post-MAX reforms increased FAA oversight of production quality, contributing to Boeing's delivery slowdowns. Administrator Bryan Bedford (2025-) oversees an agency navigating competing pressures: accelerating drone integration (commercial delivery, beyond-visual-line-of-sight operations), processing a backlog of eVTOL type certifications ([[Joby Aviation]], [[Archer Aviation]]), and maintaining safety standards under political pressure to speed up approvals.
