Bucherer is a [[Switzerland|Swiss]] luxury watch and jewelry retailer, founded in 1888 in Lucerne by Carl Friedrich Bucherer. Acquired by [[Rolex]] in August 2023, the deal made Rolex the world's largest vertically integrated watch company — owning both manufacturing and retail distribution. Bucherer operated ~100 stores globally across Europe, the US, and Asia, generating roughly CHF 2.4B in annual revenue pre-acquisition.

The acquisition was driven by succession concerns: the founding Bucherer family, led by the childless third-generation patriarch Jorg Bucherer, had no heirs. Rolex — itself a trust-owned company (Hans Wilsdorf Foundation) — stepped in as the logical buyer, given that Bucherer was already Rolex's largest authorized dealer by volume. The deal reshaped competitive dynamics across the watch retail industry. [[Watches of Switzerland]] shares dropped 29% on the announcement, and independent multi-brand retailers like [[Wempe]] faced the prospect of losing their most important brand relationship as Rolex gained a captive retail channel.
