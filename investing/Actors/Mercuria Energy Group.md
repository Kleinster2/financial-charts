## Funding history

Private; no VC-style funding rounds. Capitalization is founder equity, strategic stake sales, internal earnings, and revolving bank credit — the industry-standard working-capital structure for [[Commodity trading houses]].

## 2026 Iran war impact

Mercuria absorbed meaningful losses in the early days of the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran war]]. Per [[Oliver Wyman]]'s 2026 commodity trading report, the firm — like [[Vitol]] and [[Trafigura]] — entered the war positioned for falling oil, the consensus pre-war view. The Brent spike following the [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure inverted that trade. Losses ran into the billions across the industry in the first weeks before partial reversal by April. Mercuria's metals exposure provided some offsetting strength (metals trading margins rose ~20% industrywide in 2024 per Oliver Wyman), but the oil loss channel dominated.

Unlike [[Vitol]] ($3B), [[Trafigura]] ($3B), and [[Gunvor]] ($1.5B), Mercuria did not publicly announce a new credit facility in March 2026. See [[Commodity trading houses]] for industry working-capital dynamics.
