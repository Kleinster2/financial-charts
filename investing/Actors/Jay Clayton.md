## Career arc

**Sullivan & Cromwell:**
- Corporate and M&A partner
- Represented major Wall Street firms
- Returned between government stints

**SEC Chairman (2017–2020):**
- Appointed by Trump
- Generally considered "pro-Wall Street"
- Good reputation among practitioners
- Focused on capital formation and retail investor access

**Apollo Chairman (2021–2025):**
- Appointed when [[Leon Black]] stepped down (Epstein scandal)
- Served as independent governance layer during CEO transition to [[Marc Rowan]]
- Provided regulatory credibility during Apollo's transformation
- Resigned April 21, 2025 to take SDNY role

**SDNY US Attorney (2025–present):**
- Trump announced nomination Nov 2024
- Assumed interim role Apr 2025 when Senate confirmation stalled
- Judges appointed him Aug 18, 2025 (effective Aug 20)
- Oversees one of the most important federal prosecutor offices (Wall Street fraud, public corruption, national security)

---

## Apollo board transition

When Clayton departed:
- [[Marc Rowan]] elevated to CEO & Chairman (dual role)
- [[Gary Cohn]] appointed Lead Independent Director
- Both effective April 21, 2025

---

## Controversy

Clayton's ties to Apollo (co-founded by Leon Black, a documented Epstein client) became relevant when SDNY handles Epstein-related investigations. Critics question whether he can be impartial given the $500K/year Apollo compensation and personal relationships with Apollo's leadership.
