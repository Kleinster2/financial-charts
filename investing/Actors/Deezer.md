# Deezer

Paris-listed French music streaming service, founded 2007. Competes with [[Spotify]] and [[Apple Music]] but operates at a fraction of their scale. Largest markets are [[France]] and [[Brazil]]. Also offers white-label streaming platforms to companies. Main shareholders: [[Access Industries]] ([[Len Blavatnik]]), [[Orange]] (French telecom), and the Pinault family. CEO [[Alexis Lanternier]].

---

## Synopsis

Deezer finally posted its first net profit in March 2026 — €9mn, nearly two decades after founding. Revenue was €534mn (down 1.4% YoY), with adjusted EBITDA of €10mn versus a €4mn loss the prior year. The turnaround is real but modest: this is a subscale streaming platform that has survived by serving niche markets (France, Brazil, white-label B2B) where the dominant players are less entrenched.

The more interesting story is what Deezer is now fighting. AI-generated music fraud is overwhelming its platform: 60,000+ AI tracks uploaded daily (39% of daily intake), 13mn+ tracks tagged as AI-created in 2025, and 80%+ of AI-generated streams are fraudulent — bots repeatedly playing thousands of cheap AI-made songs to extract royalties. AI tracks are only ~3% of total streams, but 85% of those are fraudulent, versus 8% fraud across the full catalogue. The economics are simple: [[Suno]] and [[Udio]] can generate a full song from a single prompt in seconds, fraudsters upload thousands, bot-play them, and extract "a few euros or dollars" per song that compounds to real money. When detected, Deezer removes fraudulent tracks from the royalty pool, but the detection is a treadmill. [[IFPI]] CEO Victoria Oakley (Mar 19, 2026): *"This is theft... uploading tracks via distributors and deploying armies of bots to create artificial plays."*

The fraud problem is industry-wide — one executive estimated 5-10% of content across all streaming platforms is fraudulent AI. But Deezer is uniquely exposed as a smaller platform with less sophisticated detection infrastructure than [[Spotify]], and its transparency about the problem (publishing exact stats) is both commendable and a competitive vulnerability.

## AI fraud exposure (2025 data)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| AI tracks tagged | 13mn+ |
| AI uploads per day | 60,000+ (~39% of daily intake) |
| AI streams as % of total | ~3% |
| Fraudulent % of AI streams | 85% |
| Fraudulent streams as % of all streams | ~8% |
| Industry-wide fraud estimate | 5-10% of all content |

The fraud mechanics: AI tools ([[Suno]], [[Udio]]) generate thousands of songs from prompts, which are uploaded via distributors, then played by the perpetrator's bot accounts to game algorithms that select tracks for playlists and recommendations. Each fraudulent stream generates only a small payment, but the volume is the point.

[[Alexis Lanternier]] (Mar 2026): *"[Fraudsters] manage to get a few euros or dollars [per song] and then [by] the end of the month, they make real money."*

[[IFPI]] CEO Victoria Oakley (Mar 19, 2026): *"This is theft... uploading tracks via distributors and deploying armies of bots to create artificial plays... [we are] working with law enforcement to prosecute these crimes."*

When tracks are detected as fraudulent, they are removed from the royalty pool shared among legitimate artists and songwriters. Deezer does not know the identity of the fraudsters and is focusing on AI detection to distinguish legitimate from fraudulent AI-generated tracks.
