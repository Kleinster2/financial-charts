# Gen Digital

[[Gen Digital]] (Nasdaq: GEN) is the consumer-cybersecurity and digital-identity company that emerged from the 2019 [[Symantec]] split, formed by merging [[NortonLifeLock]] (the consumer-Symantec spin) with [[Avast]] in 2022. The company operates the Norton, LifeLock, Avast, Avira, AVG, CCleaner, ReputationDefender, and MoneyLion brands. Renamed *Gen Digital* on Nov 7 2022 to consolidate the multi-brand portfolio under a single corporate identity.

The vault's prior [[Symantec]] note covers the enterprise-Symantec lineage that was sold to [[Broadcom]] in 2019 for $10.7B. Gen Digital is the *consumer* descendant of the Symantec business — same brand DNA, different operating model.

## Business model

Gen Digital sells subscription-based consumer cybersecurity and digital-identity protection. The portfolio targets households globally (~500M+ users across brands), with three product clusters:

| Product cluster | Brands | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Cybersecurity / antivirus | [[Norton]], [[Avast]], [[Avira]], [[AVG]] | Endpoint protection, VPN, secure browsing |
| Identity protection | [[LifeLock]], [[ReputationDefender]] | Identity theft monitoring + remediation |
| Financial wellness | [[MoneyLion]] (acquired 2025) | Personal financial management + lending |

The structural pitch is that consumer cybersecurity has shifted from one-off product purchases to recurring digital-life protection — analogous to home/auto insurance for the cybersecurity-identity layer. The Avast and MoneyLion acquisitions broaden the platform from "PC antivirus" to "comprehensive digital-life protection."

### Strategic read

Three structural shifts visible in the May 8 print:

1. **MoneyLion integration delivering** — the 2025 [[MoneyLion]] acquisition added the financial-wellness vertical, and the FY 2026 print reflects the segment's accretive contribution. The trust-based-solutions framework (identity + cybersecurity + financial wellness) is now operationally validated as a coherent platform.
2. **Consumer-cybersecurity as defensive growth** — GEN's +12% reaction lands on the same May 8 session as the SaaS-growth derate cluster ([[Cloudflare]] -23.6%, [[HubSpot]] -19.0%, [[Toast]] -14.7%, [[MercadoLibre]] -12.7%). The dispersion is exactly the [[Ed Yardeni]] Dec 2025 sector-rotation framework playing out: enterprise SaaS multiples compressing, consumer-defensive subscription multiples expanding. GEN is the cleanest single-name confirmation of the sector-rotation thesis on the consumer-cybersecurity side.
3. **EPS +265% YoY reported flag** — the GAAP EPS growth is partly the year-over-year tax-rate normalization and acquisition-cost reset, not pure operating growth. Pro-forma EPS at +15% YoY is the cleaner number. Bear-side analysts will flag the gap, but the directional case for re-rating to higher consumer-defensive multiple is the dominant read.

### Cross-name reads

- **vs [[Symantec]] / [[Broadcom]] enterprise lineage** — Symantec-enterprise was acquired by Broadcom for $10.7B in 2019; Gen Digital (the consumer descendant) now trades at ~$22B market cap. The 2019 split created two valuable independent franchises rather than one diluted conglomerate. Broadcom's Symantec segment now generates ~$2-3B annually in enterprise-cybersecurity revenue; Gen Digital generates $5B FY 2026 in consumer cybersecurity. Combined platform value is ~$25-27B+, materially higher than the pre-2019 Symantec value the split unlocked.
- **vs [[McAfee]] / [[Trend Micro]]** — Gen Digital is the only multi-brand consumer-cybersecurity platform that operates at this scale; McAfee (now private) and Trend Micro (enterprise-leaning) don't have the same consumer breadth.
- **vs [[CrowdStrike]] / [[Palo Alto Networks]] / [[Zscaler]]** — these enterprise-cybersecurity peers trade at much higher multiples but on a different operating model. The cross-reference matters for sector-rotation reads — when enterprise cybersecurity multiples compress, GEN's defensive-consumer model is a hedge.
