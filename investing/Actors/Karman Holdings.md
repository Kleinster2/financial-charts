# Karman Holdings

**Karman Holdings** (NYSE: KRMN) is a defense and space technology company headquartered in Huntington Beach, California. The company designs, tests, manufactures, and sells mission-critical systems for missile defense, hypersonics, tactical missiles, and space launch programs. Backed by [[Trive Capital]] (controlling shareholder at IPO), Karman went public on February 13, 2025, raising $506M at $22/share — priced above the initial $18-$20 range. The stock has surged ~268% from IPO price to ~$81 (Feb 2026).

## Business segments

Karman operates across four end markets:

### 1. Hypersonics & Strategic Missile Defense
The core growth driver. Karman builds payload protection and deployment systems, aerodynamic interstage systems, and thermal protection for hypersonic vehicles and missile defense interceptors. This is the segment most exposed to near-peer threat spending ([[China]], [[Russia]], [[Iran]]).

### 2. Tactical Missiles & Integrated Defense Systems
Warheads, propulsion systems, and integrated missile subsystems for tactical applications. Includes advanced penetrator warheads designed for hardened/deeply buried targets — directly relevant to any potential [[Iran]] strike scenario targeting underground nuclear facilities.

### 3. Space & Launch
Payload fairings, interstage structures, and propulsion components for launch vehicles. Serves both government ([[NASA]], [[Space Force]]) and commercial launch customers.

### 4. Maritime Defense (new, via acquisition)
Entered Jan 2026 via [[Seemann Composites]]/MSC acquisition — advanced composite systems for submarines, UUVs/USVs, and strategic naval surface platforms.

## Key contracts & programs

- **MDA SHIELD IDIQ** (Jan 2026) — Systima facility awarded contract under the Missile Defense Agency's Scalable Homeland Innovative Enterprise Layered Defense program, with a program ceiling of **$151B**. This is the Golden Dome missile shield initiative. Karman is one of multiple awardees competing for task orders.
- **Pentagon $5M nozzle expansion** (Jan 2026) — DoD awarded Systima $5M to expand solid rocket motor nozzle production capacity, signaling supply-chain criticality.
- **Seemann/MSC acquisition** (closed Feb 2026) — expanded into maritime defense composites; increased incremental term loan to fund the deal. Raised FY2025 and FY2026 revenue guidance post-acquisition.

## Iran strike angle

Karman's most direct geopolitical catalyst is a potential US or Israeli strike on Iran's underground nuclear facilities at Fordow and Natanz. The company manufactures advanced penetrator warheads and thermal protection systems designed specifically for hardened, deeply buried targets. The US [[GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrator]] and next-generation bunker-busting systems require exactly the kind of payload protection and warhead engineering Karman specializes in. Any escalation in Iran tensions is a direct catalyst for Karman's order book.
