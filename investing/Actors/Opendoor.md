## Business model

**Traditional iBuying:** Buy homes at discount → light renovation → resell. Profit from spread minus holding costs.

**Opendoor 2.0 pivot:** Moving toward software/AI platform model with less capital at risk.

| Evolution | Model |
|-----------|-------|
| 2020-2022 | Capital-intensive spread business |
| 2023-2024 | Retrenchment, massive losses |
| 2025+ | AI platform, agent partnerships |

New products:
- **Cash Plus:** Hybrid option for sellers
- **Agent partnerships:** Distributed platform model

## Investment implications

**Bull case:**
- New CEO driving turnaround
- High insider ownership (45%) aligns incentives
- AI/platform pivot reduces capital intensity
- Q2 2025 profit shows model can work
- Largest iBuyer scale advantage

**Bear case:**
- Chronic losses, unclear path to sustained profitability
- Rising rates hurt home transaction volume
- Capital-intensive even in "asset-light" pivot
- Significant dilution continues
- Housing market sensitivity
