# Sotheby's

281-year-old auction house taken private by [[Patrick Drahi]] in 2019 for $3.7B after 31 years on NYSE. [[ADQ]] bought 24% stake in Dec 2024. Rebounded in 2025 to $7.1B total sales (+18%), inaugurating the Breuer Building in NYC with a $236.4M Klimt — the 2nd-highest auction price ever. Still carries ~$1.6B debt and has been loss-making (pre-tax -$248M in 2024). Leveraged credit play on [[Art market]] recovery.

**2025 highlights**: +18% total sales; public auction +26%; luxury +23% (fastest-growing segment, now 39% of total); online-only sales declining (-10%, down from 16% peak share to 9%). 459 sales staged (down from 504). 35% of bidders first-time; 4.5 bidders per lot on average.

**Regional split (2025 public sales)**: US 57% (+37% value), Europe 29% (UK 16%, France 8%), Asia 13%, Saudi Arabia/UAE 1%.

## Ownership & Capital

Drahi acquired Sotheby's in June 2019, delisting from NYSE. Loaded the company with debt as part of his typical LBO playbook.

**Dec 2024**: [[ADQ]] (Abu Dhabi sovereign fund) bought 24% for $909M. Proceeds plus Drahi top-up used to:
- Pay down portion of $1.6B debt
- Acquire the Breuer Building as new NYC headquarters

ADQ investment valued Sotheby's at ~$3.8B equity, roughly flat to 2019 purchase despite art market boom/bust cycle.

**Sotheby's Financial Services**: $1.3B+ in art-backed loans; completed $700M securitization. Art lending is a growing, higher-margin business line.

## Art market context

Global [[Art market]] returned to growth in 2025: $59.6B (+4%), after -12% in 2024. Public auctions led the recovery (+9%). $10M+ segment surged 30%. But the market is still 9% below 2022 peak and 7% below 2015 — no secular growth over a decade.

Sotheby's outperformed Christie's ($6.2B, +6%) for the first time in total sales, largely on the strength of the Lauder collection. Luxury segment growing faster than fine art — now 39% of total, reflecting diversification beyond traditional auction. 29% of luxury bidders under 40 vs. 17% in fine art — luxury is the gateway for younger buyers.
