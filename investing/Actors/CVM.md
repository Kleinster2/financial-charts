**CVM** (Comissão de Valores Mobiliários) — [[Brazil]]'s securities and exchange commission, the primary regulator of Brazilian capital markets since its creation in 1976. Modeled on the US [[SEC]], the CVM oversees public company disclosure, market manipulation enforcement, insider trading investigations, and fund regulation. It operates under the Ministry of Finance but with administrative autonomy, and its enforcement actions have become increasingly aggressive in recent years, particularly around related-party transactions and market manipulation schemes.

## Synopsis

The CVM's relevance to this vault centers on its enforcement actions against Brazilian market actors and its role as gatekeeper for capital markets activity. Notable ongoing investigations include the [[Nelson Tanure]] complex (insider trading at [[Gafisa]], market manipulation at [[Ambipar]], Verolme irregularities), and the coordinated probe into [[Banco Master]]'s alleged market manipulation. The regulator's effectiveness is a perennial debate in Brazilian markets — historically seen as under-resourced compared to the SEC, but under recent leadership has shown more willingness to pursue complex cases. Its decisions on enforcement directly affect governance premiums in Brazilian equities.
