## March 18, 2026 — "Misalignment" flash note

Kaneva and team flagged the price disconnect between Atlantic Basin futures and physical Middle Eastern crude. Called the disruption "arguably one of the largest exogenous supply shocks in recent history" — yet Brent trading ~$100, WTI ~$95. The apparent stability is a "mirage jointly created by regional inventory buffers, deviations in benchmark pricing structures, and policy interventions."

| Benchmark | Price (Mar 18) |
|-----------|---------------|
| Brent (futures) | ~$100/bbl |
| WTI (futures) | ~$95/bbl |
| Dubai cash | ~$155/bbl |
| Oman cash | ~$155/bbl |
| Gap (Brent vs Dubai) | ~$55/bbl |

Strait of Hormuz pre-disruption: 11.2M bbl/day crude + 1.4M bbl/day refined products. Global crude loss from blockade: 11-16M barrels/day — 3x the [[Russia]]-Ukraine war disruption.

Shipping timeline analysis: typical GCC-to-Asia voyage 10-15 days; via Suez 25-30 days; via Cape of Good Hope 35-45 days. Most Hormuz crude bound for [[China]], [[India]], [[Japan]], [[South Korea]].

Convergence warning: "If the Strait does not reopen, this divergence is unlikely to persist — Brent and WTI will ultimately reprice higher as Atlantic basin inventories are drawn down."

US and Europe entered 2026 with comfortable commercial inventories; SPR releases further dampening prompt tightness — but this is a buffer, not a solution.

*Source: Rigzone, Mar 18 2026*

## March 5, 2026 — DFC insurance analysis

Analyzed the [[US Development Finance Corporation|DFC]] capacity to backstop tanker insurance through the Strait. Concluded the DFC insurance cap is "too small for the risk."

| DFC metric | Value |
|------------|-------|
| Statutory maximum contingent liability | $205B (raised from $60B through 2031) |
| YE 2025 portfolio exposure | ~$51.5B |
| Remaining headroom | ~$154B |
| Per-project cap | ~$10.25B (5% of total) |
| Private market insurance gap | ~$352B |

Noted precedent for DFC providing political risk insurance for war-related losses (most recently [[Ukraine]]). But exceeding statutory limits "would require a change to the authorizing legislation — i.e., an act of Congress."

[[Scott Bessent]] (Treasury Secretary) responded Mar 6 on Fox Business, calling the analysis "terrible" and "completely flawed" — arguing DFC coverage only applies during transit of the high-risk zone, not the entire voyage, so actual exposure is far lower than $352B.

*Source: Rigzone, Mar 5 2026; Bloomberg, Mar 6 2026*
