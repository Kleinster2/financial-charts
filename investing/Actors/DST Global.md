**DST Global** — Yuri Milner's growth fund. Facebook, [[Airbnb]], Spotify early investor. Roughly $30B deployed across late-stage internet and technology companies.

DST Global matters because it helped institutionalize late-stage growth equity as a separate category between venture capital and private equity: very large minority checks, no board seats, and a willingness to buy into proven internet platforms before IPO. The 2009 Facebook investment gave Yuri Milner credibility as one of the first global investors to treat social networks and consumer internet platforms as long-duration infrastructure rather than speculative websites. Milner's later science-philanthropy role through [[Breakthrough Prize]] is a parallel signal: DST's founder sits at the intersection of venture capital, platform-company wealth, and prestige science funding.

## Investment style

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| No board seats | Passive investor, avoids operational control |
| Large checks | $100M-$1B scale |
| Minority stakes | Non-controlling exposure to category leaders |
| Long-term | Hold through IPO where possible |
| Global | US, [[Europe]], [[Asia]] |

The innovation was to make growth equity feel distinct from both VC and PE: DST paid high absolute valuations for already-proven platforms, but did so before public-market investors could buy them.
