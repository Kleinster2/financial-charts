#stub

[[Mosaic]] is the largest US producer of concentrated phosphate and potash. Operates integrated phosphate mining and fertilizer production in Florida and Louisiana with ~4.5 Mt P2O5/year capacity (7% of world, 60% of North America).

## Brazil plant idling (late 2025)

Mosaic idled phosphate plants in [[Brazil]] at end of 2025 due to high [[Elemental Sulfur|sulfur]] prices, which exceeded $500/Mt — more than 2.5x the historical norm of ~$200/Mt. Sulfur is a critical input for [[Sulfuric Acid]] production, which Mosaic uses to process phosphate rock. Brazil operations (Mosaic Fertilizantes, acquired from Vale in 2018) were the first casualty of the sulfur price spike that preceded the [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure.

*Source: FT (Mar 11, 2026)*

## March 2026 headwinds

MOS -6.5% (-2.3 sigma) on March 13, 2026. Multiple negative catalysts pressuring the fertilizer company:

- Barclays downgraded to Equal Weight from Overweight (March 3)
- DOJ investigating possible fertilizer price fixing (March 4)
- Scotiabank cut price target to $35 from $36
- Brazil plant idling from sulfur costs (see above)

Combination of regulatory pressure, input cost surge, and analyst downgrades weighing on the largest US phosphate producer.
