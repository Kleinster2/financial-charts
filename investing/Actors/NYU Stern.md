# NYU Stern

**NYU Stern** School of Business is the graduate business school at [[New York University]], consistently ranked among the top-10 US MBA programs with particular strength in finance and quantitative research.

## Synopsis

Stern's location in Greenwich Village places it at the intersection of Wall Street and the NYC tech ecosystem, producing a disproportionate share of finance professionals — from sell-side analysts to hedge fund managers to fintech founders. The school's finance faculty is arguably the deepest in the world, anchored by Aswath Damodaran (the definitive academic voice on valuation) and formerly Nouriel Roubini (who built his "Dr. Doom" brand partly through his Stern platform before launching Atlas Capital). The MBA program graduates ~400 students annually, with finance and consulting absorbing the majority.

For the investing vault, Stern matters as an institutional node — its alumni network surfaces repeatedly in Wall Street research, hedge fund launches, and fintech ventures. [[John DiFucci]], whose [[Guggenheim Securities]] software coverage is heavily referenced in the vault, holds a Stern MBA in Finance (1991-1993). The school also produces closely watched research through its Volatility Institute (tracking systemic risk) and its Salomon Center for the Study of Financial Institutions. Damodaran's public valuations of companies like [[Tesla]], [[Uber]], and [[SpaceX]] frequently move market narratives, functioning as an independent check on sell-side price targets.
