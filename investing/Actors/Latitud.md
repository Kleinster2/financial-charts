**Latitud** — Pre-seed fund and accelerator infrastructure focused on Latin America, headquartered in São Paulo and backed by [[a16z]], NFX, and QED, with LP capital from the founders of [[Nubank]] and [[Mercado Libre]]. Founded in 2020 by Brian Requarth (ex-VivaReal CEO), Gina Gotthilf (ex-Duolingo VP Growth), and Yuri Danichenko (ex-Brazilian B2B CTO). Investment focus: SaaS, fintech, marketplaces, proptech, with a 2025 tilt toward AI-angled deals and LatAm founders building US-headquartered companies. Functions as both a YC-style program for LatAm founders and a distribution layer for North American capital reaching the region. See [[Startup accelerators]] for landscape context and [[Brazil]] for the regional macro frame.

## Strategic position

The dominant accelerator-format channel for North American capital reaching LatAm founders. Distinct from [[Endeavor]] (which is global scale-up stage, not pre-seed) and from local Brazilian programs like ACE Startups and Cubo Itaú (which are more programmatic, less equity-driven). The a16z + NFX backing is the differentiator — Latitud is the accelerator that gives a Brazilian SaaS founder a credible Sand Hill Road network without leaving the region.

## Leadership

Three co-founders, founded 2020:

| Co-founder | Role | Prior |
|------------|------|-------|
| Brian Requarth | General Partner, public face | Co-founder + CEO of [[VivaReal]] (Brazilian proptech, sold to OLX Brasil for $600M); author of *Viva the Entrepreneur* |
| Gina Gotthilf | Co-founder, head of community | Former VP of Growth at Duolingo, scaled the company from 3M to 200M users |
| Yuri Danichenko | Co-founder, technology lead | Former CTO at a leading Brazilian B2B tech platform |

The founder mix is deliberate: a returning operator with a Brazilian unicorn exit (Requarth), a globally-tested growth executive (Gotthilf), and a domestic engineering leader (Danichenko). The team composition mirrors the kind of cross-border DNA Latitud is built to fund.

## Brian Requarth

- Co-founded VivaReal (Brazilian real estate platform). VivaReal merged with ZAP Imóveis in 2017 to form Grupo ZAP, with Requarth as chairman; Grupo ZAP was then acquired by OLX Brasil for $600M in 2020 — one of the larger Brazilian internet exits of that period
- Author, *Viva the Entrepreneur*
- Active angel investor across LatAm
