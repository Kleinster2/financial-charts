# IDG Capital

IDG Capital is one of [[China]]'s oldest and largest venture/growth equity firms, founded in 1992 as a joint venture between US-based International Data Group and Chinese government partners. With ~$20B+ in AUM, it was the pioneer of institutional venture capital in China, deploying capital before most Western VCs had offices in the country. IDG backed early rounds in [[Baidu]], [[Tencent]], [[Xiaomi]], and [[SenseTime]], establishing a track record that shaped the Chinese tech ecosystem.

The firm's US origins gave it a unique dual-market perspective, but over time the China operations became the dominant franchise. After IDG founder Patrick McGovern died in 2014, the Chinese management team (led by Hugo Shong and Zhou Quan) navigated a complex restructuring, ultimately buying out the US parent's stake. IDG Capital now operates independently, investing across VC, growth equity, and PE in technology, consumer, healthcare, and advanced manufacturing. It remains one of the few Chinese VC firms with a continuous investment record spanning the entire arc of China's tech industry — from the early internet era through mobile and into AI.
