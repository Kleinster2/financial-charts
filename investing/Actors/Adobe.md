## CEO transition (March 12, 2026)

[[Shantanu Narayen]] stepping down as CEO after 18 years, marking the end of an era for [[Adobe]]. His 100th earnings call (Q1 FY26) was his last as CEO. He will remain as board chair while Adobe conducts a search for his successor.

Leadership transition comes at a critical juncture: [[Adobe]] shares down -23% YTD 2026 amid broader software selloff driven by AI disruption fears. Stock dropped -7.6% on March 13 (-2.8 sigma move) despite record Q1 revenue of $6.4B (+12% YoY).

Timing raises questions about whether legacy software companies can successfully navigate the AI transition at the top. Narayen led [[Adobe]]'s transformation from boxed software to cloud subscriptions (2012-2017) and AI integration with [[Firefly]], but the market remains skeptical of Adobe's positioning against AI-native competitors.

FY2026 guidance was also tepid/lackluster alongside the announcement, suggesting management sees headwinds ahead. No successor named yet — board conducting external search.
