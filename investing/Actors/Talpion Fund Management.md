Talpion Fund Management — single family office in New York managing the personal fortune of [[Henry Swieca]], co-founder and former CIO of [[Highbridge Capital Management]]. Founded 2010 after Swieca's full exit from Highbridge ([[JPMorgan Chase]] bought the remaining stake in 2009). The name derives from the Biblical Hebrew "Talpiot" (castle turret). Began filing Form 13F in 2011.

## Mandate

Four asset classes:

1. Public securities — fundamental long-term plus short-term volatility across equities, credit, relative value, and macro
2. Real estate — multifamily residential in NYC, with in-house design and construction
3. Private equity
4. Venture capital — via QB1 Ventures

## Seeding model

Adopted the family-office seeding model from [[Stanley Druckenmiller]]. In 2012 seeded [[Marc Majzner]]'s [[Clearline Capital]] with $50M (plus $20M from the Swieca family); Clearline operates from Talpion offices on Talpion infrastructure, with Swieca taking a profit share. Clearline returned 26%+ net in its first year.
