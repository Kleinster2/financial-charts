#actor #government #energy

**Doug Burgum** — 55th US Secretary of the Interior (2025–present) and also chairs the newly created National Energy Council. Former Governor of North Dakota (2016–2024). Former tech entrepreneur — mortgaged farmland to invest in Great Plains Software, grew it to 2,000 employees, sold to [[Microsoft]] for $1.1B in 2001. Stanford MBA.

Called the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] the *"conclave of Silicon Valley."*

## Significance

Burgum controls federal land — which means energy permitting, mineral extraction rights, and increasingly, the physical footprint for AI data centers and critical mineral supply chains. His dual role (Interior + Energy Council chair) gives him unusual power over the energy-AI nexus. His tech background (Great Plains Software/Microsoft) makes him fluent in both energy and technology policy.

His presence at the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] reflects the convergence of energy, AI, and defense tech in the Trump administration's agenda.

## Producer call urging more drilling (April 2026)

Burgum and Energy Secretary [[Chris Wright]] held a call with oil and gas executives in mid-April 2026 to encourage higher production, per FT (Findlay, Apr 23 2026). The call followed sustained crude prices near $100 driven by the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran conflict]] and ahead of the 2026 midterm elections where affordability has emerged as a major issue.

US oil and gas rig counts remained flat following the call, and the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas|Dallas Fed]] Q1 2026 Energy Survey showed 73% of producers expecting ≤+0.25 mb/d of 2026 production growth. Direct administration pressure failed to translate to drilling activity — see [[Shale supply discipline]].

*Source: FT, Stephanie Findlay (Apr 23 2026).*
