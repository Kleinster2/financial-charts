# GenLoop

Indian AI startup building personalized LLM infrastructure — fine-tuning and deployment tools that enable enterprises to create custom language models from proprietary data. Shortlisted for the [[India AI Impact Summit 2026|IndiaAI Mission]]'s indigenous foundation model development program.

---

## Synopsis

GenLoop operates in the LLM customization layer, providing tools for enterprises to fine-tune and deploy language models on their own data without requiring deep ML engineering teams. This positions the company in a rapidly growing market segment — as the initial excitement over general-purpose chatbots gives way to enterprise-specific deployments, companies need infrastructure to turn foundation models into domain-specific tools that understand their jargon, processes, and data. GenLoop's approach targets the gap between raw model providers ([[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], open-source) and end-user enterprise needs.

The IndiaAI Mission shortlisting adds a sovereign AI dimension to GenLoop's story. India's government is actively seeking domestic alternatives to Western AI infrastructure, driven by concerns about data sovereignty, Hindi/Indic language support, and strategic dependence on US cloud providers. GenLoop's infrastructure play — enabling Indian enterprises and government agencies to run customized models locally — aligns directly with this policy priority. The challenge is familiar for Indian AI startups: the domestic market's willingness to pay for AI infrastructure is still developing, while competing globally means going head-to-head with well-funded US and Chinese alternatives. The IndiaAI Mission's compute access and funding support partially de-risk the early growth phase.
