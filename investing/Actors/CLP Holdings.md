# CLP Holdings

Hong Kong-based electric utility and one of Asia's largest investor-owned power companies. Generates, transmits, and distributes electricity across [[Hong Kong]], mainland [[China]], [[India]], [[Australia]], and Southeast Asia. [[EWH]] holding (~3.6%).

## Synopsis

CLP Holdings operates as a diversified pan-Asian power utility with a generation portfolio spanning coal, gas, nuclear, wind, and solar across five markets. The company supplies roughly 80% of [[Hong Kong]]'s electricity through its subsidiary CLP Power, giving it a regulated-monopoly income base that funds expansion into deregulated markets. Total installed capacity exceeds 20 GW, with the group targeting 7.5 GW of renewable capacity by 2030 as part of its decarbonization roadmap. Revenue runs ~HK$80B (~$10B) annually.

The investment case rests on CLP's regulated Hong Kong earnings (Scheme of Control guarantees 8% return on assets) providing visibility, while growth comes from Australian and mainland Chinese renewables. The risk is concentration: Hong Kong accounts for over half of earnings, and the city's post-2020 political trajectory has introduced governance uncertainty. India operations have been a persistent drag, with CLP exiting its coal-fired Jhajjar plant in 2022. The stock trades at ~15x earnings with a 4%+ dividend yield, positioning it as a defensive yield play within the [[EWH]] basket rather than a growth story.
