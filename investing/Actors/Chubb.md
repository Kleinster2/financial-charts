## DFC Gulf maritime reinsurance (March 2026)

On March 11, 2026, [[US Development Finance Corporation|DFC]] named Chubb as lead underwriter for its $20B maritime reinsurance facility covering Gulf shipping during the [[Iran conflict economic disruption|Iran conflict]]. [[Evan Greenberg]]: *"Chubb is proud to lead and manage this program in partnership with the United States Government."* Chubb will issue policies for eligible vessels transiting the [[Strait of Hormuz]], with "several American insurance companies" providing reinsurance alongside. Initial focus: hull & machinery and cargo coverage for war risk.

This is a significant mandate — Chubb brings decades of political risk and maritime insurance expertise, but the scale is unprecedented. [[JPMorgan]] estimates 329 vessels in the Gulf need ~$352B total coverage, far exceeding DFC's $205B statutory cap.
