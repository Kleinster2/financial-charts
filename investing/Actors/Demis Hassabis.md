## Angel investing portfolio

Hassabis's personal angel investments form a structural pattern: he invests in companies founded by ex-DeepMind colleagues and in adjacent frontier-AI labs that compete with [[Google]]'s in-house effort. The position list emerged piecemeal through 2025-2026, culminating in the [[Financial Times]] disclosure (May 19 2026) that he was a previously-undisclosed early angel in [[Anthropic]] — Google's largest external AI competitor and a company in which [[Google]] separately holds billions of equity.

| Company | Founder(s) | Hassabis tie | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Anthropic]] | [[Dario Amodei]] et al. | Early angel — undisclosed until FT May 2026 | Anthropic CEO considers Hassabis a role model (per FT) |
| [[Inflection AI]] | [[Mustafa Suleyman]] (DeepMind co-founder) | Angel | Suleyman now leads [[Microsoft AI]] after Inflection acquihire (Mar 2024) |
| [[Ineffable Intelligence]] | [[David Silver]] (DeepMind VP RL) | Angel; [[Google]] also corp-invested in seed | Apr 2026 $1.1B seed at $5.1B post |
| [[Entrepreneurs First]] | [[Matt Clifford]] | Angel | Talent-incubator that has produced multiple AI founders |

The structural read: Hassabis is simultaneously running [[Google]]'s in-house frontier AI lab and personally backing competitors. The position is apparently sanctioned within Google; the Anthropic angel stake long predates [[Sundar Pichai]]'s 2023 reorganisation that merged DeepMind with Google Brain.

[[Matt Clifford]] (FT, May 2026): "Demis has a huge amount of soft power at Google and beyond ... He was a pioneer before it was obvious what AI was, and the availability of capital for his protégés is pretty striking. That's a big driver of what has happened."

## Role in the [[DeepMind diaspora]]

The DeepMind diaspora is the network of 112+ alumni-founded startups that have raised over $14bn since 2021 (per [[PitchBook]]/[[Dealroom]] via FT). Hassabis's structural role in that network has three dimensions:

- Founder of the parent lab. The DeepMind culture he built — research-first, RL-heavy, "moonshot" project orientation — is the institutional precondition for the diaspora. Alumni carry it into new companies.
- Hub investor. The angel positions above mean he is personally exposed to the network's upside. The [[PayPal mafia]] structural analog is [[Peter Thiel]] / [[Founders Fund]].
- Visible mentor. [[Dario Amodei]] citing Hassabis as a role model is the explicit version of a pattern that operates more quietly across the network — DeepMind alumni invoke his framing in their own founder narratives.

He has not himself left [[Google]] to found a separate company (unlike [[Mustafa Suleyman]] or [[David Silver]]). The diaspora pattern remains conditional on his staying.
