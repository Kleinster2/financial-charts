# Frontline

World's largest VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier) tanker company. NYSE: FRO. Based in [[Cyprus]], incorporated in [[Bermuda]]. Fleet of ~80 vessels. Major beneficiary of [[Strait of Hormuz]] tanker rate spikes — rates quadrupled to >$400K/day by Mar 2026. Co-owner of TFG Marine bunkering JV with [[Trafigura]] and [[Golden Ocean Group]]. Founded by [[John Fredriksen]].

## CEO commentary on imaginary rates (Mar 2026)

[[Lars Barstad]], Frontline CEO, used the firm's March 4, 2026 conference call to flag the structural issue with [[Baltic Exchange]] benchmarks during the Hormuz closure: *"The TD3C index, and indexes in general, have much wider market implications than they used to."* The point is that panellist-assessed freight indices — now the settlement reference for cleared FFAs, the [[BWET]] ETF, and physical charter contracts — can diverge from actual market transactability during disruptions without any of those instruments adjusting. When the benchmark becomes an [[Imaginary freight rate|imaginary rate]], the downstream financial instruments inherit the imaginary nature. Frontline itself, as a physical operator, books revenue against fixtures that do occur — [[Atlantic basin]] voyages, [[Yanbu]] loadings, and rare risk-accepted Hormuz transits — which may diverge materially from TD3C-published levels.
