# Chris Liddell

New Zealand-born finance executive and political operator who served as Deputy Chief of Staff in the first [[Donald Trump]] administration and joined the [[Anthropic]] board in February 2026 as a political bridge-building move.

## Synopsis

Chris Liddell's career spans corporate finance, government service, and now AI governance. He served as CFO of [[Microsoft]] (2005-2009) and [[General Motors]] (2010-2013) before entering the Trump White House as Assistant to the President and Director of Strategic Initiatives in 2017, later rising to Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy Coordination. His White House role focused on regulatory modernization and government efficiency -- less visible than economic or national security roles but deeply connected to the operational levers of executive power.

The [[Anthropic]] board appointment (February 2026) is the more interesting signal. Anthropic brought Liddell on as AI companies face intensifying scrutiny from both the Pentagon and Congress over AI safety, defense applications, and China competition. Liddell's Trump administration credentials provide a direct line to Republican power centers at a time when AI regulation is becoming a partisan battlefield. For [[Anthropic]], the appointment hedges against the risk that a Trump-aligned regulatory environment could disadvantage companies perceived as aligned with Democratic priorities (safety-focused AI labs have faced criticism from some Republican quarters as overly cautious). Liddell's finance background ([[Microsoft]], [[General Motors]] CFO) also brings corporate governance depth to a board that is navigating rapid scaling toward a potential IPO.
