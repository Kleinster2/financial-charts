# Skype

Video and voice calling platform that pioneered consumer VoIP. Acquired by [[Microsoft]] in 2011 for $8.5B -- at the time one of the largest tech acquisitions ever. Originally a [[DFJ]] portfolio company, founded in 2003 by Niklas Zennstrom and Janus Friis (who also created Kazaa), with core technology built by Estonian developers Ahti Heinla, Priit Kasesalu, and Jaan Tallinn.

Skype had ~300 million monthly users at its peak and was synonymous with internet calling. [[Microsoft]] struggled to integrate it, initially maintaining it as a standalone product before gradually absorbing its features into [[Microsoft Teams]]. The Teams pivot accelerated during COVID-19, and Microsoft announced Skype's shutdown in May 2025, completing the migration. The $8.5B acquisition is widely cited as a cautionary tale of overpaying for a consumer platform that could not defend its moat against free alternatives ([[FaceTime]], [[WhatsApp]], [[Zoom]]).
