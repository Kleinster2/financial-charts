**Zhang Lei** is the founder and chairman of [[Hillhouse]], one of Asia's largest investment firms with ~$100B+ in AUM. His foundational bet — a $20M investment in [[JD.com]] that returned billions — was pitched to his Yale professor [[David Swensen]] and became the template for Hillhouse's long-duration, high-conviction approach to Chinese technology and consumer companies.

## Synopsis

Zhang Lei's investment philosophy is rooted in a specific reading of China's development trajectory: that infrastructure buildout (logistics, internet, healthcare) would create category winners worth holding for decades, not trading. His Yale MBA thesis-era pitch to David Swensen's endowment resulted in the $20M seed check for [[JD.com]] when [[Richard Liu]]'s e-commerce company was a fraction of its eventual ~$37B market cap. That single bet validated Hillhouse's model and gave Zhang Lei permanent credibility in both Chinese tech circles and Western institutional capital markets. Hillhouse subsequently took major positions in [[Tencent]], [[Meituan]], and [[PDD Holdings]], among others.

The firm's evolution from a Yale endowment-backed fund to a permanent-capital platform managing over $100B reflects both Zhang Lei's investment acumen and his navigational skill in an increasingly hostile regulatory environment. The 2021-2022 Chinese tech crackdown ([[Xi Jinping]]'s common prosperity campaign) hit Hillhouse's concentrated portfolio hard — PDD, Meituan, and other holdings saw steep drawdowns. Zhang Lei responded by diversifying into healthcare, manufacturing, and Southeast Asian markets, and by relocating Hillhouse's operational center to [[Singapore]]. His book, *The Value* (2020), became a Chinese bestseller and articulates his long-term compounding thesis, though critics note the book arrived at the peak of the very cycle it celebrated.
