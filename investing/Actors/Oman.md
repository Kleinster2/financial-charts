**Oman** — Sultanate on the southeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula, bordering the [[UAE]], [[Saudi Arabia]], and [[Yemen]]. Shares control of the [[Strait of Hormuz]] with [[Iran]] — the Omani coast forms the southern shore of the strait's narrowest point. A mid-tier oil producer (~1M bpd) and the namesake of the [[Dubai crude|Dubai/Oman]] benchmark that prices most crude bound for [[Asia]].

## Strategic significance

### Strait of Hormuz geography

Oman's Musandam Peninsula forms the southern side of the Strait of Hormuz. The navigable shipping lanes pass through Omani territorial waters. This gives Oman a geographic stake in any strait closure or reopening operation — the US Navy's instructions to commercial ships at the start of the March 2026 crisis referenced a 30-nautical-mile exclusion zone that includes Omani waters.

### Dubai/Oman benchmark

The [[Dubai crude|Dubai/Oman]] crude benchmark is the reference price for most Middle Eastern crude sold to Asia. During the March 2026 crisis, Dubai/Oman hit $166/bbl (March 23, per [[Nadia Martin Wiggen]], [[Stellen Capital]]) — the highest of any major crude benchmark globally. Only oil out of Oman was "reliably or semi-reliably loading" because Omani ports, while struck, remained partially operational.

### Neutral broker tradition

Oman has historically maintained diplomatic relationships across regional divides — friendly with [[Iran]], aligned with the GCC, and hosting US military facilities. This positioning has made it a quiet back-channel for negotiations (the US-Iran nuclear talks that led to the 2015 JCPOA used Omani mediation).
