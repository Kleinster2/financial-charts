#actor #retail #home-improvement #usa #public

[[Lowe's]] — Second-largest US home-improvement retailer behind [[Home Depot]]. NYSE: LOW. ~$83B revenue (FY25). Headquartered Mooresville, North Carolina. CEO [[Marvin Ellison]] since 2018.

The investing-vault relevance is the housing-cycle transmission. [[Lowe's]] revenue is roughly 75% DIY consumer + 25% Pro contractor, which makes it more cyclically exposed to housing turnover than [[Home Depot]] (which has roughly 50/50 DIY/Pro mix) but less exposed to commercial / new-construction volume. The May 7 [[Whirlpool]] guide-cut commentary on housing-cycle weakness is the cleanest leading-indicator read on Lowe's Q1 2026 print and the rest of the home-improvement channel.

## Why Lowe's matters

Lowe's is the cleaner DIY-consumer pulse read in the home-improvement channel. The cross-name pairings:
- [[Whirlpool]] / [[Maytag]] / [[KitchenAid]] / [[GE Appliances]] — major appliance category sold through the Lowe's channel
- [[Home Depot]] — primary competitor, more Pro-contractor exposed
- [[Floor & Decor]] — flooring-specific competitor, premium positioning
- [[Tractor Supply]] — rural / agricultural-overlap competitor
- [[Best Buy]] — adjacent appliance / electronics channel

Q1 2026 reading from [[Whirlpool]]'s May 7 commentary — that demand was "weak appliance demand reminiscent of recession levels" — is a leading indicator for Lowe's Q1 reporting period (Lowe's fiscal Q1 ends late April). Lowe's typically reports late May; the [[Whirlpool]] / [[Home Depot]] (when reported) Q1 read sets the bar for what to expect.
