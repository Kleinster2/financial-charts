#actor #regulator #uk #finance

**Financial Conduct Authority** — the UK's conduct regulator for banks, insurers, asset managers, exchanges, and consumer-finance firms. Its job is market integrity, consumer protection, and operational resilience across the parts of finance that interact directly with customers and markets.

## Supercharged Sandbox — AI experimentation environment (2026)

In late 2025 the FCA partnered with [[NVIDIA]] to enhance its existing Digital Sandbox (run on infrastructure provided by NayaOne) into a "Supercharged Sandbox" — a regulator-supplied environment where fintechs can experiment with frontier AI models under direct supervision. The novel design choice is that the FCA itself provides the compute and the data, lowering the cost barrier for early-stage firms and keeping the experimentation inside the regulatory perimeter rather than on external clouds.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Compute | GPU-accelerated virtual machines (NVIDIA H100 and A100 Tensor Core) |
| Software | NVIDIA AI Enterprise suite — RAG and MLOps tooling |
| Infrastructure provider | NayaOne (Digital Sandbox base) |
| Data | FCA-curated financial datasets |
| Use-case interest | Agentic payments, compliance agents, customer-interaction/service agents |
| Showcase event | 28–29 January 2026, firms present AI-enabled solutions to regulators + industry |
| Second intake opened | 5 May 2026 |

The second cohort was opened on May 5 2026 and is being scaled to "more firms with support from NayaOne." The pattern is a regulator deliberately closing the [AI adoption gap between regulators and the firms they supervise](AI%20in%20financial%20services.md#regulator-ai-adoption-gap) — by operating the testing environment itself, the FCA accumulates first-hand exposure to how the technology behaves in financial-services workflows.

Jessica Rusu, FCA Chief Data, Information and Intelligence Officer, framed the design intent: *"The idea is to have an environment where [innovators] come in and test and bring their solutions to life in a safe and responsible way."* (FT, Tett, May 8 2026.)

This sits alongside the FCA's [pro-innovation regulatory stance](AI%20regulation.md#uk--pro-innovation-framework) and is the most concrete UK example of a regulator using state-supplied infrastructure to compress the supervised-deployment cycle for AI in finance.

*Source: [FCA Supercharged Sandbox](https://www.fca.org.uk/firms/innovation/supercharged-sandbox); [FT, Tett, May 8 2026](https://www.ft.com/content/c0aec3de-b553-4089-b5d3-074c5b83be57).*

## Anthropic Mythos review (Apr 2026)

Reuters reported on April 12 that the FCA was in urgent talks with the [[Bank of England]] and the [[UK National Cyber Security Centre]] after [[Anthropic]]'s [[Claude Mythos|Claude Mythos Preview]] was described as capable of identifying major vulnerabilities across widely used software. The significance is that frontier-model cyber risk was being handled as a financial-system resilience issue, not just a technology-sector story.

Major UK banks, insurers, and exchanges were expected to be briefed within a fortnight.

The UK coordination mirrored a parallel US move two days earlier — on April 10, US Treasury Secretary [[Scott Bessent]] and Fed Chair [[Jerome Powell]] convened an emergency meeting with US bank CEOs at Treasury to discuss the same Mythos cyber-risk capability. See [[Claude Mythos]] for the full US sequence.
