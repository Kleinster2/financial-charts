**Las Vegas Sands** — Asia-focused casino operator and the most valuable US-listed gaming company (~$37B market cap). Owns 74.8% of [[Sands China]] (1928.HK). Operates [[Marina Bay Sands]] in Singapore and five Macau properties. Exited Las Vegas entirely in 2022 by selling the Venetian/Palazzo to [[Apollo]] and [[VICI Properties]] for $6.25B. Controlled by the [[Adelson family]] (~54% stake).

## Synopsis

Las Vegas Sands made the most consequential strategic bet in casino history: exit the market the company is named after and go all-in on Asia. The February 2022 sale of the Venetian/Palazzo/Sands Expo for $6.25B (to Apollo for operations, VICI for real estate) left LVS with two assets — [[Marina Bay Sands]] in Singapore and five Macau properties via [[Sands China]]. The bet is paying off: FY2025 revenue hit $13.0B (approaching the $13.7B pre-COVID peak), and MBS posted what CEO Rob Goldstein called "the greatest quarter in the history of casino hotels" — Q4 2025 EBITDA of $806M from a single property.

Marina Bay Sands is the crown jewel and arguably the most profitable casino on Earth. It delivered $5.59B revenue and $2.92B adjusted property EBITDA in FY2025 on a 52% margin — 43% of LVS revenue but 56% of property EBITDA. MBS commands ~72% of Singapore's casino market vs. [[Genting]]'s [[Resorts World Sentosa]] at ~28%. Singapore's regulatory moat is near-absolute: 30-year exclusivity licenses, and the regulator recently rewarded MBS with a 3-year renewal (maximum) while penalizing RWS with only 2 years for "unsatisfactory" tourism performance. The $8B MBS expansion ("IR2") broke ground July 2025 targeting 2031 completion — 570 luxury suites, a 15,000-seat arena, and its own SkyPark.

The family dynamic matters. Sheldon Adelson's death in January 2021 left his wife [[Miriam Adelson]] with ~54% control. She sold ~$2B in LVS stock in 2023 to fund the [[Dallas Mavericks]] purchase. Son-in-law [[Patrick Dumont]] became Chairman & CEO on March 1, 2026 — the first leadership transition beyond Adelson's hand-picked successor Goldstein. The Adelson political legacy (Republican mega-donor, $500M+ to campaigns) continues through Miriam, who gave $100-120M to Trump's 2024 campaign.

## Evolution

The story of Las Vegas Sands is the story of a trade-show promoter who saw casino gaming as a global growth business decades before anyone else — and bet the company on Asia twice, nearly going bankrupt both times, creating the most profitable casino empire in the world.

- **1979-1995**: [[Sheldon Adelson]] co-founded COMDEX, the computer trade show, after realizing on a flight that no trade show served computer dealers. First show at the MGM Grand with 4,000 delegates. By 1987 it was the largest trade show in Las Vegas. Adelson sold COMDEX to [[SoftBank]] in 1995 for $862M (his share >$500M). He'd already bought the Sands Hotel and Casino for $110M in 1988 and built the 1.2M sq ft Sands Expo, the largest privately owned convention facility in the US. A 1991 honeymoon in Venice with Miriam inspired the mega-resort concept that would define his career.

- **1997-1999**: Razed the original Sands Hotel and spent $1.5B building The Venetian Las Vegas. Opened May 3, 1999. The concept — convention-integrated luxury casino resort — was revolutionary and became the template for every integrated resort built since.

- **2004**: Sands Macao opened in May — China's first Las Vegas-style casino. IPO'd on NYSE in December. Adelson recognized before any other American operator that Macau's opening to foreign concessionaires (2002) represented the largest gaming opportunity in history. Within two years, Macau surpassed Las Vegas in gaming revenue.

- **2007**: The $2.4B Venetian Macao opened on what Adelson coined the "Cotai Strip." The stock hit its all-time high of $91.58 on October 2, 2007.

- **2008-2009**: The financial crisis nearly destroyed the company. The stock crashed from $91 to under $2. LVS was burning cash, construction projects were half-finished, and the company teetered on bankruptcy. Adelson poured in personal funds to keep it alive. This was the second time he bet the company — the first was building the Venetian Vegas — and the second time he survived.

- **2010**: [[Marina Bay Sands]] opened in Singapore at ~$5.5B cost. The economics were transformative: a single property generating billions in annual EBITDA. MBS established that regulatory moats (Singapore's two-casino limit) plus ultra-luxury positioning could produce casino economics far superior to Las Vegas's open-market competition.

- **2012-2019**: Peak era. Parisian Macao opened (2016). Pre-COVID peak: FY2019 revenue $13.74B, operating income $3.70B, net income $2.70B ($3.50/sh). The Adelson model — convention-integrated luxury in regulatory moat markets — was the most profitable casino strategy ever devised.

- **2020**: COVID devastated the business. Revenue collapsed 73.7% to $3.61B. Net loss $2.18B. Q2 2020 revenue down 97%.

- **2021**: Sheldon Adelson died January 11 at 87 from non-Hodgkin lymphoma. Rob Goldstein became CEO. In March, LVS announced the $6.25B sale of the Venetian/Palazzo/Sands Expo — the property that started it all — to Apollo ($2.25B for operations) and VICI ($4.0B for real estate). The pivot to Asia-only was complete.

- **2022-2025**: Aggressive buybacks: ~96M shares repurchased for $4.5B since Q4 2023. Sands China stake increased from ~70% to 74.8%, approaching the 75% Hong Kong float cap. MBS expansion ("IR2") groundbreaking in July 2025 — S$10.3B (~$8B), up from the original $3.3B estimate. The Londoner Macao renovation drove standout results (+34.9% Q4 revenue YoY).

- **2026**: Patrick Dumont (Adelson son-in-law, married to Miriam's daughter Sivan Ochshorn) became Chairman & CEO on March 1, 2026 — first leadership transition beyond the founder's direct orbit. 5-year contract through March 2031, base salary $2.5M.

## Properties

### Singapore

**Marina Bay Sands** — FY2025 revenue $5.59B, adjusted property EBITDA $2.92B (+42.4% YoY). ~72% Singapore casino market share. Casino license renewed for 3 years (maximum) in April 2025. The $8B IR2 expansion: 570 luxury suites, 110,000 sq ft MICE space, 15,000-seat arena, its own SkyPark. Architect: Safdie Architects (same as original). Target completion: 2031. Financed by S$12B (~$9B) loan secured early 2025.

## Political connections

Sheldon Adelson was the Republican Party's largest individual donor for a decade. $500M+ to Republican campaigns and super PACs (2010-2020). $424M+ to Trump and Republicans since 2016. Strong pro-Israel advocacy — credited with pushing the US Embassy move to Jerusalem.

[[Miriam Adelson]] continues the political engagement: $100-120M to Trump's 2024 campaign via Preserve America PAC. Third-largest Trump donor. Credited with influencing the appointment of [[Marco Rubio]] as Secretary of State and the nomination of [[Mike Huckabee]] as Israel ambassador.
