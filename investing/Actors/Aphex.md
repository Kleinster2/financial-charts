# Aphex

Construction technology startup focused on project planning and scheduling software for infrastructure and heavy civil construction. Originally a UK-based company, Aphex targets the $12T+ global construction industry's persistent productivity problem — a sector that has seen virtually no productivity improvement in decades despite massive digitization efforts elsewhere.

## Synopsis

Aphex develops cloud-based construction planning tools designed to replace or augment traditional project scheduling software (Primavera P6, Microsoft Project) with more collaborative, visually intuitive interfaces. The target market is large-scale infrastructure projects — highways, tunnels, rail, energy facilities — where project planning complexity creates cascading delays and cost overruns. The construction industry's productivity gap is well-documented: McKinsey estimated that construction productivity has been flat or declining for 20+ years while manufacturing productivity roughly doubled, making it one of the last major industries ripe for software disruption.

The construction tech landscape has attracted significant venture capital (Procore's $10B+ IPO in 2021 validated the category), but most investment has focused on project management and field operations rather than planning and scheduling. Aphex's niche — the pre-construction and scheduling phase — is where the highest-leverage interventions exist: a bad plan cascades into months of delays, while good planning tools can compress timelines and reduce rework. The challenge for any construction tech startup is adoption: construction project managers are notoriously resistant to new tools, and the industry's fragmented structure (thousands of subcontractors, varying standards across regions) makes scale difficult.
