## Career

### The Limited (1963-1982)

- **1963:** Aunt lent him $5,000, matched by bank loan. Opened first store "The Limited" at Kingsdale Shopping Center, Upper Arlington, Ohio. Named for its focused inventory — moderately priced skirts, sweaters, shirts
- Father had refused to adjust inventory at parents' store "Leslie's" → Wexner struck out on his own
- Second store opened August 1964; parents closed their store and joined him
- **1969:** Took Limited Brands public (NYSE: LTD)
- **1970s:** Aggressive expansion — 100th store by 1976
- **1978:** Took on significant debt to acquire Mast Industries (importer) — key competitive advantage over rivals
- Mentor: A. Alfred Taubman (shopping mall developer) — partnered on many deals

**Victoria's Secret** was the crown jewel — acquired from founder Roy Raymond for essentially nothing, transformed into a global brand through supermodel marketing and the annual fashion show (overseen by Ed Razek). By peak, VS was worth tens of billions.

### L Brands era (1995-2020)

The company renamed to **L Brands** and progressively shed non-core chains, focusing on Victoria's Secret and Bath & Body Works:

- **2012:** CNN Money named Wexner longest-serving Fortune 500 CEO
- **2015:** Harvard Business Review Top 100 Best Performing CEOs (#11)
- **2016:** HBR Top 100 (#34)
- **Feb 2020:** Stepped down as CEO, transitioned to chair emeritus
- **2021:** Victoria's Secret sold/spun off; company renamed **Bath & Body Works, Inc.**

Epstein was Wexner's only known wealth management client initially. Epstein ran his business from a house Wexner owned. The relationship gave Epstein access to Wexner's social network, foundations, and the Victoria's Secret brand as cover.

### Southern Air Transport connection

In the 1990s, Wexner and Epstein were involved in relocating **Southern Air Transport** — a CIA front organization with ties to Iran-Contra — from Miami to Columbus. Southern Air transported goods for Wexner's businesses. In 1996, Customs found hidden cocaine on one of the planes. Southern Air shut down in 1998 after receiving federal relocation aid.

## Philanthropy

Major donor to Ohio State University (Wexner Center for the Arts, Wexner Medical Center). Wexner Foundation focused on Jewish leadership development.

### Mega Group

In 1991, co-founded the **Mega Group** with Charles Bronfman — a club of wealthy pro-Israel businessmen. The group employed Frank Luntz (2003) to mobilize Israel support. Reported contacts with Mossad; described as a "vehicle for influence operations in the United States."
