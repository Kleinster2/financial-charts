#actor #energy #oil #cartel

**OPEC** — [[Oil]] cartel controlling ~40% of global production. OPEC+ includes [[Russia]] and allies (~55% of global). Sets production quotas to manage prices.

---

## Why OPEC matters

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Members | 12 countries |
| Production | ~27M bpd (OPEC), ~42M bpd (OPEC+) |
| Reserves | ~80% of global proven reserves |
| Headquarters | Vienna, Austria |

## How OPEC moves markets

| Action | [[Oil]] price impact |
|--------|------------------|
| Production cuts | Prices rise |
| Production increases | Prices fall |
| Quota cheating | Undermines cuts |
| Spare capacity signals | Caps upside |

**Saudi role:** Swing producer with 2-[[3M]] bpd spare capacity. Can flood or restrict market.

---

## Venezuela context

**Venezuela in OPEC:**
- Founding member (1960)
- Largest reserves (303B barrels)
- Production collapsed: [[3M]] bpd (2000s) → ~1M bpd (now)
- Effectively suspended due to non-compliance

**Post-Maduro implications:**
- If US rebuilds Venezuela production → more global supply
- Could pressure OPEC to cut elsewhere
- Venezuela unlikely to rejoin cartel under US control
- Net bearish for oil prices long-term

See [[Venezuela oil rebuild]] and [[Monroe Doctrine]].

## OPEC+ dynamics

**Saudi-[[Russia]] alliance (2016+):**
- Coordinates cuts between OPEC and non-OPEC
- Fragile — competing interests
- [[Russia]] often cheats on quotas
- Ukraine war strained but didn't break alliance

**Tensions:**
- UAE wants higher quota
- Iraq chronic overproducer
- African members struggling
- US shale complicates cuts (fills any gap)

## Investment implications

| Scenario | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| OPEC cuts hold | [[Oil]] prices supported, energy stocks up |
| OPEC cuts fail | Prices fall, high-cost producers hurt |
| Venezuela returns | More supply, bearish prices |
| Saudi-[[Russia]] split | Price volatility |

**Key indicator:** Monthly OPEC+ meetings and quota compliance data.

## Iran conflict response (Mar 2026)

OPEC+ decided to unwind only 206K bbl/day during the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran conflict]] — widely seen as inadequate given the scale of disruption (~20M+ bbl/day of [[Strait of Hormuz]] transit halted). [[FGE]] energy consultant Iman Nasseri (CNN, Mar 2): the response is "insignificant and basically not showing the level of concern or worry from the OPEC member countries." Market expecting emergency IEA stock release from OECD countries instead. If prices continue to reflect supply pressure, [[FGE]] believes both OPEC and OECD can bring new supply — but the signal so far is that OPEC is not stepping in.

## Price targets

OPEC broadly targets $80-90 Brent to:
- Fund Saudi Vision 2030
- Support Russian budget
- Not destroy demand

Current prices (~$70s-80s) reflecting Iran conflict supply disruption.
