## Q1 FY2027 beat — AI + buyback repricing

The May 21, 2026 Q1 FY2027 print showed low-single-digit growth but unusually strong profitability and capital return:

| Metric | Q1 FY27 | YoY / context |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Revenue | $1.239B | +5.5%; above high end of guide |
| Enterprise revenue | $755.7M | +7.2% |
| GAAP operating margin | 25.1% | +450 bp |
| Non-GAAP operating margin | 41.1% | +130 bp |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $1.55 | vs $1.43 in Q1 FY26 |
| Free cash flow | $500.5M | vs $463.4M |
| Repurchase authorization | +$1.0B | Incremental to $625M remaining |
| May 22 stock move | +9.2% to $105.64 | +3.0σ beta-adjusted vs SPY |

The market read-through is that Zoom does not need rapid top-line acceleration to create equity value if it can hold 40%+ non-GAAP operating margins, turn AI Companion / My Notes into paid usage, and retire stock aggressively from a cash-rich balance sheet. The risk is that enterprise NDR is still only 99%, so AI needs to become a renewal / expansion driver rather than just a feature wrapper.

Sources: [Zoom Q1 FY2027 press release](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/21/3299724/0/en/zoom-communications-reports-financial-results-for-the-first-quarter-of-fiscal-year-2027.html) (May 21 2026); [Zoom May 21 2026 SEC 8-K exhibit](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1585521/000158552126000069/zm-20260430ex991.htm); Yahoo chart data for May 22 close and beta-adjusted move.
