**Verint** — Customer engagement and workforce optimization software company. Being taken private by [[Thoma Bravo]] for approximately $2B (announced 2025), a deal that places it squarely in the SaaS-disruption conversation as AI reshapes the customer experience automation market.

---

## Synopsis

Verint built its business on the unsexy but essential infrastructure of customer service operations: workforce management, quality monitoring, speech analytics, and engagement data platforms used by enterprise contact centers. The company was originally spun out of Comverse Technology in 2002 and has spent two decades consolidating point solutions into an integrated CX (customer experience) platform. Revenue has been roughly flat in the $900M-$1B range, reflecting the maturity of its core market — contact center software is not a growth category when the underlying thesis is that AI will automate the contact center itself.

The [[Thoma Bravo]] acquisition at ~$2B is a bet that Verint's data assets and customer relationships are worth more than the public market gives credit for. Thoma Bravo's playbook in enterprise software — take private, cut costs, consolidate with adjacent portfolio companies, relist or sell at a higher multiple — is well-established. The SaaSpocalypse angle is relevant: Verint's seat-based licensing for human agents is precisely the model threatened by AI-driven automation (fewer agents = fewer seats = lower revenue). The counter-argument is that Verint can pivot to selling the AI automation layer itself, using its installed base as a distribution channel. Whether Thoma Bravo executes that pivot or simply extracts margin through cost cuts will determine whether this is a value creation story or a financial engineering one.
