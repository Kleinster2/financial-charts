# Saronic

Austin-based autonomous surface vessel (ASV) company building unmanned warships for the US Navy. Founded 2022 by a former Navy SEAL and three co-founders, now valued at $9.25B after a $1.75B Series D (March 31, 2026). The fastest-scaling defense startup in American history by funding trajectory — $55M to $9.25B in under three years.

---

## Synopsis

Saronic is doing to naval warfare what [[Anduril]] is doing to air defense: building a product company that ships hardware fast, rather than a services company that bids on decade-long contracts. The company designs, manufactures, and operates autonomous surface vessels across seven hull classes — from the 6-foot Spyglass swarm boat to the 180-foot Marauder cargo ship — all controlled through a single C2 platform called Echelon. The thesis is that the US Navy needs hundreds of cheap unmanned vessels to contest the Pacific against [[China]]'s 370-ship fleet, and traditional shipyards cannot build them fast enough.

The numbers reflect that urgency. A $392M Navy OTA (December 2025) for Corsair production — prototype to production contract in under 12 months — was followed by a $1.75B Series D at $9.25B (March 2026) led by [[Kleiner Perkins]]. Revenue grew from ~$12.5M (2024) to ~$200M (2025), a 1,500% increase driven by the Navy contract and manufacturing ramp. The company acquired Gulf Craft, a 60-year-old Louisiana shipyard, and is investing $300M to expand it, while planning "Port Alpha," a next-generation shipyard for mass production of hundreds of unmanned vessels annually.

The product stack is the differentiator. Most defense USV competitors offer one or two hull sizes; Saronic offers seven, from expendable swarm boats to a 180-foot cargo vessel carrying 40 metric tons. Echelon ties them into a unified fleet — mission planning, sensor fusion, real-time coordination, GPS/comms-denied operations. The [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] and [[Iran]] mine warfare underscored the case: the US decommissioned its minesweepers and now relies on untested LCS drone systems (see [[Iran conflict mine warfare]]). Saronic's vessels are designed for exactly this contested-waters mission set.

Risk is execution at scale. Building boats is harder than building drones — the jump from prototype to hundreds of hulls annually requires shipyard capacity that doesn't yet exist. The company has ~1,300 employees across Austin, Franklin (LA), and San Diego, but Port Alpha is still in planning. Valuation at $9.25B on ~$200M revenue is 46x trailing sales — pricing in a defense prime trajectory that remains unproven.

## Products

### Autonomous surface vessels

| Vessel | Length | Range | Top speed | Payload | Role |
|--------|--------|-------|-----------|---------|------|
| Spyglass | 6 ft | 30 nm | 20 kts | 40 lbs | Tactical swarm, expendable |
| Cutlass | 14 ft | 300 nm | 20 kts | 200 lbs | Loitering munitions, ISR, C2 relay |
| Corsair | 24 ft | 1,000 nm | 35+ kts | 1,000 lbs | Modular; anchor of $392M Navy OTA (~$1.2M/unit) |
| Mirage | 40 ft | 2,000+ nm | 35+ kts | 2,000 lbs | Unveiled Apr 2025 |
| Cipher | 60 ft | 3,000+ nm | 35+ kts | 10,000 lbs | Unveiled Apr 2025 |
| Marauder | 180 ft | 3,500-4,100 nm | 12 kts cruise / 25 kts sprint | 40 metric tons (150 MT max load) | Hosts 4x40' or 8x20' ISO containers. First hull completed in ~9 months |

Estimated unit prices: Spyglass ~$400K, Cutlass ~$800K, Corsair ~$1.2M. Larger vessels not disclosed.

### Echelon (C2 platform)

Proprietary command-and-control software: unified mission planning, sensor data fusion, real-time fleet management. Functional in GPS/comms-denied environments. Analogous to [[Anduril]]'s Lattice but maritime-native.
