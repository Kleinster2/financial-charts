#actor #company #cybersecurity #private

**HackerOne** — Bug-bounty and vulnerability-disclosure platform, founded 2012, San Francisco-headquartered. The market-leading platform connecting independent security researchers to corporate vulnerability programmes via managed bounty payouts + triage. Customer base spans the largest US-government and enterprise security programmes, including [[Goldman Sachs]], [[Google]], and the US Department of Defense's Hack the Pentagon programme. Privately held; raised through Series E rounds with [[Benchmark]], NEA, and others. CEO Kara Sprague (since 2023).

The investing-vault relevance routes through HackerOne's position at the centre of the [[AI cybersecurity disruption basket|cybersecurity disruption thesis]] and the canonical home case for the [[AI producer-evaluator asymmetry|producer-evaluator asymmetry framework]]. The platform's published submission data (March 2026: +76% YoY volume, 25% legitimate-vulnerability rate steady) is the cleanest empirical signature of AI breaking the producer/evaluator balance in markets that depended on input cost as the implicit filter.

## May 2026 — AI submission flood

Per the [Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/dbec4441-02dc-4053-8500-85677973d324) (Jamie John, May 17, 2026):

- Submissions +76% YoY March 2026
- Legitimate-vulnerability rate steady at 25%
- HackerOne CEO Kara Sprague: "introduced new agentic validation capabilities" this year to "help organisations manage high volumes of findings, such as those generated by models like [[Claude Mythos|Mythos]]"
- Sprague flagged seeing more recent "higher quality" AI-assisted reports — "not a strong reason to say we don't want them" given AI is helping researchers spot more flaws

The volume / legitimate-rate divergence is the cleanest empirical diagnostic of [[AI producer-evaluator asymmetry]]. The defensive adaptation (agentic validation triage) is the canonical example of the rebuild-gate-with-AI equilibrium.
