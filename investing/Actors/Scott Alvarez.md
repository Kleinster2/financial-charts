# Scott Alvarez

Former General Counsel of the [[Federal Reserve]]. Long-serving Fed lawyer who shaped the legal framework around emergency lending and crisis-era programs. Now a senior advisor on bank governance and a frequent Bloomberg commentator on Fed-related legal questions.

## Apr 24, 2026 — view on the Pirro statement

On Bloomberg Real Yield (Apr 24, 2026) following [[Jeanine Pirro]]'s announcement that the DOJ was closing the [[Jerome Powell|Powell]] investigation, Alvarez argued the closure smelled of transparency and finality *up to the last sentence* — Pirro's reservation of the right to reopen is what determines whether anything else means anything. *"That will determine whether the remainder of it really means anything."*

On Powell's options: best outcome would be discussions with Pirro's office for a final close with no reopen path, but Alvarez was skeptical that's available. He explicitly advised against requesting a presidential pardon: *"There is nothing wrong that has been done so no reason for that."*

On Powell potentially staying as Governor through January 2028: real possibility — partly to help [[Kevin Warsh]] mend fences with FOMC members Warsh has previously criticized, partly as protection against a pretextual reopen. Either outcome turns on the [[Lisa Cook]] Supreme Court case for-cause-removal ruling, which determines what real protection any sitting governor has.

*Source: Bloomberg Real Yield, Apr 24, 2026 — Scarlet Fu interview*
