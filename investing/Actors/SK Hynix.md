**SK Hynix** — Korean memory manufacturer, leader in [[HBM]] (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI.

SK Hynix is the purest listed lever on the AI memory stack. The company sits at the center of the 2026 Korea equity story because its economics are no longer driven only by generic DRAM cyclicality, but by structurally scarce high-performance memory and adjacent AI-server modules.

## Core thesis

SK Hynix dominates [[HBM]] but depends on [[TSMC]] for [[Advanced packaging|packaging]]. Now pushing to vertically integrate into 2.5D packaging to offer turn-key solutions and reduce TSMC dependency.

## Evolution

SK Hynix's last decade is the story of a commodity-memory company being repriced as an AI infrastructure bottleneck. The 2018 supercycle first showed how explosive the earnings power can be when DRAM tightens, but the 2023 drawdown reminded the market how brutal the old inventory cycle was. The 2024-2026 rerating is different because it is being driven by qualification scarcity in [[HBM]], tighter customer lock-in, and a widening ambition around packaging and server-side modules like SOCAMM2. The live equity question is whether that shift makes SK Hynix structurally less cyclical, or simply gives it a richer phase within the same memory cycle.

## TurboQuant sell-off and demand debate (Mar-Apr 2026)

[[Google]] Research's [[TurboQuant]] announcement (Mar 25, 2026) triggered two weeks of investor anxiety over Korean memory. SK Hynix and [[Samsung]] fell sharply on the first-order reading — that a 6x KV-cache compression would cut [[HBM]] demand. The sell-off was partially reversed by [[Samsung]]'s Apr 8 Q1 guidance (record ~$43B operating profit, HBM revenue tripled YoY), which served as a real-time data point against the near-term demand-destruction thesis.

Ray Wang ([[SemiAnalysis]], Seoul-based memory analyst) on the dynamic: "Memory is becoming a bit less cyclical, driven by accelerating and sustainable AI demand. Contract pricing now matters more than spot pricing." Wang's framing implies that even if TurboQuant compressed per-inference memory needs at the margin, SK Hynix's fully-contracted 2026 HBM supply and 3-5 year agreements with hyperscalers cushion the impact. See [[HBM economics]] and [[Jevons Paradox]] for the full demand-side debate.

*Source: [[Financial Times]] (Daniel Tudor), Apr 12 2026*

## SOCAMM2 rollout extends the AI-memory story (Apr 2026)

On Apr 20, SK Hynix said it had begun mass production of a 192GB SOCAMM2 module tailored for [[NVIDIA]]'s [[Rubin]] platform. The product adapts low-power mobile memory for server use and, according to the company, offers more than double the bandwidth and more than 75% better energy efficiency than conventional alternatives.

The readthrough is that SK Hynix is not just defending its lead inside [[HBM]]. It is trying to widen its position across the broader AI-memory stack, including the server-side modules that sit around training and inference systems. That mattered for the Korea tape too. As [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran-war]] energy fears faded, SK Hynix again traded as AI infrastructure rather than as a cyclical memory beta, helping pull the broader Korean equity story back toward semiconductors.
