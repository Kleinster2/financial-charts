For markets, Rodriguez matters because she became the public face of the post-Maduro [[Oil|oil]]-sector reopening pitch, including the March 25, 2026 [[FII Priority Miami 2026]] appearance aimed at Western, Saudi, and [[LATAM|Latin American]] capital.

## FII Priority Miami 2026

Rodriguez appeared by video from Caracas during the March 25 "New LATAM Order" opening of [[FII Priority Miami 2026]]. The appearance was striking because Miami had long been treated by Chavista politics as hostile territory, while the audience combined US, Saudi, and Latin American capital allocators.

Her pitch emphasized macro stability, Venezuela's large oil reserves, and a new hydrocarbons framework intended to reduce [[PDVSA]]'s monopoly position and allow more private-sector participation. The strategic read is not that Venezuela became investable overnight; it is that the post-Maduro government is trying to turn regime change into a financing channel by offering legal stability, oil-sector access, and a path back into mainstream capital markets.

The video feed cut off when Q&A began. Organizers described the interruption as technical; the market signal was the appearance itself rather than any announced deal.
