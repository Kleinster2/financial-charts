**AMC Entertainment** (NYSE: AMC) — World's largest movie theater chain (~900 theaters, ~10,000 screens, including ODEON in Europe). The defining meme stock of 2021, it survived the post-COVID box-office collapse through serial dilution (~52M → ~612M shares) and now runs a genuine operating turnaround — first $1B+ revenue quarter since 2019 in Q1 2026 — against a ~$4.0B debt stack that still consumes the operating recovery. Majority-owned by [[Dalian Wanda Group]] until dilution erased the stake. Not to be confused with [[AMC Networks]], the cable/streaming company (AMCX).

## One-line read

AMC is the operationally-recovering, financially-encumbered giant of theatrical exhibition because the 2026 box-office rebound is real (record Easter, record May, +21% Q1 revenue) but ~$450M of annual interest on ~$4B of debt eats the entire EBITDA recovery, with the bet being whether the slate-driven cash inflection arrives before the next maturity wall forces another round of dilution — the equity is a levered call on the 2026–27 film slate.

## Meme-stock structural history

The 2021 episode left a permanent capital-structure scar. The r/WallStreetBets squeeze took the stock to a split-adjusted closing peak of $625.48 (June 2, 2021); management sold equity into the frenzy to survive COVID, then kept selling.

| Event | Date | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| Meme squeeze peak | Jun 2, 2021 | Split-adjusted close $625.48 |
| APE preferred units issued | Aug 2022 | Parallel equity currency; raised ~$247M |
| APE-AMC conversion + 1:10 reverse split | Aug 24, 2023 | Court-approved; unified share classes |
| Dilution arc | 2021–2026 | ~52M shares (post-split basis) → ~612M (10x+) |
| Delaware Supreme Court | May 2025 | Affirmed dismissal of APE-holder anti-dilution suit |
| ATM program | 2025–2026 | $150M completed; Q1 2026 alone: 55.2M shares for $64.7M gross + $34.5M consent fees paid in stock |

The share count is the lens for everything: operating recovery accrues to a base 10x+ larger than the pre-COVID equity, and dilution remains the marginal funding tool ([[Dalian Wanda Group]]'s former majority stake was erased the same way).

## What to watch

- Slate delivery — *Avengers: Doomsday*, *Spider-Man: Brand New Day*, *Dune: Part Three*, *The Odyssey* are the 2026–27 attendance drivers; a franchise underperformance breaks the recovery narrative industry-wide.
- Refinancing execution — whether the January 2026 Muvico-noteholder flexibility converts into an actual term-loan/Odeon-2027 refinancing at lower coupons. Interest expense (~$450M/yr) is the single largest gap between EBITDA and the bottom line.
- FCF inflection — FY2025 was $(366)M with a positive $51M final nine months; Q1 2026 EBITDA $38.3M. Sustained positive FCF would be the first since before COVID and would slow the dilution treadmill.
- Dilution pace — the ATM is the marginal funding source (55.2M shares in Q1 2026 alone). Watch share-count growth vs operating improvement; the race defines per-share value.
- Per-patron economics — admissions $12.09 (+5.9%) and F&B $7.62 (+5.1%) at records; premium formats and pricing are the margin lever exhibition controls.
- Retail flow — the stock still trades meme episodes (+14.6% on Aron's insider buy). Sigma moves without box-office news are flow, not fundamentals.

## Analysis

AMC is two superimposed stories that the market prices as one. The operating story is genuinely good and getting better: revenue has fully recovered to ~$4.8B, per-patron economics are at records, Q1 2026 was the best since 2019, and the 2026–27 slate is the strongest since before COVID. The financial story negates most of it: ~$4B of expensive debt (12.75% coupons in the stack) consumes the EBITDA recovery, and the equity funds the gap by issuing shares — 10x+ dilution since 2021 and still running at ~55M shares a quarter.

The cluster work quantifies the consequence: exhibition has the most genuine sector factor in US-listed media (PC1 55.5%, positive intra advantage against every control), yet AMC is the loosest member of its own cohort, with 70% annualized vol and the lowest factor weight. The peer chart says the same thing in levels — IMAX +170%, CNK +140%, AMC −66% off the identical box-office recovery. The entire underperformance is capital structure. That makes the instrument choice unusually clean: the box-office recovery view belongs in CNK (clean balance sheet, highest factor loading); AMC is the same view levered through a distressed-credit-shaped equity with a retail-flow overlay — a call option whose strike is the refinancing.

What would change the read: an executed refinancing that cuts the ~$450M interest bill meaningfully, plus two consecutive positive-FCF quarters. That combination would let operating recovery reach the per-share line for the first time since the meme era — and would be visible first in the dilution pace slowing.
