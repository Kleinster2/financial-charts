# HongShan

Chinese venture capital firm, formerly Sequoia Capital China. Became independent when Sequoia split into three regional firms in 2023. Founded and led by [[Neil Shen]]. **$56B AUM**. Offices in Hong Kong, Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and Tokyo.

Key AI portfolio: [[MiniMax]], [[Moonshot AI]], [[StepFun]], [[Manus AI]]. Also early investor in [[Unitree]], ByteDance, Meituan, Pinduoduo.

The [[Manus AI]] $2B Meta exit (December 2025) was the showcase deal — a reported ~24× return from an ~$85M entry — and the validation that the [[Singapore washing]] offshore-restructuring playbook would carry HongShan's broader China-AI portfolio toward US acquirers. The April 27, 2026 [[NDRC]] order to unwind the Meta-Manus deal makes the 24× return claim provisional pending the unwind mechanics; capital may be subject to claw-back, and the broader playbook for monetizing [[MiniMax]], [[Moonshot AI]], and [[StepFun]] through similar Singapore-routed exits is now contested. See [[Singapore washing]] for the full playbook and pipeline exposure.

Raised ~$9B in 2022 from global institutional investors before US-China investment restrictions tightened. Known LPs include:
- **[[CalPERS]]** — California Public Employees' Retirement System (~$500B, largest US pension)
- **[[CPP Investment Board]]** — Canada Pension Plan (~$600B CAD)
- Plus unnamed pensions, endowments, family offices, and sovereign wealth funds from Europe, Middle East, and Southeast Asia

Also created a renminbi-denominated vehicle with more scope to invest in sensitive technologies (FT). Opened London office in Oct 2024 for European deals.
