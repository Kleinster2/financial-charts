# Leopold Aschenbrenner

**Leopold Aschenbrenner** - AI investor and writer. Former [[OpenAI]] Superalignment researcher, author of *Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead*, and founder of [[Situational Awareness LP]], a San Francisco AI-focused equity investor.

Aschenbrenner matters because he is one of the cleanest bridges between short-timeline [[AGI timelines|AGI]] forecasting and the public-market AI-infrastructure trade. His 2024 essay framed AI as an industrial mobilization: compute clusters scaling toward trillion-dollar cost, power contracts becoming strategic assets, lab security becoming a national-security problem, and the US government eventually being pulled into frontier AI. The fund is the revealed-preference layer on top of that worldview.

The note's key question is whether Aschenbrenner is a forecasting outlier whose influence fades as timelines stretch, or an early interpreter of the real physical bottlenecks in AI. The vault should track both sides: his public claims are much more aggressive than consensus, but his fund's filings are a useful market map of how an AGI-short-timeline investor separates bottleneck longs from crowded AI beta.

## Situational Awareness thesis

Aschenbrenner's June 2024 essay is important because it put an unusually specific industrial map around short AGI timelines. The model path was: [[GPT]]-4-class systems keep compounding through compute, algorithmic efficiency, and "unhobbling"; AGI by 2027 becomes plausible; superhuman AI researchers then accelerate AI research itself; and the required clusters move from billions to hundreds of billions and eventually trillion-dollar scale.

The investing content is not the headline date alone. It is the bottleneck chain:

| Bottleneck | Aschenbrenner frame | Vault home |
|------------|---------------------|------------|
| Compute | Training clusters scale from megawatts to gigawatts and eventually 10GW / 100GW thought experiments | [[AI Compute]], [[AI capex chain basket]] |
| Power | Power contracts, transformers, and generation become strategic inputs | [[Power constraints]], [[Global DC grid strain]] |
| Security | Frontier-lab secrets become state-level targets | [[AI safety]], [[Export controls]] |
| Government | A government-led national project becomes plausible as AI becomes national-security infrastructure | [[Industrial policy]], [[AGI timelines]] |

This is why the note should not file him only as an "AI doom" or "AGI timelines" person. His most durable contribution to the investing vault is the claim that AI progress converts software capability into physical-infrastructure scarcity.

## Investment vehicle

[[Situational Awareness LP]] was founded by Aschenbrenner in September 2024 as a global equity investor focused on AI. The official firm description says it looks for investments and themes materially affected, positively or negatively, by AI development across technology, energy, other economic sectors, and macro impacts.

The latest ADV-derived profile shows an already institutional-sized vehicle: $9.3B regulatory assets under management, 2 pooled vehicles, and 8 employees as of the April 27, 2026 filing. The latest public [[13F]] snapshot is even more striking: 13f.info reports $13.68B of reported value across 42 holdings as of Mar 31, 2026, filed May 18, 2026. The gap between RAUM and 13F reported value likely reflects option reporting / notional effects, so treat the 13F as a positioning map, not a simple cash-AUM number.

The Q1 2026 positioning signal is nuanced. The Market Context's read of the 13F shows a large semiconductor put overlay against [[SMH]], [[NVIDIA]], [[Oracle]], [[Broadcom]], and [[AMD]], while the long side remains tied to AI power, data-center, storage, and crypto-to-AI conversion exposures such as [[Bloom Energy]], [[CoreWeave]], [[IREN]], [[Applied Digital]], and [[SanDisk]]. That is exactly the distinction the broader [[AI Infrastructure]] hub needs: belief in AI demand does not imply unhedged ownership of every crowded AI supplier.

## OpenAI departure and security framing

Aschenbrenner worked on [[OpenAI]]'s Superalignment team, which OpenAI launched in July 2023 to pursue technical methods for steering and controlling AI systems much smarter than humans. OpenAI's own Superalignment announcement lists him among contributors to the team.

His exit became part of the public security debate. On the June 2024 Dwarkesh podcast, Aschenbrenner said OpenAI told employees he had been fired for "leaking"; his account was that the shared material was a preparedness / safety / security brainstorming document sent to three external researchers for feedback after he reviewed and redacted it. He also said a security memo warning OpenAI's board about lab-security risk was a major reason the episode became a firing rather than a warning. Treat this as Aschenbrenner's public account of a disputed employment matter, not as an independently adjudicated fact.

The strategic relevance is larger than the personnel dispute. His worldview says frontier AI labs are not ordinary startups: they hold national-security-relevant secrets, face state-actor espionage risk, and may eventually be pulled into government-led structures. Whether or not one accepts the strongest version of that argument, it explains why his investing frame is physical and geopolitical rather than purely software-multiple driven.

[[Dylan Patel]] on [[Invest Like the Best]] (Apr 23, 2026) recounted an anecdote that places Aschenbrenner at the leading edge of frontier-capability access scarcity: "One of my funniest memories in the past month and a half is myself and a buddy of mine, Leopold, being on our knees in front of an [[Anthropic]] co-founder begging him for access to [[Claude Mythos|Mythos]] and then pretending it doesn't exist cuz we knew it existed. We were like, 'Please give us access.' And he's like, 'I don't know what you're talking about.'"

The anecdote is color rather than substantive disclosure, but it illustrates that even high-profile AI investors with [[SemiAnalysis]]-tier relationships were excluded from initial [[Project Glasswing]] selection - the gating dynamic that drives [[Inference economics|the permanent-underclass thesis]].
