# Ming Yang Smart Energy

Chinese wind turbine manufacturer, one of the world's largest. Shanghai-listed (601615.SS) with London GDRs. Employs 25,000+. Privately owned but listed — critics flag [[China]]'s potential interference in nominally private companies.

---

## UK national security rejection (Mar 25, 2026)

UK government rejected Ming Yang's turbines for use in UK offshore wind projects, citing national security risks (FT, Mar 25). Decision expected to thwart the company's planned £1.5bn Scottish factory at Ardersier port in the Highlands, announced Oct 2025. Factory would have produced turbine blades and parts for UK, European, and other markets.

Key context:
- [[Donald Trump]] administration warned [[UK]] government about security risks of allowing the factory
- UK MPs had also criticized the plans
- Scotland deputy first minister Kate Forbes said the decision was "regrettable" and accused Labour of "hammering Scotland's oil and gas sector"
- Ming Yang said it was "disappointed" and would continue engaging with UK government

### Octopus Energy partnership

[[Octopus Energy]] (UK's largest household energy supplier, ~8M households) partnered with Ming Yang in Sep 2025. Scale: ~6 GW of Ming Yang turbines planned for deployment in Britain. Octopus proposed custom software to "create the highest levels of data protection and cyber security."

Octopus CEO Greg Jackson traveled to Beijing in Jan 2026 as part of PM Keir Starmer's delegation (first UK PM visit to [[China]] since 2018). 60 British businesses accompanied. Octopus head of energy generation Zoisa North-Bond (Jan 27): *"When you speak to a Chinese hardware manufacturer, actually it could save 30 per cent on developing wind here in the UK if we could access Chinese wind turbines."*

The partnership was effectively killed by the Mar 25 national security rejection.

*Source: FT, Rachel Millard, George Parker, Joe Leahy, Jan 27 2026*

### Vestas replacement

UK and Scottish governments held talks with [[Vestas]] about co-investing to build a new factory in Scotland as replacement. [[Vestas]] announced it would push ahead if it wins enough UK wind farm orders — factory could be operational by 2029, creating up to 500 skilled jobs. Ed Miliband ([[UK]] energy secretary) framed it as "clean energy mission delivering good industrial jobs."

*Source: FT, Jim Pickard and Rachel Millard (London), Simeon Kerr (Dubai), Mar 25 2026*
