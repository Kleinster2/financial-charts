**Space Capital** — New York-based venture firm focused on the space economy. Founded and led by [[Chad Anderson]]. Publishes the quarterly *Space IQ* briefing — the canonical institutional research product on space-economy investment flows. Q1 2026 *Space IQ* (recorded at NASDAQ MarketSite, released Apr 26, 2026) is the source for the Q1 2026 record numbers: $36B invested in a single quarter, $7B infrastructure, $3.8B GeoInt, $8B realized across 28 exits. Portfolio includes [[Lunar Outpost]] and [[Vast Space]].

## Space IQ Q1 2026 (Apr 26, 2026)

| Headline figure | Q1 2026 |
|---|---|
| Total space economy investment | $36B (single-quarter record) |
| Infrastructure investment | $7B (on pace to exceed full-year 2025 record) |

| Cumulative invested since 2009 | $455B across 2,400+ companies |
| Q1 share of all-time | ~8% |
| Realized exits | $8B across 28 (26 acquisitions, 2 IPOs) |

Q1 2026 framing themes (Anderson):
1. **Orbital data center platform war** — SpaceX (Terrafab + AI satellite "taller than Starship V3"), [[Blue Origin]] ([[Project Sunrise]] 51,600 sats), [[NVIDIA]] ([[Vera Rubin Space 1]]), [[Google]] ([[Project Suncatcher]])
2. **NASA contractor renegotiation** — [[Jared Isaacman]]'s Q1 2026 Starliner Type A reclassification, $20B lunar base, [[Lunar Gateway]] pause, [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] restructuring
3. **Capital efficiency** — SpaceX self-funded post-2021 ($10B equity flatlined while output compounded) vs Blue Origin (~$27B Bezos founder investment, 39 cumulative flights)
4. **GeoInt sleeper story** — $3.8B Q1 = 86% of all 2025 GeoInt
5. **Infrastructure data terminal** — [[Kayhan Space]] [[Satcat Terminal]] launches as Bloomberg-analog for orbital activity

The framing has a directionally bullish lens — Space Capital is a space-economy VC partnered with NASDAQ on the Space IQ release. Treat the Space Capital framework as authoritative on data points, but cross-check the framing against more skeptical voices like [[SemiAnalysis]] for orbital DCs (see [[Space data centers]] for [[Dylan Patel]]'s "not this decade" rebuttal).
