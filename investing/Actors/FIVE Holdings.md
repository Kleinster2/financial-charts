**FIVE Holdings** — Dubai-based vertically integrated hospitality, nightlife, and real estate conglomerate founded in 2011 as SKAI Holdings by [[Kabir Mulchandani]] (renamed FIVE in 2017). Operates four luxury hotels (1,700+ keys) across Dubai, Zurich, and Ibiza, plus the [[Pacha Group]] nightclub empire acquired for ~$330M in October 2023. Revenue AED 2,165M / $589M (FY2024, +28% YoY), EBITDA AED 766M / $208M. The business model fuses luxury hospitality with electronic music programming, nightlife, and entertainment under one vertically integrated brand. Preparing for IPO (DFM, possibly dual-listed London) at $2.5-3B valuation with [[Citi]] and [[Deutsche Bank]] as advisors. Expanding into the US via $110M [[Brooklyn Mirage]] / Pacha New York acquisition (opening June 2026) and planning $500M deployment over two years across US, Asia, and UAE.

## Synopsis

FIVE Holdings is the rare hospitality company that has turned nightlife into a core business line rather than an amenity. Founded by [[Kabir Mulchandani]] after his 2009 acquittal from fraud charges stemming from the [[Dynasty Zarooni]] collapse, FIVE built its brand on Dubai's Palm Jumeirah and Jumeirah Village before acquiring the iconic [[Pacha Group]] in October 2023 for €302.5M (~$330M). The Pacha deal transformed FIVE from a regional Dubai operator into a global entertainment-hospitality platform — Pacha Ibiza alone drew 583,834 guests across 156 events in the first nine months of 2025, with the nightclub achieving the world's first LEED Platinum certification for a nightclub (81 points). The company now generates ~30% of hospitality revenue and ~33% of EBITDA from Pacha operations.

The financial trajectory is steep: FY2024 total revenue hit AED 2,165M ($589M), up 28% YoY, with hospitality revenue of AED 1,641M ($447M) representing 70% growth over FY2023. EBITDA reached AED 766M ($208M). The balance sheet shows total assets of AED 10,743M ($2.9B) against total borrowings of AED 1,482M ($404M) and total equity of AED 6,608M ($1.8B) — a 14% leverage ratio. The $350M green bond (9.375% coupon, Nasdaq Dubai) is being repaid three years early via the $460M revolving credit facility secured September 2025 from [[Commercial Bank of Dubai]], [[AAIB]], and [[Santander]], freeing $300M+ for expansion.

The strategic bet is that the hotel + nightlife + music label + fashion line model creates higher RevPAR, longer guest stays, and stronger brand loyalty than traditional luxury hospitality. FIVE LUXE JBR scored 89 LEED Platinum points (highest five-star hotel globally), and the company claims the world's only 100% LEED Platinum hotel portfolio. The IPO, originally targeted for 2025 with [[Citi]] and [[Deutsche Bank]], has been delayed but remains live. The $110M Brooklyn Mirage acquisition (via [[Axar Capital]] bankruptcy purchase) opens the US market as Pacha New York in June 2026. Mulchandani's checkered past — 140 days in Dubai jail in 2009, the Viceroy management dispute in 2017 — is the key reputational risk for any public listing.

## Kabir Mulchandani

Born October 6, 1972 in Mumbai, India. Attended Cathedral and John Connon School (one of Mumbai's most prestigious), then [[Phillips Exeter Academy]] in New Hampshire (graduated 1992, played squash). Enrolled at [[Stanford University]] for industrial engineering but left after one year to join the family business.

*Family business — Baron International/Baron Electronics.* His mother Shakun Mulchandani founded Baron International in 1992. The family-owned Baron International and Baron Electronics were Mumbai-based consumer electronics distributors that forged partnerships with [[Akai]], [[Aiwa]], and [[Hitachi]]. In 1994, Mulchandani as Managing Director struck an exclusive distribution deal with [[Akai|Akai Electric]] and pioneered aggressive "price-cut wars" that elevated Akai's Indian color TV market share from under 3% to a leading position within one year. The businesses faced regulatory probes from India's Directorate of Revenue Intelligence beginning 1995; joint ventures terminated ~2002.

*Dubai discovery (2003).* Received a complimentary two-day Emirates Airlines stay, observed Dubai's construction boom, and decided to invest in real estate despite zero prior experience.

*Dynasty Zarooni (2005-2009).* Co-founded Dynasty Zarooni Inc in 2005 with Hilal Al Zarooni. The firm was a property-flipping enterprise that purchased and resold hundreds of properties from 13 developers, often paying as little as 10% of property values before reselling. Portfolio exceeded AED 6.5B ($1.8B) by 2008. Projects included the AED 2B Ebony and Ivory development in Jumeirah Lake Towers and Panoramic Heights in Abu Dhabi. The company also ran an investment club promising guaranteed returns. In August 2008, sensing the coming crash, Mulchandani sold all uncompleted properties. When Dubai property prices collapsed 65% after Lehman Brothers, over AED 100M ($27M) in postdated checks bounced. Buyers filed complaints alleging Mulchandani sold nonexistent properties and illegally operated the investment club.

*Arrest and acquittal (2009-2011).* Arrested January 2009 in Dubai. Initial bail set at AED 46M, re-arrested with bail raised to AED 76M. Spent 140 days in jail. Released June 2009 after Dubai Land Department cleared him, determining he lawfully resold yet-to-be-built properties and complied with regulations. Investment club allegations required two additional years to resolve, finally dismissed 2011. Mulchandani described the experience as causing "unique pain that resembles a muscle ache."

*Personal.* Married [[Nadia Zaal]] (2012), an Emirati businesswoman, co-founder of [[Zaya Group]], CEO of [[Al Barari Group]], and non-executive director at FIVE Holdings. Four children. Indian citizenship retained despite Dubai residency. Owns the yacht ASYA (Heesen, ~$25M, 10 guests, crew of 9). Owns the first [[Airbus]] ACJ TwoTwenty private jet (registration 9H-FIVE, ~$70M, acquired 2021) — chartered as "Fly FIVE" at $14,000/hour for party flights with onboard DJs, booked 50+ times since April 2023 by VIPs and heads of state.

*Philanthropy.* Founded Project Udaan (medical support for underprivileged Indian children). FIVE Cares platform has donated $1.8M+ across partnerships including New Face Medical Trust and Room to Read. Funded 800+ life-saving heart and facial surgeries at Sir Ganga Ram Hospital, India. Philanthropist of the Year, CEO Middle East Awards (2017). Post-Pacha acquisition, appointed president of Pacha Foundation (€500K budget, 35+ projects, 430% funding increase in 2025).

## Evolution

The story of FIVE Holdings is the story of a second act — a businessman who went from 140 days in a Dubai jail cell to building a $2.9B hospitality-entertainment empire in 15 years, by seeing that the boundary between hotel and nightclub was an artificial one.

- *2003:* Kabir Mulchandani, then running his family's consumer electronics distribution business in Mumbai (Baron International, which had pioneered Akai TV's Indian market dominance through aggressive price wars), discovered Dubai during a complimentary Emirates Airlines stopover. With no real estate experience, he began buying and flipping properties in the pre-crisis boom.

- *2005-2008:* Co-founded Dynasty Zarooni with Emirati partner Hilal Al Zarooni. The firm became one of Dubai's largest property flippers, amassing a portfolio exceeding AED 6.5B ($1.8B) by 2008. The model was pure arbitrage — buy at 10% down from developers, resell at markup using postdated checks as payment. When Mulchandani sensed the crash in August 2008 and dumped all uncompleted properties, it was already too late for the ecosystem of checks and counter-checks. Dubai property prices collapsed 65% post-Lehman, and the check cascade unwound.

- *2009-2011:* Arrested January 2009 on fraud and embezzlement charges. Bail set at AED 46M, then raised to AED 76M after re-arrest. The 140 days in jail became the inflection point. Dubai Land Department ultimately cleared him — he had complied with all regulations. Acquitted fully by 2010-2011 after investment club allegations also dismissed. The experience left him with a conviction to build something he owned outright rather than flipping other developers' inventory.

- *2011:* Property values bottomed. Mulchandani founded SKAI Holdings, acquiring three half-built properties from a Turkish developer. Purchased a 320,000 sq ft waterfront plot on Palm Jumeirah for AED 400M ($109M). Combined projected value of initial projects: ~$1.6B. This was the land that would become FIVE Palm Jumeirah.

- *2016-2017:* FIVE Palm Jumeirah opened (March 13, 2017) — 697 units, 1.28M sq ft, initially managed by [[Viceroy Hotel Group]] under a 30-year contract signed in 2013. Within two months, Mulchandani dramatically seized back control: on June 19, 2017, he terminated the Viceroy contract and rebranded overnight, alleging fraudulent accounting, doctored invoices, and mismanagement revealed by two accounting firms' investigations. Viceroy filed suit in LA Superior Court (fraud, extortion, conversion, unfair business practices). DIFC Courts initially injuncted Mulchandani but he prevailed — LA court dismissed all Viceroy claims in March 2018 for improper venue. The SKAI-to-FIVE rebrand happened in 2017 (the name references "five elements — earth, air, fire, water, ether"), and the hostile takeover of its own flagship hotel established FIVE as a self-managed brand.

- *2019:* FIVE Jumeirah Village opened — 501 units (247 hotel rooms, 254 serviced apartments), 940,690 sq ft, featuring a patented "corkscrew tower" design (patented in 168 countries) with "Villas in the Sky." Became the world's most-reviewed hotel on Booking.com (35,795+ reviews, 9.1/10 rating).

- *2020-2022:* Acquired a hilltop hotel property in Zurich (August 2020), opened as FIVE Zurich (June 2022) — 149 units (45 suites, 104 suite-sized rooms), 300K sq ft, 8 venues. First international property. During this period, FIVE achieved UAE's sole hotel I-REC agreement with DEWA for 100% solar power, and reduced carbon use intensity by 76.2% from 2020 baseline.

- *2023 (Pacha acquisition):* The transformative deal. Acquired 100% of [[Pacha Group]] from [[Trilantic Europe]] for €302.5M (~$330M) in October 2023. Included: Pacha Ibiza nightclub (opened 1973 by Ricardo Urgell), Destino Pacha Ibiza hotel, El Hotel Pacha, Toy Room clubs (11+ locations: London, Athens, Rome, Istanbul, Dubai, Mykonos, Mallorca, Sao Paulo), and WooMoon Storytellers (immersive events in Ibiza and Tulum). Trilantic retained the Lio experiential restaurant brand and Casa Pacha Formentera. Financed by the $350M green bond (9.375% coupon, Nasdaq Dubai, October 4, 2023) with [[Citi]] as sole structuring bank, plus [[Deutsche Bank]] and [[Emirates NBD]] Capital as global coordinators. [[Latham & Watkins]] advised. [[Clifford Chance]] advised Trilantic. The deal instantly gave FIVE a global nightlife brand founded in 1967 (Sitges) / 1973 (Ibiza) — 50+ years of brand equity.

- *2024 (FIVE LUXE and Pacha integration):* FIVE LUXE JBR launched March 31, 2024 — 444 units (222 hotel rooms, 222 residences), 823,493 sq ft, 9 venues, LEED Platinum certified at 89 points (highest-scoring five-star hotel globally). Includes SENSORIA ultra-luxury component (54 units, 215K sq ft). FY2024 revenue hit AED 2,165M ($589M), up 28% YoY, with hospitality revenue surging to AED 1,641M from AED 962M. Pacha's first full operational year delivered €112M revenue, €37.4M EBITDA. The FIVE Music label partnership with [[Warner Music Group]] expanded, and FIVE MODE fashion launched with a [[BALR]] collaboration adorned with 30,000 Swarovski crystals (worn by [[Ronaldinho]] and [[Gerard Pique|Gerard Pique]]).

- *2025 (expansion mode):* Destino Five Ibiza completed — 159 units, 426,466 sq ft, on Cap Martinet cliffs overlooking Dalt Vila. In H1 2025, Dubai properties generated $177M revenue (+24% YoY, 85% occupancy, $310 RevPAR). Pacha Ibiza had a record season. In September 2025, secured $460M revolving credit from [[Commercial Bank of Dubai]], [[AAIB]], and [[Santander]] — used to repay the $350M bond three years early, freeing $300M+ liquidity. Announced $500M deployment over two years for US, Asia, and UAE expansion. Pacha Ibiza nightclub achieved LEED Platinum certification (81 points, world's first for a nightclub, October 2025).

- *2026 (US entry):* Acquired Brooklyn Mirage / [[Avant Gardner]] site (140 Stewart Avenue, East Williamsburg, 80,000 sq ft) for $110M via [[Axar Capital]] bankruptcy sale. [[Avant Gardner]] had filed Chapter 11 in August 2025 with $155.3M in debt. Pacha New York opening June 2026. Demolition of existing Mirage structure began February 2, 2026. Bankruptcy resolved February 12, 2026 with unsecured creditor settlement modified to guarantee minimum $6.8M distribution.

## Business model

The "Vibe at FIVE" model integrates luxury hotels with dining, nightlife, live electronic music, and lifestyle (fashion, aviation) under a single vertically integrated brand. The thesis is that bundling these reduces operational costs while increasing average guest spend, engagement, and repeat visitation compared to traditional luxury hotels that outsource nightlife and entertainment.

Revenue streams: (1) hotel rooms and residences, (2) food & beverage and social events, (3) nightclub/live events (amplified post-Pacha), (4) real estate development sales, (5) FIVE Music (label, partnership with [[Warner Music Group]]), (6) FIVE MODE (fashion line; BALR collaboration), (7) Fly FIVE (private jet charter at $14,000/hour), (8) Pacha merchandise (physical retail, wholesale, e-commerce).

*Sustainability as differentiator.* World's first and only 100% LEED Platinum-certified hotel portfolio (1,571 LEED Platinum rooms vs <300 in all US hotels under LEEDv4). FIVE LUXE scored 89 LEED Platinum points (highest globally for a 5-star hotel). Carbon footprints 4-6x lower than Dubai 5-star resort average per Cornell University benchmarking. Net-Zero Luxury Living "Build-Tech" design concept introduced 2022. 100% solar power via DEWA I-REC agreement.

## Competitive positioning

FIVE positions against lifestyle hospitality brands — [[W Hotels]] ([[Marriott]]), [[Edition Hotels]] ([[Marriott]]), [[1 Hotels]] ([[Starwood Capital]]), [[SH Hotels & Resorts]] ([[1 Hotels|1 Hotels / Baccarat / Treehouse]]) — but argues differentiation through vertical integration of entertainment. Traditional lifestyle brands outsource nightlife programming; FIVE owns the entire stack from music label to nightclub to hotel to fashion line. The Pacha acquisition gave FIVE something none of these competitors have: a 50+-year-old globally iconic nightclub brand with its own cultural identity.

Key competitive metrics (Dubai, H1 2025): 85% occupancy, $310 RevPAR, 9.2-9.4/10 Booking.com ratings across properties. FIVE Jumeirah Village's 35,795+ reviews make it the world's most-reviewed hotel on Booking.com.

The ESG positioning is also differentiated — FIVE claims to be the only hospitality company with a 100% LEED Platinum portfolio and the world's only ISS 'A' ESG-rated company in its peer group.
