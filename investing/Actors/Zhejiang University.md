**Zhejiang University** (ZJU) is a top Chinese research university located in [[Hangzhou]], [[Zhejiang]] province. Consistently ranked among China's top three universities (alongside [[Tsinghua University]] and Peking University), ZJU is the most important talent pipeline for the [[Hangzhou Robotics Cluster]]. By September 2024, ZJU alumni had founded or led 102 AI startups — third-highest nationally — and produced three of the six companies in Hangzhou's "[[Hangzhou Robotics Cluster|Six Little Dragons]]" cohort: [[DeepSeek]], [[Deep Robotics]], and [[Manycore Tech]].

ZJU's strength in robotics and AI stems from purpose-built programs: the College of Control Science and Engineering, College of Information Science & Electronic Engineering, the Chu Kochen Honors College, a dedicated Robotics Engineering major (established 2019), and one of China's first 35 approved AI undergraduate programs (also 2019). The university is closely linked to [[Zhejiang Lab]] (之江实验室), a provincial research institute focused on intelligent computing and AI.

The university's role matters for investors tracking Chinese AI and robotics because it is the origin node for an unusual concentration of commercially viable spinouts — not just academic labs but companies that have reached meaningful scale (DeepSeek in LLMs, Deep Robotics in quadruped robots, Manycore Tech in humanoids).
