**Liu He** (刘鹤, born January 25, 1952) — Former Vice Premier of China (2018-2023) and chief architect of Xi Jinping's economic policies. Harvard-educated economist who served as China's top trade negotiator during the US-China trade war. Often called "China's Larry Summers." Retired 2023 but reportedly still meets with Western delegations.

## Background

**Education:**
- BS Industrial Economics, Renmin University (1983)
- MS Industrial Economics, Renmin University (1986)
- Visiting Scholar, Seton Hall University (1992-93)
- MPA, Harvard Kennedy School (1995)
- PhD Economics (dissertation topic unknown)

**Early life:**
- Born in Beijing
- Sent down to Taonan, Jilin during Cultural Revolution (agricultural commune)
- 1970: Joined 82nd Group Army, rose to deputy squad leader
- After 3 years military, assigned to Beijing Radio Factory as worker

## Intellectual contribution

**Academic credentials:** Rare among Chinese economic policymakers for serious academic reputation. Professor at Renmin, Peking, Tsinghua, and Beijing University of Aeronautics.

**Key work:** "Comparative Study of the Two Global Crises" (2012) — analysis of 1929 and 2008 financial crises.

**Publications:** 5 books, 200+ articles on macroeconomics, industrial policy, information economy.

**Founder:** Chinese Economists 50 Forum.

**Economic views:** Generally considered economically liberal.

---

## Key concepts attributed to Liu He

- "Economic new normal"
- "Supply-side reform"

These became core Xi Jinping economic doctrines.

---

## US-China trade war role

Became China's top trade negotiator during the trade war with the United States. Key interlocutor with US Treasury and USTR officials.

**2018 Davos:** Gave keynote address at World Economic Forum.

**2023 Davos:** Reportedly told CEOs "China is back" after pandemic lockdowns.

---

## Post-retirement

SCMP reports Liu He "still has a big seat at the table" and quietly meets with Western delegations despite formal retirement.
