**Standard Bank** (JSE: SBK) is Africa's largest bank by assets, headquartered in Johannesburg. The bank operates across 20 African countries and maintains a white-label client relationship with [[Saxo Bank]] for its trading platform.

## Synopsis

Standard Bank's position as Africa's largest bank by assets (~$170B) makes it the default proxy for institutional investors seeking African banking exposure. The bank operates across personal and business banking, corporate and investment banking, and wealth management, with a geographic footprint spanning 20 African countries plus select international offices. [[ICBC]] (Industrial and Commercial Bank of China) holds a 20% strategic stake acquired in 2008 for $5.5B — at the time the largest foreign investment in Africa — which provides Standard Bank with Chinese capital market access and reflects China's strategic interest in African resource financing.

The investment case for Standard Bank tracks the broader thesis on African financial inclusion and economic formalization. With banking penetration still below 50% in most Sub-Saharan African markets, the TAM expansion runway is longer than in developed markets, though it comes with currency risk (the South African rand is volatile), sovereign risk (exposure to countries with varying governance quality), and credit risk in thin markets. The [[Saxo Bank]] white-label partnership gives Standard Bank's wealth clients access to global markets trading infrastructure without building it in-house — a model that several emerging market banks have adopted as fintech raises client expectations. Revenue splits roughly 60/40 between South Africa and rest-of-Africa, making the bank a leveraged play on the South African economy with diversified African growth optionality.
