# Standard Life

**Standard Life** is a historic UK life insurance and asset management company that merged with Aberdeen Asset Management in 2017, eventually becoming [[Aberdeen|abrdn]] (2021) and then [[Aberdeen]] Group (2026).

## Synopsis

Standard Life was one of Britain's oldest financial institutions (founded 1825 in Edinburgh) and a cornerstone of the UK insurance and pensions market before its transformation through the 2017 merger with Aberdeen Asset Management. The logic was industrial: Standard Life brought a large savings and insurance book plus a growing asset management arm, while Aberdeen brought global distribution and a bond/emerging market investment franchise. The combined entity was meant to be a diversified UK-headquartered asset manager capable of competing with global scale players.

The execution has been a case study in merger dysfunction. The combined entity went through three name changes — Standard Life Aberdeen, then the widely mocked "abrdn" rebrand in 2021 (dropping vowels in a failed attempt at digital-era branding), then [[Aberdeen]] Group in 2026. Meanwhile, the asset management business suffered persistent outflows as institutional clients pulled mandates during the integration, and the insurance/savings business was eventually sold to [[Phoenix Group]] in 2018 to focus on pure asset management. The stock has significantly underperformed UK financial peers, and the entity's journey from Standard Life to abrdn to Aberdeen is a reference case for how mergers of equals in asset management frequently destroy rather than create shareholder value.
