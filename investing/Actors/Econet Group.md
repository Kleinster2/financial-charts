Econet Group is a pan-African technology and telecom conglomerate founded by [[Strive Masiyiwa]] in 1993. The holding company controls a diversified portfolio spanning mobile telecom (Econet Wireless Zimbabwe, Lesotho, Burundi), fiber and cloud infrastructure (Liquid Intelligent Technologies, operating in 40+ African countries), fintech (Cassava Technologies, including EcoCash mobile money), and mobility (Vaya Technologies for ride-hailing and logistics). Headquartered in Johannesburg, the group is one of the largest African-owned technology businesses.

Masiyiwa's founding story is a landmark in African business history — he fought a five-year legal battle (1993-1998) against the Zimbabwean government's telecom monopoly, winning a Supreme Court ruling that granted him the country's first private mobile license. The Liquid Intelligent Technologies subsidiary is particularly significant, controlling one of the largest fiber networks in Africa (~100,000 km) and positioning the group for the data center and cloud computing buildout across the continent. EcoCash processes the majority of Zimbabwe's electronic transactions, making it a de facto financial infrastructure provider in a dollarized economy.
