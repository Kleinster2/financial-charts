**Rockefeller Foundation** — One of America's oldest and largest private foundations, with an endowment of approximately $6B. Founded in 1913 by [[John D. Rockefeller]], it now focuses on health, food, energy access, and economic mobility — with increasing emphasis on climate-linked infrastructure investment.

---

## Synopsis

The Rockefeller Foundation occupies a distinctive position in the philanthropic landscape: large enough to move markets in developing-world infrastructure but small enough relative to the [[Gates Foundation]] (~$75B endowment) that it must be highly strategic about where it deploys. Under president Rajiv Shah (former USAID administrator, joined 2017), the foundation has pivoted toward catalytic capital — using its balance sheet to de-risk projects that can then attract commercial investment. This is not traditional grant-making but a quasi-investment model where the foundation takes first-loss positions to crowd in private capital.

The $20M commitment to [[Delta40]] in February 2026 exemplifies this approach. Delta40 targets climate-tech startups in Africa and South Asia — markets where the risk-return profile is too uncertain for pure commercial capital but where the foundation's backing signals institutional credibility. For investors tracking frontier-market climate infrastructure, the Rockefeller Foundation's commitments function as a quality screen: they have done diligence that most allocators cannot replicate. The foundation's portfolio companies (including [[SunFi]] via Delta40) sit at the intersection of decarbonization and financial inclusion, a theme that is drawing increasing attention from development finance institutions.
