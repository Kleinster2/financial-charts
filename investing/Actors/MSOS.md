# MSOS

The AdvisorShares Pure US Cannabis ETF — the dominant US-listed vehicle for owning American cannabis multistate operators, and the answer the [[Cannabis]] cohort collapses into. Because the US MSOs are federally illegal under [[Cannabis rescheduling|Schedule I]] and cannot be held directly on a US exchange, MSOS gains its exposure through total-return swaps referencing the MSOs ([[Green Thumb Industries]], [[Trulieve]], [[Curaleaf]], [[Cresco Labs]], [[Verano]] and peers) rather than holding the shares outright — a structural workaround that lets a US-listed fund track federally illegal equities. Launched September 2020, it is the most liquid pure-play US cannabis instrument and the natural proxy for the whole MSO trade.

## Holdings and mechanism

MSOS references a concentrated book of US MSOs via swaps with counterparty banks; the top names ([[Green Thumb Industries|GTBIF]], [[Trulieve|TCNNF]], [[Curaleaf|CURLF]], [[Cresco Labs|CRLBF]], [[Verano|VRNOF]]) typically make up the bulk of exposure. The swap structure exists because [[280E]] and [[Cannabis rescheduling|Schedule I]] status keep these companies off US exchanges and out of conventional custody — a constraint that also limits the universe of counterparties and is itself a [[Cannabis rescheduling|reform]]-sensitive risk. A leveraged sibling, [[MJ|MSOX]], offers daily-leveraged exposure to the same book.
