# Henry Farrell

Henry Farrell is SNF Agora Institute Professor of International Affairs at [[Johns Hopkins]] University and co-author, with [[Abraham Newman]], of the [[weaponized interdependence]] framework that has reshaped how scholars and policymakers think about globalization's chokepoints. Before Johns Hopkins, a long career at George Washington University. A political scientist by training, but his influence extends well into trade policy and economic statecraft.

## Why he matters to the vault

Farrell is the primary theorist of the alternative framing to [[Second China shock]] — not aggregate trade flows but strategic chokepoints. His argument: the critical feature of modern globalization is that networks (SWIFT payments, undersea cables, cloud infrastructure, semiconductor design tools, rare-earth refining) run through a small number of nodes, and whoever controls those nodes has coercive power independent of aggregate trade shares.

This framing matters for the vault because it reshapes how to read:
- [[US-China chip export controls]] — chokepoint-based coercion via [[ASML]] and [[TSMC]] node control
- [[SWIFT]] sanctions architecture
- [[Critical minerals]] — China's refining chokehold on rare earths
- [[AI]] infrastructure dependencies

Farrell and Newman's [[Underground Empire]] book (2023) is one of the most-referenced political-economy works in the 2024-2026 economic-statecraft debate and is the companion text to [[Chip War]] by [[Chris Miller]].
