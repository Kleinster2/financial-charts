# Engine No. 1

Engine No. 1 — US investment firm best known for winning three [[ExxonMobil]] board seats in 2021 through a landmark proxy campaign waged with a stake of roughly 0.02%, forcing the oil major to confront capital discipline and the energy transition. The firm has since moved beyond public-market activism toward energy and AI-infrastructure investing.

Its current vault relevance is as developer of Project Kilby — the 2.7 GW [[Permian Basin]] gas-fired power and data-centre project for which [[Chevron]] signed a 20-year electricity sale agreement with [[Microsoft]] in June 2026. The role inverts the firm's origin story: from pressuring an oil major from the outside (Exxon, 2021) to partnering with one on the inside of the AI-power build-out (Chevron, 2026).
