**MAKIINA** (fka AQL Robotics) — Finnish robotics startup building ultra-low-cost dual-arm robotic systems (<$1,200) with AI-driven learning from human demonstration. Founded 2023 by Fayez Salka. Seed stage, raised €2.25M. Building vertically integrated hardware + software stack for physical AI.

---

## Synopsis

MAKIINA's pitch is cost disruption: dual robotic arm systems manufactured in-house for under $1,200, where comparable systems from established players cost 10-50x more. The robots learn tasks through human demonstration rather than rigid programming, handling "fuzzy" processes that traditional industrial robots can't. The company builds the full stack — drone motors, actuators, power electronics, sensor units, perception stacks, and an AI robotics foundation model — designed as modular LEGO-like blocks that assemble into different form factors.

This is very early stage (seed, €2.25M raised, founded 2023) and the claims are ambitious. The "iterate on hardware at software speed" promise is one every hardware startup makes; few deliver. But the cost point matters — if MAKIINA can actually ship a functional dual-arm system for $1,200, that's a genuinely different market from the $25K-$150K robots everyone else is building. Finland-based manufacturing is an unusual choice that trades labor cost disadvantage for EU market access and supply chain resilience.

## Investment case

Bull:
- 10-50x cost advantage if $1,200 price point holds at scale
- Full vertical integration enables rapid iteration
- AI learning from demonstration addresses "fuzzy" tasks traditional robots can't handle
- European manufacturing = EU market access, supply chain diversification
- Named among 10 early-stage European startups to watch (2026)

Bear:
- Extremely early (seed stage, founded 2023)
- $1,200 dual-arm system claims need real-world validation
- Hardware startups have high mortality rates
- Finland manufacturing costs are high vs. China
- No disclosed customers or deployments
- Ambitious foundation model claims from a tiny team
