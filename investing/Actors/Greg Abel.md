# Greg Abel

Greg Abel is CEO of [[Berkshire Hathaway]] (since January 2026), succeeding Warren Buffett. Buffett remains as Chairman. Abel was previously Vice Chairman of Non-Insurance Operations and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway Energy. Born in Edmonton, Alberta (1962), he joined Berkshire's energy business through the 1999 acquisition of MidAmerican Energy. The May 2, 2026 annual meeting in Omaha was his first as CEO.

## May 2, 2026 annual meeting — first as CEO

Abel's first annual meeting addressed the structural questions investors had been waiting to hear answered in his own voice:

- Ruled out break-up or spin-offs of the conglomerate
- Ruled out large transformative M&A despite the record $397.4B cash pile
- AI deployment will be narrow and additive: "We're not going to do AI for the sake of AI"
- Cited the Buffett deepfake circulating ahead of the meeting as confirmation of AI-driven cybersecurity risk across the franchise
- Berkshire Hathaway Energy can grow its data-center footprint by ~50% over 5 years to serve hyperscaler demand, but data center energy costs must be billed to those customers, not socialized to grid consumers
- Restarted buybacks at $234M in Q1 2026, the first BRK buyback since May 2024 — implicit signal the new CEO views the stock as below intrinsic value

The combined posture — no break-up, no transformative M&A, narrow AI deployment, modest buybacks — telegraphs operational continuity with the Buffett era while introducing a more dynamic capital-return cadence than the late-Buffett 2024-25 freeze.
