Glenn Schorr is a senior research analyst at [[Evercore]] ISI covering alternative asset managers, BDCs, and financial services. He is one of the most closely followed voices on the [[Private Credit]] sector's structural evolution.

## Synopsis

Schorr's coverage universe places him at the center of the alternative asset management story — the firms turning private credit from a niche allocation into a multi-trillion-dollar asset class. His analytical framework tends toward skepticism of rapid growth claims, focusing on what isn't disclosed rather than what is. His coverage of [[Blue Owl]]'s loan sale and the broader BDC sector has been particularly influential, applying a "what wasn't sold" lens that questions whether announced portfolio dispositions represent genuine risk reduction or cherry-picked healthy assets.

His voice carried weight during the [[Blue Owl OBDC II redemption gate]] episode, where retail investors discovered that private credit's "stable NAV" came with liquidity restrictions that surfaced only under stress. For investors tracking the [[Private Credit]] sector's maturation — the shift from institutional-only to retail distribution, the concentration risks in software credits like [[Medallia]], and the growing scrutiny from regulators — Schorr's research at [[Evercore]] provides one of the most granular analytical inputs available on the sell side.
