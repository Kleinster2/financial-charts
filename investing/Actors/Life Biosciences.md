#actor #biotech #longevity #healthcare #private

**Life Biosciences** is a private cellular-rejuvenation company and, as of January 2026, the first to bring partial epigenetic reprogramming into a human clinical trial. Its Partial Epigenetic Reprogramming (PER) platform uses three of the four Yamanaka factors — OCT4, SOX2, KLF4 (omitting the oncogenic c-Myc) — to restore older or damaged cells toward a younger state. Part of the [[Longevity]] cohort and its fastest verified clinical mover.

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Private |
| Approach | Partial Epigenetic Reprogramming (PER): OCT4, SOX2, KLF4 |
| Lead asset | ER-100 |
| Regulatory milestone | FDA cleared the ER-100 IND on January 28, 2026 — first-ever cellular-rejuvenation/partial-reprogramming therapy to reach human trials |
| Phase 1 | NCT07290244 — open-angle glaucoma and non-arteritic anterior ischemic optic neuropathy (NAION); safety/tolerability/visual endpoints; initiation Q1 2026, enrollment ~March 2026 |
| Scientific lineage | Harvard-linked (David Sinclair reprogramming research) |

Life Biosciences matters out of proportion to its funding because it converted partial reprogramming from preclinical promise into an FDA-cleared first-in-human study ahead of far better-capitalized rivals [[Altos Labs]] and [[Retro Biosciences]]. The choice of optic neuropathy is a deliberate regulatory move: targeting a specific eye disease of aging gives the FDA an approvable disease indication while the underlying mechanism (cellular rejuvenation) is general — the same animal-/disease-first workaround [[Loyal]] uses for the missing "aging" indication.
