# JSW Steel

## Synopsis

[[JSW Steel]] (NSE: JSWSTEEL; BSE: 500228) is the largest private-sector steel producer in India by capacity, the flagship of Sajjan Jindal's [[JSW Group]]. The firm operates integrated steel works at Vijayanagar (Karnataka), Dolvi (Maharashtra), Salem (Tamil Nadu), and other locations, with India operations crude steel capacity of approximately 34.2 MTPA as of FY 2025 (including units under commissioning). FY 2025 consolidated production volume was 27.79 million tonnes (+4% YoY), with India operations producing 26.98 million tonnes (+6% YoY). Q4 FY 2025 consolidated crude steel production was 7.63 million tonnes (+12% YoY) at India operations capacity utilisation of ~93%. In August 2025 JSW signed a non-binding heads of agreement with [[POSCO]] of South Korea to explore a 6-MTPA integrated steel-plant joint venture in India (50:50 structure, Odisha among locations under study).

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Hindalco]] cluster validation: JSW Steel pairs tightest with [[Tata Steel]] (1Y r = 0.75) in the Indian *steel sub-cluster*. Both names form a tight steel pair distinct from the [[Hindalco]] / [[Vedanta]] non-ferrous sub-cluster at threshold 0.4. JSW Steel is the more domestically focused of the two Indian steel majors — Tata Steel has substantial European exposure via the Corus / Port Talbot legacy, while JSW's revenue is overwhelmingly Indian-domestic.

## August 2025 — POSCO joint venture

On August 18, 2025, JSW Steel signed a non-binding Heads of Agreement with South Korea's [[POSCO]] to explore a 6-MTPA integrated steel-plant joint venture in India through a 50:50 structure. Odisha is among the locations under study. The deal positions JSW for further capacity addition into the Indian steel-demand growth cycle (India steel capacity moving from 200 MT FY 2025 toward 300 MT projected by FY 2030 per IBEF) while bringing in Korean steelmaking technology and supply-chain experience.
