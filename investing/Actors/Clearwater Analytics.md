# Clearwater Analytics

SaaS platform automating the entire investment lifecycle — accounting, reconciliation, reporting, performance, compliance, and risk analytics — for institutional investors. NYSE: CWAN.

SaaS fintech that became the dominant investment accounting platform for insurers, asset managers, and corporates by solving a specific pain: reconciling portfolio data across custodians, fund admins, and brokers into a single auditable source of truth. Founded 2004 in Boise, Idaho. IPO'd September 2021 at $18/share ($5B+ valuation). Now in the final stages of an **$8.4B take-private** by [[Permira]] and [[Warburg Pincus]] (with [[Temasek]] and Francisco Partners), expected to close H1 2026. The acquisition spree through 2024-2025 — Wilshire ($40M), Enfusion ($1.5B), Beacon ($560M), BISTRO ($125M from [[Blackstone]]) — transformed CWAN from a back-office accounting tool into a front-to-back investment management platform. Q4 2025 revenue $217.5M (+72% YoY), ARR $841M, 2,500+ clients managing $10T+ on the platform. The bull case was that CWAN was becoming the Bloomberg of investment operations — a sticky, mission-critical data layer. The bear case: acquisition-fueled growth, GAAP-negative in 2025, and the PE buyers got it at a moment of multiple compression (47% premium but still well below 2024 highs).

## Acquisition history

CWAN executed an aggressive roll-up strategy from 2022-2025, building a front-to-back investment platform through targeted M&A:

| Date | Target | Price | What it added |
|------|--------|-------|---------------|
| Sep 2022 | **JUMP Technology** (France) | €75M | Data management, front-middle-back office, reporting; European presence |
| Apr 2024 | **Wilshire Advisors** analytics | $40M | Risk/performance models (Axiom, Atlas, Abacus); co-branded as Clearwater Wilshire Analytics |
| Apr 2025 | **Enfusion** (Chicago) | $1.5B | Front-office OMS/EMS, hedge fund/alt manager platform; biggest deal |
| Mar 2025 | **Beacon** | ~$560M | Cross-asset class modeling and risk analytics |
| Mar 2025 | **BISTRO** (from [[Blackstone]]) | $125M | Private/structured credit analytics and portfolio visualization |

The logic: investment accounting (CWAN's core) sits at the back of the value chain. By acquiring front-office (Enfusion OMS), risk analytics (Wilshire, Beacon), and private credit tooling (BISTRO), CWAN was building a single-vendor alternative to the fragmented legacy stack (Bloomberg AIM + Charles River + internal spreadsheets). The PE buyers presumably see room to cross-sell across these modules post-integration.

## Platform and competitive position

**Architecture:** Single-instance, multi-tenant cloud. Every client runs on the same codebase — no custom deployments. This means feature updates ship to all clients simultaneously, and data aggregation/reconciliation happens across the entire ecosystem.

**What it replaces:** Legacy investment accounting was done via on-premise software (often decades old), manual spreadsheets, or outsourced to fund administrators. CWAN automates: data ingestion from custodians/brokers → reconciliation → GAAP/IFRS accounting → regulatory reporting → performance attribution → compliance monitoring.

**Client base:** Insurers (largest vertical — investment portfolios backing policy liabilities), asset managers, corporates with treasury portfolios, pension funds. Notable clients (as of 2020): American Family Insurance, Arch Capital, Cisco, Facebook/Meta, J.P. Morgan, Oracle, Starbucks.

**Competitive landscape:**
- **Bloomberg AIM / Charles River (CRD)** — front-office focused, less accounting depth
- **SS&C Technologies** — broad but legacy architecture
- **SimCorp** (acquired by Deutsche Börse) — European stronghold
- **Eagle Investment Systems** (BNY Mellon) — legacy platform
- **Enfusion** — was competitor before CWAN acquired it

**Moat:** Switching costs. Investment accounting is deeply embedded in clients' operational workflows, audit processes, and regulatory compliance chains. CWAN's 109% net revenue retention confirms stickiness, though it's down from higher levels (111.5% analyst expectation for Q4 2025 wasn't met).

## Insights

- The 2020 growth round → 2025 take-private pipeline is textbook PE "buy and build": Permira and Warburg invested as minority growth equity, observed for five years, then took the whole company private at a moment of multiple compression. They knew exactly what they were buying.
- CWAN's acquisition spree transformed its TAM from ~$10B (investment accounting) to ~$35B+ (full investment management stack), but integration risk is real — Enfusion alone was a $1.5B bet on cross-selling hedge fund/alt manager clients a back-office solution they never asked for.
- The 47% premium looks generous but CWAN traded above $30 in 2024. PE buyers effectively got the company cheaper than public market investors six months prior, plus four acquisitions' worth of platform expansion.
- Thoma Bravo's competing offer (Nov 2025) confirms the PE thesis — multiple large PE firms saw CWAN as a platform play worth taking private.
