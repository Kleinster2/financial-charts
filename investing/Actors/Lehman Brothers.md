**Lehman Brothers** — former major US investment bank whose September 15, 2008 bankruptcy ($639B in assets) was the largest in American history and the catalytic event of the [[Global Financial Crisis]]. Founded in 1847 as an Alabama cotton brokerage, Lehman evolved over 158 years into a top-five Wall Street firm before overleveraging on subprime mortgage-backed securities destroyed it. The decision by the [[Federal Reserve]] and [[US Treasury]] not to bail out Lehman — after rescuing [[Bear Stearns]] six months earlier — remains the most debated policy choice of the crisis.

## Synopsis

Lehman's collapse matters to this vault as the definitional case study for counterparty risk, moral hazard, and the systemic consequences of letting a major financial institution fail. The post-Lehman regulatory regime ([[Dodd-Frank]], [[Basel III]], SIFI designation) shapes bank valuations and capital return capacity to this day. In the immediate aftermath, [[Barclays]] acquired Lehman's US investment banking and capital markets operations for $1.75B, while [[Nomura]] paid $225M for the Asian and European businesses — acquisitions that reshaped both firms. The Lehman estate's decade-long bankruptcy proceedings eventually returned ~$130B to creditors.
