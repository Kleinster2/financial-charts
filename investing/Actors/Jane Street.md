### ETF market-making and longer holds

Jane Street's role as a major ETF market-maker naturally creates multi-day positions, since providing liquidity in ETFs requires holding underlying baskets. This structural feature gave Jane Street an early advantage in the [[HFT-hedge fund convergence]] trend: while most prop firms went home flat nightly, Jane Street's ETF book meant it was already comfortable carrying positions.

In H1 2020, Jane Street notched $8.4bn in net trading revenues, more than twice [[Citadel Securities]]' figure. The scale of those profits showed the industry what longer holding periods could produce with the right infrastructure.

Jane Street, [[Citadel Securities]], and [[Hudson River Trading]] have all tapped debt markets to boost capital firepower, moving beyond retained profits as the sole funding source.

*Source: FT Alphaville (Sep 29 2025)*

### Record Q1 2026 — beat the bulge bracket

Jane Street posted record revenues of $16.1bn in the first quarter of 2026, a haul that beat both [[JPMorgan Chase|JPMorgan]] and [[Goldman Sachs]] over the same period and confirmed it as the market leader of the prop-trading cohort. The quarter was powered by the volatility regime detailed in [[2026 Iran conflict market impact]] — oil and Treasury-rate swings from the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]] layered on AI-driven equity volatility. [[Citadel Securities]] earned a record $4.3bn and [[Hudson River Trading]] $6.4bn in the same quarter; together the three firms generated roughly $27bn of Q1 trading revenue.

*Source: [[Financial Times]], "Citadel Securities posts record $4.3bn in trading revenues on Iran volatility" (Jill R Shah and Eric Platt, May 29 2026).*
