# Jon Gray

President and Chief Operating Officer of [[Blackstone]] — the world's largest alternative asset manager. Joined Blackstone in 1992; rose through the real-estate franchise, which he led from 2005 until promoted to President/COO in 2018. Widely considered the in-line successor to founder [[Steve Schwarzman]]. Architect of Blackstone's digital-infrastructure and data-centre franchise, including the 2021 $10B [[QTS]] take-private, the [[CoreWeave]] debt facility, and the May 2026 [[Google-Blackstone TPU cloud venture 2026|Google TPU cloud joint venture]].

## Why Gray matters

Gray is the most visible architect of [[Blackstone]]'s shift from a traditional real-estate / leveraged-buyout firm into the dominant private-capital sponsor for AI infrastructure. Each leg of Blackstone's AI infra stack — [[QTS]] for colocation, [[CoreWeave]] for GPU-cloud debt, [[Anthropic]] and [[OpenAI]] equity on the model side, and now the [[Google-Blackstone TPU cloud venture 2026|TPU cloud JV]] — bears his imprint. His public framing of the JV as a "generational opportunity to invest capital at scale building AI infrastructure" tracks his long-running thesis that hard digital infrastructure is the next durable real-asset class after physical real estate.

He is also the canonical succession candidate for [[Steve Schwarzman]] — a transition the market has been pricing in as inevitable for several years.

---

## Real-estate franchise legacy

Under Gray's leadership, Blackstone's real-estate AUM grew from a few billion in 2005 to over $300B by the late 2010s — the largest real-estate manager in the world. The data-centre pivot during his real-estate tenure (QTS, plus earlier industrial and logistics build-out) established the muscle Blackstone now uses for AI infrastructure capital deployment.
