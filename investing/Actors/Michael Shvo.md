#actor #realestate #developer

NYC real estate developer. Israeli-born, moved to NYC in his 20s. Started in taxi fleet management, became Douglas Elliman's top broker by 2003, founded own firm 2004. Married to former model Seren Shvo (introduced by Serdar Bilgili).

## Legal history

2016: Indicted by Manhattan DA for falsifying records to avoid taxes on art, jewelry, furniture, Ferrari 458 Spider. 2018: Pled guilty to criminal tax fraud (second and third degree), repaid $3.5M.

## German pension partnership

Connected to [[Deutsche Finance Group]] through Bilgili in 2018. [[BVK]] became primary financial backer. Shvo sourced deals; presented himself as "partners with the German state" backed by "the largest pension fund in Germany."

**Portfolio with BVK/Deutsche Finance:**
- 685 Fifth Avenue (NYC) — Mandarin Oriental condos
- 711 Fifth Avenue (NYC) — Coca-Cola building
- Transamerica Pyramid (SF) — ~85% leased Dec 2025, claims record $300/sqft rent
- Big Red/The Red (Chicago)
- Beverly Hills — Mandarin Oriental condos (bulk sold)
- Miami Beach — Raleigh project (site sold Oct 2025 for ~$270M)
- SoHo office building (NYC)

## Bilgili falling out

2020: Former partner Serdar Bilgili sued Shvo (since settled), alleging expense discrepancies. Bilgili's letter to BVK claimed Shvo overcharged projects for private jets and personal Miami penthouse rent. Bilgili had provided >$400M in personal loan guarantees.

## Criticisms

- Construction delays and defects alleged by Core Club (tenant at Coca-Cola building)
- Described as "master salesman and visionary focused on design and marketing" with "struggles with budgeting, construction and deadlines"
- Beverly Hills: Asking prices ~2x area average; ~10 sales in 3 years before bulk sale
- Financial reports to BVK frequently delayed, lacked crucial details

Shvo disputes characterizations, points to Transamerica leasing success and "record rents with blue-chip tenants."
