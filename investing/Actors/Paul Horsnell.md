Paul Horsnell is head of commodities research at [[Standard Chartered]] and one of the most cited energy economists in the oil markets. Previously spent over a decade at [[Oxford Institute for Energy Studies]] and served as head of commodities research at [[Barclays]]. His 2000 paper "The Strategic Petroleum Blunder?" critiqued the Clinton administration's SPR exchange mechanism as structurally flawed, arguing the swap mechanism created a hidden forward liability rather than genuine price relief. Known for rigorous empirical work on oil market dynamics, OPEC behavior, and price formation.
