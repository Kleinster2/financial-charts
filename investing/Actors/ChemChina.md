China National Chemical Corporation (ChemChina) — Chinese state-owned chemical conglomerate. Now a subsidiary of [[Sinochem Holdings]] after 2021 restructuring. Best known for the $43B acquisition of [[Syngenta Group]] in 2017, China's largest-ever overseas takeover.

## Why it matters

ChemChina was Beijing's vehicle for acquiring Western technology in chemicals and agriculture. The [[Syngenta Group]] deal gave [[China]] access to advanced seed IP, gene-editing crops, and biotech capabilities central to food security strategy. The 2021 merger with [[Sinochem Group]] created one of the world's largest chemical conglomerates under [[Sinochem Holdings]].
