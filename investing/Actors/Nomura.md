**Nomura** — [[Japan]]'s largest investment bank and securities brokerage, founded in 1925. The firm has ~26,000 employees and operates across retail brokerage (dominant in Japan), wholesale (global markets and investment banking), and asset management. Nomura's international ambitions have been a recurring source of both opportunity and pain — the 2008 acquisition of [[Lehman Brothers]]' Asia and European operations for $225M was intended to transform Nomura into a global player but instead produced years of integration struggles and cultural clashes.

## Synopsis

Nomura's vault relevance spans two dimensions. First, as a proxy for Japanese financial sector health: the stock (8604.T, ADR: NMR) benefits from the same reflation and governance reform dynamics driving the broader [[Nikkei 225]] rally, with rising rates improving net interest margins for the first time in decades. Second, as a global markets participant whose research and trading operations generate frequent analyst calls cited in this vault.

The Lehman acquisition remains the defining strategic bet. It gave Nomura instant presence in European and Asian investment banking but came with Lehman's bloated cost structure and a culture clash between Japanese consensus management and Western deal-making aggression. The integration cost billions in writedowns before Nomura finally stabilized the business around 2015-2016. The 2021 Archegos Capital blowup cost Nomura $2.9B — another reminder that global wholesale ambitions carry outsized risk.

## FY2026 earnings — record annual on Q4 miss (Apr 27, 2026)

Nomura reported FY2026 (year ending March 2026) full-year earnings on Apr 27, 2026, posting its second consecutive record annual profit driven by the [[Japan]] equity rebound, governance-reform-driven IPO/secondary deal flow, and elevated trading activity through the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran war volatility]]. Net income rose just under 3% YoY at the consolidated group level on a record-revenue base.

The market reaction was negative: the Q4 print missed consensus on retail-brokerage revenue and on principal-trading P&L, with the shares falling on the Tokyo open Apr 27. The setup recapitulates Nomura's recurring pattern — the franchise is structurally levered to capital-markets volume and Japan reflation, but quarterly volatility in trading book and one-off impairments has historically prevented the stock from receiving a multiple consistent with the steady-state earnings power.
