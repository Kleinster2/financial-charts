**Rocket Software** — Enterprise infrastructure software company specializing in mainframe modernization and hybrid IT. A [[Thoma Bravo]] portfolio company whose usage-based pricing model positions it as a potential winner from the AI-driven disruption of seat-based SaaS.

---

## Synopsis

Rocket Software operates in one of the least glamorous but most defensible corners of enterprise software: mainframe infrastructure. Its products help organizations — primarily large banks, insurers, and government agencies — manage, modernize, and extend IBM Z mainframe environments. This is classic mission-critical software: customers cannot easily switch, upgrades are multi-year projects, and the alternative to modernization is replacement at 10x the cost. [[Thoma Bravo]] acquired Rocket in 2022, adding it to a portfolio of enterprise infrastructure plays.

The company's vault relevance spiked during the [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] discussion because of its pricing model. Unlike seat-based SaaS vendors (who face revenue destruction when AI agents replace human users), Rocket charges based on usage — compute cycles, transactions processed, data volumes. If AI agents increase mainframe workloads (more automated queries, more batch processing, more API calls), Rocket's revenue goes up, not down. CEO Milan Shetti has explicitly positioned this as a structural advantage. The question is whether this narrative translates into actual revenue acceleration or remains a theoretical hedge against a disruption that may not materialize in legacy infrastructure for years.
