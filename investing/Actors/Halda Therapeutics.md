## Technology — RIPTAC "hold and kill"

Halda's [[RIPTAC]] (Regulated Induced Proximity Targeting Chimeras) platform uses a single bifunctional small molecule with two binding ends. One end binds a tumor-selective protein (the "hold" — e.g., the androgen receptor in prostate cancer); the other binds a pan-essential protein the cell needs to survive (the "kill"). Only in cells expressing the tumor target does a stable ternary complex form, neutralizing the essential protein and killing the cell — while normal cells lacking the tumor protein are spared. The design aims for a wide therapeutic window against treatment-resistant disease.

Lead candidate HLD-0915 is a once-daily oral therapy for prostate cancer, with additional preclinical/experimental programs in breast, lung, and other tumor types.

John Reed ([[Johnson & Johnson]] EVP, Innovative Medicine and R&D) said the company would focus on "advancing the potential of this promising pipeline of novel product candidates." Jennifer Taubert (J&J EVP) welcomed "the talented Halda team" toward "our shared goal of eliminating cancer."

## Deal context

Halda was the larger of [[Johnson & Johnson]]'s two oncology acquisitions in the 2025–2026 pipeline rebuild against the STELARA biosimilar [[Patent cliffs|patent cliff]], preceding the $1B [[Firefly Bio]] DAC deal (June 2026). Unlike Firefly's preclinical platform, Halda brought a clinical-stage lead asset — a nearer-term pipeline addition.
