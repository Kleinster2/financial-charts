# Cristiano Amon

**Cristiano Amon** — President and CEO of [[Qualcomm]] since June 2021. Brazilian-born (Campinas, São Paulo); only Brazilian-born CEO running an S&P 500 semiconductor company. Electrical engineering degree from Universidade Estadual de Campinas (UNICAMP). Joined Qualcomm in 1995, rose through the modem business, led the 4G/5G rollout, drove diversification beyond mobile into automotive, PC, and edge AI. Chairman of the Semiconductor Industry Association ([[SIA]]) as of 2022. Recognized as American Person of the Year at the [[Brazil Week NYC 2026]] [[BrazilCham]] gala on May 13.

## Why he matters

Amon is the single most prominent Brazil-US technology connector in the [[Qualcomm]] orbit. His selection as [[BrazilCham]]'s American honoree confirms the chamber's framing that the Brazil-US technology corridor — historically thin compared to the financial corridor — has a viable institutional connector. His career arc (UNICAMP → modem engineer → 4G/5G lead → CEO) is the most-cited Brazilian engineering success story in US semiconductors, and his SIA chairmanship gives him direct visibility into US industrial policy (CHIPS Act, export controls). The Brazilian press treats him as a strategic asset for inbound US tech investment.
