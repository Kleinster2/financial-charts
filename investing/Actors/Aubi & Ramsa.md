**Aubi & Ramsa** — Miami-based luxury ice cream brand, the world's first 21+ ice cream concept. All flavors infused with premium spirits at 4.9% ABV. Five owned-and-operated "ice cream bars" across Florida and New York, plus retail and DTC channels.

---

## Synopsis

Aubi & Ramsa occupies a niche at the intersection of premium ice cream and spirits — a category that barely existed before them. The concept is simple: small-batch ice cream made with top-shelf liquor ([[Macallan]], [[Four Roses]], Veuve Clicquot, [[Hendrick's]]), sold in a bar-like retail environment where you must be 21+ to enter. The brand is as much a platform for liquor companies — giving consumers a novel format to experience their products — as it is a food business. That dual identity (dessert shop + spirits marketing channel) is the strategic moat.

The company is tiny: 1-10 employees, no disclosed funding or revenue, bootstrapped by founders with backgrounds in ice cream production and creative direction. But the footprint punches above its weight — locations in Miami Design District, two Hard Rock casino properties, and two Manhattan locations (NoMad and Meatpacking). Retail distribution through [[Total Wine]] (14 stores) and Joseph's Classic Market (4 [[Florida]] stores), plus nationwide DTC shipping via Goldbelly. The business model is premium experiential retail with high-margin pints, not volume. A Wynwood location opened and subsequently closed, suggesting the unit economics are location-dependent. The brand plays best in luxury foot-traffic corridors and hospitality venues.

## Founders

- Matias Aubi — creative director background. Met Rafael Ramsa while both worked as creative directors at an agency network in [[Los Angeles]]. Developed the brand concept, identity, and the 21+ retail format.

- Suzanne Buenfil — co-founder, operational side. Background in ice cream production alongside Ramsa.

## Business model

- Experiential retail — bar-format stores in luxury corridors (Design District, Meatpacking) and hospitality venues (Hard Rock casinos). The 21+ gate creates exclusivity and novelty.
- Spirits brand partnerships — the stores function as a marketing platform for liquor brands. [[Don Papa]] Rum did an exclusive launch event at the Wynwood location (Mar 2021). This dual role (dessert + spirits activation) likely subsidizes or supplements the ice cream economics.
- Retail & DTC — pints through [[Total Wine]], Joseph's Classic Market, and Goldbelly for nationwide reach.
- Design scalability — partnered with Floresta Partners (Columbus, OH) to create adaptable store prototypes for non-traditional locations (casino floors, hotel lobbies). This suggests a franchise-ready or license-ready concept.
