**Kayhan Space** — Space domain awareness and traffic coordination platform. Launched [[Satcat Terminal]] on March 20, 2026 — a Bloomberg-terminal-style intelligence product for investors, insurers, and enterprises across 36,000+ tracked orbital objects, including 11,000+ satellites with daily-refreshed trajectories. Satcat Operations (the operator-facing tier) covers 90%+ of operational LEO satellites across 50+ satellite operator customers, making Kayhan the largest commercial space traffic coordination platform globally.

The Kayhan investment thesis tracks what Bloomberg did for fixed income in the 1980s: as orbital activity scales (Starlink at 10K simultaneous satellites, Project Sunrise filing 51,600 sats, SpaceX FCC filing 1M), the *information* layer becomes the choke point. Investors pricing equity, insurers underwriting risk, and operators avoiding collisions all need a shared data backbone — and one provider tends to win the standardized layer.

## Satcat Terminal — Bloomberg analog for space (Mar 20, 2026)

Per [[Space Capital]] (Chad Anderson, Apr 2026): "The orbital economy now has a data terminal. What comes next is pricing, risk models, and derivatives."

| Satcat Terminal feature | Description |
|---|---|
| AI chat agent | Plain-language queries against the catalog |
| Object catalog | 36,000+ orbital objects |
| Daily satellite trajectories | 11,000+ satellites refreshed daily |
| Use cases | Constellation health, expansion cadence, anomalous behavior |
| Audience | Investors, insurers, enterprises |

The terminal is downstream of Satcat Operations (the operator-tier traffic coordination platform). The product thesis: orbital data only becomes investable when an analyst can query "what's happening to constellation X" the way they'd query "what's happening to bond X" today.
