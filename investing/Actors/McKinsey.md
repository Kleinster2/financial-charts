# McKinsey

**McKinsey** (McKinsey & Company) — largest management consulting firm globally by revenue. One of the "Big Three" (MBB) alongside [[BCG]] and Bain. [[OpenAI Frontier]] consulting partner. Claims AI and related technology consulting accounted for 40% of revenue (2025). CEO says the firm has built 25,000 [[AI agents]] to support its 40,000-person workforce. Internal AI platform Lilli was breached by [[CodeWall]]'s autonomous AI agent in February 2026, exposing 46.5M chat messages and the firm's full AI organizational structure.

## AI and Lilli

McKinsey has leaned heavily into AI both as a revenue line and as internal infrastructure. The firm claimed in 2025 that consulting on AI and related technology accounted for 40% of revenue. Its CEO said in 2026 it had built 25,000 AI "agents" to support its 40,000 staff.

Lilli is McKinsey's internal AI platform, used by all 40,000 staff for strategy planning, data analysis, and creating project plans and presentations for clients. As of the [[CodeWall]] breach disclosure (Mar 2026), the system contained 46.5M chat messages, 57,000 user accounts, 384,000 AI assistants, and 94,000 workspaces.

### Lilli breach (Feb–Mar 2026)

[[CodeWall]], a one-person cybersecurity firm run by Paul Price, said its own AI agent gained full read and write access to Lilli's entire production database within 2 hours. The breach exposed:

- 46.5M chat messages (strategy, data analysis, client-facing work)
- 57,000 user accounts
- 384,000 AI assistants
- 94,000 workspaces — what CodeWall called "the firm's intellectual crown jewels"
- 728,000 "sensitive" file names (Excel, PowerPoint, Word) — though McKinsey says the files themselves were stored separately and "never at risk"
- Lilli's system prompts and AI model configurations — "revealing exactly how the AI was instructed to behave, what guardrails existed"

CodeWall's AI agent autonomously selected McKinsey as a target. The agent followed McKinsey's published ethical hacking guidelines and stopped itself after discovering the vulnerabilities rather than attempting to access files.

McKinsey's security team was alerted end of February 2026. The firm patched the identified holes and took its development environment offline within hours. McKinsey said a third-party forensics investigation found "no evidence that client data or client confidential information were accessed by this researcher or any other unauthorized third party."

The timing is awkward: McKinsey is actively pitching blue-chip companies on AI adoption while its own AI system was found to have fundamental security flaws. CodeWall's framing: "In the AI era, the threat landscape is shifting drastically — AI agents autonomously selecting and attacking targets will become the new normal."

Source: FT (Mar 12, 2026) — Ellesheva Kissin and Stephen Foley.

## Ownership

Private partnership — no external investors, no cap table. Partners are elected internally and hold equity stakes in the firm. McKinsey has never taken outside funding. Revenue estimated at ~$16B (2024, various press reports). The firm discloses no financials.
