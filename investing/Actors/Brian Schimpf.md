**Brian Schimpf** is the CEO of [[Anduril]], the defense technology company valued at ~$31B. He runs day-to-day operations while co-founder [[Palmer Luckey]] focuses on product vision and public-facing advocacy. Before Anduril, Schimpf was head of engineering at [[Palantir]], where he built core elements of the data infrastructure that became Palantir's government platform — experience that directly informed Anduril's software-defined defense approach.

Schimpf co-founded Anduril in 2017 alongside Luckey, [[Trae Stephens]] ([[Founders Fund]] partner), Matt Grimm, and Joe Chen. The division of labor — Luckey as the visionary and brand, Schimpf as the operational executor — mirrors patterns at other defense/tech startups where the CEO is deliberately less public than the founder. Schimpf's engineering background at Palantir is core to Anduril's thesis that defense contracting should be software-first: build autonomous systems (Lattice OS, Ghost drones, Altius munitions) using Silicon Valley engineering practices rather than traditional cost-plus procurement.

Anduril has raised over $4B in venture funding and is positioning to compete with traditional defense primes ([[Lockheed Martin]], [[RTX]], [[Northrop Grumman]]) for major DoD programs.
