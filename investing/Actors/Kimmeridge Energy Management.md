# Kimmeridge Energy Management

Kimmeridge Energy Management is an energy-focused private equity and activist investment firm founded in 2012 by Ben Dell, a former Bernstein analyst and Goldman Sachs banker. The firm manages ~$5B+ and specializes in upstream oil and gas, combining direct investment in E&P assets with activist campaigns at public companies. Kimmeridge is named after the Kimmeridge Clay formation in Dorset, England — a prolific oil source rock.

Kimmeridge's strategy centers on two pillars: acquiring and operating E&P assets directly (often in Appalachian natural gas), and running activist campaigns to unlock value at public energy companies. The firm's most notable recent move was its competing $6.0B bid for [[Ascent Resources]] in December 2025, offering a 10% premium over [[Energy Minerals Group|EMG]]'s $5.5B [[Continuation fund|continuation vehicle]] valuation. Kimmeridge has also run campaigns at SilverBow Resources and other mid-cap E&P names. Dell's thesis is that energy companies chronically underinvest in returns to shareholders and overinvest in production growth, and that activist pressure can close the valuation gap.
