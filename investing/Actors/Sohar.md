# Sohar

Omani deep-water industrial port and free zone on the [[Gulf of Oman]], roughly 200 km northwest of Muscat and — critically — outside the [[Strait of Hormuz]]. Developed from 2002 as a joint venture with the Port of Rotterdam, Sohar anchors Oman's heavy-industry diversification (refining, petrochemicals, metals) and, with [[Fujairah]], forms the eastern (Gulf of Oman) side of the Hormuz approaches.

Its relevance to the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] is positional: because Sohar and [[Fujairah]] sit on the seaward side of the chokepoint, they became the natural staging anchorage for vessels waiting out the blockade and the selective-transit regime. FT analysis of European Space Agency (ESA) Sentinel-1 radar imagery counted 441 large tanker-sized vessels clustered off Sohar and Fujairah on Jun 21, 2026, waiting for the strait's full reopening after the preliminary US-[[Iran]] deal.
