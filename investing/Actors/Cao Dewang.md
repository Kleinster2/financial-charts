Cao Dewang (also known as Cho Tak Wong) is a Chinese billionaire industrialist and the founder of [[Fuyao Glass Industry Group]], the world's largest automotive glass manufacturer by market share (~30% globally). Born in 1946 in Fuqing, Fujian Province, he built Fuyao from a small township enterprise in 1987 into a global supplier serving every major automaker, with factories in [[China]], the [[United States]], [[Russia]], [[Germany]], and [[Japan]].

Cao is also one of China's most prominent philanthropists, having donated over RMB 16B (~$2.4B) to charitable causes — primarily education and disaster relief. He was featured prominently in the Netflix/[[Higher Ground Productions]] documentary *American Factory* (2019), which chronicled the culture clash at Fuyao's Moraine, Ohio plant (a repurposed GM factory) and won the Academy Award for Best Documentary. He stepped down as chairman of Fuyao Glass in October 2025, handing the role to his son [[Cao Hui]], completing a generational transition at China's most globally visible manufacturing company.
