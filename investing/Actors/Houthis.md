# Houthis

Iran-backed militia movement in [[Yemen]], formally Ansar Allah. Part of the "axis of resistance" aligned with [[Iran]] and [[Hezbollah]]; controls much of northern and western Yemen including Sana'a. The group's Red Sea missile and drone attacks on international shipping since 2023 have been a persistent shipping-route risk (rerouting traffic around Africa via Cape of Good Hope) and a transmission channel for Iranian regional pressure. Received satellite targeting imagery from [[Chang Guang Satellite Technology]] (per FT, 2025) — the first publicly documented case of a Chinese commercial-space firm supplying targeting data to a US-designated adversary, setting the precedent for the subsequent [[IRGC Chinese satellite targeting March 2026|IRGC/TEE-01B arrangement]].

## Market-relevant dynamics

- Red Sea shipping disruption: Suez transit volumes collapsed 2023-2026 as carriers rerouted around Cape of Good Hope. See [[Sectors/Shipping]].
- Tanker war-risk insurance: Houthi targeting raises P&I club war-risk premia.
- Oil transit: Houthi-adjacent threat to Bab el-Mandeb is a secondary constraint on Gulf oil flows, on top of the [[Strait of Hormuz]] primary chokepoint. See [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]].
- Iranian escalation channel: if [[Iran]] seeks to expand war costs, re-tasking the Houthis against Red Sea shipping is a standard lever — flagged by [[Mick Mulroy]] in the [[MEI Iran war economic panel March 2026]].
