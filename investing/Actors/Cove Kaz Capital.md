#actor #company #private #mining #critical-minerals

Cove Kaz Capital — U.S. private mining company developing what its joint-venture partner describes as the world's largest known undeveloped [[Tungsten]] resource: the Northern Katpar and Upper Kairakty deposits in central Kazakhstan. JV partner is [[Tau-Ken Samruk]] (Kazakhstan's state-owned national mining company); Cove Kaz holds 70% of the operating entity Severniy Katpar LLP, Tau-Ken Samruk holds 30%. As of May 2026, the project carries up to $1.6bn in U.S. federal letters of interest ($900M from [[EXIM Bank]], $700M from [[DFC]]) and is going public on Nasdaq via a reverse merger with [[Skyline Builders]] (SKBL) announced April 30, 2026.

## Synopsis

Cove Kaz is one of the highest-profile critical-minerals plays of the second [[Donald Trump|Trump]] term: a privately-held U.S. company with no current production, going public via the [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]] operated by [[Dominari Holdings]] / [[American Ventures]], with the U.S. federal financing apparatus ([[EXIM Bank]] + [[DFC]]) backstopping the construction phase. The economic case rests on three legs: (1) tungsten's classification as a strategic critical mineral with concentrated [[China]] supply (~83% of global output); (2) the scale of the Kazakh resource (12,000 mtpa combined planned production, ~15% of current global output); and (3) the explicit U.S.-Kazakhstan supply-chain partnership angle, which the second Trump administration has pursued as part of a broader strategy to source critical minerals from non-China-aligned jurisdictions ([[Project Vault]]).

The vehicle's relevance to the vault is twofold: it is the largest mining placement in the [[American Ventures]] SPV book (Series XIX SKBL + Series XXXII SKBL II together approach the largest single SPV in the FT bubble chart), and it is the case where the federal-policy alignment is most explicit — the EXIM and DFC letters were issued concurrently with the merger announcement.

## The Kazakh tungsten deposits

The Northern Katpar and Upper Kairakty deposits lie in central Kazakhstan and were originally explored under the Soviet Union. They were assessed by the Kazakh state through Tau-Ken Samruk and brought to a Western development partner under the JV structure. Per the project's own framing, the combined resource is the largest known undeveloped tungsten resource globally.

Tungsten's importance to Western supply-chain strategy:
- Tungsten carbide is the core material for armor-piercing kinetic-energy ammunition, mining drill bits, machine-tool inserts, and high-temperature defense components
- Global production is heavily concentrated in [[China]] (~83% of mined output as of 2024)
- The U.S. has no commercial-scale tungsten mining; domestic supply has historically come from secondary recovery, recycling, and small specialty operations
- Tungsten was on the U.S. Geological Survey list of critical minerals at the start of the second Trump term and is a target of [[Project Vault]] strategic stockpiling

The 12,000 mtpa target represents a step-change in global supply: ~15% of current production from a single new operating site. Whether that target is achievable on the announced timeline is the central project-execution question.

## U.S. federal financing structure

| Agency | Instrument | Up-to amount | Purpose |
|--------|------------|--------------|---------|
| [[EXIM Bank]] | Letter of interest | $900M | Project financing for development of Northern Katpar / Upper Kairakty |
| [[DFC]] | Letter of interest | $700M | Project financing complement; supply-chain security |

A letter of interest is non-binding and does not commit the agency to fund. It signals that the project is eligible for further diligence and that the agencies are prepared to consider commitment. The combined $1.6bn ceiling is set against the ~$1.1bn estimated total development cost, providing comfortable coverage if the diligence converts to commitment.

The EXIM letter sits alongside the [[Project Vault]] $10bn EXIM commitment for critical-minerals stockpiling — the second-Trump-term EXIM has been the primary federal vehicle for critical-minerals project finance.

## The Skyline Builders reverse merger (announced Apr 30, 2026)

| Detail | Value |

The merger was announced via BusinessWire on April 30, 2026, the same day the EXIM and DFC letters were disclosed. The structure follows the same mechanic [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]] documents in detail — Dominari was the placement agent, American Ventures recapitalized the SKBL shell, and Cove Kaz will be the surviving operating entity post-merger.

The choice of [[Skyline Builders]] as the listed shell carries reputational tail-risk: SKBL was named in the [[House Select Committee on China]]'s March 8, 2026 letter to [[Dominari Securities]] requesting information on Chinese small-cap IPOs implicated in suspected ramp-and-dump manipulation. Skyline's stock dropped ~90% in a single trading session in July 2024.

## Strategic context

The deal is the most explicit federal-policy-alignment case in the [[American Ventures]] SPV book:
- Sector matches a Trump-administration priority (critical minerals; counter-China supply chain)
- Federal financing apparatus issued letters concurrent with the merger announcement
- Trump-family-affiliated brokerage ([[Dominari Securities]]) and SPV operator ([[American Ventures]]) ran the transaction
- Trump brothers are anchor LPs in the Series XIX SKBL and Series XXXII SKBL II vehicles per the FT

The FT explicitly notes there is no suggestion the brothers had advance knowledge of the EXIM/DFC letters or influenced their issuance. The structural observation is that the federal-policy event landed on the same day as the merger announcement.
