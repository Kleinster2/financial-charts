# Durational Capital

**Durational Capital** is a private equity firm based in New York, founded by Henry Kravis's former KKR colleague John Bassler. The firm focuses on acquiring consumer and retail brands that have struggled in public markets or face operational turnarounds. The name reflects the strategy — patient, long-duration capital for companies that need time to restructure without quarterly earnings pressure.

The marquee deal is [[Casper Sleep]], the DTC mattress company that Durational acquired in November 2022 for ~$286M ($6.90/share), taking it private after a disastrous public-market run. Casper had IPO'd in February 2020 at $12/share (already well below its peak private valuation of $1.1B) and proceeded to lose money every quarter. The take-private was a bet that Casper's brand recognition and customer acquisition infrastructure had value if freed from the pressure to show public-market growth metrics.

Durational represents a niche within PE: firms that specifically target post-SPAC or post-IPO consumer brands trading at distressed valuations. The DTC mattress, skincare, and meal-kit categories produced a cohort of companies that raised at inflated private valuations, failed in public markets, and became take-private candidates.
