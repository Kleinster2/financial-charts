**Patrick Pouyanné** is the CEO and Chairman of [[TotalEnergies]], the fourth-largest Western oil major by market capitalization. An École Polytechnique and Mines ParisTech graduate, he spent his early career in the French government (cabinet of PM Alain Juppé, Ministry of Industry) before joining Total in 1997. He became CEO in October 2014 following the sudden death of Christophe de Margerie in a Moscow runway accident, inheriting a company mid-transition. Pouyanné has led TotalEnergies' "multi-energy" strategy — maintaining upstream oil and gas investment while building an integrated [[LNG]] portfolio and a growing renewable power business (40 GW target by 2030). He has been openly skeptical of aggressive EU climate mandates, predicting in January 2026 that the [[Sustainable Aviation Fuel|SAF]] blending mandate would be watered down. His push to list TotalEnergies on the [[NYSE]] (exploring a primary US listing) reflects frustration with European energy sector valuations and a bet that US capital markets will reward the integrated model more generously.
