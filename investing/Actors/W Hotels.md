**W Hotels** is a luxury lifestyle hotel brand under [[Marriott International]], targeting design-conscious younger travelers with nightlife-inflected properties in major cities worldwide.

## Synopsis

W Hotels launched in 1998 as [[Starwood Hotels]]' answer to the boutique hotel movement — the concept was a full-service luxury hotel with the energy of a nightclub, featuring DJ-curated lobbies, signature cocktail bars, and design-forward rooms. The brand expanded to ~60 properties globally before [[Marriott International]]'s $13.6B acquisition of Starwood in 2016 folded W into Marriott's luxury portfolio alongside The Ritz-Carlton, St. Regis, and Edition. Within Marriott's hierarchy, W occupies the "distinctive luxury" tier — edgier than Ritz-Carlton, more established than Edition.

The W's relevance to the vault is primarily through its NYC properties and restaurant partnerships. The W New York — Union Square hosts [[Mercer Street Hospitality]]'s Seahorse restaurant (opened Sep 2025), illustrating the trend of luxury hotels outsourcing F&B to independent restaurant groups rather than running in-house operations. This model — hotel provides the real estate and foot traffic, restaurant group provides the culinary brand — has become standard in the luxury tier as hotels realized they cannot compete with dedicated restaurateurs on food quality or cultural cachet. W properties in NYC, Miami, and LA function as high-visibility restaurant platforms.
