**Guodong Zhang** is a co-founder of [[xAI]] who led pre-training of [[Grok]] models before being relieved of primary duties by [[Elon Musk]] over coding product data quality issues. His last day at xAI was March 13, 2026 — an early and high-profile departure from one of the most closely watched AI startups.

## Synopsis

Zhang was part of the founding team that Musk assembled for xAI in 2023, drawn from [[Google DeepMind]], [[OpenAI]], and other top AI labs. His role overseeing Grok pre-training put him at the center of xAI's core product: the large language model that powers the chatbot integrated into [[X]] (formerly Twitter) and is positioned as Musk's competitor to [[OpenAI]]'s GPT and [[Anthropic]]'s Claude. Pre-training — the phase where a model learns from massive text corpora before fine-tuning — is the most compute-intensive and architecturally consequential stage of LLM development. Getting it right determines the model's ceiling; getting it wrong wastes hundreds of millions of dollars in GPU time.

The departure narrative centers on data quality issues in xAI's coding products. [[Elon Musk]] reportedly blamed Zhang for quality problems that affected Grok's code generation capabilities — a domain where accuracy matters acutely because code either works or it does not, unlike the softer quality gradients of conversational AI. Being publicly relieved of duties at a Musk company carries reputational weight in both directions: Musk's management style involves frequent high-profile firings (Tesla, SpaceX, Twitter/X), so the departure may say more about organizational dynamics than about Zhang's technical competence. For the AI talent market, Zhang's availability matters — a pre-training lead with co-founder status at a $50B+ valued company will attract interest from competing labs and well-funded startups.
