**AMI Labs** (Advanced Machine Intelligence) — Frontier AI lab building world models based on JEPA architecture. Founded by [[Yann LeCun]] after leaving [[Meta]]. Raised $1.03B seed — largest in European history.

---

## Synopsis

AMI Labs is [[Yann LeCun]]'s contrarian bet against the LLM paradigm. The company builds "world models" — AI systems that learn abstract representations of physical reality from video, audio, and sensor data rather than predicting text tokens. Its technical foundation is JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture), a framework LeCun proposed in 2022 at [[Meta]]'s FAIR lab, which predicts in abstract representation space rather than at the pixel level. [[Meta]] published V-JEPA 2 (demonstrating zero-shot robot control) before LeCun's departure, effectively proving the concept while he was still at the company.

The $1.03B seed round (March 2026) at a $3.5B pre-money valuation is the largest seed round in European history. The investor list reads as a who's-who of strategic capital: [[Jeff Bezos]], [[NVIDIA]], [[Samsung]], [[Temasek]], [[Toyota]] Ventures, [[Eric Schmidt]], [[Mark Cuban]], plus French industrial dynasties (Arnault, Pinault, Mulliez, Dassault). The company launched with ~12 researchers across Paris, New York, Montreal, and Singapore, and its leadership is almost entirely ex-[[Meta]] FAIR. Initial application targets: manufacturing, robotics, aerospace, biomedical, and transport — sectors where understanding physics matters more than generating text.

The key risk is execution timeline. LeCun has argued that world models will become "the dominant AI architecture" in 3-5 years, but JEPA has not yet been proven at scale outside Meta's research context. The company is betting that the LLM paradigm will hit fundamental limits in physical world understanding — a thesis that requires years of R&D before commercial validation while competitors like [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]] ship revenue-generating products today.

## Evolution

The story of AMI Labs is the story of a fundamental disagreement about how AI should work — and one of the most consequential talent departures in tech history.

- 2022: [[Yann LeCun]] publishes the JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) position paper while at [[Meta]]'s FAIR lab, arguing that predicting in abstract representation space — not pixel-level or token-level — is the path to human-like AI understanding. The paper is widely discussed but viewed as theoretical, overshadowed by [[OpenAI]]'s GPT-4 launch and the LLM arms race.

- October 2025: [[Meta]] lays off 600 employees from its Superintelligence Labs division, including researchers from FAIR — the unit LeCun built from scratch in 2013. Around the same time, [[Mark Zuckerberg]] brings in [[Alexandr Wang]] (28, former CEO of [[Scale AI]]) to lead the new Superintelligence Labs after a $14.5B investment in [[Scale AI]]. LeCun is effectively forced to report to Wang. The friction is structural: LeCun championed open-source AI research; Wang's team favors a more closed approach. LeCun later calls Wang "young and inexperienced" and says publicly: "You certainly don't tell a researcher like me what to do."

- November 19, 2025: LeCun publicly confirms his departure from [[Meta]] after 10+ years as Chief AI Scientist. The move sends a signal through the AI industry — one of the three "Godfathers of Deep Learning" is going independent. LeCun predicts more [[Meta]] AI employee departures will follow.

- December 2025: Fortune reports LeCun is targeting a $3.5B valuation before the company has launched or hired. The audacity of the number — for a pre-revenue, pre-product company — reflects the market's appetite for founder-tier AI bets and the growing skepticism that LLMs alone can achieve general intelligence.

- January 2026: AMI Labs is formally announced. Alexandre LeBrun (ex-FAIR research engineer, founder of health AI startup Nabla) is named CEO. Laurent Solly (ex-[[Meta]] VP Europe) is COO. The leadership team is essentially a FAIR reunion: Saining Xie (Chief Science Officer, ex-[[Google DeepMind]]), Pascale Fung (Chief Research & Innovation Officer, ex-[[Meta]]), Michael Rabbat (VP World Models, ex-[[Meta]]). ~12 researchers across four offices at launch.

- March 9-10, 2026: The $1.03B seed round closes — largest seed in European history. Co-led by Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, and [[Jeff Bezos]]'s Bezos Expeditions. Strategic investors include [[NVIDIA]], [[Samsung]], [[Temasek]], [[Toyota]] Ventures, Dassault, Publicis, and French family offices (Arnault via Aglaé Lab, Pinault via Artémis, Mulliez). Individual angels include Tim Berners-Lee, [[Eric Schmidt]], and [[Mark Cuban]]. Bpifrance participates as the French sovereign digital venture fund.

## Technology

JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture):
- Learns to predict in abstract representation space, not pixel-level or token-level
- Trained on multimodal sensory data: video, audio, lidar, robot telemetry
- Aims to learn causal structure and intuitive physics — how a baby learns about gravity
- V-JEPA 2 (published by [[Meta]] before LeCun's departure) demonstrated zero-shot robot control

Thesis: LLMs are fundamentally limited because they learn only from text/tokens and cannot truly understand the physical world. [[World Models]] that learn from sensory data will enable AI systems that reason, plan complex action sequences, and operate safely in physical environments.

Target applications: Manufacturing, robotics, aerospace, biomedical, transport

## Risks

- Execution timeline: JEPA has not been proven at scale outside [[Meta]]'s research context. Commercial validation requires years of R&D while competitors ship revenue today
- Talent concentration: near-total reliance on ex-[[Meta]] FAIR team — cultural homogeneity risk
- LLM momentum: if LLMs prove sufficient for physical-world tasks (via multimodal extensions), the world model thesis weakens
- Burn rate: $1.03B is substantial but frontier AI R&D is expensive — [[Meta]] spent $37B+ on AI capex in 2024 alone
