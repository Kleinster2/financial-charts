Gentherm is the global leader in automotive thermal management technology, designing and manufacturing heated and cooled seat systems, steering wheel heaters, battery thermal management modules, and climate control solutions for passenger vehicles. Headquartered in Northville, Michigan, the company supplies nearly every major automaker globally and holds dominant market share in seat climate systems (~50%+ of the global market). Its thermoelectric technology portfolio is protected by extensive patents.

The company traces its roots to Amerigon, founded in 1991 to commercialize thermoelectric technology (originally developed for NASA). It merged with W.E.T. Automotive Systems in 2012, creating the current Gentherm. The EV transition is a structural tailwind: battery thermal management is more critical in EVs than ICE vehicles (battery performance, range, and safety all depend on precise temperature control), and heated/cooled seats replace HVAC-dependent comfort systems, improving EV range efficiency. Phil Eyler served as president/CEO from 2017 to 2025 before becoming board chair of [[Sleep Number]]. The company generates roughly $1.2B in annual revenue with mid-teens EBITDA margins.
