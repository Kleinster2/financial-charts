# Borr Drilling

International offshore jack-up drilling contractor. Listed on NYSE (BORR) and Oslo Bors. Operates one of the world's youngest and most modern jack-up fleets.

---

## Synopsis

Borr Drilling emerged from the 2014-2020 offshore drilling downturn as a consolidation play, acquiring modern jack-up rigs at distressed prices when legacy drillers went bankrupt. The fleet consists primarily of premium jack-up rigs built after 2010, giving Borr a structural age advantage — newer rigs command higher day rates, face lower maintenance costs, and satisfy increasingly stringent operator ESG requirements. The company has ~20 rigs deployed across the Middle East, West Africa, Southeast Asia, and the North Sea.

The investment case is a leveraged bet on shallow-water drilling demand. Jack-up rigs serve water depths under ~400 feet, where NOCs in the [[Middle East]] and West Africa are ramping development spending. Day rates for premium jack-ups have recovered from sub-$100K/day troughs to $150-180K/day, driving Borr toward positive free cash flow after years of debt restructuring. The risk is the balance sheet — Borr carries significant debt from its acquisition spree, and any softening in day rates or utilization could stress covenants. [[Jeff Currie]] (former Goldman commodity head) joining the board signals confidence in the offshore supercycle thesis.
