**Cariad** — [[Volkswagen]] Group's in-house automotive software subsidiary, responsible for the group's unified software platform across VW, [[Audi]], [[Porsche]], and other group brands. Has struggled with delivery delays, cost overruns, and management turnover since its 2020 founding. Slated for 1,600 job cuts by 2030 as part of VW's March 2026 restructuring.

## Strategic position

Cariad was intended to close VW's competitive gap versus [[Tesla]] and Chinese EV OEMs like [[BYD]] and [[Xiaomi]] on software-defined-vehicle capability. Execution has lagged: the E3 2.0 architecture slipped multiple times, delaying [[Audi]] Q6 e-tron and [[Porsche]] Macan EV launches. VW has turned to external partnerships — the November 2024 [[Rivian]] JV is a tacit admission that in-house development alone cannot close the gap quickly enough.
