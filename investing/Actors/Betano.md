# Betano

The dominant sports betting brand in [[Brazil]] and a top-tier operator across 20 regulated markets, owned by Greek private company Kaizen Gaming (formerly GML Interactive). **Betano** holds License #1 in Brazil's newly regulated market and commands 27% market share — more than double the next competitor by traffic.

---

## Synopsis

Betano is the international brand of Kaizen Gaming, a privately held Greek company founded in 2012 by George Daskalakis and Dimitris Maris. The company generated EUR 2.2 billion in revenue in 2024 (~50% YoY growth), with EBITDA growing ~100% YoY, making it one of the fastest-growing gambling operators globally. The corporate structure is now split: [[OPAP]] owns 100% of the Greek/Cypriot brand Stoiximan (acquired the final 15.51% for EUR 191.6M in July 2025), while the Betano international business is ~63.25% founder-owned and ~36.75% held by [[Allwyn]] (acquired from OPAP in Dec 2022 for EUR 74.2M — a stake now worth multiples of that given revenue growth). [[Allwyn]] and [[OPAP]] are merging in a EUR 16 billion deal (announced Oct 2025, expected close H1 2026), meaning the Betano minority stake will sit inside the world's second-largest listed gambling company.

Brazil is Betano's crown jewel. The country launched regulated sports betting on January 1, 2025 under [[Lei das Bets]] (Law 14,790/2023), and Betano was the first operator licensed — License #1 via SIGAP. In its first regulated year, the Brazilian market generated R$ 37 billion (~USD 7B) in GGR, making it the world's 5th-largest betting market. Betano captured 27% market share with 467 million monthly hits, more than double the #2 operator ([[Superbet]] at 225M). The strategy is football-first: naming rights for the Brasileirao Serie A, Serie B, and Copa do Brasil (~R$ 80M/season), plus a R$ 900M master sponsorship of [[Flamengo]] through 2028 — the largest in South American football history. The regulatory environment remains in flux: a proposed GGR tax increase from 12% to 18% was blocked by Congress, a 15% deposit tax (CIDE-Bets) was rejected by the Chamber in Feb 2026, but new bills keep surfacing. The social costs are severe — 27% of [[Bolsa Familia]] payments were transferred to betting sites in Jan 2025, prompting the government to block ~900,000 welfare recipients from platforms.

The key question is valuation. Kaizen Gaming is private with no disclosed plans to IPO. EUR 2.2B revenue growing at 50%, EBITDA doubling, dominant position in the world's 5th-largest and fastest-growing betting market — the implied enterprise value is likely EUR 10B+ by any public-comp framework ([[Flutter Entertainment]] trades at ~5.5x revenue, [[DraftKings]] at ~3.5x). The Allwyn stake, bought for EUR 74.2M in 2022, is an extraordinary embedded option. Risk: Brazil regulatory uncertainty (tax escalation, advertising restrictions, welfare-recipient bans reducing TAM), the social/political backlash against betting that could produce more aggressive intervention, and key-man risk with founder Daskalakis driving strategy.

## Evolution

The story of Betano is the story of two Greek entrepreneurs who built a regional online bookie into a EUR 2.2B-revenue global platform — by being first in every market that mattered and letting the football-mad cultures of Southern Europe and Latin America do the rest.

- 2012 — George Daskalakis (ex-[[bwin]] country manager since 2002, founder of Poker.gr in 2006) and Dimitris Maris (media/tech investor, MSc from UCL, later Marathon Venture Capital and Velocity Partners VC) registered Gambling Malta Limited in Athens. The initial product was Stoiximan, targeting the Greek market. [[Greece]]'s gambling landscape was dominated by [[OPAP]] (the state lottery monopoly), but online sports betting was a regulatory grey zone that Stoiximan exploited with a Malta license.

- 2014-2017 — Stoiximan grew rapidly in [[Greece]] and Cyprus, becoming the leading online sportsbook by traffic. The founders saw that Greece alone was a ceiling — 10M population, economic crisis, regulatory uncertainty. They began planning international expansion under a new brand. The name Betano was chosen for non-Greek markets, avoiding the Greek-language connotation of Stoiximan (from *stoichima*, Greek for "bet").

- 2018 — The Betano brand debuted in Romania and Portugal — two Southern European markets with established regulatory frameworks and football-obsessed cultures. Romania became Betano's first proof-of-concept outside Greece, reaching podium market share within two years.

- 2019-2020 — [[OPAP]], the Greek lottery monopoly (itself controlled by Czech investor [[Allwyn]]/KKCG), acquired a majority stake in Stoiximan (the Greek/Cypriot entity) while also taking a 36.75% minority stake in Kaizen Gaming International (the Betano international business). This gave Kaizen cash for expansion while creating the structural split that defines the company today: Stoiximan = Greek domestic (now 100% OPAP), Betano = everything else (majority founder-owned). The company reorganized as GML Interactive Ltd in 2018, then rebranded to Kaizen Gaming in 2020.

- 2020-2021 — Like every online gambling operator, Kaizen benefited from the pandemic-driven shift to digital. But the bigger move was Latin America. Betano launched in [[Brazil]] — at the time an unregulated but massive market where tens of millions were already betting on grey-market platforms. The playbook: football sponsorships first, platform second. Betano signed deals with Brazilian Serie A clubs and began building the brand before regulation even existed.

- 2022 — In December, [[Allwyn]] acquired [[OPAP]]'s 36.75% minority stake in Kaizen Gaming International for EUR 74.2 million. This implied a ~EUR 200M valuation for the entire Betano international business — a figure that looks absurdly cheap in retrospect given 2024 revenues of EUR 2.2B. The deal made strategic sense for Allwyn (lottery giant diversifying into sports betting) and financial sense for OPAP (crystallizing a gain while retaining 100% of Stoiximan). For the founders, it meant their majority stake was now backstopped by one of [[Europe]]'s largest gambling companies.

- 2023-2024 — [[Brazil]] passed [[Lei das Bets]] (Law 14,790/2023) in December 2023, creating the regulatory framework for fixed-odds betting. Betano was the first company to submit a license application via SIGAP on May 26, 2024 — and received License #1. Simultaneously, Betano locked up the most valuable football sponsorship real estate in the country: naming rights for the Brasileirao Serie A ("Serie A Betano"), Serie B, and Copa do Brasil in a 3-year deal worth ~R$ 80M/season (~USD 16M) — the CBF's largest naming rights contract ever. Revenue hit EUR 2.2B in 2024 with 50% growth and EBITDA doubling. The company expanded to 18+ regulated markets across Europe, Latin America, and [[Canada]].

- 2025 — January 1: [[Brazil]]'s regulated market went live. Betano immediately led with 27% market share and 467M monthly hits. In August, Betano signed a R$ 900 million master sponsorship with [[Flamengo]] through 2028 (R$ 265M/season + variables + R$ 12M tax incentives) — the largest sponsorship in South American football, covering men's football, women's, basketball, volleyball, and Flamengo TV. In July, Daskalakis stepped down from Stoiximan to focus exclusively on Betano international, signaling where the growth is. [[OPAP]] acquired the remaining 15.51% of Stoiximan for EUR 191.6M, completing the corporate divorce. In October, [[Allwyn]] and [[OPAP]] announced a EUR 16 billion merger — creating the world's second-largest listed gambling company and the largest listed lottery company.

- 2026 — Betano launched in Ghana in February (20th regulated market). On March 11, Kaizen acquired GameplAI, an AI-driven sports trading analytics firm (founded 2021 by Nikos Volakis and Graham Savage, B2B clients including [[Betsson]] and [[Superbet]]). In [[Brazil]], the regulatory environment remains unsettled: a proposed GGR tax hike from 12% to 18% was blocked by Congress, a 15% deposit tax (CIDE-Bets) was rejected by the Chamber in February, but new tax bills keep appearing. The [[Allwyn]]-[[OPAP]] merger is expected to close H1 2026.

### Regulatory framework

[[Lei das Bets]] — Law 14,790/2023 (signed Dec 2023). Regulates fixed-odds betting in [[Brazil]].

Regulator: SPA (Secretaria de Premios e Apostas), under the Ministry of Finance. Operators apply via SIGAP.

Tax regime (in flux):

| Item | Rate/Amount |
|------|-------------|
| GGR tax | 12% (Law 14,790/2023) |
| Proposed GGR increase | 18% (Provisional Measure 1,303/2025) — blocked by Congress |
| Alternative bill (PL 5,473/2025) | 15% (2026-2027), 18% (2028) — pending |
| CIDE-Bets (15% deposit tax) | Approved by Senate Dec 2025, rejected by Chamber Feb 2026 |
| License fee | R$ 30 million (5 years, covers up to 3 brands) |
| Capital requirement | R$ 30 million paid-in + R$ 5M financial reserves |

Enforcement:
- [[ANATEL]] blocked 18,000+ illegal betting websites since Oct 2024
- Mandatory .bet.br domain enables quick identification of licensed operators
- bet.br domain drew 1.7 billion visits in January 2025 — 2nd largest traffic source in [[Brazil]] after [[Google]]

CPI (Congressional Investigation): Senate CPI investigated gambling's impact on families, fraud, match-fixing, money laundering, advertising malpractice. Led by Senator Soraya Thronicke. Report rejected 4-3 vote — none of the proposed measures adopted.

## Strengths

- First-mover advantage in [[Brazil]]: License #1, 27% share, more than 2x the #2 operator by traffic — in a market growing from zero to world #5 in one year
- Football sponsorship moat: Brasileirao naming rights + [[Flamengo]] mega-deal create brand dominance that is extremely expensive for competitors to replicate
- Revenue scale and growth: EUR 2.2B at 50% growth with EBITDA doubling — rare combination at this scale
- Regulatory track record: live in 20 regulated markets; has navigated licensing in every jurisdiction
- Founder-led, majority founder-owned: aligned incentives, no public-market short-termism
- [[Allwyn]]/[[OPAP]] backing: 36.75% stake held by what will become the world's 2nd-largest listed gambling company
