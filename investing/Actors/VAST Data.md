**VAST Data** — Private AI data-platform and high-performance storage company focused on exabyte-scale AI and GPU-cluster workloads. New York-based, Israeli-founded, and one of the clearest AI-native challengers to legacy enterprise storage.

VAST matters because it reframes storage as a utilization problem, not just a capacity problem. In AI factories and neoclouds, the pitch is that better data architecture can lift expensive GPU utilization enough to justify premium software and systems spend. That is why VAST keeps surfacing next to [[CoreWeave]], [[Pure Storage]], and [[NVIDIA]] in the vault's AI infrastructure cluster.

---

## Distinction from Vast.ai

Not to be confused with [[Vast.ai]], the GPU rental marketplace. VAST Data sells data-platform and storage software for large-scale AI infrastructure, while Vast.ai sells access to compute capacity.

## Why VAST Data matters

The important strategic claim is that AI storage is no longer only about capacity and throughput. It is also about how quickly a cluster can turn expensive accelerators into useful output. VAST's pitch is that a unified, software-defined data layer can keep very large GPU estates fed more efficiently than legacy enterprise-storage stacks that were not built around AI factories.

That makes VAST directly relevant to both [[AI Storage]] and [[AI storage bottleneck]]. If GPU-hours are scarce and costly, then the storage vendor that improves utilization can capture budget that would otherwise have gone into incremental compute. In that sense VAST is less a traditional storage company than a data-infrastructure lever on AI capex efficiency.

---

## Platform and positioning

Official VAST materials now describe the product as an AI Operating System built on DASE architecture. The broader point is simpler: VAST is trying to unify high-performance storage, data management, and adjacent data services into a single layer for AI and large unstructured-data workloads.

That is why the company keeps winning attention in neocloud and GPU-cloud environments rather than only in classic enterprise-storage replacement cycles. The market is using VAST as a proxy for whether AI-native data architecture can displace incumbents before the incumbents' channel depth and certifications catch up.

## CoreWeave deal and AI-cloud wedge

Reuters' Nov 2025 report matters because it moved VAST from impressive private-company story into live AI-infrastructure relevance. The $1.17B CoreWeave agreement made VAST the primary data platform for a major GPU cloud and showed that the storage layer was becoming large enough to matter in its own right inside the neocloud buildout.

The readthrough is not only revenue scale. It is positioning. Once VAST becomes embedded in a large GPU cloud, it stops looking like a niche storage upstart and starts looking like part of the operating stack for AI factories. Reuters also said VAST continued serving other major customers including AWS, other neoclouds, and AI labs such as [[xAI]], which suggests the company is trying to become a horizontal data layer rather than a one-customer bet.

---

## Adoption signals

Official company materials said in late 2025 that more than 25% of the Fortune 100 and leading global GPU-cloud providers were using VAST for strategic AI initiatives. The stronger point for the vault is not logo-count vanity. It is that VAST appears to have crossed from storage-specialist mindshare into the shortlist for organizations building large AI clusters.

That is why VAST belongs as a durable actor note rather than only as a mention inside storage-sector tables.
