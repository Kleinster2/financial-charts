## Synopsis

The world's dominant [[Niobium]] producer with 77% global market share, operating the Araxa mine in Minas Gerais that contains ~70% of known global reserves. ~R$11.4B revenue (~$2.2B, 2023). Private, 70% owned by the Moreira Salles family. Now pivoting into battery materials — niobium titanium oxide (NTO) anodes for fast-charging lithium-ion batteries, with [[Toshiba]], [[Sojitz]], and [[Volkswagen]] as testing partners. Targets 25% of revenue from non-steel products by 2030. The most concentrated supply chain of any critical mineral: the [[EU]] sources 92% of its niobium from [[Brazil]], and the US has had zero domestic production since 1959.

## Strategic position

[[CBMM]] occupies a unique position in global commodities: it controls the supply of a critical mineral with no exchange-traded futures market, effectively acting as price-setter. The [[EU]] sources 92% of its niobium from [[Brazil]]; the US has had no significant domestic niobium production since 1959 (USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries, confirmed through 2025). Both the US and [[China]] are 100% import-dependent.

The Chinese consortium's 15% stake in CBMM, combined with [[CMOC Group]]'s ownership of the Boa Vista mine (∲11% global share), gives [[China]] significant influence across the niobium supply chain despite [[Brazil]]'s production dominance.

## Battery pivot

Expanding beyond steel alloys into niobium titanium oxide (NTO) battery anodes. Partnership with [[Toshiba]] and [[Sojitz]]. Targeting 25-30% of revenue from non-steel products by 2030. Launched world's first NTO anode production facility (Nov 2024).

NTO anode advantages over conventional lithium-ion anodes:
- Ultra-fast charging: 80% charge in 10 minutes
- Cycle life: 15,000+ cycles (3-5x longer than conventional Li-ion)
- Enhanced safety: thermally stable, lower fire risk
- Cold-weather performance: functional at temperatures that degrade graphite anodes

Commercial progress:
- Jun 2024: [[Toshiba]], [[Sojitz]], and CBMM unveiled an NTO-powered [[Volkswagen]] Caminhões e Ônibus electric bus prototype in [[Brazil]]. The vehicle runs daily fixed routes at CBMM's Araxa facility with pantograph charging, generating real-world data for commercial validation.
- Jun 2025: [[Toshiba]] began sample shipments of SCiB-Nb cells — the first commercial NTO battery product
- CBMM has 3 additional battery-grade niobium oxide plants planned, targeting 20kt production by 2030

Revenue target: CBMM projects 25% of revenue from battery materials by end of decade (Crawford/[[Austral Continental]], March 2026). At current ~R$11.4B revenue, that implies ~R$2.8B in battery materials revenue if realized.
