**Dean Ball** is an AI policy researcher at the [[Mercatus Center]] ([[George Mason University]]) and one of the most-cited voices in the US debate over AI regulation, known for opposing heavy-handed governance frameworks.

## Synopsis

Ball carved out an influential niche by writing against precautionary AI regulation at a time when most policy commentary was trending toward restrictive frameworks (SB-1047 in California, EU AI Act). His Substack "Hyperdimensional" became required reading for tech executives and Hill staffers because it combined technical literacy with libertarian policy instincts — arguing that premature regulation would entrench incumbents and hand AI leadership to [[China]]. His comment on the Pentagon's reported consideration of cutting [[Anthropic]] contracts — "It would be hard to think of a more strategically unwise move for the U.S. military" — captured his framing: AI policy as national security, not consumer protection.

Ball's relevance to the vault is as a barometer of the AI governance debate's trajectory. His positions track closely with the [[a16z]]/[[Andreessen Horowitz]] camp and the broader Silicon Valley resistance to regulation. When Ball's arguments gain traction (as they did in the SB-1047 veto), it signals a more permissive regulatory environment for AI companies — bullish for [[NVIDIA]], [[Anthropic]], [[OpenAI]], and the broader AI infrastructure stack. The risk is that a major AI incident (deepfake election manipulation, autonomous system failure) could flip the political environment overnight, making the anti-regulation stance a liability.
