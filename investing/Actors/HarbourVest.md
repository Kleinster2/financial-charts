**HarbourVest** — Boston-headquartered private-markets investment firm. ~$160bn+ AUM/AUA across primary fund commitments, co-investments, and secondaries. Founded 1982 as the private markets investment arm of John Hancock Mutual Life; independent since 1997. Major LP-led secondaries buyer.

---

## Why HarbourVest matters

HarbourVest is one of the largest secondaries buyers in the world, operating across the full private-markets spectrum (PE, credit, real estate, infrastructure). The firm is consistently cited as a benchmark for [[Private equity secondaries|secondaries market]] activity, and its managing directors are frequent industry commentators — the FT cited HarbourVest managing director David Atterbury in its April 2026 secondaries piece on why elite buyout firms have pivoted from dismissing secondaries to acquiring secondaries shops.

The firm's structural advantage over single-strategy secondaries specialists is integration with primary fund commitments. HarbourVest sees primary fund flow across roughly the entire GP landscape, which informs its secondaries pricing and selection. Secondaries within HarbourVest are a distribution of broader private-markets relationships rather than a standalone discipline.

---

## David Atterbury position (Apr 2026 FT)

[[David Atterbury]] (managing director, [[HarbourVest]]) framed the market evolution: "When I joined the secondaries business in 2000-ish, it was 'what's a secondary'? In the last few years, it has grown into a mainstream and rapidly expanding part of private markets."

On returns: the realized IRRs of top secondaries funds can be similar to buyout vehicles despite their lower risk, because more mature holdings closer to exit pay back cash faster. "This return profile has made the sector highly attractive to institutional investors."

The position implicitly defends the secondaries-fund value proposition against the [[Evergreen private capital vehicles#The mark-to-NAV problem for retail investors|mark-to-NAV decay critique]] — Atterbury's institutional-investor frame is the canonical sophisticated-LP view that understands the discount-to-mark-up mechanic and expects return profile decay over fund life.
