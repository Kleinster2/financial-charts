# AIA Group

**AIA Group** is the largest independent publicly listed pan-Asian life insurance and financial services company, headquartered in [[Hong Kong]] and operating across 18 markets in Asia-Pacific. It is the largest component of the [[EWH]] ETF at ~22% weight, making it effectively a single-stock bet for many investors seeking Hong Kong equity exposure.

## Synopsis

AIA was originally the Asian arm of [[AIG]] (American International Group), with roots going back to 1919 when Cornelius Vander Starr founded an insurance agency in [[Shanghai]]. AIG spun AIA off via IPO on the [[HKEX]] in October 2010 — the largest IPO in the world that year at $20.5B — as part of AIG's post-financial-crisis restructuring to repay the US government's $182B bailout. The separation freed AIA from AIG's toxic structured products exposure and established it as a standalone franchise with the most recognized life insurance brand across Asia.

The investment case for AIA rests on the structural under-penetration of life insurance in Asia. Insurance penetration (premiums as % of GDP) across AIA's key markets — [[China]], [[Thailand]], [[Singapore]], [[Malaysia]], [[India]] — remains well below Western levels, implying decades of premium growth driven by rising middle-class incomes and aging populations. AIA's agency force model (over 100,000 agents) and trusted brand give it distribution advantages that digital-first competitors struggle to replicate for high-value life products that require consultative selling. The main risk is China, which accounts for ~30% of new business value: regulatory shifts, real estate-linked investment portfolio exposure, and the post-COVID consumer spending slowdown have weighed on growth in the mainland business. Mark Tucker, the CEO who led the AIG separation and IPO, became chairman in 2017; Lee Yuan Siong has served as CEO since 2020.
