# Ninety One

Global active asset manager specializing in emerging markets, founded in 1991 as Investec Asset Management (the investment arm of [[Investec]], the Anglo-South African bank) and demerged in March 2020 with a dual listing in [[London]] ([[LSE]]: N91) and [[Johannesburg]] (JSE: NY1). The name refers to the 1991 founding year. Managed roughly £130B ([[pound sterling]]) in client assets as of its 2025 interim results, with an investment franchise concentrated in EM debt, EM equities, global multi-asset, and thematic strategies (global quality, global franchise, natural resources).

## Synopsis

Ninety One is one of a small handful of global asset managers with an EM-first identity rather than EM as a satellite allocation. That positioning makes it a useful barometer for active EM flows: when the firm's public commentary turns defensive on EM, it tends to correlate with wider de-risking in the asset class. EM debt, where Ninety One has built institutional credibility over 30 years, is the strongest franchise — the firm is regularly cited by [[FT]], [[Bloomberg]], and Reuters for EM sovereign, corporate, and local-currency views. Co-CEOs [[Hendrik du Toit]] (founding CEO) and Kim McFarland have held the dual structure since the 2020 demerger.

The firm appeared in vault coverage through [[Thys Louw]], a portfolio manager on the EM debt team, who was quoted in the March 4, 2026 [[FT]] piece on the position-squaring unwind during the Iran war: "The main thing that we have broadly seen in the recent sell-off is position squaring . . . That is true whether it is Korean equities or the Egyptian pound." Louw framed the week as a mechanical deleveraging of crowded trades — the weakest asset in a portfolio (by risk limit, not by fundamentals) gets sold first — rather than a fundamental repricing. See [[2026 Iran conflict market impact#Global equity dispersion — popular trades unwind (FT, Mar 4)]].
