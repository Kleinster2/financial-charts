## Career Arc

Mike Wilson is a [[Morgan Stanley]] lifer. He joined the firm in 1989 as an investment banker and spent over two decades working his way through the Institutional Equity Division (1995-2012), including roles as Head of Content Distribution for North American Equities.

In 2012, he was appointed CIO of Wealth Management, and later took on the broader role of Chief US Equity Strategist and Chair of the Global Investment Committee. He became one of the most-watched voices on [[Wall Street]], known for a methodical, sometimes contrarian approach to equity strategy.

Wilson's public profile peaked during 2022-2023, first as a vindicated bear, then as a cautionary tale about staying bearish too long.

## Key Theses & Frameworks

### Earnings-Driven Framework
Wilson's core approach centers on [[earnings estimates]] as the primary driver of equity prices. He tracks earnings revisions breadth, forward EPS estimates, and the gap between top-down macro signals and bottom-up analyst estimates. When he sees earnings risk, he gets bearish — sometimes too early.

### "Ice" vs "Fire" Scenarios
He's used the metaphor of "ice" (deflationary earnings recession) vs "fire" (inflationary overheating) to frame macro risks. In 2022-2023, he argued the market faced an earnings recession ("ice") that would bring the [[S&P 500]] significantly lower.

### Operating Leverage Squeeze
A key Wilson thesis: as revenue growth decelerates, companies with high operating leverage face margin compression that catches consensus off guard. This framework worked well in 2022 but failed to account for the [[AI]]-driven earnings resilience of [[Magnificent Seven|mega-cap tech]] in 2023-2024.

## Track Record

### Hits ✅
- Called the 2022 bear market — his S&P 500 target of ~3,900 was one of the most bearish and most accurate on the Street. The index bottomed near 3,577 in October 2022. He was named top strategist by multiple outlets.
- Margin compression call (2022) — correctly predicted earnings downgrades across cyclicals
- Warned about narrow market breadth — the concentration in mega-caps was a valid structural concern
- 2020 recovery call — was constructive coming out of the COVID crash

### Misses ❌
- Disastrously bearish through 2023 — maintained ~3,900 S&P target while the index rallied 24% to ~4,770. Kept calling for an earnings recession that never materialized. One of the worst major strategist calls of the year.
- Missed the [[AI]] trade entirely — his earnings pessimism blinded him to the transformative impact of [[ChatGPT]]/[[NVIDIA]] on tech earnings
- Stayed bearish into early 2024 — slow to capitulate even after the evidence was overwhelming
- Stepped back from Global Investment Committee in early 2024, with Lisa Shalett taking a larger role — widely seen as a consequence of the prolonged miss

### The 2023 Lesson
Wilson's 2023 miss is a case study in anchoring bias — his 2022 framework was correct, but he failed to update his priors when the earnings recession didn't materialize and [[AI]] capex created a new earnings cycle. The market rewarded the very stocks (tech, [[semiconductors]]) that his framework said were most at risk.

## March 25, 2026 — "S&P Profit Boom Despite Iran War" (Bloomberg)

Wilson's most bullish framing since the conflict began. Key data points:

- S&P 500 earnings expected to rise 20% over next 12 months — historically, this reading was higher only coming out of recessions
- Sell-side analysts now estimate S&P 500 Q1 profit growth at 11.9%, up from 10.9% before the war started
- "The probability remains low for this oil spike to end the business cycle" (from March 23 client note)
- ~50% of [[Russell 2000]] already down 20% from 52-week highs — Wilson sees the correction as late-stage
- S&P 500 target ~6,300 by early April (from March 16 CNBC)

Counterpoint cited in the same article: [[JPMorgan]] estimates that if oil stays at $110/bbl for the rest of the year, S&P 500 earnings estimates could shed up to 5 percentage points.

This is the opposite call from [[Ed Yardeni]] (35% meltdown probability, "none and done") and [[Lyn Alden]] (fiscal cascade risk). Wilson sees the earnings cycle as self-sustaining; the bears see the oil shock as a regime-breaker.

*Source: Bloomberg, March 25 2026*

## March 30, 2026 — "Correction nearing its end stage" (Bloomberg / MS Podcast)

Wilson's most explicitly bullish framing since the conflict began. Key points:

- S&P 500 correction *"nearing its end stage"* even as the Iran war continues
- ~50% of [[Russell 3000]] already down 20% from 52-week highs (expanded from his earlier [[Russell 2000]] stat)
- Called this a potential buying opportunity
- Warned that Fed rate hikes still pose a threat if oil stays elevated

This doubles down on his March 25 thesis (earnings resilient, low probability oil ends the cycle) and puts him squarely opposite [[Ed Yardeni]] (35% meltdown odds, "none and done" on the Fed) and [[Lyn Alden]] (fiscal cascade / sovereign debt loop). Wilson sees the earnings cycle as self-sustaining; the bears see the oil shock as a regime-breaker.

The March 31 market action (+1.14% S&P on Trump ceasefire signals) partially validates his call that the correction was bottoming.

*Sources: Bloomberg (Mar 30, 2026), Morgan Stanley Thoughts on the Market podcast (Mar 30, 2026)*
