## Q2 FY2026 earnings and Apr 29 repricing (Apr 2026)

Visa's Apr 29 move was a clean read-through on global spending and cross-border normalization: the stock moved from $309.30 to $334.86, +8.3%, after the fiscal Q2 release.

| Metric | Q2 FY2026 | YoY |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Net revenue | $11.2B | +17% / +16% constant currency |
| [[GAAP]] net income | $6.0B | +32% |
| [[GAAP]] EPS | $3.14 | +36% |
| Non-[[GAAP]] net income | $6.3B | +17% |
| Non-[[GAAP]] EPS | $3.31 | +20% |
| Payments volume | — | +9% constant dollars |
| Cross-border volume ex intra-[[Europe]] | — | +11% |
| Cross-border volume total | — | +12% |
| Processed transactions | — | +9% |

The market response was about breadth, not just EPS. Payments volume, cross-border volume, and processed transactions all grew high single to low double digits, while management framed the stack as expanding into money movement, value-added services, and "Visa as a Service" capabilities that include [[Agentic AI]] and [[Stablecoins|stablecoin]] functionality. That matters for the [[Mastercard]] comparison because the duopoly is increasingly competing around software and services layered on top of the card rails, not just acceptance footprint.

Capital return amplified the signal: Visa repurchased about 25M class A shares for $7.9B in the quarter, returned $9.2B via buybacks and dividends, authorized a new $20B multi-year buyback, and declared a $0.67 quarterly dividend.

*Source: Visa FYQ2 2026 earnings release, filed as [[SEC]] 8-K earnings exhibit (Apr 28, 2026). Price move verified in local DB: Apr 28 close $309.30 → Apr 29 close $334.86.*
