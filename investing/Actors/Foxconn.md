## Synopsis

Foxconn is the critical manufacturing chokepoint in the AI infrastructure buildout. The company holds ~40% of the global AI server market — more than [[Quanta Computer]] (~17%) and [[Wistron]] combined — and is the primary assembler of [[NVIDIA]]'s most advanced rack-scale systems (GB200 NVL72, GB300 NVL72). When [[Google]], [[Microsoft]], [[Meta]], or [[Amazon]] order AI servers, Foxconn builds most of them.

The financial profile is transforming. Cloud and networking revenue overtook consumer electronics (iPhone assembly) for the first time in 2025, hitting 41% of total revenue vs 35% for consumer. FY2025 revenue reached ~NT$8.1T (~$256B), and Chairman Young Liu guided double-digit growth for 2026. Jan-Feb 2026 revenue surged 21.6% YoY to a record NT$1.33T ($41.9B), with January alone up 35.5%.

The bull case: Foxconn is the picks-and-shovels play on hyperscaler capex ($650B+ in 2026 from Alphabet/Amazon/Meta/Microsoft alone). Every dollar of AI infrastructure spending flows through Foxconn's assembly lines. The bear case: contract manufacturing is low-margin work — Foxconn captures volume, not value. The real profits accrue to [[NVIDIA]] (chips), [[TSMC]] (fabrication), and the hyperscalers themselves.

## Apple supply-chain diversification (India)

Foxconn is [[Apple]]'s lead manufacturing partner in the multi-year shift of iPhone assembly out of [[China]] and into [[India]] (principally Tamil Nadu and Karnataka). Per the [[Financial Times]] (May 2026), Apple's diversification away from its China-dominated supply chain — built up over two decades under [[Tim Cook]] — is already under way and likely to intensify under incoming CEO [[John Ternus]], driven by US-China trade tensions and single-country concentration risk. For Foxconn the India ramp is the consumer-electronics counterweight to its AI-server pivot: even as cloud/networking (41%) overtakes consumer (35%) in the revenue mix, the iPhone franchise is being geographically rebased rather than wound down. See [[Apple#Innovation gap and the reinvestment question (FT, May 2026)]] for the strategic frame.

## AI server position

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Global AI server market share | ~40% |
| Key platforms | NVIDIA GB200 NVL72, GB300 NVL72 |
| NVIDIA relationship | PBR (Pilot Build Request) Partner — qualified for earliest-access builds |
| Competitors | [[Quanta Computer]] (~17%), [[Wistron]], [[Inventec]], [[Pegatron]] |

Foxconn assembles rack-scale AI systems for all major hyperscalers. The company's Kaohsiung Super Computing Center is built around NVIDIA [[Blackwell]] architecture — 64 racks, 4,608 Tensor Core GPUs using GB200 NVL72.

Manufacturing footprint:
- [[Mexico]] plant for Blackwell rack-scale assembly (announced 2025)
- Taiwan facilities for high-end AI server integration
- Expanding US manufacturing presence for Stargate-adjacent demand

[[Wistron]]'s 108% growth is notable — coming off a smaller base but ramping AI server output aggressively. [[Quanta Computer]] plans to double AI server production capacity by end of 2026.

## Investment relevance

Bull:
- ~40% AI server market share — dominant position in fastest-growing hardware segment
- Revenue mix shift to higher-value cloud/networking (41% > consumer 35%)
- Every dollar of hyperscaler capex ($650B+ in 2026) flows partly through Foxconn
- NVIDIA PBR partner status — first access to new platforms (GB200, GB300)
- Chairman Liu guiding double-digit 2026 growth, market share gains above 40%

Bear:
- Contract manufacturing margins are thin — Foxconn captures volume, not value
- Customer concentration: [[Apple]] + [[NVIDIA]] dominate revenue
- [[Quanta Computer]] and [[Wistron]] growing faster (108% and 50% vs 30%)
- Geopolitical risk: [[Taiwan]] Strait tensions, [[US-China decoupling]]
- AI server demand is cyclical — if hyperscaler capex pauses, Foxconn feels it first
