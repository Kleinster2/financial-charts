## Career arc

**Goldman Sachs (1990–2016):**
- 26 years, joined 1990
- Global Co-Head of Equities and FICC divisions
- President and COO (2006–2016) — decade running daily operations
- Key figure during financial crisis
- Widely expected to succeed Lloyd Blankfein as CEO (didn't)

**National Economic Council (2017–2018):**
- Director and chief economic advisor to President Trump
- Managed administration's economic policy agenda
- Key architect of 2017 [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act]]
- Resigned March 2018 — reportedly over disagreements on tariffs

**Post-government (2018–present):**
- [[IBM]] Vice Chairman (2021)
- Chairman, Pallas Advisors
- Vice Chair, Zinnia
- FDIC Systemic Resolution Advisory Committee member
- Apollo Lead Independent Director (Apr 2025)

## Apollo appointment

Replaced [[Jay Clayton]] when Clayton left for SDNY US Attorney role. Appointment effective April 21, 2025.

Cohn's statement emphasized the convergence of public and private markets as a transformational moment for Apollo. His Goldman/government background provides:
- Wall Street operational credibility
- Government/regulatory relationships
- Experience governing large, complex financial institutions
