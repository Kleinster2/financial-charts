# Spirit Airlines

**Spirit Airlines** (Spirit Aviation Holdings) — US ultra-low-cost carrier. Operations terminated May 2, 2026 after $500M White House bailout collapsed. See [[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]] for full event detail.

## Causal chain

Spirit was structurally distressed — unprofitable since post-Covid travel collapse, two bankruptcies in <12 months. The Feb 2026 emergence plan was modeled against pre-war fuel prices. The [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran war]] jet fuel shock (~$80-90/bbl pre-war → $150-230/bbl through March 2026) reset the unit economics. A $500M federal bailout collapsed Apr 30 when a key creditor group rejected the government taking equity control.

The shutdown is the first US-airline failure attributable to the Iran war, three months in.

---

## Sector legacy

Spirit pioneered the ULCC unbundled-fare model in the US (peer to Europe's [[Ryanair]] / [[Wizz Air]]). The shutdown removes ~9% of US ULCC domestic capacity. Vacated routes flow to:

- [[Frontier Airlines]] — direct US ULCC peer
- [[Allegiant Travel]] — US ULCC peer
- [[JetBlue]] — would-be 2024 acquirer; absorbs select routes opportunistically
- US legacy carriers ([[Delta Air Lines]], [[United Airlines]], [[American Airlines]]) — pricing-power beneficiaries

A320 fleet redistributes via secondary lessor market ([[AerCap]], [[Air Lease Corp]]).
