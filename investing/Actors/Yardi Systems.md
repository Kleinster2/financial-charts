**Yardi Systems** is the largest property management software provider globally, a private company founded by Anant Yardi in 1984 that quietly dominates real estate technology. Through its affiliate Cupar Grimmond, Yardi invested $337M for 60% ownership of [[WeWork]] when it emerged from Chapter 11 in June 2024 — an unusual move for a software company into distressed physical real estate.

## Synopsis

Yardi's core business is enterprise property management software used by landlords, REITs, and property managers to handle leasing, accounting, maintenance, and tenant services across residential, commercial, and mixed-use portfolios. The platform processes billions of dollars in rent payments annually and serves clients managing millions of units worldwide. Revenue is estimated north of $1B (private, no public filings), with high recurring subscription income and minimal churn given the switching costs embedded in property management workflows. Competitors include [[RealPage]] (acquired by [[Thoma Bravo]] for $10.2B in 2024), [[AppFolio]], and [[MRI Software]], but Yardi's entrenched position among large institutional landlords gives it a durable moat.

The WeWork investment through Cupar Grimmond is the anomaly that makes Yardi investable-interesting. Buying 60% of a post-bankruptcy flexible workspace company for $337M is a bet that office demand stabilizes and that [[WeWork]]'s brand and lease portfolio retain value at distressed pricing. For Yardi, there may be a strategic logic: [[WeWork]] as a customer and showcase for Yardi's property technology stack. Anant Yardi has kept the company private for 40 years, avoiding the growth-at-all-costs pressure that destroyed WeWork under [[Adam Neumann]] — the irony of Yardi now controlling WeWork is not lost.
