**Heineken** is the world's second-largest brewer by volume, headquartered in Amsterdam. Over 300 brands across 190+ countries. Significant [[Mexico]] presence -- Heineken Mexico is one of the company's largest operations and a participant in Plan Mexico.

## Synopsis

Heineken's portfolio spans the beer spectrum from premium (Heineken, Amstel, Birra Moretti) to local champions (Tiger in Asia, Dos Equis and Tecate in Mexico, Bintang in Indonesia), giving it diversification that pure-premium peers lack. The 2010 acquisition of FEMSA Cerveza (the beer division of [[Femsa]]) for ~$7.7B was the transformative deal, making Mexico Heineken's second-largest market by volume and giving it a dominant position in Latin American beer. Mexico alone contributes an outsized share of group EBITDA because of the market's structural advantages: high per-capita consumption, limited competition (duopoly with [[AB InBev]]'s Grupo Modelo), and favorable demographics.

The investment case is a tension between premium brand pricing power and volume pressure from the global shift toward spirits, cannabis, and non-alcoholic alternatives. Heineken has responded with aggressive investment in the 0.0% segment (Heineken 0.0 is the world's largest non-alcoholic beer brand) and premiumization across its portfolio. In Mexico, the company pledged investment under Plan Mexico, aligning with [[Claudia Sheinbaum]]'s industrial policy framework. The risk is input cost volatility -- barley, aluminum, energy -- and currency exposure in emerging markets that make up ~60% of revenue. Heineken trades on Euronext Amsterdam at a consistent premium to [[AB InBev]], reflecting the market's view of its portfolio quality and emerging-market growth runway.
