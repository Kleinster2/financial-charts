# SoundField

**SoundField** is a UK-originated ambisonic microphone pioneer specializing in 3D/immersive audio capture systems, acquired by [[Rode Microphones|The Freedman Group]] in 2018.

## Synopsis

SoundField was a pioneer in ambisonic microphone technology — a capture technique that records sound from all directions simultaneously, enabling 3D audio reproduction and post-production spatial manipulation. The company originated from research at the University of Reading in the 1970s and built a niche following in broadcast, film, and VR audio production. Its microphones became standard tools for immersive audio content, particularly as VR and spatial audio formats gained traction with platforms like [[YouTube]] 360 and [[Apple]]'s spatial audio ecosystem.

The 2018 acquisition by [[Rode Microphones]] (part of The Freedman Group, an Australian audio equipment conglomerate) brought SoundField's technology into a larger manufacturing and distribution network. Rode used the acquisition to launch more accessible ambisonic products (the NT-SF1) aimed at content creators rather than exclusively professional broadcast users — a move that democratized spatial audio capture. For the vault, SoundField is a minor entry that illustrates the niche audio technology M&A landscape where specialized IP gets absorbed into larger platforms. The spatial audio trend it enabled has become more relevant with [[Apple]]'s investment in spatial computing (Vision Pro) and the broader push toward immersive media formats.
