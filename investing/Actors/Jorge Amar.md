# Jorge Amar

CEO of [[Teleperformance]] as of March 15, 2026, succeeding founder Daniel Julien. Longtime Teleperformance executive who takes the helm at a critical moment as AI-driven automation threatens the core business model of the world's largest business process outsourcing company.

## Synopsis

Jorge Amar joined [[Teleperformance]] and rose through the organization's operational ranks, gaining deep experience in the company's global delivery network spanning 100+ countries and ~500,000 employees. His appointment as CEO, effective March 15, 2026, marks the first leadership transition away from founder Daniel Julien, who built Teleperformance from a French call center operation in 1978 into a ~EUR 16B revenue BPO giant. Julien's departure (he returned as CEO in 2017 after a brief absence) closes a founder-led era and raises the standard succession question: can institutional management sustain the culture and client relationships that a founder built?

The timing of Amar's ascension is what makes this consequential. Teleperformance's core business — managing customer service, technical support, and back-office operations through human labor arbitrage — faces existential pressure from AI agents. Companies like [[Intercom]] (Fin), [[Klarna]] (AI assistant replacing 700 agents), and dozens of startups are demonstrating that LLM-powered customer service can handle 50-80% of routine inquiries at a fraction of the cost of human agents. Amar inherits the strategic question of whether Teleperformance can pivot from selling labor hours to selling AI-augmented outcomes, or whether the company's 500,000-person workforce becomes a liability rather than an asset. The stock has been under pressure since [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] fears intensified.
