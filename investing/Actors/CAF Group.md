# CAF Group

## Synopsis

[[CAF Group]] (Construcciones y Auxiliar de Ferrocarriles, S.A.; BME: CAF) is a Spanish rolling-stock and rail-systems manufacturer headquartered in Beasain (Basque Country), incorporated in 1917 and listed on the Madrid Stock Exchange. The firm operates two reportable segments — Rolling stock (high-speed trains, locomotives, commuter trains, subway, streetcars; plus traction and signalling systems and energy-storage solutions) and Components and spare parts — across Spain, the rest of Europe, the United States, and Asia-Pacific, with TTM revenue ~$4.68B and order backlog above €9B. CAF's 2025 outlook (per company guidance) is near-double-digit revenue growth versus 2024 with improved profitability and stable net-debt-to-EBITDA. CAF acquired the Polish bus-and-coach maker [[Solaris]] in 2018, adding bus-fleet exposure (electric and hydrogen) to the historically rail-only platform.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Solaris]] / [[Ballard]] hydrogen-bus partnership thread: CAF's 2018 Solaris acquisition is the corporate-parent context for Solaris's selection of [[Ballard]] FCmove-SC engines for next-generation hydrogen buses (announced May 2026). CAF's group-level zero-emission strategy drives Solaris procurement decisions, providing institutional resilience to the Ballard partnership relative to standalone Solaris-only economics.
