# Gordon Hanson

Gordon Hanson is the Peter Wertheim Professor in Urban Policy at the Harvard Kennedy School and a co-author, with [[David Autor]] and [[David Dorn]], of the "China Syndrome" paper ([[Autor-Dorn-Hanson]], 2013). Prior director of the Center on Global Transformation at UC San Diego. A leading trade economist specializing in the empirical consequences of Chinese import competition for US labor markets and in US-Mexico migration and border economics.

## Why he matters to the vault

One of three co-authors of the foundational [[Autor-Dorn-Hanson]] empirical framework. Hanson's specific contribution to the trio is the trade-flow mapping — the instrumental-variable design that used other high-income-country Chinese import shares to identify the causal effect on US commuting zones, rather than contaminating the estimate with US-specific demand shocks. He is also a primary reference on US-Mexico trade and migration, which connects to [[USMCA]], [[Mexico]], and [[US-Mexico border]] themes.
