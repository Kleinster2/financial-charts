# Kuwait

Oil-rich [[GCC]] state on the northwestern Persian Gulf coast. Major OPEC producer (~2.7M bbl/day capacity). Refineries contribute to the 20% of globally traded [[Fuel oil|fuel oil]] sourced from Persian Gulf producers. Heavily dependent on the [[Strait of Hormuz]] for oil exports — all Kuwaiti crude exits via the Gulf.

## March 2026 — refinery attacks and investment loss

### Infrastructure damage

Drones struck [[Kuwait]]'s Mina Al-Ahmadi and Mina Abdullah refineries multiple times during the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran conflict]], with additional strikes on March 20 (confirmed on the day of the MEI panel). Combined refining capacity: ~640K bbl/day. Kuwait has no pipeline bypass and is 100% dependent on [[Strait of Hormuz]] for exports — making it among the most vulnerable Gulf producers.

### Refined products crisis

[[Colby Connelly]] ([[Middle East Institute|MEI]] Senior Fellow, energy expert, March 20): Kuwait became a "huge exporter of refined products" post-Russia-Ukraine war. European markets sourced much more [[Jet fuel|jet fuel]] and refined products from Kuwait as a result of replacing Russian supply. With Kuwait's refineries now attacked, this disrupts that supply chain — a distinct problem from crude supply loss because refined product shortfalls are harder to replace.

### Lost foreign investment

Kuwait was opening its oil sector to international investment for the first time — and lost a major investor as a result of the conflict (Connelly, MEI panel March 20). The above-ground risk landscape has fundamentally changed for Kuwaiti energy investment.

### GDP impact

[[Goldman Sachs]] estimated -14% GDP contraction for Kuwait in 2026 (assuming hostilities end by early May with gradual [[Strait of Hormuz]] resumption) — tied with [[Qatar]] for the worst impact among [[GCC]] states, driven by zero pipeline bypass and 100% Hormuz dependency.

Source: MEI panel, March 20 2026 — Connelly; Goldman Sachs via CFR panel (Mar 17)
