# Lori Systems

African logistics technology company connecting shippers with trucking capacity through a digital freight marketplace. [[Delta40]] portfolio company, operating in one of the continent's largest and most fragmented industries — trucking accounts for ~80% of goods movement in Sub-Saharan Africa.

## Synopsis

Lori Systems (rebranded from "Lori" in some markets) operates a technology platform that matches cargo with available trucks across African markets, primarily [[Kenya]], [[Nigeria]], [[Uganda]], and [[Ghana]]. The African trucking market is estimated at $180B+ annually but remains deeply fragmented — most capacity is owned by individual truck owners or small fleets with no digital infrastructure for matching, pricing, or tracking. Lori's platform addresses this by providing shippers (manufacturers, commodity traders, agricultural cooperatives) with a digital interface to book, track, and pay for freight, while giving truck owners consistent load availability and faster payment cycles.

The company was founded in 2016 by Josh Sandler and Jean-Claude Homawoo and has raised venture funding from investors including [[Y Combinator]], Crystal Ventures, and [[Delta40]]. The bull case is that African logistics digitization is a massive TAM with low penetration — less than 5% of trucking in most African markets is digitally intermediated, compared to 20-30%+ in the US and Europe. The bear case is execution risk: African logistics startups face infrastructure gaps (poor roads, border delays, fuel cost volatility), payment collection challenges, and intense competition from well-funded peers like [[Kobo360]], Truck It In, and locally funded alternatives. Several African logistics startups (including Sendy, which shut down in 2023) have demonstrated that high GMV growth can coexist with unsustainable unit economics.
