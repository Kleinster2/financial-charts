#actor #space #launch #defense #usa #public

**Rocket Lab** — End-to-end space company. Operates the Electron rocket (small-sat launch, 60+ missions), is finishing the medium-lift Neutron vehicle (H2 2026 debut), builds satellites and components through its Space Systems segment, and is moving into national-security primes work via Golden Dome interceptor contracts and the [[Motiv Space Systems]] robotics acquisition. Founded by [[Peter Beck]] in New Zealand, HQ in Long Beach, CA. The second-most-frequent US orbital launch provider after [[SpaceX]], and the only NASDAQ-listed pure-play space launch + systems company at scale.

## Q1 2026 print + Motiv acquisition + Golden Dome contract (May 7-8, 2026)

RKLB closed +34.2% on May 8 ($78.58 → $105.47, +7.5σ beta-adjusted) — the largest single-day move in the company's public history and the catalyst that triggered the broader [[Space basket rally May 8 2026|space cluster rally]] (RDW +20%, LUNR +20%, BKSY +22%, ASTS +15%, SPIR +15%, PL +11% same session). Three discrete catalysts landed in the same release: a record Q1 print, the definitive agreement to acquire [[Motiv Space Systems]], and selection alongside [[Raytheon]] for the [[Golden Dome|Space Based Interceptor]] (SBI) program.

### Motiv Space Systems acquisition (definitive agreement, terms undisclosed)

Rocket Lab signed a definitive agreement to acquire [[Motiv Space Systems]], a Pasadena-based robotic-arm and spacecraft-mechanism contractor whose hardware has flown on multiple NASA missions including [[Perseverance]] (Mars sample handling). Terms not disclosed.

The strategic logic is vertical integration into the on-orbit robotics layer at the moment the on-orbit servicing market is being defined by [[Golden Dome|Space-based interceptor]] contracts, the [[Orbital Reef]] commercial-station program, and DARPA's Robotic Servicing of Geosynchronous Satellites (RSGS) line. Owning the robotic arm and end-effector capability lets Rocket Lab bid full satellite-servicing or assembly-in-space packages without subcontracting to a competing prime. The acquisition complements the existing [[SolAero]] (solar cell) and [[ASI]] (separation systems) tuck-ins that built out the Space Systems vertical.

### Golden Dome Space Based Interceptor selection

Rocket Lab announced it was selected — working alongside [[Raytheon]] — to "directly support the Department of War's Space Based Interceptor (SBI) program" under the [[Golden Dome]] umbrella. No contract dollar figure was disclosed.

This is the cleanest signal that the November 2025 [[Golden Dome#Space-based interceptor competition Nov 2025|space-interceptor competition]] is moving from $10M demonstration contracts to actual production-style prime work. The published prize structure has $340M for on-orbit testing (1st place: $125M, 5th place: $40M) and $1.8B-$3.4B/year production contracts. Rocket Lab partnering with Raytheon (rather than competing as standalone prime) is the structural read — Raytheon brings interceptor heritage and DoD program-management depth; Rocket Lab brings the launch + bus + responsive-deployment capability that the SBI mission needs at scale. The pairing is competitive against the [[Northrop Grumman]] / [[Lockheed Martin]] / [[Anduril]] / True Anomaly slates also bidding for the same production runs.

### Strategic read on the trifecta

Three things changed with the May 8 print:

1. The "space stock" multiple debate gets a data point. Rocket Lab has carried a high-beta retail tag with skeptics arguing the multiple priced a Neutron success that hadn't happened. Q1's 63.5% growth on $200M revenue with the backlog stepping to $2.2B and the cash position rising to $1.21B independently of Neutron tells the market that the existing business is monetizing faster than the bear case allowed, and that Neutron is a *call option on top of* a working business, not a load-bearing assumption. The +34% move on a beat that wasn't enormous (+6% revenue beat) reflects multiple expansion on this re-rating, not a pure earnings surprise.
2. The Space Systems / launch-services product mix flips toward Space Systems. Product revenue at $127.5M now exceeds Service revenue at $72.9M, which inverts the early-2024 mix where launch (Electron + planned Neutron) was the dominant revenue line and Space Systems was the "diversifier." The Motiv tuck-in extends the Space Systems lead and recasts Rocket Lab as a vertically integrated space prime where launch is one of several lines, not the headline. That repositioning matters for valuation comps — the right cohort is now the [[Raytheon]] / [[L3Harris]] / [[Northrop Grumman]] specialized-prime band rather than pure-play launch.
3. Golden Dome turns the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|war-premium defense]] tailwind from sector-level into specific-prime exposure. The Iran war drove a sustained defense-stock outperformance in March-April 2026 ([[Iran conflict defense repricing]]) on a [[Northrop Grumman|NOC]] / [[RTX]] / [[Lockheed Martin|LMT]] / [[L3Harris|LHX]] basis. Rocket Lab's SBI selection — alongside Raytheon — is the first time the war premium routes through to a launch-tier prime, which materially expands the defense beneficiary list. Watch for follow-on SBI awards to [[Anduril]] and [[Northrop Grumman]] over the next two quarters; Rocket Lab as the launch + bus partner is a structural feature, not a one-off.

CEO [[Peter Beck]] quote: *"Rocket Lab has delivered another exceptional quarter with record financial performance of more than $200 million in revenue."*

## Neutron program

Medium-lift two-stage reusable launch vehicle, 13-tonne LEO payload, targeting H2 2026 maiden flight. Designed for constellation deployment, mid-lift national-security missions, and (per public statements) eventual human-rated payloads.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Payload to LEO | 13,000 kg |
| First stage | Reusable (booster recovery) |
| Engines | Archimedes (in-house design, methalox) |
| Maiden flight | H2 2026 (on track per Q1 release) |
| Comparable vehicle | [[SpaceX]] [[Falcon 9]] (similar class) |

Neutron is the second piece of the integrated platform — Electron handles dedicated small-sat launch (60+ flights, anchor commercial cadence); Neutron unlocks bigger national-security and constellation contracts that Rocket Lab cannot bid for with Electron. The launch is the binary catalyst that gates Rocket Lab's transition from "small-launch + Space Systems" to "full-spectrum US prime."
