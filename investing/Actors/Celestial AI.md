Celestial AI was a Santa Clara-based startup developing "Photonic Fabric" technology — optical interconnects designed to solve the memory bandwidth bottleneck in AI compute by using light instead of electrical signals to move data between compute, memory, and storage. Founded in 2020, the company raised over $250M in venture funding, including a $175M Series C in 2024 led by [[US Innovative Technology Fund]] with participation from [[Samsung]], [[Temasek]], and others.

Acquired by [[Marvell Technology]] as part of Marvell's strategy to build an end-to-end optical interconnect portfolio for AI data center infrastructure. The acquisition paired Celestial's photonic fabric IP with Marvell's existing electro-optics and custom silicon capabilities, alongside Marvell's separate acquisition of [[XConn Technologies]] (chiplet interconnect). The combined portfolio positions Marvell to compete with [[Broadcom]] and [[Intel]] in the high-growth interconnect layer of the AI infrastructure stack — the segment [[Jensen Huang]] identifies as critical in the [[Jensen Five Layer Cake]] framework.
