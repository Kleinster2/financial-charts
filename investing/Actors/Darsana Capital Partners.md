#actor #investor #hedgefund #usa #private

Darsana Capital Partners — New York long/short equity hedge fund founded Q1 2014 by [[Anand Desai]]. Intensely private. The firm's [[SpaceX]] position — entered in 2019 — now represents about 60% of its assets and is expected to be worth roughly $15bn at the [[SpaceX IPO 2026|IPO]] valuation. The [[Wall Street Journal]] first reported the expected payday on May 18, 2026; the [[Financial Times]] confirmed the same day.

Darsana is the second-largest hedge-fund winner of the [[SpaceX IPO 2026|June 2026 SpaceX listing]] — its ~$15bn stake at the $1.75T valuation framing trails only [[D1 Capital]]'s ~$20bn position. The two share a structural profile: Tiger Cub-era L/S equity firms that crossed into private growth equity in the late 2010s, took anchor positions at SpaceX funding rounds before the [[Starlink]] revenue inflection, and rode the [[SpaceX xAI merger|xAI roll-up]] without trimming. What distinguishes Darsana from [[D1 Capital]] is the concentration: ~60% of its AUM is sitting in one private name versus D1's more diversified book across [[Stripe]] / [[Databricks]] / [[Instacart]]. The firm has not publicly disclosed plans for the lock-up exit, but the IPO's custom non-standard lock-up arrangement governs the realization path. Beyond SpaceX, the public 13F book is small and concentrated — Sotera Health is the largest disclosed position. Darsana also holds [[EchoStar]], whose own SpaceX equity from the [[EchoStar#2025 Sep 8 (SpaceX spectrum deal #1)|spectrum-for-shares deals]] gives the firm a second indirect leg on the same thesis.

The [[Eton Park Capital Management|Eton Park]] lineage matters because the firm's 2017 closure produced an unusually concentrated alumni network of fund founders (Darsana is one of several spinouts). The investment style — fundamental, concentrated, willing to underwrite illiquid positions — echoes that mid-2000s multi-strategy era rather than the post-2015 pod-shop model that now dominates the Tiger Cub set.

## Evolution

The firm's arc is short and unusually concentrated for a 12-year-old hedge fund: from generalist L/S equity launch into a single private-name vehicle with a public equity book attached.

- Q1 2014 (launch): [[Anand Desai]] leaves [[Eton Park Capital Management]] as a partner and launches Darsana in New York. The launch is part of the broader Eton Park diaspora — Eric Mindich's firm was still operating but several partners spun out in this period before Mindich shut Eton Park down in 2017
- 2014-2018 (build phase): Darsana operates as a generalist long/short equity firm focused on fundamental, concentrated positions. Low public profile, no headline-grabbing trades. The 13F book stays in the low single-digit billions
- 2019 (SpaceX entry): Partner Dan Irom encounters [[SpaceX]] while researching publicly traded satellite companies. The introduction leads to Darsana participating in SpaceX financing at a ~$30-33bn valuation — alignable with the April 2019 [[SpaceX#Funding rounds (complete)|Series K]] at $33.3bn that [[Baillie Gifford]] led
- 2019-2025 (position scaling): The firm participates in multiple subsequent SpaceX financing rounds and buys shares via secondary tenders from employees. The position compounds with the [[Twitter|X]] holding that Darsana acquired separately, then converts via the 2025 X→[[xAI]] merger and the Feb 2026 [[SpaceX xAI merger]]
- May 18, 2026 (the payday surfaces): [[Wall Street Journal]] first reports Darsana's expected windfall ahead of the June 2026 SpaceX IPO; [[Financial Times]] confirms the same day. The position is now reported at ~60% of AUM and worth approximately $15bn at the FT-cited $1.75T SpaceX valuation framing

The arc is: generalist L/S equity launch from Eton Park alumni → one research detour into satellite companies in 2019 → primary capital into SpaceX at the round price → 7 years of scaling via rounds, tenders, and merger consolidation → single-name exposure now the entire identity of the firm. The pattern is uncommon. Most Tiger Cub-style L/S firms got into private growth by writing checks across many private names (e.g., [[D1 Capital]], [[Coatue]], [[Tiger Global]]); Darsana effectively bet the firm on one position and held.

## [[SpaceX]] position (2019-2026)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| First investment | 2019 |
| Entry valuation | $33B (FT) / ~$30B (other) — aligns with SpaceX's April 2019 [[SpaceX#Funding rounds (complete)\|Series K]] at $33.3bn ([[Baillie Gifford]]-led) |
| Origin of position | Partner Dan Irom researching publicly traded satellite companies, met privately held SpaceX during the research and was invited to invest |
| Subsequent participation | Several additional financing rounds + secondary tenders |
| Current exposure (FT, May 18 2026) | ~$15bn at $1.75T SpaceX IPO valuation |
| Stake share of AUM | ~60% (GMT Eight) |
| Indirect exposure | Earlier investment in [[Twitter|X]] — converted to xAI shares in the 2025 X→[[xAI]] merger, then to [[SpaceX]] shares in the Feb 2026 [[SpaceX xAI merger]] |
| Indirect exposure | [[EchoStar]] holding — EchoStar received ~2.8% of SpaceX via the [[EchoStar#2025 Sep 8 (SpaceX spectrum deal #1)\|spectrum-for-equity deal]] in Sep / Nov 2025. Darsana is an EchoStar holder per [[Financial Times]] |

The 2019 entry timing is structurally important: it precedes the [[Starlink]] revenue inflection and predates the AI compute thesis attached to SpaceX. The position was underwritten as a launch monopoly + satellite internet bet, with the [[xAI]] roll-up and orbital-DC narrative arriving years later as upside that wasn't priced into the 2019 entry. Darsana's stake grew through both new primary commitments and the X / xAI / SpaceX consolidation cycle. (The [[Wall Street Journal]] first reported the expected payday on May 18, 2026; FT confirmed the same day.)

The structural read on the position-vs-AUM ratio is harsh: at ~60% of AUM in one illiquid private name, Darsana is effectively a single-name SpaceX vehicle with a long/short equity book attached. The IPO is the realization moment for that bet — and the lock-up arrangement (a custom non-standard structure rather than the typical 6-month lockup, per the IPO event note) determines when the paper gain converts to cash.

See [[SpaceX IPO 2026#Hedge fund winners (FT May 18 2026)]] for the event-side framing and side-by-side with [[D1 Capital]].
