# Luis Garicano

Spanish economist and former politician whose vault edge is the EU competitiveness/productivity debate. He is one of the named critics (the "ABG" trio) of [[Paul Krugman]]'s [[US-Europe productivity paradox]] framing.

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Role | Professor of economics and strategy at the [[LSE]]; former Member of the European Parliament (Renew Europe / Ciudadanos, 2019-2022) |
| Field | Productivity, organizational economics, EU economic policy and competitiveness |
| Vault relevance | EU-competitiveness voice; co-author of the productivity-growth critique of the Krugman/[[Brad DeLong\|DeLong]] measurement-paradox thesis |

## Relevant analysis

With [[Philippe Aghion]] and [[Antonin Bergeaud]], named by Krugman (May 30 2026) as advancing the view that US productivity growth genuinely outpaces Europe's and that the gap is widening — the producer-surplus reading [[Brad DeLong]] contests. Garicano's broader work on EU competitiveness connects to the [[Mario Draghi report]] agenda. See [[US-Europe productivity paradox]].
