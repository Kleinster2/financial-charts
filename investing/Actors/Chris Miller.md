# Chris Miller

Associate Professor of International History at [[Tufts University]] Fletcher School and author of *Chip War: The Fight for the World's Most Critical Technology* (2022) — the canonical narrative history of the semiconductor industry's geopolitical evolution. Miller is among the most-referenced voices in vault material on US-China technology competition, semiconductor industrial policy, and economic statecraft. The May 16, 2026 Substack pair on economic-power measurement ("What is economic power?" + "How should we measure economic power?") is the cleanest articulation in the vault material of the production-vs-profit methodological debate the 2020s industrial-policy revival has surfaced — see [[Industrial policy]].

## Why he matters

Miller is the bridge between academic economic history and operational industrial policy: he wrote the canonical narrative of how semiconductors became geopolitically central, and he is now actively contributing to the policy-design debate (US-China economic-security frameworks, measurement methodology). For vault purposes, his work is the most-frequently-citable source for both historical context and contemporary policy analysis on the semiconductor + industrial-policy threads.
