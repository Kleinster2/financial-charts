## Why Grimes matters

The relationship is the asset. Grimes has been Musk's banker of record across roughly 25 years, from Tesla's pre-IPO financing through the X / xAI / SpaceX consolidations and now the SpaceX listing. The FT's May 15 reporting on the IPO names him explicitly as the "central person running the deal" within the five-bank lead group — a position not formally assigned in the prospectus (which lists banks alphabetically) but real in practice through Grimes-Musk shorthand.

His one-year stint inside the Trump administration overlapped with the policy environment that produced the AI-export-control rollbacks and the index-rule revisions ([[Nasdaq Fast Entry Rule]], the S&P 500 seasoning review) that make a $1.75T listing structurally viable. He returned to Morgan Stanley specifically as the IPO timeline crystallized.
