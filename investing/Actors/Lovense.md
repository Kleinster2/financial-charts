## Business model

**Hardware + software + platform integrations.** Three revenue streams:

1. **Direct device sales** — 25+ products from $50-200, sold via lovense.com and Amazon
2. **Cam platform integration** — free browser extension connects devices to tip systems; drives hardware sales
3. **API licensing** — cam sites integrate Lovense SDK for tip-activated vibrations

The cam integration is the [[Flywheel]]: models buy Lovense devices to earn more tips → platforms integrate Lovense to attract models → viewers tip to activate toys → models recommend Lovense to other models.

**Platform partners:** Chaturbate, Stripchat, CamSoda, Cam4, MyFreeCams, SinParty. In June 2024, became the **only** live streaming software on [[OnlyFans]] supporting tip-activated vibrations — significant competitive moat.

- **Platform dependency** — OnlyFans/Chaturbate could build competing hardware or revoke integration
- **Regulatory** — varying global standards for adult products
- **China operations** — geopolitical risk, though consumer hardware less scrutinized than tech
- **Competition** — low barriers to copy hardware; software/platform integration is the moat

## Cam platform economics

Lovense's value proposition depends on the tip economy:

| Platform | Monthly visitors | Model payout | Platform cut |
|----------|------------------|--------------|--------------|
| Chaturbate | 300-520M | $0.05/token | ~50% |
| Stripchat | 630M | $0.05/token | ~50% |

**How tip-activated toys work:** Viewer tips trigger vibration intensity based on tip amount. Creates real-time interactivity — viewers feel they're "participating." Models using Lovense report higher tip rates and engagement.

**Lovense's position:** Only hardware company with deep platform integrations. Competitors ([[Satisfyer]], [[LELO]]) make devices but don't have the SDK partnerships. This software layer is the moat — hardware is commoditized, integrations are not.
