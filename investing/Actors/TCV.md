# TCV

**Growth equity firm** — pioneered "crossover" investing (public + private from same fund). Founded 1995 by Jay Hoag and Rick Kimball. **$23B+ raised lifetime**, ~$20B AUM. Early backer of [[Netflix]] (43% pre-IPO), [[Spotify]], [[Airbnb]], [[Facebook]]. 145+ IPOs and exits.

---

## Investment model: Crossover

**First firm to invest in both public and private companies from the same fund.**

| Stage | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Private | Growth equity ($[[3M]]-$300M checks) |
| Public | Can hold through and after IPO |
| Crossover | Same fund, same team, same thesis |

**Why it works:** Can support companies through entire growth journey. No forced selling at IPO. Patient capital.

## Investment relevance

**Why TCV matters:**

1. **Crossover pioneer** — invented the model now standard at Tiger, Coatue, etc.

2. **Netflix relationship** — 25+ year board seat, signature return.

3. **Patient capital** — holds through volatility, doesn't force IPO exits.

4. **[[Fintech]] exposure** — [[Klarna]], Revolut, Brex portfolio.

5. **Growth indicator** — TCV activity signals institutional confidence in growth.
