# Fred Ehrsam

Co-founder of [[Coinbase]] and co-founder of [[Paradigm]], one of the largest crypto-focused venture funds. Board member at [[Coinbase]]. Former [[Goldman Sachs]] FX trader.

---

## Synopsis

Ehrsam co-founded [[Coinbase]] with [[Brian Armstrong]] in 2012 after meeting on a Bitcoin forum — Ehrsam was trading FX at [[Goldman Sachs]] and saw crypto as the logical next step in electronic markets. He served as Coinbase's first head of business development before stepping back from day-to-day operations in 2017. His departure was amicable (he retained a board seat and significant equity), and the timing was prescient: he left before the 2018 crypto winter and redirected his energy into the investing side.

In 2018, Ehrsam co-founded [[Paradigm]] with Matt Huang (ex-[[Sequoia Capital]]) with an initial $400M fund — one of the largest debut crypto funds ever. Paradigm has since grown to manage billions across multiple funds, investing in DeFi protocols, crypto infrastructure, and blockchain projects. The firm's thesis is that crypto represents a fundamental computing paradigm shift, not just a financial asset class. Ehrsam's dual position — co-founder and board member of the dominant US crypto exchange, plus co-founder of a major crypto VC — gives him unusual visibility across both the infrastructure layer (Coinbase) and the application layer (Paradigm portfolio). This makes him one of the most structurally important individuals in the crypto ecosystem, with influence over both where capital flows and where it trades.
