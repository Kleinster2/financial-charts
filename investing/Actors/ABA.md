# ABA

American Bankers Association — the largest trade association for the [[United States]] banking industry, representing banks of all sizes from community lenders to the largest global institutions. Founded in 1875, the ABA is the primary lobbying voice for commercial banking on issues ranging from capital requirements and fintech regulation to agricultural lending reform.

## Synopsis

The ABA's membership spans the full spectrum of US banking: community banks, regional banks, and money-center institutions like [[JPMorgan Chase]], [[Bank of America]], and [[Citigroup]]. This broad membership creates inherent tension — the interests of a $50M-asset community bank in Iowa differ sharply from those of a $4T global bank — but the ABA navigates this by focusing on shared regulatory concerns: Basel III endgame capital rules, fintech charter policy, and competition from government-sponsored enterprises. The ABA works alongside [[ICBA]] (which represents community banks exclusively) on issues where interests align, notably the campaign to reform the [[Farm Credit System]]'s tax exemptions.

The ABA reported that the US banking industry held $205B in farm loans (~38% of total farm lending) at end of 2024, making the agricultural lending reform push economically significant. The FCS, which benefits from tax-exempt status and implicit government backing, competes directly with commercial banks for farm loans — a competitive advantage the ABA argues is outdated and distortive. Beyond agriculture, the ABA is a central actor in every major banking regulation debate: it lobbied against the initial Basel III endgame proposal, pushed for fintech regulatory clarity, and advocates for bank-favorable consumer protection rules. Its annual lobbying spend consistently ranks among the top financial services trade groups.
