## Why it matters

InnoStar matters less as a merchant memory supplier today than as a possible route around the [[HBM]]/GPU memory bottleneck. The Information reported on May 29, 2026 that ByteDance is in talks with InnoStar to develop a resistive-RAM inference chip similar in spirit to [[Groq]]'s LPU, while also pursuing a separate training ASIC.

ReRAM is attractive for inference because model weights are persistent and memory movement is the cost problem. If InnoStar can place non-volatile memory close enough to compute, ByteDance could reduce per-token serving cost for [[Doubao]] / [[Volcano Engine]] workloads. The caveat is maturity: ReRAM is still an emerging memory technology, and the public reporting does not yet prove production yield, process node, compiler support, or deployment scale.

## Existing ByteDance stake

ByteDance had already taken a minority stake in InnoStar in 2024 through a Pico-linked affiliate, according to SCMP and RRAM-Info. The original use case was VR memory, not AI inference, but it established ByteDance as a strategic hardware customer before the May 2026 AI-chip report.
