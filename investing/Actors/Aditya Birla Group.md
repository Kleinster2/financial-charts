# Aditya Birla Group

## Synopsis

[[Aditya Birla Group]] is one of India's largest multinational conglomerates, headquartered in Mumbai and chaired by [[Kumar Mangalam Birla]] since 1995 (after the death of his father, Aditya Vikram Birla, the namesake). The group reported consolidated revenue of more than $67 billion for FY 2025 (year ending March 2025), with operations spanning metals, cement, fashion and retail, financial services, renewables, fibre, textiles, chemicals, real estate, trading, mining, and entertainment across 42 countries. The group's combined annual revenue figure of ~$70 billion is approximately half-derived from overseas operations — primarily through [[Hindalco]]'s [[Novelis]] subsidiary (US/Canada/EU/Brazil aluminium rolled products) and through other international fibre and metals operations. Seven publicly listed companies in the group had combined market capitalisation of $100 billion+ as of March 2024.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Hindalco]] thread: Aditya Birla Group is Hindalco's promoter (~34.6% stake as of March 2022), the controlling shareholder behind the August 2025 $10B 5-year capex plan, and the source of the broader Indian non-ferrous metals consolidation strategy that frames the [[Big Law transformation|Indian metals cluster]] thesis. Birla family wealth (Forbes December 2024) was estimated at $23.1B for Kumar Mangalam Birla.

## Positioning in vault threads

Aditya Birla Group anchors three vault threads:

- *Indian metals consolidation* (via [[Hindalco]]): the August 2025 $10B 5-year capex plan, the Birla Copper Dahej smelter expansion to world's-largest non-China complex, and the Novelis-driven downstream aluminium franchise.
- *Indian cement consolidation* (via UltraTech): India's largest cement producer; reflects the Birla group's cyclical-industrial bias.
- *Indian financial services* (via Aditya Birla Capital): NBFC, AMC, and insurance arms positioning the group in financial-services cyclicals.

The group structure is family-conglomerate rather than diversified-holding-company — operational control runs through Birla family direction across the listed entities, with the chairman exercising strategic and capital-allocation authority across the group.
