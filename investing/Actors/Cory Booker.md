# Cory Booker

US Senator from New Jersey (D) since 2013. Former mayor of Newark (2006-2013). Known for tech policy engagement, criminal justice reform advocacy, and bipartisan work on AI governance.

---

## Synopsis

Booker occupies an unusual position in the Senate — a Democrat with genuine relationships in Silicon Valley (dating back to his Newark mayoralty, when he attracted tech investment and befriended figures like [[Mark Zuckerberg]], who donated $100M to Newark schools) and a willingness to engage with Republican tech-national security framing. He spoke at the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] in May 2024, representing the bipartisan nature of the forum's approach to tech-defense convergence, which matters because bipartisan participation gives policy proposals (AI regulation, export controls, defense tech procurement reform) a higher probability of becoming law.

For markets, Booker is relevant as a bellwether for where Democratic tech policy is heading. His engagement with the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] crowd — venture capitalists, defense tech founders, national security hawks — signals that the center-left is not uniformly hostile to Big Tech or defense tech. He has also been vocal on AI governance, pushing for frameworks that regulate without killing innovation, which positions him as a potential architect of whatever AI legislation emerges. His March 2025 25-hour filibuster speech protesting the Trump administration's budget and DOGE cuts made national headlines, demonstrating a willingness to use procedural tools for political messaging.
