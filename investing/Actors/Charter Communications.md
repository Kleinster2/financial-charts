# Charter Communications

Charter Communications (NASDAQ: CHTR) — second-largest US cable operator after [[Comcast]] by broadband subscribers. Operates under the Spectrum brand: residential and SMB internet, video, voice, mobile (Spectrum Mobile MVNO on [[Verizon]]), enterprise. Pending Cox Communications acquisition expected to close summer 2026 will pull pro-forma footprint roughly level with Comcast.

Top-line revenue plateau since 2022 reflects the broadband subscriber book stabilizing through 2024 and beginning to decline through 2025 — Q1 2026 -120k net is the steepest single-quarter erosion in the recent cycle. EBITDA durability has been the bull case and remains in place; the tension is whether broadband-subscriber attrition compounds faster than mobile and Cox synergies can offset.

## Q1 2026 results (released Apr 24)

The print resolved into three messages: a worse-than-expected residential broadband loss, a continued mobile growth story that is no longer enough to offset the broadband line, and a sharply higher Cox synergy target that the market read as confirmation of the deal's strategic logic but not the timing.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Revenue | $13.6B | $13.7B | -1.0% |
| Adjusted EBITDA | $5.5B | $5.6B | -2.2% |
| Free cash flow | $1.4B | $1.6B | -$200M |
| CapEx | $2.9B | $2.5B | +$456M |
| Reported EPS | $9.17 | n/m | -$0.91 vs consensus $10.08 |

| Subscriber metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 |
|-------------------|---------|---------|
| Residential internet net loss | -120,000 | -72,000 |
| Mobile net adds | +368,000 | +486,000 |
| Total Spectrum Mobile lines | 12.0M | 10.3M |
| Video net loss | -60,000 | -181,000 |

The 120k residential broadband loss missed the ~100k consensus and was nearly twice the prior-year decline. CEO Chris Winfrey attributed the pressure to "top-of-funnel" demand challenges — fixed wireless competition, fiber overbuild, and a sluggish housing market reducing move-ins — rather than pricing strategy. He did not call a bottom.

Mobile contribution remains a bright spot: +368k lines brought Spectrum Mobile to 12.0M total at 17% YoY growth. Net additions decelerated from the 486k Q1 2025 print, but the installed base is now large enough that it materially offsets video subscriber attrition.

Video losses improved 67% YoY to 60,000 — the cleanest line on the print. The video book has shrunk to the point where the rate of decline is decelerating mechanically.

## Cox Communications acquisition

Charter announced the Cox merger in 2024. Q1 2026 raised the run-rate operating-cost synergy target to "at least $800 million" from a prior $500M estimate.

| Detail | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Combined customer base | ~70M residential + SMB |
| Combined footprint | 46 states |
| Synergies (run-rate) | $800M+ (raised from $500M) |
| Approval still pending | California |
| Expected close | Summer 2026 |
| Form | Stock-and-cash, Cox shareholders ~23% pro-forma |

Strategic rationale: scale on broadband to compete against fixed wireless and fiber, scale on mobile to amortize fixed costs, and a more national footprint to pursue enterprise and political-advertising contracts.

Capex trajectory commentary on the call paired with synergies: 2026 capex guided to $11.4B, with a path to "below $8 billion run-rate by 2028." That is the first explicit step-down guidance.

## Broadband-subscriber pressure mechanics

Charter's residential broadband book is being attacked from three vectors simultaneously:

| Vector | Mechanism | Evidence |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Fixed wireless ([[T-Mobile]] / [[Verizon]] FWA) | Cheaper price point, sufficient speed for non-power users | Continued FWA net adds at carriers |
| Fiber overbuild | AT&T, [[Verizon]] FiOS, Frontier, Lumen overbuilding within Spectrum's footprint | Charter footprint roughly 55% overbuilt by fiber per Comcast call |
| Housing turnover | New-mover gross adds depressed by sluggish home sales | Existing-home sales near multi-decade lows |

The result is what management calls "top-of-funnel" pressure — gross adds are not keeping up with churn, even when ARPU and pricing hold.

## Investment considerations

The cable thesis has compressed into a Cox-execution-and-mobile-monetization question: can the combined Charter + Cox entity convert scale into broadband retention and mobile margin faster than fixed wireless and fiber can erode the residential book.

| Lens | Read |
|------|------|
| Cash flow durability | $1.4B FCF in Q1; the book is still cash-generative |
| Leverage | Net leverage running ~4.4x post-Cox close target |
| Buyback | Active; Cox close shifts capital allocation |
| Mobile economics | MVNO drag improving; could turn positive ~2027 |
| Cox synergies | $800M target now public; execution risk on California approval |
| Pricing power | Limited; convergence push (broadband + mobile bundle) is the lever |
