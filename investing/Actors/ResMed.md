#actor #healthcare #medtech #usa #public

ResMed -- global leader in CPAP, sleep apnea, breathing-health devices, and home-care software. GLP-1 drugs are a paradoxical tailwind/headwind: obesity reduction could reduce sleep apnea prevalence long-term, but near-term GLP-1 patients are 11% more likely to initiate CPAP therapy due to increased health awareness.

The post-GLP-1 market question is no longer "does sleep apnea disappear?" but whether diagnosis, adherence, connected devices, and home-care software offset any long-run obesity-prevalence drag. ResMed's Q3 FY2026 print argues the core franchise is still compounding: revenue grew 11%, gross margin expanded nearly 300 bps, and operating cash flow was $554M. The May 1 stock decline shows the valuation bar was higher than the business result.

## Q3 FY2026 earnings and CFO transition (May 2026)

ResMed reported Q3 FY2026 after the close on Apr 30. The quarter was operationally strong: revenue up 11% to $1.431B, gross margin up 290 bps to 62.2%, non-GAAP gross margin 62.8%, operating income up 17%, and non-GAAP EPS up 21% to $2.86. The company also generated $554M of operating cash flow and returned $262M to shareholders.

The stock sold off anyway. RMD closed at $203.74 on May 1, down 4.7% from $213.81 the prior close (-3.1 sigma on the vault mover scan). The most likely read is not that the quarter was weak; it was that the market wanted a cleaner raise / acceleration setup from a high-quality medtech compounder already trying to re-rate past the GLP-1 concern. Same-day CFO succession added an extra digestion point: [[Brett Sandercock]] is retiring after 20 years as CFO, with [[Aaron Bloomer]] joining from [[Exact Sciences]] after its acquisition by [[Abbott]].

Thesis implication: ResMed remains a profitable connected-device and home-care-software compounder, but the market is now separating "GLP-1 did not break the business" from "the stock deserves a premium multiple again." Reiterated FY2026 margin guidance was enough to validate the business, not enough to excite the equity.
