# Sivers Semiconductors

**Sivers Semiconductors** — Swedish photonics and mmWave chip supplier whose stock became a May 2026 AI-optics momentum vehicle. FY2025 net sales were SEK 306.6m, operating loss was SEK -177.8m, and the May 28 close of SEK 70.20 put the Stockholm line at roughly SEK 20.8B market cap. The investable question is whether Sivers is an emerging [[Co-Packaged Optics]] winner or an asset-light design shop being valued like a proven laser bottleneck.

The company is worth tracking because it sits at the intersection of CPO scarcity, outsourced InP production, and small-cap AI optics momentum.

## Synopsis

Sivers sits in the right market at the wrong level of proof. The company sells mmWave wireless chips for SATCOM/FWA/defense and photonics products for AI data centers, LiDAR, and optical interconnects. Its 2025 annual report shows a real but small business: SEK 306.6m revenue, 40% growth, SEK -177.8m operating loss, SEK 29.7m cash at year-end, and post-year-end refinancing plus a SEK 125m directed issue. The May 2026 equity rerating moved faster than the operating evidence: local data show SIVE.ST at SEK 70.20 on May 28, versus sub-SEK 4 levels in February, while the company still discloses opportunity pipeline and development programs rather than volume CPO purchase orders. Jason's Chips' May 2026 critique is therefore useful because it separates market adjacency from qualification: being near [[Ayar Labs]], [[POET Technologies]], [[Lightmatter]], and hyperscaler optics work does not equal being a production bottleneck.

## Business mix

Sivers has two operating businesses:

| Segment | FY2025 role | What it does |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Wireless | ~70% of 2025 revenue per Jason's Chips' reading of company disclosures | mmWave beamformers, RF chips, antenna arrays for SATCOM, FWA, 5G/6G, and defense |
| Photonics | ~30% of 2025 revenue | Indium-phosphide lasers, optical amplifiers, DFB laser arrays, external laser source components for AI data centers and LiDAR |

The annual report frames Wireless as still important to revenue, especially SATCOM programs with ALL.SPACE, Intelsat, Doosan, and ESA. Photonics is the reason the equity re-rated: partnerships with [[WIN Semiconductors]], O-Net, and [[POET Technologies]] are meant to scale DFB laser arrays and external laser-source/light-engine work for data-center optics.

The trap is that the valuation is being set by the photonics option, not by the current revenue base. Jason's Chips estimates Sivers' data-center photonics revenue is much smaller than total photonics revenue because the company does not disclose data-center photonics separately and includes LiDAR inside the segment.

## Evolution

Sivers entered the 2025-2026 AI optics window as a two-segment company rather than a pure datacenter photonics supplier. Wireless still carried most revenue in 2025 through SATCOM, FWA, 5G/6G, and defense programs, while Photonics supplied the upside narrative through DFB laser arrays, external laser-source work, and CPO-adjacent partnerships. The 2026 rerating compressed the usual proof sequence: the stock began pricing a scaled photonics outcome while disclosures still emphasized development programs, pipelines, and manufacturing partners.

## May 2026 Jason's Chips critique

[[Jason's Chips]] published a May 28, 2026 short thesis arguing that the old Sivers setup at ~$130m market cap was plausible but the current ~$2.3B valuation is not supported by disclosed production evidence.

Key distinctions:

| Issue | Jason's Chips read | Vault treatment |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Opportunity pipeline | $453m pipeline is a non-binding funnel, not backlog | Source-attributed, consistent with the company using opportunity language rather than order backlog |
| NRE revenue | Customer-funded development revenue dominates near-term activity | Treat as startup-like qualification work, not recurring product proof |
| CPO thesis | Current valuation needs an order-of-magnitude photonics step-up | Correct valuation frame: wireless/LiDAR cannot carry the market cap alone |
| Bottleneck claim | Sivers lacks hard-asset share and production qualification | Source-attributed; compare against [[Lumentum]] and established InP laser manufacturers |
| Architecture | Sivers' WDM laser-array pitch may be weaker for CPO than ultra-high-power external laser sources | Technical debate belongs in [[Co-Packaged Optics]] |

The strongest part of the critique is not the social-media/pump framing; it is the mechanism. Sivers is a fabless company using Glasgow for pilot/R&D volumes and relying on [[WIN Semiconductors]] for production scaling. If CPO's scarce resource is high-yield InP manufacturing and qualified ultra-high-power laser supply, the scarce asset sits with proven producers, not with a company still converting conversations into production awards.

- Q1 2026 report and whether revenue quality shifts from development/NRE to product revenue.
