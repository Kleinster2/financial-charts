**EPAM Systems** — global software-engineering and digital-services firm headquartered in Newtown, Pennsylvania, with operating roots in Belarus / Eastern Europe and a workforce of approximately 60,000 engineers. NYSE-listed (ticker EPAM). The firm provides software engineering, custom application development, and digital transformation services to enterprise clients across financial services, healthcare, life sciences, and consumer markets. EPAM's most consequential cybersecurity exposure was its role as the supply-chain pivot in the 2024 [[ShinyHunters]] / [[Snowflake]] credential-theft campaign: developer machines belonging to EPAM engineers (storing customer Snowflake credentials in plaintext within Jira and similar developer-tooling environments) were compromised, providing ShinyHunters access to up to 165 downstream Snowflake customer organizations including [[Ticketmaster|Ticketmaster (Live Nation)]] and Santander Bank. The breach demonstrated the scale risk of multi-tenant developer-tooling configurations across services-firm workforces — EPAM's compromise affected its customers, not EPAM directly.
