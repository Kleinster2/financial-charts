# Atlas Elektronik

**Atlas Elektronik** is a German naval defense and electronics company, operating as a joint venture between [[ThyssenKrupp Marine Systems]] (TKMS) and [[Airbus]]. Headquartered in Bremen, the company specializes in sonar systems, mine countermeasures (MCM), combat management systems, and unmanned maritime vehicles for navies worldwide.

## Synopsis

Atlas Elektronik occupies a critical niche in the European naval defense supply chain — its systems go into virtually every German-built submarine and many NATO surface combatants. The product portfolio spans the SeaFox expendable mine disposal system (a market leader deployed by 15+ navies), the ARCIMS autonomous surface vessel for MCM operations, and integrated sonar/combat management suites for submarines and frigates. The company is part of the [[Saab]]-led consortium bidding for Australia's SEA1905 mine countermeasures program, one of the largest MCM procurements globally.

The TKMS-Airbus ownership structure reflects the European defense industry's tendency toward cross-shareholdings that prioritize national champions over shareholder returns. TKMS (a [[ThyssenKrupp]] subsidiary) brings submarine hull expertise; Airbus contributes electronics and systems integration capabilities. For the vault, Atlas Elektronik matters as a bellwether for European naval rearmament — NATO's post-Ukraine pivot toward maritime defense spending benefits exactly this class of subsystem maker, particularly in autonomous mine countermeasures where the technology gap with [[Russia]] and [[China]] is smallest and the operational need is most acute. Key competitor is [[Exail Technologies]] (formerly iXblue/ECA Group), which offers rival MCM systems and autonomous underwater vehicles.
