# Lei Jun

Founder, chairman, and CEO of [[Xiaomi]] (1969–). Built Xiaomi from a 2010 smartphone challenger into a vertically integrated consumer-electronics, smart-home, AI-model, and EV company. Drove the [[Xiaomi]] EV pivot announced in 2021, with the [[SU7]] sedan delivered three years later and the [[YU7]] SUV launched in 2025. Comparisons in the Chinese press to Steve Jobs reflect product-launch theatrics and personal brand more than any direct strategic parallel.

## Why Lei matters for this vault

Lei is the executive face of the [[Xiaomi]] consumer-AI thesis: he personally drives launch events (his April 2026 Beijing Auto Show keynote announced the [[YU7 GT]] as Xiaomi's first vehicle developed with European engineers in [[Munich]]) and is treated as the strategic decision-maker behind moves like the EV launch, the in-house [[Xring O1]] chip, the [[MiMo-V2-Pro]] model family, and the European premium-auto entry. His continued direct involvement in product reveals is one reason the market reads Xiaomi launches as carrying credibility comparable to a small handful of consumer-tech CEOs globally.

## Apr 2026 Beijing Auto Show keynote

At the April 2026 Beijing Auto Show, Lei said the new [[YU7 GT]] "can match the standards of a top-tier German car," positioning the model against [[Porsche]], [[BMW]], and [[Mercedes-Benz]] in Europe. He also said: "In just five years, Xiaomi has achieved remarkable milestones. Yet even today, many people still don't fully understand Xiaomi cars, and some even hold certain biases."

The framing is significant: Xiaomi's CEO is presenting the [[YU7 GT]] as a credible challenger to top-tier German premium cars and going to Europe with a model engineered partly in [[Munich]]. That is a marked shift from prior rhetoric of "Xiaomi as fast-follower" to "Xiaomi as premium-segment competitor."

*Source: FT, Apr 26 2026.*
