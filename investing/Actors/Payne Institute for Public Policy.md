# Payne Institute for Public Policy

Research institute at the [[Colorado School of Mines]] focused on energy policy, natural resources, and the geopolitics of the energy transition. Hosts non-resident fellows from across the energy-policy community, including [[Carolyn Kissane]] (NYU SPS Center for Global Affairs Associate Dean) — the institutional link between NYU's energy-policy programmes and the Colorado School of Mines technical-energy community.

## Why it matters in the vault

The Payne Institute is one of the small set of US research institutes that combines technical-energy expertise (via the Colorado School of Mines engineering and earth-sciences tradition) with policy framing. For vault purposes, the Payne Institute matters mainly as an affiliation marker — Kissane's non-resident fellowship is part of why her [[Energy Common Sense]] Substack commentary spans both the policy and physical-energy dimensions credibly. The institute is also a venue where critical-minerals research surfaces ahead of mainstream policy commentary.
