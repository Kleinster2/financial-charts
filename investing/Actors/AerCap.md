# AerCap

AerCap Holdings (NYSE: AER) is the largest aircraft lessor in the world by fleet value, headquartered in Dublin, Ireland. Lessors purchase commercial aircraft from manufacturers ([[Boeing]], [[Airbus]]) and lease them to airlines under operating-lease structures, providing the airlines with fleet flexibility and the lessor with long-duration cash flows. AerCap acquired GE Capital Aviation Services (GECAS) in 2021 for ~$31B, consolidating the global aircraft leasing market.

## Post-Spirit shutdown role (May 2026)

[[Spirit Airlines]]'s May 2 2026 shutdown ([[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]]) returns ~200 [[Airbus A320]]-family aircraft to the secondary lessor market. AerCap and [[Air Lease Corp]] are the two most likely absorbers given the scale and standardized fleet — AerCap holds significant existing A320-family lease exposure to other US ULCCs ([[Frontier Airlines]] / [[Allegiant Travel]]) and to global operators that may take ex-Spirit aircraft on standardized lease terms.

The economics for AerCap are mixed: on one hand, ~200 jets entering the secondary market depresses lease rates on the broader A320 fleet; on the other hand, the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran-war]] jet-fuel cost regime that broke Spirit increases demand for fuel-efficient newer-generation aircraft (A320neo / A321neo), which AerCap is positioned to deliver. Lease-rate factor on newer A320neo family typically commands a premium that older A320ceo aircraft cannot match.
