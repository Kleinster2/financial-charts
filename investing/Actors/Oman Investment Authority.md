Oman Investment Authority is [[Oman]]'s sovereign wealth fund, formed in 2020 by merging the State General Reserve Fund (SGRF) and Oman Investment Fund (OIF). Estimated AUM of $17-18B, investing across energy, infrastructure, and diversification projects aligned with Oman Vision 2040.

## Synopsis

OIA operates as the investment arm of Oman's economic diversification strategy, a necessity driven by the country's smaller oil reserves relative to Gulf neighbors — Oman produces roughly 1M bbl/d versus [[Saudi Arabia]]'s 9-10M, giving it less fiscal runway. The 2020 merger consolidated two overlapping funds under a single mandate, led by President Abdulsalam Al Murshidi. OIA's portfolio spans domestic infrastructure (ports, logistics, industrial zones), regional investments (GCC real estate and financial services), and international diversification (stakes in global funds and direct investments in technology and renewable energy).

Compared to the Gulf's mega-funds — [[Abu Dhabi Investment Authority]] (~$990B), Saudi [[PIF]] ($930B+), [[Qatar Investment Authority]] (~$510B) — OIA is an order of magnitude smaller. This scale constraint forces selectivity: OIA cannot compete for trophy assets but can be an effective co-investor alongside larger sovereign funds or a lead investor in mid-market Omani and regional opportunities. The Vision 2040 focus areas (logistics via Duqm port, green hydrogen, mining, tourism) are long-cycle bets. Al Murshidi's confirmed appearance at the [[Qatar Economic Forum 2026]] signals continued engagement with the regional sovereign wealth network. The key risk is Oman's fiscal vulnerability: at lower oil prices, the government draws on OIA rather than adding to it.
