# Qnity Electronics

Pure-play semiconductor materials company spun off from [[DuPont]] on Nov 1, 2025. NYSE: Q. S&P 500 member from day one. Provides materials and solutions across the entire semiconductor value chain — from chip manufacturing and [[Advanced Packaging|advanced packaging]] to high-speed interconnects, thermal management, and system-level integration.

## Synopsis

Qnity is what happens when a 200-year-old chemical conglomerate realizes its electronics division is worth more as a standalone. DuPont carved out its Electronics & Industrial segment and sent it public under a new name with a clean balance sheet, a $30B+ TAM, and immediate S&P 500 inclusion. The company makes the materials that go *into* and *around* chips — not the chips themselves. Think electroplating chemicals for advanced-node interconnects, Kapton polyimide films for flexible circuits, Pyralux materials for high-density packaging, CMP slurries, photoresists, thermal interface materials, and dielectric coatings. Two-thirds of revenue is directly tied to semiconductors. The rest serves adjacent electronics (PCBs, displays, connectors). The investment case is straightforward: as chip geometries shrink and packaging complexity explodes (chiplets, HBM stacking, CoWoS, panel-level fanout), the materials challenges multiply — and Qnity is one of the few companies with breadth across all of them. Revenue grew 10% organically in 2025, FCF margin was 21%, and Q4 earnings crushed estimates ($0.82 vs $0.55). The NVIDIA partnership announced March 18, 2026 — using AI-accelerated simulation to compress materials R&D timelines — is an ecosystem signal, not a revenue event. Competitors include [[Entegris]], Merck Electronics, Cabot Microelectronics (now CMC Materials), and Hitachi Chemical. The key risk is cyclicality: revenue dropped 15% in FY2023 during the semi downturn, and the Iran war has already hit both Qnity and Entegris ~15-20% since Feb 28.

## NVIDIA collaboration (March 18, 2026)

Qnity announced a collaboration with [[NVIDIA]] to use AI-accelerated modeling and simulation for materials R&D. Specific tools: NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano (open LLM), ALCHEMI BMD NIM (materials simulation microservice), LAMMPS Kokkos (GPU-accelerated molecular dynamics), CUDA-X accelerated Abaqus (finite element analysis).

Focus areas: advancing materials for next-gen AI chips, HPC, and [[Advanced Packaging|advanced packaging]]. The goal is compressing R&D timelines — using GPU simulation to test material properties (signal integrity at higher speeds/densities, thermal performance, reliability) before physical prototyping.

This is an ecosystem play, not a revenue deal. NVIDIA is building out its computational materials science stack (also partnering with [[Synopsys]] and [[Applied Materials]] at GTC 2026). Qnity is a key customer-facing materials supplier that validates the stack.

CTO Randy King: *"Leveraging accelerated modeling allows us to compress development timelines and bring innovation for cutting-edge applications to market faster, while optimizing performance factors such as signal integrity, reliability, and manufacturability."*

## $61.5M R&D facility (March 6, 2026)

Investing $61.5M in a new advanced semiconductor R&D and manufacturing facility. Strengthens local-for-local footprint and accelerates capacity for advanced-node and advanced packaging demand.

## Iran war impact

Morningstar noted (March 2026) that shares of semiconductor materials firms [[Entegris]] and Qnity have fallen ~20% and ~15% respectively since the Iran war began. The mechanism: supply chain disruption fears (Gulf petrochemical feedstocks affect some specialty chemical inputs), general risk-off in semi supply chain, and macro growth slowdown hitting cyclical names.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Pure-play on semiconductor materials complexity — as nodes shrink and packaging explodes, materials challenges multiply
- ~21% FCF margin with secular growth tailwinds (AI, advanced packaging, chiplets, [[HBM]])
- S&P 500 inclusion from day one = institutional ownership base
- Breadth across the value chain — few competitors match the range from fab materials to system-level interconnects
- [[DuPont]] heritage brands (Kapton, Pyralux, Kalrez) with deep customer lock-in

Bear:
- Cyclical: revenue dropped 15% in FY2023 semi downturn
- Iran war / macro risk hitting shares near-term
- Competitive: [[Entegris]], Merck Electronics, and Japanese specialty chemical companies all targeting same TAM
- CFO transition (Matt Harbaugh stepped down Jan 2026) — leadership instability risk
- Newly independent company — still proving standalone execution
