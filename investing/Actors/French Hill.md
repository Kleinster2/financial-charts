## On Powell DOJ closure (Apr 24, 2026)

After [[Jeanine Pirro]] announced the close of the [[Jerome Powell|Powell]] investigation: *"It is a step in the right direction and I applaud it. I hope that facilitates the prompt confirmation of [[Kevin Warsh]]."* Hill emphasized the importance of having a "permanent Senate-confirmed chair" for Congressional oversight purposes, and pointed to [[Scott Bessent]] as wanting "a good Fed chair that has the confidence in the president."

Hill's seat at House Financial Services makes his framing relevant for the institutional confirmation push but not for the actual vote — the bottleneck is in the Senate, where [[Tom Tillis]] is the holdout.

*Source: Bloomberg Real Yield, Apr 24, 2026*
