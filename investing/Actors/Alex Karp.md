**Alex Karp** — CEO and co-founder of [[Palantir]] (PLTR) since its founding in 2003. A philosophy PhD from Stanford (dissertation on neoclassical sociology under Jurgen Habermas at Goethe University Frankfurt), Karp is one of the most unconventional CEOs in tech: an outspoken advocate for Western defense applications of data analytics who practices Tai Chi, avoids Silicon Valley social circles, and has publicly feuded with both activist investors and tech industry norms around government contracts.

## Synopsis

Karp's vault relevance is inseparable from [[Palantir]]'s story. He co-founded the company with [[Peter Thiel]] and others, originally building data integration platforms for the [[CIA]] and US intelligence community. Under his leadership, Palantir expanded from government intelligence (Gotham) into commercial enterprise (Foundry) and most recently into AI-powered decision-making (AIP — Artificial Intelligence Platform). Karp's 2024-2025 pivot to position Palantir as the "operating system for AI" in both defense and enterprise drove the stock from ~$8 (late 2022) to $100+ (early 2026), making it one of the best-performing large-cap tech stocks of that period.

His compensation has been controversial — Karp received ~$1.1B in stock-based compensation in 2020 alone. He has been a significant seller of shares through 10b5-1 plans, though his remaining stake (~3-4%) still represents billions in value. Karp's public persona — media interviews emphasizing the moral case for Western tech companies supporting democratic governments — has become a differentiating brand asset for Palantir's government sales pipeline.
