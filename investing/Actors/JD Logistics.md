# JD Logistics

JD Logistics is [[China]]'s largest integrated supply chain logistics company (HKEX: 2618), spun out of [[JD.com]] via a Hong Kong IPO in May 2021 (raised HK$24.6B). The company operates 1,600+ warehouses covering 99% of Chinese counties, providing same-day and next-day delivery capabilities that are the backbone of JD.com's competitive advantage in e-commerce fulfillment. FY2025 revenue reached RMB 217.1B (+18.8% YoY) with operating margin of 2.4%.

JD Logistics is increasingly serving external (non-JD) clients, with third-party revenue growing as a share of total — a critical strategic shift from captive logistics arm to independent platform. The company is investing in warehouse automation (over 40 "Asia No.1" mega-warehouses with robotic sorting), autonomous delivery vehicles, and drone logistics for rural areas. International expansion is nascent but growing, with cross-border logistics services supporting JD.com's globalization push and standalone freight forwarding operations in Southeast Asia.
