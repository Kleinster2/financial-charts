**DISA** (Defense Information Systems Agency) is a US Department of Defense combat support agency providing IT infrastructure, communications, and cybersecurity to the military. It manages the [[Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability]] contract ($9B ceiling), classified networks (SIPRNet, JWICS), and serves as the central procurement authority for DoD enterprise IT — making it the gateway through which tech companies access the defense market's largest IT contracts.

## Synopsis

DISA operates the backbone of US military communications: SIPRNet (Secret Internet Protocol Router Network) and JWICS (Joint Worldwide Intelligence Communications System) are the classified networks through which intelligence and operational orders flow. The agency also manages milCloud (DoD's private cloud environment) and serves as the certification authority for commercial cloud services seeking DoD authorization — the process through which [[AWS]], [[Microsoft Azure]], [[Google Cloud]], and [[Oracle Cloud]] qualified for the [[Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability|JWCC]] multi-vendor contract. DISA's headquarters at Fort Meade, Maryland places it adjacent to the [[NSA]], reflecting the convergence of military communications and signals intelligence.

The investable angle is JWCC Next — the successor to the current $9B multi-vendor cloud contract, with solicitation expected in Q1 CY2026. JWCC Next is expected to be larger and potentially include AI/ML workloads alongside traditional cloud computing, which would benefit [[Scale AI]] (whose Donovan platform is already deployed on DISA-managed SIPRNet/JWICS) and the hyperscalers competing for cleared cloud capacity. For defense IT contractors, DISA's procurement decisions shape the competitive landscape — being on or off a DISA vehicle determines access to a customer base of 3+ million DoD users. The agency's shift toward zero-trust architecture and AI-enabled network defense creates demand that flows through DISA's contracting mechanisms to companies like [[Palo Alto Networks]], [[CrowdStrike]], and the traditional defense IT primes.
