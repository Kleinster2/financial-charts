Axa is a [[France|French]] multinational insurance and asset management group, one of the largest insurers globally by revenue and assets. Headquartered in Paris, the company operates across life and P&C insurance, health, and asset management (through [[AXA Investment Managers]]), serving over 90 million clients in 50+ countries. CEO Thomas Buberl has led since 2016, overseeing a strategic shift from a conglomerate model toward higher-margin, capital-light businesses — selling off legacy life insurance books and expanding in health and commercial lines.

In February 2026, Buberl stated that AXA's private credit exposure was "far below" European insurance peers, positioning the company as more conservative in the alternatives arms race that has drawn in [[Allianz]], [[Zurich]], and others. AXA's asset management arm manages ~EUR 800B and competes with [[Amundi]], [[PIMCO]], and [[BlackRock]] across European institutional mandates. The company trades on Euronext Paris (ticker: CS) and is a CAC 40 constituent.
