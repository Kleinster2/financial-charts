**Richard Liu** (Liu Qiangdong) — founder and chairman of [[JD.com]], [[China]]'s largest direct-sales e-commerce company. Built the business from a single market stall into a ~$37B logistics-integrated retail empire, then stepped back from day-to-day operations after a 2018 sexual assault arrest in [[Minneapolis]] before returning to an operational role in 2025 with an ambitious robotics and autonomous logistics push.

## Synopsis

Liu's story is inseparable from JD.com's identity as the anti-[[Alibaba]]. Where Alibaba built a marketplace connecting buyers to third-party sellers, Liu insisted on a first-party retail model with company-owned inventory and an in-house logistics network — a choice that depressed margins for years but created the supply chain infrastructure that now handles 700M+ customers. He started with RMB 12,000 borrowed from relatives and a counter in [[Beijing]]'s Zhongguancun electronics market in 1998, pivoted online during [[SARS]] in 2003-2004, and took JD public on [[NASDAQ]] in 2014. The logistics arm later spun out as [[JD Logistics]] (IPO 2021).

The 2018 Minneapolis arrest on sexual assault suspicion (not charged; civil suit settled October 2022) triggered his gradual retreat from the CEO role, replaced first by Xu Lei in April 2022 and then by Sandy Xu Ran from May 2023. Liu retains control through super-voting shares. His 2025 operational return centers on an aggressive automation vision: 3 million logistics robots, 1 million autonomous vehicles, and 100,000 delivery drones over five years. He also launched JD.com's food delivery service in February 2025, taking on [[Meituan]] directly — a move that signals willingness to burn cash (RMB 46.6B in New Businesses losses in FY2024) for market share.
