**thyssenkrupp Marine Systems** — German naval shipbuilder, the marine systems division of [[thyssenkrupp AG]]. Builds submarines, frigates, and corvettes for the German Navy and export customers. Includes the Howaldtswerke-Deutsche Werft (HDW) yard in Kiel, the world's leading exporter of conventional (non-nuclear) submarines. Lead foreign partner on Brazil's Tamandaré-class frigate program via the Águas Azuis consortium with [[Embraer]] Defense.

---

## Synopsis

TKMS is the second major NATO-partner naval anchor in the Brazilian defense industrial base, alongside [[Naval Group]] in submarines. Its Brazilian footprint runs through the Águas Azuis consortium (TKMS + [[Embraer]] Defense + local partners), which won the 2019 contract to build four Tamandaré-class frigates for the [[Brazilian Navy]]. Construction is at Itajaí, [[Santa Catarina]]; the first frigate Tamandaré (F200) was launched in 2024 with commissioning expected 2026-2028. The structure differs from PROSUB: where Naval Group builds under a joint venture (ICN) with Brazilian Navy capital, TKMS builds via a consortium with Embraer Defense providing the combat system integration and the local industrial footprint.

The strategic significance is that TKMS's Tamandaré program proves a model for foreign-led surface combatant construction in Brazil that is structurally distinct from Naval Group's submarine model — both heavily tech-transfer-loaded, but the Tamandaré program preserves more autonomy for the foreign prime while still satisfying Lei 12.598 indigenization requirements. The program also slots Embraer Defense into surface combatants alongside its aerospace position, consolidating Embraer's role as the central Brazilian defense systems integrator across air, land (via aerospace heritage), and now naval domains.

## Brazilian program — Tamandaré-class frigates

In 2019, the Águas Azuis consortium — led by TKMS with [[Embraer]] Defense providing combat-system integration and local industrial coordination — won the Brazilian Navy's Tamandaré-class frigate contract over competing bids from Italian (Fincantieri / Leonardo), French ([[Naval Group]]), Dutch (Damen), and Swedish ([[Saab]]) consortia. The program:

- 4 frigates total, all built at Itajaí, Santa Catarina
- Lead ship Tamandaré (F200) launched 2024; commissioning expected 2026
- Subsequent ships at approximately 18-24 month intervals
- Based on the MEKO A-100 design (TKMS family) modified for Brazilian Navy requirements
- Combat system integration via Embraer Defense (Atech subsidiary contributes radar and C4I)

The Águas Azuis consortium structurally separates the foreign prime (TKMS, providing design and construction expertise) from the Brazilian industrial partner (Embraer Defense, providing local labor, integration, and Brazilian-specific systems). This preserves more TKMS strategic autonomy than the [[PROSUB]] joint-venture model used by [[Naval Group]] / [[Itaguaí Construções Navais]].

Subsidiary of [[thyssenkrupp AG]] (Frankfurt: TKA). Has periodically been the subject of spin-off / divestiture discussions; potential separation from the parent group has been recurring news but no transaction has closed as of May 2026. German government holds golden-share-equivalent veto power over change of control given national-security implications.
