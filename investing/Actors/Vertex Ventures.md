#actor #vc #israel

**Vertex Ventures** is a global venture capital network affiliated with [[Temasek]], [[Singapore]]'s sovereign wealth fund, operating autonomous regional funds in [[Israel]], [[Southeast Asia]], [[China]], and the [[United States]]. The Israel arm — Vertex Ventures Israel — is the longest-established and most storied, with a track record dating to the 1990s.

## Synopsis

Vertex Ventures Israel operates as an early-stage investor in cybersecurity, enterprise software, and deep tech, investing out of dedicated Israel-focused funds typically in the $150-250M range. The Temasek affiliation provides patient capital and a global network (particularly into Asian markets), while the Israeli team maintains local autonomy on investment decisions. The firm's standout return is [[CyberArk]], where Vertex led the Series A/B rounds in 2002 and held a 6.8% stake at IPO — CyberArk went on to become one of the top-performing Israeli cybersecurity companies with a market cap exceeding $15B.

The broader Vertex network operates as a federation rather than a monolithic fund: Vertex Ventures SEA invests in Southeast Asian startups, Vertex Ventures HC focuses on US/Israel healthcare, and Vertex Ventures China covers the mainland. Each fund has its own GPs and LPs, but the shared Temasek relationship enables cross-pollination on deals and co-investments. For the vault, Vertex matters as a bridge between Temasek's sovereign capital and the Israeli tech ecosystem — a connection that has strategic significance as [[Singapore]] and [[Israel]] deepen bilateral tech and defense cooperation.
