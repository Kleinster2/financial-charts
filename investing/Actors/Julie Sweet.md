# Julie Sweet

Chair and CEO of [[Accenture]] (CEO since September 2019, chair since 2021). A lawyer by training — a Cravath, Swaine & Moore partner before joining Accenture in 2010 as general counsel — she became the public face of Accenture's pivot to AI-era "reinvention" services, and, on Jun 18 2026, of the market's fear that generative AI is compressing rather than expanding the IT-services wallet.

## Notable

On the Jun 18 2026 Q3 FY2026 call, Sweet conceded corporate IT budgets are not rising overall — clients are "spending it differently, but they haven't been increasing" — while maintaining Accenture is still winning AI-adoption advisory work. She attributed part of the revenue miss to the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Middle East war]] (a ~$100mn quarterly hit beyond plan, plus slower client decisions). See [[Accenture]].
