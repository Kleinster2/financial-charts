## The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis (Feb 2026)

Scenario memo written as a fictional June 2028 macro retrospective: AI agents displace white-collar work faster than consumption can adjust — unemployment peaks at 10.2%, the S&P 500 draws down 38%, "Ghost GDP" (output that never circulates as purchasing power) emerges, and the spiral terminates in a private-credit blowup. Full anatomy, market impact (Dow -822 on Feb 23), and the rebuttal landscape live in [[Citrini 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis]].

The authors' own caveat: "a scenario, not a prediction."

## Critical reception

The report drew an institutional pile-on documented in the event note. The FT Alphaville critique ("The 'extreme and improbable' economics of Citrini's AI report," Feb 24, 2026) carried the canonical line — Krishna Guha ([[Evercore]]): even if the tech and microeconomics evolved as drawn, the macro would require "a set of extreme and improbable conditions." Critics across [[Citadel Securities]], [[Deutsche Bank]], Fidelity International, and Liontrust challenged the economic logic; a White House economist called it "science fiction." The counter-pole is [[Citadel Securities 2026 Global Intelligence Crisis]].
