**Li Qiang** (李强, born July 1959) — Premier of China and second-ranked member of the Politburo Standing Committee. Xi Jinping's former chief of staff in Zhejiang who became the first Premier since Mao without prior State Council experience. Increasingly seen as Xi's key economic delegate to foreign leaders.

## Background

**Education:**
- BA, Agricultural Mechanization, Ningbo college
- Rose through Zhejiang provincial bureaucracy

**Origins:** Grew up in small town in Zhejiang. Mother sold pork, father was government worker. Partly raised by grandmother. Started career at irrigation pumping station.

**Xi relationship:** Became Xi's chief of staff in 2004 when Xi was Zhejiang Party Secretary — a relationship that shaped his political ascent.

## Governance style

**More active than predecessors:** Has chaired 8 State Council plenary meetings in just two years — matching the number held during any full five-year term by Wen Jiabao or Li Keqiang.

**Growing delegation from Xi:** Recent signals suggest Xi is delegating key economic policymaking aspects to Li Qiang, making him a more valuable interlocutor for foreign leaders and executives.

**Regular consultation:** Regularly meets with businesspeople and economists; serves as political channel for feedback on China's business environment.
