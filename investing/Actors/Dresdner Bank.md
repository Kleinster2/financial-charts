Dresdner Bank was [[Germany]]'s third-largest bank before being acquired by [[Allianz]] in 2001 for ~22B EUR in a "bancassurance" deal that proved disastrous. The acquisition thesis — that combining insurance distribution with banking would create cross-selling synergies — failed to materialize. Instead, Allianz inherited Dresdner Kleinwort (the investment bank), which hemorrhaged money during the 2008 financial crisis. After cumulative writedowns exceeding 6.4B EUR and 18,000 job cuts, [[Allianz]] sold Dresdner to [[Commerzbank]] in 2008-09 for just 5.5B EUR, destroying roughly 16B EUR in shareholder value.

Founded in 1872 in Dresden, the bank was one of Germany's "Big Three" alongside [[Deutsche Bank]] and Commerzbank for over a century. Its investment banking arm, Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein, briefly competed for European deal flow but never achieved critical mass against Anglo-American rivals. The Dresdner disaster is a canonical case study in failed financial conglomerate strategies and the dangers of buying into investment banking at cycle peaks.
