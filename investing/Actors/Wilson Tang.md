**Wilson Tang** is a New York City restaurateur best known for reviving his family's Nom Wah Tea Parlor in Chinatown and expanding into new concepts including [[Cha Cha Tang]] with [[John McDonald (restaurateur)|John McDonald]].

## Synopsis

Tang took over Nom Wah Tea Parlor -- the oldest dim sum parlor in Manhattan's Chinatown, operating since 1920 at 13 Doyers Street -- from his uncle Wally Tang in 2010 and turned it from a fading neighborhood institution into a destination restaurant. He expanded the brand to Nom Wah Nolita, Nom Wah Philadelphia, and a fast-casual spin-off, while keeping the original location's retro aesthetic. The expansion demonstrated that Chinatown dim sum could scale beyond the single-location model without losing authenticity.

His collaboration with [[John McDonald (restaurateur)|John McDonald]] on [[Cha Cha Tang]], a Chinese concept under [[Mercer Street Hospitality]], represents a crossover play -- pairing Tang's Chinese food credibility with McDonald's downtown restaurant infrastructure. Tang also runs a frozen dumpling business and has become a media-savvy figure in New York's food scene, appearing on food shows and maintaining a strong social media presence. His story is a template for the second-generation immigrant restaurateur who professionalized a family business.
