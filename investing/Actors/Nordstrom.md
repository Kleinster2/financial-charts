# Nordstrom

**Premium US department store** — went private in March 2025 via $6.25B buyout by the Nordstrom family (50.1%) and Mexican retailer El Puerto de Liverpool (49.9%). FY24 revenue ~$14.5B, comparable sales +3.6%. Fourth-generation family leadership (Erik as CEO, Pete as President). Two banners: Nordstrom (full-price luxury) and Nordstrom Rack (off-price). $24.25/share buyout = 42% premium. No longer publicly traded.

| Rationale | Long-term flexibility, founder death (Bruce, May 2024) |

**Why private:** The Nordstrom family cited the need for long-term strategic flexibility without quarterly earnings pressure. Bruce Nordstrom's death at 90 in May 2024 was a catalyst — the family wanted to reunify control.
