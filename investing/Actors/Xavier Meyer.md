Xavier Meyer leads the repositioning of [[Aberdeen]]'s Investments division, overseeing the firm's strategic pivot toward real assets, credit, and specialist equities as traditional active management faces secular fee compression.

## Synopsis

Meyer serves as Head of Investments at [[Aberdeen]] (formerly abrdn, formerly Aberdeen Standard Investments), where he is tasked with the firm's most consequential strategic challenge: differentiating a mid-tier active asset manager in a market where passive funds capture the majority of flows. His mandate centers on shifting Aberdeen's product mix toward higher-fee, higher-alpha categories — real assets (infrastructure, real estate), private credit, and specialist equity strategies — where passive alternatives are weaker and institutional demand is growing. This is the same playbook that [[Invesco]], [[Franklin Templeton]], and other traditional managers are running, but Aberdeen's execution has been uneven.

The context for Meyer's role is Aberdeen's post-merger struggles. The 2017 merger of Aberdeen Asset Management and Standard Life created a $650B+ AUM platform, but the combined firm hemorrhaged assets for years as institutional clients defected and the integration dragged. By 2024, the firm (now "abrdn") had stabilized outflows but remained subscale relative to [[BlackRock]] or [[Amundi]]. Meyer's division is where Aberdeen's turnaround thesis lives or dies — if the specialist strategies can grow fees faster than the legacy equity book shrinks, the firm survives as independent. If not, it becomes an acquisition target. His work on [[Aberdeen Investcorp Infrastructure Partners]] (the JV with [[Investcorp]] for Middle East infrastructure) is one example of the real-assets push.
