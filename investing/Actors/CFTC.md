# CFTC

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission, the U.S. federal regulator of futures and options markets. Publishes the Commitment of Traders (COT) report — the only public window into futures positioning by trader category. Weekly data (Tuesday positions, released Friday 3:30 PM ET). Traces back to 1924 (USDA); weekly since 1962. Disaggregated format since 2009 separates producers, swap dealers, managed money, and other reportables.

## Prediction markets

The CFTC is the federal regulator at the center of the US [[Prediction markets]] fight. [[Kalshi]] has been a CFTC-designated contract market since Nov 2020, while state regulators argue that sports and culture event contracts function as unlicensed gambling. CRS framed the 2026 dispute as a federal-preemption and regulatory-arbitrage problem: current CFTC leadership has taken a more permissive view of event contracts, but states and tribes are litigating whether CFTC jurisdiction blocks gambling-law enforcement.

The current rulemaking question matters directly for institutional adoption. If the CFTC creates a durable federal perimeter for event contracts, platforms can plug into brokers, prime brokers, and market makers. If state gaming authorities retain broad blocking power, the sports-driven volume base and hedge-fund access story become state-by-state rather than national.

*Sources: CRS, "Prediction Markets: Policy Issues for Congress," Mar 20, 2026; Adam Iscoe, NYT Magazine, "The Average Guys Outsmarting Wall Street on Prediction Markets," May 26, 2026.*
