## 2026: record $5bn Medallia loss

In June 2026 Thoma Bravo wrote off its stake in [[Medallia]] — the customer-experience software group it took private in 2021 — taking a ~$5bn hit, the second-largest loss in private-equity history after the TXU collapse (per Daniel Rasmussen, Verdad Advisers). A [[Blackstone]]-led lender group ([[Apollo]], [[KKR]]) took control and injected $150mn to cut the debt; lenders had already marked the loans to ~60-70 cents on the dollar. Orlando Bravo called it "a big mistake" — "we paid too much because that growth didn't materialise" — and said Thoma preferred to redeploy capital into new investments rather than pay down Medallia's debt.

The loss is the marquee instance of the early-2020s PE-software-takeover reckoning: pandemic-era valuations that have proven hard to exit, financed heavily by [[Private credit]], and now complicated by AI — Medallia sells chatbots to automate customer service, exactly the workload [[AI disintermediation|tools like Claude Code threaten]]. See [[Private credit]] for the lender-stress read-through.
