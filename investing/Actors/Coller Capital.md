**Coller Capital** — London-headquartered private-equity secondaries pioneer. ~$36bn AUM. Founded 1990 by Jeremy Coller. Being acquired by [[EQT]] for $3.7bn (announced April 2026) — the largest acquisition in the elite-firm secondaries consolidation sequence documented in [[Private equity secondaries#Elite-firm consolidation (2024-2026)|PE secondaries — Elite-firm consolidation]].

---

## Why Coller matters

Coller Capital is one of the original specialist firms that established private-equity secondaries as a credible asset class. Founded by Jeremy Coller in 1990, the firm pioneered the model of acquiring portfolios of LP interests in mature buyout funds at discounts to NAV. Coller has historically focused on the LP-led side of the [[Private equity secondaries|secondaries market]] (sale of fund stakes by limited partners), differentiating it from later-entry firms that built more around GP-led continuation vehicles.

The $3.7bn EQT acquisition is consequential as a market signal — it confirms that secondaries platforms are valuable enough that elite buyout managers will pay a major price for proven specialist capability rather than build organically.

## Business model

Historical focus on LP-led secondaries:
- Acquiring portfolios of fund stakes from pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, endowments, family offices
- Typical entry discounts of 5-20% to NAV depending on vintage and fund quality
- Secondary buyout funds + secondary credit funds + private wealth distribution

Coller also operates a wealth-distribution arm targeting accredited and HNW investors directly — predating the broader industry pivot to evergreen formats.

## Founder

Jeremy Coller — founder, longtime chief investment officer, public on the case for secondaries as an asset class. Author of "The Lives, Loves and Lies of Robert Maxwell's Bagman" and other industry-related writings; broadly recognized as one of the original architects of fund secondaries as a discipline.
