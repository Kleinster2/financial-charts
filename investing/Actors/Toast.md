## Q1 2026 print — revenue in-line, EBITDA raise, Q2 EBITDA guide light (May 7-8, 2026)

TOST closed -14.7% to $25.05 on May 8 ($29.38 → $25.05, -5.8σ beta-adjusted), the largest single-day decline since the company's 2021 IPO. The print itself was structurally fine — revenue in-line, profit beat, FY EBITDA guidance raised — but the Q2 EBITDA guide came in below Street and triggered a steep multiple compression.

| Q1 CY 2026 | Result | vs Consensus |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1.63B | In-line |
| YoY revenue growth | +21.9% | Solid |
| GAAP EPS | $0.20 | +28.6% beat |
| Q2 EBITDA guide | Below Street | Miss |
| FY 2026 EBITDA guide (raised) | $800M midpoint | Above $793.6M consensus |

The structural breakdown:

1. **Q2 EBITDA guide miss against Q1 print and FY raise** — Toast raised FY26 EBITDA guidance and beat Q1 profit, but the Q2 implied print is below sequential pacing. Hardware-cost headwinds + the new-location growth-cost cycle drove the Q2 framing, and the market is reading the Q2 print as the *peak* of margin pressure rather than the trough. Selloff prices the absence of clarity on when the margin pressure phases out.
2. **TOST joins the May 7-8 growth-software derate cluster.** Five SaaS / fintech names down on -10% to -24% sigma magnitudes on the same session: [[Cloudflare]] -23.6%, [[HubSpot]] -19.0%, [[MercadoLibre]] -12.7%, [[CoreWeave]] -11.4%, TOST -14.7%. Each had a different fundamental — TOST: Q2 EBITDA-guide light; HUBS: Q2 revenue-guide light; NET: layoffs / restructure; MELI: EPS miss; CRWV: light Q2 + capex bump. The shared trigger isn't fundamentals — it's the multiple compression the cohort carried into the prints.
3. **Restaurant-sector exposure as second-order factor** — TOST's customer base is restaurants, and the [[McDonald's]] May 7 commentary on consumer environment getting worse provides the macro overlay for why Q2 hardware/onboarding spend pressure is binding. The bear-side reading is that TOST's growth depends on restaurant-formation cycle health, and the consumer signal is weakening.

The cross-name read with [[Block]] (XYZ +6.7% same day) is the cleanest payments/fintech-vs-restaurants split: Block's payments-and-Bitcoin model is benefiting from the same factor (digital-payments cycle) while TOST's restaurant-vertical-software model is exposed to the consumer-spending side that's softening. Two adjacent fintech names, opposite reactions, same week.

Note for follow-on: Toast's growth model (land restaurant + expand product attach + monetize payments TPV) requires the consumer-side to hold up. The May 7-8 selloff is the first quarter where the market is repricing that conditional.
