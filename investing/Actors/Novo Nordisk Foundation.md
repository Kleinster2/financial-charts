**Novo Nordisk Foundation** — Danish enterprise foundation that is the controlling shareholder of [[Novo Nordisk]] (and a constellation of related life-science businesses) via its asset-management arm Novo Holdings A/S. One of the world's wealthiest charitable foundations.

Best understood as the structural reason Novo Nordisk operates differently from listed peers: the Foundation's controlling stake insulates the company from short-term shareholder pressure but concentrates governance accountability in the Foundation's own board. That accountability became visible in 2025 when the Foundation engineered the ouster of CEO [[Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen]] and installed its own chair, Lars Rebien Sørensen, as chair of Novo Nordisk — breaking the arm's-length convention typical of Danish foundation-owned companies.

## Governance structure

The Foundation holds a controlling block of Novo Nordisk's class A shares, which carry enhanced voting rights. This structure is common among large Danish industrial groups (e.g., Carlsberg Foundation) and is designed to preserve long-term ownership independence from public-market discipline.

The convention has historically been arm's-length: the Foundation chair sits on the Foundation board, and a separate Novo Nordisk board governs the operating company. The May 2025 governance reset broke that convention. Lars Rebien Sørensen — himself a former Novo Nordisk CEO (2000–2016) — moved from chairing the Foundation only to chairing both bodies, signaling the Foundation's intent to actively steer the operating company through its commercial-strategy crisis.

---

## Why this matters for Novo Nordisk

Three structural consequences:

1. Shareholder activism is structurally muted — external investors holding common shares cannot replicate the Foundation's voting power; activist campaigns to change strategy run through the Foundation, not the AGM
2. The Foundation owns the credit and the blame for strategy — Doustdar's appointment, the [[Akero Therapeutics]] acquisition, the price-cut decision are all Foundation-endorsed
3. Cultural break has political weight — ~680,000 Danish private shareholders own Novo stock directly, and sentiment that "the chair should be Danish" or "the AGM should be in Danish" has political resonance in a country where Novo is a national champion
