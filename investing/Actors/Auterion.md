**Auterion** — Zurich/Munich-based autonomy software company building a common operating system for unmanned aerial, ground, and naval systems. Founded 2018 by [[Lorenz Meier]] (co-creator of the [[PX4]] open-source drone autopilot). Reached approximately €100M in 2025 revenue, profitable. [[Rheinmetall]] became a "significant" shareholder in November 2025. Auterion's software powers [[Rheinmetall]]'s [[FV-014]] drone awarded to the [[Bundeswehr]] in April 2026.

---

## Synopsis

Auterion is Europe's software spine for drones — the thesis that the value in unmanned warfare is increasingly in the software (targeting, swarming, autonomy) not the airframe. Founded by [[Lorenz Meier]] at ETH Zurich as the commercial successor to his [[PX4]] autopilot, the company reached ~€100M revenue and profitability in 2025, unusual for a defense-tech vendor at this stage. The November 2025 Rheinmetall shareholding is the defining strategic choice: [[Europe]]'s largest land-systems prime bet on an independent autonomy vendor rather than building the software stack in-house, and [[Lorenz Meier]] accepted a minority stake rather than a full acquisition — preserving the ability to sell AuterionOS to competing hardware vendors. Apr 2026 validation: [[Rheinmetall]]'s [[FV-014]] won a €298M [[Bundeswehr]] contract with AuterionOS inside.

## Why the model works

- Open and extensible — AuterionOS runs on multiple hardware platforms; customers aren't locked to one airframe vendor.
- Swarming + GPS-denied — the two capabilities Ukraine validated as decisive; both live in software, not metal.
- Profitable at scale — ~€100M revenue with profitability in 2025 is rare among drone-stack startups, which typically burn cash chasing hardware.
- Embedded in the Rheinmetall thesis — whatever share Rheinmetall wins of the [[Bundeswehr]] three-supplier procurement flows through Auterion too.

## Structural factors

- Revenue model: software share of drone-systems sales; ~€100M 2025 revenue, profitable
- Hardware posture: AuterionOS designed as hardware-agnostic, multi-platform (contrast with [[Anduril]] [[Lattice OS]])
- [[Rheinmetall]] ownership: significant minority stake (Nov 2025); stake short of full acquisition
- [[Bundeswehr]] three-supplier procurement: [[FV-014]] ([[Rheinmetall]]) runs AuterionOS; [[Helsing]] and [[Stark]] run their own stacks
- Competing European stack: [[Helsing]] Centaur is vertically integrated and does not use Auterion
- US competitive posture: [[Anduril]], [[Shield AI]] expanding into [[Europe]] with vertically integrated offers
- Private company: no public revenue breakdown or customer-concentration disclosure
