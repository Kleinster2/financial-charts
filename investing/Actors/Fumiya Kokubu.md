## May 2026 naphtha warning

*Sources: [[Reuters]] via [MarketScreener](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/former-marubeni-ceo-warns-japan-could-face-chemical-product-shortages-from-late-june-ce7f5addd98bfe27), May 25, 2026; [[Honda]] [official profile](https://global.honda/en/about/management/fumiya_kokubu.html), as of Apr. 1, 2026.*

At a May 25 [[IEEJ]] virtual seminar, Kokubu warned that Japan could face chemical-products shortages from around the end of June, or at latest from late August / September, if it cannot replace Middle East [[Naphtha]] supply.

The warning matters because Kokubu framed the problem as a source-volume gap rather than a temporary distribution bottleneck. Japan had sourced about 40% of naphtha directly from the Middle East before the war; another roughly 40% of domestic naphtha demand comes from domestic refining, but the crude input is about 95% Middle East-dependent. He described replacement of roughly 15m kilolitres of Middle East supply as impractical, pushing the debate toward demand curbs.
