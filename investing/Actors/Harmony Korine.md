# Harmony Korine

Acclaimed indie filmmaker (Kids, Gummo, Spring Breakers) turned AI-native media entrepreneur. Runs EDGLRD, a production company exploring generative AI as a core creative tool rather than a post-production add-on.

---

## Synopsis

Korine's career arc from provocateur indie filmmaker to AI-media pioneer makes him one of the most interesting figures at the art-technology intersection. He wrote Kids at 19, directed Gummo and Julien Donkey-Boy in the 1990s Dogme-adjacent underground, then resurfaced with Spring Breakers (2012) as a surprisingly commercial director. The pivot to AI came with EDGLRD, his production company that treats generative AI tools as first-class creative instruments. Aggro Dr1ft (2023), shot using infrared cameras and heavily processed with AI visual effects, was one of the first feature-length films to use AI as a core aesthetic element rather than a cost-saving measure.

The investment relevance is indirect but thematic. Korine and EDGLRD represent the "artist adoption" proof point for [[Runway]] and similar [[AI VFX Tools]] — when a name-brand director uses AI tools for creative expression (not just efficiency), it validates the technology's artistic legitimacy and expands the market from VFX houses to independent filmmakers, advertising agencies, and content studios. Aggro Dr1ft premiered at Venice Film Festival, giving the AI-in-cinema narrative high-culture credibility. The question is whether EDGLRD becomes a production company (project-based, linear revenue) or a platform/toolmaker (scalable). Korine's relationship with [[Runway]] suggests the former for now, but the AI-native media space is evolving faster than any individual company can plan for.
