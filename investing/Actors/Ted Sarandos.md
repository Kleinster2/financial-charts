Ted Sarandos is co-CEO of [[Netflix]] (since July 2020, alongside [[Greg Peters]]) and the architect of the company's original content strategy. Joined Netflix in 2000 as VP of Content when it was a DVD-by-mail operation, became Chief Content Officer, and built the original programming machine that now spends ~$17B annually on content. Key early bets: licensing "House of Cards" (2013, Netflix's first original) and greenlighting a global content strategy that now produces in 50+ countries. Before Netflix, worked at video distribution companies including West Coast Video and East Texas Distributors — an unusual background that gave him deep knowledge of content economics at the retail level.

Sarandos became sole public-facing CEO after Reed Hastings stepped up to executive chairman in January 2023. His political connections surfaced in 2025-2026 when he visited the Trump White House multiple times, navigating Netflix's content policies amid political pressure. Total compensation ~$50M annually. Married to Nicole Avant, former US Ambassador to the Bahamas and daughter of Motown executive Clarence Avant.
