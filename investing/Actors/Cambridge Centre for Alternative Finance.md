**Cambridge Centre for Alternative Finance (CCAF)** — research centre within the [[Cambridge Judge Business School]] at the University of Cambridge, in the [[UK]], focused on alternative finance, fintech, and increasingly AI in financial services. The CCAF is the primary academic producer of cross-jurisdictional benchmarking surveys of AI adoption in financial services, in partnership with the [[Bank for International Settlements]], the [[IMF]], and the [[World Economic Forum]].

## 2026 Global AI in Financial Services Report

The benchmark survey of AI adoption across the financial services industry — see [[AI in financial services]] for full data.

| Survey detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Conducted | October 2025 – January 2026 |
| Respondents | 628 organisations |
| Jurisdictions | 151 |
| Sample | 203 fintechs / 149 incumbents / 146 AI vendors / 130 central banks + regulators |

Headline finding: 81% of financial services firms are adopting AI at some level, 40% at advanced ("Scaling" or "Transforming") stages, 52% actively adopting agentic AI. Fintechs lead incumbents on advanced adoption (47% vs 30%).

The CCAF survey is the closest the field has to an authoritative cross-jurisdictional baseline. Its findings are the structural frame against which vendor surveys (e.g., [[NVIDIA]]'s 89% revenue-boost claim) and journalistic syntheses (e.g., [[Gillian Tett]] FT, May 8 2026) are measured.
