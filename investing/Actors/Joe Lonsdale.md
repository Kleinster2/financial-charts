# Joe Lonsdale

Joe Lonsdale is a serial entrepreneur and venture capitalist, best known as a co-founder of [[Palantir]] (2003) and founder of [[8VC]], an Austin-based venture firm with ~$5B+ in AUM. He co-founded [[Erebor Bank]] with [[Palmer Luckey]], an attempt to build a tech-native defense bank. Lonsdale has been a central figure in the "PayPal Mafia"-adjacent network that connects Silicon Valley's defense-tech and government-tech sectors.

Before [[8VC]], Lonsdale co-founded Addepar (wealth management platform, ~$100B+ AUA) and OpenGov (government financial management software, acquired by [[Cox Enterprises]] in 2022). He also co-founded Formation 8, the predecessor to 8VC. Lonsdale relocated from San Francisco to Austin in 2020, becoming one of the most prominent figures in the Texas tech migration. He is politically active on the right, supporting Republican candidates and advocating for deregulation, which aligns him with the broader [[Peter Thiel]] network in the defense-tech-policy orbit that includes [[Anduril]], [[Palantir]], and the [[Hill and Valley Forum]].
