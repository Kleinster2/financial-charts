**Whole Foods** — premium natural and organic grocery chain, acquired by [[Amazon]] in 2017 for $13.7B in what became a landmark deal signaling Big Tech's entry into physical retail. Founded in 1980 in Austin, Texas by John Mackey, the chain operates 500+ stores across the US, UK, and Canada. The acquisition gave [[Amazon]] a physical retail footprint, grocery distribution infrastructure, and a beachhead for its [[Amazon Fresh]] and delivery ambitions.

## Synopsis

Whole Foods' vault relevance is primarily as a component of [[Amazon]]'s retail strategy and as a bellwether for premium consumer spending. Post-acquisition, Amazon invested in price reductions (the "Whole Paycheck" stigma was real), Prime member discounts, and technology integration (cashierless Just Walk Out, Amazon One palm payment). The chain serves as a key distribution node for Amazon's grocery delivery network and as a supplier anchor — the "Whole Foods effect" on vendor economics (driving organic/natural product mainstreaming) reshaped the broader grocery industry.

Competitors include [[Trader Joe's]] (value-oriented natural), [[Sprouts Farmers Market]] (NASDAQ: SFM, growing fast), [[Kroger]] (mainstream with expanding organic), and Costco's organic offerings. Whole Foods' exclusive relationship with [[Amazon]] Prime creates a loyalty moat but limits the standalone growth story — the chain's performance is now embedded in Amazon's retail segment and not independently reported.
