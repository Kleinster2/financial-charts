**Enhanced Games** — sports and consumer health company planning to host athletic competitions where performance-enhancing drugs are permitted, now expanding into direct-to-consumer peptide sales. Awaiting regulatory approval to list on the [[NYSE]] via a [[SPAC]]. Backed by [[Peter Thiel]] and [[Donald Trump|Trump Jr]]'s [[1789 Capital]].

## Synopsis

Enhanced Games sits at the intersection of two deregulatory tailwinds in the Trump era: relaxed attitudes toward performance enhancement in sports and [[Robert F. Kennedy Jr.|RFK Jr]]'s push to loosen [[FDA]] restrictions on peptides. The company's inaugural event — swimming, athletics, and weightlifting with permitted PED use — is scheduled for Las Vegas in May 2026, with 40+ athletes signed up. But the more commercially significant move is its pivot into consumer peptide sales, announced March 18, 2026.

The peptide expansion was triggered directly by RFK Jr's appearance on [[Joe Rogan]]'s podcast, where the US health secretary said the FDA would make "about 14" peptides "more accessible" and praised his personal use: "I'm a big fan of peptides. I've used them myself and used them with really good effect." Enhanced is among the first companies to exploit this deregulatory signal. The initial offering includes five peptides sold via an online health service, with eight additional products pending regulatory approval — all eight of which the FDA says pose "potentially significant safety risks." The company frames the sporting event's athlete research as feeding into "personalised supplement offerings."

The SPAC listing, peptide sales, and PED-friendly athletics create a provocative but internally coherent business model: monetize the growing demand for performance enhancement across both elite athletes and the broader consumer wellness market. Whether the regulatory window stays open — or snaps shut under the next administration — is the central risk.

## Peptide business

Enhanced announced its peptide consumer business on March 18, 2026. The company is selling injectable peptides — short chains of amino acids promoted for hormone action, metabolism, muscle growth, recovery, and cognitive enhancement. Many lack robust evidence of effectiveness and safety.

The product launch rides the broader wave of peptide popularity driven by the efficacy of peptide-based [[GLP-1 receptor agonists|GLP-1 drugs]] (semaglutide, tirzepatide), though the peptides Enhanced sells target the fitness and performance-enhancement market rather than obesity treatment.

The FDA has historically imposed restrictions on peptide sales and warned of significant safety risks. Many peptides consumed in the US are imported from [[China]], partly because restricted peptides cannot be produced domestically.

Edgar Asebey, partner at Frier Levitt law firm in [[Florida]] (Mar 18): *"Everybody is trying to jump into this. We have a bunch of clients who all want to be first."*

## Regulatory context

[[Robert F. Kennedy Jr.|RFK Jr]]'s peptide deregulation signal represents a case of [[Competitive deregulation]] within the FDA itself — ideological preference overriding the agency's historical risk-averse posture. The FDA did not respond to requests for comment on Kennedy's proposal. The deregulatory impulse connects to the broader [[Trump II]] pattern of loosening federal health and safety regulation, from [[Basel III]] bank capital to FDA drug approvals.
