## Synopsis

Daniel Vorcaro represents the apex of Brazilian [[Bank cronyism]] — a 40-year-old banker who learned to navigate the country's political networks with extraordinary sophistication. The son of a Belo Horizonte real estate mogul, Vorcaro parlayed family wealth into a banking empire worth R$4.7 billion by 2024, built on high-yield CDBs, political protection, and systematic fraud involving fictitious credit portfolios worth over R$12 billion.

His political network was remarkable in its scope: four sitting Supreme Court justices, two former justices, the Attorney General, former presidents, and congressional leaders. He funded luxury forums in London and Rome, paid R$129 million to Justice Moraes's wife's law firm, and spent at least $120 million on parties and trips — including a $640,000 Macallan whisky tasting and flying 30+ guests on private jets to Saint-Tropez.

The scheme unraveled when the Central Bank discovered [[Banco Master]] had only R$4 million in cash when it should have had R$3-4 billion in liquid securities. Federal Police revealed Vorcaro bribed two senior BCB officials and plotted intimidation against critics through an associate he called "Sicario" (after Mexican cartel assassins). His dramatic arrest at Guarulhos Airport while boarding a Falcon 7X to Malta crystallized the image of a connected banker trying to escape with the proceeds.

## The bribery scheme

In March 2026, Federal Police revealed Vorcaro systematically corrupted Central Bank supervision through two senior officials:

**[[Paulo Sergio Neves de Souza]]** — Former Director of Supervision (2017-2023), Copom member for 6 years. Career BCB employee since 1998, appointed under Temer, served through Bolsonaro into Lula term.

**[[Belline Santana]]** — Former head of banking supervision department.

Both provided prior review of Master's regulatory filings and helped navigate Central Bank requirements. Court rulings cited evidence of bribes paid through sham consulting contracts. Despite their assistance, the BCB ultimately blocked Master's attempted bailout via [[BRB]] and liquidated the bank.

## The "Sicario" plot

Federal Police charged Vorcaro with plotting to intimidate and attack critics through an associate he called "Sicario" — named after Mexican cartel hit men. Targets included:

- Former employees who might testify
- Domestic workers with inside knowledge  
- Journalists investigating the scandal

The intimidation plot underscores the lengths Vorcaro was willing to go to protect his empire as it crumbled.

## Arrests and legal status

**First arrest (November 17, 2025):** Dramatic detention at Guarulhos Airport while boarding his Falcon 7X to Malta via Dubai. Released November 28 with ankle monitor after 11 days in custody.

**Second arrest (March 4, 2026):** Justice André Mendonça ordered new detention citing risk Vorcaro would obstruct investigations. The arrest followed revelations about Central Bank bribery and the Sicario plot.

**Final texts:** On the night of his first arrest, Vorcaro sent "Did you manage to block it?" to an unidentified number. O Globo later reported the messages were sent to Justice Alexandre de Moraes, which Moraes denies.

## The fraud mechanics

See [[Banco Master]] for comprehensive details. Vorcaro's core scheme involved:

1. **High-yield CDBs** — Offering 140-150%+ of CDI to attract deposits
2. **Fictitious credits** — R$12.2B in fake loans to shell companies  
3. **Asset laundering** — Through [[Reag Investimentos]] funds with inflated valuations
4. **Political protection** — Delaying regulatory action for three years
5. **State bank bailout** — R$16.7B injection from [[BRB]] before final collapse

When exposed, the bank had only R$4 million in cash when it should have held R$3-4 billion in liquid assets.
