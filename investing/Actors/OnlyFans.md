Subscription platform for creators — 80% adult content, 20% fitness/music/cooking. Founded 2016 in UK by Tim and Guy Stokely, acquired by [[Leonid Radvinsky]] in 2018. The most profitable creator platform ever built: $684M pre-tax profit on $1.4B revenue (48% margin). Radvinsky died March 23, 2026 (aged 43, cancer) — his shares are held in a trust since 2024, leaving the platform's ownership and an in-progress multibillion-dollar sale process in limbo.

## Business model

Simple and brutal: 20% of everything. Subscriptions, PPV, tips, live streaming — all 80/20 split to creators. No ads, no data monetization, just rake.

| Revenue stream | Creator cut | OnlyFans cut |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| Subscriptions ($4.99-$49.99/mo) | 80% | 20% |
| Pay-per-view content | 80% | 20% |
| Tips | 90% | 10% |
| Live streaming | 80% | 20% |

Why it works:
- Highest creator payout in industry ([[Patreon]] takes 5-12%, [[YouTube]] ~45%)
- No discovery algorithm — creators bring their own audience
- Adult content tolerated (unlike [[YouTube]], [[Instagram]], [[TikTok]])
- Simple UX, reliable payments

Key insight: 60% of creator revenue comes from single purchases (PPV), not subscriptions. Single-purchase revenue grew 70% ($1.6B) from 2021-2023 vs 9% for subscriptions. Tips and PPV drive engagement.

## Creator economics

| Tier | Earnings | % of creators |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Top 1% | 33% of total revenue | ~46K creators |
| $1M+/year | 1,200+ creators | <0.1% |
| Bottom 50% | <$100/month | ~2.3M creators |

Power law distribution. Most creators earn almost nothing. Platform makes money regardless — 20% of $0 is $0, but 20% of top creators' millions adds up.

## Potential sale

[[Leonid Radvinsky]] had been working with advisers to assess options for selling out of the business (FT, May 23 2025). Multiple investment groups expressed interest, with [[Forest Road Company]] — a Los Angeles-based group whose previous investments include a Formula E racing team and a majority stake in merchant bank ACF Investment Bank — among several suitors. Indicative valuation: ~$8B.

Sale complications: financial companies and their lending banks have long considered services associated with sex work high-risk for compliance, despite the content being legal. [[OnlyFans]] has no presence on [[Apple]] or [[Google]] app stores. The last major porn site to sell was [[Pornhub]] owner [[MindGeek]], bought by [[Ethical Capital Partners]] (Canadian PE) in 2023.

After Radvinsky's death (Mar 23, 2026), the sale talks' future is unclear. Negotiations with various VC groups had been complicated by his illness (FT, Mar 23 2026). The decision now rests with the trust holding his shares.

## Risks

- Creator concentration — top 1% = 33% of revenue; if top creators leave, revenue craters
- Payment processing — [[Mastercard]]/[[Visa]] could restrict (almost happened in 2021)
- Regulation — [[UK]] age verification, US state laws
- No discovery — platform is a payment processor, not a destination; creators own the relationship
- Competition — Fansly, Fanvue growing; lower take rates possible
- Succession risk — Radvinsky's death leaves ownership in a trust; sale process uncertain
