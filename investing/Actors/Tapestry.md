## Q3 FY2026 earnings (May 7, 2026) — beat + raise, but stock fell on tariff outlook

TPR reported Q3 FY 2026 (quarter ended March 28) on May 7. Despite a clean beat-and-raise across the headline metrics, the stock closed -8.7% to $135.87 from $148.83 — the largest single-day loss in the company's recent history and a sharp reversal from the May 6 close at a multi-quarter high.

| Metric | Q3 FY 2026 | Consensus | YoY |
|--------|------------|-----------|-----|
| Revenue | $1.92B | $1.80B | — |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $1.66 | $1.31 | +27% |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 76.9% | — | +80 bps |
| Non-GAAP operating margin | 22.4% | — | +490 bps |

[[Coach]] led: +29% constant-currency to $1.7B for the quarter. Globally the company added 2.4M new customers, with Gen Z 35%+ of that total. Regional dispersion: North America +20% to $1.1B; Greater [[China]] +55% to $432M; [[Japan]] -10% to $124M; [[Europe]] +21% to $119M. The Greater China +55% number is the standout — it reverses the broader luxury-cohort weakness in [[China]] that hit [[Burberry]] / [[LVMH]] / [[Hermes]] earlier in 2025-2026.

Raised FY 2026 guide:
- Revenue: ~$7.95B (vs prior >$7.75B), implying 17% pro forma growth
- Non-GAAP EPS: ~$6.95 (vs prior $6.40-6.45), implying >35% YoY growth

So why did the stock fall? Two transmission channels visible in the post-print commentary:

1. *Tariff impact embedded.* The company called out 180 bps of operating-margin headwind in Q3 from tariffs and duties, and the FY26 guide implicitly assumes that headwind persists. Even with the +490 bps margin expansion this quarter, the tariff drag is now structurally embedded into the multi-year framework. Several outlets characterized the FY26 outlook as a "lowered profit outlook" against pre-print buy-side whispers that had reportedly priced more upside.
2. *Sell-the-news after the run-up.* TPR was at a multi-quarter high entering the print after a strong Q2 reaction. The setup priced an above-consensus beat and the actual result, while above sell-side, fell short of buy-side whispers.

The cleanest cross-name read: TPR's same-day decline lands inside a broader consumer-discretionary fade tape — [[Vital Farms]] -23.8%, [[Zoetis]] -21.1%, [[Whirlpool]] -13.9%. TPR is the luxury-adjacent end of that signal; the other three are middle-class-suburban. The FY26 guide cut at WHR + the FY26 cut at ZTS + the embedded tariff drag at TPR + the butter-exit cost-defense at VITL form a coherent four-name signal of consumer-cycle stress, even though TPR itself beat and raised. The premium-consumer cohort is bifurcating: Coach Greater China +55% vs Whirlpool / Zoetis / Vital Farms middle-class-pulse cracks.
