**FKA Twigs** (Tahliah Barnett) is a British musician and visual artist who became a case study in celebrity AI adoption by creating an AI deepfake of herself to handle press and media obligations — one of the most creative applications of generative AI by a public figure.

## Synopsis

FKA Twigs built her reputation on avant-garde music and performance art (two Mercury Prize-nominated albums, collaborations with major brands), but her vault relevance is entirely about what she did with AI. In 2023, she revealed she had trained an AI replica of herself to conduct media interviews, generate social media content, and handle routine press duties — freeing the real person to focus on creative work. This was not a gimmick or a deepfake controversy: Twigs proactively chose to automate her own public persona, making her one of the first public figures to use AI for time sovereignty rather than content scaling. She testified before a US Senate subcommittee in 2024 about the implications, arguing for creators' right to control their own digital likenesses.

The investment read-through is for the broader [[creator economy]] and AI rights landscape. Twigs's approach prefigures a world where talent creates AI versions of themselves as standard practice — handling brand partnerships, fan interactions, and content generation at scale while the human focuses on high-value creative work. This has implications for talent agencies ([[Endeavor]], [[CAA]]), media companies, and AI companies building persona tools. The legal question — who owns a celebrity's AI training data and generated output — remains unsettled and will shape the IP frameworks that determine value capture in AI-generated content. Twigs's Senate testimony helped catalyze the NO FAKES Act, which would establish federal rights to digital likeness.
