# ECA Group

ECA Group was a French underwater robotics and defense company, founded in 1936 by Marcel Gianoli and Paul Rozycki as Etudes et Constructions Aeronautiques. The company pioneered the PAP (Poisson Auto-Propulse) mine disposal drone in 1970, which became the Western world's standard naval mine countermeasure system — approximately 450 units were sold to ~20 navies over five decades. Acquired by [[Jean-Pierre Gorge]]'s Finuchem in 1992, then merged with [[iXblue]] in 2022 to form [[Exail Technologies]].

## Legacy

ECA's significance for the defense investment thesis is twofold. First, the PAP program demonstrated that underwater drones could achieve multi-decade procurement cycles — a rarity in defense. Second, ECA's successor products, particularly the [[K-STER C]] expendable mine disposal system (backlog >1,000 units) and the [[UMIS]] integrated mine countermeasure suite, are now produced by [[Exail Technologies]] and represent a growing share of [[NATO]] mine warfare spending. The rMCM program (Belgium/Netherlands, ~EUR60M) and NATO/NSPA framework orders (~EUR40M, Jan 2026) trace directly back to ECA's technology lineage.
