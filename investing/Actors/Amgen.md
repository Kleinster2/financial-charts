## Q1 2026 earnings + Tavneos FDA withdrawal proposal (Apr 30, 2026)

Amgen reported Q1 2026 before the open on Apr 30. Sales $8.62B (+5.8% YoY) beat $8.58B est; non-GAAP EPS $3.34 vs $3.07 est. CEO commentary: "16 brands achieving double-digit growth, enabling us to grow through expected patent expirations." But the print was overshadowed by three negative surprises:

1. [[Tavneos]] FDA withdrawal proposal — On Apr 27, FDA's Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER) proposed to withdraw approval of [[Tavneos]] (avacopan, ANCA-associated vasculitis), asserting "new information indicating lack of substantial evidence of effectiveness." On Apr 30 the FDA posted notice in the Federal Register formalizing the proposal. Tavneos was acquired via the [[ChemoCentryx]] purchase ($3.7B, 2022); revenue ramping ~$300M annualized. A formal withdrawal would zero the franchise.

2. Accelerating biosimilar erosion — Management guided "accelerated sales erosion over the remainder of 2026 driven by increased competition from multiple biosimilars." [[Prolia]] sales -34% YoY on patent expiration + biosimilar entry — steeper than the previous-quarter glide path implied.

3. IRS draft NOPA — CFO Peter Griffith disclosed Amgen received a draft Notice of Proposed Adjustment from the IRS for tax years 2016-2018. *"If sustained in full, the adjustments set forth in the draft NOPA could have a material impact on our financial statements."* The IRS-Amgen transfer pricing dispute (Puerto Rico manufacturing → US sales) has been running since 2017, with the original NOPA covering 2010-2015 ($10.7B in adjustments). The 2016-2018 extension expands the at-risk period.

Stock: AMGN closed May 1 at $326.87, -5.6% on the day (-3.6σ on a low-vol name). The Tavneos withdrawal is the binary item, but the bigger structural read is that biosimilar erosion is now accelerating into the [[Prolia]] / [[Xgeva]] LOE window precisely as MariTide Phase 3 data is still 6-12 months out — the hand-off from legacy revenue to MariTide is *narrower* than the previous bull thesis assumed. The IRS NOPA is a chronic capital-structure overhang (potential tens of billions of $ exposure if litigated to conclusion).

The vault [[Eli Lilly|LLY]] Q1 2026 print landed on the same day with [[Eli Lilly#Q1 2026 earnings|Mounjaro +125% to $8.66B]] — the GLP-1 dominance gap vs Amgen's monthly-dosing MariTide bet widened materially.
