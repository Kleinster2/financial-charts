# Thomas Kurian

CEO of [[Google Cloud]] since November 2018. Joined [[Alphabet]] from [[Oracle]], where he had spent 22 years and most recently led product development. Frequently named as a candidate to succeed [[Sundar Pichai]] as Alphabet CEO. Best known publicly for taking Google Cloud from a 7% global market share when he joined to roughly 14% by early 2026, while building out the proprietary [[TPU]] stack and signing the [[Anthropic]] capacity anchor.

## Why Kurian matters for this vault

Kurian is the executive who decides which AI labs and enterprises get scarce [[TPU]] capacity, which is now one of the most pivotal allocation decisions in the AI infrastructure cycle. The Apr 2026 [[Anthropic]] deal — up to $40B equity from [[Google]] plus 5 GW of [[Google Cloud]] capacity over five years (a package the [[FT]] valued at >$200B) — is the clearest example of his strategic logic: monetize TPUs through the strongest independent rival to [[Gemini]] rather than reserve them for Google's own first-party model effort. The internal tension this creates with [[Google DeepMind]] and the [[Gemini]] team is the structural feature of Kurian's tenure that is most likely to shape Alphabet's next leadership transition.

---

## Strategic positioning (Apr 2026)

In an FT interview around the Apr 2026 [[Anthropic]] announcement, Kurian framed [[Google Cloud]]'s differentiation as full-stack ownership: "We're not just a hyperscaler reselling other people's technology. Our differentiation comes down to the fact that we own the IP, the model and the chips are ours… For every dollar of revenue, we're not shipping 80 per cent of it to either a model or chip provider, which allows us to invest more."

The argument compares Google's vertical stack ([[TPU]] + [[Gemini]] + Google Cloud + Google products) against the perceived [[NVIDIA]]-dependent cost structure at [[AWS]] and [[Azure]]. Kurian named [[NVIDIA]] as the only current rival in integrated AI hardware-and-software design.

He also said nine of the top 10 AI labs use [[TPU]]s — including [[Mira Murati]]'s [[Thinking Machines Lab]] — and that [[OpenAI]] is excluded only because of its exclusivity arrangement with [[Microsoft]].

---

## Apr 2026 AI shakeout view

Kurian publicly predicted a multi-year AI shakeout, framing [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]] as facing a more difficult financial path than the hyperscalers themselves: "Those AI providers depend on private capital markets, which are reaching a saturation point. If you're going to go public, you can't be lossmaking forever. And if you stay private, you cannot raise venture money forever."

He projected: "Over the next year to two you will see some shakeout in the market. Whether particular providers are going to make it or not largely comes down to the economics."

This is the clearest hyperscaler-CEO statement to date that the private AI model layer is approaching a financing ceiling. See [[Hyperscaler capex]] and [[AI infrastructure financing risk]].
