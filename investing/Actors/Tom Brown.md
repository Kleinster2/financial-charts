**Tom Brown** — Co-founder of [[Anthropic]] and lead author of the GPT-3 paper ("Language Models are Few-Shot Learners," 2020), the work that proved scaling large language models produced emergent capabilities and launched the modern AI era.

---

## Synopsis

Brown's GPT-3 paper is one of the most consequential publications in the history of computer science. By demonstrating that a 175B-parameter language model could perform tasks it was never explicitly trained on — translation, arithmetic, code generation — simply by being shown a few examples in the prompt, the paper established the scaling hypothesis as empirical fact rather than theoretical conjecture. Every major AI investment since 2020, from [[NVIDIA]]'s data center boom to the hyperscaler capex arms race, traces back to the insight Brown's team validated: that bigger models with more data produce qualitatively different capabilities.

Brown left [[OpenAI]] in early 2021 alongside [[Dario Amodei]], [[Daniela Amodei]], and four other colleagues over disagreements about safety prioritization and the company's governance direction. The group founded [[Anthropic]], which has since become the leading safety-focused AI lab with a valuation exceeding $60B. Brown's continued presence at Anthropic (confirmed as of February 2026) is notable — unlike many co-founders of high-profile startups who depart after the founding phase, his retention suggests ongoing technical contribution. For investors in the AI stack, Brown represents the type of irreplaceable technical talent that makes AI lab equity so difficult to value: a single researcher whose judgment shaped an industry's trajectory.
