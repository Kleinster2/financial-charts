**Oppenheimer** (Oppenheimer Holdings, NYSE: OPY) is a mid-tier US investment bank and broker-dealer headquartered in New York, operating across wealth management, capital markets, and asset management.

## Synopsis

Oppenheimer occupies the middle tier of US investment banking — larger than boutiques like [[Cowen]] or [[Piper Sandler]] but far smaller than [[Goldman Sachs]] or [[Morgan Stanley]]. The firm has roots dating to 1881 (originally Oppenheimer & Co.), making it one of the oldest names on Wall Street, though various ownership changes and restructurings have reshaped the business repeatedly. The current entity, Oppenheimer Holdings (NYSE: OPY), is controlled by the Albert Lowenthal family, which owns a majority of the voting stock — an unusual governance structure for a public financial services firm.

The firm's primary relevance to the vault is as a research house. Oppenheimer analysts are regularly cited in coverage of mid-cap industrials, technology, and healthcare names. The wealth management division manages ~$40B in client assets, placing it in the upper ranks of independent broker-dealers. Revenue mix is roughly split between advisory/commissions, investment banking, and principal trading. Market cap hovers around $500M-700M, making OPY itself a micro-cap — too small for most institutional portfolios but a useful window into the economics of mid-tier financial services.
