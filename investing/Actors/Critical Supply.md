# Critical Supply

**Critical Supply** - Eduardo Castellet Nogués's trade and economic-security publication, focused on supply chains, industrial policy, critical minerals, and EU-US-China competition.

The publication is useful when it connects market structure to state leverage: who controls the port, refinery, molecule, mine, grid connection, or procurement rule that can turn ordinary commerce into coercive capacity.

## May 2026: EU-China bridge thesis

The May 27, 2026 post, "An EU-China Bridge Will Not Last," argued that [[Europe]] should not respond to more fragile US security guarantees by replacing transatlantic asymmetry with a deeper dependency on [[China]]. The piece tied that argument to three concrete control surfaces: Chinese export-manufacturing overcapacity in Europe, Chinese ownership or influence over strategic infrastructure, and conflict minerals in eastern [[DRC]].

The same post also treated US data-center power policy as an investable supply-chain issue. The useful read-through is that permitting, interconnection rules, and cost allocation increasingly determine whether AI infrastructure gets built as much as chips, cooling, or land availability do.
