## Trajectory

NeuroXess → Gestala is the canonical "invasive-then-non-invasive" arc: Peng built operational BCI experience at NeuroXess on the implantable electrode-array side (the same architectural family as [[Neuralink]] and [[Synchron]]), then identified the non-invasive ultrasound path as the addressable-market expansion play. The structural insight per Peng: "BCI has two different technological stacks. First of all is using electrical implant — we have to put electricals into your brain. The second one is ultrasound — it means we are connecting to your brain wirelessly."

Gestala launched January 2026 and closed the angel round in March — three months from founding to capital, unusually fast even by 2026 BCI standards, reflecting the founder-credibility premium combined with [[Tianqiao Chen]]'s investor network on the cap-table side.

---

## Strategic framing

Peng's positioning of Gestala against invasive BCI peers leans on three structural arguments:

- Addressable population — chronic pain (~20% of adults globally), mental-health conditions (depression, PTSD, autism, OCD), and stroke rehabilitation expand the BCI addressable population by orders of magnitude versus the spinal-cord-injury / ALS focus of invasive systems.
- Surgical-risk elimination — non-invasive ultrasound removes the binding constraint that has slowed invasive BCI clinical adoption; patients (and clinicians) hesitant on brain surgery enter the funnel.
- Whole-brain access — phased-array ultrasound can target deep-brain structures (thalamus, basal ganglia, brainstem) that implantable electrodes struggle to reach without much-more-invasive insertion.

Peng explicitly framed the BCI market as non-competitive at the current development stage: "I don't think any company in this sector treats each other as a competitor because we are doing something for helping the patients."

## Public-facing commentary

Peng's first major Western financial-press interview (Bloomberg "The China Show" 4/28/2026, Haslinda Amin) framed three messages:

- Sector legitimacy — BCI is identified as a future-industry priority in China's national five-year planning documents.
- Founder credibility — [[Tianqiao Chen]]'s neuroscience-philanthropy track record + Peng's NeuroXess operational record justify the angel-stage $100-200M valuation.
- Global-talent positioning — Gestala is hiring international researchers (some relocating from Israel and the US to China), explicit signaling that the company is positioned as a global-research-grade lab, not a Chinese-domestic copy of [[Neuralink]].
