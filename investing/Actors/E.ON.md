E.ON is [[Germany]]'s largest energy network operator and one of [[Europe]]'s biggest electric utilities by customer count, serving approximately 47 million customers across Germany, the UK, Sweden, and Central/Eastern Europe. The company underwent a radical transformation in 2016 when it spun off its conventional power generation and energy trading businesses into [[Uniper]], keeping the networks, retail, and renewables. In 2018, E.ON and [[RWE]] executed a complex asset swap: E.ON acquired Innogy (RWE's grid/retail subsidiary) for ~22B EUR, while RWE took E.ON's renewables portfolio. The result made E.ON a pure-play regulated networks and customer solutions company.

E.ON holds a 33.3% stake in [[Urenco]], the uranium enrichment consortium (alongside [[RWE]] and the Dutch government), which provides optionality on the [[Nuclear energy]] revival. The company's grid infrastructure is central to Germany's Energiewende (energy transition), requiring massive capex to accommodate distributed renewables and EV charging load growth.
