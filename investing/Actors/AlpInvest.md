#actor #privateequity #secondaries #netherlands #subsidiary

**AlpInvest** — Amsterdam-based private-equity secondaries and co-investment specialist. ~$85bn AUM. Subsidiary of [[Carlyle]] (NASDAQ: CG) since 2011. Originally created in 1999 as the private equity investment arm of Dutch pension giants [[ABP]] and PGGM (now PFZW).

---

## Why AlpInvest matters

AlpInvest is one of the largest dedicated private-equity secondaries platforms globally and was the target of the first elite-buyout-firm acquisition of a secondaries specialist (Carlyle, 2011) — the move that opened the 2024-2026 consolidation sequence documented in [[Private equity secondaries#Elite-firm consolidation (2024-2026)|PE secondaries — Elite-firm consolidation]].

The pension-fund origins matter: AlpInvest was built as the institutional in-house allocation tool for two of the largest Dutch pension funds, which means the platform was scaled from inception to handle large primary commitments, co-investments, and secondaries across the entire private equity universe — not built up gradually like specialist firms.

## Carlyle ownership

| Year | Event |

| 2011 | [[Carlyle]] acquired 60% interest |
| Multiple steps 2011-2020 | Carlyle increased ownership |
| Current | Carlyle subsidiary; ABP and PFZW remain anchor investors |

Carlyle reports AlpInvest as one of its four major segments. Per Carlyle's [[Carlyle#Business segments|segment disclosure]], AlpInvest manages ~$85bn AUM across primaries, co-investments, and secondaries.

---

## Business model

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary fund commitments | LP commitments to third-party PE funds globally |
| Co-investments | Direct equity alongside fund commitments |
| Secondaries | LP-led + GP-led, including continuation vehicles |

The platform is structurally similar to [[HarbourVest]] and [[StepStone Group]] in operating across the full private-markets stack rather than specializing in any single sleeve.
