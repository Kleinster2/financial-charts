#actor #privateequity #secondaries #usa #subsidiary

**Lexington Partners** — New York-based private-equity secondaries pioneer. ~$70bn+ AUM. Acquired by [[Franklin Templeton]] (NYSE: BEN) in April 2022 for $1bn + earnout consideration. One of the original specialist secondaries firms that established the asset class in the 1990s alongside [[Coller Capital]].

---

## Why Lexington matters

Lexington is one of the two firms (along with [[Coller Capital]]) most associated with the founding of private-equity secondaries as a discipline. The firm built the LP-led secondaries business model in the late 1980s and 1990s, when the asset class barely existed and elite buyout firms still dismissed secondaries activity as a low-prestige cleanup business.

The 2022 [[Franklin Templeton]] acquisition was an early signal of the pattern that has accelerated through 2024-2026 — large asset managers buying specialist secondaries platforms to embed in their private-markets offerings. Franklin's specific motivation was different from later acquirers: BEN was building a private-markets shelf to compete with [[BlackRock]] / [[Apollo]] / [[Blackstone]] in wealth-channel distribution.

## Franklin Templeton acquisition (Apr 2022)

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Acquirer | [[Franklin Templeton]] (NYSE: BEN) |
| Announced | November 2021 |
| Closed | April 2022 |
| Deal value | ~$1bn upfront + earnout |
| Lexington AUM at close | ~$57bn |
| Lexington AUM (current) | ~$70bn+ |
| Strategic logic | Establish private-markets platform for [[Franklin Templeton]] wealth channels |

The deal predated the broader 2024-2026 elite-firm secondaries consolidation by two years. Franklin Templeton bought capability that the buyout-firm acquirers ([[Carlyle]] / [[AlpInvest]] in 2011, [[Blackstone]] / Strategic Partners in 2013, [[Ares Management]] / Landmark in 2021, [[CVC Capital Partners|CVC]] / Glendower in 2022, [[EQT]] / [[Coller Capital]] in 2026, [[KKR]] / [[Arctos]] in 2026) were buying for largely the same reason: the rising importance of secondaries inside [[Evergreen private capital vehicles|evergreen wealth vehicles]].

## Business model

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| LP-led secondaries | Portfolio purchases of fund stakes from institutional sellers |
| GP-led / continuation vehicles | Backing continuation vehicles structured by buyout sponsors |
| Co-investments | Direct equity alongside GP relationships |

The firm runs the Lexington Capital Partners series of flagship secondary buyout funds.
