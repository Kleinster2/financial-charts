# Pentamaster

**Pentamaster** is a Malaysian semiconductor burn-in and reliability test systems provider, listed on Bursa Malaysia as 7160.KL, and a key player in the industry shift from packaged to wafer-level burn-in.

## Synopsis

Pentamaster occupies a specialized niche in the semiconductor equipment value chain: burn-in and reliability testing, where chips are stressed under elevated temperature and voltage to screen out early-life failures. Alongside [[Aehr Test Systems]], Pentamaster is riding the structural transition from single-device packaged burn-in to parallel wafer-level burn-in — a shift that dramatically improves throughput and cost economics for high-volume chip production. The company's Penang base gives it proximity to the dense Southeast Asian semiconductor ecosystem ([[Malaysia]] hosts significant back-end packaging and test capacity for global foundries).

The demand story for Pentamaster has pivoted from SiC/EV to AI/data center chips. As AI accelerators ([[NVIDIA]] GPUs, custom ASICs) require increasingly rigorous reliability screening — these are high-value, high-power devices where field failures carry enormous cost — the burn-in TAM is expanding beyond traditional automotive applications. Pentamaster competes with [[Aehr Test Systems]] on wafer-level burn-in systems, but also offers broader automation and test solutions for the factory floor. The stock is thinly traded by international standards but has attracted attention from semiconductor equipment specialists who see wafer-level burn-in as a structural growth story within the broader chip capex supercycle.
