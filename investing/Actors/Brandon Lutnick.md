# Brandon Lutnick

Brandon Lutnick became Chairman and CEO of [[Cantor Fitzgerald]] in January 2025 after his father [[Howard Lutnick]] stepped down to serve as U.S. Commerce Secretary. At 32, he took the helm of one of Wall Street's most prominent privately held financial firms, inheriting both its bond-trading franchise and its growing [[Tether]] custody business. His rapid ascent — from Cantor analyst to CEO in under a decade — drew immediate scrutiny from Congress.

## Congressional scrutiny

Senators [[Ron Wyden]] and [[Elizabeth Warren]] sent a letter (Aug 2025) demanding answers about Cantor's [[Tariff refund claims market|tariff refund claims trading]] and potential insider dealing, given his father's role setting tariff policy at Commerce. The concern: Cantor was building a trading desk around tariff refund claims at the same time [[Howard Lutnick]] was designing the tariff schedule. Rep. [[Jamie Raskin]] opened a parallel House investigation.
