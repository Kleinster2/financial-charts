# Oliver Bäte

CEO of [[Allianz]] since May 2015, overseeing the world's largest insurer by gross written premiums (~€160B) and a ~$2.9T asset management empire via [[PIMCO]] and [[Allianz Global Investors]]. Former [[McKinsey]] partner who joined Allianz's Board of Management in 2008, serving as COO and then CFO before succeeding [[Michael Diekmann]] as CEO. His tenure has been defined by the "simplicity" program — consolidating hundreds of legacy IT systems, exiting unprofitable lines, and returning capital aggressively (€30B+ in buybacks and dividends since 2015). Bäte navigated the Structured Alpha fraud crisis (2022, $6B settlement with DOJ after fund manager [[Gregoire Tournant]] pleaded guilty) without losing his position, and has steered Allianz into [[Private credit]] and infrastructure investing as traditional fixed-income yields compressed. His management style is described as intensely data-driven and intolerant of underperformance.
