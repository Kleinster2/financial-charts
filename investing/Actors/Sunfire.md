# Sunfire

Sunfire is a [[Germany]]-based private electrolyzer manufacturer, founded in 2010 and headquartered in Dresden. One of the few Western companies producing both [[SOEC]] (solid oxide) and pressurized alkaline (AEL) [[Electrolyzers|electrolyzers]] — a dual-technology positioning that no listed Western competitor matches. Backed by strategic and financial investors including Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners, Blue Earth Capital, and [[Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau|KfW]]'s investment arm. Represents the "Germany industrial SME" answer to the [[Second China shock]] in hydrogen hardware.

## Why Sunfire matters

Sunfire's dual SOEC + AEL position makes it a useful comparison point for the technology-family question the [[Electrolyzers]] space is now working through:

1. **SOEC bet** tracks alongside [[Topsoe]] and [[Bloom Energy]] — high-efficiency, high-temperature, initially high-capex, potentially unlocked by industrial-waste-heat applications and [[e-SAF|synthetic aviation fuels]] use cases.

2. AEL bet via the 2021 IHT acquisition gives Sunfire a conventional Western alkaline offering that competes head-on with Chinese alkaline imports — which is where the competitive pressure is most visible. IHT had been operating pressurized alkaline systems in industrial installations (primarily chlorine) for decades before Sunfire's acquisition re-focused them on hydrogen.

Running both technology stacks is commercially expensive but gives Sunfire positioning flexibility no Western competitor matches. The [[Topsoe]] + [[Bloom Energy]] camp bets SOEC wins; the [[Nel ASA]] + Chinese camp bets alkaline scale wins; Sunfire hedges.

## Key projects and contracts

- Multiplhy project (finnish Neste refinery) — world's first commercial high-temperature SOEC at meaningful scale
- H2 Green Steel (Sweden) — Sunfire AEL systems for [[H2 Green Steel|H2GS]] green steel facility; one of the largest Western green-steel order wins
- Salzgitter Flachstahl (Germany) — SOEC pilot for steel decarbonization
- Port of Rotterdam installations via various project operators

Whether Sunfire's order backlog translates to commercial operating success through 2027-2028 will depend heavily on whether IRA 45V persists politically and whether the EU Hydrogen Bank auctions shift toward higher-end-specification technical criteria that favor differentiated Western OEMs over low-cost Chinese alkaline imports.

## Relevance to the Second China shock frame

Sunfire is the exemplar of what a "German industrial SME / Mittelstand" response to Chinese clean-tech competition looks like when it works. The company:

- Is private, which lets it absorb the current demand-gap without public-market pressure
- Has state-linked patient capital (KfW) on the cap table
- Sits inside a supply-chain cluster (Dresden + Solothurn) with historical industrial competence
- Has differentiated in SOEC rather than trying to match alkaline on price
- Has real commercial orders, not vaporware

Whether the German Mittelstand model can defend a structural clean-tech position against Chinese incumbents is the broader question the Sunfire case tests. Early read is: barely, and only in specific segments that are either (a) technology-differentiated (SOEC, high-pressure AEL for specialty applications) or (b) geographically advantaged (short supply chains to core European customers). Volume commodity alkaline is lost.
