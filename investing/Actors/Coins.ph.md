# Coins.ph

Coins.ph is the largest crypto-native fintech and digital asset exchange in the [[Philippines]], with ~18M users as of late 2025 and licenses across e-money issuance and virtual currency exchange from the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas. Acquired in April 2022 by a consortium led by [[Wei Zhou]] (former [[Binance]] CFO) and [[Joffre Capital]] from [[Gojek]] for roughly $190M. The current strategic frame, articulated by Zhou through 2025-2026, is using stablecoins as the conduit to compress cross-border remittance costs into the Philippine peso corridor — which receives ~$35B/year from ~15-20M overseas Filipino workers, equal to over 7% of national GDP.

## Business model

Coins.ph operates the fiat off-ramp end of a [[Stablecoin sandwich]] — partners (notably [[Remitly]]) deliver value into the Philippines as stablecoins, and Coins.ph converts them into Philippine pesos for local payout, with the on-chain layer invisible to the end user. The platform also runs a spot crypto exchange, e-wallet, and Philippine real-time payments integration.

In 2025 Coins.ph executed the first integration of a stablecoin payment leg into QRPh, the Bangko Sentral's national QR payments standard — making stablecoin-funded transfers spendable at any QR-accepting merchant in the country without a bridging step.

### Remittance cost compression

Wei Zhou's framing on the cost trajectory of Philippine remittances:

| Era | Cost per $1 | Mechanism |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| 10 years ago | ~8% | Traditional correspondent banking, Western Union, MoneyGram |
| Today (2026) | 2-4% | App-based remitters ([[Remitly]], WorldRemit), better connectivity |
| Stablecoin rail | ~0.5% (~50 bps) | [[Stablecoin sandwich]] (Coins.ph / [[Remitly]]) |
| Target | 20-30 bps | Further compression Zhou expects |

Math: on $40B of annual Philippine remittance flow, each 1% saved is ~$400M staying in worker pockets.

Source: Bloomberg TV, "Stablecoins Are Quietly Changing How the World Sends Money" (May 2026).

## 2026 strategy (per Wei Zhou, year-end 2025 media briefing)

Three vectors:

1. Stablecoin-led cross-border remittance expansion as the principal growth driver
2. Global expansion beyond the Philippines (target overseas Filipino worker hubs in the Middle East, Korea, Japan, cruise industry)
3. Bridge between fiat and digital assets for a base of users entering a looser monetary environment

Zhou positions stablecoins as having "grown out of crypto" into a separate payments segment — distinct from crypto volatility and increasingly behaving as "just another word for money."

---

## Strategic position

Coins.ph is downstream of two of the biggest current stablecoin tailwinds — the [[GENIUS Act]] giving USD stablecoins regulatory legitimacy in the country that issues them, and the rise of regulated USD stablecoins like [[Circle|USDC]] as preferred infrastructure for compliant remit corridors. The corridor concentration (US/GCC/East Asia → Philippines) is unusually clean for a stablecoin sandwich because the Philippines is dollarized for receivers' financial-services demand (Zhou: "they get paid in the local dollar [USD]... the financial services they need are actually back home") while the local economy is peso.

One bank example Zhou cited: a Philippine bank that was previously top 6-7 by size moved into top 3 simply by being the first to bank crypto/stablecoin exchanges like Coins.ph — an early indicator of how stablecoin rail integration redraws domestic banking league tables.
