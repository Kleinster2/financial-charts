#actor #lithium #mining #argentina #public

**Lithium Argentina** (NYSE: LAAC, TSX: LAR) — Canadian-listed operator of **Caucharí-Olaroz**, a brine lithium operation in Jujuy Province, northwestern [[Argentina]], jointly owned with Chinese producer [[Ganfeng Lithium]]. The entity was created in a 2023 corporate separation that split the legacy Lithium Americas Corp into a US-focused parent ([[Lithium Americas]]) and this Argentina-focused company. Caucharí-Olaroz is one of the newer productive brine operations in the [[Lithium Triangle]] and a primary beneficiary of [[Argentina]]'s market-open development model.

## Caucharí-Olaroz operations

Brine lithium operation using conventional solar-evaporation technology on the Argentine side of the Puna Plateau:

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Location | Jujuy Province, [[Argentina]] |
| Technology | Solar evaporation (conventional brine processing) |
| Ownership | JV with [[Ganfeng Lithium]] |
| Q4 2025 operating rate | 97% of nameplate capacity |
| 2025 product | Primarily battery-grade lithium carbonate |
| First production | 2023 (commercial ramp 2024) |

At sub-$6,000/t cash cost, Caucharí-Olaroz sits in the middle of the global cost curve — more expensive than [[SQM]]'s Atacama brine (legacy low-cost) but well below hard-rock operators in Australia and China. The Ganfeng JV brings Chinese refining integration — concentrate and carbonate flow into [[Ganfeng Lithium]]'s downstream conversion network.

## Stage 2 expansion

Caucharí-Olaroz has submitted environmental permits and RIGI (Argentine incentive framework for large investments) applications for a **45,000 tpa Stage 2 expansion**. If approved and built, total operation capacity rises from ~40 ktpa Stage 1 toward 85 ktpa Stage 1+2.

**Timing caveat:** Argentine permit processes have historically been faster than Chilean but slower than US federal fast-tracks. Stage 2 construction start depends on RIGI approval and commodity-price environment.

### Pastos Grandes — the bigger prize

In December 2025, Lithium Argentina and [[Ganfeng Lithium]] formed a new joint venture to consolidate **Pozuelos and Pastos Grandes basins** — targeted to become [[Argentina]]'s largest lithium operation with a phased development plan to 150,000 tpa LCE. The Ganfeng-led financing structure does not require equity contributions from LAAC shareholders.

Pastos Grandes at full 150 ktpa would nearly quadruple the company's production footprint and make it one of the world's top-5 lithium producers by volume.

## Ganfeng partnership

[[Ganfeng Lithium]] is both operational partner at Caucharí-Olaroz and financier at Pastos Grandes. The relationship reflects the broader Chinese integration into Argentine upstream lithium:

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Operational JV | Caucharí-Olaroz (50/50, with LAAC as operator) |
| Financing | $130M, six-year loan facility (completed) |
| Pastos Grandes | New JV, Ganfeng-led financing, no LAAC shareholder equity required |
| Off-take | Significant portion of output flows to Ganfeng refining |

This creates a structural exposure: LAAC's upside is linked to Chinese downstream refining capacity and Chinese EV-battery demand. Western tariff architecture targeting Chinese batteries (US Section 301, EU anti-subsidy duties) would pressure the off-take channel.

## Argentina context — Milei tailwinds

[[Argentina]] runs the most open investment climate in the [[Lithium Triangle]] under Milei:

- No state-ownership mandates
- Pro-dollarization capital regime (post-2024 reforms)
- RIGI framework provides tax stability for large projects (30-year windows)
- Provincial-level royalty regimes (Jujuy is the relevant jurisdiction for Caucharí-Olaroz)
- Argentine installed LCE capacity grew from 35,500 tpa to 186,000 tpa over the 2023-2026 window — most of that capacity growth happened at LAAC and competitors

The political tailwind is the largest of any Lithium-Triangle jurisdiction. The 2027 Argentine presidential election is the forward risk — any return to Peronism would threaten the RIGI framework.

## Investment lens

LAAC is the cleanest publicly traded exposure to the Argentine side of the [[Lithium Triangle]]. The equity story has three distinct layers:

1. **Caucharí-Olaroz steady-state** — 40 ktpa at sub-$6,000 cost, Ganfeng-integrated off-take, near-100% nameplate utilization.
2. **Stage 2 expansion optionality** — +45 ktpa potential, RIGI-protected, Phase-2 equity-light via Ganfeng.
3. **Pastos Grandes call option** — 150 ktpa potential, Ganfeng-financed, no equity dilution to shareholders, but Ganfeng captures most of the upside.

The Ganfeng relationship is the structural strength (integrated Chinese refining + financing) and the structural vulnerability (Western tariff exposure, single-partner concentration risk).
