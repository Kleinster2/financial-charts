CNBC is a US business news television network launched in April 1989 as a joint venture between [[NBC]] and [[Cablevision]]. Owned by [[NBCUniversal]], a subsidiary of [[Comcast]]. Acquired its main competitor, the Financial News Network, in 1991, expanding both distribution and workforce. Under Roger Ailes in the mid-1990s, annual revenue rose from $43M to $110M. Reported $510M in annual revenue by 2006. Peak viewership of 343,000 average daily viewers in 2000 during the dot-com bubble; ratings dropped 44% by 2002 and have fluctuated since. As of 2023, CNBC had more overall day and primetime viewers than competitor [[Fox Business]].

Slated to become part of [[Versant]], the [[NBCUniversal]] cable/digital spin-off under the [[Comcast]] restructuring (2025-2026), alongside USA Network, MSNBC, and other cable properties.

Primary source for analyst/CEO interviews on energy, markets, and macro. [[Paul Sankey]] is a regular guest, particularly during the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]].
