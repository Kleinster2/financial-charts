**Jon Feltheimer** is the chief executive of [[Lionsgate]], in the role since March 2000 and the executive most responsible for building it from a fledgling independent into a major studio through a 25-year run of acquisitions. His contract was extended in April 2026 through July 31, 2031.

## Career

Feltheimer built the television division of New World Entertainment over eight years from 1984, then spent nine years at Sony Pictures as head of the Columbia TriStar Television Group, where he launched nearly 30 branded channels worldwide and orchestrated Sony's 1997 acquisition of Telemundo (with [[Liberty Media]] and [[Apollo Global Management]]).

Named CEO of Lions Gate Entertainment in March 2000, he turned a small independent into a diversified studio through serial M&A — Artisan Entertainment (2003), Summit Entertainment (2012, $412.5M, bringing Twilight), and Starz (2016, $4.4B), followed later by [[eOne]] (2023). He then oversaw the two-step unwind of that conglomerate: the [[Screaming Eagle Acquisition Corp]] de-SPAC and the May 2025 [[Starz]] separation.

---

## At Lionsgate today

The April 2026 extension through 2031 (with options on 4.5M shares at $11.07 and 666,667 RSUs) signals board intent to keep [[Lionsgate]] independent near-term, even as Feltheimer has publicly acknowledged that "doing some kind of strategic transaction down the road is something that's probably gonna happen." Working alongside vice chairman [[Michael Burns]], his dealmaking counterpart, he remains the architect of both Lionsgate's build-up and its current pure-play form. See [[Lionsgate]] → Management.
