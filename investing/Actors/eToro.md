**eToro** is a social trading and multi-asset brokerage platform based in [[Israel]]. Known for its copy-trading feature, which lets users replicate the trades of top performers. Failed a SPAC IPO attempt in 2022 before reviving traditional IPO plans.

## Synopsis

eToro built its brand on democratizing trading through social features -- the copy-trading mechanic, where retail users can automatically mirror the positions of other traders, created a viral growth loop that traditional brokerages like [[Interactive Brokers]] or [[Charles Schwab]] lacked. Founded in 2007 by brothers Yoni and Ronen Assia along with David Ring, the platform grew rapidly during the 2020-2021 retail trading boom, reaching 30M+ registered users globally. Revenue peaked alongside crypto and meme-stock volumes, then contracted as the speculative cycle unwound.

The IPO saga tells the story of eToro's valuation whiplash. A SPAC merger with FinTech Acquisition Corp V was announced in March 2021 at a $10.4B valuation, then terminated in July 2022 as public market multiples collapsed -- the same fate that hit [[Saxo Bank]]'s SPAC plans. eToro subsequently raised private capital at a reported ~$3.5B valuation (2023), a 66% hairdown from the SPAC peak. By 2024-2025, the company revived traditional IPO plans, reportedly targeting a US listing. The question is whether eToro can sustain growth outside speculative cycles: the copy-trading feature is sticky but the user base skews heavily toward crypto and momentum trading, making revenue highly correlated with market volatility and risk appetite. Commission-free trading in a market dominated by [[Robinhood]] and zero-fee incumbents leaves payment-for-order-flow and spread capture as the primary revenue levers.
