**Zhu Qiuguo** is the founder and CEO of [[Deep Robotics]] and associate professor at [[Zhejiang University]] College of Control Science and Engineering. He discovered robotics through ZJUDancer (the university's humanoid soccer robot team) as an undergraduate and went on to build one of China's leading quadruped and legged robotics companies.

## Synopsis

Zhu represents the academic-to-founder pipeline that characterizes China's robotics startup ecosystem. His research at [[Zhejiang University]] in Hangzhou — one of China's top engineering schools — produced the quadruped robots Chitu and Jueying, along with Wukong, a table tennis-playing robot that demonstrated advanced hand-eye coordination and real-time motion planning. In 2017, he spun his research out into [[Deep Robotics]], which has since commercialized quadruped platforms for industrial inspection, power plant monitoring, and hazardous environment operations.

Deep Robotics sits within the broader [[Hangzhou Robotics Cluster]], a geography that benefits from proximity to Zhejiang University's talent pipeline, local government subsidies, and the manufacturing supply chains of the Yangtze River Delta. The company has raised multiple funding rounds (details partially disclosed) and competes with [[Unitree Robotics]] (also Hangzhou-based) and global incumbents like [[Boston Dynamics]] in the quadruped space. For investors tracking Chinese robotics, Zhu is one of the key technical founders — his academic credentials give Deep Robotics access to graduate researchers, while his dual role as professor and CEO maintains the R&D pipeline. The quadruped market is still small relative to industrial arms ([[Fanuc]], [[ABB]]) or the emerging humanoid segment, but Zhu's pivot toward AI-integrated locomotion and manipulation puts Deep Robotics in the conversation as embodied AI scales.
