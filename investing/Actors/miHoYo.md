# miHoYo

Chinese gaming studio founded 2011 in Shanghai by Cai Haoyu, Liu Wei, and Luo Yuhao — three Shanghai Jiao Tong University graduates. Best known internationally for *Genshin Impact* (released September 2020), the fastest mobile game in history to cross $10B in player spending (achieved by end of 2025 per Sensor Tower / Niko Partners reporting; annual revenue peaked at ~$1.9B in 2022 and has declined since to ~$0.7B in 2024 as the game entered a mature lifecycle phase). The international-facing brand was rebranded "HoYoverse" in 2022. miHoYo is private (no IPO as of 2026) and remains founder-controlled — Cai Haoyu retains majority control.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. Chinese gaming export success — miHoYo is the canonical example of Chinese content IP succeeding globally in a way that [[Tencent]] and [[NetEase]] have largely not. The anime-stylised aesthetic + gacha mechanics + live-service operations represent a distinct content-creation paradigm
2. AI-in-content-creation early adopter — miHoYo has invested heavily in generative AI tooling for art, music, and narrative content; ahead of the curve compared to Western AAA studios on AI integration
3. IPO candidate watchlist — as a private firm with stable revenue and clear international IP, miHoYo has been speculated as a future IPO candidate. Any listing would likely target Hong Kong; would test investor appetite for Chinese gaming exposure in the post-[[Common Prosperity]]-enforcement-phase environment
