## Q1 2026 earnings and Apr 29 selloff

SoFi delivered another record quarter, but the stock moved from $18.36 to $15.52, -15.4%, on Apr 29. The divergence matters: reported growth remained very strong, so the move was more about expectations, valuation, and the decision to maintain rather than raise full-year guidance.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | YoY |
|--------|---------|-----|
| [[GAAP]] net revenue | $1.100B | +43% |
| Adjusted net revenue | $1.087B | +41% |
| [[GAAP]] net income | $166.7M | +134% |
| Adjusted EBITDA | $339.9M | +62% |
| Adjusted EBITDA margin | 31% | — |
| Members | 14.7M | +35% |
| Products | 22.2M | +39% |
| Total loan originations | $12.2B | Record |

Loan demand was broad: personal-loan originations reached $8.3B, student loans $2.6B, and home loans $1.2B. The bank-charter funding advantage remained visible, with deposits comprising more than 90% of average liabilities and deposit funding saving an annualized ~$621.8M versus warehouse facilities.

The platform read-through was mixed. Financial Services segment revenue grew 41% to $428.5M, while Technology Platform enabled accounts fell 16% YoY to 133M after a large client transitioned off the platform, though accounts still rose by 4M sequentially. SoFi also began minting [[SoFiUSD]], a [[Stablecoins|stablecoin]], and described settlement interoperability with partners including [[Mastercard]].

Full-year guidance stayed strong but not upwardly reset: adjusted net revenue about $4.655B, adjusted EBITDA about $1.6B, adjusted net income about $825M, and adjusted EPS around $0.60.

*Source: SoFi Q1 2026 earnings release, filed as [[SEC]] 8-K earnings exhibit (Apr 29, 2026). Price move verified in local DB: Apr 28 close $18.36 → Apr 29 close $15.52.*
