Federal Home Loan Banks -- a system of 11 regional government-sponsored enterprise (GSE) banks that provide low-cost funding to member financial institutions (banks, credit unions, insurers) for mortgage lending, community investment, and affordable housing.

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Established | 1932 (Federal Home Loan Bank Act) |
| Structure | 11 regional banks, cooperatively owned by member institutions |
| Regulator | Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA) |
| GSE status | Yes -- federally chartered, privately owned |
| Debt | Issues consolidated obligations (agency debt), exempt from state/local tax |

FHLB debt, like [[Farm Credit System]] debt, trades as "agency" paper with high credit ratings and state/local tax-exempt interest. Together with [[Fannie Mae]], [[Freddie Mac]], and Farm Credit, FHLBs form the core of the US agency debt market.
