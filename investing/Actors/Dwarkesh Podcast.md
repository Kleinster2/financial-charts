# Dwarkesh Podcast

Long-form interview podcast hosted by [[Dwarkesh Patel]] (formerly "The Lunar Society"), focused on AI research, geopolitics, economics, and history. The interview style is unusually technical — guests are pushed on specifics of architecture, training, and mechanism rather than allowed to stay at the level of strategy or generalisation. The May 15, 2026 [[Eric Jang]] interview "Building AlphaGo from scratch" is the canonical vault-side citation: the MCTS-vs-LLM-RL framework, the cost-curve compression evidence, and the credit-assignment-problem articulation all come from this interview.

## Why it matters

Dwarkesh Patel's selection of guests and willingness to push on technical specifics produces a higher information-density-per-minute than most AI-focused podcasts — particularly when guests are senior researchers (Demis Hassabis, Sam Altman, Dario Amodei) or technical practitioners (Eric Jang, Sholto Douglas, Trenton Bricken). For a vault that builds on primary-source-equivalent material, Dwarkesh interviews are unusually citable.

The May 2026 [[Eric Jang]] interview is a representative case: it produced the canonical articulation of the MCTS-vs-LLM-RL credit-assignment framework that doesn't exist in equally precise form anywhere else in the public AI commentary literature.
