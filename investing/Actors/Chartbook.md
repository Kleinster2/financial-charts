### Chartbook 447 — "The US economy in May 2026" (deep treatment)

The essay frames spring 2026 as a moment of structural cognitive dissonance: the US economy is simultaneously *"spiraling to infinity"* (record corporate earnings, semiconductor megacycle, $700B-market-cap [[Micron]], [[Yardeni Research|Yardeni]] target raised to 8,250) and *"collapsing to extinction"* (deteriorating fiscal arithmetic, sticky inflation, [[Lyn Alden|Alden]] fiscal-dominance regime, the [[Anthropic]] / [[OpenAI]] void declarations exposing the unauthorized-secondaries plumbing as legally fragile).

The single sharpest analytical move in the essay is the connection from **private-AI-lab valuation markups → Mag 7 reported earnings via mark-to-market accounting on equity stakes**:

| Datum | Q1 2026 figure | Source in essay |
|---|---|---|
| [[Alphabet]] + [[Amazon]] "other income" Q1 2026 | ~$53 billion combined | Goldman Sachs analysts (cited in Tooze) |
| Share of those two companies' Q1 income from "other income" | ~60% | Goldman Sachs analysts |
| Driver of the markup | [[Anthropic]] revaluation $183B (Sep 2025) → $380B (Feb 2026) | Tooze framing |

The structural read: the [[Mag 7 cluster]] earnings narrative driving the [[S&P 500]] meltup is partially a *private-market-markup pass-through*, not pure operating income. When [[Anthropic]]'s Series G repriced the stake from $183B to $380B, [[Alphabet]] and [[Amazon]] (both holders of meaningful [[Anthropic]] equity stakes per the [[Anthropic#Cap table]] section) booked mark-to-market gains that appear as "other income" on the consolidated income statement. The same dynamic ran in reverse on the [[OpenAI]] side via [[Microsoft]]'s 27% stake (see [[Microsoft#May 11, 2026 — $92B return target disclosed in Musk-OpenAI trial|Microsoft May 11 court disclosure]]).

This matters for three vault threads:

1. **[[AI infrastructure financing risk]]** — the circularity story already covered there (hyperscaler-as-investor-and-supplier) now has an *earnings-quality* dimension: meltup-cycle reported earnings are partially endogenous to the AI-lab valuation cycle they fund. The May 11 [[Anthropic]] / [[OpenAI]] void declarations sharpen the question because tokenized perp DEXs implied [[Anthropic]] at $1.6T pre-announcement — if a future round prices at that level, the hyperscaler mark-to-market other-income line item compounds again.
2. **[[Mag 7 cluster]] cluster-validation** — the May 8 falsification finding ([[Mag 7]] cluster failed the random-basket and holdout tests in the new framework) gains a complementary structural explanation. Mag 7 earnings divergence is partially driven by which Mag 7 members hold which private-AI-lab stakes, not by underlying operating-business correlation.
3. **Earnings-led-meltup framing ([[Ed Yardeni|Yardeni]] 8,250)** — Yardeni's "earnings have been phenomenal" framing is correct on the headline number but not all of those earnings represent operating-business productivity gains. The portion driven by private-AI-lab mark-to-market is real cash-flow over time only if those valuations hold or expand at the eventual liquidity event.

The structural take: the longer the AI-lab valuation cycle continues, the more endogenous the [[Mag 7 cluster]] earnings narrative becomes. If the [[SpaceX]] IPO at $1.75T closes successfully in June 2026, the cycle gets validated at the largest possible scale. If it stalls or prices below the framework, the unwind hits both the AI-lab valuations AND the hyperscaler other-income line simultaneously.

*Source: [Chartbook 447: The US economy in May 2026](https://adamtooze.substack.com/p/chartbook-447-the-us-economy-in-may), Adam Tooze Substack, May 2026; Goldman Sachs analyst commentary cited inside the essay.*
