**Day Job** is an LA-based creative agency that has built a reputation for brand identity work in tech, media, and sports — most notably designing the visual identity for [[TBPN]], the streaming sports network.

## Synopsis

Day Job operates in the high-end brand strategy and visual identity space, where a handful of agencies shape how tech and media companies present themselves to the market. Their work for [[TBPN]] — the forest-green "country club" palette combined with [[Formula 1]]-inspired sponsor logo density — established a distinctive on-air aesthetic that broke from traditional sports broadcasting's primary-color conventions. The agency's approach is design-forward: treating visual branding as a strategic lever rather than a cosmetic layer, which appeals to media startups that need to signal premium positioning from launch.

The firm is privately held and small, making it uninvestable directly. Its vault relevance is as a node in the media/sports branding ecosystem — a signal of which new entrants in streaming and sports media are taking brand identity seriously. When Day Job shows up on a project, it typically indicates a well-funded launch with ambitions beyond commoditized content delivery. The agency model in creative branding remains fragmented — no public-company pure play — but consolidation by holding companies like [[WPP]] and [[Publicis]] continues to absorb boutique agencies of this profile.
