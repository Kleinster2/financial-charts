**Starz** (Nasdaq: STRZ) is a niche premium-streaming company — the former [[Lions Gate Entertainment]] parent renamed and spun off from the [[Lionsgate]] studio on May 7, 2025. It runs a targeted, fan-driven content model (the *Power* universe, *Outlander*) around ~17.6M US subscribers, and since March 2026 has been the target of an open takeover campaign by [[Byron Allen]].

## One-line read

Starz is a deleveraging micro-cap premium streamer in play, where the bet is that an OTT-led turnaround (record 12.7M streaming subs, $204M OIBDA, falling leverage) plus an unsolicited [[Byron Allen]] takeover campaign on a ~$475M equity / ~$1B-EV base resolves either as a re-rating on improving cash flow or an outright sale — against the board's poison-pill defense of independence.

## The takeover situation

The dominant driver of STRZ since March 2026 is an open campaign by media entrepreneur [[Byron Allen]] to acquire the company. The stock has roughly doubled from his entry price.

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 2026 | [[Byron Allen]], via Allen Family Capital, buys a 10.7% stake from [[Steven Mnuchin]]'s Liberty fund — ~$25M at $13.86/share |
| March 2026 | Starz board adopts a poison pill (shareholder rights plan): 17.5% ownership trigger, 50% discount to aggressor, expires March 10, 2027 (extendable to 2029) |
| May 2026 | Allen states "I want to control Starz"; says he will buy the whole company, preferring to keep it public with greater investment, but will make a full offer if blocked |

Allen's rationale is to pair Starz's subscription streaming (SVOD) with the free ad-supported (AVOD) ecosystem he is building at [[Allen Media Group]] — including The Weather Channel and a recently acquired BuzzFeed — which he frames as "a left hook and a right hook." No formal offer price has been made. At ~$1.0B enterprise value and ~2.5x EV/OIBDA, Starz is cheap enough that the activist math is straightforward; the poison pill signals the board intends to control the timing and price of any outcome rather than block it outright.

## Corporate structure: separation from Lionsgate

Starz is the surviving entity of the old [[Lions Gate Entertainment]] conglomerate. The two-step unwind — a [[Screaming Eagle Acquisition Corp]] de-SPAC of the studio (2024), then the full split (May 7, 2025) — left [[Lionsgate]] as a pure-play studio and Lions Gate Entertainment Corp renamed to Starz Entertainment Corp as a pure-play premium-content distributor. Starz also changed its fiscal year to a December calendar (the old conglomerate reported to March 31; the studio kept March 31).

Both former halves have outperformed the market since the split: STRZ ~+150%, LION ~+70%.

## Streaming business and subscribers

Starz has transitioned from a linear premium-cable network to a digital-first streamer: roughly 70% of revenue and subscribers now come from OTT.

| Metric | Q4 2025 (ended Dec 31) | Trend |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| OTT subscribers | 12.7M | +7.6% YoY, all-time high, +370K net in quarter |
| Total US subscribers | 17.6M | +170K |
| OTT share of revenue | ~70% | rising |

Management has said it will pivot reporting away from subscriber counts toward OTT revenue, profitability, and margin — a maturity signal that mirrors the broader industry shift from growth to economics. Strategy emphasizes greater original-content ownership and expanded international sales via partnerships.

## Content strategy

Starz competes not on scale against [[Netflix]] or [[Disney]] but on targeted, fan-driven franchises with durable, predictable audiences.

| Franchise | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Power universe | Core driver — *Power Book IV: Force* season 3 grew in-season viewership 57% |
| Outlander | Flagship — final season concluding; prequel *Blood of My Blood* extends the universe |
| BMF | Continuing crime drama |
| Spartacus | *House of Ashur* revival |

The model targets specific, loyal demographics (notably underserved audiences) rather than broad general entertainment, which keeps churn and content spend (targeted below $650M in 2026) controlled relative to scale streamers.

## Financials

FY2025 (calendar year ended Dec 31, 2025) was a beat: adjusted OIBDA of $204M exceeded the $200M guidance, on revenue of roughly $1.28B (~$320M per quarter). Q4 2025 revenue was $322.8M with $55.5M adjusted OIBDA. Management frames 2026 as a positive inflection — OTT revenue growth, low-single-digit adjusted OIBDA growth, and $80–120M of positive unlevered free cash flow.

Annual figures from the official Starz releases, not `market_data.db`: the DB annual series for STRZ (inherited from the old [[Lions Gate Entertainment]] CIK) mixes pre-separation consolidated studio+Starz results — including multi-billion-dollar revenue and large impairment-driven losses — with Starz-standalone figures, and is not used here.

## What to watch

- Byron Allen's next move — whether [[Byron Allen]] makes a formal offer, the price, and whether the board engages or stays behind the poison pill. The pill expires March 10, 2027.
- Leverage trajectory — net debt $523.0M, guided to ~2.7x by year-end on positive unlevered FCF. Hitting the target supports both the equity and a takeout; missing it pressures both.
- OTT subscriber and revenue trend — 12.7M OTT subs is a record; the pivot to reporting OTT revenue/margin over sub counts means the next signal is whether OTT revenue and profitability actually inflect higher in 2026.
- Content slate performance — the *Power* universe and the *Outlander* conclusion/prequel are the audience anchors; a franchise stumble removes the cash-flow case underpinning the deleveraging guide.
- Free cash flow delivery — the $80–120M positive unlevered FCF guide for 2026 is the proof point that the standalone streamer is self-funding.

## Analysis

Starz is a small, cheap, deleveraging premium streamer that became an event-driven situation overnight. Two stories run in parallel. Operationally, the company is executing a credible OTT-led turnaround: record streaming subs, an OIBDA beat, and a deleveraging path toward 2.7x — the unglamorous case for a re-rating on improving cash flow. Strategically, [[Byron Allen]]'s campaign has put the company in play on a ~$1.0B enterprise value, and the stock has doubled off his $13.86 entry to price in some probability of a deal.

The structural tension is between a board defending independence (the poison pill) and a determined acquirer with a clear strategic logic (bolting SVOD onto his AVOD ecosystem). The poison pill is best read not as a flat refusal but as a tool to control timing and price — Starz is too small and too cheap, with too motivated a suitor, for "stay independent forever" to be the base case. The risk is the reverse: that Allen, who has a history of headline-grabbing bids that don't close, is signaling more than he can finance, and that an unconsummated campaign leaves STRZ to re-rate back down to its standalone fundamentals.

For the broader vault, Starz is the cleanest live example of small-cap media consolidation pressure — the same logic that makes [[Lionsgate]] a perennial target applies here, but with an actual named aggressor on the tape. The two former halves of the old conglomerate are now both consolidation candidates.
