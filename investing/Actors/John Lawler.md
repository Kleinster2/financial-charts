# John Lawler

## Synopsis

[[John Lawler]] is the Vice Chair of [[Ford]] Motor Company since February 2025, with portfolio responsibility for strategy, partnerships, alliances, and corporate development. He served as Ford's Chief Financial Officer from October 2020 through January 2025 — the period spanning Ford's COVID-cycle recovery, the [[Ford+]] strategy announcement, the [[Lincoln]]-Ford-and-Model-e segmentation reorganisation, and the early Trump-II-tariff disclosure cycle — before stepping into the Vice Chair role on Sherry House's appointment as CFO. Lawler's 35-year Ford career has crossed nearly every major regional and functional finance role: CEO of Ford Autonomous Vehicles (mobility-partnerships era), VP of Strategy, VP and Corporate Controller, CFO of Ford's Global Markets segment (2016-2018), and President of Ford China during the firm's record in-country performance period.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Sherry House]] succession context: Lawler's transition to Vice Chair in February 2025 was paired with House's elevation to CFO, structuring a *both-and* succession (rather than departure-replacement) that preserves Lawler's strategic-partnership authority while bringing in EV-startup financial-management experience at the top finance role. The structure is unusual — most CFO transitions are exits — and reflects the depth of Ford's Trump-II-tariff and EV-strategy operating challenges.

## Vice Chair role and Ford+ strategy

Lawler's Vice Chair portfolio (since February 2025) covers:

- *Strategy* — Ford+ growth-and-value-creation framework
- *Partnerships and alliances* — including OEM-OEM platform deals, supplier joint ventures, and the legacy Lincoln-Ford-Model-e capital-allocation framework
- *Corporate development* — M&A and restructuring options

The Vice Chair structure parallels the role [[Bill Ford]] (executive chair) plays for the founding-family side, with Lawler covering the non-family-side strategic / partnerships portfolio. The arrangement keeps Lawler operationally close to CEO [[Jim Farley]] while [[Sherry House]] runs the finance function.

## Career trajectory

Lawler's 35-year Ford tenure represents one of the deepest finance-and-operations career arcs in the auto industry. The China presidency in particular (during a period when Ford had record local market share) provides operational credibility for global strategy work. The pivot through Ford Autonomous Vehicles in the late 2010s gave him direct exposure to the mobility-partnerships side that is now in his Vice Chair portfolio.

The 2020 elevation to CFO came after the COVID-era cash-management challenge and through the [[Ford+]] strategic announcement (March 2022). His five-year CFO tenure covered the EV-strategy buildout, the Model e and Ford Pro segmentation, and the early innings of the Trump-II tariff window.
