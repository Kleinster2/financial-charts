**Start Campus** — private developer of SINES DC in Sines, Portugal: a 1.2 GW data-center campus that is Europe's largest colocation site by fully secured grid power, cooled by seawater rather than freshwater. The campus repurposes intake infrastructure from the decommissioned Sines coastal power plant — an existing-infrastructure shortcut through Europe's power-and-permitting bottleneck.

## Why it matters

- Scarce resource arbitrage: in a European market constrained by grid interconnection queues ([[Power constraints]]) and water scrutiny ([[Water constraints]]), SINES DC secured 1.2 GW of grid power and eliminated freshwater cooling in one site — featured in the FT's capacity-race visual story (Jul 31, 2025) as the European counterpoint to the US gigawatt buildouts.
- The seawater design is the template case for coastal siting: cooling capacity inherited from legacy thermal-plant infrastructure rather than built from scratch.
- Atlantic-coast siting puts the campus near subsea-cable landing routes, pitching Sines as a European AI hub rather than a colocation outpost.
