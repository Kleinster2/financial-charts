# Hiive

Hiive — pre-IPO secondary marketplace, co-founded by Prab Rattan. Direct competitor to [[Forge Global]] and [[Nasdaq Private Market]] in the brokered private-secondary trading category. Drew unusually deep [[Anthropic]] demand in 2026 (>$1.6B registered at premium to the $380B Series G) and was named by [[Anthropic]] in a blocklist of unauthorized share sales (May 11, 2026) — though only its *new offerings* were called out, not the full platform.

## Anthropic demand registration (Apr 2026)

Hiive registered >$1.6B in demand for [[Anthropic]] shares at a premium to the Feb 2026 Series G valuation of $380B. Rattan: *"The demand is one of the highest we've ever seen — it's essentially unlimited interest."* The figure is in the same order of magnitude as the >$1.6T tokenized-implied [[Anthropic]] valuation that was building on [[Ventuals]] / [[PreStocks]] perp DEXs through April-May 2026 — and demonstrates the demand-side asymmetry that ultimately triggered the [[Anthropic]] May 11 issuer pushback.

---

## May 11 2026 — Named in Anthropic blocklist

On May 11, 2026, [[Anthropic]] updated its public help-center notice on unauthorized stock sales, listing eight platforms whose offerings of Anthropic stock are declared void: [[Open Door Partners]], [[Unicorns Exchange]], [[Pachamama Capital]], [[Lionheart Ventures]], [[Sydecar]], [[Upmarket]], and *new offerings* on [[Forge Global]] and Hiive.

The qualification matters: unlike pure SPV operators on the list, Hiive (along with Forge Global) is a board-recognized infrastructure provider for *authorized* secondary transactions — the inclusion was specifically about *new* offerings on the platform that lacked direct issuer sign-off, not the platform's compliance infrastructure as a whole.

Platform response: Hiive defended its compliance posture, asserting all transfers it facilitates are issuer-approved. The dispute over which specific new offerings were considered unauthorized remained unresolved as of May 13. The structural significance is that even *authorized-secondary* marketplaces have unauthorized-offering layers on top that issuers are now naming and severing.
