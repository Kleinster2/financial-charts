## Why Amazon bought Fauna

[[Amazon]]'s in-house home robot [[Astro]] was effectively a failure — invite-only for 4+ years, business version bricked after 10 months, 800+ person team, "a few hundred units" shipped. Fauna represents a pivot:

| | [[Astro]] (in-house) | Fauna / [[Sprout]] (acquired) |
|---|---|---|
| Form | Wheeled | Bipedal humanoid |
| Design philosophy | Alexa-first, surveillance | Social-first, safety |
| Team approach | 800+ people, years of development | Startup acqui-hire |
| Target | Home monitoring | Developer platform → consumer |

Fauna's approach — build a developer platform first, let the ecosystem create use cases — is the opposite of Astro's top-down consumer product strategy.
