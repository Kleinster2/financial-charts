# Jason Kwon

Chief Strategy Officer at [[OpenAI]]. Notable for backing the [[ATOM Project]] (American Truly Open Models) — a move that signals internal recognition at [[OpenAI]] that the US open-source AI ecosystem matters for national competitiveness, despite OpenAI's own closed-source positioning.

## Synopsis

Jason Kwon serves as CSO at [[OpenAI]], where he handles corporate strategy, partnerships, and policy engagement. Before joining OpenAI, Kwon worked in venture capital and technology investing, giving him a finance-oriented lens on AI commercialization that complements the research-first perspectives of colleagues like [[Sam Altman]] (CEO) and [[Mira Murati]] (former CTO). His role has grown in significance as OpenAI navigated its 2023-2024 governance crises, nonprofit-to-profit restructuring, and the scaling of commercial revenue from ~$1B to $5B+ ARR. Kwon was part of the executive team that held the company together during the November 2023 board crisis.

The ATOM Project endorsement is the most interesting signal. ATOM is a consortium pushing for American open-source AI model development as a strategic national asset — a direct philosophical challenge to OpenAI's core business model of closed, proprietary frontier models. That OpenAI's own CSO publicly supports open-source AI development suggests one of two things: either OpenAI sees open-source as complementary (an ecosystem that grows the pie) or Kwon is hedging against the possibility that closed-source AI becomes a political liability as the technology grows more powerful. Either way, it complicates the narrative that OpenAI is purely an API-access play.
