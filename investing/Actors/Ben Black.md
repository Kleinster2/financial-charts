**Ben Black** — CEO of the [[US Development Finance Corporation|DFC]] (confirmed October 7, 2025). Son of [[Leon Black]], co-founder of [[Apollo Global Management]]. Previously worked in private equity at [[Apollo Global Management|Apollo]], as an analyst at [[Goldman Sachs]] (2007), and as a corporate attorney at [[Sullivan & Cromwell]]. Founded Fortinbras Enterprises, a New York-based credit investment fund ($122M AUM). Also served as CEO of Osiris Acquisition Corp (SPAC). UPenn, Harvard, NYU Law.

Nominated by Trump June 18, 2025. Ceremonial Oval Office swearing-in by VP [[JD Vance]] on December 12, 2025. Led DFC's March 2026 deployment of a $20B maritime reinsurance facility for Gulf shipping during the [[Iran conflict energy disruption|Iran conflict]] — the agency's most aggressive and controversial move, far outside its traditional development finance mandate.
