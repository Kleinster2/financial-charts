## AMD enterprise AI cloud MOU (May 7-8, 2026)

RXT closed +12.5% on May 8 after announcing a [[Memorandum of understanding|memorandum of understanding]] (MOU) with [[AMD]] to develop an enterprise AI cloud tailored for regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, government). The MOU is the second visible non-hyperscaler enterprise-AI cloud build-out announced this week, paired with [[Akamai]]'s $1.8B / 7-year contract with an undisclosed frontier-AI model provider.

The structural read for the third-party-cloud category:

1. **Regulated industries are an underserved AI infrastructure market.** Hyperscalers' default-public-cloud architecture creates compliance friction for FedRAMP-High, HIPAA, FINRA, and similar regulated workloads. A purpose-built AI cloud with sovereignty controls, dedicated tenancy, and audit-ready data flows is a wedge that bypasses some of the hyperscaler advantages on raw cost and scale.
2. **AMD positioning matters.** The MOU adds another anchor application for AMD's MI-series Instinct GPUs in enterprise contexts — most enterprise-AI infrastructure to date has defaulted to NVIDIA. Combined with the AMD-anchored deployments at [[OpenAI]] (announced Oct 2025) and the rumored AMD-Anthropic discussions, the AMD enterprise-GPU pipeline is starting to take shape.
3. **Rackspace's strategic re-positioning.** RXT has spent the last several years working through the post-Apollo deleveraging cycle and the painful transition from a single-product (managed hosting) to a multi-product (multi-cloud services) company. The AMD MOU is the first major new-market announcement that lands as a positive surprise rather than as a "hopefully this works" pivot.

What this does NOT change:
- The MOU is preliminary; no announced revenue, customer commitments, or capacity build-out timeline.
- Rackspace is still a small-cap (~$1B market cap range) competing against hyperscalers with multi-trillion dollar valuations.
- Regulated-industry AI cloud is a category the hyperscalers can move into themselves with focused investment if it grows.
