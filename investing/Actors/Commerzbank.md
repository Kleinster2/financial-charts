Commerzbank is [[Germany]]'s second-largest bank by assets, behind [[Deutsche Bank]]. It acquired [[Dresdner Bank]] from [[Allianz]] in 2008-09 for ~5.5B EUR — a crisis-era deal that nearly sank the combined entity. The German government injected 18.2B EUR in bailout capital (2008-2009), taking a 25% stake that was only fully exited in 2024 after 15 years. The Dresdner integration destroyed roughly 10,000 jobs and required years of restructuring under successive CEOs.

The bank's modern story centers on [[UniCredit]]'s contested takeover bid. [[Andrea Orcel]] began building a stake in late 2024 after the German government started selling its shares, eventually accumulating ~26% directly plus ~4% through derivatives (~30% economic interest). Chancellor Scholz called it an "unfriendly attack," and the deal became a proxy for European cross-border banking consolidation vs. national champions. CEO [[Bettina Orlopp]] (appointed September 2024) has pursued a standalone defense strategy anchored on improved profitability and a 3B EUR buyback program.

In March 2026, [[UniCredit]] launched a formal €35B unsolicited offer at €30.8/share — a deliberately low 4% premium. The offer is structured to clear a legal threshold, not win acceptances. Under German takeover rules, once UniCredit holds above 30% it can buy freely in the open market without triggering another mandatory bid. Above 50% gives control over ordinary affairs and Supervisory Board appointments; above 75% entitles the majority shareholder to a "domination agreement" under German law. [[Commerzbank]]'s board must review any formal offer by law, giving Orcel the audience he has struggled to secure — with the board, with [[Friedrich Merz]]'s government, and with trade unions. The board's response was predictably chilly.

The playbook echoes [[Media for Europe]]'s acquisition of [[ProSiebenSat.1]] in 2025 — a low-ball offer followed by quiet market accumulation that took MFE from 29% to 76% within months. [[Mediobanca]] analysts estimate ~€0.7B in after-tax synergies from combining Commerzbank with [[UniCredit]]'s German subsidiary HypoVereinsbank, worth ~€7B in lump sum — enough for a ~30% premium to remaining shareholders if Orcel chose to offer it. The German government retains 13% and could try to frame the situation as a national interest question. But Commerzbank stock is up 2.5x since UniCredit first revealed its stakebuilding in 2024, limiting Orcel's downside even if the bid fails.
