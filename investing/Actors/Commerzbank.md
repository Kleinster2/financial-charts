Commerzbank is [[Germany]]'s second-largest bank by assets, behind [[Deutsche Bank]]. It acquired [[Dresdner Bank]] from [[Allianz]] in 2008-09 for ~5.5B EUR — a crisis-era deal that nearly sank the combined entity. The German government injected 18.2B EUR in bailout capital (2008-2009), taking a 25% stake that was only fully exited in 2024 after 15 years. The Dresdner integration destroyed roughly 10,000 jobs and required years of restructuring under successive CEOs.

The bank's modern story centers on [[UniCredit]]'s contested takeover bid, launched in late 2024 when UniCredit built a 21%+ stake (partly through derivatives) after the German government began selling its shares. The bid triggered a political firestorm — Chancellor Scholz called it an "unfriendly attack," and the deal became a proxy for European cross-border banking consolidation vs. national champions. CEO Bettina Orlopp (appointed September 2024) has pursued a standalone defense strategy anchored on improved profitability and a 3B EUR buyback program.
