## Active Electrical Cables

**What's an AEC?**
- [[Copper]] cable with active electronics
- [[Signal]] conditioning extends reach
- Lower power than optical (short distances)
- Cost-effective for intra-rack

**Where AECs win:**
- GPU-to-switch within rack
- Short-reach high-bandwidth
- Power-sensitive applications
- Complements optical (longer distances)

## Q4 FY2026 earnings and valuation reset (June 1 2026)

Credo's June 1 print confirmed the structural demand story but also showed how tight the expectations band has become for AI interconnect winners. The company beat on revenue and earnings, guided Q1 FY2027 above the just-reported quarter, and still sold off after hours because the market wanted a larger upward inflection after the stock's run.

| Metric | Result / guide | Read-through |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| Q4 FY2026 revenue | $437.0M, +7.4% QoQ, +157.0% YoY | AEC / high-speed connectivity demand still compounding |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 68.3% | High-margin interconnect mix intact |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $1.16 | Profitability is scaling with revenue, not just hype |
| FY2026 revenue | $1.3B, more than tripled YoY | Credo has moved from niche cable supplier to scaled AI-infrastructure vendor |
| FY2026 non-GAAP net income | $662M, more than 5x YoY | Operating leverage is now central to the thesis |
| Q1 FY2027 guide | Revenue $465-475M; non-GAAP GM 67-69% | Continued growth, but not enough to satisfy elevated expectations |

The sell-off is not a demand-break signal by itself. It is a valuation/expectations reset: the same scarcity premium that lifted [[AI Infrastructure]] suppliers now requires not just strong numbers, but stronger-than-strong forward acceleration. For Credo, the note to watch is whether copper AEC strength remains the core profit pool or whether the optical expansion starts to dilute the clean intra-rack thesis.
