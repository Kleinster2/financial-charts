# Rystad Energy

Rystad Energy is a Norwegian energy research and consulting firm, headquartered in Oslo. Founded in 2004 by Jarand Rystad, it has become one of the most-cited independent sources for upstream oil and gas data, supply/demand forecasts, cost curve analysis, and energy transition metrics. Coverage spans 160+ countries across upstream, downstream, renewables, and carbon management. Rystad's data is used by oil companies, banks, governments, and institutional investors for capital allocation decisions and policy modeling.

## Business profile

Rystad differentiates from legacy energy consultancies ([[IHS Markit]], [[Wood Mackenzie]]) through granular asset-level data — it models individual fields, wells, and projects rather than relying on country-level aggregates. The UCube (Upstream Cube) database tracks ~65,000 oil and gas assets globally. Rystad's supply forecasts and breakeven cost analysis are frequently cited in sell-side research and financial media, making it a de facto standard for energy investment analysis. The firm has expanded into renewables and carbon capture economics as the energy transition reshapes client needs.
