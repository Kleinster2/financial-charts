# Gianluca Benigno

**Gianluca Benigno** - Economist and central-bank watcher. Publishes [[The Central Banks' Watcher]], a Substack focused on inflation reports, monetary-policy reaction functions, and supply-shock interpretation across the Fed, Bank of Japan, Bank of England, SNB, and other central banks.

Relevant to the investing vault because his current framework sits at the intersection of energy shocks, inflation pass-through, and central-bank credibility. That makes him a useful complement to [[Adam Tooze]] on macro-political economy and to market strategists like [[Morgan Stanley]] when the debate is whether supply shocks become policy shocks.

## May 2026 commentary

The May 12 archive state shows the latest post as "Central Bank Commentary (April 2026): Fed, BoJ, and BoE" with subtitle "A linear interpretation of a non-linear shock?" The archive immediately before that included Switzerland April CPI and the April 28 "Return of Nonlinear Inflation: Part I" on why supply-chain stress matters more than the oil price alone.

The useful vault frame: Benigno is not only tracking whether oil/gas prices rise. He is tracking whether central banks misread a supply-chain shock by applying a linear, commodity-price-only reaction function. That matters directly for the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]], where refined-product, shipping, and inventory-buffer channels can persist after front-month crude prices stabilize.
