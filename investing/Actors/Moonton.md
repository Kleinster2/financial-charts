Moonton — Shanghai-based game studio, creator of [[Mobile Legends|Mobile Legends: Bang Bang]] (110M MAU, dominant mobile MOBA in Southeast Asia). Founded 2014 by Yuan Jing. Acquired by [[ByteDance]] in March 2021 for ~$4B, then sold to [[Savvy Games Group]] in March 2026 for $6B as ByteDance retreated from gaming after failing to challenge [[Tencent]].

## Ownership history

| Period | Owner | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 2014-2021 | Independent | Founded by Yuan Jing |
| 2021-2026 | [[ByteDance]] | Acquired for ~$4B via Nuverse (ByteDance's gaming unit). Operated independently |
| 2026- | [[Savvy Games Group]] | Acquired for $6B. Management retained, employees offered incentive programs |

[[ByteDance]] acquired Moonton through its gaming unit Nuverse as part of a broader push to challenge [[Tencent]] in gaming. After spending billions on 12+ studios and failing to gain meaningful share against Tencent, ByteDance began cutting gaming jobs in late 2023 and pivoted to divesting studios. Moonton was the crown jewel — the only ByteDance gaming asset with genuine scale.

The $6B sale price gives ByteDance ~$2B return on its $4B investment. FT reported "advanced talks" on Feb 13, 2026; Bloomberg confirmed the agreement on March 20, 2026. [[Savvy Games Group]] will leave management in place.

## Mobile Legends: Bang Bang

[[Mobile Legends|Mobile Legends: Bang Bang]] (MLBB) is a free-to-play multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game, launched 2016. Particularly dominant in Southeast Asian and Latin American esports:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| MAU | ~110M |
| Total downloads | 1.5B+ |
| China mainland launch | 2025 (10 years after studio founding) |
| Esports viewing (2021) | 386M hours (ranked #1 globally in mobile esports) |
| Key markets | Southeast Asia, Latin America |

The game launched in mainland China only in 2025 — a decade after the studio was founded — suggesting regulatory or strategic delays. Its core audience remains Southeast Asian, where it is the dominant mobile MOBA.

## Geopolitical angle

The Moonton deal illustrates how US-China tensions create dealmaking opportunities for Gulf buyers. [[ByteDance]] could not easily sell a Chinese gaming studio to a US acquirer given the political environment around [[TikTok]] and Chinese tech more broadly. [[Savvy Games Group]] CEO [[Brian Ward]] (FT, 2025): geopolitical tensions between the US and China "created an opportunity" for Saudi dealmaking in China. Savvy's prior China deal — $265M in [[VSPO]] ([[Hero Esports]], 2023) — established the cross-border relationship.
