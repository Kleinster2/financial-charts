## Views

### Global trade resilience (Apr 1 2026)

Column citing [[McKinsey]] Global Institute report (*Geopolitics and the geometry of global trade: 2026 update*). Key arguments:

- 2025 was the year global trade "definitely did not die" despite [[Trump II]] tariffs
- World trade in goods grew faster than the world economy
- AI-related shipments were the most powerful engine of trade growth (+40% in [[Semiconductors]] and data center equipment, one-third of global trade growth)
- [[China]] expanded as "factory to the factories" — intermediate/capital goods exports +$223B, more than offsetting -$130B in lost US sales
- Direct [[US-China trade]] fell ~30% but US replaced ~2/3 from other exporters
- Trump's bark was worse than his bite; no retaliation cycle materialized
- Cited [[Richard Baldwin]] (IMD): US at 14% of world goods imports "does not matter all that much"
- The US is "no longer a credible world leader" — provides "an unpredictable wrecking ball" rather than reliable hegemony
- [[China]] more predictable but unable to eliminate reliance on large trade surpluses
- Questions whether [[Iran conflict oil price timeline|the Iran war]] and Gulf closure will surpass tariff war damage; suspects Trump will claim victory and end it

Also cited in [[Kenneth Rogoff]] note (endorsement of *Our Dollar Your Problem*).
