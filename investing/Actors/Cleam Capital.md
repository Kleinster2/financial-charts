Cleam Capital — a Brazilian multi-family office based in Barueri, São Paulo, run by the Lacet family, which acquires companies and works to improve their performance with a long-term, buy-and-hold horizon. The name is built from "Cruz Lacet & Akiko Momoi" (CL&AM). It executes buyouts, growth investments and secondaries across private equity, real estate, infrastructure and distressed situations, with stated interest in supply chain and logistics, fintech, media and entertainment, energy transition, proptech, climate tech and agri/foodtech. Its portfolio runs from logistics-monitoring ([[Omnilink]], [[CEABS]]) through retail automation ([[Casa Magalhães]]) to, since early May 2026, a 75% controlling stake in frozen-vegetables leader [[Grano Alimentos]].

## Why it matters

- Patient capital, not a fund clock: unlike a private-equity fund with a fixed exit horizon (the model [[Arlon Group]] ran on at [[Grano Alimentos]]), Cleam is family money buying to hold and improve. That is the structural read on the Grano handover — a consolidator with no forced exit date taking a market-leading consumer-staples asset off a fund.
- An assembled operating portfolio: the holdings cluster around asset-tracking/telemetry ([[Omnilink]], [[CEABS]]) and retail/logistics technology ([[Casa Magalhães]]), with [[Grano Alimentos]] adding a branded food platform. The thesis is operational improvement of mid-market Brazilian businesses bought from larger sellers (Pátria, Bradesco-led JV, Grupo Boticário, Arlon).
- Family-team structure: day-to-day investment and operating decisions sit with the Lacet family rather than an external GP — relevant to how it will steward Grano's R$300M → R$1B growth plan.

## Funding history

Privately held by the Lacet family; structured as a multi-family office (CL&AM = Cruz Lacet & Akiko Momoi). No outside funding rounds — capital is family/principal money deployed into control acquisitions; an acquisition vehicle rather than a fund with external LPs on the public record.
