**FTI Consulting** (NYSE: FCN) is a global business advisory firm with ~$3.5B revenue, dominating the restructuring and litigation consulting niches that thrive in periods of financial distress and regulatory complexity.

## Synopsis

FTI's investment thesis is countercyclical with a twist: the restructuring segment (Corporate Finance & Restructuring, ~30% of revenue) surges during credit downturns, while the other four segments — Forensic & Litigation Consulting, Economic Consulting, Technology (e-discovery), and Strategic Communications — provide a baseline of demand driven by regulation, litigation, and corporate crises that are cycle-agnostic. The company's restructuring practice is consistently ranked #1 by deal count, competing with [[AlixPartners]], [[Alvarez & Marsal]], and [[Houlihan Lokey]] for the largest mandates. FTI professionals regularly serve as court-appointed monitors, interim managers, and expert witnesses — giving the firm a recurring role in high-profile corporate collapses, fraud investigations, and government enforcement actions.

The stock has compounded at ~15% annually over the past decade, outperforming most professional services peers, driven by headcount growth and a steady increase in average bill rates. The risk is that restructuring revenue is lumpy — the segment can swing 20-30% year over year depending on the credit cycle — and the consulting model depends entirely on senior talent retention (key managing directors leave, clients follow). FTI has also faced criticism for its strategic communications segment, which advises governments and corporations on reputation management — sometimes on behalf of controversial clients. At ~$8B market cap and ~20x forward earnings, the stock prices in continued execution but offers genuine defensive characteristics in a downturn scenario.
