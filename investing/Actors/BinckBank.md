**BinckBank** was the largest online broker in the Netherlands, offering self-directed investing to retail clients across the Benelux and France. Acquired by [[Saxo Bank]] in August 2019 for EUR 424M and legally merged into Saxo Bank A/S on July 1, 2024, ending the BinckBank brand.

## Synopsis

BinckBank was founded in 2000 during the dot-com retail trading boom and grew to dominate Dutch online brokerage with over 600,000 client accounts and EUR 30B+ in assets under administration at its peak. The platform offered stocks, options, futures, and ETFs to self-directed investors — a straightforward execution-focused model. BinckBank was listed on [[Euronext Amsterdam]] and was a constituent of the Dutch mid-cap AMX index.

The acquisition by Denmark's [[Saxo Bank]] in 2019 was part of Saxo's European consolidation strategy — buying established retail brokerages to scale its multi-asset trading platform across jurisdictions. The EUR 424M deal valued BinckBank at roughly 2x book value, reflecting the premium for a sticky retail client base in a market where switching costs are real (tax-advantaged accounts, transfer frictions). The integration took five years: BinckBank clients were migrated to the Saxo platform in stages, and the legal merger finalized on July 1, 2024. For the Dutch retail brokerage market, BinckBank's disappearance leaves [[DeGiro]] (now owned by [[flatexDEGIRO]]) and Saxo as the primary platforms, alongside new entrants like [[Interactive Brokers]] and [[Trade Republic]] pushing zero-commission models.
