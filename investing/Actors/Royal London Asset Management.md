# Royal London Asset Management

The asset management arm of [[Royal London Group]], the [[UK]]'s largest mutual life insurance and pensions company. RLAM manages assets on behalf of Royal London's policyholders (the mutual's with-profits funds and pension schemes) plus third-party institutional and wholesale clients. As of its 2024 annual report, RLAM managed approximately £170B, making it one of the ten largest active asset managers in Britain. The mutual structure is a distinguishing feature — Royal London has no external shareholders, so RLAM's long-term investment horizon is genuinely aligned with policyholder outcomes rather than quarterly parent earnings.

## Synopsis

RLAM has built visibility through its multi-asset and fixed-income franchises. The flagship multi-asset vehicles — including the Governed Portfolios and the Global Multi Asset Portfolios — are run by a team led by [[Trevor Greetham]], the firm's head of multi-asset and a widely quoted tactical allocator best known for the "Investment Clock" framework (equities/bonds/commodities/cash rotation around the growth-inflation business cycle). Greetham's commentary regularly appears in [[FT]], [[Bloomberg]], and Reuters as a counterweight to consensus positioning — he tends to be early in calling regime shifts and is willing to run concentrated off-consensus bets.

The firm appears in vault coverage through two 2026 events. First, during the early-March Iran war position-squeeze, Greetham was quoted in the March 4 [[FT]] piece: "What we've seen is basically a reversal of the trades that were working" — selling Japanese and emerging-market stocks as the popular 2026 trades unwound. Second, in early April, RLAM was named alongside [[Schroders]], [[M&G]], [[JPMorgan]], and [[Aviva Investors]] as one of the major UK asset managers adding to government bonds on the view that the market over-priced inflation and under-priced the growth hit from the oil shock (see [[Iran conflict portfolio hedging#April update — growth repricing begins]]).
