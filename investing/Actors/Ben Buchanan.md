**Ben Buchanan** is a former White House AI policy adviser under [[Joe Biden]] who joined [[Anthropic]] after leaving government, becoming a flashpoint in the political debate over AI companies' partisan affiliations during the Trump administration's Pentagon-Anthropic standoff in early 2026.

## Synopsis

Buchanan served as Special Adviser for AI on the [[White House]] National Security Council, where he helped shape the Biden administration's executive orders on AI safety, export controls on advanced chips to [[China]], and the framework for government adoption of frontier AI models. His academic background is in cybersecurity and technology policy (Georgetown University), and he authored *The Hacker and the State* — a well-regarded book on cyber conflict that established his credentials in the national security community before he moved into AI policy.

The vault relevance is political risk, not policy substance. When [[Anthropic]] faced pushback from the Trump Pentagon over a proposed defense contract in February 2026, [[1789 Capital]] and allied conservative critics cited Buchanan's hire as evidence that Anthropic was ideologically aligned with the Democratic Party and the Biden-era AI safety establishment. The argument was that a company employing the former Biden AI adviser could not be trusted with sensitive defense work — a guilt-by-association attack that nonetheless had real consequences for Anthropic's government business pipeline. The episode illustrates how the revolving door between government and AI companies has become politicized in ways that directly affect contract awards, regulatory treatment, and investor perception of policy risk.
