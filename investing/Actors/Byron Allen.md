**Byron Allen** is a comedian-turned-media-mogul, founder and CEO of [[Allen Media Group]], known for serial high-profile bids for major media assets. In March 2026 he acquired a 10.7% stake in [[Starz]] and has since declared his intent to take control of the company.

## Background

Allen began as a stand-up comedian, breaking through on The Tonight Show and co-hosting NBC's *Real People* (1979–1984). In 1993 he founded Entertainment Studios — now [[Allen Media Group]] — building it into a TV production, broadcasting, and digital media company valued at over $4.5B by 2022.

He is known for aggressive, headline-grabbing acquisition bids, many unconsummated: ~$3B for Paramount's BET Media Group (2023), a $10B offer for ABC and Disney TV stations (2023), and earlier interest in Paramount itself. In May 2026 he agreed to buy a controlling stake in BuzzFeed for $120M, taking over as chairman and CEO.

## The Starz campaign

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 2026 | Buys 10.7% of [[Starz]] from [[Steven Mnuchin]]'s fund — ~$25M at $13.86/share |
| March 2026 | [[Starz]] board responds with a poison pill (17.5% trigger) |
| May 2026 | States "I want to control Starz"; will make a full offer if blocked |

Allen's strategic logic is to pair [[Starz]]'s subscription streaming (SVOD) with the free ad-supported (AVOD) ecosystem at [[Allen Media Group]] — "a left hook and a right hook." No formal offer price has been made. His track record of bids that don't close is the central question mark over whether the campaign converts to a deal. See [[Starz]] → The takeover situation.
