**International Olympic Committee** (IOC) — non-governmental sports organization founded 1894, headquartered in Lausanne, Switzerland. Oversees the modern Olympic Games and constitutes the apex of the international amateur-sports governance structure. Membership of approximately 100 individual members representing the IOC in their respective countries. Has a current annual budget in the low billions, primarily funded by broadcasting rights (Olympic Games), top-tier sponsorship deals, and ticket revenues during Games periods. Recognizes 206 National Olympic Committees and approximately 50 International Federations. President: Thomas Bach (term ending 2025); successor incoming. Coordinates closely with the [[World Anti-Doping Agency|WADA]] on the [[World Anti-Doping Code]]. Public position on the [[Enhanced Games]]: condemned as antithetical to the Olympic values; the IOC Athletes' Commission joined WADA in calling the event "irresponsible and immoral."
