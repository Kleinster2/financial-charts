**Alex Epstein** — fossil fuel advocate, author, and founder of the Center for Industrial Progress (CIP), a for-profit think tank based in San Diego. Author of *The Moral Case for Fossil Fuels* (2014, NYT bestseller) and *Fossil Future* (2022). Former Ayn Rand Institute fellow and Cato Institute adjunct scholar. Has become one of the most politically influential energy voices in the Trump era — directly shaped the energy provisions of the 2025 "Big Beautiful Bill" and testified before both the House Oversight and House Budget committees on US electricity policy.

## Background

Grew up in Chevy Chase, Maryland. At Duke, published The Duke Review for two years. Spent seven years at the Ayn Rand Institute writing and lecturing before founding CIP in 2011.

His core argument: fossil fuels have been humanity's greatest tool for improving living standards, and the push to eliminate them is based on flawed moral reasoning. He accepts that climate change is largely human-caused but argues fossil fuels' benefits (cheap energy, climate adaptation via heating/cooling, industrialization) far outweigh the costs. "Cold weather kills 20 times as many people as hot weather" is a signature talking point.

CIP is a for-profit entity — not a 501(c)(3) nonprofit — and does not disclose its funding. Clients have included the Kentucky Coal Association, ExxonMobil, Chevron, and Phillips 66 (per DeSmog reporting). Rolling Stone listed Epstein on its "top global warming deniers" list in 2013; Epstein rejects the "denier" label, arguing he accepts climate science but disagrees on policy responses.

## Political influence

### Trump era (2025-2026)

Epstein's influence peaked during the drafting of the 2025 reconciliation bill ("Big Beautiful Bill"). Per DeSmog and E&E News reporting:

- Directly consulted with Republican members including Rep. Chip Roy (R-TX) and Rep. Josh Brecheen (R-OK) on the bill's energy provisions
- Both lawmakers publicly thanked him on his podcast for help crafting the legislation
- Held meetings with the Doug Burgum (Energy Secretary) orbit — recorded a "secret meeting" with Sen. Thom Tillis (R-NC)
- Tillis later denounced the bill's energy provisions on the Senate floor, calling out "think tanks and people that haven't worked a day in business" setting policy — widely understood as a reference to Epstein (Tillis called him "Alex Weinstein")
- Epstein published a Substack rebuttal revealing he had recorded his own comments during the Tillis meeting

The bill proposed sweeping cuts to clean energy programs. NABTU (3M+ construction workers) called it "the biggest job-killing bill in the history of this country... the equivalent of terminating more than 1,000 Keystone XL pipeline projects." Critics dubbed it the "Big Blackout Bill" over grid reliability concerns.

### Congressional testimony

- Testified before Senate Environment and Public Works Committee on fossil fuel policy
- Testified before House Oversight Committee: "How to Solve America's Electricity Crisis" (Feb 2025)
- Testified before House Budget Committee on energy provisions of the reconciliation bill

## Why Epstein matters for investors

Epstein is the intellectual architect behind much of the current US administration's energy deregulation push. His influence affects:

- **Clean energy policy**: IRA clean energy tax credit repeal/modification, renewable energy mandates
- **[[Sustainable Aviation Fuel]]**: SAF subsidies and mandates could be affected by anti-renewables legislation
- **Natural gas/LNG**: Epstein argues for maximum fossil fuel production, supportive of LNG export expansion
- **Grid policy**: His "electricity crisis" framing drives policy toward natural gas baseload over renewables
- **Carbon pricing**: Fundamentally opposed to any carbon pricing mechanism

His views on the grid are market-relevant now: the "Big Beautiful Bill" energy provisions, if enacted, would significantly shift US energy investment incentives away from renewables toward fossil fuel infrastructure.
