## Masimo acquisition (February 2026)

On February 17, 2026, Danaher announced a $9.9B all-cash acquisition of [[Masimo]] at $180/share — a 40% premium to the $130.15 pre-announcement close. This is Danaher's largest deal since the $21.4B [[Cytiva]] takeover (2019) and follows the $5.7B [[Abcam]] acquisition (2024).

[[Masimo]] makes SET pulse oximeters and patient monitoring systems. The deal adds acute care monitoring to Danaher's Diagnostics segment, where Masimo will operate as a standalone unit. Danaher expects $125M annual cost synergies and $50M revenue synergies by year 5. Close expected H2 2026.

DHR fell 2.9% on announcement day ($212.58 → $206.36) and has continued lower to $190.00 (March 23), down ~10.6% since pre-deal. Funded via cash on hand and debt financing.

Advisers: [[Citigroup]] and Kirkland & Ellis (Danaher); [[Centerview Partners]] and [[Morgan Stanley]], Sullivan & Cromwell and White & Case (Masimo).

## Q1 2026 earnings — revenue miss, bioprocessing offsets respiratory drag (Apr 21, 2026)

Mixed print: revenue missed consensus by ~$50M while EPS beat by $0.12. Stock fell 5.4% on Apr 22 — the largest single-day drop in months — as the revenue miss combined with a tepid guide raise suggested the [[Cytiva]] bioprocessing recovery is real but that Diagnostics is dragging in the near term.

Segment dynamics:

- [[Bioprocessing]] (via [[Cytiva]]) strong. Bioprocessing orders and revenue both accelerated versus Q4 2025, confirming the recovery thesis that the inventory destocking cycle at biotech customers is behind. This is the most important structural positive for DHR — bioprocessing is ~40% of profit, and the 2023-24 destocking cycle had been the primary multiple-compression driver.
- Life Sciences better than expected. Biopharma tools and consumables better than seasonal, with new-product uptake from 2025 launches contributing.
- Diagnostics weaker. Respiratory diagnostics (flu, RSV, COVID multiplex testing) posted a lighter-than-normal respiratory season — the -2.5 point drag on core revenue. Non-respiratory diagnostics flat-to-up.

Guidance — small raise, not a catalyst. FY26 adjusted diluted EPS guidance raised to $8.35-$8.55 from $8.35-$8.50 — a $0.025 midpoint raise that reads as "in-line EPS, lower revenue," not a clean beat-and-raise. Management did not raise FY26 core revenue growth guidance, implying the better bioprocessing is offsetting respiratory and modest diagnostics softness, not augmenting it.

Why the stock fell despite EPS beat. Investors reading the print as: (1) respiratory season drag is a one-time hit, not reason for structural concern; (2) but the magnitude of the Cytiva recovery is not yet large enough to lift full-year growth above the long-term 3-5% core target; (3) meanwhile [[Masimo]] integration risk (closing H2 2026) is an unquantified variable. The stock's 5.4% drop is a re-derating on growth, not a major thesis change — but it reflects the market's impatience for the bioprocessing cycle to translate into faster top-line acceleration.

Masimo deal still on track for H2 2026 close (no new disclosure in the call beyond prior timelines).
