D-ID is an AI video avatar platform headquartered in Tel Aviv, enabling enterprises to generate synthetic talking-head videos from still images and text. The company has produced over 200M videos, with 280K+ developers on its API, positioning it as the developer-first alternative in a category dominated by [[Synthesia]] and [[HeyGen]].

## Synopsis

Founded in 2017 with face de-identification technology (hence "D-ID"), the company pivoted to generative AI avatars as the commercial opportunity in synthetic media eclipsed the privacy use case. With $48M in total funding from [[Pitango]], [[Macquarie Group]], and [[AXA]] Venture Partners, D-ID operates at a fraction of the capital intensity of competitors like [[Synthesia]] ($90M Series C at $2.1B valuation). The developer-first API strategy differentiates — rather than selling a turnkey studio product, D-ID embeds avatar generation into enterprise workflows for clients including [[PepsiCo]], [[Fidelity]], [[JP Morgan]], and [[SoftBank]].

The September 2025 acquisition of Simpleshow, a German explainer video company, added a content layer on top of D-ID's avatar engine — pairing script-to-video automation with synthetic presenters. The strategic bet is that AI avatars become infrastructure for enterprise communications (training, marketing, customer service) rather than a standalone product. The risk is commoditization: as open-source models improve at lip-sync and face generation, the moat shifts from technology to distribution and enterprise relationships.
