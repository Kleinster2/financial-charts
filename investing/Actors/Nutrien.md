# Nutrien

World's largest crop input company by revenue, formed from the 2018 merger of [[PotashCorp]] and [[Agrium]]. Produces all three primary crop nutrients (potash, nitrogen, phosphate) and operates ~2,000 retail locations across North America, South America, and Australia. Stock up ~20% since January 2026 on the [[Strait of Hormuz]] fertilizer supply disruption, as one of the few vertically integrated nitrogen producers outside the Gulf chokepoint. Senator [[Josh Hawley]] included Nutrien in a March 2026 price-gouging inquiry alongside [[CF Industries]], [[Mosaic]], [[Koch Fertilizer]], and [[Yara]].

## Hormuz exposure (2026)

Nutrien benefits from the [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure through the same mechanism as [[CF Industries]]: North American gas-fed nitrogen production becomes the supply of last resort when Gulf exports are trapped behind the chokepoint. Unlike CF (pure-play nitrogen), Nutrien is diversified across all three nutrient types plus retail distribution, giving it both production upside and demand visibility.

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Nitrogen production | North American gas feedstock |
| Potash capacity | World's largest potash producer |
| Retail network | Direct farmer relationships = real-time demand intelligence |
| Integrated model | Production + distribution = margin capture at both levels |

Stock up ~20% since January 2026, lagging [[CF Industries]] (+77% at peak) because Nutrien's diversification dilutes the nitrogen-specific upside. But the retail network provides a different edge: visibility into planting-season demand and farmer purchasing decisions that pure producers lack.

Nicholas Crown (@nicholas_crown, TikTok, April 9 2026) highlighted Nutrien alongside CF Industries as a primary beneficiary of the [[Nitrogen Trap]] — the structural vulnerability of global food production to Hormuz-concentrated fertilizer supply.

*Source: Nicholas Crown (@nicholas_crown) TikTok (April 9, 2026), Bloomberg, ainvest.com*
