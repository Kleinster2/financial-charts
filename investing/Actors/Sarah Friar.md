#actor #executive #person

Sarah Friar — CFO of [[OpenAI]] since June 2024. Joined from [[Nextdoor]] (CEO 2018-2024); previously CFO of [[Square]] (2012-2018) under [[Jack Dorsey]]. Earlier roles include [[Salesforce]] (SVP) and [[Goldman Sachs]] (managing director, internet research). Northern Irish, Oxford- and Stanford-trained engineer-turned-finance executive. Board director of [[Walmart]], [[Slack]] (until acquisition), [[Consensys]].

## Why Sarah Friar matters

The first chief financial officer at [[OpenAI]] since the company's transition to a $20B+ revenue scale and the start of the [[Project Stargate|Stargate]] / [[OpenAI Infrastructure Spend|infrastructure capex]] decade. The job sits at the intersection of two unprecedented private-company tasks: financing $600B+ of compute commitments before profitability and managing a path to public-market disclosure that has investors (per [[Jensen Huang]]) expecting an IPO this year. Her public commentary tends to bridge the [[Sam Altman]] strategic narrative and the more conservative numbers expected of a public-company CFO.

---

## Apr 28, 2026 — WSJ revenue miss attribution

The [[Wall Street Journal]]'s April 28, 2026 report that [[OpenAI]] missed internal revenue and weekly-active-user targets cited Friar's internal concerns about whether the company can fund its committed compute contracts if revenue growth does not accelerate. The CFO concern surfacing in internal documents reaching reporters — rather than in public earnings guidance — was the most load-bearing detail in the WSJ story and the proximate driver of the subsequent sell-off across [[Oracle]] (-4.05%), [[CoreWeave]] (-5.83%), [[SoftBank]] (-10% Tokyo), and the broader chip stack ([[AMD]], [[Broadcom]], [[NVIDIA]], [[Marvell]]). See [[2026 OpenAI revenue miss]] for the full event context.

The framing matters because [[OpenAI]]'s official posture has been "buy as much compute as we can." Friar's reported concern reframes that posture from spending willingness to spending solvency over multi-year contracts.
