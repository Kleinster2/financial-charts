## Q1 2026 earnings — record $63B backlog (+79% YoY) (Apr 30, 2026)

CAT reported Q1 2026 before the open on Apr 30. Sales $17.4B (+22%) vs $14.2B prior-year, adj EPS $5.54 vs $4.62 consensus (~$0.92 beat). The headline number is $63B backlog (+79% YoY) — up from the prior $39.9B record cited above. All three segments contributed.

| Segment | Q1 2026 | YoY |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Construction Industries | $7.2B sales | +38% (segment profit +50% to $1.54B) |
| Resource Industries | — | — |
| Energy & Transportation | $7.0B sales | +22% (segment profit +13% to $1.45B) |

Q1 tariff cost $600M came in well below the $800M January estimate. Management raised long-term targets on the call. Analyst response: [[Morgan Stanley]] upgraded from Underweight to Equal Weight, target $430 → $915 citing "record backlog and hyperscaler capex growth"; [[JPMorgan]] raised target to $1,125 from $860, kept Overweight, called the quarter a "resounding" beat.

Stock: CAT closed Apr 30 at $889.67 (+10%) and held the gain into May 1 — fresh 52-week high near $905. The backlog jump to $63B is the cleanest read on hyperscaler capex flowing into the gas-turbine + power-generation supply chain. Each $1 of incremental [[Microsoft]] / [[Alphabet]] / [[Amazon]] / [[Meta]] capex on a [[Hyperscaler capex|>$650B combined 2026 guide]] flows into CAT's E&T order book over a multi-quarter lag — the 79% backlog growth is now formalizing what the prior six declining-revenue quarters were under-pricing.

The CAT print + [[nVent Electric#Q1 2026 earnings|nVent's $2.6B AI-DC backlog]] + [[Generac]]'s C&I/data-center acceleration (Apr 29) form a three-way confirmation that AI-DC capex is in the ratable phase, not a one-quarter lump. The downstream-impact watch list for [[Vertiv]], [[Eaton]], [[Schneider Electric]], [[Siemens Energy]], [[Cummins]] needs to be re-checked for whether each name has captured this print's read-through.
