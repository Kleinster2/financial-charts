**Techtronic Industries** — Hong Kong-based global power tools and floor care conglomerate. Owns the Milwaukee Tool, Ryobi (power tools license), and Hoover brands. One of the largest holdings in [[EWH]] at roughly 4.5% weight.

---

## Synopsis

TTI is one of the great under-the-radar industrial stories: a Hong Kong-listed company that dominates the North American professional power tools market through its Milwaukee brand. Milwaukee has taken share relentlessly from [[Stanley Black & Decker]]'s DeWalt franchise by leading the cordless/battery-platform transition — professional tradespeople who invest in one Milwaukee battery platform buy all their tools within it, creating a razor-and-blade lock-in. TTI's revenue exceeds $13B, with power tools (primarily Milwaukee) accounting for over 80% and generating operating margins in the high teens, exceptional for the category.

The company's listing in Hong Kong creates a persistent valuation anomaly. TTI generates roughly 75% of revenue in North America, yet trades at a significant discount to what it would command on the NYSE — Stanley Black & Decker and other US-listed tool peers historically trade at higher multiples. The Horst Julius Pudwill family controls TTI through a significant equity stake. For investors in [[EWH]], TTI is one of the few holdings with genuine global revenue diversification rather than pure Hong Kong/China exposure, making it an accidental quality holding in what is otherwise a concentrated bet on the HK economy. The Ryobi brand (licensed, not owned) serves the consumer/DIY market and provides volume, while Milwaukee drives margin expansion.
