# Firework

> Disambiguation — Firework (this note) is Vincent Yang's video-commerce infrastructure company. It is a different entity from [[Fireworks AI]], which is Lin Qiao's AI inference cloud built by the ex-[[Meta]] PyTorch team. Same root word, separate companies, separate categories.

Firework provides AI video infrastructure for ecommerce websites — generates and serves product videos at scale for retailers. Per the FT (Eleanor Olcott, May 17 2026), Vincent Yang (CEO) cited a single retailer commissioning 100,000 AI-generated videos for product pages — an economically impossible volume pre-AI, now a standard request as model quality has crossed the brand-acceptable threshold.

## Why it matters per the FT

Vincent Yang's framing captures the production-utility moment for AI video:

> *"Before, the videos were cringey and robotic — they couldn't meet brand standards. Now we're at the point where you can't tell if it's AI or human."*

> *"One retailer asked us to create 100,000 videos for its product pages. Without AI, that would have been prohibitively expensive. Now, every product can have its own video, and even multiple versions tailored to different customers."*

This is the demand-side flip the [[AI Video Generation]] concept hub anchors on: model quality has crossed the threshold where ecommerce + advertising customers will commission video at volumes that were uneconomical pre-AI. Firework is the infrastructure layer letting retailers consume that capability without managing model selection, prompt engineering, or rendering capacity directly.

The 100,000-video example is the cleanest single demand-scale data point in the FT piece — and a useful frame for the [[Seedance]] / [[Kling]] / [[Hailuo]] revenue ramp going forward. Enterprise video-API usage is structurally a 1000x larger market than consumer experimentation.

*Source: [FT](https://www.ft.com/content/9804b1de-653b-40b2-bffb-17c76ebebe34), May 17 2026.*
