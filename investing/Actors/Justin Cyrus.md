## Q1 2026 — beachhead market 10x framing

In the Apr 2026 [[Space Capital]] interview, Cyrus articulated the scope shift: "Our beachhead market 10x'd over the course of the last two weeks. We're building a moon base. It's going to be built out over the course of the next 5 years. And what Lunar Outpost is doing is providing the robotic and mobility systems that are going to make that a reality."

On the structural transition from exploration to construction: "You're going from bringing 50 to 100 kilograms down to now bringing tons of surface mass down, which enables an entirely new lunar economy. You're going from exploration and science missions to now actually building a moon base. And that's a fundamental step change of what we've done over the course of the last 20 years."

On scope estimation: "We were estimating about last year a $3 billion Artemis base camp is what it used to be called to a $30 billion major infrastructure activity over the course of the next 5 years to build the first permanent outpost on another planetary body. And that's only one program within NASA."

See [[Lunar Outpost]] for company position and [[Artemis]] for the program-level architecture restructure.
