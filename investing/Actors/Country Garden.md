# Country Garden

Chinese property developer, the largest by contracted sales 2017-2022, founded 1992 in Guangdong by Yeung Kwok Keung (Yang Guoqiang). Listed on Hong Kong (2007.HK) since 2007. Country Garden defaulted on offshore dollar bonds in October 2023, becoming the second major casualty of the [[Three Red Lines]] property correction after [[Evergrande]] — and structurally more important because it was the largest sales-volume developer with the broadest exposure to lower-tier-city markets. Restructuring proceedings ongoing through 2026.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. [[Three Red Lines]] second-wave default — the canonical case of a developer that initially appeared less leveraged than [[Evergrande]] but ultimately couldn't survive the policy + macro combination. The October 2023 default reset analyst expectations about which Chinese developers could clear the policy filter
2. Lower-tier-city exposure — Country Garden's concentration in lower-tier cities (Tier 3 and below) is the cleanest data point on how the property correction has played out in non-metropolitan China. Tier-1 city property has held up much better than Country Garden's footprint
3. Founder-family governance — [[Yang Huiyan]]'s position as chair (after father's 2018 succession) is the highest-profile example of family-business governance in the Chinese property sector. The restructuring will test whether founder-family control survives or is replaced by state-aligned management
