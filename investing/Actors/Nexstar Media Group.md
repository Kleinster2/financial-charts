Nexstar Media Group — largest local television station owner in the United States. With the [[FCC]]-approved acquisition of [[Tegna]], Nexstar will operate 265 television stations across 44 states and Washington, D.C., most of which are local affiliates of [[ABC]], [[CBS]], [[Fox Corporation|Fox]], and [[NBC]].

## Nexstar-Tegna acquisition

On March 19, 2026, the [[FCC]] approved Nexstar's $3.54B acquisition of [[Tegna]], creating the largest local TV operator in the U.S. The [[FCC]] waived its rule capping any single owner from broadcasting to more than 39% of U.S. households to allow the deal. Nexstar agreed to divest six stations as a condition of approval.

The deal faces legal challenges: eight Democratic-led states filed suit in U.S. District Court in Sacramento to block the merger on March 18, 2026 (the day before FCC approval). [[DirecTV]] filed a separate lawsuit seeking to block the deal the same day.
