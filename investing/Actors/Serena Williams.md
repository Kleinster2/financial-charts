# Serena Williams

**Serena Williams** is a tennis champion turned venture investor, running Serena Ventures, a fund that has deployed across consumer, health, and AI startups. She is part of the athlete-to-VC pipeline alongside Kevin Durant, LeBron James, and others who are converting cultural capital into financial capital.

## Synopsis

Serena Ventures launched in 2014 but accelerated meaningfully after Williams's retirement from professional tennis in 2022, allowing her to shift from passive celebrity investor to active fund manager. The firm has invested in 90+ companies across consumer brands, health tech, and enterprise software, with a stated focus on diverse founders (the fund reports that 78% of portfolio companies have a diverse founder). Notable investments include [[MasterClass]], [[Noom]], and several AI health startups. The fund raised a $111M debut institutional vehicle in 2022, signaling a transition from personal capital deployment to institutional-grade fund management.

The broader pattern Serena represents — athletes and celebrities converting brand equity into investment returns — is a growing feature of the VC landscape. What distinguishes the successful versions (Serena, Kevin Durant's Thirty Five Ventures, [[Ashton Kutcher]]'s Sound Ventures) from the vanity vehicles is whether the celebrity adds genuine deal flow advantages (founders want them on the cap table for marketing and credibility) and whether the fund has professional investment staff. Serena Ventures has a professional team and has produced early markups, though the real test of returns comes with exits. Her AI health investments align with the intersection of wellness culture, wearable data, and LLM-powered diagnostics — a vertical where celebrity endorsement and consumer trust are genuine competitive advantages.
