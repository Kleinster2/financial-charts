**Carlos Slim** is a Mexican billionaire (~$99B net worth, 2025), the richest person in Latin America, who built a conglomerate empire centered on [[América Móvil]] — the region's dominant telecom operator — and Grupo Carso, a diversified industrial holding company.

## Synopsis

Slim's fortune traces to a single, perfectly timed bet: the 1990 privatization of Telmex (Mexico's state telephone monopoly), which he acquired for $1.76B in consortium with [[France Telecom]] and Southwestern Bell. The deal gave Slim control of Mexico's entire fixed-line infrastructure at a moment when telecommunications was about to become the most valuable industry in the emerging world. Telmex's mobile spinoff became [[América Móvil]], which Slim expanded aggressively across Latin America through acquisitions — by 2010, América Móvil operated in 18 countries with 250M+ subscribers, and Slim briefly became the world's richest person (surpassing [[Bill Gates]] and [[Warren Buffett]]). He retains 50-60% voting control of América Móvil, making it effectively a family-controlled company despite its $50B+ market cap.

Beyond telecom, Slim's Grupo Carso spans construction (Cicsa, which builds infrastructure for Pemex and the Mexican government), retail (Sanborns), real estate, and financial services (Grupo Financiero Inbursa). The conglomerate structure resembles a classic Latin American business group — diversified, family-controlled, with deep government relationships. Slim's investment approach is value-oriented and counter-cyclical: he bought a 6.4% stake in the [[New York Times]] in 2015 when the media company was in crisis, later converting warrants at significant profit. His fortune is overwhelmingly tied to Mexico's economic trajectory — América Móvil derives ~70% of revenue from Latin America, with Mexico the single largest market. For investors, Slim functions as a barometer of Mexican economic sentiment: his companies' stock performance, his investment decisions, and his (rare) public comments on policy all carry signal about Mexico's business environment and the government's relationship with the private sector.
