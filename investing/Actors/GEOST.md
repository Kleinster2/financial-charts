## Why it matters

GEOST is the payload piece in Rocket Lab's transition from launch-and-bus supplier to national-security space prime. Before the acquisition, Rocket Lab could build spacecraft and components but still needed external mission payloads for the highest-value defense work. GEOST brought advanced EO/IR sensing in-house, closing the gap between bus manufacturing, payload integration, and mission operations.

The first clean validation came with the May 2026 [[US Space Force|Space Systems Command]] award: Rocket Lab won a $90M contract to deliver two geostationary satellites carrying the Heimdall space-domain-awareness payload. Heimdall originated as a GEOST prototype payload program and then transitioned, under Rocket Lab ownership, into operational vehicle delivery. That makes GEOST more than a purchased capability; it is already feeding funded prime-contract work.

---

## Rocket Lab integration

Rocket Lab acquired GEOST from LightRidge Solutions in a 2025 deal with $125M cash, $150M in Rocket Lab stock, and up to $50M in earnout payments. The acquisition added more than 100 employees and facilities in Tucson and northern Virginia, then became Rocket Lab Optical Systems.

In February 2026 Rocket Lab also acquired Optical Support, Inc., a GEOST supplier of high-precision optical and optomechanical instruments. That follow-on acquisition tightened the same supply chain: GEOST brought sensor payload design; OSI brought lenses, optomechanics, machining, cleanroom assembly, and testing.
