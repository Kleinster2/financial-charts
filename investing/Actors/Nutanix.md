# Nutanix

Hybrid multicloud infrastructure software company that pioneered hyperconverged infrastructure (HCI), collapsing compute, storage, and networking into a single software-defined stack. Listed on [[NASDAQ]] (NTNX).

## Synopsis

Nutanix was founded in 2009 by former [[Google]] engineers who saw that enterprise data centers were running the same distributed systems problems Google had solved internally -- fragmented storage, manual provisioning, vendor lock-in. Their HCI platform lets enterprises run workloads across on-premises, private cloud, and public cloud (AWS, Azure, GCP) from a single management plane. The company went public in 2016 at a ~$5B valuation and spent years burning cash on a painful transition from hardware-bundled appliance sales to a pure software subscription model, which compressed revenue growth but improved unit economics.

The current setup is interesting. Nutanix completed its subscription transition (ARR ~$2B+, 90%+ recurring revenue) and reached sustained profitability under CEO Rajiv Ramaswami, who joined from [[VMware]] in 2020. The [[Broadcom]] acquisition of VMware in late 2023 became a massive tailwind -- Broadcom's aggressive price increases and bundling changes drove VMware customers to evaluate alternatives, with Nutanix as the primary beneficiary. The February 2026 equity partnership with [[AMD]] ($150M investment) deepens the hardware ecosystem relationship. The bear case is that [[VMware]] customers eventually accept Broadcom's new pricing rather than migrating, and that public cloud providers continue encroaching on on-prem workloads.
