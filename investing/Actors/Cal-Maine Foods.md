**Cal-Maine Foods** (NASDAQ: CALM) is the largest shell egg producer and distributor in the [[United States]], controlling roughly 20% of domestic production. Headquartered in Ridgeland, Mississippi, the company produces, grades, packages, and distributes conventional, cage-free, organic, and specialty eggs to grocery retailers, foodservice distributors, and egg product companies.

## Synopsis

Cal-Maine is the purest play on US egg economics — a commodity business where profitability is almost entirely determined by the spread between egg prices and feed costs (primarily corn and soybean meal). The company's scale advantage lies in its vertically integrated flock of ~45 million laying hens across operations in 18 states, plus long-term supply agreements with major retailers ([[Walmart]], [[Kroger]], [[Costco]]). Revenue and earnings are wildly cyclical: in years when avian influenza (HPAI) culls millions of birds and tightens supply, egg prices spike and Cal-Maine posts record profits; in normal supply years, margins compress to single digits.

The 2022-2024 HPAI cycle was the most profitable in company history — egg prices surged above $4/dozen at retail, and Cal-Maine reported record revenue of $3.1B and net income exceeding $800M in fiscal 2023, prompting congressional scrutiny and price-gouging allegations. The company's unique dividend policy (paying out one-third of quarterly net income as dividends) means the cash flow profile is feast-or-famine rather than steady-state. The stock is essentially a commodity call option on avian flu outbreaks — the HPAI virus is now endemic in North American wild bird populations, making future outbreaks a when-not-if proposition. Key competitor [[Vital Farms]] occupies the premium pasture-raised niche with a branded DTC model that commands higher margins but serves a smaller addressable market.
