# Braskem

[[Brazil]]'s largest petrochemicals producer and one of the top polyethylene producers globally. Controlled by [[Novonor]] (formerly Odebrecht) and [[Petrobras]]. ADR: BAK (NYSE), local: BRKM5 ([[B3]]).

---

## Synopsis

**Braskem** is a company caught between a structural problem and an ownership deadlock. Chinese petrochemical overcapacity is crushing margins industry-wide — that's the structural driver. The [[Selic]] at 15% is the accelerant, making every quarter of negative cash flow more punishing. Bonds crashed from 90 cents to the low 30s in Sep 2025 (blindsiding creditors), stabilized around 60 cents after coupon payments in Jan 2026, but the company hired [[Lazard]], [[Cleary Gottlieb]], and [[E. Munhoz Advogados]] to review the capital structure. Fitch rates it CC.

The control question is the crux. [[Novonor]] (formerly Odebrecht) has been trying to exit its Braskem stake for years — [[Nelson Tanure]]'s bid collapsed in H2 2025 after exclusivity expired. In Dec 2025, [[IG4 Capital]] signed a binding agreement to buy all of Novonor's debt backed by Braskem shares, and [[CADE]] approved the deal on Mar 6, 2026 (15-day appeal window). But the timeline has deteriorated: Valor Econômico reports Braskem may need bankruptcy protection within 2-3 months, a scenario IG4 didn't sign up for. The fund has signaled to banks it may walk.

The other co-controller, [[Petrobras]], won't inject capital without a new shareholders agreement — meaning the rescue depends on resolving the ownership first. CEO [[Magda Chambriard]]'s hedging ("could still be reversed by the tribunal" while also wanting "a new partner") suggests Petrobras is keeping options open but not writing checks. If IG4 walks, Braskem is back to square one with a ticking clock and no buyer. This is the highest-profile name in the broader [[Brazil corporate bond rout February 2026|Brazil corporate distress wave]], and the outcome — restructuring under new control vs. bankruptcy — depends on whether IG4 decides the deteriorated asset is still worth the original deal terms.

## Mar 2026 — CADE approves IG4 stake sale

[[CADE]]'s superintendence approved [[IG4 Capital]]'s acquisition of a controlling stake from [[Novonor]] (Mar 6, 2026). IG4 signed a binding agreement with Novonor and creditor banks in Dec 2025 to buy all of Novonor's debt backed by Braskem shares. The approval becomes final after 15 days if no appeal by CADE board members.

[[Petrobras]] CEO [[Magda Chambriard]] said a new shareholders agreement would be necessary before Petrobras considers a capital injection. She also noted the CADE superintendence decision could still be reversed by the administrative tribunal. Earlier that day she said Petrobras was looking forward to "an agreement with a new partner."

Before CADE's decision, Valor Econômico reported IG4 might abandon talks due to regulatory delays. Braskem continues to burn cash and may need bankruptcy protection within 2-3 months, per an unnamed executive involved in the talks. That scenario differs from when IG4 entered negotiations, and the fund signaled to banks it may back out.

Shares pared earlier gains and closed down 0.8% on the day (Mar 6).
