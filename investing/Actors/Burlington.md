# Burlington

**Third-largest US off-price retailer** — fastest growing of the big three. $10.6B revenue (FY24, +9%), 1,211 stores. ~$20B market cap. CEO Michael O'Sullivan (former TJX executive). Highest gross margins in off-price (~44%). Aggressive store expansion (100+ new stores/year target). The growth story in off-price — smaller scale but gaining faster.

## O'Sullivan turnaround

CEO Michael O'Sullivan (former TJX) joined in 2019 and transformed Burlington:

| Initiative | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Smaller stores | Downsized from 80K sq ft to 25-35K sq ft |
| Inventory turns | Faster, fresher merchandise |
| Gross margin expansion | +300bps over 3 years |
| Real estate discipline | Better locations, lower rent |

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Fastest growth in off-price (+9% vs peers +4%)
- Highest gross margins (~44%)
- Store expansion runway (1,211 → 2,000+)
- O'Sullivan execution (former TJX, proven playbook)
- EPS doubled in 2 years
- Value retail structural tailwind

**Bear:**
- Smallest scale = less buying power
- Tariff risk (apparel sourcing)
- ~25x PE — more expensive than peers
- Comp sales moderating (+0-2% guidance)
- [[Consumer]] spending uncertainty
- Less proven brand vs TJX/Ross
