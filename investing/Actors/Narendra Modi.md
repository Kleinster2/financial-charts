# Narendra Modi

Prime Minister of [[India]] since 2014. [[Bharatiya Janata Party|BJP]] leader. The political figure through whom [[India]]'s tilt within the US-[[China]] competition is currently being steered. The vault tracks Modi's role at three nodes: (1) the [[US-India trade deal February 2026|February 2026 US-India trade deal]] that briefly stabilised the bilateral relationship; (2) the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran-conflict-period]] non-alignment that kept Indian refiners ([[Reliance Industries]]) buying sanctioned Iranian crude; (3) the March 2026 fast-track approval for minority-Chinese tech investments in seven strategic sectors — the first quantitative reversal of post-2020 Indian closure to Chinese capital.

Per the FT (Gideon Rachman, May 2026), the Modi government's tilt toward Chinese-investment liberalisation is the single most consequential measurable shift since [[Trump II]] began alienating Delhi through public praise of [[Pakistan]], heavy India tariffs, and a "hellhole" social-media reference. The vault relevance is the implication for India-as-counterweight to [[China]]: a long-running US strategy that depended on Modi's government quietly closing to Chinese capital is now being reversed by the Modi government itself.
