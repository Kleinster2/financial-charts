# Matt Yglesias

Writer and policy commentator, author of the *Slow Boring* newsletter on Substack. Co-founded Vox Media in 2014 before departing in 2020 to launch Slow Boring, which became one of the top-earning political Substacks. His core thesis — the "abundance agenda" — argues that America's central economic problem is artificial supply constraints (housing, energy, infrastructure permitting), not insufficient demand or redistribution. This puts him at odds with both progressive rent-control advocates and conservative anti-spending hawks. On housing specifically, he argues cities should build abundant market-rate housing to drive affordability, rather than rely on subsidies or rent regulation — a position that has influenced the YIMBY movement. Author of *One Billion Americans* (2020), arguing the US should target population growth through immigration and family policy to maintain geopolitical competitiveness.
