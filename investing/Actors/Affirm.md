## Q3 2026 earnings — record GMV, raised guide, stock fell -5% (May 7-8 2026)

AFRM closed -5.0% on May 8 ($67.36 → $64.01, -2.2σ beta-adjusted, high-vol trigger) despite a Q3 fiscal 2026 print that beat consensus on every headline and raised full-year guidance. The selloff is the cleanest example of "priced-in beat" in the May 7-8 earnings tape — Affirm had run materially before the print and the bar was set high enough that even a strong beat couldn't sustain the multiple.

| Q3 fiscal 2026 | Result | vs Consensus |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| Revenue | $1.039B | beat ($1.012B); +27% YoY |
| Adjusted EPS | $0.30 | beat ($0.17 consensus, +76% beat) |
| Gross Merchandise Volume | $11.6B | beat ($11.0-11.3B guidance); +35% YoY |
| Transactions | +45% YoY | strong consumer engagement |
| Active merchants | 515,000 | +44% YoY |

Updated full-year guidance:

| Metric | New FY2026 guide | Prior |
|--------|------------------|-------|
| GMV | $49.27-49.57B | $48.30-48.85B |
| Revenue | $4.175-4.205B | (raised) |
| Adjusted operating margin | 28.2-28.8% | 27.4-28.1% |

The structural reads:

1. The BNPL operational thesis is delivering. 35% GMV growth + 45% transaction growth + 44% merchant growth + GAAP-positive trajectory together represent the cleanest evidence that the Buy Now Pay Later model can scale profitably at $11B+ quarterly volumes. The bear case (BNPL is structurally unprofitable / will collapse under credit-cycle pressure) is currently falsified by Affirm's print.
2. The "priced-in" framing is the right reading of the -5% reaction. AFRM had run from low-$50s to mid-$70s in the weeks before the print on Q3-expectation positioning. The beat-and-raise was already in the price; the move was a "sell the news" unwind by traders who had positioned for the print.
3. Adjacent BNPL / payments names moved in sympathy. [[Block]] (XYZ) +6.7% on its own beat-and-raise print same session; the BNPL / consumer-payments cohort had a strong week overall despite Affirm specifically selling off. The takeaway is that the consumer-payments / BNPL trade is intact at the cohort level even where individual name multiples are stretched.
