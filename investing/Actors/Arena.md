# Arena

Independent user-voted blind-test platform for ranking AI models. Users vote on which of two anonymized models gives a better response, and the platform aggregates votes into Elo-style rankings. Widely regarded as more reliable than vendor-published benchmarks, which AI labs optimize for and which often diverge from real-world preference. Covers LLMs (Chatbot Arena), text-to-video, image-to-video, and video editing as separate leaderboards.

## Why it matters

From the FT (Eleanor Olcott, May 17 2026): *"Such assessments are viewed as more reliable than most model benchmarking systems, which AI labs optimise for and are not necessarily accurate reflections of their real-world performance."*

Arena rankings are the closest the market has to a market-revealed-preference signal for AI model quality. Lab-published benchmarks (MMLU, HumanEval, etc.) measure narrow tasks the lab knows it can win; Arena measures what users prefer when they don't know which model is which. Public companies citing Arena rankings in earnings or marketing materials are leaning on the third-party-validation framing.

The May 17 2026 FT chart of video model rankings is the cleanest example to date of an Arena leaderboard contradicting an inferred narrative — the FT framing was "Chinese ahead" but the chart shows [[xAI]]'s [[Grok Imagine Video]] as the strongest non-Chinese model in two of three categories, ahead of [[Google]]'s [[Veo]] 3.1 in image-to-video. The other top-tier Chinese maker missing from the top three of text-to-video is [[Kuaishou]]'s flagship [[Kling]] line — Kling O3 Pro appears only at #4 in video edit, suggesting Kuaishou's lead is on different version segments than the headline rankings reward.
