**TKO Group Holdings** (NYSE: TKO) — Combined [[UFC]] and [[WWE]] entity, formed September 2023 when [[Endeavor]] merged UFC into publicly traded WWE. [[Ariel Emanuel]] is Executive Chair & CEO. [[Endeavor]] is the controlling shareholder (~61.7%), and [[Silver Lake]] controls Endeavor (take-private closed March 2025) — so Silver Lake sits atop the control chain.

In February 2025 TKO acquired [[IMG]], [[On Location]], and [[Professional Bull Riders]] from [[Endeavor]] in a ~$3.25B all-equity deal, broadening it from combat sports into sports media rights, hospitality, and live experiences.

The tradeable instrument — Up-C share structure, control chain, price record, and peer comparison — lives in [[TKO securities note]].

## Financials

TKO is a high-margin live-sports-rights business whose reported bottom line is distorted by its Up-C structure. Headline revenue dipped 3% in FY2025 (to $4,735.2M from $4,884.2M) only because the [[IMG]]/[[On Location]] segment lapped the 2024 Paris Olympics hospitality cycle (−31%); the core UFC and WWE rights businesses both grew. Operating income, not revenue, is the real story: it collapsed to $30.9M in FY2024 on elevated low-margin Olympics-year hospitality costs and SG&A, then rebounded to $835.0M in FY2025 as that volume rolled off and the segment mix re-weighted to high-margin media rights. Net interest expense (~$200–235M/yr on ~$3.7B of UFC-legacy term debt) is what tipped FY2024 into a net loss.

| FY (Dec 31) | Revenue | Direct op. costs | Gross profit | Operating income | Net income (total) | NI → Class A | Diluted EPS |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2023 | $3,224.8M | $1,576.8M | $1,648.0M | $375.8M | $143.0M | −$35.2M | −$0.43 |
| 2024 | $4,884.2M | $2,623.9M | $2,260.4M | $30.9M | −$245.7M | $9.4M | $0.02 |
| 2025 | $4,735.2M | $1,903.2M | $2,832.0M | $835.0M | $546.3M | $195.4M | $2.26 |

The two right-hand columns expose the Up-C: total consolidated net income of $546.3M in FY2025 splits into $350.9M attributable to noncontrolling interests ([[Endeavor]]'s OpCo units) and only $195.4M to public Class A — which is why diluted EPS is $2.26 and why FY2023 shows a Class A loss (−$0.43) despite positive total net income (most of that year's income predated the September 2023 merger or accrued to NCI). Read total net income for the business and EPS for the public share. FY2023 is a transition year — WWE full-year plus UFC from September 12.

Most recent quarter (Q1 2026): revenue $1,596.9M (+25.9% YoY — the first full quarter carrying the [[IMG]]/[[On Location]]/[[Professional Bull Riders]] assets, which closed February 28 2025), operating income $338.5M (+42.6%), total net income $249.8M, diluted EPS $1.12 (vs $0.69 in Q1 2025). The acquisition that lifted [[Endeavor]]'s stake is now fully in the run-rate.
