David is a protein bar company founded by [[Peter Rahal]] (who previously sold [[RXBAR]] to [[Kellogg]] for $600M) and [[Zach Ranen]], launched in September 2024. Legal entity is Linus Technologies, Inc.

---

## Synopsis

David is the fastest-growing protein bar brand in the US, hitting ~$102M in retail sales through H1 2025 after launching just nine months earlier. The entire value proposition rests on a single ingredient: EPG (esterified propoxylated glycerol), a modified plant fat that delivers 0.7 kcal/g vs 9 kcal/g for conventional fat, enabling the headline claim of 28g protein at 150 calories. David acquired [[Epogee]], the sole manufacturer of EPG, in May 2025 using bulk of its $75M Series A — a vertical integration move that secured supply but triggered an antitrust lawsuit (OWN Your Hunger v David) alleging monopolization (dismissed Feb 2026). A separate calorie mislabeling class action filed January 2026 claims bomb calorimeter testing shows the bars contain 268-275 calories, not 150 — David argues that method doesn't reflect FDA-approved bioavailability calculations for EPG.

[[Peter Rahal]] is a repeat CPG founder who built [[RXBAR]] from $10K in his parents' basement to a $600M exit to [[Kellogg]] in 2017, then ran [[Litani Ventures]] (53-company portfolio including 4 unicorns). His co-founder [[Zach Ranen]] came from [[Warburg Pincus]] PE and [[Evercore]] M&A. The $725M post-money valuation on ~$140-180M Year 1 revenue (4-5x multiple) is aggressive for a single-product CPG company with active litigation and single-ingredient dependency. The key risk is existential: if the FDA changes its GRAS determination or caloric value assignment for EPG, the entire product thesis collapses.

## Evolution

The story of David is the story of a repeat founder weaponizing the playbook he learned the first time — and betting everything on a single molecule.

- 2013-2017 — RXBAR prologue. [[Peter Rahal]] co-founded [[RXBAR]] with Jared Smith for $10,000 ($5K each) in his parents' basement in Chicago. The brand rode the clean-label, paleo movement — the packaging literally listed ingredients ("3 Egg Whites, 6 Almonds, 4 Cashews, 2 Dates. No B.S."). By 2017 projected net sales hit ~$120M with zero outside funding. [[Kellogg]] acquired RXBAR in October 2017 for $600M (~5x revenue), making Rahal one of the most successful bootstrapped CPG founders in a generation. He stayed as CEO under Kellogg, but it went sideways: a 20% layoff (40 employees) in December 2018 after miscalculating growth, then a 15-flavor peanut contamination recall in January 2019. The corporate parent experience taught Rahal what he wanted to do differently.

- 2019-2023 — Litani Ventures and incubation. After leaving RXBAR, Rahal launched [[Litani Ventures]], a personal family office (Chicago/Miami) that built a 53-company portfolio including 4 unicorns ([[Olipop]], [[Sprout Social]], Speak). The investing years gave him a bird's-eye view of CPG innovation, particularly emerging food science. He zeroed in on EPG — a modified plant fat made by a small company called [[Epogee]] that delivered fat's texture at a fraction of the calories (0.7 kcal/g vs 9 kcal/g). Rahal saw in EPG what he'd seen in egg whites at RXBAR: a single-ingredient advantage that could redefine a category's macro profile.

- 2023-2024 — Founding and pre-launch. Rahal incorporated Linus Technologies, Inc. and recruited [[Zach Ranen]] as co-founder and president. Ranen brought a different toolkit: Wharton undergrad, PE at [[Warburg Pincus]], M&A at [[Evercore]], and his own DTC food startup (Raize, a low-carb bakery). The company was named after Michelangelo's David — positioning around physical perfection, not health-food earnestness. They raised a $10M seed in August 2024 from Rahal himself plus [[Valor Equity Partners|Valor Siren Ventures]], [[Andrew Huberman]], and [[Peter Attia]]. Before launching, they seeded 20,000 product samples to fitness influencers and built a 40,000-person waitlist.

- September 2024 — Launch and viral breakout. David launched September 16, 2024. First week: $1M in sales. First six weeks: $3.3M revenue, 1M bars sold. The 3PL partner [[Atomix Logistics]] went from zero to 30,000 orders in the first 30 days, then 200,000+ orders in six months. The product-market fit was immediate: 28g protein, 150 calories, 0g sugar, in flavors (Cookie Dough, Fudge Brownie, Blueberry Pie) that read like desserts. The gold metallic packaging stood out on shelves and social feeds. TikTok and Instagram did the marketing — user-generated content from fitness influencers created a self-reinforcing loop.

- May 2025 — Series A, Epogee acquisition, and vertical integration. [[Greenoaks]] led a $75M Series A at a $725M post-money valuation, with [[Valor Equity Partners]] also participating. David used the bulk of the proceeds to acquire [[Epogee]], the sole manufacturer of EPG. The strategic logic was aggressive but clear: David accounted for ~90% of Epogee's revenue, so this was less diversification than supply chain lock-up. By owning the only source of the ingredient that makes the 28g/150cal claim possible, David ensured no competitor could replicate the formula. Morrison Foerster (Denver/NY) handled the legal work.

- Summer-Fall 2025 — Retail expansion and product extension. David expanded from DTC/Amazon into 3,000+ retail locations including Wegmans, Vitamin Shoppe, and Westside Market, with Kroger, [[Walmart]], and [[Target]] in the pipeline. They launched David Bronze (20g protein, 150 cal, Double Chocolate) as a lower-protein option and experimented with frozen cod fillets ($69 for four 6oz pieces) — more brand stunt than category expansion. Revenue for 2025 was tracking toward $140-180M, up from ~$8M in the partial 2024 year.

- 2025-2026 — Legal challenges and the Attia departure. The Epogee acquisition triggered an antitrust lawsuit from three competitors (OWN Your Hunger, Lighten Up Foods, Defiant Foods) alleging David monopolized EPG supply. Judge Victor Marrero dismissed the case in February 2026 — plaintiffs failed to define a relevant market. But a second legal threat emerged in January 2026: a calorie mislabeling class action filed in SDNY alleging bomb calorimeter testing showed the bars contain 268-275 calories (vs 150 advertised) and 11-13.5g fat (vs 2g advertised). David's defense: bomb calorimeters measure total heat energy, not bioavailable calories; the FDA permits EPG at 0.7 kcal/g based on GRAS status. Then in February 2026, Dr. [[Peter Attia]] — chief science officer and seed investor — stepped down after his name appeared in 1,700+ documents from the Epstein files release. Rahal confirmed the departure. David lost its most credible scientific voice at exactly the moment its calorie claims were being challenged in court.

## EPG — the molecule that makes it work

EPG (esterified propoxylated glycerol) is a modified plant fat that functions like conventional fat in food manufacturing but delivers only 0.7 kcal/g vs 9 kcal/g — a 92% calorie reduction. The FDA granted GRAS (Generally Recognized As Safe) status, which permits labeling at the lower caloric value. [[Epogee]] LLC was the sole manufacturer. David acquired Epogee in May 2025, making EPG a proprietary ingredient.

The entire David value proposition — 28g protein at 150 calories in a bar that tastes like a dessert — depends on EPG. Without it, the bar would be ~270 calories by conventional fat accounting. The calorie class action essentially argues that EPG's caloric discount shouldn't count; David argues the FDA says it does.

## Core thesis

David is a bet on three things: (1) that EPG is safe and its caloric discount survives legal and regulatory scrutiny, (2) that [[Peter Rahal]] can execute the DTC-to-retail expansion playbook a second time at higher stakes, and (3) that the protein bar category — growing ~6.3% CAGR through 2035 with North America at ~45% of the global market — has room for a premium disruptor. If all three hold, David is a generational CPG brand built on a genuine product advantage, not just influencer hype. If EPG fails — legally, regulatorily, or reputationally — the entire company unravels.

## Key risks

1. EPG regulatory risk — if the FDA changes GRAS determination or caloric value assignment for EPG, the product claim collapses and the brand is unsalvageable
2. Calorie lawsuit reputational risk — even if David wins legally, "your 150-calorie bar is actually 270 calories" is a devastating headline for a brand built on macro precision
3. Single-ingredient dependency — the entire product line depends on one molecule from one manufacturing source (now owned, but still a single point of failure)
4. Valuation — $725M on ~$140-180M Year 1 revenue (4-5x) is aggressive for a single-product CPG company with two active lawsuits and no proven retail velocity at scale
5. [[Peter Attia]] departure — lost the most credible scientific endorser at the worst possible moment (mid-calorie-litigation)
6. Price premium sustainability — at ~2x the price of Quest/RXBAR, David needs the macro story to hold; any calorie-claim erosion collapses the willingness-to-pay
