#actor #energy #policy #international

**IEA** — International Energy Agency. Western counterweight to OPEC. Publishes demand forecasts, coordinates strategic reserves, advises on energy transition.

---

## Why IEA matters

| Function | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Demand forecasts | Moves oil prices |
| Strategic reserves | Emergency supply buffer |
| Policy guidance | Shapes energy transition |
| Data authority | Trusted source for markets |

---

## Key publications

| Report | Frequency | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Oil Market Report | Monthly | Short-term price signals |
| World Energy Outlook | Annual | Long-term forecasts |
| Net Zero Roadmap | Periodic | Transition scenarios |

**Market-moving:** IEA demand revisions regularly move oil prices.

---

## Strategic Petroleum Reserves

**Coordination role:**
- Members hold 90+ days of import cover
- IEA coordinates emergency releases
- Used during Gulf War, Libya, Ukraine crises

**US SPR:** Largest at ~400M barrels (depleted from 600M+)

---

## IEA vs OPEC

| Aspect | IEA | [[OPEC]] |
|--------|-----|----------|
| Role | Consumer advocate | Producer cartel |
| Goal | Stable, affordable supply | Maximize producer revenue |
| Reserves | Strategic (emergency) | Production (commercial) |
| Transition | Promotes renewables | Defends oil demand |

**Tension:** IEA increasingly calls for reduced fossil investment; OPEC pushes back.

---

## Peak oil demand debate

**IEA position:**
- Oil demand peaks before 2030
- Transition accelerating
- Calls for reduced upstream investment

**OPEC response:**
- Demand will grow for decades
- Underinvestment risks crisis
- IEA projections are political

**Investment implication:** Who's right determines oil company valuations.
