## What it tracks

The IRF-M is the ANBIMA index of prefixado Treasury bonds — Letras do Tesouro Nacional (LTN, zero-coupon) and Notas do Tesouro Nacional série F (NTN-F, coupon-bearing). IRFM11 is the index expression of "lock in today's nominal Brazilian yield," with no inflation linkage — the opposite trade to the IPCA ([[IMAB11]]) ETFs.

## Use case

The prefixado / nominal-duration sleeve: a directional bet that nominal Brazilian rates fall (or simply locking the high nominal carry). Because it is nominal, it loses if inflation surprises higher even when real rates are stable — the cost of giving up the IPCA hedge.

## Correlation

0.39 daily-return correlation to [[EWZ]] over two years — the same rate-cycle overlap with equities as the IPCA ETFs. Prefixado duration and Brazilian stocks both rally on lower-Selic expectations. See [[Brazilian fixed-income ETFs]].
