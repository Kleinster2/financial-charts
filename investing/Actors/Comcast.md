CFO commentary on broadband competition was the first time the company explicitly framed the environment as not normalizing: "fixed wireless continues to market aggressively," "fiber overbuild is moving at a rapid pace," and "we are not assuming this gets easier anytime soon." Peacock's path to breakeven in Q2 2026 is now explicit company guidance rather than implied trajectory. Theme parks are absorbing pre-opening costs for Epic Universe (Orlando) ahead of late-2026 ramp.

## Apr 24 contagion drop

[[Charter Communications]] reported Q1 2026 the morning of Apr 24 with a -120,000 residential broadband net loss (vs ~100k consensus, -72k YoY) and an EPS miss of $0.91. CHTR closed -25.5% on the day. Comcast traded down -12.9% in sympathy on the read-across — broadband attrition in the cable footprint is structural, not cyclical, and Comcast's better Q1 broadband number (-65k) does not dilute the sector-level implication.

The dual-print confirms the [[Cable broadband subscriber meltdown April 2026]] narrative: cable broadband is post-peak even with mobile growth offsetting on the income statement. See the event note for the full read-through.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Broadband still essential utility
- Peacock reaching scale
- Theme parks = experience economy
- [[Sports rights]] (NFL, Olympics)
- Cable spinoff unlocks value

Bear:
- Cord-cutting accelerating
- Broadband competition (fiber, FWA)
- Peacock still unprofitable
- Linear networks declining
- Heavy debt load
