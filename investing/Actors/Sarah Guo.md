**Sarah Guo** is the founder and managing partner of Conviction, an AI-focused venture fund. Previously a general partner at [[Greylock Partners]], she launched Conviction in 2022 to invest exclusively in AI-native companies, making her one of the most visible VC voices in the AI infrastructure and applications space.

## Synopsis

Guo left [[Greylock Partners]] in 2022 — timing that placed her fund launch at the beginning of the generative AI wave rather than chasing it. Conviction's thesis is that AI will restructure entire industries, and the fund targets companies building AI-native products (not legacy companies bolting on AI features). Her seed investment in [[Harvey]], the AI-powered legal research platform, exemplifies the approach: backing a company that reimagines a professional workflow from scratch using LLMs rather than incrementally improving existing legal tech. The fund has reportedly raised $100M+ and deployed across AI infrastructure, vertical AI applications, and developer tools.

Her influence extends beyond capital deployment through a widely followed podcast (No Priors, co-hosted with Elad Gil) and active social media presence that has made her one of the primary interpreters of AI progress for the broader tech and investment community. For the vault, Guo matters as a sentiment indicator — her investment decisions and public commentary reflect where sophisticated AI-native capital is flowing, which sectors are seeing real product-market fit versus hype, and where the AI application layer is maturing from prototype to revenue. The transition from Greylock (generalist) to Conviction (AI-specialist) mirrors a broader pattern of top-tier VCs spinning out thematic funds to concentrate bets on AI.
