---

## Synopsis

Artists Equity is a production company built on a structural innovation: all crew participate in profit-sharing, not just above-the-line talent. Writers, editors, costume designers share upside. [[RedBird Capital]] committed at least $100M in initial funding. Three divisions: Scripted, Unscripted, Advertising.

The model got its proof of concept with The Rip (Jan 2026, Netflix) — 112.3M views in six weeks and the first time Netflix paid performance-based bonuses to ~1,200 cast and crew. Affleck said a "grand slam" payout would require ~325M views (KPop Demon Hunters level). The success triggered a multi-year first-look streaming deal with Netflix in Mar 2026, where Artists Equity serves as lead studio on straight-to-streaming titles. The company retains a separate theatrical deal with [[Sony|Sony Pictures]] — those films flow to Netflix later via Netflix's Pay-1 deal with Sony.

The bull case: profit-sharing attracts top talent willing to take lower upfront pay for upside, reducing production risk and aligning incentives. The bear case: if films underperform, the model is just lower wages with no payout — talent could sour.

## Business model — profit-sharing

Artists Equity's structural differentiator: crew at every level — not just stars and directors — participate in a film's financial upside. The model:
- Lower upfront compensation (vs market rates)
- Upside tied to measurable performance (views, box office, licensing)
- Applied to The Rip: ~1,200 cast and crew eligible for bonuses
- Designed to attract talent willing to bet on quality over guaranteed fee
