#actor #company #foodtech #uk #private

Win-Win — UK foodtech start-up using fermentation to recreate chocolate's flavour compounds from cereals and legumes, producing cocoa-free confectionery ingredients. Sits on the ingredient-substitution / fermentation side of the chocolate alternatives landscape, distinct from cell-culture approaches ([[Celleste Bio]], [[Kokomodo]], [[Food Brewer]]) and from direct biomass substitution ([[Voyage Foods]]).

## Technology

Win-Win applies precision fermentation to cereals and legumes — engineered or selected microbes transform the substrate into the flavour compounds found in chocolate, without any cocoa input. The process is closer to brewing or yogurt fermentation than to mammalian cell culture, which typically implies lower capex and energy intensity per unit output.

---

## Key voice

Raphaël Felenbok, independent adviser to the Win-Win board (FT, April 2026), offered the public "moonshot" framing for cell-cultured alternatives — arguing lab-grown technologies are less scalable than ingredient-substitution approaches given current energy costs, capex requirements, and regulatory hurdles. The comment frames the broader chocolate-alternatives landscape as still small — under 1% of the total chocolate market — with the gap between cocoa farming and cellular agriculture also potentially filled by paying smallholder farmers more or expanding large-scale cocoa farming.
