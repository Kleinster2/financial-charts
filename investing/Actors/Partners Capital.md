**Partners Capital** — London-headquartered outsourced chief investment office (OCIO) firm. Manages portfolios for endowments, foundations, family offices, and ultra-high-net-worth individuals across public and private markets. Founded 2001. Distinct from [[Partners Group]] (the Swiss private capital firm).

---

## Why Partners Capital matters

Partners Capital is an institutional-quality OCIO that allocates client capital across primary funds, secondaries, hedge funds, and public-market strategies. The firm is cited in [[Private equity secondaries#Retail crossover via evergreen vehicles|the FT's April 2026 evergreen-vehicle analysis]] for the canonical articulation of the mark-to-NAV-decay problem for retail investors in evergreen PE products.

Partner Euan Finlay (Apr 2026 FT): if early returns "had been driven by buying secondaries at a discount and marking them up, you will not benefit from that uplift" as a later investor.

The Partners Capital view sits at the sophisticated-LP end of the spectrum. As an OCIO advising institutions and ultra-HNW clients, the firm's framework directly contradicts the retail-friendly evergreen marketing pitch, which leans on high reported early IRRs to attract subscriptions. Finlay's framing is the canonical "subscribe-late-and-you-miss-the-mark-up" warning that the wealth-channel marketing literature does not address directly.

## Distinguishing from Partners Group

Not to be confused with [[Partners Group]] (Swiss private capital firm, $185bn AUM, listed SIX Swiss as PGHN). Partners Capital is an OCIO investing across asset classes; Partners Group is a private capital manager investing in private equity / debt / infrastructure / real estate.
