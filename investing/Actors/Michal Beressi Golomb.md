#actor #person #executive #foodtech #israel

Michal Beressi Golomb — CEO of [[Celleste Bio]], the Israeli cell-cultured cocoa butter start-up. In April 2026, she announced production of the world's first lab-grown chocolate bars at the [[Cadbury]] Bournville factory in Birmingham, using Celleste's cell-cultured cocoa butter.

## Public statements — April 2026

Via the FT (Apr 15, 2026):

- On the technical milestone: "The question whether we could make real chocolate, real milk chocolate, with cell-cultured cocoa butter is proven: we did it."
- On the drop-in property (vs other alternatives): "Unlike other alternatives, it performs identically to conventional cocoa butter. It's a drop-in replacement ... so it has a lot of meaning in terms of production processes for the chocolate manufacturers."
- On the process analogy: the bioreactor acts as "the new cocoa tree for the cocoa cells," giving them "water, sugar and vitamins ... to let them feel they're inside a pod on a tree."
- On colocation vision: "Imagine like Willy Wonka and Charlie and the Chocolate Factory having pipes of cocoa butter going straight to their facility — so we're already cutting CO₂ emissions in terms of shipping."
- On regulatory timeline: aiming for US and [[Israel]] approval by end of 2027; [[Europe]] likely longer.
- On scale target: 50,000 tonnes/year cocoa butter at $12,000-$15,000/tonne.
