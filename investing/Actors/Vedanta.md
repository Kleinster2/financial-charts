# Vedanta

## Synopsis

[[Vedanta]] (NSE: VEDL; BSE: 500295) is one of India's largest diversified natural-resources companies, controlling aluminium, zinc, copper, iron ore, oil and gas, and power-generation assets through a single listed entity until April 30, 2026, after which a court-approved demerger split the firm into five independently listed sector-focused companies effective May 1, 2026. The promoter is [[Anil Agarwal]], who founded what would become the Vedanta Resources group in 1976; the publicly listed Vedanta Ltd is a subsidiary of London-domiciled Vedanta Resources Plc.

Pre-demerger, Vedanta's structural position made it the closest peer to [[Hindalco]] in the Indian metals cohort — both multi-metal Indian conglomerates with substantial USD revenue exposure (Hindalco via [[Novelis]]; Vedanta via international zinc-mining and copper-trading operations). The May 2026 demerger materially changes the post-2026 cluster picture; pre-demerger cluster validation against Hindalco showed VEDL ↔ HINDALCO 1Y correlation 0.73 — the tightest pair in the Indian metals cohort.

## May 2026 demerger

In December 2025, the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) Mumbai bench approved the Vedanta demerger plan. The split became effective May 1, 2026 (VEDL trades ex-demerger 2026-04-30), creating five independently listed entities:

| Entity | Business | Promoter stake retained |
|---|---|---|
| Vedanta Aluminium Metal Ltd | Aluminium | >50% |
| Vedanta Power Ltd | Power generation | >50% |
| Vedanta Oil & Gas Ltd | Oil and gas (Cairn India legacy) | >50% |
| Vedanta Iron and Steel Ltd | Iron ore + steel | >50% |
| Vedanta Limited (Residual) | Hindustan Zinc stake; copper; ferro-chrome; international zinc | >50% |

Anil Agarwal's stated rationale (December 2025 PTI interview): "the demerger was chosen over asset sales or alternative restructuring to unlock the full growth potential of each business." Each entity is positioned as having the potential to "be as big as Vedanta in terms of revenue."

The demerger has implications for vault cluster analysis: the pre-demerger HINDALCO ↔ VEDL 0.73 correlation was a single-entity-to-single-entity pairing. Post-demerger, the cleanest comparable to [[Hindalco]] becomes Vedanta Aluminium Metal Ltd specifically, with the residual entity (zinc/copper/ferro-chrome) carrying a different exposure mix.

## Position in Indian metals cluster

Per the [[Hindalco]] cluster validation (1Y window May 2025 – April 2026, run 2026-05-06):

- VEDL ↔ HINDALCO: 0.73 (tightest pair in 4-name Indian metals cohort)
- VEDL ↔ TATASTEEL: 0.63
- VEDL ↔ JSWSTEEL: 0.44
- Hierarchical clustering at threshold 0.4: VEDL pairs with HINDALCO in the *non-ferrous sub-cluster*; TATASTEEL + JSWSTEEL form the *steel sub-cluster*. The four-name cohort does not hold as a single cluster.
- PCA: VEDL loads 0.493 on PC1 (which explains 71.04% of variance) — confirming VEDL is part of the "general Indian metals" factor.

The May 2026 demerger means this validation reflects the legacy single-entity VEDL.NS series; post-demerger cluster structure is a forward research question.
