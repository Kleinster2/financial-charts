# Harold Koh

International-law scholar at [[Yale Law School]], where he is the Sterling Professor of International Law. Former Legal Adviser to the US Department of State (2009-2013, Obama administration) and former Dean of Yale Law School (2004-2009). Co-author with [[Robin J. Brooks]] of a 2026 proposal on European interdiction architecture for the [[Russian shadow fleet]] — the legal-framework component of the Brookings-Yale Law collaboration that Brooks has been advocating since the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran-Hormuz crisis]].

## Why he matters

The legal-framework anchor for the Russian shadow-fleet interdiction proposals that [[Robin J. Brooks]] has been advocating through 2026. Koh's standing as a Sterling Professor + former State Department Legal Adviser gives the proposals the institutional legitimacy needed for European governments to take them seriously. The Brooks-Koh pairing is the operational + legal-framework complement to the [[Ben Harris]] Treasury-side enforcement work — together they form the policy stack: Treasury enforcement (Harris) + macro data (Brooks) + international legal framework (Koh).
