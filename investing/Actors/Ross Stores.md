# Ross Stores

**Second-largest US off-price retailer** (behind [[TJX]]). $21.1B revenue in FY24, operating Ross Dress for Less (1,873 stores) and dd's DISCOUNTS (360 stores). The May 2026 Q1 print changed the near-term story: sales +21%, comps +17%, EPS +37% to $2.02, FY26 EPS guide raised to $7.50-$7.74, and shares +8.1% on May 22 (+4.9σ beta-adjusted vs SPY). Tariff exposure remains the bear case, but traffic and sourcing execution are now overpowering the prior macro-guidance withdrawal frame.

## Q1 FY2026 beat — traffic resets the tariff frame

Ross reported a clean off-price demand beat for the quarter ended May 2, 2026:

| Metric | Q1 FY26 | YoY / context |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Sales | $6.0B | +21% |
| Comparable sales | +17% | Versus flat last year |
| Operating margin | 13.4% | Plan was 11.8%-12.1% |
| EPS | $2.02 | +37%; guide was $1.60-$1.67 |
| FY26 EPS guide | $7.50-$7.74 | +13%-17% vs FY25 |
| May 22 stock move | +8.1% to $234.81 | +4.9σ beta-adjusted vs SPY |

The key signal is traffic, not just pricing. Management attributed the comp strength primarily to higher customer traffic, stronger customer acquisition / engagement, and better in-store execution. That reframes Ross from "China-tariff-exposed discounter with withdrawn guidance" to "off-price share-taker with enough traffic momentum to absorb tariff noise for now."

Sources: [Ross Stores Q1 FY2026 press release](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ross-stores-reports-robust-first-quarter-sales-and-earnings-results-significantly-exceeding-guidance-302779470.html) (May 21 2026); Yahoo chart data for May 22 close and beta-adjusted move.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Off-price structural tailwind (value-seeking consumers)
- \#2 position with room to grow (TJX has 5,000+ stores vs Ross 2,233)
- dd's DISCOUNTS addresses lower-income demographic
- Consistent EPS growth
- New CEO Jim Conroy (former RH) brings fresh perspective

**Bear:**
- Tariff exposure (>50% [[China]]) — FY25 guidance withdrawn
- Traffic softening (January-February weakness noted)
- Less scale than TJX (weaker buying power)
- dd's DISCOUNTS lower-margin
- Valuation not cheap (~22x forward PE)
