**Lars Fruergaard Jørgensen** — President and CEO of [[Novo Nordisk]] from 2017 until his ouster in May 2025. Presided over the GLP-1 era — Wegovy launch (2021), peak market cap ~$650bn (2024), and the loss of US share leadership to [[Eli Lilly]] that triggered his removal.

A 33-year Novo veteran, Jørgensen was the company's longest-tenured CEO of the modern era. He was replaced in August 2025 by [[Mike Doustdar]] in a board-driven succession that broke with Danish executive-development tradition and elevated commercial pivot over R&D continuity.

## What happened on his watch

| Event | Date |
|-------|------|
| Wegovy FDA approval | 2021 |
| Compounding loophole opens | 2022 (FDA semaglutide shortage list) |
| Peak market cap ~$650bn | 2024 |
| Lilly captures dominant US GLP-1 share | 2024–2025 |
| Three guidance cuts in 2025 | 2025 |
| Board ousts | May 2025 |

The proximate cause of removal was forecasting and competitive failure: Novo modeled Wegovy demand on its older Saxenda (liraglutide) franchise, which underestimated semaglutide uptake by an order of magnitude. Five weeks of post-approval Wegovy prescriptions exceeded five years of Saxenda prescriptions, leaving Novo unable to supply demand and ceding the US market to Lilly's Zepbound and to compounders.

---

## Why his exit mattered

Three signals from the [[Novo Nordisk Foundation]]'s decision:

1. The Foundation directly engineered the change — chair Lars Rebien Sørensen also took the Novo chair seat, an arm's-length break for a foundation-controlled Danish company
2. The replacement was non-Danish and non-R&D — first such CEO in Novo's 102-year history
3. The pivot was framed as commercial, not scientific — the molecule (semaglutide) was still world-class; the marketing and channel strategy was not
