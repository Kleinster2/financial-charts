## What It Tracks

The [[Russell 1000]] Index — US large- and mid-cap, cap-weighted. In practice it tracks [[SPY]] and [[VOO]] almost tick-for-tick (the extra ~500 mid-caps move the needle little against 500 mega-caps), so it is a near-substitute for the S&P 500 core rather than a distinct exposure. The Russell complement on the small-cap side is [[IWM]] (Russell 2000).

## Use Case

A broad US large-cap core holding. Functionally redundant with an S&P 500 fund — worth knowing as an alternate label for "the US large-cap market" when it shows up in a portfolio, not as a separate bet.
