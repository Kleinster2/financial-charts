# Emilie Choi

President and COO of [[Coinbase]], responsible for corporate development, M&A, ventures, and business operations. Architected Coinbase's acquisition strategy during the 2020-2021 crypto bull run and built out the company's institutional and international businesses.

## Synopsis

Emilie Choi joined [[Coinbase]] in 2018 as VP of Corporate & Business Development and rose to President and COO by 2022, making her the highest-ranking executive after CEO [[Brian Armstrong]]. Her background spans corporate development roles at LinkedIn (where she led 30+ acquisitions including Lynda.com for $1.5B), Yahoo, and Warner Bros. At Coinbase, she led the acquisitions of Bison Trails (infrastructure, ~$80M), Tagomi (prime brokerage), and Unbound Security, plus the company's expansion into staking, NFTs, and Base (L2 blockchain). She also oversaw Coinbase Ventures, which deployed into 300+ crypto startups.

Choi's operational role matters because Coinbase's path from exchange to crypto financial infrastructure company depends on successful diversification beyond trading fees, which remain cyclically brutal. Under her leadership, the non-trading revenue mix improved from ~15% in 2020 to ~50% by 2025 (staking, custody, Base, USDC interest). The question is whether Coinbase can sustain this diversification in a bear market — subscription and services revenue is stickier than trading but still correlated to crypto sentiment. Choi is widely viewed as the operational backbone that lets Armstrong focus on product and policy.
