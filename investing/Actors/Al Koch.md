**Al Koch** is Vice Chairman and Managing Director at [[AlixPartners]], known for serving as Chief Restructuring Officer of [[General Motors]] during its 2009 bankruptcy and interim CFO of [[Kmart]] in 2002 — two of the largest corporate restructurings in American history.

## Synopsis

Koch's career defines the modern restructuring professional: a specialist called in when a company is in extremis, charged with preserving value through a process that management — often the same people who created the crisis — cannot navigate. The GM engagement was the marquee assignment. When General Motors filed for Chapter 11 in June 2009 (the fourth-largest bankruptcy in US history at $91B in assets), Koch led the financial restructuring that split the company into "New GM" (viable operations) and "Old GM" (legacy liabilities), enabling a $49.5B government bailout to be partially recovered through the 2010 IPO. The speed of the GM bankruptcy — 40 days from filing to emergence — was unprecedented for an entity of that complexity and required Koch's team to manage thousands of dealership closures, union negotiations, and supplier relationships simultaneously.

Before GM, Koch handled the [[Kmart]] interim CFO role during its 2002 bankruptcy, another multi-billion-dollar retail restructuring. His earlier career included partnership at [[EY|Ernst & Young]]'s restructuring practice. He was inducted into the Turnaround Management Association (TMA) Hall of Fame, the restructuring industry's highest individual honor. At [[AlixPartners]], Koch operates alongside [[Simon Freakley]] (Executive Chairman) in the firm's leadership structure, maintaining client relationships with boards and creditor committees who value his track record on the largest, most politically sensitive engagements.
