# Hang Seng Bank

Hang Seng Bank is a Hong Kong-based commercial bank and a subsidiary of [[HSBC]], which holds ~62% of its shares. Founded in 1933, it is one of Hong Kong's oldest locally incorporated banks and consistently ranks among the most profitable in the region by return on equity. The bank is also the publisher of the [[Hang Seng Index]], the benchmark stock index for [[Hong Kong]], which it created in 1969.

Hang Seng operates primarily in Hong Kong and mainland [[China]], with a focus on retail banking, wealth management, and commercial lending. It serves as a significant holding (~3.7% weight) in the [[EWH]] ETF. The bank benefits from Hong Kong's role as a financial gateway to China but faces structural headwinds from the territory's post-2020 political realignment and competition from mainland digital banks.
