# Wealthfront

Automated investment and financial planning platform (robo-advisor) headquartered in Palo Alto, CA. One of the two original robo-advisors alongside Betterment, now managing $80B+ in AUM and offering a full-stack consumer finance product including automated investing, tax-loss harvesting, high-yield cash accounts, and financial planning tools.

## Synopsis

Wealthfront launched in 2011 (originally as kaChing) and helped pioneer the robo-advisor category — algorithmically managed, low-cost portfolios that democratized access to strategies like tax-loss harvesting previously available only to high-net-worth clients. The company has grown AUM past $80B, driven by a combination of millennial adoption, strong cash account yields (competitive with money market funds), and a product philosophy that automates every decision rather than offering hybrid human-advisor models like Betterment or [[Schwab]] Intelligent Portfolios.

The company nearly sold to [[UBS]] for $1.4B in January 2022, but the deal collapsed by September 2022 amid regulatory complications and market conditions. That failed acquisition left Wealthfront as an independent company in a market where the economics of pure robo-advisory are increasingly questioned — Betterment has shifted toward B2B/advisor tools, and the major brokerages ([[Schwab]], [[Vanguard]], [[Fidelity]]) offer their own automated solutions with zero minimums. Wealthfront's path forward depends on whether it can evolve from a robo-advisor into a full consumer finance platform (banking, lending, planning) before the margin compression in automated investing eliminates standalone viability.
