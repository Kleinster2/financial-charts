## Views

Sharma (FT, Apr 6, 2026) argued the [[Iran]]-Hormuz oil shock differs from predecessors because governments enter with record deficits/debt and central banks are already missing inflation targets — leaving zero policy ammunition. Key data: G7 debt from 20% GDP (1970s) to >100%; global debt $348tn (record, >3x GDP); [[Federal Reserve|Fed]] missed 2% target 60 straight months; 3/4 developed central banks and 1/2 emerging missing targets; US deficit ~6% GDP (could hit ~7% with proposed $1.5tn Pentagon budget). Bond yields rising on term premium (fiscal fear), not inflation expectations — a structural break from past oil shocks. Most vulnerable: [[United States|US]], [[United Kingdom|UK]], [[Brazil]], [[Egypt]], [[Indonesia]]. Least: [[Taiwan]], [[Vietnam]], [[Sweden]].
