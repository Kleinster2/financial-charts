**Toyota Research Institute** — [[Toyota]]'s US research subsidiary (founded November 2015 with an initial $1B five-year commitment; CEO [[Gill Pratt]]), based in Los Altos, CA and Cambridge, MA. One of the labs that moved robot learning from weeks-long programming to hours-long demonstration via diffusion policies. Distinct from Woven by Toyota, the Tokyo-based software subsidiary that absorbed TRI-AD — TRI itself remains Toyota's research arm.

## Robot learning

TRI's robotics group, led by [[Russ Tedrake]], is a co-originator (with Columbia University) of the diffusion-policy approach — see [[Diffusion models (robotics)]]. Reported results (FT visual story, Nov 20 2024):

- AI-powered behaviour cloning teaches robot arms complex movements within hours instead of weeks.
- T-block demonstration: human-demonstrated pathways noised with random trajectories, de-noised into executable paths; the robot then generalises the placement task from numerous starting positions.
- Tedrake argues the proliferation of robot hardware — factory automatons to Roomba vacuums — helps fill the action-data gap that constrains large behaviour models: more deployed robots means more visual data of actions being completed.

## Boston Dynamics partnership (Oct 2024)

Research partnership with [[Boston Dynamics]] announced October 2024: developing whole-body large behaviour models for advanced humanoids, applied to the Atlas platform. Pairs TRI's learning stack with one of the field's most capable hardware platforms — the same logic [[Hyundai]] later extended with [[Google|Google DeepMind]] [[Gemini]] Robotics integration (Jan 2026).
