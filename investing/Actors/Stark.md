**Stark** — Berlin/Munich-based defense startup building weaponized drones for the [[Bundeswehr]] and [[NATO]]. Founded 2024 by [[Florian Seibel]] (also founder of [[Quantum Systems]]) after reconnaissance-focused Quantum investors resisted military applications. Backed by [[Peter Thiel]] and [[Sequoia Capital]]. One of three German suppliers in the [[Bundeswehr]]'s kamikaze-drone procurement (alongside [[Helsing]] and [[Rheinmetall]]).

---

## Synopsis

Stark is the youngest and smallest of the three German drone suppliers in the Bundeswehr's 2026 kamikaze-drone procurement, but also the one designed for nothing else. [[Florian Seibel]] founded it as a clean spin-out from his earlier reconnaissance-drone company [[Quantum Systems]] after existing investors resisted a pivot into weapons. The strategy has paid off fast: a $500M valuation within 18 months, a €269M Bundeswehr upfront contract in February 2026, and a place in the three-supplier procurement model alongside [[Helsing]] and [[Rheinmetall]]. The risks are equally clear — reported failures on all four strikes in key [[Bundeswehr]] trials in October 2025, and a [[Peter Thiel]] ownership position that has drawn political heat from the [[Germany|German]] Greens. The three-supplier structure is partly insurance against exactly these risks.

## Origin

[[Florian Seibel]] founded [[Quantum Systems]] in 2015 as a reconnaissance-drone maker. When Quantum's investors blocked a pivot to weaponized systems, Seibel spun up Stark in 2024 with co-founders [[Uwe Horstmann]] (Project A partner) and [[Johannes Schaback]]. The split lets each company stay true to its mandate: Quantum for ISR, Stark for strike.

## Thiel controversy

[[Peter Thiel]]'s stake drew political heat in February 2026. Green-party defense budget specialist Sebastian Schafer demanded transparency about Stark's ownership structure and suggested replacing Thiel with another investor. Stark stated publicly that Thiel has "no information rights relating to our products or business operations" and that any technical transfer would require German export-control approval.

The controversy reflects a broader tension: [[Germany]]'s defense procurement is leaning on private capital that is substantially US-funded, at the same time [[Friedrich Merz]]'s government is pushing "Germany First" sovereignty signals. See [[European defense spending]] — structural imbalance section.

## Military trial setback (Oct 2025)

Stark reportedly missed on all four strikes during key [[Bundeswehr]] trials in October 2025 (per drone-industry press). The failure did not prevent the February 2026 contract award but underlines the [[Pentagon]]/Pentagon-analog risk: award precedes proven performance at scale. The three-supplier structure is partly insurance against exactly this outcome.
