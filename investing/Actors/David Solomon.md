# David Solomon

CEO of [[Goldman Sachs]] since October 2018. Led the firm's pivot toward consumer banking (Marcus), then its retreat back to institutional core after consumer losses mounted. Known outside finance for his side career as DJ D-Sol.

---

## Synopsis

Solomon took the [[Goldman Sachs]] helm from Lloyd Blankfein and inherited a strategic question: how does a wholesale investment bank grow when trading revenues are structurally lower and IPO volumes are cyclical? His answer was consumer banking via the Marcus platform and the [[Apple]] Card partnership — a bet that Goldman's brand and balance sheet could compete in retail. That bet failed expensively: Goldman lost ~$3B on consumer operations before Solomon reversed course in 2023-2024, selling off the Marcus personal loan book and winding down retail ambitions to refocus on asset/wealth management and institutional trading.

The market reads Solomon as a competent steward who made one large strategic error and had the discipline to reverse it before it became existential. Goldman's stock has recovered strongly, with wealth management now positioned as the growth engine. Solomon's public commentary carries weight — his February 2026 characterization of the [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] software selloff as "too broad" signaled that Goldman's institutional desk saw the AI-driven SaaS disruption panic as overdone, which matters because Goldman's flow desk is one of the largest equity market-makers. His tenure will ultimately be judged on whether the wealth management pivot delivers durable revenue growth or whether Goldman drifts back toward the trading-dependent model he tried to diversify away from.
