**Scopely** — [[Saudi Arabia|Saudi]]-owned mobile game developer behind [[Monopoly Go!]], the second-highest-grossing mobile game of 2024 (~$2.2B player spending, AppMagic). Acquired by [[Savvy Games|Savvy Games Group]] in 2023 for $4.9B. In March 2025, agreed to acquire [[Pokémon Go]] and other games from [[Niantic]] for $3.5B, bringing total audience to 500M+ players.

---

## Synopsis

Scopely is the operational gaming arm of [[Saudi Arabia]]'s gaming ambitions. The $4.9B [[Savvy Games|Savvy Games Group]] acquisition in 2023 made it the vehicle through which [[Saudi PIF]] capital flows into mobile gaming at scale. [[Monopoly Go!]] validated the thesis — ~$2.2B in player spending in 2024 alone, second only to a handful of titles globally.

The $3.5B [[Pokémon Go]] deal (announced March 12, 2025) adds one of the most durable mobile franchises ever built: 30M monthly active players, $1B+ annual revenue across [[Niantic]]'s games business, and a pioneering augmented reality platform launched in 2016. Combined with [[Monopoly Go!]], Scopely will have 500M+ players, making it one of the largest mobile gaming publishers globally. The deal also includes a $50M investment in [[Niantic|Niantic Spatial]], the AI spin-off, plus a technology licensing agreement for continued player data sharing.

The strategic logic is scale consolidation in mobile gaming under Saudi sovereign capital. [[Saudi PIF]] earmarked ~$40B for gaming as part of [[Saudi Vision 2030]]'s economic diversification. Scopely is the execution vehicle — acquiring proven revenue-generating titles rather than building from scratch.

## Pokémon Go acquisition (March 2025)

FT (March 12, 2025): Scopely agreed to acquire [[Pokémon Go]] and other games from [[Niantic]] for $3.5B. [[Niantic]] simultaneously spinning off [[Niantic|Niantic Spatial]], a geospatial AI venture focused on "next generation maps" for machines (smart glasses, robots).

Deal structure:
- $3.5B to [[Niantic]] investors ([[Nintendo]], [[Google]], [[Coatue]], [[IVP]])
- $350M previously held on [[Niantic]]'s balance sheet also distributed to investors
- [[Niantic]] investors become shareholders in the AI spin-off
- Scopely makes $50M investment in [[Niantic|Niantic Spatial]]
- [[Niantic|Niantic Spatial]] also capitalized with €200M from [[Niantic]]'s balance sheet
- Technology licensing deal for continued player data sharing

Tim O'Brien, Scopely CRO: "Few games in the world have delivered the scale and longevity of [[Pokémon Go]], which reached over 100M players just last year."
