## Apr 17, 2026 - -12.0% / -3.7σ sigma event

LYB fell 12.0% on Apr 17, 2026 (-3.7σ beta-adjusted), making it one of the sharpest losers in the Hormuz reopening tape.

Two resets hit at once:

- Company-specific reset: LyondellBasell had already cut its quarterly dividend to $0.69 from $1.37 in February, with Reuters saying management called this one of the longest downturns in the chemicals industry. That removed part of the income-holder support under the stock.
- Sector reset: as crude collapsed on the Apr 17 reopening headline, global naphtha re-priced lower and compressed the war-premium portion of the U.S. [[Ethane|ethane]] advantage that had been helping petrochemical names.

That combination explains why LYB fell harder than the integrated oils. It had more geopolitical premium to give back and no refinery hedge after the permanent Houston shutdown in early 2025.

Next catalyst: Q1 2026 results due May 1, 2026.
