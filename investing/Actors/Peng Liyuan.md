Peng Liyuan is a Chinese folk singer, PLA major general, and spouse of [[Xi Jinping]] (married 1987). She was one of [[China]]'s most famous performing artists before Xi's rise to the paramount leadership.

## Synopsis

Peng Liyuan was a household name in China long before [[Xi Jinping]] became general secretary in 2012 — her performances at CCTV's Spring Festival Gala (China's most-watched annual broadcast) made her one of the country's most recognized cultural figures from the 1980s onward. She held the rank of civilian PLA major general through the PLA Arts Troupe, where she served as dean. Her public profile created an unusual dynamic in Chinese politics: she was more famous than her husband for most of their marriage, a reversal of the typical CPC leadership spouse pattern.

Since Xi's ascent, Peng has taken on a diplomatic soft-power role, serving as WHO Goodwill Ambassador for Tuberculosis and HIV/AIDS and accompanying Xi on state visits where her fashion choices and cultural diplomacy receive significant coverage. She is notably more publicly visible than previous Chinese first ladies, which analysts read as part of Xi's broader strategy to project a more personalized, approachable leadership image. Their daughter, Xi Mingze (born 1992), studied at Harvard under a pseudonym — a detail that surfaced publicly and created brief political sensitivity given Xi's anti-Western rhetoric. For investors, Peng is relevant only as context for understanding Xi's political persona and the soft-power apparatus of the [[CPC]].
