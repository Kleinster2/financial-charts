# Mark Barnaba

Mark Barnaba is the Deputy Chairman of [[Fortescue]] (FMG / FSUGY) and one of [[Andrew Forrest]]'s most senior public-facing operators on commercial strategy, China relationships, and the metals-side decarbonization roadmap.

## Key public positions

### Apr 28, 2026 — Bank of America Breakthrough Technology Dialogue (Singapore)

In a wide-ranging Bloomberg "The Asia Trade" interview with Haslinda Amin, Barnaba laid out the cleanest Fortescue framing on the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran shock]] cost transmission and the active [[China Mineral Resources Group|CMRG]] negotiation:

- **Diesel exposure.** ~1 billion liters/year of diesel imports into the [[Pilbara]]; a $0.50/L price move = ~$500M annual cost. Marginal cost of production "just under $20/t" in USD.
- **Real-zero grid.** Fortescue is building a 24/7 baseload renewable grid (multi-site solar + wind + battery on 220 kV / 600 km AI-optimized transmission) to displace diesel and gas in Scope 1 + Scope 2 — "real zero, not net zero." Barnaba claims no other industrial-scale equivalent exists globally.
- **CMRG terms.** Active commercial negotiation. CMRG asks: (1) move to a China-based iron-ore index away from Platts-derived global benchmarks; (2) settle a substantial portion of trade in [[Renminbi]] rather than USD. Barnaba's posture: "not unreasonable, not unexpected" — Fortescue's relative strength is its long [[China]] relationship and the symmetry that Fortescue itself buys substantial [[China]]-made batteries, solar panels, and equipment.
- **Australia macro.** [[RBA]] now operates with [[Australia]] CPI 3.7% (vs. 2-3% band, 2.5% target), wages 3.4%, productivity 1%, full employment. Expects 25bp RBA hike at the next meeting "and another later in the year" as inflation stays sticky into the imported Iran shock.

This is the clearest Fortescue communication that the metals-side decarbonization (real-zero grid) is alive and economic even though the external [[Green hydrogen]] vision has been scaled back.

*Source: Bloomberg "The Asia Trade" 4/28/2026 broadcast.*
