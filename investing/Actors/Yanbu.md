**Yanbu** — Saudi Red Sea port city and western terminus of the East-West (Petroline) crude oil pipeline. Critical bypass route when the [[Strait of Hormuz]] is disrupted.

## Strategic significance

Yanbu is [[Saudi Arabia]]'s insurance policy against Hormuz closure. The Petroline carries crude ~1,200 km from the Eastern Province oil fields to Yanbu for Red Sea export. During the [[Operation Epic Fury|March 2026 Iran conflict]], it became the only meaningful bypass.

The [[Middle East Institute|MEI]] briefing (Mar 8) estimated port loading capacity at ~3 mb/d — calling it the bottleneck, not pipe capacity. But Bloomberg vessel tracking data (Mar 17) shows Saudi Arabia has exceeded that estimate: Yanbu exports are averaging ~4.19 mb/d on a rolling 5-day basis, with peak single-day loading of 4.65 mb/d achieved on three occasions. [[Saudi Aramco]] has restored >50% of pre-war exports (~7 mb/d total) through Yanbu alone.

As of Mar 19, at least 32 VLCCs and Suezmax vessels were waiting off Yanbu to load cargoes, with more en route. Aramco has been offering long-term customers the option of receiving supply from Yanbu.

Even at ~4.2 mb/d, Yanbu cannot compensate for the ~15 mb/d Hormuz disruption — the ~11 mb/d gap remains enormous. But it keeps Saudi Arabia solvent and its customers partially supplied.
