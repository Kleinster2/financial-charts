# Rosenblatt Securities

**Rosenblatt Securities** is an independent equity research and trading firm founded in 1979, best known for its semiconductor coverage and agency trading operations.

## Synopsis

Rosenblatt occupies a distinctive position in the sell-side ecosystem: an independent, non-bank research and execution shop that has built disproportionate influence in semiconductor coverage. The firm's visibility is largely driven by [[Hans Mosesmann]], whose bullish chip calls — particularly on [[NVIDIA]], [[AMD]], and the AI semiconductor supply chain — have generated outsized media attention and client interest. In an era where the bulge brackets have cut research headcount and MiFID II has pressured independent research economics, Rosenblatt has survived by specializing deeply rather than covering broadly.

The firm's other pillar is agency trading — executing institutional orders without taking proprietary positions, which aligns its incentives with buy-side clients. This model, combined with focused research coverage, creates a boutique franchise that serves institutional investors looking for unconflicted execution and variant analytical perspectives. For the vault, Rosenblatt calls are worth tracking as a sentiment indicator in the semiconductor complex — Mosesmann's price targets on [[NVIDIA]] have consistently been among the Street's highest, making the firm a useful gauge of bull-case thinking in AI infrastructure. When Rosenblatt upgrades or raises targets, it often signals that the most aggressive buy-side positioning is intensifying.
