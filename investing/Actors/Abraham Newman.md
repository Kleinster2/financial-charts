# Abraham Newman

Abraham Newman is a professor in the Mortara Center for International Studies and the Edmund A. Walsh School of Foreign Service at [[Georgetown University]], and co-author with [[Henry Farrell]] of the [[weaponized interdependence]] framework and the 2023 book *Underground Empire: How America Weaponized the World Economy*. A political scientist whose work focuses on international regulation, data governance, and the coercive potential of economic networks.

## Why he matters to the vault

Newman is the data-governance and international-regulation half of the Farrell-Newman partnership. His specific contribution to the [[weaponized interdependence]] framework is the regulatory-chokepoint angle — the way US and EU regulatory authority over globalized networks (financial compliance, data protection, product safety) translates into coercive leverage even without formal sanctions. The Farrell/Newman work shifts the frame from "who produces what" (the [[Second China shock]] question) to "who controls the rules and the nodes the flows pass through."

Relevant to vault notes on:
- [[SWIFT]] sanctions and dollar-clearing chokepoints
- [[US-China chip export controls]] via ECCNs and the [[Entity List]]
- [[Data localization]] and cross-border data flows
- Secondary sanctions architecture
