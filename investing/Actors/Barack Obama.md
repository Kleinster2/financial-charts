#actor #person #politics #us

**Barack Obama** served as the 44th President of the [[United States]] (2009-2017), the first African American to hold the office. Post-presidency, he and [[Michelle Obama]] co-founded [[Higher Ground Productions]], a media company with an exclusive deal with [[Netflix]] to produce documentaries, scripted series, and podcasts.

## Synopsis

Obama's investment relevance is threefold: the regulatory and policy legacy of his administration (Dodd-Frank, ACA, Paris Climate Accord, Iran nuclear deal), his post-presidential media and business ventures, and his continuing influence on Democratic Party economic policy. The Obama era established the regulatory frameworks that still govern financial services ([[Dodd-Frank]], CFPB creation, Volcker Rule) and shaped the energy transition policy architecture that the Biden administration expanded. His administration also oversaw the post-2008 recovery, the longest bull market expansion in US history, and the early stages of tech platform dominance by [[Google]], [[Amazon]], [[Apple]], and [[Meta]].

[[Higher Ground Productions]] represents the clearest investable connection — the production company's deal with [[Netflix]] has produced critically acclaimed content including *American Factory* (2019 Oscar for Best Documentary, covering [[Fuyao Glass America]]'s Ohio plant and US-China labor dynamics), *Becoming*, and *Working: What We Do All Day*. The Netflix partnership is part of the broader trend of platforms paying premium prices for celebrity-affiliated production labels (the Obamas, Meghan and Harry, Shonda Rhimes, Ryan Murphy), using brand association as a subscriber acquisition and retention tool. Obama's post-presidential financial trajectory — from $1.3M net worth at inauguration to an estimated $70M+ by 2025 — primarily reflects book deals ($65M advance for the couple) and the Netflix deal.
