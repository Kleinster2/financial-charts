# Jeromin Zettelmeyer

Jeromin Zettelmeyer is director of [[Bruegel]], the Brussels-based economic think tank, since September 2022. Previously deputy director of the [[IMF]] Strategy, Policy, and Review Department (2019-2022) and German Federal Ministry of Finance director-general for economic policy under [[Wolfgang Schäuble]] (2014-2016). A central figure in European sovereign-debt restructuring thinking and a co-author of the Weder di Mauro *Sovereign Debt* handbook.

## Why he matters to the vault

Zettelmeyer is cited by [[Adam Tooze]] and others as a core interpreter of the 2020s global imbalances debate, particularly on how the [[Euro|eurozone]]'s fiscal architecture can or cannot respond to a sustained [[Second China shock]] in capital-intensive sectors. As Bruegel director he has pushed the institution toward more aggressive positions on eurobond-style common issuance, industrial policy at the EU level, and trade-defense modernization — all of which frame what "European state capacity" would actually look like if assembled in time.

Also prominent on:
- [[Argentina]] and [[Greece]] sovereign-debt restructuring case studies
- IMF program design
- Eurozone fiscal rules reform (post-Stability and Growth Pact)
