Lead Edge Capital is a New York-based growth investment firm that buys stakes in high-growth technology companies through secondary market transactions and SPV structures, typically in the late-stage private and early public phases.

## Synopsis

Lead Edge occupies a specific niche in the venture/growth ecosystem: buying shares from early employees, angels, and early-stage VCs who want liquidity before an IPO, and constructing SPVs that give its LP base access to pre-IPO allocations. This secondary-market approach avoids the competitive dynamics of primary rounds (where the company chooses its investors) and instead targets the supply side — sellers who need or want cash. The model works best in extended private markets where companies stay private for 10-15 years, creating a growing pool of shareholders seeking interim liquidity.

The firm has participated in secondary transactions for companies including [[Alibaba]], [[Spotify]], [[Uber]], and [[Snowflake]] prior to their respective IPOs. Lead Edge's value proposition to LPs is access — getting into high-profile names at a point where growth trajectory is visible but public-market pricing hasn't yet been applied. The risk is adverse selection: the best-informed insiders may sell for reasons that aren't visible to secondary buyers. Zach Ullman is a key partner. For investors tracking the [[Private market secondaries]] ecosystem, Lead Edge represents the dedicated growth-equity end of the spectrum, distinct from broader secondary funds like [[Lexington Partners]] or [[Ardian]].
