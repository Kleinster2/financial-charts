#actor #company #cybersecurity #private

**Bugcrowd** — Bug-bounty and vulnerability-disclosure platform, founded 2012 in Sydney, Australia and now San Francisco-headquartered. Direct competitor to [[HackerOne]] in the managed-bug-bounty market. Customer base includes [[OpenAI]], T-Mobile, and Motorola. Privately held. CEO Dave Gerry. Bugcrowd's positioning has historically emphasised the human-creativity-irreplaceable framing relative to HackerOne's more enterprise-managed posture.

The investing-vault relevance routes through Bugcrowd's role as the secondary canonical case for the [[AI producer-evaluator asymmetry|producer-evaluator asymmetry]] framework. The reported submission spike (reports more than quadrupled over a three-week period in March 2026; "most proving to be false") is the second platform data point confirming that the bug-bounty market has crossed the asymmetry threshold.

## May 2026 — AI submission flood

Per the [Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/dbec4441-02dc-4053-8500-85677973d324) (Jamie John, May 17, 2026):

- Reports more than quadrupled over a three-week period in March 2026
- Most submissions proving to be false
- CEO Dave Gerry on the framework: developments like [[Claude Mythos]] will assist human bug-bounty hunters, not replace them — "AI is going to help with a lot of things but we're never going to replace that human creativity"

The Bugcrowd response is the same rebuild-gate-with-AI equilibrium as HackerOne, with rhetorical emphasis on preserving the human-creativity layer rather than abandoning it.
