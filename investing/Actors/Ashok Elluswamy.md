**Ashok Elluswamy** is the head of AI software at [[Tesla]], redeployed by [[Elon Musk|Musk]] in March 2026 to reboot [[xAI]]'s Macrohard coding product after its previous leadership departed.

## Synopsis

Elluswamy is one of the most important engineers in [[Tesla]]'s autonomy stack. He joined Tesla in 2014 and rose to lead the Autopilot and Full Self-Driving (FSD) AI software team, overseeing the transition from a rules-based system to the end-to-end neural network approach (FSD v12+) that Musk has staked Tesla's robotaxi future on. His role at Tesla made him one of the few people inside the Musk ecosystem with deep production-grade AI engineering experience -- not just research credentials but the ability to ship models at automotive-grade reliability requirements.

His redeployment to [[xAI]] in March 2026 to reboot the Macrohard project (after [[Toby Pohlen]] departed in 16 days and co-founder [[Zihang Dai]] left) reveals both xAI's desperation and Musk's cross-pollination strategy. Rather than competing for external talent on the open market -- where [[Anthropic]], [[OpenAI]], and [[Google]] can offer competitive comp without Musk's management style -- Musk is drawing from his own portfolio companies. The question is whether this weakens Tesla's FSD development at a critical moment (the robotaxi timeline keeps slipping) to prop up xAI, creating a talent robbing Peter to pay Paul dynamic across Musk's empire.
