**New Mountain Finance** is a publicly traded business development company (BDC) managed by New Mountain Capital, a growth-oriented private equity firm. NMFC became a focal point of the 2025-2026 [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026|private credit liquidity stress]] after selling $477M in assets at 94% of fair value, sending shares to their lowest level since 2020.

## Synopsis

New Mountain Finance operates as the publicly traded credit vehicle for New Mountain Capital, a PE firm founded by Steve Klinsky with ~$55B AUM across private equity, credit, and net lease strategies. NMFC's portfolio consists primarily of first lien senior secured loans to upper-middle-market companies, with a stated focus on "defensive growth" sectors like healthcare, software, and business services. The BDC model — externally managed, required to distribute 90%+ of income — means NMFC's stock price is essentially a leveraged bet on the credit quality of its loan book and the manager's ability to maintain NAV.

The $477M asset sale at 94 cents on the dollar in late 2025/early 2026 crystallized the tension in the BDC model: when credit conditions tighten, externally managed BDCs face pressure to sell assets to meet obligations, but selling into a thin secondary market means accepting discounts that erode NAV, which depresses the stock further, creating a reflexive loop. NMFC's share price decline to 2020 levels — despite no widespread defaults in the portfolio — illustrates how the [[Private Credit]] sector's rapid growth created a liquidity mismatch that public market pricing exposed before private marks adjusted. The question is whether this is an isolated markdown event or the leading edge of broader BDC stress.
