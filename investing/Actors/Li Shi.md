# Li Shi

Senior Professor and Dean of the [[Institute for Common Prosperity and Development]] at [[Zhejiang University]] — the academic body specifically chartered to develop China's [[Common Prosperity]] policy framework. His March 29, 2026 lecture at [[Peking University National School of Development]] (translated by [[Pekingnology]] May 15, 2026) is the canonical mainstream-academic articulation of the 2026 reframed version of Common Prosperity — emphasising "investing in people" via endogenous-growth theory and positioning freedom as a productivity input rather than a constraint.

## Why he matters

Li Shi sits in the small set of mainstream Chinese academic economists who hold institutional positions specifically tied to the [[Common Prosperity]] framework. His 2026 lecture is structurally significant for three reasons:

1. He catalogued specific inequality metrics in a mainstream Chinese academic setting (Gini coefficient ~0.46-0.47 for a decade, wealth Gini 0.45→0.7 since 1995, ~300M with monthly income < ¥1,000) — most prior Common Prosperity discourse used the framing without the numbers
2. He reframed the policy as "investing in people" via endogenous-growth theory — moving the framework from redistribution-as-fairness toward redistribution-as-growth-policy
3. He named freedom as a productivity input, which implicitly limits how far coercive redistribution can be pushed without sacrificing the growth function — a notable rhetorical move from a senior figure at the Common Prosperity Institute

Treat his commentary as policy-aligned mainstream-academic framing rather than as dissident analysis.
