# AkademikerPension

Danish pension fund serving academics. In January 2026 it announced the sale of all of its US [[Treasuries|Treasury]] holdings, citing America's excess debt and fiscal outlook as too risky (reported FT, April 15 2026). The decision made it one of the first large institutional investors to publicly exit US [[Treasuries]] entirely on US-debt-trajectory grounds, predating the broader European-investor repositioning debate that followed the [[Liberation Day tariffs]] and the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|US-Israeli war in Iran]].

## Why it matters

AkademikerPension is small by global pension standards, but its move is a precedent datum: the gap between "hedging US exposure" (most of the market) and "exiting US Treasuries entirely" (AkademikerPension) defines the left tail of foreign-investor behavior. [[Government Pension Fund Global|GPFG]]'s April 2026 "no plans to reduce" stance anchors the right tail. The empirical question for Treasury-demand analysis is which of these two poles the median foreign institutional investor drifts toward.
