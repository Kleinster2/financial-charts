**Senegal** is a West African republic and emerging oil and gas producer, positioned to become one of sub-Saharan Africa's newest hydrocarbon exporters via the Sangomar offshore field. An [[IEA]] association country with a population of ~18M and GDP of ~$30B.

## Synopsis

Senegal's investment relevance shifted dramatically with the development of the Sangomar deepwater oil field, operated by [[Woodside Energy]] (82% working interest, with Senegal's national oil company Petrosen holding 18%). First oil was achieved in June 2024, with production ramping to an expected 100,000 bbl/d at plateau. For a country with GDP of ~$30B and no prior oil production, Sangomar represents a transformative revenue stream — potentially adding $1-2B annually in government revenue at mid-cycle oil prices. The adjacent Greater Tortue Ahmeyim (GTA) gas field, straddling the Senegal-Mauritania maritime border and operated by [[BP]], adds an LNG export dimension.

The political backdrop adds both opportunity and risk. President Bassirou Diomaye Faye, elected in March 2024 on a reform platform, has signaled intent to renegotiate hydrocarbon contracts for better terms — a pattern seen across African oil producers that creates uncertainty for operators but reflects legitimate resource nationalism. Senegal's broader economy rests on agriculture (groundnuts, fishing), services (Dakar as a West African hub), and growing digital infrastructure. The country uses the CFA franc (pegged to the euro via France), which provides monetary stability but limits policy flexibility. Senegal's democratic tradition — it has never had a coup, rare in West Africa — supports the governance premium that investors require for frontier market exposure.
