#actor #company

**Rainbow Rare Earths** (LSE: RBW, OTC: RBWRF) — Guernsey-incorporated rare earth company developing proprietary hydrometallurgical technology to extract REE from phosphogypsum waste. Two advanced projects: Phalaborwa (South Africa, DFS stage, 2028 production) and Uberaba ([[Mosaic]] JV, [[Brazil]], PFS stage, 2030 production).

---

## Synopsis

Rainbow is a bet on a single technological insight: that the billions of tonnes of [[Companionality|phosphogypsum waste]] generated by the fertilizer industry contain enough rare earth elements to build a non-mining supply chain. The company developed its extraction process at Phalaborwa, South Africa, where phosphogypsum stacks from historic phosphate mining hold 35.0 Mt at 0.44% TREO. Through iterative optimization — leach stages cut from three to two, residence time from 32 to 8 hours — Rainbow proved the process at pilot scale (launched January 2026) and published economics that rewrite the rare earth cost curve.

The Uberaba project with [[Mosaic]] (51/49 JV) delivered a PEA in March 2026: $916M post-tax NPV, 45% IRR, 1.7-year payback on $279M capex. Phalaborwa's December 2024 interim study: $611M NPV, 38% IRR, $326M capex. Combined, the two projects target ~3,800 tonnes/year of NdPr oxide and ~140 tonnes/year of dysprosium and terbium — the magnet-critical heavy REEs that the West has no other non-Chinese source for at scale. Operating costs ($38-41/kg) sit well below the $128/kg basket price inflated by China's [[Export controls|export controls]].

The risk is execution. Rainbow is pre-revenue with $3.9M cash (FY2025), burning ~$3.3M/year. The $50M DFC/TechMet commitment is earmarked for Phalaborwa construction, not pre-DFS spending. The company needs to reach FID (targeted late 2026) before the cash runway becomes critical. Market cap is ~£130M — pricing in significant success probability but leaving room for DFS catalysts. Analyst coverage is thin (Berenberg Buy at 34 GBX, Canaccord Speculative Buy at 15 GBX). See [[Phosphogypsum rare earth extraction]] for the full technology and economics.

## Evolution

The story of Rainbow Rare Earths is the story of a pivot — from a high-grade African mine that politics killed to a waste-extraction technology play that geopolitics made viable.

- 2011: Adonis Pouroulis (founder of Petra Diamonds) incorporates Rainbow in Guernsey and secures exploration rights at Gakara in Burundi — one of the highest-grade rare earth deposits in the world (run-of-mine grades ~10% TREO, vs. 0.5-2% typical). The bet is on grade overcoming Burundi's political risk.

- 2017: IPO on the London Stock Exchange. First production at Gakara begins in September — open pit bench mining at Gasagwe, producing concentrate at 52-56% TREO. Small scale: 350 tonnes exported in 2019, 500 tonnes in 2020, against a 5,000 tpa target.

- 2019: George Bennett (ex-MDM Engineering, which he founded, listed on LSE, and sold to Foster Wheeler in 2014) replaces the original CEO. Bennett begins pivoting the company's strategy toward phosphogypsum extraction — a technology play rather than a mining play.

- June 2021: Burundi government requests suspension of Gakara operations over profit-sharing renegotiation. The mine goes to care and maintenance. The asset is substantially written down. The political risk that the high grade was supposed to overcome has materialized. But by this point, Bennett has already redirected resources toward Phalaborwa.

- 2020-2023: Development of the Phalaborwa phosphogypsum project in South Africa. Rainbow secures an 85% stake (option for remaining 15%). Process optimization delivers the breakthroughs: leach stages 3→2, residence time 32→8 hours, calcium-depletion step achieving 93%+ purity. September 2021: exclusive IP license with K-Technologies (Florida) for continuous ion exchange/chromatography — eliminates toxic solvents, separates in fewer stages than traditional SX. October 2022: Phalaborwa PEA published.

- July 2023: Partnership with [[Mosaic]] announced for Uberaba, leveraging [[Mosaic]]'s existing phosphogypsum stacks in Minas Gerais. Also secures GBP 4.5M equity raise (30M shares at 15p).

- December 2023 (COP28): US DFC commits $50M through TechMet for Phalaborwa construction.

- July 2024: Ecora Resources royalty deal closes — $10M total ($8.5M royalty on Phalaborwa at 0.85% GRR, stepping to 1.10% if no production by July 2028, plus $1.5M equity subscription).

- December 2024: Phalaborwa interim study updates economics: $611M NPV (10%), 38% IRR, $326M capex, 2.0-year payback. 16-year mine life, ~1,865 tpa NdPr oxide, ~80 tpa Dy+Tb.

- March 11, 2026: Uberaba PEA completed ($916M NPV, 45% IRR). Joint Project Development Agreement signed with [[Mosaic]]. The Uberaba economics exceed Phalaborwa on every metric — higher grade (0.51% vs 0.44%), lower capex ($279M vs $326M), longer life (30 years vs 16). PFS underway, DFS targeted later 2026.

## Technology / IP

Rainbow's competitive moat is process know-how rather than patents:

- K-Technologies (Lakeland, Florida): exclusive IP license for continuous ion exchange and chromatography in the SADC region. Eliminates toxic solvents. Fewer separation stages than traditional SX (~75 mixer settlers vs 1,500+ in traditional plants). $5.5M deployment fee per project (from construction capex). IP rights for life of each deployed project.
- In-house flowsheet: mechanical reclamation → hydrometallurgical leach → CIX → impurity removal → SX separation. ~65% overall REE recovery.
- OCP/UM6P collaboration: investigating extraction from sedimentary phosphogypsum (~90% of global phosphoric acid production, lower grade at 0.40% TREO). Early R&D.
