# David Dorn

David Dorn is the UBS Chair in Globalization and Labor Markets at the [[University of Zurich]] and a co-author, with [[David Autor]] and [[Gordon Hanson]], of the "China Syndrome" paper ([[Autor-Dorn-Hanson]], 2013) that established the original [[China trade|China shock]] empirical framework. A leading European labor economist working at the intersection of trade, technology, and inequality.

## Why he matters to the vault

Co-architect of the [[Autor-Dorn-Hanson]] China shock literature. Dorn's specific empirical contribution was the commuting-zone level geography that made the effects visible and persistent. As the Zurich-based node of the trio, he is also the bridge between the US-focused first-shock literature and European work on the [[Second China shock]] hitting continental European labor markets.
