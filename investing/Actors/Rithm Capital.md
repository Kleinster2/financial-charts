**Rithm Capital** — alternative asset manager and REIT that has pivoted from pure mortgage servicing into a diversified $100B+ AUM platform through aggressive M&A. NYSE: RITM. CEO [[Michael Nierenberg]].

---

## Synopsis

Rithm is the rare financial company that successfully rebranded away from its original identity and came out bigger. Born as a Fortress-managed mortgage REIT spin-off in 2013, it internalized management in 2022, changed its name, and went on an acquisition tear: [[Sculptor Capital]] ($720M, 2023) for multi-strategy hedge fund capabilities, [[Crestline]] (~$18B AUM, 2025) for private credit, and [[Paramount Group]] ($1.6B, Dec 2025) for Class A office real estate in NYC and SF. The result is a platform managing $100B+ across mortgage servicing (Newrez), alternatives, and direct real estate — at a stock price of ~$10 against book value of $12.66, yielding 10%.

The investment question is whether Nierenberg is building a durable alternative asset manager or overleveraging a mortgage REIT into unfamiliar territory. The 2025 numbers argue for the former: $2.35 EAD per share (12% YoY growth), 19% EAD return on equity, and Sculptor growing inflows to $5.8B gross in 2025. But the discount to book persists, the office acquisition is contrarian (Class A NYC/SF at a time when the sector is still distressed), and the complexity of managing mortgage servicing, hedge funds, private credit, and office buildings under one roof is unusual.

## Evolution

The story of Rithm Capital is the story of a mortgage REIT that decided it wanted to be Brookfield when it grew up — and has been spending aggressively to get there.

- 2013 — Spun off from Newcastle Investment Corp (a [[Fortress Investment Group]] vehicle) as New Residential Investment Corp (NRZ). Pure mortgage REIT: excess mortgage servicing rights, residential securities. Fortress managed it externally, collecting 25% of FFO plus profit share. Michael Nierenberg named CEO.

- 2015-2017 — Acquisition phase I: $1.3B for Home Loan Servicing Solutions (Feb 2015), $950M for $97B of MSRs from [[Citigroup]] (Jan 2017), $190M for Shellpoint Partners (Dec 2017). Built one of the largest non-bank mortgage servicing books in the US.

- 2019 — Launched Newrez as the consumer-facing mortgage brand, consolidating origination and servicing under one roof. This moved the company from passive MSR ownership toward operating a mortgage business.

- 2021 — Newrez acquired Caliber Home Loans for $1.675B, establishing it as a top-5 non-bank mortgage originator/servicer. The acquisition closed during the COVID refi boom, giving Newrez massive volume.

- Jun 2022 — Pivotal moment: internalized management, terminating the Fortress management agreement. No longer paying 25% of FFO to an external manager. Rebranded to Rithm Capital (ticker NRZ → RITM, Aug 2022). This was the first signal that Nierenberg wanted to build an independent platform, not just a REIT.

- Nov 2023 — Acquired [[Sculptor Capital]] (formerly Och-Ziff) for $720M ($12.70/share, sweetened from initial $11.15 bid). Added ~$34B AUM in multi-strategy credit, equity, and real estate. The most ambitious move: a mortgage REIT buying a multi-strategy hedge fund.

- 2025 — Two more acquisitions in rapid succession: [[Crestline]] (private credit, ~$18B AUM) and [[Paramount Group]] ($1.6B for Class A office REIT). Crossed $100B in total AUM. Sculptor grew to $38B with $5.8B gross inflows in 2025.

- Dec 2025 — Paramount Group acquisition closed. 13 owned + 4 managed trophy office properties in NYC and SF, 13.1M sqft. Albert Behler (Paramount CEO since 1991) departed. Properties to be rebranded under Rithm.

- Feb 2026 — Reported record Q4 2025: $0.74 EAD/share, 24% ROE for the quarter. Full-year EAD $2.35/share. Stock at ~$10 vs $12.66 book — 21% discount to book value.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Trading at 21% discount to $12.66 book value — unusual for a company growing AUM
- 10% dividend yield with growing EAD ($2.35, +12% YoY)
- Platform diversification reduces mortgage cycle dependency
- Sculptor growing ($38B AUM, $5.8B inflows in 2025)
- Paramount Group properties are Class A trophy — different risk profile from commodity office
- Nierenberg has executed well on integration (Fortress exit, Sculptor absorption)

Bear:
- Complexity: mortgage servicing + hedge fund + private credit + office REIT is an unusual combination
- Office sector still structurally challenged — remote work isn't reversing
- Persistent discount to book may reflect market skepticism about the platform story
- GAAP net income ($567M) well below EAD ($2.35 × ~500M shares ≈ $1.2B) — gap suggests economic earnings include significant non-cash items
- Rate sensitivity: both mortgage servicing and office real estate correlate with rates
- Integration risk: three major acquisitions in two years
