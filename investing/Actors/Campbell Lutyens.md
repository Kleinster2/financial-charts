# Campbell Lutyens

Campbell Lutyens is a London-headquartered global private capital advisory firm, founded 1988, specializing in fund placement, secondary advisory, and GP capital advisory services. On 2026-04-30, [[Lazard]] announced a definitive agreement to acquire the firm for ~$575M total consideration plus up to $85M earnout, forming a new combined unit, Lazard CL, on close. CEO [[Gordon Bajnai]] becomes co-CEO of Lazard CL alongside [[Holcombe Green]], Lazard's incumbent global head of private capital advisory; both report to Lazard CEO/Chairman [[Peter Orszag]].

## Strategic logic

Campbell Lutyens is one of the largest pure-play global private capital advisers, sitting in the highest-multiple corner of independent advisory — fund placement and GP-led secondaries are fee-based, do not depend on M&A deal cycles, and have grown structurally through the post-2020 expansion of the private-capital alternatives universe. The acquisition gives [[Lazard]] a third reportable segment alongside Financial Advisory and Asset Management, and is the most direct pivot move yet from CEO [[Peter Orszag]] toward less cyclical revenue mix.

The deal sits in a 2025-2026 cluster of independent-adviser tuck-ins:

## Capitalization

Campbell Lutyens has been partner-owned since founding in 1988 — no outside venture or growth-equity rounds. Equity has historically rotated within the partnership; the 2026 [[Lazard]] deal is the firm's first external liquidity event. Pre-deal partner ownership is not publicly disclosed.
