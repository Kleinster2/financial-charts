**David Silver** — British computer scientist, [[Reinforcement learning]] pioneer, founder of [[Ineffable Intelligence]] (Apr 2026). Silver led [[Google]] [[DeepMind]]'s Reinforcement Learning team for over a decade and was the principal architect of [[AlphaGo]] (defeated Lee Sedol, 2016), [[AlphaZero]] (mastered chess, shogi, and Go without human games, 2017), and [[AlphaStar]] (StarCraft II Grandmaster, 2019). He stepped away from [[DeepMind]] to found [[Ineffable Intelligence]], which emerged from stealth on Apr 27, 2026 with a $1.1B seed at a $5.1B post-money valuation. Silver has committed 100% of his personal equity proceeds to charity through [[Founders Pledge]] — an unusual founder pledge for a bet of this scale, framing [[Ineffable Intelligence]] explicitly as a research mission rather than a wealth-creation vehicle.

## Why he matters

Silver is the most credentialed believer in pure-[[Reinforcement learning|RL]] paths to intelligence in the active commercial AI landscape. His core argument — [[AlphaZero]] discovered superhuman Go without ever seeing a human game — is the empirical foundation for the case that human data is a *constraint* on machine learning, not its substrate. The thesis is in tension with the dominant pre-training paradigm at [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], and most of [[DeepMind]]'s post-Gemini work.

The institutional weight on his bet — [[Sequoia Capital]] and [[Lightspeed Venture Partners]] co-leading the seed, [[NVIDIA]] $250M+, [[Google]] backing despite being his prior employer, the [[UK Sovereign AI Fund]] and [[British Business Bank]] making this the UK's flagship sovereign-AI position — signals that the market is willing to underwrite a pure-RL bet at $5.1B post-money.

The [[Founders Pledge]] commitment is notable for what it isn't: a [[Sam Altman|Altman]]-style "I'm not taking equity" governance hedge against shareholder claims. Silver is fully committed via equity but has pre-committed the proceeds to charity. The structure removes the founder-enrichment narrative that has shadowed [[OpenAI]]'s for-profit conversion (currently in jury trial — see [[Elon Musk]] v [[OpenAI]]).

## Research thesis

Silver's public framing of the [[Ineffable Intelligence]] thesis: current frontier models are bottlenecked by the volume and quality of human-generated internet text. A "superlearner" trained purely through interaction with environments — no pre-training on human text, no human demonstrations — would not have that ceiling. The path is methodologically descended from [[AlphaZero]]: tabula-rasa learning that finds optima beyond human strategy.

The open empirical question is whether environments rich enough to produce general intelligence can be specified. [[AlphaZero]] worked because Go and Chess have crisp reward signals and full observability. General intelligence does not — but Silver's bet is that the right substrate (likely simulated, possibly multi-agent) plus enough compute closes the gap.
