## What it tracks

The IMA-B is the ANBIMA index of all outstanding IPCA-linked Treasury bonds (NTN-B), across the maturity spectrum. IMAB11 gives one-ticket exposure to real (inflation-protected) Brazilian rates at a mid-to-long average duration — the index version of "own the whole NTN-B curve."

## Use case

The inflation-duration sleeve of a Brazilian fixed-income allocation — a liquid, low-cost way to hold IPCA+ duration without buying and rolling individual NTN-Bs. Pairs with the short ([[B5P211]]) and long ([[IB5M11]]) IMA-B ETFs to dial duration up or down.

## Correlation

Unlike the cash ETF, IMAB11 carries real Brazil-rate beta — 0.39 daily-return correlation to [[EWZ]] over two years — because IPCA duration and Brazilian equities both ride the Selic cycle. It is not a diversifier from the equity book. See [[Brazilian fixed-income ETFs]].
