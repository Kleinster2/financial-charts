**ArcelorMittal** is the world's largest steelmaker by output, listed on the NYSE as MT. The company was formed in 2006 through the merger of Arcelor (European steelmaker) and Mittal Steel (founded by Lakshmi Mittal), creating a global giant with operations across Europe, the Americas, Africa, and Asia. CEO Aditya Mittal (Lakshmi's son) has led the company since 2021.

ArcelorMittal produces ~70 million tonnes of crude steel annually and operates in over 60 countries. The company runs ArcelorMittal Kryvyi Rih, the largest steel plant in [[Ukraine]] — which employed ~26,000 workers pre-war but had shrunk to ~12,000 by 2025, with 2,600 employees sent to the front. The Ukraine exposure is a recurring headline risk but represents a small fraction of group output.

The strategic pivot is toward decarbonization: ArcelorMittal is investing in hydrogen-based DRI (direct reduced iron) and EAF (electric arc furnace) technology to reduce carbon intensity. The company also has a significant presence in [[India]] through a joint venture with [[Nippon Steel]], targeting India's infrastructure buildout as a growth engine. Market cap fluctuates around $20-25B, heavily cyclical and correlated to Chinese steel demand and European industrial activity.
