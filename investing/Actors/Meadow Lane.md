## Concept

Curated grab-and-go gourmet grocer — not a traditional supermarket. Chef-driven prepared foods from an on-site kitchen, plus highly curated packaged goods (one brand per category, selected as "best in class").

Format:
- Ground floor: 2,000 sq ft retail
- Basement: 2,000 sq ft production kitchen (runs 24/7)
- Designed by Brooklyn-based Sarah Carpenter
- Art program curated by Sophia Cohen of Siren Projects
- Custom HVAC scent, DJ-curated music, seasonal floral installations

| Item | Price |
|------|-------|
| Average prepared meal | $22-24 |
| Salads | $21 |
| Chicken nuggets | $15 |
| Juices | $15 |
| Signature olive oil (150-year-old trees) | $65 |
| Grapes | $12 |
| Eggs | $18 |
| Siberian caviar | $625-700 |

Named after Meadow Lane in Southampton — the Hamptons' "Billionaire Row" — where the Nussdorf family has a home.

## Family backing

The Nussdorf family founded [[Quality King Distributors]] in Queens in 1961 — a gray-market distributor of health, beauty, and pharmaceutical products. Bernard and Ruth Nussdorf started it; son Glenn Nussdorf is now CEO/Chairman.

Forbes ranked the Nussdorf family among the 150 richest families in America (~$1.6B combined net worth as of 2014). The family also operates the Nussdorf Family Foundation (~$12.6M in assets, based in West Palm Beach).

No formal outside investment round disclosed. Business appears self/family-funded via Spring St. Group (family VC/PE) and Rhizome Ventures.

## Social media strategy

Sammy Nussdorf documented the entire buildout on [[TikTok]] as "Brokeback Contessa" — construction, taste tests, product selection, legal setbacks, budgeting. Built hundreds of thousands of followers before ever opening. Inc. called it "startup TV."

Sold out of product and closed early nearly every day during opening week. Lines wrapped around the block down Harrison Street. Kitchen now runs 24/7 to keep pace with demand.

The "nepo baby" backlash has been significant — Nussdorf has openly embraced it, calling controversy "good for business."

## Expansion

Nussdorf says he's "being hounded by all the developers — New York, Abu Dhabi, Texas, [[Florida]], you name it" but wants to "get my house in order" before expansion. No second location confirmed. Future openings would be documented on [[TikTok]].

## Competitive landscape

The NYC premium grocer wave — all filling the [[Dean & DeLuca]] void:

| Store | Location | Positioning |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Meadow Lane** | Tribeca | Prepared foods + curated packaged goods; TikTok-first |
| **[[Erewhon]]** | LA (11 locations); NYC tonic bar via Kith | Ultra-premium organic; ~$253M revenue; [[Stripes LLC\|Stripes]]-backed |
| **Happier Grocery** | Soho | Premium organic; sells $120 jackets; two floors |
| **Rigor Hill** | Manhattan | Farm-to-table cafe/grocer; $48 rotisserie chicken |
| **[[Butterfield Market]]** | Upper East Side | Family-run gourmet market |
| **[[Zabar's]]** | Upper West Side | Iconic legacy NYC gourmet |
| **[[Whole Foods]]** | Multiple NYC | Mainstream premium organic ([[Amazon]]-owned) |

Air Mail framed this as a "war of the gourmet grocers" — grocery stores operating like luxury fashion houses, relying on scarcity, drops, and branding.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Filling proven demand gap (Dean & DeLuca void, [[Erewhon]] has no NYC presence)
- TikTok-native brand with built-in audience
- Opening-week demand validated concept
- Deep family capital (no dilutive funding needed)
- Prepared food model commands higher margins than traditional grocery

**Bear:**
- Single location, no proven scalability
- Founder is 28 with limited operating experience
- "Nepo baby" brand risk — cultural backlash could accelerate
- NYC rent economics brutal for 4,000 sq ft format
- Premium grocery historically hard to scale nationally
