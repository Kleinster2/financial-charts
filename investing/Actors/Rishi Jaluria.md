**Rishi Jaluria** is an [[RBC Capital]] analyst covering enterprise software, one of the more frequently cited voices on cloud infrastructure and SaaS names including [[Oracle]], [[Nutanix]], and other enterprise technology companies.

## Synopsis

Jaluria's coverage universe sits at the intersection of two of the vault's key themes: cloud infrastructure spending and AI-driven enterprise software transformation. His work at [[RBC Capital]] focuses on the companies that are either benefiting from or being disrupted by the shift to cloud-native architectures and AI integration — a coverage area that has become increasingly important as hyperscaler capex flows through the enterprise stack. His March 2026 framing of [[Oracle]]'s restructuring was a notable call that helped institutional investors contextualize Oracle's pivot from legacy database licensing to cloud infrastructure and AI workloads.

Sell-side analysts matter to the vault as attributed sources and market-moving voices. Jaluria's ratings changes and target price adjustments move stocks in the enterprise software space because his coverage combines technical understanding of cloud architecture with financial modeling rigor. Prior to RBC, he covered similar names at other firms, building a track record in the enterprise software vertical that makes his calls carry above-average weight with institutional investors. His views on cloud spending trajectories and AI adoption curves within enterprise software are useful leading indicators for the broader AI infrastructure thesis.
