#actor #energy #utility #datacenter #usa #public

**Dominion Energy** — Powers "Data Center Alley" in northern Virginia (Loudoun County), the world's largest data center cluster. Directly exposed to AI power demand surge and now the pending target in [[NextEra-Dominion merger 2026]].

Dominion is the regulated utility attached to the most strategically important AI-load corridor in the US: northern Virginia and its PJM interconnection queue. The merger agreement with [[NextEra Energy]] makes that geography the prize; Dominion contributes constrained, regulated, data-center-heavy load, while NextEra contributes the generation-development, storage, procurement, and balance-sheet machine that might satisfy it.

## What makes Dominion, Dominion

Geographic monopoly on the internet's backbone. Loudoun County hosts 300+ data centers carrying ~70% of US internet traffic. [[AWS]] US-East-1, Microsoft, Google, Meta all here. If Dominion has power issues, the internet has issues.

The math: Connected 15 data centers in 2024 totaling 1,000 MW. Plans 15 more in 2025. Since Dec 2024 IRP, data center demand forecast increased 17%; by May 2026 [[Reuters]] and Utility Dive put contracted data-center capacity around 51 GW. PJM auction prices up 833% for 2025-2026 delivery year due to AI demand. Virginia energy demand projected to rise 183% by 2040.

## New data center rate class (Nov 2025)

Virginia SCC approved new electricity rate class for large-scale AI data centers, effective January 2027:

| Requirement | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|
| Distribution/transmission demand | Pay for 85% of contracted |
| Generation demand | Pay for 60% of contracted |

Designed to protect residential ratepayers from rising costs as DC expansion accelerates.

## NextEra merger agreement (May 18, 2026)

[[Dominion Energy]] agreed to combine with [[NextEra Energy]] in an all-stock transaction; see [[NextEra-Dominion merger 2026]]. Dominion shareholders would receive 0.8138 NEE shares for each D share, own about 25.5% of the combined company, continue receiving Dominion's current quarterly dividend through closing, and receive a one-time $360M cash payment at close.

The merger turns Dominion's data-center exposure into the strategic prize. Dominion brings the regulated utility serving Virginia's data-center corridor and a large [[PJM]] pipeline; NextEra brings scale in generation development, storage, procurement, and capital access. The open question is regulatory: how Virginia, North Carolina, and South Carolina handle data-center cost allocation, bill credits, and ordinary ratepayer protection while the combined company builds for AI load.

## Power constraints

[[Ashburn]]/NoVA is power constrained:
- New projects delayed 3-5 years for grid connection
- Transmission upgrades slow (regulatory + construction)
- Pushing new DCs to other regions
- Grid upgrades require $28.3B+ in transmission alone

Dominion's limits = [[Ashburn]]'s limits = the internet's limits.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Geographic monopoly on Data Center Alley
- 47.2 GW contracted pipeline (decades of growth)
- Regulated returns on massive capex
- ~5% dividend yield
- Essential infrastructure — no substitute
- Rate class protects other ratepayers

Bear:
- Execution risk on grid upgrades ($28-270B)
- Regulatory lag (SCC rejected $257M of revenue request)
- Capital intensity (constant equity/debt raises)
- Interest rate sensitivity
- DCs may shift to other regions if delays persist
