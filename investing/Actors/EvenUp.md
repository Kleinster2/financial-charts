# EvenUp

## Synopsis

[[EvenUp]] is a US legal-AI startup focused on personal-injury (PI) plaintiff workflows — demand-letter generation, claims valuation, and case-strategy support — using a proprietary model (Piai™) trained on hundreds of thousands of injury cases and millions of medical records. The October 2025 Series E round (\$150M, valuation north of \$2B, led by Bessemer Venture Partners with REV — [[RELX]]'s venture arm — alongside B Capital, SignalFire, Adams Street, Premji Invest, Bain Capital, HarbourVest, Lightspeed, and Broadlight Capital) doubled the firm's valuation in less than twelve months from the November 2024 Series D round of \$135M at a \$1B+ valuation. Total capital raised stands at ~\$385M.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Big Law transformation]] thread. EvenUp sits in **Ring 2** of the three-ring framework documented in [[Big Law transformation]]: private growth-stage AI-legal companies that bypass [[Kirkland & Ellis]]-style scale economics by attacking specific legal practice areas with model-driven workflow products. EvenUp's PI-plaintiff focus puts it on the *opposite side* of the elite-corporate-law cohort that the rest of the Big Law transformation note covers — the firm's customers are PI plaintiff lawyers, not Am Law 100 firms — but the structural thesis is the same: legal-services revenue is being unbundled by AI tooling at the practice-area level, and Ring 2 valuations price in continued market-share capture from incumbent legal-service models.

## Product — Claims Intelligence Platform

EvenUp's core product is the *Claims Intelligence Platform™*, powered by the proprietary *Piai™* model. The platform automates and accelerates the demand-letter and claims-valuation workflow that is the operational core of plaintiff-side PI law:

- *Demand-letter generation.* Translates medical records, accident reports, and case files into structured demand letters at sub-hour turnaround. Replaces a hand-craft workflow that historically anchored small-firm overhead.
- *Claims valuation.* Pattern-match against the firm's case-history dataset (hundreds of thousands of resolved cases) to produce settlement-value estimates at the input level of the demand-letter generator.
- *Case-strategy decision support.* Identifies precedent matches, flags weak documentation, and surfaces case-pattern signals from the resolved-case base.

The dataset network effect — every resolved case adds to the training base — is a Ring-2 / startup-stage moat that the firm references in marketing as the proprietary durable advantage.

## Series E (October 7, 2025)

The Series E round closed October 7, 2025 at $2B+ post-money valuation, doubling the November 2024 Series D valuation in less than twelve months. The round structure and lead-investor identity carry several investing-vault signals:

- *RELX participation.* REV — the VC arm of [[RELX]], parent of [[LexisNexis]] Legal & Professional — joined the round. [[RELX]] / [[LexisNexis]] is itself a Ring 1 listed legal-tech proxy in the [[Big Law transformation]] framework, vulnerable to AI substitution of legal-research and content-licensing revenue. REV's EvenUp position is best read as a defensive-flank investment: a Ring 1 incumbent buying a stake in a Ring 2 disruptor whose product overlaps with adjacent LexisNexis workflow categories.
- *Bessemer lead with mainline VC and growth participants.* Bessemer Venture Partners as Series E lead pairs with B Capital, SignalFire, Adams Street, Premji Invest, Bain Capital, HarbourVest, Lightspeed, and Broadlight Capital. The line-up reads as a syndicate posturing for a 2027-28 IPO window.
- *Article framing.* Fortune, Bloomberg Law, Crunchbase, and Above the Law / Artificial Lawyer coverage all framed the round as the lead indicator for legal-tech VC funding hitting a record high in 2025-26 — i.e., EvenUp is read by the market as the canonical Ring 2 AI-legal name.

## Position in Big Law transformation

Within the three-ring framework documented in [[Big Law transformation]]:

- *Ring 1 (listed legal-tech incumbents):* [[RELX]], [[Thomson Reuters]], [[Wolters Kluwer]], [[Intapp]], [[CS Disco]] — most exposed to AI substitution.
- *Ring 2 (private growth-stage AI-legal):* [[Harvey]] (\$11B 2025 valuation), [[Legora]] (\$5.6B), EvenUp (\$2B+, this note), Hebbia (stale at last public mark).
- *Ring 3 (advisory adjacencies):* deal-data + advisory-boutique cohort.

EvenUp differs from [[Harvey]] / [[Legora]] in its target customer segment. [[Harvey]] and [[Legora]] sell into Am Law 100 corporate firms — they participate in the same elite-NY-corporate market that the rest of the Big Law transformation note covers. EvenUp sells into PI plaintiff firms, a fragmented market that has historically been served by long-tail process-and-pleadings vendors. The plaintiff-side market is *less* concentrated than corporate Big Law and *more* heavily exposed to per-case workflow optimisation, which is why EvenUp's case-volume metric (cases / week) is the relevant scaling KPI rather than fee-revenue or partner-count metrics.

The implication for the broader investing thesis: the Big Law transformation cycle's AI-pressure on legal-services pricing reaches the corporate-side market through Ring 1 incumbents and Ring 2 corporate-AI tools (Harvey, Legora), but reaches the plaintiff-side market through Ring 2 PI-vertical tools (EvenUp). Both ends of the legal-services market are being repriced by AI-driven workflow substitution; EvenUp is the cleanest pure-play exposure to the plaintiff side.

## Trajectory and forward questions

- *2027-28 IPO window.* The Series E syndicate composition (Bessemer + multi-stage growth investors + strategic from REV) and the case-volume run-rate scaling (10,000 cases / week, doubled in 6 months) both point to public-listing optionality in the 2027-28 window. A successful IPO at the \$2B+ entry valuation would mark a Ring 2 IPO comparable in significance to a hypothetical Harvey or Legora listing.
- *RELX overlap risk.* If [[LexisNexis]] product strategy increasingly emphasises plaintiff-PI workflow (rather than only Big Law corporate research), the REV stake in EvenUp becomes ambiguous: simultaneous defence of the corporate-side LexisNexis business and offence into the PI-side market. Either RELX vertically integrates EvenUp (acquisition) or REV's stake stays as a pure-financial bet on a competitor.
- *Plaintiff-bar adoption ceiling.* The PI plaintiff bar has historically been slow to adopt vendor technology beyond core practice-management software. EvenUp's near-doubling in 6 months suggests the adoption cycle is accelerating, but the upper bound of plaintiff-side AI workflow penetration is unobservable from public data.
