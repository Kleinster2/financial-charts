**Femsa** (Fomento Economico Mexicano) is a Mexican multinational conglomerate and the largest [[Coca-Cola]] bottler in the world by volume through its subsidiary Coca-Cola FEMSA (KOF). The company also operates OXXO, Latin America's largest convenience store chain with over 21,000 locations across [[Mexico]] and increasingly in [[Brazil]], [[Colombia]], and other LatAm markets. Headquartered in Monterrey, Femsa is one of Mexico's most important corporate names, listed on the NYSE as FMX and the BMV as FEMSAUBD.

The OXXO franchise is arguably the more interesting asset than the bottling operation. The stores function as neighborhood financial services points (bill payments, cash deposits, mobile top-ups) in a market where banking penetration remains low. OXXO's financial services play through Spin by OXXO (digital wallet) positions Femsa at the intersection of convenience retail and fintech — a combination that mirrors the Southeast Asian super-app model but built on physical stores rather than mobile apps.

Femsa went through a portfolio simplification in 2023, divesting its stake in [[Heineken]] (received when Femsa sold its FEMSA Cerveza beer unit in 2010) and trimming other non-core holdings. The strategy now centers on Coca-Cola FEMSA, OXXO/Proximity Americas, and digital/financial services. Revenue is approximately $35-40B annually.
