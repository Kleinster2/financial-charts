Cao Hui is the chairman of [[Fuyao Glass Industry Group]] since October 2025, succeeding his father [[Cao Dewang]] in a planned generational transition at the world's largest automotive glass manufacturer. The handover came as Fuyao faced growing complexity from EV transition demand (lighter, larger windshields with integrated sensors), US-China trade friction affecting its American operations, and the need to expand in [[India]] and Southeast Asia to serve automakers diversifying supply chains away from [[China]].

Cao Hui joined Fuyao in the early 2000s and held operational roles across the company's manufacturing and international expansion efforts before being groomed for the chairmanship. His elevation signals continuity — Fuyao remains a founder-family-controlled company — but also raises the succession risk common to Chinese manufacturing dynasties: whether the second generation can maintain the founder's relationships with government, customers, and capital markets.
