**Geoffroy van Raemdonck** — Belgian-American luxury retail executive. CEO of [[Exemplar Luxury Group]] (formerly [[Saks Global]]) since 2024. Guided [[Neiman Marcus]] through its 2020 bankruptcy, then steered the combined Saks-Neiman entity through its 2026 Chapter 11 and emergence.

## Career arc

- CEO of [[Neiman Marcus]] from 2018
- Steered NMG through [[Neiman Marcus bankruptcy 2020|2020 COVID bankruptcy]] — emerged with restructured but not eliminated debt
- Continued as CEO of NMG under Ares/CPPIB creditor ownership
- 2024: [[Saks-Neiman merger]] created [[Saks Global]] — van Raemdonck named CEO of combined entity
- Jan 14, 2026: [[Richard Baker]] replaced as CEO; van Raemdonck retained through [[Saks bankruptcy]]
- Jun 26, 2026: Emerged as CEO of renamed [[Exemplar Luxury Group]]

## Why he matters

Van Raemdonck is the through-line of the Saks-Neiman saga — the only executive to lead both companies through both restructurings. His survival as CEO across ownership changes (Ares/CPPIB → Saks/HBC → creditors post-2026) signals creditor confidence in his luxury-merchant operating thesis over financial-engineering approaches.
