**BambooHR** is a privately held human resources information system (HRIS) platform targeting small and mid-sized businesses with 50-1,000 employees, offering core HR functions including onboarding, payroll, performance management, time tracking, and employee data management.

## Synopsis

BambooHR carved out a defensible position in the SMB HR software market by being opinionated about simplicity — the product does fewer things than enterprise platforms like [[Workday]] or [[SAP SuccessFactors]] but does them with dramatically less implementation friction. Founded in 2008 in Lindon, Utah, the company has grown to serve over 33,000 organizations without taking the typical VC hypergrowth path. BambooHR raised a growth equity round in 2022 but has otherwise operated relatively capital-efficiently, relying on product-led growth and a self-serve sales motion that keeps customer acquisition costs low.

The competitive landscape has intensified: [[Rippling]] attacks from the "everything platform" angle (HR + IT + finance), [[Gusto]] competes in the micro-business segment (sub-50 employees) with payroll-first positioning, and [[Deel]] has captured the remote/global employer-of-record niche. BambooHR's moat is stickiness — once a company migrates its employee records, org charts, and performance review workflows into BambooHR, switching costs are high even if a competitor offers more features. The private status means limited financial visibility, but the company is widely estimated at $1-2B in enterprise value based on comparable SaaS multiples applied to its estimated $200-300M ARR range.
