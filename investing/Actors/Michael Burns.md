**Michael Burns** is the vice chairman of [[Lionsgate]], its longtime dealmaker since 2000, and the investment-banker-turned-executive who engineered the studio's major acquisitions and now champions its generative-AI strategy.

## Career

Burns spent his pre-Lionsgate career in finance — nine years at Shearson/American Express, then nine years running Prudential Securities' Los Angeles investment banking office, specializing in raising equity for the media and entertainment industry (1991–2000). He joined the [[Lionsgate]] board in 1999 and became vice chairman in March 2000.

As CEO [[Jon Feltheimer]]'s dealmaking counterpart, Burns orchestrated the acquisitions that scaled the studio — the $412.5M buyout of Summit Entertainment (2012) and the $4.4B purchase of Starz (2016) — over a tenure that turned a fledgling independent into a diversified global company. He co-founded the Hollywood Stock Exchange and Novica.com.

## At Lionsgate today

Burns is the public face of [[Lionsgate]]'s [[Runway]] generative-AI partnership, telling investors AI could save the studio "tens of millions" annually, and is the executive most associated with the company's M&A optionality. See [[Lionsgate]] → Runway AI partnership and Management.
