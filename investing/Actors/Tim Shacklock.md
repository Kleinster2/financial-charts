# Tim Shacklock

Tim Shacklock is a British investment banker, founder and chairman of [[Gleacher Shacklock]] (2003). Previously vice-chairman of Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein, the European investment bank formed from the 2000 merger of Dresdner Bank's IB arm with Wasserstein Perella, where he led UK corporate broking and M&A advisory. Earlier career at [[Kleinwort Benson]] as head of M&A from 1988.

Shacklock specialises in UK contested M&A and strategic corporate finance advice, with a client list concentrated in [[FTSE 100]] industrial, defence, and transport infrastructure names. Notable mandates include the £10.4B defence of [[GKN]] against [[Melrose Industries]] (2018), the £10.5B combination of [[Just Eat]] and Takeaway.com (2019), and the sale of Stansted Airport to the [[Manchester Airports Group]] consortium (2013).

In April 2026, [[Perella Weinberg Partners]] announced the acquisition of [[Gleacher Shacklock]] in a share-based deal expected to close H2 2026. Shacklock is expected to remain as chairman of the London business during integration.
