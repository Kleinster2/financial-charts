#actor #osat #packaging #test

**ASE Technology (ASX)** — world's largest OSAT (Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Test), beneficiary of AI packaging/test bottlenecks.

## Core thesis

ASE sits at the intersection of two bottlenecks: [[Advanced packaging]] and [[Final test bottleneck]]. As AI chips get bigger and more complex, ASE's capacity becomes more valuable. Not a foundry play — a "picks and shovels" play.

---

## What they do

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Assembly | Wire bonding, flip chip, advanced packaging |
| Test | Final electrical/functional test before ship |
| Materials | Substrates, leadframes |

They don't design or manufacture chips — they package and test what foundries produce.

---

## Why they benefit from AI

- Larger packages (~120mm) → more complex assembly
- Higher heat → advanced test handlers required
- [[HBM]] integration → more packaging steps
- Capacity constrained → pricing power

---

## Advanced packaging revenue doubling (Feb 2026)

ASE expects advanced packaging revenue to **double to $3.2B in 2026**, driven by AI chip demand. Capex increasing from $5.5B (2025) by an undisclosed amount.

**TSMC outsourcing to ASE** — the key catalyst:

JPMorgan (Gokul Hariharan, Feb 6, 2026): TSMC has a **15-20% supply-demand gap** in advanced packaging and will increasingly outsource to ASE as primary beneficiary:

| Product | Timeline | Detail |
|---------|----------|--------|
| [[NVIDIA]] Rubin GPUs | H2 2026 ramp | On-substrate outsourcing from TSMC — primary growth driver |
| [[AMD]] Venice CPUs | H2 2026 start | First full-process packaging products at ASE |
| AMD non-GPU products | 2026-2027 | Multiple products moving to ASE's 2.5D process |
| [[NVIDIA]] Vera CPUs | 2027 | Expected outsourcing to ASE |
| [[Amazon]] Trainium3 ASICs | 2027 | Early discussions indicate potential share gains |
| Revenue from full-process | 2026 | $300M-$400M, with further growth in 2027 |

Hariharan: "TSMC would be increasingly willing to give up more of the lower-end CoWoS processes to ASE in 2027-2028, as it readies to migrate to 3D SoICs and brings up CoPoS in 2028 for Nvidia."

**Expansion strategy:** "We are building factories from scratch. We're also acquiring existing factories from our partners, even with cleanrooms already installed." — CEO Tien Wu, Feb 5, 2026.

ASE is also expanding into **co-packaged optics (CPO)** for AI data centers — Wu called it a "paradigm shift."

*Source: EE Times, JPMorgan (Hariharan report, Feb 6, 2026)*

---

## Acquiring Taiwan panel fabs (2025-2026)

ASE is one of several semiconductor companies buying old LCD panel fabs from dying Taiwanese panel makers. The fabs have existing cleanrooms, power, water treatment, and waste management — building equivalent greenfield facilities takes 3-5 years.

Innolux's Fab 5 (a large 5th-gen LCD fab, closed summer 2025) is reportedly being eyed by ASE and other OSAT firms, with Micron removed from the shortlist after it bought a Powerchip fab in Miaoli for $1.8B. Formal announcement pending board approval.

See [[Taiwan panel-to-packaging conversion]] for the broader trend.

*Sources: TrendForce (Feb 2026), Digitimes (Mar 2026)*
