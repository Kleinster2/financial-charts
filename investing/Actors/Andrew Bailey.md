**Andrew Bailey** — Governor of the [[Bank of England]] since March 2020. Previously CEO of the [[Financial Conduct Authority]] (2016-2020) and a career Bank of England official (1985-2016). His 2026 policy problem is the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran conflict]] energy shock: UK [[Inflation|inflation]] moved back above target just as domestic growth weakened, forcing Bailey to defend the 2% anchor without treating every first-round energy price move as a reason to tighten.

## Inflation-target defense (May-Jun 2026)

Bailey's May 29 Reykjavik speech and June 2 House of Lords evidence session are a paired signal. The speech gave the framework: the BoE can look through the direct effect of higher energy prices when tightening would simply create output volatility after the shock has passed, but it has to respond if inflation expectations, wages, or broader price-setting make the shock persistent. The Lords appearance gave the political boundary: [[Reuters]] reported that Bailey rejected raising the UK inflation target to 3% and said the public needs confidence that the 2% target is real.

This shifts the Bailey note from the April "markets have gone too far" pushback to a more durable reaction function. He is not saying the BoE is dovish. He is saying the target remains fixed while the speed of return depends on the nature of the shock. The watch items are inflation expectations, wage settlements, energy prices, and whether the June 18 MPC decision keeps Bank Rate at 3.75% or validates the market's residual hike risk.

Sources: Bank of England, "Monetary Policy in an unpredictable world", May 29 2026; UK Parliament, House of Lords Economic Affairs Committee evidence session, Jun 2 2026; [[Reuters]] via MarketScreener, Jun 2 2026.
