# Changi Airport

[[Singapore]]'s international airport and [[Southeast Asia]]'s busiest air cargo hub, operated by the state-owned Changi Airport Group. A critical node in global supply chains connecting Asian manufacturing to Western consumers.

## Synopsis

Changi Airport handled ~2.1M tonnes of cargo in 2025 (+15% YoY), ranking 9th globally by international airfreight volume. The airport's strategic value extends beyond throughput -- its location at the crossroads of Indian Ocean and Pacific shipping lanes makes it the natural air-sea transshipment hub for time-sensitive cargo moving between [[China]], [[Southeast Asia]], and global markets. The Terminal 5 expansion and Changi East Industrial Zone, targeted for completion by mid-2030s, will increase cargo capacity from 3M to 5.4M tonnes/year -- a bet that air cargo volumes will continue growing as e-commerce and semiconductor supply chains demand faster transit times.

For investors, Changi matters as infrastructure that enables rather than directly profits -- the airport group is state-owned and not publicly traded, but its capacity expansion directly benefits [[Singapore]] Airlines, DHL, FedEx, and the logistics companies that operate out of its free trade zones. The airport also anchors Singapore's position as the premier wealth management hub in Asia -- high-net-worth individuals and family offices choose [[Singapore]] partly because Changi provides direct connectivity to virtually every major financial center. The Terminal 5 project is one of Singapore's largest infrastructure investments ever, reflecting the government's view that aviation connectivity is an existential national asset for a city-state with no hinterland.
