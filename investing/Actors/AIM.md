AIM — the Alternative Investment Market, the [[LSE]]'s junior market for smaller and growth companies. Launched June 1995, AIM offers a lighter-touch regulatory regime than the Main Market: no minimum market cap, no minimum free float, no trading-record requirement, and admission governed by a nominated adviser ("Nomad") rather than direct exchange vetting. Operated by [[LSEG]].

AIM matters to the [[Pension reform]] / UK capital-markets reform thread because it is the structural layer where the [[UK]]'s small-cap and growth-company listing pipeline lives — and it has been shrinking. Company count has fallen from a ~1,700 peak (2007) to roughly 600 by 2026. The [[Rachel Reeves]] government's Autumn 2024 Budget cut the inheritance-tax Business Relief on AIM shares from 100% to 50% effective April 2026, removing part of the tax incentive that had supported AIM valuations and liquidity — a move in tension with the broader reform goal of deepening [[UK]] equity markets.
