# Tony Wu

Co-founder of [[xAI]], [[Elon Musk]]'s AI lab, removed from leadership in a management overhaul around February 2026. His departure was part of a broader pattern of Musk consolidating control over [[xAI]] as the company scaled toward its Colossus supercomputer buildout and competed with [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]].

## Synopsis

Wu was among the original co-founders of [[xAI]] when Musk launched the company in July 2023, recruited alongside other high-profile AI researchers from [[Google]] DeepMind and the University of Toronto. The founding team was assembled to build Grok, [[xAI]]'s conversational AI model, and to challenge the incumbents in frontier AI development. Wu's specific technical contributions have not been publicly detailed at the same level as some co-founders, but the founding team collectively brought expertise in large language models, reinforcement learning, and distributed systems.

His removal in early 2026 fits a familiar Musk pattern: founding teams at [[Tesla]], [[SpaceX]], and [[Neuralink]] have all experienced similar purges as companies transition from startup to scale. At [[xAI]], the transition involved moving from research lab to a company operating one of the largest GPU clusters in the world (Colossus, 100K+ [[H100]] GPUs in Memphis). The leadership change signals that [[xAI]]'s priorities shifted from pure research to engineering execution and compute scaling — a phase that typically favors operators over researchers.
