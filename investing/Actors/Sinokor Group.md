# Sinokor Group

Seoul-based shipping firm. Cited by [[Breakwave Advisors]] and other market analysts as having amassed a large concentrated share of global crude tanker capacity in the run-up to the 2026 [[Strait of Hormuz]] crisis — pushing up costs as charterers scrambled for vessels and raising market concerns over concentrated ownership of compliant tonnage (Bloomberg, April 2026).

## Market role

Sinokor's capacity accumulation is one of three structural tightness drivers [[John Kartsonas]] cites for the 2026 tanker rate rally:

1. Concentrated ownership — Sinokor's large share creates pricing power in the compliant (Western-insured, non-sanctioned) fleet
2. Sanctions on [[Russia]] and [[Iran]] — shrank the pool of compliant tankers, forcing Western charterers into the narrower compliant pool where Sinokor operates
3. Fleet aging / underinvestment — years of low ordering left the compliant base constrained

Together these made the market tight *before* the first Hormuz missile was fired.

*Source: Bloomberg, April 2026.*
