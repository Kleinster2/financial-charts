OCC — Office of the Comptroller of the Currency. US federal bureau within the [[Treasury Department]] that charters, regulates and supervises national banks and federal savings associations. Independent regulator with primary jurisdiction over the largest US banks (which mostly hold national charters), and gatekeeper for the national-charter applications that determine whether non-US fintechs can operate as real US banks.

Pressure point for non-US fintechs entering the US: [[Monzo]]'s 2019 banking-licence application was rejected, leading to its retreat to a waitlist and ultimately full US exit (announced 2026). [[Revolut]] also operates in the US on a partner-bank model rather than a national charter, sidestepping OCC scrutiny.
