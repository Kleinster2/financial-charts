## Six predictions on energy reshaping (FT, Apr 7)

Interview with *Financial Times* (Energy Source, Malcolm Moore). Drew explicit parallels to the 1970s oil shocks and predicted six structural shifts from the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran war]]: (1) [[Nuclear renaissance|nuclear/SMR acceleration]] — SMRs could arrive before mid-2030s; (2) renewables push; (3) [[EV transition|EV adoption surge]] in Asia; (4) [[Natural gas]] reputation damage — "long shadow" on gas-as-bridge-fuel pitch; (5) higher [[Hormuz Permanent Risk Premium|geopolitical risk premium]] — importers now assess who sells, not just price; (6) [[Coal]] comeback — 1970s coal capacity rose 50% in a decade, pattern likely to repeat.

Also urged countries not to hoard oil and fuel — resource nationalism worsens shortages.

Historical reference: the [[UK]] fast-tracked North Sea oil licences after 1973, going from "virtually nothing" to 6M bpd in 20 years.

See [[IEA#Birol: six predictions on energy reshaping (FT, Apr 7)|IEA § six predictions]] for full detail and quotes.

*Source: FT Energy Source, Apr 7, 2026 (Malcolm Moore)*
