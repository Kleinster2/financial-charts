**GE Vernova** — gas turbines, grid gear, and electrification equipment supplier sitting at one of the tightest choke points in the AI power buildout.

GE Vernova has become one of the cleanest public-market ways to express the power side of the AI capex cycle. Hyperscalers, utilities, and merchant developers all need firm power and grid equipment faster than the supply chain can deliver it. That scarcity showed up clearly in the Apr. 22, 2026 quarter: backlog jumped by $13 billion to $163 billion, management pulled forward its $200 billion backlog target to 2027, and CEO Scott Strazik said combined gas-turbine backlog plus slot reservation agreements should reach at least 110 GW by year-end.

## Why GE Vernova matters

| Bottleneck | Why it matters |
|------------|----------------|
| Gas turbines | The fastest scalable route to firm power for AI campuses is still gas, and turbine manufacturing slots are scarce |
| Grid equipment | Even when generation is solved, interconnection still requires switchgear, transformers, and substation work |
| Installed base | GE Vernova is selling into utilities, independent power producers, and hyperscaler-adjacent projects at the same time |
| Manufacturing slots | Backlog plus slot reservations now function like a scarce asset class of their own |

The key point is not only that AI data centers need more electricity. It is that they need electricity on a timetable the grid cannot natively deliver, which pushes buyers toward the same short list of turbine and electrification vendors.

## Q1 2026 guide raise and backlog acceleration (Apr. 22, 2026)

| Metric | New | Prior |
|--------|-----|-------|
| 2026 revenue guide | $44.5 billion to $45.5 billion | $44.0 billion to $45.0 billion |
| 2026 adj. EBITDA margin guide | 12% to 14% | 11% to 13% |
| Backlog | $163 billion | $150 billion pre-quarter |
| Backlog target | $200 billion by 2027 | $200 billion by 2028 |

Quarter highlights from Reuters and company disclosures:
- Power segment core profit rose 57% to $811 million.
- Electrification core profit more than doubled to $528 million.
- Management expects higher gas-power revenue in the second half as turbine shipments increase and capacity ramps toward nearly 20 GW.
- The company now expects at least 110 GW of combined gas-turbine backlog and slot reservation agreements by year-end.
- Global tariffs are expected to cost $250 million to $350 million in 2026.

The offset remains wind. Q1 wind revenue fell 23% on weaker onshore deliveries, and segment losses widened to about $382 million. So the current GE Vernova story is not a clean company-wide margin expansion story. It is a Power plus Electrification strength story that is strong enough to drag the whole company upward despite wind still absorbing capital and management attention.

## Evolution

GE Vernova is the former GE power, renewables, and electrification portfolio separated into a standalone public company in April 2024. The spin changed the market lens. Instead of sitting inside a conglomerate discount, the power franchise now trades on its own merits, which in 2026 means the market can directly capitalize its role in the AI electricity bottleneck.

## Ratings history

This note does not yet have a full standalone agency-by-agency ratings timeline. The immediate analytical point is simpler: investors are underwriting GE Vernova as a strategic bottleneck in AI power and grid equipment even while Wind remains structurally challenged.

## Investment case

Bull case: backlog visibility, turbine-slot scarcity, electrification upside, and a cleaner post-spin multiple.

Bear case: wind losses can still consume attention and margin, tariffs are a real 2026 earnings headwind, and the stock now discounts a lot of the AI-power enthusiasm.
