**Toby Pohlen** is a former [[DeepMind]] researcher who briefly led [[xAI]]'s Macrohard project before departing after just 16 days, becoming a symbol of the talent retention crisis at [[Elon Musk|Musk]]'s AI venture.

## Synopsis

Pohlen's background at [[DeepMind]] made him a credible pick to lead Macrohard, [[xAI]]'s coding product intended to compete with [[GitHub Copilot]] and [[Cursor]]. [[Elon Musk|Musk]] assigned him the role in late February 2026, but Pohlen left roughly 16 days later -- an extraordinarily short tenure that suggests either irreconcilable differences over product direction or working conditions that proved untenable. His departure was part of a broader exodus at xAI, including co-founder [[Zihang Dai]] around the same time.

The Pohlen episode matters because it illustrates [[xAI]]'s core talent problem. Despite Musk's ability to attract top researchers with the promise of massive compute (the Memphis Colossus cluster), the company cannot retain them. DeepMind-caliber researchers leaving after two weeks signals something structurally wrong -- either the management environment, the product vision, or both. [[Ashok Elluswamy]], redeployed from [[Tesla]] to reboot Macrohard after Pohlen's exit, represents Musk's fallback strategy: staffing xAI with people already locked into the Musk ecosystem rather than competing on the open talent market.
