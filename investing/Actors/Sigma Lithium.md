**Sigma Lithium** (NASDAQ / TSXV: SGML) — Canadian-listed lithium producer operating [[Brazil]]'s flagship lithium project, **Grota do Cirilo** in the Vale do Jequitinhonha, Minas Gerais. First and largest operator in what [[Brazil]] markets as its "Lithium Valley." Differentiated by a low-cost, low-emissions spodumene-concentrate process branded "Quintuple Zero Green Lithium" (no tailings dams, no hazardous chemicals, no fossil-fuel drying, no carbonate processing, no evaporation ponds).

## Operations — Grota do Cirilo

The project sits in Araçuaí/Itinga, Minas Gerais — the heart of [[Brazil]]'s Eastern Brazilian Pegmatite Province.

| Phase | Capacity | Status |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Phase 1 | 270,000 tpy spodumene concentrate | Operating since April 2023 |
| Phase 2 | +250,000 tpy | Commissioning Q4 2025, ramp-up 2026 |
| Total | 520,000 tpy | Target run-rate 2026 |

**Production cadence:**
- 2Q 2025: 68,368 tonnes produced (ahead of target)
- H2 2025: on-target production, continued cost reduction, balance-sheet deleveraging
- Q1 2026: resumption of premium lithium-oxide concentrate sales, industrial production cadence
- 2026 run-rate guidance: ~240,000 tonnes (12-month annualized) building toward 520,000 (24-month annualized)

Sigma's "Quintuple Zero" process is a marketing wrapper for genuine differentiation: DMS-based (dense media separation), no tailings dam, no chemical reagents, renewable grid electricity, high concentrate grade (6% Li₂O). That matters because ESG-concerned European auto-OEMs and US IRA-compliant buyers have been the marginal premium buyer.

The revolver structure is notable — production-tied prepayment is an alternative to equity raises for a company still ramping. It converts offtake commitments into working capital, reducing dilution risk during the Phase 2 ramp. The BNDES loan reflects Brazil's federal development bank explicitly backing the Lithium Valley.

## The STF / community risk

Sigma has the deepest community-conflict exposure of any Brazilian lithium producer:

| Issue | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Environmental damage suit | Prosecutors filed; unresolved |
| STF case on project legality | Active |
| Water supply impacts | Disputed; local farmer organizing |
| Quilombola consultation | Allegations that no consultation occurred |

The legal-regulatory risk is the idiosyncratic part of the SGML investment case. A hostile STF ruling could force operational changes or shutdown; the Lula administration has generally backed mining expansion (Vale do Jequitinhonha job creation is politically useful), but federal/state/judicial alignment is not guaranteed.

## Strategic context

Sigma sits at the intersection of three flows the vault tracks:

1. **[[Brazil]]'s mineral-diplomacy leverage** — Sigma is the poster-child project for [[Brazil]]'s emergence as the third axis (beyond the [[Lithium Triangle]] and Australia) in global lithium supply. See [[Critical minerals]] and the Brazil cross-vault link for the mineral-diplomacy sequencing with [[India]] and the US.
2. **The concentrate-vs-processing gap** — Sigma exports spodumene concentrate to Chinese converters. The "commodity trap" critique applies: Brazil captures mining value but not the battery-grade lithium hydroxide margin. [[AMG]]'s Mibra-to-Bitterfeld route and Sigma's China-routed concentrate are the two models in play.
3. **Cost-curve positioning** — $440/t CIF China puts Sigma in the bottom half of the global cost curve — competitive with [[SQM]]'s Atacama at current spot ($23,000/t), destructive for the high-cost end ([[Albemarle|ALB]]'s Kemerton, high-cost Australian operators).
