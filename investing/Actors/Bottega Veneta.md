# Bottega Veneta

Italian luxury house known for leather goods and signature intrecciato weave. Part of [[Kering]].

## Business

- **Founded:** 1966 in Vicenza, Italy
- **Owner:** [[Kering]]
- **Signature:** Intrecciato (woven leather) — "When your own initials are enough"
- **Positioning:** Quiet luxury archetype — no visible logos

## Creative Direction

| Director | Tenure | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Louise Trotter | 2024– | Ex-Carven; current creative director |
| Matthieu Blazy | 2021–2024 | Left for [[Chanel]]; critical/commercial success |
| Daniel Lee | 2018–2021 | Revitalized brand, viral Pouch bag |
| Tomas Maier | 2001–2018 | Built quiet luxury DNA |

Blazy was bright spot in Kering portfolio during Gucci struggles. His departure to Chanel a significant loss.

## Product

| Category | Signature |
|----------|-----------|
| Leather goods | Intrecciato bags, Cassette, Pouch |
| Ready-to-wear | Elevated basics, craft-focused |
| Footwear | Lido sandals, boots |

## Positioning

**The quiet luxury archetype** — [[Modern luxury]] before the term existed:
- No logos
- Craft over branding
- "Stealth wealth" aesthetic
- Higher price = in-the-know signaling

## Kering Context

Bottega's stability contrasts with Gucci's struggles. Kering's pivot toward quiet luxury (Blazy era) was validation of brand's original DNA.
