Eight Roads Ventures is the proprietary venture capital arm of [[Fidelity International]], managing ~$11B in AUM across offices in London, Mumbai, Shanghai, and Tokyo. Originally established in 1969 as Fidelity Asia to invest Fidelity's balance sheet capital in Asian growth markets, the firm was one of the earliest institutional VCs in both [[China]] and [[India]] — backing [[Alibaba]] in its early stages and building a deep portfolio across Asian tech. The 2014 rebrand to Eight Roads Ventures reflected a broadening mandate beyond Asia to include global technology, healthcare, and consumer investments.

Unlike typical VC firms, Eight Roads invests off Fidelity's permanent balance sheet rather than raising third-party funds, which means no LP pressure to return capital on a fixed timeline. This structure allows patient capital deployment and longer hold periods. The firm typically invests at Series A through growth stages, writing checks of $10M-$50M. Its Fidelity parentage gives portfolio companies access to one of the world's largest asset managers for later-stage funding and eventual public market support.
