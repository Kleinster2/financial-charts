**Qin Gang** is a former Chinese Foreign Minister (December 2022 -- July 2023), holding the shortest tenure in PRC history. His sudden removal for "lifestyle issues" — an extramarital affair during his US ambassadorship — alongside Defense Minister Li Shangfu's parallel purge, exposed fragility in [[Xi Jinping]]'s personnel selection process.

## Synopsis

Qin's rise was a product of [[Xi Jinping]]'s preference for personal loyalty over institutional credentials. Promoted from spokesperson roles to Ambassador to the United States (2021-2023) and then directly to Foreign Minister — bypassing the normal career progression through regional postings — Qin was seen as Xi's handpicked man for the most important bilateral relationship in the world. His appointment signaled that Xi wanted a more confrontational, personally controlled foreign policy apparatus, sidelining the traditionally cautious Ministry of Foreign Affairs bureaucracy.

The fall was equally revealing. Qin vanished from public view in June 2023, was formally removed in July, and was replaced by veteran diplomat Wang Yi. The stated reason — an extramarital affair with a Chinese journalist in Washington that allegedly produced a child born in the US — was both mundane and devastating for Xi's credibility. The parallel removal of Defense Minister Li Shangfu (for corruption in military procurement) meant that two of Xi's hand-picked senior officials were purged within weeks of each other. For investors pricing China governance risk, the Qin affair confirmed that Xi's centralization of power creates single points of failure: when the paramount leader personally selects officials based on loyalty rather than competence or vetting, the error rate on those selections becomes a systemic vulnerability.
