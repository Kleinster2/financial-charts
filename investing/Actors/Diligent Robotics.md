**Diligent Robotics** — Hospital logistics robot company that built Moxi, an AI-powered mobile manipulator for supply delivery in hospitals. Raised ~$100M, deployed ~100 robots in 25+ hospitals, completed 1.25M+ deliveries. Acquired by [[Serve Robotics]] in Jan 2026 for $29M in stock — a brutal down-exit after $100M+ raised.

---

## Synopsis

Diligent Robotics is a cautionary tale for niche service robotics. The company built a genuinely useful product — Moxi autonomously navigates hospital corridors, rides elevators, opens doors, and delivers supplies to nursing stations, freeing clinical staff from logistical errands. Revenue per hospital ran $200K-$400K annually. The fleet completed 1.25M+ deliveries across 25+ hospitals including Northwestern Medicine, ChristianaCare, and Rochester General.

But $100M in venture capital created expectations that hospital-by-hospital sales couldn't meet. The acquisition by [[Serve Robotics]] (an [[Uber]] sidewalk delivery spinout) valued Diligent's common stock at just $29M — plus up to $5.3M in earnouts. That's a ~70%+ loss on invested capital. [[Tiger Global]], Canaan, and True Ventures were among investors who took the hit.

The lesson: hospital robotics has real demand but punishing unit economics. Hospitals are slow to adopt, procurement cycles are long, and each deployment requires customization. Moxi 2.0 (with [[NVIDIA]] IGX Thor / Blackwell compute, 10x the processing power) was set to ship H1 2026, scaling to 15+ units per hospital site. Whether Serve can execute on that roadmap while integrating the acquisition is an open question.

[[Serve Robotics]] (NASDAQ: SERV) is an [[Uber]] spinout focused on sidewalk delivery robots. The acquisition extends Serve's autonomy platform from outdoor delivery into indoor hospital logistics. Diligent continues as a subsidiary under Andrea Thomaz.

The deal math is stark: $29M for a company that raised $100M. Even with the earnout ($34.3M max), investors lost most of their money. The gap between peak valuation ($150M in 2023) and exit price ($29M in 2026) reflects the difficulty of scaling hospital robotics.

## Investment case

The investment case is now moot (acquired), but the lessons matter for the sector:

- Hospital robotics has genuine demand — 1.25M deliveries proves product-market fit
- But $200K-$400K/hospital/year couldn't support $100M in VC expectations
- Procurement cycles in healthcare are 12-18 months
- Each hospital is a custom deployment
- $100M raised → $29M exit = the niche service robotics trap
