# Danone

French multinational food-products company operating across dairy, plant-based alternatives, specialized nutrition, and water. One of the unexpected beneficiaries of the [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] wave, with double-digit growth in high-protein offerings as weight-loss drug users shift to protein-dense, nutrient-rich foods.

## Synopsis

Danone generates roughly EUR 27B in annual revenue across four divisions: Essential Dairy & Plant-Based (~55% of sales), Specialized Nutrition (~30%, infant formula and medical nutrition), and Waters (~15%, Evian, Volvic). The company went through a messy leadership transition in 2021 when activist investor [[Artisan Partners]] and Bluebell Capital pushed out CEO Emmanuel Faber, who had been criticized for prioritizing ESG over margins. Antoine de Saint-Affrique took over and launched a margin recovery plan focused on fewer SKUs, pricing power, and selective portfolio pruning.

The GLP-1 angle is the most interesting current catalyst. As millions of patients on [[Ozempic]], [[Mounjaro]], and related drugs shift eating patterns toward smaller, protein-rich meals, Danone's high-protein yogurt lines (Oikos, YoPRO) and medical nutrition products (Nutricia) sit directly in the demand path. The company reported double-digit growth in high-protein categories through 2025. The risk is that Danone remains a structurally low-margin business (~14% EBIT margin) in a category where [[Nestle]] and private-label competition cap pricing power. Trades at ~18x earnings on Euronext Paris.
