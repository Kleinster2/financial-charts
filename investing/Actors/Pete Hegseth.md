## Broker's attempted defense ETF purchase (FT, March 30, 2026)

Hegseth's [[Morgan Stanley]] broker contacted [[BlackRock]] in February 2026 about making a multimillion-dollar investment in [[IDEF]] (BlackRock Defense Industrials Active ETF, $3.2bn AUM, Nasdaq-listed). The inquiry came weeks before the US launched [[Operation Epic Fury|military action against Iran]] — a campaign Hegseth helped architect and publicly championed.

IDEF's largest holdings: [[RTX]], [[Lockheed Martin]], [[Northrop Grumman]], [[Palantir]] — all major [[Pentagon]] contractors.

The investment did not go through: IDEF (launched May 2025) was not yet available on [[Morgan Stanley]]'s platform. It is not known whether the broker found an alternative defense-focused fund.

The inquiry was flagged internally at BlackRock.

[[Sean Parnell]], chief Pentagon spokesperson, denied the report on X: "This allegation is entirely false and fabricated. Neither Secretary Hegseth nor any of his representatives approached BlackRock about any such investment." BlackRock and Morgan Stanley declined to comment.

IDEF performance: +28% over past year, but -13% in the past month — the aborted investment would have produced short-term losses.

*Source: FT (Murphy, Agnew, Franklin, Politi — March 30, 2026)*
