**XConn Technologies** is a silicon photonics interconnect company acquired by [[Marvell]] to strengthen its optical interconnect portfolio for AI data center connectivity.

## Synopsis

XConn specialized in chiplet-to-chiplet interconnect technology using silicon photonics -- the critical plumbing that allows multiple chiplets in advanced semiconductor packages to communicate at high bandwidth and low latency. As the semiconductor industry moves toward heterogeneous chiplet architectures (driven by the limits of monolithic scaling), the interconnect layer becomes a bottleneck. XConn's technology addressed this by using optical rather than electrical signaling between chiplets, offering higher bandwidth density and lower power consumption at distances beyond what copper traces can handle.

[[Marvell]]'s acquisition of XConn was part of a broader optical interconnect buildout that also included [[Celestial AI]], another photonics startup. The strategic logic is clear: as AI clusters scale to tens of thousands of GPUs, the data movement problem eclipses the compute problem. [[NVIDIA]]'s NVLink, [[Broadcom]]'s switch ASICs, and Marvell's optical interconnects are all fighting for share of the same dollar -- the cost of connecting accelerators inside and across racks. Marvell is positioning itself as the interconnect fabric provider for custom AI accelerators, particularly for hyperscaler customers building their own silicon. XConn's chiplet interconnect IP complements Marvell's existing portfolio of PAM4 DSPs, coherent optics, and Inphi-derived data center connectivity products.
