# Old Mutual

**Old Mutual** is a South African financial services group offering insurance, banking, and asset management, listed on the JSE as OML.JO.

## Synopsis

Old Mutual is one of Africa's oldest financial institutions (founded 1845 in Cape Town) and the continent's largest insurance and asset management group by AUM. The company spent decades as a London-listed, globally diversified conglomerate before executing a dramatic restructuring in 2018: it demerged [[Quilter]] (UK wealth management), sold its US asset management arm (now Brightstone Capital), and relisted on the JSE as a pure-play African financial services company. The managed separation was designed to unlock value from businesses that had been trading at a conglomerate discount, but the relisted Old Mutual has struggled to re-rate — South African macro headwinds (load-shedding, rand weakness, slow growth) have kept the stock range-bound.

The investment case for Old Mutual is a bet on South African and pan-African financial inclusion. The company serves ~12 million customers across South Africa, East Africa, and other markets through insurance, lending, and savings products. Its Nedbank stake (~20%) provides banking exposure. For the vault, Old Mutual is relevant as a barometer of South African institutional health — its performance tracks the same structural challenges (emigration of skilled workers, fiscal strain, infrastructure decay) that affect the broader JSE. The Quilter spinoff is a useful case study in conglomerate breakups: Quilter has outperformed as a standalone UK wealth manager, validating the strategic logic but also highlighting the South African discount that Old Mutual couldn't escape.
