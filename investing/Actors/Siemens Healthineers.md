**Siemens Healthineers** (Frankfurt: SHL) is a medical technology company majority-owned by [[Siemens]], focused on medical imaging, diagnostics, and advanced therapies. The company is one of the three dominant players in medical imaging alongside [[GE HealthCare]] and [[Philips]].

## Synopsis

Siemens Healthineers was carved out of [[Siemens]]' healthcare division via IPO in March 2018, with Siemens retaining a ~75% stake. The company operates across three segments: Imaging (CT, MRI, X-ray — the core franchise), Diagnostics (lab testing, point-of-care), and Advanced Therapies (image-guided interventional procedures). The 2020 acquisition of Varian Medical Systems for $16.4B added radiation oncology to the portfolio, making Healthineers a full-spectrum cancer treatment platform — from diagnosis (imaging) through treatment planning to radiation delivery.

The separation story is the current investment catalyst. [[Siemens]] has been preparing a full spin-off of its remaining Healthineers stake, with preparations on track as of February 2026. A complete separation would remove the overhang of a controlling shareholder and allow Healthineers to trade on its own fundamentals — which are strong. Revenue runs at approximately EUR 22B, with Imaging generating the highest margins. The AI angle is meaningful: medical imaging is one of the most advanced applications of AI in healthcare, with Healthineers deploying AI-powered diagnostic tools across its installed base of scanners. The company's Erlangen campus houses one of the largest concentrations of medical imaging R&D globally. Competitor dynamics with [[GE HealthCare]] (spun from [[General Electric]] in 2023) and [[Philips]] (recovering from a recall crisis) create a three-player oligopoly with rational pricing.
