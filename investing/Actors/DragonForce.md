#actor #threat-actor #cybercrime #ransomware

**DragonForce** — Ransomware-as-a-Service operation that emerged in 2023 and rose to high public visibility in 2025 as the encryption partner used by [[Scattered Spider]] in the April-May 2025 UK retail wave that hit [[Marks & Spencer]], Co-op, and Harrods. DragonForce's branding origin is contested — the name was previously associated with a Malaysian hacktivist collective active circa 2021-22 — but the 2024-2025 operation is a distinct cybercriminal RaaS program built on a forked version of the leaked LockBit Black ransomware codebase. The group's notable structural innovation is offering a "white-label" affiliate model in which sub-groups can rebrand the DragonForce locker under their own ransomware names, complicating attribution. The arrangement with Scattered Spider was operationally similar to Scattered Spider's prior partnership with [[BlackCat]] in 2023: the access broker supplies social-engineered initial access, the RaaS affiliate handles the encryption phase. DragonForce's M&S engagement — which ultimately produced approximately £300M of operating-profit impact and ~6 weeks of online operational disruption — was one of the highest-impact cybercrime engagements of 2025.
