The Alameda Corridor is a $2.4B, 20-mile freight rail expressway connecting the [[Port of Los Angeles]] and [[Port of Long Beach]] to the transcontinental rail network near downtown LA. Completed in 2002 after decades of planning delays, the project consolidated 90 at-grade rail crossings into a single below-grade trench (the "Mid-Corridor Trench"), eliminating traffic conflicts and cutting transit time from the ports to the rail yards from over two hours to under 45 minutes.

The corridor handles roughly 40% of all containerized imports entering the [[United States]] by rail, making it critical national trade infrastructure. It is operated by the Alameda Corridor Transportation Authority, a joint powers authority of the cities of [[Los Angeles]] and [[Long Beach]]. Financing came primarily from revenue bonds backed by container fees, a [[Department of Transportation]] loan, and port contributions. [[Steve Soboroff]] and [[Philip Anschutz]] were key figures in breaking the multi-decade political deadlock that had stalled construction.
