# Birkenstock

German heritage footwear brand — 250-year-old sandal maker turned global luxury player. IPO October 2023 at $46/share (NYSE: BIRK). €2.1B revenue FY2025 (+18% CC), 32% EBITDA margin. [[L Catterton]] owns ~81%. Closed-toe expansion driving growth beyond sandals.

---

## May 13, 2026 — Q2 FY2026 earnings miss, -12.9% reaction

BIRK closed May 13 at $33.07, down -12.9% (-5.3σ) on the Q2 FY2026 print. Revenue €618.33M came in below consensus (~€625-630M expected), and EPS €0.50 missed the €0.59 consensus by ~15%. Stock made an intraday low of $32.44 — a violent re-rating from the May 12 close of $37.95. May 14 saw a partial recovery to $34.13 (+3.2%); after-hours $34.94.

| Metric | Q2 FY2026 | Q2 FY2025 | Consensus | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | €618.33M | €574.33M | ~€625-630M | Miss vs consensus, +7.7% YoY |
| EPS | €0.50 | €0.55 | €0.59 | Miss vs consensus, -9% YoY |

Headwinds cited by management: FX (USD/EUR), tariffs (Trump trade-policy environment), and broader geopolitical uncertainty. The setup is unusual: a premium-priced consumer-discretionary name that took 13% off in a session that was already negative on macro (CPI shock + Brazil-cluster sell), and where the operating thesis (closed-toe expansion + APAC growth) wasn't the issue — translation effects and external policy noise dominated the headline.

FY2026 full-year guidance reaffirmed despite the miss:
- Revenue growth: +13-15% constant-currency
- Adj. EBITDA margin: 30.0-30.5%
- Adj. EPS: €1.90-2.05

The reaffirmation matters: management is telling investors that the Q2 miss is a translation-effects / one-time-tariff issue, not a demand or pricing problem. Multiple analysts cut price targets in the hours after the print despite the maintained guide, suggesting the sell-side is taking the Q2 print as a leading indicator rather than noise. The -12.9% reaction implies the market priced both the miss and a downward-revision-to-come.

What to watch:
- Q3 FY26 print — first read on whether the Q2 miss was contained or widened
- APAC (China/Japan/Korea) growth rate — if it falters, the structural thesis weakens; FY25 it was the +34% engine
- DTC vs wholesale mix — DTC margin sensitivity to FX/tariffs is different from wholesale
- L Catterton actions — at 81% holder concentration, any secondary offering would compound the move

*Sources: Birkenstock Q2 FY2026 release (May 13, 2026); StockAnalysis.com BIRK; local `prices_long` BIRK May 12-14 verified.*

## Investment thesis

Bull:
- 250-year heritage, iconic brand
- 32% EBITDA margin — luxury-level profitability
- Closed-toe expansion (38% of revenue) extends seasonality
- APAC +34% — massive runway in Asia
- L Catterton/LVMH expertise in brand elevation
- $200M buyback authorized for FY2026

Bear:
- L Catterton controls 81% — limited float
- IPO stumble (-12.6% day one) still remembered
- Fashion risk — sandals could go out of style
- Premium valuation (~5x revenue)
- Currency headwinds (EUR reporting, global sales)
