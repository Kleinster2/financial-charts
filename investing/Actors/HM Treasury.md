HM Treasury — the [[UK]] government's economic and finance ministry. Responsible for public finances, fiscal policy, tax policy, spending control, and financial-services policy. Headed politically by the [[Rachel Reeves|Chancellor of the Exchequer]]; the senior civil servant is the Permanent Secretary. The Treasury is the department behind the [[Pension reform]] package, the Mansion House reform agenda, and the courting of [[UK]] fintech listings.

HM Treasury is the [[UK]] analogue of the US [[Treasury Department]] — it sets the fiscal stance, sponsors financial-services legislation, and is the government voice in any negotiation over capital-markets structure. It works alongside the [[Bank of England]] (monetary policy + prudential regulation via the PRA) and the [[Financial Conduct Authority]] (conduct regulation), but the Treasury sets the policy frame both regulators operate within.
