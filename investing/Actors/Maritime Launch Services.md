**Maritime Launch Services** is the Canadian company developing [[Spaceport Nova Scotia]], a commercial orbital launch complex in Canso, Nova Scotia. It matters for the vault as part of the allied launch-capacity theme: launch infrastructure is becoming a sovereign-access asset, not just a real-estate project.

MLS is an infrastructure option on Canadian sovereign launch access. Its value rises if satellite demand makes non-traditional spaceports strategically useful, but the company still has to convert licensing and partnership announcements into repeatable orbital launch operations.

## Isar Aerospace letter of intent (May 2026)

On May 26, 2026, Maritime Launch Services and [[Isar Aerospace]] signed a letter of intent to advance orbital launch readiness from Nova Scotia. The agreement pairs Isar's Spectrum launch vehicle with Spaceport Nova Scotia, giving Isar a possible North American site for mid- to high-inclination missions while MLS gains a European launch-vehicle partner with an in-house manufacturing model and an existing polar launch site in Norway.

The market read is capacity and redundancy. Spaceports are becoming part of the launch supply chain because demand growth can be constrained by licensing, range scheduling, airspace, security, and pad process even when rockets exist. A Canadian site with German launch-vehicle access fits the same pattern as [[PLD Space]] in Europe: allied governments and operators want ways to reach orbit without concentrating every mission around US launch providers or a small number of traditional ranges.

## Evolution

Maritime Launch Services sits in the non-traditional spaceport category identified by the CSF / Rational Futures launch-capacity report: potentially useful for resilience, but difficult to finance on private launch fees alone. The May 2026 Isar letter of intent is therefore best read as a partner validation signal rather than proof that the site has solved throughput economics.
