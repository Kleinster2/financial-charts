## Business model

Hercules lends to high-growth, venture-backed companies that traditional banks won't touch. The loans are senior secured (first lien), typically 3-4 year terms at interest rates well above prime. Revenue comes from:

1. Interest income on loans (largest component)
2. Fees (origination, unused commitments, prepayment penalties)
3. Warrant gains when portfolio companies IPO or get acquired

As a BDC, Hercules is required to distribute at least 90% of taxable income to shareholders, making it a high-yield income vehicle. The stock trades at a premium or discount to net asset value depending on market conditions.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Largest and most established venture debt specialist — scale and brand moat
- High dividend yield from mandatory BDC distributions
- Portfolio diversification across 700+ companies reduces single-name risk
- Growing market — venture debt demand increasing as companies delay IPOs
- Senior secured loans = first in line in liquidation

Bear:
- Rising interest rates compress demand — borrowers more rate-sensitive
- Default risk in economic downturns — tech/biotech companies can fail rapidly
- SVB collapse created temporary market share gain that may normalize
- Warrant income is lumpy and depends on IPO/M&A market
