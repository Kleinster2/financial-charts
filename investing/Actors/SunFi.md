**SunFi** — Nigerian solar fintech platform that provides financing for distributed solar installations. A [[Delta40]] portfolio company backed by the [[Rockefeller Foundation]]'s catalytic capital.

---

## Synopsis

SunFi addresses the core obstacle to solar adoption in Nigeria: not technology cost (panels are cheap) but financing access. Nigeria has one of the world's worst electricity grids — chronic blackouts push businesses and households to rely on diesel generators, which are expensive and polluting. Solar is the obvious alternative, but most potential customers cannot afford the upfront cost. SunFi provides the credit layer: it finances solar installations for homes and businesses, structuring repayment as monthly installments that are typically lower than what customers were spending on diesel.

The model sits at the intersection of two structural themes that are drawing increasing institutional attention: African distributed energy and climate-linked fintech. SunFi's inclusion in the [[Delta40]] portfolio — a fund backed by the [[Rockefeller Foundation]]'s $20M commitment — signals that it has passed the diligence bar of serious development-finance allocators. The risk profile is classic frontier-market fintech: the unit economics work at small scale, but scaling requires navigating currency volatility (the naira has devalued sharply), payment collection in a largely informal economy, and the regulatory unpredictability of Nigerian state-level governance. For the vault, SunFi is a reference point for the broader African off-grid solar fintech category.
