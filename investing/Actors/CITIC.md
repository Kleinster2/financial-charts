**CITIC** (China International Trust Investment Corporation) is [[China]]'s largest state-owned conglomerate, founded in 1979 by Rong Yiren under [[Deng Xiaoping]]'s reform and opening-up initiative. Originally created as a vehicle to attract foreign investment into China, CITIC has evolved into a sprawling group spanning financial services, resources, manufacturing, engineering, and real estate. The listed subsidiary CITIC Limited (HKG: 0267) serves as the group's primary public market vehicle.

The financial services arm is the most significant: [[CITIC Securities]] (600030.SS / 6030.HK) is China's largest securities firm by revenue and a dominant force in domestic IPO underwriting, trading, and asset management. [[CITIC Capital]] handles private equity and alternative investments. CITIC Bank (a joint venture with [[Banco Bilbao Vizcaya Argentaria|BBVA]]) is a mid-tier commercial bank. Beyond finance, CITIC has major mining operations (CITIC Pacific Mining in Australia produces magnetite iron ore), engineering and construction businesses, and real estate development.

As a state-owned enterprise reporting to China's State Council, CITIC serves as both a commercial entity and a policy tool — it has been used to channel state capital into strategic sectors and to facilitate China's outbound investment program.
