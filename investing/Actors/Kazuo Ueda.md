# Kazuo Ueda

Kazuo Ueda became Governor of the [[BOJ]] in April 2023, the first academic economist to lead the bank in its 140-year history. He inherited the most expansive monetary policy experiment ever conducted — negative interest rates, [[yield curve control]], and a balance sheet exceeding 130% of GDP — and was tasked with normalizing it without crashing the yen carry trade or triggering a JGB sell-off. His cautious approach earned him the reputation of a methodical, data-dependent central banker.

## Policy trajectory

Ueda ended negative interest rates with the first rate hike in 17 years (March 2024), raising from -0.1% to 0-0.1%. A second hike to 0.25% followed in July 2024, triggering a violent yen carry trade unwind that sent the Nikkei down 12% in a single session (Aug 5, 2024). He raised again to 0.5% in January 2025, then to 0.75% by early 2026. Each move has been incremental, signaling well in advance — but the carry trade unwind demonstrated that even telegraphed normalization carries risk when the prior regime was a decade of financial repression.
