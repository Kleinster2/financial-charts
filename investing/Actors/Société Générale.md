# Société Générale

One of [[France]]'s three largest banks alongside [[BNP Paribas]] and [[Crédit Agricole]], and a globally systemically important bank (G-SIB) with operations across retail banking in France, international retail (notably [[Central and Eastern Europe]] and Africa), asset management, and investment banking. Founded in 1864 by a group of industrialists and bankers — the name literally means "General Society" — and headquartered at the La Défense tower complex west of [[Paris]]. Listed on [[Euronext Paris]] (GLE.PA).

## Synopsis

SocGen's investment bank punches above its weight in equity derivatives and structured products — the business built by former trader Jean-Pierre Mustier in the 1990s and 2000s became one of the world's largest equity derivatives books, though it also produced the Jérôme Kerviel rogue trading scandal (€4.9B loss, 2008). Post-Kerviel, the bank retrenched but retained a strong franchise in equity-linked notes, quant research, and FX strategy. The FX research team, led by [[Kit Juckes]] as chief FX strategist, is one of the most-cited desks on the sell side for dollar, euro, and yen commentary — Juckes' morning notes are widely read among macro hedge funds and real-money allocators.

The firm appears in vault coverage through Juckes' March 4, 2026 [[FT]] commentary on the dollar reset during the Iran war: the bank's view is that the war-driven dollar bounce will persist even if the conflict is short-lived, "because the dollar index fell further [last year] than was justified by [relative interest] rate moves." This framing — that 2025's dollar weakness over-shot the fundamentals and the Iran crisis is providing the reset — is a notable contrarian call relative to consensus forecasts (see [[Iran conflict FX dynamics#The positioning story]]) that still expect EUR/USD to grind higher once hostilities pass. CEO Slawomir Krupa has been running a cost-cutting program since 2023 focused on French retail banking restructuring.
