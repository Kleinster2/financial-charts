Aberdeen Investcorp Infrastructure Partners is a joint venture between [[Aberdeen]] and [[Investcorp]] focused on infrastructure investments in the Middle East and globally, notable as a co-investor in [[Saudi Aramco]]'s Jafurah gas development.

## Synopsis

AIIP was formed to combine [[Aberdeen]]'s asset management capabilities with [[Investcorp]]'s Middle Eastern deal sourcing and investor relationships, targeting the infrastructure investment boom driven by Gulf state diversification programs ([[Saudi Arabia]]'s Vision 2030, [[UAE]] energy transition). The JV sits within [[Xavier Meyer]]'s strategic push to reposition Aberdeen toward real assets and specialist strategies — categories where fee compression is less severe than in traditional equities and where institutional investors (sovereign wealth funds, pension funds) are increasing allocations.

The most visible deal is the co-investment in [[Saudi Aramco]]'s Jafurah Midstream Gas Company (JMGC), alongside [[Global Infrastructure Partners]] and [[Hassana Investment Company]] (the investment arm of Saudi Arabia's General Organization for Social Insurance). Jafurah is Saudi Arabia's largest non-associated gas development — a $10B+ program to produce 2 Bcf/d of gas by 2030, central to the kingdom's strategy to free oil for export by using gas domestically for power generation and petrochemicals. The midstream JV (pipelines, processing, compression) is the toll-road asset within this mega-project — lower commodity risk than upstream, with long-duration contracted cash flows. For AIIP, it demonstrates the JV's access to Gulf infrastructure deal flow that neither parent could secure independently. The broader significance: Middle Eastern infrastructure is attracting global PE capital at scale, and JVs like AIIP are the channel.
