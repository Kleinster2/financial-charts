#actor #company #consumer #cosmetics #brazil #private

Grupo Boticário — Brazil's largest cosmetics, fragrance and personal-care group, privately held and franchise-driven, founded in 1977 by Miguel Krigsner as a single compounding pharmacy in Curitiba, Paraná. Total sales (GMV) reached R$35.7 billion in 2024, up 19% year on year, across a multi-brand portfolio led by O Boticário, [[Eudora]], Quem Disse, Berenice?, The Beauty Box and [[Vult]], sold through more than 4,000 stores in Brazil and 50+ countries plus tens of thousands of multi-brand points of sale. It appears in the investing vault as the seller of retail-automation company [[Casa Magalhães]] to [[Cleam Capital]] in March 2025 — one of the deals that built Cleam's operating portfolio alongside its 2026 purchase of [[Grano Alimentos]].

## Why it matters

- Scale leader in Brazilian beauty: at R$35.7B GMV, Grupo Boticário is the dominant force in Brazilian cosmetics. O Boticário has overtaken rival [[Natura]] in sales, led by fragrances — the Malbec line alone is reported above R$1 billion.
- Multi-brand, multi-channel: the model spans franchised stores, owned stores, e-commerce ([[Beleza na Web]]), and a vast multi-brand POS network — a distribution machine, not a single-brand retailer. The 19% 2024 growth is described by management as sustainable, with an R$50B ambition.
- Portfolio pruning: the March 2025 sale of [[Casa Magalhães]] (a retail-automation business it had bought in 2021) to [[Cleam Capital]] is the vault-relevant thread — Boticário shedding a non-core tech asset that became a building block of Cleam's logistics/retail-tech roll-up.

## Funding history

Privately held and family-controlled (Krigsner family); no public funding rounds or listing on record. Growth has been self- and franchise-funded rather than venture- or public-market financed.

## Leadership

- Miguel Krigsner — founder (chairman); the group is professionally managed under a CEO with the Krigsner family retaining control.
