# Hu Jintao

Hu Jintao served as President of [[China]] (2003-2013) and General Secretary of the CCP (2002-2012). He presided over China's WTO-era economic boom, during which GDP quadrupled and China overtook [[Japan]] as the world's second-largest economy (2010). His tenure included the 2008 Beijing Olympics and the post-GFC stimulus that turbo-charged infrastructure investment. His consensus-based "collective leadership" style — power shared among the Politburo Standing Committee — was the CCP governance norm that [[Xi Jinping]] systematically dismantled after taking over.

Hu rose through the Communist Youth League (tuanpai) faction, which emphasized technocratic governance and incremental reform. His presidency was marked by rapid growth but also rising corruption, environmental degradation, and growing inequality that his successor used as justification for the anti-corruption campaign. Hu was publicly escorted out of the 20th Party Congress closing ceremony in October 2022 in footage that symbolized Xi's complete consolidation of power — the leader who embodied collective rule physically removed from the room where one-man rule was formalized.
