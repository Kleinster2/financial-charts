**National Data Administration** (国家数据局) — Chinese government agency responsible for data governance and digital economy policy, established 2023 under the State Council. It sits atop China's effort to treat [[Data as an asset|data as a factor of production]] — overseeing dozens of data exchanges and the policy machinery that lets enterprises book data resources on their balance sheets. Director Liu Liehong coined the official term for AI tokens (词元, cíyuán) in March 2026, declaring them "the value anchor of the intelligent era."

## Data-asset regime

The NDA is the institutional layer on top of China's broader doctrine that [[Data as an asset|data is a factor of production]] — a designation made by the Central Committee and State Council in March 2020, placing data alongside land, labour, capital, and technology. The pieces it coordinates:

| Pillar | Action |
|---|---|
| Doctrine | Data declared 5th factor of production (2020) |
| Accounting | MOF "Interim Provisions on Accounting for Enterprise Data Resources," effective 1 Jan 2024 — data bookable as inventory or intangible asset |
| Markets | Oversees dozens of data exchanges (Shanghai, Shenzhen, Guiyang) to price traded data |
| Activation | "Data Element X" three-year action plan (Jan 2024, 17 ministries) targeting data use across 12 sectors |

Take-up of balance-sheet recognition has been thin — only ~2% of A-share firms (~100 companies) had disclosed data on the balance sheet by April 2025 — with [[China Unicom]]'s Rmb204mn filing the marquee example. The regime also has a fiscal edge: indebted [[LGFV|local government financing vehicles]] have used recognized data assets as loan collateral, a use the [[US-China Economic and Security Review Commission]] flagged in 2024.
