# RJ Scaringe

**RJ Scaringe** is the founder and CEO of [[Rivian]], holding an MIT PhD in mechanical engineering with a dissertation on slant-axis engine design.

## Synopsis

Scaringe founded Rivian in 2009 (originally Mainstream Motors) with a vision for electric adventure vehicles, then spent nearly a decade in stealth mode — a patience rare in the startup world that reflected both the capital intensity of automotive manufacturing and Scaringe's engineering-first approach. He secured [[Amazon]] as both a strategic investor and fleet customer (100,000 electric delivery vans) and brought in [[Volkswagen]] for a software joint venture, assembling a capital and partnership base that enabled Rivian's $66B IPO in November 2021 — the largest US IPO that year and the sixth-largest in US history at the time.

The post-IPO trajectory has tested Scaringe's credibility. Rivian's stock collapsed from $170+ to under $10 as production ramp struggles, supply chain constraints, and cash burn rates revealed the distance between IPO narrative and manufacturing reality. Scaringe has navigated this by securing the VW JV ($5.8B commitment), cutting costs, and guiding toward positive gross margin on vehicles — a milestone the company approached in late 2025 with $5.4B in revenue and $6.6B in liquidity. His dual role as visionary founder and operational CEO is the central question for Rivian shareholders: the product vision (R1T, R1S, and the upcoming R2 mass-market platform) is compelling, but execution in automotive manufacturing requires a different skill set than the one that built the brand. His continued presence as CEO is both Rivian's greatest asset and its key-person risk.
