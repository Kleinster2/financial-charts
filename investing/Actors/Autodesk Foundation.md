Autodesk Foundation is the philanthropic and impact investment arm of [[Autodesk]], deploying grants and equity investments into startups using technology and design to address social and environmental challenges.

## Synopsis

The Foundation operates at the intersection of corporate philanthropy and impact investing, with a portfolio spanning climate tech, resilient infrastructure, and inclusive economic development. Unlike purely grant-making corporate foundations, Autodesk Foundation takes equity positions in early-stage startups — effectively functioning as an impact-focused VC arm. The portfolio companies often leverage [[Autodesk]]'s own design and engineering software (AutoCAD, Revit, Fusion 360), creating a strategic flywheel: the Foundation supports startups that become Autodesk users and demonstrate the software's applicability to social-impact use cases, which in turn strengthens Autodesk's brand positioning in the sustainability market.

The Foundation's relevance to the vault is through its portfolio companies that intersect with investable themes. [[Delta40]], in the Health & Resilience portfolio, is one example. The Foundation also supports companies working on affordable housing construction technology, clean energy access in emerging markets, and climate adaptation infrastructure — sectors where venture-scale returns are increasingly plausible as regulatory tailwinds (building codes, emissions mandates, climate disclosure rules) create market demand. For [[Autodesk]] itself, the Foundation serves a dual purpose: it generates goodwill with the architecture, engineering, and construction (AEC) community that comprises Autodesk's core customer base, and it provides early signal on emerging use cases for Autodesk's platform.
