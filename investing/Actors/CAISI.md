**CAISI** (Center for AI Standards and Innovation) — the US government's AI model-evaluation and standards body, housed in the [[Commerce Department]]'s NIST. It is the cooperative channel between frontier AI labs and Washington: the office that tests new models before and after release, and the home of the only standing memorandum of understanding between [[Anthropic]] and the US government.

## From "safety" to "standards"

CAISI began as the US AI Safety Institute (AISI), created under the [[Biden]] administration (Executive Order 14110, Oct 2023; established at NIST in 2024) to evaluate frontier-model capabilities and risks. In 2024 it signed memorandums of understanding with [[Anthropic]] and [[OpenAI]] giving the government pre- and post-release access to major new models for joint capability and safety evaluation.

In June 2025 the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration renamed it the Center for AI Standards and Innovation and reframed its mission from "safety" toward "standards, innovation, and security," positioning it as industry's primary point of contact inside the government for model testing and collaborative research. Its evaluation agreements now span [[Anthropic]], [[OpenAI]], [[Google]], [[Microsoft]], and [[xAI]].

## Role in the Anthropic export-ban episode

CAISI is the evaluation/cooperation lane in Anthropic's three-front relationship with the government — distinct from the coercive [[Pentagon AI access dispute 2026|Pentagon / NSPM-11]] military-use lane and the [[Export controls|Commerce export-control]] lane. Reporting around the [[Claude Fable 5]] launch indicates the lane is under strain: after a [[Donald Trump|Trump]] executive order requiring labs to hand new models to the government 30 days before general release, officials reportedly directed CAISI to stop publishing its model evaluations.
