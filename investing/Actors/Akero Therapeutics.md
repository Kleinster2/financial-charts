**Akero Therapeutics** — US clinical-stage biotech focused on metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH). Acquired by [[Novo Nordisk]] in December 2025 for up to $5.2bn ($4.7bn upfront + $0.5bn CVR tied to regulatory approval) — the first major M&A under CEO [[Mike Doustdar]] and Novo's signal that it intends to expand from obesity into obesity-adjacent comorbidities.

The lead asset is efruxifermin, an FGF21 analog in late-stage trials that has shown reversal of liver scarring in MASH patients. MASH is a fast-emerging therapeutic category with high overlap to obesity — the strategic logic is that Novo's obesity sales force already calls on the same prescribers, and that some GLP-1 patients will progress (or have already progressed) to MASH.

## Strategic rationale

Three reasons MASH fits Novo's pivot:

1. Comorbidity overlap — obesity and MASH share patient populations; GLP-1 prescribers are a natural channel for an FGF21 analog
2. Pipeline diversification — Novo's 2025 revenue was ~95% obesity/diabetes vs. [[Eli Lilly]]'s 73% cardiometabolic with diversification across immunology, oncology, neuroscience; Akero begins to widen the franchise
3. Late-stage de-risking — efruxifermin is in Phase 3, not early discovery; Novo paid for clinical de-risking, not platform optionality

The deal was announced before Novo's February 2026 guidance shock and before the [[Foundayo|Lilly oral pill]] launch — the timing reflects the board's recognition that the obesity-only thesis was becoming insufficient.
