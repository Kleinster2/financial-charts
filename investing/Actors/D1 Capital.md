D1 Capital Partners — Dan Sundheim's crossover fund. Founded July 2018 in New York. Style: concentrated long/short equity in public markets alongside large growth-equity stakes in private companies. The firm's defining position is [[SpaceX]], entered in 2020 at the Series M $36bn valuation, now expected to be worth roughly $20bn at the [[SpaceX IPO 2026|June 2026 IPO]] valuation — the largest single-fund SpaceX windfall reported in the public press ([[Financial Times]], May 18 2026). Sundheim told investors in 2025 that he did not intend to sell SpaceX shares "despite very substantial inbound interest" — the position has accordingly compounded through every secondary tender and the [[SpaceX xAI merger]] without being trimmed.

Beyond SpaceX, D1 holds anchor positions in [[Stripe]], [[Databricks]], and [[Instacart]] / CART, alongside an active long/short equity book in public markets. The firm carries the Tiger Cub label (Sundheim was [[Viking Global]] CIO) but its private-market exposure is much closer to a growth-equity sponsor than to a traditional L/S hedge fund — the FT used the firm as an exemplar of the "lines on Wall Street started to blur" between [[Private markets|private equity]], [[Venture Capital|venture capital]], and hedge funds.

## [[SpaceX]] position (2020-2026)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| First investment | 2020 |
| Entry valuation | $36B — aligns with SpaceX's February 2020 [[SpaceX#Funding rounds (complete)\|Series M]] at $36bn |
| Subsequent participation | Multiple secondary tenders and primary rounds through the 2020-2026 valuation arc |
| Reported current exposure ([[Financial Times]] May 18 2026) | ~$20bn at the $1.75T SpaceX IPO valuation framing — the largest hedge-fund SpaceX windfall reported in the public press |
| Sundheim 2025 investor letter stance | No intent to sell "despite very substantial inbound interest" |
| Lock-up | Custom IPO lock-up arrangement applies (not the standard 6-month structure); see [[SpaceX IPO 2026#What makes this IPO structurally unusual]] |

The 2020 entry at the $36bn Series M valuation places D1 ahead of the [[Starlink]] revenue inflection and well before the [[xAI]] roll-up that drove the valuation to $1.25T (Feb 2026) and onward to the $1.75T-$2T IPO target framing. The position has compounded approximately 50x from entry to the IPO target — without ever being trimmed.

The lock-up structure governs the realization path. SpaceX raised ~$10bn over 20+ years as a private company, creating substantial pent-up insider liquidity demand, and the IPO uses a custom non-standard lock-up rather than the typical 6-month construct (per [[SpaceX IPO 2026#What makes this IPO structurally unusual]]). Whether early holders like D1 wait longer than 6 months or get a faster path remains structurally undecided.

See [[SpaceX IPO 2026#Hedge fund winners (FT May 18 2026)]] for the event-side framing and side-by-side with [[Darsana Capital Partners]].
