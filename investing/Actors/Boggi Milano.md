Italian menswear brand positioned between mass-market and luxury. Founded 1939 in Monza, now ~244 stores in 60+ countries. Owned by the Zaccardi family via [[Brian & Barry Group]]. Aggressively expanding into the US with a $42M investment plan targeting 18 standalone stores by 2030.

## Business model

- Direct retail (standalone stores), multi-brand wholesale, e-commerce
- In-store iPad ordering adds ~10% to physical store revenue
- Made-to-measure services in flagship locations
- Competes with [[Brunello Cucinelli]], [[Zegna]], [[Loro Piana]] at roughly one-third the price point
- Core demographic: men aged ~46–48
- 65% of revenue generated outside Italy

## US expansion

$42M investment plan over five years (2025–2030):
- Target: 18 standalone US stores, $120M US revenue, ~17% EBITDA margin by 2030
- NYC stores (2025): SoHo (115 Mercer St), Madison Avenue (527 Madison at 54th, 8,810 sq ft), Columbus Circle (The Shops, 450 sqm)
- Miami: Brickell City Centre opening Feb 2026
- Pipeline: Chicago, Boston, Washington DC, Texas, California
- [[Bloomingdale's]] partnership: 7 shop-in-shops (59th St flagship + Midwest/Florida)
- US warehouse opened Sep 2025 for online fulfillment

## Evolution

- 1939 — Founded by Paolo Boggi, single shop in Monza specializing in tailored suits
- 1966 — First Milan store, Galleria Passarella near Piazza San Babila
- 2002 — Milanese group Fin.part acquires 90% for €8.65M; Paolo Boggi retains 10%
- 2003 — Acquired by [[Brian & Barry Group]] (Zaccardi family) for €13.2M. The Zaccardis started in childrenswear (grandfather's tailor shop in Monza), converted to luxury multibrand retail in 1985
- 2025 — Named official formalwear outfitter/licensee of FIFA Men's and Women's World Cups through 2027
- 2025 — US retail debut: three NYC stores + Bloomingdale's concessions
- 2025 — New 10,000 sqm automated warehouse in Concorezzo (Monza province)
