## Svalbard Treaty (1920)

Unique international arrangement:

| Provision | Implication |
|-----------|-------------|
| Norwegian sovereignty | Norway governs |
| **Demilitarized** | No military bases or fortifications |
| Equal access | All treaty signatories can conduct economic activity |
| Equal taxation | Norway can't discriminate against foreigners |
| Environmental rules | Norway sets conservation policy |

**Signatories:** 46 countries including US, Russia, [[China]], most of [[Europe]].

**Key constraint:** Norway cannot militarize — creates strategic gap in Arctic defense.

## Strategic significance

1. **Demilitarized gap:** No NATO bases despite Norwegian sovereignty
2. **Russian foothold:** Barentsburg gives Russia presence in NATO member's territory
3. **Research cover:** Countries use science stations for Arctic presence
4. **Treaty constraints:** Norway can't respond militarily to gray zone activities
5. **Climate monitoring:** Key location for Arctic change observation
