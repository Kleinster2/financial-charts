Percy Liang is a Stanford Computer Science associate professor and co-founder of [[Simile]], an AI startup building tools for structured knowledge extraction. He is the founding director of the Center for Research on Foundation Models (CRFM) at Stanford HAI — his group coined the term "foundation model" in a 2021 paper that reshaped how the industry and policymakers discuss large-scale AI systems. PhD from UC Berkeley under Michael Jordan and Dan Klein. Recipient of the Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers and the IJCAI Computers and Thought Award.

Liang also leads the HELM (Holistic Evaluation of Language Models) benchmarking project, which became one of the most widely referenced independent evaluation frameworks for LLMs, providing standardized comparisons across dozens of models on accuracy, calibration, robustness, fairness, and efficiency.
