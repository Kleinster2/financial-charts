Bangladesh is a South Asian country and the 8th most populous globally (~170M). Major garment exporter, with fuel oil providing ~11% of power generation.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| GDP | ~$460B |
| Population | ~170M |
| Currency | Bangladeshi taka (BDT) |
| Fuel oil share of power | ~11% |
| Key export | Garments/textiles |

## Iran war exposure — remittance + sovereign credit + energy

Bangladesh sits in three of the Iran-war second-order channels simultaneously, making it analytically one of the most-exposed countries to the crisis even though it has not yet appeared in the headline-news cycle.

| Channel | Bangladesh data | Vault note |
|---|---|---|
| Remittance flows | ~5 million workers in Gulf (1 of 2 largest concentrations alongside [[Pakistan]]); 2025 total remittances ~$30bn; majority from GCC; 3-5% of GDP | [[Iran conflict remittance flows]] |
| Sovereign credit | "Heavily indebted" per [[S&P Global]] (Sifon-Arevalo, Mar 12); Asia second-most-exposed region; rating B2/B+/B+ pre-crisis with no action yet | [[Iran conflict sovereign credit risk]] |
| Energy supply | ~2/3 of [[LNG]] imports from [[Qatar]] via Hormuz; multiple fertilizer plants halted in March; boosted [[Coal|coal]] consumption as substitute | [[Iran conflict country responses]], [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] |

Documented incidents through May 17, 2026:

- Mar 8, 2026 — Mohammad Abdullah Al Mamun, a 35-year-old Bangladeshi worker, killed in a missile strike on his Saudi Arabia camp (the only fatality of a migrant worker from the named-cohort countries reported through May 17). See [[Iran conflict remittance flows#Documented incidents]].
- Through March 2026 — at least 40,000 Bangladesh workers reported stuck at home, unable to depart to their Gulf workplaces due to airspace closures and disrupted consular processing. See [[Iran conflict airline disruption]].
- March 2026 — multiple fertilizer plants halted as [[Qatar]] LNG flows interrupted; coal consumption increased.

The structural set-up is among the most fragile in the South Asian cohort: garment-export economy (sensitive to global recession risk per [[Apostolos Tzitzikostas]]'s May 14 framing), high LNG dependence on a single Hormuz-transiting supplier ([[Qatar]]), heavily-indebted sovereign with limited fiscal buffer, and outsized GCC labor-corridor exposure. The 2022 [[Russia]]-[[Ukraine]] precedent — which triggered a [[Sri Lanka]] default — is the explicit comparison the IEA drew on May 17.
