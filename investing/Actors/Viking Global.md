## AI operations — VikingGPT 2.0

Viking developed a proprietary generative AI chatbot, VikingGPT 2.0. Usage in August reached 3 questions per working minute across the firm — a 400% increase over the same period the prior year.

| Use case | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Research | Analysts use VikingGPT to get up to speed on businesses faster |
| Thesis testing | Deputy CIO Previn Mankodi used it as a "skeptical avatar" to challenge investment theses |
| PE operations | Private equity staff use it for sourcing and dealmaking |
| Operations | External AI resources for compliance, coding efficiency |

[[Andreas Halvorsen]] (investor letter): *"Lest you worry we are ceding key decisions to the machines — quite the contrary. We have observed that the closer you get to needing to make a judgment call, the less useful AI is."*

Staff are encouraged to experiment "in a carefully governed environment with clear guardrails." The firm has an AI working group that meets to outline goals and share findings.

### AI investment stance

Viking has not taken large directional bets on AI stocks, in contrast to peers like [[Coatue]] (which raised $1B+ for AI bets and is heavily positioned across the AI stack).

[[Andreas Halvorsen]] (investor letter): *"At current valuations, we believe the range of outcomes remains too wide to make a big directional bet."*

Sky-high valuations provide little room for error. Picking winners is difficult because many AI applications will be embedded in services companies already offer.

Halvorsen: *"Will AI prove to be a powerful deflationary force that justifies record valuations? Perhaps. But the data so far shows that the cost is high, and the benefits are unpredictable, and appear small to date."*

This caution came at a cost — the Equities Fund gained just +0.7% in Q3 and +5.6% YTD through September, missing the tech rally.

In the same period, [[Kenneth Griffin|Ken Griffin]] ([[Citadel]]) said GenAI "isn't helping hedge funds create market-beating returns and hasn't yet meaningfully impacted the industry."

*Source: Bloomberg, Viking investor letter*
