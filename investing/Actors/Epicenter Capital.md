Backed by [[Philippe Laffont]] and Thomas Laffont (co-founders of [[Coatue]]), [[Coatue]] CIO Jaimin Rangwalla, and former [[Coatue]] trader Daniel Senft. Opened to outside investors July 2025. ## Eve

The fund's AI system and operational core. Eve is not a chatbot or research tool -- it is an autonomous agent that participates in every part of the business without requiring human prompts to take action.

What Eve does:
- Builds dozens of sub-bots and AI agent teams, writes their code, assigns them tasks
- Scours disclosures of 13,000+ companies
- Listens to podcasts, scrutinizes social media posts, summarizes news
- Monitors earnings calls, tracks CEO statements, parses corporate filings
- Conducts due diligence, brainstorms stock picks
- Generates a daily podcast for Kishore's commute each morning
- Maintains a repository of memories for evaluating company performance and the fund's own investment process
- When given feedback, recodes its own programming -- learns like a junior analyst, only faster

Infrastructure: Eve runs on [[Anthropic]]'s ClaudeCode. Eve's ClaudeCode agent independently suggested migrating servers from [[Amazon|AWS]] to [[Cloudflare]], cutting compute costs 90%.

Kishore in investor letter: "Eve allows us to consume 10x more information in 10x less time. It helps us identify new potential investment ideas and accelerates our research. Eve's repository of memories allows us to better evaluate company performance as well as our investment process."

Eve has already accomplished tasks Kishore thought impossible six months prior. Example: trawled through scattered corporate documents across multiple filings to analyze a company's new product rollout -- work that would take a human analyst a week. Eve did it overnight.

## Investment approach

Epicenter makes only long bets. Highly concentrated portfolio in companies Kishore believes will define the next decade. Public markets initially.

Kishore on LinkedIn (2025): "Epicenters are the origin of waves that ripple outward, influencing and reshaping everything around them. Our portfolio is highly concentrated in the companies we believe will define the next decade, initially in the public markets."

The thesis is that Eve allows three people to outperform funds employing hundreds by consuming and synthesizing vastly more information.

## Significance

[[Philippe Laffont]] rarely backs spin-outs from [[Coatue]]. The Laffont brothers, Rangwalla (CIO of publics), and Senft (former trader) all personally invested -- a strong endorsement from the Coatue inner circle.

Epicenter represents an extreme version of the AI-native fund model: the technology is not a research tool but a teammate that operates autonomously. If the model works, it suggests hedge funds may not need large analyst teams. Eve costs thousands of dollars per year vs. hundreds of thousands for a human analyst.
