**Estonia** is a Baltic state and [[EU]] member (since 2004) with a population of ~1.4 million, known globally as a pioneer in digital governance. The country's e-Residency program, digital ID system, and X-Road data exchange infrastructure made it the template for digital-first government services — a model studied and partially adopted by dozens of countries.

Estonia's economy (~$40B GDP) is small but punches above its weight in technology. [[Skype]] was developed in Estonia (by Jaan Tallinn, Ahti Heinla, and Priit Kasesalu), and the country has produced more unicorns per capita than almost any other nation, including [[Wise]] (formerly TransferWise), Bolt, Pipedrive, and Veriff. Tallinn has become a fintech and cybersecurity hub, supported by NATO's Cooperative Cyber Defence Centre of Excellence (CCDCOE), which is headquartered there.

As a NATO and eurozone member bordering [[Russia]], Estonia is among the most hawkish European voices on defense spending and Russian containment. Defense spending has exceeded 2% of GDP since 2022 and is targeted to reach 3%+. The country's digital infrastructure was partly motivated by its 2007 experience as the target of massive Russian cyberattacks — the first state-level cyber conflict, which catalyzed both Estonian and NATO cyber defense capabilities. Estonia is an [[IEA]] member since 2014.
