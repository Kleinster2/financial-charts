# Capgemini

**Capgemini** — [[France|French]] IT services and consulting company. One of the largest global IT services firms. Listed on [[Euronext]] Paris (CAP / CAPP.PA), with €22.5B of 2025 revenue and more than 420,000 employees.

The investment question is whether Capgemini can turn [[Agentic AI]] into higher-value enterprise-transformation work before the same tools compress the labor-billing model that supports traditional [[IT services]].

## Why Capgemini matters

Capgemini sits at the center of the AI-services paradox. On the bear side, [[AI agents]] automate coding, testing, application maintenance, and back-office process work -- exactly the repeatable tasks that made global delivery profitable. On the bull side, enterprise AI deployment is not a software-license sale; it requires data cleanup, governance, workflow redesign, cloud architecture, security controls, change management, and industry-specific process knowledge. That is the layer Capgemini is trying to own.

This makes Capgemini a clean European read-through for the broader [[AI disintermediation]] debate: does AI destroy the implementation middleman, or does it create a larger transformation budget that only firms with existing C-suite relationships can capture?

## 2026 Capital Markets Day -- AI as addressable-market expansion

On May 27, 2026, Capgemini used its Capital Markets Day to argue that [[Agentic AI]] expands its client-spending pool beyond traditional IT budgets. [[Reuters]] reported that CEO Aiman Ezzat framed client demand as a broad operating-model change rather than a standard technology upgrade, which matters because budget ownership shifts from the CIO's software stack to business functions trying to re-architect workflows.

The core company claim: AI is not only a threat to outsourced implementation work; it is also a trigger for bigger, cross-functional transformation programs. CTO Franck Greverie said the pipeline of business opportunities already exceeded $12B. [[OpenAI]]'s Nate Harbacek, speaking at the event, framed the enterprise shift as moving from individual experimentation to scaled deployment where whole workflows are re-architected.

Capgemini also tied the AI-services pitch to sovereignty. Management said it is working with [[AWS]], [[Google Cloud]], and [[Microsoft]] on region-specific cloud and AI offerings for clients and governments that want local data, hosting, and regulatory control. This is the services-layer version of the [[Sovereign AI race]]: the value pool is not only GPUs and data centers, but also the compliant integration work that makes those systems usable inside regulated institutions.

The market was not fully convinced. Capgemini closed May 27 at €100.65, down 3.5% on Euronext Paris, with more than 1.1M shares traded. The reaction says investors still see execution risk: the company must prove that agentic-AI consulting converts to revenue and margin faster than AI reduces demand for routine outsourcing.

Capgemini's official 2028 targets put numbers around the pitch:

| Target | 2028 ambition |
|--------|---------------|
| Revenue CAGR, 2025-2028 | +5.5% to +7.5% at constant currency, with about 2 points from M&A |
| Operating profit before acquisition-related expenses | 12.1% to 12.3% of revenue, up 130-150 bps from 2025 |
| Organic free cash flow | Above €6B cumulative over 2026-2028 |
| 2026 revenue growth guide | +6.5% to +8.5% at constant exchange rates, with 4.5-5 points inorganic |

The strategic read is simple: Capgemini is selling itself as the "human systems integrator" for AI agents. If that works, IT services shifts from cheap labor arbitrage to AI operating-model design. If it fails, the same agentic stack becomes a margin-compression machine.

## Evolution

Capgemini was founded in Grenoble in 1967 by Serge Kampf as Sogeti and built itself through services acquisitions into one of [[Europe]]'s largest technology consultancies. The 2000 acquisition of Ernst & Young Consulting gave the firm a global consulting platform; later acquisitions pushed it deeper into engineering, cloud, data, and digital transformation.

The 2026 AI strategy is the next version of that services roll-up logic. Instead of selling only cheaper delivery capacity, Capgemini is trying to sell the operating-system change around enterprise AI: process redesign, controls, security, cloud integration, and adoption programs.
