**MAS** — [[Singapore]]'s central bank, financial regulator, and national financial development agency, all in one institution. Founded 1971. Uniquely uses the exchange rate (SGD nominal effective exchange rate band) rather than interest rates as its primary monetary policy tool. Regulates banking, insurance, securities, and digital payment tokens under the Financial Services and Markets Act (FSMA). Manages a portion of Singapore's foreign reserves alongside [[GIC]].

MAS's positioning as the global leader in regulated digital asset innovation was largely the architecture of [[Sopnendu Mohanty]], its founding Chief FinTech Officer (Aug 2015 - 2024). Under his tenure MAS built APIX (cross-border fintech API exchange), the Singapore FinTech Festival (~60,000 attendees), and Project Ubin (multi-phase blockchain wholesale settlement experiment). On stablecoins specifically, Singapore was among the first jurisdictions globally (alongside [[Japan]]) to require 100% liquid reserve backing — a regulatory output that excluded under-reserved structures by design while giving compliant issuers a clear scale path. Mohanty's framing: existing payment regulation with incremental coverage for stablecoin-specific technical disclosure is sufficient; no need for a new regulatory category.
