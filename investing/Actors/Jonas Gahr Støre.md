# Jonas Gahr Støre

Jonas Gahr Støre is a Norwegian politician serving as Prime Minister of [[Norway]] since October 2021, leading the [[Labour Party (Norway)]]. His government raised the [[Wealth tax]] from 0.85% to 1.1% in 2022, triggering significant [[Capital flight]] as wealthy Norwegians relocated to [[Switzerland]] and other low-tax jurisdictions. Despite the controversy, Støre won re-election in September 2025.

Before becoming PM, Støre served as Foreign Minister (2005-2012) under Jens Stoltenberg and Health Minister (2012-2013). His wealth tax increase was part of a broader fiscal tightening package aimed at reducing reliance on [[Government Pension Fund Global|oil fund]] withdrawals. Critics argued the policy was self-defeating — several billionaire departures (including [[Kjell Inge Røkke]]) reduced the domestic tax base. Supporters countered that the departures were marginal relative to total revenue. Støre's government also expanded [[Norway]]'s defense spending commitments in response to the Russia-Ukraine war, reaching the NATO 2% GDP target.
