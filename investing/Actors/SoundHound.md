# SoundHound

**SoundHound** is a voice and agentic-AI company providing conversational intelligence across restaurants, automotive, telecom, financial services, healthcare, retail, and smart devices. The company began with Houndify and SoundHound Chat AI, then broadened into enterprise agent platforms through products such as Smart Answering, Smart Ordering, Dynamic Drive-Thru, Amelia, and Autonomics. The strategic question is whether SoundHound becomes a scaled voice-AI operating layer or remains a high-beta thematic small cap dependent on acquisition integration.

## May 2026 voice-AI re-rating setup

SOUN closed at $9.20 on May 1, 2026, up 15.6% from $7.96 the prior day. The move was not driven by a fresh May 1 company announcement. The market read-through was [[Twilio]]: TWLO's Q1 print showed voice revenue growing 20% y/y, with AI use cases and conversational workflows becoming a visible growth catalyst. That turned SoundHound into the cleanest small-cap beta expression of the same voice-AI demand signal ahead of its own Q1 report on May 7.

The setup was built in the prior week. On Apr 21, SoundHound agreed to acquire [[LivePerson]] in an all-stock deal valuing LivePerson equity at $43M, with a $250M implied enterprise value after debt/cash adjustments. The company expects to receive $74M of LivePerson cash before repayment of 2026 convertible notes, retire discounted debt with cash/equity, and leave the combined company with no debt. Management framed the transaction as a step-change in enterprise distribution: LivePerson brings digital messaging, nearly one billion messages per month, customers across 30+ countries, and long-tenured relationships in banking, airlines, automakers, telecom, healthcare, insurance, energy, travel, hospitality, and retail. SoundHound guided to at least $350-400M of 2027 revenue, including at least $100M from LivePerson customers, with a possible $500M opportunity from the existing combined base.

On Apr 23, SoundHound also renewed and expanded its [[Casey's General Stores]] partnership. Casey's deployed SoundHound AI ordering agents across more than 2,600 locations, after more than 21M guest interactions and millions of food orders. The operational read-through matters: this is not just a demo workload, it is a phone-ordering automation layer inside a Fortune 500 convenience-store and pizza chain where missed calls, hold times, and kitchen routing affect store-level throughput.

The thesis implication is that voice AI is moving from "assistant feature" to transaction infrastructure. [[Twilio]] proves the API volume side; Casey's proves restaurant/retail workflow depth; [[LivePerson]] gives SoundHound a digital-messaging customer base to cross-sell into. The risk is integration and valuation: SoundHound is using equity to buy a legacy customer base and asking the market to underwrite 2027 revenue scale before the LivePerson deal has closed.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Voice AI demand is now showing up in public-company revenue lines, not just product demos.
- [[LivePerson]] gives SoundHound enterprise distribution and digital-message volume that it lacked as a primarily voice-first vendor.
- [[Casey's General Stores]] is a scaled proof point: 2,600+ deployed sites, 21M+ interactions, millions of orders.
- The combined company target of at least $350-400M 2027 revenue creates a clearer path toward institutional scale.

Bear:
- Still unprofitable and trades on AI-optionality rather than durable earnings power.
- LivePerson is an integration-heavy acquisition of a distressed legacy asset; cross-sell and debt-restructuring execution matter.
- SOUN's May 1 move was mostly a sympathy trade off [[Twilio]], not a same-day company-specific catalyst.
- Restaurant voice ordering and enterprise customer-service AI are becoming crowded markets.

## Watch

- Q1 2026 results on May 7: revenue growth, backlog, LivePerson close conditions, and profitability path.
- Whether LivePerson customers adopt SoundHound voice products or remain a low-growth digital-messaging base.
- Casey's renewal metrics: order accuracy, labor savings, call containment, and expansion beyond prepared-food phone orders.
