# ExxonMobil

ExxonMobil (NYSE: XOM) is the largest US integrated oil & gas major — upstream production, downstream refining, and chemicals — and the bellwether of the [[Oil and gas equity beta]] cohort. Integration (refining/chemicals partly hedging crude) does not decouple its stock from the crude-price factor: it trades as oil-equity beta alongside the pure E&P names.

## AI power — off-grid Permian gas (2026)

ExxonMobil is racing [[Chevron]] to convert prolific [[Permian Basin]] natural gas into behind-the-meter electricity for AI data centres. Exxon partnered with [[NextEra Energy]] to develop natural gas-fired power plants (~1.2 GW, with carbon capture per NextEra disclosures) serving hyperscaler customers including [[Google]]. The model mirrors Chevron's [[Chevron#Power production — the AI-electricity pivot (Jun 2026)|Project Kilby]]: monetize stranded associated gas (negative Waha Hub prices) by building generation at the wellhead and selling firm power under long-term contracts, sidestepping multi-year grid-interconnection queues. See [[Power constraints#Oil majors into behind-the-meter gas (Jun 2026)|oil-major BYOG]] and [[AI-power IPPs]].
