## CEO succession (Mar 2026)

[[James Quincey]] stepped down Mar 31, 2026 after 9 years as CEO, transitioning to Executive Chairman. [[Henrique Braun]] (COO since Jan 2025) succeeded him.

Quincey told CNBC (Mar 26): the company made significant progress pre-AI, but "now there's a huge new shift coming along" and Coca-Cola needs someone with the energy to pursue "a completely new transformation of the enterprise." He was 60 at departure. Under his tenure the company added 10+ billion-dollar brands.

Braun, who had overseen all operating units worldwide as COO, inherits the AI transformation mandate alongside continued portfolio expansion.

## Q1 2026 results (Apr 28, 2026) — first quarter under Henrique Braun, FY guidance raised

KO reported a clean Q1 2026 beat — comparable EPS +18%, revenue +12%, raised FY guidance — and the stock closed +3.9% to $78.35 (3.5σ idiosyncratic move on a normally low-vol staple). The first quarter under [[Henrique Braun]] sets the bar for the AI-transformation mandate Quincey handed down: Q1 came in above expectations on a portfolio that is increasingly sugar-free and small-pack.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Net revenues | $12.5B | +12% YoY; beat $12.24B consensus |
| Organic revenue growth | +10% | 8% concentrate sales + 2% price/mix |
| Unit case volume | +3% globally | — |
| Comparable EPS | $0.86 | +18% YoY |
| Sparkling soft drinks volume | +2% | Coca-Cola Zero Sugar +13% |
| Water | +5% | — |

### Raised guidance

| Line | Old | New |
|---|---|---|
| Comparable EPS growth | 7-8% | 8-9% |
| Organic revenue growth | 4-5% | reiterated |

The EPS guide raise came from a lower effective tax rate, not operating margin expansion. That's a quality-of-earnings mark-down, but the underlying organic revenue (+10%) and the EPS beat itself indicate operations are running ahead of the FY plan.

### What the print shows about the portfolio shift

Coca-Cola Zero Sugar +13% volume continues to outpace the legacy brand — the variant is now the company's fastest-growing flagship product, and the GLP-1 / health-trend overhang that has weighed on sugary beverages broadly is being absorbed at the brand level by sugar-free SKUs rather than at the company level by pricing alone. Water +5% and tea +8% confirm the [[Consumer Staples]] thesis that beverage volume growth has shifted to non-soda categories where KO has built out (Smartwater, Gold Peak, etc.) rather than fled.

### Why the move was 3.5σ on a low-vol staple

Two factors compressed the move. (1) Bond proxy demand: in the day's risk-off environment (driven by the [[2026 OpenAI revenue miss]] sell-off), defensive staples caught a flight-to-quality bid. KO's 2.9% dividend yield and 62-year streak make it the cleanest "AI capex skepticism" hedge among large caps. (2) Tax-rate mechanic: the FY guidance raise was driven by lower tax rate, which mechanically expands the EPS multiple even at unchanged operating profit — and quants/index funds rebalance on those mechanical EPS moves.
