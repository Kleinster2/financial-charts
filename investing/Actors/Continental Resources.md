## May 2026 Iran-war drilling reversal

[[Harold Hamm]] told the [[Financial Times]] in May 2026 that [[Continental Resources]] planned to lift 2026 capital expenditure by about $300 million to $2.8 billion in response to higher oil prices after the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]]. That reversed the January 2026 posture, when the company had said it would pause new drilling in [[North Dakota]] and not operate a rig there for the first time in three decades.

The point for the vault is not that Continental alone can offset the [[Strait of Hormuz]] supply shock. It is that privately controlled shale operators can move before public peers because they do not face the same quarterly investor penalty for volume growth. That makes Continental one of the first entity-level tests of whether [[Shale supply discipline]] is bending under a sustained forward-curve signal.

*Source: [[Financial Times]], Jamie Smyth and Stephanie Findlay, "US oil producers increase output to capture price surge from Iran war," May 23 2026.*
