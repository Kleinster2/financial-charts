# Murata Manufacturing

Japanese electronic components giant and the world's largest manufacturer of MLCCs (multilayer ceramic capacitors) — the tiny passive components found in virtually every electronic device. Revenue ~JPY 1.6T (~$11B), market cap ~$30B. Joined [[QuantumScape]]'s ceramic separator supply chain ecosystem in 2025, bringing its ceramics expertise to solid-state battery manufacturing.

## Synopsis

Murata Manufacturing dominates the global MLCC market with ~40% share, a position built on decades of materials science expertise in ceramics. MLCCs are fundamental to electronics — a single smartphone contains 800-1,000 capacitors, an electric vehicle contains 10,000+, and the count grows with every generation as devices become more complex. Murata also produces RF modules, sensors, filters, and power supplies, serving [[Apple]], [[Samsung]], [[NVIDIA]], and every major electronics OEM. The company's revenue is highly correlated to global electronics production cycles, making it a proxy for device demand.

The QuantumScape partnership is the strategic angle worth tracking. [[QuantumScape]]'s solid-state batteries use ceramic separators — and Murata's core competency is precision ceramic manufacturing at scale. When QuantumScape needed a supply chain partner who could produce ceramic components with the tolerances required for battery separators (sub-micron precision), Murata was a natural fit. This partnership, announced in 2025, gives QuantumScape access to Murata's manufacturing know-how and capacity, while giving Murata a potential entry into the solid-state battery value chain — a market that could eventually rival MLCCs in scale if EVs transition from lithium-ion to solid-state. The risk is cyclicality: Murata's earnings can swing 30-40% in a single year depending on smartphone and automotive production volumes, and the QuantumScape opportunity remains pre-revenue.
