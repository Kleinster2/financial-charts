# Mercedes-Benz

**German luxury automaker** — world's largest luxury car brand by revenue. Spun off from Daimler AG in 2021 (trucks became Daimler Truck). Partner in [[ACC]] battery JV with [[Stellantis]] and [[TotalEnergies]].

## EV strategy

Mercedes originally targeted 50% EV sales by 2025, later walked back to "market dependent." Partnered in [[ACC]] for European battery supply — Germany and Italy gigafactories now shelved (Feb 2026). Shifting toward hybrid/PHEV bridge strategy alongside pure BEV.

## Q1 2026 — Iran-war commodity squeeze (FT, May 3)

CFO Harald Wilhelm (Q1 2026 earnings call): *"We do expect raw material [costs] to step up further in the remainder of the year, higher than what we anticipated at the beginning of the year."* If the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]] continues for a "longer period," Wilhelm said Mercedes "cannot rule out the possibility of bottlenecks in individual commodities."

Mercedes joins the Detroit Big Three ([[Ford]], [[General Motors]], [[Stellantis]]) in flagging Iran-war commodity transmission, but with less hedge cover than [[Stellantis]] disclosed for Q1. The European read-through extends beyond Mercedes — every European OEM faces the same naphtha/aluminium/DRam input stack. Mercedes did not quantify the 2026 hit at the same level as the Big Three (GM up to $2B, Ford up to $2B, Stellantis ~€1B).

Two layered policy threats compound the European OEM picture:

1. The 50% [[Section 232]] aluminium tariff (US import side) — affects Mercedes vehicles built in the US for the US market.
2. [[Donald Trump]]'s May 1 threat of separate tariffs on European-made cars imported into the US — would directly hit Mercedes US-bound exports from Sindelfingen and other German plants.

See [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] § Detroit auto OEMs and § European-car tariff threat.
