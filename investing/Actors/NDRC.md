**NDRC** — China's "super ministry" for economic planning. Sets Five-Year Plans, approves major projects, controls prices. The most powerful economic policy body in China.

---

## Why NDRC matters

| Function | Scope |
|----------|-------|
| **Macro planning** | Five-Year Plans, GDP targets, economic strategy |
| **Project approval** | Major infrastructure, foreign investment |
| **Price controls** | Energy, utilities, key commodities |
| **Industrial policy** | Sector development, strategic industries |
| **Regional development** | Belt and Road, regional coordination |

**"Super ministry"** — broader authority than any single US agency. Combines roles of OMB, Commerce, Energy, and parts of Treasury.

## Recent policy signals

### Jan 2026: Services stimulus

**Wang Changlin (Deputy Head) announced:**
- New policies to spur domestic spending
- Focus: **Services sector consumption** (shift from infrastructure)
- Addressing supply-demand imbalances
- Spread across 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030)

This signals pivot from investment-led to consumption-led growth model.

### 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030)

| Theme | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| **AI** | Central priority |
| **Self-sufficiency** | Chips, tech, critical materials |
| **Consumption** | Rebalancing toward domestic demand |
| **Green transition** | Energy, EVs, carbon targets |

Three-stage rollout starting March 2026.

## Historical role

| Era | NDRC function |
|-----|---------------|
| **1950s-80s** | Central planning (as State Planning Commission) |
| **1998** | Renamed State Development Planning Commission |
| **2003** | Renamed NDRC, expanded scope |
| **2010s** | Supply-side reform, Belt and Road |
| **2020s** | Tech self-sufficiency, dual circulation |
