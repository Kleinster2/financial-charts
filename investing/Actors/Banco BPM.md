**Banco BPM** — Italy's third-largest bank by assets, formed in 2017 from the merger of Banco Popolare and Banca Popolare di Milano. Headquartered in Milan, the bank operates primarily in Italy's wealthiest northern regions (Lombardy, Veneto) with a focus on retail, SME lending, and wealth management. CEO Giuseppe Castagna has steered the bank through post-merger integration and improved profitability, making it an attractive acquisition target.

## Synopsis

Banco BPM sits at the center of a three-way M&A dynamic that encapsulates European banking consolidation tensions. [[UniCredit]] launched an unsolicited €10.1B all-share bid in November 2024, which Banco BPM's board rejected as significantly undervaluing the bank. Meanwhile, [[Crédit Agricole]] has built a 19.8% strategic stake — the largest single shareholding — partly through its 39% ownership of Agos, a consumer finance joint venture with Banco BPM. The Italian government, under PM [[Giorgia Meloni]], has signaled discomfort with a UniCredit takeover that could reduce competition in Italian banking, while Crédit Agricole's growing influence raises questions about whether the bank ultimately ends up in French hands.

The outcome matters beyond Italy: it tests whether European banking M&A can actually happen across borders despite political resistance, and whether [[Andrea Orcel]]'s vision of a pan-European banking champion (via UniCredit) is achievable or structurally blocked by national interests.
