## Business model

TD Synnex is the middleman of the global IT supply chain — a role that sounds unglamorous until you realize it's the single best real-time demand indicator for enterprise technology spending. The company buys from vendors (NVIDIA, Dell, HPE, Cisco, Microsoft, etc.) and sells to a network of 150,000+ resellers, managed service providers, and system integrators across 100+ countries.

The 2021 merger of SYNNEX Corporation and Tech Data (owned by [[Apollo]]) created the world's largest IT distributor, roughly 2x the size of nearest competitor [[Ingram Micro]].

## Why TD Synnex matters for AI

TD Synnex is a confirmation signal, not a speculation vehicle. When Hyve billings grow 95% and gross billings jump 24%, it validates that hyperscaler AI infrastructure spending is translating into real physical orders — not just announcements and RPOs. The distinction matters: [[Oracle]] can report $553B in remaining performance obligations, but TD Synnex confirms that someone is actually buying and racking the hardware.

The company sits at the intersection of every major AI infrastructure buildout: NVIDIA GPU shipments flow through its distribution network, hyperscaler custom rack builds go through Hyve, and enterprise AI adoption shows up in its reseller billings.
