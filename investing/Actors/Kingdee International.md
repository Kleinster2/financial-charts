## Evolution

- 1993 — Founded Aug 8 in Shenzhen by Xu Shaochun with RMB 5,000 borrowed capital and 20 employees
- 1998 — RMB 20M from [[IDG]] — first VC in China's financial software sector
- 1999 — Released K/3 — China's first internet-based three-tier ERP
- 2001 — IPO on HKEX GEM (8133.HK)
- 2005 — Transferred to HKEX Main Board (268.HK)
- 2014 — Xu smashes on-premise servers at company event. Cloud pivot begins
- 2015 — [[JD.com]] invests HK$1.3B for ~10%. AWS and Kingsoft partnerships
- 2017 — Overtakes international peers in China SaaS (IDC)
- 2018 — Launches Kingdee Cloud Cosmic PaaS for large enterprises. [[Huawei]] partnership
- 2023 — [[Qatar Investment Authority]] invests $200M. Revenue crosses RMB 5.68B
- 2024 — Cloud reaches 81.6% of revenue. Losses narrow to RMB 142M. OCF surges 43%
- 2025 — DeepSeek integration. Cosmic AI Agent Platform 2.0. FY2025 = first profitable year

## Investment case

Bull:
- #1 China SaaS ERP with Gartner/IDC recognition
- Cloud transition essentially complete (84% of revenue)
- FY2025 = first profitable year — inflection for re-rating
- AI monetization at >RMB 150M contracts in H1 alone
- Xinchuang mandate displacing SAP/Oracle in SOEs
- Operating cash flow already RMB 934M+ and growing
- QIA + JD.com as strategic shareholders validate platform
- Jefferies: 70% net profit CAGR over next 3 years

Bear:
- HK$35B market cap still prices in significant growth premium
- Yonyou remains larger by total revenue
- NDR below 100% in SME segments (Galaxy 95%, Stellar 93%, Jingdou 88%)
- International revenue still marginal
- China macro/SME spending weakness
- SME churn risk if economy weakens
