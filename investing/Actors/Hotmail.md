# Hotmail

Hotmail was one of the first free web-based email services, launched on July 4, 1996 by Sabeer Bhatia and Jack Smith. The name was styled "HoTMaiL" as a reference to HTML. It pioneered viral growth — every outgoing email included a footer inviting recipients to sign up, a tactic that helped it reach 12M users in 18 months. [[DFJ]] (Draper Fisher Jurvetson) led the seed investment of ~$300K, and the viral marketing playbook Hotmail demonstrated became a foundational case study in Silicon Valley growth strategy.

[[Microsoft]] acquired Hotmail in December 1997 for ~$400M (reported range $400-500M), making it one of the first major internet acquisitions. At the time of sale, Hotmail had ~10M users and was growing faster than any media company in history. Microsoft migrated it from FreeBSD/Unix servers to Windows NT (a notoriously painful process), rebranded it as MSN Hotmail, then Windows Live Hotmail, and finally retired the brand in 2013 when it became Outlook.com.
