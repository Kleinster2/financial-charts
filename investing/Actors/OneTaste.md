OneTaste — San Francisco "orgasmic meditation" company founded by [[Nicole Daedone]] in 2004. Marketed a 15-minute clitoral stroking practice as wellness/spiritual education. Courses ranged from $499 to $16,000; all-access annual subscriptions at $60,000. Revenue reached $12M by 2017. Federal forced labor conspiracy conviction of Daedone (9 years) and Rachel Cherwitz (6.5 years) in 2025-2026.

## Timeline

- 2004 — Founded in San Francisco by [[Nicole Daedone]] and Robert Kandell.
- 2009 — New York Times profile provided first national exposure.
- 2013 — Included in Inc. 5000 fastest-growing companies.
- 2014 — Robert Kandell sold his stake for $1.5M.
- 2015 — Paid a former employee a six-figure settlement for sexual assault and labor violations.
- Jun 2018 — Bloomberg expose detailed exploitative business practices and pressure on employees to incur debt.
- Oct 2018 — Closed all US locations after Bloomberg report.
- 2017 — Daedone sold ownership to three participants for $12M.
- Nov 2020 — BBC Radio 4 released 10-part investigative podcast "The Orgasm Cult."
- Nov 5, 2022 — Netflix documentary *Orgasm Inc: The Story of OneTaste* released.
- 2022 — Rebranded as "Institute of OM."
- Jun 6, 2023 — Grand jury indicted Daedone and Rachel Cherwitz on forced labor conspiracy charges (EDNY).
- May 5, 2025 — Trial began, EDNY.
- Jun 9, 2025 — Jury convicted both defendants. Deliberation under two days.
- Mar 18, 2026 — Court seized $12M from Daedone's 2017 sale (asset forfeiture).
- Mar 30, 2026 — Daedone sentenced to 9 years. Cherwitz received 6.5 years. Both held at MDC Brooklyn.
