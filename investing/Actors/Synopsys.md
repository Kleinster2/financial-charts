## TSMC AI partnership expansion (Apr 22, 2026)

Synopsys and [[TSMC]] announced an expanded multi-year collaboration covering TSMC's most advanced process and packaging nodes — 3nm and 2nm families, A16 with Super Power Rail, and A14 — with silicon-proven IP and certified EDA flows positioned at the AI-system level rather than the single-die level.

| Layer | Synopsys deliverable | TSMC node |
|-------|----------------------|-----------|
| High-speed serial | PCIe 7.0 IP, 224G IP (co-packaged optical Ethernet, UALink) | 3nm/2nm/A16/A14 |
| Memory interface | HBM4, DDR5 MRDIMM Gen2, LPDDR6/5X/5 | 3nm/2nm/A16 |
| Die-to-die | UCIe 64G (tape-out completed) | 3nm/2nm/A16 |
| Mobile | M-PHY v6.0 (first low-power version on TSMC N2P) | N2P |
| Multiphysics | Optical, electrical, thermal — agentic Fusion Compiler runs on A14 | A14 |
| 3DIC | Enhanced 5.5x reticle interposer support | All |

What is new versus the prior partnership: the announcement frames Synopsys's AI-driven flows as system-level enablement (chips + interposer + thermal + optical) rather than just digital implementation. Agentic Fusion Compiler on A14 is the most concrete disclosure — Synopsys now ships AI-first place-and-route at the leading process node. PCIe 7.0 and HBM4 silicon-proven on 2nm sets the floor for next-gen AI accelerator design starts.

The strategic read for the broader stack: the moat for [[NVIDIA]] / [[AMD]] / hyperscaler-custom-silicon designs at 2nm and below now runs through certified Synopsys IP plus TSMC manufacturing. There is no third-party path that does not depend on this combination at the leading node.

Electronic Design Automation:
- Software to design chips
- Simulation & verification
- Physical layout
- Manufacturing prep

Without EDA tools, no chips get made.
