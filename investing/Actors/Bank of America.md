**Bank of America** — Second-largest US bank. [[Consumer]] and investment banking. Q4 2025 beat on EPS and revenue.

Bank of America matters because it sits between the retail economy and the capital-markets cycle. The consumer and small-business deposit base gives it a huge funding engine, while the Merrill, markets, and corporate-banking arms let it monetize periods when trading and advisory activity pick up. That mix makes BAC one of the cleaner ways to watch whether US large-bank earnings are being driven by rate leverage, fee recovery, or outright consumer strength.

The open question is whether the franchise can keep narrowing the gap with [[JPMorgan Chase]] without taking on the same execution and valuation premium. Q1 2026 strengthened the case for operating improvement, with a record-like trading quarter, higher buybacks, and a raised NII outlook. But the note still turns on whether those gains are durable enough to justify a rerating, or whether BAC remains a solid but structurally second-tier money-center bank.

## Evolution

The story of Bank of America is the story of a national retail-bank franchise built through scale, then stress-tested by the financial crisis, and finally rebuilt into a steadier capital-return machine.

- 1998-2007: NationsBank acquired BankAmerica and created the modern coast-to-coast franchise. The strategic prize was scale: a national deposit base, broad branch footprint, and enough breadth to compete directly with the other money-center banks.
- 2008-2011: The crisis-era acquisitions of Countrywide and [[Merrill Lynch]] made BAC much more important, but also much messier. Merrill brought wealth management and institutional strength; Countrywide brought years of legal and credit pain. The bank spent much of the next decade cleaning up that balance-sheet and litigation overhang.
- 2012-2026: Moynihan's BAC became a simpler, better-capitalized institution built around consumer deposits, wealth, and measured capital return. The current debate is not survival or cleanup. It is whether that cleaner profile can turn into a sustained earnings and valuation catch-up versus JPM without BAC losing its risk discipline.

Structural read: The trading-driven beat is the pattern the [[Basel III]] capital relief and the Trump-era dealmaking rebound were set up to produce — IB +21% runs ahead of the M&A backlog and is the early read-through for [[Morgan Stanley]]/[[Goldman Sachs]]/[[JPMorgan Chase]] that also reported today.
