**Petco** — US pet health and wellness retailer trading on NASDAQ as WOOF. One of the two dominant US pet retail chains alongside [[Chewy]], with ~1,500 locations and a services-heavy model built around vet clinics, grooming, and training.

---

## Synopsis

Petco sits in a structurally attractive market — US pet spending exceeds $140B annually and has grown through every recession since tracking began — but the company itself has struggled to capitalize. The 2021 IPO at $18/share followed a leveraged buyout history (three PE owners in two decades), and the stock has since cratered as the post-COVID pet adoption boom faded and consumers traded down from premium to value brands. The debt load from its PE era remains a persistent overhang.

The investment question is whether Petco's services pivot — embedding veterinary clinics and grooming into stores — can differentiate it from [[Chewy]]'s e-commerce dominance and [[Amazon]]'s commodity pricing. Services carry higher margins and drive repeat foot traffic, but execution has been uneven. The company's early participation in [[Google]]'s Direct Offers agentic commerce program suggests management recognizes that the next retail battleground is AI-mediated discovery, not just shelf space. Whether that is strategy or desperation depends on whether the core business stabilizes first.
