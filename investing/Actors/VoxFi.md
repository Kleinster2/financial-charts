**VoxFi** — Brazilian [[prediction markets|prediction market]] platform. Founded 2025 in São Paulo by [[Fernando Carvalho]] (ex-[[QR Capital]], launched [[QBTC11]] — first Bitcoin ETF in Latin America) and [[Luis Felipe Carvalho]] (co-founder of Trampolin, acquired by [[StoneCo|Stone]]; co-founder of [[NG.CASH]]). Currently in beta/pre-launch. Categories: politics, economy, sports, entertainment. Tagline: "Sua Opinião Tem Valor."

---

## Synopsis

VoxFi is a bet on Brazil becoming a major [[prediction markets]] jurisdiction — and arriving early with local knowledge. The founder pedigree is the story: Fernando Carvalho navigated [[CVM]] approval for Brazil's first crypto ETF ([[QBTC11]] on [[B3]], 2021), and Luis Felipe Carvalho built and exited a BaaS platform to [[StoneCo|Stone]] (2021), then co-founded [[NG.CASH]] (backed by [[a16z]], NEA, [[Monashees]], $26.5M Series B in Aug 2025). Both have done the hard regulatory work before.

The timing is tight. [[Kalshi]] launched in [[Brazil]] via [[XP]] International/Clear Corretora on March 9, 2026. [[B3]] got [[CVM]] approval in February 2026 to offer event contracts as financial derivatives (professional investors only, >R$10M). Previas is structuring under CVM Resolution 88. Futuriza targets March 2026 launch. VoxFi is still in beta with no disclosed funding, no regulatory filing, and no public user metrics. The regulatory framework itself is unresolved — the SPA (sports betting regulator) says no one is currently authorized to offer prediction markets, while the CVM is moving to classify them as derivatives. VoxFi's legal approach is unknown. In a market this early, founder credibility and regulatory navigation skill are the moat — and Fernando has both.

## Regulatory environment

The regulatory question is unresolved and cuts across three bodies:

| Regulator | Jurisdiction | Stance |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| SPA (Secretaria de Premios e Apostas) | Sports/event betting under [[Lei das Bets]] | Says no one is currently authorized for prediction markets |
| [[CVM]] | Securities/derivatives | Approved [[B3]] to offer event contracts as financial derivatives (Feb 2026) |
| [[Banco Central do Brasil]] | Payment systems | Not yet weighed in on prediction market payments |

The SPA's "not authorized" statement doesn't make prediction markets illegal — it means they're not yet classified under the sports betting framework. The CVM's B3 approval suggests it may claim jurisdiction via the derivatives route. The outcome matters enormously: a CVM derivatives classification favors exchanges and fintech-structured platforms; an SPA gambling classification favors licensed betting operators.

[[Kalshi]]'s XP partnership sidesteps the question entirely by offering through offshore accounts — Brazilian investors access Kalshi via XP's international arm, avoiding domestic regulation.

## Market context

Brazil is an ideal prediction market jurisdiction:
- R$100B+ in online betting handle (2025), ~25M unique bettors
- World-leading fintech adoption ([[Pix]]: 87% adult penetration)
- Deep interest in politics, sports (football), and economic indicators (Selic, USD/BRL)
- Regulatory precedent for novel financial products ([[QBTC11]], [[Lei das Bets]])

The demand signal is strong — [[Polymarket]] already runs 217 Brazil-related markets (including 2026 presidential: Lula 45%, Flavio Bolsonaro 38%). The question is whether a domestic platform can capture this demand before [[Kalshi]]/[[B3]]/Polymarket own it.
