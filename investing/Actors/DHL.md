**DHL** is the world's largest logistics company by revenue, operating express delivery, freight forwarding, supply chain management, and e-commerce services across 220+ countries as a division of [[Deutsche Post]] AG.

## Synopsis

DHL's investment relevance comes from its position as the global logistics infrastructure layer — a proxy for cross-border trade volumes, e-commerce penetration, and supply chain complexity. The business is structured in four divisions: Express (time-definite international delivery, highest margins at ~15% EBIT), Global Forwarding/Freight (ocean/air freight brokerage), Supply Chain (contract logistics), and eCommerce Solutions (parcel delivery). Express is the crown jewel — a duopoly with [[FedEx]] and [[UPS]] in international time-definite, generating EUR 24B+ revenue with returns on invested capital above 20%. The [[Formula 1]] logistics sponsorship (~EUR 40M/year) is more than marketing: DHL actually operates the series' freight logistics, moving 1,400 tons of equipment between races worldwide.

The company sits at the center of several structural themes. Nearshoring and supply chain diversification increase complexity (more routes, more customs, more warehousing), which benefits DHL's integrated model. E-commerce penetration in emerging markets — particularly [[Southeast Asia]], [[Latin America]], and [[India]] — drives parcel volume growth. The risk side is cyclical: global trade slowdowns hit forwarding and freight directly. Revenue fell 15% in 2023 as the post-COVID logistics boom unwound, and the freight forwarding division's margins compressed sharply. The company's 2025 "Strategy 2025" targets EUR 8.5B+ EBIT, implying margin recovery that depends on trade volume normalization.
