# Karthik Rao

CEO of [[Nielsen]] since September 2023. A 23-year Nielsen veteran who rose through the company as COO and CEO of Audience Measurement before being elevated to the top role, replacing David Kenny.

## Synopsis

Rao is the insider's insider at [[Nielsen]] -- joining in 2001 and spending over two decades navigating the company through its most turbulent period: the loss of MRC accreditation (2021), the take-private by [[Elliott Management]] and Brookfield for $16B (2022), and the fundamental challenge of measuring audiences across an increasingly fragmented streaming landscape. His elevation from COO to CEO in September 2023 signaled that the PE owners preferred operational continuity over a transformational outsider hire. Kenny moved to Executive Chairman, maintaining a role in strategic direction while Rao focused on execution.

The task facing Rao is existential for Nielsen's relevance. The company's legacy panel-based measurement system -- the "Nielsen rating" that governed $70B+ in annual US TV ad spending for decades -- has been challenged by alternatives from [[Comscore]], VideoAmp, and the streaming platforms themselves (which argue they can provide census-level data superior to panel sampling). Nielsen One, the cross-platform measurement product combining linear TV and streaming into a single currency, is Rao's bet. If Nielsen One becomes the industry standard, the company retains its monopoly on audience measurement and justifies the $16B take-private price. If the market fragments into multiple measurement currencies, Nielsen's pricing power erodes. Rao's career at the company -- starting at [[Ogilvy & Mather]] in India before joining Nielsen -- gives him deep client relationships, but the question is whether relationships can substitute for technological superiority in a data-science arms race.
