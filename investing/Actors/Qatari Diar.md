**Qatari Diar** is the real estate investment arm of the [[QIA|Qatar Investment Authority]], responsible for developing and managing mega-projects both domestically and across a global portfolio spanning [[Europe]], [[Africa]], and [[Southeast Asia]].

## Synopsis

Qatari Diar's flagship is Lusail City, a $45B planned city covering 38 square kilometers north of Doha, designed to house 450,000 people and serve as [[Qatar]]'s modern administrative and commercial center. The project accelerated ahead of the 2022 FIFA World Cup, which anchored the Lusail Stadium, and now functions as the proving ground for Qatar's post-hydrocarbon urbanization model. Internationally, Qatari Diar holds stakes in London's Chelsea Barracks redevelopment, The Shard (via a Qatari royal family consortium), and multiple hospitality/residential developments across North Africa.

The entity matters to the vault as a window into how Gulf sovereign capital deploys into global real estate at scale. Unlike private developers, Qatari Diar operates on state timelines with patient capital — projects can run 15-20 years from masterplan to completion. The risk is execution complexity on simultaneous mega-projects, plus the reputational exposure that comes with sovereign development (labor conditions, political entanglements). Qatari Diar's willingness to absorb upfront losses for strategic positioning — building entire cities to attract corporate tenants — makes it a model for how [[GCC]] states convert hydrocarbon wealth into physical infrastructure.
