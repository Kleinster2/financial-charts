VOO is [[Vanguard]]'s S&P 500 ETF and one of the three dominant vehicles for passive large-cap US equity exposure, alongside [[SPY]] ([[State Street]]) and IVV ([[iShares]]/[[BlackRock]]). Launched in September 2010, it has grown to approximately $1.2T in AUM, making it one of the largest ETFs in the world. VOO's 0.03% expense ratio (3 basis points) undercuts SPY's 0.0945% and matches IVV's 0.03%, reflecting [[Vanguard]]'s cost-leadership strategy rooted in its mutual ownership structure (Vanguard is owned by its funds, which are owned by their shareholders — so fee reductions flow directly to investors).

VOO's rise illustrates the generational shift from actively managed mutual funds to passive index ETFs: it didn't exist until 2010 (35 years after Vanguard's first index fund), yet now rivals SPY — which launched in 1993 — in total assets. The fee compression it helped drive has reshaped the entire asset management industry, contributing to margin pressure at [[Fidelity]], [[T. Rowe Price]], and other active managers.
