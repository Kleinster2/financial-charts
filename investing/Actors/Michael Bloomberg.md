## Career

**1966–1981: Salomon Brothers**
Started in entry-level position after Harvard MBA. Within 15 years, became partner leading block trading operations. Pushed out when Phibro acquired Salomon in 1981.

**1981: Founded Bloomberg LP**
Took **$10M severance** from Salomon and founded Innovative Market Systems (renamed Bloomberg LP in 1986) with Thomas Secunda, Duncan MacMillan, and Charles Zegar. First terminal shipped to Merrill Lynch in 1982.

**2002–2013: NYC Mayor**
Elected 109th mayor of New York City in November 2001, weeks after 9/11. Served three consecutive terms.

| Achievement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Budget | Turned $6B deficit into $3B surplus |
| Crime | Reduced by one-third |
| Graduation rates | Increased 42% |
| Life expectancy | Increased 3 years |
| Smoking ban | First major city indoor workplace ban (2003) |
| Trans fat ban | First US city (2006) |
| Carbon footprint | Reduced 13% |

**2014–2023: Return to Bloomberg LP**
Returned as CEO after mayoral terms.

**2020: Presidential campaign**
Ran for Democratic nomination. Spent ~$1B of personal funds. Dropped out after Super Tuesday, endorsed Biden.
