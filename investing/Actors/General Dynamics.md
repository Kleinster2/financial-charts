## Gulfstream certification dispute (Feb 2026)

[[Donald Trump]] threatened to decertify all Canadian-built aircraft (i.e. [[Bombardier]]) and impose 50% tariffs unless [[Canada]] certified Gulfstream business jets. Transport Canada certified the G500 and G600 on Feb 15, 2026. The G700 and G800 remain pending due to a fuel-system icing concern — the [[FAA]] and EASA granted temporary exemptions, but Canada held to stricter standards.

The dispute exposed the competitive tension: Bombardier's Global Express competes directly with Gulfstream in the ultra-long-range segment. See [[Canada Gulfstream certification]] for full timeline.

## Q1 2026 earnings and defense backlog signal (Apr 2026)

General Dynamics' Apr 29 move was an order-book repricing: the stock moved from $313.68 to $338.73, +8.0%, after Q1 results showed both operating leverage and very strong defense demand.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | YoY / note |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Revenue | $13.481B | +10.3% |
| Operating earnings | $1.420B | +12.0% |
| Diluted EPS | $4.10 | +12.0% |
| Operating cash flow | $2.2B | 192% of net earnings |
| Orders | $26.6B | 2.0x book-to-bill |
| Backlog | $130.8B | — |
| Total estimated contract value | $188.4B | backlog + potential contract value |

The segment mix was the important detail. Marine Systems revenue rose 21.0% to $4.343B and operating earnings rose 26.4% to $316M, while Aerospace revenue rose 8.4% and the defense segments posted a 2.2x book-to-bill. That makes the quarter less about one-time margin and more about submarine/shipbuilding demand converting into funded backlog.
