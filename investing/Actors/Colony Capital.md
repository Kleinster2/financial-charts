Colony Capital was a global real estate and investment firm founded by [[Thomas Barrack]] in 1991. Originally focused on distressed real estate — buying portfolios from the RTC during the S&L crisis — it grew into a diversified alternative asset manager with $44B in AUM at peak. Under CEO [[Marc Ganzi]] (appointed 2020), the firm pivoted aggressively toward digital infrastructure (data centers, cell towers, fiber), selling off legacy real estate and hospitality assets. The transformation culminated in a June 2021 rebrand to [[DigitalBridge]], marking one of the most dramatic corporate pivots in alternative asset management.

Barrack's tenure was marked by both deal-making flair and controversy. He served as chair of Trump's 2017 inaugural committee and was indicted in 2021 on charges of illegal foreign lobbying on behalf of the [[United Arab Emirates]], though he was acquitted in 2022. The firm's legacy portfolio included casinos, hotels, and healthcare real estate that became deadweight during the digital pivot.
