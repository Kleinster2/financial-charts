**Holly Kim** is the founder and managing partner of [[Glendon Capital Management]], a Los Angeles-based distressed debt and special situations fund managing approximately $5B in assets. Before founding Glendon, Kim was a veteran of [[Oaktree]] Capital Management, where she spent over a decade working under [[Howard Marks]] in the distressed debt group — one of the premier training grounds for credit investors.

Kim founded Glendon in 2014, building it into one of the larger woman-founded alternative investment firms. The fund specializes in distressed corporate credit, structured products, and special situations — the kind of deep-value, complex credit analysis that requires legal, operational, and financial expertise simultaneously.

Glendon gained particular visibility in March 2026 when the firm challenged [[Blue Owl]] Capital's OBDC (Blue Owl BDC) portfolio valuations, flagging [[Peraton]] (a [[Veritas Capital]] portfolio company) as an example of potentially aggressive marks in private credit portfolios. The critique fit a broader theme of private credit valuation opacity and the risk that BDC NAVs may not reflect true underlying credit quality — a concern that has grown as private credit AUM has expanded past $1.5T.
