## Key insights (Odd Lots, Mar 4 2026)

- China imported 11.6M bpd crude in 2025; 1.4M bpd from [[Iran]] (12%), almost entirely via [[Chinese teapot refineries|teapots]]
- National oil companies ([[Sinopec]], [[CNPC]]) exited Iran trade entirely due to US dollar system exposure risk; teapots stepped in because they don't need dollar access
- China's strategic + commercial oil stockpiles provide ~120 days of net crude import cover at 2025 levels
- China imports ~1/3 of LNG from Middle East (mostly [[QatarEnergy]]) — no strategic gas reserve equivalent
- 43% of China's natural gas production from unconventional sources (2025), but growth was a "shale evolution, not revolution"
- China's diesel demand already peaked (property collapse); gasoline peaking (EVs); overall oil demand peak moved to 2027
- China positioning as green tech superpower — countries swapping energy import bills for Chinese renewable technology purchases
