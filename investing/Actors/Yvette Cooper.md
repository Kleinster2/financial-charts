# Yvette Cooper

Yvette Cooper is the UK's Foreign Secretary and a recurring diplomatic actor for China, India, Ukraine, Iran/Hormuz, and Hong Kong files. For the investing vault, her relevance is the Starmer government's security-and-economy China posture: engage where trade, finance, energy, AI, climate, and global-crisis coordination matter, while preserving UK national-security challenge points.

## June 2026 China visit

Cooper visited China from June 1 to 3, 2026, at [[Wang Yi]]'s invitation and held the 11th China-UK Strategic Dialogue in Beijing on June 2. Reuters reported that the broader China-India trip was framed around global issues including the [[Strait of Hormuz]], the Russia-Ukraine war, and the Ebola outbreak, with China stops in Beijing and [[Shenzhen]] and a science-and-technology program.

The market relevance is not that UK-China risk disappeared. It is that the Starmer government is trying to keep a high-level economic and technology channel open while defining a narrower but still active security perimeter around Chinese firms, data, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and critical infrastructure.
