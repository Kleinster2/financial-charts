# Mike Cannon-Brookes

Co-founder and co-CEO of [[Atlassian]] (TEAM), the Australian enterprise software company he started with [[Scott Farquhar]] in 2002 with $10,000 in credit card debt. The two built Atlassian into a ~$50B market cap company without traditional enterprise sales teams — relying instead on bottoms-up developer adoption of [[Jira]], Confluence, and Bitbucket. Cannon-Brookes is also one of Australia's most prominent climate investors: his family office Grok Ventures led a A$5B bid to acquire [[AGL Energy]] (Australia's largest carbon emitter) in 2022, which failed but resulted in AGL splitting its coal and clean energy businesses. He invested in Sun Cable, a proposed Australia-to-Singapore solar power transmission project that went into administration in 2023 before being restructured. Net worth ~$10B, making him one of Australia's richest people.
