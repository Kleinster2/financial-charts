# China Media Group

[[China]]'s state media conglomerate formed in 2018 by merging CCTV (China Central Television), China National Radio, and China Radio International. The most powerful broadcast entity in the world by audience reach.

## Synopsis

China Media Group operates the single most-watched annual television broadcast on Earth: the Spring Festival Gala (Chunwan), which draws ~700-800M viewers across ~80% of active TV sets in [[China]]. The 2018 consolidation under CMG unified China's previously fragmented state broadcasting apparatus under direct [[Chinese Communist Party]] control, reporting to the CCP's Propaganda Department rather than the State Council. This structural change increased political control over broadcast content and made CMG a direct instrument of [[Xi Jinping]]'s information strategy.

For investors, CMG matters in two ways. First, the Spring Festival Gala has become a showcase for Chinese technology companies -- the [[2026 Spring Festival Gala]] featured [[AGIBOT]] and [[Yuhang Hu]]'s Elf Xuan robot, providing prime-time exposure to hundreds of millions of viewers and signaling state endorsement of the robotics sector. Companies featured on the gala receive a legitimacy boost that translates into stock price impact for listed entities. Second, CMG's CCTV channels control the most valuable advertising inventory in Chinese media, and CCTV bidding (especially the annual auction for ad slots) is a barometer of corporate confidence in the Chinese consumer economy. CMG also operates CGTN (China Global Television Network), the English-language international channel that carries Chinese state narratives globally.
