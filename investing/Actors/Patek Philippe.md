# Patek Philippe

Patek Philippe is a Swiss luxury watchmaker and one of the "Holy Trinity" alongside [[Audemars Piguet]] and [[Vacheron Constantin]]. Family-owned by the Stern family since 1932, the company produces ~70,000 watches per year — a deliberate production constraint that sustains extreme secondary-market premiums. Revenue is estimated at CHF 2.2B+ (2023). The Nautilus (ref. 5711, discontinued 2021) became the most sought-after luxury watch of the 2020s, with secondary-market prices reaching 3-5x retail before normalizing.

## Investment relevance

Patek is privately held and not directly investable, but its pricing dynamics function as a luxury demand indicator. Secondary-market Patek prices (tracked by WatchCharts, Chrono24) peaked in early 2022 alongside crypto and growth stocks, then corrected ~30-40% — correlating with risk-asset repricing more than traditional luxury cycles. The watch market's recovery trajectory signals high-net-worth discretionary spending patterns. Patek's refusal to expand production, unlike [[Rolex]] which has been incrementally scaling, keeps the supply-demand imbalance structural rather than cyclical.
