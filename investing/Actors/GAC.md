**GAC** (HKEX: 2238, SHA: 601238) is a Guangzhou-based state-owned automaker navigating the treacherous transition from JV-dependent ICE manufacturer to EV competitor, with its Aion brand as the primary bet.

## Synopsis

GAC's story is the Chinese auto industry's structural dilemma in miniature. The company built its business on joint ventures with [[Toyota]] (GAC Toyota) and Honda (GAC Honda), which together generated the majority of profits through sedan and SUV sales to Chinese consumers. But the EV transition is destroying the JV model: Chinese consumers are shifting rapidly to domestic EV brands ([[BYD]], [[NIO]], [[Li Auto]]), and the Japanese partners' EV lineups are uncompetitive in China. GAC Honda sales fell ~30% in 2024, and GAC Toyota has been losing share steadily. The company's response has been Aion, its own EV brand launched in 2020, which reached ~480,000 units sold in 2024 — making it a top-five Chinese EV brand — primarily through affordable models priced at RMB 100,000-200,000 targeting ride-hailing and budget consumers.

The second vector is autonomous driving: GAC selected [[WeRide]] as a major ADAS provider (WePilot system) for multiple passenger car models, positioning itself as a deployment platform for L2+ autonomy. Revenue is ~RMB 130B, but profitability is compressed as Aion's growth requires heavy subsidies and the JV contributions shrink. The stock trades at depressed valuations (sub-0.5x book) reflecting the market's skepticism that GAC can complete the ICE-to-EV transition profitably while its JV cash cow deteriorates. The competitive moat is thin — Aion competes on price in the most brutal segment of the Chinese EV market, and GAC lacks the vertical integration ([[BYD]]'s batteries, [[Tesla]]'s software) that creates durable advantages.
