## Why he matters

Waldron sits at the point where franchise strategy and day-to-day execution meet. In April 2026, after Goldman's Q1 results disappointed on FICC relative to peers, he publicly defended the fixed-income quarter as good in absolute terms, arguing that volatile rates and commodities markets can leave a desk "in traffic" even when the broader franchise is functioning well. That makes him a useful gauge for whether Goldman treats a weak quarter as an operational issue, a positioning issue, or just noise.
