**IREN (Iris Energy)** — former [[Bitcoin]] miner turned vertically integrated AI cloud operator. The May 2026 sequence moved the story from "power-rich miner pivoting to AI" to "contracted Blackwell capacity owner": $9.7B [[Microsoft]] contract, $3.4B [[NVIDIA]] managed-cloud contract, up to $2.1B NVIDIA investment right, and a $1.6B [[Dell]] purchase agreement for air-cooled [[Blackwell]] systems at Childress.

The research question is whether secured power plus fast GPU procurement can compound before hardware depreciation and financing costs catch up. IREN has the strongest customer validation in the crypto-to-AI cohort, but its equity increasingly prices execution speed rather than current financials.

## May 2026 NVIDIA-Dell capacity conversion

The Reuters headline is the procurement leg of a larger May 2026 chain. On May 7, IREN signed a five-year, ~$3.4B AI infrastructure cloud services contract with [[NVIDIA]]. The service is for NVIDIA internal AI and research workloads, using managed GPU cloud services rather than bare-metal colocation. IREN said the contract would be serviced by air-cooled [[Blackwell]] systems deployed within roughly 60MW of existing data centers at its Childress, Texas campus.

On May 26, IREN filed the supply-side piece: IE US Hardware 4 Inc., a wholly owned subsidiary, entered into purchase documentation with [[Dell|Dell Marketing L.P.]] for GPUs and ancillary products and services for about $1.6B. The equipment is for the same Childress deployment and is payable in installments within 30 days of each shipment tranche. IREN unconditionally guarantees the subsidiary's obligations.

This matters because the risk moves from "can IREN find a customer?" to "can IREN finance, receive, install, and commission Blackwell capacity on time?" The company is targeting early 2027 commissioning. If commissioned, IREN says ARR would move from $3.7B to $4.4B, but that $4.4B figure includes assumptions: roughly $1.9B average annual revenue from the Microsoft contract, $0.7B from the NVIDIA contract, and $1.8B from planned GPU deployments at British Columbia and Childress. It is a run-rate target, not fully contracted revenue.

Tape: IREN was +5.2% on the May 26 session / after-hours screen, while Dell was +3.3%, NVIDIA was roughly flat-to-down, and the broader crypto-to-AI cohort was also bid. The move supports the IREN-specific PC3 / idiosyncratic-capacity read in [[Crypto-to-AI]], but it was not isolated from the broader AI infrastructure tape.

## Financials

Q3 FY2026 captured the transition cost: reported revenue fell while IREN decommissioned mining hardware ahead of GPU installation and billing. The market is no longer valuing the company on current mining revenue; it is valuing contracted AI cloud capacity and the ability to finance/commission it.

|--------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Total revenue | $144.8M | $184.7M | Lower average Bitcoin price and mining-hardware decommissioning |
| Net income / loss | -$247.8M | -$155.4M | Includes non-cash impairments and capped-call fair-value losses |
| Adjusted EBITDA | $59.5M | $75.3M | Transition quarter before AI cloud billing step-up |
| Cash | $2.6B at Apr. 30, 2026 | — | Near-term capex funding source alongside operating cash flow and GPU financing |
| ARR under contract | $3.1B | — | Targeting $3.7B exiting CY2026 |

The May 26 Dell agreement adds a financing clock. Hardware payments are due within 30 days of shipment tranche, while revenue depends on delivery, installation, commissioning, and utilization. That timing gap is the core financial risk.

- Valuation stretched after run
- Customer concentration shifts from Microsoft-only to Microsoft + NVIDIA, but still concentrated
- Execution on Childress and Sweetwater buildout
- GPU financing and post-shipment payment obligations scale faster than reported revenue
- Competition from other miners
- Bitcoin volatility still exposure
