# Chinese Academy of Sciences

China's national academy for natural sciences and high-technology research. CAS is a state research system rather than a listed company, but it matters to the investing vault because many Chinese technology sectors pass through CAS-linked institutes, labs, spinouts, and industrialization programs before becoming public-market or private-company stories.

## Why it matters

CAS is one of the clearest institutional bridges between Chinese state research and commercial technology. In robotics, the important node is the [[Shenyang Institute of Automation]], which developed China's first industrial robot prototype in 1980 and the SZJ-1 teach-and-playback industrial manipulator in 1982. In semiconductors and AI, CAS-linked people and institutes recur across talent pipelines, research programs, and company formation.

The investment read is structural: when a Chinese company appears to have emerged suddenly, check whether the underlying capability came from a CAS institute, national laboratory, or long-running state program. The institutional memory can be decades older than the commercial surface.

---

## Robotics lineage

CAS's Shenyang Institute of Automation became a core node in China's robotics history under Jiang Xinsong. The sequence runs from late-1970s robotics planning into 1980s industrial robots, 863 Program intelligent-robot research, undersea and mobile robotics, and then Siasun's commercialization path around 1999-2000.

This is the background behind [[Irene Zhang]]'s May 2026 ChinaTalk piece: China's current robotics wave is not just [[Unitree]] and humanoid media attention. It rests on a state-research base that spent decades working on control systems, precision instrumentation, autonomous movement, undersea vehicles, welding robots, and production-line automation.
