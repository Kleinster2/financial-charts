**AMC Networks** (Nasdaq: AMCX; renamed AMC Global Media Inc. April 8, 2026) — small-cap premium cable-network and streaming company: AMC, BBC America, IFC, Sundance TV, and the targeted streamers AMC+, Acorn TV, Shudder, ALLBLK. Walking Dead, Better Call Saul, Interview with the Vampire. First cable company to formally partner with [[Runway]] for AI-powered pre-viz and marketing (Jun 2025). Dolan-family controlled. Not to be confused with [[AMC Entertainment]], the theater chain (ticker AMC).

## One-line read

AMC Networks is a Dolan-controlled small-cap running the managed transition from declining cable networks to niche streaming because linear affiliate revenue is melting faster than targeted-streamer growth replaces it, with the bet being that ~$200M+/yr of free cash flow against a ~$1.3B pro-forma net-debt stack (maturity wall now pushed to 2032) carries the equity until either streaming scale or a strategic transaction resolves the endgame — which the Class B super-vote ensures happens only on the family's terms.

## Financials

FY2025 (reported Feb 11, 2026) marked the crossover: streaming became the largest single revenue source in the domestic segment — streaming revenue +12% to $677M against the structural decline of linear affiliate fees. CEO Kristin Dolan called it "a key milestone in our transition from a cable networks business to a global streaming and technology focused content company." Free cash flow of $272M beat the company's own raised forecast.

| Year (Dec FY) | Revenue | Operating income | Net income |
|---------------|---------|------------------|-----------|
| 2021 | $3,077.6M | $489.9M | $250.6M |
| 2022 | $3,096.5M | $86.9M | $7.6M |
| 2023 | $2,711.9M | $388.4M | $215.5M |
| 2024 | $2,421.3M | $(39.6)M | $(226.5)M |
| 2025 | $2,311.8M | $133.3M | $89.4M |

The arc is the story: revenue down ~25% from the 2022 peak as cord-cutting erodes affiliate and ad revenue, with profitability whipsawed by content write-offs (2024's loss) — but cash generation persistent throughout. Q1 2026: revenue $542.1M (−2%), adjusted operating income $69M (−34%, 13% margin), FCF $65M, a GAAP net loss of $(18.9)M on refinancing and content costs. FY2026 guidance: ~$2.25B revenue, ~$350M AOI, ≥$200M FCF. Figures verified against the official releases; DB annual series (`income_statement_annual`) is clean for AMCX and matches.

## Apr 2026 — debt maturity wall pushed out

Exchange offer announced February 23, 2026: any-and-all swap of the 10.25% senior secured notes due 2029 into new 10.50% senior secured notes due 2032. Outcome: $830.6M exchanged at early settlement plus $30.7M late-tendered — 98.43% of the outstanding principal; the residual redeemed April 6, 2026 at 105.125% plus accrued interest. Pushes the maturity wall out three years at a 25bp coupon cost — removing the near-term refinancing overhang. [[Seeking Alpha]] upgraded to Buy in the same window, citing stabilizing revenue and the improving streaming transition.

## Ownership and the Dolan question

The Dolan family holds 100% of the Class B super-voting shares — absolute voting control regardless of economic stake, the same structure the family used at Cablevision and uses at MSG. Practical consequences: hostile bids are impossible, activists have no lever, and any strategic outcome happens on the family's timing. Analysts floated privatization and merger scenarios through 2025–2026 (media-M&A outlooks regularly list AMCX among potential targets); management has publicly reiterated a standalone focus. Contrast with [[Starz]]: similar business, no controlling holder — which is why Starz has an active takeover campaign and AMCX has only speculation. The rebrand to AMC Global Media and the 2032 maturity push both read as commitments to running the transition longer, not staging an exit.

## What to watch

- FCF delivery vs the ≥$200M 2026 guide — the entire equity case rests on cash conversion persisting while revenue shrinks; Q1 delivered $65M.
- Streaming revenue growth vs linear decay — streaming (+12% in 2025) must outgrow the affiliate/ad melt; watch whether the crossover milestone extends to consolidated revenue stabilization (guide: ~$2.25B, still down YoY).
- Leverage path — pro forma 3.5x net leverage post-exchange; deleveraging from $200M+ FCF against a $1.3B stack is slow but viable. A re-levering event would break the thesis.
- Dolan family signals — any 13D/A activity, privatization chatter hardening, or a strategic partner announcement. The Class B structure means the only deal that happens is one the family initiates.
- Content franchise health — the Anne Rice universe and Walking Dead extensions carry AMC+; a flagship miss pressures both streaming ARPU and the licensing market for the library.
- [[Runway]] AI integration — cost-side experiment shared with [[Lionsgate]]; watch for disclosed savings or visible AI-assisted output.

## Analysis

AMCX is the control case for the question hanging over all small-cap premium content: what is a shrinking-but-cash-generative niche content business worth when nobody can force a sale? The business itself sits between [[Starz]] (nearly identical model — targeted streamers, ~$1–2.3B revenue, heavy debt, linear melt) and the conglomerate networks. But the capital-structure difference drives everything: Starz, with no controlling holder, trades takeover probability ([[Byron Allen]] at the gate); AMCX, with the Dolan Class B super-vote, trades only its own cash flow. The market prices that difference — STRZ has tripled off its lows on deal tape; AMCX's ~52% move tracks fundamentals (debt exchange, streaming crossover) with no control premium.

The 2026 moves — maturity wall pushed to 2032 at +25bp, the AMC Global Media rebrand, reiterated standalone language — collectively signal the family intends to run the transition, not sell into weakness. That makes the stock a pure execution story: if FCF holds ≥$200M while streaming replaces linear, the equity compounds quietly from a depressed base; if cash conversion cracks before the transition completes, there is no acquirer backstop because no acquirer can move without the Dolans. The {WBD, PSKY, AMCX} 3Y cluster branch and the 0.38/0.31 pairings with PSKY/STRZ say the market trades it as part of the legacy-media restructuring corner — but the restructuring option is uniquely gated here.
