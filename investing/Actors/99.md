**99** — [[Didi]]'s [[Brazil]] subsidiary, operating ride-hailing and food delivery (99Food). Originally pulled out of food delivery in 2023, relaunched in 2025 as part of a combined R$7.6B ($1.5B) investment pledge with [[Keeta]] ([[Meituan]]) to challenge [[iFood]]'s ~80% market share in [[China|Chinese]]-backed push into Latin America's largest consumer market. Three former [[iFood]] employees investigated by police for data theft later found working at 99.

## History in Brazil

[[Didi]] acquired 99 in 2018 to enter the Brazilian ride-hailing market. 99Food launched as a delivery service but withdrew in 2023 — one of two international challengers (alongside [[Uber Eats]], which left in 2022) that failed to dislodge [[iFood]]. Relaunched in 2025 with significantly larger capital backing and Brazilian government support.

---

## Corporate espionage link

Three of four former [[iFood]] employees investigated by police for suspected data theft went to work for 99. Judge-ordered search-and-seizure warrants executed in October 2025. One restaurant owner told FT he was contacted by 99 representatives who had specific details about his [[iFood]] contract. 99 stated it "did not tolerate or endorse any form of misconduct involving the use of external data obtained through illegal means." See [[Chinese entry into Brazil food delivery 2025]].
