Butterfield Market is a family-owned gourmet grocery on the Upper East Side of Manhattan, operating since 1915 at 1114 Lexington Avenue (between 77th and 78th Streets). It is one of New York City's longest-running independent specialty food shops, known for curated charcuterie, prepared foods, artisanal cheeses, and its signature frozen yogurt — a neighborhood institution since the 1970s.

Butterfield occupies a specific niche in NYC's premium grocery landscape: neighborhood-scale, personal-service retail in an era dominated by upscale chains. It competes for the affluent Upper East Side shopper alongside [[Eli's]], [[Agata & Valentina]], and national premium chains like [[Whole Foods]] ([[Amazon]]). The store's longevity reflects the durability of the high-end independent grocer model in dense, wealthy urban neighborhoods — a segment that has proven resilient even as mid-market grocery faces consolidation pressure.
