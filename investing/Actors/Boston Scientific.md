## Why Boston Scientific matters

Growth leader in medtech. While [[Medtronic]] and [[Abbott]] grow mid-single digits, Boston Scientific has delivered 18%+ CAGR in market cap over 5 years. The company's focused portfolio in high-growth interventional markets is outperforming diversified peers.

EP/ablation strength. Boston Scientific's FARAPULSE pulsed field ablation system was first-to-market in the US (2024) and is driving share gains against [[Medtronic]] and [[Abbott]].

## Q1 2026 earnings — +9% on beat, FY EP guide cut to ~10% growth as PFA competition bites (Apr 22, 2026)

BSX reported a strong headline Q1 and the stock jumped 9% — the highest single-day gain in two years — but the read underneath is mixed. Management cut full-year 2026 global [[Electrophysiology|EP]] growth guidance to about 10% with US growth in the mid-single-digits, down from the prior mid-teens-plus assumption. The cut is a direct acknowledgment that pulsed-field ablation ([[PFA]]) competition from [[Medtronic]], [[Johnson & Johnson]], and [[Abbott]] is re-dividing a market BSX briefly dominated with [[Farapulse]].

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $5.203B | $4.66B | +11.6% reported / +9.4% organic |
| GAAP net income | $1.341B | — | — |
| GAAP EPS | $0.90 | — | — |
| Adjusted EPS | $0.80 | — | Beat |

Guidance reset:

| Line | New (Apr 22) | Prior | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY26 global EP growth | ~10% | Mid-teens+ | ~5-6 points cut |
| FY26 US EP growth | Mid-single-digits | High-teens | ~10+ points cut |
| FY26 organic growth (total) | 6.5%-8% | Above 8% | Narrowed and cut |

Why the stock rallied anyway. Three reasons:

1. Reset is now priced. BSX had already fallen ~28% YTD entering the print, with the CHAMPION-AF reaction in March compounding disruption fears. The guide cut was expected to be worse; the actual reset reads as "bounded damage, not accelerating erosion."
2. Farapulse China approval. [[NMPA]] approval for the OPAL HDx Mapping System with FARAVIEW within the quarter builds out the full PFA platform — management called this the most competitive PFA system in the market. China approval is the clearest geography-specific positive in the print.
3. WATCHMAN stabilization. WATCHMAN grew 19% organically in Q1, though US volumes began declining in mid-February on "practice pattern shifts." The fact that it is not collapsing post-CHAMPION-AF is itself a re-rating datapoint.

The structural question unchanged. The competitive landscape in pulsed-field ablation now has four credible platforms — Farapulse (BSX), PulseSelect (Medtronic), VARIPULSE (J&J), and Volt PFA (Abbott). The [[CHAMPION-AF]] reaction, the mid-February US WATCHMAN slowdown, and the EP guidance cut together describe a company moving from share leadership to share defense in its highest-growth segment. The thesis now rests on [[FARAVIEW]] mapping integration and platform stickiness, not Farapulse hardware alone.

Read-across. BSX's guide cut is the first explicit corporate acknowledgment that PFA pricing and share are compressing faster than models assumed. [[Medtronic]] (reports mid-May) and [[Johnson & Johnson]] (reported Apr 15) face the opposite side of the ledger — their PFA franchises gained share, but at what price realization remains the open question.

## March 30: BSX -9% (2.7σ) on CHAMPION-AF trial results

Boston Scientific fell 9% on March 30, 2026 ($69.17 to $62.93) after the CHAMPION-AF trial results for [[Watchman FLX]] disappointed investors despite meeting primary endpoints.

The trial tested Watchman FLX as a first-line alternative to NOACs (novel oral anticoagulants) for stroke prevention in non-valvular [[atrial fibrillation]] patients:

| Endpoint | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Primary safety (36 months) | Met — Watchman FLX statistically superior to NOACs |
| Primary efficacy | Met |
| Ischemic stroke rate | Slightly higher vs NOACs — the number that spooked the market |

The trial met all primary and secondary safety and efficacy endpoints, but the slightly higher ischemic stroke rate compared to NOACs raised questions about whether Watchman FLX can expand beyond its current addressable market (patients who can't tolerate blood thinners) into the broader first-line stroke prevention population. That expansion was the growth thesis embedded in the stock's ~40x earnings multiple.

Separately, on March 28 the HI-PEITHO trial for the EKOS Endovascular System showed a 61% reduction in the primary composite endpoint for intermediate-risk acute pulmonary embolism vs anticoagulation alone — a positive result that was overshadowed by the CHAMPION-AF reaction.
