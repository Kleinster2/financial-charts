#actor #brand #confectionery #uk

Cadbury — British chocolate brand, owned by [[Mondelez]] since 2010 (after Kraft Foods' £11.5B acquisition of Cadbury plc). Founded in 1824 by John Cadbury in Birmingham. The Bournville factory in Birmingham — purpose-built as a model industrial village in 1879 — remains [[Mondelez]]'s flagship UK chocolate production site and hosted the April 2026 production of [[Celleste Bio]]'s first cell-cultured cocoa butter chocolate bars.

## Bournville — the factory

Bournville opened in 1879 as a purpose-built model village around the Cadbury factory, part of the Quaker founders' reformist vision combining manufacturing, housing, and worker welfare. It remains an operating chocolate factory under [[Mondelez]] and — as of April 2026 — served as the production venue for [[Celleste Bio]]'s first lab-grown chocolate bars, signaling [[Mondelez]] willingness to associate the cell-cultured cocoa technology with its premium British chocolate identity.
