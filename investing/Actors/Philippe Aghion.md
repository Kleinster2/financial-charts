# Philippe Aghion

French economist and a leading theorist of [[Schumpeterian growth]] (innovation-driven growth through "creative destruction"). His vault edge is the productivity/innovation lens on the EU competitiveness debate — he is one of the named critics (the "ABG" trio) of [[Paul Krugman]]'s [[US-Europe productivity paradox]] framing.

## Relevant analysis

Named (with [[Antonin Bergeaud]] and [[Luis Garicano]]) as a critic of [[Paul Krugman]]'s thesis that the US-vs-EU productivity-growth gap is a measurement artefact. Per Krugman's May 30 2026 response, the ABG position holds that US productivity growth genuinely outpaces Europe's and that US technology leadership drives widening wage/profit gaps — a producer-surplus reading that [[Brad DeLong]] contests via the product-wage/user-wage distinction. See [[US-Europe productivity paradox]].
