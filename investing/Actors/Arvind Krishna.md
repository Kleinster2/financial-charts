#actor #person #ceo

**Arvind Krishna** has served as CEO of [[IBM]] since April 2020, architect of the company's strategic pivot from legacy IT services toward hybrid cloud (via [[Red Hat]]) and enterprise AI (via [[watsonx]]). An IIT Kanpur and University of Illinois PhD graduate, Krishna spent 30 years inside IBM before ascending to the top job.

## Synopsis

Krishna's defining move was engineering the $34B acquisition of [[Red Hat]] in 2019 while still leading IBM's Cloud & Cognitive Software division — the largest software acquisition in history at the time, and the bet that IBM's future lay in hybrid cloud infrastructure rather than consulting-heavy IT services. As CEO, he followed through by spinning off the managed infrastructure services business as [[Kyndryl]] in November 2021, shedding $19B in low-growth revenue to focus the remaining IBM on higher-margin software and consulting. The result: IBM's revenue growth turned positive in 2022 for the first time in a decade, and the stock hit all-time highs in 2024-2025 as the [[watsonx]] AI platform gave IBM a credible narrative in the enterprise AI market.

The bear case on Krishna's IBM is that the company remains a subscale player in both cloud (distant fourth behind [[AWS]], [[Azure]], and [[Google Cloud]]) and AI (watsonx competes with [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], and hyperscaler-native offerings). IBM's hybrid cloud thesis depends on regulated industries (banking, government, healthcare) preferring on-premises control over public cloud — a valid insight, but one that limits the addressable market. Krishna has navigated these constraints skillfully, but the open question is whether IBM can sustain mid-single-digit growth or whether the Red Hat acquisition merely stabilized a declining franchise.
