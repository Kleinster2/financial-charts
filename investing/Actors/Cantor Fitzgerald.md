**Cantor Fitzgerald** — Wall Street financial services firm founded in 1945, specializing in fixed income brokerage, investment banking, commercial real estate, and special situations. Now run by [[Brandon Lutnick]] after his father [[Howard Lutnick]] became [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s Commerce Secretary in January 2025. The firm lost 658 of its 960 New York employees in the September 11, 2001 attacks — the largest single-firm loss of that day — and rebuilt under Howard Lutnick's leadership into a diversified financial services group.

## Synopsis

Cantor's current investment relevance stems from the Lutnick political connection. The firm entered the [[Tariff refund claims market]] in mid-2025, buying rights to IEEPA tariff refunds at cents on the dollar — trading ~$10M+ with capacity for "several hundred million." This created a conflict-of-interest scandal given Howard Lutnick's role architecting the tariff policy his firm was betting against. Brandon Lutnick has also been active in crypto/SPAC deals, including involvement with [[Tether]] and [[21 Capital]], a Bitcoin acquisition vehicle.

The firm's core business remains institutional fixed income brokerage (US Treasuries, agencies, corporates) where it is one of the 24 primary dealers authorized to trade directly with the [[Federal Reserve]]. Real estate operations (through Cantor Commercial Real Estate and affiliate [[Newmark Group]]) and investment banking round out the revenue base.
