**Cantor Fitzgerald** — Wall Street financial services firm founded in 1945, specializing in fixed income brokerage, investment banking, commercial real estate, and special situations. Now run by [[Brandon Lutnick]] after his father [[Howard Lutnick]] became [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s Commerce Secretary in January 2025. The firm lost 658 of its 960 New York employees in the September 11, 2001 attacks — the largest single-firm loss of that day — and rebuilt under Howard Lutnick's leadership into a diversified financial services group.

## Synopsis

Cantor's current investment relevance stems from the Lutnick political connection. Two Commerce-linked transactions in 2025–2026 have driven the firm's most visible deal flow and anchored the [[Lutnick family conflicts of interest|family conflict-of-interest]] story. The firm entered the [[Tariff refund claims market]] in mid-2025, buying rights to IEEPA tariff refunds at cents on the dollar — trading ~$10M+ with capacity for "several hundred million" — while Howard Lutnick, as Commerce Secretary, was architecting the tariff policy those refund rights were betting against. Then in January 2026, Cantor was named lead placement agent (with [[Moelis]] as co-agent) on the $1.5B [[PIPE financing]] that funded [[USA Rare Earth]] alongside the Commerce Department's $1.6B letter of intent. The [[CHIPS Program Office]] required USAR to raise ≥$500M of private capital as a precondition for federal funding, effectively mandating the placement-agent mandate Cantor won. Senators [[Elizabeth Warren]], [[Chris Van Hollen]], and [[Ron Wyden]] sent a letter on February 26, 2026, alleging the Commerce Department "created demand" for Cantor's services; Rep. [[Zoe Lofgren]] of the House Science Committee sent a follow-up on March 19 focused on the 10% federal equity stake. Brandon Lutnick has also been active in crypto/SPAC deals, including involvement with [[Tether]] and [[21 Capital]], a Bitcoin acquisition vehicle.

The firm's core business remains institutional fixed income brokerage (US Treasuries, agencies, corporates) where it is one of the 24 primary dealers authorized to trade directly with the [[Federal Reserve]]. Real estate operations (through Cantor Commercial Real Estate and affiliate [[Newmark Group]]) and investment banking round out the revenue base. The [[USA Rare Earth]] PIPE was one of the largest single banking mandates for the firm in recent years; PIPE placement fees of 3–5% on $1.5B imply ~$45–75M gross to the co-agent pool.
