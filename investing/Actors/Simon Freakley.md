**Simon Freakley** is Executive Chairman of [[AlixPartners]] (January 2025-present), having served as CEO from 2016 to 2025. A career restructuring professional, he built AlixPartners into the dominant name in crisis management and turnaround consulting, positioning the firm as the first call when major corporations face existential threats.

## Synopsis

Freakley's career arc traces the evolution of the restructuring advisory industry from a niche discipline into a premium consulting segment. Before AlixPartners, he served as CEO of [[Kroll]] Inc. (the investigations and risk consultancy) and CEO of Zolfo Cooper Europe (a restructuring boutique). He took the AlixPartners CEO role in 2016 and oversaw a period of significant growth, expanding the firm beyond its core restructuring practice into broader operational consulting, digital transformation, and financial advisory. Under his leadership, AlixPartners became the go-to adviser for some of the highest-profile corporate crises of the era — pandemic supply chain disruptions, crypto collapses, and distressed retail and real estate situations.

The transition to Executive Chairman in January 2025 is the standard succession play for founder/builder-type CEOs at professional services firms: maintain client relationships and firm culture while handing operational management to a successor. For the restructuring market, AlixPartners' positioning matters because distressed activity is cyclical — the firm thrives when default rates rise and corporate balance sheets crack. With interest rates elevated and pandemic-era forbearance fully unwound, the 2025-2026 cycle should be favorable for AlixPartners' core business. Freakley's role as chairman keeps the firm's most connected relationship manager active while the new CEO handles internal scaling.
