**Khaled Mohamed Balama** — Governor of the Central Bank of the UAE since 2021. In April 2026 he emerged as a key wartime financial-plumbing actor after raising with [[Scott Bessent]] and US officials the possibility of a dollar [[Currency swap lines|swap line]] or other backstop for the [[UAE]] if the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] deepened.

## April 2026 wartime liquidity request

Balama met [[Scott Bessent]] plus Treasury and [[Federal Reserve]] officials in Washington and floated a precautionary dollar backstop while the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] was choking Emirati oil revenues and pressuring confidence in Dubai and Abu Dhabi as financial hubs. The [[UAE]] had not made a formal request, but the discussion itself mattered because the dirham's dollar peg and the country's financial-hub model made access to official dollar liquidity the real margin of safety.

Secondary reproductions of the WSJ report added that the Emiratis were also pointing to more than 2,800 Iranian drones and missiles fired at the country, about $270 billion of foreign-exchange reserves backing the peg, and the possibility of shifting some oil sales into [[Chinese yuan]] if dollar liquidity tightened. That makes Balama important less as a domestic technocrat than as the official carrying Gulf war damage directly into the architecture of dollar diplomacy.
