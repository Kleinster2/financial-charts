**Haun Ventures** is a crypto and web3 venture firm founded by Katie Haun, a former federal prosecutor and [[a16z]] general partner who raised $1.5B for her debut fund — one of the largest first-time fund raises in VC history.

## Synopsis

Katie Haun's path to crypto VC was unusual: as an Assistant US Attorney in San Francisco, she led the prosecution of corrupt Secret Service agents who stole Bitcoin during the Silk Road investigation, making her one of the first federal prosecutors to understand crypto's mechanics from the inside. She joined [[a16z]] in 2018 as the firm's first female general partner and co-led its crypto funds before departing in 2022 to launch Haun Ventures with a $1.5B Fund I (split between a $500M early-stage vehicle and a $1B acceleration fund). The fund size was extraordinary for a solo GP with no prior fund track record, reflecting both the 2021-2022 crypto euphoria and Haun's unique positioning as someone who understood both the regulatory landscape (from prosecution) and the VC playbook (from a16z).

The firm invests across crypto infrastructure, DeFi, and web3 applications, with portfolio companies including [[Erebor Bank]] and stakes in several Layer 1 and Layer 2 protocols. The challenge since launch has been timing: Fund I deployed into a crypto winter that saw [[Bitcoin]] fall from $69K to $16K, [[FTX]] collapse, and regulatory hostility peak under the SEC's Gary Gensler. The recovery in 2024-2025 (Bitcoin above $100K, regulatory thaw under the Trump administration) has likely improved marks, but the ultimate returns depend on whether the funded protocols achieve product-market fit beyond speculation. Haun's regulatory expertise — she sits on the boards of multiple crypto companies and advises on compliance — is a genuine competitive advantage as the industry moves from gray-area to regulated financial infrastructure.
