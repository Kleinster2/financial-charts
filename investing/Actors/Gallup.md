**Gallup** is the world's most recognized polling and analytics organization, a private company whose brand equity in public opinion measurement has been parlayed into a larger management consulting and workforce analytics business.

## Synopsis

Founded by George Gallup in 1935, Gallup built its reputation on election polling and public opinion surveys — the "Gallup Poll" became synonymous with American polling itself. But the company's revenue model shifted decades ago from media-facing polling (which generates brand recognition but thin margins) toward corporate consulting: employee engagement measurement (the Q12 survey is used by Fortune 500 companies worldwide), organizational culture diagnostics, and strengths-based leadership development (CliftonStrengths, formerly StrengthsFinder, has been taken by 30M+ people). The Gallup World Poll, surveying 160+ countries annually on wellbeing and economic confidence, provides macro sentiment data that is genuinely useful for understanding EM consumer trends before they appear in hard economic data.

Gallup's partnership with [[Simile]] to build nationally representative AI polling panels (announced 2025-2026) signals the company's response to the existential challenge facing traditional polling: response rates have fallen from ~35% in the 1990s to under 5%, making phone-based surveys increasingly expensive and potentially unreliable. AI-generated synthetic panels — trained on real demographic and attitudinal data to simulate representative populations — could dramatically reduce polling costs while maintaining accuracy. If it works, it would be the most significant methodological shift in polling since random sampling. For investors, Gallup itself is not investable (private, employee-owned), but the AI polling technology has implications for media companies, political consultancies, and market research firms like [[NIQ]] and [[Ipsos]] that depend on traditional survey methods.
