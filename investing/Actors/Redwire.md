**Redwire** — integrated [[Space]] and [[Defense]] technology company that sells spacecraft components, autonomous space platforms, microgravity payload infrastructure, optical and RF systems, and, after the [[Edge Autonomy]] acquisition, uncrewed airborne systems.

The story is that Redwire is trying to graduate from a small SPAC-era space-infrastructure supplier into a scaled multi-domain defense contractor. The June 2025 Edge Autonomy acquisition added Stalker and Penguin UAS platforms, moved the company closer to fielded defense demand, and made the revenue base less dependent on long-duration space hardware programs.

The tension is execution. FY2025 revenue rose to $335.4M and backlog reached $411.2M, but the company still posted a $226.6M net loss and negative adjusted EBITDA. The Jan. 27, 2026 selection for [[MDA]]'s $151B SHIELD IDIQ tied Redwire to the [[Golden Dome]] narrative, yet IDIQ membership is a vendor-pool right to compete for task orders, not guaranteed revenue.

## Business

Redwire combines legacy space hardware with newer autonomous defense systems. Its Space Tech business includes power generation, avionics, sensors, structures, mechanisms, RF payloads, robotic systems, spacecraft platforms, and in-space manufacturing / microgravity research infrastructure. Customers include civil-space, commercial-space, and national-security programs.

Defense Tech was strengthened by the acquisition of [[Edge Autonomy]], which brought Stalker and Penguin UAS systems, optical sensors, RF payloads, and production capacity across the US and European footprint. Redwire said it delivered more than 100 Stalker/Penguin systems after the acquisition closed in June 2025.

The company reorganized in January 2026 around two segments: Space Tech and Defense Tech. That structure makes the investment question cleaner: can Redwire convert space heritage into production-scale defense systems without recurring program charges overwhelming gross margin?

---

## Defense positioning

Redwire's January 2026 SHIELD selection puts it inside [[MDA]]'s Scalable Homeland Innovative Enterprise Layered Defense IDIQ, a multi-vendor contract vehicle with a $151B ceiling. The relevant read-through is optionality: Redwire can compete for task orders tied to homeland missile defense, space sensors, autonomous platforms, modeling and simulation, and multi-domain operations.

The caveat is just as important. SHIELD does not itself book $151B to Redwire, and being one of thousands of eligible vendors leaves task-order share uncertain. The market treated the announcement as a [[Golden Dome]] call option: RDW closed at $14.20 on Jan. 27, 2026, up 29.6% from $10.96 on Jan. 26, then closed at $13.29 on Jan. 28.
