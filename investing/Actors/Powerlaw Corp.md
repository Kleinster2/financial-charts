#actor #fund #usa

Powerlaw Corp — non-diversified closed-end fund listed on [[Nasdaq]] under PWRL since May 27, 2026, giving retail investors access to top private AI/defense/space companies. Part of the [[Akkadian Ventures]] platform (advised by Powerlaw Fund Adviser, LLC). Listed via direct listing — resale of existing shares, not an IPO. Trades at a premium to NAV: ~$17.50 (Jun 17, 2026) versus $15.31 NAV/share (May 31), down from a ~$28 debut pop.

## Listing structure

Direct listing — listed 43,242,931 shares of common stock for resale by existing stockholders; began trading May 27, 2026 at a $27.68 open. Not a traditional underwritten IPO; no new capital raised. Stifel determined when shares were ready to trade and approved the opening price.

Reverse stock split Dec 23, 2025: every 12 shares consolidated into 1.

Selling stockholders: up to 103.9M shares (20% of outstanding) registered for resale. Largest: PowerLaw10 Cayman, LP with 55.2M shares (10.64%).

### Lock-up schedule (directors, officers, certain stockholders)

| Milestone | Cumulative % released |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Listing date | 25% |
| Day 30 | 45% |
| Day 90 | 70% |
| Day 180 | 100% |

Dynamic adjustment: if average closing price for 10 trading days before a release date exceeds 400% of NAV, tranche increases by 5% of outstanding. If below 200% of NAV, tranche decreases by 5%. Minimum tranche at Day 90/180 is 20%.

## NAV methodology

NAV computed quarterly (not daily like mutual funds).

- Board designated the Adviser ([[Akkadian Ventures|Akkadian CEF Manager]]) as valuation designee under Rule 2a-5
- Private/illiquid securities: fair value determined by the Adviser per Board-approved procedures. Factors: portfolio company earnings, market comparisons, recent secondary transaction pricing, DCFs, comparable public company valuations
- Forward contracts: fair valued based on recently observed transactions, adjusted for credit risk, share ratio, SPV fees
- SPV investments: value equals estimated fair value of underlying securities, discounted for SPV-level fees
- Independent third-party valuation firms may be engaged at Board's direction
- ASC 820 (Fair Value Measurements) framework

Conflict: The Adviser determines valuations and earns a management fee based on those valuations. Interested Directors are associated with the Adviser.

## Tax treatment

Intends to elect RIC status (taxable year ending Jul 31, 2026). Must distribute at least 90% of investment company taxable income annually to maintain status. If RIC qualification fails, subject to corporate income tax on all income.

Distributions of taxable income: ordinary income. Capital gain dividends: long-term capital gains rate (max 20%). Opt-out DRIP: distributions automatically reinvested unless stockholder opts out — still taxable.

No regular distributions anticipated — fund focuses on capital appreciation.

## Risk factors

Structure:
- Newly formed entity, limited operating history
- Non-diversified — can concentrate heavily in single issuers (top 2 positions up to 25% each)
- Direct listing with no underwriter or price stabilization
- Management fee payable even if NAV declines
- Quarterly NAV only — stale pricing between calculations
- Adviser sets valuations and earns fees on those valuations

Investments:
- All investments in private, illiquid securities
- Forward contract risk — counterparties may default; portfolio companies may actively obstruct transactions
- SPV layering adds structural complexity
- Drag-along rights — majority shareholders can force fund to sell at unfavorable prices
- Lock-up provisions (typically 180 days post-IPO of underlying holdings)
- Technology sector concentration — all holdings in tech
- Limited financial information available on private companies
- Portfolio companies may not recognize or approve the fund's investment and may seek to invalidate it

Precedent: [[Linqto]], a similar platform offering pre-IPO equity to retail investors, filed Chapter 11 in Jul 2025. Held >$500M in securities, 11,000+ customers. Found to have structured investments through SPVs owned by the company itself, sold [[Ripple]] shares at markups above SEC-permitted 10% cap.

## Why it matters

Retail investors have been largely shut out of the AI private company boom. [[OpenAI]] discussing fundraising at up to $830B valuation, [[SpaceX]] at $1.25T (with xAI), [[Anthropic]] at $380B — all private. US public companies fell from ~7,000 in 2000 to ~4,000 in 2024. The best-performing companies are staying private longer, locking retail out of the highest-growth period.

PWRL would be among the first pure-play listed vehicles for this exposure, but faces the highest fee load (3.63% total) and the valuation conflict inherent in adviser-set NAV.

---

## Investment case

PWRL offers rare retail access to a concentrated book of the highest-profile private AI/defense/space names ([[OpenAI]], [[SpaceX]], [[Anthropic]], [[Anduril]]), run by an [[Akkadian Ventures|Akkadian]] platform with a 15-year secondary-market track record (750+ transactions, zero counterparty defaults); if any holding IPOs, the fund marks up toward its public valuation. Against that, the structure carries the heaviest cost and conflict load among peers — 3.63% total expenses (vs 1.85% for [[Fundrise]]), an adviser that both sets the quarterly NAV and earns fees on it, illiquid holdings priced only monthly, no board seats or distributions, and no underwriter price stabilization on the direct listing. The closed-end wrapper usually trades at a discount to NAV, but PWRL — like [[Destiny Tech100|DXYZ]] — has so far traded at a premium, one that has compressed from ~90% at the debut pop toward ~14% by mid-June; the [[Linqto]] bankruptcy remains the cautionary precedent for retail private-market vehicles.
