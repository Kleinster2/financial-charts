**Ineffable Intelligence** — London-based [[AI]] research lab founded by [[David Silver]] (former Google [[DeepMind]] VP of Reinforcement Learning, creator of [[AlphaGo]] / [[AlphaZero]] / [[AlphaStar]]). Emerged from stealth on Apr 27, 2026 with a $1.1B seed round at $5.1B post-money valuation — a record-breaking European seed and one of the largest AI seed rounds anywhere. Co-led by [[Sequoia Capital]] and [[Lightspeed Venture Partners]]; [[NVIDIA]] contributed at least $250M; other backers include [[Google]], [[DST Global]], [[Index Ventures]], [[EQT]], [[BOND Capital]], the [[UK Sovereign AI Fund]], and the [[British Business Bank]]. Silver committed 100% of his personal equity proceeds to charity via [[Founders Pledge]]. The founding thesis is structural: today's foundation models trained on human-generated internet text have a fundamental ceiling, and the path past that ceiling is reinforcement learning with no pre-training and no human data — a "superlearner" that discovers all knowledge through its own experience.

## Why this matters

The thesis is the bet — and it is the most direct institutional challenge to the [[OpenAI]] / [[Anthropic]] / [[Google]] [[Gemini]] frontier-model paradigm to date. The dominant approach since GPT-3 has been pre-training on internet text (next-token prediction) followed by post-training (RLHF, instruction tuning) to align the model. Silver's argument is that this paradigm asymptotes against the volume and quality of human-generated text — and that the next regime is one where models discover knowledge from their own experience in interactive environments, the way [[AlphaZero]] discovered superhuman Go without human games.

The institutional weight behind the bet is structural. [[NVIDIA]]'s $250M+ contribution to a seed round is unusual — typically reserved for compute-anchored relationships. [[Google]] funding a former DeepMind VP's competing lab signals that [[Google]] sees the RL-first thesis as worth hedging into rather than competing against directly. The [[UK Sovereign AI Fund]] and [[British Business Bank]] backing positions [[Ineffable Intelligence]] as the [[UK]]'s flagship sovereign-AI bet — comparable in symbolism to [[France]]'s [[Mistral]] or the [[UAE]]'s [[G42]] / [[Falcon]].

The $5.1B post-money seed valuation is the price the market is putting on a bet with no product, no model, no revenue. By comparison, [[Mistral]] at the same revenue stage was valued ~$2B in early-2024; [[xAI]]'s Series A at $24B was a [[Elon Musk]]-specific premium. [[Ineffable]] sits closer to [[Thinking Machines Lab]] ($12B at $2B raised) — a founder-credibility valuation.

## Strategic positioning

| vs | [[Ineffable Intelligence]] differentiation |
|---|---|
| [[OpenAI]] | RL-first, no-human-data thesis vs OpenAI's scale-pretraining + post-training paradigm |
| [[Anthropic]] | No safety-first framing; the bet is on capability frontier through experience-based learning |
| [[Google DeepMind]] | Direct lineage (Silver was VP of RL) but scope narrower — pure RL focus, no consumer product surface |
| [[Thinking Machines Lab]] ([[Mira Murati]]) | Both are ex-frontier-lab founder bets; Ineffable's thesis is more methodologically specific |
| [[SSI]] ([[Ilya Sutskever]]) | Both reject the current paradigm; SSI's "safe superintelligence" framing is alignment-led, Ineffable's is method-led |

The competitive question is whether the RL-from-experience thesis requires environments rich enough to produce general intelligence — or whether (as [[Yann LeCun]] has argued for years) world-model and embodied learning are the actual next regime, and pure RL is too narrow a paradigm.

## Open questions

- What environments will [[Ineffable Intelligence]] train in? RL-from-experience requires a substrate. [[AlphaZero]] had Go and Chess; the analog for general intelligence is the binding question.
- Does the no-pre-training claim hold operationally, or does some bootstrap from existing models become necessary at scale?
- What is the timeline to first model release? Pure-RL training from scratch is compute-intensive and typically slower-to-product than fine-tuned pre-trained baselines.
- Does [[NVIDIA]]'s strategic position translate to a compute commitment beyond the equity? Compute access is the binding constraint for any RL-first lab.
