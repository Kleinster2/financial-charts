Jaeger-LeCoultre is a Swiss luxury watchmaker founded in 1833 in Le Sentier, Vallée de Joux. Owned by [[Richemont]], it is known as the "watchmaker's watchmaker" for its history of supplying movements to other houses, including [[Patek Philippe]] and [[Audemars Piguet]].

## Synopsis

With over 1,400 calibres developed across nearly two centuries, JLC holds one of the deepest movement libraries in haute horlogerie. The Reverso, created in 1931 for polo players who needed a watch that could withstand mallet strikes, remains one of the most iconic and commercially important designs in luxury watchmaking — its Art Deco flip-case is instantly recognizable and supports a robust secondary market. The Master and Polaris collections round out the portfolio, spanning dress watches to sports-luxury. JLC's positioning within [[Richemont]]'s Specialist Watchmakers division places it alongside [[Vacheron Constantin]] and [[A. Lange & Söhne]], though JLC operates at a slightly more accessible price point (entry ~$6K vs. $20K+ for the peers).

The investment relevance sits at the [[Richemont]] portfolio level: JLC is a margin contributor in a conglomerate where [[Cartier]] and [[Van Cleef & Arpels]] dominate revenue. JLC's value to Richemont is partly strategic — its manufacture capabilities (in-house movement production from raw materials) provide supply chain depth that few luxury groups can match. The brand's challenge is differentiation in a market where [[Rolex]], [[Patek Philippe]], and [[Audemars Piguet]] command disproportionate secondary-market premiums. JLC trades at retail, not above it — a sign of strong production discipline but limited speculative demand.
