# Brad Setser

Brad Setser is a senior fellow at the [[Council on Foreign Relations]] and the most-cited specialist on global trade and capital-flow data. His running blog work on Chinese trade mismeasurement, US deficits, and balance-of-payments arithmetic has made him the reference point for [[Adam Tooze]], [[Michael Pettis]], and most serious writers on [[Global imbalances]]. Prior US Treasury official (2009-2011, under [[Tim Geithner]]); then [[Office of the US Trade Representative|USTR]] senior adviser (2021-2022) in the Biden administration.

## Why he matters to the vault

Setser's core contribution is the insistence that trade and balance-of-payments data as published by national statistical agencies and the [[IMF]] systematically misstates China's surplus — because of transfer pricing in Hong Kong / Singapore / tax-haven routing, and because of state-firm financing flows that disappear into "errors and omissions." His reconstructed numbers are typically 1-3 percentage points of GDP higher than the published Chinese surplus. This matters because the published number is the reference that informs most academic and policy analysis; Setser's number is the one [[Pettis]], Tooze, and the [[Second China shock]] framing implicitly use.

Setser also tracks:
- Central-bank reserve composition and its shifts (dollar share, gold, euro)
- Cross-border bond flows, especially Japanese and Chinese purchases of Treasuries
- Sovereign debt distress cases ([[Argentina]], [[Sri Lanka]], [[Zambia]])
- Taiwan's life insurers as a quasi-central-bank holder of US fixed income
