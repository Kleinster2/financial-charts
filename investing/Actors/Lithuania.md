# Lithuania

Baltic state and [[EU]] member since 2004, [[NATO]] member since 2004, eurozone since 2015, and [[IEA]] member since 2022. Population ~2.8M with a GDP of ~$75B. Lithuania has emerged as one of Europe's most hawkish voices on [[Russia]] and [[China]] — it was the first EU country to allow [[Taiwan]] to open a representative office under its own name (2021), triggering Chinese trade retaliation and effectively making Vilnius a test case for EU solidarity against [[China]]'s economic coercion. Defense spending has risen sharply post-2022, reaching ~2.75% of GDP, well above the NATO 2% target. The economy is driven by a growing fintech sector (Vilnius hosts 100+ licensed fintech firms, including [[Revolut]]'s EU banking license), logistics, and laser manufacturing (Lithuanian companies supply ~50% of the global scientific laser market).
