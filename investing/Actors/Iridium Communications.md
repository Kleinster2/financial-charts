**Iridium Communications** — Operator of a global low-earth-orbit satellite-communications network: 66 cross-linked operational satellites providing truly global (pole-to-pole) voice and data coverage over licensed L-band spectrum, serving ~2.55M subscribers across government, defense, maritime, aviation, and IoT. NASDAQ: IRDM. In June 2026 it agreed to be acquired by [[Rocket Lab]] for ~$8.0 billion (see [[Rocket Lab-Iridium acquisition 2026]]).

---

## Synopsis

Iridium is the rare listed pure-play that owns an operating global constellation and the licensed spectrum under it. Its second-generation Iridium NEXT fleet (deployed 2017-2019 aboard [[SpaceX]] [[Falcon 9]]) delivers coverage no terrestrial network or regional satellite operator can match, anchored by a long-standing US Department of Defense gateway relationship and a growing IoT / short-burst-data franchise. The recurring, sticky, government-weighted subscriber base and the scarce global L-band licenses are exactly what make it a strategic target — and in June 2026 [[Rocket Lab]] agreed to buy it to add an operating network and spectrum to its launch-and-systems stack.

## Pending acquisition by Rocket Lab (Jun 29 2026)

On June 29 2026, [[Rocket Lab]] agreed to acquire Iridium for $54.00 per share — $27.00 cash plus Rocket Lab stock (exchange ratio with a $67.50-$112.50 collar) — an enterprise value of about $8.0 billion, unanimously approved by both boards and expected to close around mid-2027 subject to an Iridium shareholder vote and regulatory approvals.

IRDM re-rated to the offer immediately, closing $54.92 on June 29 (+26.2% from the June 26 close), slightly through the headline $54 on the stock-component upside. The full deal economics, strategic rationale, and cohort event-tape are in [[Rocket Lab-Iridium acquisition 2026]]. For Iridium holders the open questions through close are the regulatory path (L-band transfer + national-security customer continuity) and Rocket Lab share-price risk outside the collar band.

## Business

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Voice & data | Satellite phones and handheld terminals; the only truly global mobile-satellite voice service |
| IoT / short-burst data | Low-power device connectivity (asset tracking, telemetry) — the fastest-growing line |
| Broadband (Certus) | Mid-band L-band broadband for maritime, aviation, and land-mobile |
| Government | Dedicated US DoD gateway (EMSS); resilient comms for defense and federal users |

The cross-linked architecture (satellites relay to each other, not just to ground stations) is the technical differentiator: it gives genuine global coverage including the poles and oceans without dependence on local ground infrastructure, which is why defense, maritime, and aviation customers anchor the base.
