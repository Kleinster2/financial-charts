# Peng Shaozong

Former official with [[China]]'s [[NDRC]] (National Development and Reform Commission). Currently vice-president of the [[China Society for Economic Reform]]. Author of an unusually candid late-March 2026 policy paper warning China "must abandon wishful thinking" and begin planning for worst-case scenarios stemming from the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]].

## Why this matters

Peng's critique is notable because it lands from inside the Chinese policy establishment during the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]], not from a Western commentator. The "abandon wishful thinking" line signals internal recognition that the [[Fortress China]] framework has gaps — a rare public admission from someone with NDRC credibility. Policy prescription goes further: pre-emptive SPR release (not just during shortages) and acceleration of supply diversification beyond the Middle East. The paper also called out [[Helium]] and other industrial gases as high-end manufacturing chokepoints.

See [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade#Peng Shaozong — "abandon wishful thinking" (late March)|supply chain cascade note]] for the FT data on cost passthrough.
