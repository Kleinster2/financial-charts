## Technology — degrader-antibody conjugates

Firefly's platform fuses two modalities: the tumor-selective delivery of an [[Antibody-drug conjugate]] (ADC) with a targeted [[Protein degrader|protein degrader]] payload instead of a classic cytotoxin. The antibody homes to tumor cells; the degrader payload then catalytically destroys a disease-driving intracellular protein.

The pitch is a proprietary linker engineered for payload stability — addressing what Firefly board member Jerel Davis (Versant Ventures) called the "Achilles heel" of traditional ADCs: imperfect, premature payload release in circulation. Lead preclinical candidates target tumors driven by the [[KRAS]] oncoprotein across multiple solid-tumor types.

John Reed ([[Johnson & Johnson]] head of R&D) framed the rationale: the technology could "overcome the limitations of current treatments and diversify our pipeline with preclinical candidates for treating multiple types of solid tumors."

---

## Deal context

The acquisition fits [[Johnson & Johnson]]'s oncology-pipeline rebuild against the STELARA biosimilar [[Patent cliffs|patent cliff]], following its $3.05B purchase of [[Halda Therapeutics]] (closed December 2025). At $1B for a preclinical, founded-2022 platform, it is a bet on the DAC modality rather than near-term revenue — years of trials separate the deal from any approval.
