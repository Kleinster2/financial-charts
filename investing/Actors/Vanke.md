# Vanke

Chinese property developer, second-largest by revenue (after [[Country Garden]] in peak years), founded 1984 in Shenzhen by [[Wang Shi]]. Listed on Shenzhen (000002.SZ) since 1991 and Hong Kong (2202.HK) since 2014. Vanke is the "policy-protected" survivor of the post-[[Three Red Lines]] property correction — included in the central-government "whitelist" of systemic developers eligible for continued financing, while peers ([[Evergrande]], [[Country Garden]], [[Sunac]], [[Kaisa]], [[Shimao]]) defaulted or restructured.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. [[Three Red Lines]] survivor — the canonical case of how the 2020 deleveraging policy played out for a developer with state-aligned ownership ([[Shenzhen Metro]]) and conservative balance sheet posture. Vanke's survival vs Country Garden / Evergrande default is the clearest data point on which Chinese developers can clear the policy filter
2. Property-correction reference point — Vanke's revenue and unit-volume trajectory is the closest available proxy for the broader Chinese property cycle, given its scale and survival
3. State-private hybrid governance — Shenzhen Metro's 27% stake makes Vanke effectively a state-private hybrid, which is the governance archetype the Chinese property sector is consolidating toward (inference)
