# Longi Green Energy

Longi Green Energy Technology is a [[China]]-based solar-and-hydrogen manufacturer, listed in Shanghai (601012.SS). The world's largest manufacturer of monocrystalline silicon solar cells and modules by volume since roughly 2020, and since 2021 the parent of Longi Hydrogen — the company's electrolyzer subsidiary that is now the single largest alkaline [[Electrolyzers|electrolyzer]] producer globally. Longi is therefore the cleanest case study of Chinese clean-tech verticalization: the same corporate group that broke Western solar manufacturing is explicitly replicating the playbook in hydrogen hardware.

## Why Longi matters

Longi is the reference example of the [[Second China shock]] pattern operating across two clean-tech sectors simultaneously:

1. **Solar** — Longi is the single largest monocrystalline producer globally. The 2011-2020 collapse of Western solar-module manufacturing is substantially attributable to Longi and peer Chinese firms (Trina, JA Solar, Jinko) reaching cost points Western competitors could not match.

2. Hydrogen — Longi Hydrogen, launched in 2021, reached multi-GW annual manufacturing capacity faster than any Western competitor has managed in over a decade of trying. The company explicitly credits shared infrastructure with the solar parent: stamped-metal supply chains, process engineering, state subsidy access, and balance-sheet support.

The pattern matters because it directly rebuts the "solar was a one-time adjustment, Western clean-tech manufacturing will hold in other sectors" argument. Longi has now demonstrated the playbook is replicable.

---

## The solar-to-hydrogen logic

Longi's decision to enter electrolyzers was strategic, not opportunistic:

- Both alkaline electrolyzers and solar modules use similar high-throughput stamped-metal assembly
- Both benefit from the same Chinese industrial-policy subsidy architecture
- Solar cost reductions had flattened by 2019-2020; hydrogen offered a new growth vector at still-high starting prices
- Longi's solar customers (large utility-scale project developers globally) are the same customers who need electrolyzers for hydrogen-adjacent projects — shared customer relationships lowered go-to-market costs
- Chinese state "new productive forces" and dual-circulation policy explicitly target clean-tech verticalization

Longi is not alone in this pivot — [[Sungrow]] (solar inverters → hydrogen) and JinkoSolar have made parallel moves. But Longi's scale makes it the reference example.

## The current competitive position

As of 2026, Longi's position is:

| Sector | Global position |
|---|---|
| Monocrystalline silicon wafers | #1 globally by volume |
| Solar modules | #1-3 globally by volume (competes with JinkoSolar, Trina) |
| Alkaline electrolyzers | #1 globally by manufacturing capacity |
| PEM electrolyzers | Developing, not yet at scale |
| SOEC | Not present |

The financial reality is less triumphant: Chinese solar has been in brutal price deflation through 2023-2025, which has compressed Longi's margins in its primary solar business. The hydrogen subsidiary is not yet a material revenue contributor. The company is one of the most visible casualties of the Chinese overcapacity problem that [[Michael Pettis]] and the [[Second China shock]] framework identify — a problem for the *profitability* of Chinese firms, even while being a severe problem for their Western competitors.
