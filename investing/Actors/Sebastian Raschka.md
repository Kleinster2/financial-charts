**Sebastian Raschka** — Machine learning researcher and educator, best known for his from-scratch AI implementation books that have become standard references for practitioners who want to understand LLM architecture at the code level rather than the API level.

---

## Synopsis

Raschka occupies a unique niche in the AI ecosystem: the explainer-practitioner who builds working implementations of frontier architectures from the ground up. His books — notably "Build a Large Language Model from Scratch" (GPT-2 architecture) and "Build a Reasoning Model from Scratch" (RL-based reasoning) — strip away the abstraction layers and show exactly how these systems work. His philosophy is precise: "If there is code, and the code works, you know it's correct. There's no misunderstanding." This approach has made him influential among the engineering cohort that actually builds and fine-tunes models, as opposed to the API-consumer layer.

His views on the AI landscape carry signal for investors. On architecture: the fundamental transformer architecture has not changed meaningfully since GPT-2 — gains come from post-training techniques (RLVR, RLHF), data quality, and inference-time compute scaling, not architectural breakthroughs. On open models: Chinese open-weight models (like [[DeepSeek]]) are popular partly because their licenses are truly unrestricted, unlike [[Llama]] or [[Gemma]] which impose user limits and reporting requirements. This is a structural insight — license friction shapes adoption curves, which shapes which models become ecosystem standards. Raschka's background includes academic research in computational biology and maintaining mlxtend, a popular ML library. He is active on YouTube with technical courses.
