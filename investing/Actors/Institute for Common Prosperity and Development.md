# Institute for Common Prosperity and Development

Academic body at [[Zhejiang University]] specifically chartered to develop the intellectual scaffolding for China's [[Common Prosperity]] policy framework. [[Li Shi]] is Senior Professor and Dean. The Institute represents the academic-mainstream wing of the Common Prosperity discourse — distinct from the policy-implementation cluster at the [[Central Financial and Economic Affairs Commission]] and from the regulatory-enforcement bodies that operationalised the 2021-2023 enforcement phase.

## Why it matters

The Institute is positioned at the intersection of academic economics and CCP policy doctrine: it has formal institutional standing to articulate what [[Common Prosperity]] means at the academic level, which gives its output more policy-signalling weight than that of a typical university research group. The 2026 reframing of Common Prosperity as "investing in people" via endogenous-growth theory is the kind of substantive rearticulation that requires this institutional position to gain traction (inference — the framework's reception depends on Li Shi's standing at the Institute, not just on the argument's analytical merits).

Zhejiang province itself is the designated [[Common Prosperity]] pilot zone (a status conferred by Xi in 2021), which gives the Institute additional weight as the academic home of the pilot.
