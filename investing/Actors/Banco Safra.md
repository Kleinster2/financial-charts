**Banco Safra** — Brazilian financial services company headquartered in São Paulo, ranked 6th-7th among private banks in [[Brazil]]. Founded in 1955 by the [[Safra family]] after their migration from [[Lebanon]], the bank operates across investment banking, private banking, asset management, and retail banking (neobank AgZero). Part of the broader [[J. Safra Group]] alongside [[J. Safra Sarasin]] (Switzerland) and [[Safra National Bank of New York]] (US), collectively one of the largest private banking dynasties in the world.

## Synopsis

Banco Safra's vault relevance stems from its position as a major private-market player in Brazilian finance and the [[Safra family]]'s broader global banking network. The family's combined wealth (~$20B+) makes them one of the richest banking dynasties globally. In Brazil, Banco Safra competes against the "big five" ([[Itaú Unibanco]], [[Banco do Brasil]], [[Bradesco]], [[Santander Brasil]], [[BTG Pactual]]) from a niche of high-net-worth private banking and corporate lending. The bank's conservative risk management — a Safra family hallmark across all entities — has historically produced lower returns but also fewer blowups than peers.

[[David Safra]], the current head of Brazilian operations, has pushed into digital banking through AgZero and expanded the asset management franchise. The bank remains entirely family-owned with no IPO plans, limiting direct investment access but making it relevant as a counterparty, co-investor, and private banking competitor in the Brazilian market.
