# China Guardian

**The only publicly listed major auction house globally** (HKEX: 1672.HK). Largest auction house in Mainland China by sales ($587M in 2025, -2%). Founded 1993 in Beijing. Focused on Chinese paintings, calligraphy, ceramics, and traditional art — almost entirely domestic market. Buyer numbers quadrupled since 2015; 36% of buyers under 40. Revenue has contracted sharply from peak ($952M in 2019) alongside China's broader luxury/discretionary spending slowdown and property crisis.

## Market context

China = 14% of global art market ($8.5B in 2025, +1%). Within China:
- Mainland auction sales grew ~6% in USD terms
- Hong Kong contracted (-5%)
- **Settlement risk**: Only 42% of lots over RMB 10M ($1.5M) fully settled in Mainland China (down 8pp YoY, from 59% in 2021-22). This means ~58% of high-value transactions have delayed or partial payment — a material credit risk unique to the Chinese auction market

The Chinese art market is almost entirely domestic: buyers are overwhelmingly Chinese; the art is Chinese classical/traditional; and cross-border flow is minimal compared to the US/UK hubs.

## Investing relevance

- **Only publicly traded pure-play auction house** (HKEX: 1672.HK) — unique exposure to global art market
- **China discretionary proxy**: Revenue tracks Chinese consumer confidence, property market, and luxury spending — same forces driving weakness in LVMH China, Kering, Burberry
- **Settlement risk**: 42% settlement rate on high-value lots is a significant receivables/credit quality concern not present at Western houses
- **Demographic tailwind**: 36% of buyers under 40, buyer base quadrupled since 2015 — suggests long-term base-building even as cyclical revenues decline
- **Currency**: Sales in RMB; USD reporting affected by CNY/USD fluctuations
