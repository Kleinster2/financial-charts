Reese Jones — biophysicist turned tech entrepreneur and venture capitalist, best known for founding [[Farallon Computing]] (1986) and [[Netopia]], co-founding [[Singularity University]], and inventing core networking technology including the CAT5 ethernet cable. Infrastructure-first investor focused on health, biology, mobile, and internet.

## Career evolution

- 1984 — Founded BMUG while a grad student at [[UC Berkeley]]; grew it into the largest nonprofit computer user group of its kind.
- 1986 — Founded [[Farallon Computing]] in Alameda, CA. Invented PhoneNET, enabling AppleTalk networks over ordinary telephone cables (RJ-11 jacks). Farallon became one of the most influential early Macintosh networking companies.
- ~1986-1996 — CEO of Farallon for ~10 years, overseeing PhoneNET and Media divisions.
- Farallon rebranded to [[Netopia]], shifting focus to broadband equipment and internet infrastructure.
- Feb 2007 — [[Motorola]] acquired [[Netopia]] for $208M.
- Post-exit — Transitioned to venture strategy, engaging with ~12 companies across innovation, startup, financing, development, IPO, and acquisition cycles.
- Co-founded [[Singularity University]] (Associate Founder); currently serves as Trustee.

## OneTaste involvement

Jones was the primary financial backer of [[OneTaste]], a San Francisco "orgasmic meditation" company founded by [[Nicole Daedone]] in 2004.

### Relationship and financial role

- Jones and Daedone were in a relationship from 2005 to 2011 and lived together.
- Prior to 2009, Jones made working capital loans to Caravan Retreats (OneTaste's predecessor) for media equipment, premises improvements, and a new San Francisco center.
- Government filings state Jones invested at least $800,000 from 2007 through 2011.
- Jones was never a shareholder or executive in OneTaste Inc. or Caravan Retreats.
- In 2009, during the financial crisis, Jones requested loan repayment. OneTaste began repaying in 2009; the loan was fully repaid by October 2012.
- Described in trial testimony as the person who "kept the company afloat" — an "open secret" within OneTaste.

### Trial testimony (US v. Daedone, EDNY, 2025)

Three witnesses testified they were coerced into becoming "handlers" for Jones:

- Victims stated they were required to live with Jones, cook for him, perform daily sexual acts at his direction, and provide domestic labor.
- Former CTO Christopher Hubbard testified he "worked with Daedone to attract tech executive Reese Jones and get him to invest" and that OneTaste organized elaborate sexual experiences for Jones to secure his funding — including a "Wizard of Oz" themed event where "Jones followed a yellow brick road to a bed, with sound and visual effects reminiscent of the tornado from the film."
- Former member Dana Gill testified she "was instructed to have sex with Jones and engage in activities like walking him around on a leash or whipping him."
- Prosecutors stated: *"These women lived with him, they cooked for him and they gave him daily hand jobs... They did this so that Reese Jones would continue to fund the company."*

Jones was not charged. The prosecution's theory framed Jones as a beneficiary of Daedone's forced labor scheme, not a co-conspirator. OneTaste's defense and Jones's counter-narrative dispute the coercion characterization.

### OneTaste case outcome

- May 5, 2025: Trial began, EDNY.
- Jun 9, 2025: Jury convicted [[Nicole Daedone]] and Rachel Cherwitz of forced labor conspiracy. Deliberation lasted less than two days.
- Mar 18, 2026: Court seized $12M from Daedone's 2017 sale of the company.
- Mar 30, 2026: Daedone sentenced to 9 years. Cherwitz received 6.5 years.
- Defense counsel (Jennifer Bonjean) indicated intent to appeal. Attorney Alan Dershowitz announced lobbying the Trump administration for pardons.
