**Lithium Americas Corp** (NYSE / TSX: LAC) — Canadian-headquartered developer of the **Thacker Pass** lithium mine in Humboldt County, Nevada — the largest known measured lithium resource in the United States. The project is a joint venture with [[General Motors|GM]] (62% LAC / 38% GM) and is being financed through the largest US Department of Energy battery-materials loan on record. Thacker Pass is the flagship of US-sourced lithium: the only development-stage project that could plausibly reduce American dependence on Chinese and Latin American supply at scale.

Thacker Pass is significant for two structural reasons:

1. **US-domestic supply.** Current US lithium production runs through [[Albemarle|ALB]]'s Silver Peak operation in Nevada (~5,000 tpa) and emerging DLE projects in the Smackover Formation (see [[Direct lithium extraction]]). Thacker Pass at 40,000 tpa Phase 1 would nearly double US domestic production in a single project.
2. **IRA / CHIPS-adjacent policy capture.** Thacker Pass was named a "critical project" by the Trump administration and secured a $2.23B loan from the DOE's Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing program — the single largest battery-materials federal loan to date.

## Project status — Thacker Pass

**Stage 1 construction progress (end-2025):**

| Dimension | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Detailed engineering design | 93% complete |
| Procurement | 60% complete |
| Current construction workforce | ~900 (Mar 2026) |
| Peak workforce target | ~1,800 by year-end 2026 |
| Mechanical completion | Late 2027 target |
| Full production ramp | 2028 |

The project uses **clay-based extraction** — a lithium-from-claystone process that is technologically less mature than hard-rock spodumene processing or brine evaporation. This is the primary execution risk.

## Financing structure

Thacker Pass financing is the most politically-coordinated lithium project build in history:

| Source | Amount | Type |
|--------|--------|------|
| **[[US Department of Energy]] ATVM loan** | **$2.23B** | Debt, lowest-cost federal source |
| **[[General Motors]]** | ~$650M + ~$330M | 38% JV equity + LAC share investment |
| **Orion Resource Partners** | $250M | Private-equity investment in LAC |

Total Phase 1 project budget: approximately $3.5B including contingency. The 2026 construction capex guide of $1.3-1.6B is the binding near-term number.

**GM off-take:** [[General Motors|GM]] has a binding off-take agreement for the initial output of Thacker Pass Phase 1 — targeting 1 million GM EVs per year worth of battery-grade lithium carbonate.

## Political and permitting context

Thacker Pass secured final federal permits in 2023 after multiple legal challenges from environmental groups and local Paiute tribes (Fort McDermitt and Summit Lake). The Ninth Circuit ruled in favor of the project in July 2023; subsequent challenges have been rejected or settled.

Under the Trump administration, the project benefits from:

- Critical-minerals fast-track designation
- Tariff architecture favoring US-sourced battery materials
- Preserved DOE ATVM loan (despite broader DOE loan-program scrutiny)
- Energy-dominance framing aligned with Nevada congressional delegation

The political-capture angle makes Thacker Pass relatively insulated from the kind of regime volatility that affects [[Chile]] ([[José Antonio Kast]] transition) and [[Bolivia]] (stalled China/Russia deals) — but it remains exposed to federal priorities more broadly.

## Corporate separation (2023)

Lithium Americas Corp was created through a 2023 corporate separation that split the legacy LAC entity into two publicly traded companies:

- **Lithium Americas Corp (LAC)** — North American operations, primarily Thacker Pass. This note.
- **Lithium Argentina (LAAC)** — Argentine operations (Caucharí-Olaroz brine JV with [[Ganfeng Lithium]]). See [[Lithium Argentina]].

The separation isolated the US-domestic political-capture story from the Argentine-Chinese-joint-venture story. Each entity now trades on its own capital structure and catalyst path.

## Investment lens

LAC is the leveraged single-project US-domestic lithium bet. Upside depends on:

- Thacker Pass execution (clay-processing technology proving at scale)
- Commodity-price environment during Phase 1 ramp (current $23,000/t spot is strongly supportive vs the DOE underwriting assumptions)
- Federal-policy continuity (DOE loan preserved, critical-minerals designation maintained)
- GM off-take contract performance (GM demand for Bolt / Equinox EV battery materials)

Downside risks:

- Cost overruns (Phase 1 target $3.5B could blow out at 2026-2028 construction inflation)
- Technology risk (clay-based extraction is unproven at commercial scale)
- Environmental / permitting residual risk (ongoing legal challenges)
- Single-project concentration (no other producing assets; all risk concentrated on Thacker Pass)
