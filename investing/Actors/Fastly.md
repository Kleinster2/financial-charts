## Anthropic Managed Agents selloff (Apr 10, 2026)

Fastly fell 21.7% on Apr 10, 2026, from $29.46 to $23.07, after [[Anthropic]] launched [[Anthropic Managed Agents]].

The market's logic was that Anthropic had moved beyond selling the model and into selling the runtime: long-lived sessions, sandboxed code execution, checkpointing, credentials, scoped permissions, tracing, and tool orchestration. For a name like Fastly, that was a direct hit to the "agent traffic needs neutral infrastructure" thesis.

Why Fastly was hit harder than peers:
- its equity story was already tightly linked to developer adoption and edge-runtime demand
- it has less of the broad platform / government-security narrative that cushions [[Cloudflare]]
- investors read the product launch as Anthropic absorbing part of the exact layer Fastly hoped to monetize

What did not change:
- Anthropic did not replace global content delivery, perimeter security, or multi-model network control
- Fastly still matters where customers want neutral infrastructure, performance tuning, and non-Anthropic workloads
- the repricing was about who owns the control plane, not about edge traffic disappearing

## Why the stock had been working before this

Fastly had more than doubled during the February 2026 rotation because the market initially treated infrastructure software as the clean beneficiary of AI disruption in application software. [[Anthropic Managed Agents selloff April 2026]] showed that even within software, the winners are narrower than "infrastructure good, apps bad."
