# Kevin McCarthy

Kevin McCarthy is a former US Congressman from California who served as Speaker of the House of Representatives from January to October 2023, making his the shortest speakership since 1876. He was removed via an unprecedented motion to vacate led by Republican hardliners, and subsequently resigned from Congress in December 2023. His brief speakership and ouster illustrated the fracturing of the Republican caucus that continues to shape fiscal policy and governance risk.

McCarthy attended the inaugural [[Hill and Valley Forum]] dinner in March 2023 while serving as Speaker, signaling early bipartisan political support for the Silicon Valley-Washington defense-tech nexus. His removal was triggered by a deal he struck with Democrats to avoid a government shutdown — a pragmatic move that infuriated the House Freedom Caucus. After leaving Congress, McCarthy transitioned to the private sector, joining lobbying and advisory roles. His ouster elevated [[Mike Johnson]] to the speakership and demonstrated that the Speaker's chair had become a structurally unstable position given narrow Republican majorities.
