# Marubeni

One of [[Japan]]'s five major *sogo shosha* (general trading houses), founded 1858. Diversified across agriculture, energy, metals, power, transport, food, finance, and consumer products. One of the five trading houses [[Warren Buffett]] / [[Berkshire Hathaway]] accumulated stakes in starting 2019 (see [[Buffett Japan sogo shosha trade]]).

## Why Marubeni matters

Together with [[Mitsubishi]], [[Mitsui]], [[Itochu]], and [[Sumitomo Corp]], Marubeni is one of the five Japanese trading houses that operate at the intersection of physical commodity logistics, energy, and emerging-market industrial finance. Their business model — bridging price and information gaps across fragmented goods markets — has come back into market focus in 2024-2026 as the [[Law of one price]] retreats. See [[Buffett Japan sogo shosha trade]] for the full investment frame.

## May 2026: Kokubu naphtha warning

*Source: [[Reuters]] via [MarketScreener](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/former-marubeni-ceo-warns-japan-could-face-chemical-product-shortages-from-late-june-ce7f5addd98bfe27), May 25, 2026; [[Honda]] [official profile](https://global.honda/en/about/management/fumiya_kokubu.html), as of Apr. 1, 2026.*

[[Fumiya Kokubu]], Marubeni president and CEO from 2013 to 2019 and later chairman / executive corporate adviser, warned at a May 25 [[IEEJ]] virtual seminar that [[Japan]] could face chemical-products shortages from around the end of June if Middle East [[Naphtha]] supply is not replaced.

The relevance to Marubeni is informational, not a direct earnings line. Kokubu's warning shows why the *sogo shosha* network matters in a fragmented commodity regime: trading-house executives see the source-volume problem before it becomes a retail shortage. In this case, the source problem is Japan's layered Middle East exposure: direct naphtha imports plus domestic naphtha derived from Middle East crude.
