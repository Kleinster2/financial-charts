**Helibras** (Helicópteros do Brasil S.A.) — Brazil's only domestic helicopter manufacturer, wholly owned subsidiary of [[Airbus]] Helicopters since 1980. Based in Itajubá, southwestern [[Minas Gerais]]. Has produced and delivered over 800 helicopters across civilian and military variants, the largest single share being the Eurocopter AS350 Écureuil (Esquilo). Lead Brazilian manufacturer of the H225 multi-role helicopter for all three Brazilian armed services, and of the H145M attack/utility variant under a R$1B 2025 Brazil-France letter of intent for production.

---

## Synopsis

Helibras is the cleanest single example of [[Brazilian defense industrial base|Channel 1 — full foreign subsidiary]] in the Brazilian defense industry. Founded in 1978 as a Franco-Brazilian joint venture, it became fully Aérospatiale-owned (later [[Airbus]] Helicopters) in 1980, predating the Lei 12.598 regime by three decades. The Itajubá plant has been the indigenous Brazilian helicopter assembly point ever since, producing the Esquilo (AS350) line under license through the 1990s-2000s, the EC725 / H225M (the Brazilian Armed Forces' heavy-lift workhorse) from 2010 onward, and now scaling up for the H145M order announced under [[Lula]]'s government in 2025.

The structural significance is twofold. First, Helibras embeds the entire Airbus rotorcraft technology stack inside Brazilian sovereign industry — every Brazilian Armed Forces helicopter procurement effectively buys from a French-controlled but locally-employing entity, satisfying the END indigenization requirement while preserving French export economics. Second, Helibras anchors Itajubá as a secondary aerospace cluster, complementing [[Embraer]]'s São José dos Campos position. The 2025 R$1B H145M production investment scales the plant materially and signals that Airbus is willing to commit further capital to maintain the Brazilian beachhead under BRICS-shifted political conditions.

## H145M order (2025)

In 2025, Brazil and France signed a letter of intent for production of the H145M multipurpose helicopter at the Helibras Itajubá plant. The Brazilian Army is the primary customer, with planning for 10 to 12 units, deliveries 2028 (4) and 2030 (4), expected to support logistics and light attack missions. The estimated investment is approximately R$1B (~$190M). The agreement reflects [[Lula]]'s industrial-policy emphasis on indigenous production and is structurally consistent with the END mandatory tech-transfer regime.
