**MotoGP** is the premier motorcycle racing series globally, acquired by [[Liberty Media]] in July 2025 for EUR 4.2B (~$5.0B). Liberty took an 84% stake with Dorna Sports management retaining 16%, housing the property under the FWONA/FWONK tracking stocks alongside [[Formula 1]].

## Synopsis

The MotoGP acquisition is [[Liberty Media]]'s second major motorsport bet after [[Formula 1]] and the clearest expression of its thesis that live sports with global broadcast reach are undermonetized media assets. At EUR 4.2B, the deal valued MotoGP at roughly 15-17x revenue — a premium that implies Liberty sees the same playbook it ran with F1 (hospitality upgrades, expanded race calendar, US market penetration, digital/social media growth) as transferable to two-wheel racing. The series broadcasts to 207 countries and has deep cultural roots in Southern Europe, Southeast Asia, and Latin America, markets where F1's footprint is thinner.

The strategic logic is portfolio diversification within motorsport. [[Formula 1]] trades at a significant premium to other sports properties because of its combination of global reach, wealthy demographics, and limited calendar scarcity (24 races/year). Adding MotoGP gives Liberty a second global series with a distinct fan base and lower per-event production costs. The risk is that motorcycle racing lacks F1's crossover cultural momentum — the Drive to Survive effect that doubled F1's US audience has no equivalent in MotoGP, and the economics of motorcycle teams (lower budgets, manufacturer-dependent) limit the franchise model that has driven F1 team valuations above $1B each.
