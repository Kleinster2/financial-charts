**Bugatti** is a French hypercar manufacturer, now part of Bugatti Rimac -- a joint venture between [[Volkswagen]]/Porsche AG (55%) and [[Rimac|Rimac Group]] (45%), formed in 2021. Produces ultra-limited hypercars with production measured in hundreds, not thousands.

## Synopsis

Bugatti exists at the apex of automotive manufacturing where the product is less a car than a statement of engineering excess. The Veyron (2005-2015, 450 units) and Chiron (2016-2024, 500 units) each carried $2-3M+ price tags and each lost money per unit for [[Volkswagen]] -- the brand's value was always about halo effect and engineering credibility rather than profit contribution. The 2021 restructuring into a JV with Rimac, the Croatian EV hypercar startup, was Volkswagen's way of offloading the financial burden while preserving the brand. Mate Rimac, the 36-year-old Croatian engineer who founded [[Rimac|Rimac Group]], now runs the combined entity from a new campus near Zagreb.

The UAE is Bugatti's most successful dealership market globally, operated by [[Al Habtoor Group]] with the world's largest Bugatti showroom. This geographic skew -- Gulf wealth as the primary customer base -- makes Bugatti a barometer for ultra-high-net-worth spending in the region. The Chiron successor (Tourbillon, revealed 2024) uses a hybrid V16 powertrain co-developed with Rimac, pricing from EUR3.8M. For investors, Bugatti matters as a signal: the pace of hypercar orders (typically sold out years in advance) reflects top-of-pyramid wealth confidence. When Bugatti production slots trade at premiums, the luxury cycle is healthy.
