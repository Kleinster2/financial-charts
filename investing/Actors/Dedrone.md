# Dedrone

Counter-drone detection and defense technology company. Acquired by [[Axon]] in late 2024 for ~$209M, adding airspace security to Axon's law enforcement technology stack.

---

## Synopsis

Dedrone built a sensor-fusion platform that detects, classifies, and tracks unmanned aerial systems (drones) using a combination of RF sensors, radar, cameras, and acoustic detectors. The technology addresses a growing threat vector — unauthorized drones over critical infrastructure, prisons, stadiums, military bases, and government buildings. Dedrone's system identifies drone type, operator location, and flight path, enabling both passive monitoring and active mitigation (when paired with jamming or kinetic defeat systems from partners).

[[Axon]]'s $209M acquisition makes strategic sense within the broader counter-drone boom driven by [[Ukraine]] conflict lessons. The [[Drone warfare]] revolution demonstrated that cheap commercial drones can threaten billion-dollar assets, creating urgent demand for detection systems from military, law enforcement, and critical infrastructure operators. For [[Axon]], Dedrone extends the company beyond body cameras and Tasers into airspace security — a market projected to grow at 25-30% CAGR through 2030. The acquisition price was modest relative to the opportunity, suggesting [[Axon]] got the deal done before the market fully priced the counter-drone TAM expansion. Dedrone's technology now integrates with Axon's evidence management platform, creating a sensor-to-courtroom pipeline for drone incursion cases.
