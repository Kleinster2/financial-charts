Jason Calacanis is an angel investor, entrepreneur, and media personality. He is a co-host of the [[All-In Podcast]] and is best known for his early-stage investment in [[Uber]], which returned an estimated 5,000x on his initial check.

## Synopsis

Calacanis built his investing reputation through prolific seed-stage activity in the 2010s San Francisco ecosystem, writing small checks into hundreds of startups and hitting outsized returns on [[Uber]] (invested at the seed round), Calm, and Robinhood. His earlier entrepreneurial career included founding Weblogs Inc (sold to AOL in 2005) and running the LAUNCH accelerator and conference, which served as a deal-flow engine for his angel portfolio. His investing style is high-volume, conviction-driven at the seed stage — the opposite of concentrated growth equity.

As a co-host of the [[All-In Podcast]] alongside [[Chamath Palihapitiya]], [[David Friedberg]], and [[David Sacks]], Calacanis plays the provocateur and dealmaker role. The podcast has become one of the most influential forums in tech investing, with episodes regularly moving discourse on AI policy, venture trends, and macro positioning. Calacanis's public influence exceeds his AUM — he shapes narrative more than allocations. His involvement with the LAUNCH fund (now investing from later vintages) and frequent public commentary on startup valuations make him a barometer for early-stage sentiment in [[Silicon Valley]].
