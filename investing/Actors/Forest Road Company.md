# Forest Road Company

LA-based investment firm that targets "funky, interesting investments where larger asset managers don't play" (company president Jeremy Tarica). Launched CenterNode, a $750M distributed solar and battery fund, in Apr 2026 — described as the largest standalone US solar/battery fund by committed capital. Also owns a Formula E racing team. Previously led an unsuccessful bid to buy [[OnlyFans]].

---

## CenterNode fund ($750M, launched Apr 2026)

Targets distributed solar sector: community-based and off-grid projects of 5-10 MW (enough to power up to 8,000 homes). Backed by [[Liberty Mutual]] Investments among others.

### Thesis

The [[One Big Beautiful Bill Act]] killed tax credits (worth 30% of total system costs) for solar/wind projects that begin construction after Jul 4, 2026. Traditional financiers pulled back, creating a capital gap for smaller projects. Larger projects (50 MW+) still attract institutional capital from banks like [[JPMorgan]]; sub-10 MW projects have "virtually zero" financing options.

CEO Zachary Tarica (Apr 2): *"If you were trying to build a 50MW project, you'd have absolutely no problem going to JPMorgan and getting some large fund to give you half a billion dollars. But if you wanted to build a 10MW project, there's virtually zero regional community banking effort to service you, and there's no capital market solutions."*

### Structure

- Construction loans, mezzanine debt, preferred equity — no common equity risk
- Target returns: 2-3x conventional deal rates
- Speed is key: developers need to begin construction before Jul 4 2026 deadline to lock in ITC
- High-risk, high-reward: many remaining projects may be under-developed, requiring fast-turnaround diligence

### Demand backdrop

US electricity demand projected to rise 25% by 2030 (ICF International), driven by data centers, electrification of transport and heating. Utility bills already rising, boosting the case for localized energy sources.

*Source: FT, Martha Muir (New York), Apr 2 2026*
