**Aqua Capital** is a private equity firm focused on sustainable agribusiness and food-value-chain investments across the Americas, founded and led by managing partner Sebastián Popik. It manages over $1.1B in AUM and has made 45+ platform investments since inception.

Aqua runs control buyouts across agricultural inputs, food, and logistics in Latin and North America. Its Fund III closed in 2023 at $450M, oversubscribed against a $400M target. Popik (Harvard MPP; previously Pampa Capital and Salutia) positions the firm around sustainability and value-chain consolidation.

The relevance for portfolio analysis is the retail-distribution angle: Aqua's agribusiness FIP is sold through the [[XP]] advisory channel, and it is a recurring example of the high-fee retail private-equity products on Brazilian brokerage shelves — long lock-ups, opaque marks, and an administration fee that makes them attractive to distributors rather than to end investors. It is the canonical case for the view that retail PE below ~R$50m is the most expensive, least efficient tier of the market.
