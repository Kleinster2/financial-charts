**Matt Knight** is the former VP and CISO at [[OpenAI]], serving as the company's first security hire in 2020 and building the security program that protected hundreds of millions of users through ChatGPT's hypergrowth phase. He resigned in late January 2026.

## Synopsis

Knight's tenure at [[OpenAI]] spanned the company's transformation from a research lab to a consumer product serving 200M+ weekly users, making his security mandate one of the most consequential in tech. He built the security organization from scratch, navigating challenges unique to AI companies: model weight protection, prompt injection defense, abuse prevention at scale, and the novel attack surfaces that emerge when a model can execute code and browse the web. His final major project was [[Aardvark]], a security research agent launched in Q4 2025 that represented OpenAI's first foray into selling AI-powered security tools.

His departure in January 2026 — after 5.5 years — fits a pattern of senior technical leaders leaving OpenAI during 2024-2026, following the exits of co-founders [[Ilya Sutskever]] and [[John Schulman]], safety leads, and other key personnel. For investors tracking [[OpenAI]]'s organizational stability, the CISO departure is particularly notable given the increasing regulatory scrutiny of AI safety and the company's expanding attack surface as it moves into enterprise and government contracts.
