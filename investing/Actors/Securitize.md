# Securitize

SEC-registered transfer agent and broker-dealer specializing in tokenization infrastructure. The central picks-and-shovels company in the institutional tokenization ecosystem — backed by [[BlackRock]] and [[Ark Invest]], named as [[Intercontinental Exchange|NYSE]]'s first digital transfer agent, and the issuance platform behind the largest tokenized fund in the world.

Going public via SPAC merger with Cantor Equity Partners II (CEPT). Post-merger ticker: **SECZ** on [[Nasdaq]].

## What they do

Securitize operates across the full tokenization stack:

| Function | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Transfer agent** | SEC-registered. Tracks ownership, handles corporate actions when securities are issued and settled on blockchain |
| **Issuance** | Mints blockchain-native securities for corporate and ETF issuers |
| **Broker-dealer** | Licensed to participate in trading on tokenized platforms |
| **Platform design** | Co-designing NYSE's Digital Trading Platform architecture |

The transfer agent role is critical: in the tokenized world, someone still needs to maintain the authoritative record of who owns what, process dividends, handle voting, and manage corporate actions. Securitize is building this for blockchain rails.

## Investment thesis

**The pure-play.** Securitize benefits regardless of which exchange architecture wins (NYSE vs Nasdaq), which asset class gets tokenized first, or which blockchain is used. As long as institutional tokenization grows, Securitize is the infrastructure layer.

**Bull case:**
- Only public company whose entire business is tokenization infrastructure
- Positioned on both sides — building NYSE's platform while listing on Nasdaq
- BlackRock relationship is a moat (BUIDL is the flagship product)
- Revenue growth (841%) suggests product-market fit
- First-mover in institutional transfer agent role for blockchain securities
- TAM: if even 1% of the $126T global equity market tokenizes, the transfer agent revenue alone is massive

**Bear case:**
- $1.25B valuation on $110M projected revenue = ~11x forward. Premium justified only if growth sustains
- SPAC structure (CEPT) — SPACs historically underperform post-merger
- Regulatory risk: if [[CLARITY Act]] fails, the entire tokenization push could stall
- Competition: DTCC could build competing infrastructure; Nasdaq may choose a different partner
- Concentrated customer risk (BlackRock is a huge portion of AUM)
