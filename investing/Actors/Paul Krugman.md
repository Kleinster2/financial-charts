# Paul Krugman

American economist, columnist, and Nobel laureate. Distinguished Professor of Economics at the CUNY Graduate Center, focused on trade theory, economic geography, fiscal/monetary policy, and inflation. Long-running public commentator with one of the largest audiences in macro discourse — his daily Substack newsletter reaches 350,000-450,000 readers per post (mid-2025 figure). Often cited as a mainstream Keynesian counterpoint in market debates.

## Why he matters

Three structural reasons for vault relevance:

1. Nobel-laureate authority on trade theory and economic geography — the analytical framework behind much of the [[Second China shock]] and [[Global imbalances]] discourse traces to his 1980s work
2. Daily Substack at 350K-450K readers makes him one of the most-influential public-facing macro voices, often setting framing for the broader US center-left commentariat
3. Recurring sparring partner / interlocutor with the heterodox-macro voices the vault tracks ([[Robin J. Brooks]], [[Brad DeLong]], [[Adam Tooze]]) — Krugman's positions are the mainstream-Keynesian benchmark against which the vault's more contrarian framings are read
