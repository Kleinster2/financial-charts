#actor #defense #uae #state-owned

**EDGE Group** — Abu Dhabi state-owned defense and advanced-technology conglomerate, formed in November 2019 by consolidating 25+ UAE defense entities under a single holding. Controlled by the Tawazun Council; majority of equity ultimately sits with the UAE state. Operates in unmanned systems, missiles and weapons, electronic warfare, cyber, space, and platforms & systems. EDGE is the spearhead of the UAE's transition from arms importer to exporter and is the primary vehicle for UAE defense-industrial investment abroad.

---

## Synopsis

EDGE has done in five years what would normally take a generation: build a top-25 global defense conglomerate by consolidating UAE-domiciled programs, then bolt on minority and majority stakes in foreign technology holders to fill capability gaps. The Brazil playbook — [[SIATT]] (50% acquired September 2023) for anti-ship missiles, [[Condor Non-Lethal Technologies]] (51% acquired May 2024) for crowd control, plus a Letter of Intent with the Brazilian Navy on counter-drone systems (April 2025) — is the cleanest single illustration of the strategy. Brazil offers what the UAE cannot easily get domestically: an established missile R&D base, a non-aligned export passport, and a Lula government soliciting BRICS-aligned capital. The São José dos Campos cluster places EDGE's new 6,000 m² SIATT plant in the same city as [[Embraer]] HQ.

The pattern repeats in [[Switzerland]], [[South Africa]], and [[Saudi Arabia]]. At IDEX 2025, EDGE signed $2.9B of new orders and partnerships in a single show. Group revenue is roughly $5B (2023, last disclosed) with management targeting top-10 global by 2030. The capital base — sovereign — means EDGE can underwrite multi-year capability build-outs without the quarterly-earnings discipline that constrains [[Lockheed Martin]] or [[RTX]]. The constraint is execution: assembling a coherent product roadmap across 25 legacy entities plus a stream of foreign acquisitions is non-trivial, and EDGE has yet to prove the consolidated platform can deliver flagship programs at scale outside the UAE order book.

## Brazil portfolio

### SIATT — 50% stake (September 2023)

[[SIATT]] is a São José dos Campos-based smart-weapons specialist founded 2015 by ex-Mectron and ex-[[Embraer]] engineers. Prime contractor on the Brazilian Navy's [[MANSUP]] anti-ship missile program. EDGE acquired 50% in September 2023 — its first Brazil investment — then in April 2025 signed a strategic license agreement for the IP of the MANSUP extended-range variant, opening a path to co-export the missile into Gulf, African, and Asian markets that Brazilian export-control rules alone could not reach. The new 6,000 m² SIATT headquarters and production facility in São José dos Campos opened September 2025 — same city as [[Embraer]].

### Condor Non-Lethal Technologies — 51% stake (May 2024)

[[Condor Non-Lethal Technologies]] is a Rio de Janeiro-based maker of riot control munitions, tear gas, pyrotechnics, and training systems. EDGE took 51% control in May 2024. Slots into the Platforms & Systems sleeve and gives EDGE a non-lethal product line that broadens its addressable market into law-enforcement and public-order procurement worldwide.

### Brazilian Navy LOI — counter-drone (April 2025)

At LAAD 2025 in Rio, EDGE and the Brazilian Navy signed a Letter of Intent for joint development of a counter-drone system, with a joint working group established to advance the partnership. [[Counter-UAS]] is the highest-growth UAE export priority after the post-2023 wave of Iranian and Houthi drone strikes on Gulf targets; co-development in Brazil offers production capacity and a Latin American export channel.

## Customer relationships with Brazilian primes

EDGE is also a major customer of Brazilian defense exports independent of its equity stakes. In May 2026, the UAE Ministry of Defence announced the purchase of 10 [[C-390 Millennium]] military transport aircraft from [[Embraer]] — the first Middle Eastern customer for the C-390. The deal followed UAE President [[Mohamed bin Zayed]]'s February 2026 meeting with [[Lula]] and is consistent with the UAE's broader BRICS-alignment posture since joining the bloc in January 2024.

## Strategic posture

EDGE's playbook combines three things that single-jurisdiction Western primes cannot easily replicate: sovereign-capital patience, willingness to acquire minority and majority stakes in foreign tech holders rather than build everything in-house, and a non-aligned export passport that opens markets foreclosed to US ITAR-controlled product. The UAE's 2024 BRICS accession amplified this by creating a political framework for co-investment with [[Brazil]], India, and other emerging-market defense bases.

The 2025 Stimson Center report frames the company as the central exhibit in the UAE's transition from arms importer to arms competitor with the West. The Brazil portfolio is the cleanest single case study supporting that thesis.
