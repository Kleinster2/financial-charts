**Luxembourg** is a small Western European grand duchy with outsized financial significance. A founding member of the [[EU]], [[NATO]], and the [[IEA]] (1974), it hosts several key European institutions including the European Court of Justice, the European Investment Bank, and the European Stability Mechanism. Luxembourg's economy is dominated by financial services — it is the EU's largest investment fund domicile (over EUR 5T in fund assets) and the second-largest fund center globally after the [[United States]]. The country's favorable regulatory framework, multilingual workforce, and tax treaty network have made it the preferred European base for UCITS fund structures, private equity vehicles, and holding companies. GDP per capita ranks among the highest in the world (~$130,000), though this is inflated by the ~200,000 cross-border commuters from France, Belgium, and Germany who work in Luxembourg but aren't counted in the population denominator.
