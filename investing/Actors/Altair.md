# Altair

Altair Engineering — simulation, high-performance computing, and data analytics software company, acquired by [[Siemens]] in 2024 and integrated into the Digital Industries division. The acquisition gave [[Siemens]] a comprehensive simulation software portfolio to complement its existing Teamcenter PLM and NX CAD platforms, creating one of the deepest industrial software stacks in the market.

## Synopsis

Altair was founded in 1985 in Troy, Michigan, originally focused on structural optimization software for the automotive industry. Over four decades it expanded into a broad simulation platform covering structural analysis (OptiStruct), computational fluid dynamics, electromagnetic simulation, and data analytics/AI. The company went public in 2017 (NASDAQ: ALTR) and operated at roughly $600M in annual revenue before the [[Siemens]] acquisition. Its differentiation was the "units" licensing model — a token-based system that let engineers flexibly access any tool in Altair's suite, reducing shelfware and increasing adoption across engineering organizations.

[[Siemens]] acquired Altair to close the simulation gap with [[Dassault Systemes]] (which owns SIMULIA/Abaqus) and [[Ansys]] (acquired by [[Synopsys]] in 2024 for $35B). The acquisition created a -70bps drag on Digital Industries margin in Q1 FY2026, reflecting the integration cost and Altair's lower-margin profile relative to [[Siemens]]' existing software business. The strategic logic is sound — simulation is increasingly critical in digital twin workflows, autonomous vehicle development, and AI-driven design optimization — but execution risk exists in integrating Altair's product suite with [[Siemens]]' Xcelerator platform without alienating existing customers who chose Altair precisely because it was vendor-neutral.
