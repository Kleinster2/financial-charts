**Tao Group** is one of the largest nightlife and restaurant hospitality companies in the [[United States]], operating high-revenue venues in [[New York]], Las Vegas, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Singapore.

## Synopsis

Tao Group Hospitality runs a portfolio of premium nightclubs, restaurants, and event spaces that collectively generate over $500M in annual revenue, making it among the highest-grossing hospitality operators in North America. The flagship Tao restaurant/nightclub in NYC (opened 2000) became the template: Asian-fusion dining that converts to a nightclub after hours, maximizing revenue per square foot. The portfolio now includes Marquee, Lavo, Avenue, Beauty & Essex, and Cathédrale, spanning the full spectrum from nightlife to upscale dining.

[[Madison Square Garden Entertainment]] (MSGE) acquired a controlling 67.5% stake in 2017 for ~$181M, valuing Tao Group at ~$268M — a bet on experiential hospitality that predated the post-COVID boom in experience spending. The relationship gives Tao access to MSGE's venue network (the Sphere, MSG, Radio City) while MSGE gets hospitality operating expertise. John Villa, now executive chef at [[Mercer Street Hospitality]]'s Seahorse, formerly ran Tao Group's culinary division. The business model — premium nightlife with high cover charges, bottle service, and celebrity-driven traffic — is inherently cyclical and discretionary, but Tao has proven resilient across multiple downturns by constantly refreshing the venue portfolio.
