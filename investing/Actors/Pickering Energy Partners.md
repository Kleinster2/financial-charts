# Pickering Energy Partners

Houston-based energy-sector financial services platform founded in 2019 by [[Dan Pickering]] after his departure from [[Tudor Pickering Holt]] (which he had co-founded in 2007 and which was acquired by [[Perella Weinberg Partners]] in 2016). PEP combines investment banking, asset management, and research focused on the energy value chain — upstream, midstream, services, and increasingly the energy transition.

Dan Pickering's monthly commentary is widely cited by financial media (Bloomberg, FT, CNBC) as a sell-side-style read on energy-sector dynamics from a privately held boutique perspective.

## Research voice

Dan Pickering's monthly commentary is the firm's most-public output. Tracks oil/gas market dynamics, OPEC policy, US shale capital allocation, and energy-transition flows.

Notable 2026 calls:

| Date | Call |
|---|---|
| Dec 2025 / Jan 2026 (pre-Iran) | 2026 oil average $45-55/bbl; oversupply, 500-750mb inventory build |
| Feb 2026 (post-Iran initial) | Geopolitical premium changes longer-term story; prices no longer need to fall to low-$50s to discourage supply |
| Apr 2026 (FT, Findlay) | "Most companies are taking a wait and see or do nothing approach to their 2026 budget" |

The post-Iran update is structurally important: Pickering's view that supply discipline has decoupled from price signal is one of the analyst-house framings underpinning the [[Shale supply discipline]] concept.
