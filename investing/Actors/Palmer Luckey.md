## Personal background

Eldest of four siblings, grew up in Long Beach, CA. Mother homeschooled him, allowing pursuit of electronics and engineering. Started building a VR headset in 2009 at age 16, attracting attention from investors including [[Peter Thiel]].

Grandfather was a pilot who flew as a civilian operator for the U.S. military during Operation Desert Storm (1991). Luckey did an internship on VR headsets for military veterans' PTSD treatment. Collects retired military vehicles — bought a UH-1 Huey helicopter (used for dramatic entrance at his wedding) and a Mark V Special Operations Craft (retired Navy boat, takes visitors out on Pacific Ocean jaunts). Also maintains a $3.8M property across from his Newport Beach home for his car collection.

Taste for cosplaying anime characters. After the [[Meta]] firing, flew to Japan for an anime convention with Nicole Edelmann, photographed in matching costume. Started growing a goatee. The billowing mullet and Hawaiian shirts are the signature look.

## Facebook exit (2016-2017)

Before the 2016 election, news leaked that Luckey donated $9,000 to a pro-Trump organization that funded an anti-Hillary Clinton billboard. Silicon Valley backlash was severe — [[Meta]] employees demanded his firing, posted on internal boards accusing him of funding antisemitic, misogynistic, and racist messages.

Luckey drafted a response blaming media distortion. [[Meta]] executives told him not to send it. [[Mark Zuckerberg]] personally suggested Luckey explain he was Libertarian and that his Trump support was misunderstood. Luckey did not comply. Fired March 2017 without explanation.

Luckey was furious. Founded [[Anduril]] as, by his own admission, a middle finger to [[Mark Zuckerberg]]. Considered startups targeting obesity or the prison system first, but defense tech was closer to his heart and unpopular in Silicon Valley — which appealed to his contrarian streak.

## On building companies (TBPN, Feb 2026)

Started Oculus solo at 19 with no co-founders. Brought on people months later he's "honored to call co-founders" — and others he's not. Multiple people now falsely claim Oculus co-founder status, including one with a documentary in production.

On vibe coding: self-described terrible software engineer. Built Oculus by focusing on opto-mechanics and product integration, not programming. Had he spent 2 years learning to code, he'd have been 2 years behind on everything else. Views vibe coding as especially beneficial for hardware people ("shape rotators, not word cells").

On VR: remains optimistic. [[Meta]] not retreating — fired 10% of Reality Labs but that's ~6 months of natural churn pulled forward. Still spending more on VR than anyone by an order of magnitude. Apple Vision Pro used engineering samples ($1K/display at 10% yield) hauled into the present — future headsets from all players will match that visual fidelity in smaller, lighter form factors.
