## February 2026 reset and recovery — guidance cut, class actions, enterprise pivot

In February 2026 Monday.com cut its FY2026 revenue target ~$40M below its previously "confident" $1.5B threshold and withdrew its FY2027 $1.8B revenue target entirely. Management cited deteriorating SMB customer acquisition driven by [[Google]] algorithm changes that compressed organic-search funnels — historically the primary top-of-funnel for Monday's product-led growth motion. MNDY fell ~21% on the day. Multiple securities-fraud class actions followed alleging executives concealed the deceleration ahead of the Feb 9 print; the lead-plaintiff deadline is May 11, 2026, the same date as the Q1 2026 earnings call.

The stock declined ~79% from its 52-week high of $317 to a March-April 2026 trough in the high $50s before stabilizing. Consensus analyst price target ~$155 (vs ~$67 spot mid-April) implies analysts have already priced the SMB drag as structural while still underwriting the enterprise-momentum recovery — large-customer ARR was +74% YoY at the Feb print. 21 of 26 analysts maintain Buy ratings; the bull thesis is conditional on Q1 2026 confirming enterprise momentum offsets ongoing SMB headwinds.

---

## May 1, 2026 — recovery rally into Q1 print

MNDY closed May 1 at $72.74, +10.4% from Apr 30 (+2.5σ on 60-day vol; β-adjusted). No company-specific news catalyst was reported on the day — the move tracked a [[SaaS]] / Israeli-tech sector rally on [[Twilio]]'s +23.8% Q1 print (rev $1.41B, +20% reported), which was read as positive read-through for usage-based / enterprise SaaS exposure into the May 11 MNDY print. The recovery rally is the second-largest single-day MNDY gain since the February reset, and brings the stock back near the $65-75 range it traded in pre-reset.

Three things will determine whether the recovery extends past the May 11 print:

| Variable | Bull case | Bear case |
|---|---|---|
| Enterprise ARR growth | Sustains +60-75% YoY pace | Decelerates as deal cycle matures |
| SMB customer count | Stabilizes ([[Google]] algo headwind absorbed) | Continues to decline; net adds turn negative |
| FY2026 guide vs Feb cut | Reaffirmed or modestly raised | Cut again; lawsuit overhang compounds |

See [[Monday.com securities]] for the price detail. The lead-plaintiff deadline overlap with the earnings date means the May 11 session will combine securities-litigation and earnings-driven volatility in one print — execution risk is asymmetrically high.
