# Bulgari

**Italian jewelry house** — Roman glamour, bold colors, architectural designs. Founded 1884 by Greek silversmith Sotirio Voulgaris in Rome. Acquired by [[LVMH]] in 2011 for €4.3B. Record year in 2025 despite broader luxury slowdown. Iconic Serpenti and B.zero1 collections. Expanding into luxury hotels.

## Watches

Bulgari holds multiple world records for ultra-thin movements:

| Record | Model | Thickness |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Thinnest tourbillon | Octo Finissimo | 1.80mm |
| Thinnest minute repeater | Octo Finissimo | 6.85mm |
| Thinnest perpetual calendar | Octo Finissimo | 5.80mm |

Watches ~29% of Bulgari revenue historically. Positioned as Roman alternative to Swiss dominance.

## Leadership transition (2026)

**Jean-Christophe Babin** (CEO 2013-2026): Transformed brand — watchmaking credibility, hotel expansion, Valenza factory, La Scuola Bulgari jewelry school. Stepping down after 12 years.

**New roles for Babin:**
- Chairman, Bulgari Board
- CEO, Bulgari Hotels
- President, Bulgari Foundation
- CEO, LVMH Watches (Hublot, Zenith, TAG Heuer)

**Laura Burdese** (CEO from July 2026): Joined LVMH 2015 as CEO of Acqua di Parma. Bulgari CMO 2022, Deputy CEO July 2024. Prior: 17 years at Swatch Group, CEO of Calvin Klein watches.

## Investment case

*Note: Bulgari is part of [[LVMH]], not separately traded.*

**Bull:**
- Record performance while luxury peers struggled
- Serpenti franchise with Year of the Snake tailwind
- Hotel expansion builds brand in key markets
- Watch credibility (ultra-thin records)
- Strong [[China]] positioning

**Bear:**
- Buried in LVMH conglomerate — no pure-play exposure
- [[China]] dependence (largest market)
- Leadership transition risk
- Jewelry cyclical with wealth effect
- Competition from [[Cartier]] ([[Richemont]]), [[Tiffany]] (sister brand)
