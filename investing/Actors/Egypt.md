# Egypt

Egypt is the largest Arab country by population (~105M) and a linchpin of Middle Eastern geopolitics and trade infrastructure. GDP of ~$400B (2024) belies an economy under persistent foreign currency pressure — the EGP has devalued repeatedly (most recently ~40% in March 2024) as the government struggles to service ~$165B in external debt. The Suez Canal, which handles ~12-15% of global trade, generates ~$8B/year in revenue and is Egypt's most strategically significant asset. [[IMF]] programs ($3B in 2022, expanded repeatedly) and Gulf sovereign wealth fund investments (Ras al-Hekma deal with [[Abu Dhabi]]'s ADQ, $35B in Feb 2024) have been the primary stabilization mechanisms.

## Investment relevance

Egypt matters for global portfolios through two channels. First, the Suez Canal — [[Houthi]] attacks on Red Sea shipping (2023-24) diverted traffic around the Cape, cutting canal revenues by ~40% and demonstrating the fragility of this trade chokepoint. Second, Egypt is a major emerging market banking play; [[Credit Agricole]] operates Credit Agricole Egypt, and several Gulf banks have expanded there. The Ras al-Hekma deal — effectively a $35B Gulf bailout disguised as a real estate investment — showed how GCC sovereign wealth is becoming the lender of last resort for stressed MENA sovereigns.
