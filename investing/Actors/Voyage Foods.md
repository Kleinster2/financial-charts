Voyage Foods — US foodtech start-up producing cocoa-free "chocolate" and other reformulated ingredients from non-traditional substrates (grape seeds, sunflower protein). Distribution partner: [[Cargill]], the world's largest agricultural commodities trader. Voyage sits on the ingredient-substitution side of the chocolate-alternatives landscape — distinct from cell-based peers ([[Celleste Bio]], [[Kokomodo]], [[Food Brewer]]) which aim to replicate cocoa butter at the molecular level.

## Technology and positioning

Voyage reformulates chocolate's sensory profile (flavour, mouthfeel, melting behaviour) using ingredients that do not derive from cocoa plants. Because the output is not technically chocolate under most jurisdictions' labeling rules, the product category sits outside of cell-cultured alternatives that aim for direct drop-in compatibility.

The scalability argument (per industry adviser Raphaël Felenbok, FT April 2026) favours ingredient substitution over cell culture on near-term economics: lower energy intensity, lower capex per unit output, and fewer regulatory novelty questions. The trade-off is that Voyage's output cannot be labeled or sold as "chocolate" in most markets — it competes on price and functional equivalence, not identity.

---

## Cargill partnership

[[Cargill]]'s partnership with Voyage is part of a dual-track exposure to chocolate alternatives: Cargill also collaborates with [[Kokomodo]] on cell-based cacao. The pairing gives Cargill optionality across two unrelated technology routes — ingredient substitution (Voyage) and cell culture (Kokomodo) — without committing to either as the winning path.
