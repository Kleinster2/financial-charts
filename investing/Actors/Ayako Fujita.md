# Ayako Fujita

Ayako Fujita is the chief Japan economist at [[JPMorgan Chase|J.P. Morgan]] Securities Japan. She is one of the most-cited sell-side voices on [[BOJ]] policy timing and the Japanese inflation cycle, frequently appearing on Bloomberg TV's Asia coverage to interpret BOJ board signals and JGB / FX implications for global investors.

## Key calls

### Apr 28, 2026 — BOJ April hold call + JGB stress-point framing

On Bloomberg "The Asia Trade" (4/28/2026), Fujita made two anchor calls heading into the [[BOJ]]'s Apr 28 monetary policy decision:

- **April hold, June live.** April was always likely a hold meeting, "primarily driven by uncertainty" around the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran shock]]. The next genuinely live BOJ target is the June meeting, not May. Even with hawkish-leaning data through mid-April, Governor [[Kazuo Ueda]] cannot credibly pre-commit to a June hike because the duration of the Iran energy shock is unknown.
- **JGBs, not FX, are the binding pressure point.** Fujita's framing: "if they try to contain currency pressure, all of this pressure will probably go to JGB side." FX intervention by [[Ministry of Finance (Japan)|MoF]] is a short- to medium-term tool that cannot durably reverse a yen-weakness trend driven by rate differentials and oil-import financing. The medium-term lever is monetary policy — and to the extent the BOJ stays patient on rates, the long end of the curve has to absorb both the inflation passthrough and the fiscal cost of energy subsidies.
- **Energy-subsidy political risk.** Government subsidies have so far capped Japanese energy CPI passthrough, but Fujita flags that these subsidies cannot last indefinitely. Once they step down, household energy inflation resumes and the BOJ faces a delayed reflation pulse — even if domestic consumption demand has not deteriorated.

This was Fujita's cleanest articulation that the binding constraint on Japanese policy is fiscal-monetary, not FX-monetary, even with [[USDJPY]] near 160.

*Source: Bloomberg "The Asia Trade" 4/28/2026 broadcast.*
