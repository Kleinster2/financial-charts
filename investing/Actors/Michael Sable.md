# Michael Sable

CEO of [[Venture Global]], the fast-growing US [[LNG]] developer that went public in January 2025 in one of the largest energy IPOs in years.

## Synopsis

Michael Sable built [[Venture Global]] from a startup into the second-largest US LNG exporter by taking a contrarian approach to project development -- smaller, modular liquefaction trains using [[Baker Hughes]] technology rather than the mega-train approach favored by [[Cheniere]] and others. This allowed faster construction timelines and lower per-unit costs, at least in theory. The Calcasieu Pass facility in Louisiana achieved first LNG production in 2022, ahead of schedule relative to competitors' timelines.

The controversy around Sable and Venture Global centers on commercial disputes with offtake customers. Several buyers, including European utilities and Asian trading houses, have alleged that Venture Global continued selling cargoes on the spot market at inflated prices during the 2022 energy crisis rather than honoring long-term contract commitments. These disputes are playing out in arbitration and have cast a shadow over the company's January 2025 IPO (ticker VG), which priced at $25/share and valued the company at ~$60B enterprise value. The IPO raised ~$1.75B. Sable's ability to navigate these legal challenges while bringing the larger Plaquemines LNG facility online will determine whether Venture Global can sustain its valuation premium over [[Cheniere]].
