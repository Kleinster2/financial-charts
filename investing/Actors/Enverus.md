## May 2026 shale capex signal

The [[Financial Times]] cited [[Enverus]] data showing that publicly listed shale producers raised 2026 capital-expenditure forecasts by $490 million in first-quarter results compared with guidance issued three months earlier. [[Alex Ljubojevic]] framed the shift as the point where the 2027 [[Brent crude]] price had become attractive enough for operators to add activity.

In the vault, this belongs in [[Shale supply discipline]] as an intermediate signal: public producers are not returning to a pre-2020 growth model, but the capex line is no longer frozen either.

*Source: [[Financial Times]], Jamie Smyth and Stephanie Findlay, "US oil producers increase output to capture price surge from Iran war," May 23 2026.*
