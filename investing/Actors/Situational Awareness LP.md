# Situational Awareness LP

**Situational Awareness LP** - [[Leopold Aschenbrenner]]'s San Francisco global equity investor focused on [[AI Infrastructure]] and the economic consequences of [[AGI timelines|AGI]]. It is the investable expression of Aschenbrenner's *Situational Awareness* worldview: AI progress is not just a software story, but a capital, power, compute, security, and macro story.

The firm matters because it turns an unusually explicit frontier-AI forecast into a public-market position book. Aschenbrenner's essay said the AI race would create trillion-dollar clusters, power bottlenecks, lab-security pressures, and state involvement; the fund's 2026 public filings show that framework being expressed through semiconductors, AI power, GPU rental, storage, crypto-to-AI campuses, and option overlays rather than through generic software beta.

The key distinction is that the fund is not simply "long AI." Its May 2026 13F signal was a barbell: long exposed infrastructure bottlenecks while using large put positions against the crowded semiconductor complex. That makes it a useful sentiment read for the vault: the same investor can believe AI demand is structurally real while hedging or shorting parts of the supplier chain after a violent rerating.

## Evolution

The story of Situational Awareness LP is the conversion of a viral AGI forecast into a public-market allocation vehicle.

- 2024 - [[Leopold Aschenbrenner]] published *Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead*, arguing that AI progress would force a real-world industrial mobilization around compute, power, lab security, and government involvement. In September 2024, he founded the fund to express that view as an equity strategy rather than only an essay.
- 2025-2026 - The fund institutionalized quickly. The latest ADV-derived profile shows $9.3B of regulatory assets under management, 2 pooled vehicles, and 8 employees as of April 2026. The Q1 2026 [[13F]] then made the strategy visible: large reported value, concentrated AI-infrastructure exposure, and a semiconductor put overlay that separates physical-bottleneck conviction from crowded supplier-chain beta.

## Investment frame

The official firm description is broad but specific enough to be useful: Situational Awareness says AI will be the dominant driver of global market returns over the next decade, and it looks for investments and themes materially affected, positively or negatively, by AI development. The mandate explicitly spans technology, energy, other sectors, and macro impacts.

## 13F signal (Q1 2026)

The Q1 2026 [[13F]] is the first filing that turns Situational Awareness from an idea into a visible market signal. 13f.info reports 42 holdings and $13.68B of reported value as of Mar 31 2026, filed May 18 2026. A secondary filing read by The Market Context decomposes that into a large semiconductor put overlay layered on top of long AI-infrastructure and [[Crypto-to-AI pivot|crypto-to-AI]] exposures.

The directional read:

- Crowded-chip hedge - the largest reported positions were puts on [[SMH]], [[NVIDIA]], [[Oracle]], [[Broadcom]], and [[AMD]]. This is not anti-AI in isolation; it is a statement that parts of the AI supplier chain had become expensive or vulnerable enough to hedge.
- Physical bottlenecks still long - long exposure remained tied to [[Bloom Energy]], [[CoreWeave]], [[IREN]], [[Applied Digital]], [[SanDisk]], and related power/data-center/storage names.
- [[Crypto-to-AI pivot|Crypto-to-AI]] conversion - the smaller positions in miners and hosting operators align with the [[Crypto-to-AI pivot]] thesis: power sites and electrical infrastructure can become AI hosting assets if economics and interconnects work.
- Next filing matters - the Q2 2026 [[13F]], due in August 2026, will show whether the semiconductor puts were a quarter-end hedge, a broader short view, or a rolling overlay against AI-infrastructure longs.

## Why it matters

For this vault, Situational Awareness LP is a source of revealed preference about the [[AI Infrastructure]] trade. Aschenbrenner's public writing says AI progress forces a real-world buildout: power, transformers, data centers, fabs, security, and government attention. The fund's public filings show which pieces of that buildout he is willing to own, hedge, or short after the market has repriced.

The most useful read is not "copy the [[13F]]." The filing is stale by construction, options can distort reported value, and the strategy may have changed after quarter-end. The durable value is taxonomy: AI infrastructure is no longer one trade. The fund separates physical bottleneck longs from crowded semiconductor beta, which is exactly the distinction the vault needs to track across [[Power constraints]], [[AI capex chain basket]], [[Crypto-to-AI pivot]], and [[AI infrastructure financing risk]].
