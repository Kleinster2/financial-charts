**Firefly Aerospace** — Texas-based small/medium-lift launch vehicle developer. [[Firefly Alpha]] returned to flight on March 11, 2026 with a successful orbital launch from Vandenberg ("Stairway to Seven" mission, FLTA007) — the first flight after two 2025 mishaps grounded the vehicle. Block II configuration upgrade is next, with US Space Force VICTUS HAZE Jackal mission slated NET Q2 2026. One of the new entrants [[Space Capital]] (Q1 2026) flagged as absorbing commercial demand SpaceX is too capacity-constrained to serve.

## Q1 2026 — Alpha return to flight

Per [[Space Capital]] (Chad Anderson, Apr 26, 2026): "Two new vehicles came online in Q1 — [[Firefly Aerospace|Firefly Alpha]] and [[Ariane 64]]."

Alpha return-to-flight context:
- 2 mishaps in 2024-2025 grounded the vehicle
- Mar 11, 2026 launch from Vandenberg validated key systems for the Block II upgrade
- "Stairway to Seven" mission (FLTA007) achieved nominal performance
- Block II flight target: US Space Force VICTUS HAZE Jackal mission, NET Q2 2026

Alpha's relevance is broader than its own manifest. Anderson's framing: SpaceX is capacity-constrained by internal demand (Starlink, [[Direct to Cell]], orbital data center constellation deployment), so commercial and institutional launch demand needs alternative providers. Alpha, [[New Glenn]], [[Rocket Lab]] Neutron, and [[Ariane 64]] together absorb that gap.

## Lunar program — Blue Ghost

Firefly's Blue Ghost lunar lander (Mission 1, March 2025) successfully landed on the Moon — making Firefly the first US commercial company with a successful lunar landing on the first attempt. Multiple follow-on Blue Ghost missions are on contract with NASA's [[CLPS]] (Commercial Lunar Payload Services) program. The Q1 2026 NASA architecture shift toward permanent lunar surface presence (see [[Artemis]]) increases Blue Ghost's strategic relevance.
