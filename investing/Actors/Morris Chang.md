#actor #person #semiconductors

Morris Chang (張忠謀) — founder of [[TSMC]] (1987), architect of the pure-foundry business model that reshaped the semiconductor industry. By separating chip design from fabrication, Chang enabled the fabless revolution — freeing [[NVIDIA]], [[Apple]], [[AMD]], [[Broadcom]], and others to focus on design while pooling demand to justify the enormous capex of leading-edge fabs. The TSMC model is the direct counterpoint to the IDM (integrated device manufacturer) approach that [[Intel]] used and [[Elon Musk]] proposes with [[TERAFAB]].
