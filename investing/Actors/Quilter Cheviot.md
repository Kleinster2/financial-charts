**Quilter Cheviot** is the discretionary fund management arm of [[Quilter]], one of the UK's largest wealth management groups. The firm provides bespoke investment management to private clients, charities, and financial intermediaries.

## Synopsis

Quilter Cheviot manages approximately GBP 25-30B in discretionary assets, making it one of the larger DFM operations in the UK alongside [[Rathbones]], [[Brewin Dolphin]] (now part of [[RBC Capital]]), and [[Charles Stanley]]. The business model is classic UK wealth management: discretionary mandates where portfolio managers make investment decisions on behalf of clients, typically charging 0.5-1.0% of AUM annually. The client base skews wealthy (minimum account sizes around GBP 250K) and institutional (charity and trust mandates).

Within the [[Quilter]] group, Quilter Cheviot is the higher-margin, relationship-driven business that contrasts with Quilter's mass-market advised platform (Old Mutual Wealth/Quilter Investors). The DFM model generates stickier revenue than platform businesses because switching costs are higher — clients develop personal relationships with portfolio managers, and transferring bespoke portfolios is operationally complex. For investors tracking UK wealth management consolidation, Quilter Cheviot represents the type of asset that acquirers covet: recurring revenue, high retention, and a client base that grows through intergenerational wealth transfer. The UK DFM market has been consolidating steadily, with [[RBC Capital]]'s Brewin Dolphin acquisition being the highest-profile deal.
