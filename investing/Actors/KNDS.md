# KNDS

KNDS — the Franco-German land-systems group behind the Leopard 2 and Leclerc main battle tanks, formed in 2015 by merging Germany's Krauss-Maffei Wegmann (KMW) and France's Nexter. Europe's largest tank maker and a central beneficiary of [[European rearmament]] and the [[Long global rearmament]] cycle. Heading for a dual Frankfurt/Paris IPO in July 2026.

## Ownership and cap table

KNDS has no venture/funding-round history — it is a 2015 merger-of-equals between a state-owned French champion and a German family business, so its "cap table" is a sovereign/family ownership structure rather than priced financing rounds:

| Stage | French state | German state | Wegmann family | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| 2015–2026 | 50% | — | 50% | KMW (family) + Nexter (French state, ex-GIAT) merger of equals |
| Jun 2026 deal | 50% | 40% (golden share) | exiting (~10% residual routed to the IPO) | €15–18bn implied valuation; budget-committee approval due ~Jun 24 |
| Post-IPO (2–3 yrs) | ~30% | ~30% | — | Both states trim toward ~30% via the Frankfurt + Paris float; equal voting rights and security oversight retained regardless of stake size |

The €15–18bn valuation is the first market-reference price ever put on the group; before the Jun 2026 deal it had never raised external equity.

## June 2026 — German state stake + dual IPO

On Jun 22 2026 the German government announced plans to take a 40% stake in KNDS, buying out the German Wegmann family (whose planned exit opened the door) and joining the French state (50%) as a shareholder, with a German "golden share." Germany's budget committee was set to approve by Jun 24; the deal valued KNDS at €15–18bn. Both governments plan to trim to ~30% within 2–3 years of a listing while retaining equal voting rights and security oversight. The stake clears the path for KNDS's planned dual IPO in Frankfurt and Paris in July 2026 — a marquee European defense listing in the [[2026 IPO pipeline]]. The structure (state anchor shareholders + golden share + equal Franco-German governance) is industrial policy as much as capital-raising: it keeps Europe's main-battle-tank champion under joint sovereign control while opening it to public capital for the rearmament build-out.

*Source: [[Financial Times]] / Euronews / Bloomberg, "Germany to take 40% stake in Leopard tank maker KNDS" (Jun 22 2026).*
