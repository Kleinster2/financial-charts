# bwin

Austrian-founded online sports betting and gaming brand, now a subsidiary of [[Entain]] (formerly GVC Holdings). One of Europe's earliest internet gambling operators, with a corporate history that traces the consolidation arc of the entire online betting industry.

## Synopsis

bwin launched in 1997 as betandwin.com, making it one of the first online sports betting platforms in Europe. The company went public on the Vienna Stock Exchange and became the dominant European online bookmaker by the mid-2000s, sponsoring Real Madrid and AC Milan jerseys when online gambling brands were still considered disreputable by major sports leagues. In 2011, bwin merged with PartyGaming (owner of PartyPoker, itself a refugee from the US Black Friday poker crackdown) to form bwin.party digital entertainment. GVC Holdings then acquired bwin.party in 2016 for ~GBP 1.1B, absorbing the bwin brand into what would become [[Entain]].

The bwin story matters as a case study in how first-mover advantage in online gambling eroded through regulatory fragmentation and consolidation. George Daskalakis, who later co-founded [[Betano]] (now [[Entain]]'s fastest-growing brand), was bwin's country manager in Greece from 2002 -- illustrating how talent from early European operators seeded the next generation of brands. Today bwin operates as one of [[Entain]]'s portfolio brands alongside Ladbrokes, Coral, and [[Betano]], primarily serving German, Italian, and Central European markets. The brand retains recognition in European football betting but lacks the growth trajectory of newer mobile-first brands.
