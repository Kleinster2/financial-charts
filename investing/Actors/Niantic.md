**Niantic** — Creator of [[Pokémon Go]] (2016), the augmented reality mobile game that pioneered real-world digital overlays. Spun out of [[Google]]'s mapping unit in 2015. Valued at $9B in 2021. Sold its games business to [[Scopely]] for $3.5B in March 2025, pivoting to geospatial AI as "Niantic Spatial." Investors include [[Nintendo]], [[Google]], [[Coatue]], and [[IVP]].

---

## Synopsis

Niantic's trajectory is a case study in pivoting from consumer gaming to enterprise AI. The company rode [[Pokémon Go]]'s 2016 viral launch to a $9B valuation by 2021, but the augmented reality gaming market never expanded beyond that single breakout hit. The real asset turned out to be the mapping data — billions of geotagged images captured by players, forming a "next generation map" that makes the physical world machine-readable for smart glasses, robots, and autonomous systems.

The March 2025 deal splits the company cleanly. [[Scopely]] gets the games (including [[Pokémon Go]]'s 30M monthly players and $1B+ annual revenue) for $3.5B. Niantic Spatial keeps the geospatial AI platform, capitalized with a $50M [[Scopely]] investment plus €200M from its own balance sheet. Founder John Hanke leads the AI entity, positioning it as infrastructure for the spatial computing era: "Niantic is building the models that will help AI move beyond the screen and into the real world."

The $3.5B games sale versus the $9B peak valuation reflects the AR gaming discount — but the spin-off structure lets investors (including [[Nintendo]] and [[Google]]) keep exposure to both the gaming cash flows (via Scopely proceeds) and the AI upside (via Niantic Spatial equity).
