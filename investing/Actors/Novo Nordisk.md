**Novo Nordisk** — GLP-1 leader. Ozempic, Wegovy. $33B semaglutide run-rate. Stock -50% in 2025 on growth concerns; ~95% revenue concentrated in obesity/diabetes franchise.

Dominant GLP-1 franchise with ~$33B annualized semaglutide revenue across [[Ozempic]] (diabetes), [[Wegovy]] (obesity), and Rybelsus (oral). Stock fell 50% in 2025 — worst year on record — after three guidance cuts and sharp growth deceleration. [[Eli Lilly]]'s Mounjaro/Zepbound has seized 58% US GLP-1 share vs Novo's 42%; Lilly briefly crossed $1tn market cap in 2025 while Novo dropped from peak ~$650bn (2024) to a position outside Europe's top three pharma names (now trailing [[AstraZeneca]], [[Roche]], and [[Novartis]]).

The diagnosis the [[Novo Nordisk Foundation]] reached in 2025: the science (semaglutide) was world-class, but the commercial model was not. Novo had modeled Wegovy demand on its older Saxenda (liraglutide) franchise; five weeks of post-FDA-approval Wegovy prescriptions exceeded five years of Saxenda prescriptions, leaving Novo unable to supply demand and ceding US market share to compounders and to Lilly. The Foundation ousted CEO [[Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen]] in May 2025 and installed [[Mike Doustdar]] in August — the first non-Danish CEO in Novo's 102-year history. The Foundation chair simultaneously took the Novo chair seat, breaking arm's-length convention.

The pivot since: an attempt to operate like a consumer-goods company. See [[Pharma consumerization]] for the broader pattern. Board hires from [[Mars]], H&M, [[Procter & Gamble]], and [[Optum]]. Telehealth distribution partnerships with [[Hims & Hers]], [[Ro]], Weight Watchers, and LifeMD. Up-to-50% US list-price cuts effective 2027. The $5.2bn [[Akero Therapeutics]] acquisition (closed Dec 2025) signals diversification into MASH and obesity comorbidities.

## Consumerization pivot (2025–)

The strategic frame for the Doustdar era is that Novo is rebuilding itself as a consumer-goods-style operator rather than a science-led one. See [[Pharma consumerization]] for the industry pattern; Novo is the most-watched case study.

**Board and executive reshape:**

| Hire | Source | Role at Novo | Date |
|------|--------|--------------|------|
| Poul Weihrauch | CEO of [[Mars]] | Board observer; expected non-exec director 2027 | March 2026 |
| Helena Saxon | Executive at H&M | Non-exec director | 2025–2026 |
| Jamey Millar | [[Procter & Gamble]] / [[Optum]] (UnitedHealth) | Head of US operations | February 2026 |
| Hong Chow | Ex-Merck Healthcare Germany | Head of product & portfolio strategy | February 2026 |

The hires are deliberate: consumer-goods, retail, and PBM expertise rather than pharma-industry insiders.

**Channel and pricing moves:**

- Telehealth distribution partnerships with [[Hims & Hers]] (settled March 2026), [[Ro]] (preferred D2C partner), Weight Watchers, LifeMD
- In-house ecommerce platform NovoCare expanded with rolling monthly subscription pricing — deeper discounts for longer subscriber commitments
- February 2026: announced US list-price cuts of up to 50% on key drugs effective 2027 — explicitly framed as a Trump-pressure response and a volume play
- Wegovy current US cash price: ~$349/month for 2.4mg dose (vs ~£200 in the UK)

**M&A:**

- October 2025: agreed to acquire [[Akero Therapeutics]] for up to $5.2bn ($4.7bn upfront + CVR); closed December 2025. First major M&A under Doustdar; expansion into MASH (obesity comorbidity)
- Q4 2025: lost [[Metsera]] bidding war to [[Pfizer]]
- $4bn+ invested in expanded US production capacity since 2022 shortage

## Price cuts

**Initial US price reduction** to improve access and counter compounding pharmacies (announced 2024–2025; implemented across NovoCare and TrumpRx self-pay channels).

**February 2026 follow-up:** Up to **50% list-price cut** on key US drugs, effective 2027 — explicitly framed as a response to [[Trump II|Trump administration]] MFN pressure and an attempt to convert lost margin into volume. See [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] for the full pricing matrix.

### Hims & Hers distribution deal (March 2026)

Novo dropped its copyright lawsuit against [[Hims & Hers]] (filed Feb 2026 over compounded oral semaglutide) and signed a distribution partnership. Hims distributes branded Ozempic and Wegovy at Novo's self-pay prices; stops advertising compounded GLP-1s except rare clinical cases. CEO [[Mike Doustdar]] (Mar 9): *"We reserve [the right] to bring [legal proceedings] back if need be."* Oral Wegovy now has 600,000+ scripts. HIMS +41% on deal; NVO Copenhagen +2.1%.

## 2026 guidance shock (Feb 3, 2026)

Novo issued the worst guidance in a decade. Stock fell **-16.4% on the day** (Feb 4), down **~20% for the week**.

### 2026 outlook

| Metric | 2026 Guide | 2025 Actual |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Sales growth (CER) | **-5% to -13%** | +10% |
| Operating profit growth (CER) | **-5% to -13%** | Growth |
| Consensus expectation | -2% sales | — |

**First contraction in nearly a decade.** Ends years of double-digit percentage gains since Wegovy launch (June 2021).

### Drivers of decline

- **US pricing pressure:** Trump MFN drug pricing deals + "unprecedented" competitive pricing
- **Competition:** [[Eli Lilly]] Mounjaro/Zepbound gaining share rapidly (58% US GLP-1 share)
- **Patent expiries:** Semaglutide patents expiring in [[Canada]], [[China]], [[India]], [[Brazil]] in 2026
- **Compounding pharmacies:** Continued erosion of US market

### Executive departures

Two top executives leaving simultaneously:

| Departing | Role | Replacement | Background |
|-----------|------|-------------|------------|
| Dave Moore | Head of US operations | **Jamey Millar** | Ex-[[UnitedHealth Group]] |
| Ludovic Helfgott | Head of product & portfolio strategy | **Hong Chow** | Ex-Merck Healthcare ([[Germany]]) |

### CEO commentary

CEO **Mike Doustdar**: "In 2026, Novo Nordisk will face pricing headwinds in an increasingly competitive market." But "very encouraged by the promising early uptake from the U.S. launch of Wegovy pill."
