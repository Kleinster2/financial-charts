## Key positions

Mollick's work tracks the practical frontier of what AI can do in knowledge work. Core theses:

- AI is reshaping software itself, not just augmenting it — the window for humans to influence AI's trajectory is closing
- Moved from "co-intelligence" framing (human + AI collaboration) to "delegated intelligence" (AI operating autonomously)
- Recursive self-learning is the next frontier: AI systems that improve themselves and build the next model, not just the product
- Skeptical of both doom and dismissal — focuses on what's actually happening in enterprise and education

### "The Shape of the Thing" (Mar 2026)

Article arguing AI recursive self-improvement is now actively happening (not theoretical). Featured a case study of a company whose "software factory" product takes a company roadmap and lets agents build the entire application — code, testing, evaluation loop — with no human code writing or review. Core thesis: closing the evaluation loop lets AI align products for production autonomously, and the companies doing this will operate at "machine time" vs human time.
