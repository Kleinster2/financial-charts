## Why Rocket Lab acquired Motiv

Vertical integration into the on-orbit robotics layer at the moment the on-orbit servicing market is being defined by [[Golden Dome|Space-based interceptor]] contracts, the [[Orbital Reef]] commercial-station program, and DARPA's Robotic Servicing of Geosynchronous Satellites (RSGS) line. Owning the robotic arm + end-effector capability lets Rocket Lab bid full satellite-servicing or assembly-in-space packages without subcontracting to a competing prime.

Complements [[Rocket Lab]]'s existing Space Systems tuck-ins:

| Year | Acquisition | Capability |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| 2020 | Sinclair Interplanetary | Star trackers, reaction wheels |
| 2021 | Advanced Solutions Inc | Spacecraft flight software / mission ops |
| 2021 | Planetary Systems Corp | Separation systems |
| 2022 | [[SolAero]] | Space-grade solar cells |
| 2026 | Motiv Space Systems | Robotic arms, spacecraft mechanisms |

The Motiv acquisition extends the [[Space Systems]] segment's vertical integration from passive structures (solar, separation, comms) into active robotic systems — the layer needed for on-orbit servicing, satellite assembly, and Space-Based-Interceptor mission profiles.

## NASA heritage

Motiv hardware has flown on multiple [[NASA]] missions. Most notable: the sample-handling robotic system on [[Perseverance]] (Mars 2020 rover, landed February 2021). Mars-mission heritage matters in the space-systems contracting market because NASA-flight-qualified hardware unlocks classified-mission contracts that prefer flight-qualified vendors.
