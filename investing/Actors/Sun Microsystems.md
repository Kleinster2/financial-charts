**Sun Microsystems** was a computer systems and software company that created [[Java]], Solaris, SPARC, MySQL, and NFS, acquired by [[Oracle]] in January 2010 for $7.4B after peaking at ~$200B market cap during the dot-com bubble.

## Quick stats

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Founded | 1982 (Stanford University) |
| Headquarters | Santa Clara, CA |
| Peak market cap | ~$200B (2000) |
| Acquired by | [[Oracle]] (Jan 2010, $7.4B) |
| Key products | [[Java]], Solaris, SPARC, MySQL, NFS |

## Related

- [[Oracle]] — acquirer ($7.4B, 2010); Sun gave Oracle Java, MySQL, and hardware capabilities
- [[Java]] — programming language/platform created at Sun, now maintained by Oracle
- [[IBM]] — rival bidder for Sun before Oracle's acquisition
