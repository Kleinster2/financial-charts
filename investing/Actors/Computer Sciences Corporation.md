**Computer Sciences Corporation** (CSC) — Pioneer IT services company. Founded 1959. Major federal contractor. Merged with [[HPE Enterprise Services]] to form [[DXC Technology]] (2017).

## Founders

| Name | Background |
|------|------------|
| **Roy Nutt** | IBM engineer, part of team that created Fortran |
| **Fletcher Jones** | Managed computer center at North American Aviation |

Started with $100 in capital. By 1963, CSC was largest software company in the US and first software company listed on American Stock Exchange.

---

## Why CSC mattered

Pioneer of IT services industry:

- **First movers**: 1959 founding — software industry barely existed
- **Federal anchor**: Major IT provider to US government since 1961
- **Scale**: By 2016, ~70,000 employees, $7B+ revenue
- **DXC merger**: 49.9% of combined entity

---

## Federal government business

CSC was one of the largest federal IT contractors:

| Agency | Relationship |
|--------|--------------|
| NASA | Computational lab contract (1966) — early landmark deal |
| DoD | Major contracts including $186M Air Force automation |
| FBI, CIA, DHS | Law enforcement/intelligence IT services |

**2012**: Federal contracts = 36% of CSC revenue.
