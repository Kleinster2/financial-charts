# Gusto

Gusto is a US-focused SMB payroll, benefits, and HR platform serving 400,000+ businesses. Founded in 2011 as ZenPayroll by Josh Reeves, Edward Kim, and Tomer London, the company rebranded to Gusto in 2015 to reflect its expansion beyond payroll into benefits administration, workers' comp, time tracking, and hiring tools. The platform targets companies with 1-100 employees — a segment largely underserved by incumbents like [[ADP]] and Paychex, which historically focused on mid-market and enterprise.

## Business profile

Gusto's product suite covers the full SMB employer lifecycle: payroll processing, tax filing (federal/state/local), health insurance brokerage, 401(k) administration, workers' compensation, and HR compliance tools. The company also operates Gusto Wallet, an employee financial wellness app. Revenue model is subscription-based with per-employee-per-month pricing. The primary competitive moat is integration depth — once a small business runs payroll, benefits, and compliance through Gusto, switching costs are high. [[Rippling]] is the most direct competitor, pursuing a similar "all-in-one HR" strategy but targeting slightly larger companies.
