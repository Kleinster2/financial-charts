# Cairn India

## Synopsis

[[Cairn India]] is the operational identity of what is, post-May 1, 2026, the demerged [[Vedanta]] oil-and-gas vertical — currently branded as "Cairn Oil & Gas" within Vedanta and slated to list as Vedanta Oil and Gas Limited (formed by renaming the existing Malco Energy Limited / MEL shell, subject to Registrar of Companies approval). The legacy Cairn India Limited was India's largest private-sector oil and gas explorer, originally established in 2006 from the restructuring of Cairn Energy Plc into three separately listed companies, with the Rajasthan Block RJ-ON-90-1 (acquired by Cairn from Royal Dutch Shell's 1992 production-sharing contract) as its anchor asset. The 2003-2004 Mangala field discovery — 3.7 billion barrels of oil equivalent — was at the time the world's largest onshore oil discovery of 2004 and triggered Cairn India's 2007 NSE/BSE listing. [[Vedanta]] founder [[Anil Agarwal]] acquired 58.8% of Cairn India from Cairn Energy in August 2010 for $8.67 billion (closed December 2010), then took a further 18.5% via open-market purchases including a 10.4% block from Petronas at $1.5 billion. Cairn India was delisted from NSE/BSE on April 25, 2017 following its merger into Vedanta Limited, where it operated as the oil-and-gas segment until the May 2026 demerger.

The investing-vault relevance runs through three threads. First, [[Vedanta]] May 2026 demerger: the oil-and-gas vertical is one of the four spin-out entities, with Vedanta shareholders receiving 1 share of Vedanta Oil & Gas Ltd for every 1 share of Vedanta Ltd held on the May 1, 2026 record date; listing is expected within 1–2 months. Second, [[India]] energy security: Cairn Oil & Gas contributes approximately 25% of India's domestic crude oil production (the largest private-sector contributor) and management has guided to a 4x ramp to 1 million bpd via a $5B investment programme — a structural play on the [[India Energy]] import-substitution thesis at a moment when Iran-war supply risk has materialised in the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] thread. Third, post-demerger cluster picture: as a standalone listed pure-play upstream E&P, the demerged entity is the cleanest Indian comparable to Reliance KG-D6 (now a Reliance Industries segment) and ONGC, opening a new cluster validation node that did not exist while the asset traded inside the Vedanta conglomerate.

## Position in Vedanta demerger

The demerged entity inherits the Cairn Oil & Gas business unit from the pre-demerger Vedanta Limited. Per the published demerger structure, the existing shell company Malco Energy Limited (MEL) — already a Vedanta-group entity — is the receiving company and will be renamed Vedanta Oil and Gas Limited subject to Registrar of Companies approval. The 1:1 share ratio means existing VEDL shareholders receive one share of the new oil-and-gas entity for each VEDL share held on the May 1, 2026 record date, alongside the residual VEDL share and the three other demerged entities (Vedanta Aluminium Metal Ltd, Vedanta Power, Vedanta Iron and Steel Ltd).

Per the [[Vedanta]] note's demerger table, the oil-and-gas entity is positioned by [[Anil Agarwal]] (in his March 2026 Business Today interview) as having the potential to "be as big as Vedanta in terms of revenue" on a standalone basis — a claim that the 4x production ramp from ~84,000 BOEPD toward 300,000–500,000 BOEPD (and ultimately 1 million BPD) would, if realised, support over a multi-year horizon.

## Position in India energy thread

Cairn Oil & Gas's ~25% share of India's domestic oil and gas production places the demerged entity at the centre of the [[India Energy]] import-substitution thesis. India imports roughly 85% of its crude oil; any meaningful expansion of domestic upstream capacity is a sovereign-priority objective, which is why the Vedanta letter to shareholders frames the energy vertical as "expected to play a critical role in meeting India's rising energy demand, while also improving domestic output from existing reserves."

The 2026 [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] thread reinforces the strategic value of domestic upstream capacity: with Strait of Hormuz transit risk having driven Brent to elevated levels through Q1-Q2 2026, the marginal economics of Rajasthan production improve materially, and the political case for accelerating the $4–5B capex programme strengthens. Cairn has not been singled out in the vault's existing Iran-cascade analysis as a direct beneficiary, but the structural positioning is consistent with the broader thesis.
