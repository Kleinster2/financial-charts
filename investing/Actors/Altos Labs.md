#actor #biotech #longevity #healthcare #private

**Altos Labs** is a private cellular-rejuvenation biotech pursuing partial epigenetic reprogramming to restore aged cells to a younger state. Launched in 2022 with ~$3B in initial funding — the largest biotech startup launch on record, described at the time as a "Manhattan Project for longevity" — it recruited marquee academic talent on million-dollar salaries. Part of the [[Longevity]] cohort.

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Private |
| Founded | 2022 |
| Approach | Partial cellular reprogramming (Yamanaka-factor based) |
| Funding | ~$5.56B raised (early 2026); ~$6.33B estimated valuation |
| Founding scientist | Juan Carlos Izpisúa Belmonte |
| CMO / product head | Joan Mannick (appointed to signal a shift toward the clinic) |
| Clinical program | None disclosed as of April 2026 (least transparent of the reprogramming peers) |
| Backers | Yuri Milner, Jeff Bezos, others |

Altos is the best-capitalized private longevity company but among the slowest to the clinic — capital has not bought speed in aging biology, with smaller rival [[Life Biosciences]] reaching FDA-cleared first-in-human reprogramming (Jan 2026) ahead of it. The Mannick CMO hire is the clearest public signal that Altos is moving toward a disclosed therapeutic pipeline.
