# Kyrsten Sinema

**Kyrsten Sinema** is a former US senator from Arizona who served 2019-2025, switching from the Democratic Party to Independent in December 2022 and declining to seek re-election.

## Synopsis

Sinema became one of the most consequential swing votes in the 117th and 118th Congresses, single-handedly blocking or reshaping major Democratic legislative priorities. Her opposition to eliminating the filibuster, her insistence on stripping corporate tax increases from the [[Inflation Reduction Act]], and her resistance to Build Back Better's original scope gave her outsized influence on the shape of US industrial policy. The final IRA — which channeled hundreds of billions into clean energy, EV, and semiconductor incentives — bears her fingerprints in what it excluded (carried interest reform, higher corporate rates) as much as what it included.

For the investing vault, Sinema matters as the archetype of the centrist veto player whose preferences shaped the policy environment for energy transition and tech reshoring investments. Her attendance at the [[Hill and Valley Forum]] in May 2024 — a bipartisan defense-tech gathering — signaled her post-Senate positioning in the policy advisory space. Her legacy is most visible in the IRA's design: generous enough to redirect capital flows into clean energy but structured to avoid antagonizing corporate interests, creating the specific subsidy architecture that companies like [[Tesla]], [[First Solar]], and domestic battery manufacturers now depend on.
