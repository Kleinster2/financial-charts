## May 2026 hawkish hold

On May 28, 2026, the Bank of Korea held the policy rate at 2.50% but shifted the reaction function toward future hikes. The official bridge is the important part: the bank lifted its 2026 GDP forecast to 2.6% from 2.0%, because stronger semiconductor and IT exports were expected to add 0.7 percentage points to growth, more than offsetting the 0.4 percentage-point drag from the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Middle East war]] and energy shock. The supplementary budget and stock-market wealth effect added another 0.2 and 0.1 percentage points, respectively.

That makes the BoK the cleanest official confirmation of the Korea two-clock story. Energy, FX, housing, and household debt are pulling policy tighter now; [[Korea Memory]], [[HBM]], and the [[Kospi]] are extending the growth runway. The rate decision was not dovish despite the hold: inflation forecasts rose to 2.7% for headline CPI and 2.4% for core inflation, two board members preferred an immediate 25 bp hike, and the bank said future rate increases would be timed against inflation pressure, domestic recovery, exchange-rate volatility, Seoul-area housing, and household debt.

The policy read-through is that Korea's AI-memory boom is not just an equity story. It is large enough to change the central-bank growth forecast, but not enough to remove the imported-inflation problem created by the war. That is why the [[Korean won]] can look fundamentally cheap while the BoK still leans hawkish: the external surplus helps real activity, but oil, dollar strength, housing, and debt keep financial-stability risk alive.
