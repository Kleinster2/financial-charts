**Electronic Arts** — Sports gaming leader (EA Sports FC, Madden, The Sims). Subject of $55B take-private — the largest leveraged buyout ever — by a [[Saudi PIF]]/[[Silver Lake]]/[[Affinity Partners]] consortium assembled by [[Jared Kushner]]. Banks launched $18B in debt sales in March 2026, pricing at a premium to compensate for deal size and AI disruption fears in the software credit market.

---

## Synopsis

[[Electronic Arts]] sits at the intersection of two colliding forces: a record-breaking LBO that values the company at $55B, and a credit market rattled by AI disruption fears after software debt sold off in early 2026. The $18B debt package — $5.75B in leveraged loans, $9B in high-yield bonds, $3.25B in bank-held term loans — is the largest junk bond and loan offering tied to a single buyout. [[JPMorgan Chase]] is leading syndication, privately asking its largest asset management clients for $500M+ commitments each.

The deal's creditworthiness rests on three pillars: recurring revenue from live services (Ultimate Team, subscriptions), a $36B equity cushion from the consortium, and EA's exclusive sports licenses (NFL, La Liga, Premier League) that create a moat AI cannot easily replicate. Management pitched investors that AI would be a boon — reducing engineering headcount while physics-engine simulations remain beyond GenAI's reach. Skeptics point to [[Google]]'s [[Project Genie]], which demonstrated text-to-virtual-world generation, as evidence the moat is thinner than it looks.

Pricing tells the story of market nervousness: initial talk at 3.5-3.75pp above benchmark resembles B-rated credit (average 3.8pp), not the BB/Ba3 rating the loans are expected to receive (BB averages 2.4pp). Banks are offering a concession to move $18B quickly — the "de-risk now" posture reflects broader anxiety about sitting on LBO paper during the Iran conflict and AI sell-off.

### Deal genesis

[[Egon Durban]] ([[Silver Lake]] co-head) had studied an EA takeover since 2011 but never executed. [[Jared Kushner]] was the unlock — he convinced [[Saudi PIF]] to back the bid, leveraging deep ties built as top envoy during Trump's first presidency. The two began sketching out the deal in early 2025, with EA codenamed "Eagle."

[[Saudi PIF]] already held billions in EA shares before the deal. The fund's deputy governor Turqi Alnowaiser led PIF-side negotiations. EA management made several trips to the kingdom as the transaction progressed. The deal ultimately won backing from Crown Prince [[Mohammed bin Salman]] and [[JPMorgan Chase]] CEO [[Jamie Dimon]].

Person briefed on talks (Sep 2025): *"Kushner has a personal relationship and he has deep ties in Saudi Arabia. He is very comfortable operating in the Middle East. It created a basis of trust."*

### Negotiation timeline

- Early 2025: Kushner and Durban begin planning
- ~Sep 1 (Labor Day): first real bid lands — below EA's expectations but close enough to create line of sight
- EA retains [[Goldman Sachs]] and [[Wachtell Lipton]] (law firm); defense project codenamed "Project Oak"
- Mid-Sep: consortium readies knockout bid, selects [[JPMorgan Chase]] as sole arranger
- Sep 17: Durban calls Dimon. JPM commits to $20B financing package — delivered in under a week. Parallel: Dimon had backed Silver Lake and [[Michael Dell]]'s [[EMC]] acquisition a decade earlier with a $50B debt package
- Sep 29: deal announced at $210/share

### Strategic thesis

- [[Silver Lake]] believes AI will lead to an explosion of leisure time, fueling spending on sports and entertainment. Adds to Silver Lake's sports portfolio (parent of [[UFC]])
- Consortium plans additional M&A using EA as a platform — may fund acquisitions with EA's private shares or additional borrowing
- [[Saudi PIF]]'s [[Savvy Games]] unit had earmarked $38B for gaming investments. PIF also holds stakes in [[Nintendo]] and [[Take-Two]]
- AI as cost-cutting tool: reducing game production expenses via AI engineering

### Regulatory outlook

Expected smooth approval. Multiple people cited Kushner and Saudi influence on the Trump White House as de-risking regulatory clearance.

Person close to Trump administration (Sep 2025): *"What regulator is going to say no to the president's son-in-law?"*

## Debt offering (Mar 2026)

$18B total financing package to fund the take-private. Largest junk bond and loan offering tied to a single LBO.

| Tranche | Size | Pricing | Notes |
|---------|------|---------|-------|
| Leveraged loans (USD + EUR) | $5.75B | 3.5-3.75pp above benchmark, 98.5 cents | Expected BB/Ba3 rating |
| Secured high-yield bonds | Part of $9B | ~7.5% yield | Similar to loan yields |
| Unsecured high-yield bonds | Part of $9B | ~8.5% yield | Higher risk tranche |
| Bank-held term loan | $3.25B | — | Launched late Jan 2026 |

Lead arranger: [[JPMorgan Chase]] (12+ banks in syndicate). JPM privately asking largest asset management clients for $500M+ minimum commitments. JPM organized one-on-one investor meetings with CEO Andrew Wilson during its annual leveraged finance conference in Miami (early Mar 2026).

Pricing context: initial talk resembles B-rated credit (30-day average 3.8pp above benchmark per [[PitchBook]] LCD data), despite expected BB/Ba3 ratings. BB-rated companies averaged 2.4pp above benchmark in the same period. The premium compensates for deal size and AI disruption overhang.

[[Neuberger Berman]] global head of non-IG credit Joseph Lynch (Mar 18): *"Banks want to de-risk. Now we have this backlog of macro concerns, banks are saying 'let's not sit on this for another month, let's just get this off our books right now.'"*

Marina Lukatsky, PitchBook LCD global head of research (Mar 18): *"When you have a larger equity component, there's a bigger cushion to soften the blow if something happens to the company's performance in the future."*

Institutional investor (committed across both currency tranches, Mar 18): *"The initial price talk looks pretty attractive to us. We think they're pricing it at the right level for the risk that the business carries."*

Institutional investor (declined, Mar 18): *"Anything that has AI disruption risk is an absolute non-starter for us."*

### Management pitch to investors (Mar 18)

- AI would provide a boon to gaming — eventually reducing engineering workforces
- GenAI cannot replicate the physics simulations EA's gaming engines process
- Framed coexistence as Steven Spielberg vs. MrBeast — premium craft alongside AI-generated content
- Recurring revenue from game subscriptions + advertising growth potential bolsters credit case

### Market headwinds

Video game stocks under pressure as new AI models unveiled, including [[Google]]'s Project Genie debut — an AI "world model" prototype that creates video game-like virtual worlds from short text prompts. Broader software debt sell-off pushing up junk borrowing costs.

## Investment case

Bull:
- $55B takeout at $210/share (25% premium)
- EA Sports dominance + exclusive licenses (NFL, La Liga, Premier League)
- Live services recurring revenue ($2B+ OCF)
- College Football record success
- $36B equity cushion de-risks credit
- Consortium plans to use EA as M&A platform

Bear:
- Acquisition pending (limited public equity upside)
- AI disruption risk — Google Project Genie, software debt sell-off
- Debt load: $18B+ in junk-rated paper
- Mobile gaming weakness
- Competition from [[Take-Two]] (2K), [[Activision Blizzard]]
