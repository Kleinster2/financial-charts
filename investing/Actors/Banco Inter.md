## Evolution

- 1994 — Founded as Intermedium Financeira in Belo Horizonte. Part of [[MRV Engenharia]] economic group. Started with SMB credit (1999), payroll credit (2001), real estate credit (2007).
- 2008 — Received multiple bank license from [[Banco Central do Brasil]].
- 2015 — Launched first 100% digital checking account in [[Brazil]] (free, no fees). The digital pivot.
- 2017 — Rebranded from Intermedium to Banco Inter. 370K+ digital customers.
- 2018 (Apr) — IPO on B3 at R$18.50/share. Raised R$722M. First digital bank IPO in Brazil. Migrated to AWS (first LatAm bank in cloud). 1.4M+ customers by year-end.
- 2019 — [[SoftBank]] invested 8.1% stake (~R$760M), increased to 14.94%. Inter Shop marketplace launched. 4M+ customers.
- 2020 — Acquired DLM Invista (became Inter Asset). 8.5M customers.
- 2021 — Acquired Usend (US expansion). Third follow-on raised R$5.5B. 16M+ customers.
- 2022 (Jun) — Migrated from B3 to NASDAQ. BIDI11 delisted, listed as INTR. Corporate restructuring approved by 85%+ of shareholders. Launched Global Account. 24M customers.
- 2023 — FINRA license (May). Launched Inter&Co Securities (US broker). Acquired YellowFi. 30M+ customers.
- 2024 — João Vitor Menin appointed Global CEO. Launched 100% digital Consignado, Dollar Credit Card. 36M customers. Net income R$973M (3x vs FY2023).
- 2025 — Record year: R$1.3B net income (+45%), ROE crossed 15%. 43M+ clients. Credit portfolio R$48.3B (+36%, 3x Brazilian market growth).

## Business: super-app model

Banking: 100% digital free checking, savings, Pix (8.5% market share), debit/credit cards, FX
- Daily logins: 21.5M (Dec 2025). Monthly transactions: ~1B. Processing rate: 32,000/minute.

Credit: payroll loans, mortgages (+48% YoY), home equity (+35%), credit cards (+29%), consumer finance (Pix credit, BNPL), SMB credit

Investments: Inter DTVM (CVM-accredited), Inter Asset. Inter&Co Securities (US, FINRA-licensed)

Insurance: Inter Seguros. Inter Seguros-Sompo partnership (2021)

Marketplace: Inter Shop — integrated e-commerce with cashback. Cumulative GMV R$16B through Jan 2025. ~13% take rate. 3.4M active marketplace customers.

Global: Dollar account, dollar credit card, US broker. 1.5M+ global customers.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Hypergrowth: revenue +45%, net income +45%, credit portfolio +36% (3x market)
- ROE trajectory: 15.1% and climbing toward 30% target
- Super-app engagement: 21.5M daily logins, 1B monthly transactions
- Cost advantage: R$13.8/month cost-to-serve vs traditional banks at multiples of that
- 43M clients with only 58% activation — monetization runway
- Low-cost funding: 65.6% of CDI

Bear:
- 90+ day NPL rising (4.7%) — credit quality risk as portfolio grows aggressively
- 60/30/30 target requires ROE to double and efficiency ratio to improve 15pp — ambitious
- [[Nu Holdings]] at 127M clients dwarfs Inter's 43M
- [[Brazil]] macro: high [[Selic]] rate environment
- Menin family concentration — founder/family control
- Stock at $8.51 — well below NASDAQ migration levels
