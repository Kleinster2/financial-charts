## Q1 2026 earnings, EYLEA pressure, and Limerick margin issue (Apr 2026)

Regeneron sold off despite headline growth: the stock moved from $731.77 to $686.36, -6.2%, after Q1 results. The market focus was the ophthalmology decline and manufacturing-margin issue, not the Dupixent growth engine.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | YoY |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Total revenue | $3.605B | +19% |
| [[GAAP]] net income | $727M | -10% |
| [[GAAP]] EPS | $6.75 | -7% |
| Non-[[GAAP]] net income | $1.040B | +12% |
| Non-[[GAAP]] EPS | $9.47 | +15% |
| [[Dupixent]] global sales | $4.9B | +33% |
| [[EYLEA HD]] U.S. sales | $468M | +52% |
| [[EYLEA HD]] + [[EYLEA]] U.S. sales | $941M | -10% |
| [[Libtayo]] global sales | $438M | +54% |

The core tension is now explicit: [[Dupixent]] is still compounding, but the eye-care franchise has not yet stabilized. [[EYLEA HD]] grew strongly, yet combined [[EYLEA HD]] plus legacy [[EYLEA]] U.S. sales fell 10% YoY as original EYLEA volume and pricing declined.

Margins added a separate problem. [[GAAP]] gross margin on net product sales fell to 76% from 81%, mainly because of unabsorbed manufacturing costs and inventory write-offs/reserves tied to a temporary interruption at the Limerick, [[Ireland]] bulk-manufacturing facility. Initial production resumed in Q2, and management said normal production was expected by the end of Q2, with no expected product-availability impact.

Pipeline/regulatory positives partly offset the selloff: FDA approvals expanded [[Dupixent]] in young children with chronic spontaneous urticaria and allergic fungal rhinosinusitis, [[EYLEA HD]] gained dosing intervals up to 20 weeks / 5 months for wAMD and DME responders, [[Otarmeni]] was approved for genetic hearing loss, and the board authorized a new $3B buyback.

*Source: Regeneron Q1 2026 earnings release, filed as [[SEC]] 8-K earnings exhibit (Apr 29, 2026). Price move verified in local DB: Apr 28 close $731.77 → Apr 29 close $686.36.*
