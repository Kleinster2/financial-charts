# Deborah Hersman

Transportation safety expert and former NTSB chair. Joined the [[Lyft]] board in February 2026, bringing autonomous vehicle and safety governance expertise at a critical moment for the ride-hailing industry's AV transition.

---

## Synopsis

Hersman chaired the [[National Transportation Safety Board]] (NTSB) from 2009-2014, overseeing investigations into some of the decade's highest-profile transportation accidents. After the NTSB, she served as CEO of the National Safety Council (2014-2020), then moved into corporate safety advisory roles. Her appointment to the [[Lyft]] board in February 2026 is strategically significant — [[Lyft]] is positioning for autonomous vehicles to eventually replace human drivers, and having the former top US transportation safety regulator on the board provides both genuine safety governance and regulatory credibility.

The board appointment matters for [[Lyft]]'s competitive position against [[Uber]] and [[Waymo]]. As AV deployment expands, the companies that can demonstrate safety governance to regulators will get deployment approvals faster. Hersman's NTSB background gives [[Lyft]] a credibility asset that money alone cannot buy — she investigated the crashes, wrote the safety standards, and knows every federal and state regulator personally. For investors evaluating [[Lyft]]'s AV strategy (which relies on partnerships with AV developers rather than building in-house), Hersman's presence signals that the company is serious about the transition, not just talking about it in earnings calls.
