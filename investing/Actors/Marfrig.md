**Marfrig** is the Brazilian beef major that, since September 2025, has been the controlling half of MBRF Global Foods — the food company formed by combining Marfrig with BRF. It trades on B3 as `MBRF3` (legacy `MRFG3`) and is one of the largest food producers in the world, with roughly R$160B (~$30B) in revenue.

Founded and chaired by Marcos Molina (control through MMS Participações), Marfrig built a global beef franchise that includes about 80% of US-based National Beef, then took majority control of BRF (pork and poultry) in 2022. On 22 September 2025 the two merged into MBRF Global Foods; BRF became a wholly-owned subsidiary and its holders received Marfrig shares (0.8521 ADS per BRF ADS). Miguel Gularte is global CEO, reporting to chairman Molina; the company targets roughly R$1B/year in synergies and has signaled an eventual NYSE listing, mirroring [[JBS]].

For portfolio purposes the source reference was credit, not equity: Marfrig appeared as a "Marfrig CRA" — an agribusiness-receivables certificate — inside a Brazilian fixed-income sleeve. See [[Brazilian fixed income]].
