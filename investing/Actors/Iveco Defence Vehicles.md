**Iveco Defence Vehicles** — Italian armored-vehicle and military-truck manufacturer, subsidiary of [[Iveco Group]] (formerly part of CNH Industrial, spun off January 2022). Builds the Centauro armored vehicle family, Lince light armored vehicles, military trucks, and the VBTP-MR Guarani 6x6 amphibious armored personnel carrier developed jointly with the [[Brazilian Army]]. The principal foreign land-systems anchor in the [[Brazilian defense industrial base]], operating a Brazilian production plant at Sete Lagoas, Minas Gerais, since the 2009 Guarani contract.

---

## Synopsis

Iveco Defence Vehicles is the third-pillar foreign anchor in the Brazilian defense industrial base, complementing [[Airbus]] / [[Helibras]] in helicopters, [[Naval Group]] in submarines, and [[thyssenkrupp Marine Systems]] in frigates. Its [[VBTP-MR Guarani]] contract — signed December 2009 for 2,044 vehicles over an 18-year period (R$6B / ~$3.37B) — is the largest single Brazilian Army land-systems contract and the structural reason Iveco operates a Brazilian armored-vehicle assembly line at Sete Lagoas, Minas Gerais. The program has now produced export units (Philippine Army received its first Guarani in 2025), making Brazil a Latin American hub for medium-class armored vehicle production.

The ownership chain is worth attention because it has shifted recently: IDV was part of [[CNH Industrial]] through 2021, became part of [[Iveco Group]] when that entity was spun off in January 2022, and is now in scope for further transition as [[Tata Group]] (India) is in the process of acquiring Iveco Group. The implications for the Brazilian defense industrial base depend on what Tata does with the defense unit — the Italian government is expected to require carve-out protections for sensitive defense assets, and the Brazilian operation could be subject to similar treatment. Iveco's Sete Lagoas plant is not at imminent risk regardless because of contractual continuity on Guarani deliveries through 2030.

## Brazilian program — VBTP-MR Guarani

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Vehicle | 6x6 amphibious wheeled APC; 14.5t empty, 17.7t GVW |
| Crew + dismounts | 3 + 8 |
| Top speed | >90 km/h on road |
| Co-developer | [[Brazilian Army]] |
| Lead contractor | Iveco Defence Vehicles (Sete Lagoas plant) |
| Contract date | December 2009 |
| Contract value | R$6B (~$3.37B) |
| Contract period | 2012 — 2030 |
| Total units | 2,044 |
| First foreign customer | Philippines (first delivered 2025) |

The VBTP-MR Guarani is the modernization successor to the EE-11 Urutu APC, designed to replace the entire Urutu fleet under the Brazilian Army's "Urutu-III" modernization program. The vehicle's amphibious capability, modern protection, and modular weapons-station options have made it competitive in Latin American export markets despite the long Brazilian Army production-priority queue.

The Iveco-BAE Systems Joint Venture supplied the AAV-replacement ACV-1.1 amphibious combat vehicle to the US Marine Corps — the SuperAV platform is the basis. This US JV is operationally separate from the Brazilian operation but reflects the same industrial template (foreign prime + local industrial partner).
