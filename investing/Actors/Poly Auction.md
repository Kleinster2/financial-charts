# Poly Auction

Second-largest Mainland China auction house ($397M in 2025, +20%). Subsidiary of **China Poly Group**, a state-owned conglomerate with interests in defense, real estate, and culture. Rebounded 20% in 2025 after a -67% collapse in 2024, but still far below its $1.4B peak (2019). Online sales doubled to 20% of total. The SOE parentage makes Poly a window into Chinese state cultural policy and the intersection of state capital with the art market.

## Ownership

**China Poly Group Corporation** — one of China's largest state-owned enterprises, ranked among the Fortune Global 500. Core businesses:

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Defense/military | Arms trading, defense technology |
| Real estate | One of China's largest property developers |
| Culture/arts | Poly Auction, Poly Art Museum, Poly Theatre |
| Energy | Mining, resources |

The cultural arm (including Poly Auction) is a prestige/soft-power vehicle for the Chinese state. Poly Art Museum holds significant Chinese antiquities, some repatriated from overseas — aligning with China's cultural patrimony agenda.

## Investing relevance

- **No direct exposure** — subsidiary of SOE conglomerate
- **SOE cultural policy proxy**: Poly's operations reflect Chinese state priorities around cultural patrimony, art repatriation, and soft power
- **China property linkage**: Parent China Poly Group is a major property developer — Poly Auction's revenue decline mirrors the broader China property crisis and consumer confidence deterioration
- **Settlement risk applies**: Mainland China auction settlement rates = 42% for high-value lots (systemic issue, not Poly-specific)
- **Online pivot**: 20% online share (doubled) — most aggressive digital adoption among major Chinese houses
