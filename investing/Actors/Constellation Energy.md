## PJM BYOG risk (Jan 2026)

PJM's "Bring Your Own Generation" proposal threatens incumbent generators:

| Proposal | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Expedited interconnection | Only for *new* capacity that offsets large loads |
| Curtailment risk | New large loads without matching generation can be switched off |
| Capacity auction reform | June deadline to study alternatives |

Translation: Era of hyperscalers buying existing nuclear capacity at premium prices may be ending. PJM wants Big Tech to bring new power, not commandeer the existing grid.

Market reaction: Constellation shares fell sharply on Jan 17 alongside [[Vistra]] and [[Talen Energy]] when PJM released these proposals.

### Data center pipeline: no new announcements

The key disappointment. CEO acknowledged major deals with tech companies are still expected but none ready to announce — regulatory pressure and scrutiny around data center development making large-scale projects more complex to finalize. Existing pipeline: [[Microsoft]] TMI, [[Meta]] Louisiana, 5,650+ MW of long-term clean energy agreements locked in across nuclear, geothermal, storage, and DC-related deals.

This reinforces the [[BYOP|BYOG]] risk from PJM (Jan 2026): the era of easy hyperscaler nuclear PPAs may be slowing as regulators intervene.

### Nuclear fleet update

Fleet producing 180M+ MWh annually at capacity factors ~4% above industry benchmarks — generating 8M+ MWh of excess output (enough for ~6.6M homes). 22,000+ MW nuclear capacity. Diversifying fuel sourcing via long-term agreements extending into 2030s.
