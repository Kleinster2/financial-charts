**CRU** (Commission for Regulation of Utilities) is Ireland's independent energy and water regulator, responsible for licensing and overseeing electricity generation, transmission, and distribution.

## Quick stats

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Full name | Commission for Regulation of Utilities |

| Jurisdiction | [[Ireland]] |
| Regulates | Electricity, gas, water |

The CRU imposed an effective moratorium on new data centre connections in 2021, then lifted it in December 2025 with conditions requiring 80% of DC power from additional Irish renewable generation within six years. Flagged ~5.8GW of additional DC demand capacity needed medium-term.
