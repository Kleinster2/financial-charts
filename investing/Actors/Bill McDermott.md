## Origin story

Born to working-class parents in Long Island — father was a cable maintainer. At 16, bought and operated a local delicatessen (1978). Early entrepreneurial grit became a signature part of his leadership narrative.

---

## SAP era (2002-2019)

Joined SAP America as CEO in 2002 when the company was struggling in the US market. Rose through ranks:
- 2008: Executive Board
- 2010: Co-CEO (with Jim Hagemann Snabe)
- 2014: First American to become sole CEO of [[SAP]]

**Results:** Market cap $39B → $163B during tenure. Made SAP competitive with [[Oracle]] and [[Salesforce]] in cloud.

**Compensation:** In 2016, highest-paid DAX executive at EUR11.6M. Named "Manager of the Year" by Handelsblatt.

Departed October 2019 after nearly two decades.

---

## ServiceNow era (2019-present)

Joined November 2019 as President & CEO. Became Chairman October 2022.

**Results:**
- Revenue nearly tripled (FY2019 ~$3.5B → FY2025 $13.2B)
- Positioned company as [[AI agents]] leader
- Aggressive M&A: [[Moveworks]] ($2.85B), [[Armis]] ($7.75B), [[Veza]] (>$1B)

**Strategy:** "Platform of platforms" — expanding ServiceNow beyond IT service management into HR, customer service, security, and now agentic AI.

**Compensation:** In 2021, 3rd highest-paid S&P 500 CEO at $165M+ (mostly equity).
