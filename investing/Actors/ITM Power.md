# ITM Power

ITM Power is a [[United Kingdom|UK]]-listed PEM [[Electrolyzers|electrolyzer]] manufacturer, founded in 2000 as a spinout from Loughborough University. Listed on [[AIM]] (London's growth-company market, ticker ITM.L). One of the earliest pure-play PEM electrolyzer specialists globally. The company's trajectory from peak 2021 valuations (£3B+ market cap) to multiple restructurings through 2024-2026 is one of the clearest case studies of Western hydrogen-equipment stress under the [[Second China shock]].

## Why ITM matters

ITM specialized in [[PEM]] electrolyzers — the technology family better suited to ramp with variable renewable electricity (wind, solar) than alkaline. That positioning looked correct in the 2020-2022 hydrogen-capex boom when PEM was expected to dominate green-hydrogen buildouts. The reality has been more complicated: alkaline stayed cost-competitive longer than expected, Chinese alkaline scaled faster than Western PEM, and PEM's own cost curve depended on scarce iridium catalyst that did not benefit from the same scale economics as alkaline.

The company has gone through multiple restructurings:
- 2022: founder/CEO Graham Cooley departed after factory delivery delays
- 2023: new CEO Dennis Schulz (ex-[[Linde]]) appointed with turnaround mandate
- 2024: further headcount cuts, geographic scope narrowing
- 2025: backlog rebuild progress but margin recovery slower than planned
- 2026: still contesting competitive positioning against [[Siemens Energy]] Silyzer and Chinese incumbents at the large-project tender stage

---

## Positioning in the second-shock frame

ITM is caught between two structural pressures:

1. **Within the West**, [[Siemens Energy]]'s Silyzer unit has the advantage of embedding in a larger grid-equipment business that can cross-subsidize — ITM has no such parent. Cummins Accelera and [[Plug Power]] similarly draw on parent balance sheets ITM cannot match.

2. Against Chinese imports, the alkaline-PEM competition ITM's technology stack was designed around has tilted back toward alkaline on price — which ITM does not make. Chinese PEM, where it exists (small), is less mature than Chinese alkaline, which gives ITM a narrow technology window but not a cost shield.

The company's best surviving commercial path appears to be large-project PEM tenders where technical differentiation matters (grid-balancing applications, nuclear-adjacent projects via [[EDF]] partnership, specific defense/aerospace applications). Volume commodity markets are structurally lost to Chinese alkaline competitors.
