# Ithaca Holdings

Holding company founded by [[Ravi Gupta]] ([[Sequoia Capital]] partner) and [[John Hegeman]] (ex-[[Meta]] CRO) to acquire and transform businesses using frontier AI. Raising $1B+ as of March 2026.

---

## Synopsis

**Ithaca Holdings** is a new holding company raising at least $1 billion to acquire one or more public or private companies and infuse them with frontier technology, particularly AI. The pitch is explicitly Berkshire Hathaway-style: buy enduring businesses, then use AI to drive step-function improvements in their operations and products. The founding team combines Gupta's decade at [[KKR]], operator experience as [[Instacart]] COO/CFO, and growth investing at Sequoia with Hegeman's 17 years building Meta's $160B+ ad revenue engine. The thesis is that most incumbent businesses haven't yet been transformed by AI — and the right operator-investors can acquire them cheaply relative to their post-AI potential. No acquisitions announced yet.

## Strategy

Ithaca's stated mission: "use frontier technology to build products that meaningfully improve people's lives." The model is to identify market-leading technology businesses — public or private — acquire them, and apply AI to transform operations, products, or both.

This sits at the intersection of two trends:
1. **AI-native PE/holdco** — the bet that AI doesn't just create new companies but can dramatically improve existing ones through operational transformation
2. **Operator-led holding companies** — Gupta and Hegeman are both operators, not pure financiers. The pitch is that they'll run the businesses, not just own them

The Berkshire comparison is directional, not structural — Buffett buys cash-generative businesses and lets them run. Ithaca plans to actively intervene with technology. Closer comparisons might be Constellation Software (acquire and hold vertical SaaS) or 3G Capital (acquire and transform through operational playbooks) — but with AI as the transformation lever instead of zero-based budgeting.

## Insights

- The $1B+ raise for a holding company with no target yet announced signals significant LP appetite for the "AI transformation" thesis — investors are backing the team and the idea, not a specific deal
- Gupta leaving Sequoia (one of the most valuable seats in venture capital) for this suggests he sees more upside in operating/owning businesses than in funding them
- Hegeman's departure from Meta came amid the company's aggressive AI pivot — he likely has deep conviction about AI's practical applications from watching Meta's own transformation
- The holding company structure (vs. a fund) means permanent capital with no return timeline — a deliberate choice to avoid the PE pressure to exit within 5-7 years
- Name overlap with Scooter Braun's Ithaca Holdings (music, acquired by [[HYBE]] for $1.05B in 2021) is coincidental — completely unrelated entities
