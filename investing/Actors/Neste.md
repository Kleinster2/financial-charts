## Why Neste matters

Neste is the global benchmark for [[Sustainable Aviation Fuel|SAF]] production at scale. While most refiners are still piloting SAF, Neste has been producing renewable fuels commercially for over a decade. Their HEFA (hydroprocessed esters and fatty acids) pathway uses waste and residue feedstocks — used cooking oil, animal fat waste, and other residues. Refineries in Porvoo (Finland), Rotterdam (Netherlands), and Singapore give global coverage.

The company has invested nearly $10B transforming from a traditional oil refiner into a renewable fuels producer. That includes a €2.5B expansion at its Rotterdam refinery, expected to lift capacity by 60%+ once fully expanded in 2028.

## China SAF competition warning (FT, Mar 15, 2026)

[[Heikki Malinen]] warned the EU [[Sustainable Aviation Fuel|SAF]] market risks a price war with Chinese producers, drawing a direct parallel with the destruction of the European steel industry by Chinese overcapacity.

[[Heikki Malinen|Malinen]] (Mar 15): *"The dynamics of the European steel industry have been severely impacted negatively because of oversupply coming from China. We really hope that this will not happen to SAF."*

Small but growing volumes of Chinese SAF have begun entering the European market. [[Argus Media]] analyst Aidan Lea confirmed Chinese exports increased since Q4 2025 and are likely to continue rising through 2026. Asian producers are increasing output while European margins are under pressure from oversupply and weak demand.

Malinen called it "critical" for Neste to ensure a "level playing field" given ~$10B invested in the transformation. He argued the EU should consider anti-dumping duties on SAF imports — the bloc already imposes tariffs on similar products.

Malinen (Mar 15): *"It would be a very bad message [from the EU], you invest €2.5bn [in Rotterdam and] the mandates were supposed to come, then the mandates don't come, and then on top of that, you have imports, which are not level."*

The threat is compounded by industry pushback against the EU's ReFuelEU mandates. [[TotalEnergies]] CEO [[Patrick Pouyanné]] said in January 2026 he expected the 2% blending rule to be watered down, drawing parallels with revisions to EU car emissions policy.

## Strategic positioning

Despite stronger margins for renewable diesel from heavy-duty transport, Neste will not significantly divert production away from SAF — maintaining its position as a "reliable supplier" to airlines.

[[Heikki Malinen|Malinen]] (Mar 15): *"We need to take care of our core customers. Even though other markets may be more lucrative, short term, we look at this long term . . . We're in this for the long haul."*

SAF and conventional [[Jet fuel]] prices rose following the US/Israeli attack on [[Iran]] and disruption in the Strait of Hormuz. However, SAF feedstock is not reliant on the Middle East and the product is unlikely to benefit from the crisis given its persistent cost premium (2-4x conventional).
