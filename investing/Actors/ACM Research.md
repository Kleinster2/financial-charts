American semiconductor equipment company — the leading Chinese domestic supplier of single-wafer wet cleaning tools. Founded by David Wang in Fremont, California in 1998, but the overwhelming majority of operations and revenue flow through its Shanghai subsidiary, ACM Research (Shanghai), which is separately listed on the SSE STAR Market (688082).

ACM sits at the intersection of three dynamics that determine its value: the China [[WFE]] localization wave (beneficiary), U.S. [[export controls]] (threat via Entity List), and the technology transition from cleaning-only to a broader front-end and advanced packaging portfolio (growth optionality). Record FY2025 revenue of $901M (+15% YoY) and 2026 guidance of $1.08-1.175B (+21-30%) reflect strong execution in a market where China's 50% domestic equipment mandate is a structural tailwind. But gross margins compressed to 44.4% from 50.1%, partly from competitive pricing pressure in semi-critical products — the cost of winning volume in a crowded domestic market.

The dual-listing structure is both asset and liability. ACM Shanghai's STAR Market listing gave the parent access to ~$623M via a private share offering (Sep 2025), and the parent has been monetizing — selling 1.3% of its ACM Shanghai stake for $111M in Feb 2026. The Entity List placement (Dec 2024) hit the Shanghai subsidiary and its Chinese/Korean sub-entities, but not the U.S. parent directly. The legal separation is load-bearing: it's what allows ACM to maintain U.S. operations (new Oregon facility, expected 2H2026) and pursue non-China customers like [[Intel]] (tested ACM wet etch tools for 14A process, Dec 2025) and Singapore foundries. Kerrisdale Capital went long in Jan 2025 at ~$1.2B market cap calling it a potential "10-bagger" — stock has since roughly tripled to ~$2.7-3.9B.

Open questions: Can ACM diversify revenue meaningfully outside China before export controls tighten further? The HFCA Act delisting risk lingers (auditor was switched from BDO China to Armanino to mitigate). The $4B long-term revenue target requires both geographic expansion and product diversification into furnace, PECVD, track, and panel-level packaging — all competitive markets where ACM is the challenger. At SEMICON China 2026, Wang argued that future AI gains depend on semiconductor equipment that hasn't been invented yet — a self-serving framing from an equipment CEO, but directionally correct: the next node transitions require new process tools, and ACM is building optionality across several of them.

## Core business

ACM's bread and butter is **single-wafer wet cleaning** — the most repeated process step in semiconductor manufacturing (up to 200+ cleans per wafer). Cleaning directly impacts yield, and ACM's proprietary technologies give it a niche:

| Technology | What it does |
|------------|-------------|
| **SAPS** (Space Alternated Phase Shift) | Megasonic cleaning with alternating phase patterns — better random defect removal than jet spray |
| **TEBO** (Timely Energized Bubble Oscillation) | Controlled bubble oscillation for damage-free cleaning at sub-7nm |
| **Tahoe** | Next-gen front-end cleaning platform — major performance breakthrough announced 2024 |
| **Ultra C** | Single-wafer cleaning tool family |

## Competitive position

### In China (dominant)

ACM is the #1 domestic cleaning equipment vendor. China's 50% domestic equipment mandate (Dec 2025) is a structural tailwind — fabs building new capacity must source at least half their equipment domestically. In cleaning, ACM has the technology lead over other domestic players.

### Globally (niche challenger)

| Segment | Global leaders | ACM's position |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| Cleaning | [[Lam Research]], [[Tokyo Electron]], SCREEN | Distant #4-5, but gaining with differentiated tech |
| ECP/Plating | Lam Research, Tokyo Electron | Niche in advanced packaging |
| Furnace | Tokyo Electron, Kokusai Electric | New entrant |
| PECVD | [[Applied Materials]], Lam Research | Early stage |

The $21B addressable WFE market is large, but cleaning is ACM's only segment with proven competitiveness outside China.

## Structure and regulatory risk

The corporate structure is unusual and load-bearing:

```
ACM Research, Inc. (USA, NASDAQ: ACMR)
  └── 73.7% → ACM Research (Shanghai) (China, SSE STAR: 688082)
        ├── ACM Research (Wuxi)
        ├── ACM Shengwei
        ├── ACM Beijing
        └── CleanChip
              ├── ACM California
              └── ACM Korea
```

**Entity List (Dec 2024):** ACM Shanghai and its Chinese/Korean subsidiaries were added to the BIS Entity List. The U.S. parent was explicitly *not* listed. This creates a firewall: U.S. operations can serve non-China customers, while the Shanghai sub navigates export restrictions for its China business. The Oregon R&D/production facility (opening 2H2026) is the physical embodiment of this strategy.

**HFCA Act risk:** ACM was named by the SEC in Mar 2022 as facing potential delisting. Mitigated by switching auditors from BDO China Shu Lun Pan (Shenzhen) to Armanino (San Ramon, CA). But the underlying risk — a U.S.-listed company with 90%+ China revenue — remains if U.S.-China tensions escalate.

**Intel controversy (Dec 2025):** Reuters reported Intel tested ACM wet etch tools for its 14A process. ACM denied the specific allegations. The episode highlights the tension: ACM's technology is good enough for leading-edge evaluation, but the Entity List creates procurement friction.

## Evolution

The story of ACM Research is the story of a Silicon Valley startup that became China's semiconductor equipment champion — by design rather than accident.

**1998:** David Wang founds ACM in Fremont, CA. Wang has a PhD in Precision Engineering from Osaka University and BS from Tsinghua — a classic "sea turtle" (Chinese returnee) profile. Initial focus was stress-free copper polishing (CMP alternative). Found no traction.

**2005-2006:** Pivots to wafer cleaning. Forms joint venture with Shanghai Venture Capital Co. to create ACM Research (Shanghai). The JV structure — U.S. parent, China operations — would become the company's defining feature and greatest source of both opportunity and risk.

**2013:** First major customer win: [[SK Hynix]] begins ordering SAPS cleaning tools. The validation from a top-3 memory maker legitimizes the technology.

**2017:** IPO on NASDAQ (Nov 2017). Raises capital for expansion. Same month, forms ACM Research Korea to serve SK Hynix locally.

**2021:** ACM Shanghai lists on the SSE STAR Market (Nov 2021), creating the dual-listing structure that would later prove critical for fundraising. The STAR listing gave ACM access to China's domestic capital markets at China-tech multiples.

**2022:** SEC names ACMR under HFCA Act delisting threat (Mar 2022). Stock drops 27%. ACM switches auditors to a U.S.-based firm, resolving the immediate issue. Plans Korea equipment factory (Apr 2022) — hedging against supply chain nationalism.

**2024:** Revenue hits $782M. Company crosses into profitability scale. Dec 2024: BIS places ACM Shanghai on Entity List as part of tightened export controls. Parent entity explicitly excluded.

**2025:** Record $901M revenue but margin pressure. ACM Shanghai raises $623M via STAR Market private offering (Sep). Ultra C Tahoe breakthrough. First deliveries to Singapore. Intel wet etch testing reported (Dec). Kerrisdale Capital publishes long thesis calling ACMR a potential 10-bagger at $1.2B market cap.

**2026:** Feb — reports Q4 2025, reaffirms $1.08-1.175B guidance. Sells 1.3% of Shanghai stake for $111M. Oregon facility opening expected 2H. Mar — Wang speaks at [[SEMICON China 2026]], argues future AI progress depends on semiconductor equipment not yet invented.
