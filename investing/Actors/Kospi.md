# Kospi

[[South Korea]]'s headline stock index — the Korea Composite Stock Price Index, compiled by the Korea Exchange (KRX) and covering all common stocks traded on the KRX main market. Heavy tech and industrials weighting, dominated by [[Samsung]], [[SK Hynix]], [[Hyundai]], and [[LG Energy Solution]]. The USD-accessible proxy is [[EWY]].

## Synopsis

Kospi sits at the intersection of the global semiconductor cycle, AI memory demand, and the Korean export economy. Its composition makes it a leveraged play on DRAM/HBM pricing via Samsung and SK Hynix, which together drive a disproportionate share of index moves. That concentration also makes it vulnerable: when memory pricing corrects or foreign flows reverse, Kospi tends to amplify the move. Kospi was the world's best-performing major index in early 2026, up roughly 45% YTD through Feb 27 on AI memory enthusiasm and the "diversification away from US" flow trade. Valuations stretched to above 20x trailing earnings — the highest since the Covid-era liquidity peak.

The Iran strike exposed the crowding. Kospi fell roughly 19% in the week after the Feb 27 US-Israel strikes on Iran, including a record -12% single-session drop on Mar 4, 2026 — the worst one-day move in the index's history. The mechanism was position squaring: the most successful trade of the year became the most forced-to-unwind trade, compounded by [[South Korea]]'s status as the world's eighth-largest oil importer (direct terms-of-trade hit from higher crude) and the broader "sell the winners" de-risking sweeping Asian markets. See [[2026 Iran conflict market impact]].

## April 2026: war discount fully retraced

By Apr 20, the market had effectively unwound the Iran-war drawdown and returned to trading as a leveraged expression of the global AI memory cycle rather than as a pure energy-import casualty. Leadership reverted to [[Samsung]] and [[SK Hynix]], while [[Goldman Sachs]] raised its 12-month [[Kospi]] target to 8,000 from 7,000 and argued that 2026 earnings could grow 220% with the index still trading around 7.5x forward earnings.

The distinction is between **shock sensitivity** and structural identity. The March selloff showed how violently Kospi can de-rate when oil spikes and crowded foreign positions unwind. The April recovery showed the underlying earnings engine was still semiconductors, industrials, and AI-linked capex. In other words, the Iran shock interrupted the Korea trade, but it did not redefine it.

## May 2026: record high despite war drag

[[Reuters]]' May 27 cross-asset graphic reported [[Kospi]] at a record high three months into the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]]. That is the strongest possible confirmation of the April read: Korea remains an energy importer and therefore vulnerable to the Hormuz shock, but the index's marginal driver is still AI memory. [[SK Hynix]] topping $1T in market value the same day turns the index-level move into a constituent-level signal.

The useful framing is not "war no longer matters." It is "war is no longer the dominant clock for Korea equities." Oil, FX, and bonds are pricing the 3-6 month supply shock; Kospi is again pricing the 1-3 year AI capex and HBM earnings cycle.

The [[Bank of Korea]] made that market read official on May 28. Its 2026 forecast bridge assigned +0.1 percentage points of GDP to the stock-market boom and +0.7 percentage points to stronger semiconductor and IT exports, versus a -0.4 percentage-point drag from the Middle East war. The index is no longer only reflecting expected earnings; it is feeding back into domestic demand and investment through the wealth-effect channel the central bank now includes in the growth forecast.
