# Kospi

[[South Korea]]'s headline stock index — the Korea Composite Stock Price Index, compiled by the [[Korea Exchange]] (KRX) and covering all common stocks traded on the KRX main market. Heavy tech and industrials weighting, dominated by [[Samsung Electronics]], [[SK Hynix]], [[Hyundai Motor]], and [[LG Energy Solution]]. The USD-accessible proxy is [[EWY]].

## Synopsis

Kospi sits at the intersection of the global semiconductor cycle, AI memory demand, and the Korean export economy. Its composition makes it a leveraged play on DRAM/HBM pricing via Samsung and SK Hynix, which together drive a disproportionate share of index moves. That concentration also makes it vulnerable: when memory pricing corrects or foreign flows reverse, Kospi tends to amplify the move. Kospi was the world's best-performing major index in early 2026, up roughly 45% YTD through Feb 27 on AI memory enthusiasm and the "diversification away from US" flow trade. Valuations stretched to above 20x trailing earnings — the highest since the Covid-era liquidity peak.

The Iran strike exposed the crowding. Kospi fell roughly 19% in the week after the Feb 27 US-Israel strikes on Iran, including a record -12% single-session drop on Mar 4, 2026 — the worst one-day move in the index's history. The mechanism was position squaring: the most successful trade of the year became the most forced-to-unwind trade, compounded by [[South Korea]]'s status as the world's eighth-largest oil importer (direct terms-of-trade hit from higher crude) and the broader "sell the winners" de-risking sweeping Asian markets. See [[2026 Iran conflict market impact#Global equity dispersion — popular trades unwind (FT, Mar 4)]].
