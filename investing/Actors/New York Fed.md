# New York Fed

Federal Reserve Bank of New York. The most operationally important of the 12 [[Federal Reserve]] district banks because it is the institution that actually executes monetary policy in markets — open market operations, the discount window, the standing repo facility, and the [[ON RRP]] facility all sit at the New York desk. President sits as a permanent voting member of the [[FOMC]] (one of two permanent voting seats; the other is the Fed Chair). Current president: [[John Williams]].

## Why it matters

Three operational responsibilities make the NY Fed a market-data source even outside its policy role:

- Effective Fed Funds rate publication. Each morning the NY Fed publishes the prior business day's volume-weighted median of overnight unsecured interbank lending. This is the rate that [[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)|ZQ futures]] settle to.
- SOFR publication. Each morning the NY Fed publishes the prior business day's volume-weighted median of overnight Treasury repo trades. This is the rate that [[SOFR futures (SR3)|SR3 futures]] settle to, and the replacement for LIBOR in most US dollar contracts.
- Foreign custody account. The NY Fed holds [[Treasuries]] on behalf of foreign central banks. Weekly reports of changes to the custody account are watched as a proxy for foreign sovereign Treasury demand. See [[NY Fed custody holdings]].
