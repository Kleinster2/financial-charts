**Social Capital** — Venture capital firm co-founded by [[Chamath Palihapitiya]] and [[Mamoon Hamid]] in 2011. Early investor in [[Slack]], [[Intercom]], and Netskope. Evolved from traditional VC to a more technology-driven, concentrated investment approach under Palihapitiya after most partners departed.

---

## Synopsis

Social Capital launched in 2011 as a conventional Silicon Valley venture firm with a strong initial roster: Palihapitiya's Facebook pedigree attracted co-founder [[Mamoon Hamid]] and a team that sourced early bets in [[Slack]] (Series A), [[Intercom]] (Series A lead), and enterprise security company Netskope. The [[Slack]] investment alone — which exited at $27.7B when [[Salesforce]] acquired it in 2021 — validated the firm's early-stage enterprise thesis. But the partnership did not hold. Hamid left for [[Kleiner Perkins]] in 2017, and several other partners followed, leaving Palihapitiya as the dominant figure.

The post-exodus Social Capital became something different: less a traditional multi-GP fund and more a vehicle for Palihapitiya's personal brand and market views. He returned outside capital to LPs around 2018, converting to a family office / technology holding company model. The SPAC era (2020-2021) became his primary deal vehicle — he launched multiple SPACs including the [[Virgin Galactic]] merger that briefly made him a billionaire. The SPAC implosion and subsequent regulatory tightening damaged his credibility with institutional allocators, but Social Capital continues to operate as a technology-focused investment platform. For the vault, it is primarily relevant as a historical investor in enterprise software names and as a case study in how VC brand equity can be both created and destroyed by a single personality.
