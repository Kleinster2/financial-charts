# Miguel McKelvey

American architect and co-founder of [[WeWork]] with [[Adam Neumann]]. McKelvey met Neumann at a Tribeca rooftop party around 2005 and tipped him off about affordable commercial space in Dumbo, Brooklyn. They co-founded GreenDesk in 2008 — an eco-friendly coworking space — then sold it and launched WeWork in 2010. McKelvey served as chief culture officer and oversaw design and workplace experience, while Neumann handled fundraising and expansion. He was the quieter co-founder, largely absent from the media circus that surrounded WeWork's failed 2019 IPO and Neumann's ouster. McKelvey departed WeWork in 2020, before the company's eventual SPAC listing (2021) and bankruptcy (2023). His architecture background informed WeWork's distinctive office aesthetic — the exposed brick, communal kitchens, and "millennial-corporate" design language that became ubiquitous in coworking.
