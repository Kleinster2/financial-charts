**NEOM** — $500B Saudi mega-project in northwest [[Saudi Arabia]], wholly owned by [[Saudi PIF]]. Originally marketed around The Line smart-city vision, now being reprioritized around [[Oxagon]], industrial/logistics infrastructure, data centers, and phased tourism assets as PIF moves into its 2026-2030 value-realization strategy.

## The Line

170km linear city concept:
- 500m tall mirrored walls
- 200m wide
- 9 million residents (target)
- No cars, no streets
- AI-managed infrastructure

The Line remains the symbolic centerpiece, but it is no longer the must-have 2030 deliverable. Governor [[Yasir Al-Rumayyan]]'s Apr 2026 comments treated The Line as desirable but less urgent than the industrial/logistics assets that make NEOM economically useful sooner.

## 2026 strategy reset

The Apr 2026 [[Saudi PIF]] strategy keeps NEOM as one of six domestic ecosystems, but it changes the internal priority order. The investment logic is now less "build the future city by 2030" and more "extract economic utility from the parts of NEOM that support industrials, logistics, clean energy, data centers, and tourism."

| NEOM component | Reset implication |
|----------------|-------------------|
| [[Oxagon]] | Prioritized because it maps to industrials/logistics and can support trade, manufacturing, and data-center infrastructure |
| The Line | Still under development, but no longer treated as essential by 2030 |
| [[Trojena]] | Prestige tourism timeline weakened after the 2029 Asian Winter Games postponement |
| Data centers | More important as [[Saudi Vision 2030]] pivots from utopian urban design to sovereign AI infrastructure |

The FT's Apr 15 strategy piece and Al-Rumayyan's Al Arabiya comments both point to the same discipline: "must-have" NEOM infrastructure is protected; "good-to-have" architecture and prestige tourism gets phased.
