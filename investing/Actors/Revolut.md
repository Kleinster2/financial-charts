[[Switzerland]]: Currently operates via [[Lithuania]]n license (cards, payments, accounts, trading). Established Revolut Swiss NewCo SA (Jan 2026). Seeking full Swiss banking license.

[[UK]] banking licence granted (March 2026): Full licence from the [[Bank of England]]'s Prudential Regulation Authority after 4+ years of application. 13M UK customers notified via email — "more banking features soon" but "nothing will change" immediately. Previous restricted licence (July 2024) limited operations. Full licence enables direct deposit-holding under FSCS protection, cheaper deposit-funded lending, and removes the regulatory credibility overhang that some argued was slowing international expansion (though [[Chime]] built 9.5M US active users without one).

## UK deposit economics

The real prize from the full licence is FSCS-protected deposits, which should convince customers to hold larger balances.

| Metric | Revolut | [[Monzo]] | [[NatWest]] |
|--------|---------|-------|---------|
| Avg customer balance | ~£575 | ~£1,400 | £20,000+ |

FT analysis (Mar 16, 2026): if Revolut lifts average UK balance from £575 to £1,000 across 13M customers, that represents incremental £5.5bn in deposits. At current rates, ~£200M additional annual interest income just from parking deposits at the [[Bank of England]] — before any lending activity. Higher deposits would also likely drive more transaction income as customers use cards more frequently.

Lending was not technically blocked by the licence absence — non-bank lenders ([[Together Group]], [[NewDay]]) operate at scale without one. But bank status enables deposit-funded lending at lower cost. Job ads suggest Revolut is building out lending capability. FT warns to move gradually — accounting conventions punish rapid loan book growth, as [[Klarna]]'s post-IPO travails demonstrated.

## Investment case

Bull:
- $75B and profitable — rare fintech combination
- 72% revenue growth at scale
- [[UK]] banking licence unlocks lending + FSCS-protected deposits
- 65M customers, adding 1M/17 days
- IPO catalyst in 2026
- Global expansion ([[India]], [[LATAM]])

Bear:
- Private — no direct investment yet
- Lending scale-up risk — accounting conventions punish fast loan book growth ([[Klarna]] precedent)
- Regulatory scrutiny (compliance history)
- Competition from traditional banks — closing avg balance gap (£575 vs £20,000+ at [[NatWest]]) is a tough ask
- FX revenue sensitivity
