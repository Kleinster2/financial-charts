**Marco Rubio** is the US Secretary of State, appointed in January 2025 by President [[Donald Trump]]. A former Republican Senator from Florida (2011-2025), Rubio was among the most hawkish voices in Congress on [[China]], [[Cuba]], and [[Venezuela]], and ran unsuccessfully for the Republican presidential nomination in 2016. As Secretary of State, he has pursued an assertive posture on Latin American and Indo-Pacific affairs, including announcing [[US Chile cable visa sanctions]] in February 2026 (visa revocations for Chilean officials over subsea cable infrastructure decisions favoring Chinese suppliers). His tenure reflects the Trump administration's merger of trade policy and diplomatic leverage — using visa and sanctions tools to enforce technology supply chain alignment with US interests, particularly in telecommunications infrastructure and semiconductor equipment.
