# Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas

Eleventh District regional bank of the [[Federal Reserve]] System, headquartered in Dallas. Covers Texas, northern Louisiana, and southern New Mexico — the geographic core of [[US Shale]] activity. Best known to oil markets for the quarterly **Dallas Fed Energy Survey**, the most-cited sentiment gauge for US upstream oil & gas.

## Dallas Fed Energy Survey

Quarterly anonymous survey of >100 oil & gas executives in the Eleventh District. Produces the most-watched sentiment indices for the US upstream sector: Business Activity Index, Company Outlook Index, plus topical questions specific to the quarter.

The anonymous format is the survey's distinguishing feature — executives speak candidly about politics, regulation, breakeven pressures, capex constraints. Special-question commentary from each release is read across the analyst community.

## Why the Dallas Fed matters for markets

| Reason | Detail |
|---|---|
| Eleventh District = US shale | The geographic district happens to enclose the [[Permian Basin]], Eagle Ford, and most US frac services |
| Anonymous executive commentary | Respondents speak more openly than on earnings calls; SEC-style attribution risks absent |
| Quarterly cadence | Faster than EIA AEO annual reports, deeper than weekly rig counts |
| Cross-references | [[IEA]] Oil reports, [[EIA]] AEO, OPEC monthly reports, sell-side analyst notes all triangulate against the Dallas Fed survey |
| President's commentary | Logan speeches on monetary policy carry weight given Eleventh District's energy-economy exposure |
