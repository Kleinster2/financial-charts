# Wang Chuanfu

Founder, chairman, and CEO of [[BYD]], who took a Shenzhen battery manufacturer and turned it into the world's largest EV company by volume, overtaking [[Tesla]] in 2024 global sales.

## Synopsis

Wang Chuanfu founded [[BYD]] in 1995 as a rechargeable battery maker, initially supplying [[Motorola]] and [[Nokia]], before pivoting into automobiles in 2003 by acquiring a struggling state-owned car company. The move was widely mocked -- a battery chemist entering China's brutal auto market -- but Wang understood that the future of automobiles was fundamentally a battery problem, not a combustion engineering problem. [[Berkshire Hathaway]]'s Charlie Munger persuaded Warren Buffett to invest $232M in BYD in 2008, calling Wang "a combination of Thomas Edison and Jack Welch." That stake peaked at ~$9B before Berkshire began trimming in 2022.

The vindication came in 2024 when BYD surpassed [[Tesla]] in global EV sales volume and began exporting aggressively -- becoming the trigger for [[EU]] and [[United States]] tariff actions against Chinese EVs. Wang attended [[Xi Jinping]]'s February 2025 private sector symposium, signaling his status as a national champion. His net worth (~$17B as of 2025) makes him one of China's wealthiest industrialists, but what sets Wang apart is vertical integration: BYD makes its own batteries, semiconductors (IGBT chips), and even builds its own factories. This manufacturing depth, combined with relentless cost discipline, is the structural advantage that makes BYD the most dangerous competitor in the global auto industry.
