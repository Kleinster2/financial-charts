# Grüns

Grüns is a US-based nutritional supplements brand founded in 2023 by Chad Janis, specializing in greens supplement gummies. The company was built on the insight that the biggest barrier in supplements is adherence — people don't take them consistently — and the solution is making daily nutrient intake an enjoyable ritual via gummy format. Each daily serving contains 60+ ingredients including organic fruits, vegetables, vitamins, minerals, and prebiotic fiber. Grüns became the #1 greens supplement on [[Amazon]] and in US retail within two years of launch, shipping 10 million gummies per day to 1 million customers. Distributed at [[Target]], [[Walmart]], [[Costco]], Sam's Club, Sprouts, and direct-to-consumer via gruns.co.

On April 9, 2026, [[Unilever]] announced it will acquire Grüns for an undisclosed amount (Inc. reported ~$1.2B). The deal is Unilever's first acquisition since the March 2026 [[McCormick]] food-business merger, and signals Unilever's post-food pivot toward beauty, wellbeing, and premium growth categories under new CEO Fernando Fernandez.

## Synthesis

Grüns sits inside a broader pattern of [[Unilever]] assembling a supplements rollup within its Wellbeing division. The sequence is clear: Olly Nutrition (2019), SmartyPants Vitamins (2020), Nutrafol (2022, hair growth), and now Grüns (2026, greens). Each acquisition adds a different consumer health vertical — general vitamins, science-backed formulas, hair biology, and now daily greens — building a portfolio that covers the supplement aisle from multiple angles. Liquid I.V. (acquired 2020, hit $1B in revenue by 2025) shows the margin potential of scaling a digitally native wellness brand through Unilever's distribution network.

The $1.2B price tag for a 3-year-old company valued at $500M in its last round just a year prior implies Unilever is paying a ~2.4x markup over the Series B — aggressive but consistent with consumer health M&A multiples, where DTC brands with proven retention and repeat-purchase dynamics command premium valuations. [[Jostein Solheim]] (Unilever Wellbeing CEO): *"What sets Grüns apart is its focused portfolio of science-backed products that people genuinely enjoy, trust and consistently use. This combination of efficacy and experience is powerful."*
