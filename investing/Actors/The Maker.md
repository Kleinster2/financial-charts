# The Maker

Lifestyle fragrance brand born from a boutique hotel in Hudson, NY. Founded by [[Lev Glazman]] and [[Alina Roytberg]], the duo behind [[Fresh Beauty]] (sold to [[LVMH]] in 2000). The hotel opened in 2020 as a 4-year renovation project; fragrance launched in 2021. The thesis is experiential-to-product: the hotel is the brand world, the perfume is the souvenir. With an estimated ~$12M revenue run rate (industry sources, Aug 2023), investment from [[True Beauty Ventures]] (Jul 2024), and distribution through [[Sephora]] North America, The Maker is a textbook example of [[World Building as CPG Moat]] — the product succeeds because the world exists, not the other way around.

## Synopsis

The Maker is the second act for one of beauty's most successful founding duos. Glazman and Roytberg co-founded Fresh Beauty in 1991 from a tiny Boston apothecary, grew it into a global skincare brand, and sold a majority stake to LVMH in 1999–2000. After years of advisory work and global travel, they channeled hotel nostalgia into The Maker — first as a boutique hotel in Hudson, NY (opened 2020), then as a fragrance line (launched 2021). The strategic logic: build an immersive physical world first, then let products emerge as extensions of that world. This inverts the traditional CPG playbook where product comes first and brand story is retrofitted. Stag (leather and wood) won the Fragrance Foundation's indie fragrance of the year in 2022; Lover (sandalwood, fig, vetiver) is the current hero product. True Beauty Ventures made its first investment in the brand in July 2024, with capital earmarked for scaling Sephora distribution and international expansion.

## Evolution

The story of The Maker is the story of second-time founders weaponizing nostalgia. Glazman's origin with fragrance begins in 1960s Soviet Leningrad, where his mother spent six months' rent on black-market Lancôme Climat. That formative sensory experience drove decades of self-taught perfumery. He and Roytberg met in the early 1980s in New York — she was studying fashion at Parsons — and in 1991 they co-founded Fresh Beauty from "Nuts about Beauty," a small Boston apothecary. Fresh built a global following around natural ingredients and became one of LVMH's acquisitions in 1999–2000. The founders stayed on in advisory roles, travelling extensively.

Those hotel stays planted the seed. In 2016, they began renovating a building in Hudson, NY — a bohemian jewel box of 1,000+ lamps, antiques, and art across 11 rooms, each themed after a creative archetype (the architect, the writer, the artist). The hotel opened in 2020, immediately becoming a tastemaker retreat for the East Coast creative class. A perfume cabinet in the stairwell let guests sample Glazman's personal fragrance library. In 2021, six genderless fragrances launched — Fire, Libertine, Naked, Paradiso, Wild, and Stag — along with candles. Stag won the Fragrance Foundation's indie fragrance of the year in 2022. The line expanded to Sephora US and Canada (2024), a NYC retail location, and online direct. True Beauty Ventures invested in July 2024, validating the second-time founder playbook that Rich Gersten (TBV co-founder) explicitly cited as an investment thesis.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Second-time founders with a proven exit ([[Fresh Beauty]] → [[LVMH]])
- Experiential moat: hotel creates physical brand world that competitors can't replicate
- Clean fragrance trend + genderless positioning in a $50B+ global fragrance market
- Sephora distribution provides scale runway
- Low CAC — the hotel, press coverage, and word-of-mouth drive organic demand

**Bear:**
- Fragrance is brutally competitive with low switching costs
- Revenue likely still small (~$12M estimate is from 2023, before Sephora scaling)
- Hotel is a single location — brand world is geographically concentrated
- No disclosed financials; true margins and growth rate unknown
- Founders are advisory-era age; succession planning unclear
