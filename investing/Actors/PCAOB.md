**PCAOB (Public Company Accounting Oversight Board)** — the US nonprofit that oversees and inspects the auditors of public companies, created by Sarbanes-Oxley (2002) and overseen by the [[SEC]]. Central to the [[Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act|HFCAA]]: the act's delisting trigger is the PCAOB being unable to inspect a foreign issuer's audit firm. After an August 2022 protocol with Chinese regulators, the PCAOB secured complete access to inspect mainland China and Hong Kong audit firms for the first time (December 2022), defusing the immediate HFCAA delisting threat — access it must re-confirm annually.
