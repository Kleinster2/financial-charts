**Formosa Plastics Group** (FPG) is [[Taiwan]]'s largest industrial conglomerate, founded in 1954 by Wang Yung-ching, who built it from a small PVC processor into a sprawling empire spanning petrochemicals, plastics, electronics, steel, healthcare, and education. The group is not a single listed entity but a collection of four core publicly traded companies: Formosa Plastics Corporation (1301.TW), Nan Ya Plastics (1303.TW), Formosa Chemicals & Fibre (1326.TW), and Formosa Petrochemical (6505.TW), plus dozens of subsidiaries.

FPG's relevance to the semiconductor story comes through [[Nanya Technology]] (2408.TW), in which the group holds a ~50%+ stake. Nanya is Taiwan's DRAM manufacturer, competing against [[Samsung]], [[SK Hynix]], and [[Micron]] — though at a fraction of their scale. The group also operates Formosa Sumco Technology (silicon wafers) and has investments across the semiconductor supply chain.

The petrochemical business remains the revenue core but faces structural headwinds from Chinese overcapacity in ethylene and propylene (see [[Industrial commodities]]). FPG's US subsidiary, Formosa Plastics Corporation, USA, operates the Point Comfort complex in Texas. The group also made headlines for the massive Formosa Ha Tinh Steel complex in [[Vietnam]], one of Southeast Asia's largest integrated steel mills, which caused a major environmental disaster in 2016 (marine life killed along 200km of Vietnamese coastline, resulting in a $500M settlement).

Wang Yung-ching died in 2008; the group is now managed by the second generation of the Wang family.
