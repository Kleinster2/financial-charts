## Synopsis

Affleck has quietly built two businesses that matter beyond Hollywood. [[InterPositive]], founded in 2022 and operated in stealth, builds AI tools for post-production — relighting shots, fixing continuity, adjusting environments, adding VFX — trained on a production's own dailies. Not generative AI, not text-to-video. Practical filmmaker tools. [[Netflix]] acquired it Mar 5, 2026 for up to $600M (structured with earnouts), one of Netflix's largest acquisitions ever. The 16-person team was integrated into Netflix's engineering workforce; Affleck became a senior adviser.

Separately, [[Artists Equity]] — the production company he co-founded with [[Matt Damon]] and [[Gerry Cardinale]] ([[RedBird Capital]]) in Nov 2022 — signed a multi-year first-look streaming deal with Netflix in Mar 2026. Artists Equity's model is distinctive: profit-sharing with all crew, not just above-the-line talent. Writers, editors, costume designers participate in upside. The Rip (Jan 2026, Netflix) was the catalyst — 112.3M views in six weeks, and the first time Netflix paid performance-based bonuses to ~1,200 cast and crew. Artists Equity retains a separate theatrical deal with [[Sony|Sony Pictures]].

The dual play — AI tools reducing post-production costs at Netflix scale, plus a locked-in content pipeline from proven filmmakers — gives Netflix both a margin lever and a talent magnet.
