RWE is a German multinational energy company and one of Europe's largest electricity producers, operating across wind, solar, hydro, biomass, gas, and (until recently) coal. Ticker: RWE (Frankfurt). The company underwent a radical transformation under CEO Markus Krebber: swapped its conventional generation assets with [[E.ON]]'s renewables portfolio in 2018-2020, then acquired Con Edison Clean Energy Businesses for $6.8B (2023) to become a top-5 US renewables operator. Holds a 33.3% stake in [[Urenco]], the uranium enrichment consortium. Total generation capacity of ~40 GW across 30+ countries. Revenue ~€24B (FY2024). The RWE-E.ON asset swap was one of the largest corporate restructurings in European utility history, effectively splitting Germany's energy sector into a networks company (E.ON) and a generation company (RWE).
