# Beatrice Weder di Mauro

Beatrice Weder di Mauro is a Swiss-Italian economist, professor of international economics at the [[Geneva Graduate Institute]], and president of [[CEPR]] (Centre for Economic Policy Research) from 2018 onward. Former member of the [[German Council of Economic Experts]] (the "five wise men," 2004-2012) — the first woman and first foreign-born economist to hold that role. A central figure in the European macro-policy debate on global imbalances, trade, and the [[Euro]] system.

## Why she matters to the vault

Weder di Mauro is one of the European economists most cited by [[Adam Tooze]] and others in the 2026 [[Global imbalances]] debate. Her framing of the [[Second China shock]] centers on what European fiscal and industrial-policy institutions can realistically do inside the EU's fragmented decision architecture — she is skeptical of the US-style state-capacity response being replicable in Europe on a credible timeline. CEPR working papers under her presidency are a primary publication venue for the Pettis/Baldwin/Setser axis of work.

Also active on:
- Euro-area fiscal rules reform
- [[EU]] capital-markets union
- Sovereign debt restructuring (she co-edited *Sovereign Debt: A Guide for Economists and Practitioners*, 2022)
