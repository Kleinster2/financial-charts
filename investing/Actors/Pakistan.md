## Iran war response

- March 12, 2026: Suspended 78 mmcfd of [[LNG]] supply to the fertilizer sector to prioritize residential/domestic demand. Pakistan is near-100% [[Qatar]] LNG dependent — the most exposed major South Asian economy to Hormuz-transiting gas. See [[Iran conflict country responses#Reference — export bans and hoarding]].
- May 17, 2026 ([[Financial Times|FT]]): Adopted a formal four-day working week alongside [[Sri Lanka]] and [[Philippines]] — a step beyond voluntary conservation into government-mandated demand destruction. The [[IEA]] framed the measure as "evoking memories" of the 2022 post-[[Russia]]-[[Ukraine]] energy crisis that triggered emerging-market debt crises. See [[Iran conflict country responses#Pattern 2 — four-day working weeks]].

The combination — near-total Qatar LNG dependency, [[IMF]] program constraints, fertilizer-sector LNG suspension already in place, and a four-day working week imposed mid-May — places Pakistan among the most fiscally and energetically constrained countries in the global emergency-measures pool of 76 nations.
