**Jamie Raskin** is a US Representative from [[Maryland]] (D-8th district), serving since 2017. Constitutional law professor turned congressman, he is the ranking member of the House Judiciary Committee and has emerged as one of the most aggressive Democratic investigators of Trump-era financial conflicts, including the [[Tariff refund claims market]] probe targeting [[Howard Lutnick]] and [[Cantor Fitzgerald]].

## Synopsis

Raskin's political profile was shaped by two defining episodes: leading the second Trump impeachment prosecution in January 2021 (just days after the death of his son Tommy) and his sustained focus on institutional corruption and conflicts of interest. As ranking member of House Judiciary, he lacks subpoena power in the Republican-controlled House but uses public letters, hearing testimony, and media to pressure investigations forward. His February 2026 inquiry into [[Howard Lutnick]]'s [[Cantor Fitzgerald]] and the [[Tariff refund claims market]] questioned whether the Lutnick family used nonpublic information gained through Howard Lutnick's role as Commerce Secretary to trade tariff refund claims.

Before Congress, Raskin was a constitutional law professor at [[American University]] for 25 years and served in the Maryland State Senate. His legal background gives him an unusually technical command of constitutional arguments — he was the lead impeachment manager arguing incitement of insurrection based on First Amendment doctrine. He also co-signed investigations into [[Jared Kushner]]'s [[Affinity Partners]] with Senator [[Ron Wyden]]. For markets, Raskin matters as a driver of headline risk for companies and individuals in the Trump orbit — his investigations rarely produce immediate legislative action given divided government, but they surface documents and create reputational pressure that affects deal-making and confirmation processes.
