# Council of Economic Advisers

US government agency within the Executive Office of the President that advises the president on economic policy. Established by the Employment Act of 1946. Composed of three members — a chair (Senate-confirmed, Cabinet-rank under some administrations) and two additional members — plus a professional staff of economists drawn largely from academia for short rotations.

Produces the annual Economic Report of the President, provides economic analysis to the White House, and represents the administration's economic thinking in inter-agency and congressional processes. Chairs have typically been academic economists: recent holders include [[Jared Bernstein]] (2023–2025), Cecilia Rouse (2021–2023), and, under prior administrations, Christina Romer, Ben Bernanke, Greg Mankiw, and Austan Goolsbee.

## Function

- Advise the president on US and global economic conditions
- Evaluate federal economic policy for internal consistency
- Coordinate with Treasury, OMB, and NEC on economic decisions
- Interface with the Federal Reserve and international counterparts on macro issues
