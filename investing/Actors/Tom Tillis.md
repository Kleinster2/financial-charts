## Fed confirmation holdout (2026)

After DOJ served [[Jerome Powell|Powell]] with grand jury subpoenas in January 2026, Tillis publicly stated he would oppose all Fed nominees "until this legal matter is fully resolved." Position remained firm as of the April 14, 2026 [[Scott Bessent]] interview — with [[Kevin Warsh]]'s Senate Banking Committee hearing scheduled for April 21, 2026, the Tillis holdout puts confirmation before the May 15 Powell chair-term expiration in doubt. Bessent's answer on Tillis: "I think Senator Tillis believes that Kevin Warsh is an excellent candidate" — but no vote commitment.

### Apr 24, 2026 — DOJ closes investigation, Tillis silent

[[Jeanine Pirro]] (DC US Attorney) announced the DOJ is closing the Powell investigation but reserved the right to reopen. Tillis had not commented as of the Bloomberg Real Yield broadcast that afternoon. Mike McKee's read: DOJ would not have moved without first checking with Tillis's office, so the working assumption is he is satisfied — but the lack of an explicit Tillis statement leaves the confirmation timing genuinely uncertain. The Pirro reservation language ("would not hesitate to reopen if facts warrant") is the open question Tillis has to interpret: a fully resolved matter, or a hold-fire. [[Polymarket]] saw confirmation-by-May-15 odds spike to 82% on the news.
