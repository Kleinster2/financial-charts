# Equinox Gold

Equinox Gold (NYSE American / TSX: EQX) is an Americas-focused gold producer headquartered in Vancouver, transformed by its June 2025 merger with Calibre Mining into one of the larger producers in the hemisphere — operating mines across the US, Mexico, Brazil, Nicaragua, and Canada, anchored by two long-life Canadian cornerstones, Greenstone (Ontario) and Valentine (Newfoundland). Founded by serial mining entrepreneur [[Ross Beaty]], it grew by acquisition (Leagold in 2020, then Calibre in 2025) into a roughly 1-million-ounce-plus annual producer, and serves in the vault as the principal larger Americas peer for [[Aura Minerals]].

The financial signature is a [[Foreign private issuer]] one: EQX files [[SEC]] 6-K interims under IFRS, so the standard vendors return empty statements and the bottom line is noisy. FY2025 revenue roughly doubled to $1.82B as Greenstone ramped and Calibre consolidated mid-year, with operating income of $432M; but reported net income of $221M sits below FY2024's $339M even though 2024 revenue was lower, because the 2024 figure carried large non-operating gains and the 2025 line absorbs merger accounting, interest, and the Greenstone non-controlling interest. The equity re-rated with the 2025–26 gold bull, though it lagged the highest-beta names — EQX returned less than [[Aura Minerals|AUGO]] over the post-IPO window. Instrument detail and the peer price record live in [[Equinox Gold securities note]].

One-line read: Equinox is a newly-enlarged Americas gold producer levered to ramping its two Canadian cornerstones toward a 1.2Moz run-rate, where the bet is that Greenstone/Valentine execution and a firm gold tape convert a messy, merger-year income statement into clean cash flow.

## Operations

Equinox runs an Americas portfolio spanning five countries, reshaped by the Calibre merger into a two-cornerstone-plus-satellites structure:

| Country | Assets |
|---|---|
| Canada | Greenstone (Ontario, commercial production Nov 2024) and Valentine (Newfoundland, first pour mid-2025) — the two long-life cornerstones |
| Brazil | Aurizona and the legacy Leagold mines |
| United States | Mesquite and Castle Mountain (California) |
| Mexico | Los Filos — suspended in 2024 amid community blockades, the portfolio's main overhang |
| Nicaragua / Nevada | Operations added through the Calibre merger |

The strategic logic is a shift in center of gravity toward Canada: Greenstone and Valentine are long-life, lower-cost, tier-one-jurisdiction assets meant to anchor cash flow as higher-cost or higher-risk legacy mines (Los Filos) roll off.

## Financials

EQX is a working example of the [[Foreign private issuer]] data problem: it files 6-K interims under IFRS, the standard vendors return an empty annual statement, and the merger year layers non-operating items and a Greenstone non-controlling interest on top.

| Metric (US$M) | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Revenue | 1,088 | 913 | 1,817 |
| Gross profit | 109 | 206 | 643 |
| Operating income | 50 | 152 | 432 |
| Net income | 29 | 339 | 221 |

*Operating-level figures (revenue, gross, operating income) reconcile across years; the net line does not move with them — FY2024 net of $339M exceeds FY2025's $221M on lower revenue because 2024 carried large non-operating gains and 2025 absorbs merger accounting, finance cost, and the Greenstone NCI. Source: StockAnalysis extract of the IFRS statements; figures in US$M, cross-checked at the operating level.*

The operating story is clean: revenue dipped in 2024 as Los Filos came offline, then roughly doubled in 2025 as Greenstone ramped and Calibre consolidated from mid-year, lifting operating income from $50M (2023) to $432M (2025). The reported bottom line is the part to distrust — which is the FPI lesson, not an EQX-specific quirk.

## What to watch

- Cornerstone ramps — Greenstone and Valentine reaching nameplate; the path to the ~590koz combined and the 1.2Moz total run-rate is the whole growth case.
- Gold price — an unhedged Americas producer; spot gold drives revenue and the margin against an ~$1,800–1,900/oz AISC.
- AISC trend — costs are high relative to peers; whether the Canadian cornerstones pull the blended AISC down as Los Filos and higher-cost legacy mines roll off.
- Los Filos resolution — the suspended Mexican mine is the main overhang; a restart, sale, or write-down each move the asset base.
- Balance sheet — merger-related debt and the Greenstone partner structure against rising cash flow; deleveraging as production scales.

## Analysis

Equinox is a scale-by-acquisition story now at its most consequential inflection. Two mergers (Leagold 2020, Calibre 2025) built the footprint, but the value case rests on execution at two specific assets — Greenstone and Valentine — that are supposed to shift the company from a higher-cost, multi-jurisdiction collection toward a Canada-anchored, long-life producer. The FY2025 numbers capture the company mid-transition: revenue doubled and operating income rose 5x, but the reported net line is dominated by merger accounting and non-operating items, which is exactly the [[Foreign private issuer]] reading — judge it on production, AISC, and cash flow, not the IFRS bottom line.

Against [[Aura Minerals]], EQX is the larger, more diversified, but higher-cost and messier name: roughly 4x Aura's production with a tier-one Canadian core, but an AISC near $1,800–1,900/oz versus Aura's ~$1,478, a suspended Mexican mine as overhang, and a noisier income statement. In the post-2025 gold rally EQX underperformed the higher-beta AUGO — the trade-off between a cleaner, faster-compounding small-cap and a larger, lower-multiple producer still working through integration. The shared driver is gold; the differentiator is execution risk and cost structure.
