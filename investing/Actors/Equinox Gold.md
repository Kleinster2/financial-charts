# Equinox Gold

Equinox Gold (NYSE American / TSX: EQX) is a North-American-focused gold producer headquartered in Vancouver, in the middle of a fast transformation. Two 2025 mergers (Leagold 2020, then [[Calibre Mining]] in June 2025) built scale; then in January 2026 it sold its entire Brazil operations for up to $1.015B and used the proceeds to repay ~$990M of debt, cutting net debt to roughly $77M and funding an inaugural dividend; and in May 2026 it agreed an all-stock at-market merger with [[Orla Mining]] to create a ~1.1Moz North American senior producer. Founded by serial mining entrepreneur [[Ross Beaty]], it serves in the vault as the principal larger gold peer for [[Aura Minerals]].

The financial signature is a [[Foreign private issuer]] one: EQX files [[SEC]] 6-K interims under IFRS, so the standard vendors return empty statements and the reported bottom line is noisy. FY2025 revenue roughly doubled to $1.82B as Greenstone ramped and Calibre consolidated mid-year, with operating income of $432M; but the Brazil operations were classified as held-for-sale/discontinued, which is why the available extract's net line ($221M, below FY2024's $339M) does not reconcile with its continuing-operations operating line. The post-Brazil balance sheet is the cleaner story — net debt near zero, a new dividend — and the Orla merger is the dominant catalyst. Instrument detail and the peer price record live in [[Equinox Gold securities note]].

One-line read: Equinox is a North American gold producer mid-transformation — Brazil sold to near-eliminate debt and start a dividend, now merging with [[Orla Mining]] to become a ~1.1Moz senior producer — where the bet is that the Greenstone/Valentine/Musselwhite Canadian core and a clean Q3-2026 Orla close convert scale and deleveraging into a re-rating.

## Operations

Two corporate actions reshaped the portfolio in 2026: the January Brazil-operations sale removed the Brazilian mines (Aurizona and the legacy Leagold assets), and the pending Orla merger adds new ounces. The current and pending footprint:

| Region | Assets |
|---|---|
| Canada | Greenstone (Ontario, commercial production Nov 2024) and Valentine (Newfoundland, first pour mid-2025) — the long-life cornerstones; Musselwhite (Ontario) arrives via [[Orla Mining]] |
| United States | Mesquite and Castle Mountain (California); South Railroad (Nevada development) via Orla |
| Mexico | Los Filos — suspended in 2024 amid community blockades; Camino Rojo arrives via [[Orla Mining]] |
| Nicaragua | Operations added through the [[Calibre Mining]] merger |
| Brazil | Sold January 2026 for up to $1.015B — no longer in the portfolio |

The strategic logic is a clean pivot to North America: exit Brazil, deleverage, and combine with Orla's Canadian (Musselwhite) and Mexican (Camino Rojo) production around the Greenstone/Valentine Canadian core.

## Financials

EQX is a working example of the [[Foreign private issuer]] data problem compounded by corporate actions: it files 6-K interims under IFRS, the standard vendors return an empty annual statement, and the 2025 figures straddle the Calibre consolidation and the Brazil-operations held-for-sale classification.

| Metric (US$M) | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Revenue | 1,088 | 913 | 1,817 |
| Gross profit | 109 | 206 | 643 |
| Operating income | 50 | 152 | 432 |
| Net income (as reported) | 29 | 339 | 221 |

*Operating-level figures (revenue, gross, operating income) reconcile across years; the net line does not move with them. Source: StockAnalysis extract of the IFRS statements, cross-checked at the operating level; figures in US$M.*

The operating story is clean: revenue dipped in 2024 as Los Filos came offline, then roughly doubled in 2025 as Greenstone ramped and Calibre consolidated from mid-year, lifting operating income from $50M (2023) to $432M (2025). The reported bottom line is the part to distrust — FY2024 net of $339M sits above FY2025's $221M on lower revenue because of large non-operating items and, in 2025, the Brazil held-for-sale/discontinued classification ahead of the January 2026 sale. That is the FPI lesson, not an EQX-specific quirk: judge it on production, AISC, and now the post-sale balance sheet, not the IFRS net line.

Income-statement Sankey deferred: the 2025 statement splits continuing operations (the reconciling operating line) from the discontinued Brazil operations (which carry the gain and reclassification driving the total net line), so, given the limited financial disclosure in the available 6-K extract, a single-year Sankey would misstate the bridge. A clean Sankey needs the continuing-operations income statement from the 6-K/MD&A. The peer price chart lives in [[Equinox Gold securities note]].

## What to watch

- Orla merger close — the dominant catalyst; a clean Q3 2026 close creates the ~1.1Moz senior producer, while a regulatory delay (Canadian/Mexican competition) or a topping bid changes the math. Break fees are $475M (EQX) / $250M (Orla).

## Analysis

Equinox is a scale-by-consolidation story that, in the space of nine months, cleaned itself up and then doubled down. The Calibre merger (June 2025) added Valentine and the Canadian tilt; the Brazil sale (January 2026) did the unglamorous but decisive work — converting a higher-cost, higher-risk South American book into ~$1B of cash, near-zero net debt, and a first dividend; and the Orla merger (May 2026) spends the resulting balance-sheet strength on another leg of scale, toward a ~1.1Moz-now / >1.9Moz-medium-term North American senior producer. The throughline is [[Ross Beaty]]'s recurring playbook — assemble, scale, list, repeat — now executed at senior-producer size.

The financials still demand the [[Foreign private issuer]] reading: the reported net line is noise (non-operating items plus the discontinued-Brazil classification), so the signal is production, AISC, and the post-sale balance sheet, not the IFRS bottom line. Against [[Aura Minerals]], EQX is the larger, more diversified, higher-cost name — roughly 4x Aura's production with a tier-one Canadian core, but an AISC near $1,800–1,900/oz versus Aura's ~$1,478 — and in the post-2025 gold rally it underperformed the higher-beta AUGO. The investable question is no longer integration risk but merger-close risk and whether the cleaner balance sheet earns a re-rating toward the C$25–35 analyst range.
