**Boaz Weinstein** — American hedge fund manager. Founder of [[Saba Capital]] (2009). Former [[Deutsche Bank]] managing director (youngest in bank history at 27). Chess Life Master at 16. Pioneer in credit default swap trading. Known for profiting from JPMorgan's "London Whale" loss (2012) and activist campaigns against closed-end funds.

## Career

### Early career

- Age 15: Intern at [[Merrill Lynch]]
- Won Newsday stock-picking contest as high school junior (beat 5,000 students)
- 1995: Joined Merrill Lynch debt trading
- 1997: [[Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette]] — floating rate notes

### Deutsche Bank (1998-2009)

Joined as sole CDS trader. Built proprietary trading group managing ~$30B in positions.

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1998 | Joined; profited during [[Russia]] default, LTCM collapse |
| 1999 | Promoted to VP |
| 2001 | Youngest managing director in Deutsche Bank history (age 27) |
| 2006 | Saba group gained ~$900M |
| 2007 | Named internal group "Saba Principal Strategies"; gained ~$600M |
| 2008 | Lost ~$1.8B (only losing year in 11 at Deutsche Bank) |
| 2009 | Spun out as [[Saba Capital]] with 15 team members + all IP |

### Saba Capital (2009-present)

Founded with $140-160M. Now ~$6B AUM. See [[Saba Capital]] for full history.
