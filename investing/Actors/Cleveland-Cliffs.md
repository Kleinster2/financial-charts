# Cleveland-Cliffs

**Cleveland-Cliffs (CLF)** — the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America and, unlike the [[Nucor]]/[[Steel Dynamics]] mini-mills, an integrated blast-furnace producer that is self-sufficient in iron ore (it mines its own pellets). Headquartered in Cleveland, OH; NYSE-listed. The largest supplier of steel to the US auto industry, built through the acquisitions of AK Steel, ArcelorMittal USA, and Stelco. Its integrated, iron-ore-exposed, highly leveraged model gives it the cohort's highest volatility (~71%) and makes it the looser steel name — it joins the tight EAF pair only at distance 0.46 in the [[Steel and aluminum equity beta]] cohort, trading partly on iron-ore and auto-cycle dynamics the mini-mills do not share.
