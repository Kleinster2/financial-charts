## Public positioning

Hussein's framing of Ridges in interviews is blunt: AI agents will either make coders 100x more productive, or make them obsolete — and the economics of decentralized inference decide which. He cites the $400B global software engineering labor market as the addressable pool and treats [[Cursor]] / [[Claude Code]] as the incumbent products to undercut on price rather than outcompete on model quality.
