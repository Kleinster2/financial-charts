#actor #bci #healthcare #china #private

Gestala — Chinese non-invasive ultrasound brain-computer interface (BCI) startup, founded January 2026 by serial entrepreneur [[Phoenix Peng]] (also co-founder of [[NeuroXess]], an implantable BCI competitor) and gaming mogul [[Tianqiao Chen]] (former richest man in China; founder of [[Shanda Games]]; longtime neuroscience investor via the [[Tianqiao and Chrissy Chen Institute|TCCI]]). Closed a $21.6M angel round at a US$100-200M valuation in March 2026 — the largest early-stage funding in China's BCI industry to date. The company uses phased-array ultrasound to non-invasively stimulate or suppress neural activity, including in deep-brain structures, without surgery.

## Technology

Phased-array ultrasound for non-invasive neuromodulation. The system uses focused ultrasound transmitted through the skull to either stimulate or suppress neural activity at a precise location — including in deep-brain structures such as the thalamus, basal ganglia, and brainstem that implantable electrode systems struggle to reach without invasive insertion.

The technology distinguishes Gestala from the dominant electrical-implant approach used by [[Neuralink]], [[Synchron]], [[Merge Labs|Merge Labs]] (the Sam Altman-backed BCI), and [[NeuroXess]] (Peng's prior company). The trade-off: non-invasive ultrasound has lower spatial and temporal resolution than direct electrode contact, but eliminates surgical risk and addresses a much broader patient population — chronic-pain sufferers (~20% of adults globally per Peng's framing), mental-health patients, and stroke-rehab patients who cannot or will not undergo brain surgery.

Peng's framing on Bloomberg "The China Show" 4/28/2026: "BCI has two different technological stacks. First of all is using electrical implant — we have to put electricals into your brain. The second one is ultrasound — it means we are connecting to your brain wirelessly. So that's what we are doing right now."

Gestala's "much bigger patient group" claim (chronic pain, mental health, stroke rehab) is the structural argument for non-invasive: addressable population is orders of magnitude larger than the spinal-cord-injury / ALS focus of invasive players. The capability ceiling is also lower (no high-bandwidth motor-control output), but that ceiling matches the indications Gestala is targeting.

## Strategic positioning

The combination of [[Tianqiao Chen]] as co-founder/chairman and [[Phoenix Peng]] as operational CEO is the structural differentiator. Chen has been one of the largest private funders of neuroscience research globally — the [[Tianqiao and Chrissy Chen Institute]] at Caltech and the Chen Institute at Caltech for Brain Research are the canonical references. Peng's prior NeuroXess founding gives him operational BCI experience plus the network of researchers and clinicians needed to run trials in China. The investor syndicate ([[Fourier Intelligence]], Tsing Song Capital, Gobi Ventures) layers exoskeleton/humanoid-robotics integration onto the Gestala platform — the rehabilitation use case explicitly assumes a robotic delivery channel for stimulation patterns.

Peng noted on Bloomberg that Gestala is hiring international talent (some moving from Israel and the US to China), with a target of ~20% of core team coming from global backgrounds — a deliberate signaling of the company's positioning as a global-research-grade lab, not a Chinese-domestic copy.

The "national five-year program" framing Peng cited as one rationale for the angel valuation: BCI is officially identified as a strategic future-industry priority in China's planning documents, which provides regulatory tailwinds and may unlock state-affiliated capital in subsequent rounds.

## Investment / market lens

Pre-revenue. The valuation ($100-200M) is reading the founder-credibility premium — Peng's NeuroXess track record + Chen's neuroscience-philanthropy network — rather than any operational fundamentals. Comparable: [[Mira Murati]]'s [[Thinking Machines Lab]] at $12B post-Series A is a pure founder-credibility valuation; Gestala sits at a much earlier stage (angel round) but carries similar founder-credibility logic.

The structural read for the broader BCI sector: Gestala's $21.6M is the largest angel round in China's BCI industry, but it sits inside a sector that has seen funding velocity accelerate sharply in 2025-2026 (per TechCrunch's "China's brain-computer interface industry is racing ahead" framing, Feb 2026). The non-invasive vs invasive split is the structural axis; Gestala's bet is that addressable population scales the addressable market faster than capability ceiling limits it.
