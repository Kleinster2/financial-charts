# Jim Farley

## Synopsis

Jim Farley is the President and CEO of [[Ford]] Motor Company, a position he has held since October 1, 2020, when he succeeded Jim Hackett. Born June 10, 1962, he holds a B.A. from Georgetown University and an MBA from UCLA Anderson. His pre-[[Ford]] career was at [[Toyota]] (1990-2007), where he ran the launch of [[Lexus]] and then Toyota's US marketing organisation. He joined [[Ford]] in 2007 as Group Vice President of Global Marketing, then ran Ford of Europe (2017-2019) and Ford South America before being elevated to Chief Operating Officer in 2020 and to CEO six months later.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Ford]] strategic thread now running through [[Sherry House]] (CFO, February 2025), [[John Lawler]] (Vice Chair, February 2025), and [[Bill Ford]] (Executive Chair). Farley is the architect of the [[Ford+]] plan announced in March 2022 and of the 2023 Model E / Ford Blue / Ford Pro segmentation that broke out the EV business as a separate reporting segment. He authored the late-2025 / 2026 strategic pivot away from pure-EV across the F-Series — the F-150 Lightning production wind-down, the shift toward extended-range EVs (EREV), the 2027 Universal EV Platform target, and the personal posture of importing a [[Xiaomi]] [[SU7]] for tear-down ("[[China|Chinese]] EVs are an existential threat"). The unresolved 2026 questions on his desk: the [[Xiaomi]] / [[BYD]] US-JV question (publicly denied; FT had four sources), the [[Pentagon]] weapons-supply preliminary talks (April 2026), the Iran-war commodity squeeze, and a succession question that has no public heir-apparent in the sixth year of his tenure.

His May 2026 commentary on the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war commodity shock]] sits alongside [[Sherry House]]'s Q1 2026 quote — the FT article (Inagaki / Davies / Ash, May 3, 2026) framed both as the public-facing voices on Ford's $2bn 2026 supply-chain hit, the [[Novelis]] body-sheet substitution into 50% [[Section 232]] [[Aluminum]] tariffs, and the +16% LME aluminium re-rating that hits the all-aluminium F-Series body line hardest.

## Position in Ford strategic thread

Farley's six-year run as CEO maps onto the structural inflection in US auto: post-COVID demand reset, the Trump-I and Trump-II tariff cycles, the [[China]] EV scale-up, the US EV slowdown, and now the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war commodity shock]]. Each of his major strategic moves is a position on one of these inflections.

The [[Ford+]] plan (March 2022) was the framework: separate the legacy ICE business (Ford Blue), the commercial / fleet business (Ford Pro), and the EV business (Model E) into distinct P&Ls so the market could value them separately. The 2023 reorganisation made the segmentation operational. The bet was that Model E would build EV scale fast enough to offset the inevitable margin compression from [[Tesla]] and [[China]] — the bet has not paid off on the timetable Farley initially indicated.

The 2025-2026 strategic pivot is the recalibration. F-150 Lightning production is winding down despite leading H1 2025 electric-pickup sales (13,029 vs [[Tesla]] Cybertruck 10,712). The 2027 Universal EV Platform pushes affordable EVs ($30K target, midsize pickup) further out. Extended-range EVs (battery + combustion generator) become the bridge. The [[Xiaomi]] / [[BYD]] US-JV question is the unresolved high-stakes lever — Farley's personal SU7 tear-down signals he sees Chinese cost engineering as the binding constraint, but the political backlash (Rep. Moolenaar; "dangerous domino effect") and Ford's own public denial show how narrow the path is.

The April 2026 [[Pentagon]] weapons-supply preliminary talks add a fourth strategic thread: defense-revenue option-value on idle capacity, but at the risk of distracting from the EV pivot already mid-stream. The [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war commodity shock]] is the fifth — Farley and [[Sherry House]] now communicate joint commodity guidance to investors, with the $2bn 2026 hit stacking on top of the $6bn (Big Three combined) residual tariff hit.

The succession question is structurally open. Farley is in his sixth year. There is no publicly-identified internal heir, and the chair-side block under [[Bill Ford]] has not signalled a timeline. [[John Lawler]]'s February 2025 Vice Chair elevation pulled him into a strategy-and-partnerships role rather than positioning him as CEO successor. [[Sherry House]] is six months into the CFO seat — too early to position as successor without further runway. Sourcing on a named successor candidate is thin in the materials reviewed for this stub.

## Career

- 1990–2007 — [[Toyota]] / [[Lexus]] North America. Led [[Lexus]] marketing, ran the [[Scion]] brand launch, and ultimately ran Toyota US marketing.
- November 2007 — Joined [[Ford]] as Group Vice President, Global Marketing.
- 2010s — Rotated through senior marketing, sales, and regional leadership roles at [[Ford]].
- 2017 — President and CEO, [[Ford]] of Europe.
- 2019 — President, [[Ford]] South America (in addition to Europe).
- April 2020 — Promoted to Chief Operating Officer, [[Ford]] Motor Company.
- October 1, 2020 — Promoted to President and CEO, [[Ford]] Motor Company, succeeding Jim Hackett.
- March 2022 — Announced the [[Ford+]] plan.
- 2023 — Implemented Model E / Ford Blue / Ford Pro three-segment reorganisation.
- 2025 — Wound down F-150 Lightning production; pivoted toward extended-range EVs and 2027 Universal EV Platform.
- February 2025 — Paired with new CFO [[Sherry House]] and Vice Chair [[John Lawler]] in the senior team realignment.
- April 2026 — [[Pentagon]] weapons-supply preliminary talks disclosed (FT / WSJ).
- May 2026 — [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war commodity-shock]] guidance: $2bn 2026 supply-chain hit, +$1bn YoY.
