## Merz-Meloni defense axis (Feb 2026)

Merz has aligned with Meloni, presenting [[Germany]] and Italy as Europe's two manufacturing powerhouses. Perverse given Olaf Scholz's stated fear of a post-fascist Italian government.

Defense angle: [[Leonardo]] as partner for [[Rheinmetall]]/[[Airbus]], partly because [[Dassault Aviation]] is uncooperative on FCAS. Italy and Germany share industrial manufacturing DNA that [[France]] and Spain don't.

The debt constraint: Italy can't match German defense spending (~$189B by 2029) without EU common debt issuance. Italian debt at ~140% of GDP makes national defense ramp-up dangerous — risks bond market panic. Italy's main leverage is Germany's need for allies in EU negotiations.

Atlanticist play: Meloni performing the Atlanticist role with Trump while maintaining her post-fascist political lineage. This alignment with Germany adds European credibility.

## Pax Silica / trusted technology axis (Jun 2026)

Italy signed the Joint Statement on AI Opportunity at the June 2026 [[Pax Silica]] summit in Washington through Ambassador Francesco Varricchio, special envoy for innovation and new technologies to Foreign Minister Antonio Tajani. Decode39 described the signature as a step into US-led cooperation on [[Artificial intelligence]], trusted technologies, and resilient supply chains; Reuters and ANSA separately framed formal Pax Silica signing or accession as forthcoming.

Treat the status as inside the Pax Silica architecture but pending formal roster confirmation. The investment read-through is that Italy's Atlanticist role is broadening from defense and NATO logistics into technology supply-chain diplomacy: Rome can become a European node in the [[State Department]] trusted-tech ecosystem, but the tension with EU strategic autonomy remains unresolved.
