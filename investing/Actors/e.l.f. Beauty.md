# e.l.f. Beauty

e.l.f. Beauty is a mass-market beauty company known for "dupe" positioning — offering products that closely replicate prestige brands at a fraction of the price. NYSE: ELF. The company's $1B acquisition of [[Rhode]] ([[Hailey Bieber]]'s skincare brand) in May 2025 was the largest creator-brand acquisition in history, signaling e.l.f.'s ambition to move beyond value cosmetics into premium skincare. Revenue reached ~$1.3B in FY2025 (ended Mar 2025), with 23 consecutive quarters of net sales growth.

## Business profile

e.l.f.'s core strategy is offering prestige-quality cosmetics at drugstore prices, typically 50-70% below comparable products from [[Estee Lauder]] or [[L'Oreal]]. The brand built its following through TikTok virality and Gen-Z engagement — it was among the first beauty brands to launch a TikTok campaign (2019). International expansion (UK, Canada) and the skincare push via Rhode represent the next growth vectors. The risk is that the Rhode acquisition's $1B price tag ($212M TTM revenue, ~4.7x) requires sustained growth to justify.
