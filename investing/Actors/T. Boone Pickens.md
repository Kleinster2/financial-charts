# T. Boone Pickens

Texas oilman, corporate raider, and one of the defining figures of the 1980s hostile-takeover era — alongside [[Carl Icahn]] — whose unsolicited bids against bloated US conglomerates pressured corporate boards into either breaking up or accepting takeovers. Pickens's Mesa Petroleum-led runs at Gulf Oil, Phillips Petroleum, and Unocal in 1983-85 are the canonical case studies that motivated [[Marty Lipton]]'s 1982 [[Poison pill]] and the broader takeover-defence architecture that [[Wachtell Lipton]] built its M&A franchise around.
