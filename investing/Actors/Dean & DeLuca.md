Dean & DeLuca was an iconic NYC gourmet grocery chain founded in 1977 in SoHo by Joel Dean and Giorgio DeLuca. The store pioneered the concept of the upscale food hall in America — displaying cheeses, olive oils, and specialty ingredients as luxury goods rather than staples, influencing a generation of food retail from [[Whole Foods]] to Eataly. At its peak, the brand operated stores across the US, Japan, and the Middle East, with the SoHo flagship on Broadway becoming a tourist destination and a symbol of Manhattan food culture.

The brand's decline began after Thai developer Pace Development acquired majority control in 2014, paying roughly $140M for the right to expand globally. Pace loaded the business with debt while pursuing rapid international expansion that never generated adequate returns. By 2019, vendors were suing over unpaid invoices, the SoHo flagship closed, and employees were left without severance. Dean & DeLuca formally ceased US operations in 2020. Its collapse left a gap in the NYC premium grocery market now being addressed by [[Meadow Lane]], [[Erewhon]], Happier Grocery, and other entrants.
