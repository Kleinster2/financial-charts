**Evan Spiegel** is co-founder and CEO of [[Snap]], the company behind Snapchat, and one of the youngest self-made billionaires in tech history.

## Synopsis

Spiegel co-founded Snapchat at Stanford in 2011 with Bobby Murphy and Reggie Brown (who was later pushed out and settled for $157.5M). The core insight — ephemeral messaging that disappears after viewing — created a new communication paradigm that [[Facebook]] tried to acquire for $3B in 2013; Spiegel's refusal remains one of the most consequential turns in social media history. He took [[Snap]] public in 2017 at $17/share ($24B valuation) with a dual-class structure that gave him and Murphy control, then watched the stock crater to $5 as Instagram Stories cloned the core product and user growth stalled. The stock has traded in a wide range ($5-83) since IPO, reflecting the market's oscillating confidence in Spiegel's ability to build a sustainable business beyond the core messaging app.

Spiegel's relevance to investors is as a product visionary with an uneven business track record. He has consistently been ahead on product (Stories, AR lenses, Spectacles, Spotlight short video), but [[Snap]] has struggled to convert cultural relevance into durable revenue growth against [[Meta]]'s ad targeting superiority. The AR glasses bet (Spectacles) represents Spiegel's longest-running thesis — that computing will move to the face — but the category has consumed billions in R&D with no commercial traction. His personal net worth (~$3-4B) is almost entirely tied to [[Snap]] stock, making him deeply aligned with shareholders but also creating existential risk if he's wrong on AR.
