**Federation of German Industries (BDI)** — Umbrella industry association representing ~100,000 German industrial companies across sectors. Central political voice for German manufacturing; its presidents have historically been among the most influential non-political figures in Berlin. In early 2026, the BDI leadership publicly declared [[Germany]] in the deepest industrial crisis of the Federal Republic era.

## 2026 crisis framing

BDI president has characterized the current downturn as the "deepest crisis in the history of the Federal Republic" — a formulation that aligns with the data: four consecutive years of industrial production decline, manufacturing share of GDP at ~19.4% (near multi-decade lows), and ~124,000 industrial jobs lost in the 12 months ending Sept 2025.

BDI's advocacy themes:
- Electricity price relief for energy-intensive industry (steel, chemicals)
- Accelerated permitting reform
- Corporate tax competitiveness versus [[United States]] and [[China]]
- Nuclear re-evaluation (politically constrained)

BDI supported the 2025 [[Schuldenbremse reform]] and €500B infrastructure fund in principle but has warned against deployment into social transfers rather than productive investment — echoing the [[Deutsche Bank]] and German Council of Economic Experts skeptic case.
