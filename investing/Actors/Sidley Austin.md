# Sidley Austin

## Synopsis

[[Sidley Austin]] is a global Am Law 100 firm headquartered in Chicago and New York with a top-10 US revenue position. The firm operates a broad-spectrum corporate-and-litigation practice — M&A, capital markets, banking, restructuring, antitrust, life sciences, white-collar — across the Americas, Europe, and Asia. In the [[Big Law transformation]] framework, Sidley sits in the scale-plus-pay tier alongside [[Latham & Watkins]] and [[Kirkland & Ellis]] — an integrated multi-office firm with a non-lockstep compensation model — though it has not been at the centre of the Wachtell-side partner-poaching narrative documented in the May 2026 FT cluster.

Sidley appeared in [[Debevoise]]'s February 2026 restructuring lateral push as one of three source firms ([[DLA Piper]], Sidley, Cooley) where Debevoise hired partners to build out its restructuring practice — a bidirectional flow signal that scale-plus-pay firms are themselves laterals targets when elite-NY firms expand specific practices.

## Position in Big Law transformation

Sidley operates in the scale-plus-pay cohort that the [[Big Law transformation]] thesis treats as the structural beneficiary of the Wachtell / Cravath / Debevoise partner-flow cycle. The firm has not been the *primary* destination for Wachtell's 2025-26 partner exodus (the May 2026 FT identified [[Kirkland & Ellis]] and [[Latham & Watkins]] as the dominant capture firms), but Sidley's scale and broad practice mix make it a natural secondary destination for partners who want non-lockstep economics with a less-dominant individual brand.

[[Debevoise]]'s February 2026 hires *from* Sidley Austin (alongside [[DLA Piper]] and Cooley) for restructuring partners are the bidirectional signal: scale-plus-pay firms are themselves vulnerable to lateral departures into the modified-lockstep / non-equity-tier tier as elite-NY firms expand specific practice areas. The pattern is consistent with the broader thesis that the legal market is rebalancing across the entire elite cohort, not only at the lockstep-holdout end.
