**Francisco Gomes Neto** is CEO of [[Embraer]] since May 2019, leading the company's turnaround after the collapsed [[Boeing]] joint venture and overseeing a stock price recovery from single digits to all-time highs.

## Synopsis

Gomes Neto inherited [[Embraer]] at its nadir. The [[Boeing]] joint venture for commercial aviation -- which would have created a new entity combining Boeing's scale with Embraer's regional jet expertise -- collapsed in April 2020 when Boeing walked away, citing Embraer's failure to satisfy closing conditions (Embraer sued, arguing Boeing used COVID as a pretext to escape a deal it regretted). The termination left Embraer without a partner, hemorrhaging cash, and with a stock price below $5. Gomes Neto's response was a restructuring that cut headcount, consolidated operations, and refocused on three growth verticals: defense (Super Tucano, C-390 Millennium), executive aviation (Phenom and Praetor jets), and the E2 next-generation regional jet family.

The results have been striking. Embraer's backlog rebuilt to record levels ($22B+ by 2025), driven by the E195-E2's positioning as the most efficient narrowbody for routes under 150 seats -- a niche neither [[Boeing]] nor [[Airbus]] serves well. The defense division gained traction as [[Brazil]]'s military modernized and NATO allies sought non-US alternatives. The stock went from ~$4 in 2020 to $50+ by early 2026, a 10x+ recovery. Gomes Neto's tenure is a case study in post-crisis industrial turnaround: no financial engineering, no dramatic pivots, just operational discipline and backlog execution. The question now is whether he can sustain growth as supply chain constraints limit E2 delivery ramp and the eVTOL bet ([[Eve Air Mobility]]) remains pre-revenue.
