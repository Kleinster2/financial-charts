**Plug Power** — Hydrogen fuel cell company. Material handling (forklifts), [[Green hydrogen]] production. Struggling financially despite big vision.

Plug matters because it is still the clearest public-market attempt to build a vertically integrated hydrogen company: fuel cells, electrolyzers, hydrogen production, delivery, and storage-adjacent [[Infrastructure]]. That strategic ambition keeps the stock interesting, but the market still treats every project win through the filter of cash burn, funding risk, and whether hydrogen demand is becoming commercial fast enough to matter.

## 2026 project momentum

### Apr 2, 2026 — [[Hy2gen]] Courant FEED award

Plug said it won the FEED contract to supply a 275 MW GenEco PEM electrolyzer system for [[Hy2gen]] Canada's Courant project in [[Canada]]. The facility is intended to use low-carbon hydro power in Québec to produce low-carbon ammonia and then renewable ammonium nitrate for mining demand.

Readthrough: this is the kind of project award Plug still needs to keep proving the electrolyzer story is real at industrial scale, not just a warehouse-fuel-cell legacy business with a hydrogen narrative attached.

### Apr 15, 2026 — [[H2CAST]] cavern-fill milestone

Plug said the German [[H2CAST]] pilot reached full cavern fill after its earlier hydrogen deliveries and follow-on contract, with roughly 90 metric tons transferred into the caverns overall. The more important point is not the headline tonnage by itself, but that the story moved from hydrogen delivery into demonstrated underground [[Storage]].

Readthrough: this strengthens Plug's claim that its [[Europe]] business can span production, logistics, and storage-adjacent [[Infrastructure]] rather than only selling equipment.

### Apr 20, 2026 — squeeze readthrough

The Apr 20 move now reads less like a completely unexplained spike and more like a high-beta squeeze on top of two recent project validations: a large electrolyzer award and a fresh storage milestone. That still does not fix Plug's liquidity and cash-burn problem, so it looks more like improved news flow than a clean fundamental rerating.

## Evolution

- 2000s-2010s: Plug built its base business around hydrogen fuel cells for warehouse and material-handling fleets, especially with large retailers like [[Amazon]] and [[Walmart]].
- 2020-2021: the market re-rated Plug as a broader hydrogen-platform story, not just a forklift-fuel-cell vendor, and the stock became a flagship speculative name in the clean-hydrogen trade.
- 2022-2025: the integrated-hydrogen buildout proved much harder than the bull case assumed. Revenue grew, but losses deepened, capital needs stayed high, and the equity story turned into a balance-sheet and execution debate.
- 2026: recent wins like [[Hy2gen]] and [[H2CAST]] show the industrial project pipeline is still alive, but the stock continues to trade more on financing confidence than on backlog alone.
