**Liquid AI** — [[MIT]] CSAIL spinout (2023) commercialising [[Liquid neural networks]] as general-purpose foundation models that run on edge devices. Founders [[Ramin Hasani]] (CEO), Mathias Lechner (CTO), Alexander Amini, and [[Daniela Rus]].

The company's wager is that the [[Transformer|transformer]] is not the last word in model architecture. Its Liquid Foundation Models run multimodally on phones, laptops, and vehicles at a fraction of transformer compute, and the December 2024 Series A — $250M led by chipmaker [[AMD]] at a ~$2.4bn valuation — reads as a strategic bet on efficient edge inference rather than datacentre scale.

## What it is building

Liquid Foundation Models (LFMs) — multimodal general-purpose models built on the [[Liquid neural networks|liquid architecture]] rather than the standard transformer, designed to run on phones, laptops, wearables, vehicles, and appliances. The pitch is transformer-class capability at a fraction of the compute and memory, which is why [[AMD]] — betting on efficient edge inference over GPU-cluster scale — led the Series A. First LFMs (1B, 3B, 40B-MoE) shipped September 2024; general availability targeted from 2025.
