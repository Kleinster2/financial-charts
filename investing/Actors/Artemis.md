## Lunar Gateway — paused (Mar 2026)

[[Lunar Gateway]] was paused by [[Jared Isaacman]] as part of the Q1 2026 architectural restructure, with hardware to be repurposed for surface use. Prior plan:

| Module | Contractor | Pre-pause status |
|--------|------------|------------------|
| PPE (Power) | [[Maxar]] | Integration testing |
| HALO (Habitat) | [[Northrop Grumman]] | Stress testing complete |
| I-HAB | Thales Alenia Space | Development |

Pre-pause launch was Dec 2027 on [[SpaceX]] Falcon Heavy. Under the new architecture, astronauts dock [[Orion]] directly with [[Starship]] in Earth orbit and Starship carries the capsule toward the lunar surface. Gateway PPE/HALO investments may be redeployed as surface modules.

## Permanent lunar base architecture (Mar 25, 2026)

Isaacman announced a $20B over 7-year program to build a permanent base near the lunar south pole featuring habitats, pressurized rovers, and nuclear power systems. Two crewed landings per year. [[Lunar Outpost]] (Justin Cyrus) framed the scope shift: "Artemis base camp went from estimated $3B to $30B major infrastructure activity over the next 5 years to build the first permanent outpost on another planetary body" — meaning the surface program is now ~10x its prior scope.

Starship-native economy enables the scope change: prior architecture brought 50-100 kg per landing to the surface; Starship brings tons per landing — power, communications, habitats, launch/landing pads, surface infrastructure. The cost differential per [[Voyager]]'s 10K disclosure for [[Star Lab]]: $90M / $900/kg for Starship versus $4B+/flight for [[Boeing]] SLS.

| Element | Contractor | Status (Q1 2026) |
|---------|------------|------------------|
| Surface base / habitats | TBD (rebid) | $20B over 7 years |
| Surface mobility (rovers) | [[Lunar Outpost]] et al. | Many rovers needed (Cyrus, Apr 2026) |
| Lunar lander #1 | [[SpaceX]] Starship HLS | Active |
| Lunar lander #2 | [[Blue Origin]] Blue Moon | Active |
| Nuclear power | Multiple programs | Surface power systems |
