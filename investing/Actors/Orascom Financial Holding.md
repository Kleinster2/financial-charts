**Orascom Financial Holding** is an Egypt-listed financial-services investment company, now described in market profiles as O B Financial Holding, that was created from the financial-services spinout of [[Orascom Investment Holding]] and trades on the [[Egyptian Exchange]] as OFH.CA.

The note matters for [[HOF Capital]] because [[Onsi Sawiris]] is not merely adjacent to the Sawiris/Orascom network; he was named chairman of OFH at its listing/spinout. That gives the HOF founder graph a concrete operating-capital bridge into Egyptian fintech, consumer finance, and non-bank financial-services assets rather than a generic "family network" reference.

## Synopsis

OFH was created to house Orascom's financial-services investments separately from the broader Orascom Investment Holding portfolio. The 2021 listing announcement framed the company as a technology-driven financial-services investor targeting fintech, consumer-centric finance, and financial inclusion in Egypt. At launch, the transferred portfolio included a minority stake in [[Sarwa Capital]] and a majority stake in [[Beltone Financial Holding]].

The company then became part of a second transaction in 2024. [[B Investments]] acquired roughly 70% of OFH through a non-cash share-swap mandatory tender offer valued at EGP 1.46B, according to Grant Thornton's advisory announcement. B Investments' 1H24 materials describe OFH as a financial-services investment company with three main assets: about 29.25% of [[Contact Financial Holding]], 100% of [[Klivvr]], and a cash balance of roughly EGP 401M.

The HOF read is that OFH is not a venture-style asset; it is the Egyptian operating-finance leg of the Onsi Sawiris network. It shows why HOF's LP-network/commercial-access pitch should be read through actual financial-services operating assets, not only through US venture logos.

## Evolution

2020: [[Orascom Investment Holding]]'s board approved separating financial-services operations so shareholders could target those assets more directly.

2021: OFH began trading on the [[Egyptian Exchange]] under OFH.CA after the demerger. The company positioned itself as a tech-driven financial-services investor focused on financial inclusion, [[Fintech]], and consumer-centric products.

2024: [[B Investments]] acquired roughly 70% of OFH through a non-cash share-swap mandatory tender offer. That transaction made OFH part of B Investments' portfolio and reframed the asset base around [[Contact Financial Holding]], [[Klivvr]], and cash.

2025: Market profiles describe the company as O B Financial Holding after a January 2025 name change, while legacy OFH/Orascom references remain important for the HOF founder graph.

## HOF relevance

The useful HOF read is concrete: [[Onsi Sawiris]]'s OFH chairmanship means HOF's "global LP and operating network" pitch is connected to actual financial-services assets in Egypt. OFH touches consumer finance, structured finance, fintech payments, and local capital-market governance. That is exactly the kind of non-US operating context that explains why HOF markets itself as a network platform rather than a conventional venture shop.
