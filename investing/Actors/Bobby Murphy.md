**Bobby Murphy** — Co-founder and CTO of [[Snap]], responsible for the company's engineering architecture and AR platform since its founding in 2011.

---

## Synopsis

Murphy co-founded [[Snap]] alongside [[Evan Spiegel]] while both were students at [[Stanford]], building the original disappearing-photo app that became Snapchat. As CTO, he has overseen the technical evolution from a messaging novelty to an AR platform — Snap's Lens Studio and AR try-on tools represent the company's strongest moat against [[Meta]]'s Reels onslaught. Murphy keeps a notably low public profile compared to Spiegel, rarely speaking at conferences or doing press, which makes him one of tech's most quietly powerful CTOs.

His economic stake in [[Snap]] is substantial — Murphy holds Class C supervoting shares alongside Spiegel, giving the two co-founders effective control of the company regardless of the public float. This dual-class structure has been both a governance concern for institutional investors and the reason Snap has been able to pursue long-term AR bets despite persistent pressure on ad revenue from [[Meta]] and [[TikTok]]. At Snap's ~$15-18B market cap range, Murphy's stake represents a multi-billion-dollar position tied entirely to Snap's ability to monetize its 400M+ daily active user base.
