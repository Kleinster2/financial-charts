#actor #person #founder #philanthropist #china #neuroscience

Tianqiao Chen — Chinese-born entrepreneur, investor, and prolific neuroscience philanthropist. Founder of [[Shanda Games]] (one of China's first major online-gaming companies, IPO 2004); briefly the richest man in China during the mid-2000s gaming boom. Now headquartered in Singapore, Chen runs [[Shanda Group]] as a private investment vehicle and has redirected most of his fortune toward neuroscience research via the [[Tianqiao and Chrissy Chen Institute|TCCI]] (with wife Chrissy Luo) and the [[Chen Institute at Caltech]] for Brain Research. Co-founder and chairman of [[Gestala]] (non-invasive ultrasound BCI, January 2026).

## Trajectory

Shanda Games (Shanghai-headquartered MMORPG operator) was a pioneer of the Chinese online-gaming industry, riding the broadband-rollout wave of the mid-2000s. Chen took the company public in 2004 and was at one point briefly the wealthiest individual in China. Through the 2010s he progressively shifted away from operational tech and toward investment + philanthropy, with neuroscience emerging as the dominant focus by ~2015-2016. The pivotal commitment was the December 2016 $115M gift to Caltech that established the [[Chen Institute at Caltech|Chen Institute for Brain Research]] — at the time among the largest single gifts to a US university for neuroscience.

In China, the [[Tianqiao and Chrissy Chen Institute]] (TCCI) sponsors basic neuroscience research and clinician-investigator programs, with explicit emphasis on translating basic-science discoveries into mental-health and BCI applications. Chen's stated framing: "the brain is the last frontier."

## BCI investing

By 2025-2026 Chen had moved beyond philanthropy into operating-company investment in the BCI sector. Key investments / co-foundings:

- **[[Gestala]] (January 2026)** — co-founder and chairman of Phoenix Peng's non-invasive ultrasound BCI startup. Closed a $21.6M angel round at a $100-200M post-money valuation in March 2026 — the largest early-stage BCI funding round in China. The pairing of Chen (capital + scientific network) with Peng (operational BCI experience from prior co-founding [[NeuroXess]]) was structural to the round's oversubscription.

The Gestala chairmanship is the most operationally engaged role Chen has taken since exiting Shanda Games' operational management. The thesis underpinning it — non-invasive ultrasound as the next-generation BCI substrate — aligns with TCCI's basic-research portfolio while moving Chen into a position that monetizes the alignment commercially.

## Public profile

Chen is intensely media-shy by Chinese-billionaire standards; the Bloomberg "The China Show" 4/28/2026 broadcast referenced him via [[Phoenix Peng]]'s framing during the Gestala interview rather than directly. Peng described Chen on-air as "previously the richest man in China and he invest heavily both in neuroscience and AI" — the cleanest current public-press reference to Chen's current investment focus.

The combination of Singapore-domicile + Caltech-philanthropy + China-operational BCI investment positions Chen across the US-China research diplomacy axis at a moment when that axis is structurally tightening (per [[Singapore washing]] / [[NDRC]] / [[BIOSECURE Act]] dynamics). Whether his cross-border philanthropy + investment posture survives the offshore-corridor closure is an open question for 2026-2027.
