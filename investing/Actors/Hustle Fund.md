# Hustle Fund

Pre-seed and seed-stage venture capital firm founded by Elizabeth Yin and Eric Bahn in 2017. Known for high-velocity, small-check investing — the firm writes $25K-$200K checks into 100+ companies per year, betting on volume and speed rather than deep diligence at the earliest stages.

## Synopsis

Hustle Fund operates at the opposite end of the VC spectrum from firms like [[Andreessen Horowitz]] or [[Sequoia Capital]]. Where those firms concentrate large checks into a few deals with extensive diligence, Hustle Fund deploys small checks rapidly, treating pre-seed investing as a numbers game. The logic is that at the earliest stages, signal-to-noise is so low that spending weeks on diligence for a $50K check destroys more value than it creates. Elizabeth Yin (ex-500 Startups, ex-LaunchBit founder) and Eric Bahn (ex-Hustle Inc., ex-Beat the GMAT founder) built the firm around this thesis, raising multiple funds totaling $50M+ in AUM across their core and angel-sidecar vehicles.

The firm's portfolio includes [[Sacra]] (private company research), and hundreds of other pre-seed bets across SaaS, fintech, e-commerce, and creator economy startups. Hustle Fund also runs educational content and community programs (Angel Squad, an angel investing course) that function as both deal flow and revenue. The model's returns depend on a few outliers from each vintage covering the losses on the majority — standard power-law dynamics, but amplified by the ultra-early stage and tiny check sizes. Yin has been vocal about democratizing venture access and published data on fund performance, which is unusually transparent for the VC industry.
