## Fiscal Q2 2026 results (Apr 28, 2026) — record inflows lift AUM to $1.68T

Franklin Resources reported fiscal Q2 2026 results that beat consensus on EPS and inflows materially. The stock closed +6.9% to $29.46 — a 5.2σ idiosyncratic move and the largest single-day percentage gain in over a year. The print resolved the question of whether the company could re-establish positive net flows after the [[Western Asset Management]] outflow drag of 2024-2025.

| Metric | Fiscal Q2 2026 | Fiscal Q2 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Net income | $268.2M | — |
| GAAP EPS | $0.49 | $0.29 |
| Adjusted EPS | $0.71 | $0.59 (Fiscal Q1) |
| Revenue | $2.29B | — |
| AUM | $1.68T | $1.54T |
| Long-term net inflows | $16.9B | — |
| Beat consensus | by $0.16 ($0.55 estimate) | — |

Notable: all asset classes saw record gross sales, signaling broad-based client demand — not concentrated in one product wrapper. The flows beat is the highest-quality leg of the print: BEN had been losing share to the [[Big Three]] passive players ([[BlackRock]], [[Vanguard]], [[State Street]]) and to [[Capital Group]] / [[T. Rowe Price]] in active equity for several quarters. Q2's broad strength reverses that narrative.

### What changed vs Fiscal Q1

In Fiscal Q1 (ending Dec 31, 2025), Franklin reported $28B long-term net inflows ex-Western Asset, $34.6B total ex-Western, AUM $1.68T. The Q2 number ($16.9B) is lower in absolute terms but the headline framing is "record" because it represents broad-based demand without Western Asset offsetting. The margin profile is materially better — adjusted EPS $0.71 vs $0.70 in Q1 on a slightly different mix.

### Strategic context — why the market re-rated

Three intersecting narratives moved the stock:

1. ETF / SMA / Canvas growth — Franklin's smaller, faster-growing wrappers continue to take share against the large active mutual funds where the company has been losing ground. Canvas (custom direct-indexing platform) has been one of the standout product launches of the past 18 months and the inflows confirm enterprise adoption.
2. Alternatives traction — Franklin's alternatives platform (post-Lexington Partners acquisition) is a growth anchor; the Apr 28 print included alternatives in the "record long-term net inflows" framing
3. Western Asset stabilization — the structural outflow problem that drove BEN to a discounted P/E vs peers in 2024-2025 has stopped getting worse; this is the read-through that rerated the stock from "trading at distressed multiple" to "trading at peer-cohort multiple"

### Read-through

- [[BlackRock]] — Franklin's $1.68T AUM is small vs BlackRock's ~$11T; BEN at +6.9% on flows news is a re-rating story not a market-share-shift story
- [[T. Rowe Price]] — direct active manager peer; T. Rowe's flows trajectory is the relevant comp
- [[Invesco]] / [[Janus Henderson]] — smaller active managers; the cohort re-rates together if market accepts that the active-management outflow trend is bottoming
- [[Lexington Partners]] — Franklin's secondaries/alternatives subsidiary; if alternatives flows continue at current pace, this becomes a third growth pillar alongside ETFs and SMAs
- [[Crusoe Energy]] / [[Databricks]] / [[Stripe]] — Franklin's private-tech portfolio is a fee/upside layer; the pre-IPO portfolio doesn't drive the headline AUM but matters for the long-tail return profile

### Watch for

- Western Asset franchise health — separate disclosure (assets, flows, performance) at next quarter
- Canvas (direct indexing) AUM update — independent disclosure
- ETF AUM share growth — relative position vs [[BlackRock iShares]] and [[Vanguard]] in active ETF wrappers
- Alternatives flow cadence — Lexington's secondaries platform is the next leg
- Pre-IPO portfolio markups/markdowns — Crusoe, Stripe, Databricks fair-value moves flow into BEN's mark-to-market
