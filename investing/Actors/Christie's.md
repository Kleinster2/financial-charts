# Christie's

World's second-largest auction house by total sales ($6.2B in 2025, +6%). Owned by the Pinault family through Groupe Artémis — the same family holding company that controls [[Kering]] (Gucci, Saint Laurent, Balenciaga). No direct investable exposure, but Christie's performance is a window into Pinault family capital allocation and an indirect read on [[Kering]]'s strategic direction. Founded 1766 in London.

## Ownership

Wholly owned by **Groupe Artémis**, the Pinault family holding company controlled by **François-Henri Pinault** (also CEO of [[Kering]]). Artémis acquired Christie's in **1998**. The holding sits alongside Kering, Stade Rennais FC, and other Pinault assets.

The Pinault connection means Christie's competes with LVMH-affiliated interests (Arnault family are major collectors; LVMH's Tiffany and Louis Vuitton overlap in ultra-luxury). No indication of an IPO or sale — the family treats Christie's as a prestige/cultural asset rather than a financial one.

## 2025 performance

**Regional split (auction sales)**: US 55% (up 7pp), Europe 31% (UK 18%, France 8%), Hong Kong 15% (down 2pp).

**Regional growth**: US +15%, UK +6%, France -3%, Hong Kong -5%.

**Buyers by origin**: Americas 41%, Europe 33%, Asia-Pacific 23% (down 3pp).

**Demographics**: 46% of new bidders/buyers were Gen Z or millennials. 81% of bids placed online. Luxury = 38% of new buyers; contemporary art = 33%.

## Investing relevance

- **No direct exposure** — private, family-held
- **Indirect via [[Kering]]** (EPA: KER) — same controlling family, but Christie's P&L does not flow through Kering financials
- **Competitive read**: Christie's vs. [[Sotheby's]] performance signals relative strength in [[Art market]]; Christie's public auction outpaced Sotheby's in 2024 but Sotheby's leapfrogged in 2025 total sales
- **Luxury signal**: 46% of new buyers Gen Z/millennial; luxury segment = 38% of new buyer acquisition — same demographic driving [[Luxury]] brand growth
