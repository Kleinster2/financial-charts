# Kjell Inge Røkke

Norwegian billionaire industrialist and chairman of [[Aker ASA]], the industrial investment conglomerate he built from a single trawler. Røkke was [[Norway]]'s richest person for decades before relocating to Lugano, [[Switzerland]] in 2022 after the government raised the [[Wealth tax]] from 0.85% to 1.1%. He wrote a widely publicized open letter to Aker's board describing the move as a "difficult choice" driven by an unsustainable tax burden on illiquid industrial holdings. The relocation made him the highest-profile example of Norwegian [[Capital flight]] — at least 50 wealthy Norwegians left the country in 2022 alone.

## Career arc

Started as a fisherman in the North Sea, buying his first trawler in the late 1970s. Built a fishing empire in the US Pacific Northwest before returning to Norway. Acquired a controlling stake in Aker in the mid-1990s and transformed it from a struggling shipyard into a diversified industrial group spanning oil services ([[Aker Solutions]], [[Aker BP]]), seafood ([[Aker BioMarine]]), and renewable energy. The [[REV Ocean]] research vessel, the world's largest, was his personal philanthropic project — launched 2024.
