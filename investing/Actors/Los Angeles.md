# Los Angeles

Los Angeles is the second-largest US city by population (~3.9M city, ~10M county) and a significant case study for municipal fiscal policy, particularly around homelessness spending and accountability. The city and county combined budget ~$1.8B annually for homelessness services (FY25-26) with limited measurable outcomes — the 2025 homeless count showed 72,308 in the county (-4% YoY) despite years of multi-billion-dollar investment. The [[Port of Los Angeles]], the nation's busiest container port, handles ~40% of US inbound container traffic alongside neighboring Long Beach.

## Investment relevance

LA matters to portfolios through several channels: the port complex is a direct indicator of US-China trade volumes and tariff impacts; the entertainment industry (Hollywood, streaming) anchors media sector valuations; the January 2025 wildfires exposed ~$30B+ in insured losses, stressing property insurance markets and accelerating insurer withdrawals from [[California]]. The city's chronic fiscal challenges — pension obligations, homelessness spending, infrastructure maintenance — make it a bellwether for [[Blue city fiscal crisis]] dynamics.
