**Bedrock Capital** — Early-stage venture firm built around a "narrative violation" thesis: the best investments hide inside widely held assumptions that turn out to be wrong. Co-founded in 2017 by Geoff Lewis (ex-[[Founders Fund]]), Eric Stromberg, and Anu Hariharan (ex-[[Andreessen Horowitz]]).

---

## Synopsis

Bedrock's investment philosophy centers on identifying startups whose success would require the market to be wrong about something fundamental. Lewis articulated this as "narrative violation" — the idea that consensus narratives (ride-sharing will commoditize, crypto is a fad, remote work won't scale) create structural mispricings in venture. The firm invests at seed and Series A, typically writing $1-5M initial checks with significant reserves for follow-on. AUM across multiple funds has grown past $1B.

The portfolio skews toward enterprise software, fintech, and frontier tech. The marquee investment is [[Rippling]] (Series B, Series D), where Bedrock backed Parker Conrad after the market wrote him off following his Zenefits ouster — a textbook narrative violation that has appreciated dramatically as Rippling grew into an $11B+ HR/IT platform. Other notable positions include Matterport (pre-SPAC) and various AI infrastructure plays. The firm's risk is concentration: narrative violation works spectacularly when right but produces zeros when the consensus was correct for good reason.
