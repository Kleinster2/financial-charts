**Russ Tedrake** — MIT professor and vice-president of robotics research at the [[Toyota Research Institute]]. One of the leading figures in robot learning; the TRI group he runs co-originated diffusion policies ([[Diffusion models (robotics)]]) and is building whole-body large behaviour models with [[Boston Dynamics]] for the Atlas humanoid.

## On the 2024 robot-learning inflection

From the FT visual story (Nov 20, 2024):

- On investor and industry interest: *"The floodgates have really opened"* — tech giants jumping in, start-ups springing up, *"everybody's optimistic that it's coming."* Context: more than $11bn of robotics and drone VC deals done by late October 2024, surpassing 2023's $9.72bn full-year total but below 2022's $13.23bn ([[PitchBook]]).
- On the data bottleneck: argues the proliferation of robot hardware — from factory floors to Roomba vacuums — should help fill the action-data gap constraining large behaviour models. The more robots exist, the more visual data of completed actions becomes available.
