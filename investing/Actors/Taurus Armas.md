**Taurus Armas** — Brazilian small-arms manufacturer based in São Leopoldo, [[Rio Grande do Sul]]. One of the world's largest producers of handguns by volume; substantial US market presence via Taurus Holdings (Bainbridge, GA). Listed on the [[B3]] under tickers TASA3 (common) and TASA4 (preferred); ADR trades over-the-counter as TAEKY. 52.67% controlled by [[CBC]] (Companhia Brasileira de Cartuchos) since 2015 (CADE-approved acquisition).

---

## Synopsis

Taurus is the most internationally visible Brazilian firearms brand, distinguished by deep US distribution (Taurus USA's plant in Bainbridge, GA, produces directly for the US civilian market) and by ownership entirely within the Brazilian-controlled [[CBC]] holding structure. The 2015 CBC acquisition resolved a multi-year period of corporate instability and operational turnaround pressure; under CBC ownership, the company has stabilized operations and expanded its product portfolio. The brand is the most recognizable Brazilian defense / firearms name in global civilian markets and is a meaningful contributor to Brazilian non-oil manufacturing exports.

Strategically, Taurus operates at the intersection of three threads relevant to the [[Brazilian defense industrial base]]: it provides domestic military and police small-arms supply (sidearms, submachine guns, shotguns), it generates US-market civilian revenue that hedges against Brazilian domestic-demand cyclicality, and it sits within a Brazilian-capital holding ([[CBC]]) that is itself an active acquirer of European ammunition assets (MEN, Sellier & Bellot) and a strategic minority shareholder in Colt CZ Group. The combined CBC-Taurus platform is the most globally integrated Brazilian-controlled defense industrial position.

## Investment frame

Taurus is interesting as a B3-listed exposure to US civilian firearms demand denominated in a Brazilian-controlled, BRL-hedged structure. Revenue is meaningfully sensitive to US political cycles around firearms (election years, federal-policy shifts) and to BRL-USD exchange rate dynamics (a weaker BRL benefits export economics). The company is a small-cap by Brazilian standards but offers idiosyncratic positioning that does not cleanly map to broader Brazilian industrials or defense-prime cohorts.
