**Corbin Capital Partners** is a multi-strategy hedge fund based in New York. Deputy CIO of credit John Cocke has been a vocal critic of private credit's liquidity mismatch problem.

## Synopsis

Corbin Capital operates across equity, credit, and event-driven strategies, managing several billion dollars in AUM. The firm was founded by Craig Perry in 2003 and has maintained a relatively low profile compared to larger multi-strategy peers like [[Citadel]] or [[Millennium Management]]. Its credit arm, overseen by deputy CIO John Cocke, focuses on structured credit, distressed situations, and capital structure arbitrage -- positioning that gives the firm a front-row seat to stress in credit markets.

Corbin's vault relevance comes primarily through Cocke's public warnings about the [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]]. His statement that "you cannot create liquidity from an illiquid asset class" crystallized the core risk in the retail private credit expansion: funds like those offered by [[Apollo]], [[Blackstone]], and [[KKR]] marketed interval funds and semi-liquid vehicles to retail investors holding inherently illiquid loans. When redemption requests exceeded expectations in 2025-2026, the structural mismatch became visible. Corbin's perspective matters because multi-strategy funds like it stand to benefit from private credit distress -- they can buy discounted loan books from forced sellers -- making Cocke's warnings both analytically credible and self-interested.
