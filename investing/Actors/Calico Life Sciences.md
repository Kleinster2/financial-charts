#actor #biotech #longevity #healthcare #private

**Calico Life Sciences** is [[Alphabet]]'s aging-biology research company, founded in September 2013 and led by Arthur Levinson, former CEO of [[Genentech]]. It became part of [[Alphabet]] in the 2015 Google restructuring. Calico is the deep-pocketed incumbent of the [[Longevity]] cohort — research-led and historically opaque about its therapeutic pipeline.

Calico predates the current reprogramming wave and is structured as a long-horizon research bet inside [[Alphabet]] rather than a venture-backed clinical-stage company. The wind-down of the AbbVie collaboration removes a major external validation/funding leg and refocuses the question of how much therapeutic output the program has produced after a decade.

## Funding history

Calico is not venture-funded; it is bankrolled by [[Alphabet]] plus a long-running AbbVie research alliance.

| Round / commitment | Date | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| AbbVie collaboration | Sep 2014 | up to $1.5B combined | $250M each initially; costs/profits shared |
| AbbVie extension | Jun 2018 | +$1B ($500M each) | Alliance reported to reach ~$3.5B total |
| [[Alphabet]] equity | 2013– | Undisclosed | Google/Alphabet founding and ongoing backing |

The AbbVie alliance is being wound down (not extended), removing the largest external funding/validation leg.
