# John Ternus

Senior Vice President of Hardware Engineering at [[Apple]], overseeing the products that generate ~80% of Apple's revenue. At 50, he is 15 years younger than [[Tim Cook]] and at least a decade younger than every other member of Apple's executive team — making him the frontrunner to become Apple's next CEO.

---

## Synopsis

Ternus has been at [[Apple]] for 25 years, rising through hardware engineering to SVP around 2021. His portfolio now extends beyond hardware to include both hardware and software design teams — a consolidation of authority that gives him the broadest product mandate of any Apple executive. He launched integrated hardware-software products, managed the transition to [[Apple Silicon]], and oversees the AI device pipeline including the [[Apple Watch]], N50 smart glasses, and AI Pin. [[Mark Gurman]] (Bloomberg, Mar 23, 2026) identifies him as the clear successor to [[Tim Cook]], with "all signs" pointing to Ternus.

The succession logic is partly demographic. Cook is 65. [[Craig Federighi]] (late 50s) has publicly stated he does not want the CEO role. [[Eddy Cue]] (services) and [[Greg Joswiak]] (marketing) are both over 60. [[Jeff Williams]], the former COO who had been groomed for the role via expanded product and design responsibilities, retired at the end of 2025. The new CFO ([[Kevan Parekh]]) is relatively young but Apple has never promoted a CFO to CEO. The new COO is ~60. Every plausible internal candidate has either aged out, left, or declined — except Ternus.

Apple's track record with external hires is poor. [[Angela Ahrendts]] (retail, from Burberry) lasted five years. Carol Surface (chief people officer) lasted two. [[John Giannandrea]] (from [[Google]]) was characterized as "a complete disaster" by Gurman. Apple functions like a family business — succession stays internal.

Ternus is known for political acumen, empowering leaders beneath him, and getting deep into technical details. Gurman frames him as a "maestro of engineering" — a different archetype from Jobs (visionary/designer) and Cook (operations/supply chain), but a continuity pick in the sense that he has worked under Cook for well over a decade. Apple has been positioning him for the role: keynote appearances, meetings with government regulators, press tours at global Apple campuses.

- Empowering leaders under him
- Getting into technical weeds — hands-on engineering engagement
- Bringing devices from concept to market

## Missteps

Two notable hardware failures occurred under Ternus when he ran Mac Hardware Engineering in the mid-2010s:

| Product | Issue |
|---------|-------|
| Butterfly keyboard | Mechanism broke frequently, required multiple redesigns, became a class-action lawsuit |
| Touch Bar | Replaced function keys with OLED strip; widely disliked, eventually removed |

Gurman's defense: both were pushed by Apple's design team at the time — Ternus was the "engineering implementer" executing on design mandates, not the originator. Since Ternus became SVP and took oversight of both hardware and software design teams, product quality has "improved dramatically" (MacBook Air/Pro durability, iPhone build quality).

## Succession timeline

Cook departure timing directly determines Ternus's path:

| Scenario | Cook departs | Ternus age | Runway as CEO |
|----------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| Near-term | 2027 | 51 | 15-20 years |
| Mid-range | 2029 | 53 | 15+ years |
| Extended | 2031 | 55 | 10-15 years |

The longer Cook stays, the stronger Ternus's position becomes — all other candidates age out further. Gurman estimates Cook departure in 1-5 years.
