Central Bank of Ireland — [[Ireland]]'s central bank and financial regulator, part of the [[ECB|Eurosystem]]. Sets monetary policy via the [[ECB]] Governing Council; supervises Irish-domiciled banks, fund managers, and insurers under the [[EU]]'s Single Supervisory Mechanism. Post-[[Brexit]] it has become a key [[EU]] passporting gateway for [[UK]] financial firms — granting EU banking licences enables those firms to operate across the [[EU]] from a single Irish hub.

[[Monzo]] secured its EU banking licence here in December 2025, enabling the Spanish and Irish outposts opened in 2026. The CBI has been the licensing route for several other UK-origin firms expanding into the EU after [[Brexit]].
