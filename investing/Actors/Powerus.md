# Powerus

Roll-up of three small drone companies formed in 2025, going public via reverse merger with [[Aureus Greenway Holdings]] (Nasdaq: AGH), a Trump-family-backed golf course holding company. Expected to trade as **PUSA** on Nasdaq by summer 2026. The company is backed by Eric Trump and Donald Trump Jr. through American Ventures and plans to target the Pentagon's $1.1B [[Drone Dominance]] initiative. The conflict-of-interest angle is the defining feature: the president's sons are investing in a company seeking contracts from the president's Pentagon, financed through entities the Trump family already has stakes in.

## Synopsis

Powerus is a drone roll-up with no disclosed revenue, a CEO with no drone industry experience, and $9M in initial working capital — but it has two things that matter: Brett Velicovich's genuine special operations credentials and Ukrainian drone connections, and the Trump family's financial backing at the exact moment the Pentagon is ramping drone procurement from $0 to $1.1B. The company acquired three small subsidiaries ([[Kaizen Aerospace]], [[Tandem Defense]], [[Agile Autonomy]]) over six months and builds heavy-lift UAS (500lb+ payloads), tactical defense platforms, and maritime surveillance/autonomous vessel conversions. The 10,000 drones/month production target would exceed the entire US industry's current combined output (~8,300/month) — a claim that deserves heavy skepticism given the $9M capital raise. The real question isn't whether the tech works — it's whether Powerus wins Pentagon contracts on merit or on proximity to the White House. [[DroneXL]] called it plainly: "Expect Powerus to announce its first Pentagon contract within 12 months of listing."

## The conflict-of-interest structure

The layers are worth naming plainly:

1. **The Trump brothers** invest in Powerus via American Ventures
2. **Powerus** seeks contracts from the **Pentagon**, which the Trump brothers' father oversees as Commander-in-Chief
3. **Dominari Securities** — where both Trump brothers hold ~6% — is the financial adviser and placement agent
4. **Unusual Machines** — where Trump Jr. is a shareholder and adviser — is both an investor in Powerus and reportedly a Powerus customer/supplier
5. **No blind trust** — Trump gave his adult sons control of his businesses during his second term; ethics experts say this is insufficient protection against conflicts

This is the second Trump family drone deal in a month. Eric Trump backed [[XTEND]] (Israeli drone maker) in a $1.5B reverse merger with JFB Construction in February 2026.

**Broader pattern:** Financial Times analysis found at least four portfolio companies of [[1789 Capital]] (VC firm where Trump Jr. is a partner) won $735M+ in Trump administration contracts in 2026, including a $620M Pentagon loan to Vulcan Elements (rare earth magnet startup with ~30 employees at the time).

## Ukrainian technology pipeline

Velicovich has been involved in funneling drone technology to Ukrainian forces since early 2022. Powerus is pursuing acquisitions or technology licenses from Ukrainian drone makers to white-label and build domestically.

Velicovich to WSJ: "There does need to be an American face in front of it or behind it."

This is the standard US defense roll-up playbook: source foreign technology, re-badge as domestic, sell to the Pentagon under "Made in America" requirements. Legal, potentially effective, but the procurement selection process becomes hard to evaluate when the company is backed by the president's family.

A US-Ukraine drone technology transfer arrangement has been under discussion at the government level for over a year.

## Skepticism

- **10,000 drones/month** would exceed the entire US industry's current combined output (~8,300/month across all manufacturers). $9M doesn't build factories at that scale.
- **CEO has no drone experience** — Andrew Fox ran a facilities management company for 30 years.
- **No disclosed revenue, margins, backlog, or customer base.**
- **Three acquisitions in six months** with no disclosed terms — classic roll-up opacity.
- **The reverse merger vehicle** (AGH) was a sub-$1 golf course holding company. This is a shell company transaction.
- Velicovich's Fox News segment showed Ukrainian drone footage while describing it as American technology — gap between media persona and operational ground truth.
