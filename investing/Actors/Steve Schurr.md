## Synopsis

Schurr is the canonical 2025 illustration of how the multi-manager pay arms-race works. A former [[Financial Times|FT]] hedge fund correspondent who crossed to the buy-side via Kynikos and [[Point72]], rose to Balyasny's leadership team in 2023 as senior MD of Fundamental Equities, then was poached by [[Millennium]] for ~$100mn against a competitive bidding field. The package follows the standard [[Pod shop compensation|pod-shop template]] (cash advance + team budget + carry + reported accelerator) and the move arrived during Balyasny's broader equities-division rebuild. The FT used the deal as the lead for its September 22, 2025 explainer "How top hedge funds can pay traders $100mn" — making Schurr's career, more than any other recent move, the public exemplar of nine-figure PM economics. Begins at Millennium in 2026 after one-year garden leave.

## The 2025 Millennium move

Bloomberg first reported on April 29, 2025 that [[Millennium]] had agreed a deal with Schurr including upfront compensation, performance-based carry, and team-hiring budgets, with potential payout exceeding $100mn. The package structure followed the standard [[Pod shop compensation|multi-manager template]]: cash advance for foregone earnings and garden-leave time, team budget to recruit analysts, profit-share contract, and reportedly an accelerator clause on first-tranche gains.

The move was significant in two directions:

For [[Balyasny]], Schurr's departure came at a delicate moment — the firm was working on revamping its equities division and Schurr had been a leadership-team member running the Fundamental Equities book. His exit triggered a broader rebuild of the equities team.

For [[Millennium]], Schurr's arrival fits the standard [[Izzy Englander]] strategy of paying top-of-market to absorb proven PMs from peer pod shops. The talent flow from Balyasny → Millennium had multiple instances in 2024-2025 beyond Schurr.

The $100mn figure became the framing data point in the [[Financial Times|FT]]'s September 22, 2025 explainer "How top hedge funds can pay traders $100mn," which used Schurr's deal as the opening illustration before walking through the structural mechanics of pod-shop pay. See [[Pod shop compensation]] for the full breakdown.

## Style and reputation

Equities specialist with deep fundamental research orientation. The career arc through Kynikos (short-selling), Point72 (long/short equity), and Balyasny (multi-strat fundamental equities) suggests strength in single-name research with both long and short conviction. The leadership-team promotion at Balyasny in 2023 signalled the firm viewed him as a team-builder, not just a single-pod producer — which is consistent with the team-hiring budget reportedly included in the Millennium package.

The FT-background detail is unusual for the industry. Most senior PMs come up through the buy-side; Schurr's prior career as a journalist covering hedge funds gave him an unusually broad view of the industry's structure before he entered it. The fact that the FT used his move as the framing for an industry explainer suggests his transition has remained a topic of industry interest.
