**Varda Space Industries** is a private space-manufacturing company building free-flying orbital capsules that process materials in microgravity and return them to Earth. Its vault relevance for [[HOF Capital]] is the dual-use overlap: the same reentry platform that can process pharmaceuticals also creates a high-cadence hypersonic test environment for government customers.

Varda turns lower launch costs into an industrial-space thesis. The company was founded in January 2021 by [[Will Bruey]] and [[Delian Asparouhov]], with backing from [[Khosla Ventures]], [[Lux Capital]], [[Founders Fund]], Caffeinated Capital, [[General Catalyst]], and Also Capital. Its first capsule, W-1, grew Form III ritonavir crystals in orbit and reentered in February 2024; later W-series missions shifted the platform toward repeatable government reentry and hypersonic-test work. That makes Varda a useful bridge between [[Space]], pharma, and defense-tech private capital.

## Why it matters

Varda represents the convergence of space, pharma, and defense - three sectors that rarely overlap. The same reentry vehicle technology that returns drugs from orbit can also serve as a hypersonic testbed. This dual-use nature makes it attractive to both commercial pharma customers and defense customers that need more repeatable high-speed flight data.

Part of the broader defense tech wave emanating from the [[Peter Thiel]]/[[Founders Fund]] network — alongside [[Anduril]], [[Palantir]], and Hadrian.

It is a test of HOF's "frontier technology plus strategic network" pitch: the company needs capital, government/customer access, technical credibility, and downstream pharma/commercial relationships. That is exactly the kind of multi-sided commercialization problem HOF says its LP network can help solve.

## Evolution

2021: Varda was founded by [[Will Bruey]] and [[Delian Asparouhov]] to industrialize low Earth orbit. The strategic insight was that reusable launch had lowered access costs enough for the question to shift from "can we get to space?" to "what can we make or test there?"

2023-2024: W-1 launched in June 2023 and reentered in February 2024 after processing ritonavir crystals in orbit. That mission gave Varda the proof point it needed: a private capsule could process material in orbit and return it through a regulated reentry path.

2024: The company announced a $90M Series B after W-1, shifting the story from concept to scaling orbital pharma manufacturing and reentry infrastructure. For HOF, this is the stage where capital, technical execution, and customer-development networks all start to matter.

2025-2026: Later W-series missions moved Varda deeper into defense-adjacent reentry testing. W-5 launched in November 2025 and reentered in January 2026 with government testbed framing, reinforcing that Varda's platform is dual-use: pharma processing on one side, hypersonic/reentry data on the other.
