# AdaniConneX

AdaniConneX is the 50:50 Indian data-center joint venture between [[Adani Group]] and [[EdgeConneX]]. It is the Adani ecosystem's dedicated vehicle for turning land, power, construction, renewable-energy access, and global data-center operating expertise into hyperscale and edge data-center capacity across [[India]].

The investor read is infrastructure capture, not model capture. AdaniConneX can be an important beneficiary of Indian AI and cloud demand even if the model weights and cloud economics remain with foreign hyperscalers such as [[Google Cloud]].

The joint venture exists because neither side alone had the full stack. Adani contributes the local physical-infrastructure layer; EdgeConneX contributes the hyperscale data-center operating model and customer-facing expertise.

The financing pattern matters because Indian data centers are not only a demand story. They are a project-finance problem: land, permits, grid access, cooling, clean-power procurement, construction execution, and bankable hyperscaler demand all have to line up.

## Evolution

AdaniConneX began in 2021 as the Adani / EdgeConneX vehicle for Indian data centers, with a 1 GW target and launch markets in Chennai, Navi Mumbai, Noida, Vizag, and Hyderabad. The first strategic problem was not AI but national cloud and data-demand growth.

By 2023-2024, the company had moved from platform concept to project-finance execution: $213M for Noida and Chennai, then a larger sustainability-linked framework with an $875M initial commitment and a $1.44B ceiling.

The 2026 [[Google Cloud]] Vizag AI hub turns that platform into an AI-infrastructure actor. The same construction, renewable-power, and data-center capabilities now sit inside the [[India AI]] producer-vs-tenant debate.

## Google Vizag AI hub

AdaniConneX is one of two named local infrastructure partners for [[Google Cloud]]'s $15B, 2026-2030 Visakhapatnam AI hub, alongside [[Nxtra]] by Airtel. [[Google Cloud]] says the hub includes three data-center campuses, expanded fiber, cable-landing infrastructure, and a long-term clean-energy strategy.

This is the concrete side of the [[India AI]] buildout. AdaniConneX helps make compute physically possible in India; it does not by itself prove that India owns the AI model layer. That is why the company belongs in both [[AI infrastructure deals]] and the "producer vs tenant" debate in [[India AI]].

## Grid-risk read-through

[[Asia Tech Lens]]' May 2026 India data-center grid-risk note sharpens why AdaniConneX matters. Its edge is not only Indian land and data-center construction. It is the ability to package land, power access, renewable procurement, construction execution, and bankable hyperscaler demand into powered capacity.

The risk is the same point in reverse: a 1 GW platform target is only valuable if capacity is deliverable at the site level. Transmission delays, renewable curtailment, and local grid-quality problems mean AdaniConneX's power and interconnection execution are central to the [[India AI]] read, not implementation details.
