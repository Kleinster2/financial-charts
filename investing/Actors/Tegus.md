# Tegus

Expert network and primary research platform serving institutional investors, competing with [[AlphaSense]], [[GLG]], and [[Sacra]] in the $2B+ expert network market. Differentiates through a library model — pre-recorded interview transcripts searchable by topic, company, and expert — rather than the traditional brokered-call model.

## Synopsis

Tegus was founded in 2018 in Chicago and has grown rapidly by building a searchable database of expert interview transcripts alongside on-demand expert calls. The platform targets hedge funds, private equity firms, and corporate strategy teams who need primary research without the friction and cost of scheduling individual expert calls through legacy networks like [[GLG]] or Guidepoint. The library model creates a compounding data asset: every transcript added makes the platform more valuable, and the marginal cost of serving an additional user approaches zero.

The competitive landscape shifted in 2023-2024 as [[AlphaSense]] raised $650M+ and began integrating AI-powered search and summarization across its own expert transcript library. Tegus responded by layering AI features over its corpus, but the real question is whether the expert network business becomes commoditized by LLMs that can synthesize public information well enough to reduce demand for expensive primary research. For now, the $200K+ annual contracts suggest institutional buyers still value proprietary expert access, but the AI overhang is real.
