Philips is a Dutch health technology company (AMS: PHIA, NYSE: PHG) that transformed from a sprawling electronics conglomerate into a focused medical devices and health informatics business through two decades of divestments. Spun off its semiconductor division as [[NXP]] in 2006, its lighting division as [[Signify]] in 2016, and its domestic appliances unit to Hillhouse Capital in 2021. The 2020-2023 Respironics CPAP/ventilator recall — affecting 5.5 million devices with degrading foam — resulted in a $1.1B US settlement, a consent decree with the FDA, and wiped roughly 70% off the share price from 2020 highs. Under CEO Roy Jakobs (since October 2022), the company is executing a turnaround focused on image-guided therapy, patient monitoring, and connected care.
