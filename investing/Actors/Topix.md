# Topix

The Tokyo Stock Price Index — [[Japan]]'s broadest equity benchmark, covering all common stocks listed on the first section of the [[Tokyo Stock Exchange]] (roughly 2,000+ names before the 2022 market restructuring and still the widest-scope index post-restructuring). Weighted by free-float-adjusted market capitalization. Sits alongside the price-weighted Nikkei 225 as the two headline Japanese benchmarks, but is the preferred reference for institutional allocation and index funds because it captures the full market rather than a curated 225-name basket. USD-accessible proxies include [[EWJ]] (broad Japan) and [[DXJ]] (currency-hedged).

## Synopsis

Topix is the clearest read on the Japanese equity story: corporate governance reform, the end of deflation, and the gradual pivot from dollar diversification flows. Its broad sectoral composition — banks, autos, industrials, trading houses, technology — makes it less susceptible than Nikkei to single-name distortions (Fast Retailing dominates Nikkei due to price weighting, for example). Topix rose roughly 16% YTD through Feb 27, 2026, extending a 2025 run driven by [[BOJ]] rate normalization, [[Tokyo Stock Exchange]] pressure on companies trading below book value, and record retail inflows through the new [[NISA]] tax-advantaged accounts.

The Iran strike exposed the positioning. Topix fell roughly 8% in the week after Feb 27 — less severe than [[Kospi]]'s -19% but sharp enough to erase more than a quarter of YTD gains in five sessions. The drivers were familiar: [[Japan]] is a net energy importer (direct terms-of-trade hit from the oil spike), the [[Japanese yen]] moved on contradictory haven-vs-import signals (see [[Iran conflict FX dynamics#Yen: caught between haven and energy]]), and the broader "sell the winners" de-risking swept Japanese equities alongside Korean and European peers. The positioning-squeeze mechanism documented in [[2026 Iran conflict market impact#Global equity dispersion — popular trades unwind (FT, Mar 4)|the FT's cross-country dispersion data]] hit Topix through the same channel as every other non-US winner.
