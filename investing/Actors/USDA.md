**USDA** (United States Department of Agriculture) is the federal cabinet department responsible for agriculture policy, food safety, rural development, and forestry, with outsized influence on commodity markets through its crop reports and lending programs.

## Synopsis

The USDA operates at the intersection of agricultural policy and financial markets. Its monthly WASDE (World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates) reports move grain, oilseed, and livestock futures globally — a single revision to corn yield estimates can shift billions in commodity values within minutes. The department's Farm Service Agency (FSA) serves as lender of last resort for farmers who cannot access [[Farm Credit System]] or commercial bank credit, though FSA holds less than 4% of total farm debt. USDA also administers SNAP (food stamps), which at ~$110B/yr is by far the department's largest line item and a critical demand driver for the food retail chain.

Under the [[Trump]] administration (2025-), Secretary Brooke Rollins oversees a department facing DOGE-era budget pressure and deregulatory priorities. The tension is between farm-state Republican constituencies who depend on USDA programs (crop insurance, conservation payments, rural broadband subsidies) and the administration's cost-cutting agenda. For investors, USDA matters through three channels: commodity market-moving data releases, the SNAP program's impact on food retailers like [[Walmart]] and [[Dollar General]], and agricultural lending policy that affects the [[Farm Credit System]] and rural bank portfolios. The [[Avian Influenza]] response (flock depopulation orders, indemnity payments) is a current example of USDA's direct market impact — its decisions determine the timeline for egg and poultry price normalization.
