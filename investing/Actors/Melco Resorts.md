**Melco Resorts & Entertainment** — Macau-based casino operator controlled by [[Lawrence Ho]] via [[Melco International]] (200.HK, 51.2% ownership). Operates City of Dreams Macau, Studio City, Altira, plus properties in Manila, Cyprus, and Sri Lanka. Market cap ~$2.5B — the cheapest of the six Macau concessionaires by a wide margin. FY2025 revenue: $5.16B.

---

## Synopsis

Melco is the Ho family's second act in Macau gaming. [[Lawrence Ho]] — son of [[Stanley Ho]] (by second wife Lucina Laam) — built the company from a JV with [[James Packer]]'s [[Crown Resorts]] into an independent concessionaire, spending $900M in 2006 to acquire the last of Macau's six gaming licenses. The Crown partnership eventually dissolved and the company rebranded from Melco Crown to Melco Resorts. While [[SJM Holdings]] (run by Lawrence's half-sister [[Daisy Ho]]) carries the Stanley Ho legacy, Lawrence built his own operation focused on the premium mass market and geographic diversification.

FY2025 delivered $5.16B revenue (+11%) and $1.43B group property EBITDA (+17%), with Macau property EBITDA up 25% to $1.23B. But the stock tells a different story: at ~$6.42, MLCO trades at just ~$2.5B market cap — less than half its $5.16B annual revenue and a fraction of peers. The discount reflects $6.75B in total debt, limited free cash flow after heavy capex (Studio City Phase 2 cost $1.2B, City of Dreams Sri Lanka cost $1.2B), and Lawrence Ho's strategy of geographic expansion into smaller markets (Cyprus, Sri Lanka, Philippines) rather than Singapore or Las Vegas. The analyst consensus is bullish (9 buys, 0 sells, average target $10.28), implying the market is mispricing the Macau recovery.

The geographic expansion is the differentiator. City of Dreams Mediterranean in Cyprus (opened July 2023) is Europe's first integrated resort with a 30-year license and 15-year exclusivity. City of Dreams Sri Lanka (opened August 2025, $1.2B) is pitched as South Asia's gateway — Lawrence Ho has said "Sri Lanka can be to India what Macau is to China." If that thesis pans out against India's ~1.4 billion population, it's transformative. If not, it's two expensive resorts in small markets funded by Macau cash flows.

## Evolution

The story of Melco Resorts is the story of a son building his own gaming empire in the shadow of his father's — separate from, and in competition with, the Stanley Ho dynasty's original [[SJM Holdings]].

- **2004**: [[Lawrence Ho]]'s [[Melco International]] partnered with [[James Packer]]'s [[Crown Resorts]] to form Melco Crown Entertainment. The JV was a bet that a young, Western-connected operator could compete in Macau against the established concessionaires.

- **2006**: Melco Crown spent $900M to purchase the last of Macau's six gaming sub-concessions from [[Wynn Resorts]]. This was the entry ticket — without a concession, no amount of capital could buy access to the world's largest gaming market.

- **2007-2009**: Altira Macau opened in 2007 (VIP-focused, 14-time Forbes 5-star). City of Dreams Macau opened in 2009 — the flagship integrated resort on Cotai, later expanded with the Zaha Hadid-designed Morpheus tower, one of the most architecturally significant buildings in Asia.

- **2015**: City of Dreams Manila opened, extending the brand to the Philippines. Studio City Macau also opened — a Hollywood-themed resort that diversified away from the pure-VIP model toward premium mass.

- **2018-2020**: [[Stanley Ho]] retired as SJM chairman in June 2018 and died in May 2020. The family's gaming interests had long been divided: SJM Holdings (under half-sister [[Daisy Ho]]) retained the original concession, while Lawrence's Melco operated under its own license. Crown Resorts gradually exited the JV; the company rebranded to Melco Resorts & Entertainment.

- **2020-2022**: COVID devastated Macau gaming. Melco's revenue collapsed. The company used the downtime to invest: Studio City Phase 2 ($1.2B, Zaha Hadid Architects' Epic Tower, indoor waterpark) opened in late 2022/early 2023.

- **2022-2023**: New 10-year Macau gaming concession awarded effective January 1, 2023. Investment commitment: MOP 11.82B (~$1.46B), of which MOP 10B (~$1.24B) in non-gaming. Notably, Studio City Phase 2's $1.2B was NOT counted toward this pledge. City of Dreams Mediterranean opened in Cyprus July 2023 — Europe's first integrated resort, 30-year license with 15-year exclusivity.

- **2025**: City of Dreams Sri Lanka opened August 2, 2025 ($1.2B) — South Asia's first fully integrated resort. Revenue reached $5.16B, approaching Macau recovery maturity. The stock surged 53.6% in September 2024 on recovery signals but remains deeply discounted vs. peers.

- **Japan**: Originally bid for Osaka, dropped September 2019 to pivot to Yokohama with a potential $10B project. Abandoned Yokohama September 2021 after anti-IR candidate won the mayoral election. No active Japan bid remains.
