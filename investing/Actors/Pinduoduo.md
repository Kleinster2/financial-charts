# Pinduoduo

Chinese e-commerce platform founded 2015 by [[Colin Huang]] (黄峥), parent of [[Temu]] (international expansion launched September 2022) and the domestic Pinduoduo marketplace. Listed on [[Nasdaq]] as PDD in July 2018; reorganised as PDD Holdings (Ireland-domiciled) in 2023 to insulate group structure from US-China geopolitical risk. The group competes with [[Alibaba]] and [[JD.com]] as the third pillar of Chinese e-commerce — with a structural cost advantage from rural+lower-tier-city user base and a controversial supplier-pricing model.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. [[Common Prosperity]] enforcement-era case: among the major tech platforms hit by 2021-2022 regulatory action; ¥10bn donor pledge under the "third distribution" framing
2. [[Temu]] international expansion — sub-$ shipping model that has been the most disruptive new-entrant force in US/EU e-commerce 2023-2026; subject of [[Section 321]] de minimis policy debate
3. Cost-discipline competitor — sustained gross-margin advantage vs [[Alibaba]] Taobao and [[JD.com]] through team-buying mechanics and lower-tier-city distribution; the supplier-pricing model is the source of regulatory friction in both China (anti-monopoly) and the US (forced-labour scrutiny)
