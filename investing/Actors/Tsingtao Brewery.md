Tsingtao Brewery — One of the world's largest beer producers and the second-largest brewer in China by volume. Founded 1903 in Qingdao, Shandong, by German colonial settlers. Dual-listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange (600600.SS) and Hong Kong Stock Exchange (0168.HK). Iconic Chinese consumer brand with significant export presence; the canonical pre-1949 Chinese consumer brand that survived state ownership.

## Apr 28, 2026 — Q1 mixed, profit beat on margin expansion

Per Bloomberg "The China Show" 4/28/2026 broadcast, Tsingtao reported Q1 2026 net income of RMB 1.8B, +5% year-over-year and above analyst estimates. Revenue fell 1.5% YoY, missing estimates. The mix — declining revenue with rising profit — reflects margin expansion through pricing or cost discipline rather than volume growth, consistent with the broader [[China consumer]] sector dynamic where premiumization is offsetting weak headline volume in the post-2022 consumption-recovery cycle.

The print is one of multiple Q1 beats reported on the same broadcast, alongside [[WuXi AppTec]] (revenue +29%) and [[Montage Technology]] (net income +60%). The Tsingtao result is the cleanest read on Chinese consumer-staple resilience — beer is non-discretionary at the category level even when premiumization decisions tighten consumer pricing power.
