Latvia is a Baltic state and [[EU]] member with a population of approximately 1.8 million, situated between [[Estonia]] to the north and [[Lithuania]] to the south. It became the [[IEA]]'s 32nd member in 2024.

## Synopsis

Latvia's economy is small (~$45B GDP) but strategically positioned within the Baltic corridor that has become a frontline of [[NATO]]'s eastern deterrence posture. Since Russia's 2022 invasion of [[Ukraine]], Latvia has accelerated defense spending toward the NATO 2% target and deepened integration with Baltic neighbors on energy independence — completing the desynchronization of its electricity grid from the Russian system in 2025. The capital Riga serves as a regional services hub, particularly for fintech, IT outsourcing, and transit logistics connecting Scandinavia to Central Europe.

For investors, Latvia's relevance is primarily as a component of broader Baltic and European defense/infrastructure themes rather than a standalone equity market. The country's banking sector underwent a painful cleanup after the ABLV Bank money-laundering scandal (2018), which led to tighter AML enforcement across the Baltics. Latvia's adoption of the euro (2014) and EU/NATO membership provide institutional stability, but demographic decline (population has shrunk ~25% since 1990 from emigration) constrains long-term growth. The Riga real estate market and Baltic logistics corridors are the most investable angles, though liquidity is limited.
