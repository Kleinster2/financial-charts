**Aaron Levie** is the co-founder and CEO of [[Box]], a cloud content management and collaboration platform. He founded the company in 2005 while a student at the University of Southern California (which he dropped out of to run Box full-time). Levie took Box public on the NYSE in January 2015 at $14/share; the stock initially popped to $24 but spent years trading below IPO price as the market questioned whether Box could compete against [[Microsoft]] (SharePoint/OneDrive), [[Google]] (Drive), and [[Dropbox]].

Levie is known for his prolific social media commentary on enterprise software, AI, and cloud trends — one of the more visible SaaS CEO voices on X/Twitter. He has pushed Box toward AI-powered content management, integrating LLM capabilities (Box AI) to let enterprises search, summarize, and generate content across their stored documents. The AI pivot is an attempt to differentiate Box from commodity cloud storage and move the product toward higher-value knowledge management.

Box had revenue of ~$1.1B in FY2025. The company operates at roughly break-even to low single-digit operating margins, having spent years prioritizing growth over profitability before shifting under activist pressure.
