Aneel Bhusri is the co-founder of [[Workday]] and returned as sole CEO in February 2026 to steer the company through the [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] — the $285B software selloff triggered by [[Anthropic]]'s [[Claude Cowork]] launch. Bhusri co-founded Workday in 2005 with Dave Duffield after the pair lost control of [[PeopleSoft]] to [[Oracle]]'s hostile takeover. He served as co-CEO from IPO (2012) through 2022, then transitioned to executive chairman before coming back as the AI disruption threat demanded a founder's urgency.

Before Workday, Bhusri was a general partner at [[Greylock Partners]] (1999-2009) where he led investments in enterprise software. His return to the CEO seat echoed a familiar Silicon Valley pattern — founders reclaiming the helm when existential threats emerge — and signaled that Workday's board viewed the agentic AI disruption of HR and finance SaaS as a bet-the-company moment.
