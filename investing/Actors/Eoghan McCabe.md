# Eoghan McCabe

Co-founder and CEO of [[Intercom]]. Stepped down as CEO in 2020, returned October 2022, and restructured the company around AI-first customer service. Previously co-founded Contrast (software design consultancy) and Exceptional (bug-tracking tool, sold to Rackspace 2011).

## Synopsis

Eoghan McCabe is a serial Irish entrepreneur whose career arc illustrates the founder-return playbook. He co-founded [[Intercom]] in 2011 with [[Des Traynor]], Ciaran Lee, and David Barrett, building it into a $1.3B+ valued customer messaging platform serving 25,000+ businesses. He stepped back from the CEO role in 2020 (replaced by Karen Peacock), then returned in October 2022 after concluding the company had drifted from its product-led roots. The return coincided almost exactly with ChatGPT's launch, and McCabe pivoted Intercom's entire strategy toward AI-powered customer service.

The AI pivot has been aggressive and early by enterprise SaaS standards. Intercom launched Fin, an AI customer service agent powered by [[OpenAI]]'s models, in mid-2023 — well before most competitors had shipped AI features beyond cosmetic integrations. By 2025, Fin was resolving ~50% of support conversations for customers who activated it, which represents genuine product-market fit rather than demo-ware. McCabe has been vocal about AI replacing, not augmenting, human support agents — a position that's honest but politically uncomfortable in the SaaS world. The company raised at $1.3B post-money in its 2018 Series D (led by [[Kleiner Perkins]]) and has not disclosed a more recent valuation. Whether Intercom can re-rate as an "AI company" or remains a messaging tool with AI features is the core question.
