# Brendan Carr

Chairman of the [[Federal Communications Commission]] (FCC) since January 2025, appointed by [[Donald Trump]]. Previously served as FCC Commissioner from 2017-2025. Known for an aggressive regulatory stance on Big Tech and early advocacy for banning [[TikTok]].

---

## Synopsis

Carr represents the new breed of Republican tech regulator — less focused on traditional telecom deregulation and more on using the FCC's platform to challenge [[Big Tech]] dominance and national security risks from Chinese technology. He called for [[TikTok]]'s removal from app stores before Congress acted, framing it as a surveillance threat rather than a competition issue. His appointment as chairman signals the Trump administration's intent to use the FCC as a lever against tech platforms perceived as politically hostile or security risks.

The market relevance is primarily through regulatory uncertainty for social media and telecom companies. Carr's FCC has signaled interest in spectrum policy (affecting [[T-Mobile]], [[AT&T]], and satellite players like [[Starlink]]), content moderation rules (affecting [[Meta]], [[Alphabet]]), and foreign ownership restrictions. He attended the inaugural [[Hill and Valley Forum]] dinner in March 2023, placing him within the tech-national security nexus that increasingly drives telecom and platform regulation. For investors, Carr's tenure means the FCC is less a sleepy infrastructure regulator and more an active participant in the tech policy wars.
