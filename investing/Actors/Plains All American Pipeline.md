#actor #energy #midstream #pipeline

**Plains All American Pipeline** (NASDAQ: PAA) is one of the largest crude oil and NGL transportation, storage, and gathering operators in [[North America]], moving roughly 6 million barrels per day across ~18,000 miles of pipeline. Structured as a master limited partnership under general partner [[Plains GP Holdings]] (PAGP), which simplified its governance in 2019 to a single share class.

## Synopsis

Plains sits at the intersection of every barrel that moves from the [[Permian Basin]] to Gulf Coast export terminals and refining complexes. The partnership generates ~$2.6B in adjusted EBITDA (2024), overwhelmingly from its Crude Oil segment, with NGL and supply/logistics contributing smaller but steady fee-based streams. The Permian concentration is both the thesis and the risk: as long as US shale production grows, Plains captures volumetric upside on existing pipe without capex; if production plateaus, the entire growth narrative stalls. Distribution yield runs 7-8%, and the partnership has steadily deleveraged from 3.5x to sub-3x since the 2020 distribution cut.

The company's reputation carries permanent scar tissue from the May 2015 Refugio Beach oil spill, when Pipeline 324 ruptured near Santa Barbara and released ~120,000+ gallons of crude onto 3,700 acres of coastline. Plains was convicted on nine criminal counts, paying $3.3M in criminal fines, $61M in civil penalties, and $230M in lawsuit settlements. The incident killed pipeline expansion projects along the California coast for a generation and remains a case study in midstream operational risk.
