**Wonder Dynamics** is an AI visual effects tool for compositing CG characters into live-action footage. Co-founded by Nikola Todorovic and actor Tye Sheridan. Acquired by [[Autodesk]] in May 2024 and rebranded to Flow Studio.

## Synopsis

Wonder Dynamics attacked the most labor-intensive bottleneck in visual effects: the manual process of tracking, lighting, and compositing CG characters into live-action scenes. Their Wonder Studio tool automated much of this pipeline, allowing a single artist to do work that traditionally required a team of motion capture technicians, 3D animators, and compositors. The pre-acquisition product generated significant buzz in the indie film community, with Steven Spielberg and Joe Russo as advisors lending credibility.

[[Autodesk]]'s acquisition for an undisclosed sum (the $12.5M in pre-acquisition funding suggests a modest but meaningful exit) was a strategic play to embed AI-native VFX tools into the Maya/3ds Max ecosystem before competitors like [[Adobe]] or [[Unity]] could build equivalent capabilities. Rebranded as Autodesk Flow Studio, the technology now sits inside Autodesk's media and entertainment division. The deal exemplifies the acquire-to-integrate model in creative AI: startups prove the concept, incumbents buy the team and technology, and the standalone product disappears into a larger platform. For Autodesk investors, Flow Studio is part of the AI story that justifies premium multiples on a company that otherwise grows at low single digits.
