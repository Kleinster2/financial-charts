**Reserve Bank of India** is India's central bank. Governor: [[Sanjay Malhotra]] (Dec 2024–). Manages monetary policy, FX reserves (~$563B as of Mar 2026), and rupee intervention. Launched the most aggressive FX crackdown since 2013 in late March/early April 2026 — capping bank positions and banning NDF contracts to sever offshore speculation channels.

## April 2026 FX crackdown

Two-step intervention after the [[Indian rupee]] hit record low 95.21/USD on April 1, driven by [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]] oil shock and capital outflows:

1. **March 27:** Capped banks’ net open rupee positions at **$100M** (down from 25% of capital). Effective April 10.
2. **April 1:** Banned banks from offering rupee NDF contracts to any client. Banned rebooking of cancelled forwards. Required proof of hedging need for deliverable contracts. Barred related-party rupee derivatives.

Result: Rupee surged 1.8% on April 2 (best day since 2013), closing at 93.10. Estimated banking losses ₹4,000-5,000 crore ($470-590M), manageable vs ₹5.5 lakh crore sector PBT. See [[Indian rupee#April 2026 crackdown — "toughest measures in a decade"]] for full detail.

MPC meeting April 6-8: rates expected on hold.
