# Bonhams

Fourth-largest global auction house ($970M in 2025, -2%). Acquisitive under PE ownership — bought Skinner (US, 2022), Bukowskis (Nordics), Cornette de Saint Cyr (France). Operating in 24 countries. Owned by Epiris, a UK-based private equity firm, since 2018. Founded 1793 in London.

## Ownership & acquisitions

**Epiris** (formerly Electra Partners) acquired Bonhams in **2018**. Under PE ownership, Bonhams has pursued a buy-and-build strategy:

| Acquisition | Year | Geography | Rationale |
|-------------|------|-----------|-----------|
| Skinner | 2022 | US (Boston) | US regional presence |
| Bukowskis | — | Nordics | Scandinavian expansion |
| Cornette de Saint Cyr | — | France | Paris market access |

The acquisitive strategy aims to build scale against the Big 3 (Sotheby's, Christie's, Phillips). Bonhams now operates in 24 countries — broader geographic reach than Phillips.

## Investing relevance

- **No direct exposure** — PE-owned
- **PE roll-up model**: Epiris building toward an eventual exit (IPO or trade sale). Acquisitions suggest they're building scale for a liquidity event
- **Scale vs. Big 3**: At $970M, Bonhams is comparable to Phillips ($927M) but with broader geographic footprint
- **Hong Kong best since 2007**: +21% combined sales — counter to the HK contraction narrative at other houses
