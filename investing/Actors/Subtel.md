# Subtel

[[Chile]]'s Subsecretariat of Telecommunications, the regulatory body overseeing telecom infrastructure, spectrum allocation, and connectivity policy. Operates under the Ministry of Transport and Telecommunications. Responsible for licensing submarine cable landings, a role that placed it at the center of US-China tech competition in Latin America.

Two Subtel officials -- Undersecretary Claudio Araya and cabinet head Guillermo Petersen -- were targeted by [[US Chile cable visa sanctions]] (February 2026) over their roles in advancing the [[Chile-China Express]] submarine cable project. The sanctions marked a significant escalation in US efforts to block Chinese-built undersea cable infrastructure in the Americas, turning a bureaucratic telecom regulator into a geopolitical pressure point.
