**Switch** is a private US data-center operator founded by Rob Roy and owned by [[DigitalBridge]] and IFM, with [[SoftBank]] reportedly in acquisition talks around a ~$50B valuation as of December 2025.

Switch matters in the HOF graph because it links [[Fady Yacoub]] to data-center and infrastructure operating context before he co-founded [[HOF Capital]]. The company itself is a scarce powered-campus asset: energy-efficient designs, large hyperscale sites, and AI-density work make it relevant to the same power/capacity bottlenecks tracked in [[AI infrastructure financing]].

## Evolution

Pre-2022: Switch operated as a public data-center company built around large, energy-efficient campus designs.

2022: [[DigitalBridge]] and IFM took Switch private for $11B, turning it into an infrastructure/private-equity data-center platform.

2025: SoftBank acquisition talks reportedly valued Switch around ~$50B including debt, reflecting how AI infrastructure scarcity repriced powered data-center campuses.
