# Sumitomo Corp

One of [[Japan]]'s five major *sogo shosha* (general trading houses), traces lineage to the Sumitomo zaibatsu (founded 17th century; modern trading entity formed 1919). Diversified across metals, transport, infrastructure, media, real estate, mineral resources, and energy. One of the five trading houses [[Warren Buffett]] / [[Berkshire Hathaway]] accumulated stakes in starting 2019 (see [[Buffett Japan sogo shosha trade]]).

Distinct from [[SMBC|Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation]] (a separate Sumitomo-group bank) and [[Sumitomo Chemical]] (a separate Sumitomo-group chemicals company). The trading-house entity is the one Buffett bought.

## Why Sumitomo Corp matters

Together with [[Mitsubishi]], [[Mitsui]], [[Itochu]], and [[Marubeni]], Sumitomo Corp is one of the five Japanese trading houses operating at the intersection of physical commodity logistics, energy, and emerging-market industrial finance. The business model came back into market focus in 2024-2026 as the [[Law of one price]] retreated and basis between regional commodity grades widened. See [[Buffett Japan sogo shosha trade]] for the full investment frame.
