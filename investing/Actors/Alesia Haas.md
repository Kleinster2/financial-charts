**Alesia Haas** is CFO of [[Coinbase]], overseeing finance and investor relations for the largest U.S. crypto exchange through the industry's most volatile cycles, from the 2021 direct listing through the 2022 crypto winter to the 2024-2025 recovery.

## Synopsis

Haas joined [[Coinbase]] in 2018, prior to the company's April 2021 direct listing on Nasdaq — making her the architect of Coinbase's public market financial framework during the most turbulent period in crypto history. Her background includes Sculptor Capital Management (fka Och-Ziff, where she was CFO during its own crisis period) and OneWest Bank, giving her experience managing financial operations in environments where regulatory scrutiny and market volatility are constants. At Coinbase, she navigated the 2022 crypto winter (revenue dropped ~60% from $7.8B to $3.1B), the SEC enforcement action in 2023, and the subsequent recovery driven by the Bitcoin ETF approval cycle.

Haas has been increasingly vocal about [[Coinbase]]'s AI strategy, particularly [[AgentKit]] and the "agentic commerce" vision — the thesis that AI agents will need native crypto payment rails for autonomous transactions. She frames this as a multi-year bet that positions Coinbase at the intersection of the two largest tech cycles (AI and crypto). For investors, Haas matters as the financial discipline voice at a company historically prone to over-hiring in bull markets and painful cuts in downturns: Coinbase laid off ~20% of staff in 2022 and another 20% in early 2023. Her capital allocation decisions — stock buybacks initiated in 2024, the balance sheet management during crypto winter — determine whether Coinbase converts crypto cyclicality into long-term shareholder value or remains a leveraged bet on coin prices.
