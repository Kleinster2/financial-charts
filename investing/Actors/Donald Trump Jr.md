#actor #person #politics #usa

Donald Trump Jr. — Executive Vice President of the [[Trump Organization]] and partner at [[1789 Capital]]. Eldest son of President [[Donald Trump]] and the more publicly-active of the two Trump brothers managing the family's commercial portfolio during the second Trump term. ~6.28% shareholder of [[Dominari Holdings]] (combined ~12.56% with brother [[Eric Trump]]), advisory-board member at Dominari since late 2024, anchor LP across most [[American Ventures]] SPVs, shareholder and advisory-board member at [[Unusual Machines]], and partner at the politically-conservative VC firm [[1789 Capital]] whose portfolio companies have collectively won $735M+ in Trump-administration contracts in 2026.

## The Dominari relationship

Don Jr. and [[Eric Trump]] befriended [[Kyle Wool]], CEO of [[Dominari Securities]], in 2024. Both joined Dominari's advisory board in late 2024 around the same time. The brothers' combined ~12.56% stake plus advisory-board roles plus anchor-LP positions in American Ventures' SPVs are the central marketing wedge for the [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]]. The FT reported in May 2026 that Eric Trump and Don Jr "are anchor investors in most of [American Ventures'] deals" and that "Republican types affiliated with them" round out each SPV with ~$500K checks.

## 1789 Capital

Don Jr. is a partner at [[1789 Capital]], the politically-conservative VC firm founded by [[Omeed Malik]] in 2022. The firm gained attention for declining a nine-figure allocation to [[Anthropic]] in February 2026, citing ideological concerns. Per FT analysis, at least four 1789 portfolio companies won $735M+ in Trump administration contracts in 2026, including a $620M Pentagon loan to Vulcan Elements (a rare-earth-magnet startup with ~30 employees at the time of the loan). The firm is a structurally distinct vehicle from American Ventures: 1789 is a venture-capital fund deploying GP capital into early-stage equity; American Ventures is an SPV factory deploying LP capital into reverse-merger recaps.

## Conflicts framing

The structural argument from critics (mirroring the [[Lutnick family conflicts of interest]] template):
- The Trump brothers' commercial vehicles (Dominari, American Ventures, Unusual Machines, 1789 Capital) hold positions in companies that benefit from federal procurement, federal financing, or federal regulatory tailwinds
- The administration directs procurement, financing, and regulatory tailwinds toward sectors in which the brothers have positions
- Even if the brothers themselves are not directing those policy moves, the alignment compresses the time interval between policy signal and aligned-capital deployment
- The brothers' spokespeople characterize the positions as "passive" with no government interface; critics argue passive equity ownership and policy upside can coexist without operational involvement
