# Tarun Chhabra

Former [[NSC]] senior director for technology and national security, hired by [[Anthropic]] in a government affairs capacity. His recruitment became a political flashpoint during the Pentagon-Anthropic standoff of February 2026, with critics citing the revolving door between AI policy-making and AI companies.

## Synopsis

Chhabra served on the [[NSC]] during the [[Biden]] administration, where he was a principal architect of the [[United States]]' semiconductor export controls targeting [[China]]. His portfolio covered AI governance, chip restrictions, and technology competition policy — the exact regulatory surface area that [[Anthropic]] now navigates as a frontier AI lab. The hire placed him alongside other former government officials at AI companies, a pattern that includes [[Ben Buchanan]] (also at [[Anthropic]]) and various [[Google]] DeepMind and [[OpenAI]] government affairs hires.

The political liability surfaced in February 2026 when the Pentagon's evaluation of [[Anthropic]] for defense contracts drew scrutiny. Critics argued that hiring the architects of AI policy creates a regulatory capture dynamic — the people writing the rules walk across the street to the companies those rules govern. For [[Anthropic]], the calculus is straightforward: navigating Washington requires people who understand how policy is made. The risk is that each such hire becomes ammunition for competitors and skeptics questioning whether safety-focused AI labs are genuinely independent of the policy apparatus they helped create.
