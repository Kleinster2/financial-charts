**Metropolitan Park** — $8.1B integrated casino-entertainment complex at Willets Point, Queens, adjacent to Citi Field. JV between [[Steve Cohen]] (via New Green Willets LLC) and [[Hard Rock International]] ([[Seminole Tribe of Florida]]). Won one of three New York downstate casino licenses December 15, 2025 (30-year term). Target opening: June 2030.

---

## Synopsis

Metropolitan Park is the largest of the three approved New York City casino projects and the one with the most complex stakeholder web. [[Steve Cohen]] — who owns 97%+ of the [[NY Mets]] and controls the Citi Field parking lots through New Green Willets LLC — partnered with [[Hard Rock International]] (owned by the [[Seminole Tribe of Florida]]) to transform 50 acres of surface parking into a $8.1B casino resort, public parkland, and entertainment district. The applicant entity is Queens Future LLC, d/b/a Hard Rock at Metropolitan Park.

The project cleared every institutional hurdle: unanimous Community Advisory Committee vote (September 2025, the only NYC bid to achieve this), NYC Council zoning approval 41-2 (March 2025), unanimous GFLB recommendation (December 2025), and Gaming Commission license (December 2025). This happened despite significant grassroots opposition — 68.6% of Flushing Asian American voters opposed it (AALDEF/MinKwon survey), 30+ faith leaders representing 10,000+ congregants demanded rejection, and State Sen. [[Jessica Ramos]] fought it throughout.

The economics as projected: $3.9B annual revenue, $850M annual tax revenue by year 3 of stable operations, $33.5B in cumulative 30-year tax revenue. The $500M license fee is on a 30-year term. Construction prep began February 2026. The project includes 25 acres of public parkland, 450 affordable housing units in Corona, and a revamped ADA-accessible Mets-Willets Point 7-line subway station. Cohen's involvement is personal (not through [[Point72]]) — his $17-22B net worth provides backstop capacity that no other bidder could match.

## NYC casino license competition

New York authorized 3 downstate commercial casino licenses. Started with 11 bidders, narrowed to 3 winners.

### Winners (all approved December 15, 2025)

| Project | Operator | Location | Investment |
|---------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Metropolitan Park** | [[Hard Rock International]] / [[Steve Cohen]] | Willets Point, Queens | $8.1B |
| [[Resorts World NYC]] | [[Genting Group]] | Aqueduct, Queens | $5.5B |
| Bally's Bronx | [[Bally's Corporation]] | Ferry Point Park, Bronx | $4B |

### Voluntary withdrawals

| Bidder | Location | Why |
|--------|----------|-----|
| [[Las Vegas Sands]] | Nassau Coliseum, Long Island | iGaming risk, weak returns (Apr 2025) |
| [[Wynn Resorts]] / [[Related Companies]] | Hudson Yards, Manhattan | Community opposition (May 2025) |
| [[Saks Global]] | 611 Fifth Ave, Manhattan | No operator, landmark constraints (Apr 2025) |
| [[MGM Resorts]] | Empire City, Yonkers | Competitive clustering, 15-yr only (Oct 2025) |

### CAC rejections (all September 2025)

| Bidder | Location |
|--------|----------|
| [[SL Green]] / [[Caesars Entertainment]] / [[Roc Nation]] | Times Square (1515 Broadway) |
| [[Silverstein Properties]] / Rush Street / Greenwood | Near Hell's Kitchen |
| [[Soloviev Group]] / [[Mohegan]] | East Side near UN |
| [[Thor Equities]] / [[Chickasaw Nation]] | Coney Island |

Manhattan was completely shut out — all four Manhattan bids failed at the community level.
