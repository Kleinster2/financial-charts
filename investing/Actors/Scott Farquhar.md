Scott Farquhar is the co-founder of [[Atlassian]] (TEAM), the enterprise software company behind [[Jira]], Confluence, Trello, and Bitbucket. Co-founded the company with [[Mike Cannon-Brookes]] in 2002 in Sydney with $10,000 in credit card debt — no venture capital. Atlassian went public on the NASDAQ in December 2015 at a $4.4B valuation and grew to a $40B+ market cap. Farquhar served as co-CEO alongside Cannon-Brookes until stepping back in August 2024, making Cannon-Brookes sole CEO. One of Australia's wealthiest individuals with a net worth estimated at A$18B+. Known for the unusual co-CEO structure that ran Atlassian for over two decades, a governance model that is rare among tech companies of that scale.
