## FY2025 earnings (reported March 25, 2026)

Chewy reported FY2025 results that showed the customer relationship thesis playing out. Revenue grew 6.2% YoY (~8.3% adjusted for an extra week in FY2024). The key metric: NSPAC hit $591, up 2.2% — customers are spending more per year. Active customer count grew 4% YoY, the first meaningful expansion in multiple quarters. Autoship sales continue to accelerate, now over 76% of revenue. The company is profitable and FCF positive. FY2026 guidance: revenue growth of 8-9%, with net income expected to grow meaningfully. Stock surged 13.3% on the results.

The story is shifting from "can Chewy retain customers" to "can it expand share of wallet across pharmacy, vet telehealth, and premium pet services." Management appears to be delivering on the latter.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Pet spending resilient — recession-proof category
- Autoship creates subscription-like retention
- Pharmacy and vet telehealth expanding TAM
- Profitability improving — now FCF positive
- Customer growth returned (+4% YoY in FY2025)
- 8-9% revenue growth guided for FY2026

Bear:
- [[Amazon]] competition in pet supplies
- Margin pressure from food category (low margin)
- Customer acquisition cost rising
- Stock trades at premium to consumer peers
