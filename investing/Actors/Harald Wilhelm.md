# Harald Wilhelm

## Synopsis

[[Harald Wilhelm]] is the Chief Financial Officer of the [[Mercedes-Benz]] Group AG (formerly Daimler AG) — a position he has held since May 2019 after joining the Board of Management on April 1 of that year. Born April 23, 1966 in Munich, Wilhelm holds a Master of Business Administration from Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich (1991). His career bridges two of Germany's largest industrial complexes: Daimler-Benz Aerospace (1991-2000), where he ran M&A through the Aerospace consolidation that became EADS, and the [[Airbus]] Group (2000-2018), where he rose to CFO of both the Group and the Commercial Aircraft division through 2018. He returned to the Daimler / Mercedes-Benz orbit in 2019, immediately preceding the [[Daimler|Daimler-Mercedes carve-out]] that separated the truck business as Daimler Truck.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Mercedes-Benz]] Iran-war commodity commentary thread on the Q1 2026 earnings call. Wilhelm's quote — *"We do expect raw material [costs] to step up further in the remainder of the year, higher than what we anticipated at the beginning of the year"* — and his subsequent caveat that Mercedes "cannot rule out the possibility of bottlenecks in individual commodities" if the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]] continues for "a longer period" anchored the EU-OEM read of the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] alongside Detroit's parallel commentary from Ford's [[Sherry House]] and GM's [[Mary Barra]].

## Position in Mercedes / Iran-war commodity thread

Wilhelm's Q1 2026 earnings-call commentary was the cleanest EU-OEM articulation of the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] commodity transmission. Mercedes-Benz disclosed it had hedged most of its Q1 commodity exposure but flagged ~€1B 2026 cost step-up consistent with the BCG Waas 6-month supplier renegotiation lag documented in the broader thread. Wilhelm's "bottlenecks in individual commodities" caveat was the most direct CFO-level acknowledgment that EU OEMs see the [[Aluminum|aluminium]] +16% LME re-rating as durable through 2H 2026 absent supply restoration.

The implication for the EU-OEM cohort: Mercedes's hedge cover means Q1-Q2 2026 P&L impact is muted, but Q3-Q4 2026 and FY 2027 are where the commodity step-up concentrates. Wilhelm's investor-call posture across the next two quarters is the binding signal for whether the +16% aluminium re-rating fully passes through Mercedes's cost base or is partially absorbed by hedging gains.

## Career trajectory

Wilhelm's two-firm career — Aerospace + Auto — places him in a small group of European CFOs whose finance experience spans both industrial complexes. The Airbus tenure (2000-2018) covered the formation of EADS, the integration of Aerospatiale-Matra and DASA, the post-2008 commercial-aircraft super-cycle, and the A380 / A350 program-cost crises. The Daimler / Mercedes-Benz return in 2019 took him into the [[Daimler]] truck-spinoff (Daimler Truck), the COVID-period earnings cycle, the EV-strategy transition, and now the post-2024 Trump-II / [[2026 Iran conflict|Iran war]] commodity-shock window.
