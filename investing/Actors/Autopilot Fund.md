Autopilot Fund is a seed-stage [[venture capital]] firm, notable as an investor in [[Sacra]] (the private-company research platform).

## Synopsis

Autopilot Fund operates in the seed-stage VC segment, writing early checks into startups before product-market fit is established. The fund's investment in [[Sacra]] — a research platform that produces institutional-quality analysis on private companies, positioning itself as a "Bloomberg for private markets" — is representative of the thesis: backing infrastructure and tooling companies that serve the venture ecosystem itself. Sacra's model (subscription-based research on companies like Stripe, Databricks, Anduril before they go public) addresses a genuine information gap — private company data is fragmented across PitchBook, Crunchbase, and leaked decks, and none of those sources provide the analytical depth that public company investors take for granted.

Seed-stage VC is the highest-risk, highest-return segment of venture investing. Funds like Autopilot write small checks ($250K-$1M typically) into dozens of companies, expecting most to fail but needing one or two to return the fund many times over. The math works when the fund can access high-quality deal flow at reasonable valuations — which is why seed funds often specialize by sector or geography to develop proprietary sourcing advantages. Autopilot's portfolio positioning in venture infrastructure (tools for investors, founders, and operators) suggests a thesis around the professionalization of the startup ecosystem itself.
