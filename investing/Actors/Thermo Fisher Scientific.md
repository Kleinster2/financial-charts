# Thermo Fisher Scientific

**Thermo Fisher Scientific** is the world's largest life sciences instrumentation company, formed from the 2006 merger of Thermo Electron and Fisher Scientific. Operates across four segments: Life Sciences Solutions, Analytical Instruments, Specialty Diagnostics, and Laboratory Products & Biopharma Services.

## Q1 2026 results + end-market warning (Apr 23, 2026)

Thermo Fisher beat Q1 expectations and raised full-year profit guidance, but the stock sold off because management made clear that academic and government demand in the U.S. and [[China]] is not normalizing this year and because the company added an inflation cushion for the Iran conflict.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Adjusted EPS | $5.44 vs $5.24 consensus |
| FY2026 adjusted EPS guide | $24.64-$25.12, raised from $24.22-$24.80 |
| Benefit cited | Strong Q1 + Clario acquisition |
| U.S./China academic-government demand | Still below normalized levels |
| Management macro caveat | Placeholder added for future inflation risk tied to Iran war |
| Stock reaction | $466.70 close, -9.2% on Apr. 23 |

The market read the print as a quality-of-growth issue rather than a simple beat. Management said U.S. end markets should improve only modestly over the year, not return to normal, and finance chief Jim Meyer said the company felt it was appropriate to build a buffer into guidance for oil-driven cost pressure it may not be able to fully offset. That turns Thermo into a useful macro tell: one of the cleanest life-sciences franchises is still dealing with weak public-research funding and now has to price geopolitical inflation into guidance.
