## Why WHOOP matters

WHOOP tripled its valuation in one round — from ~$3.6B to $10.1B. The screenless wristband tracks recovery, strain, and sleep without a display, positioning it as a continuous health monitoring platform rather than a smartwatch competitor. The Abbott investment signals a push beyond fitness into clinical-grade health data.

## Product

Screenless wristband — no display, continuous biometric monitoring:
- Recovery score (HRV, resting heart rate, sleep)
- Strain tracking (cardiovascular load)
- Sleep coaching
- Subscription tiers: One ($199/yr), Peak ($239/yr), Life ($359/yr) — Life includes the WHOOP MG with FDA-cleared on-demand ECG and AFib detection
- Used by professional athletes, military, consumers

The Abbott partnership signals expansion from fitness tracking toward clinical health monitoring — potentially bridging consumer wearables and medical devices.

---

## Google Fitbit Air challenge (May 2026)

[[Google]] launched [[Fitbit Air]] on May 7, 2026 at $99 hardware with optional [[Google Health Premium]] subscription ($99/yr or free with [[Google AI Pro]]). Three-year total cost of ownership runs $99-$396 for Air vs $597-$1,077 for WHOOP. Stephen Curry headlines the Fitbit Air Special Edition, directly attacking WHOOP's "elite athletes wear it" brand moat.

WHOOP's defense levers:

- FDA-cleared on-demand ECG (WHOOP MG / Life tier) — Air offers only background AFib via PPG, not on-demand ECG. The clinical-tier differentiation holds.
- [[Abbott]] partnership signals — medical-device legitimacy that a consumer-AI funnel cannot match.
- Athlete brand depth — Curry endorsement is real but WHOOP has been worn by athletes across NFL, NBA, MLS, F1, military for years.

The structural risk: WHOOP's $10.1B valuation underwrites continued subscription growth at premium price points. A subscription-optional $99 alternative with Google distribution compresses the new-customer funnel; cutting WHOOP's subscription price to defend would break the unit-economics math the Series G underwrote. Full launch context: [[Fitbit Air launch May 2026]].
