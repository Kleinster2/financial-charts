Josh D'Amaro — CEO of [[Disney]] (announced February 2026; effective shortly before the FT's June 20 2026 profile). Previously Chairman of Disney Experiences (parks, cruises, products). Second former parks head to become CEO after [[Bob Chapek]] (who lasted less than three years, 2020-2022). Seen by many in Hollywood as a natural heir to [[Bob Iger]] in temperament and style.

## The parks pitch that made his name

During the pandemic, with parks closing worldwide and Disney "in a state of perpetual crisis" (Iger's words), D'Amaro took a binder to Iger laying out an aggressive parks-and-cruise investment plan. One page carried his pledge: "If you fund this, I can deliver results." Iger came down to his office, having torn that page out, and said: "We're going to do this." Iger signed off the $60bn parks investment plan in 2023; four new cruise ships are being built.

---

## Handover and the Iger desk

D'Amaro took over Iger's top-floor office at Disney headquarters in Burbank. He kept Iger's desk — a 1930s Kem Weber piece designed for the Disney Studios, with "cubby" drawers for animators — calling it "a story piece." Iger had the desk removed when he vacated the office for Chapek in 2021; on Iger's 2022 return it was restored, and D'Amaro insisted on keeping it.

D'Amaro's first memo to staff set out just three guidelines, two of them — great storytelling and technology — direct echoes of Iger's 2005 plan. His framing: "If you do what you did yesterday as a proxy for what the next day should look like, you're going to find yourself stuck or irrelevant."

## The succession question

Like Chapek, D'Amaro came from the parks division, and Hollywood remains suspicious of executives whose background is selling merchandise and tickets rather than "talent whispering." His lack of media experience is a worry for some executives. [[Steven Spielberg]] told the FT that Iger "can sit in a room with Ryan Coogler and Jon Favreau and JJ Abrams and myself, and it's like Bob is just simply one of us" — the implicit question is whether D'Amaro can speak that same creative language.

[[Bob Iger]] says D'Amaro has what it takes to win doubters over, and has pledged not to impose: "if you want advice, you call me... I'm not going to call to give you advice."

---

## Baptism of fire: the Kimmel/FCC escalation

D'Amaro's early tenure coincided with the escalation of the [[Jimmy Kimmel]] / Trump / FCC saga. After Kimmel's "Lady Trump" joke, [[Donald Trump]] told ABC to fire Kimmel and the FCC ordered Disney into an early ABC licence-renewal process. Disney stood firm; Iger said he "wholeheartedly" endorsed the stance. The episode is an early test of D'Amaro's handling of political pressure on Disney's [[ABC]] network.
