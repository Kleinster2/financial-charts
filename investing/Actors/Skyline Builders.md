Skyline Builders Group Holding Ltd. (Nasdaq: SKBL) — Hong Kong-based construction company that completed an early-2025 IPO underwritten by [[Dominari Securities]] and is now the listed shell for the reverse merger with [[Cove Kaz Capital]] (Kazakh tungsten developer) announced April 30, 2026. Named in the [[House Select Committee on China]]'s March 8, 2026 letter to Dominari requesting information on Chinese small-cap IPOs implicated in suspected ramp-and-dump manipulation. Stock dropped ~90% in a single trading session in July 2024 after listing.

## Pre-merger profile

Skyline Builders was a Hong Kong construction services company whose Nasdaq IPO in early 2025 was led by [[Dominari Securities]]. The company's operating business was modest in scale, and its stock traded thinly following the IPO. The single-session ~90% decline in July 2024 (note: the FT places this in 2024, which would predate the early-2025 IPO date — the stock-decline date may correspond to a pre-IPO ADR or a pricing event; precise date deserves verification) is the specific pattern the [[House Select Committee on China]] flagged as consistent with ramp-and-dump activity.

The committee's March 8, 2026 letter to Dominari Securities CEO [[Kyle Wool]] requested information on the firm's role facilitating IPOs of Chinese stocks implicated in suspected pump-and-dump schemes — the committee's specific term is "ramp-and-dump." Of 29 Chinese-issuer IPOs underwritten by Dominari since 2020, 11 (>30%) fit the manipulative profile per the committee.

## The Cove Kaz reverse merger (Apr 30, 2026)

Skyline serves as the public-listing vehicle for [[Cove Kaz Capital]]'s tungsten development. The structure follows the standard [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]]:
1. Skyline IPO (early 2025) creates the listed shell
2. American Ventures Series XIX SKBL + Series XXXII SKBL II raise capital from Dominari LP base
3. SPVs recapitalize Skyline, clean cap table, prepare for merger
4. Cove Kaz operating assets merge in
5. Combined entity inherits SKBL listing; carries $1.6bn EXIM/DFC letters of interest

Skyline's pre-existing reputational issues (House committee letter; July 2024 single-session decline) do not appear to have prevented the deal from being announced; whether they affect post-deal trading liquidity for the combined Cove Kaz entity is an open question.
