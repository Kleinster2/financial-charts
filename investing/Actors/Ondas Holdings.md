# Ondas Holdings

US-based provider of autonomous drone and industrial wireless connectivity systems (Nasdaq: ONDS). Operates through Ondas Autonomous Systems (drones, via subsidiary American Robotics) and Ondas Networks (industrial wireless). The drone unit develops the Optimus, Scout, and Raider Autonomous Drone Platforms. Top-ten holding in the [[DRNZ]] drone ETF and one of the few public small/mid-caps with platforms on the DCMA Blue List — the Pentagon's curated list of NDAA-compliant drone systems vetted for federal procurement.

## Key products

- Optimus System — automated drone-in-a-box for persistent autonomous fixed-site monitoring; received FAA waivers for beyond-visual-line-of-sight (BVLOS) automated operations without a pilot or visual observer; DCMA Blue List approved (2026).
- Scout System — military-grade autonomous drone (developed via Israeli AirObotics acquisition); used by Israeli Defense Forces.
- Raider — tactical drone platform.
- Ondas Networks — private wireless infrastructure for railroads, mining, critical industry (separate business unit).

## Investment context

Public-market proxy for the [[Replicator Initiative]] / [[Defense Autonomous Warfare Group]] thesis at the autonomous-platform layer (not the autonomy-stack-software layer, which is private — [[Anduril]] Lattice OS, [[Shield AI]] Hivemind). The DCMA Blue List inclusion is a structural credentialing event: Optimus is now in the catalog federal buyers can purchase from without standalone vetting.

The 605% revenue growth is from a small base ($7M to $50M); the more meaningful signal is the FY2026 forecast at $170-180M, which implies the company is transitioning from R&D / early deployment to commercial / DoD scale. Backlog growth (+180%) is consistent with that transition.
