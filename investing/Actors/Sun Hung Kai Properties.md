**Sun Hung Kai Properties** — [[Hong Kong]]'s largest property developer by market cap, with a diversified portfolio spanning residential, commercial, and retail properties across Hong Kong and mainland China. A core holding in the [[EWH]] ETF at roughly 4.4% weight.

---

## Synopsis

SHKP is the bellwether for Hong Kong real estate and, by extension, a proxy for confidence in the city's economic future. The company controls a land bank and investment property portfolio worth over HKD 700B, anchored by iconic developments like the International Commerce Centre (Hong Kong's tallest building) and a pipeline of residential projects. The Kwok family has controlled SHKP since its 1972 founding, giving it a longer strategic horizon than most developers — but also creating governance questions typical of Hong Kong family conglomerates.

The investment case for SHKP is inseparable from the macro backdrop. Hong Kong residential prices have fallen roughly 25% from their 2021 peak, hammered by the combination of rising US rates (which transmit directly via the [[Hong Kong dollar]] peg), mainland Chinese economic weakness reducing cross-border buying, and post-2019 emigration reducing local demand. SHKP trades at a steep discount to its net asset value — a persistent feature of HK developers that has widened further in the current downturn. The bull case rests on rate cuts (the Fed cutting would immediately ease HK mortgage costs via the peg) and a stabilization of mainland confidence. The bear case is that Hong Kong's structural role as a China gateway is permanently diminished, in which case the NAV discount is justified.
