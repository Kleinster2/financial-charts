**Banco Digimais** — Brazilian bank controlled by Bishop [[Edir Macedo]] ([[Igreja Universal do Reino de Deus]], [[Republicanos]] party). Formerly Banco Renner. Estimated negative equity of R$8.5 billion. Operating in the red for four years. Same playbook as [[Banco Master]] — inflated fund portfolios, above-market CDB rates, FGC guarantee as marketing hook. Direct fraud connection to Master through shared credit originators ([[Reag Investimentos]], [[Banco Master]]).

---

## Synopsis

Banco Digimais is Brazil's next banking crisis after [[Banco Master]] — and the two are directly connected. The bank, controlled by Bishop [[Edir Macedo]] (founder of the [[Igreja Universal do Reino de Deus]] and owner of TV Record), has an estimated negative equity of R$8.5 billion after four years of operating losses. Its modus operandi mirrors Master's: offering CDB rates up to 125% of CDI, using the [[FGC]] guarantee as a lure, distributing through [[XP Inc]] and [[BTG Pactual]] platforms, and inflating fund portfolios to improve the balance sheet.

The fraud connection to Master is direct. An audit of the EXP1 fund — a R$720 million FIDC structured by Digimais — revealed that 22,000 of 55,000 credit contracts (R$500M of R$650M) were fictitious, originated by [[Reag Investimentos]] and [[Banco Master]]. When confronted, Digimais confirmed the credits didn't exist. The broker administering Digimais's funds (ID corretora) has ex-Master executives among its partners and is suspected of absorbing Reag's 600+ funds after Reag's liquidation.

The political dimension adds a layer Master didn't have: Macedo's [[Republicanos]] party controls a significant portion of the evangelical caucus (bancada evangélica) in Congress. A hard regulatory action against Digimais in 2026 — an election year — risks provoking the evangelical wing of the Centrão, which Lula needs to compete against Flávio Bolsonaro. The bank has also hired [[Aldemir Bendine]] (ex-Banco do Brasil, ex-Petrobras president, arrested in [[Lava Jato]]) as president — seen as a political bridge to the PT-aligned Central Bank under [[Gabriel Galípolo]].

## Evolution

The story of Banco Digimais is a story of an evangelical media empire's attempt to enter banking — and its collision with the same fraudulent credit ecosystem that destroyed [[Banco Master]].

- **2009-2013:** Bishop [[Edir Macedo]]'s first foray into finance. In 2009, Macedo acquired 40% of Banco Renner, a traditional institution from Porto Alegre linked to the Renner retail family. The [[Banco Central do Brasil]] had always viewed the intersection of financial institutions and religious groups with suspicion.

- **2020:** Macedo's holding completed the full acquisition and rebranded to Banco Digimais. The bank focused on used vehicle financing (over 70% of business) and payroll-deductible lending (consignado). With ~R$9 billion in assets, it operated as a banco múltiplo but without retail current accounts.

- **2022-2024:** The BCB demanded repeated adjustments — asset marking revisions (including precatórios), credit portfolio repricing. The bank began operating in the red. BCB demanded R$250 million capital injection; Macedo complied, but in exchange withdrew R$2 billion in Record TV group investments from the bank — a net loss of liquidity.

- **January 2025:** Maurício Quadrado — ex-partner of [[Banco Master]]'s [[Daniel Vorcaro]] — announced purchase of Digimais via his new BlueBank vehicle for R$800 million, planning to merge retail and wholesale banking in a BTG-style model. By April 2025, the deal collapsed without documentation even reaching the BCB. Quadrado cited "challenging market environment."

- **Late 2025:** [[Nubank]], pressured by BCB to acquire a banco múltiplo license, evaluated buying Digimais. Declined in November 2025, citing reputational risk and lack of strategic alignment.

- **February 2025:** Digimais structured a R$720 million FIDC called EXP1 — 80% held by the bank (with CCBs as collateral), 20% by Roberto Campos Marinho Filho's Yards Capital (also the fund manager). The credit portfolios feeding EXP1 came from [[Banco Master]], [[Reag Investimentos]], and Fictor.

- **August 2025:** International audit (UHY International Bendoraytes) revealed massive fraud in EXP1: of 55,000 credit contracts worth R$650 million, 22,000 were fictitious (no documentation backing them). The fake contracts — worth R$500 million — were the largest in the portfolio, all originated by Master and Reag. Digimais confirmed the fraud when confronted, admitted the credits didn't exist, and offered replacement portfolios (from the same originators — Yards declined).

- **November 2025:** After Master's collapse, Digimais's EXP1 fraud exposure became clear — same scheme as the R$12.7 billion BRB fraud, smaller scale. The fund administrator ID corretora — which replaced Reag as fund administrator — grew suspiciously, with patrimony jumping to R$52 billion. Market suspects ID absorbed Reag's 600+ liquidated funds. ID's partner Rodrigo Balassiano was formerly an executive at Banco Master's broker (ID says for ~50 days). ID's founding partner José Roberto Giancoli Filho has multiple CVM investigations (2008-2011) and 204 pending lawsuits.

- **Late 2025-early 2026:** Digimais replaced its president, bringing in [[Aldemir Bendine]] — ex-president of [[Banco do Brasil]] (2009-2015) and [[Petrobras]], arrested in 2017 during [[Lava Jato]] for alleged R$3 million bribe from [[Odebrecht]]. Market sees Bendine as Macedo's bridge to negotiate with [[Gabriel Galípolo]]'s BCB. The irony: a far-right evangelical bishop hiring a PT-linked executive to manage a debt crisis with a left-leaning central bank.

- **February 12, 2026:** Yards Capital notified Digimais demanding recompra of R$462.2 million in unsupported titles. Digimais under Bendine refused the refund. Yards alleges Digimais also committed appropriation — contacting original debtors to redirect payments away from EXP1 and into the bank directly. Lawsuit pending at 13ª Vara de São Paulo.

- **March 2026:** Piauí published deep investigative piece. FGC proposal: 70% of R$8.5B debt, Macedo covers 30%. Macedo resisting. Market watches whether BCB will act decisively or be constrained by electoral politics.

## The political dimension

Digimais sits at the intersection of evangelical politics and election-year pragmatism.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Controller | [[Edir Macedo]] — founder of [[Igreja Universal]], owner of TV Record |
| Political arm | [[Republicanos]] party |
| Congressional power | Major component of bancada evangélica; significant Centrão bloc |
| Election year stakes | Lula needs evangelical/Centrão support vs Flávio Bolsonaro |
| BCB dilemma | Technical action against Digimais risks "apocalyptic fury" from evangelicals |

The same political calculus that delayed [[Banco Master]]'s liquidation for three years — pressure from connected politicians — now applies to Digimais through a different channel. Where Vorcaro's protection ran through the STF and Centrão bosses, Macedo's runs through the evangelical caucus.

| Metric | Value |
