# Allegiant Travel

Allegiant Travel Company (NASDAQ: ALGT) is a Las Vegas-based US ultra-low-cost carrier focused on leisure travel from small/mid-sized US cities to vacation destinations. Distinct ULCC operating model — point-to-point flights from underserved markets, vertically integrated travel ancillaries (hotels, car rentals, tickets bundled with flights), and a smaller / older fleet than [[Frontier Airlines]] / [[Spirit Airlines]] historically operated.

## Post-Spirit shutdown context (May 2026)

[[Spirit Airlines]]'s May 2 2026 shutdown ([[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]]) removes a direct ULCC competitor. Allegiant's model is more leisure-specific than Spirit's broader point-to-point; the route-level overlap is smaller but the capacity-removal pricing-power benefit accrues to Allegiant on routes where the carriers competed directly. The fleet redistribution from ex-Spirit jets (~200 [[Airbus A320]]-family aircraft via [[AerCap]] / [[Air Lease Corp]]) potentially gives Allegiant access to standardized A320-family capacity at favorable lease terms.

Like [[Frontier Airlines]], Allegiant carries minimal jet-fuel hedging coverage — the ULCC business model historically does not lock in fuel costs at the rate European peers ([[Ryanair]] / [[EasyJet]] 70-80% covered) do. This makes Allegiant exposed to the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran-war]] jet-fuel cost regime.
