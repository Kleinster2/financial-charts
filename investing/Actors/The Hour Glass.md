# The Hour Glass

[[Singapore]]-based luxury watch retailer, publicly listed on SGX (AGS.SI). Founded in 1979 by Henry Tay, the company operates ~50 boutiques across Asia-Pacific and ranks as one of the largest independent multi-brand luxury watch retailers in the region, alongside [[Watches of Switzerland]] in the UK/US and [[Wempe]] in Europe.

## Synopsis

The Hour Glass occupies a structural position in the luxury watch distribution chain — authorized dealer status for brands like [[Rolex]], [[Patek Philippe]], Hublot, and [[IWC]] creates a moat that is nearly impossible to replicate. These brands tightly control distribution, and gaining or losing an authorization can make or break a retailer's economics. The company's geographic footprint spans [[Singapore]], Malaysia, Australia, [[Japan]], Thailand, and Vietnam, giving it exposure to the full spectrum of Asian luxury demand, from established Japanese collectors to the growing Vietnamese affluent class.

The investment case tracks two macro forces: the secular growth of Asian luxury consumption (driven by rising wealth in Southeast Asia and Chinese tourist spending) and the structural supply constraints imposed by brands like [[Rolex]] that deliberately under-produce to maintain pricing power. Henry Tay's family retains control, which means the business operates with a long-term orientation rare among public retailers. The risk is concentration — the company's fortunes are tied to a handful of Swiss brands that could shift distribution strategy, and luxury watch demand is cyclical, as the 2023-2024 secondary market correction demonstrated.
