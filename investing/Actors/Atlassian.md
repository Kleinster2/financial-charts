## Synopsis

Atlassian is the dominant developer collaboration platform — [[Jira]] is the de facto standard for issue tracking, [[Confluence]] for enterprise wikis, [[Trello]] for kanban, [[Bitbucket]] for code repos. Revenue hit $1.59B in Q2 FY2026 (+23% YoY) with the first-ever $1B+ cloud quarter, RPO at $3.8B (+44% YoY), and 600+ customers above $1M ARR. The financials look strong on paper.

The March 11, 2026 layoff of 1,600 workers (~10% of headcount) is the company's third round in two years and by far the largest. CEO [[Mike Cannon-Brookes]] framed it as proactive "self-funding" for AI and enterprise sales, but the stock's collapse from $242 to ~$73 (70% drawdown) tells a different story. R&D took the heaviest hit (900+ of 1,600 cuts), CTO [[Rajeev Rajan]] is stepping down, and [[Professionals Australia]] accused the company of "AI washing" — using AI as cover for cost cuts. The [[Block AI layoffs February 2026|Block comparison]] is apt: [[Block]] cut 40% of its workforce a month earlier with the same AI framing. The pattern is spreading across SaaS.

The bull case rests on Rovo (AI assistant, 5M+ MAU), cloud migration tailwinds, and high switching costs. The bear case: AI makes it faster and cheaper for competitors ([[Linear]], [[Notion]], [[GitHub]]) to build alternatives, potentially rendering large modular SaaS platforms obsolete. At $73, TEAM trades at ~13x forward revenue — cheap by SaaS standards, but justified if the competitive moat is narrowing.

## March 2026 layoffs

Announced March 11, 2026 via CEO blog post + Loom video. 1,600 jobs (~10% of global workforce). Restructuring expected complete by end of June 2026 (end of Q4 FY2026).

Charges: $225–236M total (severance: $169–174M; office space reductions: $56–62M).

### Teams affected

| Region | Cuts | % of total |
|--------|------|------------|
| R&D | 900+ | ~56% |
| North America | ~640 | ~40% |
| [[Australia]] | ~480 | ~30% |
| India | ~250 | ~16% |
| Other (incl. Middle East) | ~230 | ~14% |

Washington state WARN Act filing: 63 workers specifically.

### CTO departure

CTO [[Rajeev Rajan]] stepping down March 31 after nearly 4 years. Role split:
- Taroon Mandhana — CTO Teamwork
- Vikram Rao — CTO Enterprise & Chief Trust Officer

### CEO framing

[[Mike Cannon-Brookes]]: *"We're doing this to self-fund further investment in AI and enterprise sales, while strengthening our financial profile."*

*"It would be disingenuous to pretend AI doesn't change the mix of skills we need or the number of roles required in certain areas."*

*"We're also changing the way we work and reorganising around our System of Work to move faster."*

In Loom video: acknowledged it was "a very tough day" and "not made lightly."

### Severance

- Minimum 16 weeks separation pay + 1 additional week per year of service
- Pro-rated FY26 bonus
- $1,000 technology payment
- 6 months extended healthcare coverage

### Layoff history

| Date | Cuts | % of workforce | Stated reason |
|------|------|----------------|---------------|
| March 2023 | ~500 | ~5% | Rebalancing toward cloud/ITSM |
| 2025 | ~150 | ~1% | Support role eliminations |
| March 2026 | 1,600 | ~10% | AI + enterprise pivot |

Third round in two years. Each larger than the last.

### Labor pushback

[[Professionals Australia]] (union) accused Atlassian of making workers redundant without consultation. Director Paul Inglis said hundreds of Australian workers had joined the union to seek a say in how AI is used at work. Critics called it "AI washing" — using AI framing as cover for cost cuts at a company whose stock has collapsed 70%.

### Peer comparison (AI-linked tech layoffs, 2026)

| Company | Cuts | % | Framing |
|---------|------|---|---------|
| [[Block]] | 4,000 | 40% | [[Block AI layoffs February 2026|AI replacement]] |
| Atlassian | 1,600 | 10% | AI + enterprise pivot |
| [[Shopify]] | ~100 (partnerships) | targeted | Restructuring |

TechCrunch explicitly drew the Block comparison: *"Atlassian follows Block's footsteps and cuts staff in the name of AI."*

5M+ active search users (up from 3M+ earlier reports).

Capabilities:
- Find — search across all connected tools
- Learn — AI-driven insights from company data
- Act — [[AI agents]] for task automation (virtual teammates for workflows, create/edit Jira issues and Confluence pages, custom skill development)

## Investment case

Bull:
- Developer ecosystem lock-in (Jira is deeply embedded in enterprise workflows)
- Rovo AI differentiation — 5M+ users, bundled free
- Cloud migration tailwind (26% YoY cloud growth)
- High switching costs
- $225M+ in cost savings from restructuring
- Trading at ~13x forward revenue — cheap for SaaS

Bear:
- AI lowers barriers for competitors ([[Linear]], [[Notion]], [[GitHub]]) to replicate
- Third layoff round in two years — organizational instability
- CTO departure during critical AI pivot
- 70% stock decline suggests market pricing in structural risk
- Data Center revenue declining
- "AI washing" narrative risks — if Rovo doesn't deliver meaningful productivity, the layoffs look like simple cost cuts
