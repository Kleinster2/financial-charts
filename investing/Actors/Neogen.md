[[Neogen]] — Animal genomics, food safety, and biosecurity company. NASDAQ: NEOG. Headquartered Lansing, Michigan. Pending acquisition target by [[Zoetis]], H2 2026 expected close per [[Zoetis]] May 7 Q1 commentary.

The company operates two main segments: (1) Food Safety — diagnostic kits for foodborne pathogens, mycotoxins, GMOs, and allergens, used by food processors and ag producers; (2) Animal Safety — biosecurity / sanitation / veterinary instruments + the genomics testing business that came from the May 2022 GeneSeek acquisition and the September 2022 reverse-Morris-Trust acquisition of [[3M]]'s Food Safety business.

The 3M Food Safety reverse-Morris-Trust deal (~$5.3B value) was the largest M&A deal in Neogen's history and is the primary asset base [[Zoetis]] is acquiring through the pending deal — Zoetis gains the food-safety + genomics testing platform, expanding into adjacent cattle / livestock genomics testing where [[Zoetis]] does not currently operate.

## Why Neogen matters

The pending [[Zoetis]] acquisition is the closest dated catalyst on the Zoetis name post the May 7 Q1 reset. Neogen is roughly $800M-$900M revenue (FY26 estimated) — small relative to [[Zoetis]]'s ~$9.5B revenue but strategically additive in three ways: (1) cattle / livestock genomics testing (no current Zoetis footprint), (2) food-safety diagnostics (no current Zoetis footprint, adjacent to animal-health vertical), (3) biosecurity / sanitation (limited current Zoetis footprint).

The acquisition was struck before the May 7 [[Zoetis]] consumer-pulse fade; the strategic rationale (livestock-genomics adjacency at a moment when companion-animal organic growth is flattening) now reads as more important rather than less. Closing risk: regulatory (HSR + EU + ROW antitrust) plus Neogen-side covenant compliance under the integration period.
