# Kenneth Rogoff

**Role:** Economist, Harvard professor, former IMF chief economist
**Affiliation:** Harvard University (since 1999)
**Known for:** Debt/growth research, *This Time Is Different*, chess grandmaster

One of the most influential macroeconomists of his generation—and one of the few world-class academics who was also world-class at something entirely unrelated (chess). Rogoff's research on sovereign debt, exchange rates, and financial crises shapes how policymakers think about fiscal sustainability.

## Key contributions

### 1. This Time Is Different (2009)

Co-authored with Carmen Reinhart:

| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| 800 years of financial crises | Patterns repeat |
| Debt thresholds matter | High debt correlates with slower growth |
| "This time is different" | Famous last words before every crisis |

**The controversy:** The "90% threshold" (growth slows when debt/GDP exceeds 90%) was challenged after a coding error was discovered. The specific threshold was weakened, but the broader relationship held.

See: [[This Time Is Different]]

### 2. Exchange rate research

| Paper | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| "The Purchasing Power Parity Puzzle" (1996) | Why PPP deviations persist |
| Exchange rate models | Short-term unpredictability |

**The insight:** Exchange rates are nearly impossible to forecast in the short term—a humbling finding for macro forecasters.

### 3. The Curse of Cash (2016)

Rogoff's argument for eliminating large-denomination bills:

| Argument | Reasoning |
|----------|-----------|
| Cash enables crime | Drug trade, tax evasion, corruption |
| Cash limits monetary policy | Can't have deeply negative rates |
| Eliminate $100 bills, €500 notes | Keep small bills for convenience |

**The proposal:** Phase out large bills over 10-20 years. Small transactions remain cash-based; large transactions go digital.

| Supporter view | Critic view |
|----------------|-------------|
| Reduces crime | Surveillance state |
| Enables negative rates | Hurts the poor |
| Modernizes payments | Privacy concerns |

### 4. Our Dollar, Your Problem (2025)

Rogoff's latest book—a warning about dollar dominance:

| Thesis | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Dollar's rise involved luck | Competitors (yen, ruble, euro) self-destructed |
| Era of stability ending | Low rates, low inflation, low volatility over |
| Multiple challenges converging | Yuan, crypto, sanctions blowback, fiscal dysfunction |
| Hegemony will diminish | Gradual erosion, not sudden collapse |

**Recognition:** National bestseller; FT "Best Summer Books 2025: Economics"

Martin Wolf (FT): "In this brilliant book, Rogoff warns that the primacy of the dollar is at risk."

See: [[Our Dollar Your Problem]]

### 5. Time consistency

Work on central bank credibility and commitment:

| Concept | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Time consistency problem | Why promises to fight inflation aren't credible |
| Central bank independence | Solution to credibility problem |
| Rules vs. discretion | Rules can bind future behavior |

See: [[Time consistency]]

## Investment relevance

| Lesson | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Debt levels matter** | Watch debt/GDP trajectories |
| **Crises recur** | "This time is different" is a warning sign |
| **Exchange rates are unpredictable** | Don't bet on short-term currency moves |
| **Cash elimination trend** | Payment system evolution |
| **Dollar dominance eroding** | Diversify currency exposure |

### For sovereign analysis

| Rogoff framework | Questions to ask |
|------------------|------------------|
| Debt/GDP level | Above historical thresholds? |
| Growth trajectory | Can they grow out of debt? |
| Currency regime | Flexibility to adjust? |
| Crisis history | Serial defaulter? |
