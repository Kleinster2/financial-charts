## Iran war commentary (May 17, 2026)

[[Paul Horsnell]] cited under his OIES affiliation in the FT's May 17 peak-summer-crunch piece — the framing centered on minimum operating levels for global oil inventories ("depends by country and by product"; pipelines, refineries, and tanks cannot be drained to headline numbers without damage or stoppage) and on historical price context ($105 still short of $140 18-year high; $90 was "normal" not long ago). See [[Paul Horsnell#Iran war stockpile arithmetic — minimum operating levels (FT, May 17)]].

OIES's commentary niche on this kind of crisis is its institutional history: it has hosted multi-decade research on oil-market microstructure and OPEC behavior, giving it deeper grounds for the "system inventory vs available inventory" distinction than purely sell-side desks tend to publish.

*Source: FT, "Iran energy crisis enters new phase as peak summer season approaches," May 17, 2026.*
