#actor #company #private #finance #spv

American Ventures — Palm Beach-based SPV factory that channels capital from family offices and wealthy individuals into reverse-merger deals targeting U.S. companies in sectors aligned with the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration's policy priorities. Wholly 90%-owned by [[Dominari Holdings]] (Nasdaq: DOMH), with [[Donald Trump Jr.]] and [[Eric Trump]] as anchor investors in most of its vehicles. Based near the [[Trump National Golf Club Jupiter]] in Florida — the Trump sons' principal business office.

## Synopsis

American Ventures is the recap leg of the [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]] operated by Dominari. It raises capital into special-purpose vehicles, deploys that capital into freshly-IPO'd small-cap shells (often previously underwritten by [[Dominari Securities]]), cleans up the shells' cap tables, then funds the reverse mergers that bring chosen private operating companies onto Nasdaq.

As of early April 2026 SEC filings, the firm managed $1.04bn in assets across 21 special-purpose vehicles. The portfolio spans drones, AI robotics, mining, healthcare, nuclear energy, crypto, logistics, and several "unknown" categories per the FT's reading of regulatory filings. The firm's stated thesis, per a Dominari spokesperson: *"American Ventures' investment thesis is to target and invest in U.S.-based leaders in new technologies that create American jobs and reduce U.S. dependence on foreign resources."* A person familiar with the operation framed it differently: *"We have great vehicles that can go in any direction. American Ventures pivots towards where the puck is going. We're not trying to rebuild the steel industry. That's fucking dead."*

## How the SPV factory operates

| Stage | Step |
|-------|------|
| Capital raise | American Ventures raises into a series-numbered SPV from family offices, HNW individuals, and the Trump brothers as anchors. Typical other-investor checks ~$500K |
| Target identification | American Ventures pairs the SPV with a Dominari-underwritten public shell + a private operating company in a designated sector |
| Cap table cleanup | The SPV recapitalizes the shell, retires "toxic debt," and pays down legacy obligations |
| Reverse merger | The private operating company merges into the cleaned shell. The shell's old business is wound down |
| Aftermarket | The combined company keeps the shell's listing and stockholder base; American Ventures (and SPV LPs) retain post-deal equity |

The five-stage flow is documented in detail in [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]].

## Where the policy thesis meets the SPV book

The SPV-by-sector breakdown maps onto sectors the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration has prioritized in its second term:

| Administration priority | American Ventures SPV exposure |
|-------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Domestic drone production (Trump executive order; Pentagon's [[Drone Dominance]] $1.1bn initiative) | Series XVI AGH ([[Powerus]]) — largest SPV |
| AI / autonomous systems for defense | Series XIV JFB ([[XTEND]]) |
| Domestic critical-minerals supply (counter-China; [[Project Vault]]; [[EXIM Bank]] / [[DFC]] financing) | Series XIX SKBL + Series XXXII SKBL II ([[Cove Kaz Capital]]) |
| Crypto-as-economic-policy ("crypto capital of the world") | Tron deal, Dogehash/Thumzup, Hemi, Jewel Bancorp |
| Nuclear-energy revival | Undisclosed nuclear startup SPV |

The mapping does not establish causation. The FT explicitly notes there is no suggestion the Trump brothers had advance knowledge of the federal-policy events that have followed merger announcements (USAF Powerus order; EXIM/DFC Cove Kaz letters; Pentagon [[Unusual Machines]] motor contract). As a structural observation, however, the alignment compresses the gap between policy signal and aligned-capital deployment to weeks, not quarters. See [[Trump family investment portfolio]] and [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]] for the framing.

## Investor base

Per the FT, American Ventures' LP roster is structured as:
- Trump brothers as anchor investors in most SPVs
- Family offices and HNW individuals
- "Republican types affiliated with them" contributing ~$500K typical checks
- Strategic co-investors with policy-adjacent profiles ([[Aliya Capital]], [[Protego Ventures]], [[Unusual Machines]] in the JFB/XTEND deal)

A backer who has invested in at least three American Ventures vehicles, to the FT: *"I'm bullish on companies that defend western civilisation. That's important to them, too."*

## Risks

- Concentrated counterparty exposure: every SPV's exit is mediated by a single broker-dealer ([[Dominari Securities]]) and a single SPV operator (American Ventures itself)
- Liquidity overhang on shell stockholder bases at lockup expiry
- Federal-policy reversal if administration changes
- House China Committee escalation (via the Dominari channel) could dent the operator's broker-dealer license
- Justin Sun crossover (Tron principal + [[World Liberty Financial]] investor + WLF dispute) creates a counterparty-network conflict
