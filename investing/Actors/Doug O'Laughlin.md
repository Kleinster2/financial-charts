# Doug O'Laughlin

President of [[SemiAnalysis]] and author of the [[Fabricated Knowledge]] newsletter on [[Substack]]. Co-runs the firm with founder [[Dylan Patel]]; the public-facing analyst pairing makes [[SemiAnalysis]] one of the few semiconductor research shops with two senior independent voices on Substack. Based in NYC, ex-Bowie Capital ([[Texas]]) — buy-side background that brings investor framing to the firm's reports.

The role inside the firm is operational and cultural as much as analytical. Doug is the executive who pulled non-technical [[SemiAnalysis]] staff into [[Claude Code]] adoption starting late-Dec 2025 — the proximate cause of the firm's $7M token-spend run rate by April 2026 — and the public face of the firm's [[Memory shortage 2025-2026|memory]] and [[GPU depreciation cycle|GPU-cycle]] coverage. The Patel / O'Laughlin pairing maps cleanly onto the firm's two analytical lenses: Patel handles supply-chain and frontier-lab compute; Doug handles capital-cycle and capital-allocation framing.

## Role at SemiAnalysis

[[Dylan Patel]]'s framing on [[Invest Like the Best]] (Apr 23, 2026): O'Laughlin "led the charge in the sense of like non-technical people using AI for coding" — the executive who pulled [[SemiAnalysis]]'s broader staff onto [[Claude Code]] starting late December 2025 with the launch of [[Claude Opus|Opus 4.6]]. The token-spend inflection that took [[SemiAnalysis]] from "tens of thousands" of dollars/year on AI subscriptions to a $7M annual run rate by April 2026 traces directly to that cultural pull. See [[Idea-execution inversion]] for the broader frame.

A veteran portfolio manager, cited in industry coverage, said every buy-side shop would "drop everything to hire" O'Laughlin — a high signal of the firm's reputational pull on the sell side.

## Fabricated Knowledge

Doug's standalone newsletter pre-dates his full-time role at [[SemiAnalysis]] and continues alongside it. Coverage emphasis differs from the [[SemiAnalysis]] newsletter:

| Theme | Treatment |
