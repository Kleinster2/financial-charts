**ARK Invest** — thematic ETF manager founded by [[Cathie Wood]] in 2014, focused on "disruptive innovation" across genomics, autonomous technology, fintech, space, and AI. The flagship ARKK (Innovation ETF) became the defining retail investor vehicle of the 2020-2021 growth bubble, peaking at ~$60B AUM before declining ~75% as rising rates crushed long-duration growth stocks. ARK's AUM bottomed near ~$10B in 2023 before partially recovering on AI enthusiasm.

## Synopsis

ARK's investment significance extends beyond its own performance: the firm's daily trade disclosures and [[Cathie Wood]]'s public commentary function as a real-time signal of retail/thematic sentiment. When ARKK flows turn positive, it historically correlates with risk-on rotation into speculative growth. Wood's high-conviction calls — a $2,000 [[Tesla]] price target, early bets on [[Coinbase]] and [[Roku]], concentration in [[Palantir]] — generate both outsized returns in momentum markets and painful drawdowns in corrections.

The firm's open research model (publishing all models and trade rationale) is unusual in asset management. ARK's Monte Carlo price targets, built on aggressive adoption curves, are frequently criticized by traditional analysts but capture a genuine investor audience that treats disruption as a sector thesis rather than individual stock-picking. Current AUM ~$15B (early 2026).
