## Pershing Square takeover bid (Apr 7, 2026)

[[Pershing Square]] submitted a non-binding takeover proposal valuing UMG at ~€55.8B ($64.4B) — a 78% premium to Apr 2 close.

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Offer price | €30.40/share |
| Cash component | €5.05/share (€9.4B total) |
| Stock component | 0.77 shares of New UMG per share |
| New listing | NYSE (Nevada corp, US GAAP) |
| Share cancellation | 17% of outstanding |
| Expected close | Year-end 2026 |

**Structure:** UMG merges with Pershing Square SPARC Holdings. [[Michael Ovitz]] proposed as chairman. New UMG would be eligible for S&P 500 inclusion.

**[[Bill Ackman]]'s thesis:** Deep discount due to [[Bolloré Group]] overhang, postponed US listing, suboptimal shareholder communication.

**Reaction:** UMG shares surged 13-29% on Apr 7 (had fallen 23% YTD). ING analysts: non-binding and possibly likely to fail, but "has the merit of raising valid questions."

See [[Pershing Square UMG takeover bid]] for full details.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Irreplaceable catalog (Beatles, Queen, Elton John)
- Streaming growth + ARPU expansion
- AI licensing revenue
- Superfan monetization (vinyl, merch, VIP)
- Publishing = recurring royalties

**Bear:**
- DSP concentration (Spotify, Apple, Amazon)
- Artist power shifting (Taylor Swift model)
- AI disruption to creation
- K-pop label competition
- Currency exposure (EUR-listed)
