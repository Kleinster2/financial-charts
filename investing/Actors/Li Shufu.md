**Li Shufu** is the founder and chairman of [[Geely]], China's largest private automaker and one of the most acquisitive automotive groups globally. A self-made billionaire who started with refrigerator parts in the 1980s, Li built Geely into a conglomerate spanning mass-market cars, luxury marques, and financial services.

## Synopsis

Li's career arc is a case study in how Chinese private capital used the 2008-2018 window of Western corporate distress and openness to execute transformative acquisitions. The 2010 [[Volvo Cars]] purchase for $1.8B from [[Ford]] — which had paid $6.4B in 1999 — gave Geely access to safety and platform engineering that underpins its current EV lineup. The 2017 acquisition of a 9.7% stake in [[Daimler]] (now [[Mercedes-Benz]]) for ~$9B made Li the single largest shareholder in Germany's premier luxury automaker, though the stake has since been partially unwound. The [[Saxo Bank]] investment followed the same pattern: acquire Western financial infrastructure to serve Chinese capital flows.

The investment case around Li is really about [[Geely]]'s portfolio architecture. Through direct ownership, joint ventures, and strategic stakes, Li controls or influences [[Volvo Cars]], [[Polestar]], [[Lotus]], [[London EV Company]], [[Proton]], and [[Smart]] (JV with Mercedes). Net worth estimated at $16-18B. The risk is overextension — Geely's debt load from acquisitions competes with the capital needed for the EV transition, and the [[Daimler]] stake lost value as European luxury autos derated.
