# Lucid Motors

## Synopsis

[[Lucid Motors]] (NASDAQ: LCID; legal name Lucid Group, Inc.) is a US electric-vehicle manufacturer headquartered in Newark, California, with manufacturing in Casa Grande, Arizona, and a vertically integrated assembly plant in Saudi Arabia. The firm went public via a July 2021 SPAC merger with Churchill Capital Corp IV, with Saudi Arabia's [[PIF|Public Investment Fund]] as the controlling-shareholder anchor. Lucid produces two vehicle families — the Air luxury sedan (in production since 2021) and the Gravity SUV (production began December 2024) — both positioned at the high end of the US EV market against [[Tesla]]'s Model S and Model X. FY 2025 deliveries reached 15,841 vehicles, FY 2025 revenue $1.354B (+67.6% YoY from $807.8M FY 2024), and FY 2026 production guidance is 25,000–27,000 vehicles. Saudi Arabia accounted for $80.4M Q4 2025 revenue (+112% sequential) and $161.8M FY 2025 revenue.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Sherry House]] career-arc thread: House was Lucid CFO from May 2021 through December 2023, leading both the July 2021 SPAC public listing and the buildout of US (Casa Grande) and Saudi Arabian manufacturing. Her February 2025 move to [[Ford]] CFO reflects the EV-market labour-pool reverse-flow into legacy auto — Ford gaining a CFO with direct EV-startup financial-management experience at a moment when Ford's own EV strategy (Ford Model e) is under operating pressure.

## Position in EV / auto cluster

Lucid is positioned as a high-end Tesla competitor with a luxury-sedan-and-SUV-only product mix and a Saudi sovereign-fund anchor that distinguishes its capital structure from peer US EV startups (Rivian, Lordstown, Faraday Future, etc.). The PIF anchor provides ongoing capital injections that have cushioned Lucid through a multi-year cash-burn period — total losses since inception exceed $10B — but the company has not yet reached operating breakeven. The Q4 2025 Saudi revenue jump (+112% sequential to $80.4M) reflects local Saudi Gravity assembly coming online and fleet/government deliveries; sustainability of that volume into 2026 is one of the binding forward questions.
