# Arkady Volozh

Founder of [[Yandex]] and CEO of [[Nebius]], the AI infrastructure company spun out of [[Yandex]]'s international operations after a full divestiture from [[Russia]]. Born in Soviet Kazakhstan in 1964, based in Israel. EU sanctions applied in 2022 were lifted in 2024 after Volozh completed the separation from Russian operations. Net worth approximately $2.6B (Bloomberg, November 2025).

## Synopsis

Volozh built [[Yandex]] from a search engine in 1997 into Russia's dominant internet company — search, maps, ride-hailing, e-commerce, fintech — with a peak market cap exceeding $30B. The 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine triggered EU sanctions against Volozh personally, which forced a radical restructuring: [[Yandex]]'s international technology assets (cloud, AI, autonomous driving, edtech) were separated from the Russian domestic business and reorganized as [[Nebius]], a Netherlands-domiciled AI infrastructure company. The Russian operations were sold to a consortium of local investors for roughly $5.2B in a deal structured to comply with Kremlin capital controls.

The reinvention as [[Nebius]] is one of the more unusual stories in tech: a founder who spent 25 years building Russia's most important internet company effectively abandoned it to launch an AI infrastructure play in Europe and the Middle East. [[Nebius]] operates GPU cloud computing clusters (focused on [[NVIDIA]] hardware), AI development platforms, and retains the Toloka data labeling business. The company listed on NASDAQ in late 2024 and has attracted investment from prominent tech investors. The bull case is that Volozh brings genuine technical depth and operational experience to the AI infrastructure buildout, in a market where European alternatives to US hyperscalers are scarce. The bear case is that [[Nebius]] is starting from a small base with limited brand recognition outside the ex-Soviet tech ecosystem, and the AI cloud market is capital-intensive with entrenched incumbents.
