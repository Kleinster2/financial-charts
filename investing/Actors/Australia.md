## Fuel supply crisis — 32 to 10 days of cover (March 23)

[[Nadia Martin Wiggen]] ([[Stellen Capital]], March 23, 2026) identified [[Australia]] as the single most acute supply vulnerability among OECD countries during the [[Strait of Hormuz]] crisis:
- Pre-crisis fuel cover: ~32 days
- Week 2: dropped to ~15 days
- Week 3 (March 23): down to ~10 days

Australia bought 3 cargoes of [[Gasoline|gasoline]] from the US Gulf Coast — a completely abnormal trade pattern given the distance. What Australia needs most is refined products from [[Japan]], [[South Korea]], and [[China]], but Japan is releasing more crude than products from its SPR, South Korea imposed a partial diesel export ban, and China banned refined product exports entirely.

Gold mines are shutting down due to [[Diesel fuel|diesel]] shortages — the mining complex is the transmission mechanism from energy disruption to metals markets and broader economic impact.

*Source: Nadia Martin Wiggen (Stellen Capital), Other People's Money / Monetary Matters Network, March 23, 2026*
