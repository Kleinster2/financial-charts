Fathom — AI meeting notetaker founded 2020 by [[Richard White]] (ex-[[UserVoice]]), [[Y Combinator]] W21. San Francisco. Bot-based capture origin; added bot-free capture on April 15 2026 ("Fathom 3.0"). Last disclosed valuation $90M post-money on the September 2024 [[Telescope Partners]]-led Series A. ARR $30M (CY 2025 per [[Latka]]) on ~100 employees (Apr 2026). Distinct from [[Fathom Events]] (movie theatre chain).

One-line read: Fathom is the largest-installed-base bot-based AI notetaker now copying [[Granola]]'s no-bot architecture (April 15 2026), with the bet being that distribution depth ([[HubSpot]] 2025 Most Used App, 8,500+ HubSpot installs, 500K+ users) plus speaker diarization buys time for AI-model gains to compress the accuracy gap before [[Granola]]'s acquisition curve converts the installed base.

## Founder

[[Richard White]] — founder/CEO since 2020. Previously founded [[UserVoice]], one of the leading enterprise customer-feedback platforms (Fortune 500 customers, acquired/active independently). Before [[UserVoice]], White worked on Kiko (a calendar startup) in [[Y Combinator]]'s first batch (2005) with [[Justin Kan]] and [[Emmett Shear]] — the same pair who later founded [[Twitch]]. Serial-founder, B2B-SaaS arc with one prior at-scale build.

White's stated product philosophy is that he deferred heavy AI-feature investment until "[[GPT-4]] and [[Claude]] 2" matured — i.e., he timed Fathom to product-market fit with the base model rather than racing ahead of it. This shaped the bot-based-first architecture: lower technical risk on capture, lean on improving LLMs for the value layer.

## Product

Core: AI meeting notetaker that captures, transcribes, summarises and routes meeting content into CRMs and downstream workflows. Native integrations with [[Zoom]], [[Microsoft Teams]] and [[Google Meet]].

The April 15 2026 platform update ("Fathom 3.0" per Chatgate / TechCrunch) is the most structurally important product event in the company's history because it adopts the architecture the company was previously displaced on:

| Feature (April 2026 update) | Detail |
|-----------------------------|--------|
| Capture modes (new) | Bot-free transcription, bot-free audio capture, or full video-with-bot — choosable meeting-by-meeting |
| Speaker diarization | "Who said what" attribution in bot-free transcripts — [[Richard White]]'s pitched differentiator vs [[Granola]] |
| Live summaries | Generated during meetings rather than post-hoc |
| Ask Fathom | Conversational search expanded from single meeting to user's entire library and (with admin grant) team / org-wide |
| iOS mobile app (new) | First-party mobile; supports in-person meeting recording |
| Public API | Programmatic access for developers / agents |
| [[MCP]] server | Surfaces meeting content to [[ChatGPT]], [[Claude]], etc. |
| Asana integration (new) | Meeting action items → trackable tasks |
| AI scorecards | Configurable meeting-type scorecards for coaching with timestamped notes |
| [[HubSpot]] / [[Salesforce]] sync | Contact match, deal updates, activity logging |

Existing baseline product (pre-3.0): bot-based capture on Zoom / Teams / Meet, summaries, action items, CRM sync, [[HubSpot]] / [[Salesforce]] integrations.

### Pricing

| Plan | Price (monthly) | Price (annual) | Key features |
|------|----------------|----------------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 | Unlimited meeting capture + transcription; advanced AI summaries capped at 5 per month; API + MCP access |
| Premium | $20/user | $16/user | Unlimited AI summaries |
| Team | $19/user | $15/user | Admin controls, team features (2-user minimum) |
| Business | $34/user | $25/user | CRM field sync, deal views, coaching scorecards (2-user minimum) |

The 5-summary monthly cap on the Free tier was introduced as part of the 2026 pricing reset; recordings and transcription remain unlimited. [[Richard White]]: *"From the most generous free tier in the market — including API and MCP access, unlimited meeting captures — to business plans packed with competitive features at a fraction of the cost"* (Apr 15 2026).

The Business tier at $25/user/year-paid is structurally below [[Granola]]'s Enterprise plan ($35+/user) for an overlapping feature set — the distribution-on-price thesis pricing in.

AI meeting notes is now a crowded category with two architectural cohorts and a third hybrid that the April 2026 launch creates.

|------------|---------------|-------|
| Fathom (post Apr 2026) | Hybrid — bot or bot-free, meeting-by-meeting | 500K+ users; HubSpot Most Used App 2025 |

| [[Otter.ai]] | Bot-based + local | 278 employees Apr 2026; founded 2016 |

The April 15 2026 platform update is explicitly framed by [[TechCrunch]] (article title: *"Fathom adds a bot-less meeting mode in a bid to take on Granola"*) as a competitive response to [[Granola]]. [[Richard White]] frames the differentiator as accuracy rather than capture mode: *"A lot of these bot-less tools don't indicate who said what in their captured transcript."* He attributes the timing to AI-model improvements in the six preceding months enabling on-device speaker diarization.

### The accuracy thesis

[[Implicator.ai]] (Oct 8 2025) framed Fathom's positioning as a distribution-vs-accuracy bet: free pricing and [[HubSpot]] marketplace lock-in trying to compensate for transcription accuracy that lags competitors. The piece cited eWeek summer-2025 testing — Fathom 3.5/5 on transcription, vs Avoma 5.0/5 and Notta AI 4.5/5 — and concluded the quality is *"fine for internal notes, fragile for audit trails."* The April 2026 launch of speaker diarization is the company's response to that critique: ship the architecture, ship the missing accuracy primitive, let the marketplace position carry the rest.

## Analysis

The Fathom story turns on three structural facts that did not all exist six months ago. First, Fathom is the largest-installed-base AI notetaker in the bot-based cohort by user count (500K+) and has the deepest single-distribution lock-in ([[HubSpot]] 2025 Most Used App, 8,500+ active HubSpot installs). Second, [[YipitData]]'s Apr 2026 Feb-cohort read confirmed that the bot-free architecture pioneered by [[Granola]] was actively displacing Fathom — customers adopting [[Granola]] dropped Fathom; the reverse pattern was rare. Third, on April 15 2026 Fathom shipped a capture-mode rewrite that explicitly absorbs the [[Granola]] architecture — bot-free transcription with speaker diarization — alongside an iOS app, a public API, and an [[MCP]] server. The vault note's central question is whether Fathom 3.0 arrived early enough to convert installed-base inertia into a defensible moat, or late enough that the [[Granola]] cohort already captured the migration.

The funding picture sharpens the timing question. The Series A closed Sep 2024 at $90M post-money on $10M ARR — a 9× trailing-ARR multiple, clean for the vintage. By CY 2025 the company tripled ARR to $30M but cut headcount from 171 (Nov 2025 per [[Latka]]) to ~100 (Apr 2026 per company materials). That is not a growth-at-all-costs posture; it is a margin reset, consistent with a company either preparing for an upmarket Series B raise into the $30M ARR base, or pulling forward break-even in a softening fundraising environment, or both. The Sep 2024 round is 20 months stale as of May 2026, which is long for an AI [[SaaS]] company growing 3× per year and short for one in margin-discipline mode. If a Series B prints in 2026, the headline multiple becomes the cleanest signal we have on whether the displacement narrative reset the valuation or whether the marketplace position protected it.

The [[Richard White]] pitch — distribution plus diarization — is structurally different from [[Granola]]'s. [[Granola]] is selling a notepad as a context-infrastructure platform, betting on [[MCP]]-ecosystem retention and an enterprise renewal cohort at $35+/seat. Fathom is selling a notepad as the embedded layer of an existing workflow ([[HubSpot]] / [[Salesforce]] / [[Zoom]] / [[Asana]]), betting that the installed base in those workflows is stickier than a new pure-play platform. Both can be right about meeting notes commoditising while disagreeing about where the value migrates. The intersection — both companies converge on bot-free capture + speaker diarization + [[MCP]] + a public API — is the part of the category that is now genuinely commoditised. The differentiation has moved one layer up to which downstream stack the meeting context lands in.

Two read-throughs follow. The first is to [[Granola]]'s "[[Otter.ai]] / [[Fireflies.ai]] / Fathom can't easily retrofit because architecture" bear case: that case is now empirically wrong on the Fathom leg. [[Otter.ai]] and [[Fireflies.ai]] have not yet shipped bot-free; whether they can do so quickly is now the test of the architecture-moat thesis on the rest of the cohort. The second is to the [[Zoom]] Apps Fund / marketplace-incumbent question — whether [[Microsoft]] ([[Teams]] / Copilot) or [[Google]] ([[Workspace]] / [[Meet]]) folds native AI notes into the meeting client free of charge, which would re-set the price floor for every third-party in the category to zero.

The narrative the file should resolve into: Fathom 3.0 is a defensive product launch that adopts the competitor's architecture and pitches accuracy + distribution as the differentiator. The next [[YipitData]] read tells you whether the customers were moving because of architecture (in which case the displacement slows) or because of momentum (in which case it does not).
