## Key positions

Chair Steffen Meister warned (Mar 12, 2026) that [[Private Credit Software Concentration|private credit default rates]] could double from the ~2.6% decade average to above 5% in coming years, driven by AI-disruption bifurcation and the asymmetric payoff structure of credit (capped upside, full downside). Argued that the leverage-on-diversified-portfolio strategy that worked at low default rates will break down.

---

## Evergreen secondaries allocation

Partners Group's flagship US private wealth fund — one of the early movers in the [[Evergreen private capital vehicles|evergreen vehicle]] format — allocates roughly 17% to [[Private equity secondaries|secondaries]], a meaningful share that supports the fund's ability to deploy monthly subscription inflows faster than primary fund commitments allow. The allocation sits between [[Hamilton Lane]]'s ~50% secondaries-heavy evergreen and [[Blackstone]]'s BXPE at ~5%.

The firm's positioning in the wealth-channel pivot is structurally distinct from US peers: Partners Group built its private-wealth distribution out of Switzerland on a multi-decade horizon, predating the US elite-firm rush into the format. The Swiss base (governance, regulatory framework, distribution culture) gave the firm an early-mover advantage in European HNW channels.
