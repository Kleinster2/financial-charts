# Frontier Airlines

Frontier Airlines (NASDAQ: ULCC, parent: Frontier Group Holdings) is a Denver-based US ultra-low-cost carrier operating an [[Airbus A320]]-family fleet on point-to-point domestic and Latin America routes. As of May 2026 Frontier is the largest pure-play US ULCC by capacity following the [[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]] removed ~9% of US ULCC domestic capacity.

## Post-Spirit shutdown context (May 2026)

[[Spirit Airlines]]'s May 2 2026 shutdown ([[2026 Spirit Airlines shutdown]]) removed a direct ULCC competitor on overlapping routes. Frontier is positioned to absorb capacity at vacated gates and to acquire ex-Spirit slots / aircraft on the secondary market ([[AerCap]] / [[Air Lease Corp]] redistribution of ~200 ex-Spirit A320-family jets). The dynamic is partially offsetting — Frontier shares fuel-cost exposure to the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran-war]] jet-fuel shock that drove Spirit under, but with a relatively stronger balance sheet and post-emergence positioning.

The watch question: does Frontier's hedging coverage (historically minimal for US ULCCs vs European peers like [[Ryanair]] / [[EasyJet]] at 70-80% covered through 2027) hold up if jet-fuel prices stay above $150/bbl? Per [[Bernstein]] research, fuel + related expenses at ~35% of revenue is the structural baseline for ULCCs even in benign fuel-price regimes.
