# Gregoire Tournant

Gregoire Tournant was the lead portfolio manager of the Structured Alpha hedge funds at [[Allianz Global Investors]] US. Indicted in May 2022 for fraud, conspiracy, and obstruction after orchestrating a scheme that defrauded investors of ~$11B during the March 2020 COVID crash. Tournant and colleagues systematically altered risk reports sent to pension fund clients — in one case changing a projected 42.15% crash loss to 4.15% by deleting the "2." The funds had been marketed as hedged, low-risk strategies to institutional investors including [[Arkansas Teacher Retirement System]] and the Teamsters.

When markets crashed in March 2020, the Structured Alpha funds lost 80-97% of value in weeks, exposing the fraud. [[Allianz]] paid $6B+ in settlements and restitution, including a $675M SEC penalty and a $2.3B DOJ criminal fine. The parent company's US asset management arm was barred from SEC-registered advisory services for 10 years. Two other portfolio managers — Stephen Bond-Nelson and Trevor Taylor — pleaded guilty and cooperated. Tournant fled to [[France]], which does not extradite its own citizens, complicating US prosecution efforts.
