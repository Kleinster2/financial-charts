# California

California is the largest US state by GDP ($4.1T, 2024), equivalent to the world's fifth-largest economy. Home to [[Silicon Valley]], Hollywood, and the nation's largest agricultural sector. Its regulatory decisions — from pharmaceutical compounding to utility oversight to emissions standards — often set de facto national policy due to market size. The state's Board of Pharmacy adopted strict "essentially a copy" compounding rules (Jun 2025) that directly impacted the [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] compounding market.

## Investment relevance

California's regulatory posture creates investment signals across multiple sectors. Its wildfire liability regime forced [[PG&E]] into bankruptcy (2019) and continues to pressure utility insurance markets. CARB emissions standards, adopted by 17 other states, effectively set the national floor for EV transition policy. The [[Port of Los Angeles]] and Port of Long Beach together handle ~40% of US container imports, making the state a chokepoint for trade policy.
