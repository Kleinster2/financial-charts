# Marc Lynch

Professor of Political Science and International Affairs at George Washington University; Director of the Institute for Middle East Studies at GWU; Director and founder of the Project on Middle East Political Science (POMEPS); editor of the Columbia Studies on Middle East Politics series at Columbia University Press. Frequent contributor to *Foreign Policy* and *Foreign Affairs*. Coined the term "Arab Spring" in a 2011 *Foreign Policy* essay.

In the investing vault, Lynch is most often cited for analytical frames on Gulf politics — particularly the structural relationship between [[Saudi Arabia]] and the [[UAE]] — that recur across multiple vault notes. His March 2026 *Foreign Policy* essay on Riyadh's pre-Iran-war push to assemble a [[Turkey]]-[[Qatar]]-[[Egypt]]-[[Pakistan]] alignment partially against the UAE-[[Israel]] axis is the canonical reference for the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|war-induced pause]] in Saudi-UAE rivalry and the underlying disagreements that the war-induced reunification did not resolve.
