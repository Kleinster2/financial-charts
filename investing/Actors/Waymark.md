# Waymark

Detroit-based AI platform that generates broadcast-quality TV commercials from text prompts, targeting the SMB advertising market where traditional production costs ($15-30K per spot) are prohibitive.

## Synopsis

Waymark occupies a narrow but potentially lucrative niche: automating TV ad production for small and medium businesses that can afford to buy local TV airtime but cannot afford to produce professional commercials. Founded in 2010 (originally as a manual video production service), the company pivoted to AI-generated video and now partners with major broadcast groups including E.W. Scripps, Fox TV Stations, Spectrum Reach, and Gray TV. These partnerships are the distribution moat -- the broadcasters integrate Waymark into their ad sales pipeline, effectively bundling production with airtime purchases.

The total funding (~$6M) is modest, suggesting either capital efficiency or limited ambition. Co-founder Nathan Labenz has become a visible AI commentator, which raises the company's profile beyond its revenue scale. The competitive risk is existential: as general-purpose video generation models from [[OpenAI]] (Sora), [[Google]] ([[Veo]]), and [[Runway]] improve, Waymark's specialized ad-generation capability could be replicated by any platform. The defense is workflow integration -- Waymark is embedded in broadcaster sales processes, not competing on raw video quality. Whether that integration is sticky enough to survive the commoditization of AI video generation is the core question.
