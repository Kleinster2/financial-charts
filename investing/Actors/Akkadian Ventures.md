Akkadian Ventures — San Francisco-based venture-secondaries firm specializing in direct secondary investment: buying shares in private companies from existing holders (early employees, small investors seeking liquidity). A private investment firm, not a fund itself — it operates a family of fund vehicles, including the listed [[Powerlaw Corp]] (PWRL), which began trading on [[Nasdaq]] May 27, 2026.

## How it works

Akkadian buys secondary-market shares in private companies from existing backers — typically early employees, angels, or small funds seeking liquidity before an IPO. These are packaged into fund vehicles (SPVs, blind pool funds, and now the listed [[Powerlaw Corp]]). Instruments include direct equity, employee share buyouts, SPVs, forward contracts, and convertible notes.

Investment Committee: Black + Dinsdale. Akkadian also manages 14–17 other pooled vehicles with ~$800–857M in assets. No dedicated investment allocation — uses rotational/percentage allocation methods.

Conflict: other Akkadian funds may carry performance fees / carried interest, creating an incentive to favor those over [[Powerlaw Corp]] (which has no performance fee).
