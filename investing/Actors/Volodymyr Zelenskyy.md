## Apr 2026 — Papperger exchange

Responded to [[Armin Papperger]] ([[Rheinmetall]] CEO) comparing Ukraine's drone industry to "Legos" made by "Ukrainian housewives": "If every Ukrainian housewife can produce drones, then every Ukrainian housewife can be the CEO of Rheinmetall." The line landed days before the Bundestag approved Rheinmetall's €298M [[FV-014]] drone contract, underscoring the narrative tension between legacy European primes and Ukraine's proven wartime drone production. [[Oleksandr Kamyshin]] also responded, citing 11,000 Russian tanks destroyed by Ukrainian drones.

## May 2026 — "Long-range sanctions" framing + oil-industry targeting commitment

After Russia's May 14 record-breaking aerial attack on Kyiv (1,400+ drones + dozens of missiles in 24 hours; killed 24 in the capital), Zelenskyy announced Ukraine was "defining targets for our next long-range sanctions against Russia" (May 16 post-meeting with Ukrainian military + intelligence chiefs). The follow-through landed Sunday May 17 with one of the largest Ukrainian barrages since 2022 — 556 drones intercepted across Russia, ~120 over Moscow per Mayor Sergey Sobyanin, and a strike on the Moscow Oil Refinery (owned by [[Gazprom]]'s [[Gazprom Neft]] subsidiary).

Zelenskyy's quoted framing (May 17, X):

- "Our responses to Russia's prolongation of the war and its attacks on our cities and communities are entirely justified."
- "Ukrainian long-range sanctions reached the Moscow region, and we are clearly telling the Russians: their state must end its war."
- Target classes Ukraine reserves the right to strike: "Russia's oil industry, military production and those directly responsible for committing war crimes against Ukraine and Ukrainians."

The "oil industry" formulation is the analytically important commitment — it formalizes a Ukrainian doctrine of systematic Russian refinery / pipeline / storage targeting rather than retaliation against population centres. Combined with Russia's Donbas territorial demands and the Trump administration's [[2026 Iran conflict|Iran war]] diplomatic-bandwidth absorption, the peace track has stalled, and the May 17 strike pattern is the operating equilibrium pending the next inflection.

See [[Ukraine war#May 17, 2026 — escalating drone war + peace talks stalled (FT)]] for the full strike-totals + strategic context.
