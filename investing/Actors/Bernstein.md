**Bernstein** — premier sell-side equity research and brokerage firm, now part of [[AllianceBernstein]] (AB) following a 2024 rebrand. Founded in 1967 by Sanford C. Bernstein as an independent research boutique, the firm built its reputation on rigorous, data-driven analysis that frequently challenged consensus — a contrarian streak that persists. Bernstein's research franchise is consistently ranked among the top 3 globally by institutional investor surveys.

## Synopsis

Bernstein's vault relevance is as a high-signal research source. The firm houses several analysts whose calls move markets and inform this vault's analysis. [[Stacy Rasgon]] (semiconductors) is widely regarded as the best chip analyst on the Street — his notes on [[NVIDIA]], [[AMD]], and [[Intel]] are among the most closely followed. Other notable coverage includes technology hardware, media, and healthcare. Bernstein's annual Strategic Decisions Conference is a major catalyst event where CEOs make market-moving comments.

The firm's integration into [[AllianceBernstein]] (a ~$700B AUM asset manager, ticker AB) gives it distribution scale while preserving research independence. The research operation is funded by commission revenue and client relationships rather than investment banking, which is core to its credibility — Bernstein analysts are not conflicted by IPO/M&A deal flow.
