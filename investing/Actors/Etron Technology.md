**Etron Technology** is a Taiwanese fabless IC design house specializing in memory and mixed-signal chips, now pivoting to AI-edge memory through a joint venture with [[Nanya Technology]].

## Synopsis

Etron occupies a niche in the memory IC design chain — designing DRAM interfaces, known good die (KGD) solutions, and mixed-signal controllers rather than manufacturing memory itself. Founded in 1991 by Nicky Lu (a former [[TSMC]] executive), the company historically served the consumer electronics market with low-power SRAM and specialty DRAM products. Revenue has been modest (~NT$5-7B range), and the stock trades on the TWSE with limited international visibility. The company's trajectory changed with the August 2025 joint venture with [[Nanya Technology]] (80:20 Nanya:Etron, NT$500M capitalization) to develop AI memory design services for edge computing.

The JV thesis is that the AI inference buildout will create demand for customized memory solutions at the edge — not the HBM products that [[SK Hynix]] and [[Samsung]] dominate for data center GPUs, but lower-power, application-specific memory for on-device AI in smartphones, automotive, and IoT. Etron's design IP combined with [[Nanya]]'s manufacturing capability (Nanya is [[Taiwan]]'s largest DRAM maker by capacity after [[Micron]]'s local operations) could position the JV as a memory design services provider for fabless AI chip companies. The risk is execution and scale: the AI edge memory market is still nascent, and competing against the vertical integration of [[Samsung]] and [[SK Hynix]] in any memory segment is structurally difficult.
