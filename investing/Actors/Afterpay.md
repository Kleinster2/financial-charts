# Afterpay

Australian buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) company acquired by [[Block]] in January 2022 for ~$13.9B in stock, down from the $29B announcement value in August 2021 as [[Block]] shares collapsed. The largest and most controversial acquisition in [[Block]]'s history.

## Synopsis

Afterpay was founded in 2014 by Nick Molnar and Anthony Eisen in Sydney, launching a pay-in-four model that let consumers split purchases into interest-free installments while merchants paid a ~4-6% transaction fee. The model exploded during COVID as young consumers (Millennials and Gen Z) embraced it as an alternative to credit cards. At peak hype in early 2021, Afterpay had ~16M active users and was processing ~A$22B in gross merchandise value. [[Block]] announced the acquisition in August 2021 at a $29B all-stock valuation -- the largest fintech M&A deal in history at the time.

The deal closed in January 2022, by which time [[Block]] shares had fallen ~50% from their peak, compressing the deal value to ~$13.9B. This timing -- announcing at the absolute top of the BNPL bubble and closing on the way down -- made the acquisition a case study in peak-cycle M&A. Afterpay now operates as Clearpay in the UK/Europe and is integrated into both the [[Square]] seller ecosystem (merchant-side) and [[Cash App]] consumer ecosystem. The BNPL model itself faces margin pressure from credit normalization, regulatory scrutiny (Australia, UK, EU all imposing new disclosure rules), and competition from [[PayPal]] Pay in 4, [[Klarna]], and Apple Pay Later. For [[Block]] investors, Afterpay's integration success and contribution to ecosystem GMV are key metrics to watch.
