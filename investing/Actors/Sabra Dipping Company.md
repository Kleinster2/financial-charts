**Sabra Dipping Company** — The leading US hummus and dips brand by market share. Originally a joint venture between [[PepsiCo]] and Israel's Strauss Group, now a wholly-owned [[PepsiCo]] subsidiary after the 2024 buyout of Strauss's 50% stake.

---

## Synopsis

Sabra dominates the US hummus market with roughly 60% share, a position built on distribution muscle from [[PepsiCo]]'s Frito-Lay network and the first-mover advantage of bringing hummus from ethnic niche to mainstream American snacking. The brand's trajectory mirrors the broader "better for you" snacking trend that has reshaped the US packaged food landscape over the past decade. Hummus went from zero to a $1B+ US category, and Sabra rode nearly all of that growth.

The 2024 full acquisition by [[PepsiCo]] (buying out Strauss Group's 50% JV stake) simplified the ownership structure but also signals PepsiCo's conviction that the dips and spreads category has further room to grow. For investors in [[PepsiCo]], Sabra is a small but telling data point about portfolio strategy: the company is doubling down on the health-adjacent snacking segment even as its core carbonated beverage and salty snack businesses face volume pressure from GLP-1 weight-loss drugs. Whether hummus consumption proves resistant to the GLP-1 demand destruction hitting processed snacks is an open question.
