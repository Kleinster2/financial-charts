## Q1 FY2026 Earnings (Jan 28, 2026)

**First US traffic growth in 2 years — turnaround working.**

| Metric | Actual | Estimate | Result |
|--------|--------|----------|--------|
| **EPS (adj)** | $0.56 | $0.59 | Miss |
| **Revenue** | $9.92B | $9.67B | Beat |
| **Global comp sales** | +4% | +2.3% | Beat |
| **US comp sales** | +4% | — | Strong |
| **Traffic** | +3% | — | **First growth in 2 years** |

### Segment performance

| Region | Comp Sales | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| US | +4% | Holiday menu success (Bearista cup, peppermint mocha) |
| International | +5% | Broad strength |
| China | +7% | JV with Boyu Capital pending |

### Why EPS missed despite strong sales

- Turnaround/restructuring costs
- Higher coffee prices
- Tariff pressure on margins
- Net income $293M vs $781M YoY

**Stock reaction:** +8-9% premarket.

### FY2026 Guidance (first since Oct 2024 suspension)

| Metric | Guidance | Consensus |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| EPS | $2.15-$2.40 | $2.35 |
| Same-store sales | At least +3% | — |

**CEO Niccol:** "Our Q1 results demonstrate our 'Back to Starbucks' strategy is working and we believe we're ahead of schedule."

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Turnaround working (traffic +3%, first growth in 2 years)
- Brian Niccol track record (Chipotle success)
- Loyalty program moat (75M members)
- China recovery (+7% comps)
- Investor day catalyst (Jan 29)

**Bear:**
- EPS still pressured by turnaround costs
- Coffee commodity inflation
- Tariff exposure
- Labor costs/unionization
- China execution risk
- Premium valuation
