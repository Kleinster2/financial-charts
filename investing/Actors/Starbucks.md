## Q2 FY2026 earnings and turnaround acceleration (Apr 2026)

Q2 turned the Starbucks turnaround from a traffic story into an earnings-recovery story. The stock moved from $97.28 to $105.50, +8.4%, after the company reported transaction-led comps, margin expansion, and raised FY2026 guidance.

| Metric | Q2 FY2026 | YoY / note |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Consolidated net revenue | $9.5B | +9% / +8% constant currency |
| Global comparable sales | +6.2% | transactions +3.8%, ticket +2.3% |
| North America comps | +7.1% | transactions +4.4% |
| U.S. comps | +7.1% | transactions +4.3% |
| China comps | +0.5% | transactions +2.1%, ticket -1.6% |
| [[GAAP]] EPS | $0.45 | +32% |
| Non-[[GAAP]] EPS | $0.50 | +22% |
| Non-[[GAAP]] operating margin | 9.4% | +120 bps |

The quality of the beat was traffic-led. That is the core validation for [[Brian Niccol]]'s Back to Starbucks strategy because price alone would have been a weaker read-through. The caveat is that North America margin still contracted to 9.9% from 11.6% as labor investment, tariff pressure, coffee inflation, and mix offset the comp leverage.

The [[China]] structure also changed. The [[Boyu Capital]] joint venture closed in April: Boyu-managed funds now own 60% of Starbucks China retail operations, while Starbucks retains 40% and licenses the brand and IP. Guidance now assumes China retail shifts to a JV/licensee structure in the second half of FY2026.

FY2026 guidance moved to global and U.S. comp growth of at least 5%, non-[[GAAP]] EPS of $2.25-$2.45, slightly better non-[[GAAP]] consolidated operating margin, and 600-650 net new coffeehouses globally.

*Source: Starbucks Q2 FY2026 earnings release, filed as [[SEC]] 8-K earnings exhibit (Apr 28, 2026). Price move verified in local DB: Apr 28 close $97.28 → Apr 29 close $105.50.*

## Investment case

Bull:
- Turnaround working (traffic +3%, first growth in 2 years)
- Brian Niccol track record (Chipotle success)
- Loyalty program moat (75M members)
- China recovery (+7% comps)
- Investor day catalyst (Jan 29)

Bear:
- EPS still pressured by turnaround costs
- Coffee commodity inflation
- Tariff exposure
- Labor costs/unionization
- China execution risk
- Premium valuation
