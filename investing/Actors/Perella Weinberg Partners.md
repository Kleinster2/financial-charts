# Perella Weinberg Partners

Perella Weinberg Partners (NASDAQ: PWP) is an independent global advisory firm specialising in M&A, capital markets, shareholder advisory, and restructuring. Founded 2006 by [[Joseph Perella]], [[Peter Weinberg]], and [[Terry Meguid]] after Perella and Weinberg each left decades-long careers at [[Morgan Stanley]] and [[Goldman Sachs]] respectively. Went public in June 2021 via merger with FinTech Acquisition Corp IV, a [[SPAC]] sponsored by Betsy Cohen — one of the rare boutique advisory listings from the 2020–2021 SPAC boom to trade well above its debut.

On 2026-04-13 PWP announced the acquisition of London boutique [[Gleacher Shacklock]], the latest in a buy-and-build series (Gleacher 2026, [[Devon Park]] secondaries 2025, [[Tudor Pickering Holt]] energy 2016) aimed at widening the platform into adjacencies that the large integrated banks have traditionally dominated. Stock doubled from its 2021 SPAC-merger reference price and is +25% YTD 2026, an outlier against the SPAC-era cohort — [[Blue Owl]], the largest Wall Street firm to list via SPAC, is -34% YTD and below its 2021 reference.

## Synopsis

- Independent advisory only — no trading book, no lending, no underwriting risk. Revenue is pure fee income from M&A, restructuring, and capital markets advisory. See [[Boutique advisory consolidation]] for sector context.
- [[Joseph Perella]] and [[Peter Weinberg]] each left the integrated banks at the senior partner level (Perella founded Wasserstein Perella in 1988; Weinberg ran Goldman Sachs International and was part of the [[Sidney Weinberg]] family lineage). The pedigree drew Fortune 500 mandates from day one.
- Public via [[SPAC]] (FinTech Acquisition Corp IV) in June 2021. Unlike most 2021 SPAC listings, PWP had real revenue and profit at merger, not projections. Stock has doubled since — a rare SPAC-era survivor in the Wall Street listings cohort (contrast [[Blue Owl]] -34% YTD 2026, below its 2021 SPAC debut).
- Buy-and-build expansion strategy post-IPO. SPAC currency funds tuck-in advisory acquisitions that widen product scope (energy, secondaries, UK industrials) without building from scratch. [[Gleacher Shacklock]] acquisition 2026-04-13 is the third major deal.
- League-table positioning: #20 globally by M&A advisory deal value in Q1 2025 ($21.2B), behind [[Evercore]] ($104.1B, #6), [[Lazard]] ($87.5B, #7), and [[Centerview Partners]] ($85.8B, #8). The Gleacher deal is intended to narrow the gap in EMEA, where Evercore responded first by buying [[Robey Warshaw]] in 2025-07 for £146M / $196M.
- 2025 financials marked recovery: revenue $750.9M (third-highest in firm history), operating income +$48M vs -$78.5M in 2024, net income +$35.5M vs -$64.7M. Management added 25 senior bankers YTD through Q3 2025 (18% of partner base), and European revenue grew >50% YoY in Q3.

| Founder | Prior role | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| [[Terry Meguid]] | Head of Worldwide IB, [[Morgan Stanley]] | Left Morgan Stanley alongside Perella in 2005 |

PWP was founded in 2006 out of [[Morgan Stanley]] after Perella and Meguid left following a boardroom fight over then-CEO [[Philip Purcell]]. Weinberg joined from [[Goldman Sachs]] to complete the founding team. The pattern — senior bankers leaving integrated firms to build independent advisories — had already produced [[Evercore]] (Roger Altman ex-[[Lehman Brothers]], 1995), [[Lazard]] (much older), [[Centerview]] (Blair Effron and Robert Pruzan, 2006), and [[Moelis]] (Ken Moelis ex-UBS, 2007). PWP is part of that independent-advisory wave.

## Notable advisory mandates

- Advised [[Covestro]] on its 2024 sale to [[ADNOC]] (enterprise value €14.7B, equity value €11.7B at €62/share). Largest-ever [[GCC]] acquisition of a European chemicals company.
- Typical mandate mix: large-cap M&A sell-side, independent fairness opinions, restructuring (where the firm competes with [[Houlihan Lokey]] and [[PJT Partners]]), capital markets advisory, and shareholder defence.

## 2021 SPAC listing

Merger partner: FinTech Acquisition Corp IV, a [[SPAC]] sponsor Betsy Cohen (FinTech Acquisitions series). Closed June 2021. Unlike contemporary SPAC listings of pre-revenue concept companies, PWP had a decade-plus track record and positive EBITDA at the time of de-SPAC. The FinTech IV trust was nearly fully subscribed without major redemptions — atypical for 2021, when mean redemption rates were already rising toward the eventual 80–90% peaks. See [[SPAC]] for the broader structural failure pattern; PWP sits in the small list of de-SPAC advisers that cleared merger with real businesses attached.

## Q1 2026 earnings — revenue -30% YoY, back-half guide (May 1, 2026)

PWP reported Q1 2026 before the open on May 1. The print was the first material miss of the YTD outperformance run and confirmed the deal-cycle drag flagged across the [[Boutique advisory consolidation|boutique advisory cohort]]:

| Metric | Q1 2026 | YoY |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $148.9M | -30% (vs $211.8M Q1 2025 record) |
| Adjusted comp & benefits | $117.1M | -17.5% |
| GAAP comp & benefits | $122.1M | -18.2% |
| Adjusted EPS | $0.05 | vs $0.17 consensus |
| Quarterly dividend | $0.07/share | unchanged |
| Cash | $78M | no debt |

Revenue declined on fewer fee-paying clients and lower closings across both M&A and financing/capital solutions, partially offset by higher average fee per client. Management commentary framed 2026 as "meaningfully back-half weighted" — closings deferred rather than lost. The [[Gleacher Shacklock]] integration (announced 2026-04-13) added five partners and three managing directors in the UK during the quarter; revenue contribution is back-half loaded. The dividend was held flat alongside the print.

PWP closed May 1 at $20.61, -9.4% from Apr 30 (-4.2σ on 60-day vol; β-adjusted). The reaction read as expectations reset rather than thesis break: 2025 revenue of $750.9M had set a high bar, the senior-banker additions priced in stronger Q1 conversion, and the back-half-weighted tone deferred validation by 6 months. The [[Lazard]] Q1 print on the same day showed the same Financial Advisory dynamic — net revenue -4% as several large transactions slipped — confirming the deal-cycle drag is sector-wide, not PWP-specific. See [[Perella Weinberg Partners securities]] for the May 1 price detail.

## Why PWP matters

The firm is a test case for two theses:
1. Independent advisory firms capture an increasing share of high-margin M&A fees as clients value conflict-free advice over integrated balance-sheet services. See [[Boutique advisory consolidation]].
2. [[SPAC]] currency — even post-2024 SEC crackdown — can still be deployed productively by real businesses for tuck-in M&A. PWP has used public-stock consideration for [[Devon Park]] (undisclosed) and [[Gleacher Shacklock]] (>$71M stock) without diluting ROE to levels that broke [[Blue Owl]].

The 2026 Gleacher deal also reads as a defensive response to [[Evercore]]'s July 2025 acquisition of [[Robey Warshaw]], a move that reshaped the UK boutique league tables. Both deals concentrate mid-teen-count-partner London shops inside larger US-listed platforms — consolidation at the top of the independent tier while the mega-banks ([[Goldman Sachs]], [[Morgan Stanley]], [[JPMorgan]]) retain the largest integrated mandates.
