**Arbor Realty Trust** — Mortgage REIT and direct lender specializing in multifamily and CRE. **Top 10 GSE lender for 10+ years.** Public (NYSE: ABR). Balance sheet model — originates and holds loans, unlike fee-based brokers.

## Insights

- **Balance sheet lender** — Unlike Walker & Dunlop or Berkadia (originate-to-sell), Arbor holds loans on balance sheet. Different risk/return profile — earns spread, not fees.
- **High dividend yield** — ~12% yield reflects mortgage REIT structure. Required to distribute 90% of taxable income.
- **Small balance specialist** — Top 3 Freddie Mac Small Balance Loan lender, top 5 Fannie Mae Small Loans. Niche in smaller multifamily deals.

## Investment implications

**Bull case:**
- 12% dividend yield attractive in rate environment
- Multifamily focus (housing shortage tailwind)
- Small balance niche less competitive
- GSE relationships provide origination flow
- S&P 600 inclusion improves liquidity

**Bear case:**
- Credit risk on balance sheet loans
- Rate sensitivity (spread compression)
- CRE stress could hit multifamily
- Mortgage REIT volatility
- Dividend sustainability if credit losses rise
