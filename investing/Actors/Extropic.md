**Extropic** is a private physics-based AI computing company founded in 2022 by [[Guillaume Verdon]] and [[Trevor McCourt]], building [[Thermodynamic computing]] hardware and software for energy-efficient probabilistic AI workloads. Its relevance to [[HOF Capital]] is post-GPU optionality: a small, technical bet that AI compute may need new physical primitives rather than only larger GPU clusters.

Extropic's pitch is that useful AI computation can harness physical noise instead of suppressing it. The company describes XTR-0 as a silicon thermodynamic processor and pairs the hardware with software such as the THRML library and denoising thermodynamic models. That makes Extropic more speculative than HOF's trophy AI lab positions, but potentially more convex if energy efficiency becomes the binding constraint for generative AI inference.

## HOF second-level read

Extropic is the high-variance technical edge in the HOF cluster. Where [[Anthropic]], [[OpenAI]], and [[xAI]] express demand for AI compute, Extropic is a bet on changing the supply curve of compute itself. The note should be read as a technology option, not as evidence that thermodynamic computing is already commercially proven.

## Evolution

2022: [[Guillaume Verdon]] and [[Trevor McCourt]] founded Extropic to commercialize a physics-based computing idea rather than another model wrapper. The premise was that AI's long-run bottleneck may be energy and sampling efficiency, not just model architecture.

2023: Extropic announced a $14.1M seed round with [[HOF Capital]] participating. This is the HOF-relevant moment: the company was still early and technical, but the investor list showed venture appetite for post-GPU compute primitives.

2024-2026: Extropic moved from stealth language toward a public thermodynamic-computing platform, including XTR-0, THRML, and denoising thermodynamic models. The commercial evidence remains early, so the note treats Extropic as a technology option rather than a proven semiconductor company.
