Fenty Beauty is a cosmetics brand founded by [[Rihanna]] in 2017 as a joint venture with Kendo, the beauty incubator owned by [[LVMH]]. The brand launched with 40 foundation shades (later expanded to 50+), a range that was unprecedented in the prestige beauty market at the time. This inclusivity-first approach generated $100M in sales within the first 40 days — an industry record for a beauty launch — and forced competitors including [[Estee Lauder]] and L'Oreal to rapidly expand their own shade ranges in what the industry dubbed "the Fenty Effect."

Rihanna owns 50% of the venture, making it the most financially significant celebrity beauty brand in history. Revenue exceeds $600M annually, and Forbes valued the brand at ~$2.8B in 2021, which made Rihanna a billionaire (the first from Barbados). The brand's success validated the celebrity-to-brand pipeline that [[Kylie Cosmetics]], [[SKIMS]], and [[Rhode]] have since followed, though Fenty's Kendo/LVMH manufacturing and distribution infrastructure gave it a quality and scale advantage that pure-DTC celebrity brands struggle to match. Fenty Skin (skincare, launched 2020) and Fenty Fragrance extend the brand across categories.
