**Veritas Capital** is a New York-based private equity firm specializing in government services and technology-enabled solutions for the public sector. Founded in 1992 by Robert B. McKeon, the firm has completed over 90 acquisitions with a focus on companies that serve federal agencies across defense, healthcare, education, and national security. AUM exceeds $45B.

Veritas operates a distinctive playbook: acquire mid-cap government IT and defense contractors, consolidate them into platforms, and sell to strategic buyers or larger PE firms at defense-sector multiples. The strategy works because government contracting has high barriers to entry (clearances, past performance requirements, ITAR compliance) and sticky, long-cycle revenue. [[Peraton]] is the flagship — assembled from Northrop Grumman IT, Harris IT, and DXC Technology's US public sector business, then sold to [[Veritas Capital|Veritas]]-backed [[Peraton]] in a series of deals.

McKeon died in 2012; the firm continued under managing director Ramzi Musallam, who became CEO and has led the largest fundraises in the firm's history.
