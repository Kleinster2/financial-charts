David Friedberg is the CEO of The Production Board, a holding company and venture platform investing in science-based businesses across food, agriculture, energy, and climate. He is a co-host of the [[All-In Podcast]], one of the most influential tech-investor media properties.

## Synopsis

Friedberg's credibility rests on the Climate Corporation exit — he founded the agricultural data company in 2006, built weather-risk models for farmers, and sold it to [[Monsanto]] for approximately $1.1B in 2013. That exit funded The Production Board, which takes a holding-company approach rather than a traditional fund structure: it incubates and operates companies rather than writing checks passively. Portfolio companies span cultured meat, precision fermentation, advanced materials, and energy infrastructure — the common thread being deep science with long commercialization timelines.

As a co-host of the [[All-In Podcast]] alongside [[Chamath Palihapitiya]], [[Jason Calacanis]], and [[David Sacks]], Friedberg occupies the "science explainer" niche. His influence extends beyond the podcast into policy discussions around agricultural technology, climate adaptation, and food security. The investing lens: Friedberg sits at the intersection of venture capital and industrial science — sectors where regulatory approval timelines and capital intensity create barriers to entry but also moats for first movers.
