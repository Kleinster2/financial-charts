# Sunac

Chinese property developer founded 2003 in Tianjin by [[Sun Hongbin]]. Listed on Hong Kong (1918.HK) since 2010. Sunac defaulted on offshore dollar bonds on May 11, 2022 (grace-period expiry on a $29.5M coupon payment on a $741.6M / 7.95% bond) — earlier than [[Country Garden]], months after [[Evergrande]]'s December 2021 default — and completed an offshore-debt restructuring in November 2023. The firm is one of the more aggressive players in the pre-2020 Chinese property M&A market (acquired Tianjin Wanda Cultural Tourism assets from [[Wanda]] in 2017 for ~RMB 63B) and the restructuring has involved the broadest creditor mix of any major Chinese developer default.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. Second major default (after Evergrande) — Sunac's May 2022 default established the precedent that the [[Three Red Lines]] correction would cascade beyond the most-leveraged firm. Sunac was widely viewed as more conservatively managed than Evergrande, and its default reset the analyst expectation about which developers could survive
2. Wanda asset transaction — the 2017 acquisition is the canonical case of Chinese property-cycle M&A at peak narrative; Sun Hongbin and [[Wang Jianlin]] handled it as a leverage-up-the-balance-sheet trade. The subsequent default makes the deal a useful reference point for cycle-top M&A signals
3. Restructuring precedent — the November 2023 restructuring is the template Chinese developers have used since: debt-to-equity conversion, new-note issuance, partial recovery for offshore creditors. Country Garden's 2024-2026 restructuring largely follows the Sunac template
