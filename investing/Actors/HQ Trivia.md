# HQ Trivia

Live mobile trivia game show app that briefly became a cultural phenomenon in 2017-2018, peaking at over 2M concurrent players before collapsing from unsustainable economics, internal dysfunction, and founder tragedy. A cautionary tale in viral consumer apps and the gap between engagement and monetization.

## Synopsis

HQ Trivia launched in August 2017, created by [[Vine]] co-founders Rus Yusupov and Colin Kroll. The concept was simple — a live, twice-daily trivia game hosted by Scott Rogowsky where players answered 12 questions for a share of a cash prize pool (typically $5,000-$25,000). The app went viral almost immediately, reaching 2M+ concurrent players by early 2018 and becoming the #1 free app in the App Store. The live format, charismatic host, and real cash prizes created appointment viewing on smartphones — something no other app had achieved at that scale. HQ raised $15M at a $100M valuation from Founders Fund and others.

The collapse was swift and total. Co-founder Colin Kroll died of an accidental drug overdose in December 2018, leaving Yusupov as sole leader. Concurrently, the audience cratered — average concurrent players dropped from 2M+ to under 100,000 by mid-2019 as the novelty wore off and prize pools couldn't scale with audience decline. The company cycled through formats (HQ Words, HQ Sports), fired Rogowsky, burned through cash, and explored sales to multiple buyers. HQ shut down in February 2020 with 25 employees. The app was briefly relaunched and re-shut-down multiple times afterward. As an investment case study, HQ illustrates the fragility of attention-based consumer apps that lack network effects, content moats, or recurring revenue — peak virality can coexist with zero durable value.
