# Rachel Reeves

Rachel Reeves — [[UK]] Chancellor of the Exchequer (Labour, from July 2024). First woman to hold the role. Two distinct policy threads run through her chancellorship: (1) [[UK]] capital markets reform, where she has been actively courting major private fintechs for London listings as part of a broader push to deepen [[UK]] equity markets; (2) crisis management on insurance and shipping during the [[Iran conflict economic disruption|2026 Iran conflict]] disruption.

Reeves is the public face of the [[UK]] government's pitch to fintechs like [[Revolut]] and [[Monzo]] that London should be the listing venue. The pitch comes with structural reforms — listing-rules changes, pension reform pushing UK pension funds toward UK equities, Mansion House speeches as the policy delivery vehicle — but is running against the gravitational pull of [[NYSE]] valuations and US institutional depth. Whether either neobank ultimately lists in London is a real test of the reform package.

## UK capital markets reform / fintech listing push

The [[UK]] government's listing-venue pitch has had mixed traction. [[Revolut]] continues to lean [[NYSE]] for its 2026 IPO despite repeated [[UK]] government overtures and the March 2026 full UK banking licence from the [[Bank of England]]. [[Monzo]]'s May 2026 FY26 results call (Diana Layfield: "we're not in a hurry") opened the door wider — Monzo is profitable, well-capitalised, and Layfield has held discussions with [[UK]] ministers on a London listing.

The broader reform package includes:
- Listing rule modernisation (allowing dual-class shares, easing free-float requirements)
- [[Pension reform]] — Mansion House Compact / Mansion House Accord pushing UK DC pension schemes toward higher UK equity allocations
- [[AIM]] reforms (junior-market structure) — reviewing junior market structure
- ISA reforms — increasing allowance for UK-listed equities

The structural problem the reforms try to solve: [[UK]] equity markets have lost relative scale to [[NYSE]] and [[Asia]] over two decades; UK domestic institutions have systematically reduced UK equity weight; major UK-domiciled tech and fintech firms have defaulted to US listings.

A Monzo London listing would be a real political win for Reeves given Revolut's expected defection. A Revolut London listing would be the headline win but is the less likely outcome.
