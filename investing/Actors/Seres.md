Seres (formerly Cyrus / Sokon) is a [[Chongqing]]-based EV manufacturer best known as the manufacturing partner behind the [[Aito]] brand co-developed with [[Huawei]]. Seres builds and assembles the vehicles; Huawei contributes the smart-cabin, ADAS, and software stack. Listed in [[Shanghai Stock Exchange|Shanghai]] under 601127.SS.

## Why Seres matters

Seres is one of the cleanest examples of [[Reverse tech transfer in China auto|reverse tech transfer]] inside [[China]]: a domestic OEM whose competitive moat is being attached to [[Huawei]]'s software/ADAS rather than built independently. The [[Aito M9]] flagship — full-size SUV with on-board fridge, karaoke, projector headlights, and (optional) on-board toilet — is the canonical case study of feature-rich, software-defined vehicle product positioning that has reset the Chinese ultra-luxury segment.

By embedding Huawei tech, Seres has effectively traded a pure-OEM future for a co-development model where the brand identity and consumer pull come substantially from [[Huawei]] (HarmonyOS, ADrive). This is the opposite of the foreign-JV pattern of the 1990s-2010s — here the *Chinese* OEM is the manufacturing partner, and the technology stack belongs to a different domestic company.

| Model | Segment | Distinctive features |
|-------|---------|------------------------|
| [[Aito M5]] | Mid-size SUV | First Aito; HarmonyOS cabin; ADAS via Huawei |
| [[Aito M7]] | Larger SUV | Higher-volume seller |
| [[Aito M9]] | Full-size luxury SUV | On-board refrigerator, karaoke system, projector headlights (color), licensed on-board toilet option, Huawei ADrive autonomy |

[[Aito]] has risen to the top of [[China]]'s ultra-luxury segment by offering feature combinations no foreign luxury OEM can match domestically — the M9's dynamic-color projector headlights are a clean example of "feature-as-marketing" displacement of conventional luxury cues.
