**Saint-Gobain** — French multinational and one of the world's oldest companies (founded 1665 by royal charter of Louis XIV to manufacture mirrors for the Palace of Versailles). Now a diversified construction materials and high-performance solutions group with ~160,000 employees across 75+ countries. Revenue ~€47B (2024). The company has transformed under CEO Benoit Bazin (since 2021) from a conglomerate-discount industrial into a focused, higher-margin building materials platform through aggressive portfolio pruning and €8B+ in disposals.

## Synopsis

Saint-Gobain's investment case centers on the building renovation mega-cycle: European energy efficiency mandates (EU Green Deal, national retrofit programs) drive demand for its insulation, glazing, and gypsum products. The company estimates that ~75% of its revenue is exposed to renovation/retrofit activity, positioning it as a structural beneficiary of the regulatory push to decarbonize building stock. Margins have expanded from ~6% operating margin pre-Bazin to ~10%+ as the portfolio shifted toward higher-value solutions and away from commoditized distribution.

In the glass segment specifically, Saint-Gobain competes with [[AGC Inc.]], [[NSG Group]], and [[Fuyao Glass Industry Group]] in automotive and flat glass, though this is now a smaller portion of total revenue (~15%) versus construction products (~60%). The stock trades on Euronext Paris (SGO) and has been a strong performer since the 2021 transformation began.
