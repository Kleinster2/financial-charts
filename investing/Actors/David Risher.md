**David Risher** is CEO of [[Lyft]], brought in as an outsider in April 2023 to turn around a company that had lost its way competitively and financially under co-founders Logan Green and John Zimmer.

## Synopsis

Risher's appointment was a desperation move by a board watching [[Lyft]] bleed market share to [[Uber]] (falling from ~38% US rideshare to ~26%) while burning cash. His background was unusual for a rideshare CEO: VP of retail at [[Amazon]] in the 1990s (employee #37), then over a decade running Worldreader, a nonprofit distributing e-books in developing countries. The board bet that operational discipline from Amazon and a fresh perspective would matter more than mobility industry experience. Early returns were encouraging — Risher cut 30% of staff in his first weeks, eliminated the bike and scooter divisions, simplified pricing, and brought Lyft back to adjusted EBITDA profitability.

The question for investors is whether Risher can close the structural gap with [[Uber]] or whether [[Lyft]] is permanently the #2 player in a market that rewards network effects. His strategy has been to stop competing on breadth (no food delivery, no freight, no international) and focus on rider experience and driver supply in the US and Canada. The risk is that scale economics in rideshare are brutal — [[Uber]]'s 74% market share means better driver utilization, lower wait times, and more pricing power. Risher has steadied the ship, but the stock (which was ~$50 at IPO) trades in the $12-15 range, reflecting permanent discount status.
