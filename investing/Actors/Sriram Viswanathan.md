## Background

Spent roughly two decades at [[Intel Capital]], investing across the semiconductor and hardware stack. Left to found IndusAge Partners, a cross-border US-India venture firm. IndusAge later merged with another fund to form [[Celesta Capital]] (~2016), where Sriram serves as Founding Managing Partner alongside [[Lip-Bu Tan]], Nicholas Brathwaite, and Michael Marks.

On the AI infrastructure opportunity: "Semi was a four-letter word in Silicon Valley, and now it's sexy." His view is that the innovation constraint shifted from software/SaaS business models to raw compute and energy — and that's what makes hardware investable again.

Key thesis from a March 2026 interview: the constraint in AI infrastructure flows sequentially — GPU → memory → networking → power management → energy — and each shift opens a new venture window. On [[NVIDIA]]'s moat: "It's no longer just about semiconductors. It's about the software ecosystem."
