# Sam Woods

Deputy Governor of the [[Bank of England]] for prudential regulation and chief executive of the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA), the UK's bank regulator. In the vault he is the official face of the UK leg of the [[Competitive deregulation]] race: on June 19 2026 the PRA proposed diluting the UK's Basel III market-risk (FRTB) rules "to support international alignment" with US and EU easing.

## Notable

On June 19 2026 the PRA under Woods proposed lowering capital for thinly traded, hard-to-value securities and extending model-monitoring periods — a roughly one-third (£700mn) capital cut for the 11 banks doing 95% of affected UK capital-markets activity (more than two-thirds of it via UK subsidiaries of Wall Street banks). Woods said the rules "may be more capital-intensive and operationally burdensome than originally intended." The PRA simultaneously published a system-wide stress test for the $16tn private markets (see [[Private credit]]).
