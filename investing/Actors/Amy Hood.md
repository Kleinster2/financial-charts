**Amy Hood** — CFO of [[Microsoft]] since 2013. First female CFO in company history. Architect of cloud pivot capital allocation and major acquisitions.

---

## Background

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Born | August 9, 1971 |
| Hometown | Morehead, Kentucky → Nashville, Tennessee |
| Education | BA Economics ([[Duke University]] 1994), MBA ([[Harvard Business School]]) |
| Pre-Microsoft | [[Goldman Sachs]] (8 years, investment banking) |
| At Microsoft | 2002 - present (24 years) |
| CFO since | May 8, 2013 |

Father was a doctor, mother taught nursing. Was on math team in school. Notably studied economics rather than accounting — atypical for CFO track.

---

## Career at Microsoft

| Period | Role |
|--------|------|
| 2002-2006 | Senior Director, Investor Relations |
| 2006-2010 | Chief of Staff, Server & Tools Business |
| 2010-2013 | CFO, Microsoft Business Division |
| 2013-present | EVP & CFO |

Worked under [[Satya Nadella]] in Server & Tools before both rose to top roles.

---

## Key moves as CFO

**Capital allocation:**
- Shifted investment from Windows to Azure cloud
- Oversaw 57+ deals
- Managed $75B+ Activision regulatory gauntlet

**Major acquisitions:**
- [[LinkedIn]] — $26B (2016)
- [[GitHub]] — $7.5B (2018)
- [[Nuance]] — $19.7B (2021)
- [[Activision Blizzard]] — $69B (2023) — largest tech acquisition in history

**Results:**
- Stock +300% in first 5 years as CFO
- Navigated AI capex surge ($83B TTM) while maintaining shareholder returns
