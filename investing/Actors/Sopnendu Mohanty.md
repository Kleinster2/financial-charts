# Sopnendu Mohanty

Sopnendu Mohanty was the founding Chief FinTech Officer of the [[MAS|Monetary Authority of Singapore]] from August 2015 until his departure to private-sector ventures in 2024-2025, a near-decade tenure during which he built the architecture that established [[Singapore]] as the global leader in regulated digital asset innovation. Before MAS he spent more than two decades at [[Citigroup]], most recently as Global Head of the Consumer Lab Network and Programs. He is now co-founder and Group CEO of Global Finance & Technology Network (GFTN), a global advisory and investment firm with Forums, Advisory, Platform, and Capital operating arms. Awarded Market Reformer of the Year by Euromoney/IFL.

## What Mohanty built at MAS

The Singapore digital asset framework is essentially Mohanty's architecture. Concrete programs and infrastructure built under his tenure:

- API Exchange (APIX) — cross-border fintech API integration platform
- Singapore FinTech Festival — annual industry convening, now ~60,000 attendees
- Project Ubin — the multi-phase blockchain-based wholesale payments and settlement experiment that informed [[MAS]]'s digital assets posture
- Payment Rails infrastructure development
- Data exchange platforms

The regulatory output most relevant to stablecoin market structure is the 100% liquid-reserve framework requirement Singapore adopted alongside [[Japan]] — among the first jurisdictions globally to mandate full backing. This gave compliant issuers a clear path while excluding under-reserved structures by design.

## Public framing on stablecoin regulation

Mohanty's stated view (Bloomberg TV, May 2026): the principle of payment regulation should not change just because the rail is new. KYC, AML, terrorism financing checks are "hygiene" — non-negotiable across any rail. Existing payment regulation with incremental coverage for stablecoin-specific technical requirements is sufficient; there is no need for a new regulatory category.

This positions Mohanty as a methodological centrist in the global stablecoin regulatory debate — distinct from the EU's [[MiCA]] framework (separate licensing regime) and from the US [[GENIUS Act]] approach (federal-level new statute). Singapore's approach under his architecture treats stablecoin issuance as a payments service requiring existing payments licensure plus incremental technical disclosure, not a new asset-class category.

The framing was reinforced by his Singapore-specific framing: "we don't do technology for sake of technology" — innovation focus subordinated to use-case validation. The single biggest unsolved payment-system problem he identifies is cross-border payments, which is where stablecoins are a great product fit.

## Why Singapore became the regulatory leader

Mohanty's argument for why Singapore got to the front of the pack:

1. The country's structural focus on use cases rather than technology in isolation
2. Willingness to write well-defined regulation early — eliminating ambiguity for legitimate operators while excluding under-reserved structures
3. Building public-goods infrastructure (APIX, FinTech Festival, Project Ubin) that anchors industry to the jurisdiction
4. Treating regulatory clarity as a scale enabler — compliance costs are high but amortize across $100B+ infrastructure throughput
