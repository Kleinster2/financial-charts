[[HawkEye 360]] — Public RF signal-intelligence (SIGINT) satellite-data company. NYSE: HAWK since May 7, 2026 IPO. ~$3.15B initial market cap. Headquartered Herndon, Virginia. Founded 2015 by former defense officials. The world's first commercial space-enabled defense technology company built exclusively around radio-frequency signals intelligence — designs and operates a constellation of small satellites that collect RF data, then processes it through proprietary signal-processing and AI-enabled analytics to detect, geolocate, classify, and track emitters (radars, jammers, vessels, communications networks).

The investing-vault relevance is the cleanest pure-play public-equity exposure to the [[Space-based ISR|space-based ISR]] / [[Golden Dome]] / [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war intelligence-cycle]] thesis. The May 7 NYSE debut at +30% (closing $34.00 vs $26 IPO price) is the cleanest single-name confirmation of the defense-tech IPO momentum thread that has run through 2025-2026. The contrast with the same-day [[BlackSky]] -25.4% Q1 print is the diagnostic divergence — both are space-intelligence names, but the market is sharply differentiating between RF-SIGINT (HAWK) and optical-imagery (BKSY) business models.

## Q1 2026 / first-day reaction context

May 7, 2026 was a notable single-day for the space-intelligence cohort:

| Name | Move | Catalyst |

| [[Planet Labs\|PL]] | (modest) | No own catalyst |
| [[Spire Global\|SPIR]] | (modest) | No own catalyst |

The HAWK / BKSY divergence is striking: in absolute dollar terms HAWK closed at $34 with $3.15B market cap; BKSY closed at $30.24 with ~$400M market cap. BKSY had been the cleanest existing public-equity expression of the space-intelligence thesis until today; HAWK now displaces it as the larger, profitable pure-play. The weakness in BKSY was specifically attributed to government-commercial-imagery cycle softness (per [[BlackSky]] May 7 Q1 section); HAWK's strength on debut tests the same channel from the SIGINT side and produces the opposite result. The differentiator is contract-type: HAWK's backlog is primarily multi-year IDIQ awards, while BKSY operates on annual government-commercial-imagery contract cycles.

For the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war intelligence cycle]] thread: the war's RF-spectrum demands (jammer detection, comms intercept, threat-emitter geolocation) are exactly the workflow HAWK is built to monetize. Government IDIQ awards spiked in 2025 in part because of this dynamic.
