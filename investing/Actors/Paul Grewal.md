**Paul Grewal** — Chief Legal Officer of [[Coinbase]]. Former federal magistrate judge. Leads the company's regulatory and litigation strategy at a moment when crypto's legal framework is still being written.

---

## Synopsis

Grewal is the public face of [[Coinbase]]'s legal war with the [[SEC]]. Before joining in 2020, he served as a US Magistrate Judge in the Northern District of California and held senior legal roles at [[Meta|Facebook]], giving him both courtroom credibility and big-tech regulatory experience. His appointment signaled that Coinbase was preparing for a protracted legal battle — not just compliance, but constitutional-level arguments about regulatory overreach and the definition of securities.

The stakes of Grewal's work extend beyond Coinbase. The outcomes of [[Coinbase]]'s SEC litigation and its advocacy for clear crypto legislation will shape the regulatory architecture for the entire digital asset industry. If Coinbase wins on the merits — that most tokens listed on its exchange are not securities — it opens the floodgates for US-based crypto trading. If it loses, the exchange faces existential restructuring. Grewal's ability to frame crypto regulation as a due-process issue rather than an enforcement one has made him the most consequential legal figure in the space.
