# Wedbush

Mid-tier [[United States]] investment bank and brokerage, founded in 1955 and headquartered in Los Angeles. Best known for its equity research arm, particularly [[Dan Ives]]' hyperbolically bullish tech and AI coverage, which drives outsized retail and media attention relative to the firm's institutional footprint.

## Synopsis

Wedbush Securities occupies an unusual niche on Wall Street: a family-controlled, mid-tier broker-dealer that punches above its weight in media influence through a single analyst. [[Dan Ives]] — their managing director of equity research — has become one of the most-quoted tech analysts in financial media, with a consistently bullish posture on [[Apple]], [[Tesla]], AI infrastructure, and cybersecurity names. His calls generate significant retail attention and social media engagement, though institutional investors often discount the analysis as promotional rather than rigorous. The firm's research is widely distributed through financial news aggregators, giving it visibility that firms with stronger institutional coverage lack.

Beyond the research arm, Wedbush operates a meaningful securities clearing and execution business, including electronic market-making and clearing services for other broker-dealers. The firm also has a wealth management division and institutional sales trading operation. Founded by Edward Wedbush, the firm remains private and family-controlled, which means it operates outside the quarterly earnings pressure that shapes publicly traded peers. The investment relevance is primarily as a signal amplifier — when Ives issues a price target or upgrades a name, the retail flow impact can be measurable in the short term, even if institutional desks dismiss the call.
