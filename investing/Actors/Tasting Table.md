**Tasting Table** was a food-focused digital media publication co-founded by [[John McDonald (restaurateur)|John McDonald]] in 2008, one of the early email-native food content brands before the category was absorbed by larger platforms.

## Synopsis

Tasting Table launched in 2008 as a daily email newsletter covering food, dining, drinks, and recipes — the thesis was that food enthusiasts wanted curated editorial delivered to their inbox rather than browsing food blogs. Co-founded by restaurateur [[John McDonald (restaurateur)|John McDonald]] and media entrepreneur Geoff Bartakovics, the publication built a loyal subscriber base through city-specific editions (NYC, LA, Chicago, SF) with restaurant reviews, chef profiles, and recipe content. At its peak it reached several hundred thousand email subscribers, establishing credibility in a space that would later be dominated by Instagram food culture.

The publication was acquired by [[Static Media]] (formerly Literally Media) in 2016, joining a portfolio of niche content sites including Mashed, The Daily Meal, and Grunge. Under Static Media's ownership, Tasting Table pivoted from email-first to SEO-driven web content — high-volume food articles optimized for Google search traffic. The editorial voice shifted from curated NYC dining authority to mass-market recipe and food explainer content. For the vault, Tasting Table matters primarily through the McDonald connection: it illustrates how a restaurateur (who went on to build [[Mercer Street Hospitality]]) leveraged media as a parallel venture to the restaurant business, a playbook that has since become common (Momofuku/David Chang, Milk Bar, etc.).
