## Synopsis

**Anduril** is the highest-profile insurgent in the U.S. defense-industrial base — a software-first weapons company built on the thesis that autonomous systems will replace the crewed platforms that legacy primes charge hundreds of millions to maintain. The pitch: AI-backed drones, fighter jets, submarines, and combat vehicles that sit dormant until activated, eliminating the personnel and logistics cost of traditional arsenals. Luckey calls them "Twinkies" — long shelf life, push a button when you need them.

The business has scaled fast: ~$2B revenue in 2025, $6B+ in global contracts, nearly $7B raised at ~$31B valuation. Anduril won a spot in the Air Force's Collaborative Combat Aircraft program (April 2024) over [[Boeing]], [[Lockheed Martin]], and [[Northrop Grumman]], and has picked up targeted contracts — $99M Air Force autonomy software (Feb 2025), Microsoft's Army VR headset program (Feb 2025), $642M Marine Corps counter-drone (Mar 2025). A $1B factory near Columbus, Ohio is planned for up to 4,000 workers.

The bear case is real: Ukraine field tests exposed weaknesses (Ghost drones failed on GPS loss), the company has less infrastructure and manufacturing experience than the primes it's trying to replace, and scaling from prototype to production at defense scale is the graveyard of ambitious startups. Pentagon officials privately flag this as the key risk. But Luckey's positioning with the Trump administration — he personally suggested renaming the Department of Defense to the Department of War — and [[Pete Hegseth]]'s enthusiasm for AI in defense have made Anduril the administration's favorite disruptor. IPO discussions are underway.

## Meta partnership (May 2025 → Jan 2026)

Reconciliation timeline:
- Sep 2024: [[Meta]] invited Luckey to visit. Luckey posted photo with Meta's advanced AR glasses.
- Sep 2024: Andrew Bosworth (Meta CTO) publicly apologized for prior comments about Luckey's 2017 firing. Luckey responded: "I am infamously good at holding grudges, but Meta has changed a lot over the past 8 years."
- May 2025: Anduril and [[Meta]] announced partnership on VR/AR technologies for the battlefield
- Jan 2026: AI-powered military helmet partnership formalized

AI-powered military helmet:
- Combines Anduril defense AI + Meta Reality Labs hardware
- VR/AR features for battlefield awareness
- Luckey regains access to Meta's VR patent portfolio, many of which he originated at Oculus

Andrew Bosworth (Meta CTO):
> Silicon Valley's new openness to work with US military is a "return to grace."

Significance: Major consumer tech company partnering with defense startup. The deal also represents personal reconciliation between Luckey and the company that fired him.

## [[Ukraine]] connection

Luckey visited [[Ukraine]] for a week in 2022, observed how $3K Chinese consumer drones (half the cost of a traditional artillery shell) were precisely attacking targets.

Field test results (mixed):
- **Ghost drones**: failed when they lost GPS signal — Ukrainian army stopped using them
- **Altius munitions**: more successful but expensive compared to alternatives — Anduril says deliveries continue
- Experience informed Anduril's autonomy-first design philosophy — systems that can operate without GPS or continuous comms

## Rare earth exposure

Autonomous systems depend on rare earth permanent magnets:

| Product | Rare earth use |
|---------|----------------|
| Ghost drones | Motor magnets (Nd, Dy) |
| Roadrunner | Jet engine components |
| [[Sensors]] | Various REEs |

Dec 2025 military denial: [[China]] blocks all rare earth exports to military-affiliated buyers. See [[Rare earth leverage]].

**Risk**: As a defense startup, Anduril has less stockpile buffer than [[Lockheed Martin]]. Supply chain disruption could delay production.

**Mitigation**: Smaller product volumes than legacy primes. [[AUKUS]] partners ([[Australia]]) developing rare earth capacity.

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Trump allocated $1T+ for military modernization — Anduril directly aligned
- CCA win over all three legacy primes validates technology credibility
- ~$2B revenue, $6B+ contracts — real business, not just demos
- Software-first model = higher margins than cost-plus legacy contractors
- Autonomy thesis ("Twinkies") — systems that sit dormant until needed, no crew costs
- [[AUKUS]] tailwind, [[Meta]] partnership expands capabilities
- IPO path gives liquidity event

**Bear:**
- Ukraine field tests exposed weaknesses (Ghost GPS failure)
- Pentagon officials privately flag scaling risk — less infrastructure than primes
- Still has to prove mass production capability (Ohio factory not yet built)
- Government contracting concentration risk
- Competition from both legacy primes and startups ([[Skydio]], [[Shield AI]])
- Private = illiquid until IPO
- Rare earth supply vulnerability
- $31B valuation prices in a lot of execution

## Trump administration alignment

Luckey personally suggested to Trump before inauguration (Jan 2025) that the Department of Defense be renamed the Department of War. Trump later did exactly that.

Defense Secretary [[Pete Hegseth]] visited Anduril HQ (Dec 2025), posted praise. Army Secretary Daniel Driscoll called Luckey "an amazing innovator." Senator Kevin Cramer (R-ND) created the Senate Defense Modernization Caucus after visiting Anduril (Feb 2024).

Hegseth's push to apply AI expansively in defense led to a clash with [[Anthropic]] over using its technology in classified systems — Anduril benefits from the broader push toward Silicon Valley defense partnerships.
