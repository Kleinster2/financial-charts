## Alibaba Token Hub

In March 2026, CEO Eddie Wu created "Alibaba Token Hub" as a **first-tier business group** — consolidating Tongyi Laboratory, MaaS Business Line, [[Qwen]], Wukong, and AI Innovation under one structure. Wu leads directly.

This is the most significant organizational signal: Alibaba is treating token production and distribution as important as commerce or cloud infrastructure. The vertical integration is complete:

| Layer | Alibaba asset |
|-------|--------------|
| Model | [[Qwen]] (open-source, 1B+ downloads) |
| Cloud | Alibaba Cloud (#1 China) |
| Enterprise | Model Studio, DingTalk AI |
| Silicon | [[Zhenwu 810E]] (AI accelerator), [[XuanTie C950]] (RISC-V CPU) |

## Strategy: full-stack integration

Alibaba's competitive theory: own every layer and monetize the total stack, not just the token.

**Revenue over volume:** Measures success by total AI revenue, not token consumption. When [[Volcano Engine]] advertises 49.2% MaaS share, Alibaba counters that MaaS token counts are "the tip of the iceberg" — most enterprise [[Qwen]] deployments are private, invisible to external data.

**Open-source as IaaS funnel:** [[Qwen]]'s 1B+ downloads aren't altruism — they starve MaaS-only competitors of the private deployment market while creating a pipeline back to Alibaba Cloud. Developers fine-tune Qwen locally, but production scale requires cloud compute. Open-source generates IaaS demand.

**MaaS as largest product:** Management guided that MaaS is expected to become Cloud Intelligence Group's single largest revenue product — bigger than traditional compute, storage, or database services.

**Core customers:** Financial services and retail account for ~45% of revenue. These are compliance-heavy, hybrid-cloud buyers who value security, data sovereignty, and integration over token price.

## Competitive position

| Metric | Alibaba Cloud | [[Volcano Engine]] | [[Tencent Cloud]] | [[Huawei Cloud]] |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Cloud share | **~35%** (#1) | Late entrant | ~20% (#2-3) | #2-3, declining |
| MaaS tokens | 27% | **49.2%** | — | — |
| Self-dev model share | **17.7%** | 14.1% | — | — |
| Revenue growth | **+36%** | Explosive (small base) | +22% | **-3.5%** external |
| Strategy | Full stack | Commodity tokens | Aggregator | Hardware stack |

**Key advantage over Volcano Engine:** Alibaba generates real revenue at scale. ¥43.28B quarterly vs Volcano Engine's ¥10B annual *target*. The token volume lead doesn't matter if it can't convert to margin.

**Key vulnerability:** Developer ecosystem loyalty. If Volcano Engine's near-zero pricing captures the next generation of AI developers, enterprise contracts follow them upward.
