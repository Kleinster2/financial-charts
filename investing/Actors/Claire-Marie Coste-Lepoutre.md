Claire-Marie Coste-Lepoutre is the CFO of [[Allianz]], appointed in January 2024 as part of CEO [[Oliver Bäte]]'s leadership refresh. She oversees Finance, Risk, Actuarial, Legal, and Compliance across the world's largest insurer by premiums.

## Synopsis

Coste-Lepoutre came to [[Allianz]] from [[AXA]], where she spent over two decades rising through finance and risk roles, most recently as Group Chief Risk Officer. Her appointment signaled Bäte's intent to professionalize the C-suite after the [[Gregoire Tournant]] Structured Alpha fraud debacle — bringing in an external hire with deep risk management credentials to restore credibility with regulators and investors. The breadth of her mandate (five functions reporting to one CFO) is unusual for a European insurer of Allianz's scale and reflects a consolidation of oversight.

Her public stance on [[Private Credit]] — declaring in February 2026 that she was "very comfortable" with Allianz's exposure — matters because it positions the firm as a willing LP at a time when competitors are pulling back amid BDC write-downs and liquidity concerns in retail private credit vehicles. Allianz's insurance float gives it duration tolerance that shorter-horizon investors lack, making it a natural anchor buyer if spreads widen. Whether her comfort is warranted depends on underwriting discipline in an asset class where vintage matters enormously.
