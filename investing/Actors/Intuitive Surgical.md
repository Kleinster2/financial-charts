## Q1 2026 earnings — blowout procedure growth, da Vinci 5 adoption >50%, guidance raise (Apr 22, 2026)

Pulled the earnings report forward from Apr 28 to Apr 22 and delivered one of the cleanest beats in medtech this cycle. Revenue of $2.77B (+23% YoY) blew past the $2.62B consensus by $150M — the largest revenue beat since the post-COVID procedure recovery. Stock closed +7.2% on Apr 22 and has room to run as FY26 EPS consensus resets higher.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $2.77B | $2.25B | +23% |
| Revenue vs consensus | $2.77B | $2.62B | Beat by $150M |
| Net income | $821.5M | $698.4M | +17.6% |
| Recurring revenue share | 86% | — | Structural |
| da Vinci systems placed | 431 | 367 | +17% |
| — of which da Vinci 5 | 232 | — | >50% of placements |
| Worldwide procedure growth | +17% YoY | — | Above 14-15% trend |

Three dynamics drove the beat:

1. da Vinci 5 adoption inflection. 232 of the 431 systems placed were the next-gen da Vinci 5 — well over half, versus analyst expectations of ~40%. The da Vinci 5 is a higher-ASP, higher-compute, software-rich platform that pulls through higher recurring instrument and service revenue. Adoption faster than expected is the cleanest structural positive in the print.

2. Procedure volume acceleration. 17% worldwide procedure growth is a meaningful uptick from the 14-15% trend of the prior four quarters. This is broad-based — US, Japan, and Europe all contributed. Bariatric and [[GLP-1|obesity]] surgery trends continue to be a tailwind rather than a headwind, despite earlier concerns that GLP-1s would reduce surgical volume.

3. Recurring revenue at 86%. Service, instruments, and accessories are now 86% of total sales — the highest share in company history and the fundamental reason ISRG trades at a persistent premium to medtech peers. Each new system placement unlocks a multi-year recurring stream; the installed-base compounding story continues.

Guidance raise. ISRG raised FY2026 revenue and procedure-growth guidance, acknowledging that the da Vinci 5 mix is tracking ahead of the original plan. Guidance wasn't the largest source of upside on the day — the Q1 beat itself was — but it confirms the better trajectory is durable.

Read-across. ISRG's procedure growth acceleration is the medtech counterpart to [[Boston Scientific]]'s same-day guide cut on EP/WATCHMAN. The two prints together describe the shape of 2026 medtech: procedure volume is strong overall, but competitive share shifts are brutal within subsegments (PFA competition from [[Medtronic]]/[[Johnson & Johnson]]/[[Abbott]] vs da Vinci's structural leadership in soft-tissue robotics). Robotic surgery has no credible challenger at the high end — [[Medtronic Hugo]] and [[Johnson & Johnson|Ottava]] remain years behind on ecosystem depth.

AI-in-surgery positioning. The da Vinci 5 platform is Intuitive's primary vehicle for AI-assisted surgery features (force feedback, case-learning, workflow automation). The Q1 adoption rate de-risks the multi-year AI monetization story — the hardware installed base is where the software upside compounds from.
