# MOFCOM

China's Ministry of Commerce — the central government body responsible for foreign trade, foreign investment review, technology import/export licensing, and trade negotiations. Administers the Technology Import and Export Administration Regulations (TIEAR) and the Catalogue of Technologies Prohibited or Restricted from Export, the legal hooks Beijing has increasingly used to assert extraterritorial reach over Chinese-founded technology that has been corporately offshored.

| Function | Scope |
|----------|-------|

| Foreign investment review | Inbound FDI screening |
| Technology export control | TIEAR, restricted-tech catalogue, license issuance |
| Outbound M&A reporting | Reviews mainland-China-developed technology in cross-border deals |
| Anti-monopoly review | Concurrent jurisdiction with [[NDRC]] in some cases |

## Recent significance

### Jan 8, 2026 — Meta-Manus investigation opened

MOFCOM spokesperson He Yadong announced a formal investigation into [[Meta]]'s $2B acquisition of [[Manus AI]] under tech-export-control law, focused on whether the underlying [[AI agents|AI agent]] technology was substantially developed in mainland China and whether the July 2025 offshore [[Singapore washing|restructuring]] to [[Singapore]] required an export license. The investigation set the framework that [[NDRC]] used to order the deal unwound on April 27, 2026. See [[Manus AI]] and [[Singapore washing]].

### Pattern

MOFCOM and [[NDRC]] now coordinate as paired regulators on cross-border technology transactions: MOFCOM opens the export-control review, NDRC takes the foreign-investment-prohibition action. The Manus case is the cleanest live precedent for this pairing.
