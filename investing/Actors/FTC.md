**FTC** (Federal Trade Commission) — Independent US regulatory agency with twin mandates over consumer protection and competition. Created 1914 (Federal Trade Commission Act). Five commissioners, no more than three from one party, confirmed by the Senate. The agency is the primary US enforcer of advertising-disclosure rules, deceptive-practice statutes, antitrust against non-bank companies, and an expanding list of digital-economy issues including dark patterns, data brokers, and AI-generated content.

## Why it matters in this vault

The FTC is the most frequently invoked US regulator across vault notes that touch:

- Advertising and endorsement disclosure (the Endorsement Guides, last updated 2023). Relevant to [[Doublespeed]], [[Synthetic creators]], [[Influencer-to-brand playbook]], and large-influencer compliance.
- Antitrust enforcement against tech and consumer companies. The FTC sued [[Meta]] (Instagram/WhatsApp divestiture), brought non-compete bans, and investigated [[Amazon]].
- Data-broker and privacy enforcement. Recent activity around location data brokers, surveillance vendors, and X / [[TikTok]] data flows.
- AI-specific guidance. The FTC has issued multiple statements warning that "AI-generated" or "synthetic" content is held to the same deceptive-practice standards as any other content.
