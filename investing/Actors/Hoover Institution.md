# Hoover Institution

Stanford University-based public policy research center, founded 1919 by Herbert Hoover. Generally right-of-center orientation; produces work on national security, economic policy, history, and US-China strategic competition. Relevant to this vault as a venue for visiting fellows writing on globalisation and geopolitics, including [[Patrick Foulis]] (FT contributing editor, working on a globalisation book).

## Why Hoover matters here

The institution is one of a small number of US think tanks where economists, national-security scholars, and journalists writing on the same fragmentation/globalisation theme cross paths. Its visiting-fellow program produces book-length output that lands in market-moving form (FT/Bloomberg essays before book publication). For our purposes Hoover is a discoverability node — when an FT-or-similar contributor adopts a Hoover affiliation, the resulting work is more likely to be conceptually load-bearing for the markets/macro literature than the typical opinion column.
