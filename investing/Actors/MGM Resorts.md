**MGM Resorts International** — largest casino operator on the [[Las Vegas]] Strip by property count, running asset-light after selling real estate to [[VICI Properties]]. Market cap ~$9.4B. ~56% owner of [[MGM China]] (2282.HK). 50/50 partner in [[BetMGM]] JV with [[Entain]]. Building Japan's first integrated resort in [[Osaka]].

---

## Synopsis

MGM is the textbook asset-light casino transformation. Between 2016 and 2022, the company systematically stripped its real estate into REITs — first [[MGM Growth Properties]] (spun off 2016), then sold to [[VICI Properties]] for $17.2B (2022) — while retaining operating rights under a triple-net master lease at ~$860M/yr with 2% annual escalators. The result: MGM operates 15 casino resorts it doesn't own, freeing capital for buybacks and growth. Since 2021, the company has retired ~48% of its shares outstanding, from ~490M to ~256M.

The growth story has three legs. [[MGM China]] (56%-owned, HKEx-listed) delivered $4.5B revenue in FY2025 (+11% YoY) as Macau's post-COVID recovery matures — MGM Cotai was the standout with Q4 property revenue up 34% to $786M. [[BetMGM]], after years of losses, hit EBITDA-positive in Q3 2025 and delivered $220M EBITDA for FY2025 (from -$244M the prior year), guiding $300-350M for FY2026 and targeting $500M by FY2027. It made its first cash distributions to parents: $270M in 2025. And the Osaka IR — Japan's first casino, a JV with [[ORIX]] — broke ground April 2025 targeting a fall 2030 opening at JPY 1.6T (~$10B) total investment.

The risk is structural: permanent lease obligations to VICI that escalate annually, $6.2B in long-term debt, and BetMGM still a distant third behind [[FanDuel]] (~43% share) and [[DraftKings]] (~34%). Las Vegas Strip revenue actually declined 4% in FY2025 to $8.4B as the post-COVID normalization phase set in. The stock has never recovered its 2007 pre-crisis high of $94.23 — at $36.68, it trades at less than half that peak.

## Evolution

The story of MGM Resorts is the story of acquisition-fueled empire-building followed by a radical asset-light pivot — a company that spent two decades buying every casino on the Strip, nearly died in the financial crisis, then spent the next decade selling the real estate underneath its own properties.

- **1986-1993**: [[Kirk Kerkorian]] — the Armenian-American billionaire who already owned Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer — founded Grand Name Co. in 1986 as a subsidiary of his Tracinda Corporation. Renamed MGM Grand Inc. in 1987, it went public December 13, 1989, and opened the MGM Grand Hotel and Casino in 1993, billed as the world's largest hotel. Kerkorian's strategy was simple: use the MGM brand's glamour to build the biggest, most recognizable properties on the Strip.

- **2000**: Merged with [[Steve Wynn]]'s Mirage Resorts for $4.4B plus $2B assumed debt, rebranding as MGM Mirage. This single deal added the [[Bellagio]], Treasure Island, and The Mirage to the portfolio. Wynn, who had pioneered the modern luxury casino-resort with The Mirage (1989) and Bellagio (1998), took his billions and went to build [[Wynn Resorts]].

- **2004-2005**: Acquired Mandalay Resort Group for $7.9B (closed April 2005), adding [[Mandalay Bay]], [[Luxor]], and [[Excalibur]]. In the same period, formed a JV with [[Pansy Ho]] for a Macau sub-concession, entering Asia's fastest-growing gaming market.

- **2007-2009**: CityCenter, a $8.5B JV with [[Dubai World]] (the most expensive privately funded US construction project), nearly killed the company. The financial crisis froze credit markets mid-construction. Dubai World sued MGM for mismanagement and declined a $220M payment; MGM covered it. CityCenter opened in 2009 amid write-downs exceeding $1B. The experience proved that megaproject risk on the Strip was existential.

- **2010-2016**: Renamed MGM Resorts International (2010). Took MGM China public on the HKEx (2011), retaining 51% (later increased to 56%). The pivotal strategic move came in 2016: creation of [[MGM Growth Properties]] as a publicly traded REIT, transferring real estate while retaining operations. This began the asset-light transformation.

- **2020**: COVID hit MGM harder than almost any US company. Properties shuttered across the portfolio. The stock crashed to $5.90 (March 18, 2020) — a 95% decline from its 2007 peak. CEO Jim Murren departed; Bill Hornbuckle took over as acting CEO in March 2020.

- **2021-2022**: The asset-light pivot accelerated. MGM bought Dubai World's CityCenter stake for $2.1B, sold Aria + Vdara real estate to [[Blackstone]] for $3.89B (leased back at $215M/yr), sold The Mirage to [[Hard Rock International]] for $1.075B, acquired the Cosmopolitan operations from Blackstone for $1.625B, and bought [[LeoVegas]] for $604M for European iGaming reach. VICI completed its $17.2B acquisition of MGM Growth Properties (April 2022), becoming the Strip's largest landowner.

- **2023**: The September cyberattack by [[ALPHV]]/BlackCat ransomware (via [[Scattered Spider]]) shut down operations for ~10 days, costing ~$100M. Attackers used social engineering — impersonating an employee via LinkedIn details to trick IT helpdesk into resetting MFA. MGM refused to pay the ransom (unlike [[Caesars Entertainment]], which paid ~$15M in a parallel attack). Also: 10-year Macau concession renewed, and MGM withdrew its $2.3B New York casino bid at Empire City Yonkers.

- **2025-2026**: Osaka IR broke ground (April 2025) targeting fall 2030 opening. BetMGM turned EBITDA-positive and made first distributions to parents ($270M). Revenue hit $17.5B but Las Vegas Strip declined 4%, signaling normalization. The aggressive buyback machine continues — 37.5M shares repurchased for ~$1.2B in 2025, with a new $2B authorization.

## VICI Properties relationship

MGM created [[MGM Growth Properties]] as a REIT in 2016, transferring real estate while retaining operating rights. VICI acquired MGP for $17.2B (closed April 2022), becoming the Strip's largest landowner with 660+ acres.

Master lease terms: 25-year initial term, three 10-year renewals. Initial annual rent ~$860M. Escalator: 2.0%/yr for first 10 years, then greater of 2.0% or CPI (capped at 3.0%). Covers 15 casino resorts in 8 states. The implication: MGM has capital flexibility but permanent, escalating lease obligations.

## BetMGM

50/50 JV with [[Entain]] (which provides the technology platform). FY2025: net revenue $2.8B (+33% YoY), EBITDA $220M (vs. -$244M loss prior year — a $464M swing). First cash distributions to parents: $270M in Q4 2025. FY2026 guidance: $3.1-3.2B revenue, $300-350M EBITDA. Targeting $500M EBITDA by FY2027.

Market position: distant third behind [[FanDuel]] (~43% share) and [[DraftKings]] (~34%). BetMGM's share is roughly 30-35% of either leader's. The path to profitability came from rationalizing marketing spend, not gaining share.
