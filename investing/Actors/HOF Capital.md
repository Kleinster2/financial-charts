**HOF Capital** is a New York-based, multi-stage [[Venture capital]] firm founded in 2016 by [[Hisham Elhaddad]], [[Onsi Sawiris]], and [[Fady Yacoub]]. The firm's current self-description is "idea to IPO" capital for ambitious technology founders, with an LP network marketed as a commercialization channel rather than just a source of fund capital.

The vault read is that HOF is a private-capital network firm: its strategic LP base is part of the investment product, giving portfolio companies a route to enterprise customers, distribution, hiring, advice, and follow-on financing. That makes HOF relevant to [[AI infrastructure financing]] and frontier-tech private markets even without a public security or liquid price series.

## Synopsis

HOF's edge is not only check-writing capacity. The firm presents itself as a bridge between frontier-technology startups and a global base of strategic investors, family capital, financial institutions, operating companies, and high-profile individuals. Its official partner page lists investors across 37 countries, 240 industry-leading investors in its funds, and brands employing 1.2M people. That matters for the portfolio because HOF is selling founders access to customers, distribution, hiring, strategic advice, and follow-on capital through the LP base.

The firm has scaled from a 2016 emerging manager into a large frontier-tech platform. Cooley's 2022 fund-formation note said HOF had more than $1B of AUM after a $300M early-stage fund; HOF's current founder bios describe the platform as over $10B AUM. The important read is the direction of travel: HOF moved from early-stage access plus global LP relationships into a multi-stage AI/frontier-tech allocation machine with visible exposure to [[Anthropic]], [[SpaceX]], [[Commonwealth Fusion Systems]], [[Crusoe Energy]], [[xAI]], [[SoFi]], [[Klarna]], [[UiPath]], [[OpenAI]], and [[Neuralink]].

The founder story is central. TechCrunch's 2022 fund article described [[Hisham Elhaddad]], [[Onsi Sawiris]], and [[Fady Yacoub]] as Egyptian entrepreneurs who had emigrated to the US before founding HOF in 2016. Their pitch was partly a response to a venture market they saw as too closed to outsiders. HOF's answer was to make the founders' global networks and STEM/operator backgrounds into the product: Elhaddad as capital-formation and frontier-tech lead, Sawiris as the Orascom/Sawiris-linked portfolio-acceleration and enterprise-software connector, and Yacoub as the software/AI investor with prior exposure to technology investing and operating-company work.

For the vault, HOF belongs in the same mental bucket as private-capital firms whose LP network is itself part of the investment product. The commercial promise is that a founder backed by HOF gets capital plus buyer introductions and global market access. The risk is that the brand can become a secondary-access wrapper around already-crowded trophy private names unless HOF continues to originate early and convert its LP network into genuine company-level revenue.

## Evolution

2016: HOF launched as a New York venture firm built around global founder access and a strategic-investor network.

2021: HOF Capital / HOF Capital Management, LLC became an [[SEC]]-registered investment adviser, giving the firm a public regulatory identity via IAPD.

2022: The firm raised a $300M early-stage fund and publicly framed its strategy as thesis-driven investing in category-defining technology companies from pre-seed through Series A. At that point, HOF was already described as having more than $1B AUM and 240 LPs across 37 countries.

2026: HOF's own team pages now describe the platform as over $10B AUM, and the visible portfolio references skew heavily toward frontier AI, space, fusion, compute infrastructure, fintech, and other trophy private-market assets.
