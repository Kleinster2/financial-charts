# Center on Budget and Policy Priorities

US progressive think tank focused on federal and state fiscal policy, with emphasis on how budget and tax decisions affect low- and moderate-income households. Founded in 1981 and headquartered in Washington, D.C. Publishes detailed analysis of the federal budget, tax policy, Medicaid and SNAP financing, Earned Income Tax Credit, and state-level fiscal stress.

Core audiences include congressional staff, state policymakers, and media. Influential in progressive fiscal-policy debates on deficits, distributional incidence of tax changes, and the design of safety-net programs. Produces regular estimates of the distributional impact of tax bills that feed into the broader Washington analytical ecosystem alongside the Tax Policy Center and Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget.

## Focus areas

- Federal budget and deficit analysis
- Federal tax policy (distributional impact, reform proposals)
- Medicaid, SNAP, and other means-tested program financing
- State fiscal policy and state budget stress
- Earned Income Tax Credit and Child Tax Credit design
- Low-income housing finance
