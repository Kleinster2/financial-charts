# UiPath

**UiPath** ([[NYSE]]: PATH) — robotic process automation (RPA) and enterprise automation platform. Pivoting to "agentic automation" — combining deterministic RPA, [[Agentic AI]], and enterprise orchestration. $1.61B revenue (FY2026). Founded 2005 in Romania by [[Daniel Dines]], HQ in New York.

UiPath is the public-market test of whether legacy RPA gets displaced by AI agents or becomes the control/orchestration layer that makes AI agents auditable inside enterprises. The bull case is FY2026 profitability, cash, buybacks, and deterministic workflow governance; the bear case is that native AI-agent stacks absorb automation without a separate RPA platform. The forward signal is whether ARR growth reaccelerates while GAAP profitability holds.

## Core thesis

The existential question: does [[Agentic AI]] kill RPA or does UiPath become the orchestration layer that governs AI agents in enterprise workflows? The Q4 results suggest the pivot is working financially (profitability turnaround, ARR growth), but the stock's -30% YTD decline says the market hasn't bought the narrative. The $500M buyback signals management thinks the stock is cheap. The bear case: AI agents will automate workflows natively without needing a separate orchestration platform. The bull case: enterprises need governance, auditability, and deterministic guardrails around AI agents — exactly what UiPath's existing platform provides.
