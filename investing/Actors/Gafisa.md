**Gafisa** is a Brazilian real estate developer listed on [[B3]] as GFSA3, now controlled by controversial investor [[Nelson Tanure]]. Once one of Brazil's largest homebuilders, Gafisa has been reduced to a governance cautionary tale — under [[CVM]] insider trading investigation with charges filed in December 2025.

## Synopsis

Gafisa was founded in 1954 and at its peak in the mid-2000s was among Brazil's top residential developers, listed both on [[B3]] and (briefly) [[NYSE]]. The company rode the Lula-era housing boom and benefited from the [[Minha Casa Minha Vida]] program that subsidized low- and middle-income housing. But the post-2014 Brazilian recession and the Gafisa-specific missteps — overexpansion, project delays, and write-downs — crushed the stock from peak levels. The [[NYSE]] ADR was delisted as market cap collapsed.

The current chapter is dominated by [[Nelson Tanure]], who accumulated a controlling stake through a series of opaque transactions that minority shareholders and regulators have challenged. Tanure is a serial Brazilian corporate raider who has taken control of distressed companies across sectors (media, telecom, real estate). Under his control, Gafisa pivoted toward higher-end developments and corporate restructuring, but the [[CVM]] filed insider trading charges in December 2025, alleging that Tanure-linked trades exploited material nonpublic information. For investors, Gafisa is less a real estate play than a governance risk trade — the underlying development business has value, but minority shareholder protections under Tanure's control are the binding constraint. The stock trades at a deep discount to NAV, which is either a value trap or a mispriced option on regulatory resolution.
