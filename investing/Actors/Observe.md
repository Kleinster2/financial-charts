**Observe** was an AI-powered IT observability platform acquired by [[Snowflake]] for $1B in February 2026 — Snowflake's largest acquisition ever and a signal of how the data platform war is expanding into operational intelligence.

## Synopsis

Observe was founded in 2017 by Jeremy Burton (former president of [[Dell]] EMC's marketing and cloud division) with a thesis that observability — monitoring application performance, infrastructure health, and security events — should be built on a data lake rather than on purpose-built siloed tools. Traditional observability vendors like [[Datadog]], [[Splunk]] (now [[Cisco]]), and [[Dynatrace]] ingest logs, metrics, and traces into proprietary stores. Observe's architecture instead used a cloud data warehouse as the backend, allowing customers to query operational data alongside business data using SQL. The approach resonated with organizations already invested in cloud data platforms, though Observe remained small relative to the ~$50B+ observability TAM.

The $1B [[Snowflake]] acquisition makes strategic sense on both sides. For Snowflake, it fills a gap in operationalized data use cases — customers already store massive amounts of log and event data in Snowflake but lacked native tools to monitor and alert on it. The deal positions Snowflake against [[Databricks]] (which has its own observability integrations) and the standalone vendors in a market growing 15-20% annually. For Observe, the acquisition provided exit at a premium to its last private valuation, validating the data-lake-native observability thesis even if the standalone business had not achieved escape velocity. The integration risk is whether Snowflake can retain Observe's engineering team and ship a product that competes with [[Datadog]]'s developer experience — historically the moat in observability.
