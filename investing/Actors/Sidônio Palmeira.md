**Sidônio Palmeira** is [[Brazil]]'s Secretário de Comunicação Social (Secom) under President [[Lula]] (Lula III government). A Bahia-based political marketing strategist who ran Lula's successful 2022 campaign, Palmeira replaced Paulo Pimenta as Secom chief in early 2025 and has rapidly expanded his portfolio beyond communications into de facto economic policy influence. In 2026, with Lula's approval ratings under pressure ahead of the election cycle, Palmeira intervened on smartphone and BK import tariffs (arguing against price increases that would hurt consumers), and emerged as a voice on [[Petrobras]] fuel pricing decisions — pushing to keep gasoline and diesel prices low despite the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Hormuz-driven]] surge in crude costs. His growing influence signals that electoral marketing calculus is overriding technocratic economic policymaking in Brasilia, with [[Fernando Haddad]]'s Finance Ministry losing ground to Secom on decisions with direct consumer-price impact.
