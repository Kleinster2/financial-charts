**The Weather Company** — Weather data and technology platform operating weather.com and providing B2B weather data, forecasting APIs, and advertising solutions. Acquired by [[IBM]] in 2016, then sold to Francisco Partners in 2023 for approximately $1B.

---

## Synopsis

The Weather Company is an unusual asset: a consumer media brand (weather.com is one of the highest-traffic websites in the US) grafted onto a B2B data infrastructure business. [[IBM]] acquired the B2B division in 2016 as part of its Watson/AI pivot, betting that weather data would demonstrate the value of AI-powered analytics to enterprise customers. The consumer properties (The Weather Channel TV network had already been separated) generated advertising revenue while the B2B APIs served airlines, insurers, agriculture, and logistics companies that need hyper-local weather forecasting.

The 2023 sale to Francisco Partners for ~$1B was a concession that the IBM Watson strategy had not panned out. IBM paid significantly more than $1B for the original acquisition and spent years integrating weather data into Watson products that never gained enterprise traction at scale. For Francisco Partners, the play is classic PE: strip away the IBM overhead, optimize the advertising business, and potentially sell the B2B data operation separately at a higher multiple. The Weather Company is relevant to the vault as a case study in data-as-a-service business models and as a reference for the evolving weather data market, where competitors include [[Google]]'s DeepMind weather AI and startups using satellite imagery. David Kenny, who led the company under IBM, went on to become Executive Chairman of [[Nielsen]].
