# Sebastian Gunningham

Sebastian Gunningham has been CEO and a board member of [[Remitly]] (NASDAQ: RELY) since February 19, 2026, succeeding co-founder [[Matt Oppenheimer]] in the role. His career arc is a three-decade run through enterprise software, marketplace scaling, and financial services transformation: VP of enterprise at [[Apple]], more than a decade as SVP of North American industrial sector and Latin America at [[Oracle]], CEO of Peace Software, more than a decade at [[Amazon]] as SVP of Marketplace and a member of the executive S-team overseeing ~30,000 staff across Marketplace technology, Search, Payments, Fulfillment by Amazon, and the website platform. He was vice chairman and co-CEO of [[WeWork]] from 2018 to April 2020, chairman of [[Santander]] Consumer Finance and vice chairman of Openbank 2021-2025 (also senior advisor to the Santander group chairman on technology), and CEO of Material Bank 2023-2025.

## Why Gunningham at Remitly

The Remitly board's selection of Gunningham — versus an internal fintech promote — signals a deliberate shift from founder-led growth-at-all-costs to scaled-operator playbooks. Three relevant strands:

1. Amazon S-team experience covered payments, marketplace network effects, and FBA-style logistics — directly transferable to Remitly's payouts network (~170 corridors, multiple delivery modes) and to scaling Remitly Business.
2. Santander Consumer Finance and Openbank tenures put him inside a regulated multi-jurisdiction financial group through a digital and AI transformation — exactly the operational profile Remitly needs as stablecoin rail integration intersects with [[GENIUS Act]] implementation and EU/Asia regulatory frameworks.
3. Material Bank CEO role (2023-2025) was a more recent operator-of-record stint after the WeWork turbulence, restoring his bench profile before the Remitly mandate.

## Public framing on stablecoins (Q1 2026 earnings, Bloomberg TV)

Gunningham's articulated stablecoin posture at Remitly:

- The transaction engine decides per send whether stablecoin rails beat alternatives on price and speed
- Remitly adopts stablecoins selectively, not as a primary product surface
- Direct customer demand for stablecoin balances is still low — receivers want dollar-denominated accounts, not crypto UX
- Regulatory picture remains too murky to push aggressively into customer-facing stablecoin products

This is a more measured posture than competitors like [[MoneyGram]] (Stellar partnership) or pure stablecoin-native challengers ([[Bitso]] Business, [[BVNK]]). The framing assumes stablecoin disruption is a routing improvement rather than a product replacement — durable thesis if Remitly's user base stays sender-focused, vulnerable if receivers start preferring stablecoin-denominated wallets.
