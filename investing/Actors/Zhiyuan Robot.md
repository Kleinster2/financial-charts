**Zhiyuan Robot** (also known as Agibot) is a [[China|Chinese]] humanoid robotics startup and unicorn, part of the wave of Chinese companies racing to commercialize general-purpose humanoid robots for factory and service applications.

## Synopsis

Zhiyuan Robot was founded in 2023 by a team with roots in [[China]]'s AI and robotics research ecosystem, and it rapidly achieved unicorn status — one of China's 247 unicorns as of March 2026 per Tracxn. The company's approach combines large-scale AI models (for robot learning and control) with proprietary hardware design, targeting humanoid robots that can operate in unstructured factory environments alongside human workers. This "embodied AI" approach — where the robot learns tasks through simulation and real-world data rather than explicit programming — mirrors the strategy of [[Tesla]] Optimus, [[Figure AI]], and [[Unitree]], but Zhiyuan benefits from China's lower manufacturing costs and deeper pool of robotics engineering talent.

The competitive landscape in Chinese humanoid robotics is intense. [[Unitree]] has gained attention with its more affordable quadruped and humanoid platforms, [[UBTech Robotics]] (listed in Hong Kong) has government and education contracts, and dozens of smaller startups are chasing similar targets. Chinese government policy actively supports the sector — the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology's 2023 humanoid robot development plan set a 2025 target for mass production readiness and a 2027 target for global competitiveness. For investors, Zhiyuan represents the pure-play Chinese humanoid bet: if China achieves cost-competitive humanoid manufacturing before Western companies (plausible given China's robotics supply chain advantages), companies like Zhiyuan are positioned to capture the industrial adoption wave. The risk is that the sector remains pre-commercial, burning cash on R&D with no clear timeline to unit economics.
