# Artemis Analytics

Artemis Analytics is an institutional crypto and stablecoin data platform that has positioned itself as "the Bloomberg Terminal for digital assets" — providing onchain metrics, DeFi revenue rankings, stablecoin payment-volume analytics, developer activity tracking, and cross-chain flow data to institutional investors. Founded by CEO Jon Ma (Harvard Business School alumnus) and CTO Anthony Yim, with a team drawn from Ribbit Capital, Venmo, and BlackRock. The platform's institutional clients include [[McKinsey]], T. Rowe Price, VanEck, [[Visa]], [[Circle]], and Sequoia. Artemis's stablecoin payment-volume methodology underpins [[McKinsey]] / [[Matt Higginson]]'s February 2026 paper "Stablecoins in payments: What the raw transaction numbers miss," which established the field-standard distinction between crypto-internal stablecoin transfers and real payment flows.

Note: distinct from [[Artemis]] (NASA's lunar exploration program). The names share no relationship and the entities are unrelated.

## What Artemis measures

Three measurement layers most relevant to the institutional stablecoin debate:

| Layer | What it captures |
|-------|------------------|
| Stablecoin circulation | Total tokens issued, by issuer, by chain, time series |
| Onchain transfer volume | Raw transaction count and value across all stablecoin movements |
| Real payment volume | Filtered subset — transfers matching payment patterns (B2B, remit, payroll), excluding exchange settlement, DeFi rotation, market-making |

The third layer is the analytical innovation. Most crypto data platforms publish only the raw onchain figure, which produces the "trillions in stablecoin transactions" headline that has dominated coverage. Artemis's filtered measurement produces the $390B/year and $1-2B/day figures that [[Matt Higginson]] used to recalibrate the institutional understanding of stablecoin payments scale.

## Geographic origination data (cited by McKinsey)

The February 2026 McKinsey/Artemis collaboration revealed that approximately 60% of stablecoin payments originate from Asia — a finding that surprised the research community given the dominant North America/Europe framing in industry coverage. This is the load-bearing data point behind the argument that policy decisions in [[MAS]] / [[HKMA]] / [[JFSA]] are now structurally consequential for the global stablecoin market.

## Position in the data-vendor ecosystem

Artemis's pitch is essentially that hedge fund analysts and corporate strategists were previously hand-patching together onchain metrics from 10+ different sites — Glassnode for some, Dune for others, Token Terminal for revenue, raw block explorers for the rest. The team's founding insight was that institutional buyers wanted one source of truth tailored for stablecoins and DeFi fundamentals, in the same way Bloomberg Terminal aggregated equity, fixed income, and FX data for traditional finance.

The Goldsky case study and the platform's own published research suggest Artemis is now the data spine for both consulting research ([[McKinsey]]) and institutional asset allocation (T. Rowe Price, VanEck, Sequoia portfolio company diligence). The Circle and Visa partnerships are notable — Artemis is the source of truth even for some of the issuers and rails being measured.

## Investment relevance

Artemis is private and not yet listed, but the company sits at the intersection of three durable institutional flows: institutional crypto allocation (still single-digit penetration at most large allocators), stablecoin integration by traditional finance (Visa, Circle, Stripe/Bridge), and consulting/research demand for measurement standards as the [[GENIUS Act]] and [[MiCA]] frameworks force compliance reporting on real metrics rather than promotional figures.
