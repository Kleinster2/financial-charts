# Lovehoney Group

#actor #private #sextech #conglomerate

**Industry:** [[Sextech]]

World's largest sexual wellness company by revenue — formed 2021 from merger of UK-based Lovehoney and Germany-based WOW Tech Group. Portfolio includes We-Vibe, Womanizer, and Lovehoney retail brands.

## Corporate history

### Lovehoney (UK)
- Founded 2002 in Bath, UK by Neal Slateford and Richard Longhurst
- Online adult retailer → expanded to own-brand products
- Licensed deals: Fifty Shades of Grey, Sqweel

### WOW Tech Group (Germany)
- Formed 2018 from merger of Womanizer Group + Standard Innovation (We-Vibe)
- Johannes Plettenberg acquired Womanizer from founder [[Michael Lenke]] in 2017
- Standard Innovation founded by Bruce and Melody Murison in Ottawa, Canada (creators of original We-Vibe)

### Merger (2021)
CDH Investments (Chinese PE) facilitated the merger, taking majority stake in combined entity. Valued at $1B+.

## We-Vibe

**The original couples vibrator.** Founded 2008 by Bruce and Melody Murison (Ottawa, Canada). Bruce's engineering background + personal need = We-Vibe.

**Privacy scandal (2016):** Settled class action for $3.75M after app was found to collect usage data without consent. Led to industry-wide privacy improvements.

Key products: We-Vibe Sync, Chorus, Moxie (wearable)

## Womanizer

**Invented clitoral suction technology.** Created 2014 by [[Michael Lenke]] in Bavaria after reading study that ~50% of women have difficulty orgasming. Wife Brigitte helped test prototypes.

Johannes Plettenberg acquired brand from Lenke, built it into global player. Now part of Lovehoney Group.

Key products: Womanizer Premium, Liberty, Duo
