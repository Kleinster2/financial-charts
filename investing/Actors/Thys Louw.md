# Thys Louw

Portfolio manager on the emerging market debt team at [[Ninety One]], the EM-specialist asset manager demerged from [[Investec]] in 2020. Cited in the vault for his March 4, 2026 [[FT|Financial Times]] commentary on the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Iran war]] sell-off, where he framed the cross-asset rout as mechanical position squaring rather than fundamental repricing: "The main thing that we have broadly seen in the recent sell-off is position squaring ... That is true whether it is Korean equities or the Egyptian pound." The observation — that [[Crowded trade risk|crowded positioning]], not geopolitics, determined which assets fell hardest — is a useful lens for interpreting war-week price action.
