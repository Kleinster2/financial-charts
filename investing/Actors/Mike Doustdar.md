**Mike Doustdar** — President and CEO of [[Novo Nordisk]] since August 7, 2025; first non-Danish CEO in the company's 102-year history. [[Iran]]ian-born Austrian national, joined Novo as a Vienna office clerk in 1992, rose through Emerging Markets and International Operations.

His mandate from the board: pivot Novo from a Danish-led, science-anchored organization into a US-style consumer-facing company that can compete with [[Eli Lilly]] in obesity. The pivot includes board-level FMCG hires, telehealth partnerships, US list-price cuts of up to 50%, and the $5.2bn [[Akero Therapeutics]] acquisition into MASH. See [[Pharma consumerization]] for the broader pattern.

## CEO mandate

Doustdar replaced [[Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen]], who was ousted in May 2025 after Wegovy demand exceeded forecasts but Lilly's Mounjaro/Zepbound captured the dominant US share. The board's brief was explicit: bring "speed, patient-centricity, [and] competition knowledge" — qualities the company had lost during the Saxenda-modeled forecasting era.

His own framing (FT, April 2026): *"I was appointed as a lifetime person not to change the culture of the company. I was appointed to bring speed, patient-centricity, competition knowledge — that exists in all companies but more when you're in the front lines than when you're in the centre."*

## Significance for the company

Doustdar is the structural break with Danish tradition that the [[Novo Nordisk Foundation]] decided was needed. Three departures from precedent:

1. First non-Danish CEO since 1923
2. Career path entirely in markets, not R&D (the prior CEO was a finance man, but the company's identity is research-led — Doustdar's identity is commercial)
3. Foundation chair Lars Rebien Sørensen took the company's chair seat at the same time, breaking the arm's-length convention typical of Danish foundation-owned firms

The combination signals that Novo's challenge is no longer scientific (semaglutide remains world-class) but commercial — and the board owns that diagnosis directly.
