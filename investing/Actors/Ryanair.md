# Ryanair

**Ryanair** — [[Ireland|Irish]] ultra-low-cost carrier and Europe's largest budget airline by passenger volume. ~200M passengers/year. Ticker RYAAY (ADR) / RYA (LSE). 70-80% fuel hedged on rolling 12-month contracts, limiting near-term exposure to oil price spikes.

## Why fuel exposure dominates

[[Bernstein]] analysts (cited by [[Financial Times|FT]] Apr 27) calculated that fuel and related expenses amounted to about 35% of Ryanair's revenue in 2025. That is one of the highest fuel-cost ratios in any consumer-facing industry and explains why airlines — Ryanair among the most exposed — feel oil shocks first on the income statement, even before route or fleet decisions reshape volume. The 70-80% rolling 12-month hedge buffers the spike but does not eliminate the cost-share problem; coverage rolls off and renewal happens at higher strikes when crude is elevated.

This high cost-share is the demand-side reason airlines are first-movers in the [[Refining slate rigidity]] framework — cuts in jet demand are the most price-elastic adjustment in the [[Crack spread|refined-product complex]], even though those cuts do not by themselves rebalance [[Oil|crude]].
