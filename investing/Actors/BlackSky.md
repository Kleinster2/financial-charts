## Q1 2026 earnings (May 7, 2026) — small-cap Q1 reaction, -25%

BKSY reported Q1 2026 May 7 morning at 8:30 AM EDT. Stock closed -25.4% on May 7 to $30.24 from $40.51 — the largest single-day move in the company's history and the worst small-cap mover in the May 7 vault tape after [[Vital Farms]].

Pre-print Street consensus (per [[GuruFocus]]):
- Revenue: $28.07M expected
- EPS: -$0.40 per share

The size of the reaction (-25%) implies revenue / forward-bookings missed materially against the modest consensus, plus the elevated short interest profile typical of small-cap GEOINT names amplified the move. The IdioVol of 101% (per `quick_movers.py` output) signals BKSY's residual move is highly unstable post-earnings.

The structural setup matters: BKSY's primary customer is the [[NGA]] (National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency) under the [[NGA EOCL contract]] framework. Q1 2026 government-customer cadence has been broadly weak through the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran war]] period — government commercial-satellite orders were displaced toward classified-mission cycles during the Hormuz crisis acute phase. The thread-relevant context: [[ServiceNow]] flagged 75bps of Middle East government-deal-delay at its Apr 22 print. BKSY is the smaller-scale read on the same dynamic — government customers prioritized urgent-mission spend, deferring commercial satellite refresh cycles.

Cross-name reads:
- [[Planet Labs]] (peer) — likely sympathy decline; PL reports Q1 in early June
- [[Spire Global]] (peer) — RF/weather satellite, less optical-imagery exposure
- [[Palantir]] (analytics partner) — different business model, not directly comparable on the day
- [[Iridium Communications|Iridium]] / [[Globalstar]] — adjacent but different segment

The BKSY -25% pairs with [[Stem]] -16.5% as the two clearest small-cap blow-ups in the May 7 tape — both companies dependent on government / utility customer cadence that has slowed in the war-economy environment. It is in tension with the [[Axon]] +12% federal-grant tailwind on the same day; the difference is that AXON has multi-year [[Safer Skies Act]] grant visibility while BKSY operates on annual government-commercial-imagery contract cycles.

### Same-day [[HawkEye 360]] +30% IPO debut (May 7, 2026)

The most diagnostic comparison from the May 7 tape is BKSY -25.4% on the same day [[HawkEye 360]] (NYSE: HAWK) IPO'd at +30% (closing $34.00 from $26 IPO price, $3.15B initial market cap). Both are space-intelligence pure-plays, but they differ on the dimension that mattered today:

| Dimension | BlackSky | HawkEye 360 |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Sensor type | Optical imagery | RF SIGINT |
| Customer contract structure | Annual government-commercial-imagery cycle | Multi-year IDIQ awards |
| FY 2025 financials | Q1 2026 miss ($28M est rev, -$0.40 EPS) | $117.7M rev (+74%), $24.8M EBITDA, +$2.7M net income |
| Backlog trajectory | (unstable) | $302.7M (+587% YoY from $44.1M) |
| FY 2025 profitability | GAAP loss | Positive GAAP net income |
| May 7 close | $30.24 | $34.00 |
| May 7 market cap | ~$400M | ~$3.15B |

The market is now sharply differentiating within the space-intel cohort. Optical-imagery operators ([[BlackSky]], [[Planet Labs]]) face annual-cycle commercial-imagery weakness; RF-SIGINT operators ([[HawkEye 360]] primary, [[Spire Global]] adjacent) benefit from multi-year government-IDIQ tailwinds tied to the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war intelligence cycle]].

For BKSY positioning, the immediate reset is whether it pivots into RF-SIGINT-adjacent capabilities (it has historically focused on optical) or accepts the cyclical-imagery valuation discount as durable. The HAWK debut creates a natural read-across question for the next BKSY earnings cycle.
