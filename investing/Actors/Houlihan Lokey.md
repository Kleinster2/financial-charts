## Big Law transformation linkage

Houlihan Lokey is one of the cleanest [[Big Law transformation]] beneficiaries on the advisory side. The firm's restructuring franchise overlaps directly with the [[Liability management exercises|LME]] practice area where [[Kirkland & Ellis]] dominates legally — drop-down financings, uptiering exchanges, distressed exchange offers, creditor-on-creditor violence — and HLI-style independent restructuring boutiques sit on the financial-adviser seat across the same deal universe. As the legal advisory layer concentrates around Kirkland's LME franchise, the universe of restructuring mandates expands; HLI's #1 US restructuring league-table position translates that volume into fee revenue.

A second mechanism is *conflict-spillover capture*. The 2025 Altice litigation case — where [[Kirkland & Ellis]] hired in September 2025 and was forced to withdraw in November after [[Apollo]] / [[Ares Management]] / [[BlackRock]] objections (documented in the [[Big Law transformation]] note) — illustrates how Kirkland's scale generates conflicts that send mandates to firms without those entanglements. Boutique advisers with no balance-sheet, lending, or trading-book conflicts pick up the resulting fragments. HLI's pure-fee-income posture is structurally well-positioned for this dynamic.

See [[Big Law transformation]] for the Ring 3 trade-construction framing (long Ring 2 + Ring 3 restructuring, underweight Ring 1 incumbents most exposed to AI substitution).
