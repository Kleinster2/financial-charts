# Enplas

Japanese semiconductor test socket manufacturer listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange. Produces precision IC test sockets used in final test and burn-in, plus optical lens components for LED and sensor applications. Enplas is a key beneficiary of rising test socket complexity driven by AI chip thermal and frequency demands -- as chip power envelopes grow (particularly for [[HBM]] and AI accelerators), test sockets must handle higher currents, tighter pitch, and more extreme thermal cycling.

Founded in 1962 in Saitama, Japan. The company holds significant market share in the global test socket market alongside [[WinWay Technology]] (Taiwan), [[LEENO]] and [[ISC Co]] (Korea). Test sockets are consumable -- they degrade with use and must be replaced regularly, creating a recurring revenue dynamic tied to test volumes.
