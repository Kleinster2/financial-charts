# Latham & Watkins

Second-largest US law firm by revenue (2025), behind [[Kirkland & Ellis]]. Global, transactional, and one of the most aggressive lateral recruiters in the [[Big Law transformation]] cycle. Recruited four [[Wachtell Lipton]] partners in 2025 alone — the largest single-firm capture from Wachtell on record.

## Position in Big Law transformation

Latham follows Kirkland's scale-plus-pay model with comparable willingness to recruit aggressively at the top of the market. Where Kirkland is most associated with private equity and restructuring, Latham's franchise spans capital markets, M&A, structured finance, and global cross-border work — broader than Kirkland but with similar economics: discretionary partner compensation, large lateral budget, no formal lockstep.

The 2025 recruit of four [[Wachtell Lipton]] partners is the most visible single move of the current cycle. Per the FT, the four were "younger stars" — i.e., mid-career partners structurally underpaid by lockstep relative to peer market rates. Together with [[Josh Feltman]]'s $80M / 3-year package to [[Kirkland & Ellis]], this brings Wachtell's 2025 partner exodus to eight, an unprecedented number for a firm that historically had near-zero lateral departures.
