# Evergrande

Chinese property developer founded 1996 in Guangzhou by [[Hui Ka Yan]] (Xu Jiayin). Was the largest Chinese developer by total liabilities at the time of default ($300B+ in stated liabilities, possibly higher with off-balance-sheet items). Defaulted on offshore dollar bonds in December 2021 after months of stress visible since mid-2021. Ordered into liquidation by a Hong Kong court on January 29, 2024 — the first major Chinese developer liquidation order. Founder [[Hui Ka Yan]] detained by authorities September 2023 on charges related to financial-misconduct investigations. The Evergrande default is the canonical case of the [[Three Red Lines]] policy producing systemic financial-stability stress.

## Why it matters in the vault

Three structural channels (inference):

1. [[Three Red Lines]] first major default — Evergrande was in the worst (red) tier of the policy at launch. Its December 2021 default was the proof that the policy would be enforced even on a firm of Evergrande's scale and political profile, and triggered the broader property-correction cascade
2. Liquidation order precedent — the January 29, 2024 Hong Kong court order is the first major Chinese developer liquidation. Sets the template for how offshore creditor disputes resolve when restructuring fails. Mainland China asset recovery for offshore creditors has been minimal
3. Hui Ka Yan detention — September 2023 detention of the founder is the highest-profile founder-detention in the broader [[Common Prosperity]] enforcement era, alongside the 2018 [[Wu Xiaohui|Anbang]] case. The detention reset analyst expectations about which Chinese billionaire founders remained politically protected
