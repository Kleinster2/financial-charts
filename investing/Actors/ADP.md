## Q3 FY2026 earnings and payroll resilience (Apr 2026)

ADP's Apr 29 move was a low-volatility macro signal: the stock moved from $199.17 to $215.06, **+8.0%**, after Q3 results beat expectations and management raised FY2026 guidance.

| Metric | Q3 FY2026 | YoY / note |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Revenue | $5.9B | +7% reported / +6% organic constant currency |
| Net earnings | $1.4B | +9% |
| Adjusted EBIT | $1.8B | +10% |
| Adjusted EBIT margin | 30.2% | +80 bps |
| Diluted EPS | $3.38 | +10% |
| Adjusted diluted EPS | $3.37 | +10% |

Business-line data kept the employment read-through constructive rather than euphoric. Employer Services revenue rose 7% reported and 5% organic constant currency, U.S. pays per control rose about 1%, and Employer Services margin expanded 130 bps. PEO Services revenue rose 7%, but average worksite employees paid rose only 2% to about 762,000 and PEO margin contracted 120 bps.

Client-funds economics remained a tailwind: interest on funds held for clients rose 14% to $404M, with average client-funds balances up 9% to $48.3B and average yield up 10 bps to 3.3%.

FY2026 guidance moved higher: revenue growth of 6-7%, adjusted EBIT margin expansion of 70-80 bps, and adjusted diluted EPS growth of 10-11%. The read-through for the vault is that payroll/HCM demand is still expanding, but the employment signal is moderate — pays per control around +1%, not a hiring boom.

*Source: ADP Q3 FY2026 earnings release, filed as [[SEC]] 8-K earnings exhibit (Apr 29, 2026). Price move verified in local DB: Apr 28 close $199.17 → Apr 29 close $215.06.*
