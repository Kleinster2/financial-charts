# LIDE

**LIDE** — Grupo de Líderes Empresariais. Brazilian business leaders networking organization founded in 2003 by [João Doria](obsidian://open?vault=Brazil%20News%20%26%20Analysis&file=People%2FJo%C3%A3o%20Doria). Began with 122 affiliated companies; now 1,700+ member companies representing roughly 54% of Brazilian GDP (as of 2017). Global CEO is João Doria Neto (son of the founder); senior Doria is vice-chairman of the Advisory Board. Operates 20 units in Brazil and 13 international bases across five continents (including New York, Washington, England, Germany, Argentina, Australia, China, Paraguay). Hosts the annual Brazil Investment Forum in NYC, which reached its 15th edition during [[Brazil Week NYC 2026]] at the Harvard Club on May 12.

## Why it matters

LIDE functions as the parallel right-of-center business convening network in Brazil, distinct from the older industry federations (FIESP, CNI). Its Doria lineage gives it center-right political positioning, which is why the 15th Brazil Investment Forum at [[Brazil Week NYC 2026]] became the natural stage for right-of-center 2026 candidates — Flávio Bolsonaro and Zema spoke at the Harvard Club May 12, Augusto Cury (Avante) addressed political polarization, and Caiado (PSD) sent remarks remotely; Tarcísio worked a parallel closed-bank track at Citi/BlackRock rather than the LIDE stage. For US institutional investors trying to read Brazilian political risk, the LIDE forum remains the highest-density single-day briefing event available outside Brazil even when the right-wing field fragments across venues.
