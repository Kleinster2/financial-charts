# Alstom

French rail transport manufacturer and the world's second-largest rolling stock producer after [[CRRC]], covering trains, signaling systems, and rail infrastructure services. Listed on [[Euronext Paris]] (ALO).

## Synopsis

Alstom's current form is the product of a transformative and troubled acquisition: the $8.4B purchase of [[Bombardier]]'s rail division in January 2021. The deal doubled Alstom's revenue to ~EUR 16B and gave it Bombardier's extensive North American and German order books, but it also loaded the balance sheet with debt and integration challenges. By 2023-2024, the cash flow deterioration was severe enough to require a EUR 1B rights issue (Sep 2024) -- a humbling moment for a company that had positioned itself as the European champion against Chinese competition.

The strategic logic was sound: European rail is a structural growth market driven by decarbonization mandates, urban transit investment, and the EU's push to shift freight from road to rail. Alstom's product range spans high-speed trains (TGV successor Avelia), regional trains, metros, trams, and the ERTMS digital signaling standard that will eventually unify European rail networks. The problem is execution -- legacy Bombardier contracts with unfavorable terms continue to weigh on margins, and the company competes against [[Siemens]] Mobility (which tried and failed to merge with Alstom in 2019, blocked by EU regulators) and [[CRRC]] (which dominates on price in emerging markets). CEO Henri Poupart-Lafarge needs to demonstrate that the Bombardier integration can deliver the margin expansion that justified the deal.
