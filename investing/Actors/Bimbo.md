**Bimbo** (BMV: BIMBOA) is the world's largest baking company by revenue, a [[Mexico|Mexican]] multinational operating in 35+ countries with over 200 brands, 200+ plants, and a distribution network reaching 3.6 million points of sale daily. Founded by Lorenzo Servitje in 1945, the company remains controlled by the Servitje family through its holding structure.

## Synopsis

Grupo Bimbo built its dominance through a combination of distribution density in Mexico (where its bread, pastries, and snack brands are ubiquitous) and aggressive international acquisitions — Sara Lee's North American bakery business (2011), Canada Bread (2014), Bays English Muffins, and dozens of regional bakers across Latin America, Europe, and Asia. The result is a company generating ~$20B in annual revenue with the largest direct-store-delivery (DSD) network in the baking industry, reaching more retail locations daily than [[Coca-Cola]] or [[PepsiCo]] in many markets. The distribution infrastructure is the real asset — it creates barriers to entry that pure-play bakers cannot replicate.

The investment case is a defensive consumer staples play with emerging market growth optionality. Mexico and Latin America provide mid-single-digit organic growth, while the US/Canada business (roughly 50% of revenue via brands like Bimbo, Thomas', Entenmann's, and Sara Lee) is mature but generates stable cash flow. Margins are compressed by commodity exposure (wheat, sugar, vegetable oils) and the labor-intensive DSD model, keeping operating margins in the 8-10% range — below packaged food peers like [[Nestle]] or [[Mondelez]] that rely on warehouse distribution. The stock is liquid on the BMV but trades at a premium to Mexican consumer staples peers, reflecting the scarcity value of a globally diversified Mexican multinational.
