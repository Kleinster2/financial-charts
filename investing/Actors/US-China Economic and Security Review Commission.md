**US-China Economic and Security Review Commission** (USCC) — bipartisan US congressional commission created in 2000 to monitor and report to Congress on the national-security implications of the US-China economic relationship. Publishes an influential annual report. In its 2024 report it flagged China's recognition of data as a balance-sheet asset and the use of data as loan collateral by indebted [[LGFV|local government financing vehicles]], noting the actual market value of such [[Data as an asset|data assets]] remains "difficult to determine." [[Mike Kuiken]] serves as vice-chair.
