# Lazard

Lazard ([[NYSE]]: LAZ) is the oldest of the global independent advisory firms, founded 1848 in New Orleans by the Lazard brothers as a dry-goods and metals importer before pivoting through gold trading and into investment banking. Today the firm operates two reportable segments: Financial Advisory (M&A, restructuring, sovereign and private capital advisory) and Asset Management (active equity and fixed income strategies, $259B AUM as of Q1 2026). After the [[Campbell Lutyens]] acquisition announced 2026-04-30, a third unit — Lazard CL — will be carved out as a global private capital advisory platform on close.

[[Peter Orszag]] (former [[Congressional Budget Office|CBO]] and [[Office of Management and Budget|OMB]] director under [[Barack Obama]]) became CEO and Chairman in October 2023 succeeding Ken Jacobs. The firm is in the middle of an explicit pivot: rebalancing the product mix from cyclical M&A toward private capital advisory and active asset management at a moment when Asset Management posted its highest quarterly net inflows in nearly two decades.

## Synopsis

- 1848-founded New Orleans dry-goods house pivoted through gold trading into 19th-century investment banking — the oldest of the surviving independent advisers and the structural template that [[Joseph Perella|Perella]], [[Roger Altman]], [[Blair Effron|Effron]], and [[Ken Moelis|Moelis]] later replicated. See [[Boutique advisory consolidation]] for the post-2005 wave.
- Two operating segments through Q1 2026: Financial Advisory ($356M Q1 net revenue, -4% YoY) and Asset Management ($309M Q1 net revenue, +17% YoY adjusted / +42% reported). Q1 2026 firmwide net revenue $757M (+17% YoY).
- April 2026 [[Campbell Lutyens]] acquisition (~$575M consideration + up to $85M earnout) creates a third reportable unit, Lazard CL, on close. Combined platform targets ~$500M 2027 revenue, 280+ advisory professionals, 18 offices.
- M&A advisory league table: Q1 2025 #7 globally by deal value ($87.5B). Co-leader of [[Brazil]] restructuring league with [[Houlihan Lokey]] following the Feb 2026 [[Brazil corporate bond rout February 2026|Brazilian corporate bond rout]] (advising [[Shell]] on [[Raizen]], [[Braskem]] on its restructuring).
- Asset Management is mid-pivot: net inflows $9B in Q1 2026 (highest in ~20 years), driven by active emerging-markets and international equity strategies — a structural rebound from a decade of net outflows that had compressed the segment toward break-even.
- The firm trades like a hybrid: cyclical advisory revenue tied to global M&A volume, plus an asset-management book exposed to global equity beta. Sector correlation favors small-cap financials over diversified banks (similar profile to [[Perella Weinberg Partners]]).

## Q1 2026 earnings — record AM inflows, Campbell Lutyens deal, advisory drag (May 1, 2026)

Lazard reported Q1 2026 before the open on May 1. The print was the cleanest demonstration to date of the [[Peter Orszag]] pivot — advisory backed off as deal closings slipped, while asset management inflected to its best quarter in nearly two decades.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | YoY |
|---|---|---|
| Net revenue | $757M | +17% |
| Adjusted EPS | $0.42 | n/a (vs cons miss on advisory mix) |
| GAAP EPS | $0.91 | (mark on Lazard CL transaction-related items) |
| Financial Advisory net revenue | $356M | -4% (deal-closing slippage to back half) |
| Asset Management net revenue | $309M | +17% adj / +42% reported |
| AUM | $259B | record |
| AM net inflows | +$9B | highest quarterly in ~20 years |
| Adj comp ratio | 69.9% | seasonally high; FY targeted ~65.5% |

The Asset Management inflection is the single most important data point: $9B of net inflows after a decade of net outflows in the segment. Strategy mix was concentrated in active emerging-markets and international equity — exactly the categories where ETFs and US-domiciled large-cap passive vehicles have been bleeding mandates. This is the same category-rotation thesis that supports [[Robin J. Brooks]]' framing of late-cycle USD-funded carry unwinds and the resulting capital reallocation to non-US equities. If sustained, the AM trajectory underwrites a re-rating away from "financial advisory cyclical" toward "diversified financial."

Financial Advisory was -4% on slipped closings — same pattern that hit [[Perella Weinberg Partners]] (-30% revenue) and that confirms the deal-cycle drag is sector-wide rather than firm-specific. Management framing matched PWP's "back-half-weighted" guide: pipeline intact, closings deferred 6 months by [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran-conflict]]-related buyer caution.

LAZ closed May 1 at $45.13, -6.9% from Apr 30 (-3.1σ on 60-day vol; β-adjusted). The selloff weighed the advisory miss and Lazard CL transaction dilution heavier than the AM inflow inflection — investor reaction read as "we'll re-underwrite when 2027 accretion is closer to print." See [[Lazard securities]] for the price detail.

## April 2026 — Campbell Lutyens acquisition

Announced 2026-04-30, closed concurrently with the Q1 print:

| Term | Value |
|---|---|
| Total consideration | ~$575M |
| Structure | Cash + stock at closing + 2-year delayed tranche |
| Earnout | Up to $85M based on multi-year performance criteria |
| New segment | Lazard CL — leading global private capital advisory platform |
| Combined 2027 revenue target | ~$500M |
| Combined headcount | 280+ advisory professionals |
| Offices | 18 globally |
| Co-CEOs | [[Holcombe Green]] (Lazard PCA head) + [[Gordon Bajnai]] (Campbell Lutyens CEO) |
| Reports to | [[Peter Orszag]] (Lazard CEO/Chairman) |
| Expected accretion | 2027 EPS+, before synergies |
| Closing | Calendar 2026, subject to regulatory approval |

Strategic logic: private capital advisory is the highest-multiple corner of independent advisory — fund placement, GP-led secondaries, GP capital advisory — and it is one of two segments (with restructuring) that does not depend on M&A deal cycles. [[Campbell Lutyens]] is the largest pure-play in the category. The deal mirrors [[Perella Weinberg Partners]]' [[Devon Park]] secondaries acquisition (2025) and [[Evercore]]'s [[Robey Warshaw]] UK boutique tuck-in (2025) — three of the top independent advisers consolidating high-margin advisory adjacencies in 18 months.

## Brazil restructuring book (Feb 2026)

Advising [[Shell]] on the [[Raizen]] restructuring; co-advising [[Braskem]] alongside [[Houlihan Lokey]] following the Feb 2026 [[Brazil corporate bond rout February 2026|Brazilian corporate bond rout]]. Lazard's emerging-markets restructuring desk has historically dominated sovereign and quasi-sovereign mandates (Argentina 2020, Greece 2012, Iceland 2009), and the Brazil book extends that franchise into corporate.
