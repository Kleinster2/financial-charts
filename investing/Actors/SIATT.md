#actor #defense #brazil #private

**SIATT** (Sistemas Integrados de Alta Tecnologia) — São José dos Campos-based Brazilian smart-weapons specialist, founded 2015 by ex-Mectron and ex-[[Embraer]] engineers. Prime contractor for the Brazilian Navy's [[MANSUP]] anti-ship missile program and Brazil's only domestic smart-missile integrator at production scale. 50% owned by [[EDGE Group]] (UAE state-owned defense conglomerate) since September 2023.

---

## Synopsis

SIATT is the cleanest single demonstration of how UAE capital is rebuilding Brazil's missile industrial base. When [[Embraer]] divested defense electronics specialist Mectron to Odebrecht Defesa in 2011 and the parent group later collapsed in [[Lava Jato]], a generation of Brazilian missile engineers scattered. SIATT was founded in 2015 to reconsolidate the talent and bid for the Brazilian Navy's [[MANSUP]] anti-ship missile, the country's flagship indigenous-missile program. The Navy awarded development to a consortium led by SIATT, which carried the program to first operational test firings.

[[EDGE Group]]'s 50% acquisition in September 2023 — its first Brazil investment — solved SIATT's two structural constraints: insufficient capital to scale beyond a single Navy customer, and Brazilian export-control rules that complicate sales into Gulf and Asian markets. EDGE provides both. The April 2025 strategic license for the MANSUP extended-range IP and the September 2025 opening of a new 6,000 m² São José dos Campos facility codify the integration. SIATT is now the spear of EDGE's Latin American expansion and the central node connecting UAE defense capital to Brazilian aerospace talent.

## Programs

### MANSUP anti-ship missile

[[MANSUP]] (Míssil Antinavio Nacional de Superfície) is the Brazilian Navy's indigenous anti-ship cruise missile, intended to replace Exocet across the surface fleet. SIATT is the prime integrator. The base variant is in production; the extended-range variant — licensed to [[EDGE Group]] for export markets in April 2025 — is the joint development that opens the largest commercial upside. The MANSUP-ER positions Brazil-UAE jointly against MBDA Exocet, [[Boeing]] Harpoon, [[RTX]] NSM, and Chinese C-802 derivatives in the global anti-ship export market.

### Counter-drone (Brazilian Navy LOI, April 2025)

EDGE and the Brazilian Navy signed a Letter of Intent in April 2025 for joint development of a counter-drone system, with SIATT as the Brazil-side integrator. Counter-UAS is the highest-priority UAE export-capability gap after the 2023-2024 wave of Iranian and Houthi drone strikes on Gulf targets; co-development in Brazil delivers production capacity and a Latin American export route.

### Coastal surveillance and maritime security

Adjacent to the missile work, SIATT develops radar, signals-intelligence, and command-and-control systems for maritime domain awareness — capabilities that integrate naturally with [[MANSUP]] and with EDGE's broader surveillance portfolio.

## Ownership and capital

EDGE's 50% stake was completed September 2023 — the firm's first Brazilian acquisition and the model for the May 2024 [[Condor Non-Lethal Technologies]] deal. The remaining 50% is held by the original founding shareholders. Capital from EDGE underwrote the São José dos Campos facility expansion (6,000 m², opened September 2025) which is expected to generate approximately 200 direct jobs and 600 indirect jobs in the region.

## Geographic positioning

SIATT sits in São José dos Campos — the same São Paulo state city that houses [[Embraer]] HQ, ITA (Brazil's elite aerospace engineering university), the DCTA (Aerospace Science and Technology Department of the Brazilian Air Force), and the broader Brazilian aerospace cluster. The co-location is not incidental: SIATT recruits ITA graduates and ex-Embraer engineers, and the proximity makes co-development with [[Embraer]] defense programs natural even though SIATT is independent of Embraer.
