# Baltic Exchange

The Baltic Exchange is a London-based maritime benchmark and information provider that publishes the freight rate indices underpinning global shipping derivatives, physical charters, and freight-linked ETFs. Founded in 1744 as a coffee house for shipbrokers, it is owned by the Singapore Exchange ([[SGX]]) since 2016. Its indices include the Baltic Dry Index (BDI) and its dry bulk components (BCI Capesize, BPI Panamax, BSI Supramax), the Baltic Dirty Tanker Index (BDTI), and route-specific benchmarks including **TD3C** (270,000mt [[Ras Tanura]] → China VLCC) and **TD20** (130,000mt West Africa → UK/Continent Suezmax) that settle [[BWET]].

Rates are assessed daily by a panel of independent shipbrokers who submit estimated rates based on actual fixtures, consensus market levels, and — during extreme disruption — hypothetical pricing. The methodology under stress became controversial during the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]]: see [[Imaginary freight rate]].

## 2026 methodology controversy

During the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]], the Baltic continued publishing TD3C daily assessments despite the physical route being effectively closed. Panellists referenced Yanbu fixtures with added Hormuz-transit risk premia — assessments Lloyd's List characterized as "imaginary." The Baltic launched **TD34** (Gulf of Oman → China) as a trial alternative route but did not suspend TD3C. [[Frontline]] CEO [[Lars Barstad]] commented that Baltic indices "have much wider market implications than they used to" given their use in FFAs, [[BWET]], and charter contracts.
