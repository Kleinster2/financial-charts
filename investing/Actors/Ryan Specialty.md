## Q1 2026 earnings — guide cut + margin compression (Apr 30, 2026)

Ryan Specialty reported Q1 2026 after the close on Apr 30. The headline numbers beat consensus, but the guide cut and underlying margin compression drove the tape:

| Metric | Q1 2026 | YoY |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Revenue | $795.2M | +15.2% |
| Organic revenue | — | +11.8% |
| Adj EBITDAC | $232M | +15.7% |
| Adj EBITDAC margin | 29.2% | +10 bps |
| Adj EPS | $0.47 | +20% |
| Operating margin | 11.9% | vs 14.5% Q1 2025 |
| Free cash flow | -$181M | vs -$160M Q1 2025 (worsened burn) |

Guidance cut: FY2026 organic revenue outlook reduced to mid-single digits (from prior guide), adj EBITDAC margin to decline 100-150 bps for the full year. Q2 organic growth guided near 0%. Management cited worsening property rate declines, heightened competition, and slower market conditions.

Stock: RYAN closed May 1 at $30.76, -11.5% on the day (-3.7σ) — touched a 52-week low intraday at $29.91. Stock is down 38.9% YTD and trades 57% below the $71.97 52-week high (June 2025). [[Piper Sandler]] cut its target to $35 from prior level on May 1. The print sits in the [[Insurify insurance broker selloff February 2026|February broker selloff]] continuum — soft property rates + commoditizing distribution economics + cyclical premium-cycle peaking dynamics, with the Q1 print formalizing what the tape was already pricing.

The contrast with [[AIG]]'s Q1 2026 print (underwriting income tripled, +5.2% on May 1) is sharp: AIG sits on the carrier side capturing P&C pricing power, while RYAN sits on the broker side facing the rate-decline cycle and customer-side margin pressure. The vault investment case for [[Brown & Brown]] / [[Kinsale Capital]] / [[Arthur J. Gallagher]] needs a downstream check for whether the soft-market read is broker-specific or sector-wide.
