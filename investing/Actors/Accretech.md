# Accretech

Tokyo Seimitsu Co., Ltd. — top-tier Japanese semiconductor wafer prober manufacturer. Produces precision probing systems for advanced logic and memory testing at the wafer level. As AI drives more wafer-level test (known-good-die testing before assembly), probers have evolved from commodity tools to critical infrastructure in the semiconductor supply chain.

## Synopsis

Accretech occupies a specialized niche in [[Semiconductor Test]]: wafer probing, the step where individual dies are electrically tested before being cut and packaged. The company's probers must achieve micron-level positioning accuracy across 300mm wafers running at temperatures from -40C to 200C — an engineering challenge that limits the competitive field to Accretech, [[FormFactor]] (which provides probe cards), and [[Tokyo Electron]] (which has a smaller prober business). The rise of advanced packaging ([[TSMC]]'s CoWoS, chiplets, 3D stacking) has made wafer-level test more important: when multiple dies are bonded into a single package, identifying defective dies before assembly saves enormous cost.

The AI tailwind for Accretech is structural. [[NVIDIA]]'s [[H100]] and [[B200]] GPUs use CoWoS packaging that bonds multiple HBM memory dies to a logic die — every one of those dies must be tested at the wafer level, multiplying the number of probe touchdowns per finished chip. Similarly, [[HBM]] memory from [[SK Hynix]] and [[Samsung]] requires extensive wafer-level testing. This shifts probing from a commodity step to a throughput bottleneck, and Accretech's advanced probers command premium pricing in this environment. The company also has a precision measurement equipment business (surface metrology, roundness testers) that provides diversification but is less relevant to the AI semiconductor cycle.
