**Guilherme Benchimol** is the founder and Executive Chairman of [[XP Inc]], [[Brazil]]'s largest independent investment platform. Built the company from a 25 sqm office in Porto Alegre (2001) into a publicly listed firm that rewired how Brazilians invest.

## Synopsis

Benchimol's achievement was dismantling the oligopoly of Brazil's five largest banks over retail investment distribution. Before [[XP Inc]], Brazilian retail investors were captive to their bank's in-house funds and products -- high fees, limited choices, and blatant conflicts of interest. Starting as a training and education company in 2001 (co-founded with Marcelo Maisonnave in Porto Alegre), XP pivoted to an open-architecture investment platform that gave independent financial advisors (IFAs) the tools to offer clients products from any provider. The IFA network became XP's distribution moat: thousands of advisors built their businesses on XP's platform, creating switching costs that rival those of any fintech globally.

Control is the defining feature of the Benchimol story. Through a dual-class share structure, he holds 70.94% of total votes via XP Control LLC despite a much smaller economic stake -- a governance structure that survived the 2019 Nasdaq IPO and [[Itau Unibanco]]'s 2017 acquisition of a 49.9% stake (later reduced). The dual-class structure gives Benchimol veto power over any strategic decision, which is both XP's strength (long-term vision without activist pressure) and its risk (no accountability mechanism if the founder misjudges). He stepped back from CEO to Executive Chairman, but the control structure ensures he remains the ultimate decision-maker. XP's ~$10B market cap makes him one of [[Brazil]]'s wealthiest self-made founders.
