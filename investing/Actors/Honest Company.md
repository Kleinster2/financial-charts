# Honest Company

Honest Company is a baby, beauty, and household products company founded by [[Jessica Alba]] in 2011. It IPO'd in May 2021 at a $1.4B valuation during the peak of the SPAC/IPO bubble, then saw its stock collapse — market cap fell to ~$200M by 2025 (~$6/share). Revenue was $346M in 2023. The company is the only publicly traded creator-led consumer brand, making it a cautionary tale for the [[Influencer-to-brand playbook]].

Honest built its brand on "clean" ingredients and transparency, initially gaining traction in diapers and baby products before expanding into skincare and beauty. The company faced multiple product-quality controversies (sunscreen effectiveness, ingredient labeling) that undermined its core brand promise. Post-IPO, it struggled with margin compression from commodity cost inflation and an inability to build durable pricing power against private-label competitors. CEO changes (Alba stepped back from day-to-day in favor of professional management) stabilized operations but did not reverse the valuation decline.
