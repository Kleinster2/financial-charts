#actor #finance #venturecapital #usa #private

**Torch Capital** is a New York-based early-stage venture fund founded by Jonathan Keidan, distinguished by its direct cap-table co-investment model that avoids layered SPV structures common in emerging manager VC.

## Synopsis

Torch Capital operates in the pre-seed and seed space with a thesis centered on consumer technology, fintech, and marketplace businesses. What sets the firm apart structurally is its co-investment approach: rather than pooling capital into blind-pool vehicles with management fees and carry, Torch offers its LP network direct cap-table positions in individual deals. This eliminates the SPV layering that has proliferated across emerging manager VC and gives LPs deal-level transparency and selection — a model that resonates with sophisticated family offices and high-net-worth individuals who want control over their exposure.

Keidan, the founder, built his network through media and entertainment connections before transitioning into venture. The firm is small by AUM standards but punches above its weight in NYC deal flow, particularly in consumer-facing startups where Keidan's relationships with founders, media figures, and brand operators generate proprietary access. The model's limitation is scalability — direct cap-table co-investing requires more operational overhead per deal than pooled funds, capping the number of investments Torch can manage simultaneously.
