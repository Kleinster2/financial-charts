**Bill Gurley** is a general partner at [[Benchmark]], one of Silicon Valley's most influential venture capitalists, known for the legendary [[Uber]] investment and for his increasingly vocal role in AI and tech policy debates.

## Synopsis

Gurley's track record at [[Benchmark]] spans over two decades and includes some of the defining venture investments of the internet era: [[Uber]] (2011, ~$12M for ~12% stake, returning $7B+ to the fund), [[Zillow]], [[GrubHub]], [[OpenTable]], and [[Stitch Fix]]. The Uber deal alone ranks among the most profitable venture investments in history and cemented Benchmark's reputation as the premier early-stage fund. Gurley is also central to Benchmark's distinctive equal-partnership model — unlike Sequoia or Andreessen Horowitz, Benchmark has no junior partners, no growth fund, and splits carry equally among ~5-6 partners, which Gurley has publicly championed as alignment-optimal.

Beyond deal-making, Gurley has become one of the most visible VC voices on structural issues. He publicly backed the [[ATOM Project]] (American Truly Open Models) in 2025, signaling VC support for U.S. open-source AI leadership against [[China]]ese proprietary alternatives — a position that aligned Benchmark with the open-source AI lobby against closed-model companies like [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]]. He co-hosts [[BG2Pod]] with Brad Gerstner ([[Altimeter Capital]]), a podcast that functions as a signal channel for the tech investment elite, featuring guests like [[Jensen Huang]] and [[Satya Nadella]]. Gurley's public commentary moves markets less through specific stock calls and more through framing — his multi-year campaign against "gross margin" deception in tech IPOs (the "unit economics" critique) influenced how institutional investors evaluate growth-stage companies. His regulatory critique of rideshare and gig-economy regulation shaped the political environment around [[Uber]]'s public market debut.
