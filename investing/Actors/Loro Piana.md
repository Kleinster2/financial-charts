**Loro Piana** is an Italian ultra-luxury cashmere and wool house, subsidiary of [[LVMH]] since the 2013 acquisition of an 80% stake for approximately EUR 2B. Founded in 1924 in Quarona, Italy, the brand operates at the apex of the luxury textile pyramid, sourcing the world's finest raw materials and selling finished garments at price points that make even other luxury houses look accessible.

## Synopsis

Loro Piana matters to the [[LVMH]] investment case as a pure expression of the "quiet luxury" trend — understated, material-obsessed, and aimed at clients who view conspicuous logos as vulgar. The brand controls its own supply chain from raw fiber to finished product, maintaining exclusive relationships with Mongolian cashmere herders and Peruvian vicuna breeders. This vertical integration creates pricing power that is nearly impossible to replicate: Loro Piana cashmere sweaters retail for $2,000-5,000, and the brand's vicuna products (the rarest natural fiber, yielding ~150g per animal per year) can exceed $20,000 per garment.

Within [[LVMH]]'s Fashion & Leather Goods division, Loro Piana is grouped alongside [[Dior]], [[Fendi]], [[Celine]], and [[Louis Vuitton]], but its economics are distinct. Revenue is smaller (estimated EUR 1.5-2B) but margins are among the highest in the group due to direct retail and the absence of discounting. The Piana family retained 20% and operational control, a structure [[Bernard Arnault]] has used to keep founder DNA alive post-acquisition. The brand has expanded cautiously into womenswear, accessories, and homewares, but textiles remain the core identity.
