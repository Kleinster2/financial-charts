**IRGC** — [[Iran]]'s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps. Elite military, intelligence, and economic force operating parallel to Iran's conventional armed forces. Controls the Strait of Hormuz northern shore via its naval arm (IRGCN). Oversees Iran's ballistic missile and drone programs. Extensive economic interests through construction, energy, and telecom conglomerates (Khatam al-Anbiya, etc.). US-designated Foreign Terrorist Organization since 2019.

## Aerospace Force -- missile and drone programs

The IRGC Aerospace Force manages [[Iran]]'s entire ballistic missile and combat drone inventory -- 3,000+ missiles across 16+ types, from short-range battlefield systems to the [[Khorramshahr]] MRBM (2,000 km at full payload). Also developed the [[Qaem-100]] solid-fuel space launch vehicle, which may have been weaponized for the [[Diego Garcia attack 2026|March 2026 Diego Garcia attack]]. The Aerospace Force controls the [[Fattah]] series (claimed hypersonic), [[Sejjil]] (solid-fuel MRBM), Kheibar Shekan, and the [[Shahed-136]] one-way attack drone. Israeli strikes in October 2024 destroyed 12-20 planetary mixers critical to solid rocket fuel production, severely constraining the program's replenishment capacity. See [[Iranian missile arsenal]] for full analysis.

## Aerospace Force -- satellite ISR

The IRGC Aerospace Force also oversees Iran's military space programme -- distinct from the civilian [[Iranian Space Agency]]. Iran's indigenous satellites ([[Noor-2]] at 12-15m resolution and [[Noor-3]] at ~5m) are insufficient to identify aircraft or monitor base-level activity. In late 2024 the IRGC Aerospace Force closed that gap via a Rmb250mn (~$36.6mn) contract with [[China|Chinese]] commercial operator [[Earth Eye Co]] for the 0.5-metre-resolution [[TEE-01B]] satellite, with ground-segment operation through [[Emposat]]'s third-country stations. During the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|March 2026 war]], the IRGC tasked TEE-01B to surveil at least ten US military sites across [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Jordan]], [[Bahrain]], [[Iraq]], [[Kuwait]], [[Djibouti]], and [[Oman]], plus civilian Gulf infrastructure. See [[IRGC Chinese satellite targeting March 2026]] for targeting detail and [[Chinese dual-use commercial satellite exports]] for the framework.
