Jason Windsor is the CEO of [[Aberdeen]] (since October 2023), leading the firm's turnaround from years of AUM decline and the widely mocked "abrdn" rebrand.

## Synopsis

Windsor inherited a firm in strategic disarray. [[Aberdeen]]'s 2017 merger with Standard Life was supposed to create a diversified asset management champion, but instead produced years of net outflows, brand confusion, and underperformance relative to peers like [[Schroders]] and [[Legal & General]]. The 2021 rebrand to "abrdn" — removing the vowels from Aberdeen — became a symbol of corporate dysfunction, widely ridiculed in the financial press and offering no commercial benefit. Windsor's first major decision was reversing it, rebranding to Aberdeen Group in February 2026 — an admission that the previous management's priorities were misaligned.

The turnaround thesis under Windsor centers on simplification: reducing the number of investment strategies, cutting costs, and refocusing on areas where Aberdeen has genuine competitive advantage (emerging markets, private markets, multi-asset). The challenge is structural — mid-sized active managers face a barbell squeeze between low-cost passive giants ([[Vanguard]], [[iShares]]) and specialized boutiques. Aberdeen's AUM has roughly halved from peak levels, and reversing outflows requires sustained investment performance that takes years to demonstrate. Windsor's credibility rests on whether operational cleanup can stabilize the base before strategic repositioning bears fruit.
