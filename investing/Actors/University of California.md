**University of California** — The UC system's investment office manages approximately $180B+ across endowment, pension, and working capital pools, making it one of the largest institutional investors in the United States. Its allocation decisions function as market signals, particularly in alternative assets.

---

## Synopsis

The UC system's investment arm oversees a combined portfolio that rivals many sovereign wealth funds in scale. The bulk sits in the UC Retirement Plan (~$100B) and the General Endowment Pool (~$20B), with the remainder in short-term and total-return pools. The investment office has historically been a sophisticated allocator across public equities, fixed income, private equity, real estate, and absolute return strategies. Its decisions carry outsized weight because of the institutional credibility the UC brand confers — when the UC Regents commit capital, other allocators notice.

The defining recent event was the $4B commitment to [[Blackstone]]'s BREIT (Blackstone Real Estate Income Trust) in January 2023. BREIT had been experiencing a wave of redemption requests that threatened to spiral into a liquidity crisis — the non-traded REIT had gated withdrawals at its 5%-of-NAV quarterly limit, and investor confidence was eroding. The UC commitment, structured at NAV with a six-year lockup and minimum return guarantees, was explicitly designed to stabilize the fund. It worked: the commitment signaled that a sophisticated institutional investor had done diligence and was willing to lock up capital at current valuations, which slowed redemption requests and prevented a fire-sale liquidation. For the vault, this episode is a key precedent for the [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]] — it demonstrated both the fragility of semi-liquid alternative vehicles and the power of anchor commitments to arrest confidence spirals.
