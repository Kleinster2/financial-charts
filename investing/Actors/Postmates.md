**Postmates** — On-demand delivery platform founded in 2011 in San Francisco. Acquired by [[Uber]] for ~$2.65B in an all-stock deal in December 2020, now fully absorbed into [[Uber]] Eats and Uber Direct.

---

## Synopsis

Postmates was a pioneer of the gig-economy delivery model, launching in 2011 — before [[DoorDash]], before Uber Eats existed as a standalone product. It raised over $900M in venture capital from investors including [[Founders Fund]], [[Tiger Global]], and [[Spark Capital]], reaching a peak private valuation of ~$2.4B. But Postmates never achieved the scale economics needed to compete in what became a brutal four-way fight with [[DoorDash]], [[Grubhub]], and Uber Eats for US delivery market share.

The $2.65B acquisition by [[Uber]] in 2020 was strategically logical for both sides. Uber got Postmates' fleet density in Los Angeles and other West Coast markets where Uber Eats was weaker, plus a merchant network that filled geographic gaps. For Postmates, it was an exit below its last private valuation ambitions but above the alternative of slow attrition. The brand was retired by 2023, with the Postmates merchant base and delivery network folded entirely into Uber's platform. The deal illustrates the endgame of delivery platform wars: consolidation is the only exit for subscale players.
