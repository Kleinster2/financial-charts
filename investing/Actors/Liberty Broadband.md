# Liberty Broadband

Liberty Broadband (NASDAQ: LBRDA / LBRDK) — [[John Malone]]-controlled tracking-stock vehicle holding ~26% of [[Charter Communications]] plus a small consolidated investment in Alaska Communications. Functions as the principal Liberty-complex exposure to the US cable broadband thesis; trades as a discounted Charter proxy with embedded tax-efficient acquisition optionality.

---

## Why Liberty Broadband matters

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Tickers | LBRDA (Class A), LBRDK (Class C), GLIBA (Series A) |
| Primary holding | ~26% of [[Charter Communications]] |
| Secondary holding | Alaska Communications Systems (consolidated) |
| Control | [[John Malone]] (10%+ super-voting B shares) |
| Structure | Tracking stock spun off from [[Liberty Media]] (2014) |

The vehicle exists to give Malone a tax-efficient, low-friction way to hold and increase Liberty's Charter stake without triggering the Reverse Morris Trust constraints from the original 2014 spin. Buybacks at the LBRDK level translate to higher per-share Charter exposure over time, and Liberty has historically traded at a discount to look-through Charter NAV.

---

## Charter merger announcement (2024)

In November 2024 Charter announced an all-stock acquisition of Liberty Broadband — collapsing the holding-company discount and giving Liberty shareholders direct Charter equity. The deal:

| Detail | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Structure | All-stock; Liberty shareholders receive Charter shares |
| Exchange ratio | 0.236 CHTR per LBRDK |
| Headline value (announce) | ~$98 per Liberty share at deal-date Charter price |
| Charter shares retired | ~45M (roughly 30% of Charter float held by Liberty) |
| Closing target | June 30, 2027 |
| Conditions | Charter–Cox merger close (expected summer 2026) |

Pro-forma the deal collapses the Liberty-Charter look-through structure. Liberty Broadband as a standalone entity has a finite remaining life, with the merger closing roughly 14 months after Charter–Cox.

## Apr 24 read-through

The Apr 24 [[Charter Communications]] -25.5% selloff translated almost mechanically into LBRDK weakness — Liberty Broadband NAV is ~99% Charter equity, so the look-through discount widened only marginally. The standalone Liberty thesis is now an exchange-ratio + close-timing trade: investors holding LBRDK before the June 2027 close are effectively holding a delayed Charter call option with the Liberty discount as a small additional cushion.
