Galp is [[Portugal]]'s largest energy company and one of the few European oil majors to have made a transformative discovery in the current decade. The company's Mopane find offshore [[Namibia]] (PEL 83 license, 80% operated stake) is estimated to hold 10B+ barrels of oil equivalent in place, potentially the largest deepwater discovery since [[Brazil]]'s pre-salt. Appraisal drilling in 2024-2025 has consistently expanded the resource estimate, making Mopane the single most important exploration story in global E&P and the primary driver of Galp's stock re-rating (~60% rally in 2024).

Beyond Mopane, Galp operates refineries in Portugal (Sines, 220kbd capacity), upstream assets in [[Brazil]] (Santos/Campos basin pre-salt participation) and Mozambique (Rovuma LNG, though this has been delayed by insurgency), and a growing renewables portfolio (solar, green hydrogen). The company's partnership with [[TotalEnergies]] in Namibia provides operational credibility — TotalEnergies holds a 10% stake in PEL 83 and has operated nearby blocks. CEO Filipe Silva (appointed 2024) is navigating the tension between accelerating Mopane development (first oil targeted ~2029) and managing shareholder expectations on capital returns.
