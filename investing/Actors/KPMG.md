## Name origin

The initials represent the founders of predecessor firms:

- **K** — Piet Klynveld (Dutch firm, 1917)
- **P** — William Barclay Peat (London firm, 1870)
- **M** — James Marwick (New York firm, 1897)
- **G** — Reinhard Goerdeler (German accounting leader)

Formed in 1987 when Peat Marwick Mitchell merged with Klynveld Main Goerdeler.

## Scandals and controversies

### Carillion audit failure (UK, 2023)

Fined £21M by the FRC for failing to follow "the most basic and fundamental audit concepts" on [[Carillion]]'s audits. For three years before Carillion's collapse, it was not subject to reliable audits.

### Netherlands exam cheating (2024)

**Largest PCAOB fine ever** — $25M. From 2017-2022, hundreds of KPMG Netherlands professionals engaged in improper answer sharing on mandatory training exams about auditing standards and ethics. Misconduct reached partner level, revealing "inappropriate tone at the top."

### US regional bank failures (2023)

Congressional report criticized KPMG for "willful blindness" for issuing clean audits before the failures of [[Silicon Valley Bank]], [[Signature Bank]], and [[First Republic Bank]].

### India (2024)

Fined ₹10 crore (~$1.2M) for lapses auditing Coffee Day Enterprises. Two partners barred for up to 10 years.

### Other issues

- **France (2023)** — Sued for €700M over H2O fund losses
- **Entain (2025)** — FRC investigating 2022 audit related to Turkish subsidiary corruption

## Restructuring

KPMG plans to reduce economic units from 100+ to as few as 32 through national partnership mergers. First major merger: UK + Switzerland (2024).
