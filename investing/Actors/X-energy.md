| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Design | Xe-100 (HTGR) |
| Output | 80 MW electric per unit |

## Xe-100 design

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Type | High-temperature gas reactor |
| Coolant | Helium |
| Fuel | TRISO particles ("meltdown-proof" by design) |
| Temperature | 750°C+ |
| Modularity | 80 MW units, scalable in clusters |

The TRISO fuel is the safety case. Tiny uranium kernels in ceramic shells will not melt down at any plausible accident temperature, which is the technical basis for X-energy's "meltdown-proof" framing. Coupled with passive cooling and a relatively simple plant footprint (less concrete, fewer moving parts vs PWRs), the build economics depend on serial repetition — which is the explicit theme [[Clay Sell]] returned to on the IPO day, framing replication as the route to "incredibly economic" nuclear.

## Regulatory progress

[[Clay Sell]] on the [[NRC]] (Apr 24 2026): the agency reform — bipartisan legislation plus Trump's appointment of a new [[NRC]] chairman — has been "key to launching this new era of nuclear." X-energy's specific regulatory milestones:

| License | Status |
|---------|--------|
| Fuel facility license | Already granted (under construction) |
| First powerplant construction license | Targeted by end-2026 |

Both timelines, if hit, would be in record time per Sell. The "meltdown-proof" TRISO fuel design is part of why the NRC review path has compressed — passive safety reduces the licensing surface vs traditional PWRs.

## IPO debut (Apr 24, 2026)

X-energy priced its IPO at $23, well above the $16-$19 marketed range, on 44,254,659 Class A common shares — an upsizing that reflected demand from AI-power-buildout investors. Shares opened $30.11 (+31%) and closed the first day at $29.20 (+27%), implying an ~$11.9B equity valuation. The offering is expected to close Apr 27 2026 with a 30-day greenshoe of up to 6,638,198 additional shares.

| Greenshoe | Up to 6,638,198 shares (30-day option) |

The pricing tape and the day-1 reception both speak to demand. X-energy is the first sizable advanced-reactor company to come public via a traditional IPO; [[Oklo]] (OKLO) and [[NuScale]] (SMR) both took the SPAC route. That distinction matters because the IPO valuation reflects book-building demand from institutional investors at marketed terms, not a SPAC-trust unwind.

The strategic context: AI-driven power demand has converted the nuclear-revival thesis from a 2030s policy story into a here-and-now data-center procurement story. [[Amazon]]'s presence in X-energy's customer list is the bridge — the same Amazon that committed $5B+ to [[Anthropic]] this month is buying its long-dated power capacity through Xe-100 deployments. The IPO debut tests whether the public market is willing to price that bridge before the reactors are operating.

## Investment angle

Tailwinds: industrial-heat market sits beyond grid-power competition, the [[Dow]] + [[Amazon]] + [[Energy Northwest]] customer triple validates three orthogonal use cases (industrial, data-center, utility), DOE ARDP funding underwrites the first commercial unit, in-house TRISO fuel manufacturing creates vertical integration, the 140+ unit backlog gives replication economics, and the AI-power thesis has converted nuclear into a near-term procurement story rather than a 2030s policy bet.

Risks: $0 revenue with first commercial reactor not arriving until the 2030s makes execution a multi-year drag; first-of-kind Xe-100 risk because the design has not been built at scale; HALEU supply chain still developing (see [[Centrus Energy]]); any NRC slippage moves the entire pipeline; and the IPO opened above the marketed range, leaving entry multiples rich relative to SMR peers ([[Oklo]], [[NuScale]]).
