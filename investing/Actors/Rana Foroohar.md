**Rana Foroohar** is the [[Financial Times]]' global business columnist and CNN's global economic analyst, focused on the intersection of economics, geopolitics, technology, and business. Author of multiple books on financial-services excess, Silicon Valley power, and the future of capitalism. The kind of recurring FT byline whose views are worth tracking as a leading-indicator on framing shifts in the consensus financial-press worldview.

## Why she's a tracked actor

Foroohar's FT columns frequently sit a few weeks ahead of the consensus on framing shifts in major economic threads — particularly on industrial policy, supply-chain resilience, and the China-US tech split. Tracking her columns helps anticipate where the FT editorial board and the broader financial-press narrative will move next. She is one of the recurring FT bylines (alongside [[Martin Wolf]], [[Gillian Tett]], [[Chris Giles]], [[Robert Armstrong]]) whose individual views shape FT positioning more than a typical staff reporter.
