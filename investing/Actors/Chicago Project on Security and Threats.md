# Chicago Project on Security and Threats

Research center at the [[University of Chicago]] focused on national + international security, political violence, and suicide terrorism. Founded in 2004 by [[Robert Pape]] as the Chicago Project on Suicide Terrorism (CPOST), with original focus on the causes and effects of suicide terrorism. Renamed in 2014 as the scope broadened to issues of global security and threats. Pape remains the founding director. CPOST is best known publicly for its empirical research on the January 6, 2021 US Capitol attack — the American Political Violence research stream produced findings on the demographics, geography, and ideological coherence of the participants that became standard reference material.

## Why it matters in the vault

CPOST is the institutional anchor for [[Robert Pape]]'s analytical framework — the Escalation Trap Substack pieces draw on CPOST's longer-horizon research on how political violence and strategic miscalculation unfold. The center's American Political Violence research stream is the most empirically rigorous publicly-available data on contemporary US political-violence dynamics, which makes it a credible source whenever the vault touches on US democratic-stability questions in the [[Trump II]] administration thread.
