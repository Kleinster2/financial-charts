## Performance fee warning (Mar 12, 2026)

CVC cut performance fee (carried interest) expectations to **€600-700M** for 2026-2027 combined — well below analyst expectations of **€1B+**. Stock fell **7%** to ~€11, well below **€14 IPO price** (Apr 2024). Down **40%+** over 12 months.

**Root cause:** 2020 flagship buyout fund (deployed during the 2021 vintage peak) underperformed. Won't generate carry until **2028 or 2029**. CEO [[Rob Lucas]]: "The 2021 vintage is always going to be a more challenged vintage."

The entire PE industry has struggled to exit holdings since 2022 rate rises. Funds deployed during 2021 pandemic-era dealmaking frenzy — when multiples peaked — are the most challenged cohort. CVC still expects next fundraise "same size or larger" than its record **€26B** vehicle (2023).

[[Citigroup]] analyst Nicholas Herman: cutting carry "makes sense, especially given a tougher macro backdrop." [[Barclays]] analyst Michael Sanderson: core business performing as expected but "when people are going to have to cut numbers, and it's a decent-sized cut, there's little you can respond to."

*Source: FT (Alexandra Heal), Mar 12, 2026*
