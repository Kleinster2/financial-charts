## Apr 24, 2026 — Warsh path and balance-sheet floor

On Bloomberg Real Yield (Apr 24, 2026), reacting to [[Jeanine Pirro]]'s announcement that the DOJ was closing the [[Jerome Powell|Powell]] investigation:

- Rates response is "very consistent with the idea that the market is now expecting [[Kevin Warsh|Warsh]] will be the head of the FOMC in June."
- A confirmed Warsh "needs to build consensus" — won't simply cut rates by fiat. The current FOMC majority view is zero or one cut in 2026.
- On the curve: pain trade is the *steepening* — almost everyone came into 2026 long the steepener and got washed out.
- On the 2Y: yield selloff "has come almost entirely from inflation expectations." If oil drops back to ~$50/bbl over the next two-three months, 2Y could fall another 15-20bps without difficulty.
- On Warsh's minimalist Fed balance sheet aspiration: structurally cannot get below ~**$2.7T** without rewriting the post-GFC reserve rules that govern why banks hold reserves at the Fed. *"Otherwise money markets around the funding area at the front end of the curve will go awry — that's why they created the [[Standing Repo Facility]]."*
- On Powell's final FOMC: it is "Jay Powell's swan" — expects a personal/legacy framing rather than substantive policy signal.

*Source: Bloomberg Real Yield, Apr 24, 2026*
