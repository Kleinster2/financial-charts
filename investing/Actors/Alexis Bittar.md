# Alexis Bittar

**Alexis Bittar** is a Brooklyn-based jewelry and accessories brand founded by designer Alexis Bittar (b. 1968). The label is known for hand-carved Lucite jewelry that blends sculptural art with fashion — a signature material and technique that distinguishes it from precious-metal-focused fine jewelry competitors.

The brand's trajectory is a case study in founder-driven luxury: Bittar built the label from a street-selling operation in the 1990s into a nationally distributed fashion brand, won CFDA Accessory Designer of the Year (2010), then sold to [[Brooks Brothers]] pre-2015. When Brooks Brothers went bankrupt in 2020, Bittar reacquired his namesake brand and relaunched with new retail concepts and expanded product categories (handbags, home decor). The reacquisition-and-relaunch pattern mirrors other founder buybacks in fashion.

## Business

- Signature: Hand-sculpted Lucite jewelry — mixed metals, semi-precious stones, distinctive textures
- Categories: Earrings, necklaces, bracelets, rings, handbags, home decor
- Retail: Own boutiques (NYC, San Francisco) plus wholesale via [[Saks Fifth Avenue]], Neiman Marcus
- Private company — not publicly traded
