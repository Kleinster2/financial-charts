## Citi-HPS EMEA direct lending partnership (May 18, 2026)

On May 18, 2026, [[Citigroup]] and HPS announced a €15B ($17.48B) private capital program to expand direct lending across EMEA over an initial five-year term. The structure mirrors the September 2024 [[Citigroup|Citi]]-[[Apollo]] $25B North American program: Citi originates deals through its Investment, Corporate, and Commercial Bank teams using existing client relationships; HPS supplies capital from its $381B Private Financing Solutions platform.

Geographic scope: Continental Europe + UK initially, with Middle East "eventually." Borrower scope: sub-investment-grade corporate and sponsor-owned borrowers. Eligible instruments: broad range of sub-IG debt.

For HPS specifically the deal is the highest-profile validation yet of the [[BlackRock]] integration. The AUM trajectory ($100B at acquisition → $381B March 2026) substantially exceeds the integration playbook BlackRock has executed on prior alternative-asset acquisitions, and pairing BlackRock-scale capital with Citi-scale origination is a structural combination that no organic private credit firm can match. [[Matthieu Boulanger]] (HPS partner, head of Europe): the deal will let HPS "leverage Citi's extensive network and origination pipeline in EMEA, further strengthening our ability to deliver tailored financing options to a broad range of borrowers."

Full event detail: [[Citi-HPS direct lending EMEA partnership May 2026]]. Structural pattern: [[Bank-private credit partnerships]].
