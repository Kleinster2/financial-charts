# Brian Armstrong

CEO and co-founder of [[Coinbase]], who built the largest US cryptocurrency exchange from a 2012 [[Y Combinator]] startup into an S&P 500 company.

## Synopsis

Brian Armstrong left his engineering role at [[Airbnb]] in 2012 to co-found [[Coinbase]] with [[Fred Ehrsam]], betting that crypto needed a regulated, user-friendly on-ramp for mainstream adoption. The timing was prescient -- Bitcoin was trading under $10. Armstrong's core insight was that crypto's TAM depended on making buying as simple as buying stocks on [[Charles Schwab]], and that regulatory compliance (rather than the crypto-anarchist ethos) was the path to institutional legitimacy. [[Coinbase]] went public via direct listing in April 2021 at a ~$86B valuation, near the peak of the crypto bubble.

Armstrong's leadership style is distinctively political for a tech CEO -- he published a 2020 blog post declaring Coinbase an "apolitical" company focused on its mission, encouraging employees who wanted to engage in social activism to leave (with a severance package). This drew criticism at the time but proved prescient as other tech companies grappled with internal political conflicts. On crypto regulation, Armstrong has been aggressive: publicly feuding with SEC Chair Gary Gensler, lobbying for crypto-specific legislation, and positioning [[Coinbase]] as the "good actor" in an industry plagued by fraud ([[FTX]], [[Terra/Luna]]). Net worth fluctuates dramatically with crypto cycles -- ranging from ~$2B to $15B+ -- making him one of the most volatile entries on the Forbes billionaires list.
