## Vykat XR

Vykat XR (formerly DCCR) is a once-daily oral extended-release tablet of diazoxide choline, approved for adults and children ≥4 years with Prader-Willi syndrome. It is the first drug ever approved to treat hyperphagia — the chronic, life-threatening hunger that defines PWS.

PWS is a rare genetic neurodevelopmental disorder caused by abnormal gene expression on chromosome 15, affecting ~10,000 patients in the US. Beyond hyperphagia, patients experience cognitive impairment, psychiatric challenges, and metabolic dysfunction. Hyperphagia drives compulsive food-seeking behavior and is the primary cause of morbidity and mortality.

The drug generated $190M in 2025 revenue within roughly nine months of launch (approved March 26, commercial launch April 14, 2025). Q4 alone was $92M, showing accelerating adoption. [[Cantor Fitzgerald|Cantor]] analyst Josh Schimmer projects Vykat XR surpassing $1B in annual sales by 2029. IP estate extends into the mid-2040s.

## Launch disruptions and class action

The launch was not seamless. On November 5, 2025, Soleno reported disappointing information about the Vykat XR launch trajectory. Stock declined ~12% over two trading days. A securities class action was filed (Hagens Berman, Case No. 3:26-cv-01979, N.D. Cal.) covering purchasers between March 26 and November 4, 2025. Lead plaintiff deadline: May 5, 2026.

An August 2025 Stat News article referenced "unprecedented catastrophes in post-approval history" related to the launch, triggering earlier stock pressure.

Despite the rocky start, Q4 2025 revenue of $92M demonstrated recovery. [[Oppenheimer]] maintained an $80 price target. Consensus analyst rating remained Buy with an average PT of ~$105 (pre-acquisition).

## Neurocrine acquisition (Apr 6, 2026)

[[Neurocrine Biosciences]] announced a definitive agreement to acquire Soleno for $53.00 per share in cash, representing total equity value of $2.9B.

|--------|-------|
| Price | $53.00/share |

SLNO surged ~33% on announcement (Apr 6). Neurocrine CEO [[Kyle Gano]] called Vykat XR "a potential blockbuster in the making" but said no immediate plans for European launch — US focus for now. He noted starting patients at younger ages and lower BMI could help maintain weight over time.

[[RBC Capital]] analyst [[Brian Abrahams]] called the deal a "third leg to the story" for Neurocrine, noting accretion potential and competitive positioning alongside [[Ingrezza]] and [[Crenessity]].
