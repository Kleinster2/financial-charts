**Paul Graham** — Programmer, essayist, investor. Co-founded [[Y Combinator]] (2005) and Viaweb (sold to Yahoo for $49M, 1998). **212 essays** at paulgraham.com that codified startup culture. Coined "ramen profitable," "do things that don't scale," "default alive." Described as a "hacker philosopher."

## The essays

Paul Graham has written **212 essays** at paulgraham.com. They became the operating manual for a generation of founders.

See [[YC startup philosophy]] for the full framework — core mantras, survival concepts, counterintuitive advice, and how the ideas spread beyond YC.

**Key essays:** "Do Things That Don't Scale," "Startup = Growth," "Ramen Profitable," "Default Alive or Default Dead?," "Founder Mode" (2024).

**Non-startup essays:** "Beating the Averages" (Lisp), "Why Nerds Are Unpopular" (high school dynamics), "Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule" (productivity).

| **The philosophy** | Created [[YC startup philosophy]] — now the default startup framework |

## Investment relevance

**Why track Paul Graham:**

1. **Cultural influence** — His ideas shape how founders think, which shapes what gets built.

2. **Pattern recognition** — Essays distill patterns from 5,300+ YC companies.

3. **Contrarian signal** — When PG writes something new (e.g., "Founder Mode" 2024), it often signals a shift in startup thinking.

4. **Hacker News** — The forum he created is where tech news breaks.
