**Hagag Group Real Estate Entrepreneurship Ltd** (TASE: HGG) is an Israeli luxury real estate developer founded in 1950, controlled by brothers Eido Hagag and Isaac Hagag since 2010. Headquartered in [[Tel Aviv]], the company has executed over 100 projects — primarily luxury residential towers, commercial offices, hotels, and urban renewal — concentrated in Tel Aviv's prime locations.

## Key projects

- Meier on Rothschild — 41-storey luxury tower designed by architect Richard Meier. Won a 2011 design award. One of Tel Aviv's most prestigious addresses.
- H Infinity Tower — tallest residential tower in [[Israel]], 48+ storeys, 287 luxury apartments near Kikar Ha'Medina. Average price ~67,660 ILS/sqm (up from 53,600 at 2021 launch).
- H-BLVD (Sde Dov) — landmark residential development featuring Israel's first outdoor street mall: 40,000 sqm boulevard integrating retail, dining, entertainment.
- Saharon — large urban development in southern Tel Aviv in collaboration with the Tel Aviv Municipality.
- H-Tower Recital — prestigious office tower in Israel's business center.
- Urban Renewal (Tama 38) — dedicated subsidiary managing ~60 projects in prime Tel Aviv, focused on earthquake-proofing old buildings, adding shelter rooms and elevators.

## Leonardo DiCaprio hotel (2025)

In July 2025, Hagag received final construction approval for a luxury hotel at the Herzliya Marina in partnership with Leonardo DiCaprio. The Tel Aviv District Planning Committee approved 51,000 sqm of construction rights, up to 14 storeys. The project emphasizes environmental sustainability. The Hagag brothers acquired the land option in 2015 and finalized the purchase in 2017 for 78M ILS (~$23M).

---

## European operations

The family also controls Hagag Development Europe (TASE: HGGE, market cap ~564M ILS), which entered Romania in 2017 and has become a prominent Bucharest developer. European portfolio valued at 300M+ EUR with 130M EUR in further planned investment. Key projects include H Stirbei Palace (luxury retail), H East Residence (568 units), and H Herastrau Park (mixed-use).
