**Sir Alex Younger** served as Chief of the Secret Intelligence Service ([[MI6]]) from 2014 to 2020, making him one of the longest-serving heads of British foreign intelligence in the modern era. Before becoming chief (known internally as "C"), Younger spent his career in MI6 with operational postings that reportedly included Afghanistan and the Middle East.

After leaving MI6, Younger joined [[Goldman Sachs]] International as a paid adviser in 2021 — part of a broader trend of intelligence chiefs moving into financial services advisory roles. The value proposition is geopolitical risk assessment: Goldman and similar firms increasingly need intelligence-grade analysis of sanctions risk, supply chain vulnerability, and country risk that goes beyond what traditional sell-side research provides. Younger sits on the Goldman Sachs International advisory board alongside other former government officials.

His appointment drew some controversy in the UK, with questions about the revolving door between intelligence services and finance. Younger has also taken board and advisory positions with cybersecurity firms.
