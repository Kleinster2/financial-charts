# Solaris

## Synopsis

[[Solaris]] (Solaris Bus & Coach) is a Polish bus manufacturer headquartered in Bolechowo, founded in 1996 and acquired by Spain's [[CAF Group]] in 2018. The firm is Europe's leader in zero-emission city-bus deliveries — the *Solaris Urbino 18* won European Bus of the Year 2025 and the company delivered 225 hydrogen-powered buses in 2024, the highest single-year hydrogen-bus delivery count in Europe. Cumulative deliveries exceed 28,000 vehicles across 33 countries.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Ballard]] partnership thread: Solaris formally selected Ballard as the fuel-cell supplier for its next-generation hydrogen bus platform in May 2026, integrating Ballard's FCmove-SC engine into the Solaris Generation 2 FCEV bus. This pairs with the parallel Wrightbus selection of Ballard's FCmove-HD for the [[Wrightbus|Hydroliner Gen 2.0]] in the UK — the two anchor European hydrogen-bus customers selecting Ballard simultaneously for their next-generation platforms. The Q1 2026 [[Ballard]] EBITDA inflection (+37 percentage points YoY) was attributed in part to this two-anchor European bus-partnership flywheel.

## Position in Ballard partnership thread

Solaris and [[Wrightbus]] form the two-anchor European hydrogen-bus flywheel underpinning the [[Ballard]] Q1 2026 margin inflection. Solaris's volume position (225 deliveries in 2024 — #1 in Europe) makes it the larger of the two; Wrightbus is the UK / Northern Ireland anchor with the world-first double-decker product. Both selected Ballard's FCmove platform for the next-generation cycle — Solaris on FCmove-SC (May 2026), Wrightbus on FCmove-HD (May 2025) — which is the first time both leading European OEMs are aligned on Ballard simultaneously.

The CAF-Group ownership matters for the Solaris-side adoption story. CAF is a publicly listed Spanish rolling-stock group with broader European tender exposure; Solaris's procurement decisions reflect CAF's group-level zero-emission strategy rather than standalone-company economics. This adds institutional resilience to the Ballard partnership relative to a smaller standalone Polish OEM.
