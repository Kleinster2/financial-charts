Arlon Group — the food and agribusiness private-equity arm of [[Continental Grain Company]], formed in 2008 and backed by Continental Grain together with [[Rabobank]]. With roughly $1.5 billion in assets under management, Arlon invests in middle-market businesses across the entire food-and-agriculture value chain in the Americas. It takes its name from [[Arlon]], the Belgian town where [[Continental Grain Company]] was founded in 1813. In Brazil it held frozen-vegetables leader [[Grano Alimentos]] from February 2015 until selling 75% control to [[Cleam Capital]] in early May 2026 — a roughly decade-long hold, advised by [[Rabobank]].

## Why it matters

- Captive, not independent: Arlon is [[Continental Grain Company]]'s vehicle for mid-market food/ag deals, sometimes described as the Fribourg family office's private-equity arm. Its sector conviction — "invest across the entire food value chain" — is inherited from a 200-year-old grain house, not assembled from third-party LPs.
- The [[Rabobank]] thread: Rabobank co-backs Arlon and also advised the Grano sale — the Dutch food-and-agri bank sits on both sides of this network, which is typical of how concentrated the global agribusiness finance world is.
- Latin America build-out: Arlon, Continental Grain and VR Investments set up a Latin America Advisory Board and named Guillermo Bilbao a Managing Principal — the institutional push behind the Brazilian (Grano) and broader LatAm food/ag positions.

## Funding history

Sponsored and principally backed by [[Continental Grain Company]], with [[Rabobank]] as co-investor. Arlon is the captive investment arm rather than an independent fund raising primarily from third-party LPs; its ~$1.5B AUM traces to its two institutional backers and the Fribourg family balance sheet.
