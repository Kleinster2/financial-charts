Mast Brothers is a Brooklyn-based artisanal chocolate maker and specialty food retailer, known for bean-to-bar chocolate production and the Mast Market retail concept. Wholesale partner of [[Irving Farm Coffee]] for curated organic and biodynamic coffee selections.

## Synopsis

Founded in 2007 by brothers Rick and Michael Mast in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, Mast Brothers became an early emblem of the artisanal food movement — hand-wrapped bars with decorative paper, bean-to-bar production claims, and a factory-retail space that doubled as a consumer experience. The brand rode the premium craft wave that also lifted companies like Blue Bottle Coffee and Mast's neighbor Brooklyn Brewery. At peak hype (~2013-2015), Mast bars retailed for $8-10 and were stocked in high-end grocers and gift shops nationally.

The brand suffered a credibility crisis in 2015 when food journalists revealed that early Mast Brothers chocolate was remelted industrial couverture, not bean-to-bar as marketed — a scandal that crystallized consumer skepticism about artisanal food claims more broadly. The brothers pivoted to the Mast Market retail concept, a curated specialty food store in Brooklyn carrying their chocolate alongside partner products like [[Irving Farm Coffee]]'s organic selections. The business remains private and small-scale. For investors, Mast Brothers is less an investment target and more a case study in the fragility of premium DTC food brands built on authenticity claims — the same vulnerability that later hit brands across the broader craft food and beverage space.
