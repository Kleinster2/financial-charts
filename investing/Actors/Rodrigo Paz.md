**Rodrigo Paz** — President of [[Bolivia]] from November 8, 2025. Won the October 2025 election as the first non-MAS president in two decades, replacing Luis Arce at the end of Bolivia's worst economic crisis in 40 years. Market-relevant primarily for his framework on the long-stalled Uyuni lithium development — "modernization without privatization" — which is now the central variable in Bolivia's lithium production trajectory.

---

## Why Paz matters for markets

| Lever | Stated position | Policy implication |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Uyuni lithium development | "Modernization without privatization" | Renegotiation of CBC (Chinese) and Rosatom (Russian) DLE contracts blocked by prior congress |
| Foreign investment posture | Transparency + local-community engagement required | Terms similar to [[Chile]]'s Nova Andino Litio framework plausible |
| [[YLB]] role | Supports state anchor; rejects pure-state-operation premise | YLB likely to partner with foreign operators rather than develop DLE alone |
| Macro orthodoxy | Center-right, pro-investment, IMF-engaged | Boliviano stabilization priority; dollar-denominated financing path reopening |

**Contracts under review:** The Arce-era $1B CBC ([[CATL]]-linked Chinese consortium) and $1B Rosatom Russian agreements were blocked by Congress in July 2025. Paz has committed to scrutinize both for transparency and local-community benefit. Expected outcome is renegotiation rather than cancellation, with timelines and new terms unresolved as of April 2026.

**Congressional constraint:** Paz's center-right coalition does not have majority control. Lithium legislation requires coalition-building with the center-left opposition — Potosí and Oruro departmental political weight is the gatekeeping variable.

## Political context

- Son of former President Jaime Paz Zamora (1989-1993)
- Political tradition: Movimiento de Izquierda Revolucionaria (MIR), now aligned with center-right PDC coalition
- Won October 2025 runoff against Jorge "Tuto" Quiroga
- Inaugurated November 8, 2025
- First non-MAS president since Evo Morales's 2006 inauguration (MAS ruled 2006-2019 and 2020-2025)
