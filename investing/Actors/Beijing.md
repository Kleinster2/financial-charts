**Beijing** — capital of [[China]], seat of the CPC Central Committee, State Council, and all major regulatory bodies ([[PBOC]], [[CSRC]], [[MOFCOM]]). Population ~21.5M. In financial/geopolitical context, "Beijing" is often shorthand for the Chinese central government and its policy apparatus — distinct from [[Shanghai]] (financial center) and [[Shenzhen]] (tech/manufacturing hub). Home to [[Zhongguancun]], China's premier technology cluster, and headquarters of major tech firms including [[JD.com]], [[ByteDance]], [[Baidu]], and [[Meituan]].

## Synopsis

Beijing matters to investors primarily as the locus of Chinese policy decisions: tech regulation cycles (the 2020-2022 crackdown that wiped ~$2T from Chinese tech, followed by the 2023-2024 easing), capital controls, [[Belt and Road]] direction, trade negotiation posture, and industrial policy (semiconductor self-sufficiency, EV subsidies, AI investment). When the vault references "Beijing" in an analytical context, it typically means the central government's policy stance rather than the city's local economy.

The city's own economy is dominated by services (~83% of GDP), state-owned enterprises, and the tech sector anchored in Zhongguancun's Haidian District. Unlike [[Shanghai]] or [[Shenzhen]], Beijing's economic significance for investors is less about local companies and more about the regulatory and political signals that emanate from it.
