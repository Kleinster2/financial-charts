Vitro is a Mexican glass manufacturer founded in 1909 in Monterrey, one of the oldest industrial companies in [[Mexico]] and a pillar of the Monterrey business elite (Grupo Monterrey). Produces flat glass for automotive and architectural applications, primarily serving the Americas market. Ticker: BMV: VITROA. The company went through a tumultuous restructuring in 2010-2014 after a $1.2B debt crisis triggered by failed hedging on natural gas derivatives during the 2008 financial crisis — Vitro's Chapter 15 bankruptcy and Mexico-based concurso mercantil became a landmark cross-border insolvency case that tested the limits of international creditor enforcement. Emerged leaner and refocused on core glass operations after divesting its containers (Anchor Glass) and housewares businesses. Competes globally with [[AGC Inc.]], [[Saint-Gobain]], [[NSG Group]], and [[Fuyao Glass Industry Group]].
