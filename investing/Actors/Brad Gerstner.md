**Brad Gerstner** is the founder and CEO of [[Altimeter]], a technology-focused investment firm managing both public equities and late-stage private venture positions. He is one of the most visible public commentators on AI, foundation-model economics, and the ongoing [[Anthropic]] / [[OpenAI]] / [[NVIDIA]] capital cycle.

## Synopsis

Gerstner founded [[Altimeter]] in 2008 and built it into a crossover firm known for taking private positions in late-stage tech companies and maintaining them through IPO into public markets. Altimeter participated in [[Anthropic]]'s Series G at $380B and is a meaningful investor in [[OpenAI]] secondaries, making Gerstner one of the few senior investors with direct dollar exposure to both frontier labs. The firm's SPAC activity during 2020-2021 was mixed — Altimeter Growth Corp 1 took [[Grab]] Holdings public in a $40B deal that subsequently collapsed — but the private AI book has become the defining story of the current vintage.

Gerstner co-hosts [[BG2Pod]] with [[Bill Gurley]] ([[Benchmark]]), which has become a signal channel for the tech investment elite. He appears frequently as a fill-in host on [[All-In Podcast]] and uses both venues to lay out [[Altimeter]]'s thesis positions publicly — including the April 10 2026 presentation of [[Anthropic]]'s Q1 monthly revenue adds, the $80-100B projected exit run rate, and public endorsement of [[Ridges AI]] (SN62) as a live open-source threat to [[Claude Code]] economics. His public commentary is analytically dense and tends to move secondary-market sentiment on private AI names.
