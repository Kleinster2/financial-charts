**Bank for International Settlements (BIS)** — The "central bank of central banks." Basel-based institution (est. 1930) that fosters international monetary cooperation, hosts banking supervision standards (Basel Accords), and provides banking services to central banks.

*Not to be confused with [[BIS|Bureau of Industry and Security]] (US Commerce export controls).*

---

## Why BIS matters

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Central bank coordinator | Hosts regular meetings of central bank governors |
| Standard-setter | Houses [[Basel Committee]] — source of Basel I/II/III capital rules |
| Research | Publishes influential work on global liquidity, financial stability |
| Banking services | Gold custody, FX transactions, securities settlement for central banks |
| Innovation | BIS Innovation Hub researches CBDCs, tokenization, fintech |

When central banks need to coordinate on financial stability, they do it at the BIS.

| Committee | Focus |

## Innovation Hub

Launched 2019 under [[Hyun Song Shin]]'s interim leadership. Centers in Switzerland, Hong Kong, Singapore, London, Toronto, Stockholm, and more.

**Focus areas:**
- CBDC prototypes (Project mBridge, Project Helvetia)
- Cross-border payments
- Tokenization of financial assets
- Suptech/regtech for supervisors

## Basel Accords

The BIS-hosted [[Basel Committee]] produced the global bank capital framework:

| Accord | Year | Key feature |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Basel I | 1988 | Minimum capital ratios |
| Basel II | 2004 | Risk-weighted assets, internal models |
| Basel III | 2010–2023 | Post-crisis reforms (higher capital, liquidity ratios, leverage limits) |

These rules govern how much capital banks worldwide must hold.
