# Noah Barkin

China-Europe analyst, senior advisor in [[Rhodium Group]]'s China practice, and visiting senior fellow in the [[German Marshall Fund]]'s Indo-Pacific Program. Author of the *Watching China in Europe* Substack and co-editor, with Adam Cox, of *EMU Explained: The Impact of the Euro* (Kogan Page / Reuters, 2nd ed. 1998). Tracks the EU-China relationship through trade, investment, technology, and security lenses — a niche underserved by US-centric China commentary.

Prior career: 25 years as a journalist in Berlin, Paris, London, and New York. At [[Reuters]] he served as bureau chief, regional news editor, and roving Europe correspondent. Writing has appeared in [[The New York Times]], [[The Atlantic]], [[Foreign Policy]], [[Politico]], and major European publications. The journalism background is the structural reason Barkin's China-Europe coverage carries a different texture than think-tank-only voices on the same beat — he reports as much as he analyses, and his sourcing network across European foreign ministries reflects two decades of correspondent work.

## What he covers

- German and EU-level China policy (de-risking, screening regimes, FDI restrictions)
- European industrial response to Chinese subsidies and overcapacity (EVs, solar, steel)
- China's diplomatic posture toward Europe and EU member states
- Spillovers from US-China dynamics into European strategic options
