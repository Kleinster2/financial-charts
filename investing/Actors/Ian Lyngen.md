## Apr 24, 2026 — equilibrium rates and Warsh's balance-sheet posture

On Bloomberg Real Yield (Apr 24, 2026):

- Range view: 2Y around 3.80%, 10Y around 4.30% — "the market is happy with this equilibrium" and the range can persist through the next FOMC absent a payrolls shock.
- Two-camp framing: one camp focused on Iran-war inflation; the other on medium-term consumer demand damage. The standoff is the lack of curve volatility.
- On the 4.25% 10Y level (the duration-add trigger funds like [[Vanguard]] are watching): 10-year yields decompose into lower growth expectations and term premium; 4.25% prices roughly 2% growth. Whether the level drifts lower (4.15% / 4.00%) over the year depends on how [[Kevin Warsh|Warsh]] handles the Fed balance sheet.
- On next week's data: 1.5% real personal consumption is consensus for Q1 — in line is a shrug; a meaningful miss would reprice consumption damage from higher gasoline prices, particularly into the 5-7Y belly.
- On the FOMC presser: looking for greater clarity on the medium-term inflation impact of the [[Iran]] war. Energy prices "have arguably stabilized" and the market wants more than wait-and-see.
