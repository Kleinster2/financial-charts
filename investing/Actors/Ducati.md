# Ducati

Italian premium motorcycle manufacturer owned by [[Lamborghini]]/Audi/[[Volkswagen]] Group. Known for high-performance sportbikes and the Panigale, Monster, and Multistrada lines. Relevant to the investing vault as a [[QuantumScape]] technology validation partner — the Ducati V21L race bike debuted with QSE-5 solid-state cells at IAA Munich in September 2025, marking the world's first real-vehicle demonstration of QuantumScape's technology.

## Synopsis

Ducati sits within [[Volkswagen]]'s empire as a niche prestige brand, acquired by Audi in 2012 for EUR 860M as part of a broader luxury portfolio strategy (alongside [[Lamborghini]] and Bentley). Annual production runs ~60,000 units with revenue around EUR 1B, making it tiny relative to VW's EUR 300B+ group revenue but disproportionately valuable as a halo brand. The company dominates MotoGP with its Desmosedici GP bike and uses racing as its primary engineering and marketing platform.

The QuantumScape connection is what makes Ducati worth tracking. The V21L electric race bike, built for the MotoE World Championship, became the first production-adjacent vehicle to use [[QuantumScape]] QSE-5 solid-state battery cells. This matters because solid-state batteries have been perennially "five years away" — Ducati putting them in a race bike that actually runs at competition speeds validates the technology in ways that lab results cannot. For QuantumScape investors, Ducati is a credibility marker. The partnership also reflects VW Group's broader solid-state battery strategy, as VW was QuantumScape's original automotive backer ($300M invested pre-IPO).
