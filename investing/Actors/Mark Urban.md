# Mark Urban

British defence and diplomatic journalist, former Diplomatic Editor of [[BBC]] Newsnight, author of more than a dozen books on military history, intelligence, and modern warfare. Writes for the Sunday Times and publishes the War and Peace [[Substack]], launched in 2025 with 4,000+ subscribers. Trustee at the Imperial War Museum.

Background combines four decades of frontline reporting with archival military history. Covers defence, diplomacy, intelligence, counter-terrorism, and geopolitics, with occasional posts on media coverage of war. Relevant to the investing vault for reading on defense-industrial capacity, intelligence and escalation dynamics, and the military side of geopolitical risks that move oil, defense equities, and sovereign risk pricing.

## Iran war commentary (2026)

### "The New Iran War" (early April)

Urban's framing piece on the US-Israel strike campaign against Iran. Core argument: legal gold standard (UN Chapter VII authorization) has applied to only three wars in history, so the debate should center on whether the intervention serves legitimate purposes rather than on formal legal authorization. Documents Iran's regional role — Assad's Syria (600,000 deaths), Houthi sponsorship in Yemen, militia network in Iraq — as the moral case. Notes the coalition flew 1,500 sorties in twelve hours with fewer aircraft than the 1991 Iraq campaign, evidence of the tanker/basing advantage. Key concern raised: decapitation of Iran's leadership may prolong rather than shorten conflict since regime survival instincts lock in resistance.

*Source: [The New Iran War](https://markurban.substack.com/p/the-new-iran-war), Mark Urban on War and Peace*

### "Reflecting on Iran as the US blockade comes into force" (Apr 13)

Recorded live video discussion with Nick Cohen on Apr 13, 2026 — the day the US naval blockade of Iranian ports came into force after weekend peace talks failed. Video-only content, no transcript accessible. Topic confirmed: reflection on the transition from kinetic conflict to economic blockade and what it signals about the campaign's endgame.

The Apr 13 timing is significant — Urban is publishing on the blockade day, aligned with [[Helima Croft]]'s same-day "prepare for further oil disruptions" warning and one day before [[Robin J. Brooks]]' Apr 14 "Catalyst for oil prices to fall" piece. The defense-journalism read versus the commodity-analyst read are converging on the same inflection point: the blockade is either the end-phase pressure tool or the escalation trigger.
