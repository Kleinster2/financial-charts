# Link REIT

Asia's largest REIT by market cap. Owns and manages retail, office, and car park properties across [[Hong Kong]], mainland [[China]], [[Australia]], [[Singapore]], and the [[United Kingdom]]. [[EWH]] holding (~3.1%). Originated from the privatization of Hong Kong public housing estate retail facilities in 2005, giving it a uniquely defensive portfolio anchored in non-discretionary neighborhood retail.

## Synopsis

Link REIT listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in 2005 as part of the Housing Authority's divestiture of retail and car park assets within public housing estates — essentially, the shops, wet markets, and parking lots that serve Hong Kong's 2M+ public housing residents. This origin gave Link a portfolio of assets with captive foot traffic and limited competition, since residents of public housing estates shop at their estate's retail podium by default. The initial portfolio of 180 properties has since been optimized through asset enhancement (renovating tired government facilities into modern retail) and geographic diversification into mainland China, Australia, Singapore, and the UK. Market cap runs ~HK$120B (~$15B), with total assets under management exceeding HK$250B.

The investment case turns on whether Link can sustain rental growth as Hong Kong's retail market evolves post-pandemic and as the company pushes into new geographies where it lacks the structural advantages of its home market. Hong Kong still accounts for ~75% of revenue, with the remainder split across China (Grade A office in Shanghai and Shenzhen), Australia, and Singapore. Distribution per unit has grown consistently, yielding ~5-6% currently. The risk is dual: Hong Kong's structural challenges (population outflow, mainland China integration uncertainty) pressure the core portfolio, while international expansion into competitive REIT markets dilutes the captive-audience moat that made Link special in the first place.
