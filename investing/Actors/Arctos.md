**Arctos** — Sports and entertainment private equity firm. ~$15B AUM. Being acquired by [[KKR]] for $1.4B.

---

## Why it matters

Arctos is the largest institutional investor in professional sports franchises — first platform approved to invest in multiple franchises across MLB, NBA, NHL, MLS, and global leagues.

---

## KKR Acquisition (Feb 2026)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Deal value | $1.4B initial |
| Additional consideration | Up to $550M (tied to KKR share price + performance) |
| AUM acquired | ~$15B |
| New vertical | "KKR Solutions" |
| Vesting | Through 2033 |

Creates KKR's platform for sports, GP solutions, and secondaries.

The deal sits inside the broader 2024-2026 elite-firm [[Private equity secondaries|secondaries consolidation]] sequence — alongside [[EQT]]'s parallel $3.7bn acquisition of [[Coller Capital]] (Apr 2026), and earlier moves by [[Ares Management]] (Landmark Partners 2021), [[CVC Capital Partners|CVC]] (Glendower Capital 2022), [[Apollo]] (Apollo S3 organic build), [[Carlyle]] ([[AlpInvest]] 2011), and [[Blackstone]] (Strategic Partners 2013). Across the cohort, the strategic motivation is the same: secondaries deploy capital faster than primary buyouts and plug operationally into wealth-channel [[Evergreen private capital vehicles|evergreen vehicles]]. The sports-franchise sleeve is incidental — KKR was buying secondaries capability bundled with Arctos's sports differentiation, not the other way around.
