**AEL Sistemas** — Porto Alegre-based Brazilian defense electronics specialist, wholly owned subsidiary of Israel's [[Elbit Systems]]. Originally founded as Aeroeletronica; acquired in full by Elbit in 2001. Principal Brazilian importer and integrator for Elbit's UAV product family (Hermes 450, Hermes 900), avionics, electronic warfare, and command-and-control systems. The most strategically embedded Israeli defense entity in Latin America and a central node in Brazilian Armed Forces ISR (intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance) capability.

---

## Synopsis

AEL Sistemas is the cleanest Israeli-side counterpart to [[Helibras]] in the Brazilian defense industrial base: a fully foreign-owned Brazilian subsidiary, embedded for decades, satisfying Lei 12.598 EED requirements while preserving foreign parent control. The 2001 Elbit acquisition of Aeroeletronica predated the [[Brazilian defense industrial base|END / Lei 12.598 framework]] but the AEL platform proved well-suited to it — the company has been the primary in-country integrator for major Brazilian Air Force (FAB) drone and avionics programs since the early 2010s.

The flagship achievement is the introduction of the [[Hermes 450]] UAV into FAB service via a 2011 joint venture with [[Embraer]] (Embraer-majority structure), followed by Brazilian operational deployment in 2014. AEL also imports [[Hermes 900]] for FAB and supports the Brazilian Army's Israeli-origin radar and EW systems. Adjacent sister entity Ares Aeroespacial (also Elbit-owned) handles complementary product lines. The combined Elbit-Brazil presence (AEL + Ares) has been a recurring source of public controversy given Israel's geopolitical exposure, but the EED-designated status and embedded employment have kept the relationship stable across [[Lula]] and [[Bolsonaro]] administrations.
