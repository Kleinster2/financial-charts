# Michael Bernstein

Stanford Computer Science professor (Bass University Fellow) and co-founder of [[Simile]], an AI company focused on human-computer interaction. Bernstein has eight best paper awards across CHI, CSCW, and UIST — the top conferences in human-computer interaction and social computing — and over 100,000 citations. PhD from MIT. On leave from [[Stanford]] until Fall 2026 to work on Simile full-time with co-founder [[Percy Liang]] (also Stanford CS faculty). His research spans crowdsourcing systems, social computing, and AI-augmented interaction — the "Generative Agents" paper (2023), which simulated a town of 25 AI agents with emergent social behavior, became one of the most cited AI papers of that year and directly influenced the AI agent paradigm that companies like [[Manus AI]] and [[Anthropic]] are building on.
