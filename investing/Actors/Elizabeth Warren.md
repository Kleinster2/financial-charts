# Elizabeth Warren

**Elizabeth Warren** is a US Senator from [[Massachusetts]] (D), serving since 2013. She is the ranking member of the Senate Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs Committee, making her one of the most influential voices on financial regulation, consumer protection, and corporate accountability in Congress. Before entering politics, Warren was a Harvard Law School professor specializing in bankruptcy law and a leading academic critic of predatory lending practices.

Warren was the driving force behind the creation of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) after the 2008 financial crisis, though she was blocked from leading it and ran for Senate instead. She ran for the Democratic presidential nomination in 2020, finishing third in delegates. Her policy platform has consistently focused on wealth taxes, Big Tech antitrust, bank regulation, and corporate governance reform.

In the vault context, Warren is relevant for her investigative work on conflicts of interest at the intersection of government and finance. She co-signed the August 2025 letter with [[Ron Wyden]] to [[Brandon Lutnick]] probing [[Cantor Fitzgerald]]'s potential conflicts of interest in the [[Tariff refund claims market]], and has been a persistent critic of the revolving door between Wall Street and government positions.
