Flightradar24 is the world's most popular real-time flight tracking platform, using a global network of 40,000+ ADS-B receivers to track aircraft positions worldwide. Founded in 2006 in Sweden, the platform serves both consumers (travel tracking) and professional users (airlines, airports, media, government agencies). It became a critical open-source intelligence tool during geopolitical events — from the MH17 shootdown (2014) to the [[Operation Epic Fury|2026 Iran conflict]], where FR24 data on airspace closures and route diversions provided real-time visibility into the conflict's impact on commercial aviation.

The company is privately held and profitable, operating on a freemium model with premium subscriptions ($1.50-$50/month) and a commercial data API business. In 2022, a consortium including Permira bought a majority stake valuing the company at ~$750M. The ADS-B receiver network — crowdsourced from aviation enthusiasts who host small receivers — gives FR24 coverage that would cost billions to replicate through traditional infrastructure. Competitor [[Cirium]] (owned by [[RELX]]) focuses more on airline scheduling and analytics data, making the two companies complementary rather than directly competitive.
