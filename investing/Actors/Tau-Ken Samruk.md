Tau-Ken Samruk — Kazakhstan's state-owned national mining company, a subsidiary of the [[Samruk-Kazyna]] sovereign wealth fund. Joint-venture partner with [[Cove Kaz Capital]] (U.S.) on the Northern Katpar and Upper Kairakty tungsten deposits in central Kazakhstan, holding 30% of the operating entity Severniy Katpar LLP (Cove Kaz holds 70%). The JV is the principal vehicle for what its sponsors describe as the world's largest known undeveloped tungsten resource and the keystone of a U.S.-Kazakhstan critical-minerals supply-chain partnership.

## Vault relevance

Tau-Ken Samruk is the Kazakhstan state's principal counterparty in the Trump-administration-aligned U.S.-Kazakhstan tungsten initiative. The 30% stake in the Severniy Katpar JV gives the Kazakh state a meaningful equity participation while ceding majority control and operational responsibility to U.S. capital — a structure that matches the Kazakh sovereign-wealth fund's broader pattern of inviting Western JV partners into resource-development projects.

The JV's relevance to the [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]] (and therefore to the [[Trump family investment portfolio]]) is downstream: the U.S. publicly-listed vehicle for the JV's economics is the [[Skyline Builders]] / [[Cove Kaz Capital]] reverse merger (announced April 30, 2026, [[American Ventures]] Series XIX SKBL + Series XXXII SKBL II).
