# Lingotto

Lingotto — [[Exor]]-owned investment manager headquartered in London, launched in 2023 as the Agnelli family's external asset-management platform. The firm is wholly owned by Exor and runs multiple distinct investment strategies, including the growth-focused Innovation Strategy led by [[James Anderson]] and [[Morgan Samet]].

Lingotto matters because it gives [[Exor]] a way to turn permanent-family-capital discipline into a scalable third-party asset-management business. The Innovation Strategy is the vault-relevant piece: it spans venture through public markets, takes concentrated long-duration positions, and is now using the AI infrastructure cycle to argue that the profit pool is shifting away from platform buyers toward hardware bottlenecks such as [[NVIDIA]], [[TSMC]], and [[ASML]].

## Evolution

- 2022-2023: [[Exor]] reorganized investment activities previously associated with PartnerRe under the Lingotto name and appointed Enrico Vellano CEO. The logic was to give the Agnelli platform a dedicated asset-management arm rather than leaving investment activity embedded inside the holding-company balance sheet.
- 2024-2025: Lingotto broadened into four strategies and passed the $10B AUM mark at the firm level, according to Exor's 2025 reporting. The Innovation Strategy, led by [[James Anderson]] and [[Morgan Samet]], became the vault-relevant strategy because it spans venture through public markets and concentrates on exponential-growth companies.
- 2026: The May FT article turned Lingotto Innovation into a live source for the AI capex thesis. Anderson and Samet argued that the AI buildout changes Big Tech platform economics and shifts marginal pricing power toward hardware suppliers.

## Innovation strategy

Lingotto's Innovation Strategy is designed for long-duration, concentrated exposure to companies with exponential growth potential. The official Lingotto materials describe it as a multi-stage growth strategy able to invest from venture through public markets, with aligned long-term LPs including Exor and Covea.

The May 21, 2026 FT article makes the strategy relevant to the AI capex map. Anderson and Samet framed the AI buildout as a structural break in platform economics: hyperscalers still need the infrastructure, but the old low-capex, high-cash-flow software/platform model is being replaced by a more capital-intensive contest. Their conclusion routes the profit pool toward hardware chokepoints rather than the buyers of capacity. In vault terms, Lingotto is articulating the same buyer-vs-supplier split already formalized in [[AI hyperscalers]] and [[AI capex chain basket]].

The portfolio disclosure in the article also matters: Lingotto Innovation retained a sizeable [[NVIDIA]] position and a small [[Tesla]] position, while Anderson said a future [[Anthropic]] IPO would be more culturally attractive to him than [[OpenAI]]. Treat those as disclosed positioning and preference, not a generalized ranking of the companies.
