## Connected Vehicle Rule casualty (Jun 2026)

In June 2026, U.S. reporting said [[Commerce Department]] / [[BIS]] authorities had not granted Polestar authorization under the [[Connected Vehicle Rule]]. The practical effect is forward-looking: existing U.S. inventory and service continue, but future model-year sales are blocked unless Polestar can satisfy the rule or restructure the relevant software/control exposure.

This is the cleanest current test of whether a Western-facing brand can carry a [[China]]-linked EV stack into the U.S. market. [[Volvo Cars]] received authorization while Polestar did not, even though both are connected to [[Geely]], so the issue is not merely brand ownership. It is the regulator's view of the vehicle software, control, and data-risk perimeter.

See [[Polestar US connected-vehicle ban 2026]] for the event tape. PSNY fell 8.12% from the Jun 25 close to the Jun 26 close while [[QQQ]] fell 1.38%, leaving a model-adjusted abnormal move of about -7.55% in the local event-tape calculation.

## What to watch

- Authorization remedy - whether Polestar can redesign, segregate, or document its software/control stack enough to regain U.S. access.
- Europe focus - whether the company offsets lost U.S. optionality by leaning harder into [[Europe]], its largest growth market.
- Volvo contrast - whether [[Volvo Cars]]' approval terms reveal a template for China-linked brands.
- Geely pathway - whether the decision forces [[Geely]]'s U.S. strategy away from Polestar and toward authorized structures.
