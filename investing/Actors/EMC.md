#actor #storage #historical

**EMC Corporation** — Enterprise storage pioneer. Founded 1979. Acquired [[VMware]] (2004). [[Dell]] acquired EMC for $67B (2016) — largest tech deal ever.

Started with memory boards for minicomputers (1981). Pivoted to storage in late 1980s.

---

## Why EMC mattered

Created enterprise storage market:

- **Symmetrix (1990)**: Flagship storage array, gold standard for enterprise IT
- **VMware acquisition (2004)**: $625M — created virtualization empire
- **Data Domain (2009)**: $2.1B — deduplication leadership
- **Scale**: $24B+ revenue, dominant in enterprise storage

## Dell acquisition (2016)

**Largest tech acquisition ever** (at time):

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Announced | Oct 12, 2015 |
| Closed | Sep 7, 2016 |
| Price | $67B ($24.05/share cash + VMware tracking stock) |
| Debt raised | ~$70B |
| Assets gained | EMC storage, 81% of VMware, RSA, Data Domain |

Transformed Dell from PC company to enterprise IT leader.

---

## Post-acquisition

- **Dell EMC brand**: Used 2016-2020, then consolidated to Dell
- **VMware**: Remained separate subsidiary until 2021 spin-off
- **RSA**: Sold to Symphony Technology Group (2020) for $2.1B
