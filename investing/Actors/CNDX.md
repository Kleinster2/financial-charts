## What It Tracks

The Nasdaq-100 — the 100 largest non-financial Nasdaq names, the same index as [[QQQ]]. The difference is the wrapper: Irish UCITS, USD-denominated, and accumulating (dividends are reinvested inside the fund rather than distributed).

## Use Case

The structure, not the exposure, is the point. For non-US investors — Brazilians especially — an Irish-domiciled accumulating ETF reduces the dividend-tax drag and US estate-tax exposure that come with holding US-domiciled funds like [[QQQ]] directly. The trade-off Corano flags: UCITS menus capture the broad index but miss the individual US high-flyers a direct US account can hold. Same Nasdaq-100 return profile as QQQ before tax/structure effects.
