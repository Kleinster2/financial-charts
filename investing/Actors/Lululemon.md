# Lululemon

**Premium athletic lifestyle brand** — first to cross $10B revenue (FY24: $10.6B, +10%). Athleisure pioneer with yoga/running heritage. International expansion is the growth story (China +22%, 130+ stores, $1B revenue). But US growth stalling (+2% Q1 2025), stock lost ~50% in 2025, CEO Calvin McDonald departing Jan 2026. Valuation compressed from 40x to ~14x forward earnings.

## The two-speed story

### US (70%+ of revenue) — stalling
| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Comp sales | -1% (Q1 FY25) |
| Competition | Alo Yoga, Vuori, Nike women's |
| Market saturation | Core customer penetrated |
| Innovation gaps | Fewer "must-have" new products |

### International (20% of revenue) — booming
| Market | Growth | Stores |
|--------|--------|--------|
| China | +22% (Q1 FY25) | 130+ (from 10 in 2018) |
| [[Europe]] | Double-digit | Expanding |
| APAC | Strong | New markets |
| New entries | Italy, Denmark, Belgium, Turkey, Czech [[Republic]], [[India]] (2026) |

The bull thesis: Only 20% international today. If Lululemon reaches Nike/Adidas-level international mix (50%+), revenue doubles from here.

## Investment case

Bull:
- International runway massive (only 20% today)
- China $1B and growing +20%+
- 59% gross margins, 24% operating margins
- 14x earnings = historically cheap
- Brand loyalty extremely high
- [[India]] entry 2026 = next China-like opportunity

Bear:
- US stalling (-1% comps), 70% of revenue
- Growth deceleration (10% → 5-7% → ?)
- CEO departure adds uncertainty
- $240M tariff headwind
- Competition intensifying (Alo, Vuori)
- Athleisure as category may be maturing
