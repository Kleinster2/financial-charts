# Brock Pierce

Early crypto entrepreneur, investor, and one of the more colorful figures in digital asset history. Co-founder of [[Blockchain Capital]], [[Tether]], and the [[EOS]] Alliance. Former child actor turned digital currency evangelist.

---

## Synopsis

Pierce's career arc traces the weird genealogy of crypto's early days. He went from child acting (The Mighty Ducks) to founding Internet Gaming Entertainment (IGE), a virtual currency exchange for MMO games, to becoming one of Bitcoin's earliest institutional advocates. He co-founded [[Blockchain Capital]] in 2013, one of the first dedicated crypto VC funds, and was involved in the creation of [[Tether]] (USDT) — now the most traded stablecoin by volume and a systemic pillar of crypto market plumbing. His involvement with the [[EOS]] $4.1B ICO (2017-2018) remains one of the largest token raises ever.

The investment relevance is indirect but persistent. Pierce sits at the nexus of crypto's institutional and retail worlds, and his early introductions shaped key relationships — he reportedly introduced Jeffrey Epstein as an investor to [[Coinbase]] in 2014, a connection that surfaced during later controversies. He ran a quixotic independent presidential campaign in 2020. Pierce's significance is less as a current market mover and more as a node in crypto's founding network: [[Blockchain Capital]], [[Tether]], [[EOS]], and [[Coinbase]] all trace back through him, making him a recurring character whenever the origins of major crypto institutions are examined.
