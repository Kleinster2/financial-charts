**Piaget** is a Swiss luxury watchmaker and jeweler, founded in 1874 in La Cote-aux-Fees. Owned by [[Richemont]], Piaget is known for pioneering ultra-thin movements since 1957 and for high jewelry watches that blur the line between horology and haute joaillerie.

## Synopsis

Piaget sits within [[Richemont]]'s Specialist Watchmakers division alongside [[Jaeger-LeCoultre]], [[Vacheron Constantin]], and [[A. Lange & Sohne]], but occupies a distinct niche: the intersection of ultra-thin mechanical watchmaking and high jewelry. The brand holds multiple records for the thinnest mechanical movements ever produced, a technical specialization that differentiates it from jewelry-first competitors. This dual identity — serious manufacture credentials combined with gem-setting artistry — positions Piaget at a price tier above fashion watches but below the ultra-complicated offerings of [[Patek Philippe]] or [[Audemars Piguet]].

Within the [[Richemont]] portfolio, Piaget is a smaller contributor than [[Cartier]] or [[Van Cleef & Arpels]] but represents an important capability. The brand operates both a movement manufacture in La Cote-aux-Fees (one of the few houses making movements and cases in-house) and a jewelry/dial atelier in Geneva's Plan-les-Ouates. Key collections include the Altiplano (ultra-thin dress watch, the brand's signature), Polo (sports-luxury), and Limelight (high jewelry). Revenue is not disclosed separately but is estimated in the EUR 500M-800M range, with Asia-Pacific and the Middle East as the strongest growth markets.
