## Biden-era clean-energy implementation

Under [[Joe Biden]], DOE became one of the central implementation agencies for the [[Inflation Reduction Act]] and IIJA clean-energy buildout. The May 2026 Volts interview with Betony Jones, who served as director of the Office of Energy Jobs and senior advisor for labor to Secretary Jennifer Granholm, frames DOE's role as more than grant administration: the department used community benefits plans, prevailing-wage/apprenticeship incentives, and labor/community agreements to make workforce supply and local acceptance part of project design.

The Roosevelt Institute's April 2026 "The Receipts" report says DOE tied 74% of major IIJA/IRA clean-energy project funding, or $91B across 110 major projects, to formal labor or community agreements. The key implementation lesson is administrative capacity: recipients interviewed by Jones and Joe Peck did not primarily identify labor/community standards as the source of delay; they pointed to permitting, supply-chain constraints, learning curves, and government contracting timelines. That puts DOE's 2021-2024 execution record in the [[Industrial policy]] note's state-capacity bucket: the standards can be useful, but only if the agency can make compliance legible and fast enough for private project sponsors.

*Sources: [Volts](https://www.volts.wtf/p/a-limited-defense-of-bidens-everything), May 27 2026; [Roosevelt Institute, "The Receipts"](https://rooseveltinstitute.org/publications/the-receipts-the-untold-and-underappreciated-outcomes-of-bidens-clean-energy-strategy/), Apr 16 2026.*
