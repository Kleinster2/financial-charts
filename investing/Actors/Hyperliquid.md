# Hyperliquid

Hyperliquid is a high-performance Layer-1 blockchain and decentralized perpetual futures exchange, co-founded by former [[Hudson River Trading]] trader [[Jeff Yan]] in response to the [[FTX]] collapse in 2022. It has quietly become a major venue for leveraged digital-asset trading — and, as of March 2026, the first decentralized platform to host an officially licensed [[S&P 500]] derivative.

---

## Synopsis

Hyperliquid occupies a unique position in crypto infrastructure: a fully on-chain orderbook exchange that handles institutional-grade volume without venture capital backing. Yan built it as a direct response to FTX's implosion, and the no-VC posture is a point of pride in an industry scarred by the blowups that followed the last cycle's easy money. The platform's HIP-3 standard allows third-party builders — most notably [[trade.xyz]] — to deploy custom perpetual markets tracking assets beyond crypto, including commodities and equities. An upgrade in 2025 enabled this expansion, and builders quickly created contracts tracking oil, gold, silver, and equity indices.

The [[S&P Global]] licensing deal (Mar 2026) is the legitimacy inflection. [[S&P Dow Jones Indices]] — which has for decades licensed the world's most followed equity benchmark only to stalwarts of regulated finance — granted [[trade.xyz]] the right to offer the first officially licensed [[S&P 500]] perpetual on Hyperliquid. The contract uses official S&P data during US trading hours, institutional data provider quotes during extended sessions, and a trade.xyz-designed internal model when all traditional markets are closed. For Hyperliquid, the value is unambiguous: the world's preeminent index provider has conferred institutional legitimacy that trading volume alone could not deliver.

## How it works

Hyperliquid runs a fully on-chain orderbook — not an AMM — meaning it operates more like a traditional exchange than most DeFi platforms. The HIP-3 standard enables third-party developers to launch custom perpetual contracts on the platform's infrastructure. Perpetuals are settled in stablecoins (primarily [[USDC]] from [[Circle]]).

A 2025 upgrade expanded beyond crypto, allowing builders to deploy contracts tracking traditional assets (commodities, equity indices). This created a bridge between crypto market infrastructure and traditional finance instruments.

## S&P 500 perpetual (Mar 2026)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Announced | March 18, 2026 |
| Licensed by | [[S&P Dow Jones Indices]] |
| Built by | [[trade.xyz]] |
| Contract type | [[Perpetuals|Perpetual]] (never expires) |
| Eligible users | Non-US investors |
| Settlement | Stablecoins |
| Data source (market hours) | Official S&P index data |
| Data source (extended hours) | Institutional data providers |
| Data source (all markets closed) | trade.xyz internal model |

This is the first officially licensed S&P 500 derivative on a decentralized platform. Existing S&P 500 perpetuals on Hyperliquid (launched by other developers) relied on third-party price feeds. The distinction matters more to institutional participants than retail speculators.

A built-in funding rate mechanism charges traders a fee when their positions push the contract price too far from the actual index, keeping the perpetual tethered to the benchmark.

Cameron Drinkwater, chief product and operations officer at S&P Dow Jones Indices: *"We believe digitally-native investors should demand the institutional-quality standards that define our indices"* (Mar 18, 2026).

S&P's standard disclaimer notes it does not sponsor the products built on its licenses or vouch for their suitability.

## Iran conflict weekend trading

During the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran conflict]] ([[Iran]] crisis, Mar 2026), Hyperliquid perpetuals — particularly oil, gold, and silver contracts built by [[trade.xyz]] — served as one of the few open windows into how traders were pricing the conflict when traditional markets were closed during weekends. The oil tracking contract accumulated ~$500M in daily trading volume. This is exactly the 24/7 access value proposition that the [[S&P 500]] perpetual extends to equity index exposure.

## Founding context

[[Jeff Yan]] built Hyperliquid in direct response to the [[FTX]] collapse (November 2022). Unlike most crypto ventures of its generation, it took no venture capital funding. In an industry where easy VC money fueled the leverage and fraud that caused the 2022 blowups, the self-funded model was both philosophical and practical — no outside investors meant no misaligned incentives.
