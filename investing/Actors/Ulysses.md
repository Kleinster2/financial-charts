#actor #defense-tech #autonomous #maritime #private

**Ulysses** — San Francisco-based maritime robotics company building small, low-cost autonomous underwater and surface vehicles (UUVs/USVs). [[Andreessen Horowitz]]'s American Dynamism fund led the company's $38M Series A in April 2026; total new funding ~$46M including an $8M seed round led by Pebblebed and follow-on participation from [[Booz Allen Hamilton|Booz Allen Ventures]], Harpoon, Genius Ventures, Lowercarbon Capital, ReGen Ventures, and Superorganism. Founded by Akhil, Will, Colm, and Jamie, the team came out of an Irish drone-building background.

## Why Ulysses matters

The maritime domain has seen "remarkably little innovation" over the last half-century despite rising strategic importance — that is the framing [[Erin Price-Wright]] used on Bloomberg Tech (Apr 24 2026) when explaining a16z's thesis. Manned submarines cost multiple billions, take a decade to build, and require ~130-person crews; many real-world tasks (seafloor inspection, oil and gas inspection, deep-sea mining surveys, port inspection, undersea cable monitoring, defense reconnaissance) do not require a manned crew at all.

Ulysses' approach is to build commercial use cases first, then defense:

| Phase | Use cases | Why it matters |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Commercial-first | Seagrass planting, seafloor restoration, oil and gas inspection, port inspection | Customers cannot pay more than ~$40-60K per unit, forcing cost-curve discipline |
| Field operations | Real ocean missions, not lab demos | Builds operational maturity before defense buyers ask hard questions |
| Defense expansion | UUV/USV missions for [[Department of War]] | Same cost-disciplined product, now serving asymmetric maritime missions |

The cost gap is the punchline: Price-Wright described UUVs at $40-60K versus manned submarines at multi-billions per unit, framing the price differential as "two or three orders of magnitude" for "virtually the same thing" in many use cases. Her argument is that the discipline of building for $40K commercial customers is what eventually displaces incumbents like [[Lockheed Martin]] in the maritime portion of the defense supply chain.
