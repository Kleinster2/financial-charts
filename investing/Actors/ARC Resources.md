## Asset base

| Region | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| [[Montney]] (NE BC + N Alberta) | Core position; pure-play scale |
| Cardium (Pembina, Alberta) | Conventional oil exposure |

The Montney shale is the structural driver. Liquids share (condensate + NGLs) is the variable that makes ARC's economics differ from a pure dry-gas producer — condensate prices track oil, not Henry Hub, so realized barrel pricing benefits from oil-linked cuts of the production stream.

Canada's largest condensate producer matters specifically because condensate is the diluent for [[oil sands]] bitumen blending — ARC sits structurally upstream of the [[Canadian Natural Resources|CNRL]] / [[Suncor]] / [[Cenovus]] heavy-oil complex.

## Apr 27, 2026 — Shell acquisition

[[Shell]] agreed to acquire ARC for ~US$16.4B enterprise value.

### Terms

- Equity value: ~US$13.6B
- Assumption of ~US$2.8B net debt + leases
- Per-share consideration: CAD 8.20 cash + 0.40247 [[Shell]] ordinary shares for each ARC share
- Mix: ~25% cash / ~75% stock

### Strategic rationale (Shell)

- Adds 370,000 boe/d immediately; lifts Shell's production CAGR from 1% to 4% through 2030 vs 2025 baseline
- ~US$250M annualised synergies expected within a year of closing
- Diversifies upstream away from Middle East exposure (Hewson framing, [[AJ Bell]])

### Process

- Subject to ARC shareholder, court, and regulatory approvals
- Expected to close in H2 2026

ARC stock surged ~21% on the announcement.

*Sources: CNBC Apr 27 2026; BOE Report Apr 27 2026; GlobeNewswire Shell announcement Apr 27 2026; Capital Brief; Investing.com.*
