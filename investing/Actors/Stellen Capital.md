## Analytical approach

Stellen's edge is in physical market flows — tracking which ships are going where, what cargoes are loading, and what the shipping rate curve implies about supply/demand balance. During the March 2026 [[Strait of Hormuz]] crisis, Wiggen provided the most granular public assessment of practical pipeline throughput ([[East-West Pipeline]] at 2.5-3M bpd sustainable vs 7M announced), physical crude pricing ([[Dubai crude]] at $166/bbl), Asian refiner trade flow diversions ([[Japan]]/[[Thailand]]/[[South Korea]] redirecting to USGC), and the commercial trust problem (go-quiet-then-strike strategy making insurance permanent).

Their analytical framework starts with shipping signals (fleet movements, charter rates, insurance pricing), then assesses commodity shortage/surplus dynamics globally, then constructs trades across the full chain — not just crude or LNG, but the shipping leg, the product spread, and the logistics backup.
