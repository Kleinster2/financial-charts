# REX Financial

Miami-based exchange-traded product (ETP) issuer specializing in alternative-strategy ETFs and ETNs — leveraged/inverse single-stock products (T-REX), options-based covered call income funds (FEPI, AIPI), MicroSectors ETNs (with [[BMO]]), and spot crypto ETFs (REX-Osprey). Surpassed $5B in combined AUM by mid-2024, with MicroSectors ETNs alone accounting for ~$11.8B. The firm's 60+ products target retail traders and income-seekers with high-octane exposure tools that legacy issuers like [[ProShares]] and [[Direxion]] don't offer — specifically 2x/–2x daily single-stock leverage, which REX pioneered in the US. The bull case: first-mover in the fastest-growing ETF niches (single-stock leverage + covered call income), with a prolific launch cadence and Tuttle Capital partnership keeping product costs low. The bear case: concentrated in retail-facing, high-expense-ratio products (avg ~1.34%) that could face regulatory scrutiny, and the covered call income funds distribute primarily return of capital — not true income.

2x leveraged and –2x inverse daily-target ETFs on 40+ single stocks and crypto tokens. First suite of its kind in the US — launched Oct 2023, gathered $1B+ AUM within 8 months.

**March 2026 filings:** REX and Tuttle filed for 2x leveraged ETFs tied to SpaceX and Anthropic common stock — ahead of anticipated IPOs. The filings signal how aggressively the firm stakes claims on yet-to-exist tickers to beat competitors to market.

### Covered call income ETFs

Options-based income strategies writing covered calls on concentrated tech/AI baskets. Weekly and monthly distribution schedules targeting high yields.

| Ticker | Name | AUM | Distribution Rate | Frequency |
|--------|------|-----|-------------------|-----------|
| FEPI | REX FANG & Innovation Equity Premium Income | ~$588M | ~25% | Weekly |
| AIPI | REX AI Equity Premium Income | — | ~34% | Monthly |
| CEPI | REX Crypto Equity Premium Income | — | — | — |

**Critical caveat:** FEPI, AIPI, and CEPI distributions consist of 100% estimated return of capital (ROC) — not investment income or capital gains. The headline distribution rates are mechanically high but mask NAV erosion.

**International expansion:** FEPI launched as a UCITS ETF via HANetF in Jan 2026 — REX's first European product.

## Competitive positioning

REX occupies a specific niche: the **aggressive retail ETP provider** offering products legacy issuers won't or can't launch fast enough.

| Competitor | Overlap | REX advantage |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| [[ProShares]] | Leveraged/inverse | ProShares does indexes, REX does single stocks (2x) |
| [[Direxion]] | Leveraged | Direxion does 3x sector ETFs, REX does single-stock 2x |
| [[GraniteShares]] | Single-stock leverage | GraniteShares in Europe; REX pioneered in US |
| YieldMax (Toroso/Tidal) | Covered call income | FEPI competes directly with YMAX/TSLY/NVDY |
| [[Tuttle Capital Management]] | T-REX co-manager | Partner, not competitor — Tuttle provides regulatory infrastructure |

**Moat:** Speed to market. REX files for products before underlying stocks even IPO (SpaceX, Anthropic). The firm treats SEC filings as territorial claims.

## Business model

REX is an **asset-light ETP sponsor** — it designs products and manages them but relies on partners for:
- **Distribution:** Foreside Fund Services (FINRA member)
- **ETN issuance:** BMO (MicroSectors)
- **Sub-advisory / co-management:** Tuttle Capital Management (T-REX)
- **Crypto custody/expertise:** Osprey Funds (REX-Osprey)

Revenue comes from management fees (expense ratios averaging ~1.34% for MicroSectors, 0.65% for FEPI). At $5B+ ETF AUM and ~$11.8B MicroSectors AUM, implied fee revenue is substantial — though REX shares fees with partners.

## Insights

- REX is essentially an **ETF factory** — it doesn't manage money in the traditional sense; it manufactures wrappers around exposure and sells access at premium expense ratios
- The covered call income products (FEPI, AIPI) are yield traps for unsophisticated retail — 100% ROC distributions mean investors are getting their own capital back, not income
- The pre-IPO ETF filings (SpaceX, Anthropic) represent a new frontier in competitive dynamics: first-filer advantage for yet-to-exist securities
- Greg King's career arc (Barclays → Credit Suisse ETP → Global X → REX) traces the evolution of the ETP industry from institutional to retail-focused alternative products
- The BMO MicroSectors partnership is the cash cow — FNGU alone at $6.9B with 2.60% expense ratio implies ~$179M/yr in gross fees (split between BMO and REX)
