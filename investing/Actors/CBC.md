**CBC** (Companhia Brasileira de Cartuchos) — Brazil's principal ammunition manufacturer and one of the largest small-arms and ammunition holding groups globally. Operates as CBC Global Ammunition, owning operations in Brazil (CBC), Germany (MEN), the Czech Republic (Sellier & Bellot), and the United States (Magtech). Controls 52.67% of [[Taurus Armas]] (Brazilian small-arms maker) since 2015 and holds a 27-28% stake in Colt CZ Group as its second-largest shareholder. The single most important Brazilian-domiciled defense industrial holding by international footprint.

---

## Synopsis

CBC is the most underappreciated entity in the Brazilian defense industrial base because it inverts the standard narrative — rather than receiving foreign capital, it deploys Brazilian capital across the European and US small-arms supply chain. Starting from an ammunition-manufacturing base in Brazil, CBC acquired the German military and law-enforcement ammunition maker MEN in 2007, the Czech ammunition specialist Sellier & Bellot in 2009, and 52.67% of [[Taurus Armas]] in 2015 (with CADE approval). The Colt CZ stake (acquired 2023 via the Czech Republic-incorporated holding structure) makes CBC the second-largest shareholder in one of the world's major small-arms platforms.

The structural significance is that the most visible "foreign-target" Brazilian small-arms firm — Taurus, which carries the iconography of Brazilian gun manufacturing — is in fact domestically controlled by CBC. This inverts the working assumption that the [[Brazilian defense industrial base]] is principally a foreign-capital-receiving system: in small arms and ammunition specifically, Brazilian capital is itself the consolidator. CBC's portfolio also gives Brazil a non-NATO route into European and US ammunition supply, which has strategic significance under conditions of European rearmament and ammunition-supply tightness following the 2022-2025 wars in Ukraine and the Middle East.

## Brazilian operations

CBC's Brazilian ammunition plant at Ribeirão Pires is one of the largest in Latin America. The company supplies the Brazilian Armed Forces and law-enforcement agencies, plus civilian retail, plus a substantial export business. The 2015 acquisition of [[Taurus Armas]] integrated Brazilian pistol and revolver production into the CBC platform; the combined entity is the largest single Brazilian source of small arms and ammunition, with substantial US market presence (Taurus has long operated a US subsidiary, Taurus Holdings, based in Bainbridge, GA).

---

## International strategy

The acquisitions of MEN (Germany), Sellier & Bellot (Czech Republic), and the Colt CZ stake position CBC as a substantial player in European small-arms consolidation. European rearmament since the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine has tightened ammunition supply and increased the value of established production capacity; CBC entered this cycle with European production already in hand. The Colt CZ stake gives CBC influence over one of the few Western small-arms firms with both military and civilian product lines and significant US distribution.

## Funding history / ownership

CBC is privately held by Brazilian family ownership. No public equity rounds disclosed; the company has financed its international acquisitions through cash flow, bank debt, and structured transactions. Detailed cap-table breakdown not publicly available.
