Strive Masiyiwa is a Zimbabwean billionaire entrepreneur, founder and executive chairman of [[Econet Group]], and one of Africa's most influential business figures. Built Econet Wireless into Africa's largest independent telecom group after a five-year legal battle (1993-1998) against the Mugabe government for a mobile license — a fight that went to the Zimbabwe Supreme Court and established precedent for private telecom operation across the continent. Also founded Liquid Intelligent Technologies, a pan-African fiber, satellite, and cloud infrastructure business spanning 15+ countries. Forbes estimated net worth ~$2.7B. Sits on the boards of [[Netflix]], Unilever, [[National Geographic]], and the Rockefeller Foundation. Through the Higherlife Foundation, has funded education for 250,000+ orphans across Africa. His business trajectory — fighting a dictator for a telecom license, then building pan-continental infrastructure — is a case study in emerging market entrepreneurship under hostile regulatory conditions.
