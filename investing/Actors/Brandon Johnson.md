# Brandon Johnson

Mayor of Chicago since May 2023. Former Cook County commissioner and [[Chicago Teachers Union]] organizer. Presides over one of the most acute municipal fiscal crises in the US, with a $35.9B unfunded pension liability (~25% funded ratio) and chronic annual budget shortfalls.

---

## Synopsis

Johnson won the 2023 mayoral race as the CTU-backed progressive candidate, defeating Paul Vallas in a runoff that split Chicago along familiar lines: public-sector unions and the South/West sides vs. downtown business interests and the lakefront. His administration has been defined by the pension math — Chicago's four pension funds are collectively ~25% funded, the worst ratio of any major US city, requiring escalating annual contributions that crowd out services. The FY2026 budget closed a $1.15B gap through ~$500M in new taxes and fees, but the structural deficit persists.

The market signal came in January 2026 when S&P revised Chicago's debt outlook to negative after the city split a pension payment due to a cash crunch — a move that raises borrowing costs and signals deteriorating creditworthiness. Johnson's approval rating cratered to 6.6% in February 2025 (recovering to 31% by October 2025), reflecting broad dissatisfaction across political lines. Chicago is the canary for the [[Blue city fiscal crisis]] thesis: progressive governance layered on top of legacy pension obligations and a shrinking tax base creates a fiscal doom loop where tax increases drive out the residents needed to pay them.
