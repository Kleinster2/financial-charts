# Gleacher Shacklock

Gleacher Shacklock is a London-based independent M&A advisory boutique founded in 2003 by [[Tim Shacklock]], formerly vice-chairman of Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein. The partnership has approximately 30 advisory professionals (50 total staff) and specialises in UK takeovers, particularly in aerospace and defence, financial services, utilities, and transportation infrastructure. Its mandate list includes some of Britain's most heavily contested M&A battles of the past two decades.

On 2026-04-13 [[Perella Weinberg Partners]] announced the acquisition of Gleacher Shacklock. Consideration is entirely share-based: 1,127,529 PWP shares at closing plus 2,255,058 shares in three annual installments, plus contingent consideration tied to client engagement fees. Per SEC filing, the at-closing stock component is valued at >$71M. Expected close H2 2026 subject to regulatory approval. [[Mayer Brown]] advised PWP; the target retains its London partnership base.

## Synopsis

- Founded 2003 by [[Tim Shacklock]] after he left Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein. The firm has never had a parent company, outside capital, or external shareholders — ownership is held entirely by working partners. This is the structural feature that makes it attractive as a standalone acquisition target: a clean cap table.
- Sector specialisation: UK aerospace and defence (e.g. [[GKN]] defence, [[BAE Systems]], [[Smiths Group]]), financial services, mining (Endeavour Mining), transport infrastructure (Stansted Airport sale to IFM, Manchester Airports Group).
- Notable mandates include the £10.4B GKN defence against [[Melrose Industries]] (lost in 2018, the largest hostile UK takeover since 2009), and advising Just Eat on the £10.5B contested combination with Takeaway.com (later acquired by [[Prosus]] in 2025).
- Strategic rationale for [[Perella Weinberg Partners]]: London is the largest M&A advisory market in Europe. PWP already has London presence but wanted a local brand with multi-decade UK plc relationships. The deal mirrors [[Evercore]]'s July 2025 acquisition of [[Robey Warshaw]] (£146M / $196M) — both deals tuck small London advisory shops into larger US-listed platforms. See [[Boutique advisory consolidation]].

## Why Gleacher matters

The Gleacher brand is a specific type of asset — a roster of UK corporate relationships at the FTSE 100 / FTSE 250 level, built up over two decades by a small group of partners. The value is in those relationships plus the reputation of being conflict-free (no integrated bank with trading positions or lending exposure). That package is exactly what PWP's growth strategy requires for EMEA: PWP has the balance-sheet currency from being public; Gleacher has the client roster that takes a decade to build organically.

The Melrose-GKN loss in 2018 actually reinforced Gleacher's reputation for tough UK defence work — the fact that it was the sell-side adviser on the most bitter UK contested takeover of the 2010s brought it more mandates, not fewer, as boards want an adviser that has lived through a genuine battle. This is the inverse of the "must win the deal" calculus that drives [[SPAC]] sponsors toward any merger; in advisory work, representing the losing side of a known fight is also a marketing asset.
