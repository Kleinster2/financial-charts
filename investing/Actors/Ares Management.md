**Ares Management** — [[Alternative asset manager]]. $596B AUM. NYSE: ARES. Founded 1997. Dominant in [[Private credit]].

---

## Why Ares matters

World's largest [[direct lender]]. As banks retreated from [[middle-market]] lending post-[[GFC]] ([[Basel III]] constraints, risk aversion), Ares filled the gap. [[Private credit]] grew from niche to $2T market; Ares commands ~12% global market share.

## Direct lending moat

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Global market share | ~12% |
| Dry powder | $150B (Q2 2025) |
| Q3 origination | $15.2B |
| Average LTV | 42% |
| Investment pros | 600+ |

Largest self-originating direct lender to US and European middle markets. Ares Capital Corp ([[ARCC]]) is largest US BDC by market cap.

**Competitive advantages:**
- Scale begets deal flow — better pricing, selection
- One-stop financing — can hold entire deals
- Speed and certainty — sponsors prefer reliability
- Low leverage — positioned to gain share in credit cycle

## Altice litigation

[[Altice]] (now Optimum Communications) sued Ares, [[Apollo]], and [[BlackRock]] in late 2025, alleging "illegal cartel" among lenders holding majority of its $26B debt. [[Patrick Drahi]] creditor management as telecom empire restructures.
