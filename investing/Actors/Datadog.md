## Q1 2026 earnings (May 7, 2026) — accelerating, not normalizing

Datadog reported Q1 2026 on May 7 before the open and the stock closed +31.3% to $188.72 from $143.71 — the largest single-day move in the company's public history and the biggest beat reaction in the [[SaaS]] cohort this earnings cycle. The setup matters: DDOG had been priced at a discount to peers heading in on the [[AI agent tokens|"AI customers might build in-house"]] thesis (the bear case in the section above), and the Q1 print broke that frame.

| Metric | Q1 2026 actual | Consensus | Beat |
|--------|---------------|-----------|------|
| Revenue | $1,006M (+32% YoY) | ~$967M | +4% |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $0.60 | $0.42 | +43% |
| GAAP EPS | $0.15 | — | — |
| Non-GAAP operating income | $223M (22% margin) | — | — |
| Operating cash flow | $335M | — | — |
| Free cash flow | $289M | — | — |
| Cash + securities | $4.8B | — | — |
| $100K+ ARR customers | ~4,550 (+21% YoY) | — | — |

[[Datadog]] guided FY 2026 revenue to $4.30-4.34B and Q2 to roughly +30% YoY. The 32% revenue growth at $1B run-rate is reaccelerating from the Q3 2025 +28% print — uncommon for a SaaS scaling through the $3-4B range — and the implied Q4 exit rate from the FY26 midpoint of $4.32B is roughly $1.13-1.14B (+30% YoY), consistent with a step-up in AI workload monetization rather than a one-quarter blip.

The bear-case test on AI-customer concentration ([[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], [[Cursor]], [[Scale AI]], [[Replit]]) is provisionally answered the other way: customers are still expanding usage rather than building in-house. Management commentary on the call (per [[Investing.com]] transcript) tied AI volume to the broader AI capex acceleration covered in [[AI compute demand curve]] and [[AI capex chain basket]] — DDOG is now the cleanest infrastructure-monitoring read on whether [[Anthropic]]'s and [[OpenAI]]'s capex (see [[OpenAI]] $50B 2026 compute plan + [[Anthropic]]-[[SpaceX]] May 6 deal) translates to durable workload monitoring spend.

### Sympathy moves — software-AI cohort repriced

The DDOG print catalyzed a software-AI rally that pulled in three vault peers without their own catalysts:

| Ticker | Move May 7 | Read |
|--------|-----------|------|
| [[MongoDB\|MDB]] | +14.3% (to $303.27) | Atlas database for AI-native workloads; sympathy on usage-monetization read |
| [[Snowflake\|SNOW]] | +8.8% (to $152.10) | Data warehouse for analytics; sympathy on AI workload monetization. Snowflake reports Q1 FY27 May 27 — sympathy is positioning ahead of own print |
| [[Zscaler\|ZS]] | +9.4% (to $151.90) | Cybersecurity zero-trust; partial sympathy via [[Fortinet]] beat (same day; see [[Fortinet]]) but DDOG read still amplifies the SaaS bid |

The cleanest cross-name framing: DDOG's beat shifts the burden of proof in the SaaS-AI bear case (consolidation, in-housing, hyperscaler-bundle compression) from "presumed underway" back to "not yet visible." That has implications for [[AI SaaS Disruption]] and the [[SaaS stock meltdown 2026]] event note — the meltdown was earlier in the year, and Q1 2026 prints are starting to show the thesis was at least partially overstated for the AI-native names.
