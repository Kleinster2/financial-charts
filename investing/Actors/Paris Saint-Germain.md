# Paris Saint-Germain

French football club owned by Qatar Sports Investments (QSI), a subsidiary of [[QIA]] (Qatar Investment Authority), since 2011. Chaired by Nasser Al-Khelaifi, who also chairs [[beIN Media]] and serves on the UEFA executive committee. QSI's acquisition transformed PSG from a mid-tier Ligue 1 club into one of the world's richest sporting franchises, spending over €1.5B on transfers in a decade — including the €222M world-record signing of Neymar from [[Barcelona]] in 2017 and the free-agent acquisition of [[Lionel Messi]] in 2021. Despite domestic dominance (10+ Ligue 1 titles since 2011), PSG has never won the [[UEFA Champions League]], the strategic objective that justifies Qatar's investment as a soft-power vehicle. Post-Messi and post-[[Kylian Mbappé]] (departed for [[Real Madrid]] in 2024), PSG has pivoted toward a younger squad model with reduced wage spending.
