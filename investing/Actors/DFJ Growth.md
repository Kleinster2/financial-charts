# DFJ Growth

Growth-stage venture fund affiliated with the [[Draper Fisher Jurvetson]] network. Focused on later-stage investments in companies that have proven product-market fit and need capital to scale.

---

## Synopsis

DFJ Growth operates as a distinct vehicle from [[Draper Fisher Jurvetson]]'s early-stage funds, targeting Series B through pre-IPO rounds where companies have established revenue but need $50-200M+ to achieve market dominance. The fund benefits from deal flow through the broader DFJ network — Tim Draper's ecosystem of affiliated funds across multiple geographies (DFJ, DFJ Dragon in China, DFJ JAIC in Japan) creates a pipeline of companies that graduate from early-stage DFJ funds into DFJ Growth's sweet spot.

The marquee investment is [[SpaceX]] — DFJ Growth participated in the $1B Series G round in January 2015 alongside [[Google]], [[Fidelity]], [[Valor Equity Partners]], [[Founders Fund]], and [[Capricorn Investment Group]]. That round valued SpaceX at approximately $12B, meaning early participants captured enormous appreciation as SpaceX's valuation grew past $200B by 2025. The SpaceX position alone likely returned the fund multiple times over, illustrating the power-law dynamics of growth-stage venture: one position in the right rocketship (literal and figurative) can define an entire fund's track record. DFJ Growth's access to SpaceX came through Tim Draper's early-stage relationship with [[Elon Musk]], demonstrating how network effects in venture create compounding advantages across fund stages.
