# Hyundai

Korean automotive conglomerate and the world's #4 automaker by volume, increasingly valued as a robotics play via 88% ownership of [[Boston Dynamics]]. Traded sideways at 100K-300K KRW for a decade, then doubled after the Atlas humanoid debut at [[CES 2026]] (Jan 5). Now pulled back to 492K KRW (~27% off 674K peak). Forward PE ~9.6x.

## Synopsis

The stock story has completely changed. After a decade in the wilderness, [[Boston Dynamics]]' Atlas humanoid robot debut at CES 2026 sent Hyundai from 300K to 674K KRW in weeks. The rerating thesis is sum-of-parts: BNP Paribas calculates the core auto business trades at just 10.3x PE after stripping out stakes in [[Hyundai Motor India]] and [[Boston Dynamics]]. If a BD IPO (potentially 2027 Nasdaq) prices anywhere near analyst estimates — KB Securities at 128T won, Hanwha at 150T won, even conservative 40-60T won — the auto business is essentially free at current levels. Chairman [[Chung Euisun]] holds 22.6% of BD personally (paid ~$220M in 2021, now potentially worth $13.6B+), and IPO proceeds give him the war chest to unwind Hyundai's circular ownership structure — the only major Korean chaebol that still has it. Resolution of the Korea discount is a second catalyst. The bear case: 22% earnings cuts on US tariff risk (25% on Korean imports, deal to lower to 15% not yet ratified), Chinese EVs eating market share outside US/India, and $20B in US investment commitments that are capital-heavy political insurance.

## Boston Dynamics IPO catalyst

[[Boston Dynamics]] IPO is the single biggest near-term catalyst. Key facts:

- Hyundai affiliates (Motor, Mobis, Glovis) own ~68%, Chairman [[Chung Euisun]] personally owns 22.6%, [[SoftBank]] retains 9.5%
- [[SoftBank]] has a put option exercisable by June 2026 (one-year grace period after missed 2025 IPO commitment). Hyundai expected to acquire SoftBank's remaining stake, then initiate IPO process
- CEO Robert Playter departed Feb 2026, replaced by interim CEO/CFO Amanda McMaster — commercial pivot signal away from engineering-driven culture
- Dedicated robotics and AI strategy task force established under Vice Chair Chang Jae-hoon
- Atlas production target: 30,000 units by 2028 at $130-140K each (briefed to analysts at CES)
- [[Google DeepMind]] partnership for Atlas AI brain — previously the knock was Tesla Optimus had the data advantage. DeepMind collaboration changes the equation
- [[NVIDIA]] collaboration on autonomous driving

## Governance restructuring

BD IPO proceeds give [[Chung Euisun]] the financial foundation to unwind Hyundai's circular ownership — a decade-long investor complaint.

Three ownership loops exist within the group, all routing through [[Hyundai Mobis]] (de facto holding company, 22.4% stake in Hyundai Motor) → Hyundai Motor → [[Kia]] → back to Mobis. Two secondary loops run through [[Hyundai Glovis]] and [[Hyundai Steel]].

Chung's personal stake in Mobis is just 0.3%. His father (Honorary Chair Chung Mong-koo) owns 7.29% of Mobis — inheritance at 60% tax rate = ~4T+ won in tax alone. Chung needs to buy out Mobis shares held by Kia and Hyundai Steel to consolidate control.

BD IPO resolves this: $13.6B+ from Chung's 22.6% stake → covers inheritance tax + Mobis share purchases → governance restructuring → Korea discount unwind.

Yuanta Securities analyst Kim Yong-min: "Should Hyundai acquire SoftBank's remaining stake in June and initiate IPO process, a 2027 listing could well be the trigger for the restructuring."

## Tariff exposure

Analysts cut 2026 earnings estimates by 22% (2027 by 21%) on US tariff risk. Trump threatened to hike Korea tariffs from 15% to 25% after Korean National Assembly failed to ratify the trade deal into law.

Mitigation:
- $20B US investment commitment including $5.8B Louisiana steel plant — political insurance
- CEO Muñoz: "The best way for Hyundai to navigate tariffs is to increase localization"
- Metaplant Georgia (300K vehicle capacity) produces IONIQ 5, IONIQ 9 domestically
- Pulled IONIQ 6 from US market (all units imported from Korea, tariff-exposed)
- Pulled 2026 Kona Electric from US (low demand + tariff math)

Hyundai Motor President Sung Kim (Feb 24) urged Korean legislature to swiftly pass the $350B US investment package legislation, warning Trump admin may escalate tariff pressure despite Supreme Court legal setback.

## Investment case

Bull:
- Sum-of-parts: core auto at 10.3x PE after stripping BD + India → auto business nearly free if BD IPO prices high
- BD IPO catalyst (2027) — valuation estimates 40-150T KRW
- Governance restructuring → Korea discount unwind
- [[Google DeepMind]] + [[NVIDIA]] partnerships validate robotics/AV story
- Atlas 30K units by 2028 at $130-140K = potential $4B+ revenue stream
- KRW weakness = export tailwind
- Record US sales, hybrid leadership
- Solid-state battery optionality

Bear:
- 22% earnings cut on US tariff risk (25% rate, deal not ratified)
- Chinese EVs taking share outside US/India
- BD not yet commercially proven at scale — valuation is forward-looking
- $20B US investment = capital-heavy political insurance
- Circular ownership unresolved (though IPO may fix this)
- Immigration raid at Georgia plant (2025) — political/labor risk
