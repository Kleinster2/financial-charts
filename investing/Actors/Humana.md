## Jan 27, 2026: Medicare rate shock

**Stock fell ~21%** — from ~$265 to ~$210 — worst performer among managed care insurers.

Part of sector-wide [[Medicare Advantage]] rate shock — see concept note for full policy context.

### Why Humana hit hardest

**Pure-play exposure:** ~85% of revenue from Medicare Advantage (vs ~30% for UNH). Maximum sensitivity to CMS rates.

| Issue | Humana impact |
|-------|---------------|
| Flat 2027 rates | ~85% of business affected |
| Chart review rule change | Heavy reliance on risk adjustment |
| Medical cost inflation | Margins already compressed |
| Star ratings pressure | Quality bonuses at risk |

## Investment case

**Bear:**
- **Extreme MA concentration** (~85% revenue) = maximum CMS exposure
- Flat 2027 MA rates (0.09% vs expected 4-6%)
- Chart review rule change eliminates diagnosis mining
- Star ratings pressure (quality bonuses at risk)
- Medical cost inflation compressing margins
- No diversification into employer/Medicaid like peers

## Q1 2026 print + 2026 Star Ratings stabilization (May 8, 2026)

HUM closed +11.3% to $274.96 on May 8 ($247.12 → $274.96, +4.2σ beta-adjusted) after the Q1 print and updated 2026 Medicare Advantage Star Ratings disclosure resolved one of the bear-case overhangs the stock has carried since the Jan 27 -21% rate-shock day.

### 2026 Star Ratings — stabilization, not improvement

| Star tier | 2026 (current) | 2025 (prior) | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| ≥4 stars | 20% of MA members | Slightly higher in 2025 | Slight decline |
| 4.5 stars | 14% of MA members | 3% | Step-change improvement |

The 4.5-star jump from 3% → 14% of members is the most operationally significant change — it's the band where bonus-payment multipliers are highest, and the increase materially lifts the FY27 economics that the prior rate cuts had compressed.

### Why the market re-rated despite mixed Stars data

Cantor Fitzgerald's Sarah James flagged Humana is showing *"signals that the back-half of the year could be difficult to manage"* given continued year-over-year Star Ratings pressure on bonus payments. But the +11% reaction is the market reading:

1. **MA membership growth despite the Jan rate shock** — Humana retained MA enrollment growth through the rate-cycle change, suggesting the company has pricing power in the segment
2. **Margin expansion expectations affirmed** — Humana expects to *double its pre-tax margin in individual MA plans next year*, excluding the impact of star ratings
3. **Cost trends across managed care space** — broader managed-care cost-trend signal improved, lifting [[UnitedHealth]] and [[CVS Health]] in sympathy

The +11.3% reaction is consistent with the [[Medicare Advantage]] sector having priced max pessimism on Jan 27 (rate-shock day) and now de-rating that pessimism as the operational evidence supports a less-bad trajectory than the rate-shock day pricing implied. The cohort-wide re-rating moved [[Humana]] from 52-week-low territory back toward mid-cycle valuation.

### Cross-name read — the H2 macro split

Humana's +11.3% reaction lands the same day as [[Cloudflare]] -23.6%, [[MercadoLibre]] -12.7%, [[HubSpot]] -19.0% — but Humana's signal is *opposite* in macro mechanism: HUM rallied on improving operational visibility into 2026, while NET / HUBS / MELI sold off on the same dimension. The dispersion within the +/-10σ tape supports the [[Ed Yardeni|Yardeni]] earnings-led-melt-up framework — the names with operating-leverage visibility re-rate up, the names where Q2 guide cuts the visibility re-rate down. Healthcare/insurer in upper Yardeni rotation list (Dec 2025) is operationally tracking the framework.
