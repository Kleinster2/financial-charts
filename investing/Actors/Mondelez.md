## Cocoa supply diversification — Celleste Bio partnership

Mondelez is the equity backer behind [[Celleste Bio]], the Israeli foodtech start-up that produced the world's first lab-grown chocolate bars in April 2026. Production took place at Mondelez's [[Cadbury]] Bournville factory in Birmingham, using Celleste's cell-cultured cocoa butter as a drop-in replacement for conventional cocoa butter.

Context (per FT, Apr 15, 2026): cocoa bean prices ran from under $3,000/tonne in early 2023 to a peak near $12,000/tonne in 2024; cocoa butter topped €40,000/tonne in late 2024 before easing to ~€5,000. The price shock squeezed chocolate manufacturers' margins across the industry. Mondelez's Celleste stake is part of the confectionery industry's broader response — [[Cargill]] is partnering with [[Kokomodo]] (cell-based) and [[Voyage Foods]] (cocoa-free); [[Lindt Spruengli]] is backing [[Food Brewer]] (cell-based).

Strategic read: Mondelez is positioning on the drop-in replacement pathway specifically — Celleste's CEO [[Michal Beressi Golomb]] claims cell-cultured cocoa butter "performs identically to conventional cocoa butter," which if validated at scale would spare Mondelez the reformulation friction that slows ingredient substitution alternatives. Target commercial timeline: US/Israel approval end-2027; EU later. Target scale: 50,000 tonnes/year at $12,000-$15,000/tonne.

See [[Cocoa]] for the underlying commodity dynamics and [[Cellular agriculture]] for the technology landscape.
