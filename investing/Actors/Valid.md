**Valid S.A.** — Brazilian digital security and identification solutions company, a key player in [[Brazil]]'s ICP-Brasil digital certificate ecosystem. Founded 1957, provides smart cards, digital certificates, government IDs, and IoT connectivity solutions across [[Brazil]], Argentina, and Spain.

## Synopsis

Valid operates in the regulated digital identity infrastructure market, benefiting from [[Brazil Digital Certificate Ecosystem|Brazil's government-mandated digital certificate requirements]]. The company serves major telecom operators (Telefônica Brasil, TIM, Claro), banks (Itaú Unibanco, Banco do Brasil, Bradesco, Santander Brasil), and government agencies including Brazil's Department of Transit. With R$2B in annual revenue (2022) and ~R$49M quarterly net income, Valid trades at VLID3 on [[B3]] with a market cap around R$1.7-2.1B.

The investment case centers on Brazil's accelerating digitization: PIX payments, gov.br digital services, eSocial compliance, and mandatory digital certificates for tax filings create recurring revenue streams with regulatory moats. Valid is positioned as one of the few pure-play exposures to this trend, competing against Serasa Experian (subsidiary of [[Experian]]), Certisign, Soluti, and government-owned Serpro in the Autoridades Certificadoras (AC) market.

Key business segments include: (1) **Digital Certification** — ICP-Brasil certificates for individuals (e-CPF) and businesses (e-CNPJ), (2) **Smart Cards & SIMs** — top-5 global SIM manufacturer with 900M connected devices, (3) **Government IDs** — driver's licenses, identity documents, (4) **Banking & Payments** — EMV payment cards, top-10 global credit/debit card manufacturer. The SIM business provides international diversification with connectivity solutions for IoT and device makers globally.

Risks include concentration in regulated markets, government dependency, competitive pressure from larger players like Serasa Experian, and exposure to Brazil's political cycles. However, Serasa Experian recently **stopped renewing digital certificates** (Nov 2025), potentially reducing competitive pressure in the AC market.

## Business Segments

### ID & Digital Government
**Digital identity, interoperability, and digital services.** Authorized under ICP-Brasil to issue digital certificates (e-CPF, e-CNPJ, NF-e). Valid is one of the main Autoridades Certificadoras alongside Serasa Experian, Certisign, Soluti, and Serpro. Certificates require in-person validation and expire annually (A1) or every 1-3 years (A3), driving recurring revenue.

**Government partnerships include:** State governments for digital ID initiatives (Paraná CNH issuance, Piauí digital identity program, Espírito Santo police digitization, Amazonas digital transformation strategy).

### Banking & Payments  
**EMV payment cards and digital banking services.** Valid ranks as a **top-10 global credit and debit card manufacturer**, serving major Brazilian banks including Itaú Unibanco, Banco do Brasil, Bradesco, Santander Brasil, and Caixa Economica Federal.

### Trusted Connectivity
**Solutions for connectivity providers, device makers, and IoT service providers.** Valid is the **top-5 largest SIM manufacturer worldwide** with **900M connected devices** using Valid technology. This segment provides international diversification beyond Brazil's regulated markets.

### Security Printing
**Traditional security printing services** to financial institutions, telecom companies, state governments, and public agencies. Includes driver's licenses, identity cards, and other secure documents. In 2010, produced 465.1M cards, 15.4M driver's licenses/identity cards, and 12.3K tons of security paper.

## Growth Drivers

### Government Digitization
- **PIX adoption** — Brazil's instant payment system requires digital certificates for business integration
- **gov.br platform** — unified government services portal driving digital certificate demand
- **eSocial compliance** — mandatory digital labor reporting system
- **NF-e electronic invoicing** — required for business-to-business transactions
- **Tax filing digitization** — Receita Federal requirements

### International Expansion
- **Diaspora opportunity** — ~4M Brazilians abroad need certificates for Brazilian bureaucracy, premium pricing ($275 vs ~R$150-200 domestic)
- **SIM manufacturing** — global IoT and connectivity growth
- **LATAM expansion** — operations in Argentina and Spain

### Market Consolidation
- Serasa Experian exit from certificate renewals
- Potential acquisition targets among private ACs
- Government push for digital identity standardization

## Risks

### Government Dependency
- Heavy reliance on government contracts and regulated markets
- Exposure to Brazil political cycles and policy changes
- Potential changes to ICP-Brasil framework

### Competition
- Larger players with more capital (Experian, though withdrawn from renewals)
- Government-owned Serpro with direct federal ties
- New entrants in e-signature and digital identity

### Concentration Risk
- Geographic concentration in Brazil (though mitigated by global SIM business)
- Sector concentration in regulated digital identity markets
- Customer concentration among major banks and telecoms

### Technology Risk
- Need for continuous investment in security and compliance
- Potential disruption from blockchain-based identity solutions
- Changes in international smart card/SIM standards

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Pure-play exposure** to Brazil's digital transformation, one of few public options
- **Regulatory moats** with ICP-Brasil authorization and compliance expertise
- **Recurring revenue** from annual certificate renewals and government contracts
- **International diversification** through global SIM manufacturing
- **Market share gains** possible from Serasa Experian's withdrawal from renewals
- **Undervalued** relative to growth potential of Brazil's digitization push

### Bear Case
- **Government dependency** creates political and regulatory risks
- **Competitive pressure** from larger players and government-owned alternatives
- **Technology disruption** risk from blockchain and alternative identity solutions
- **Economic sensitivity** to Brazil's macroeconomic cycles
- **Limited scale** compared to global technology and payments companies
- **Execution risk** on international expansion and new product development
