McLaren is a British racing team and supercar manufacturer, majority-owned by Bahrain's Mumtalakat sovereign wealth fund. The 2024 [[Formula 1]] constructors champion, claiming $161M in prize money.

## Synopsis

McLaren Group encompasses McLaren Racing (F1, IndyCar, Formula E), McLaren Automotive (supercars), and McLaren Applied (technology licensing). The F1 team's 2024 constructors championship — its first since 1998 — marked a dramatic turnaround from the competitiveness nadir of 2015-2018, when the Honda engine partnership produced some of the worst results in the team's history. Team principal Andrea Stella and the technical leadership rebuilt the car's aerodynamic philosophy, and driver [[Lando Norris]] emerged as a championship contender. The $161M prize payout reflects F1's growing revenue pool under [[Liberty Media]]'s commercial management.

The Mumtalakat ownership (majority stake acquired through multiple investments since 2007) connects McLaren to the broader Gulf sovereign wealth strategy of diversifying into prestige brands and sports properties. McLaren Automotive sells approximately 3,500-4,000 supercars annually in the $200K-$2M+ range, competing with [[Ferrari]] and Lamborghini. Unlike Ferrari (publicly listed, ~$80B market cap), McLaren remains private, with recurring reports of IPO or sale exploration. The brand's valuation is complicated by the cyclicality of supercar demand and the capital intensity of both F1 racing and automotive R&D. The [[FIA]]'s 2026 engine regulation changes — increasing the electrical component of the power unit — create both risk and opportunity as teams adapt to the new formula.
