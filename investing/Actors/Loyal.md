#actor #biotech #startup #longevity #private

**Loyal** — clinical-stage animal-health company (legal entity Cellular Longevity) building toward the first FDA-approved drug for lifespan extension in any species.

Founded by [[Celine Halioua]] in 2019, Loyal runs a three-drug canine pipeline (LOY-001, LOY-002, LOY-003) that attacks aging through two mechanisms: dampening the IGF-1 growth-factor pathway tied to large-breed aging, and correcting age-related metabolic dysfunction. Lead program LOY-002, a daily pill for senior dogs, had two of the three FDA technical sections accepted as of January 2026, leaving only the manufacturing review before it can reach the market under conditional approval. The company has raised $250M+ across five rounds; its February 2026 Series C drew a public-markets growth investor ([[Baillie Gifford]]) alongside [[Longevity]] specialist [[age1]] — a signal that canine healthspan is being underwritten as a future market, not a science experiment.

## Pipeline

Loyal pursues lifespan extension through two mechanistic bets: dampening the IGF-1 / growth-factor pathway implicated in large-breed aging (LOY-001, LOY-003), and correcting metabolic dysfunction in old age (LOY-002).

| Drug | Form | Target population | Mechanism | Status |
|------|------|-------------------|-----------|--------|
| LOY-001 | Vet-administered injection | Large- and giant-breed dogs ≥7 yr, ≥40 lb | IGF-1 reduction | RXE accepted Nov 2023; advancing |
| LOY-002 | Daily flavored pill | Senior dogs ≥10 yr, ≥14 lb (all sizes) | Metabolic drivers of aging | RXE + safety accepted; manufacturing section pending |
| LOY-003 | Daily pill | Large- and giant-breed dogs (same population as LOY-001) | IGF-1 reduction | Pre-approval; funded by Series C |

LOY-003 is the oral version of the large-breed program — a daily pill is far more commercially scalable than LOY-001's clinic-administered injection, which is why both advance in parallel. Loyal has guided to conditional availability for the large-breed programs in 2026.

## LOY-002 — lead program

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Type | Prescription daily flavored tablet |
| Eligibility | Dogs aged 10+ and ≥14 lb |
| Target | Metabolic drivers of aging in senior dogs |
| Goal | Extend healthy lifespan; [[Celine Halioua]] has said she hopes to add roughly one year (~5 "dog years") |
| FDA pathway | Conditional approval via the FDA Center for Veterinary Medicine (Expanded Conditional Approval) |
| RXE (effectiveness) | Accepted Feb 2025 |
| TAS (target animal safety) | Accepted Jan 2026 |
| CMC (manufacturing) | Final section, submission in progress |
| STAY study | 1,300 dogs across ~70 vet clinics — largest clinical trial in veterinary medicine history |
| Est. price | ~$40–90 / month (company guidance) |

Conditional approval would let Loyal market LOY-002 while it completes a longer confirmatory study. If granted, it would be the first FDA-approved drug for lifespan extension in any species. On reported timelines, conditional approval could land in late 2026 or 2027 if the manufacturing review proceeds without setbacks.

### FDA section progress (LOY-002)

The conditional-approval application has three technical sections. RXE establishes a reasonable expectation that the drug works; TAS establishes safety in the target animal; CMC establishes that the drug can be manufactured consistently to quality standards. As of January 2026 the first two are accepted and only CMC remains — the same three-section structure that LOY-001 navigates separately for the large-breed population.

## Strategic significance

Loyal is a proof-of-concept vehicle for the broader [[Longevity]] thesis. Dogs age several times faster than humans, so a canine lifespan-extension result reads out in years rather than decades, making dogs an efficient model for whether "aging" can be treated as a regulable indication. The company is effectively building the regulatory category — getting the FDA to accept "extend healthy lifespan" as an approvable claim — which does not exist today. If the science and the playbook translate, the read-through to human geroscience is the real prize.

Commercially, the addressable market is large and underpenetrated: 90M+ pet dogs in the US inside a $150B+ US pet-care industry, with owners increasingly willing to spend on health and longevity for animals. The incumbent that owns the veterinary channel is [[Zoetis]] — the dominant animal-health company and the natural competitor, distribution partner, or acquirer in any pet-longevity category Loyal succeeds in creating.
