# Dun & Bradstreet

Commercial data and analytics company providing business decisioning data to companies worldwide. Operator of the D-U-N-S Number system, the global standard for business identification used by over 500M business records. Has been through two take-private cycles in four years — the kind of financial engineering that screams "valuable asset, mediocre operator."

## Synopsis

Dun & Bradstreet maintains the world's most comprehensive commercial database, assigning D-U-N-S Numbers that function as the Social Security numbers of the business world. The US government, major banks, and procurement departments globally require D-U-N-S registration, creating an entrenched moat that persists regardless of management quality. Revenue runs ~$2.3B annually across two segments: Finance & Risk (credit decisioning, compliance, supply chain risk) and Sales & Marketing (lead generation, audience targeting). The data asset is genuinely irreplaceable — 185 years of continuous commercial record-keeping.

The ownership history tells a story of a company that's worth more than its operators extract. Taken private in 2019 by CC Capital, [[Cannae Holdings]], and Thomas H. Lee Partners for ~$5.4B. Re-IPOed in 2020 at $22/share. Taken private again in 2022 by [[Clearlake Capital]] for ~$6.4B ($11.7B enterprise value). Each cycle loads leverage, extracts fees, and promises "operational transformation." The bull case is that AI and LLM-driven analytics could finally unlock the data asset's potential — D&B's structured business data is exactly what enterprise AI products need for B2B applications. The bear case is that the company has been promising transformation since the 1990s while competitors like [[ZoomInfo]], Moody's Analytics, and S&P Market Intelligence have built faster-moving alternatives. Historically owned [[Nielsen]] (1984-1996).
