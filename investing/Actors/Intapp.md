# Intapp

US public legal-tech and professional-services-software company. NASDAQ: INTA. ~$1.79bn market cap (April 2026). Two flagship product lines: DealCloud (relationship and pipeline management for M&A and PE) and Intapp Terms (engagement / risk / business-development software). Sells into law firms, [[Private equity]] shops, investment banks, and consulting firms — the [[Big Law transformation]] cohort directly.

## Vault read — levered to Kirklandisation directly

Of all listed legal-tech names, Intapp is the most directly levered to the [[Big Law transformation]] thesis. The mechanism: corporate-law firms running scale-plus-pay models (i.e., [[Kirkland & Ellis]], [[Latham & Watkins]], [[Paul, Weiss]]) operate on PE-shop infrastructure — they need DealCloud-style relationship and pipeline management to coordinate large origination teams across hundreds of partners and thousands of mid-tier lawyers. Lockstep firms ([[Wachtell Lipton]] historically) needed this less because the cross-practice integration was solved socially within an 80-partner room.

The 52-week drawdown reflects the broader legal-tech AI-disruption overhang ([[RELX]], [[Thomson Reuters]], [[Wolters Kluwer]] all faced similar pressure) but Intapp's workflow-and-relationship layer is structurally less substitutable by general-purpose AI than ediscovery ([[CS Disco]]) or legal research ([[RELX]]'s LexisNexis).
