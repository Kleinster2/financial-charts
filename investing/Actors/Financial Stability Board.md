#actor #regulator #global #finance

**Financial Stability Board (FSB)** — international body that monitors and makes recommendations about the global financial system. Hosted at the [[Bank for International Settlements]] in Basel. Coordinates the work of national financial authorities and international standard-setters (Basel Committee, IOSCO, IAIS) to address vulnerabilities affecting global financial stability. Created at the G20 Pittsburgh Summit in 2009 to succeed the Financial Stability Forum.

## Mid-2026 focus areas

The FSB's 2026 work programme has three structural themes:

| Theme | Status |
|---|---|
| Non-bank financial intermediation (NBFI) including private credit | Active; major report May 2026 |
| AI and financial-stability implications (cyber, herding, model risk, cloud concentration) | Active; ongoing monitoring |
| Crypto-asset regulation | Implementation phase |

### Report on Vulnerabilities in Private Credit (May 6 2026)

The May 2026 FSB report on private credit was the largest single piece of work to date on the intersection of private credit and the AI capex cycle. Key findings:

| Finding | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total private credit market | $1.5–2 trillion (2024); top markets US ($1T), Euro Area + [[UK]] |
| AI share of 2025 private credit deals | >33% (vs ~17% five-year average) |
| Bank-to-private-credit interconnectedness | Three channels: direct fund lending; financing riskier fund portfolios; lending to firms that also borrow from PC |
| AI-loan-specific vulnerabilities | Electricity supply, data center construction delays, AI demand oversupply |
| Tail-risk framing | "Sharp correction in asset valuations… could lead to sizable credit losses to private credit investors" |
| Data gaps | "Significant data challenges" frustrated nearly a year of work |

This is the cleanest external statement to date that the AI capex cycle and the private-credit fragility story are structurally linked rather than parallel concerns. The report's data-gap acknowledgment is itself meaningful — the FSB cannot reliably measure exposure paths it has been told to monitor.

See [[AI infrastructure financing risk]] for the mechanism-level analysis. See [[Private Credit Software Concentration]] for the BDC-level exposure data.

*Source: [FSB: Report on Vulnerabilities in Private Credit, May 6 2026](https://www.fsb.org/2026/05/report-on-vulnerabilities-in-private-credit/)*
