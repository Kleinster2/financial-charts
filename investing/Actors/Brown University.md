## May 2026 fuel-cost estimate

The [[Financial Times]] cited [[Brown University]] research estimating that Americans had spent more than $40 billion extra on fuel since the start of the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran war]], or about $316 per household. The estimate is useful as the consumer-cost counterpart to [[Rystad Energy]]'s producer cash-flow estimate: the same elevated oil price that boosts US E&P cash flow functions as a household tax.

*Source: [[Financial Times]], Jamie Smyth and Stephanie Findlay, "US oil producers increase output to capture price surge from Iran war," May 23 2026.*
