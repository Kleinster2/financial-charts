# France

**France** — World's 7th largest economy. [[Luxury]] dominance, nuclear power, defense industry, strategic autonomy.

France is Europe's second-largest economy after [[Germany]] and the world's seventh, distinguished by four structural advantages: global dominance in [[Luxury]] (the [[LVMH]] / [[Hermès]] / [[L'Oréal]] / [[Kering]] cluster represents the world's largest concentration of luxury market cap), an independent nuclear weapons program with ~70% nuclear electricity output, a state-protected defense industrial base ([[Dassault Aviation]], [[Thales]], [[Safran]], Naval Group, MBDA), and a long tradition of strategic autonomy that now drives Franco-German friction over [[EU-Mercosur agreement|EU-Mercosur]], [[FCAS]], and "buy European" procurement. The structural challenge is fiscal: France runs the [[Europe|eurozone]]'s largest persistent deficit, inheriting a four-century catalogue of sovereign credit episodes that no modern constitutional structure has fully overwritten.

## "Buy European" agenda

Macron pushes "buy European" — procurement preference for European defense and industrial goods, paired with joint [[EU]] borrowing for defense. [[Germany]] prefers "made with Europe" (supply chain integration) as less protectionist.

### EV subsidy design

France doesn't directly mandate "buy European" — instead uses carbon lifecycle standards. [[China|Chinese]] EVs produced with coal-based electricity don't qualify. French production, powered by nuclear, does. Regulatory protectionism via the carbon route.

### Nuclear deterrence question

As [[Germany]] emerges as Europe's dominant conventional military power ($189B budget by 2029), whether France would extend its nuclear umbrella to cover Europe — and on what terms — becomes a live question.

## Franco-German friction (Feb 2026)

Three sources of German frustration with France:

| Issue | French position | German frustration |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| [[EU-Mercosur agreement]] | French bloc in EU Parliament | Obstructing finalized deal |
| [[FCAS]] (6th-gen fighter) | [[Dassault Aviation]] uncooperative with [[Airbus]] on tech sharing | Paris hasn't intervened |
| "Buy European" | Procurement preference | Too protectionist; threatens [[China]] business |

[[Friedrich Merz]] has responded by aligning with [[Italy]]'s Meloni — presenting Germany and Italy as Europe's manufacturing/defense powerhouses. [[Leonardo]] as alternative defense partner to [[Dassault Aviation]] / French industry.

### New friction (Feb 2026)

Berlin explicitly criticized France for not spending enough on weapons. France's yawning deficit limits military spending, but Paris notes [[Germany]]'s decades of underspending and free-riding under the Western nuclear umbrella.

### Nuclear deterrence bargain

As [[Germany]] emerges as Europe's dominant conventional military power (€162B budget by 2029), France's nuclear deterrent becomes potential leverage. Proposed grand bargain: France shares nuclear umbrella with Germany in return for German willingness to contemplate joint defense financing. Even Bundesbank chief Joachim Nagel has made the case for mutual European debt.

### Franco-German tank (MGCS)

Also falling significantly behind schedule, compounding [[FCAS]] frustration.

## Evolution

| Year | Inflection |
|------|------------|
| 1789 | Revolution; modern French state founded |
| 1797 | *Tiers consolidé* — two-thirds of pre-Revolutionary debt repudiated; foundation of all subsequent French sovereign debt (see Cross-vault) |
| 1871-72 | Thiers's Liberation Loans absorb 5 bn gold-franc indemnity to Germany; oversubscribed 14× on second tranche; high point of pre-WWI French sovereign credit |
| 1914-28 | WWI inflation and Poincaré stabilization erase ~80% of pre-war real bond value — silent default by monetary means |
| 1944 | Liberation; Fourth Republic |
| 1958-60 | Fifth Republic established under de Gaulle; *force de frappe* nuclear program initiated; doctrine of strategic autonomy formalized |
| 1981-83 | Mitterrand socialist victory, brief nationalization wave, U-turn after 1983 to align with EMS |
| 1986 | Chirac privatization wave begins state retreat from industrial holdings |
| 1999 | [[Euro]] adoption — surrenders independent monetary policy |
| 2017 | Macron elected; business-friendly reforms, labor code overhaul, pension reform attempts |
| 2022 | EU strategic autonomy push following [[Russia]] invasion of [[Ukraine]]; "buy European" agenda |
| 2024-26 | Persistent fiscal deficit becomes binding constraint on military spending and Franco-German bargaining |
| 2026 | Gold reserve repatriation completed; no French gold remaining in [[United States]] vaults |

## Gold repatriation (completed Apr 2026)

[[France]] completed a years-long programme to repatriate its [[Gold]] reserves and no longer holds any gold in the [[United States]]. France is part of a growing trend of central banks repatriating gold from foreign vaults, reflecting broader de-dollarization and sovereignty concerns in reserve management.
