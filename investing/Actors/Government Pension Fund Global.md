# Government Pension Fund Global

The world's largest [[Sovereign Wealth Funds|sovereign wealth fund]] (~$1.7T AUM as of early 2025), managed by [[Norges Bank]] Investment Management (NBIM) on behalf of the Norwegian Ministry of Finance. Funded entirely by [[Norway]]'s oil and gas revenues, the fund was established in 1990 to invest surplus petroleum wealth for future generations. The government caps annual withdrawals at 3% of the fund's value (the "fiscal rule"), forcing reliance on non-oil tax revenue including the [[Wealth tax]].

NBIM holds stakes in over 9,000 companies across 70+ countries, making it one of the largest single shareholders globally — owning roughly 1.5% of all listed equities worldwide. The fund allocates ~70% to equities, ~25% to fixed income, and ~5% to unlisted real estate. It publishes all holdings and voting records, making it one of the most transparent institutional investors. NBIM has increasingly used its ownership position to push ESG standards, particularly on climate disclosure and executive pay, though its ethical exclusion list (coal, tobacco, certain weapons) draws criticism from both sides — too aggressive for free-market purists, too lax for climate activists.
