## South Africa expansion

[[Chery]] entered [[South Africa]] in 2021. By 2025, it displaced [[Volkswagen]] as SA's #3 automaker (after [[Toyota]] and [[Suzuki Motor]]). [[Nissan]] announced it will cease local manufacturing in June 2026 after 60+ years, selling its SA facilities to Chery — the first Chinese automaker to acquire a full manufacturing plant in the country.

[[China]]'s share of SA car imports by volume climbed to 27% (2025) from 21% (2024). For trucks: ~50% from ~30%. Chery operates showrooms under its Omoda and Jaecoo brands in the Eastern Cape.

*Source: Bloomberg (Parker/Hill), March 18, 2026*
