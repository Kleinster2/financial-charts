Starling Bank — [[UK]] digital bank, SMB-focused. ~4.2M customers, ~£2.5–3B last private valuation. Profitable since FY21. Founded 2014 by Anne Boden; Boden departed CEO role in 2023, Raman Bhatia (ex-[[HSBC]] UK Digital) took over June 2024.

Starling differs from [[Monzo]] and [[Revolut]] in that its profitability story is built on SME banking — it grew its book sharply via [[UK]] government-backed pandemic loans (Bounce Back Loan Scheme), then layered in business current accounts. Less consumer-app glamour, sturdier per-customer economics. Banking-as-a-Service ("Engine") is a separate growth lever — selling Starling's core banking platform to other neobanks.

## Leadership

### Raman Bhatia (CEO, Jun 2024–)

Ex-[[HSBC]] UK Digital lead; before that ran Octopus Energy's retail division. Brought in to take Starling toward an IPO and re-energise growth after Boden's departure.

### Anne Boden (Founder; CEO 2014–2023)

Founded Starling and built it to ~£600M revenue and profitability. Stepped down 2023 amid Bounce Back Loan oversight issues and a £29M FCA fine in 2024 for sanctions screening failures.
