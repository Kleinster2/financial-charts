**Valero** — Largest independent US refiner. Gulf Coast heavy crude capacity. Major Venezuela beneficiary.

## Why Valero matters for Venezuela

**Gulf Coast refineries built for heavy crude:**

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Refinery design | Configured for heavy, sour crude |
| Historical source | Venezuela (pre-collapse) |
| Current source | [[Canada]], [[Mexico]], other |
| Venezuela upside | Natural fit, lower transport cost |

**If Venezuela supply returns:** Valero's Gulf Coast refineries get cheaper, closer feedstock.

## Heavy crude advantage

**Why heavy crude matters:**

- Heavy crude trades at discount to light
- Complex refineries can process it profitably
- Venezuela crude is heavy/sour
- Gulf Coast = shortest shipping route

**Competitors for Venezuela crude:** [[Phillips 66]], [[PBF Energy]], [[Citgo]]

## Investment case

**Bull:**
- Venezuela supply = margin boost
- Renewable diesel growth
- Strong cash returns (buybacks, dividends)
- Energy security demand
- Refining capacity constrained

**Bear:**
- Cyclical (crack spreads volatile)
- EV long-term headwind
- Regulatory/environmental costs
- Refinery maintenance capital intensive
