## What it tracks

Tesouro Selic (Letras Financeiras do Tesouro, LFT) — Brazilian government bonds whose coupon floats with the Selic policy rate, so their price barely moves (near-zero duration). LFTS11 packages that into an exchange-traded, daily-liquid wrapper: it accrues the Selic, roughly a flat upward line.

## Use case

The cash leg of a portfolio — where a Carteira Aberta guest moved his "caixa" out of bank renda-fixa funds, for the daily liquidity and the 15% long-term tax. The one Brazilian fixed-income ETF that genuinely behaves like cash.

Uncorrelated with both equities and duration bonds — because it has no duration, it does not load on the Selic-cycle factor that links stocks and long bonds. The true diversifier in the family. See [[Brazilian rate cycle]].
