**Jared Isaacman** — NASA administrator since 2025. Billionaire founder of [[Shift4]] (NYSE: FOUR), commercial astronaut who privately commanded [[Inspiration4]] (Sep 2021, first all-civilian orbital mission) and [[Polaris Dawn]] (Sep 2024, first commercial spacewalk). As administrator, has driven the most significant restructuring of NASA's contractor relationships in a generation in Q1 2026: reclassified [[Boeing Starliner]]'s 2024 crew flight test as a [[Type A mishap]], paused [[Lunar Gateway]], announced $20B over 7 years for permanent lunar base, and proposed alternative [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] framework.

## Q1 2026 actions

| Date | Action |
|---|---|
| Feb 19, 2026 | Reclassified [[Boeing Starliner]] 2024 CFT as [[Type A mishap]] (alongside [[Challenger]], [[Columbia]], [[Apollo 1]]) |
| Mar 25, 2026 | Announced $20B over 7 years for permanent lunar base near south pole; paused [[Lunar Gateway]] |
| Q1 2026 | Proposed [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] alternative framework — NASA-designed core module attached to ISS |
| Q1 2026 | Stripped SLS of core role in lunar architecture; [[SpaceX]] [[Starship]] now docks with [[Orion]] in Earth orbit |

The pattern across these actions: legacy fixed-price prime contractors ([[Boeing]], [[Northrop Grumman]] on Gateway, [[Maxar]] on PPE) lose scope; [[SpaceX]] gains lunar lander role, Earth-orbit docking, and Starship-native economics; new entrants ([[Vast Space|Vast]], [[Voyager]], [[Lunar Outpost]]) are pulled into the architecture as commercial-tier suppliers.

The economics enabling the pivot: [[Voyager]]'s 10K disclosed [[Starship]] pricing at $90M / $900/kg vs SLS at $4B+/flight (>$10K/kg). Per [[Space Capital]] (Apr 2026): "That's a generational step change in cost structure, even if that pricing will rise as demand scales."
