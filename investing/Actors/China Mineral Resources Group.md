# China Mineral Resources Group

China Mineral Resources Group (CMRG) is the central state-owned aggregator that consolidates [[China]]'s purchasing of imported iron ore on behalf of the country's steel-mill complex. It was created in 2022 under [[SASAC]] supervision to give Chinese steelmakers unified counterparty leverage in their negotiations with the Pilbara majors ([[BHP]], [[Rio Tinto]], [[Fortescue]]) and [[Vale]] — the explicit policy goal being to repatriate pricing power for a commodity where China is the largest single buyer (~70% of global seaborne iron ore demand) but historically a price-taker.

## Strategic role

CMRG is structurally analogous to OPEC on the buy side: a single large counterparty negotiating across miners that previously sold to fragmented Chinese mill counterparties. Public reporting since 2023-2024 has indicated CMRG is pushing two structural changes to the iron-ore trade:

1. **China-based pricing index.** Move benchmark pricing away from [[Platts|Platts-derived]] global indices toward a China-priced index that better reflects the Chinese demand footprint and reduces dependence on a Western pricing benchmark.
2. **[[Renminbi]] settlement.** Settle a meaningful share of iron-ore trade in [[Renminbi|RMB]] rather than USD, advancing the [[de-dollarization]] thread that has surfaced in Saudi-China oil deals, Russian crude exports, and (in 2026) the [[Hormuz Transit Regime|Iranian Hormuz transit fees]].

Both asks structurally compress miner pricing power and route export earnings through Chinese-controlled infrastructure (clearing, indexing, settlement).

## Apr 28, 2026 — Fortescue confirms active CMRG talks

[[Mark Barnaba]] (Fortescue Deputy Chairman) on Bloomberg "The Asia Trade" 4/28/2026 confirmed Fortescue is in active CMRG negotiation, with the China-based index and RMB-invoicing requests on the table. Barnaba's framing: "not unreasonable, not unexpected" — Fortescue is positioning the asks as part of a normal commercial dialogue rather than a one-sided concession, citing Fortescue's high two-way trade with China (large iron-ore exports to China matched by large imports of Chinese batteries, solar panels, equipment).

This was the cleanest public confirmation that CMRG terms-of-trade renegotiation is active, not theoretical, with the Western mining major most exposed to Chinese demand.

*Source: Bloomberg "The Asia Trade" 4/28/2026 — Mark Barnaba interview.*
