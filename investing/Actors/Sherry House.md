# Sherry House

## Synopsis

[[Sherry House]] is the Chief Financial Officer of [[Ford]] Motor Company, effective February 6, 2025 — the first woman to hold the role in Ford's history. She succeeded John Lawler, who had been CFO since 2020. House joined Ford in June 2024 as Vice President of Finance with responsibility for financial planning and analysis across Ford Blue, Ford Pro, and Ford Model e. Prior to Ford, she was CFO of [[Lucid Motors]] (May 2021 – December 2023), where she led the firm's July 2021 SPAC-merger public listing and the build-out of US and Saudi Arabian manufacturing.

The investing-vault relevance is the [[Ford]] Iran-war commodity-shock commentary on the Q1 2026 earnings call. House's "we were already seeing global industry shortages. Then you had the Middle East" quote framed the [[Aluminum]] +16% LME re-rating and [[Novelis]] supply-disruption pass-through documented in [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]]. As the first investor-facing CFO under the post-tariff / Iran-war operating environment, her communication posture on commodity guidance is the working signal for whether Ford's $2bn 2026 commodity hit converts to additional structural margin compression or fades by 2H.

## Position in Ford / Iran-war commodity thread

House inherited the CFO role at the inflection point between Trump-II tariff onset (February 2025) and the Iran-war commodity shock (March-May 2026). Her Q1 2026 earnings-call quotes anchored the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Detroit OEM commodity-shock thread]]: Ford's $2bn 2026 commodity hit (+$1bn YoY), the ~$1bn from [[Novelis]]-overseas substitution into 50% Section 232 [[Aluminum|aluminium]] tariff, and the +16% LME aluminium re-rating that fed both the F-Series (all-aluminium body since 2014) and the broader EU OEM cohort.

The investor-relations posture from House — "still globally short, then you had the Middle East" framing — is structurally positioned to be more cautious-on-commodities than predecessor John Lawler's tariff-era guidance, given the layered exposure from Section 232 plus Iran-war supplier renegotiations. Forward CFO commentary cycles (Q2 / Q3 2026) are the working signal for whether Ford's commodity guide stabilises or extends into a multi-quarter compression.

## Position in CFO-pipeline / female-CFO trend

House is one of a cluster of female CFOs at major US automakers — alongside Paul Jacobson (m, GM) [the cohort isn't all-female] and the broader 2020s rise of female CFOs in industrial America. The 2024-25 appointments (House at Ford; previously [[Mary Barra]]'s elevation to GM CEO) bookend a period of demographic change at the top of US auto finance leadership.
