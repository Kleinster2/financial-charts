## Why he matters

Varadhan is one of the best indicators of how much Goldman still identifies as a markets franchise first. His background is not generic bank management; it is macro trading. That is why the Q1 2026 FICC miss matters culturally as much as financially. The FT article on the rates-desk miss described him as the senior trader above [[Anshul Sehgal]], and that hierarchy captures the point: Goldman's markets leadership still comes out of rates and macro.

He also appears in other market-structure contexts. Existing vault work on [[HFT-hedge fund convergence]] already cited him on the tension of prop firms becoming both clients and competitors for bank prime brokers.
