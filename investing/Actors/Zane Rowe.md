# Zane Rowe

CFO of [[Workday]], with a background spanning enterprise tech and aviation. Previously served as CFO of VMware (2016-2019) and held senior finance roles at [[Apple]] and [[United Airlines]]. His CFO tenure at [[Workday]] coincides with the company's push to transition from a pure subscription SaaS model toward consumption-based pricing and AI-powered products.

## Synopsis

Rowe joined [[Workday]] as CFO in 2019, bringing operational discipline from his VMware stint where he helped manage the company's transition under [[Dell|Dell Technologies]]' ownership. At [[Workday]], he oversees the financial strategy of a company generating $8B+ in annual revenue, with a core HCM and financial management franchise that dominates the large-enterprise segment. His tenure has spanned [[Workday]]'s evolution from high-growth SaaS to a maturing platform company navigating AI disruption and the [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|software repricing]] triggered by AI agents.

The key challenge Rowe manages is [[Workday]]'s margin expansion amid slowing organic growth. The company's subscription revenue growth has decelerated from 20%+ to mid-teens, and the market is increasingly pricing in the risk that AI-powered tools (including [[Claude Cowork]], [[Salesforce]] Agentforce, and [[Microsoft]] Copilot) could automate significant portions of the HR and finance workflows that [[Workday]] currently serves. Rowe's financial engineering — share buybacks, cost optimization, and selective M&A — determines whether [[Workday]] can maintain its premium valuation multiple as the growth-to-value transition continues.
