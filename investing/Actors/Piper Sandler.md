# Piper Sandler

Piper Sandler (formerly Piper Jaffray) is a US middle-market investment bank and institutional securities firm headquartered in Minneapolis. The firm rebranded in 2020 after acquiring Sandler O'Neill, a boutique known for financial services M&A, for ~$485M. The combined platform covers healthcare, technology, financial services, and energy with ~1,600 employees. Notable as the incubator for [[Gene Munster]], [[Doug Clinton]], [[Andrew Murphy]], and Mike Olson, who all left to co-found what became [[Deepwater Asset Management]].

## Business profile

Piper Sandler's core strength is sector-focused equity research and middle-market advisory. The firm consistently ranks among the top US equity research shops in healthcare and technology. Its advisory business focuses on deals in the $100M-$2B range — below bulge bracket attention but large enough to generate meaningful fees. The Sandler O'Neill acquisition added a dominant financial institutions group that advises on bank and insurance M&A.
