**Yasir Al-Rumayyan** — Governor of [[Saudi PIF]] since 2019 (Managing Director 2015-2019), chairman of [[Saudi Aramco]] and [[Ma'aden]]. A central figure in [[Saudi Vision 2030]] execution, overseeing the transformation of PIF from a passive $150B holding company into a $1.15T sovereign wealth fund.

## 2026 strategy reset

Al-Rumayyan is the public face of [[Saudi PIF]]'s Apr 15, 2026 strategy reset. His message has two halves that sit in tension:

| Message | Investment read-through |
|---------|-------------------------|
| PIF is not retreating from global deals | Strategic international investments such as [[Electronic Arts]] and the [[Paramount Global]] / [[Warner Bros Discovery]] financing remain live if they fit the theme. |
| Capital deployment must become more efficient | Domestic priorities get sorted into must-have vs good-to-have, with [[Oxagon]], AI infrastructure, [[Expo 2030]], and the 2034 [[FIFA]] World Cup protected while prestige projects get phased. |

On [[NEOM]], Al-Rumayyan publicly rejected a cancellation frame but clarified the internal priority order: [[Oxagon]] is the must-have industrial/logistics pillar, while [[NEOM|The Line]] is no longer essential by 2030. On the Iran war, he said the conflict adds pressure to reposition priorities, but framed the review process as continuous rather than a one-off wartime reaction.
