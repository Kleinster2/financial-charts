#actor #investor #finance #alternative-asset-manager #canada

Brookfield Asset Management (BAM) — the asset-light, publicly-listed pure-play alternative-asset manager spun out of [[Brookfield]] Corporation (BN) in December 2022, with BN retaining ~73%. It collects fees on $603B of fee-bearing capital ($1T+ total group AUM); FY2025 fee-related earnings were $3.0B (+22%). This is the Brookfield entity in the listed alt-manager cohort ([[Alternative asset managers basket]] / ALTM). The owner-operator activities — owning renewables, [[Westinghouse]], the [[Radiant (Brookfield)|Radiant]] GPU platform, and the AI-infrastructure fund's balance-sheet capital — sit in the parent [[Brookfield]] (BN); BAM is the fee engine on top.

One-line read: the fee-stream slice of Brookfield — annuity-like FRE on real-assets capital — that re-rated with the alt-manager complex.

What to watch: fee-bearing capital growth and FRE (the stable engine); fundraising ($112B in FY2025); flows into the AI-infrastructure fund (managed by BAM, capital from BN + LPs); and the BN ~73% ownership overhang on the float.

## Why it matters

- The pure fee play: BAM is how you own Brookfield's fee stream without the balance sheet — asset-light, ~$3.0B FRE on $603B fee-bearing capital, the annuity-like economics the alt-manager re-rating rewards.
- Real-assets-fed FRE: unlike insurance-fed [[Apollo]] or credit-fed [[Blue Owl]], BAM's fee base is infrastructure, renewables and real estate plus the new AI-infrastructure fund — see [[Alternative asset manager]] for the sub-model map.
- The BN relationship: [[Brookfield]] Corporation owns ~73% and supplies balance-sheet co-investment; BAM supplies the management platform. The owner-operator content (Radiant, Westinghouse, Bloom Energy, renewables) is the BN platform.
