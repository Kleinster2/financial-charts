# MultiPlan

**MultiPlan** rebranded to [[Claritev]] in February 2025.

## Synopsis

MultiPlan was a healthcare cost management platform that went public via [[SPAC]] merger with Churchill Capital Corp VII in 2020 at an $11B enterprise value — one of the largest SPAC deals of the era. The stock subsequently collapsed from its ~$10 SPAC price to under $1, becoming a poster child for SPAC value destruction. The company's core business (out-of-network repricing and payment integrity for health insurers) faced both competitive erosion and controversy over whether its services actually reduced costs for patients or merely shifted them. The February 2025 rebrand to Claritev was an attempt to reset the narrative, though the underlying business challenges remained.

The MultiPlan-to-Claritev trajectory is a reference case for SPAC-era excess: a leveraged, private-equity-owned business (Hellman & Friedman) that used the SPAC vehicle to de-risk sponsor returns while leaving public market investors holding a declining asset. The original $11B valuation implied growth and margin expansion that never materialized, and the stock's decline to penny-stock territory wiped out billions in retail and institutional capital.

See [[Claritev]] for current coverage.
