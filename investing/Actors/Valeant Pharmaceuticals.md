**Valeant Pharmaceuticals** (now Bausch Health Companies) — Canadian pharmaceutical company that became the poster child for drug price gouging and financial engineering in the 2010s. Under CEO Mike Pearson, Valeant pursued an acquisition-and-price-hike model: buy pharma companies, slash R&D to near zero, raise drug prices aggressively (66% average increase in 2015). [[Bill Ackman]] / [[Pershing Square]] held a ~5.7% stake worth ~$3.9B. The [[Valeant Philidor scandal]] (Oct 2015) — a secretly controlled specialty pharmacy — triggered the collapse. Stock fell from $262 (Aug 2015) to under $11 by 2017. Ackman lost ~$4B. Renamed Bausch Health in July 2018.

## The Pearson model (2008-2015)

Mike Pearson joined as CEO in February 2008, hired by ValueAct Capital (activist shareholder). His model was radical for pharma: treat R&D as waste. Instead of developing new drugs, acquire companies that owned existing drugs, cut their R&D and sales forces, and raise prices on captive patient populations.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| R&D spending | Cut to ~3% of revenue (industry norm: 15-20%) |
| Acquisitions | 100+ in 7 years |
| Drug price increases | 66% average in 2015 (5x nearest peer) |
| Key acquisitions | Bausch & Lomb ($8.7B, 2013), Salix Pharmaceuticals ($14.5B, 2015) |

The strategy produced stunning financial results in the short term. Revenue grew from ~$760M (2008) to ~$10.4B (2015). The stock rose from ~$14 to $262. Pearson was featured on the cover of Forbes ("America's Most Controversial CEO") and celebrated by value investors as a visionary capital allocator.

The model had a structural flaw: it required perpetual acquisitions to grow, since the legacy businesses were being stripped of R&D and would eventually decline. And the price increases — charging $2,100 for a drug that had cost $53, or hiking heart medications 300% overnight — created political risk that Pearson dismissed.

## Ackman's involvement (2015-2017)

[[Bill Ackman]] / [[Pershing Square]] entered Valeant in February 2015, buying a 5.7% stake at ~$161/share (~$3.9B). Valeant became ~25% of the fund. Ackman had previously partnered with Pearson on a ~$47B hostile bid for [[Allergan]] in 2014 — Pershing built a 9.7% Allergan stake before the bid went public, earning ~$1B in a single day when the stock jumped. (Later settled insider trading claims for $290M with the SEC; Allergan was ultimately acquired by [[Actavis]] instead.)

Ackman described Pearson as "one of the best executives I've ever met" and Valeant as "Berkshire Hathaway for pharma." He was wrong on both counts.

## Collapse (Oct 2015-2017)

See [[Valeant Philidor scandal]] for the triggering event.

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Oct 19, 2015 | Citron Research (Andrew Left) publishes report alleging secret Philidor relationship |
| Oct 26, 2015 | Valeant confirms Philidor ties; stock drops 30% in 3 days |
| Oct 30, 2015 | Valeant severs Philidor relationship |
| Nov 2015 | Congressional investigation begins (Senate Aging Committee, House Oversight) |
| Mar 15, 2016 | Pearson fired as CEO; stock below $27 |
| Mar 2016 | Valeant discloses "typo" overstating guidance by $600M |
| Jun 2016 | Pearson testifies before Congress alongside [[Martin Shkreli]] |
| Mar 13, 2017 | Ackman sells entire position at ~$11/share |
| Jul 2018 | Renamed Bausch Health Companies |

Stock price: $262 (Aug 2015) → $27 (Mar 2016) → $11 (when Ackman exited). Peak-to-trough decline: ~96%.

Ackman's loss: ~$4B, or roughly $7.7M per trading day that he held the position. It was the largest single-position loss in Pershing Square's history.

## Drug pricing controversy

Valeant was at the center of the 2015-2016 drug pricing backlash, alongside [[Martin Shkreli]]'s Turing Pharmaceuticals (which raised Daraprim from $13.50 to $750). Specific Valeant examples:

| Drug | Indication | Price increase |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Nitropress | Heart failure | +212% |
| Isuprel | Heart arrhythmias | +525% |
| Cuprimine | Wilson's disease | +5,787% (from ~$53 to ~$2,100) |
| Syprine | Wilson's disease | +3,150% |

[[Hillary Clinton]] tweeted about drug pricing on September 21, 2015, wiping $15B off biotech stocks in a single day. Valeant was her prime example.

## After Pearson

Joseph Papa replaced Pearson as CEO in May 2016. The company renamed itself Bausch Health Companies in July 2018, attempting to distance from the Valeant brand. It has since operated as a much smaller, debt-laden specialty pharma company (~$8B in debt). Bausch & Lomb was prepared for a spin-off IPO but the transaction was repeatedly delayed.
