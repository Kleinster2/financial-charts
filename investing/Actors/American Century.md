American Century Investments is a Kansas City-based asset manager founded in 1958 (originally Twentieth Century Mutual Funds), managing over $260B in assets. The firm's distinguishing feature is its ownership structure: the Stowers Institute for Medical Research, a cancer research nonprofit founded by company founder James Stowers Jr., holds the largest equity stake (~40%). This means a significant share of American Century's profits fund biomedical research — a unique alignment in the asset management industry.

American Century is the parent of [[Avantis]] Investors, the fast-growing systematic ETF brand launched in 2019 that has become central to the firm's growth strategy. Avantis funds use a rules-based, factor-tilted approach (value, profitability, small-cap) that competes directly with [[Dimensional]] and [[Vanguard]] factor products. American Century was among the six firms (alongside [[Capital Group]], [[Dimensional]], [[JPMorgan]], [[T. Rowe Price]], and [[Fidelity]]) that captured roughly half of all active ETF inflows in 2025, largely driven by Avantis momentum.
