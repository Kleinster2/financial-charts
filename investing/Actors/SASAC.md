State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission (SASAC) — ministerial-level agency under [[China]]'s State Council that supervises 97 central state-owned enterprises with >90 trillion RMB (~$12.7T) in total assets. Founded 2003. Appoints senior SOE executives, sets strategy, and evaluates performance.

## Scope

SOEs under SASAC oversight account for ~60% of [[China]] market cap (2019), ~23-28% of GDP, and dominate strategic sectors: 80% of domestic crude oil, 70% of natural gas, 60% of power supply.
