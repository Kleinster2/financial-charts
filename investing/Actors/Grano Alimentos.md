Grano Alimentos — Brazil's market leader in frozen vegetables (>40% share), based in [[Serafina Corrêa]], [[Rio Grande do Sul]]. Founded in 2000 by Edi Deitos. In early May 2026 control passed from the agribusiness fund [[Arlon Group]] (a ~10-year holder since February 2015) to [[Cleam Capital]], a Barueri family office that bought a 75% stake and set a R$1 billion revenue target. The deal is a private-capital exit-and-consolidation story in Brazilian frozen foods, not a public-market event.

## Why it matters

- Control change: the headline is the handover from [[Arlon Group]] to [[Cleam Capital]]. Arlon — the food/agribusiness fund created by [[Continental Grain Company]] — exited after roughly a decade in which, per CEO Fernando Giansante, "Grano tripled its productive capacity and significantly expanded its market share." Cleam, a multi-family office run by the Lacet family, is buying for long-term consolidation rather than a fund's fixed exit horizon.
- Deal structure: Cleam took 75% of the operation, "with potential for expansion depending on minority shareholders' decisions" — so the remaining ~25% stays with minorities for now. Value was not disclosed; [[Rabobank]] advised.
- Growth plan: management frames a jump from R$300M (2025) toward R$1B over ~5 years, via wider frozen-vegetable distribution into the North, Northeast and Center-West (today ~70% of sales sit in the South and Southeast), new frozen-fruit lines, more exports, and the co-packer channel.
