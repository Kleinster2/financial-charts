Frank McCourt is a Boston real estate developer and investor who owned the [[Los Angeles Dodgers]] from 2004 to 2012. His Dodgers tenure became one of the most contentious ownership sagas in MLB history: he purchased the team for $430M, loaded it with debt to fund a lavish lifestyle, went through a tabloid divorce that entangled the franchise's finances, and ultimately had the team seized by MLB Commissioner Bud Selig in April 2011. The franchise was sold to the Guggenheim Group (led by Mark Walter and Magic Johnson) for $2.15B in 2012 — a 5x return that McCourt achieved despite being forced out.

McCourt's post-Dodgers career pivoted to technology and blockchain. He founded Project Liberty in 2021, committing $500M to build a decentralized social networking protocol (DSNP), and launched a bid to acquire [[TikTok]]'s US operations in 2024-2025 when the platform faced a congressional ban. The McCourt Group's real estate portfolio includes major properties in the Boston Seaport district and French assets including the Marseille waterfront development. He appointed [[Steve Soboroff]] as Dodgers vice chairman in April 2011 — one day before MLB seized control of the franchise.
