# Liftoff Mobile

Mobile ad tech and app marketing platform specializing in programmatic user acquisition and re-engagement for mobile apps. [[Blackstone]]-backed since 2021. Pulled its planned IPO in February 2026 due to [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] market conditions — one of several tech IPOs shelved as AI disruption fears froze the window.

## Synopsis

Liftoff operates a demand-side platform (DSP) that helps mobile app developers acquire and retain users through programmatic advertising, leveraging machine learning to optimize ad spend across in-app, mobile web, and connected TV inventory. The company merged with Vungle (a mobile video ad network) in 2021 after [[Blackstone]] acquired both, creating a combined entity with ~$1B in annual revenue. The Liftoff+Vungle combination gives the platform both demand-side (user acquisition) and supply-side (ad monetization) capabilities, a vertical integration play similar to what [[AppLovin]] has built.

The pulled IPO is the key data point. Liftoff was reportedly targeting a $5B+ valuation when it shelved the offering in February 2026 as the [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] sell-off rattled tech multiples. The timing was particularly bad because [[AppLovin]] — Liftoff's closest public comparable — had seen its stock rise 700%+ in 2024-2025 on the back of its AI-powered advertising engine (AXON), creating favorable comps that evaporated when the market turned. Liftoff's delay signals that the ad tech IPO window, which briefly opened in late 2025 as AppLovin validated the category, has closed again. For [[Blackstone]], which took a controlling stake at a reported $1.5B valuation, the exit timeline extends. The company remains well-positioned in mobile ad tech but needs market conditions to stabilize before revisiting public markets.
