## Why Sloss matters for this vault

His prior remit — data-centre efficiency, AI diffusion, *infrastructure capital structures, long-term capacity and supply assurance* — is precisely the function Google is externalising in the [[Google-Blackstone TPU cloud venture 2026|May 2026 JV]]. The hire is functional continuity, not a side bet: Google is sending the person who built the internal version of this capability to run the externalised version. That makes the JV strategically core to Alphabet, not an experimental side-channel.

His SRE founding is also relevant context: SRE turned reliability engineering into a software discipline at Google scale, and the new JV will need exactly that kind of operational architecture if it is to scale from 500 MW (2027) to the multi-GW footprint implied by Blackstone's full $25B leveraged commitment.

---

## SRE background

The SRE practice Sloss founded is now industry standard at large software shops. The team writes its own software, owns the production stack, and treats reliability as a software-engineering problem rather than an operations one. The two SRE books — "Site Reliability Engineering" (2016) and "The Site Reliability Workbook" (2018) — are the canonical references; Sloss wrote the introduction to both.

The Google-Cloud-internal version of SRE underpins how [[Google Cloud]] markets its differentiation versus [[AWS]] and [[Azure]]. Sloss running an external TPU-cloud venture means that operational architecture goes with him.
