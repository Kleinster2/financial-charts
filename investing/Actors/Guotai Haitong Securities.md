# Guotai Haitong Securities

Chinese investment bank formed from the 2025 merger of Guotai Junan Securities and Haitong Securities — the largest brokerage consolidation in [[China]]'s history, creating one of the country's dominant securities firms.

---

## Synopsis

The Guotai Junan-Haitong merger was orchestrated as part of Beijing's push to build "national champion" financial institutions capable of competing with [[Goldman Sachs]], [[Morgan Stanley]], and [[JPMorgan Chase]] on global capital markets. Guotai Junan was already China's largest brokerage by net assets; absorbing Haitong (historically a top-five player) created a combined entity with massive retail brokerage, investment banking, asset management, and proprietary trading operations. The merger followed the pattern of state-directed consolidation seen across Chinese industries — when Beijing decides a sector needs fewer, larger players, it happens.

For markets, Guotai Haitong matters in two ways. First, it is the lead underwriter for major Chinese tech IPOs, including the [[Zhipu]] Shanghai Star Market listing — making it a gatekeeper for Western investor access to China's AI champions. Second, the merger itself is a signal about Beijing's intentions for the financial sector: the government wants 2-3 world-class securities firms, not dozens of mid-tier players cannibalizing each other's margins. This consolidation wave is investable through the survivors (Guotai Haitong, [[CICC]], [[CITIC Securities]]) and bearish for smaller brokerages facing margin compression. The combined entity's market share in A-share trading, IPO underwriting, and wealth management makes it a proxy for Chinese capital markets activity broadly.
