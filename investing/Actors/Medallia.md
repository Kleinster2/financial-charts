Medallia is a customer experience management software company, taken private by PE and now a poster child for software credit write-downs across the BDC sector. Loan values slashed by both [[FS KKR Capital Corp]] and [[Blackstone]] Secured Lending Fund in Q4 2025.

## Synopsis

Founded in 2001, Medallia built an enterprise platform for capturing and analyzing customer feedback — surveys, reviews, social media signals, and operational data — serving large enterprises in hospitality, retail, financial services, and telecom. The company went public in 2019 (peaking around $8B market cap) before being taken private by [[Thoma Bravo]] in a $6.4B LBO in October 2021, near the top of the software valuation cycle. The debt stack supporting the LBO was distributed across BDCs and CLOs at the peak of private credit's expansion into leveraged software buyouts.

By Q4 2025, Medallia had become the most visible example of the [[Private Credit Software Concentration]] problem. Both [[FS KKR Capital Corp]] and [[Blackstone]]'s Secured Lending Fund marked down their Medallia loan positions, signaling that the company's revenue trajectory could not support the leverage applied at the 2021 acquisition price. The write-down rippled through the BDC sector, contributing to the broader [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]]. For investors, Medallia illustrates the vintage risk in private credit: loans originated during peak-valuation, peak-liquidity conditions in 2021 carry embedded losses that only surface when the credit cycle turns. The CX software category itself faces secular headwinds as generative AI threatens to commoditize survey-based feedback collection.
