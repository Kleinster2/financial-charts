# Octopus Energy

[[UK]]'s largest household energy supplier (~8M households). Three arms: retail (energy supply), generation (Octopus Energy Generation, offshore wind), and technology (Kraken, £25M UK government investment). CEO Greg Jackson is a UK government adviser. Advocates embracing [[China|Chinese]] energy technology to reduce costs.

---

## Ming Yang partnership and UK-China energy debate

Octopus announced in Sep 2025 a partnership with [[Ming Yang Smart Energy]] to deploy ~6 GW of Ming Yang turbines in Britain. Proposed custom software layer for "highest levels of data protection and cyber security."

Zoisa North-Bond (CEO, Octopus Energy Generation, Jan 27 2026): *"We look to China and we see the sheer innovation and speed at which they have deployed wind models and how much they have invested in innovation -- and you cannot ignore that. When you speak to a Chinese hardware manufacturer, actually it could save 30 per cent on developing wind here in the UK if we could access Chinese wind turbines."*

On security concerns: *"It shouldn't be a blocker -- it's about how you design software that's fit for the market. We know we can design software combined with Chinese hardware that could allay some of those concerns."*

Greg Jackson traveled to Beijing as part of PM Keir Starmer's delegation (Jan 2026, first UK PM visit to China since Theresa May 2018). 60 British businesses accompanied Starmer, including [[HSBC]], [[GSK]], Jaguar Land Rover, Brompton Bikes, [[KPMG]], [[McLaren]].

The partnership was effectively blocked when [[UK]] rejected [[Ming Yang Smart Energy]]'s turbines on national security grounds (Mar 25, 2026).

*Source: FT, Rachel Millard, George Parker, Joe Leahy, Jan 27 2026*
