# Nordea Asset Management

Nordic asset manager and the asset management arm of [[Nordea]] Group, the largest financial-services group in the Nordic region. Active fund manager with a notable European equity and fixed-income franchise. Source of the [[Energy security as investment thesis|"sovereignty and autonomy"]] framing of the 2026 European renewables rally as articulated by CIO Kasper Elmgreen in the FT May 3 2026 piece.

## Why this matters for the vault

Elmgreen's framing in the May 3 FT piece is the cleanest articulation of the structural argument behind the 2026 renewables rally: that the agenda has shifted from [[ESG]] / climate-policy to sovereignty and autonomy in [[Europe]], and to AI data center power provisioning in the [[United States]]. The framing has been used as one of the source voices for the [[Energy security as investment thesis]] concept note.

Specifically, Elmgreen (May 3 2026 FT):
- On Europe: "The agenda around clean tech in the past was driven by an [environmental, social and governance] agenda and a political will to tackle climate change. Now, in Europe, it is about sovereignty and autonomy."
- On the US: "In the US it is about: how do we build enough power for the AI data centres? It's all hands on deck . . . they need more generation and they need to invest in the grid."
- On the Ukraine starting point: Successive crises since [[Ukraine war|Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine]] had sounded a "massive wake-up call" about energy autonomy, driving flows into clean energy stocks on the back of "a multiyear capex cycle in Europe."
