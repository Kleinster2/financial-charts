**Rebellions** — South Korea's largest AI chip startup. Inference accelerators challenging [[NVIDIA]]. Hired [[JPMorgan Chase|JPMorgan]] for KOSPI IPO (Mar 3, 2026). Korea's first AI chip unicorn after merging with [[SK Telecom]]'s Sapeon subsidiary (Dec 2024).

Rebellions is Korea's answer to the AI chip arms race — a startup betting that inference, not training, is where the market is heading. Founded in 2020 by ex-[[Morgan Stanley]] quant Park Sung-hyun and ex-[[IBM]] chip designer Oh Jin-wook, the company merged with [[SK Telecom]]'s Sapeon AI chip subsidiary in Dec 2024 to become Korea's first AI chip unicorn at $1.4B. The flagship REBEL-Quad chiplet delivers 1 PFLOP FP16 on [[Samsung]] 4nm with 144GB HBM3E — competitive on paper with [[NVIDIA]]'s inference lineup at a fraction of the power. Backed by [[Samsung]], [[Arm]], [[SK Hynix]], and [[KT Corp]], Rebellions has the Korean semiconductor ecosystem behind it. The IPO via [[JPMorgan Chase|JPMorgan]] (announced Mar 3, 2026) will be the first test of whether the market values a Korean inference chip challenger. The risk: $2M in 2023 revenue means commercial traction is still unproven, and NVIDIA's CUDA moat in inference remains deep.

## Competitive positioning vs [[NVIDIA]]

Focused on inference (not training) — a different market from NVIDIA's GPU-centric training dominance.

- 5x power efficiency vs NVIDIA A100
- Half the latency vs NVIDIA A2 in language model inference
- Beat global chip giants in MLPerf inference benchmarks (2023)
- CEO Park's stated ambition: outrival [[Qualcomm]] within a decade

## Investment case

Bull:
- Korea's first AI chip unicorn — Samsung + SK ecosystem backing
- REBEL-Quad is competitive on paper (1 PFLOP FP16, UCIe-Advanced, HBM3E)
- Inference market growing faster than training as AI deploys
- MLPerf benchmark results validate performance claims
- IPO at $1.4B+ reasonable vs peers ([[Cerebras]], [[Groq]], [[SambaNova]])

Bear:
- $2M revenue in 2023 — still proving commercial traction
- NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem moat in inference is deep
- Samsung 4nm yields historically worse than [[TSMC]]
- Small team competing against trillion-dollar incumbents
