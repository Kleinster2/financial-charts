# John Kartsonas

John Kartsonas is the founder and Managing Partner of [[Breakwave Advisors]], the asset manager behind the [[BDRY]] (dry bulk) and [[BWET]] (crude tanker) shipping ETFs. A former shipping analyst at Carlyle Group and Bear Stearns, Kartsonas launched Breakwave in 2016 with the explicit goal of creating the first retail-accessible instruments for direct freight-rate exposure. BDRY launched in March 2018; BWET launched May 3, 2023.

During the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]], Kartsonas became a frequent media commentator on tanker market dynamics as BWET rose more than 800% YTD, becoming the best-performing US-listed ETF of 2026. His commentary has appeared in Lloyd's List, Zacks ETF Spotlight, and the Washington Post. His consistent public message during the run has been cautionary: shipping rates mean-revert hard, and the 2021-22 BDRY round trip (+390% then -78%) is the base case for how these cycles resolve.

In the April 2026 Bloomberg flow-paradox story, Kartsonas framed the next leg as a possible shift from war disruption to fundamentals: concentrated tanker ownership, [[Russia]] / [[Iran]] sanctions shrinking the compliant fleet, and an aging global vessel base. He also emphasized the mechanical downside: if tanker rates fall, [[BWET]] falls with them.
