Experian is one of the world's three major consumer credit bureaus (alongside [[Equifax]] and [[TransUnion]]), holding credit data on over 1.4 billion consumers and 200 million businesses globally. Incorporated in Dublin and operationally headquartered in London, the company generates revenue through credit reporting, decision analytics, marketing services, and consumer direct products (credit monitoring, identity protection). North America accounts for roughly 60% of revenue, with Brazil (via Serasa Experian) and the UK as the next-largest markets.

The company was formed in 1996 through the merger of several credit data businesses, but its roots trace to the 1800s through predecessor entities. Experian's moat is its data — credit bureau data is nearly impossible to replicate, and the three-bureau oligopoly in the US market creates durable pricing power. The Serasa Experian subsidiary was for decades the dominant credit bureau in [[Brazil]], though [[Valid]]'s exit from the adjacent authentication market in February 2024 reshaped the competitive landscape.
