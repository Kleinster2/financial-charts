# Edward Herlihy

Senior partner at [[Wachtell Lipton]], protégé of [[Marty Lipton]], and the firm's largest individual revenue generator — the lawyer who, per the FT (May 2026), "has advised on virtually every major bank-sector transaction over the past three decades." Now 79, Herlihy remains the centre of gravity inside the firm, with much of its real authority concentrated around him despite the 2023 transition of executive committee co-chairs to [[Andrew Nussbaum]] and [[William Savitt]].

## Why he matters

Bank-sector consolidation in the US — the post-1990s wave that produced [[JPMorgan Chase]], [[Bank of America]], [[Citigroup]], and the post-2008 reshuffling — almost always passed through Wachtell Lipton with Herlihy as senior partner on one side. The 2008 [[Bank of America]] / [[Merrill Lynch]] transaction is a representative anchor; the breadth of his involvement across three decades is the structural reason Wachtell has the bank-sector franchise it does.

Within the firm, Herlihy is the load-bearing rainmaker that the [[Big Law transformation]] frame depends on: the partner whose continued presence anchors a multi-decade institutional knowledge base that scale-plus-pay firms (which retire partners by mid-60s) structurally cannot replicate. The 2025-26 lockstep modifications at Wachtell — promoting [[Jacob Kling]] (40, Herlihy's protégé) and David Lam (50) to M&A co-heads — are read in the FT account as an explicit succession plan to keep the bank-sector franchise inside the firm after Herlihy.
