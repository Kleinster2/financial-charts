**PAL Robotics** — Barcelona-based service robotics company building humanoid and mobile manipulation platforms for research and commercial use. Founded 2004. Part of PAL Technology Group (Abu Dhabi). Products include TIAGo (mobile manipulator), REEM (humanoid), Stockbot (retail inventory), ARI (social robot). Revenue ~$5.6M (2026). Open-source ROS contributor.

---

## Synopsis

PAL Robotics is one of Europe's oldest service robotics companies — founded in 2004, a full decade before the current humanoid wave. The company sells research platforms (TIAGo, REEM-C) to universities and commercial robots (Stockbot for retail inventory) to enterprises. Revenue is modest (~$5.6M) but real, unlike many robotics startups that burn through VC before generating sales.

The ownership structure is unusual: PAL is part of PAL Technology Group from Abu Dhabi, giving it a non-traditional funding base outside Silicon Valley VC. The company operates primarily on EU grant funding and product sales rather than venture rounds — total disclosed external funding is minimal (~$331K EU grant in 2021).

PAL's strategic position is as a platform provider for the European robotics research ecosystem. TIAGo is widely used in EU-funded research projects (PILLAR-Robots and others). The company contributes heavily to open-source ROS packages. This makes PAL a quiet infrastructure player rather than a headline-grabbing startup — low growth ceiling but sustainable.

## Investment case

Bull:
- One of Europe's most established robotics companies (20+ years)
- Real revenue ($5.6M) from product sales
- TIAGo is a standard research platform across European universities
- Open-source ROS contributions build ecosystem lock-in
- Abu Dhabi parent provides patient capital

Bear:
- Tiny revenue for a 20-year-old company
- European robotics market lags US/Asia in commercial adoption
- Research platforms are low-margin, low-volume
- No known VC backing limits growth ambitions
- Competing with cheaper Chinese platforms entering the market
