**Brian & Barry Group** is an Italian retail holding owned by the Zaccardi family (Carlo, Claudio, Roberto). Parent of [[Boggi Milano]]. Originally a childrenswear tailor shop in Monza; converted to luxury multibrand retail in 1985.

## Synopsis

The Zaccardi family built Brian & Barry from a modest Monza tailoring shop into a luxury retail holding whose primary asset -- [[Boggi Milano]] -- has become one of Italy's most internationally ambitious menswear brands. The acquisition of Boggi in 2003 for EUR13.2M was the transformative deal: it gave the family a scalable brand with international potential beyond the single-location multibrand retail model. The group also operates the Brian & Barry Building in central Milan, a multi-story concept store housing fashion, food, and lifestyle brands.

The investment relevance is through [[Boggi Milano]], which has expanded to 200+ stores across 40+ countries. Brian & Barry Group's private, family-controlled structure means the growth is self-funded or bank-financed -- no PE or IPO in sight. This is the classic Italian family holding model: tight ownership, conservative leverage, multi-generational horizon. The EUR13.2M Boggi acquisition price in 2003 now looks like the bargain of the decade in Italian fashion retail given the brand's current international footprint. The group's challenge is capital allocation: funding Boggi's expansion (7 new US shop-in-shops at [[Bloomingdale's]] alone in 2025) while maintaining the Milan property and other retail operations.
