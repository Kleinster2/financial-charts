**Pronto** — [[Anthony Levandowski]]'s autonomous vehicle company, focused on off-road industrial haulage (mining, quarries, construction). Camera-only AHS (autonomous haulage system), OEM-agnostic — retrofits existing [[Caterpillar]], [[Komatsu]], and other haul trucks for Level 4 autonomy. Founded 2018. Being acquired by [[Atoms]] ([[Travis Kalanick]]'s company) as of March 2026. Key customer: [[Heidelberg Materials]] (100+ autonomous trucks, exclusive N. America AV provider, 2M+ tons hauled).

---

## Synopsis

Pronto occupies an interesting niche in the autonomous vehicle landscape: instead of trying to solve the hardest version of the problem (on-road, mixed traffic, pedestrians), Levandowski pivoted to the "super-obvious application" of controlled off-road environments — quarries and mines where routes are predictable, speeds are low, and there are no pedestrians. The technology works: 2M+ tons hauled autonomously at the Lake Bridgeport quarry in Texas (N. America's first fully autonomous mixed-fleet quarry), and [[Heidelberg Materials]] committed to 100+ trucks globally. The [[Atoms]] acquisition reunites Levandowski with Kalanick — the two central figures from [[Uber]]'s self-driving car debacle — in a business that sidesteps the on-road regulatory and technical challenges that made that earlier venture so contentious.

## Evolution

- 2018: Founded by [[Anthony Levandowski]] and Ognen Stojanovski (COO, Stanford research scholar, formerly government relations at Otto). Started as on-road trucking ADAS. In October 2018, completed a coast-to-coast autonomous drive (SF to NYC, 3,100 miles, modified Toyota Prius) — entirely camera-based, no human intervention. First self-driving company to achieve this.
- 2019: Pivoted to off-road autonomy. Levandowski: "It became clear we couldn't do on-road autonomy on a timeframe that we want as a business." Controlled environments (quarries, mines) are a tractably simpler problem.
- Feb 2025: [[Heidelberg Materials]] deal announced — 100+ autonomous haul trucks deployed globally over 3 years, exclusive AV provider in North America. Follows 2023 pilot at Bridgeport, TX quarry.
- Jul 2025: Acquired SafeAI (competitor, 12-person team, had raised $38M Series B). Added ASIL D-certified safety framework (highest automotive safety integrity level) and multi-sensor capability (cameras + radar + lidar).
- Jan 2026: Lake Bridgeport site surpassed 2M+ tons hauled autonomously — N. America's first fully autonomous mixed-fleet quarry.
- Mar 13, 2026: [[Travis Kalanick]] announced [[Atoms]] acquisition on TBPN. Deal expected to close imminently. Kalanick was already the largest investor.

## Competitive landscape

| Company | Approach | Scale |
|---------|----------|-------|
