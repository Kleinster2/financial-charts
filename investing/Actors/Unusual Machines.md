Unusual Machines (Nasdaq: UMAC) — U.S. drone components and small-UAS company. [[Donald Trump Jr.]] is a shareholder and member of the advisory board, making Unusual Machines one of the earliest documented Trump-family drone-sector positions. Recurring co-investor in [[American Ventures]]-channeled defense-drone reverse mergers ([[XTEND]] / [[JFB Construction Holdings]] PIPE, [[Powerus]] / [[Aureus Greenway Holdings]] private placement). Won a Pentagon motor contract in 2025; a Powerus customer/supplier per existing vault notes.

## Role in the Dominari pipeline

Unusual Machines plays two simultaneous roles in the [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]]:
1. As an LP / co-investor: it participates in the strategic PIPEs and private placements that close concurrent with the reverse mergers (XTEND PIPE; AGH/Powerus placement)
2. As a commercial counterparty: it is reportedly a Powerus customer and/or supplier, so capital flows from American Ventures SPVs into Powerus and back through Unusual Machines

This circular structure compresses the boundary between LP, strategic, and customer in the same transaction stack — a feature the [[Trump family investment portfolio]] note flags as the "circular structure" of the drone quartet.

## Federal-policy alignment

The Pentagon motor contract win (3,500 units initial with 20,000 expected) preceded and supported the bull case for further Trump-family drone-sector investments. Combined with the [[FCC]] foreign-drone ban in late 2025 (which removed [[DJI]] from federal procurement) and Pentagon Secretary [[Pete Hegseth]]'s reclassification of small drones as "consumable" supplies, the policy stack created a procurement environment that has favored U.S. drone components players including Unusual Machines.
