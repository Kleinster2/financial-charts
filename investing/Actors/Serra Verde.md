Serra Verde Group — operator of the Pela Ema ionic-clay rare earth mine and processing plant in Goiás state, [[Brazil]]. One of the only non-Chinese producers of the four magnetic rare earths (neodymium, praseodymium, [[Dysprosium|dysprosium]], [[Terbium|terbium]]) plus yttrium at meaningful scale. Announced April 20, 2026 as an acquisition target of [[USA Rare Earth]] in a ~$3B transaction (~$2.5B in USAR stock + $300M cash), which converts Serra Verde from an independent producer into the heavy-rare-earth leg of USAR's vertically integrated supply chain.

## Synopsis

The Serra Verde asset has always been strategically valuable out of proportion to its size. Ionic-clay deposits are the only geology outside [[China]] that economically produces [[Dysprosium|dysprosium]] and [[Terbium|terbium]] — the two heavy rare earths most critical to high-coercivity neodymium magnets for EV traction motors, wind turbines, and precision-guided munitions. Pela Ema targets ~5,000 tonnes/year of rare earth oxide and came online with Denham Capital–led financing plus US government–linked debt. The April 2026 [[USA Rare Earth]] acquisition reframes it: it is no longer a standalone Brazilian producer but the upstream feed for USAR's Oklahoma magnet line, operating under a politically connected US parent funded by the [[CHIPS Program Office]] and a [[Cantor Fitzgerald]]-placed $1.5B PIPE.

The transaction also matters for [[Brazil]]. It is the largest inbound US rare-earth mining acquisition in Latin America, shifts a producing critical-minerals asset from independent ownership to a US-listed parent with federal equity participation, and tests how [[Brazil]]'s mining regulator CPRM and foreign-investment framework handle strategic-commodity transfers to a Commerce Department–backed buyer. See [[Rare earth leverage]] for the broader US–China rare earth competition framing and [[India-Brazil critical minerals MOU 2026-02]] for the parallel diplomatic track.

## Evolution

- **Pre-2026 — standalone producer**: Pela Ema ramps under Denham Capital–led ownership, secures ~$465M in US government–linked financing; positioned as the flagship Brazilian critical-minerals project and a central piece of the US–Brazil supply-chain diversification push.
- January 18, 2026 — initial US financing reporting: Mining.com / [[Financial Times]] detail the ~$465M US package, framing Serra Verde as the linchpin for non-Chinese heavy-rare-earth supply.
- April 20, 2026 — USA Rare Earth acquisition announced: USAR agrees to acquire Serra Verde Group for ~$3B (~$2.5B in USAR stock + $300M cash). [[Barbara Humpton]] frames the combined company as "the global champion in rare earths." Serra Verde's Thras Moraitis characterizes the deal as "the defining Western player in one of the most critical industries of our time." Closing subject to Brazilian regulatory approval.
