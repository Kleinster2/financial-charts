# LIXIL

Japanese building materials and housing equipment conglomerate, and the world's largest manufacturer of water technology and housing products by revenue. Listed on the [[Tokyo Stock Exchange]] (5938.T).

## Synopsis

LIXIL sits at the intersection of two structural themes: Japan's aging housing stock (requiring renovation) and global premiumization of bathroom/kitchen fixtures. The company assembled its current form through aggressive M&A -- acquiring [[Grohe]] (German luxury faucets) in 2014 and American Standard (mass-market fixtures) in the same deal, paying ~$4B total for the two brands. This gave LIXIL a global footprint spanning luxury (Grohe), mid-market (American Standard), and domestic Japanese (INAX) segments. The INAX brand commands ~23% of Japan's toilet market, second to [[Toto]]'s ~60%.

The investment case hinges on whether LIXIL can extract the margin improvement that justified the Grohe/American Standard acquisitions. The company has struggled with execution -- multiple CEO changes, a 2019 boardroom crisis that ousted CEO Kinya Seto, and persistent underperformance versus [[Toto]] on operating margins. The current strategy under CEO Kinya Seto (reinstated) focuses on premiumization and digital transformation of the bathroom/kitchen space. For a Japanese industrials investor, LIXIL trades at a discount to [[Toto]] on most multiples, reflecting the market's skepticism about management stability and M&A integration.
