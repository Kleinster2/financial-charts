# Kenta Kon

Kenta Kon is the incoming CEO and President of [[Toyota]], effective April 2026, succeeding [[Koji Sato]]. He previously served as Toyota's CFO, where he oversaw the company's financial strategy during a period of record profitability driven by hybrid vehicle demand and yen weakness. His elevation from CFO to CEO signals Toyota's board prioritizing capital allocation discipline and shareholder returns as the company navigates the transition from internal combustion to electrification.

Kon's appointment comes as [[Toyota]] faces strategic questions about its EV timeline. The company bet heavily on hybrids and hydrogen fuel cells while rivals like [[BYD]] and [[Tesla]] scaled battery EVs. That bet paid off in the near term — Toyota's hybrid sales surged as consumers balked at BEV prices — but the company still needs a credible pure-EV strategy for markets like [[China]] and the [[EU]] where regulatory mandates are tightening. As a finance-background CEO, Kon is expected to take a more returns-focused approach to EV investment than a product-engineering leader might.
