# Nordex

German wind turbine manufacturer, Frankfurt-listed. World's #2 onshore wind turbine maker outside [[China]]. Majority-owned by [[Acciona]] since the 2016 merger that folded Acciona Windpower into Nordex Group. Direct expression of the [[Energy security as investment thesis|European energy-security capex cycle]] — onshore wind is the fastest-deploying renewable category and the most exposed to [[EU]] sovereignty-driven order books.

## Why Nordex matters

The European energy-security thesis splits into offshore wind (long lead time, [[Orsted|Ørsted]] is the lead-buyer expression) and onshore wind (shorter project cycle, fastest path to operational autonomy). Nordex is the cleanest equity expression of the onshore leg outside [[China]]. With production sites distributed across six countries and an installed base of 29 GW, the company's order book translates EU sovereignty rhetoric into operational manufacturing capacity faster than any offshore developer can.

The 2016 [[Acciona]] merger gave Nordex the financial backing and global project pipeline of a top-tier Iberian operator while preserving the manufacturing standalone-listed entity. The capital structure means Acciona's renewable developer arm uses Nordex turbines while Nordex retains independent customer relationships across [[Europe]], the Americas, and emerging markets.

## 2026 performance — energy-security re-rating

Per FT, May 3 2026 report on clean-energy fund flows: Nordex was the YTD leader in the FT-identified European renewables cohort at +67%. The DB confirms +66.6% YTD as of May 3 close (€48.52), with subsequent pullback to +55.5% as of May 15 (€45.28). The May moderation tracks the broader renewables-basket consolidation while the cohort retains its YTD advantage over [[SPY]].

The rally is driven by the same set of factors the FT identifies for the cohort:
- Multiyear capex cycle in [[Europe]] driven by [[Energy security as investment thesis|energy-security framing]] rather than [[ESG]] / climate-policy framing
- [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran war]] oil-price shock making fossil-fuel imports more expensive and politically fraught
- Onshore wind's relatively short project cycle (1-3 years from FID to operation) gives it the fastest exposure to autonomy-driven order acceleration

## Product portfolio

| Platform | Class | Use case |

| Delta4000 | 3-5 MW+ class onshore | Current-generation flagship platform; large-rotor designs for low-wind sites |
| N163 | ~5.7 MW onshore | Specific Delta4000 model; widely deployed in [[Germany]] tenders |
| N175 | 6.X MW onshore | Largest current Nordex model |

The product focus is onshore — Nordex does not compete in offshore wind, leaving that to [[Vestas]], [[Siemens Energy|Siemens Gamesa]], and [[Ming Yang Smart Energy]]. The strategic implication is that Nordex's order book is more diversified across smaller projects in more countries, which insulates it from single-project schedule risk that has hurt [[Orsted|Ørsted]] and the offshore-developer cohort.

---

## Geographic exposure

Six manufacturing countries: Germany, Brazil, India, Mexico, Poland, Spain. Recent 2026 expansion focus includes [[South Africa]] (Feb 2026 announcement) and ongoing German tender wins (40 turbines, 279 MW for wpd in nine 2026 projects).

The geographic spread matters because tariffs and local-content rules vary by jurisdiction. Nordex's Mexico and Brazil plants serve the Americas without the import friction US-listed wind suppliers face; the Spain and Poland plants serve the EU sovereignty cycle.
