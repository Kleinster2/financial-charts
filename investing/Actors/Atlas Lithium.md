**Atlas Lithium** (NASDAQ: ATLX) — US-listed developer of the **Neves Project** in [[Brazil]]'s Vale do Jequitinhonha lithium belt. Holds 539 km² — the largest lithium-concentrate footprint in [[Brazil]]. The project's definitive feasibility study produced the lowest cost curves and fastest paybacks of any development-stage Brazilian lithium name, and Neves was named in the Japan-US Critical Minerals partnership Joint Fact Sheet (April 2026) as the only [[Brazil]]-based lithium project on the list.

## Neves Project — status and timeline

| Milestone | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| DFS | Completed (145% IRR) |
| Permits | All secured — installation, mining concession, water use rights, vegetation clearance |
| Processing plant | **Delivered to [[Brazil]]** — ready for assembly and pre-operational testing |
| Project management contract | Final-stage contracting early 2026 |
| Phase 1 design | **150,000 tpy modular DMS (Dense Media Separation) plant** |
| Phase 2 | Expansion plans under development |
| First production | Targeted after Phase 1 commissioning |

The plant was shipped from South Africa and arrived at the Port of Santos in March 2025; assembly is the binding near-term milestone. DMS processing is the same low-cost, low-ESG-impact approach [[Sigma Lithium]] uses at Grota do Cirilo — spodumene concentrate output, no chemical reagents, no tailings dam.

## US-Japan critical minerals partnership

Announced April 2026 via the **Joint Fact Sheet for Japan-U.S. Critical Minerals Project Cooperation**. Atlas was named as the **only Brazil-based lithium project** on the list — a significant diplomatic flag for both companies and countries. The governments of [[Japan]] and the [[United States]] are contemplating financial backing for Neves development.

This matters for three reasons:

1. **Capital cost de-risking** — Government loan guarantees on a 145%-IRR project push equity-returns extraordinarily high.
2. **Off-take positioning** — Japan-US backing typically comes paired with off-take preferences for allied battery supply chains.
3. **Signal to [[Brazil]]'s mineral-diplomacy calculus** — The Lula government's February 2026 [[India]] MOU was framed as a "peer pilot" before engaging asymmetric partners. The Atlas-Neves inclusion suggests the US is positioning regardless of Brazil's formal negotiating sequence — identifying specific projects rather than country-level agreements.

### Mitsui & Co. investment

**US$30 million** investment from [[Mitsui]] & Co. (Japanese trading house). The capital is tied to **off-take agreements for both Phase 1 and Phase 2** of lithium-concentrate production. Mitsui's involvement is the pattern Atlas-style US-listed developers are using to raise growth capital: concentrate off-take commitments from Japanese/Korean converters or EV OEMs, matched to equity or convertible capital.

## Business model — "low-capex, high-IRR" development

Atlas differs from [[Sigma Lithium]] operationally:

| Dimension | Atlas Lithium | Sigma Lithium |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Scale | Phase 1 150 ktpa → expanding | Phase 1 270 ktpa → Phase 2 520 ktpa |
| Cost | $489/t production | $440/t CIF China |
| Capital intensity | Modular DMS — lower capex | Larger integrated plant |
| Partner structure | Mitsui off-take, Japan-US gov flag | BNDES loan, production-backed revolver |
| ESG positioning | No tailings, no reagents | "Quintuple Zero" branded |
| Stage | Development (first production targeting) | Producing since April 2023 |

The cost differential ($489/t vs $440/t CIF) favors Sigma at China-delivered basis, but Atlas's smaller modular footprint allows faster Phase 1 commissioning and simpler permitting. Both are beneficiaries of the Lithium Valley narrative and the Apr 16 commodity rally.

## Risks

- **Construction and ramp** — Phase 1 not yet producing; execution risk through 2026.
- **Spodumene market concentration** — Atlas exports concentrate; Chinese converters remain the dominant buyers of Brazilian hard-rock output (see [[AMG]]'s concentrate-to-China routing).
- **Community and regulatory** — Same Jequitinhonha environment as [[Sigma Lithium]] (STF case, water-supply allegations, quilombola consultation). Atlas has secured all major permits but operates in a contested landscape.
- **Small-cap liquidity and dilution** — NASDAQ: ATLX is a micro-cap; capital-raise dependency on commodity-price environment.
