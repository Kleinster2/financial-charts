# Cape of Good Hope

**Cape of Good Hope** is the southern tip of Africa used as an alternative shipping route when the Suez/Red Sea corridor is disrupted. Rerouting adds 10-14 days for Middle East-to-Europe voyages. Not a bottleneck but a cost and time penalty. Saw surge traffic during the 2024 Houthi disruption and the 2026 [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure.

## May 2026 route-comparison read

The FT's May 24 visual story contrasts the Chinese-owned BYD Shenzhen taking the faster [[Suez Canal]] route to Europe in July-August 2025 with [[Wallenius Wilhelmsen]]'s Manon taking the longer Cape route in March-April 2026. By day 30, the Suez vessel was already in the Mediterranean while Manon was still off West Africa.

That route comparison is the cleanest visual expression of the resilience trade-off. The Cape route reduces exposure to [[Bab el-Mandeb]] and some piracy/security risks, but it absorbs time, fuel, vessel capacity, and working capital. The FT also notes average daily transits by the Cape route rose 77% in 2024 from a year earlier as Red Sea disruption pushed carriers away from Suez.
