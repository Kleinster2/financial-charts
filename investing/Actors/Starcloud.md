## Why Starcloud matters

Space-based compute solves two constraints simultaneously: power (unlimited solar in orbit) and cooling (radiative cooling in vacuum). As AI inference demand drives terrestrial data center power consumption toward unsustainable levels, orbital compute becomes a serious infrastructure thesis rather than science fiction. In-Q-Tel backing signals intelligence community interest in sovereign, hard-to-disrupt compute infrastructure.

## The thesis

Terrestrial AI data centers face compounding constraints:
- **Power:** Hyperscaler capex heading toward $650B in 2026, with ~30% going to energy
- **Cooling:** GPU clusters require 30kW+ per rack, liquid cooling
- **Land/permitting:** Data center buildout facing local opposition, grid constraints
- **Sovereignty:** Ground-based infrastructure vulnerable to physical/cyber attack

Space-based data centers potentially solve power (solar), cooling (radiative in vacuum), and sovereignty (orbital assets harder to target). The tradeoff: latency (orbital round-trip) and launch costs.

Best suited for: batch inference, training runs, non-latency-sensitive workloads. Not suited for: real-time consumer inference, low-latency trading.
