**Crusoe Energy** — AI cloud powered by stranded natural gas. Blue Owl $15B JV partner.

Crusoe is the power-first AI-infrastructure developer in the vault: it began as a stranded-gas [[Bitcoin]] miner, then pivoted into GPU cloud and hyperscale data-center development as AI compute became the higher-value load. The bull case is cheap power and project-finance access through [[Blue Owl]]; the bear case is site concentration, permitting, emissions scrutiny, and hyperscaler tenancy risk.

---

## Why Crusoe matters

Crusoe solves two problems at once — stranded gas monetization and AI compute:

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Model | Capture flared natural gas → power AI data centers |
| Advantage | Cheap, stranded power source |
| Blue Owl JV | $15B for Abilene, [[Texas]] facility |
| Scale | 1.2 GW planned capacity |

---

## Business model

The insight:
- [[Oil]] wells flare (burn) natural gas that's uneconomical to pipe
- Crusoe puts generators + data centers at well sites
- Converts waste gas to AI compute
- Cheaper than grid power, reduces emissions

## Evolution

- Started with [[Bitcoin]] mining (2018)
- Pivoted to AI/ML workloads
- Now building hyperscale AI facilities

## Abilene tenant saga (Mar 2026)

The ~700MW expansion site adjacent to [[Oracle]]/[[OpenAI]]'s [[Project Stargate|Stargate]] campus — developed by Crusoe with [[Blue Owl]] financing — was originally intended for Oracle/OpenAI but both walked after negotiations stalled ([[Bloomberg]], Mar 6). [[NVIDIA]] had put down a $150M deposit on the future capacity and initially approached [[Meta]] as a replacement tenant. Ultimately, [[Microsoft]] signed the lease ([[Bloomberg]], Mar 24).

This validates Crusoe's position as a landlord/developer rather than just a compute provider — the Abilene site attracted three different hyperscaler-class tenants in sequence. The 700MW lease with Microsoft is separate from the existing 1.2 GW Stargate campus that Oracle continues to operate.
