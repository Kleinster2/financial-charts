**Wynn Resorts** — premium casino operator with properties in Las Vegas, Macau, and Boston. Building the first casino resort in the [[UAE]] at [[Al Marjan Island]] ($5.1B). Market cap ~$10B. ~72% owner of [[Wynn Macau]] (1128.HK). Largest shareholder: [[Tilman Fertitta]] (~12.3%). Founder [[Steve Wynn]] forced out in 2018 over sexual misconduct allegations.

---

## Synopsis

Wynn occupies the premium tier of global casino operators — fewer properties than [[MGM Resorts]] or [[Las Vegas Sands]], but higher-end positioning and industry-leading design standards. The company runs four operating segments: Wynn Las Vegas + Encore (FY2025: $2.57B revenue, 35% EBITDAR margin), Wynn Palace Cotai ($2.31B, 30%), Wynn Macau Peninsula ($1.41B, 29%), and Encore Boston Harbor ($847M, 28%). Total FY2025 revenue: $7.14B, adjusted property EBITDAR: $2.22B.

The growth catalyst is the UAE. Wynn Al Marjan Island in Ras Al Khaimah — a 70-story, $5.1B integrated resort — holds the first commercial gaming license ever issued in the Gulf states. The UAE established the General Commercial Gaming Regulatory Authority (GCGRA) in September 2023 as a federal entity specifically to enable this. Opening target: spring 2027. The project briefly paused in February 2026 during the Iran conflict (debris from an intercepted drone fell ~15km from the site) but resumed quickly. A second resort plot (1.49M sq ft) has been secured with exclusive Wynn operating rights, though no timeline exists. If the UAE model works, it opens a new geographic category for casino operators.

The balance sheet is the risk. Total debt: $10.55B. Total equity: negative $1.1B. That's the legacy of the Boston Harbor sale-leaseback ($1.7B to [[Realty Income]], Dec 2022), heavy borrowing for Macau and UAE construction, and the capital destruction from the Steve Wynn departure. The founder's forced resignation in February 2018 over decades of sexual misconduct allegations triggered $176M+ in cumulative penalties and settlements, a board overhaul led by ex-wife [[Elaine Wynn]], and a complete reputational reset under CEO Craig Billings. The stock has never recovered to its 2018 pre-scandal levels.

## Evolution

The story of Wynn Resorts is the story of a visionary founder who invented the modern luxury casino, lost his company to a #MeToo scandal, and left behind both an irreplaceable design sensibility and a balance sheet stretched across three continents.

- **1989-2000**: [[Steve Wynn]] opened The Mirage in 1989 ($630M, financed largely with [[Michael Milken]] junk bonds), inventing the modern Las Vegas Strip. Treasure Island followed (1993, $450M), then the Bellagio (1998, $1.6B) — the most opulent casino-hotel of its era. In 2000, Wynn sold Mirage Resorts to MGM Grand for ~$6.4B. Five weeks before the sale closed, he bought the Desert Inn for $270M as the site for his next project. He was 58, had just sold one of the most valuable casino companies in the world, and was starting over.

- **2002**: Wynn Resorts IPO'd on NASDAQ October 25, 2002. The thesis was simple: Steve Wynn would build the world's most luxurious casino resort, again.

- **2005-2008**: Wynn Las Vegas opened April 28, 2005 ($2.7B — the most expensive resort ever at the time). Encore followed December 22, 2008 ($2.3B, 48-story tower, 2,034 all-suite rooms). Combined: ~4,750 rooms on the Strip's most valuable real estate.

- **2006-2016**: Wynn Macau opened September 5, 2006 ($1.2B) — among the first truly integrated Las Vegas-style resorts in Macau. Wynn Macau Ltd IPO'd on the HKEx in October 2009. Wynn Palace opened on Cotai August 22, 2016 ($4.2B), cementing the company's premium position in Asia's largest gaming market.

- **January 2018**: The Wall Street Journal published an investigation into decades of sexual misconduct allegations against Steve Wynn. He resigned as chairman and CEO on February 6, 2018. The consequences cascaded: a $20M fine from the Nevada Gaming Commission (largest in state history at the time) and a $35M penalty from Massachusetts. Wynn sold his entire ~12% stake for ~$2.1B. He later paid a $10M personal fine to Nevada regulators in 2023 and permanently exited the gaming industry. Cumulative penalties and settlements: $176M+.

- **2018-2019**: Ex-wife [[Elaine Wynn]] won a proxy fight ("Restore Wynn" campaign), becoming the largest individual shareholder at ~9.5M shares. Matt Maddox became CEO, inheriting the reputational crisis. Encore Boston Harbor opened June 23, 2019 ($2.6B) — it has since generated ~$3.4B in cumulative GGR and over $1B in Massachusetts state taxes.

- **2020-2021**: COVID collapsed revenue from $6.61B (2019) to $2.10B, producing a $2.07B net loss. Wynn Interactive was created (October 2020) around the $80M BetBull acquisition, and a SPAC merger with [[Austerlitz Acquisition Corp]] (Bill Foley) was announced at $3.2B implied valuation. The SPAC deal was terminated in November 2021 — Maddox cited unsustainable customer acquisition costs across online sports betting.

- **2022-2023**: Craig Billings became CEO February 1, 2022. The Encore Boston Harbor sale-leaseback to [[Realty Income]] for $1.7B closed in December 2022 (30-year triple-net lease, $100M initial rent, 1.75% annual escalation). Macau concession renewed for 10 years with MOP 16.5B (~$2.2B) non-gaming investment commitment. WynnBET was shut down across 8 states in August 2023 — the online experiment was over.

- **2024-2025**: [[Tilman Fertitta]] became the largest individual shareholder, accumulating ~12.3% (13M shares) by April 2025. He's a passive investor — required to divest operating casino roles due to his appointment as US Ambassador to Italy under Trump. Elaine Wynn died at 82 in April 2025.

- **2025-2026**: The Wynn Al Marjan Island tower topped out at 283m (70 floors) in December 2025. Construction paused briefly in February-March 2026 during the Iran conflict but resumed with enhanced security. Target opening: spring 2027.

## Properties

### Las Vegas
**Wynn Las Vegas + Encore** — ~4,750 combined rooms. FY2025 revenue: $2.57B, EBITDAR: $902M (35.1% margin). The highest-margin segment.

### Macau (via Wynn Macau Ltd, ~72% owned)
- **Wynn Palace** (Cotai) — opened 2016, $4.2B, 1,706 rooms. FY2025 revenue: $2.31B
- **Wynn Macau** (Peninsula) — opened 2006, $1.2B, 1,010 rooms. FY2025 revenue: $1.41B

10-year concession (Jan 2023 – Dec 2032). 570 tables, 1,100 machines allocated. MOP 16.5B (~$2.2B) non-gaming investment commitment.

### Boston
**Encore Boston Harbor** — opened June 2019, $2.6B, Everett MA. FY2025 revenue: $847M. Real estate sold to [[Realty Income]] for $1.7B (Dec 2022). 30-year lease at $100M/yr initial rent, 1.75% annual escalation.

### UAE (under construction)
**Wynn Al Marjan Island** — Ras Al Khaimah. 70-story tower (283m structural, 352m with spire). 1,530 rooms, 275 tables, 2,000+ machines, 225,000 sq ft gaming floor, 24 restaurants, theater, nightclub. Total investment: $5.1B. First commercial gaming license in the Gulf states (GCGRA, October 2024). Target: spring 2027. ~18,000 construction workers on site. Second plot (1.49M sq ft) secured with exclusive operating rights.
