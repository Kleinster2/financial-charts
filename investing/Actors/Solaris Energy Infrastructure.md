**Solaris Energy Infrastructure** — Houston-based distributed-power provider (NYSE: SEI), the listed pure-play on behind-the-meter mobile turbine power for AI data centers. Formerly Solaris Oilfield Infrastructure, transformed by the September 2024 acquisition of Mobile Energy Rentals. Not to be confused with [[Solaris]], the Polish bus manufacturer.

One-line read: SEI is the speed-to-power landlord of the AI buildout — it leases gas-turbine capacity faster than grids can interconnect, anchored by [[xAI]]'s Colossus — because the interconnection queue is the binding constraint ([[Power constraints]]), with the bet being whether 88% single-customer concentration converts into a diversified contracted fleet before grid supply and permit regimes close the window.

---

## Synopsis

SEI sells time. Its mobile gas turbines deliver powered capacity in months while utility interconnections take years — the same arbitrage [[BYOP]] describes, packaged as a rental fleet with multi-year contracts. The model was bolted onto a small oilfield-logistics company in one stroke: the $200M Mobile Energy Rentals acquisition (announced July 9, 2024; closed September 11, 2024) brought the turbine fleet, the [[xAI]] relationship, and a new name. Power Solutions revenue went from $38.6M (2024) to $333.5M (2025); average deployed capacity from ~230 MW to ~630 MW; group EBITDA from $100.0M to $219.7M. By Q1 2026 the fleet averaged ~910 MW earning revenue and the company guided pro forma generation capacity to ~3,100 MW with recent and planned additions — up from the ~2,200 MW by early 2028 in the FY2025 10-K, filed only months earlier.

The concentration is the story's other half: a single data-center customer — [[xAI]], via the consolidated Stateline Power JV — accounted for 88% of FY2025 Power Solutions segment revenue (96% in FY2024). Diversification is in motion: a Q1 2026 rental agreement for >500 MW with "an affiliate of an investment grade, global technology company and industry leader in the evolving artificial intelligence computer space" (10-K language; counterparty unnamed), and a third contract (April 24, 2026) for >600 MW on a 10-year term with a 5-year extension option. Tenors run two to seven years on the existing book; the April contract's decade term marks the shift from rental fleet toward contracted-IPP economics.

The bear file is unusually specific. [[Morpheus Research]]'s March 17, 2025 short report ("How A Crumbling Texas Oilfield Services Company Gambled It All On A Convicted Felon And The World's Richest Man") alleged MER was a ~$2.5M-revenue equipment lessor "based out of a condo with zero employees, no turbines"; that MER co-owner John Tuma was previously convicted of environmental crimes and of lying under oath; and that the Memphis turbines operated without air permits. The stock fell 16.9% that day. A securities class action (Pirello v. Solaris, 4:25-cv-01455, S.D. Tex., filed March 28, 2025) followed; the company calls it without merit. What happened since cuts both ways: the EPA closed the "non-road engine" permit loophole (January 2026) that enabled the Memphis deployment speed — daily fines up to $10K per violation now apply and new entrants cannot replicate the approach — while the contract book tripled and earnings compounded.

## Stateline Power JV (the xAI anchor)

- Structure: Stateline Power, LLC — 50.1% SEI / 49.9% MZX Tech LLC (an affiliate of CTC, the [[xAI]] data-center entity). Consolidated as a VIE; formed April 28, 2025. SEI contributed ~$86.4M of non-cash assets and pre-funded expenses; the customer side ~$86M cash.
- Contract: up to ~900 MW of generation capacity supporting the customer's data center; initial term seven years; revenue commencing 2026.
- Financing: $518.5M project facility for the ~900 MW buildout ($186.0M drawn at FY2025 year-end; $332.5M to draw through 2026 on equipment deliveries); a $550M senior secured term-loan term sheet was executed at formation.
- Scale: Stateline is expected to hold ~41% of Power Solutions generation assets — the 10-K explicitly flags it as concentrating the business in off-grid AI power demand.
- Operating context: at [[Colossus]] (Memphis/Southaven), the turbines bridge until grid substations energize, after which they shift to backup — see [[xAI]] for the [[TVA]] connection and substation timeline, and [[Power constraints]] for the pattern.

## The short case, on the record

[[Morpheus Research]] (Mar 17, 2025) — first report from the Hindenburg-alumni shop: MER's pre-acquisition substance (~$2.5M revenue, condo office, no employees or turbines per the report), John Tuma's prior conviction for environmental crimes and lying under oath plus involvement in an $800M turbine scandal with bid-rigging allegations, single-customer dependence, and unpermitted Memphis turbines. Stock -16.9% on publication. Pirello v. Solaris (4:25-cv-01455, S.D. Tex.) asserts §10(b)/20(a) claims over the MER acquisition disclosures; SEI calls the suit without merit and is defending. The 10-K now carries an explicit short-selling-strategies risk factor. The permit thread resolved structurally: the EPA closed the "non-road engine" loophole in January 2026 — existing deployments pay up to $10K/day per violation under new permits, and the speed trick is unrepeatable, which cuts against new entrants more than against the installed fleet.

## Analysis

Three tensions hold the structure. First, concentration against duration: 88% of power revenue from one customer whose own credit is venture-stage, but the diversification contracts (>500 MW investment-grade counterparty, >600 MW on a 10-year term) are arriving faster than the bear case assumed — the mix shift, not the headline growth, is the thing to track. Second, speed against permanence: the entire franchise monetizes grid delay, so SEI's pricing power decays exactly as interconnection queues clear or as permit regimes (post-loophole) slow mobile deployments; the 10-year April 2026 contract reads as management converting a temporary arbitrage into contracted annuity before the window narrows. Third, growth against the balance sheet: $902.5M of converts plus a project facility funded a fleet that must roughly triple (910 MW → 3,100 MW) on schedule; both converts in the money means the funding cost was cheap but the share count is the release valve.

## What to watch

- Customer concentration % — Power segment revenue share of the xAI/Stateline complex (88% FY2025). Falling toward 50% as contracts 2-3 commence validates the contracted-IPP story; sticky >80% keeps SEI a single-credit derivative of [[xAI]].
- Capacity guidance vs deliveries — the ~3,100 MW pro forma path (10-K said ~2,200 MW by early 2028 only months earlier). Further raises signal order momentum; slippage on turbine deliveries breaks the growth multiple.
- Sequential EBITDA profile — Q2 guide $83-93M and Q3 $80-95M sit below Q1's $95.1M; clean explanation (timing, JV consolidation) versus utilization softness decides whether Q1 was a peak or a step.
- Stateline transition mechanics — substation energization at Memphis/Southaven shifting turbines to backup; where the freed mobile capacity redeploys, and at what rates, is the first test of fleet reusability.
- Converts and overhang — 2031s ($57.20 conversion) settlement intentions and any registration-rights selling from the ~28% Class A block; both put supply into strength.
- Pirello docket — motion-to-dismiss outcome; survival into discovery would re-arm the Morpheus file.
