# Pekingnology

China-focused Substack publication run by Zichen Wang, formerly a [[Xinhua]] journalist, focused on high-fidelity translation of Chinese policy texts, speeches, and academic lectures for an English-language audience. The publication's signature is publishing primary-source Chinese policy material — Politburo readouts, Party academic lectures, regulatory documents — translated with minimal editorial overlay, rather than the more common Western-analyst-paraphrasing model. The May 15, 2026 translation of [[Li Shi]]'s lecture on [[Common Prosperity]] is the primary mainstream-academic source for the 2026 reframing of that policy framework.

## Why it matters

For a vault that builds on primary-source verification (per [docs/research-workflow.md](../../docs/research-workflow.md) "Vault as Navigation, Primary Sources for Citation"), Pekingnology is structurally valuable because it surfaces material that would otherwise require Mandarin reading capability. The May 2026 [[Li Shi]] translation is an example: the original lecture was at [[Peking University National School of Development]] and would not have been accessible in English without this translation.

Treat Pekingnology output as primary-source-equivalent translation; the editorial overlay is minimal, but the selection of what to translate is itself an editorial choice that should be considered (inference).
