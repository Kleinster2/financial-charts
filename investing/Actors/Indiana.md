# Indiana

US state in the Midwest, population ~6.8M, with an economy anchored by advanced manufacturing, life sciences, and agriculture. Home to [[Eli Lilly]]'s global headquarters in Indianapolis, making the state a central node in the [[GLP-1 receptor agonists]] supply chain and regulatory landscape.

## Synopsis

Indiana's relevance to the investing vault runs through two channels: [[Eli Lilly]] and pharmaceutical regulation. Lilly is the state's largest company by market cap and one of the largest private employers, with its Indianapolis campus serving as the global headquarters for [[Mounjaro]] and [[Zepbound]] production. The state's economy benefits directly from Lilly's $9B+ manufacturing expansion across Indiana (including the $3.7B Lebanon, IN facility for injectable drugs), which is creating thousands of construction and permanent jobs. Indiana's GDP of ~$465B makes it a mid-tier state economically, but the Lilly concentration means its fortunes are increasingly tied to the GLP-1 cycle.

On the regulatory side, Indiana passed compounding legislation aligned with federal GLP-1 guidelines, establishing a state reporting system for compounding pharmacies and a medical spa registration framework starting January 2027. This matters because compounding pharmacies have been a major source of off-brand semaglutide and tirzepatide, and Indiana's approach — regulation rather than prohibition — positions the state as a test case for how states manage the tension between branded pharma interests ([[Eli Lilly]], [[Novo Nordisk]]) and patient access to cheaper compounded versions. The regulatory outcome in Indiana will likely influence other states' approaches.
