# Jordan Schneider

Founder and editor-in-chief of [[ChinaTalk]], a hybrid think tank and media outlet covering [[China]], [[US-China relations]], AI, and semiconductors. Supplies non-partisan analysis to audiences in Silicon Valley and Washington, with 70,000+ free subscribers on [[Substack]]. Host of the ChinaTalk Podcast — 250+ episodes of interview-based programming on Chinese tech, economics, and politics, also distributed via Lawfare. Previously worked at [[Rhodium Group]] and [[Bridgewater]]. Holds an MA in economics from Peking University's Yenching Academy and a BA in history from Yale.

Concurrent think-tank affiliations: Adjunct Senior Fellow at the [[Center for a New American Security]] (CNAS), Fellow at the [[Andrew W. Marshall Foundation]] (defense / net-assessment lineage), and contributor at the [[Foreign Policy Research Institute]] (FPRI). The CNAS + Andrew Marshall Foundation affiliations are the connective tissue between his China-tech analysis and the US defense / strategy establishment — ChinaTalk content reaches Pentagon and Congressional readers via these institutional channels, not just Substack distribution.

Publishes original analysis alongside podcast interviews with policymakers, researchers, and operators on Chinese industrial policy, export controls, AI chip supply chain, and the technology dimension of great-power competition. Research has appeared in Foreign Affairs, Foreign Policy, Wired, and Lawfare. ChinaTalk readership explicitly includes "key leadership in the White House and senior appointees and career officials at every relevant US agency, and senior Congressional staff."
