## Warsh-Fed rate-hike call (Jun 19, 2026)

Quoted in the FT's cross-CB read on the [[US-Iran framework deal June 2026|US-Iran framework MOU]], on the inflation fight under new Fed chair [[Kevin Warsh]]. His claim: "If he wants to bring inflation down, the overwhelming likelihood is that he will have to raise rates." The supporting frame — "we've had inflation above 2 per cent for an extended period of time and it's a problem across a number of categories" — anchors the call in the persistence of core-PCE pressure rather than the energy-shock narrative other commentators leaned on.

The more durable line is Wilcox's rebuttal to the "oil down, tariffs pass through, inflation drifts away" scenario: "A plausible case has been made that if oil prices come down, if the tariffs pass through, the inflation problem will drift away. But the fact of the matter is that we've been trying to tell that story now for quite a while and it hasn't come true." This is the persistent-inflation camp's answer to the peace-dividend disinflation trade, and it directly validates the [[Kevin Warsh]] hawkish debut at the Jun 17 FOMC (statement stripped of easing language, dot plot flipped to 9 of 18 projecting a 2026 hike).

The dual affiliation (Peterson + Bloomberg Economics) gives Wilcox both the policy-institution voice and the sell-side-research distribution channel — useful for tracking when the persistent-inflation view crosses from heterodox to consensus.

*Source: [[Financial Times|FT]], "Iran deal brings little relief for inflation-wary central banks" (Sam Fleming / Amy Borrett / [[Claire Jones|Claire Jones]]), Jun 19 2026.*
