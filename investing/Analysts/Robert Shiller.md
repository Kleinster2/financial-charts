# Robert Shiller

Yale economist and Nobel laureate (2013, shared with Eugene Fama and Lars Peter Hansen) whose work is the academic anchor for the vault's behavioral and narrative threads. Best known for the cyclically adjusted price-to-earnings ratio (CAPE / Shiller P/E), the Case-Shiller home-price index, "Irrational Exuberance" (2000), and "Narrative Economics" (2019) — the formal argument that contagious stories drive economic and market behavior.

## Why in the vault

Shiller's narrative economics supplies the theoretical foundation for [[Narrative lifecycle investing]]: an economic narrative is a contagious story that changes how people make economic decisions, and reinforcing stories form "narrative constellations" that amplify each other. That is the academic frame the [[Jefferies]] / [[Laurence Alexander]] staged-lifecycle model operationalizes for trading. Shiller also sits among the [[Behavioral finance]] pioneers (alongside Kahneman, Thaler, Tversky) for "Irrational Exuberance," which diagnosed asset bubbles as narrative-and-sentiment phenomena.
