## Notable frameworks

### Narrative lifecycle + AI thesis lock-in (Jun 2026)

Alexander's chemicals team published a research note arguing that a great deal of modern quant trading uses [[Generative AI|large language models]] to build and track investment narratives, and that locating a theme on a fixed lifecycle — genesis, validation, adoption, euphoria, first cracks, exhaustion — is often more valuable than the underlying fundamentals, because price moves on the change in investor conviction. The note's analytical contribution is the claim that AI alters the cycle along three vectors (speed, evidence composition, failure detection) and introduces [[Thesis lock-in]]: models defend an encoded thesis by reinterpreting negative evidence rather than calling the turn. The team runs AI screens to place live themes — data centres, robotaxis, alkene/olefins supply — on the lifecycle. See [[Narrative lifecycle investing]] for the full framework and the June 2026 current-state map (which places Datacenter AI Capex in the euphoria phase, "at risk of cracks").

The note was reported by [[Bryce Elder]] at FT Alphaville (Jun 9 2026), who wrapped it in the conceit that Aristotle's Poetics supplies the structure — a framing the FT treated as decorative over the substantive lifecycle model.

*Source: Jefferies chemicals research (Laurence Alexander et al.), via [[Financial Times|FT]] Alphaville, "What Aristotle can teach us about AI-enabled quantitative investment," Jun 9 2026.*
