## Research lens

Castellet's useful lane is not generic China commentary. The sharper frame is control-surface analysis: ports, buses, telecom towers, data centers, medicines, minerals, and other physical or regulatory chokepoints where market access can become state leverage.

## May 2026: EU-China bridge

In the May 27, 2026 Critical Supply post, Castellet argued that a European "Sino-alignment" impulse is strategically confused. The reasoning is structural: [[Europe]] and [[China]] both rely on export-goods manufacturing, making the relationship competitive in autos, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, solar, rail, and aviation. The US relationship is asymmetrical and politically strained, but more complementary because the US export mix is more services-heavy.

The same piece treated eastern [[DRC]] and US data-center interconnection policy as part of the same wider field: supply chains are not only trade flows, they are political control surfaces.
