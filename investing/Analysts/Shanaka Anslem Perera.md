## Relevant analysis

### The Great Unverification (May 2026)

Perera's May 29 2026 private-markets note is useful because it separates price verification from process verification. A selected private-credit loan sale can clear near par and still fail to prove that every retail wrapper holding similar assets could exit at NAV. The market can validate a slice while the product structure rations liquidity for the whole investor base.

The durable read is that semi-liquid private-credit and private-capital vehicles sell a collective liquidity option. The option is valuable while few holders exercise it; when redemption demand rises above the tender cap, NAV can remain process-certified while realized exit liquidity is prorated. That is exactly the tension in the 2026 retail private-credit crisis.
