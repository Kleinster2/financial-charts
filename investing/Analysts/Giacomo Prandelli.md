## Key thesis: AI compute is structurally tied to US gas (May 1, 2026)

Argument: hyperscaler reliability requirements (99.99% uptime, 12-36 month deployment cycles) plus AI capex scale ($1.1-1.2T 2025-2027 vs $200B 2024 baseline) make gas the only viable scaling solution. Solar (23% capacity factor) and wind (34%) cannot reliably power 100+ MW campuses; battery storage doesn't span multi-day weather; nuclear takes 7-10 years. Gas plants deploy quickly + pair naturally with renewables.

Valuation gap: tech sector 34% of S&P 500 trades at high-20s-to-mid-30s P/E for AI demand; energy sector 3.35% trades at mid-teens P/E for the supply infrastructure making compute possible.

Named beneficiaries (turbine + power + LNG layer): [[GE Vernova]], [[Siemens Energy]], [[Mitsubishi]], [[Vistra]], [[Constellation Energy]], plus hyperscaler customers [[Amazon|AWS]] and [[Meta]] as long-term contract counterparties.

See [[Power constraints#"$1T AI trade runs on gas" — the structural valuation gap (May 1, 2026)|Power constraints]] for the full ingest.

## Key thesis: US is the global strategic reserve (May 4, 2026)

Argument: with Saudi 3+ mb/d spare capacity stranded by Hormuz, [[2026 UAE OPEC exit|UAE OPEC+ exit]], and [[Qatar]]/[[Kuwait]] force-majeure-on-exports, US storage tanks and production capacity are the only meaningful oil reserve the global market can actually access.

Data points:
- US crude exports 6.4 mb/d (record); combined crude + refined 12.9 mb/d (highest in EIA history)
- US near-net-crude-exporter status on weekly basis (first since WWII)
- One-week US system drawdown ~26 M barrels (commercial 6.2M + SPR 7.1M)
- China liquidating 1.3B-barrel 2025 stockpile (March imports -2.5% YoY)
- Storage-buffer depletion timeline: September 2026

Pairs with [[Helima Croft]] "fine print on reopening" + [[Adam Tooze]] "big oil not coming to Trump's rescue" capital-discipline read: there is no faster supply rescue available, which concentrates the strategic-reserve role on the US Gulf Coast export apparatus.

See [[US Energy Independence Six Countries#May 4, 2026 — "America Is Now the World's Strategic Reserve" (Giacomo Prandelli)|US Energy Independence Six Countries]] for the full ingest.

## Key thesis: next $5T commodity has no wells (May 11, 2026)

Prandelli's May 11 archive preview frames the next AI-infrastructure commodity as something without wells, pipelines, or tankers. The visible premise: [[BlackRock]] cannot deploy $100B in AI infrastructure without a futures curve; the winners are the merchants, clearinghouses, and market-makers that can turn data-center capacity, power access, or compute-linked rights into a tradable market.

The investable read is that AI infrastructure is moving from "build physical assets" to "financialize scarce capacity." This extends the May 1 gas thesis:

This belongs with [[AI infrastructure financing]] and [[Power constraints]] rather than only the Hormuz thread. The commodity is not crude or gas itself; it is the tradable right to reliable AI infrastructure capacity.

## Key thesis: Europe traded Russian pipeline leverage for US LNG dependence (May 27, 2026)

Prandelli's public May 27 post shifts the European gas story from "can Europe survive without [[Russia]]?" to "can Europe survive without the [[United States]]?" The visible premise is that Russian pipeline gas to the [[EU]] collapsed from 165 bcm in 2019 to roughly 20 bcm in 2025, while US [[LNG]] into Europe rose from 5 bcm to 81 bcm over the same window. IEEFA's January 2026 analysis independently supports the direction of the claim: if announced contracts materialize and EU gas-demand cuts falter, the US could supply 75-80% of EU LNG imports by 2030.

The market hook is storage and curve shape. Europe entered refill season with gas storage around the mid-30s percent range, below a normal near-50% seasonal level, and [[TTF]] backwardation made the usual summer-injection/winter-withdrawal economics unattractive. Reuters, via [[Equinor]] executives at the Flame conference, reported that if [[Strait of Hormuz]] disruption lasted another 1-3 months, Europe risked critical storage shortfalls; [[ICIS]] modeling separately put a three-month Hormuz closure scenario above €90/MWh for TTF. That is severe enough to force demand destruction and fuel switching, but still well below the August 2022 TTF peak of €345/MWh.

The durable ingestion is therefore the public thesis only: the value chain exposed to Europe needing more US LNG includes exporters, carriers, carrier technology, Gulf Coast midstream, NGL/LPG adjacency, integrated-major LNG terminals, and global LNG trading.
