## Relevant analysis

### Agentic AI token economics (May 2026)

DeLong's May 29 2026 AI note is useful because it pushes the AI ROI debate down to the unit that [[Agentic AI|agentic systems]] actually burn: inference runs. The optimistic story is that agents can do more work by taking more shots, using tools, retrying, and checking themselves. The hard constraint is that every extra run consumes tokens, power, latency, and capex capacity.

The investing read is not "agentic AI does not work." It is that useful agentic workflows have to be measured as reasoning per token, per kWh, and per capex dollar. The strongest use cases still look like coding and other domains with crisp feedback loops. Open-ended knowledge work, management memos, legal analysis, and strategy work can look productive locally while shifting cost into verification labor and token budgets.

*Source: [Brad DeLong, "Agentic AI is a bonfire of the tokens", May 29 2026](https://braddelong.substack.com/p/agentic-ai-is-a-bonfire-of-the-tokens).*
