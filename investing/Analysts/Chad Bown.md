# Chad Bown

American economist specializing in international trade policy, tariffs, and trade wars. His vault edge is granular, data-grounded analysis of how the rules-based trading system is unwinding and how states should play a strategic, defensive trade game.

## Relevant analysis

### How to Win a Trade War (May 2026)

On [[Paul Krugman]]'s Substack podcast (May 30 2026), Bown and co-author [[Soumaya Keynes]] argued the post-WWII free-trade consensus has collapsed under geopolitical conflict ([[Second China shock]], Russia's war, [[Made in China 2025]], [[Dual circulation]]), so countries must now play strategic, defensive trade — stockpiling, selective [[Industrial policy|industrial policy]], maintaining critical manufacturing capacity, and coordinating with allies against China rather than fighting intra-Western tariff battles.

His sharpest specific point: China has inverted the West's early-2000s forced-technology-transfer playbook by licensing the outbound flow of its own battery/EV technology — telling [[BYD]] and [[CATL]] they may not independently transfer technology to European partners, with Beijing controlling foreign access. See [[China retaliatory toolkit]].

*Source: [Paul Krugman, "How to Win a Trade War" (podcast with Chad Bown and Soumaya Keynes), May 30 2026](https://paulkrugman.substack.com/p/how-to-win-a-trade-war).*
