## Relevant frameworks

### AI R&D automation speed-up (May 2026)

Greenblatt's May 27 2026 post argues that full AI R&D automation can produce a large acceleration even if the recursive software-improvement loop does not become a sustained [[Software-only singularity]]. The core distinction is between:

- a one-time speed-up from replacing human research labor with AI research labor
- a higher return to marginal compute because compute can now improve and replicate the AI labor force doing the research

His headline model reference is that with a subcritical `r=0.7`, one model run produces about 3.5 years of AI progress in the first year after full automation, with no additional compute scaling. He explicitly treats that as more aggressive than his actual expectation; an adjusted version that accounts for earlier partial automation gives roughly 2.5 years of progress in the first year.

For the investing vault, the point is not the exact number. The point is that compute becomes both experimental capital and labor capital once AI systems can do the R&D. That tightens the link between [[AI compute demand curve]], model-lab financing, and frontier capability progress.
