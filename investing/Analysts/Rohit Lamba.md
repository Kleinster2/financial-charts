## Relevant analysis

### India's AI policy: producer or tenant (May 2026)

Lamba and [[Raghuram Rajan]] argue that India's AI policy should not confuse infrastructure hosting with AI capability. The May 2026 essay uses [[Google Cloud]]'s Visakhapatnam hub as the example: the land, power, buildings, and operations are Indian, while the advanced silicon design, foundry layer, and model weights remain foreign.

The framework extends their broader services-led-growth work. India's 1990s IT-services bargain created valuable firms and income growth, but did not create the next-layer institutions: research universities, deep-tech capital, manufacturing depth, and patient industrial finance. The AI risk is a repeat with more lock-in: Indian-language inference running on foreign models can become recurring rent to foreign model owners unless India builds domestic model, data, and research capability.

For the vault, Lamba is useful because he sits between the optimistic diffusion camp and the pessimistic "India has no AI trade" camp. His point is not that diffusion is worthless; it is that diffusion without production leaves India a tenant of someone else's intelligence.

*Source: [On India's AI Policy](https://rohitlamba.substack.com/p/on-indias-ai-policy-concrete-the), May 7 2026.*
