## Relevant analysis

### Hormuz re-mining attempt (May 2026)

Stewart's May 26 2026 transcript on renewed mine-laying in the [[Strait of Hormuz]] supplied the tactical layer behind the late-May reopening debate. U.S. Central Command said U.S. forces conducted self-defense strikes in southern Iran against missile launch sites and Iranian boats attempting to emplace mines; additional defense reporting attributed the detailed two-boat / Bandar Abbas SAM-site account to U.S. officials and Jennifer Griffin's Fox reporting.

His useful distinction was between a formal ceasefire and operational normalization. A ceasefire can remain technically alive while the strait remains commercially unreliable if the [[IRGC]] can keep adding mine risk, threaten aircraft from coastal air defenses, or create enough uncertainty that shipowners and insurers stay out. The note fits directly into [[Hormuz Permanent Risk Premium]] and [[Hormuz Transit Regime]]: reopening is not a press release, but a verified operating state.

Stewart also flagged a same-day UKMTO / Reuters tanker incident off Oman as potentially relevant but unclaimed. The cause remained unknown in Reuters reporting, so this vault should treat it as maritime-risk context rather than proof of Iranian retaliation.
