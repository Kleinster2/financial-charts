## AgroForum Cuiabá 2026

At [[BTG Pactual]]'s May 22, 2026 AgroForum Cuiabá panel, Almeida framed [[Brazil]]'s high-rate problem as a fiscal spending-growth problem rather than a permanent country-risk destiny. His comparison was the 2016-2019 Temer-era rate compression: controlling real federal spending growth and slowing mandatory expenditure, especially pensions and indexed transfers, allowed inflation expectations and [[Selic]] to fall rapidly.

For the 2026 setup, Almeida argued that Brazil does not need an [[Argentina]]-style shock because it enters the election year with mid-single-digit inflation, large reserves, and a private capital-market financing base. The required adjustment is narrower but politically difficult: slow the growth of mandatory federal spending so the DI curve can believe in a lower terminal Selic.
