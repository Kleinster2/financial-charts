## Notable frameworks

### Zuckerberg discount

Armstrong's May 28, 2026 FT column frames [[Meta]] as the live example of a founder-control discount: a company can have excellent operating performance and still trade below comparable growth peers if public shareholders do not trust the controller's capital allocation and cannot replace him. The piece uses that Meta analogy to warn that [[SpaceX]] may deserve a similar or larger governance discount in its [[SpaceX IPO 2026]] because [[Elon Musk]] keeps supervoting control while public investors fund a combined launch, satellite, AI, and [[Twitter|X]] stack.

*Source: [[Financial Times|FT]], "SpaceX and the Zuckerberg discount," May 28 2026: https://www.ft.com/content/e0485f5a-e50a-4dea-9e42-3dc38d82111b.*
