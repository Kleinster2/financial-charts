## Relevant analysis

### AI verification labor (May 2026)

Smith's May 27 2026 Noahpinion public preview frames the post-AI labor question around alignment in the broad operational sense: as models become more agentic and take on longer, messier tasks, humans remain valuable because they know what they want and can tell whether the system is still working toward it.

The practical labor implication is not that humans simply disappear from knowledge work. The job mix shifts toward specifying goals, checking outputs, correcting drift, and keeping AI agents aligned with user and firm intent. In the near term this shows up as verification work around AI output. In the longer-term version Smith sketches, it becomes supervision of autonomous systems that might otherwise reward-hack, pursue the wrong objective, or wander away from the human task.

For the investing vault this connects [[AI labor displacement]] to [[Enterprise AI adoption]]: if agentic systems need humans to keep them on task, then the displacement story is also a management and governance story. Firms do not just buy models; they need operating processes that turn agent output into verified work.
