## Relevant analysis

### Enterprise AI and robotics peer effects (May 28, 2026)

Klement's May 28, 2026 post on peer effects in AI/robotics adoption is useful because it separates two stories that are often conflated. The easy story is that managers will adopt AI once they realize peers are moving faster. The paper he cites, Cullen, Faia, Guglielminetti, Perez-Truglia, and Rondinelli's [[Italy|Italian]] central-bank experiment, is narrower: managers underestimated peer adoption, but corrected information moved intended robotics adoption and did not show a statistically clear AI effect.

The investing read is that competitive fear is real, but its channel is more legible in physical automation than in generic AI. Robotics capex can respond to visible peer deployment. Enterprise AI still needs proof of workflow fit, data readiness, governance, and ROI before "our competitors are doing it" becomes a procurement trigger.

*Sources: [Klement on Investing, May 28 2026](https://klementoninvesting.substack.com/p/if-your-peer-does-it-do-you-do-it); [Cullen et al., "The Innovation Race", NBER WP 34532](https://www.nber.org/papers/w34532); [Panmure Liberum profile](https://panmureliberum.com/people/joachim-klement-1/).*
