# Simon Johnson

Simon Johnson — MIT Sloan economist, 2024 Nobel laureate in economics (shared with [[Daron Acemoglu]] and James Robinson, for work on how institutions shape prosperity), and former chief economist of the [[IMF]] (2007–08). Co-author with Acemoglu of "Power and Progress" (2023), whose central claim — that technology's gains reach workers only when institutions and choices steer it toward augmentation rather than pure automation — is the framework underlying his AI-labor-displacement views.

## Why he matters here

A heavyweight academic voice in the [[AI labor displacement]] debate. In an FT interview (Jun 22 2026) he argued "nobody needs as many white-collar workers as they used to," and that AI displacement "this time really could be different" (with [[Daron Acemoglu]] and labor economist David Autor) — the automation-not-augmentation case made by economists with maximal credibility on technology and labor. His relevance to the vault is as the most credentialed proponent of the structural (not cyclical) reading of white-collar displacement.
