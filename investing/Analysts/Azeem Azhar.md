## Relevant analysis

### AI productivity ladder (May 2026)

Azhar and Nathan Warren's May 27 2026 Exponential View essay reframes enterprise AI disappointment as a general-purpose-technology adoption problem. The useful distinction is between:

- Stage 1: individual tools that make workers faster
- Stage 2: agents that automate workflows inside the old process geometry
- Stage 3: operating-model redesign that changes decision speed across the firm

The investment implication is that usage, token burn, and local productivity are insufficient indicators. Firm-level ROI should appear where AI changes the throughput constraint, not merely where it adds more output to an unchanged approval or management queue. See [[Enterprise AI adoption]] and [[Productivity J-curve]].
