## Iran-deal commentary (Jun 19, 2026)

Quoted in the FT's cross-CB read on the [[US-Iran framework deal June 2026|US-Iran framework MOU]]. His framing condensed the asset-manager view: the agreement had removed a "massive tail risk" under which oil could have surged toward $180/bbl on shortages — the [[Iran conflict oil price timeline#May 17 — Peak-summer crunch in view, Brent $105, scenario reset to $150-180 (FT)|$180 scenario]] his colleague [[Paul Diggle]] had been examining — but the inflation hazards had not disappeared. Top central-bank policymakers were "not ready to call the all-clear — that is for sure."

The quote ties Bartholomew to Aberdeen's broader commodity-bullish macro posture documented in the [[Aberdeen]] note (commodities as "critical for portfolio resilience," per Robert Minter; the Diggle $180 scenario work in May). His contribution is the framing rather than a new forecast: the distinction between a removed tail-risk and a remaining central case is the operative asset-allocation read.

*Source: [[Financial Times|FT]], "Iran deal brings little relief for inflation-wary central banks" (Sam Fleming / Amy Borrett / [[Claire Jones|Claire Jones]]), Jun 19 2026.*
