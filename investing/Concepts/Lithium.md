## Direct lithium extraction — supply revolution?

[[Direct lithium extraction]] (DLE) could reshape the supply side. The technology separates lithium from underground brines in hours (vs 18 months for evaporation ponds), with 70-90% recovery rates vs 40-60%, using less land and water. The US Smackover Formation (Arkansas and neighboring states) holds up to 19M tonnes of lithium resource (USGS, Oct 2024).

Key DLE players: [[Standard Lithium]]/[[Equinor]] ($1.5B project, Arkansas), [[Exxon|ExxonMobil]]/Saltwerx (target: lithium for 1M+ EVs by 2030), [[Rio Tinto]] ($2.5B Rincon expansion, Argentina). At least 36 DLE projects worldwide, 13 in [[China]] (Columbia University). All-in costs currently ~$10,000/t — most need $18-20K/t prices to proceed, but high-concentration Smackover brines may undercut that.

US producers accuse [[China]] of deliberately crashing prices (~$9,000/t, down 80% from 2022) to frustrate new entrants. Beijing has also restricted export of lithium processing technologies.

## Data center demand and geopolitical vulnerability

Data centers rely almost entirely on lithium-ion battery systems for uninterruptible power supply (UPS), bridging outages until backup generators start. In 2024, the US imported $15.3B of lithium batteries from [[China]] — roughly a quarter of China's total lithium battery exports (FT, May 2025).

This creates a supply-chain chokepoint: [[China]] controls 60%+ of global lithium refining, and Beijing has already demonstrated willingness to restrict critical battery technologies and processing for [[Lithium|lithium]] and [[Gallium|gallium]]. The 2023 [[Germanium|germanium]]/gallium export controls provide precedent for using critical minerals as geopolitical leverage.

Alternatives emerging for data center storage:
- Organic flow batteries — non-toxic, non-flammable organic molecules in saltwater; no lithium, cobalt, or nickel. Prometheus Hyperscale (US data center developer) signed a deal for deployment at scale (May 2025)
- [[Sodium-ion batteries]] — avoid lithium entirely; [[CATL]] scaling by end of 2026

For [[Tianqi Lithium]] and [[Ganfeng Lithium]], any shift in US data center demand away from Chinese-refined lithium batteries represents a risk to one of their key export markets.
