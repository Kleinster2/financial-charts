# Iran transnational repression

Pattern of [[Iran]]-orchestrated covert activity directed at dissidents, journalists, former senior US officials, and Israel-linked targets on Western soil. The investing-vault relevance is narrow but not zero: this is the asymmetric retaliation channel through which the [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|2026 conflict]] continues *after* the active war ends, with implications for political-risk premia, executive-protection costs, and the security overhead of doing business with sanctioned Iran exposure.

## Operational structure (per FT, May 2026)

Per Mossad terminology, Iranian special operations divide into two units:

| Unit | Mossad designation | Purview | Operational since |
|---|---|---|---|
| IRGC division | Unit 400 | Iranian targets only (dissidents, exiles, journalists) | Long-running |
| Quds Force Special Operations Division | Unit 840 | Foreign nationals (Israeli diplomats, intelligence officers, businesspeople; Western political figures) | 2012 |

Public Unit 840 attempts attributed by FT sources include: Cyprus, Ethiopia, Kenya, UAE, Colombia (Israeli diplomats / intelligence officers); Ghana, Tanzania, Senegal (Israeli businesspeople).

## Post-war state and capacity constraints

The March 2026 strikes that killed [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Supreme Leader Khamenei]] also killed National Security Council chief Ali Larijani and intelligence minister Esmail Khatib — the senior figures who would coordinate large transnational operations. Per FT-quoted experts (John Raine of [[IISS]]; Jonathan Hackett, former US Marine SOF), this *delays but does not eliminate* the retaliation timeline: succession-driven loss of operational network influence is the same dynamic that immediately constrained Quds Force activity after the 2020 Qassem Soleimani killing. The capacity gap closes over months, not weeks.

In parallel, John Raine's framing — "revenge is as much a religious obligation as an emotional response" — implies the timeline is *long*. Risk premia priced in front-month volatility likely understate the multi-quarter persistence of the channel.
