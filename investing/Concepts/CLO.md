# CLO

Collateralized loan obligation — structured vehicle that pools leveraged loans and issues tranches of debt and equity against them. Typically leveraged 9-10x equity, versus ~1x for [[BDC]]s holding similar assets. Popular with insurance companies due to high ratings on senior tranches and beneficial capital treatment. The global CLO market reached ~$1.3T outstanding by 2025, with US CLOs comprising roughly two-thirds.

## Structure

CLOs issue a capital stack of tranches rated AAA down to BB/B, plus an unrated equity tranche. The manager (typically a credit fund) earns fees for selecting and trading the loan pool. Senior tranches have never experienced a rated default in the post-crisis era, making them attractive for insurance and bank balance sheets. Equity tranches earn 12-20% returns in benign credit environments but absorb first losses.

| Structure | Leverage | Transparency | Investor base |
|-----------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| BDC | ~1x | Public filings | Retail + institutional |
| CLO | 9-10x | Limited | Insurance, banks, pensions |

## Why it matters now

In the [[Blue Owl OBDC II redemption gate]], [[Barclays]] warned that loans sold from Blue Owl's BDCs would likely be repackaged into Blue Owl-managed CLOs and purchased by [[Kuvare]] (Blue Owl's own insurer). This shifts the same private credit assets from transparent, low-leverage BDC structures into opaque, high-leverage CLO structures — making systemic risk harder to track. See [[PE-insurance convergence]].

CLO BBB stands out: 7.2% yield sitting in the 75th percentile of its 8-year range — the richest carry on a historical basis across all major credit sectors. [[Rick Rieder]] ([[BlackRock]]) allocates 36.6% of [[BINC]] to securitized products (CLOs, [[MBS market|ABS]], CMBS) as of April 3, 2026, making structured credit the fund's largest sleeve. The appeal: high carry with structural protections (subordination, overcollateralization) that IG corporates at 8.5% weight cannot match at current spread levels.
