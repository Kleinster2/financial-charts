# Previdência privada

Brazil's private-pension wrappers — PGBL and VGBL — tax-advantaged vehicles that hold funds for retirement. Less an instrument than a tax-and-discipline shell wrapped around an underlying fund.

Chart data unavailable — this is an instrument category, not a single tracked price series.

## Mechanics

- PGBL: contributions are deductible up to 12% of taxable income, but the entire balance (principal + gains) is taxed on withdrawal — best for those who file the full tax return and want the deferral.
- VGBL: funded with after-tax money; only the gains are taxed on withdrawal — best for those who take the simplified return or have maxed the PGBL deduction.
- Both can elect a regressive tax table that falls to 10% for money held 10+ years, plus succession advantages (no probate, faster transfer).

## Risks / watch-outs

- The real value is the tax deferral and the illiquidity-enforced discipline — the money is awkward to touch, which keeps the investor from "torrar" (blowing) it.
- The catch ([[Brazilian fixed income]]): roughly 98% of pension funds reportedly lag the CDI on heavy administration fees, so the wrapper's tax edge can be eaten by the underlying fund's cost. Portability between providers exists and is worth using.
