Rare earth elements (REE) are a group of 17 metallic elements critical to permanent magnets (NdFeB for EVs and wind turbines), electronics, defense systems, and catalysts. [[China]] controls ~60% of mining and ~90% of processing. Extraction via sulfuric acid roasting requires 3.4-4.0 tonnes H2SO4 per tonne of rare earth oxide (REO). Global recycling rate remains below 5%.

## Diversification push (2025-2026)

The combination of [[China]]'s 2024-2025 export controls and US tariff escalation triggered a global scramble to build non-[[China]] supply chains. Key developments:

- [[Brazil]]-[[India]] MOU on rare earths (Feb 2026) — see [[India-Brazil critical minerals MOU 2026-02]]
- US-[[Brazil]] partnership talks (Jan-Mar 2026) — FT, NYT
- [[Japan]] deep-sea mining tests ([[Japan Chikyu rare earth mission 2026-01]])
- US VC surge: $628M invested in rare earth startups (2025, +2,900% YoY)
- [[Project Vault]]: $12B US strategic minerals stockpile
