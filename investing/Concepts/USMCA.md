**USMCA** — US-Mexico-[[Canada]] Agreement. Replaced NAFTA in 2020. Stricter auto rules, digital trade provisions, 6-year sunset review.

---

## Overview

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Parties | US, Mexico, [[Canada]] |
| Population | 500M |
| Combined GDP | ~$28T |
| Signed | Nov 2018 |
| In force | Jul 2020 |
| Replaced | NAFTA (1994) |
| Review clause | Every 6 years |

---

## Key terms

### Tariff elimination

| Category | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| Industrial goods | 99%+ duty-free (NAFTA continuity) |
| [[Agriculture]] | Mostly open |
| Dairy | US gained [[Canada]] access (3.6% market) |
| Steel/aluminum | Side agreements, Section 232 |

### Auto rules of origin (strictest globally)

| Requirement | USMCA | vs NAFTA |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| Regional content | 75% | 62.5% |
| Steel/aluminum | 70% North American | New |
| Labor value content | 40-45% at $16+/hr | New |
| Core parts | 75% regional | New |

**Impact:** Forces production toward US/[[Canada]] (high-wage) from Mexico (low-wage).

### Labor provisions

| Provision | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Rapid Response Mechanism | Facility-level enforcement |
| Right to organize | Mexico labor reform required |
| Forced labor ban | Import prohibition |
| Wage requirements | $16/hr auto content |

**Mexico labor reform:** Constitutional changes to enable independent unions.

### Digital trade

| Provision | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Data localization ban | No forced local servers |
| Cross-border data flows | Protected |
| Source code | Cannot require disclosure |
| Customs duties | Banned on digital products |

Most advanced digital trade chapter of any FTA at signing.

### Dairy ([[Canada]] concessions)

| Product | US access |
|---------|-----------|
| Fluid milk | 3.6% of Canadian market |
| Cheese | Increased TRQs |
| Butter | Limited |
| Class 7 pricing | Eliminated |

[[Canada]] dismantled supply management partially — politically sensitive.

### Sunset clause

| Provision | Terms |
|-----------|-------|
| Initial term | 16 years |
| Joint review | Every 6 years |
| Extension | 16 years if agreed |
| Default | Expires without review |

**2026 review:** First joint review due. Political uncertainty given elections.

## Investment implications

### Winners

| Sector | Beneficiary |
|--------|-------------|
| **US auto workers** | Wage content rules favor US/[[Canada]] |
| **US dairy** | [[Canada]] market access |
| **Mexico manufacturing** | Nearshoring wave (post-COVID) |
| **Digital platforms** | Data flow protections |
| **Mexican labor** | Union rights, wage floors |

### Losers

| Sector | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Mexico low-wage auto** | Shifted toward higher-value |
| **[[Canada]] dairy farmers** | Lost protection |
| **Supply chain complexity** | Stricter rules of origin |

### Nearshoring beneficiaries

| Company | Exposure |
|---------|----------|
| Industrial [[REITs]] | Prologis, FIBRA Macquarie |
| Auto suppliers | Lear, Aptiv, Magna |
| Electronics | Foxconn Juarez, [[Samsung]] Tijuana |
| Railroads | Kansas City Southern, [[Union Pacific]] |

See [[Nearshoring thesis]].

## Comparison to NAFTA

| Dimension | NAFTA | USMCA |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Auto content | 62.5% | 75% |
| Labor | Weak side agreement | Enforceable, rapid response |
| Digital | None | Comprehensive chapter |
| Dairy | Protected | US access |
| Sunset | None | 16yr with 6yr review |
| Dispute resolution | Ch. 19, 20 | Modified, Ch. 11 investor-state limited |
