**Retail investor flows** — Self-directed retail trading volumes have remained structurally elevated since the 2021 meme stock phenomenon. Pre-pandemic: $0-1B daily net flows. Post-2021: $1-3B daily, with spikes to $3.5B. This isn't a bubble hangover — it's a permanent shift in market participation.

## The structural shift

| Period | Daily net flows | Character |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Pre-2020 | $0-500M | Low, stable |
| Jan 2021 peak | $3.5B | Meme squeeze |
| 2022-2023 | $500M-1.5B | Elevated but calmer |
| 2024-2025 | $1-2B | New normal |
| Late 2025 spikes | $2.5-3B | AI/crypto enthusiasm |

Source: VandaTrack (self-directed retail investor flow in US stock market, Jan 2014 - Jan 2026).

---

## Why it persists

1. **Commission-free trading** — [[Robinhood]] forced industry to zero commissions; no friction to re-enter
2. **Platform stickiness** — 90% of Robinhood's 2.5M Jan 2021 accounts still active (Jan 2026)
3. **Sophistication growth** — Retail moved from meme stocks → retirement accounts, options, crypto, prediction markets
4. **New asset classes** — Crypto, sports betting, prediction markets absorbed speculative demand
5. **Social infrastructure** — Reddit, X, Discord provide permanent information-sharing networks

## Market structure implications

### For companies
- **Shelf registration arms race** — Companies pre-file paperwork to quickly issue stock during meme surges (Mudrick Capital advice, now standard legal counsel)
- **Meme as capital strategy** — [[GameStop]] raised billions; AMC avoided bankruptcy through equity issuance
- **Retail as price-setter** — In thematic/small-cap stocks, retail can dominate price discovery

### For institutions
- **VandaTrack** — Launched early 2021; institutional clients use retail flow data for risk management and idea generation
- **Social monitoring** — Trading desks check X/Reddit for unexplained moves (Jefferies)
- **Short interest awareness** — Managers far more careful about crowded shorts post-2021
- **Meme ETFs** — Roundhill MEME ETF (relaunched 2025, actively managed, weekly rebalance)

### For brokerages
- **Arms race** — [[Robinhood]], [[Interactive Brokers]], Charles Schwab competing on features for increasingly sophisticated retail
- **26.9M** funded customers at Robinhood (Nov 2025)
- **Reddit presence** — Even Fidelity now operates its own Reddit investment forum

## Regulatory context

The 2021 meme craze prompted regulatory action, but most reforms have been delayed or withdrawn:

| Reform | Status |
|--------|--------|
| T+1 settlement | Implemented (reduces clearing crisis risk) |
| Tick size reduction (½¢) | Upheld, compliance Nov 2026 |
| Exchange fee reduction (-67%) | Upheld, compliance Nov 2026 |
| PFOF ban | Not pursued |
| Gamification rules | Withdrawn (Trump SEC) |
| Short sale transparency | Delayed by lawsuits |

Gary Gensler: Risk of brokerage shutoffs is "far less likely" post-T+1. But "not impossible" in extreme stress.

## Unpatched systemic risk

Thomas Peterffy ([[Interactive Brokers]]): The mechanisms that would have caused a GameStop-style spiral remain unpatched as of Jan 2026.

**IBKR proposals (not adopted):**
- Daily short interest publication
- Rising margin requirements as SI exceeds 50% of float
- Position limits when SI hits 100%

Current rules still allow short interest to exceed 100% of float through rehypothecation.
