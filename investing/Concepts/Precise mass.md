# Precise mass

Doctrinal term coined by [[Michael Horowitz]] (CFR) for the production-side inversion of the precision-strike paradigm: where precision once meant a handful of [[Tomahawk]]s or stealth bombers, it now means cheap one-way drones built from commercial parts and launched in swarms. Precision plus mass. The thesis is that this combination becomes "a regular feature of warfare, just like machine guns or tanks" (per Horowitz, cited in Zakaria/WashPost March 20, 2026).

The investable read: precise mass is the structural bull case for the attritable cohort ([[AeroVironment]], [[Kratos]], [[Anduril]], [[Shield AI]], [[Helsing]], [[Stark]]) and the bear case for incumbent prime-contractor unit economics that depend on every platform surviving every mission. The [[Russian sovereign drone ecosystem]] is the leading empirical instantiation of the production curve precise mass requires.

## Cost asymmetry

| Platform | Unit cost | Philosophy |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| F-35 | ~$80M | Must survive every mission |
| MQ-9 Reaper | ~$30M | Expensive, limited numbers |
| XQ-58 Valkyrie | ~$3M | Designed to be lost |
| LUCAS | ~$35K | One-way attack, reverse-engineered Shahed |
| Switchblade 600 | ~$50K | One-use, anti-armor |
| FPV drone (Ukraine) | ~$500 | Disposable, mass-produced |

For the cost of one F-35: 26 Valkyries, 1,600 Switchblades, or 160,000 FPV drones. Quantity has a quality all its own.

## Production-curve evidence

- Ukraine ~50,000 FPV drones/month domestic production
- Russia 404 Shahed-type drones/day, targeting 1,000/day; state targets of 130,000 large UAS/yr by 2030 and 350,000/yr by 2035 ([[Russian sovereign drone ecosystem]])
- US [[Lockheed Martin]] Patriot interceptor capacity ~600 in 2025, targeting 2,000 by 2027 — the production asymmetry is the strategic problem, not the technology gap
