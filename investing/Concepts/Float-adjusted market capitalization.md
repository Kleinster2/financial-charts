# Float-adjusted market capitalization

Float-adjusted market capitalization weights a company by the shares available to public investors rather than by every share economically outstanding.

---

## Synthesis

Float adjustment is an index-capacity rule, not a valuation opinion. It asks how much of a company public investors can actually buy, so a low-float mega-IPO can be economically enormous while still receiving a modest initial weight in broad investable indexes. The rule prevents passive funds from treating unavailable insider, founder, employee, or locked-up shares as if they were freely tradable supply.

The [[SpaceX IPO 2026]] roadshow makes the mechanism unusually visible. At the Jun. 4 2026 launch terms, SpaceX is offering 555,555,555 Class A shares at an expected $135/share, or about $75.0B of public float before any over-allotment. Against a reported $1.75T-$1.77T company value, that is only about 4% of the company. A pure float-adjusted index sees the investable piece first; the full economic value can matter later only as more locked-up or insider shares become float.

[[Nasdaq 100]] now uses a hybrid. Its May 1 2026 methodology update keeps full company market capitalization for ranking and Fast Entry eligibility, but caps low-float weighting at three times free-floating shares for eligible listed securities. For SpaceX's base roadshow float, that means about $225B of modified market capitalization for Nasdaq-100 weighting under a simple all-else-equal assumption, not $75B and not $1.75T-$1.77T. Final index weight still depends on final pricing, share-class treatment, free-float files, and the Nasdaq 100's ordinary weighting constraints.

## Mechanics

| Item | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Total market cap | Share price times all economic shares outstanding |
| Public float | Shares actually available to public investors |
| Float percentage | Public float divided by total shares outstanding |
| Float-adjusted market cap | Share price times public float shares |
| Index consequence | Weight follows investable supply instead of headline company value |

## Correlation structure

Float adjustment is not a tradable factor by itself. Its market expression is mechanical: lower eligible float reduces initial index weight and passive demand in products such as [[VTI]], while later insider sales, lockup expiries, or index-file updates can raise weights even if the stock price is unchanged.
