**Inflation Reduction Act** — 2022 U.S. law combining climate subsidies, clean-energy industrial policy, EV incentives, and tax changes, and one of the main federal policy drivers behind reshoring and grid-investment demand.

---

## Why it matters

For the vault, the IRA matters less as a budget bill than as a capital-allocation regime. It pushed private investment toward batteries, EV supply chains, clean power, transmission, and related industrial buildout.

---

## Labor/community implementation layer

The Biden administration's IRA implementation paired clean-energy tax credits and DOE grant/loan programs with workforce and community conditions: prevailing wage, registered apprenticeship incentives, domestic-supply-chain requirements, and community benefits plans. The live debate is whether those conditions turned the IRA into a slower "everything bagel" policy or whether they were a coalition-building and project-risk-management layer attached to an otherwise massive capital-allocation regime.

The strongest defense of the standards comes from Betony Jones and Joe Peck's April 2026 Roosevelt Institute report, then Jones's May 2026 Volts interview. Their evidence: DOE tied 74% of major IIJA/IRA clean-energy project funding, or $91B across 110 major projects, to legally binding labor or community agreements. Jones says the firms interviewed did complain about friction, but the recurring complaints were permitting delays, supply-chain constraints, steep learning curves, and government contracting timelines rather than the labor/community standards themselves. In the report's framing, community benefits plans made developers address workforce supply, local opposition, and benefit-sharing early enough to reduce execution risk.

That distinction matters because IRA risk is not only "credits survive or credits die." It is also administrative capacity: whether federal and state agencies can process grants, tax guidance, contracts, interconnection, permitting, domestic-content rules, and compliance paperwork fast enough for private capital to trust the signal. The [[One Big Beautiful Bill Act]] rollback damaged the policy-continuity side of that equation, but it does not prove that labor/community standards were the binding bottleneck. For investable clean-energy projects, the more useful screen is sponsor capability: can the developer secure labor, community support, permits, grid access, equipment supply, and tax-credit monetization before the policy window moves?

*Sources: [Volts, "A limited defense of Biden's everything-bagel industrial policy"](https://www.volts.wtf/p/a-limited-defense-of-bidens-everything), interview with Betony Jones, May 27 2026; [Roosevelt Institute, "The Receipts"](https://rooseveltinstitute.org/publications/the-receipts-the-untold-and-underappreciated-outcomes-of-bidens-clean-energy-strategy/), Apr 16 2026.*
