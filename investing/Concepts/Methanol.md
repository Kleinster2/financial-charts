# Methanol

The simplest alcohol (CH₃OH). Produced commercially from synthesis gas (a mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide, or "syngas") derived from natural gas or coal. Core feedstock for formaldehyde, acetic acid, MTBE, biodiesel, and — most importantly for the 2026 [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] — methanol-to-olefins (MTO) plants that produce [[Ethylene|ethylene]] and [[Propylene|propylene]].

---

## Synopsis

Methanol has two faces. As a chemical, it is an input for formaldehyde, acetic acid, and fuel additives — a mature global market. As a petrochemical feedstock, it is the pivot of [[China]]'s MTO industry, which converts methanol to the olefins ([[Ethylene]], [[Propylene]]) that feed [[Polyethylene]] and [[Polypropylene]] producers. That second role makes methanol a hidden transmission channel for Middle East energy prices into domestic Chinese plastics.

The 2026 Iran war exposed this transmission. The [[FT]]'s NBS/Wind chart (April 11) shows domestic China methanol at ~2,000 Rmb/tonne pre-war, jumping to ~2,800 Rmb/tonne in the post-strike weeks. Because the [[FT]] identifies the Middle East as a "key source" of Chinese methanol imports (alongside [[Polyethylene]] and sulphur), a Hormuz closure tightens the entire downstream polymer chain in China — the fortress's thinnest wall.

## Production

| Route | Feedstock | Geography |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Natural gas reforming | CH₄ → syngas → methanol | Middle East, Trinidad, US Gulf, Russia |
| Coal gasification | Coal → syngas → methanol | [[China]] inland (Shaanxi, Inner Mongolia, Ningxia) |
| Biomass / CO₂ capture | Emerging "green methanol" | Small scale, premium market |

Global capacity is heavily weighted to low-cost gas producers in the Middle East and coal producers in China. Prices in import-parity regions (China coastal, South Korea) track Middle East export rates closely.

## Major end uses

| End use | Share | Detail |
|---------|-------|--------|
| MTO/MTP (olefins) | ~30% | [[Polyethylene]], [[Polypropylene]] via [[Ethylene]]/[[Propylene]] |
| Formaldehyde | ~25% | Adhesives, construction, plastics |
| Fuel (MTBE, biodiesel, direct) | ~20% | Gasoline additive, marine fuel (shipping decarbonization) |
| Acetic acid | ~10% | Vinyl acetate, PTA, solvents |
| Other chemicals | ~15% | DMF, DME, methacrylates |

## Shipping and marine-fuel angle

Methanol is increasingly used as a marine fuel — [[Maersk]] has been the most public convert. The same Hormuz crisis that disrupts feedstock supply also raises bunker costs on methanol-fueled vessels, tightening the cost base at both ends of the supply chain.
