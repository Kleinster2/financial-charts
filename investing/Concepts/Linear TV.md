**Linear TV** — traditional scheduled television broadcast via cable, satellite, or over-the-air, where viewers watch programs at set times determined by the network. In secular decline since ~2012 as audiences shift to [[Streaming]] and on-demand consumption. US pay-TV households peaked at ~100M in 2013 and have fallen below 70M, with [[Cord-cutting]] accelerating at ~5-6M households per year. The economics are in a doom loop: subscriber losses reduce affiliate fee revenue, which cuts programming budgets, which accelerates subscriber losses.

## Investment relevance

Linear TV remains relevant to investors through several channels. First, legacy media companies ([[Comcast]], [[Warner Bros Discovery]], [[Paramount Global]], [[Disney]]) still derive the majority of their operating profit from linear networks even as they invest billions in [[Streaming]] — the cross-subsidy dynamic defines their earnings trajectory. Second, the collapse in linear advertising spend (~$60B US in 2024, down from $80B+ peak) is redistributing to connected TV (CTV), [[YouTube]], and digital platforms. Third, live sports — the last category with appointment-viewing economics — commands escalating rights fees as the scarce inventory keeping linear bundles together. The unwinding of the linear bundle is the defining structural theme in media investing.
