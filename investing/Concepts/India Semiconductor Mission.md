#concept #india #semiconductors #industrial-policy

**India Semiconductor Mission (ISM)** — the Indian government's flagship incentive program to attract semiconductor fabrication and packaging investment to India, administered under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY). The scheme subsidizes up to 50% of project cost for approved fab and packaging plants, with the stated goal of building a domestic semiconductor stack and reducing import dependence.

The ISM is the policy vehicle behind India's bid to stand up local wafer capacity. Its credibility test is execution: whether approved projects ([[Tata Electronics]]/[[PSMC]] Dholera fab, [[Micron]] Sanand packaging, CG Power–[[Renesas]] fab, Kaynes) convert subsidy approvals into operating facilities. See [[India execution gap]] for the broader thesis on whether India can turn semiconductor policy ambition into running fabs, and [[Semiconductor incentive programs]] for the global pattern (US [[CHIPS Act]], EU Chips Act, Korea, [[Japan]]) the ISM joins.
