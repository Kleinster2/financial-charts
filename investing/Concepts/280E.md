# 280E

Section 280E of the US Internal Revenue Code — the tax provision that defines the economics of the American cannabis industry. It bars any business "trafficking" in a Schedule I or II controlled substance from deducting ordinary business expenses, allowing only cost of goods sold. Because cannabis is Schedule I (see [[Cannabis rescheduling]]), plant-touching [[Green Thumb Industries|US MSOs]] are taxed on gross profit rather than net income, producing effective federal tax rates that can exceed 70% and structurally suppressing the cash flow of even profitable operators. It does not apply to the federally legal [[Canopy Growth|Canadian LPs]].

## Why it matters

280E is the single largest swing factor in US MSO valuation. A rescheduling to Schedule III would remove cannabis from 280E's reach, restoring normal expense deductibility and sharply improving after-tax cash flow — which is why the [[Cannabis rescheduling]] reform track is the dominant catalyst for the [[Cannabis]] cohort. The asymmetry it creates (US MSOs penalized, Canadian LPs not) is part of why the US MSO bloc trades as its own tight sub-cluster (sub-intra 0.84) within the broader cannabis complex, even as the shared reform catalyst keeps the whole group co-moving.
