#concept #energy #aviation #climate

**Sustainable Aviation Fuel** — drop-in replacement for conventional [[Jet fuel]], produced from non-petroleum feedstocks (waste oils, biomass, captured CO₂). Chemically identical to Jet-A1 but with up to 80% lower lifecycle carbon emissions.

## China competition risk (Mar 2026)

[[Neste]] CEO [[Heikki Malinen]] warned the EU SAF market risks a price war with Chinese producers, drawing a direct parallel with the destruction of the European steel industry by Chinese overcapacity. [[Argus Media]] confirmed Chinese exports increased since Q4 2025 and are likely to continue rising through 2026.

The threat is two-pronged:
- Supply side: Asian producers increasing output while European margins already under pressure from oversupply and weak demand
- Demand side: industry pushback against mandates. [[TotalEnergies]] CEO [[Patrick Pouyanné]] (Jan 2026) said he expects the EU 2% blending rule to be watered down, paralleling revisions to EU car emissions policy

SAF remains 2-4x more expensive than conventional [[Jet fuel]]. Some airlines have revised interim SAF targets downward. Growth slowed following the Russia-Ukraine war and energy price backlash. If mandates weaken AND Chinese imports increase, European producers like [[Neste]] (which invested ~$10B in the transition, including €2.5B on Rotterdam expansion) face a potential squeeze.

Malinen called for EU anti-dumping duties on SAF imports — the bloc already imposes tariffs on similar products. *(FT, Mar 15, 2026)*
