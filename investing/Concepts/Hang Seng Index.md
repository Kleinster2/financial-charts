# Hang Seng Index

The **Hang Seng Index** (HSI) is the benchmark stock market index for [[Hong Kong]], comprising the 80 largest companies listed on the [[HKEX|Hong Kong Stock Exchange]]. Published by [[Hang Seng Bank]]'s Hang Seng Indexes Company, it is the most widely tracked gauge of Hong Kong-listed equities and, by extension, the primary liquid proxy for Chinese large-cap exposure outside the mainland.

## Synopsis

The HSI gained 28% in 2025 — its best year since 2017 — and briefly traded above 28,000 in early 2026, driven by a combination of [[PBoC]] stimulus, the [[DeepSeek]] AI catalyst reigniting tech valuations, and repositioning by global funds that had been maximum-underweight [[China]] since 2021. Valuations remain structurally cheap at ~11.5x forward P/E versus the [[S&P 500]] at ~22x, reflecting the persistent risk discount from regulatory unpredictability, [[Xi Jinping]]'s interventionist track record, and Hong Kong's erosion as an autonomous financial center post-2020 National Security Law.

The index composition has shifted dramatically over the past decade — tech and "new economy" names ([[Tencent]], [[Alibaba]], [[Meituan]], [[BYD]]) now dominate weight, displacing the old property developer and banking heavyweights (HSBC, CK Hutchison, Sun Hung Kai). This means the HSI increasingly trades as a China tech index with a Hong Kong listing wrapper, and its correlation with mainland sentiment has risen accordingly. The Hang Seng Tech Index (HSTECH), launched in 2020, captures this shift explicitly. For portfolio construction, the HSI offers the cheapest beta to Chinese consumption and tech growth, but carries binary event risk from Beijing policy decisions that no valuation metric can hedge.
