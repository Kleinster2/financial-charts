#concept #fixed-income #credit

**Investment grade bonds** — Corporate debt rated BBB-/Baa3 or higher. Lower yield than high yield but lower default risk. Primary vehicle: LQD (iShares IG Corporate Bond ETF).

## What defines investment grade

| Rating agency | IG threshold |
|---------------|--------------|
| S&P/Fitch | BBB- and above |
| Moody's | Baa3 and above |

Below these thresholds = high yield ("junk").

---

## Key drivers

| Driver | Impact on IG bonds |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Interest rates** | Duration risk — prices fall when rates rise |
| **Credit spreads** | Widen in risk-off, compress in risk-on |
| **Corporate earnings** | Affect credit quality, downgrade risk |
| **Fed policy** | QE = spread compression, QT = widening |

---

## IG vs High Yield vs Treasuries

| Characteristic | Treasuries | IG Corporate | High Yield |
|----------------|------------|--------------|------------|
| Default risk | None | Very low | Moderate |
| Spread | 0 | 80-150bp | 300-500bp |
| Duration | Varies | ~8-9 years | ~4 years |
| Volatility | Low | Moderate | Higher |
| Correlation to equities | Low | Moderate | High |

---

## Fallen angels

Companies downgraded from IG to HY:
- Forced selling by IG-only mandates
- Often creates value opportunity
- Recent examples: Ford (2020), many energy names

## When to own IG bonds

| Environment | IG bonds |
|-------------|----------|
| Rates falling | Good (duration benefit) |
| Rates rising | Bad (duration hurt) |
| Spreads compressing | Good |
| Risk-off / recession | Mixed (spreads widen but rates fall) |
| Fed cutting | Generally good |
