# Brazilian fixed income

The menu of instruments that fills the "renda fixa" and income sleeves of a typical Brazilian retail or private-banking portfolio. The vault does not track these as individual price series — they are local, retail, and issuer-specific — but they recur in portfolio reviews, so this note is the hub, and each instrument type has its own concept note (linked in the map below).

The macro backdrop: Brazil's policy rate (Selic) runs high (~14-15% in 2026), so cash-like local fixed income offers a real return rare in developed markets. The recurring advisory lesson is that liquidity and fees matter more than the headline rate, and that prefixados (fixed-rate, longer-dated) trade away liquidity for a thin spread.

Chart data unavailable — these are local retail instrument categories, not single tracked price series. (The per-instrument concept notes carry their mechanics; the listed [[FII]] sleeve has its own price chart.)

## Instrument map

| Instrument | What it is | Watch-outs |
|---|---|---|
| Tesouro Direto / NTN-B | Sovereign bonds (Selic/pós, Prefixado, IPCA+, Renda+) | Prefixado and IPCA+ are marked-to-market; selling early can realize losses |
| [[CDB]] | Bank time deposit, FGC-insured to R$250k per CPF per institution | Mid-tier banks pay more; laddering across FGC limits is common |
| LCI / LCA | Tax-exempt real-estate / agribusiness-backed bank notes | Lock-up periods; lower gross rate offset by the tax exemption |
| [[Debêntures]] | Corporate bonds (e.g. [[Petrobras]], [[Equatorial]]) | Credit + liquidity risk; incentivadas are tax-exempt |
| [[CRI]] / [[CRA]] | Securitized real-estate / agribusiness receivables (e.g. a [[Marfrig]] CRA) | Tax-exempt but illiquid; secondary sale takes a mark-to-market hit |
| FIDC | Receivables-backed funds | Opaque underlying credit |
| [[COE]] | Certificado de Operações Estruturadas — the BR structured note | High embedded fees, frequent underperformance / default. See [[Structured products]] |
| Fundos / [[Previdência privada]] (PGBL, VGBL) | Funds and tax-advantaged pension wrappers | High admin fees; most pension funds reportedly lag CDI; the value is illiquidity-enforced discipline |
| [[FII]] | Listed real-estate funds (Brazilian REITs) | Hard to select; opaque; the best ones are closed |

## Recurring advisory lessons

- Liquidity over rate: do not lock into a prefixado out to 2030 for ~1% extra when a 100%-of-CDI bank [[CDB]] is fully liquid.
- "Free" advice via commission is the most expensive — distributors push the highest-fee, longest-lock products ([[COE]], retail-PE FIPs like [[Aqua Capital]]'s agribusiness fund, certain FIIs).
- Offshore wrappers sold for asset-shielding (e.g. Investors Trust) carry multi-year locks and exit penalties that can cost more than 60% of capital — see [[Structured products]].
- Credit managers such as [[JGP]] hold these instruments inside funds; local corporate-distress / judicial-recovery (RJ) risk is the live concern.
