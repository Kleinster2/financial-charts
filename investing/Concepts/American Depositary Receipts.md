# American Depositary Receipts

An American Depositary Receipt (ADR) is a [[Depositary receipts|depositary receipt]] issued by a US depositary bank against shares of a foreign company, letting that company's equity trade in dollars on a US exchange or over the counter. It is the largest and most developed DR market and the default way non-US companies — from [[TSMC]] and [[Alibaba]] to the Chinese state telecoms — reach US investors without a US incorporation. Each ADR represents a set number of underlying shares (the ratio), trades and settles in USD, and the certificate is the ADR while the underlying deposited share is the American Depositary Share (ADS); the terms are used interchangeably in practice. Like every depositary receipt the ADR is organized along two axes — sponsored vs unsponsored, and the listing level — and on US markets the wrapper almost always travels with [[Foreign private issuer]] status (20-F/6-K, IFRS).

## The level structure

| Level | Where it trades | Capital raise | SEC filing | Disclosure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Level I | OTC (Pink Sheets) | No | Form F-6 | Minimal; relies on the Rule 12g3-2(b) exemption; can be unsponsored |
| Level II | NYSE / Nasdaq listed | No | Form F-6 + 20-F | Full periodic reporting; sponsored |
| Level III | NYSE / Nasdaq listed | Yes (public offering) | Form F-1 + 20-F | Full reporting + offering disclosure; sponsored |
| Rule 144A | Private (QIBs only) | Yes (private placement) | None (exempt) | For qualified institutional buyers ($100M+); no public registration |

The level is the US analogue of the BDR's nível: Level I is the unsponsored/OTC floor, Level III is the only one that lets the company raise new capital, and 144A is a private-placement side door to US institutions. Form F-6 registers the receipts; the 20-F is the annual report.

## Sponsored vs unsponsored

A sponsored ADR is created with the company's involvement under a deposit agreement with a single depositary; an unsponsored ADR is created by a depositary bank on its own, off the company's home listing, with no company participation — and only Level I can be unsponsored. Most OTC foreign-stock ADRs are unsponsored Level I; every listed (Level II/III) ADR is sponsored.

## The China-ADR cluster

The most consequential ADR story in the vault is the US-listed China complex and its delisting risk, which runs on two separate tracks:

- [[Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act]] — the audit-transparency track: US-listed foreign issuers face delisting if the [[PCAOB]] cannot inspect their auditors. It threatened the entire China-ADR universe until the Aug/Dec 2022 audit-access breakthrough defused it.
- [[Executive Order 13959]] — the ownership track: bans US persons from holding designated Chinese military-linked companies, which forced the 2021 delisting of the China telecom ADRs ([[China Mobile]], [[China Telecom]], [[China Unicom]]) from the NYSE.

Many large China ADRs are also multi-structured — a US ADR plus a Hong Kong H-share plus sometimes a mainland A-share — so the ADR is one leg of a multi-listing, and the delisting tracks push capital from the ADR toward the H-share. See [[China special valuation]] for the re-rating of the names that stayed onshore.

## Synthesis

The ADR is the depositary-receipt template at its largest scale, and the vault touches it constantly because it is the wrapper on most non-US names traded in the US. Two readings matter. First, the level/sponsorship structure is the US mirror of the BDR's níveis — the same sponsored-vs-unsponsored and listing-tier logic — so a reader who learns one learns the other. Second, the ADR is where the US weaponized-interdependence tools land: [[Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act]] (audit) and [[Executive Order 13959]] (ownership) both operate by threatening the ADR's listing, which is why the China-ADR delisting cluster is an ADR story before it is a geopolitics story. The ADR is the instrument; [[Foreign private issuer]] is its reporting regime; and the [[Brazilian Depositary Receipts|BDR]] is its mirror image one hemisphere south.
