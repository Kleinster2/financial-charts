#concept #space #policy

**CLPS (Commercial Lunar Payload Services)** — [[NASA]] program awarding fixed-price contracts to commercial providers for lunar surface delivery of NASA payloads. Launched 2018 as part of the broader push to commercialize NASA's customer relationships. The program seeded [[Firefly Aerospace]] (Blue Ghost lander, Mission 1 successful Mar 2025), [[Intuitive Machines]] (multiple missions), [[Astrobotic]], and several other commercial lunar providers. Strategic relevance grows materially under [[Jared Isaacman]]'s Q1 2026 lunar architecture restructure: NASA's $20B over 7-year permanent lunar base program needs many more landers and surface payloads than CLPS originally planned.

## Why CLPS matters

CLPS is the lunar-surface analog of the Commercial Resupply Services (CRS) and Commercial Crew programs that seeded [[SpaceX]] and [[Boeing]] for low Earth orbit. The pattern: NASA shifts from cost-plus prime contracts to fixed-price commercial purchases, accepting some failures in exchange for cost reduction and supplier diversity.

Q1 2026 architecture shift impact: with the lunar base program ($20B / 7 years) requiring many landers, many rovers, and full surface infrastructure, CLPS-tier providers ([[Firefly Aerospace]], [[Intuitive Machines]], [[Astrobotic]]) and surface-mobility providers ([[Lunar Outpost]]) are positioned for material scope expansion. [[Lunar Outpost]] CEO [[Justin Cyrus]] described the beachhead market 10x'ing from ~$3B to ~$30B+ over a two-week window following the Mar 2026 NASA announcement.
