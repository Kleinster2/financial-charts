#concept #space #government

**Lunar Gateway** — Planned [[NASA]] space station in lunar orbit (NRHO — Near Rectilinear Halo Orbit). Designed as the staging point for Earth-Moon transit, lunar surface missions, and (originally) future Mars missions. **Paused** by [[Jared Isaacman]] in March 2026 as part of the Q1 2026 architectural restructure: the $20B over 7-year permanent lunar base near the south pole replaced Gateway-orbit-rendezvous as the [[Artemis]] architecture, with hardware to be repurposed for surface use.

## Why pause

Per [[Jared Isaacman]]'s Q1 2026 architecture restructure, Gateway's original justification — orbital rendezvous as the way to stage lunar surface missions — was undercut by the structural cost shift to [[Starship]]-native economics. With Starship at $90M / $900/kg (per [[Voyager]]'s 10K disclosure for the [[Star Lab]] contract) and [[Boeing]] SLS at $4B+/flight, the architecture redraws around direct Earth-orbit-to-Lunar-surface transit:

- [[SpaceX]] Starship docks with [[Lockheed Martin]] [[Orion]] in Earth orbit
- Starship carries the capsule + crew toward the lunar surface
- Surface base + power/comms/habitats replaces the orbital staging requirement

Gateway hardware (PPE, HALO, I-HAB) is being assessed for surface deployment — power systems and habitation modules are repurposable in principle, though the engineering for surface operations versus lunar-orbit operations is not trivially equivalent.
