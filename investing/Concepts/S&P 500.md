# S&P 500

US large-cap equity benchmark tracking 500 companies. Maintained by [[S&P Global]]. Market-cap weighted, ~$50T total. The default benchmark for US equity performance.

## Seasoning rule review (Mar 2026)

[[S&P Dow Jones Indices]] is reviewing the 12-month "seasoning" requirement — the rule that a company must be publicly listed for at least 12 months before S&P 500 inclusion. Published "Seasoning to Taste" blog post (Mar 3), then escalated to actively engaging stakeholders (Bloomberg, Mar 14). No decision yet; formal consultation would take several weeks.

Driven by the upcoming mega-IPO wave: [[SpaceX]] ($1.75T target), [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]] — all considering listing in 2026. If the 10 largest venture-backed US companies all listed and joined, they would make up ~4.5% of the index — more than the weight of the energy sector.

~$24T in assets benchmarked to the index. Immediate post-IPO inclusion of a $1.75T company would trigger massive passive buying flows. [[Nasdaq]] and [[FTSE Russell]] are running parallel consultations on fast-tracking large IPOs into their own indices.
