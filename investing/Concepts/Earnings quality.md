**Earnings quality** — How reliable and sustainable reported earnings are. Cash backing, accounting conservatism, persistence.

---

## Quality dimensions

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Cash backing** | Earnings convert to cash |
| **Persistence** | Repeatable, not one-time |
| **Conservatism** | Not aggressive accounting |
| **Transparency** | Clear, understandable |

---

## Cash conversion check

| Ratio | Formula | Good |
|-------|---------|------|
| CFO/Net income | Operating CF / NI | >100% |
| FCF/Net income | FCF / NI | >80% |
| Accruals ratio | (NI - CFO) / Assets | <5% |

---

## Accruals warning

| Accrual level | Interpretation |
|---------------|----------------|
| Negative | High quality (cash > earnings) |
| Low positive | Normal |
| High positive | Earnings ahead of cash |
| Rising | Deteriorating quality |

---

## Red flags

| Signal | Concern |
|--------|---------|
| Revenue recognition changes | Pulling forward |
| Rising receivables | Collection issues |
| Falling reserves | Releasing cushions |
| Non-GAAP divergence | Adjustments masking |
| Frequent restatements | Control issues |
| Auditor changes | Disagreements |

---

## Revenue quality

| Signal | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Organic growth | Sustainable |
| M&A-driven | Integration risk |
| Price vs. volume | Mix matters |
| Customer concentration | Risk level |
| Recurring vs. one-time | Predictability |

---

## Expense quality

| Signal | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Capitalizing vs. expensing | Aggressive? |
| Depreciation assumptions | Reasonable life? |
| Stock comp treatment | Included in costs? |
| Restructuring frequency | One-time or ongoing? |

---

## Non-GAAP adjustments

| Adjustment | Validity |
|------------|----------|
| Amortization of intangibles | Often reasonable |
| Stock-based comp | Real cost, include |
| Restructuring (repeated) | Suspicious |
| "One-time" items (recurring) | Red flag |
| Litigation reserves | Case-by-case |
