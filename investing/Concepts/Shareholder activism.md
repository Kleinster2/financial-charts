**Shareholder activism** — The use of an equity stake to pressure corporate management into changes the investor believes will unlock value. A $19B+ annual deployment category (Elliott alone in 2025) with academic evidence of ~7% abnormal returns around 13D filings and persistent long-term value creation.

---

## Taxonomy

| Axis | Spectrum |
|------|----------|
| **Constructive ↔ Confrontational** | [[ValueAct Capital]] quiet engagement ↔ [[Elliott Management]] public campaigns, litigation, proxy fights |
| **Long ↔ Short** | Long activists buy stakes and push for change ↔ Short activists publish research alleging fraud (see [[Activist short selling]]) |
| **Operational ↔ Strategic** | [[Starboard Value]] margin improvement, cost cuts ↔ [[Third Point]] breakups, M&A |
| **ESG** | [[JANA Partners]] / Engine No. 1 — governance and climate-focused campaigns |

## Key strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 13D filing | Crossing 5% ownership with activist intent triggers Schedule 13D disclosure |
| Private engagement | Letters to management, behind-the-scenes negotiation |
| Public campaigns | Open letters, white papers, investor presentations |
| Proxy fights | Nominating alternative board candidates for shareholder vote |
| Board seats | Representation via settlement or contested election |
| Breakup campaigns | Pushing spin-offs, divestitures, or sales to close [[Conglomerate discount]] |
| Capital return | Buybacks, special dividends, leverage optimization |
| Operational activism | Margin improvement, cost cuts, management changes |
| CEF activism | Exploiting [[NAV discount]]s, pressuring boards ([[Saba Capital]] specialty) |

## Academic evidence

**Brav, Jiang, Partnoy & Thomas (2008)**, *Journal of Finance*: Studied 2001-2006 interventions. ~7% abnormal return around 13D announcement with no reversal over the following year. Activists achieved success or partial success in two-thirds of cases. Targets saw higher payouts, improved operating performance, and increased CEO turnover.

**Bebchuk, Brav & Jiang (2015)**, *Columbia Law Review*: Examined five-year post-intervention windows. No evidence that initial stock gains reverse over the long term — refuting the "short-termism" critique. Activist interventions, including adversarial ones, showed persistent value creation.

## Regulatory framework

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Schedule 13D** | Required within 5 business days of crossing 5% with activist intent (shortened from 10 calendar days, effective Feb 5, 2024) |
| **Schedule 13G** | Passive filing; accelerated deadlines effective Sep 30, 2024 |
| **13D amendments** | Due within 2 business days of material changes (replaced "promptly" standard) |
| **XML mandate** | Since Dec 18, 2024, all 13D/G filings must use structured XML format |
| **HSR Act** | Pre-merger notification required above ~$119.5M threshold; large activist stakes may trigger filing |
| **Poison pills** | Board defense diluting activist stakes (typically triggered at 10-15% ownership) |

## Historical evolution

| Era | Characteristics |
|-----|----------------|
| **1980s corporate raiders** | Icahn, Pickens — hostile takeovers, greenmail, leveraged buyouts |
| **2000s hedge fund professionalization** | Analytical rigor, public presentations, legal infrastructure |
| **2010s constructive turn** | ValueAct, Trian — companies proactively engaging activists |
| **2020s democratization** | First-time activists at 29%, settlements replacing proxy fights, ESG activism (Engine No. 1 at ExxonMobil) |
