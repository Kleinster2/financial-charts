**Dollar cost averaging** — Investing fixed amounts at regular intervals. Reduces timing risk. Behavioral discipline.

---

## How it works

| Period | Price | Amount | Shares |
|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| Month 1 | $50 | $1000 | 20 |
| Month 2 | $40 | $1000 | 25 |
| Month 3 | $60 | $1000 | 16.7 |
| **Total** | **Avg $48.39** | **$3000** | **61.7** |

Buys more shares when prices are low.

---

## DCA vs lump sum

| Factor | DCA | Lump sum |
|--------|-----|----------|
| Expected return | Lower (2/3 of time) |Higher |
| Volatility | Lower | Higher |
| Regret risk | Lower | Higher |
| Behavioral | Easier | Harder |

Studies show lump sum wins ~67% of time, but DCA wins psychologically.

---

## When DCA makes sense

| Situation | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Regular income | Match cash flows |
| High uncertainty | Reduce timing risk |
| High volatility | Average cost down |
| Emotional investor | Discipline aid |
| Large windfall | Reduce regret |

---

## When lump sum wins

| Situation | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Long time horizon | Market rises over time |
| High conviction | Maximize exposure |
| Rising market | Opportunity cost |
| Tax advantage | Maximize compounding |

---

## DCA frequency

| Frequency | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|------|------|
| Weekly | More averaging | Higher transaction costs |
| Bi-weekly | Matches paycheck | Common choice |
| Monthly | Simple, low cost | Less averaging |
| Quarterly | Minimal maintenance | More timing risk |

---

## Behavioral benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Removes emotion | Automatic investing |
| Reduces regret | No single decision |
| Builds habit | Consistent saving |
| Handles volatility | Turns drops into opportunity |
