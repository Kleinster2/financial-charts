**Selic** — Brazil's policy interest rate, set by COPOM ([[Banco Central do Brasil|BCB]]'s monetary policy committee). The overnight rate on government securities repos. [[CDI]] tracks Selic closely. Among highest real rates globally.

---

## Definition

Selic (Sistema Especial de Liquidação e de Custódia) has two related meanings:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Selic target | Policy rate set by COPOM (8 meetings/year) |
| Selic effective | Actual overnight rate on gov't securities repos |

The effective rate typically equals the target. BCB conducts open market operations to maintain alignment.

## Current level

| Date | Selic target | Move | Framing |
|------|--------------|------|---------|
| Feb 2026 | 15.00% | Peak of cycle | Tight; inflation watch |
| Apr 16, 2026 | 14.75% | -25bp (Mar COPOM) | First cut after 2024-25 hike cycle |
| Apr 29, 2026 | 14.50% | -25bp | Hawkish: "inflation moving further away from target"; cycle still on but pace under question |
| May 9, 2026 | 14.50% (unchanged) | April IPCA prints at 4.39% YoY (vs 3.81% Mar) | Rate-cut path now at risk; market repriced forward DI curve hawkishly |

COPOM hiked aggressively through 2024-2025 (10.75% → 15%) and began cutting in 2026, with two 25bp moves in March and April. The April 29 cut came with notably hawkish framing — BCB describing inflation as "accelerated, moving further away from target" and citing geopolitical uncertainty (Middle East). Ten days later, the May 9 April IPCA print at 4.39% YoY (up from 3.81% in March) brushed the 4.5% upper tolerance band, putting the remaining cut path at risk. Forward 2027 Selic guidance now ~13.75% (Trading Economics model) vs prior Focus survey 12.5% year-end 2026 — meaning the market priced ~125bp less easing into the curve over the two weeks. Election-year fiscal uncertainty ([[2026 Brazil presidential election]]) and [[Petrobras]] / Brent transmission through diesel imports ([[Brazil's diesel paradox|paradox]]) compound the inflation risk. See [[Brazil corporate bond rout February 2026]] for corporate impact and [[Brazil]] for the May 13 equity-cluster fallout.

## Fiscal last-mile diagnosis (BTG AgroForum, May 2026)

[[Mansueto Almeida]]'s AgroForum Cuiabá framework is that [[Selic]] is high because Brazil has not yet solved the final spending-growth constraint, not because a double-digit real rate is structurally unavoidable. He used the Temer-era sequence as the benchmark: when the government slowed real federal spending growth and made pension reform credible, the market rapidly repriced Selic from the mid-teens toward single digits.

For the 2026 cycle, the diagnosis is narrower but still binding. Almeida argued that Brazil enters the election year with mid-single-digit inflation, deep reserves, low unemployment, and private capital markets capable of funding infrastructure without a BNDES credit boom. The unresolved variable is mandatory spending growth. If that slows, the DI curve can price a lower terminal rate; if it does not, the central bank stays trapped between fiscal impulse, indexation, and inflation expectations.

## COPOM (Monetary Policy Committee)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Members | 9 (BCB president + 8 directors) |
| Meetings | 8 per year (every ~45 days) |
| Decision | Majority vote |
| Communication | Statement same day, minutes 6 days later |
| Guidance | Forward guidance on likely path |

COPOM targets IPCA inflation (headline CPI). Current target: 3% ± 1.5pp.

## Historical context

| Period | Selic | Context |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 1999 | 45% | Post-Real Plan crisis |
| 2003 | 26.5% | Lula transition |
| 2012-2013 | 7.25% | Historical low |
| 2016 | 14.25% | Dilma impeachment |
| 2020 | 2.00% | COVID emergency cut |
| 2022-2023 | 13.75% | Post-COVID tightening |
| 2025-2026 | 12-15% | Inflation fighting cycle |

Brazil has never had sustained low rates — structural factors (fiscal, indexation) keep real rates high.

## Relationship to other rates

| Rate | Relationship to Selic |
|------|----------------------|
| [[CDI]] | Tracks Selic very closely (few bp below) |
| [[Cupom cambial]] | Selic minus expected FX depreciation |
| DI futures | Market expectations of future Selic path |
| IPCA+ bonds | Real rate = nominal Selic expectation minus inflation |

### Why CDI ≈ Selic

Both are overnight unsecured interbank rates:
- Selic — repos collateralized by government securities
- CDI — unsecured interbank deposits

Spread is minimal because credit risk between major banks is negligible overnight.

## Impact on markets

### Equities

High Selic = headwind for equities:
- Risk-free rate competes with equity returns
- Higher discount rate reduces valuations
- Debt service costs rise for leveraged companies

### Fixed income

High Selic = attractive carry:
- Foreign investors earn high nominal yields
- But currency risk offsets (BRL volatility)
- [[Cross-currency basis]] affects hedged returns

### Derivatives

DI futures at [[B3]] = most liquid interest rate market in LatAm:
- Curve extends 10+ years
- Hedge funds actively trade rate expectations
- Volume rises with volatility

## Real rate context

Brazil's real rate (Selic minus inflation) among highest globally:

| Country | Policy rate | Inflation | Real rate |
|---------|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| [[Brazil]] | 14.50% | 4.39% (April IPCA YoY) | ~10.1% |
| [[Mexico]] | 10.00% | ~4% | ~6% |
| US | 4.50% | ~3% | ~1.5% |

High real rates reflect:
- Fiscal credibility concerns
- Inflation indexation legacy
- History of default/restructuring
- Shallow domestic savings

## Forward guidance

COPOM provides explicit forward guidance:
- "Expects to raise rates by X bp at next meeting"
- "Sees need for significantly contractionary policy"
- "Will remain vigilant on inflation expectations"

Markets react sharply to guidance changes — COPOM communication is key event.
