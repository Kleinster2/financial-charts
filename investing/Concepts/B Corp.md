# B Corp

Certification from B Lab for companies meeting high standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency. Over 8,000 companies across 90+ countries hold the designation, which requires scoring 80+ on the B Impact Assessment and amending corporate governing documents to consider all stakeholders — not just shareholders.

## Synopsis

B Corp certification emerged in 2006 as part of the broader ESG movement, attempting to create a verifiable standard for companies that claim to balance profit with purpose. The assessment covers five dimensions: governance, workers, community, environment, and customers. Companies must recertify every three years, and the process has become more rigorous over time — notable decertifications (Nespresso's parent [[Nestle]] failed in 2023; Brewdog was stripped in 2024) have demonstrated that the standard has teeth, or at least sharper teeth than most ESG labels.

The investment relevance is primarily as a brand differentiator in consumer-facing categories. Companies like [[Vital Farms]], [[Patagonia]], and Ben & Jerry's ([[Unilever]]) use B Corp status to signal values alignment to a premium customer base willing to pay up for ethical credentials. The risk is credibility erosion: as the number of certified companies has grown from hundreds to 8,000+, skeptics argue the certification is becoming a marketing exercise rather than a meaningful operational constraint. The legal component — amending corporate governance documents to protect stakeholder consideration — is the most substantive element, as it provides some legal defense for boards that prioritize social goals over short-term shareholder returns. Whether that protection holds up in a hostile M&A scenario remains largely untested.
