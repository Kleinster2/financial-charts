**Venture capital** — asset class focused on funding early-stage, high-growth private companies in exchange for equity, with returns driven by a power-law distribution where a small number of outsized winners (10-100x) compensate for a majority of losses. US VC deployment peaked at ~$345B in 2021 before collapsing to ~$170B in 2023 as rising rates repriced risk and the IPO window closed. The 2024-2025 recovery has been heavily concentrated in AI, with [[Anthropic]], [[OpenAI]], and infrastructure plays absorbing a disproportionate share of late-stage capital.

## Investment landscape

VC matters to public market investors through three channels: IPO supply (VC-backed companies are the primary source of high-growth public listings), private-to-public valuation transmission (markdowns/markups in VC portfolios signal public market direction for growth stocks), and competitive dynamics (VC-funded startups reshape industries, creating both disruptors and disrupted). The "tourist capital" phenomenon of 2020-2021 — hedge funds and sovereign wealth funds flooding into late-stage VC — inflated valuations and created a $2T+ unrealized value overhang that continues to pressure the market.

Key stages: Seed ($1-5M), Series A ($10-30M), Series B ($30-80M), growth/late-stage ($100M+), pre-IPO. The industry is concentrated: the top 20 firms capture the majority of returns, driven by access to the best deals (proprietary deal flow) and board seats that improve outcomes.
