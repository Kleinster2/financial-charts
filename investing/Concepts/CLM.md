Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM) — enterprise software that manages the full contract lifecycle: authoring, negotiation, execution, compliance, obligation tracking, and renewal. Market ~$1.5-3B (2025), growing 10-13% CAGR. A prime [[AI disintermediation]] target.

## Why CLM matters

Inefficient contracting costs enterprises ~9% of annual revenue. CLM software speeds up approvals ~50% and lowers management costs ~25%. 78% of organizations invested in CLM over the past 5 years.

The contract is the atomic unit of business relationships — every procurement, sale, partnership, and employment starts with one. Whoever controls contract intelligence controls a high-leverage data layer across the enterprise.

## AI disruption dynamics

CLM is one of the clearest [[AI disintermediation]] targets in enterprise software:

What AI can do now:
- Contract drafting from templates and natural language prompts
- Clause extraction and risk scoring
- Obligation tracking and compliance monitoring
- Redlining and negotiation suggestions
- Metadata extraction across thousands of contracts

What still needs humans:
- Strategic negotiation judgment
- Novel clause construction for unique deals
- Cross-functional stakeholder alignment
- Regulatory interpretation in ambiguous cases

The disruption pattern: AI-native startups (Avokaado, Leah) can offer 80% of CLM functionality without the heavy implementation overhead and tech debt that plagues traditional platforms. Enterprise incumbents ([[Icertis]], [[DocuSign]]) are responding with agentic AI features — but their advantage is integration depth and switching costs, not the AI itself.

90% of procurement leaders surveyed are considering or already using AI agents for contract operations. 42% of organizations currently implementing AI in contract management.

## Connection to enterprise software disruption

CLM sits at the intersection of several [[Claude Cowork disruption February 2026|SaaSpocalypse]] themes:
- Information intermediary (extracts value from contract data) — classic disintermediation target
- High implementation cost + customization → tech debt → vulnerability to simpler AI-native alternatives
- Enterprise integration moats (SAP, Salesforce, Oracle) provide defense but also lock-in
- Legal/compliance requirements create regulatory moats that pure AI tools can't easily bypass
