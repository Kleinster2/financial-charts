# Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles

Iconic media franchise owned by [[Paramount Global]], spanning animated series, feature films, toys, and consumer products since the original Mirage Studios comic in 1984. Licensed to [[Mattel]] for toys starting 2027, shifting from the previous long-term arrangement with [[Hasbro]]/Playmates and representing a meaningful IP win for [[Mattel]]'s post-[[Barbie]] franchise expansion strategy.

## Synopsis

TMNT has been one of the most durable children's entertainment franchises for four decades, cycling through multiple animated series reboots, five theatrical films, and consistent merchandise revenue. The franchise is controlled by [[Paramount Global]] through its Nickelodeon division, which acquired the rights from Mirage Studios. Recent animated iterations (Rise of the TMNT, Mutant Mayhem) have kept the IP culturally relevant with younger audiences, and the 2023 Mutant Mayhem film grossed $180M worldwide — a solid commercial performance that validated the franchise's ongoing appeal and greenlit a sequel.

The toy license shift from Playmates Toys to [[Mattel]] in 2027 is the vault-relevant angle. [[Mattel]] has been aggressively pursuing franchise licensing deals in the wake of the Barbie film's $1.4B box office success, seeking to prove that its toy-to-entertainment pipeline works in reverse as well (entertainment-to-toy). Securing TMNT gives [[Mattel]] a proven boys' action franchise to complement its portfolio of girls' brands ([[Barbie]], [[Hot Wheels]], Polly Pocket). For [[Paramount Global]], the license deal generates steady royalty income from one of its most recognizable IP assets, partially offsetting the streaming losses at Paramount+.
