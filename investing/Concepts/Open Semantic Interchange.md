# Open Semantic Interchange

Open, vendor-neutral specification for the semantic model — how business definitions (a metric like "active customer," its dimensions and calculation logic) are written in a standard machine-readable form so they stay consistent across BI tools and AI agents. Launched by [[Snowflake]] on September 23, 2025 with founding partners including [[Salesforce]], dbt Labs, [[BlackRock]] and RelationalAI, and grown into a 50+ organization coalition. The incumbents' collaborative answer to the same pressure behind [[Open Knowledge Format]]: as AI agents become the primary consumer of metadata, the description layer commoditizes — OSI is the industry choosing to standardize it on terms it co-governs rather than cede it to one vendor.

---

## Synthesis

OSI matters to the [[Data catalog disruption]] thesis as the structural counter-move: rather than be disrupted by an open knowledge/metadata format, the catalog vendors, warehouses, BI tools and dbt formed one. By mid-2026 membership spanned essentially every relevant name — warehouses ([[Snowflake]], [[Databricks]]), catalog pure-plays ([[Collibra]], [[Alation]], [[Atlan]]), [[Informatica]] / [[Salesforce]], ThoughtSpot, Starburst, Qlik, Strategy — and [[Google]] and AWS, which joined in November 2025. That breadth is the tell: when every competitor joins a standard, the standard's job is to commoditize a layer nobody wants a rival to own, pushing the real competition up-stack (governance, serving, UX). The contrast with [[Open Knowledge Format]] is altitude and governance: OSI is a consortium-governed structured semantic-model spec (metrics/business logic); OKF is a Google-led broad knowledge format (markdown prose for agents). Google sits in both camps. There is no public price series to chart. (Market data not applicable.)
