# Frontier models

The leading-edge tier of general-purpose AI models — those defining what is currently possible in reasoning, coding, multimodal understanding, and agentic workflows. The frontier is a moving target; a model is "frontier" only while it is at or near the top of cross-lab benchmarks. Below the frontier sits a much larger population of open-weight and specialized models that trail by one or two generations.

## Characteristics

- Multi-hundred-billion to trillion-parameter scale (or highly efficient MoE equivalents).
- Training runs at the frontier of available compute — typically 10²⁵–10²⁶ FLOPs.
- Multimodal input (text, image, voice, video) becoming table stakes.
- Reasoning/thinking modes with parallel subagent architectures.
- Tool use, agentic task execution, long-horizon planning.
- Usually proprietary — the open-weight frontier trails by months.

## Why it matters

Frontier models set the ceiling for what downstream products can do. Every capability unlock at the frontier cascades through enterprise AI tooling, consumer assistants, and autonomous agents within quarters. The labs operating at the frontier — [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], [[Google DeepMind]], [[Meta]] — account for the lion's share of global AI compute spending and the strategic partnerships behind it ([[NVIDIA]], hyperscaler deals, sovereign compute contracts).
