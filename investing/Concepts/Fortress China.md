# Fortress China

[[Xi Jinping]]'s multi-year drive to insulate [[China]]'s economy from external shocks — a unified framework connecting energy self-sufficiency, strategic commodity reserves, dual circulation, rare-earth/critical-mineral control, and the export-ban toolkit. The [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] is the first large-scale real-world stress test. The [[FT]]'s April 11 2026 reporting on "cracks" turned the phrase from analyst shorthand into mainstream framing.

---

## Synopsis

The Fortress framework assumes that great-power friction, tariff regimes, and kinetic conflict will disrupt import chains — so the state pre-positions inventory, dual-sources critical inputs, and holds retaliatory leverage to deter coercion. Every pillar is a defensive response to a specific vulnerability identified in earlier shocks: the 2018 US-China trade war (semiconductors), the 2020 Covid supply shock (medical/PPE/logistics), 2022 Russia-Ukraine (commodity weaponization), and the 2026 Hormuz crisis (energy + industrial gases). The policy cadence is Xi's, but the plumbing lives at [[NDRC]], [[He Lifeng]]'s economic cluster, and the SOE buffer layer.

In the opening weeks of the 2026 Iran war, the fortress worked: strategic petroleum reserve absorbed oil price shock, SOE refiners absorbed margin loss, LNG diversification to Russia and Australia limited gas exposure, and a de facto export ban on diesel/jet fuel/some [[Fertilizer|fertilisers]] protected domestic supply. By week 6 it cracked: [[China PPI]] turned positive year-on-year for the first time since 2022, [[Polyethylene]] prices roughly doubled, high-grade [[Helium]] spot rose 110%, and energy-intensive sector costs rose up to 25% ([[Peng Shaozong]], former [[NDRC]], via [[FT]] April 11). Xi's April 7 response was structural: accelerate renewables, nuclear, and coal as the self-sufficiency stack. See [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]] for the data.

## First stress test — 2026 Hormuz crisis

Weeks 1-3 (Feb 28 - Mar 20): Fortress absorbed the shock. Strategic reserves untouched, Gulf oil partially replaced via Russia, Venezuela, and selective Iran passage.

Weeks 4-6 (Mar 21 - Apr 11): Cracks. [[China PPI]] turned positive year-on-year (first time since 2022); [[Polyethylene]] prices doubled; some [[Carbon fibre]] up 20%; high-grade [[Helium]] +110% spot; +25% cost passthrough in logistics, transport, aviation, shipping, steel (Peng Shaozong). Small exporters in [[Wenzhou]], [[Yiwu]] absorbing margin; SOE oil sector absorbing refining margins.

Structural response (Apr 7): Xi's CCTV speech reframes renewables + nuclear + coal as the energy-security stack — not as climate policy. The transition from "Fortress defends" to "Fortress adapts by accelerating self-sufficiency."
