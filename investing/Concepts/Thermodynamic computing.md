Thermodynamic computing is a physics-based computing approach that tries to use thermodynamic processes and physical noise as a computational resource rather than treating noise only as an error to suppress. In the HOF cluster, it matters because [[Extropic]] is trying to turn that idea into AI hardware.

---

## Synthesis

The investment question is whether AI's energy constraint eventually forces new compute primitives. GPUs scale by adding more digital arithmetic and memory bandwidth; thermodynamic computing asks whether probabilistic AI workloads can be mapped onto physical systems that naturally sample, relax, and exchange energy. If that works, it could change the cost curve for inference or generative sampling. If it does not, the concept remains an elegant physics analogy rather than a semiconductor market.

Chart data unavailable: there is no public market or usage series for thermodynamic-computing hardware adoption in the vault. Treat this as an early technical concept note tied to private-company research, not as a chartable market cohort.
