# TSMC N2

[[TSMC]]'s 2nm-class logic node — the company's first gate-all-around (nanosheet) process, ramping to high-volume manufacturing through 2026. N2 is the density leader of the 2nm-class generation at roughly 313 MTr/mm2 (high-density), ahead of [[Intel 18A]]'s ~238 MTr/mm2, and is the node on which [[Apple]]'s flagship silicon is expected to run.

N2's one architectural lag is power delivery: unlike [[Intel 18A]], N2 does not include backside power delivery, which TSMC defers to a later node (A16). So for one generation the 2nm-class split is "TSMC denser, Intel ahead on backside power," with TSMC's decisive advantage being yield — its mature-node track record clears 90% while Intel 18A runs ~60-75% in 2026. N2 is the benchmark against which the [[Foundry Wars]] now measure Intel's comeback. See [[Intel 18A]] for the head-to-head.
