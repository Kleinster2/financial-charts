# Dynamic TAO

Dynamic TAO (dTAO) is the late-2025 / early-2026 upgrade to [[Bittensor]] that replaced flat TAO emissions with per-subnet alpha tokens. Each of Bittensor's ~128-129 subnets now mints its own alpha token. Alpha/TAO is priced continuously by an internal automated market maker, so capital flows between subnets are set by markets rather than by governance.

---

## Why it matters

Under pre-dTAO emissions, all TAO holders benefited uniformly from any subnet's growth — a free-rider structure that weakened per-subnet accountability. dTAO changes this:

- Each subnet is now investable directly via its alpha token (e.g., SN62 alpha for [[Ridges AI]]).
- Subnet owners and miners hold meaningful stakes in their own subnet's token, aligning incentives.
- Subnet-level market caps become legible — top subnets (Ridges, Targon SN4, Gradients SN56) now visibly separate from long-tail subnets.

---

## Market structure effects

| Before dTAO | After dTAO |
|-------------|-----------|
| Flat emissions across all subnets | Per-subnet alpha tokens |
| Governance-driven capital allocation | Market-priced allocation via AMM |
| No subnet-level equity equivalent | Alpha tokens = subnet equity proxy |
| Single TAO exposure | TAO base + 128+ alpha token exposures |
