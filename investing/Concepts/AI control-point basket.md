# AI control-point basket

Plain English: this is the part of software that can still matter under AI because it owns a bottleneck. The question is not whether "software" wins. The question is whether a company still controls data, identity, workflow context, or compute access that agents have to pass through.

[[Software AI bifurcation]] implies that some software names should trade less like seat-compression victims and more like stack owners. This basket tracks the representative public names for that middle bucket.

---

## What it is testing

This is not a pure winners basket. It is a control-points basket.

The thesis is that companies with durable access to enterprise data, distribution, identity, or compute can still defend economics even as agentic workflows spread. That does not mean they are immune. It means they should hold up better than the software names whose value proposition is mostly selling seats into tasks AI can absorb.

### Why equal-weight?

There was no single catalyst that cleanly revealed the right weights the way Feb 3-4 did for [[AI workflow disruption basket|AIWD]] or Apr 10 did for [[Edge control-plane risk basket]]. This basket comes from the taxonomy itself, so equal-weighting is the cleanest way to test whether the category exists at all.

---

## Why this matters

If the taxonomy is right, the software conversation should stop being "apps lose, infra wins" or "software is cheap." The more precise framing is:

- workflow software without a hard bottleneck gets repriced lower
- control-point owners hold up better, even if they are not fully immune
- runtime/perimeter names face a separate control-plane question

AICP is the public test for that middle layer.
