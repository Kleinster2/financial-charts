# March 2026 jobs report

**Economic indicator** — Monthly employment data showing mixed signals with strong job gains but weakening labor force participation and wage growth concerns.

---

## April 3, 2026 — Initial release

**Key headlines:**
- **NFP:** +178K vs 59K est, vs -133K revised Feb. Jan revised UP +34K to 160K. 3-mo avg ~68K
- **Unemployment:** 4.3%. Labor force shrank 396K. Participation: 61.9% (lowest since Nov 2021)
- **Household survey:** 64K FEWER employed. U-6: 8%. Avg weeks unemployed: 25.3
- **Wages:** +0.2% MoM, +3.5% YoY (lowest since May 2021). Hours: 34.2 (-0.1)

**Sector breakdown:**
- Healthcare: +76K (Kaiser strike returnees ~35K)
- Construction: +26K
- Transport: +21K
- Fed govt: -18K
- Financial: -15K

**Fed implications:** 77.5% prob holds through 2026. Virtually no April FOMC move. Fed funds at 3.5-3.75%

**Commentary:** Heather Long (Navy Federal): "rocky year... almost no hiring since last April"

**Source:** CNBC Apr 3, BLS
