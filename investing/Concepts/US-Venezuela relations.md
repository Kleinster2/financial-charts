**US-Venezuela relations** — 26 years of escalating tensions. From Chavez to Maduro capture. Sanctions, failed regime change (Guaidó), diplomacy (Grenell), intervention (Rubio).

## Obama era (2009-2017)

**Strategy:** Targeted sanctions on individuals, not economy.

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2014 | Venezuela [[Defense]] of Human Rights Act |
| Mar 2015 | EO 13692 — Venezuela declared "threat to national security" |
| 2015 | 7 officials sanctioned for human rights abuses |
| 2016 | Additional individuals added |

**Impact:** Limited. Maduro consolidated power.

## Trump 1.0 (2017-2021)

**Strategy:** Maximum pressure, Guaidó recognition, regime change.

### Sanctions escalation

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| Aug 2017 | EO 13808 — Venezuela cut from US financial markets |
| May 2018 | Debt purchase ban |
| Jan 2019 | [[PDVSA]] sanctioned, assets blocked |
| Aug 2019 | Full asset freeze on Venezuela government |
| 2019-2020 | [[Secondary sanctions]] on oil traders |

### Guaidó gambit (failed)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jan 23, 2019 | Guaidó declares himself interim president |
| Jan 23, 2019 | US recognizes Guaidó (first country) |
| Feb 2019 | 60+ countries recognize Guaidó |
| Apr 30, 2019 | Coup attempt fails — military doesn't defect |
| May 2020 | "Operation Gideon" invasion fails |
| 2020-2022 | Support erodes |
| Jan 2023 | Opposition dissolves Guaidó interim government |

**Why Guaidó failed:**
- Military stayed loyal to Maduro
- No actual power, just recognition
- Repeated fiascos (coup, invasion)
- Trump "took the hit" for backing a "failure"

## Biden era (2021-2025)

**Strategy:** Conditional engagement, Chevron license.

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2021 | Maintained Trump sanctions initially |
| Mar 2022 | Direct talks with Maduro |
| Nov 2022 | Chevron license to resume operations |
| Oct 2023 | Temporary sanctions relief (oil, gas, gold) |
| Jan 2024 | GL 43 revoked after Machado ban |
| Apr 2024 | [[Oil]] license (GL 44) not renewed |
| Jan 2025 | Final sanctions on 8 officials |

**Chevron license:**
- Only US company with Venezuela access
- ~150,000 bpd production
- Proceeds blocked from Maduro government

| Date | Action |

**Grenell's approach:**
- Diplomacy, engagement
- Counter [[China]] via detente

- Seen as too soft
- Administration shifted to force
