# IaaS

IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) is the cloud computing model where providers deliver virtualized compute, storage, and networking on demand. It is the foundational layer of the cloud stack, sitting below PaaS (Platform as a Service) and SaaS (Software as a Service). IaaS is where AI infrastructure spending concentrates — GPU clusters, training compute, and inference capacity all run on IaaS platforms. The global IaaS market was ~$150B in 2024, growing at ~25% annually, with AI workloads as the primary growth driver.

The market is an oligopoly dominated by four hyperscalers, each pursuing a hybrid strategy of [[NVIDIA]] GPUs plus custom silicon. [[Amazon|AWS]] leads with Trainium/Inferentia chips alongside NVIDIA. [[Microsoft|Azure]] leverages its [[OpenAI]] partnership and Maia custom chips. [[Google|GCP]] deploys TPUs for its own AI workloads. [[Oracle Cloud Infrastructure|OCI]] is the fastest-growing player, having bet exclusively on NVIDIA GPUs and winning large AI training contracts (including [[OpenAI]] and [[xAI]]) by offering bare-metal GPU clusters with low-latency networking.
