# CRI

A Certificado de Recebíveis Imobiliários is a securitization of real-estate receivables — rents, mortgage payments, build-to-suit cash flows — packaged by a securitizadora and sold to investors. The fixed-income building block of Brazilian real estate.

Chart data unavailable — this is an instrument category, not a single tracked price series.

## Mechanics

- Income-tax-exempt for individuals, which lifts the net yield relative to a taxed instrument of the same gross rate.
- Pós / pré / IPCA+ structures, backed by a specific pool of property receivables rather than a bank's balance sheet.
- The building block behind many "paper" ([[FII|papel]]) real-estate funds, which hold portfolios of CRIs.

## Risks / watch-outs

- Illiquid — there is a secondary market, but selling before maturity means a mark-to-market hit.
- Credit risk sits with the underlying receivables (the tenants / borrowers), not a bank — diligence on the pool matters.
