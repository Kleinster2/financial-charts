# Standing Repo Facility

A [[Federal Reserve]] facility, made permanent in July 2021, that lets primary dealers and eligible banks borrow cash overnight against Treasuries and agency debt at a rate set by the Fed. Designed as a backstop for short-term funding markets — limits how high overnight repo rates can spike, especially during quarter-end balance-sheet pressure or reserve scarcity episodes.

---

## What it does

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Counterparties | Primary dealers + eligible depository institutions |
| Collateral | US Treasuries, agency debt, agency MBS |
| Tenor | Overnight |
| Rate | Set at the top of the federal funds target range (currently 3.75% with 3.50–3.75% range) |
| Aggregate cap | Limited daily total (>$500B as of 2024) |

Mechanically: when overnight repo rates threaten to spike above the Fed's policy band — as in September 2019 — the SRF lets eligible institutions borrow at a known rate, dampening the spike.

---

## Why it exists — September 2019 lesson

The Fed had been running QT and reserves had fallen to levels banks deemed uncomfortable. Quarter-end Treasury settlements, corporate tax payments, and dealer balance-sheet constraints collided on Sep 16-17, 2019. Overnight repo rates spiked from ~2.2% to over 10% intraday before the Fed intervened with emergency open-market operations. The SRF formalized that intervention into a permanent backstop two years later — the institutional answer to the 2019 squeeze.

---

## Why it matters for the Warsh balance-sheet question

[[Kevin Warsh]] is on record wanting a much smaller Fed balance sheet. [[Ira Jersey]] ([[Bloomberg Intelligence]], Apr 24 2026) argues that without rewriting the post-GFC reserve rules that govern bank reserve demand at the Fed, the balance sheet structurally cannot fall below ~**$2.7T** without breaking front-end funding markets. The SRF is the engineered backstop that permits the Fed to operate closer to the floor of "ample reserves" without re-running 2019 — but its existence does not eliminate the constraint, only smooth it. Any aggressive Warsh-era QT push runs the risk of the SRF being repeatedly invoked as a sign of stress rather than as a quiet cushion.
