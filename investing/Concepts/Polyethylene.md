# Polyethylene

The world's most-produced plastic — the polymerized form of [[Ethylene|ethylene]]. Core feedstock for plastic bags, bottles, film, piping, insulation, geomembranes, and the packaging that underlies global consumer goods. Three main grades: low-density (LDPE), linear low-density (LLDPE), and high-density (HDPE), each with distinct cracker/reactor chemistry and end uses.

---

## Synopsis

Polyethylene sits at the center of the petrochemical supply chain. Upstream: crude oil and natural gas (ethane from shale, naphtha from crude distillation) feed [[Ethylene|ethylene]] crackers. Downstream: PE resin goes to extruders, injection molders, and blown-film converters that serve consumer goods, packaging, construction, and agriculture.

The 2026 [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] produced a structural repricing: Middle East feedstock and resin exports were cut off simultaneously, causing domestic [[China]] [[Polyethylene|polyethylene]] prices to roughly double ([[Cameron Johnson]], [[Tidalwave Solutions]] via [[FT]], April 11), [[China PPI|factory-gate inflation]] in China to turn positive year-on-year for the first time since 2022, and US Gulf Coast producers ([[LyondellBasell]], [[Dow]]) to ramp to near 100% utilization as the Hormuz beneficiary trade (see [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade#US polyethylene/ethylene boom — Hormuz beneficiary trade (March 19)|US PE boom section]]).

## Grades and uses

| Grade | Density (g/cm³) | Typical uses |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| LDPE | 0.910-0.925 | Flexible film, plastic bags, squeeze bottles |
| LLDPE | 0.915-0.925 | Stretch wrap, agricultural film, heavy-duty bags |
| HDPE | 0.941-0.965 | Milk jugs, detergent bottles, piping, geomembranes |
| UHMWPE | >0.930 (high MW) | Medical implants, ballistic protection, bearings |

## Feedstock routes

| Route | Cracker input | Geography |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| [[Ethane]] cracker | Ethane from natural gas / shale NGLs | US Gulf Coast dominant; [[Qatar]], [[Saudi Arabia]] |
| Naphtha cracker | Naphtha from crude distillation | East Asia (Korea, Japan, China), Europe, Middle East |
| Coal-to-olefin (CTO) / MTO | [[Methanol]] → olefins | China (inland coal belt) |

Middle East petrochemical complexes (Qatar, Saudi, UAE) run mostly on ethane — low cost, high margin. Their output transits the [[Strait of Hormuz]] to global customers.

## 2026 Hormuz crisis impact

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Feb 28 2026 | [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] begins; Middle East petchem exports disrupted |
| March 19 | US Gulf Coast ethylene spot at 30.25¢/lb (Mont Belvieu, [[Enterprise Products]]); ~12% jump in two days |
| March 19 | North American PE producer operating rates climbing toward ~100%; US ethane cracker utilization in low 90% range |
| April 11 | Domestic China PE prices roughly doubled (Tidalwave Solutions, via FT) |
| April 11 | [[China PPI]] turns positive year-on-year for first time since 2022 |

Beijing's [[Fortress China]] export-ban watchlist (April 2026) includes plastics — any new ban would convert China's domestic shortage into a leveraged tool against third-country manufacturers.
