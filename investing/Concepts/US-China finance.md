## Synthesis

US-China finance is the capital-market and payments-system layer of the broader strategic competition. The U.S. side still has the deeper securities markets, dollar clearing, sanctions reach, and official FX-surveillance machinery. The China side is building defensive redundancy through domestic capital markets, renminbi settlement, CIPS, gold accumulation, and balance-sheet opacity.

## Policy Pillars (Trump II)

*   Capital Market Access: Restricting Chinese companies' access to US capital markets and financial services.
*   Currency Manipulation: Aggressive rhetoric and potential countermeasures against alleged currency manipulation.
*   Sanctions: Targeted sanctions on Chinese financial institutions or individuals supporting designated entities (e.g., military, human rights abuses).
*   Decoupling: Encouraging US investors to divest from Chinese assets and restricting US pension fund investments.

---

## Key Actions / Developments (Jan 2026)

*   Delisting Threat: Renewed threats to delist Chinese companies from US stock exchanges that fail to comply with PCAOB auditing standards.
*   Investment Bans: Expanded executive orders prohibiting US investments in Chinese companies linked to the military or surveillance tech.
*   Currency [[Watchlist]]: [[China]] remains on the Treasury's currency manipulation watchlist; rhetoric intensified.
*   CIPS Integration: Scrutiny of [[China]]'s Cross-Border Interbank Payment System (CIPS) and its potential to circumvent SWIFT.

## US Leverage

*   Dollar Dominance: The unparalleled role of the USD as the world's reserve currency and in international trade.
*   SWIFT System: Control over the global interbank financial messaging system.
*   Capital Markets: The depth and liquidity of US stock and bond markets.

---

## [[China]]'s Countermeasures / Defenses

*   Internal Capital Markets: Development of domestic capital markets and encouragement of renminbi-denominated transactions.
*   CIPS: Promotion of CIPS as an alternative to SWIFT, especially for [[Belt and Road]] countries.
*   [[Gold]] Reserves: Accumulation of gold and other non-dollar reserves.
*   Digital Currency: Accelerated development and internationalization of the Digital Yuan (e-CNY).

## Currency Transparency and Hidden Intervention (June 2026)

[[Brad Setser]]'s hidden-reserve work turns currency manipulation from a single official-reserve question into a banking-system transparency question. If foreign-currency inflows are held as deposits or warehoused by state commercial banks, the U.S. policy fight shifts from "what did the [[PBOC]] buy?" to "where did the Chinese state financial system put the surplus dollars?" That matters for [[US-China finance]] because Treasury surveillance, TIC holdings, sanctions exposure, and de-dollarization narratives all depend on whether the measured dollar stock is the real dollar stock.

## Investment Implications

*   Chinese Equities: Increased risk for Chinese companies listed on US exchanges; potential for forced delistings.
