**China government bond yields** — the yields on Chinese sovereign debt (CGBs), most-watched at the 10-year tenor. They fell sharply through 2024–2025 toward ~1.6–1.8%, reflecting deflationary pressure, a weak property sector, and accommodative policy — a partial "Japanification" of [[China|Chinese]] rates. The low-yield environment is the core macro driver of the [[中特估]] SOE re-rating: with bonds yielding under 2% and property no longer a reliable store of value, domestic capital rotated into high-dividend state-owned equities (banks, energy, telecoms at 4–7% yields) as bond substitutes.
