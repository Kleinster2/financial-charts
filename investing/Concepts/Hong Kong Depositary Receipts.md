# Hong Kong Depositary Receipts

A Hong Kong Depositary Receipt (HDR) is a [[Depositary receipts|depositary receipt]] listed on the Stock Exchange of Hong Kong (SEHK), letting a foreign company's shares trade in Hong Kong without a primary share listing. HKEx launched the framework on 1 July 2008 (Chapter 19B of the Main Board Listing Rules), positioning Hong Kong as a depositary-receipt venue alongside its ordinary-share listings.

## Status

The HDR has been largely underused. The first and most prominent HDR was [[Vale]] S.A., the Brazilian miner, listed by introduction on 8 December 2010 (stock code 06210); only a handful of issuers ever followed. HDR issuers must meet the same listing requirements as ordinary-share issuers, which removed most of the regulatory advantage the format might have offered and left it a niche route. In practice, foreign companies seeking Hong Kong access have overwhelmingly chosen ordinary-share secondary or dual-primary listings instead — the path the US-listed China ADRs took for their [[Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act|delisting-insurance]] homecoming.

## Synthesis

The HDR is the depositary-receipt template under Hong Kong rules — technically available since 2008 but rarely chosen, with Vale's 2010 listing still its signature case. Its marginal status is the lesson: a depositary-receipt format only takes hold where it offers a real regulatory or access advantage over a direct listing, and in Hong Kong, where ordinary-share secondary listings are straightforward, it did not.
