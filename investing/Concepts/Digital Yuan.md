# Digital Yuan

China's sovereign central bank digital currency (CBDC), issued by [[PBOC]]. Known domestically as e-CNY and internally during development as DCEP (Digital Currency Electronic Payment). The world's most advanced major-economy retail CBDC pilot — live in tens of cities covering hundreds of millions of potential users, with cumulative transaction volume measured in the trillions of yuan. Structurally distinct from privately-issued stablecoins: e-CNY is a direct liability of the PBOC, not a token backed by fiat reserves.

Two-tier architecture: [[PBOC]] issues e-CNY to commercial banks, banks distribute to users. The intent is to mirror physical cash — PBOC-issued, legal tender, accepted everywhere — but in digital form with programmable features (conditional transfers, expiry dates, smart-contract triggers).

---

## Structural features

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Issuer | [[PBOC]] (direct liability) |
| Architecture | Two-tier (PBOC → commercial banks → users) |
| Reserve backing | n/a (sovereign liability, like physical cash) |
| Programmability | Limited to PBOC-sanctioned functions |
| Surveillance | Full transaction visibility to PBOC |
| Interest-bearing | No |
| Foreign use | mBridge cross-border pilot (BIS + PBOC + HKMA + BoT + CBUAE) |

## vs private yuan stablecoins

See [[Offshore yuan stablecoins]] for the comparison table. The short version: e-CNY is the domestic retail rail, a private CNH stablecoin (if Beijing authorizes one) would be the offshore institutional rail. They serve different users and different use cases, but compete for Beijing's scarce political capital in the digital yuan strategy.

## Strategic role

### Domestic
Reduces dependence on Alipay / WeChat Pay duopoly. Gives PBOC direct visibility into payment flows. Enables monetary policy tools not possible with cash (expiry dates for stimulus vouchers, conditional transfers).

### International
mBridge pilot — a multi-CBDC cross-border settlement experiment — has been operational with [[HKMA]], Bank of Thailand, and Central Bank of UAE. BIS exited in 2024. Politically sensitive: framed in Western media as a dollar-bypass system, though actual transaction volumes remain modest. See [[De-dollarization]] and [[US-China finance]].

---

## Adoption challenges

- Limited offshore utility without CNY capital-account opening
- Competes with deeply-entrenched Alipay and WeChat Pay domestically
- Consumer adoption incentives (red-packet giveaways) have not converted into sustained daily use at scale
- Foreign merchants and banks hesitant to onboard due to PBOC surveillance architecture
