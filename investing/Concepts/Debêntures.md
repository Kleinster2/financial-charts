# Debêntures

Brazilian corporate bonds — a company borrows directly from investors rather than from a bank. The workhorse of the corporate-credit sleeve in a Brazilian portfolio.

Chart data unavailable — this is an instrument category, not a single tracked price series.

## Mechanics

- Rate flavors mirror the rest of renda fixa: pós-fixado (% of CDI), prefixado, and IPCA+.
- The key retail distinction is debêntures incentivadas — infrastructure bonds whose coupons are income-tax-exempt for individuals — versus comuns (taxed).
- A secondary market exists but is thin; pricing is mark-to-market, so exiting early can cost.

## Risks / watch-outs

- Credit risk is the point — issuer quality and the judicial-recovery (recuperação judicial / RJ) risk of Brazilian corporates are the live concerns.
- The Carteira Aberta reviews flagged exactly this: private-credit funds (e.g. [[JGP]]) trimming exposure on rising local corporate distress.
- Liquidity risk compounds credit risk — the two move together in a stress.
