# Carbon fibre

High-strength, low-weight fibre composed of 90%+ carbon, made by carbonizing polyacrylonitrile (PAN) precursor (dominant) or pitch (niche). Used in composite form (CFRP — carbon fibre reinforced polymer) for aerospace airframes, wind turbine blades, auto body panels, pressure vessels (hydrogen storage), sporting goods, and increasingly consumer electronics and robotics.

---

## Synopsis

Carbon fibre is an engineered material, not a commodity — pricing is by grade (aerospace-grade vs industrial-grade), tow size (1K through 50K), and tensile modulus/strength rating. But it sits downstream of petrochemicals: the PAN precursor is made from [[Propylene|propylene]] via acrylonitrile, some with intermediate steps going through the Middle East. The April 11 2026 [[FT]] reporting (via [[Cameron Johnson]], [[Tidalwave Solutions]]) cited some [[China]] carbon fibre prices up 20% since the Iran war started — meaningful because the material was not previously on the market's radar as a Middle East dependency.

## Production chain

| Step | Input | Output |
|------|-------|--------|
| Acrylonitrile synthesis | [[Propylene]] + ammonia + O₂ | Acrylonitrile (AN) |
| Polymerization | Acrylonitrile | Polyacrylonitrile (PAN) |
| Spinning | PAN | White PAN precursor fibre |
| Stabilization | Heat 200-300°C in air | Stabilized fibre |
| Carbonization | 1,000-3,000°C in inert gas | Carbon fibre (~50% carbon yield) |
| Surface treatment + sizing | Proprietary | Finished fibre, ready for composite lay-up |

Each step raises the cost significantly. Aerospace-grade T1100-class fibre sells at ~$50-80/kg; industrial-grade 50K tow at ~$15-25/kg.

## 2026 Hormuz crisis impact

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Feb 28 2026 | [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] begins; propylene feedstock routes disrupted |
| April 11 | Some China carbon fibre prices up 20% since war start ([[Cameron Johnson]] / [[Tidalwave Solutions]] via [[FT]]) |

The transmission runs: Middle East feedstock disruption → [[Propylene]] tightness → acrylonitrile margin squeeze → PAN precursor cost rise → finished fibre repricing. Auto and consumer goods manufacturers are the visible downstream hit; aerospace programs have longer contractual cycles that delay the signal.
