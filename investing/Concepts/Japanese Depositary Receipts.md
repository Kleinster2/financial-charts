# Japanese Depositary Receipts

A Japanese Depositary Receipt (JDR) is a [[Depositary receipts|depositary receipt]] that lets foreign securities — shares, bonds, or ETFs — trade domestically in Japan. Structurally it differs from the ADR/GDR template: a JDR is a beneficiary certificate of a beneficiary-certificate-issuing trust under Japan's Trust Act, with the foreign securities held as the entrusted assets. The Tokyo Stock Exchange (JPX) runs a JDR listing scheme for foreign companies.

## Synthesis

The JDR is the depositary-receipt idea expressed through Japanese trust law rather than a deposit agreement — the receipt is a trust beneficiary certificate, not a bank-issued depositary share — which is why it can wrap bonds and ETFs as easily as equities. It remains a niche listing route; like the [[Hong Kong Depositary Receipts|HDR]] it exists as a framework more than a busy market, used where a foreign issuer wants a Japanese trading line without a primary share listing.
