# Yield curve control

Monetary policy framework where a central bank targets a specific yield on government bonds at a chosen maturity, committing to buy or sell unlimited amounts to maintain that target. The [[BOJ]] introduced YCC in September 2016 under [[Haruhiko Kuroda]], targeting the 10-year [[JGB]] yield at ~0% with a tolerance band while keeping the short-term rate at -0.1%. The band was widened multiple times in 2022-2023 (from +/-0.25% to effectively 1.0%) before [[Kazuo Ueda]] formally abandoned YCC in March 2024 as part of the BOJ's normalization. The [[Reserve Bank of Australia]] also briefly experimented with YCC (2020-2021) targeting the 3-year yield before abandoning it.
