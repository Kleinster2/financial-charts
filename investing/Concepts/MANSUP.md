**MANSUP** (Míssil Antinavio Nacional de Superfície) — Brazilian Navy's indigenous anti-ship cruise missile program, intended to replace MBDA Exocet across the surface combatant fleet. Prime contractor is [[SIATT]] (São José dos Campos). The extended-range variant (MANSUP-ER) is co-developed with [[EDGE Group]] (UAE) under an April 2025 strategic license, positioning the Brazil-UAE pair as a joint exporter against [[Boeing]] Harpoon, [[RTX]] Naval Strike Missile, MBDA Exocet, and Chinese C-802 derivatives.

## Program structure

The base MANSUP variant entered operational testing in the late 2010s and is now in low-rate production for Brazilian Navy use. The MANSUP-ER extended-range variant was the focus of the April 2025 license agreement with [[EDGE Group]], which acquired the intellectual property of the ER variant for joint export marketing. Brazilian export-control rules alone constrain SIATT's reach into Gulf-aligned, African, and Asian customers; the EDGE partnership unlocks those channels.

---

## Strategic significance

MANSUP is the most advanced indigenous Brazilian missile program at production scale, and its co-development with the UAE is the cleanest single illustration of how BRICS-aligned defense industrial cooperation can build export-competitive product without going through Western technology gatekeepers. The program's success underwrites the broader thesis that the [[São José dos Campos]] aerospace cluster ([[Embraer]] + [[SIATT]] + ITA + DCTA) can host high-end weapons R&D outside the US/EU axis.
