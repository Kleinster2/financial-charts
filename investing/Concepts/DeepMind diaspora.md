# DeepMind diaspora

One-line read: a single AI research lab — [[Google DeepMind]] — has become the dominant source of new frontier-AI founders globally, with 112 alumni-founded startups in the 18 months to May 2026 and at least $14bn in aggregate capital raised. The structural parallel is the [[PayPal mafia]]: dense talent, a defining institutional culture, and a hub investor ([[Demis Hassabis]]) recycling capital and signal into the next generation.

---

## Synthesis

The 2010-2025 history of [[Google DeepMind]] reads like a research lab. The 2024-2026 history reads like a founder factory. Two reinforcing dynamics produced the shift. First, the post-2023 AI capital glut — frontier labs at $300B+ valuations, sovereign funds chasing AI exposure, [[NVIDIA]] writing $250M strategic checks into seed rounds — created founder economics that an in-house DeepMind researcher cannot match no matter what Google pays. Second, the Google Brain merger in 2023, the [[Gemini]] productisation push, and the post-[[ChatGPT]] commercial reorientation pulled DeepMind's research culture toward shipping, surfacing the cohort of researchers whose loyalty was to open questions rather than to any company. The combination produced an exit wave, and the exit wave produced the first AI talent network with PayPal-mafia structural properties: a defining institutional culture, a shared technical vocabulary, mutual investment patterns, and a hub investor in [[Demis Hassabis]] himself — angel investor in [[Anthropic]], [[Inflection AI]], [[Ineffable Intelligence]], and [[Entrepreneurs First]], among others. The network has not yet produced its [[Tesla]] or [[LinkedIn]] — the companies are 0-3 years old — but the capital backing them (>$14bn raised by mid-2026) means the market is already paying PayPal-mafia-comparable prices.

The 70/28 US/UK split is the structural fact. Most other "AI mafia" candidates (OpenAI talent exodus, [[Meta]] FAIR departures) concentrate in [[Silicon Valley]]. The DeepMind diaspora has a second pole in [[London]] — specifically the King's Cross / [[UK]] AI cluster around Google's $1bn London headquarters — which makes it the first frontier-AI founder network with serious geographic dispersion. The [[UK Sovereign AI Fund]] and [[British Business Bank]] backing [[Ineffable Intelligence]] formalises London's claim to a piece of the network.

## Hassabis as hub investor

[[Demis Hassabis]] is the [[Peter Thiel]] of this network — running the parent lab while also angel-investing the network's outgoing founders. Confirmed angel positions (per FT, May 2026):

| Company | Founder(s) | Hassabis position |
|---|---|---|
| [[Anthropic]] | [[Dario Amodei]] (no DeepMind tenure, but considers Hassabis a role model per FT) | Early angel — previously undisclosed |
| [[Inflection AI]] | [[Mustafa Suleyman]] (DeepMind co-founder, ex-) | Angel |
| [[Ineffable Intelligence]] | [[David Silver]] (DeepMind VP of RL, ex-) | Angel (alongside Google corp investment) |
| [[Entrepreneurs First]] | [[Matt Clifford]] | Angel |

The pattern is structurally unusual: Hassabis is simultaneously running [[Google]]'s in-house frontier AI lab and personally backing labs that compete with that effort. The FT reports Anthropic CEO [[Dario Amodei]] considers Hassabis a role model. [[Google]] separately holds billions in Anthropic equity via its corporate cloud-partnership stake — meaning Hassabis is exposed to the same competitor at both the personal and corporate level. The conflict is real but apparently sanctioned within Google; the Anthropic stake long predates [[Sundar Pichai]]'s 2023 reorganisation.

Per [[Matt Clifford]] (FT, May 2026): "Demis has a huge amount of soft power at Google and beyond ... He was a pioneer before it was obvious what AI was, and the availability of capital for his protégés is pretty striking. That's a big driver of what has happened."

## Geographic concentration

The DeepMind diaspora's distribution is the cleanest empirical case for a second AI hub outside [[Silicon Valley]]:

| Hub | Approximate share | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| [[Silicon Valley]] / US | ~63% (70/112) | [[Anthropic]] (SF), [[Physical Intelligence]] (SF), [[Reflection AI]] |
| [[London]] / [[UK]] | ~25% (28/112) | [[Ineffable Intelligence]], [[Isomorphic Labs]], stealth startups at King's Cross |
| [[Paris]] / [[Europe]] (rest) | ~12% (14/112) | [[Mistral]] (Paris, ex-DeepMind founder) |

[[Google]]'s $1bn King's Cross headquarters anchors the London concentration. [[Tantum Collins]], ex-DeepMind and ex-[[White House]], is co-founding a stealth AI startup at King's Cross with two other former DeepMind researchers (FT, May 2026) — anecdote consistent with the geographic data. UK government policy (UK Sovereign AI Fund, British Business Bank) explicitly funds this cluster as the UK's frontier AI position.

## Inside Google: alumni promoted to senior roles

The diaspora is not only outbound. DeepMind alumni who stayed at [[Google]] have moved into senior roles as the company races to compete with [[OpenAI]] and the labs its own alumni founded:

| Person | DeepMind role | New role | Year |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Koray Kavukcuoglu]] | CTO, Google DeepMind | Chief AI Architect, [[Google]] (SVP, reports to [[Sundar Pichai]]) | Jun 2025 |
| [[Pushmeet Kohli]] | VP Research, lead of AlphaFold work | Chief Scientist, [[Google Cloud]] (alongside DeepMind role) | Apr 2026 |

Kavukcuoglu moved from London to Mountain View on appointment; Kohli retains his AlphaFold-adjacent DeepMind leadership while taking the Cloud role. Both promotions strengthen [[Google]]'s internal AI architecture talent depth at the moment when DeepMind alumni outside the company are raising the largest seed rounds in history.

## Cross-investment patterns

Pattern-level claims about reciprocal investment within the network are still under-documented relative to PayPal mafia. Confirmed reciprocal links (May 2026):

- [[Demis Hassabis]] → [[Anthropic]], [[Inflection AI]], [[Ineffable Intelligence]], [[Entrepreneurs First]] (all angel)
- [[Google]] (corp) → [[Anthropic]] ($13B invested; up to $43B committed), [[Ineffable Intelligence]] (seed participant) — corp-level positions hedging the diaspora
- [[NVIDIA]] → [[Ineffable Intelligence]] ($250M+ at seed), [[Anthropic]] ($10B Series F), [[AMI Labs]] (seed) — third-party hub investor pattern, distinct from network-internal capital but functionally similar
- [[Sequoia Capital]] + [[Lightspeed Venture Partners]] → multiple alumni rounds (Ineffable seed co-leads; Mistral seed; Anthropic Series G)

The network does not yet have internal partnership-style cross-investment of the type [[Founders Fund]] / [[Sequoia]] (via [[Roelof Botha]]) / [[Greylock]] (via [[Reid Hoffman]]) produce in the [[PayPal mafia]]. That gap may close as alumni founders accumulate liquidity (none has had a major exit yet; Inflection's Microsoft acquihire was structurally an acquisition rather than a founder windfall).

## How it compares

| [[PayPal mafia]] | [[PayPal]] (1998-2002) | [[eBay]] $1.5B (2002) | ~$1T market cap | ~20 core | Mature; multiple decacorns produced |
| [[OpenAI talent exodus]] | [[OpenAI]] (2021-2025) | — (in-progress) | $50B+ at peers (SSI $32B, TML $12B) | ~10 marquee | Recent; concentrated in SV |
| DeepMind diaspora | [[Google DeepMind]] (2014-2026) | — (in-progress) | $14bn+ raised; portfolio value much higher | 112 (Evertrace) | Distinctive London hub; in-house hub investor |
| [[Meta]] FAIR departures | [[Meta]] (2024-2026) | — | $1B+ (LeCun's [[AMI Labs]]) | Smaller cohort | Adjacent — overlapping researcher pool |

The DeepMind diaspora is distinguished from the [[OpenAI talent exodus]] by three structural features: scale (112 vs ~10 marquee founders), geography (UK pole vs SF concentration), and the active role of the parent CEO as hub investor (Hassabis vs no equivalent at OpenAI — [[Sam Altman]]'s portfolio is broader but his personal exit from OpenAI is the unresolved governance overhang).

## What to watch

- Whether any DeepMind-alumni startup reaches a public-market exit. [[Anthropic]] IPO (Wilson Sonsini engaged, [[Jensen Huang]] expects 2026) is the closest candidate but the founders are ex-OpenAI, not ex-DeepMind — the Hassabis angel position is the only DeepMind link. The first true alumni-founded IPO will set valuation comps for the rest.
- Reciprocal investment patterns. As alumni founders accumulate paper wealth from up-rounds, whether they start angel-investing into the next cohort — the PayPal-mafia signature — or remain operator-focused.
- The London/King's Cross hub's durability. If [[UK Sovereign AI Fund]] backing persists and produces 2-3 alumni-founded London unicorns, the geographic claim solidifies. If alumni gravitate back to SF for liquidity, the UK pole hollows out.
- [[Google]]'s talent retention. The Kavukcuoglu and Kohli promotions are an explicit response to the diaspora — internal opportunities meant to keep the next cohort inside. The 2026 alumni founder count is the metric.
- Whether [[Demis Hassabis]] himself ever leaves [[Google]] to found something. The PayPal mafia model required Thiel to depart PayPal; Hassabis has not. If he does, the network reshapes around a new node.
