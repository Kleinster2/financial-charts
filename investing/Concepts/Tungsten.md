#concept #critical-minerals #mining #commodities

Tungsten — refractory metal (chemical symbol W; atomic number 74; melting point ~3,422 °C, the highest of any pure metal) used principally as tungsten carbide for armor-piercing ammunition, drill bits, machine-tool inserts, and high-temperature defense components. Global mine production is concentrated in [[China]] (~80% of mined output, ~83% of refined output as of 2024), making tungsten one of the most concentrated critical-mineral supply chains in the world. The U.S. has no commercial-scale tungsten mining and depends on imports, recycling, and small specialty operations. The metal entered acute scarcity in 2025–2026 after Beijing imposed export controls.

## Why tungsten matters

| End use | Share of demand | Why tungsten |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| Tungsten carbide (cemented carbide) | ~55% of global tungsten consumption | Hardness second only to diamond; used for cutting tools, mining drill bits, machine-tool inserts |
| Steel alloys | ~17% | High-temperature strength; used in tool steels, high-speed steels |
| Mill products and chemicals | ~13% | Filaments, electrodes, contacts, X-ray targets |
| Superalloys | ~8% | Jet engines, gas turbines |
| Defense / armor-piercing | embedded across categories | Kinetic-energy penetrators (sabot rounds); replaces depleted uranium for cleaner ammunition supply |

The defense angle is structurally why tungsten enters strategic-minerals programs. There is no near-term substitute for tungsten carbide in armor-piercing kinetic-energy ammunition; the only meaningful alternative — depleted uranium — carries radiological-handling burdens that Western militaries have moved away from. Reduced access to tungsten translates directly into reduced Western armor-piercing ammunition production capacity within months.

## Supply concentration

| Geography | Share of mined production | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------|
| [[China]] | ~80% | Concentrated in Jiangxi, Hunan, Henan provinces |
| Vietnam | ~5–6% | Nui Phao mine; partially tied to Chinese off-take |
| Russia | ~2–3% | Sanctioned out of Western supply post-2022 |
| Bolivia, Rwanda, Austria, Portugal, Spain | small individually | Tier-2 producers |
| United States | ~0% commercial-scale | Some specialty / secondary operations only |
| [[Kazakhstan]] (planned) | ~15% upon Cove Kaz development | The largest known undeveloped resource (Northern Katpar + Upper Kairakty); see [[Cove Kaz Capital]] |

The Kazakh Cove Kaz / [[Tau-Ken Samruk]] JV would, on completion, displace Vietnam as the second-largest tungsten producer globally and fundamentally re-shape the supply-chain map.

## China export controls (Feb 2025 onward)

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| February 4, 2025 | China issues export-control regulations on tungsten products including ammonium paratungstate (APT) |
| 2025 (full year) | APT export volume falls from 782 tonnes (2024) toward dramatically lower levels (243 tonnes in first 11 months of 2025) |
| Late 2025 / early 2026 | China confirms only 15 companies authorized to export tungsten in 2026–27 |

The 70% year-on-year decline in APT export volume (2024 → 2025) is the principal driver of the 2026 price explosion. The 15-exporter cap is the structural constraint; even if any of those 15 want to ship, license-issuance pace is the gating factor.

## APT price trajectory

Ammonium paratungstate (APT) is the canonical traded form of refined tungsten and the market benchmark for downstream pricing. Prices quoted per metric tonne unit (mtu):

| Date | Rotterdam APT (USD/mtu) | Source |
|------|-------------------------|--------|
| Early 2024 | ~$300–350 | Multi-year base |
| Late 2024 / early 2025 | ~$350–400 | Pre-export-control levels |
| January 2026 | ~$1,100–1,150 | Initial response to China cap |
| February 2026 | ~$1,944 | Acceleration as inventory thinned |
| April 2026 | ~$3,185 | Rotterdam ATH; up ~350% YTD; up ~900% over 12 months |

Western and U.S. carbide producers report inventory levels near zero and delivery backlogs of months. The price signal has not yet converted into commensurate Western supply response — mine-development cycles are long, and the lead time from financing decision to commercial production is typically 5–10 years.

## U.S. policy and federal financing response

The second Trump administration has identified tungsten as a strategic critical mineral and has positioned EXIM and DFC financing behind non-China supply development:

| Program / instrument | Tungsten-relevant action |
|---------------------|--------------------------|
| [[Project Vault]] (Feb 2026) | $12bn strategic critical-minerals stockpile; $10bn [[EXIM Bank]] commitment; tungsten in scope |
| EXIM letter of interest for [[Cove Kaz Capital]] (Apr 2026) | up to $900M for Northern Katpar / Upper Kairakty development |
| DFC letter of interest for [[Cove Kaz Capital]] (Apr 2026) | up to $700M complementary financing |
| U.S.-Kazakhstan critical-minerals partnership | Tungsten as keystone resource |

Total federal letter-of-interest support for the Cove Kaz / Tau-Ken Samruk project is ~$1.6bn against ~$1.1bn estimated total development cost. Letters of interest are non-binding, but the size and structure indicate clear administrative priority.

## Investor implications

- Existing equity exposures: very limited globally. North American Tungsten Corp, Almonty Industries (ASX/TSX-listed; Sangdong mine in South Korea), Guoco Group (HK), and a handful of small-cap explorers
- The Cove Kaz vehicle (via [[Skyline Builders]] reverse merger announced Apr 30, 2026; see [[Sponsored reverse merger pipeline]]) is the largest U.S. publicly-traded tungsten development play once the merger closes
- Carbide tooling end-customers (industrial machinery, oil and gas drilling, defense primes) face margin compression and inventory-driven order delays
- The structural setup parallels rare-earth-magnet financing in 2024–2026: a single dominant Chinese producer; export controls as policy instrument; Western federal financing flowing to specific JV targets; a multi-year supply response
