# Single stock dispersion

#concept #markets #volatility

**Single stock dispersion** — the magnitude of individual stock price moves relative to the overall index. High dispersion means stocks are making large moves in different directions while the index stays flat. Distinct from [[Breadth divergence]], which measures how many stocks move up vs down; dispersion measures how far they move.

---

## The concept

Dispersion = the spread of individual stock returns around the index return.

When dispersion is high but index volatility is low, it signals that large moves are happening beneath the surface but **cancelling each other out** at the index level. Winners and losers are both making extreme moves, but netting to flat.

| Regime | Index vol | Stock vol | What it means |
|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **High dispersion, low index vol** | Low | High | Micro narratives dominate; stockpicker's market |
| **Low dispersion, low index vol** | Low | Low | Everything correlated and calm |
| **High dispersion, high index vol** | High | Very high | Crisis with differentiation (2008-09) |
| **Low dispersion, high index vol** | High | High | Macro-driven selloff, everything moves together |

The gap between single-stock moves and subdued index performance reached its **highest level since 2009**, per [[Citadel Securities]].

### What's driving it

**AI repricing.** "The threat posed by AI has accelerated repricing across vulnerable business models, intensifying rotation even as headline performance remains contained" — Scott Rubner, [[Citadel Securities]].

Three forces creating extreme individual moves:

1. **Strong earnings broadly** — 12% YoY growth, 9% revenue growth, 7/11 sectors expanding margins. Individual names getting rewarded or punished based on results ([[Deere]] +10% on earnings beat, [[Spotify]] +15% then −8% in days)
2. **Mag 7 drag** — [[Amazon]], [[Microsoft]], [[Alphabet]] sold off on $660bn combined AI capex plans (+60% YoY). Tech giants' spending is both an existential bet and a weight on returns
3. **AI disruption repricing** — software and logistics stocks hit on fear that AI threatens their business models, creating sharp losers alongside sharp winners in other sectors

### The active management revival

This is the best environment for stockpickers in almost two decades:

- **>50% of active large-cap funds outperforming** — highest hit rate since ~2007
- Active underperformed for years because [[Mega-cap tech acronyms|mega-cap tech]] concentration meant passive automatically held the biggest winners
- With Mag 7 lagging and breadth at multi-decade highs, concentration is now a *headwind* for passive
- "Violent rotations" from tech to consumer durables and other sectors ([[Barclays]], Venu Krishna)

### The index paradox

Manish Kabra ([[Société Générale]]): "The macro message underlying the index is actually boom. Revenue is growing at 9 per cent. Seven out of 11 sectors have seen profit margin expansion." The lack of excitement in tech is **masking** broad-based strength.

The implication: in an earnings-driven market (vs multiple-expansion), index returns stay muted but **internal returns can be phenomenal**. This is structurally different from the 2020-24 regime where you just needed to own mega-cap tech.

## Implications

**For index investors:** Flat index ≠ flat market. The average stock is doing well; the index is misleading because of cap-weighting and Mag 7 drag.

**For active managers:** Best environment in years. Fundamental research is being rewarded. Dispersion creates both winners and losers — you just need to be on the right side.

**For the AI trade:** The market is simultaneously pricing in AI's enormous infrastructure cost (punishing the spenders) and its disruptive potential (punishing the disrupted). The beneficiaries — picks-and-shovels names, infrastructure software, sectors gaining from rotation — are where the 20%+ moves are happening.

**For [[Separately Managed Accounts|SMAs]]:** This is the pitch. When dispersion is extreme, concentrated active management with real research can meaningfully differentiate from passive. The 2009 comparison is apt — post-GFC was also a great vintage for active.

## The dispersion trade

The dispersion trade is a structured way to bet on high dispersion / low correlation. Mechanically: **sell index options** (which gain value when the index moves a lot) and **buy single-stock options** on the index's constituents (which gain value when individual stocks move a lot). Hedged properly, this is a pure bet on correlation — not on the level or overall volatility of the market.

**Why it pays:** Some real-money investors naturally sell single-stock options (buy-write strategies) while others naturally buy index options (crash insurance). Hedge funds step in to take the other sides, effectively betting against correlation. This has been a structural feature of markets, not just a tactical trade.

**AI as a fundamental catalyst:** The two dominant market theses — "AI will eat everything" (winners replace losers, trillions redirected) or "AI is a bubble" (it all collapses) — both imply high dispersion. Either way, some stocks go up a lot and others go down a lot, but the overall index may not move much. You don't have to pick which thesis wins — you can bet on "either AI will be big or it won't" via the dispersion trade.

**2026 YTD through Feb 13:** Dispersion was **the largest since at least 1994** (WSJ), signaling unusually high divergence between individual stock returns.

### Iran correlation shock (Mar 2026)

The 2026 Iran conflict jolted the dispersion trade. Geopolitical events spike **correlation** — everything sells off together, index options become expensive, and the trade loses money.

- Implied one-month correlation jumped to **highest level since November** on Tuesday (Mar 4)
- Single stocks and indexes both tumbled — the cancelling-out dynamic broke
- Amy Wu Silverman (head of derivatives strategy, [[RBC]]): *"We could be at an important inflection point. One thing geopolitical events can trigger is a spike in correlation."*

The tension: AI makes dispersion go up, but war makes correlations go up. It's not clear which effect wins.

*Source: Bloomberg (Bernard Goyder, Mar 5 2026), WSJ, FT*
