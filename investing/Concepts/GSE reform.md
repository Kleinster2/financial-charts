#concept #housing #policy #finance

**GSE reform** — Effort to end [[Fannie Mae]] and [[Freddie Mac]] conservatorship (since 2008). Trump administration actively considering privatization. Complex undertaking — unlikely before 2026 at earliest. Stock prices doubled on speculation.

---

## Current status

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Conservatorship start | September 2008 |
| Duration | **16+ years** |
| Combined assets | ~$7 trillion |
| Market share | ~70% of new mortgages |
| Stock move on speculation | **~2x** |

**National Housing Conference:** "The last major piece of unfinished business from the financial crisis."

---

## Timeline of recent events

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jan 2, 2025 | FHFA requires Treasury consent for exit |
| May 21, 2025 | Trump: "giving very serious consideration" to privatization |
| 2025 | H.R.1209 introduced (End of GSE Conservatorship Preparation Act) |

---

## Why it's complicated

| Challenge | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Capital deficiency** | GSEs need massive capital raise |
| **Mortgage rate impact** | Treasury: "most important metric" |
| **Market disruption** | $7T in assets |
| **Affordable housing mission** | Must preserve or replace |
| **Congressional role** | May need charter changes |
| **Timeline** | Former Freddie CEO: 2026 "might not be feasible" |

**Treasury Secretary Bessent:** "Anything that is done around a safe and sound release is going to hinge on the effect on long-term mortgage rates."
