#concept #energy #commodity #geopolitics

**Strategic Petroleum Reserve** — government-controlled crude oil stockpiles held to buffer supply shocks and maintain national security during energy crises. The US SPR is the world's largest at ~415M barrels (Feb 2026), stored in salt caverns along the Gulf Coast. Global government-controlled reserves total ~1.5B barrels across IEA members and non-IEA countries like [[China]], with an additional ~2.7B barrels held by private industry under IEA mandates.

## US Strategic Petroleum Reserve

### Infrastructure

Four underground salt cavern sites along the Gulf of America:

| Site | Location | Capacity | Caverns |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| Bryan Mound | Freeport, TX | 254M bbl | 20 |
| Big Hill | Winnie, TX | 170M bbl | 14 |
| West Hackberry | Lake Charles, LA | 227M bbl | 22 |
| Bayou Choctaw | Baton Rouge, LA | 63M bbl | 6 |

Salt caverns are created by solution mining — pumping fresh water into salt domes to dissolve the salt, creating massive underground chambers. Seven barrels of water dissolve enough salt to create one barrel of storage capacity. The geology provides natural containment, low leakage, and easy pressure management.

### US inventory history

| Date | Inventory | Context |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Dec 2009 | 727M bbl | Peak — full authorized capacity |
| Feb 2022 | 580M bbl | Pre-Ukraine |
| Oct 2023 | ~351M bbl | Post-Biden drawdown trough |
| Feb 2024 | 360M bbl | Refill underway |
| Feb 2025 | 395M bbl | Continued refill |
| Feb 2026 | 415M bbl | Still well below capacity |

The 2022-2023 drawdown was the largest emergency release in SPR history. Biden released ~180M barrels total — 30M in March 2022 (IEA-coordinated response to Russia's invasion of Ukraine), then 1 mb/d for six months starting April 2022. Treasury estimated the releases reduced gasoline prices by up to $0.40/gallon. The Biden administration began refilling at ~$70/bbl, buying back at lower prices than the average ~$96/bbl release price.

## IEA framework

All 31+ IEA member states must hold reserves equal to 90 days of the previous year's net oil imports. Net exporters are exempt (Canada, Norway, US, etc.). The IEA can coordinate collective releases — it has done so three times:

| Release | Year | Trigger | Volume |
|---------|------|---------|--------|
| Gulf War | 1991 | Iraq invasion of Kuwait | 2.5M bbl/d authorized |
| Libya | 2011 | Libyan civil war disruption | 60M bbl over 30 days |
| Ukraine | 2022 | Russia invasion | 182.7M bbl total (60M IEA + US unilateral) |

| Country | Reserves (est.) | Days cover | Notes |

| [[China]] | ~1.2B bbl (Barclays) | ~104 days import cover | Largest non-IEA reserve; mostly above-ground tanks |
| [[Japan]] | ~583M bbl (govt + private) | ~224 days | IEA member; oil-sharing agreements with S. Korea, NZ |
| [[South Korea]] | ~146M bbl | ~90 days | IEA member; oil-sharing with Japan |
| [[India]] | ~39M bbl (govt) | ~9.5 days imports | Three underground facilities; expanding |
| [[Germany]] | ~282M bbl (total) | ~90 days | Mix of govt and industry |

China's SPR is opaque — Beijing does not publish official figures. The 1.2B barrel Barclays estimate is one of the higher credible estimates. China built reserves in three phases starting 2004, targeting 90 days of import cover by 2020. Actual fill levels are debated.

## Investment implications

### As a price management tool
SPR releases put downward pressure on oil prices by increasing short-term supply. The 2022 releases were credited with moderating the Brent spike from ~$130 to sub-$100. But SPRs are finite — every barrel released is a barrel not available for the next crisis. The US SPR at 415M barrels is still only 58% of authorized capacity.

### Current relevance: 2026 Iran conflict
The [[Iran conflict energy disruption|Strait of Hormuz disruption]] has removed up to 15+ mb/d of crude and 4+ mb/d of products from transit. As of March 8, 2026:
- Trump administration has not expressed interest in an SPR release
- IEA could coordinate a multilateral release, but no announcement yet
- China's 1.2B barrel buffer gives it ~104 days of import cover even without Hormuz transit
- The disruption is a transit problem, not a supply problem — SPR releases only partially address it since the crude still needs to reach refineries

### Structural concerns
- US SPR at 415M barrels vs. 727M peak — significantly depleted heading into a major crisis
- Max drawdown rate of 4.4 mb/d means even a full release cannot replace Hormuz transit volumes (15+ mb/d crude)
- Salt cavern integrity degrades with repeated fill/draw cycles — some caverns need remediation
- Political pressure to release for price management (elections) can conflict with genuine emergency preparedness
