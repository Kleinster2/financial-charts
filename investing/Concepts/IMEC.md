IMEC (India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor) — US-led plan for a multimodal trade corridor connecting [[India]] through the [[Gulf Cooperation Council|Gulf]] to [[Europe]] via rail, shipping, and pipelines. Announced at the G20 New Delhi summit (September 2023) as a counterweight to China's [[Belt and Road Initiative]]. Originally included a pipeline route to the Israeli port of [[Haifa]], making it politically complex.

---

## Revival in context of Hormuz bypass (Apr 2026)

The [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure has revived interest in IMEC as a framework for overland energy and goods corridors. A Gulf official told the FT (April 2, 2026) that IMEC is one option being considered for new bypass infrastructure. [[Yossi Abu]] ([[NewMed Energy]] CEO) expressed confidence that pipelines to the Mediterranean will be built, whether terminating at Israeli or Egyptian ports.

See [[Gulf pipeline bypass planning 2026]] for the full April 2026 bypass planning landscape.
