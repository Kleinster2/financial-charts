#concept #oncology #biopharma

**RIPTAC** (Regulated Induced Proximity Targeting Chimeras) — a "hold and kill" targeted-oncology mechanism pioneered by [[Halda Therapeutics]], using a single bifunctional small molecule to kill cancer cells selectively while sparing normal tissue.

---

## Mechanism

A RIPTAC molecule has two binding ends. One binds a tumor-selective protein (the "hold" — e.g., the androgen receptor in prostate cancer); the other binds a pan-essential protein the cell requires to survive (the "kill"). A stable, lethal ternary complex forms only in cells that express the tumor-selective target, neutralizing the essential protein and killing the cell. Normal cells lacking the tumor protein never form the complex and are spared — the source of the intended wide therapeutic window against treatment-resistant disease.

Unlike a [[Protein degrader]] (which destroys a target) or a classic inhibitor (which blocks one protein's function), a RIPTAC weaponizes proximity: it co-opts an essential protein into a tumor-conditional kill switch.

---

## Lead application

[[Halda Therapeutics]]'s HLD-0915 is a once-daily oral RIPTAC for prostate cancer (androgen-receptor "hold"), with preclinical programs in breast, lung, and other tumors. The platform anchored J&J's $3.05B acquisition of Halda (closed December 2025).
