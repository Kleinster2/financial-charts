# Refunding announcement

Quarterly process by which the [[US Treasury]] announces its borrowing needs, auction sizes, and issuance strategy for the coming quarter. Released in three stages: Sources and Uses (Monday), auction sizes (Wednesday morning), and TBAC recommendations (Wednesday afternoon). Typically occurs in the first week of February, May, August, and November. The refunding determines the mix of bill vs. coupon issuance, which has first-order effects on [[NY Fed custody holdings|custody holdings]], [[ON RRP]] usage, and bank reserves.

---

## Refunding components

| Component | Timing | Content |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Sources and Uses statement | Monday | Borrowing need estimate, TGA target, financing assumptions |
| Marketable borrowing | Wednesday AM | Auction sizes for coupons (2y, 3y, 5y, 7y, 10y, 20y, 30y), FRN, TIPS |
| TBAC recommendations | Wednesday PM | Treasury Borrowing Advisory Committee report |

---

## Why it matters

The bill-vs-coupon mix is the key structural variable. Heavy bill issuance can drain money funds from [[ON RRP]] without stressing duration demand; heavy coupon issuance requires absorbing term premium and competes with foreign [[NY Fed custody holdings|custody]] buying. The November 2023 refunding was a market-moving event when Treasury shifted toward more bills and less coupons than expected, triggering a rally in long rates. The QRA has become one of the most-watched macro fixtures on the calendar.
