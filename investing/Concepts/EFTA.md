**EFTA** — European Free Trade Association. Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein. Alternative to [[EU]] membership with extensive FTA network.

---

## Overview

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Members | 4 |
| Population | 14M |
| Combined GDP | ~$1.2T |
| Founded | 1960 |
| FTAs | 29 (with 40 countries) |

---

## Members

| Country | Population | GDP | Notes |
|---------|------------|-----|-------|
| **Switzerland** | 8.8M | $800B | Financial, pharma hub |
| **Norway** | 5.4M | $480B | [[Oil]] fund, energy |
| **Iceland** | 370K | $27B | Fishing, geothermal |
| **Liechtenstein** | 40K | $6B | Customs union with CH |

**Former members:** UK, Denmark, Portugal, Austria, Sweden, Finland (all joined [[EU]]).

---

## EFTA vs [[EU]]

| Dimension | EFTA | [[EU]] |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Members | 4 | 27 |
| Single market | Via EEA (3 of 4) | Full |
| Customs union | No | Yes |
| Common trade policy | No | Yes |
| FTA flexibility | Independent | [[EU]]-wide |
| Switzerland | Bilaterals, not EEA | — |

### EEA (European Economic Area)

| Status | Countries |
|--------|-----------|
| EEA members | Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein |
| Not EEA | Switzerland (bilateral agreements) |

EEA = single market access without [[EU]] membership.

---

## Switzerland's unique position

| Arrangement | Coverage |
|-------------|----------|
| 120+ bilateral agreements | Sector-by-sector [[EU]] access |
| Schengen | Free movement of people |
| Not EEA | Rejected 1992 referendum |
| Not customs union | Independent trade policy |

**Swiss model:** Maximum flexibility, complex legal patchwork.
