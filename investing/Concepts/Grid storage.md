# Grid Storage

Battery energy storage systems (BESS) for utility-scale applications. Distinct market from EV batteries — different buyers, use cases, and economics.

---

## Why grid storage matters

| Driver | Dynamic |
|--------|---------|
| **Renewable intermittency** | Solar duck curve, wind variability |
| **Grid stability** | Frequency regulation, voltage support |
| **Peak shaving** | Avoid expensive peaker plants |
| **Resilience** | Backup during outages |
| **Transmission deferral** | Storage cheaper than new lines |

## Technology comparison

| Chemistry | Duration | Cost | Maturity |
|-----------|----------|------|----------|
| **[[Lithium]]-ion (LFP)** | 2-4 hours | Lowest | Dominant |
| **Iron-air** | 100+ hours | Very low $/kWh | Emerging |
| **Flow batteries** | 4-12 hours | Medium | Niche |
| **Compressed air** | 8-24 hours | Site-dependent | Limited |
| **Pumped hydro** | 8-24 hours | Lowest (at scale) | Geography-constrained |

## Investment dynamics

**Bull case:**
- Renewable buildout requires storage
- IRA standalone storage credit (30-50%)
- Utility procurement accelerating
- Cost curve still declining

**Bear case:**
- [[Lithium]] price volatility
- Interconnection queues (3-5 year waits)
- Utility procurement cycles slow
- Competition intensifying (margins)
