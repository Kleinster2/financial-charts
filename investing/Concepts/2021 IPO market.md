# 2021 IPO market

The 2021 IPO market was the cycle peak: zero-rate liquidity, retail participation, venture-markup momentum, and the [[SPAC]] boom all hit at once. It is the benchmark year for the [[2026 IPO pipeline]], but it is also the cautionary base rate: a record issuance window can be good for private sellers and still leave public buyers with weak forward returns.

## Synthesis

The important feature of 2021 was not only the volume. It was the market's willingness to underwrite long-duration growth, pre-profitability stories, direct listings, and capital-intensive businesses at the same time. That made the IPO window broad rather than selective. [[Consumer]], [[Fintech|fintech]], software, EV, biotech, and [[SPAC]]-backed projections could all find public capital.

That breadth is exactly why 2021 matters for later notes. The same window that allowed [[Rivian]], [[Allbirds]], [[Robinhood]], [[Nubank]], [[UiPath]], [[GlobalFoundries]], [[AppLovin]], [[Coinbase]], and [[Roblox]] to become public also created a cohort with very different forward outcomes. The winners were not the whole class. The durable lesson is that issuance capacity and investor return are separate questions.

## Regime read-through

| Indicator | 2021 read | Later-cycle consequence |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Record proceeds | Public capital absorbed a huge amount of venture and sponsor supply | The 2022 freeze was more violent because so many exits had been pulled forward |
| SPAC boom | 613 blank-check IPOs created a parallel listing channel | De-SPAC quality and redemption problems became part of the 2022-2024 trust deficit |
| VC-backed issuance | 168 VC-backed IPOs raised $74.7B | Late-stage private marks reset when public comps collapsed |
| Retail / first-day demand | Many issuers could sell narrative before public-market proof | First-day heat stopped being a durable quality signal |
| Mega-deal memory | 2021 set the proceeds benchmark used in later pipeline comparisons | [[2026 IPO pipeline]] needs to beat the 2021 record without the same zero-rate backdrop |
