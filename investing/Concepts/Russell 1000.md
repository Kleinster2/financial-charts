# Russell 1000

US large- and mid-cap equity index — the 1,000 largest companies in the Russell 3000, roughly 93% of total US equity market cap. Operated by FTSE Russell (a subsidiary of [[LSE Group]]). Tracked by [[IWB]] (iShares Russell 1000 ETF) for cash investors.

In practice the Russell 1000 tracks the [[S&P 500]] almost tick-for-tick — the extra ~500 mid-caps barely move a cap-weighted index dominated by mega-caps — so the vault treats it as a near-synonym for "the US large-cap market," not a distinct exposure. Its informative sibling is the [[Russell 2000]] small-cap index; the Russell 1000 minus Russell 2000 spread is the large-vs-small-cap (rate / recession-beta) read.

## Correlation structure

The Russell 1000 is functionally the US large-cap market, so it carries no distinct correlation signature versus the [[S&P 500]] — its tracker [[IWB]] has an average daily-return correlation to [[SPY]] of ~0.99, effectively the same series. The informative spread is Russell 1000 minus [[Russell 2000]] (large- minus small-cap), which reads the rate / recession channel rather than earnings.
