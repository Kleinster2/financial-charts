**Cyclical vs defensive** — Sector classification by economic sensitivity. Cyclicals outperform in expansions, defensives in contractions.

---

## Sector classification

| Cyclical | Defensive |
|----------|-----------|
| Consumer Discretionary | Consumer Staples |
| Industrials | Utilities |
| Materials | Healthcare |
| Financials | Telecom |
| Energy | |
| Technology (some) | |

---

## Cyclical characteristics

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| High beta | Moves with market |
| Earnings volatility | GDP-sensitive |
| Operating leverage | High fixed costs |
| Discretionary demand | Can be deferred |

---

## Defensive characteristics

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Low beta | Less market sensitivity |
| Stable earnings | Non-discretionary demand |
| Dividend focus | Income generation |
| Pricing power | Essential products |

---

## Economic cycle positioning

| Phase | Favor |
|-------|-------|
| Early recovery | Cyclicals (financials, discretionary) |
| Mid-expansion | Tech, industrials |
| Late cycle | Energy, materials |
| Recession | Defensives (staples, utilities, healthcare) |

## Portfolio implications

| Strategy | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Risk-off | Rotate to defensives |
| Risk-on | Rotate to cyclicals |
| Balanced | Mix both |
| All-weather | Equal weight |
