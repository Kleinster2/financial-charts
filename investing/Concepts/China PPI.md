# China PPI

China's Producer Price Index — the factory-gate inflation measure published by [[NBS|National Bureau of Statistics]]. Tracks prices of goods leaving Chinese factories before wholesaling and retail. A leading indicator for Chinese corporate margins, a reliable read on domestic industrial demand, and a transmission channel from global commodity shocks into Chinese CPI.

---

## Synopsis

China's PPI has been in deflation territory for most of the post-Covid period — a headline symptom of the property-driven demand collapse that the [[China economic transition]] note tracks. PPI turning negative year-on-year starting mid-2022 was a reliable signal that industrial overcapacity had outrun demand. The March 2026 print — the first positive year-on-year reading since 2022, per the [[FT]] (April 11) — is the arithmetic confirmation that the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] has pushed cost inflation through the Chinese producer chain.

The switch from negative to positive PPI is not a demand-led recovery. It is an input-cost shock: higher [[Polyethylene]] (~2x), [[Polypropylene]] (~6,500 → ~9,000 Rmb/tonne), [[Methanol]] (~2,000 → ~2,800 Rmb/tonne), and [[Petroleum benzene]] (~5,500 → ~8,500 Rmb/tonne) prices being passed through at the factory gate. Energy-intensive sectors (logistics, aviation, shipping, steel) reporting up to 25% cost increases ([[Peng Shaozong]]). The FT's "Fortress China cracks" framing rests on this single data point.

---

## Structure

PPI is a composite across manufacturing, extraction, and utilities. [[NBS]] tracks prices across 300+ markets, covering ~2,000 wholesalers, agents, and dealers. Market prices include producer prices, circulation expenses, profits, and taxes.

| Component | Weighting |
|-----------|-----------|
| Manufacturing | Largest (~70%) |
| Mining & extraction | ~15% |
| Utilities | ~5-10% |

## Why the March 2026 print matters

1. Confirms commodity inflation pass-through: the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] disrupted oil, gas, and petchem imports for ~45 days before the PPI read.
2. Breaks a 3+ year deflation streak: markets had priced sustained Chinese industrial overcapacity.
3. Puts Beijing in a bind: [[PBOC]] easing cycle runs into imported cost inflation; stimulus could compound the problem.
4. Signals [[China PPI]] has become a war-premium transmission channel, not just a demand-cycle indicator.

## Historical context

| Year | PPI yoy trajectory | Driver |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| 2021-22 | Positive | Post-Covid commodity boom |
| 2022 mid | Peak | Russia-Ukraine feedstock shock |
| 2022 late - 2026 Feb | Negative | Property crash, industrial overcapacity, weak domestic demand |
| March 2026 | Positive (first time since 2022) | Iran war feedstock shock (not a demand recovery) |

| [[Polypropylene]] | ~6,500-7,000 | ~9,000 | ~30-40% |
