**Insider buying** — When executives buy company stock with their own money. Bullish signal. "Skin in the game."

---

## Why it matters

| Principle | Logic |
|-----------|-------|
| Information advantage | Insiders know most |
| Aligned incentives | Own money at risk |
| Confidence signal | Voluntary purchase |
| Asymmetric | Buy for one reason, sell for many |

**"Insiders sell for many reasons, but buy for only one."**

---

## [[Signal]] strength

| Type | Strength |
|------|----------|
| CEO buying | Strongest |
| CFO buying | Very strong |
| Multiple insiders | Cluster = strong |
| Directors | Moderate |
| 10% owners | Context dependent |

---

## Amount matters

| Size | [[Signal]] |
|------|--------|
| >$1M | Very significant |
| $500K-$1M | Significant |
| $100K-$500K | Notable |
| <$100K | Weak signal |

Relative to net worth matters.

---

## Context considerations

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| After earnings | Material info processed |
| During decline | Catching falling knife or informed? |
| At new highs | Confidence or hubris? |
| First purchase | New conviction |
| Repeated buys | Sustained conviction |

---

## Red flags

| [[Signal]] | Concern |
|--------|---------|
| Selling into strength | Taking profits |
| CFO selling | Financial concerns? |
| Cluster selling | Coordination |
| 10b5-1 modifications | Timing manipulation |
