**Private markets** — Companies that haven't gone public. Historically outperformed public markets but inaccessible to retail. Tokenization changing this.

**Total ~$1.26T** (2025), down from ~$1.46T (2024). Alts AUM projected $20T (2025) → $30T (2030).

### Key shifts

**Capital rotating away from PE/VC toward infrastructure, secondaries, and credit.** Of the 17 funds that closed on $10B+ in 2025, only 5 were primary PE — vs 11 of 20 in 2024. Infrastructure, secondaries, and private debt are now raising at PE-scale.

**Concentration accelerating across all strategies.** Experienced managers taking record shares. Emerging managers being squeezed out — PE: 88% to experienced GPs. Private debt: 91.7%. First-time debt managers raised just 14 funds ($2.4B) — record low. The big are getting bigger.

**Distributions crisis constraining LPs.** Distributions as % of NAV: ~17% (vs 10-year avg of 26%). With realizations subdued, LPs have less cash to reinvest → constraining new commitments → reinforcing concentration toward established managers.

### Evergreen / semi-liquid fund explosion

The fastest-growing channel — and now the one under stress:

| Metric | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
|--------|------|------|------|------|
| **Evergreen fund net AUM** | $244.8B | $301.9B | $411.9B | **$493.4B** |
| Direct lending AUM | — | — | — | **$209.4B** (#1 strategy) |
| Real estate AUM | — | — | — | $113.6B (#2) |
| PE evergreen AUM | — | — | — | $51.0B |

- Growth >20% every year since 2022
- **107 new evergreen fund launches** through Nov 2025 (record, smashing 71 in 2023); 36 were alternative credit
- Direct lending grew 55.4% (2023), 66.1% (2024), 27.7% (first 9 months 2025)
- BDCs are the largest structural category within evergreen

This is the retail pipeline that the [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]] is now testing. The industry scaled a nearly $500B semi-liquid base — mostly direct lending — on the assumption that redemptions would stay within 5% quarterly caps.

## Why private markets matter

| Metric | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| Performance | Private equity outperformed public for 25 years |
| Access | Historically limited to institutions, accredited |
| AI companies | Best AI companies are private (OpenAI, Anthropic) |
| Staying private longer | Companies IPO later, retail misses growth |

## The access problem

**Retail locked out of wealth creation:**

| Stage | Who benefits |
|-------|--------------|
| Seed/Series A | VCs, angels |
| Growth rounds | PE, growth equity |
| Pre-IPO | Institutions, secondaries |
| IPO | Finally retail — but insiders cashing out |

**Quote:** "Retail gets the exhaust."

## Why companies stay private

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Disclosure burden | SEC filings, transparency |
| Quarterly pressure | Short-term focus |
| Cost | Compliance, legal, accounting |
| Control | Founders retain power |
| Capital available | Plenty of private capital |

**Result:** Best companies stay private longer → retail locked out.

## Regulatory framework

| Regulation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Reg D | Accredited investors only |
| Reg CF | Crowdfunding, up to $5M |
| Reg A+ | Mini-IPO, up to $75M |
| Accredited rules | $200K income or $1M net worth |

**Trend:** SEC may loosen accredited definition.

---

## Investment case

**Bull on private markets access:**
- Historical outperformance
- AI wealth creation happening private
- Tokenization democratizing
- Regulatory tailwinds

**Bear:**
- Adverse selection (best don't need retail)
- Illiquidity
- Lack of disclosure
- Fraud risk
