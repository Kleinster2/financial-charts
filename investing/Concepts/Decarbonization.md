# Decarbonization

## Synopsis

[[Decarbonization]] is the umbrella concept for the sector-by-sector emission-reduction pathways across power generation, heavy transport, industrial heat, and primary materials production. The investing-vault relevance is the pathway map: which technologies plausibly displace incumbent emission sources in each sector, and which named actors carry exposure to each pathway. Pathways differ in maturity, capital intensity, and customer-concentration profile — power-generation decarbonisation runs through utility-scale gas-bridge and nuclear-restart projects; heavy-transport decarbonisation runs through battery-electric for light vehicles and hydrogen fuel-cell for heavy/long-range applications; primary-materials decarbonisation (aluminium, steel) is the longest-horizon pathway and depends on cheap low-carbon hydrogen.

This is a stub-level umbrella concept that maps the vault's decarbonisation entities and pathways across sub-concepts. The vault's working frame is that decarbonisation is sector-fragmented and partnership-and-customer-specific rather than a single tradable theme — formal cluster validation across decarbonisation names is deferred and would require sub-cohort analysis ([[Hydrogen economy]] cluster vs power-gen turbines vs industrial materials) rather than a single decarbonisation cluster.

## Pathway map

### Power generation

- [[Vistra]], [[Constellation Energy]] — independent power producers; nuclear-anchored fleets, gas-flexible
- [[GE Vernova]], [[Siemens Energy]], [[Mitsubishi]] — gas-turbine majors; AI-era bridge supply per [[Power constraints]]
- See [[Power constraints]] for the gas-and-AI complement framing and the named beneficiary cohort

### Heavy transport

- [[Hydrogen economy]] — hydrogen fuel-cell pathway (sub-concept; covers heavy-duty trucks, buses, certain rail and maritime)
- [[Ballard]] — primary fuel-cell exposure via [[Wrightbus]] + [[Solaris]] OEM partnerships
- [[Toyota]] — fuel-cell tech supplier to [[CaetanoBus]]
- [[Ford]], [[General Motors]], [[Tesla]], [[Mercedes-Benz]], [[BYD]] — battery-electric light-vehicle pathway across western and Chinese OEMs

### Industrial materials

- [[Aluminum]] — primary aluminium electrolysis is electricity-intensive; hydrogen-based reduction is a long-horizon pathway
- [[Alcoa]], [[Hindalco]] — Western and Indian aluminium majors
- [[Tata Steel]], [[JSW Steel]] — direct-reduced-iron via hydrogen as a long-horizon steel-decarbonisation path

### Industrial heat and process

- [[Air Liquide]], [[Linde]], [[Air Products]] — industrial-gas majors with large existing hydrogen-production bases and capex into low-carbon hydrogen
- [[Plug Power]], [[Bloom Energy]], [[Nel ASA]], [[ITM Power]] — pure-play electrolyser and fuel-cell exposures

## Investing themes

The decarbonisation umbrella is not a single tradable cluster. The investable sub-themes are sector-distinct and trade on sector-specific catalysts rather than a unified theme rotation.

The pure-play decarbonisation names — electrolysers and fuel-cell pure-plays ([[Plug Power]], [[Bloom Energy]], [[Nel ASA]], [[ITM Power]]) — have suffered substantial multi-year drawdowns reflecting slower-than-expected adoption curves and capital-intensive build-outs. The market has repeatedly de-rated breadth-of-portfolio plays in favour of named partnership wins.

The names that work are those with secured large-scale OEM partnerships rather than those with the broadest product portfolios. [[Ballard]]'s Q1 2026 EBITDA inflection driven by the [[Wrightbus]] + [[Solaris]] dual-anchor adoption is the cleanest recent example — a name-specific story tied to two specific OEM customers, not a sector-wide hydrogen rally.

Power-generation decarbonisation (gas turbines as AI bridge per [[Power constraints]]) is currently the most working sub-theme by share-price action — the gas-turbine majors and IPP utilities have re-rated on AI-capex demand, while the pure-play renewables names have not.

## Vault read on decarbonisation as a theme

Decarbonisation entities have not held together as a single tradable cluster across recent windows. The sub-cohorts trade on different catalysts: power-generation names trade on AI-capex and grid-queue dynamics; heavy-transport fuel-cell names trade on OEM partnership wins and customer-concentration risk; primary-materials decarbonisation trades on commodity prices and tariff/trade dynamics rather than emission-reduction milestones. The investing-vault working frame is that decarbonisation exposure is pathway-specific and partnership-and-customer-specific — the names that win are those with secured anchor-customer demand in each sub-pathway, not the names with the broadest decarbonisation-strategy disclosures.
