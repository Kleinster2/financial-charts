**Valuation multiples** — Ratios comparing price to fundamentals. P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/S. Relative valuation tool.

---

## Common multiples

| Multiple | Formula | Best for |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **P/E** | Price / EPS | Profitable companies |
| **EV/EBITDA** | EV / EBITDA | Capital-intensive |
| **P/S** | Price / Revenue | Growth, unprofitable |
| **P/B** | Price / Book | Financials, asset-heavy |
| **P/FCF** | Price / FCF | Cash generators |
| **EV/Revenue** | EV / Revenue | Early-stage |

---

## P/E ratio

| P/E Range | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| <10 | Cheap or distressed |
| 10-15 | Value |
| 15-20 | Fair |
| 20-30 | Growth premium |
| >30 | High growth or expensive |

**Forward P/E** uses estimated earnings.
**Trailing P/E** uses last 12 months.

---

## EV/EBITDA

| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| <6x | Cheap |
| 6-10x | Fair |
| 10-15x | Premium |
| >15x | Expensive |

**Advantage**: Capital structure neutral.

## Growth-adjusted multiples

| Multiple | Formula |
|----------|---------|
| **PEG** | P/E / Growth rate |
| **EV/EBITDA/G** | EV/EBITDA / Growth |

| PEG | Interpretation |
|-----|----------------|
| <1 | Cheap for growth |
| 1-2 | Fair |
| >2 | Expensive |

---

## Enterprise value

**EV = Market Cap + Debt - Cash**

| Component | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Market cap | Equity value |
| + Total debt | Creditor claims |
| - Cash | Offsetting asset |
| = EV | Total firm value |

---

## When to use each

| Situation | Multiple |
|-----------|----------|
| Comparing peers | EV/EBITDA |
| Profitable companies | P/E |
| Unprofitable growth | EV/Revenue, P/S |
| Asset-heavy | P/B |
| [[Banks]] | P/B, P/E |
| [[REITs]] | P/FFO |
| Cash machines | P/FCF |

---

## Pitfalls

| Issue | Problem |
|-------|---------|
| Earnings manipulation | P/E distorted |
| One-time items | Adjust to normalized |
| Different accounting | GAAP vs. non-GAAP |
| Growth differences | Can't compare 5% to 30% |
| Cyclical peaks | Earnings inflated |

## Tech vs non-tech P/E divergence (Apr 2025 – Mar 2026)

![[sp500-tech-vs-extech-pe-apr25-mar26.jpg]]
*Source: [[All-In Podcast]], FactSet, [[RBC Capital|RBC Wealth Management]], multpl.com, worldperatio.com. Apr 3, 2026.*

The [[S&P 500]] forward P/E gap between tech ([[XLK]]) and non-tech widened through late 2025, then compressed as the [[Iran conflict]] repriced growth expectations.

| Date | Info Tech (XLK) | S&P 500 ex-Tech | Blended S&P 500 | Gap |
|------|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----|
| Apr 2025 | ~28x | ~20x | ~25x | ~8x |
| Aug 2025 | ~30.5x | ~21x | ~26.5x | ~9.5x |
| Dec 2025 (peak) | ~32.5x | ~21.5x | ~27x | ~11x |
| Mar 2026 | ~31x | ~20.5x | ~27.2x | ~10.5x |

Key observations:
- **Tech peaked at ~32.5x** in Dec 2025, then compressed ~1.5 turns by Mar 2026 — driven by rising yields from the Iran oil shock and the broader risk-off rotation into energy/defense
- **Non-tech held relatively flat** in the 20-21.5x range throughout, with slight expansion from Apr to Dec 2025 (energy, defense, utilities benefiting from conflict)
- **The ~10-11x gap remains historically wide** but narrower than the ~12x December peak. [[Mike Wilson]] ([[Morgan Stanley]]) flagged that S&P fwd P/E compressed 17% from its 2025 high — this chart shows where that compression concentrated
- **Blended S&P at ~27.2x** — still above long-run average (~18-20x), reflecting AI/tech premium persistence
- The gap narrows further if the war extends ([[Ed Yardeni]]'s "lost decade" scenario) or widens again on a rapid Hormuz reopening ([[Mike Wilson|Wilson]]'s base case)
