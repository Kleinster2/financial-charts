# Defense Autonomous Warfare Group

Successor to the [[Replicator Initiative]]'s autonomous-systems line of effort. Established within DoD as the operational vehicle for scaling attritable-autonomous procurement after Replicator 1 missed its Aug 2025 numerical target. Mission framed as a "pathfinder" and "multi-year investment" — evaluating, purchasing, and modifying drone systems already in production rather than commissioning new program-of-record platforms. Runs exercises and wargames for large-scale autonomous employment, particularly in the Pacific theater (Taiwan-contingency framing).

The structural significance is the budget trajectory: $225.9M FY2026 → up to ~$55B requested FY2027 (a ~240x year-over-year jump), tucked inside the broader ~$102.5B "next-generation technology and autonomy" bucket and a ~$75B total drone-and-counter-drone budget. If Congress appropriates near the request, DAWG becomes one of the largest single line items in the defense budget — an industrialization signal for autonomous warfare comparable to the Cold War-era prime-aviation program rollups.

## Why DAWG, not just Replicator 2

Replicator 1's Aug 2025 outcome (hundreds rather than thousands of ADA2 systems delivered) was a credibility test — DoD missed it visibly. DAWG is the institutional response: rather than retire the experiment, DoD reorganized it under a permanent banner and asked Congress for a budget two orders of magnitude larger. The implicit message is that the procurement-model lessons from Replicator 1 — cross-service aggregation, faster contract cycles, marketplace acquisitions, willingness to lose units — were judged successful even though the unit-count headline was not.

The bet is that 2026-2027 production capacity, software-stack maturity ([[Anduril]] Lattice OS, [[Shield AI]] Hivemind, [[Skydio]] Autonomy), and command-structure adaptation are now ready to actually field at industrial scale.

---

## Operations

- Wargames + concept-of-operations development for autonomous engagements (live-fire and simulation, conducted in "secure spaces" rather than the Western Pacific proper).
- Catalog-style procurement of drone systems already in production, with modifications as needed — distinct from program-of-record commissioning.
- Cross-service funding aggregation rather than consolidating to a single budget line.
