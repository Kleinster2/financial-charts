# Joint Interagency Task Force 401

DoD's lead organization for counter-small unmanned aerial systems (C-sUAS) capability delivery, established Aug 28, 2025 by Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth memo. Replaces the prior Joint Counter-small Unmanned Aircraft Systems Office, which was widely viewed as too slow. JIATF-401 reports to the Deputy Secretary of Defense and operates as the procurement vehicle for the [[Replicator Initiative|Replicator 2]] line of effort.

The director holds acquisition + procurement authority plus special hiring authorities — explicit reform signals targeting program speed and talent gaps. Joint manning is provided by the Military Services. Authorized users span DoD installations, FBI, DHS, and local law enforcement.

## Counter-UAS marketplace

Operational Feb 2026, hosted on the Common Hardware Systems electronic catalog. Lets installation commanders browse, compare, and order validated counter-drone equipment through a single online platform — described in DoD press as the "Amazon for drone defense." First acquisition Jan 2026: Fortem Technologies DroneHunter F700 (autonomous interceptor with AI-guided radar and tethered-net capture). Pipeline through 2026 expands to directed-energy, kinetic-interceptor, and electronic-warfare counter-UAS systems.

The marketplace model itself is procurement-reform-as-product: instead of a program-of-record per counter-drone capability, JIATF-401 maintains an approved-vendor catalog that bases buy from quickly. If it works, it's a template for other rapid-acquisition domains.

## Operations

- Southern border C-sUAS — rapid deployment of integrated sensors + interceptors against cartel drone incursions and homeland surveillance threats.
- Force-protection at military installations globally.
- Wargames + concept-of-operations development for autonomous engagements.
- C-UAS Low-Cost Sensing Challenge (with the Defense Innovation Unit and US Army) — sensor procurement for distributed counter-drone networks.
