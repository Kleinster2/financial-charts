# ON RRP

Overnight Reverse Repurchase Agreement facility operated by the [[Federal Reserve]]. Counterparties (mostly money market funds) lend cash to the Fed overnight in exchange for Treasury collateral, earning a floor rate set by the FOMC. Acts as a soft floor for money-market rates and a parking spot for excess system liquidity that doesn't end up in bank reserves.

| Current usage | Significantly below peak as QT drains liquidity |

---

## Role in Treasury market structure

ON RRP usage moves inversely with reserves: when money funds shift cash into the facility, reserve balances fall; when they move out (into T-bills or repo), reserves rise. The 2023-2025 drawdown of ON RRP balances — driven by T-bill issuance attracting money fund cash — was a key cushion that delayed the liquidity impact of [[Fed balance sheet QT|QT]]. Once ON RRP approaches zero, further QT drains directly from bank reserves, which is why the FOMC tapered QT pace in June 2024.
