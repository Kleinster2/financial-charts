#concept #finance #banking #credit

**Significant risk transfer** — [[Banks]] sell credit-linked notes to offload loan portfolio risk, manage capital ratios, and free balance sheet for more lending.

---

## How SRTs work

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Bank identifies loan portfolio to hedge |
| 2 | Structures credit-linked notes tied to portfolio |
| 3 | Sells notes to institutional investors |
| 4 | Investors absorb first-loss or mezzanine tranche |
| 5 | Bank retains senior tranche, reduces RWA |

**Result:** Bank transfers credit risk without selling loans outright. Frees regulatory capital.

---

## Why banks use SRTs

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Capital efficiency** | Reduces risk-weighted assets (RWA) |
| **Balance sheet** | Frees capacity for new lending |
| **Credit hedging** | Transfers default risk to willing buyers |
| **Regulatory** | Helps meet Basel III/IV requirements |

## AI infrastructure SRTs (Jan 2026)

**Emerging use case:**

[[Banks]] increasingly using SRTs to manage concentrated data center/AI infrastructure exposure:

| Bank | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| [[Morgan Stanley]] | Exploring | Data center loan portfolio, early talks |
| [[JPMorgan Chase]] | Marketed 2025 | SRT deals |
| [[Citigroup]] | Marketed 2025 | SRT deals |
| [[Goldman Sachs]] | Marketed 2025 | SRT deals |

**Why data center SRTs:**
- [[Banks]] heavily exposed to small group of AI borrowers
- [[Oracle]] CDS spreads spiking — credit stress signal
- Hyperscalers borrowing tens of billions
- Concentration risk concerns

**Market growth:** Global SRT issuance hit **$41B in 2025** (up from $29B in 2024) — record pace. Banks mostly from Europe and North America are using SRTs to hedge a **wider range of loans** beyond the original concentrated AI/DC exposure use case. *(Source: Crescent Capital, Bloomberg Mar 2026)*

## Risks

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Moral hazard** | Bank may relax underwriting if risk transferred |
| **Complexity** | Hard to value, model correlation |
| **Basis risk** | Notes may not perfectly hedge loans |
| **Liquidity** | Secondary market thin |
