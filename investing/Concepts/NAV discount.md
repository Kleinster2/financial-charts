**NAV discount** — When a fund or holding company's market price trades below the per-share value of its underlying assets. Discount = (Market Price - NAV) / NAV. US closed-end funds average ~5-8% discounts historically; holding companies can sustain 25-50%+.

---

## Key drivers

| Driver | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Management fees | A 1.5% annual fee capitalized at ~10x ≈ 15% "fair" discount for a fund with no alpha |
| Illiquidity of holdings | Funds holding private/illiquid assets face wider discounts due to NAV uncertainty |
| Sentiment / demand | Retail-heavy investor bases amplify discount swings |
| Governance | Entrenched boards that resist shareholder activism sustain discounts |
| Tax overhang | Embedded unrealized capital gains create latent tax liability |
| Leverage | Leveraged CEFs face wider discounts in rising-rate environments |

---

## Strategies to close the discount

| Strategy | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| Share buybacks | Accretive when buying below NAV ([[Berkshire Hathaway]]'s approach at 1.2x book) |
| Tender offers | Fixed-price or Dutch auction at/near NAV |
| Managed distribution | High payouts attract income buyers |
| Conversion to open-end | Eliminates discount entirely but forces liquidity on illiquid portfolios |
| Liquidation / wind-down | Terminal value realization |
| Board reconstitution | [[Saba Capital]]'s approach — CEF activism targeting entrenched boards |
| US listing / relisting | Broader investor base + index eligibility (Ackman's UMG thesis) |

---

## CEF vs holding company dynamics

**Closed-end funds** have defined portfolios and regulatory obligations (Investment Company Act of 1940 in the US), giving activists legal levers. Discounts are more mean-reverting.

**Holding companies** ([[SoftBank]], [[Exor]], [[Cosan]]) have operating businesses, strategic rationale, and founder control that make discounts more persistent and harder to arbitrage. Discounts can be structural.

## Activist CEF campaigns (2024-2026)

[[Saba Capital]] (Boaz Weinstein) has been the most active CEF activist, pressuring boards and exploiting NAV discounts. In Feb 2026, Saba and [[Cox Capital Partners]] launched mini-tender offers for [[Blue Owl]] BDCs (OBDC II, OTIC, OCIC) at 20-35% discounts to NAV — extending CEF activism into non-traded BDCs.
