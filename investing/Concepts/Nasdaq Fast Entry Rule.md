**Nasdaq Fast Entry Rule** — Proposed index methodology change (filed SEC Feb 20, 2026) allowing megacap IPOs to join the [[Nasdaq 100]] in ~1 month instead of waiting up to a year. Threshold: market cap in NDX top 40 (~$100B+). Waives seasoning and liquidity requirements. Designed to attract [[SpaceX]], [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]].

## Mechanics

Under the proposal, a newly listed [[Nasdaq]] company is evaluated for index inclusion. If its market cap ranks within the top 40 NDX constituents, it enters as a "fast entry" addition — exempt from the current 3-month seasoning and $5M daily traded value liquidity requirements.

The NDX temporarily exceeds 100 members until the next annual reconstitution (December). No existing constituent is removed to make room.

Additional proposed changes:
- Full market cap (including unlisted shares) evaluated for eligibility
- Low-free-float threshold revised from 10% to 20%
- Adjustments to removal criteria for smaller-cap constituents
