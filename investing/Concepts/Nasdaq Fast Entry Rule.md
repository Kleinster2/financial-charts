#concept #index #regulation

**Nasdaq Fast Entry Rule** — Proposed index methodology change (filed SEC Feb 20, 2026) allowing megacap IPOs to join the [[Nasdaq 100]] in ~1 month instead of waiting up to a year. Threshold: market cap in NDX top 40 (~$100B+). Waives seasoning and liquidity requirements. Designed to attract [[SpaceX]], [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]]. It now sits inside a broader index-provider race: [[FTSE Russell]] has also added fast-entry mechanics for large IPOs.

## Mechanics

Under the proposal, a newly listed [[Nasdaq]] company is evaluated for index inclusion. If its market cap ranks within the top 40 NDX constituents, it enters as a "fast entry" addition — exempt from the current 3-month seasoning and $5M daily traded value liquidity requirements.

The NDX temporarily exceeds 100 members until the next annual reconstitution (December). No existing constituent is removed to make room.

Additional proposed changes:
- Full market cap (including unlisted shares) evaluated for eligibility
- Low-free-float threshold revised from 10% to 20%
- Adjustments to removal criteria for smaller-cap constituents

## FTSE Russell parallel rule

[[FTSE Russell]] consulted in February 2026 on fast entry for sizeable IPOs in the Russell U.S. Equity Indexes, explicitly citing [[SpaceX]], [[OpenAI]], and [[Anthropic]]. Its proposed timing is faster than the Nasdaq 100 mechanism: eligible IPOs can be added after market close on the fifth trading day, aligned with FTSE Global Equity Index Series fast-entry timing.

Reuters reported on May 26, 2026 that FTSE Russell said [[SpaceX]] appears eligible under the newly announced rules. FTSE Russell used roughly $70B of investable market capitalization, above the $17.5B Russell Top 500 market-adjusted breakpoint and the $13.5B FTSE GEIS fast-entry threshold. Final index treatment still depends on later filings and actual IPO terms.
