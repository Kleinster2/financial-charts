#concept #index #regulation

**Nasdaq Fast Entry Rule** — May 1 2026 [[Nasdaq 100]] methodology change allowing top-40 megacap IPOs to enter the index after roughly 15 trading days instead of waiting for the normal seasoning path. The same methodology package removed the minimum free-float criterion and capped low-float index weights at three times free-floating shares. Designed around the 2026 mega-IPO wave: [[SpaceX]], [[OpenAI]], and [[Anthropic]].

## Mechanics

Under the implemented rule, a newly listed [[Nasdaq]] company is evaluated for index inclusion after its seventh trading day. If its full market capitalization ranks within the top 40 current [[Nasdaq 100]] constituents and it satisfies the other applicable eligibility criteria, it can enter as a Fast Entry addition, typically after 15 trading days with announcement after the tenth trading day.

The NDX can temporarily exceed 100 members after a Fast Entry event. No existing constituent has to be removed at the moment of addition.

The low-float change is the part that matters for [[SpaceX IPO 2026]]. Full market capitalization, including listed and unlisted shares for direct listings and primary ADRs, is used for ranking and inclusion. Weighting uses modified market capitalization instead: only eligible listed share classes count, and each low-float security's TSO is capped at three times its free-floating shares. Nasdaq's own FAQ gives the example of a $1T company with $60B of float being weighted off $180B, not $1T and not $60B.

Sources: [Nasdaq methodology PDF](https://indexes.nasdaq.com/docs/Methodology_NDX.pdf), accessed Jun. 7 2026; [Nasdaq 2026 NDX changes FAQ](https://indexes.nasdaqomx.com/docs/2026_May_NDX_Changes_FAQ.pdf), accessed Jun. 7 2026; [Nasdaq consultation conclusion](https://indexes.nasdaqomx.com/docs/Nasdaq-100_Index_Consultation_February_2026_Summary_of_Responses_and_Conclusion.pdf), accessed Jun. 7 2026.

## FTSE Russell parallel rule

[[FTSE Russell]] consulted in February 2026 on fast entry for sizeable IPOs in the Russell U.S. Equity Indexes, explicitly citing [[SpaceX]], [[OpenAI]], and [[Anthropic]]. Its proposed timing is faster than the Nasdaq 100 mechanism: eligible IPOs can be added after market close on the fifth trading day, aligned with FTSE Global Equity Index Series fast-entry timing.

Reuters reported on May 26, 2026 that FTSE Russell said [[SpaceX]] appears eligible under the newly announced rules. FTSE Russell used roughly $70B of investable market capitalization, above the $17.5B Russell Top 500 market-adjusted breakpoint and the $13.5B FTSE GEIS fast-entry threshold. Final index treatment still depends on later filings and actual IPO terms.

## Correlation structure

The rule is an index-flow mechanic, not a standalone return series. Its traded expression is through [[QQQ]], [[Nasdaq 100]] index-linked products, and low-float IPOs whose post-listing demand can be pulled forward by Fast Entry inclusion.
