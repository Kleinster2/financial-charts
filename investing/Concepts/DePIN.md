# DePIN

DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks) is the category of crypto protocols that use token incentives to bootstrap physical or compute infrastructure — wireless networks, storage, compute, sensors, mapping, energy. Participants contribute hardware or bandwidth; the network pays them in a native token; service buyers pay for usage.

| Mapping / sensors | Hivemapper, DIMO |

---

## Relevance to AI

The AI subcategory is the fastest-growing and most investable. [[Bittensor]] sits here as a decentralized protocol paying miners to supply machine intelligence. Adjacent networks (Render for GPU rendering, io.net for distributed inference) address the same "decentralized compute marketplace" thesis with different token designs.

The core investment claim for DePIN-for-AI: commodity compute and inference are structurally cheaper to supply via token-subsidized peer-to-peer networks than via centralized cloud providers, because miners accept below-cost payments for speculative token upside. This pushes long-run unit prices well below hyperscaler pricing.
