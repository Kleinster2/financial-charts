## US EV investment reversal (2025-2026)

Between 2022 and 2025, the US auto industry invested ~$200 billion in EV and battery factories. In 2025, net announced investment turned negative for the first time — more than $20 billion in previously announced investments wiped out (Atlas Public Policy, as of March 30, 2026).

![[us-ev-battery-investment-atlas-2026.png]]
*Net announced investments in US EV and battery manufacturing. Source: Atlas Public Policy via WSJ*

![[us-ev-sales-monthly-bmi-2026.png]]
*US EV sales, monthly. Sales cliff after EV tax credit expiration. Source: Benchmark Mineral Intelligence via WSJ*

Congress and the Trump administration eliminated the $7,500 EV buyer tax credit and significant fuel-economy/clean-air regulations. J.D. Power estimated overall vehicle sales fell >10% in March 2026 YoY, with EVs losing market share.

Tier 1 supplier damage:
- [[Magna]] — $575M St. Clair, Michigan factory for [[General Motors|GM]] battery enclosures now mostly empty
- [[Dana]], [[BorgWarner]] — slashed jobs and closed plants
- Multiple smaller EV parts manufacturers shut down entirely
- [[General Motors|GM]] paid ~$400M to suppliers for modified/eliminated contracts; only partially covers losses

The [[Iran]] war and resulting gas price spike have not yet reversed buyer behavior. GM CFO Paul Jacobson: 4-6 months of sustained high gas prices needed before consumers reconsider fuel-efficient vehicles.

*Source: WSJ (Sharon Terlep), March 31, 2026*
