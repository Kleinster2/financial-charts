#concept #memory #contracts #hbm

**Strategic Customer Agreements** (SCAs) are the multi-year, locked volume-and-price contracts [[Micron]] uses to convert memory from a spot-priced commodity into committed long-cycle supply. CEO [[Sanjay Mehrotra]] introduced the term as a formal framework with the fiscal Q3 2026 print (June 24 2026), describing the agreements as significantly enhancing "the durability and predictability of Micron's strong financial performance."

---

## Why it matters

Commodity DRAM and NAND priced on quarterly spot and short contract resets — the mechanism behind [[Micron]]'s five boom-bust cycles in 25 years. SCAs replace that with multi-year locked volume and price, the same structure as the first-of-its-kind [[NVIDIA]] [[Vera Rubin]] five-year HBM agreement (March 2026) but extended across the customer book rather than a single flagship.

The shift is the core of the [[Memory squeeze thesis]]. It is Micron's own argument that 2026's strength is structural (locked multi-year contract demand) rather than cyclical (a spot spike that mean-reverts into a forward glut). The bear case the thesis tracks is a forward glut breaking the [[Memory shortage 2025-2026]] super-cycle; the SCA is the company's counter — converting the spot-vs-contract divergence into contract. Whether the agreements hold their pricing as [[HBM]]4 matures and [[Samsung]] / [[SK Hynix]] capacity catches up is the open question.

*[[Micron]]'s revenue and net income through fiscal Q3 2026.
