#concept #regulation #sports

**World Anti-Doping Code** — binding harmonized framework governing anti-doping rules across international sport, established 2003 and most recently revised effective 2021. Maintained by the [[World Anti-Doping Agency|WADA]] and adopted by the [[International Olympic Committee|IOC]], national olympic committees, and most major international sport federations. The Code defines anti-doping rule violations (presence, use, refusal, tampering, possession, trafficking, administration, complicity, prohibited association, acts to discourage reporting), prescribes sanctions (typically four years for a first violation; eight years or lifetime for repeat offenders), and establishes the [[Court of Arbitration for Sport]] as the appellate body. The Code's reach is structurally limited to signatories but in practice covers nearly the entire elite-sports world.

## Synthesis

The Code's relevance to the [[Enhanced Games]] is direct and asymmetric. Article 2 of the Code defines the anti-doping rule violations, and Article 2.10 ("Prohibited Association") plus Article 2.11 ("Acts by an Athlete or Other Person to Discourage or Retaliate Against Reporting to Authorities") have been interpreted by [[World Anti-Doping Agency|WADA]] to capture athlete participation in unsanctioned events that explicitly permit prohibited substances. The result is that an Olympic-pathway athlete who competes in the Enhanced Games faces multi-year competitive ineligibility under the Code, enforced through their national federation and appealable only through the [[Court of Arbitration for Sport]].

The athlete-selection bias for the Enhanced Games follows mechanically. Athletes whose Olympic-eligibility window is closing (late-cycle competitors, retiring veterans) face dramatically lower opportunity cost from Code-imposed sanctions than active prime-career Olympic athletes. The Enhanced Games's ability to recruit at scale therefore depends on a steady pool of late-cycle and post-cycle elite athletes willing to accept WADA sanctions in exchange for the Enhanced compensation package — which is exactly the demographic the confirmed athlete list reflects.
