**The critical distinction:** Publicly traded BDCs price in real-time and can trade at discounts to NAV. Non-traded BDCs offer quarterly "liquidity" windows â€” but the underlying loans are illiquid. This mismatch is the structural vulnerability exposed by the [[Private credit retail liquidity crisis 2026]].

## The 5% quarterly redemption mechanic

Non-traded BDCs typically allow investors to redeem up to **5% of NAV per quarter**. This works when:
- Redemption requests are small (1-2% of NAV)
- New inflows offset outflows
- Underlying loans can be sold or mature naturally

It breaks when:
- Redemption requests exceed the 5% cap â†’ investors get pro-rated
- Multiple quarters of excess requests â†’ queue builds, confidence erodes
- Fund must sell illiquid loans at discounts to meet redemptions â†’ NAV declines â†’ more redemptions

This is the **bank run dynamic** that hit [[Blue Owl]] OBDC II in Feb 2026.

## The retail push (2020-2025)

PE firms spent 5 years marketing non-traded BDCs to retail investors as "bond alternatives":

| Pitch | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| "8-12% yields" | Real, but from risky middle-market loans |
| "Quarterly liquidity" | 5% cap; underlying loans are illiquid |
| "Low volatility" | Quarterly marks hide real-time losses |
| "Diversified credit" | 14-16% software concentration (all-time high) |
| "Professional management" | Fees are earned on deployment, not avoiding losses |

The target: **$80T in retail/HNW wealth** that institutional PE firms couldn't previously access.

## Key metrics to watch

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------------|
| **NAV/share** | Are underlying loans holding value? |
| **Non-accruals** | What % of loans aren't paying interest? |

| **Distribution coverage** | Can NII cover the dividend? |

## Regulatory framework

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| 70% test | Must invest 70%+ of assets in "qualifying" (private) assets |
| Leverage | 2:1 debt-to-equity max (raised from 1:1 in 2018) |
| Distribution | 90%+ of taxable income to maintain tax pass-through |
| Board | Independent directors required |
| Valuation | Quarterly fair value (board-approved, third-party valuers optional) |
| SEC reporting | 10-K, 10-Q, proxy filings |

**The 2018 leverage change** (from 1:1 to 2:1) doubled the industry's capacity to take risk â€” right before the biggest credit expansion in history.
