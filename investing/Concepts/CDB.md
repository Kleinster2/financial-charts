# CDB

A Certificado de Depósito Bancário is a Brazilian bank time deposit — the investor lends to a bank for a rate, the local analogue of a US CD. It is the workhorse of the Brazilian renda-fixa sleeve.

Chart data unavailable — this is an instrument category, not a single tracked price series.

## Mechanics

- Three rate flavors: pós-fixado (a % of CDI / Selic), prefixado (a fixed rate), and IPCA+ (inflation plus a spread).
- FGC-insured up to R$250,000 per CPF per institution (the Fundo Garantidor de Créditos). This is why investors ladder across mid-tier banks — smaller, higher-paying banks are effectively "free" credit risk up to the FGC limit.
- Liquidity varies: daily-liquidity CDBs (often ~100% of CDI) versus term CDBs with a carência (lock-up) that pay more for giving up access.

## Risks / watch-outs

- Above the FGC limit it is unsecured bank credit — the issuer's health matters.
- Prefixado and IPCA+ CDBs are marked-to-market if sold early; a term CDB held to maturity is not.
- The recurring advisory lesson ([[Brazilian fixed income]]): don't trade away liquidity for a thin prefixado spread.
