# Chinese Depositary Receipts

A Chinese Depositary Receipt (CDR) is a [[Depositary receipts|depositary receipt]] traded on a mainland Chinese exchange — Shanghai's STAR Market or Shenzhen — that represents shares of a foreign-registered, China-incorporated company. It is the instrument China designed to bring its overseas-listed tech champions home. The State Council set out the framework in early 2018, explicitly to let foreign-registered Chinese companies (including those with variable-interest-entity (VIE) structures, common among US-listed China tech) raise equity domestically.

## The homecoming mechanism

The CDR was created as the mainland counterpart to the [[American Depositary Receipts|ADR]] — a way to reverse the US-listing wave by letting names like [[Alibaba]] and [[Xiaomi]] list shares at home. The pilot stalled at the gate: [[Xiaomi]] filed for a CDR in 2018, then withdrew it indefinitely and listed in Hong Kong instead, and the first wave fizzled.

The first actual CDR came in 2020: Xiaomi-backed Ninebot — the Cayman-incorporated Segway scooter maker, with a VIE structure — sold CDRs on the STAR Market at ¥18.94 and closed its debut at ¥38.50 (+103%), the first foreign-registered VIE company to list on a mainland exchange. [[Lenovo]] later won shareholder approval for a STAR Market CDR plan. The format remains the policy tool for the homecoming theme, gaining renewed relevance whenever US-listed China ADRs face delisting pressure under the [[Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act]] or [[Executive Order 13959]].

## Synthesis

The CDR is the mirror of the China-ADR story: where the [[American Depositary Receipts|ADR]] brought Chinese companies to US capital, the CDR is Beijing's mechanism to bring them back, complete with accommodation for the VIE structures that defined the original US listings. Its history is one of policy ambition outrunning use — the 2018 pilot stalled, Ninebot's 2020 listing was the proof of concept, and the format's relevance rises and falls with the US listing/delisting cycle rather than with steady issuance. It is the youngest and most politically charged member of the [[Depositary receipts]] family.
