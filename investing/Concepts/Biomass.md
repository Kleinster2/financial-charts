#concept #energy #renewables

**Biomass** — organic material (wood, agricultural residues, bagasse) used as feedstock for energy generation or biofuel production. Two main paths: combustion (power/heat) and conversion ([[E2G|cellulosic ethanol]], biogas).

---

## Two paths

| Path | Output | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Combustion** | Electricity + heat | Wood pellets → power plants, bagasse → co-generation |
| **Conversion** | Liquid fuel / biogas | Bagasse → [[E2G]], corn stover → cellulosic ethanol |

---

## Key feedstocks

| Feedstock | Cost (dry MT) | Primary use |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Sugarcane bagasse | $38-40 | E2G ethanol, co-generation ([[Brazil]]) |
| Corn stover | ~$90 | E2G ethanol (US) |
| Wood pellets | Varies | Power generation (EU, Asia) |
| Wheat straw | Varies | E2G ethanol (EU) |
| Switchgrass | Varies | Energy crop (marginal land) |

---

## Power generation

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Global output (2023) | ~700 [[TWh]] |
| Projected (2030) | ~1,250 TWh (IEA) |
| Largest producer | [[Raizen]] (1.3 GW, Brazil — sugarcane bagasse) |
| Largest pellet producer | Enviva (6.2M MT/yr — emerged from Ch. 11 Dec 2024) |

**Carbon debate:** Biomass burning emits ~150% CO2 of coal per unit energy. Classified "renewable" because regrowth absorbs carbon — but regrowth takes decades while emissions are immediate.
