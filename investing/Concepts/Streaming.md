**Streaming** — direct-to-consumer video delivery over the internet, bypassing traditional cable/satellite distribution. The dominant secular trend in media since ~2015, accelerated by COVID-era subscriber surges. The industry has shifted from growth-at-all-costs (2019-2022) to a profitability phase, with most major services implementing ad tiers, password-sharing crackdowns, and content spending discipline. Global streaming revenue exceeded $100B in 2024.

## Investment landscape

The streaming wars produced a clear winner ([[Netflix]] at 300M+ global subs) and a field of platform holders trying to make the economics work. The key debate has shifted from "who wins subscribers" to "who achieves sustainable margins." [[Netflix]] runs ~28% operating margins; legacy media streamers ([[Disney]]+, Max, Peacock, Paramount+) are still working toward breakeven. The ad-supported tier is now the primary growth vector — [[Netflix]]'s ad tier reached 70M+ MAU by late 2024, and [[Amazon]]'s default-on Prime Video ads gave it instant scale. Music streaming ([[Spotify]], [[Apple Music]]) and live sports streaming (ESPN+, [[Amazon]] Thursday Night Football, [[YouTube]] Sunday Ticket) are adjacent but distinct markets.

## AI-generated scripted-series signal

[[Kalshi]]'s KXAISTREAMSERIES-27 market prices which streaming platform owner will release a fully AI-generated multi-episode scripted series before Jan. 1, 2027. At the May 20, 2026 read, [[Amazon]] led at 24c last, [[Netflix]] followed at 21c, and [[Disney]], [[Paramount Global]], and [[Apple]] clustered around 9c-10c. The signal is not that audiences already want AI-written shows; it is that the first public test is expected to come from a platform with distribution scale, experimentation capacity, and enough legal control to absorb the backlash.
