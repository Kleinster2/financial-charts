# Digital Markets Act

The Digital Markets Act is the [[EU]]'s ex ante gatekeeper regime for large digital platforms that act as bottlenecks between business users and end customers.

---

## Synthesis

The DMA matters for investors because it converts dominant digital distribution positions into conduct-rule exposure before a conventional antitrust case is fully litigated. The Jun 2026 cloud findings show the regime moving from consumer-facing platforms into cloud and AI infrastructure: [[AWS]] and [[Microsoft Azure]] can be treated as gateways even when the Commission's theory rests on lock-in, ecosystems, switching costs, and AI procurement rather than only simple size thresholds.

---

## Gatekeeper mechanism

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Gatekeeper | A large platform company designated by the [[European Commission]] because it controls an important access point between businesses and users |
| Core platform service | The service covered by the designation, such as app stores, messaging, search, operating systems, ads, or cloud computing |
| Conduct obligations | Rules designed to keep markets contestable and fair once a platform is designated |
| Compliance runway | Designated services generally receive a period to bring the service into full compliance |

The investment question is not only whether a company is fined. It is whether the covered service loses default bundling power, preferential treatment, data leverage, or switching-cost advantages that support margins and cross-selling.

---

## Cloud extension (Jun 2026)

On Jun 25 2026, the Commission reached a preliminary position that [[Amazon]] and [[Microsoft]] should be DMA gatekeepers for [[AWS]] and [[Microsoft Azure]]. The Commission framed cloud as a strategic gateway for EU business users, with AI tools and partnerships becoming a decisive procurement factor.

See [[EU cloud DMA preliminary findings 2026]].
