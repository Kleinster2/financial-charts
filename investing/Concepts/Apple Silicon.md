# Apple Silicon

Umbrella term for [[Apple]]'s in-house-designed processors — the A-series (iPhone/iPad), M-series (Mac/iPad Pro), and related chips (S, H, U, C-series) — fabricated primarily by [[TSMC]] at the leading edge. The 2020–2023 Mac transition from Intel x86 to Apple-designed M-series chips is the canonical "Apple Silicon transition." Apple's design organization extracts more performance per node from [[TSMC]] processes than any competitor (see [[Customer lock-in via co-design]]), and the program is the design-and-manufacturing moat referenced throughout [[Apple]]'s strategy notes.
