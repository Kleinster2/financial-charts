**East Data West Compute** (东数西算, dōngshù xīsuàn) — [[China]]'s national plan, launched 2022, to route data-processing demand from the data-rich, power-constrained eastern coast to large compute hubs built in the energy-rich western provinces (Inner Mongolia, Gansu, Ningxia, Guizhou). A state-directed allocation of AI/computing infrastructure across the country's power and land geography. The telecoms — [[China Unicom]], [[China Mobile]], [[China Telecom]] — are core builders, using their fiber, land, and power footprints to host the intelligent-computing capacity (China Unicom reported 45 EFLOPS in 2025).
