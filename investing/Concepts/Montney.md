# Montney

A Triassic-age unconventional oil and gas play straddling northeastern [[British Columbia]] and northwestern [[Alberta]] — [[Canada]]'s most prolific liquids-rich shale resource. The play is layered (multiple stacked horizons), liquids-rich (condensate + NGLs alongside dry gas), and the structural driver of Canadian gas and condensate growth in the 2020s.

---

## Why Montney matters

| Aspect | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Geology | Triassic siltstone/shale; multiple stacked benches |
| Geography | NE British Columbia + NW Alberta |
| Mix | Liquids-rich gas (condensate + NGLs alongside dry gas) |
| Strategic role | Primary supplier of condensate diluent for [[oil sands]] bitumen blending |
| LNG link | Feeds [[LNG]] Canada export capacity on the BC coast |

The condensate angle distinguishes Montney from US dry-gas plays. Bitumen from Alberta oil sands cannot be pipelined without diluent; condensate from the Montney is the closest physical supply, structurally tying Montney economics to oil prices through the diluent market rather than to Henry Hub.
