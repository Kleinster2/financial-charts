#concept #investing #fundamentals #valuation

**Owner earnings** — [[Warren Buffett]]'s preferred measure of the cash a business actually generates for its owners, introduced in the 1986 [[Berkshire Hathaway]] annual report. Defined as reported earnings plus depreciation/amortization and other non-cash charges, minus the average annual maintenance capex required to preserve competitive position and unit volume. It is the conceptual ancestor of [[Free cash flow]], differing mainly in its insistence on *maintenance* capex (not total capex) and in pre-dating the modern [[Stock-based compensation]] debate.

## Quick stats

| Element | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Reported earnings | Starting point |
| + Non-cash charges | D&A and similar added back |
| − Maintenance capex | The hard, judgment-heavy subtraction Buffett emphasizes |
| Modern wrinkle | SBC, a non-cash charge Buffett's era lacked, should be subtracted (he later called options "compensation... an expense") |

Buffett's framing: accounting earnings are a starting point, not the answer — "the question is which figure... is the relevant one for investors," and owner earnings is his answer. Chart data not applicable — definitional concept.
