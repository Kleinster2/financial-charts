#concept #saudi #events #investment

**Future Investment Initiative** — [[Saudi PIF]]'s flagship annual investment conference, organized by [[Richard Attias & Associates]] and operated through the FII Institute (a PIF non-profit). Launched 2017 in [[Riyadh]] as "Davos in the Desert." Expanded into the FII PRIORITY satellite franchise in 2022, rotating through Miami, Hong Kong, Rio de Janeiro, Tirana, and Tokyo. Chaired by [[Yasir Al-Rumayyan]]; CEO [[Penny Richards]] (appointed 2025). Claims $250B+ in opportunities generated through its gatherings over the past decade. The Riyadh flagship draws 6,000+ delegates; PRIORITY editions draw 1,000-2,000.

## Significance

FII is [[Saudi Arabia]]'s primary platform for global investor engagement — the soft-power complement to [[Saudi PIF]]'s hard capital deployment. Regular attendees include CEOs of [[BlackRock]], [[Goldman Sachs]], [[Citigroup]], [[JPMorgan Chase]], and other major financial institutions. The conference franchise has two functions: (1) showcasing PIF and [[Saudi Vision 2030|Vision 2030]] to global capital allocators, and (2) providing a venue for deal-making between sovereign wealth funds, private equity, and corporate leadership.

The 2018 Riyadh edition was mass-boycotted after the murder of [[Jamal Khashoggi]] at the Saudi consulate in Istanbul days before the scheduled conference. By 2024, the boycott was fully over.

The PRIORITY satellite franchise launched in 2022 to extend FII's reach beyond the Gulf. The Miami edition targets the US-[[Latin America]] investment corridor; Hong Kong covers Asia-Pacific; Rio targets South America; Tirana covers Europe. Each edition carries its own theme and agenda while maintaining PIF as founding partner.

Since 2025, FII has become the premier channel for Saudi-US economic diplomacy. President [[Donald Trump]] became the first sitting US president to address FII at the February 2025 Miami edition, announcing a $600B Saudi investment commitment (later expanded to $1T). He returns as closing keynote at [[FII Priority Miami 2026]].
