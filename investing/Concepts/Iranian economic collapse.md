### Why 2025? The triggers

Iran has faced USD shortages since 2018. Why did the rial collapse 78% specifically in 2025?

**Specific triggers — not just accumulated pressure:**

| Date | Trigger | Impact |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Dec 2024** | Assad falls in Syria | Lost $50B investment, Hezbollah supply route cut |
| **Jan 2025** | Trump inaugurated | "Maximum pressure 2.0" begins |
| **Jun 2025** | 12-day war with Israel | Rial lost 40% from Jun-Dec alone |
| **Sep 2025** | [[UN snapback]] triggered | UK/France/Germany restored UN sanctions |
| **Oct 2025** | Ayandeh Bank collapses | 7M depositors trapped, bank runs, panic |
| **Oct-Nov 2025** | Chinese teapot quotas exhausted | Primary buyers stopped buying |
| **Dec 2025** | Preferential FX rate ended | Importers forced to open market rate |
| **Late 2025** | 52M barrels stuck floating | Delivery bottleneck, no USD inflows |

### Assad's fall — the confidence shock (Dec 2024)

Often overlooked: Syria collapsed in December 2024, just before the rial's 2025 freefall.

**What Iran lost:**

| Asset | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Investment in Assad regime | ~$50B over a decade |
| Mediterranean access | Strategic outlet gone |
| Land corridor to Hezbollah | Supply route severed |
| Regional deterrent credibility | "Axis of Resistance" shattered |

> "We were defeated, and defeated very badly, we took a very big blow." — Brig. Gen. Behrouz Esbati, Dec 31, 2025

> Assad's collapse "set the Iranian project in the region back by 40 years." — HTS leader Ahmed al-Sharaa

**Combined with Hezbollah decimation (2024):** Israel killed most of Hezbollah's leadership and degraded its operational capacity throughout 2024. Syria's fall then cut the resupply route.

**Confidence mechanism:** Iranians watching the regime lose Syria, lose Hezbollah, then lose the June 2025 war concluded the Islamic Republic was failing. Capital flight accelerated.

### [[Ayandeh Bank]] collapse (Oct 2025)

**Iran's largest private bank failure** — a Ponzi scheme that finally unwound.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Debt | 5 quadrillion rials ($4.67B) |
| Deposits trapped | 2.5 quadrillion rials ($2.34B) |
| Depositors affected | 7 million |
| Branches | 270 (150 in Tehran) |
| Losses | ~$5.2B |

**What happened:**
- Oct 25, 2025: Bank declared bankrupt
- Merged into state-owned Bank Melli
- Panic queues across Tehran
- Authorities arrested people for "spreading rumors"

**How it operated — Ponzi dynamics:**
- Offered highest interest rates in Iran to attract deposits
- Used new deposits to pay old depositors
- Funneled money into illiquid real estate (especially Iran Mall megaproject)
- Borrowed heavily from Central Bank (which printed money to support it)
- Contributed ~40% of banking system's debt to CBI
- Added ~5% to annual inflation alone

**Contagion risk:**

| Bank | Status |
|------|--------|
| Ayandeh | Collapsed |
| Sepah Bank | Near insolvency |
| 4 other major banks | Near insolvency |

**Confidence impact:** 7 million depositors learned their savings were trapped. Rush to convert remaining deposits to USD accelerated rial decline.

### UN snapback — the overlooked catalyst

In September 2025, Britain, France, and Germany triggered the "snapback" mechanism under UN Security Council Resolution 2231, restoring multilateral sanctions suspended under JCPOA.

**Why this mattered:**
- Transformed US unilateral sanctions into global sanctions
- European companies now legally barred (not just cautious)
- Insurance, shipping, banking all re-restricted globally
- Signal that diplomacy was dead

> "The snapback has had immediate and extensive negative effects on the rial." — Analysts

### Israel war (June 2025)

12-day conflict created:
- Direct military costs
- "No war, no peace" psychology
- Capital flight acceleration
- Regional deterrent credibility destroyed

**Post-war:** Rial lost ~40% of remaining value (Jun-Dec 2025).

### Domestic policy trigger (December 2025)

Iran ended multi-tier exchange rate system for imports:
- Previously: Importers got preferential rate for "essential goods"
- New policy: All imports at open market rate
- Effect: Massively increased USD demand on black market

**Mechanical driver:** More buyers competing for same USD supply = price spikes.

### China oil bottleneck — the fall accelerant

**Why the rial accelerated in Oct-Nov 2025:** Iran's only significant USD source (oil to China) seized up.

**The mechanics:**

```
Iran exports oil → Shadow fleet → Ship-to-ship near Malaysia →
    → Chinese "teapot" refineries → Payment (delayed, discounted, in yuan)
```

**What broke in fall 2025:**

| Problem | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Teapot quotas exhausted** | Chinese independent refiners ran out of import quota allocation |
| **US sanctioned teapots directly** | Treasury hit Shandong Shouguang Luqing (Mar 2025), Shandong Shengxing (Apr 2025) — first-ever teapot designations |
| **Floating storage backup** | 52M barrels stuck at sea — highest in 2.5 years |
| **Malaysia/Indonesia relabeling harder** | Tighter scrutiny on fake origin certificates |

**Floating storage detail:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Iranian crude floating | 52M barrels (late 2025) |
| Location | Near Malaysia, Singapore, South China Sea |
| Waiting for | Ship-to-ship transfer, quota allocation, payment |

**The paradox:** Iran's "exports" looked high on paper (2M+ bbl/day), but:
- Oil was leaving ports but not arriving at buyers
- Payments delayed months behind deliveries
- Teapots near quota limits stopped buying
- Even delivered oil paid in yuan at steep discounts

**Net effect:** USD inflows collapsed even though export volumes held up. The rial tracks USD inflows, not barrels shipped.

**US enforcement escalation:**

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| Mar 2025 | First teapot sanctioned (Shandong Shouguang Luqing) |
| Apr 2025 | Second teapot sanctioned (Shandong Shengxing) |
| Ongoing | Shadow fleet vessels designated |
| Ongoing | Ship-to-ship transfer networks targeted |

> "With nearly nine in ten barrels ultimately destined for China, calibrated enforcement targeting Chinese refiners, traders, ports, and financial intermediaries can materially affect Tehran's export capacity and revenues." — FDD analysis

### Bazaar protests force CB governor out (Dec 29, 2025)

Mohammad Reza Farzin didn't resign voluntarily — the bazaar forced him out.

**The trigger — electronics merchants break first:**

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| Dec 28, AM | Dollar hits 145,000 tomans (1.45M rials) — record |
| Dec 28 | Electronics shopkeepers at **Alaeddin Shopping Centre** shut stores |
| Dec 28 | **Charsou Mall** merchants join strike |
| Dec 28-29 | Spreads to Saadi Street, Grand Bazaar, Isfahan, Shiraz, Mashhad |
| Dec 29 | Farzin resigns |

**Why electronics merchants first:** They import goods priced in USD. When the rial crashes overnight, their inventory cost basis is destroyed — they can't restock without taking losses on every unit.

> "Every transaction is a loss. Every day is a step backward." — Merchant

**Why the bazaar matters:**

The Tehran Grand Bazaar isn't just a market — it's a political power center. Bazaari merchants helped fund the 1979 revolution. When the bazaar turns against the regime, it's existential.

| Bazaar grievance | Impact |
|------------------|--------|
| **Can't restock** | Import costs jumped overnight, every new purchase is a loss |
| **Inventory devalued** | Bought at old rate, must sell below cost or price out customers |
| **Gold spiking** | Alternative store of value also unaffordable |
| **No ability to hedge** | Currency swings too fast to price anything |
| **Customers broke** | Shops offering installment plans for *clothes* — unprecedented |

**Inflation context (Dec 2025):**
- Official inflation: 52%+
- Food prices: +66% YoY
- Former VP estimate: heading to 55%

**What Farzin told parliament (Dec 2025):** Hard currency supply to the Central Bank had "declined in recent months" — oil revenues weren't arriving.

**His track record:**
- Appointed Dec 2022 at 430,000 IRR/USD
- Resigned Dec 2025 at 1,380,000 IRR/USD
- **220% depreciation on his watch**

**Replacement:** Abdolnaser Hemmati (CB governor 2018-2021) — previously failed to stabilize the rial during Trump's first term.

### The compounding sequence

```
Jan 2025: Trump returns → market panic
Jun 2025: Israel war → confidence shattered
Sep 2025: UN snapback → multilateral isolation
Dec 2025: FX policy change → demand shock
Dec 28: Rial hits record → bazaar protests erupt
Dec 29: CB governor forced out → policy vacuum
Late Dec: Protests turn political → regime crackdown
```

**Key insight:** 2025 had multiple discrete shocks, not just accumulated pressure. The war, snapback, and bazaar revolt each transformed the situation.

## Transmission mechanism

### How sanctions cause collapse

```
US sanctions
    → Oil exports blocked (except China)
        → USD inflows collapse
            → Central bank can't defend rial
                → Currency depreciates
                    → Import prices spike
                        → Inflation
                            → Living standards collapse
                                → Protests
```

### Why it's self-reinforcing

| Stage | Feedback loop |
|-------|---------------|
| Currency falls | → Iranians rush to buy USD |
| USD demand spikes | → Currency falls faster |
| Inflation rises | → Workers demand higher wages |
| Costs rise | → More inflation |
| Confidence falls | → Capital flight accelerates |

## Banking and financial isolation

### Why banking is the kill switch

**The USD system is the global economy's operating system.** Cut a country off from USD clearing and SWIFT, and you've effectively disconnected them from world trade:

```
Normal trade:
  Iranian company → Iranian bank → SWIFT → Correspondent bank → USD clearing → Foreign bank → Foreign company

Post-sanctions:
  Iranian company → ??? → no SWIFT → no correspondent → no clearing → ❌
```

### SWIFT cutoff

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| Mar 2012 | First SWIFT disconnection (nuclear sanctions) |
| Jan 2016 | SWIFT restored (JCPOA) |
| Nov 2018 | SWIFT disconnected again (Trump withdrawal) |
| Current | Blocked — no access to global messaging |

**What SWIFT does:** Secure messaging between 11,000+ banks globally. Without it, banks can't communicate payment instructions. Iran can't tell a German bank to pay a supplier.

**Not technically sanctions:** SWIFT is Belgian, but complied with US/EU pressure. Shows extraterritorial reach of US financial power.

### Correspondent banking

Even when SWIFT was restored (2016-2018), major banks refused to touch Iran:

| Bank | Position |
|------|----------|
| [[JPMorgan]] | Refused Iran business |
| HSBC | Refused |
| Standard Chartered | Refused |
| Deutsche Bank | Refused |
| BNP Paribas | Refused (after $8.9B fine in 2014) |

**Why banks stay away:**
1. **Compliance risk** — One mistake = billions in fines
2. **Reputational risk** — "Bank that does business with Iran"
3. **Litigation risk** — US plaintiffs can sue for terrorism links
4. **Uncertainty** — Sanctions can snap back anytime
5. **De-risking** — Easier to avoid entire country than monitor transactions

**BNP Paribas precedent (2014):** $8.9B fine for violating Sudan, Cuba, Iran sanctions. Largest sanctions fine ever. Made every compliance officer terrified of Iran exposure.

### Central Bank of Iran (CBI) sanctions

| Tool | Effect |
|------|--------|
| SDN listing | CBI blocked from USD system |
| Correspondent bans | No bank will hold CBI accounts |
| Asset freezes | $100B+ in frozen reserves globally |
| Secondary sanctions | Any bank touching CBI faces US penalties |

**Frozen reserves:** Iran has ~$100B+ in overseas accounts it cannot access. These show up in "foreign reserves" but are unusable.

### Alternative payment channels

How Iran survives without Western banking:

| Channel | How it works | Limitations |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Yuan settlement** | China pays for oil in RMB, Iran buys Chinese goods | Can only spend in China |
| **Barter** | Oil for goods (Chinese machinery, etc.) | Inefficient, unfavorable terms |
| **Hawala** | Informal trust-based transfers | Small scale, high fees |
| **Gold** | Physical delivery | Logistically difficult |
| **Crypto** | Increasingly used | Volatile, traceable |
| **Front companies** | Shell companies in UAE, Turkey | Risk of exposure |

### CIPS as alternative

| System | Operator | Iran access |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| SWIFT | Belgian (US-influenced) | Blocked |
| CIPS | China | Limited access |

**CIPS (Cross-Border Interbank Payment System):** China's SWIFT alternative. Iran can use for China trade, but CIPS only handles ~3% of SWIFT volume and most global banks don't connect to it.

### Why banking isolation is so effective

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **No alternatives at scale** | CIPS too small, crypto too volatile |
| **Banks self-censor** | De-risking exceeds legal requirements |
| **Sticky even after relief** | Banks stayed away 2016-2018 |
| **Compounds other sanctions** | Can't pay for imports even if allowed |
| **Hits ordinary Iranians** | Medicine, food imports disrupted |

### The 2016-2018 lesson

JCPOA (Iran nuclear deal) lifted sanctions, but:

| Expected | Actual |
|----------|--------|
| Trade normalizes | Banks refused to reconnect |
| Investment flows in | Companies stayed away |
| Economy recovers | Modest improvement only |

**Why it failed:** Sanctions relief was legally real but practically useless. No major bank would process Iran transactions. The compliance/litigation risk was too high.

**Investment implication:** Even if future sanctions relief occurs, Iran will face years of banking hesitancy. The Venezuela parallel applies — [[Venezuela]] faces same "no bank will touch us" problem.
