#concept #brazil #fiscal #policy

**Arcabouço Fiscal** — [[Brazil]]'s fiscal framework since August 2023, replacing the 2016 spending cap (Teto de Gastos). Combines a primary-result target band with a real-growth ceiling on federal spending. Designed to allow some counter-cyclical spending while anchoring debt sustainability expectations. Architect: [[Fernando Haddad]] as Finance Minister under [[Lula]] III. In practice through 2025-2026: markets treat the framework as credibility-positive versus the prior Dilma-era regime but insufficient to stabilize debt/GDP, which is projected toward 95% by 2026.

---

## Synthesis

The central insight: the Arcabouço is more credible than the post-Teto vacuum but materially less tight than the 2016 cap — it lets spending grow with revenue rather than freezing in real terms. The constraint: debt/GDP is projected toward 95% by end-2026 absent primary surpluses, and the framework's mechanics allow that trajectory. The counter-example: markets reward the framework in [[Selic]] carry flows ([[Banco Central do Brasil|BCB]] credibility + ~10.9% real rate) but haven't repriced Brazil risk downward, precisely because framework compliance ≠ fiscal sustainability. The one-line: every durable Brazilian fiscal framework eventually gets replaced when it becomes politically binding, and the Arcabouço's test arrives in the [[2026 Brazil presidential election]] window.

## Structure

| Element | Mechanics |
|---------|-----------|
| Primary target band | Target ± 0.25pp tolerance. Target: 0.0% (2025), gradual path to surplus |
| Real spending growth | Capped at 70% of real revenue growth, with 0.6-2.5% annual floor/ceiling |
| Triggers | Missing the target band triggers spending restrictions on select lines |
| Exclusions | Some social spending (health, education) partially protected |

Key difference from the 2016 Teto: the framework allows spending to grow with revenue rather than freezing in real terms. Political durability higher; fiscal tightness lower.

## Why it matters for allocators

Rate path: [[Selic]] and the DI curve price continued fiscal slippage. Real rates above 10% (14.75% [[Selic]] against 3.81% IPCA as of April 2026) reflect carry trade flows attracted by [[Banco Central do Brasil|BCB]] credibility, not fiscal credibility. Any material framework violation or suspension would widen risk premium.

Debt trajectory: Federal gross debt was ~78% of GDP entering 2026, projected toward 95% by end of 2026 on current path. Absent primary surpluses, the arithmetic requires either stronger growth, lower rates, or currency strength to stabilize — all three are election-sensitive.

Currency: [[Brazilian real]] volatility in 2025-2026 has tracked fiscal slippage narratives. Framework breaches historically produce 3-5% BRL moves.

## Historical context

| Regime | Period | Outcome |
|--------|--------|---------|
| LRF (Fiscal Responsibility Law) | 2000-2014 | Established primary surplus culture |
| Primary deficits | 2014-2016 | Dilma impeachment, lost investment grade |
| Teto de Gastos (2016 spending cap) | 2016-2023 | Real-terms freeze; politically unsustainable by 2022 |
| Arcabouço Fiscal | Aug 2023 – present | Haddad replacement; primary band + real-growth ceiling |

The Teto's collapse reflects a broader Brazilian fiscal cycle: every durable framework eventually gets replaced when it becomes politically binding. The Arcabouço's test is whether it survives 2026 electoral pressure intact.
