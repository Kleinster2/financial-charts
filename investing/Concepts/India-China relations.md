**India-China relations** — Two Asian giants. 2020 clash froze ties; US tariffs now pushing rapprochement.

---

## Current status (Jan 2026)

**Thawing.** India planning to lift 2020 restrictions on Chinese firms.

### Recent developments

| Date | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 2020 | Galwan Valley clash — 20+ Indian soldiers killed |
| 2020 | India imposes restrictions on Chinese govt contract bids |
| 2021 | Chinese project wins fall 27% |
| 2024 | Modi visits China (first in 7 years) |
| 2024 | Direct flights resume |
| 2024 | Business visa process streamlined |
| Jan 2026 | India plans to scrap contract restrictions |

### Why thawing now

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **US 50% tariff on India** | Pushed India toward China |
| **US-Pakistan warming** | India feels abandoned |
| **Infrastructure shortages** | Chinese equipment needed |
| **Power sector bottleneck** | 307 GW target at risk |

## 2020 restrictions (being lifted)

| Restriction | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| Registration + security clearance for Chinese bidders | Effective ban |
| Contracts blocked | $700-750B value |
| Chinese wins fell 27% (2021) | $1.67B down from prior year |
| CRRC disqualified | $216M train contract |
| Power equipment curbs | Thermal capacity expansion slowed |

### Gauba Committee

High-level committee headed by former cabinet secretary Rajiv Gauba recommended easing restrictions. Finance Ministry working to remove registration requirement.

**Final decision:** Modi's office.
