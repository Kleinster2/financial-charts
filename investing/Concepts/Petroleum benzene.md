# Petroleum benzene

Benzene (C₆H₆) produced from petroleum sources — the aromatic building block for styrene, phenol, cyclohexane, nitrobenzene (→ aniline), and alkylbenzene. Distinct from coal-derived benzene (negligible in modern markets). "Petroleum benzene" is the common Chinese market label for the standard grade, tracked by [[NBS]] alongside [[Polyethylene]], [[Polypropylene]], and [[Methanol]] as a factory-input bellwether.

---

## Synopsis

Benzene is the entry point to the aromatics complex (benzene, toluene, xylenes — "BTX"). Downstream it becomes:
- Ethylbenzene → styrene → polystyrene, ABS, SBR (synthetic rubber)
- Cumene → phenol → bisphenol A → polycarbonate, epoxy
- Cyclohexane → caprolactam / adipic acid → nylon
- Nitrobenzene → aniline → MDI (polyurethane)
- Linear alkylbenzene (LAB) → detergents

Production comes from steam-cracker co-product (pyrolysis gasoline), catalytic reformate at refineries, and toluene disproportionation (TDP). Middle East refineries and petchem complexes are major benzene exporters alongside Korea, Japan, and the US Gulf Coast.

The [[FT]]'s NBS/Wind chart (April 11 2026) shows domestic China petroleum benzene at ~5,500 Rmb/tonne pre-war, jumping to ~8,500 Rmb/tonne in the post-strike weeks — roughly a 55% spike, proportionally the largest among the four commodities charted (PE, PP, methanol, benzene).

## End uses

| Derivative | End product |
|-----------|-------------|
| Styrene | Polystyrene, ABS, SBR rubber, EPS (insulation) |
| Cumene → phenol | Polycarbonate, epoxy resin, nylon-6 |
| Cyclohexane | Nylon (caprolactam, adipic acid) |
| Nitrobenzene → aniline | MDI for polyurethane foams, coatings |
| LAB | Detergents (surfactants) |
