# China platform regulation

The 2020–2023 regulatory campaign that reshaped [[China]]'s internet-platform sector — the dominant shared driver behind the [[Chinese internet]] ADR cohort, and the force that fused the names into a single "China-risk" trade at its 2022 peak (cohesion 0.80). What began as an antitrust and financial-stability action widened into data-security, labor ([[China platform labor regulation]]), and content fronts before the late-2022/2023 thaw. The textbook case of policy risk as a non-fundamental, non-diversifiable factor — exogenous and political, dominant over business fundamentals while active.

![[china-platform-regulation-chart.png]]
*The crackdown's market signature: [[KWEB]] (the China-internet ETF) vs [[SPY]], normalized since 2019. The roughly −80% drawdown from the Feb 2021 high to the Oct 2022 low — and the failure to re-converge with US equities since — is the country-risk factor being priced.*

## Timeline (acute phase)

- Nov 3 2020 — [[Ant Group]]'s ~$34bn dual listing suspended 48 hours before pricing, days after [[Jack Ma]]'s Bund Finance Summit speech criticizing regulators; the opening shot.
- Apr 2021 — [[Alibaba]] fined ¥18.2bn (~$2.8bn) for "choose one of two" (er xuan yi) exclusivity conduct — then a record antitrust penalty.
- Jun–Jul 2021 — [[Didi]] pulled from app stores days after its $4.4bn NYSE IPO, under a data-security review; the "double reduction" policy effectively banned for-profit K-12 tutoring overnight, erasing the education sub-sector.
- 2021–2022 — gaming-approval freeze and minor-playtime limits hit [[Tencent]]/[[NetEase]]; data-security, algorithm, and overseas-listing rules formalized.
- Late 2022–2023 — the thaw: [[Ant Group]]'s ~$985mn restructuring fine (Jul 2023) framed as "rectification largely complete," gaming approvals resumed, and Beijing pivoted to a pro-growth "support the platform economy" stance.

## Quick stats

- Peak-to-trough: [[KWEB]] fell roughly −80% from its Feb 2021 high to the Oct 2022 low.
- The overhang compounded with the [[Chinese Depositary Receipts|HFCAA delisting]] threat — two simultaneous, jurisdiction-level risks that swamped each platform's idiosyncratic story.
