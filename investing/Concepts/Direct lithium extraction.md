**Direct lithium extraction** — Emerging technology that separates [[Lithium|lithium]] from underground brines using solvents or ceramic filters, bypassing traditional evaporation ponds. Advocates compare it to the shale revolution — a technology unlock that could reshape global supply chains and loosen [[China]]'s stranglehold on lithium processing.

---

## How it works

DLE typically uses solvents or ceramic materials to separate lithium from brines. It can process oilfield brines that are unsuitable for evaporation, opening up resources in regions like the US Gulf Coast.

| Method | Recovery rate | Time to lithium | Land use | Water use |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|----------|-----------|
| Evaporation ponds | 40-60% | Up to 18 months | High | High |
| DLE | 70-90% | Hours | Low | Low |
| Hard rock mining | Varies | Weeks (after mining) | High (open pit) | Moderate |

Source: Wood Mackenzie

## Cost benchmarks

| Production method | All-in cost ($/t LCE) |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Most competitive evaporation/hybrid | ~$6,000 |
| Oil brine DLE (industry avg) | ~$10,000 |
| [[Standard Lithium]] (Benchmark estimate) | ~$10,735 |
| [[Standard Lithium]] (own claim, cash operating) | <$6,000 |
| Breakeven for most new DLE capacity | $18,000-20,000 |

Source: Benchmark Mineral Intelligence (May 2025)

Most projects need substantially higher lithium prices to proceed, but players with high-concentration brines (like the Smackover) claim they can undercut that threshold.

## The Smackover Formation

The key US resource. An underground brine reservoir extending across Arkansas, Louisiana, Texas, Alabama, Mississippi, and Florida — the same geological formation that fueled Arkansas's early-20th-century oil boom.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| USGS resource estimate (SW Arkansas) | Up to 19M tonnes lithium |
| Brine concentration (Standard's area) | 400+ mg/L |
| Existing infrastructure | Oil and chemical facilities (Lanxess bromine, etc.) |
| Companies active | [[Standard Lithium]], [[Exxon|ExxonMobil]]/Saltwerx, [[Occidental Petroleum]], [[Equinor]], ~12 total |

Even if only a fraction is recoverable, the Smackover could easily satisfy total current US lithium demand (FT, May 2025).

## Challenges

- Oilfield brines require extensive pre-treatment to remove contaminants — each brine has a unique impurity profile, complicating standardization (Benchmark)
- Eramet's Latin American plant faced delays even after years of testing; hybrid models (DLE + evaporation) likely to dominate near-term
- US producers losing Biden-era IRA tax incentives as Congressional Republicans propose cuts
- [[China]] accused of deliberately crashing lithium prices to frustrate new DLE entrants — lithium carbonate slumped 80% to ~$9,000/t from Nov 2022 peak
- Beijing has restricted export of lithium processing technologies, adding a technology chokepoint atop the refining chokepoint
- Royalty politics: Arkansas landowners seeking 2.5-12.5% rates, squeezing project economics

Federico Gay (Benchmark Mineral Intelligence): "DLE technology could potentially reshape global lithium markets over the next decade and help US-based companies gain a foothold in the industry. But first they must prove they can overcome technical, financial and strategic challenges."

---

## The shale analogy

Andy Robinson ([[Standard Lithium]] co-founder): "DLE could do for the US lithium industry and economy what fracking did for the US oil industry almost 20 years ago."

Parallels: proven resource base + technology unlock + existing oil/gas infrastructure + energy security narrative + government support. The question is whether cost curves come down as fast as they did in shale.
