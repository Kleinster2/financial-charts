#### April 2026 wheat rally — hedge funds net bullish for first time in ~4 years

Managed money turned net long wheat for the first time since mid-2022, driven by a convergence of supply shocks:

**1. Black Sea winterkill (supply)**
Extreme cold (-15°C to -30°C) across southern [[Ukraine]] and central [[Russia]] in Jan-Feb 2026, with historic snow cover deficit leaving winter wheat exposed. Spring thaw revealed severe damage:
- Russia 2026/27 harvest estimate slashed to **83.8-85.5M mt** (from 90M+ prior cycles)
- Ukraine forecast at **14.5M mt** — an **11-year low** (despite 4% more planted area, 37% rated "poor")
- Sea of Azov ice + port congestion at Novorossiysk further disrupting Black Sea shipments

**2. US Plains drought (supply)**
Persistent dryness threatening US winter wheat yields. USDA Prospective Plantings (Mar 31): US wheat acreage at **43.8M acres** — **lowest since records began in 1919**. Domestic inventories down to 1.30B bushels.

**3. [[Fertilizer]] and fuel shock (cost/supply)**
Iran war shut Strait of Hormuz shipping from ~130 ships/day to single digits. ~30% of globally traded fertilizer transits the Strait. Urea surged from **$450+/ton to $700+/ton** (+50%) in three weeks. Nitrogen fertilizer = 33-44% of wheat operating costs. No strategic reserves for fertilizer exist (unlike oil).

Farmers responding by shifting acreage from nitrogen-intensive crops (corn, wheat) toward soybeans — USDA shows corn plantings down 3.5M acres, soy up 3.5M acres. But wheat planted area was already at record lows before the war.

**Price path:** SRW wheat hit **$6.16/bu** (Mar 31) — highest since Jun 2024. Analysts warn sustained disruption could push toward **$8.00+** levels that triggered global food inflation alarms in 2022. The spring "green-up" phase (April) will reveal true winterkill extent.

**Comparison to 2022:** That rally was Russia-Ukraine war (direct military disruption of Black Sea exports). This time: climate damage + energy-driven input cost shock + logistics disruption — a more multi-layered supply squeeze.

**Food security risk:** Import-dependent regions (North Africa, Middle East) face 2010-style price spike risk. Historical precedent: Russia's 2010 heatwave and export ban triggered civil unrest.
