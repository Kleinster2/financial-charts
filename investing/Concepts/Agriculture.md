#concept #commodities #agriculture

**Agriculture commodities** — Grains (corn, wheat, soybeans), softs (sugar, coffee, cocoa), and livestock. Weather-sensitive, policy-driven, and essential. Primary vehicles: DBA (Invesco DB Agriculture Fund), individual ETFs (CORN, WEAT, SOYB).

## Grains overview

### Corn

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| US production | ~15B bushels/year |
| Key uses | Feed (40%), ethanol (35%), exports |
| 2026 outlook | Stable, $4.10-$4.20/bu |
| Key factor | Global feed competition from wheat |

### Soybeans

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| US production | ~4.5B bushels/year |
| Key uses | Crush (meal + oil), exports |
| 2026 outlook | Supported by biofuel demand |
| Key factor | >50% of US soy oil to biofuels |

China remains dominant export buyer — USDA reported 706K MT single-day sale to China (Jan 2026).

### Wheat

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| US production | ~1.8B bushels/year |
| Key uses | Food, feed |
| 2026 outlook | ~$5.10/bu avg |
| Key factor | Competition from Russia, Canada, Argentina |

## Key drivers

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Weather** | Droughts, floods = supply shocks |
| **China demand** | Largest soy importer |
| **US dollar** | Strong USD hurts US exports |
| **Biofuel mandates** | Corn ethanol, soy biodiesel |
| **Trade policy** | Tariffs, export restrictions |
| **Input costs** | Fertilizer, fuel, labor |

---

## Farm economics (2026)

US farmers facing 10-year high gap between costs and prices received:
- Operating costs rising (fertilizer, fuel, labor)
- Commodity prices range-bound
- Margins compressed
