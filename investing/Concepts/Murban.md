**Murban** — Abu Dhabi's flagship crude oil grade and the benchmark for Middle East physical oil pricing. Traded on the Intercontinental Exchange ([[ICE]]) Futures Abu Dhabi (IFAD) since March 2021. Light, sweet crude (API ~39°, sulfur ~0.8%).

## Why Murban matters

Murban and Dubai are the physical benchmarks for crude flowing east — the prices that Asian refiners actually pay for Gulf barrels. When the [[Strait of Hormuz]] is disrupted, the Brent-Murban spread blows out because Murban reflects physical tightness in the Gulf while Brent reflects global futures sentiment.

### March 2026 pricing

During [[Operation Epic Fury]], Murban/Dubai surged to **>$103/bbl** — hitting triple digits while [[Brent]] remained at ~$92. The $11+ premium over Brent reflects the physical market reality: Gulf crude is scarce, trapped behind a closed Strait, and the barrels that do trade command massive premiums.
