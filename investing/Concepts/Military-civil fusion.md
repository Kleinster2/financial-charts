# Military-civil fusion

[[China|Chinese]] state doctrine ("MCF") that systematically erodes the boundary between civilian and military technology development, with the goal of producing military capability through the commercial technology sector. Formalised under [[Xi Jinping]] and embedded in 14th/15th Five-Year Plans, MCF relies on dedicated investment vehicles ("national military-civil fusion funds"), university-industry linkages through the [[Seven sons of national defence]], and talent rotation between state institutions (e.g., [[China Academy of Space Technology]]) and ostensibly commercial firms. The MCF architecture is the upstream explanation for cases like [[Earth Eye Co]] / [[Emposat]] ([[Chinese dual-use commercial satellite exports]]): commercial firms doing business that would previously have required direct state participation, with plausible deniability built in by design.

## Mechanism in commercial-space cases

1. Commercial firm is founded by veterans of state space institutions (e.g., [[Richard Zhao]] — 15 years at [[China Academy of Space Technology]] before founding [[Emposat]]).
2. Talent hired from [[Seven sons of national defence]] universities.
3. Capital provided through MCF-linked funds.
4. Firm nominally operates as commercial entity with civilian use cases ("agriculture, ocean monitoring, disaster management").
5. State benefits from dual-use capability without direct state action visible to foreign intelligence.

## Policy lever

MCF is the rationale behind US sanctions targeting Chinese commercial firms that appear civilian but are flagged by intelligence or Congressional reports as MCF-linked. The [[House China Select Committee]] has been a primary source of such flagging in the space sector (including [[Emposat]]). [[Secondary sanctions]] and [[Export controls]] are the main tools.
