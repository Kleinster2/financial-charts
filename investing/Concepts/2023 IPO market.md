# 2023 IPO market

The 2023 IPO market was the first real reopening test after the 2022 freeze. Activity improved, but the window stayed narrow: marquee issuers could price, but they needed conservative floats, anchor demand, profitability narratives, or strategic scarcity.

## Synthesis

2023 did not reopen the broad 2021 market. It reopened a narrow path for credible, scaled issuers. The market could absorb high-profile deals, but the structures were defensive: small floats, anchor investors, cautious valuation bands, and more emphasis on visible profitability or strategic control.

The main vault signal is [[Arm Holdings]]. Arm's September 2023 IPO began as a [[SoftBank]] monetization and smartphone-IP royalty story, then later became one of the strongest positive comps for AI-compute scarcity. That later rerating is why Arm matters to the [[2026 IPO pipeline]]: the public market did not just pay for the IPO; it paid again when the company could be recast as an AI-infrastructure bottleneck.

## Regime read-through

| Indicator | 2023 read | Later-cycle consequence |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Higher activity | Deal count rose from 71 to 108 | Issuers learned the window was open, but not forgiving |
| Concentrated proceeds | A few large deals carried the year | Mega issuers still needed scarcity or scale |
| Small floats | Public supply was rationed | First-day demand became easier to engineer than durable aftermarket returns |
| AI scarcity | [[Arm Holdings]] became the best later positive comp | AI-linked infrastructure could detach from generic IPO base rates |
| [[Consumer]] / platform caution | [[Instacart]], [[Klaviyo]], and [[Birkenstock]] showed mixed reception | Brand familiarity was not enough without valuation discipline |
