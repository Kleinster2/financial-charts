# European Depositary Receipts

A European Depositary Receipt (EDR) is a [[Depositary receipts|depositary receipt]] issued by a European bank and listed on a European exchange (historically Euronext), representing shares of a company traded outside the issuing bank's country and typically denominated in euros. It is the euro-zone counterpart to the ADR and GDR.

## Synthesis

The EDR is the euro-denominated, Europe-listed member of the [[Depositary receipts]] family — the same access-wrapper template aimed at continental European investors. It is the least-used of the major regional formats: the [[Global Depositary Receipts|GDR]] (London / Luxembourg) absorbed most cross-border European DR activity, leaving the EDR a thin, largely historical instrument. It earns a place in the taxonomy as the EUR analogue rather than as a live market.
