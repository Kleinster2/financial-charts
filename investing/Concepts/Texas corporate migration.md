**Texas corporate migration** — Texas emerging as the new Delaware for corporate incorporation. A 2025 state law enhanced business protections, and major companies are redomiciling to take advantage of reduced shareholder litigation risk, friendlier courts, and anti-activist tools.

---

## Why Texas

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **2025 business protection law** | Stock ownership thresholds for shareholder lawsuits, limiting frivolous litigation |
| **Anti-activist provisions** | Companies can raise the bar for shareholder proposals |
| **Judicial familiarity** | Energy, tech, defense companies argue Texas courts understand their business |
| **Political alignment** | Business-friendly state government, no state income tax |
| **Climate lawsuit shield** | Moving away from blue-state AGs who file climate suits |

## Delaware's loss

Delaware has been the dominant state for corporate incorporation for over a century — ~67% of Fortune 500 companies are incorporated there. The advantages: specialized Chancery Court, deep body of corporate case law, business-friendly statutes. But recent shareholder-friendly rulings (Tesla pay package, demand futility standards) have pushed companies to look for alternatives.

Texas is positioning itself as the business-friendly alternative — less shareholder litigation risk, more management-friendly courts, and new statutory protections that explicitly target the corporate governance concerns driving departures from Delaware.

---

## Investment implications

**Governance impact:** Redomiciling to Texas reduces shareholder rights — harder to bring derivative suits, higher ownership thresholds for proposals, management-friendly courts. This is a net negative for activist investors and governance-focused funds, but a net positive for management teams seeking insulation.

**Not just cosmetic:** The 2025 Texas law's ownership thresholds for lawsuits and proposal barriers are substantive — they change the balance of power between shareholders and boards.
