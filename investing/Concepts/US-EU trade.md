## Digital services tax flashpoint (Jun 2026)

The unresolved item inside the [[Turnberry Agreement]] framework is digital taxation. The 15%-cap deal covers goods; it never settled the [[EU]]'s push to tax US tech-platform revenue via [[Digital services tax|digital services taxes]], which the US treats as discriminatory. With the [[OECD]] [[Pillar One]] multilateral fix stalled, the standstill broke down, and on June 26 2026 [[Donald Trump]] threatened a 100% tariff on any country that imposes a DST — explicitly overriding the goods deal due to start July 4. It is the cleanest case of a failed multilateral rule converting straight into bilateral coercion, and the most likely trigger for the [[EU]] Anti-Coercion Instrument below. See [[Digital services tax]].

## EU Anti-Coercion Instrument ("the bazooka")

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Created | 2023 |
| Used | Never (as of Jan 2026) |
| Trigger | Economic coercion by foreign power |
| Scope | Broader than tariffs |

Potential measures if activated:
- Tariffs
- Intellectual property restrictions
- Market access restrictions for US companies (e.g., communications sector)

Why "bazooka": Like ECB's OMT or [[Germany]]'s €500B fiscal fund — a powerful tool held in reserve as deterrent, designed to never be fired but credible enough to change behavior.

Status: Macron has suggested activating it in response to Greenland/tariff pressure.

## WTO implications

US actions have paralyzed WTO dispute settlement system:
- Appellate Body non-functional (US blocks judge appointments)
- Bilateral deals like Turnberry operate outside WTO framework
- Rules-based trading system under strain
