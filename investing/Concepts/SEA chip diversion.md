Chips flowing from [[Taiwan]] through [[Southeast Asia]] to [[China]] — representing ~15-20% of NVIDIA's GPU sales. **Current tailwind, future risk.**

---

## The dynamic

NVIDIA publicly claims "zero [[China]] forecast" while chips reach [[China]] through SEA. This is extra revenue today, but carries embedded risks.

**Jensen quotes (Dec 2025 Bloomberg):**
- "Our forecast for [[China]] is zero. All of our guidance starts at zero."
- "The Chinese market is very large this year. My guess is probably about $50 billion."
- "We've repeatedly tested and sampled data centers around the world and found no diversion."

**Key insight (Kakashiii analysis):**
- Jensen's "$50B guess" isn't a guess — it's based on actual gray market flows
- Allowing legal [[China]] sales wouldn't *increase* NVIDIA sales — just convert gray to legal
- "Zero forecast" creates upside surprise optics when revenue is already baked in

## Legal enforcement

### SMCI co-founder indictment (Mar 19, 2026)

The largest export control prosecution yet. Three [[Super Micro Computer]] affiliates charged with diverting $2.5B in [[NVIDIA]] AI servers to [[China]] through an unnamed SEA company (2024-2025):

| Defendant | Role | Status |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Yih-Shyan "Wally" Liaw | SMCI co-founder, board member, SVP | Arrested |
| Ruei-Tsang "Steven" Chang | SMCI Taiwan general manager | Fugitive |
| Ting-Wei "Willy" Sun | Third-party contractor | Arrested |

The scheme matches the exact pattern the SEA import data suggested: US assembly → Taiwan staging → SEA company (fake end-user) → unmarked reboxing → China. DOJ has surveillance video of workers using hair dryers to swap serial numbers onto thousands of dummy servers. Liaw pushed for [[Blackwell]] B200 allocation and accelerated shipments ahead of new export rules.

$510M diverted in a three-week sprint (Apr-May 2025). Total: $2.5B.

This confirms the SEA diversion thesis is not speculation — it's a documented, industrial-scale pipeline operating through a major US-listed server maker.

**Prior action (Jan 2026):**
- US District Court, Northern District of California
- Charged individuals with NVIDIA GPU smuggling from US
- Smaller scale than SMCI case

## Implications

**For [[NVIDIA]]:**
- **Today:** Gray market = tailwind, ~15-20% extra revenue
- **Risk:** Legal exposure if found complicit, investor trust if becomes scandal
- **Upside:** If export controls relax, gray converts to clean with same revenue
- **Downside:** If enforcement succeeds, could lose meaningful revenue

**For [[Export controls]]:**
- Policy may be less effective than assumed
- SEA = major leakage route
- Enforcement playing catch-up
- Chinese DC buildout in SEA = legal workaround

**For [[Southeast Asia tech race]]:**
- Chinese DC buildout = real infrastructure investment
- SEA countries benefit either way (investment or transit)
- Positioned as "neutral ground" for AI compute
- Creates geopolitical complexity for [[ASEAN]]
