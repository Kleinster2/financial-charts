# Russell 2000

US small-cap equity index — the smallest 2,000 companies in the Russell 3000. Operated by FTSE Russell (a subsidiary of [[LSE Group|London Stock Exchange Group]]). Tracked by [[IWM]] (iShares Russell 2000 ETF) for cash investors and the [[Russell 2000 futures (RTY)|RTY futures]] contract on [[CME Group|CME]] for institutions.

The vault uses Russell 2000 as the **small-cap rate-beta proxy**. Small caps carry higher floating-rate debt loads, are more sensitive to credit conditions, and lack the international revenue diversification of the [[S&P 500]] — so they tend to react more violently to repricing of the rate path, dollar strength, and recession risk. When the Russell 2000 moves materially more than the S&P 500 in the same direction, the move is usually rate-channel or recession-channel rather than earnings-driven.
