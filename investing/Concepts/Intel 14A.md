# Intel 14A

[[Intel]]'s 1.4nm-class logic node — the successor to [[Intel 18A]] and Intel's first to use High-NA EUV lithography ([[High-NA EUV]]). 14A targets roughly 15-20% better performance-per-watt than 18A, with risk production around 2027 and volume production no earlier than ~2028-29. Where 18A is largely an internal-products-plus-anchor-customer node, 14A is the node Intel is positioning as its external-foundry vehicle — CEO [[Lip-Bu Tan]] has said the company is "going big time into 14A."

14A is where the [[Intel Foundry Services]] external thesis is meant to scale. Two prospective 14A customers reportedly took early PDK access with decisions due in H2 2026; [[Apple]]'s future smartphone-class chips are reported to target 14A (its M7 uses [[Intel 18A|18A-P]] first), and [[Elon Musk]]'s [[TERAFAB]] is a named candidate. The bet: 18A rebuilds customer trust, 14A converts it into a high-volume merchant foundry business on High-NA economics. See [[Intel 18A]] for the current node and the foundry context.

*Chart not applicable — a process node has no standalone price series; Intel's foundry re-rating is charted in [[Intel 18A]] and tracked via the [[Intel securities note]].*
