# 2022 IPO market

The 2022 IPO market was the hard reset after the 2021 boom. [[Inflation]], rate hikes, the [[Ukraine war]], public-growth multiple compression, and recession risk shut the IPO window for almost everyone except issuers with special strategic reasons to move.

## Synthesis

2022 turned the IPO market from a price-discovery venue into a survival filter. The problem was not simply that companies could not get out. It was that public comps had moved so far down that going public would have crystallized valuation damage for late-stage private holders, employees, and crossover investors.

That reset is why the [[2026 IPO pipeline]] still carries a backlog story. The companies that skipped 2022 did not disappear; many kept raising privately, cutting burn, or waiting for a cleaner market. The 2026 supply question is partly a delayed consequence of the 2022 shutdown.

## Regime read-through

| Indicator | 2022 read | Later-cycle consequence |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Deal count collapse | IPOs fell from 397 to 71 | Private-company backlog began building |
| Proceeds collapse | Fundraising fell from $142.4B to $7.7B | Scale issuers avoided public marks unless forced |
| VC drought | VC-backed deals fell to 14 | Late-stage venture exits became concentrated in secondaries and structured private rounds |
| SPAC reset | Blank-check issuance fell 86% year over year | Public investors became more skeptical of projection-led listings |
| Index drawdown | IPO Index underperformed the S&P 500 by almost 39 points | Aftermarket discipline became the main reopening condition |
