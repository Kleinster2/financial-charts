# Short interest

#concept #markets #signals

**Short interest** — Percentage of shares sold short as a fraction of free float. Contrarian indicator: low short interest after a parabolic run can signal crowded longs with no one left to buy; high short interest can signal skepticism or set up squeezes.

---

## What short interest tells you

| Level | [[Signal]] |
|-------|--------|
| **High** (>5-10%) | Bearish conviction, potential squeeze setup |
| **Rising** | Growing skepticism, hedging activity |
| **Low** (<1%) | Consensus bullish, bears capitulated |
| **Falling after run-up** | Shorts covering, capitulation |

---

## Interpretation framework

### Low short interest + parabolic run = caution signal

When a stock 3-4x's and short interest *falls*, it suggests:
- Bears have given up
- Thesis is consensus (everyone agrees)
- Crowded long positioning
- **Who's left to buy?**

Not necessarily bearish — could mean fundamentals are bulletproof. But worth monitoring.

### High short interest = potential energy

High short interest creates:
- Squeeze risk (forced buying on rallies)
- Natural buying pressure if thesis plays out
- Contrarian opportunity if shorts are wrong

## TSMC case study (Jan 2026)

See [[TSMC]] for full quarterly history. Summary:

| Period | SI % | Stock | Context |
|--------|------|-------|---------|
| 2010-11 | **1.5-1.7%** | NT$60-80 | Historical peak |
| 2023 avg | 0.3-0.5% | NT$400 | Pre-AI baseline |
| **Q1 2026** | **0.5%** | NT$1,600+ | ATH, 4x run |

**The puzzle:** Stock 4x'd, short interest stayed flat/low.

**Interpretation:**
- AI monopoly thesis is consensus — no one wants to fight it
- [[Taiwan]] geopolitical risk NOT being hedged via shorting (surprising)
- Could be crowded, or could be that fundamental case is bulletproof

**Watch for:** Rising short interest = emerging skepticism on AI demand or [[Taiwan]] risk.

## April 2026 short squeeze — Goldman Sachs PB data

The most documented short squeeze since [[GameStop]] (2021), triggered by [[Trump II|Trump]]'s ceasefire announcement with [[Iran]] on April 8, 2026.

### Pre-squeeze positioning

[[Goldman Sachs]] prime brokerage data revealed extreme short-side crowding ahead of the ceasefire:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Selling pace | Fastest in 13 years |
| Short:long ratio | **7.6 to 1** |
| ETF short concentration | 76% of shorts in index/ETF products |
| US-listed ETF shorts | +17.2% |
| Gross leverage | **2.9x books** (highest in 5 years) |
| Single-stock de-risking | Fastest pace in 2+ years |

### Squeeze mechanics

- GS most-shorted basket surged **7.1% in a single session** when covering began
- Hedge funds closed shorts at fastest pace since March 2020 (Bloomberg, April 8)
- Funds bought tech, media, telecom for first time in 4 months (covering-driven, not conviction)
- Selling continued in cyclicals: industrials, materials, financials

### Why this matters structurally

This was not a retail-driven meme squeeze (à la [[GameStop]]). This was institutional: 76% of shorts were in ETFs and indices, not single stocks. The 7.6:1 short-to-long ratio reflected macro fear (recession, [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis|Hormuz]] supply shock, tariff drag), not company-specific bearishness.

Critical nuance: gross leverage at 2.9x means the system is fragile in **both directions**. The same leverage that amplified the squeeze amplifies the next shock. If the ceasefire breaks, the violent reversal could be equally severe — and faster, because the newly closed shorts won't have had time to rebuild.

*Source: [[Goldman Sachs]] PB data, Bloomberg (April 8, 2026), ainvest.com, Nicholas Crown (@nicholas_crown)*

| Long thesis + high short interest | Potential squeeze adds upside |
| Short thesis + low short interest | Less crowded, but also less catalyst |
| Short thesis + high short interest | Crowded short, squeeze risk |
