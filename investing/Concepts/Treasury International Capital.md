Treasury International Capital (TIC) — US Treasury Department reporting system that tracks cross-border securities transactions and holdings. Published monthly and quarterly. The primary source for measuring foreign ownership of US assets, including Treasury securities.

---

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Publisher | US Treasury Department |
| Frequency | Monthly (flows), quarterly (holdings) |
| Coverage | Cross-border purchases/sales of securities |
| Key metric | Foreign holdings of US Treasuries |

## The $1.4T undercounting problem

[[Federal Reserve|NY Fed]] research (2026) found that TIC data systematically undercounts cross-border hedge fund Treasury flows by ~$1.4T. Most leveraged funds are domiciled in the [[Cayman Islands]] for tax and regulatory reasons, but TIC reporting fails to capture the full scale of their positions.

Correcting for this, the Cayman Islands — not [[China]] or [[Japan]] — is the largest foreign holder of US Treasuries. Between 2022 and 2024, hedge funds absorbed 37% of net Treasury notes/bonds issuance, nearly matching all other foreign investors combined (OFR data).

The implication: the conventional narrative of declining foreign Treasury demand (ownership below 30%, down from 46% in 2008) is misleading. Demand has shifted from price-insensitive central banks to price-sensitive leveraged funds. The total hasn't fallen as much as reported — but the composition has become more fragile.

*Source: NY Fed research, FT (Gillian Tett, Apr 3 2026)*
