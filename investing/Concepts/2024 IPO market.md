# 2024 IPO market

The 2024 IPO market was a better reopening, but still not a boom. Issuance rose, larger deals performed well, and several technology names gave investors a reason to revisit the IPO window. The market still did not absorb the kind of trophy-private-company supply implied by the [[2026 IPO pipeline]].

## Synthesis

2024 proved that the IPO market could function again. It did not prove that every backlog issuer should rush out. Renaissance Capital framed the year as solid but underwhelming: proceeds were up more than 50% from 2023, yet activity was still slow by historical standards, and many private technology companies stayed private because the public-private valuation gap had not fully closed.

The vault signal is that 2024 created a narrow set of useful comps for 2026. [[Astera Labs]] showed that an AI-infrastructure bottleneck can produce extraordinary aftermarket performance. [[Reddit]] showed that a familiar [[Consumer|consumer]]-platform asset could work if it reached profitability and gave investors a credible data-licensing / AI-angle overlay. [[Rubrik]] and [[Tempus]] added enterprise-data and health-data versions of the same question: can public markets pay for infrastructure-like data access rather than just generic software growth?
