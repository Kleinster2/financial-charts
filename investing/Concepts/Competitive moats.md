#concept #investing #strategy #moat

**Competitive moats** — Sustainable competitive advantages. Buffett framework. Source of pricing power and returns.

---

## Moat types

| Moat | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Network effects** | Value grows with users | Visa, [[Meta]] |
| **Switching costs** | Expensive to leave | [[Salesforce]], [[SAP]] |
| **Intangibles** | Brands, patents, licenses | [[Coca-Cola]], [[Pfizer]] |
| **Cost advantage** | Lower costs than rivals | Costco, GEICO |
| **Efficient scale** | Natural monopoly dynamics | Utilities, exchanges |

---

## Network effects

| Type | Strength |
|------|----------|
| Direct | Strong (phone network) |
| Indirect | Moderate (platform + apps) |
| Two-sided | Variable (marketplaces) |
| Local | Weaker (rideshare) |

See [[Network effects]] for detail.

## Switching costs

| Type | Example |

## Efficient scale

| Characteristic | Example |
|----------------|---------|
| Limited market size | Regional utilities |
| High fixed costs | Exchanges, rail |
| Regulatory barriers | [[Telecom]] |

---

## Moat durability

| Moat | Durability |
|------|------------|
| Network effects | High (if strong) |
| Switching costs | Medium-high |
| Brands | Medium (requires reinvestment) |
| Cost advantage | Medium (can be competed away) |
| Patents | Low (expire) |

---

## Moat erosion signals

| [[Signal]] | Concern |
|--------|---------|
| Market share loss | Competition winning |
| Pricing pressure | Moat weakening |
| Customer churn rising | Switching costs falling |
| R&D decline | Innovation gap |
| Disruption | Technology shift |

---

## Moat assessment framework

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Why do customers stay? | Identify switching costs |
| Can competitors replicate? | Durability check |
| Is the moat widening? | Trajectory |
| What's the disruption risk? | Technology threats |
