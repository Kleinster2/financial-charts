[[Homebuilders]] — US public homebuilder cohort. The cyclical-coincident read on US new-construction volume, mortgage-rate sensitivity, and housing-cycle inflection. The sector includes a clear top-10 by volume / market cap, with structural divergence between entry-level / first-time-buyer focused builders and luxury / move-up segments.

The investing-vault relevance is dual: (1) homebuilder volume is the cleanest leading indicator on consumer-cyclical names like [[Whirlpool]] / [[Lowe's]] / [[Home Depot]] / [[Sherwin-Williams]] / [[Floor & Decor]]; (2) the segment-level dispersion (entry-level vs luxury) is itself a high-frequency consumer-pulse signal that frames the [[2026 recession risk]] thread.

## Why homebuilders matter

The cohort is the cleanest large-cap-listed read on three macro variables:

1. *Mortgage rates / 30-year fixed.* Entry-level builders ([[D.R. Horton]], [[KB Home]], [[Meritage Homes]], [[LGI Homes]]) carry the highest beta to mortgage-rate moves because their customer base is the most rate-sensitive. Luxury ([[Toll Brothers]]) carries lowest rate beta because cash buyers + jumbo financing + price points where the mortgage payment is a smaller share of disposable income.
2. *Buy-down rate-buying capacity.* Through 2024-2026 rate-shock cycle, builders have used rate-buy-downs as a primary demand-stimulus tool — paying mortgage points to lower a buyer's effective rate. The capacity to keep buying rates depends on order-book gross margin, which makes the buy-down lever asymmetric: works well at high gross margin, breaks down as gross margin compresses.
3. *Inventory turn.* Spec-home inventory (built-and-unsold) carries higher cost per day than land-banked-but-unbuilt. The homebuilder cohort's spec-vs-presale ratio is a real-time consumer-pulse signal — rising spec ratios = builders absorbing demand softness rather than passing it through.

## May 2026 read

The Q1 2026 [[Whirlpool]] guide cut from FY26 GAAP EPS ~$6.25 → $2.45-2.95 is the cleanest single-name signal that the housing recovery anticipated for H1 2026 has not arrived. The downstream test for the homebuilder cohort:
- Did Q1 2026 order-book volumes hold up vs prior Q4 2025? ([[D.R. Horton]] / [[Lennar]] / [[NVR]] / [[PulteGroup]] reports cluster mid-April to mid-May)
- How aggressive are the buy-down incentives running, and does that compress gross margins materially?
- What's the spec-inventory ratio doing — rising or holding?

The cleanest cross-name framing: homebuilders' Q1 commentary will tell whether the [[Whirlpool]] guide cut is a Whirlpool-specific operational issue or a sector-wide housing-cycle signal. If the homebuilder cohort confirms decelerating volume + rising buy-down incentives + rising spec ratios, it strengthens the [[2026 recession risk]] consumer-side framing materially.
