## 2025 retrospective (McKinsey Global Institute)

Source: [[McKinsey]] Global Institute, *Geopolitics and the geometry of global trade: 2026 update* (preliminary evaluation of 2025), cited in [[Martin Wolf]] (FT, Apr 1 2026).

World trade in goods grew faster than the world economy in 2025 despite [[Trump II]] tariffs. Five facets:

1. [[US-China trade|US and Chinese exports]] both reached new highs. Direction of trade shifted from geopolitically distant partners (US-China) more than from geographically distant ones. [[EU]] lost market share in [[China|Chinese]] markets. [[India]] stood out for increased geographical distance of trade (smartphone shipments to US).
2. AI-related shipments became the most powerful engine of goods trade. [[Semiconductors|Semiconductor]] and data center equipment shipments rose 40% between 2024 and 2025. AI-related exports accounted for one-third of global trade growth. Asian hubs — [[Taiwan]], [[South Korea]], [[ASEAN|parts of SE Asia]] — supplied the world, particularly the US. [[Export controls]] restricted [[China|China's]] AI-related trade growth to 16%. McKinsey expects AI-related capacity to continue driving world trade in 2026.
3. [[China]] expanded its role as a "factory to the factories." While direct exports to the US fell, [[China]] increased exports of machinery and inputs to neighbors and emerging economies. Intermediate and capital goods exports rose $223B in 2025, more than offsetting a $130B reduction in exports to the US. Deepened role as supplier of inputs rather than exporter of final goods.
4. Direct [[US-China trade]] fell ~30% in 2025. The US replaced about two-thirds of lost Chinese imports from other exporters. Chinese consumer goods exporters (EVs, toys) cut prices by an average of 8% to find new buyers. [[ASEAN]] exports thrived. [[EU]] firms faced a double squeeze from diverted Chinese exports and higher US tariffs.
5. Trump's bark was worse than his bite — he did not do all he threatened. No retaliation cycle against the US. No imitation of US repudiation of WTO commitments. [[Richard Baldwin]] (IMD Lausanne): the US at 14% of world goods imports does not matter all that much. US + [[China]] together = 25% of world trade. The rest of the world kept trading because it depends on it.

Wolf's editorial: the US is "no longer a credible world leader" and provides "an unpredictable wrecking ball" rather than reliable hegemony. [[China]] is more predictable but unable to eliminate reliance on huge trade/current account surpluses. The 2026 question is whether [[Iran conflict oil price timeline|the Iran war]] and Gulf closure will cause more damage than the 2025 tariff war. Wolf suspects Trump will claim victory and end it.
