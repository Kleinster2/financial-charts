The 1973 oil crisis began when OAPEC members (led by [[Saudi Arabia]]) proclaimed an oil embargo against nations supporting [[Israel]] in the Yom Kippur War. Crude prices quadrupled from ~$3 to ~$12/bbl. Established oil supply disruption as a geopolitical weapon and reshaped Western energy policy. Frequently invoked as the benchmark for severe supply shocks — [[Paul Sankey]] compared the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] to 1973 as "possibly" comparable.
