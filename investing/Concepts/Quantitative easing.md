# Quantitative easing

Unconventional monetary policy in which a central bank buys long-duration securities (Treasuries, agency MBS) to expand its balance sheet, push down long rates, and increase bank reserves. The [[Federal Reserve]] used QE in four major rounds: QE1 (2008-10), QE2 (2010-11), QE3 (2012-14), and QE4/pandemic QE (2020-22). The 2020-22 round expanded the Fed balance sheet from ~$4T to ~$9T, creating the reserve stockpile that [[Fed balance sheet QT|quantitative tightening]] has been drawing down since mid-2022.

See [[Monetary policy primer]] for broader framework and [[Fed balance sheet QT]] for the current unwind.
