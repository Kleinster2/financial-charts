India Energy — structural energy profile of the world's third-largest energy consumer, defined by extreme import dependence and household-level vulnerability to oil shocks.

## Refining

India is one of the world's largest refining hubs, processing imported crude into exportable products — diesel, jet fuel, and petrochemicals shipped across Asia and Europe.

| Refinery | Operator | Capacity |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Jamnagar (Gujarat) | [[Reliance Industries]] | 1.24M bpd (world's largest) |
| Panipat (Haryana) | [[Indian Oil]] | ~0.3M bpd |
| Koyali (Gujarat) | [[Indian Oil]] | ~0.3M bpd |
| Mangalore | MRPL ([[ONGC]]) | ~0.3M bpd |
| Vizag | [[HPCL]] | ~0.2M bpd |

India's refined product exports totaled $70.2B (7.8% of global), making refining a major foreign exchange earner. The business model — import cheap crude, export high-value products — depends on access to discounted feedstock, which the [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran conflict]] disrupted.

## Crude supply — sources and sanctions arbitrage

India's crude sourcing shifted dramatically during 2024-2026 as sanctions enforcement and geopolitics reshaped flows.

### Pre-conflict

| Supplier | Share | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| [[Iraq]] | ~25% | Largest single supplier |
| [[Saudi Arabia]] | ~15% | Aramco contracts |
| [[Russia]] | ~15% (down from peak >2M bpd mid-2024) | US pressure reduced to 1.06M bpd by Feb 2026 |
| [[UAE]] | ~10% | Murban grade |

### Iran conflict (Feb 28 onward)

The [[Strait of Hormuz]] closure cut off Iraq, Saudi (pipeline-constrained), and UAE supply simultaneously. Indian refiners scrambled:

- [[Russia]]n crude: India purchased ~30M barrels immediately after a US waiver (mid-March). [[Indian Oil]] took ~10M bbl, [[Reliance Industries]] at least as much. Russian grades offered at $2-8/bbl premium to Brent — a reversal from the prior discount. At least seven tankers diverted mid-voyage from [[China]] to India.
- [[Iran]]ian crude: [[Reliance Industries]] purchased 5M barrels from [[NIOC]] (March 24) — India's first Iranian crude buy since May 2019. Offered at $6-8/bbl premium to Brent. Payment sought in USD within 7 days; some accepting INR.

US Ambassador Sergio Gor called India "a great partner in maintaining stable oil prices" (March 19). See [[Iran conflict sanctions paradox]] for the strategic implications.

## LPG crisis (March 2026)

India takes 90% of LPG imports from the Middle East. The Hormuz closure created an acute shortage — government invoked the Essential Commodities Act, suspended commercial LPG supply, and prioritized household cooking gas.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Black market LPG price | 2-3x normal (~₹1,900/$20) in New Delhi |
| Restaurant LPG dependency | 80% of restaurants use LPG (NRAI) |
| Storage buffer | Most restaurants carry only days of supply |
| Alternative supply transit time | US LPG: 40+ days to arrive |

The restaurant sector is the visible casualty. Sree Annapoorna (58-year-old chain, 15 banquet halls, Coimbatore) cut its menu and turned away wedding clients on an auspicious Hindu date (Mar 15). CEO Jegan Damodarasamy (Bloomberg, Mar 15): *"We've been in business for about 58 years. This is the first time we've done that."* ~1M delivery workers ([[Swiggy]]/[[Zomato]]) affected by restaurant closures. [[Infosys]] (300,000+ employees) trimmed cafeteria menus, asked staff to bring food from home.

LPG-laden trucks becoming theft targets. Smugglers exploiting price differentials across borders (from [[Malaysia]], [[Bangladesh]]).

See [[Iran conflict economic disruption]] for the full Hormuz crisis and [[Nitrogen Trap]] for the fertilizer dimension.

## Fertilizer — ammonia dependency

India's 32 ammonia plants were ordered to operate at 70% of gas allocation during the conflict. India formally asked [[China]] for urea (March 12). The fertilizer supply chain is a second-order energy vulnerability — natural gas is 60-80% of nitrogen fertilizer production costs.

See [[Nitrogen Trap]] and [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade]].

## Energy diplomacy

Foreign Minister [[S. Jaishankar]] told parliament (Mar 8, 2026) that the region was "key to India's energy security" and that "supply chain disruptions and a climate of instability are serious issues."

India's energy diplomacy during the Iran conflict revealed a pragmatic stance: accepting US waivers to purchase Russian and Iranian crude while publicly supporting "peace and diplomacy." The Brownsville refinery project — [[Reliance Industries]] anchoring the first new US refinery in ~50 years — serves as a bilateral goodwill gesture even as India buys sanctioned crude.
