#concept #commodities #critical

Yttrium is a rare earth element used in phosphors, ceramics, alloys, superconductors, and specialized defense / electronics applications. In the 2025-2026 rare earth control cycle it matters because [[China]] added yttrium to the restricted export basket alongside heavier magnet-critical elements such as [[Dysprosium]] and [[Terbium]].

## Synthesis

Yttrium is not the tightest magnet bottleneck, but it is part of the same coercive toolkit. Its strategic value comes from being low-volume, specialized, and hard to substitute quickly in downstream electronics and defense supply chains. That makes [[Yttrium|yttrium oxide]] useful to track alongside [[Gallium]], [[Dysprosium]], and [[Terbium]] when [[China]] uses licensing, customs delays, or destination-specific restrictions to pressure import-dependent countries.
