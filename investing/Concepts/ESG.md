## 2026 framing shift — ESG agenda displaced by energy-security agenda

Per FT, May 3 2026 (Emily Herbert and Ramsay Hodgson), asset managers explicitly named the 2026 renewable equity rally as a *displacement* of the ESG framing rather than its continuation. [[Nordea Asset Management]] CIO Kasper Elmgreen: "The agenda around clean tech in the past was driven by an [environmental, social and governance] agenda and a political will to tackle climate change. Now, in Europe, it is about sovereignty and autonomy."

The structural read: ESG attracted capital while climate-policy was the dominant political framing; the 2022 [[Ukraine war|Russia-Ukraine war]] + 2026 [[2026 Iran conflict market impact|Iran war]] shifted European political framing to autonomy and US framing to AI-power provisioning. Both new framings fund the same renewable capex categories that ESG previously funded, but without ESG's political vulnerability ("woke capitalism" backlash) and without ESG's regulatory burden.

The flows are not an ESG resurgence — they are confirmation that the *capital flow* survived even as the *political framing* moved on. The [[Inflation Reduction Act]] subsidy removal under Trump did not break the flows, which is the cleanest possible test. See [[Energy security as investment thesis]] for the full framework.
