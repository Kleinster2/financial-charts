#concept #regulation #banking

**G-SIB** — Global Systemically Important Banks. Designation by the [[Financial Stability Board]] for banks whose failure would pose systemic risk. Subject to additional capital surcharges above [[Basel III]] minimums.

---

## Quick stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| US G-SIBs | 8 |
| Surcharge range | 1.0-3.5% additional CET1 |
| Determined by | Size, interconnectedness, substitutability, complexity, cross-jurisdictional activity |

US G-SIBs: [[JPMorgan]], [[Bank of America]], [[Citigroup]], [[Goldman Sachs]], [[Morgan Stanley]], [[Wells Fargo]], [[Bank of New York Mellon]], [[State Street]].

---

## Mar 2026 reform

[[Michelle Bowman]] announced plans to reduce G-SIB surcharges by lowering the short-term funding risk component and adjusting for inflation/growth to prevent automatic increases as balance sheets grow. Net effect: "modest decrease in the surcharges" for all eight US G-SIBs. See [[Basel III]]. Source: FT (Mar 12, 2026)
