# Dividend growth

An equity strategy that selects companies with long, unbroken records of raising their dividends — a proxy for durable free cash flow, balance-sheet discipline, and a management commitment to shareholders, rather than for high current yield. The canonical screen is the [[S&P 500]] Dividend Aristocrats (25+ consecutive years of increases), tracked by [[NOBL]].

As a factor it overlaps quality and low-volatility: dividend growers skew to consumer staples, industrials, and healthcare, lag in momentum / tech-led rallies, and cushion in drawdowns. In portfolio terms it is the liquid, growing-income answer to property-fund (FII) income — durable, rising payouts from stable companies versus single-asset rental yield.
