#concept #space #policy

**Commercial Crew Program (CCP)** — [[NASA]] program awarding fixed-price contracts to commercial providers for crew transport to and from the [[ISS]]. Established 2014 with two awards: [[SpaceX]] ($2.6B for [[Crew Dragon]]) and [[Boeing]] ($4.2B for [[Boeing Starliner|Starliner]]). The structural template for [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] (CLD) and [[CLPS]] (Commercial Lunar Payload Services) — NASA shifts from cost-plus prime contracts to fixed-price commercial purchases, accepting some failures in exchange for cost reduction and supplier diversity.

## Why CCP matters

The structural read in Q1 2026: the program was designed for two providers, but only one ([[Crew Dragon]]) is operational and rotating crew, while [[Boeing Starliner]] is now under [[Type A mishap]] regulatory pressure that materially extends certification. The two-provider redundancy concept the program was built on is, in effect, single-provider in practice.

This matters beyond CCP itself. The success of [[Crew Dragon]] is what made NASA's broader commercialization framework — [[CLPS]] (lunar payloads), [[Commercial LEO Destinations]] (post-ISS stations) — credible to industry investors. The framework is the institutional memory that ~$4B private capital across CLD candidates depends on.
