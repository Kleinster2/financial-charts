# Cannabis rescheduling

The US federal-policy catalyst that drives the entire [[Cannabis]] equity complex. Cannabis is a Schedule I controlled substance under the federal Controlled Substances Act — the most restrictive tier, which is why [[Green Thumb Industries|US MSOs]] cannot list on US exchanges, face the punitive [[280E]] tax, and are shut out of normal banking. Two reform tracks move the stocks: administrative rescheduling to Schedule III (which the DEA began reviewing in 2024, and which would lift the [[280E]] burden), and legislative banking access (the SAFE/SAFER Banking Act). Because both are US federal events, they move US MSOs and Canadian LPs together — the Canadian names are held as call options on US market access — which is why the [[Cannabis]] cohort trades as one ETF-replicable beta rather than splitting by jurisdiction.

*This is a federal-policy concept with no price series of its own — no price data available to chart — so its market expression is the [[Cannabis]] cohort and its performance chart.*

## Why it matters

A Schedule III reclassification would end the application of [[280E]] to plant-touching cannabis businesses, restoring normal deductibility and materially improving MSO cash flow without changing federal legality. Banking reform would lower capital costs and broaden the investor base. The mere prospect of either has repeatedly produced sector-wide spikes (the mid-2024 DEA-review optimism is visible across every name in the [[Cannabis]] performance chart), followed by fades as timelines slip. This shared, binary, US-federal catalyst is the reason cannabis is a single co-moving factor: see [[Vault cluster taxonomy]] on how a shared catalyst re-fuses jurisdictions that the business model would otherwise separate.
