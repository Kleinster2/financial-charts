## Consulting-channel validation

The Frontier strategy depends on services partners because scaled enterprise agents are workflow projects, not simple software installs. Reuters' coverage of [[Capgemini]]'s May 27, 2026 Capital Markets Day made that explicit: [[OpenAI]] VP of global business Nate Harbacek told the event that companies are moving from individual AI use to enterprise deployment at scale, where entire workflows get re-architected. Capgemini described itself as a founding member of OpenAI's Frontier Alliance and positioned the relationship as a way to sell agentic-AI transformation across business functions.

That makes Frontier a distribution architecture as much as a product. OpenAI supplies the agent platform; consulting partners supply process mapping, controls, compliance, cloud integration, and change management. The risk is margin leakage to services firms, but the benefit is enterprise reach into regulated and legacy environments where OpenAI cannot simply self-serve adoption.

*Source: [Reuters via Investing.com, May 27 2026](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/capgemini-says-ai-widens-client-spending-pool-4712824).*
