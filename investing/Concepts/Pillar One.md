# Pillar One

Pillar One is the market-country leg of the [[OECD]]/G20 two-pillar international tax deal: it would reallocate a slice of the largest multinationals' taxing rights to the jurisdictions where their users and customers sit, regardless of physical presence. It was designed as the multilateral replacement for unilateral [[Digital services tax|digital services taxes]] — countries would repeal DSTs in exchange for the reallocation. (Pillar Two, the better-known companion, is the 15% global minimum corporate tax.)

Pillar One has not fully entered force: the multilateral convention stalled, and the US Senate never ratified it. That failure is what reopened the DST fight — without the deal, the standstill that froze unilateral digital taxes lost its rationale, and the US-EU [[Digital services tax|DST]] confrontation reignited (see the June 2026 [[Donald Trump|Trump]] 100% tariff threat).
