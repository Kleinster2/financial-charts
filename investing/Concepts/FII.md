# FII

A Fundo de Investimento Imobiliário is a listed Brazilian real-estate fund — the local REIT. It pays monthly, income-tax-exempt distributions to individual holders and trades on B3 under `*11` tickers; the IFIX is the benchmark index.

## Three families

| Family | What it holds | Tracked examples |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| Tijolo (brick) | Physical property — logistics, malls, offices | [[HGLG11]], [[XPLG11]], [[BTLG11]] (logistics); [[XPML11]] (malls) |
| Papel (paper) | Portfolios of [[CRI]] / real-estate credit | [[RECR11]], [[VGIP11]], [[VGHF11]] |
| Híbrido / agro | Mixed, or agribusiness ([[CRA]]) receivables | [[TRXF11]], [[SNAG11]] (Fiagro) |

## Key finding — FIIs are not Brazil-equity beta

Daily-return correlation of the tracked FIIs to [[EWZ]] (Brazilian equities) is essentially zero — HGLG11 ~0.11, XPML11 ~−0.02, RECR11 ~0.00. FIIs trade on local interest rates and distribution yield, not equity beta: when the Selic falls, FII prices rise (the yield re-rates), largely independent of the Ibovespa. They are a rate-driven income sleeve, distinct from the equity book — which is exactly why they show up as a separate allocation in Brazilian portfolios.

## Risks / watch-outs

- Hard to select and opaque — past distributions do not predict future ones, and a single-asset fund concentrates vacancy risk.
- The best funds are often closed; the advisory caution ([[Brazilian fixed income]]) is to keep FIIs a modest, diversified sleeve, not a core.
