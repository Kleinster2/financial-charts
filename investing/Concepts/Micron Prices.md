# Micron Prices

**Micron Prices** — MU went from $49.83 (Dec 2022) to $461.73 (Mar 18, 2026) — a 9.3x move in 39 months. Two dynamics: the absolute price (what put MU where it is) and the relative price (MU against its peers and the semiconductor complex).

---

## Absolute price

The absolute price chain for MU traces a path from commodity-cycle trough to AI-driven repricing, with each move building on the last.

| Date | Price | Event | Effect |
|------|-------|-------|--------|
| Dec 2022 | $49.83 | Memory downturn trough — negative GM, inventory glut, crypto winter | Stock at 2020 levels despite 3 years of earnings |
| May 2023 | $65.44 | Early AI hype — ChatGPT launched Nov 2022, NVIDIA data center revenue inflecting | MU recovering with broad semis, SMH rallying |
| Jun 2023 | $64.50 | China's Cyberspace Administration bans Micron from critical infrastructure | Stock drops ~7% in days, then stabilizes — China revenue was ~25% but largely commoditized |
| Jun 2024 | $140.25 | Peak of first AI wave — HBM narrative takes hold, memory shortage thesis emerging | MU doubles from year-end 2023. But NVDA and SMH running harder |
| Sep 2024 | $106.93 | Q4 FY2024 guidance disappoints — revenue recovery slower than hoped | -24% from June peak. Market questioning memory cycle timing |
| Dec 2024 | $103.47 | Q1 FY2025 guidance weak — DRAM pricing not yet inflecting, HBM ramp slower than bulls hoped | Gives back entire 2024 gain. SMH at $241, MU at same level as Dec 2023 |
| Jun 2025 | $125.80 | Recovery begins — DRAM contract prices starting to firm, HBM3E qualified with [[NVIDIA]] | Slow grind up, still underperforming SMH |
| Sep 2025 | $161.61 | Q4 FY2025 — gross margins back to 40%, HBM revenue accelerating | Breaking out of the $100-130 range |
| Dec 2025 | $225.43 | Q1 FY2026 — $13.6B revenue (+57% YoY), 56.8% GM, "largest ever supply-demand gap" | +40% in 3 months. Memory shortage confirmed. Q2 guide $18.7B |
| Jan 2026 | $365.00 | Syracuse groundbreaking + [[Howard Lutnick]] tariff threats to Korea + DRAM prices +55% QoQ | +62% in one month. Industrial policy narrative compounds the shortage story |
| Feb 2026 | $410.34 | HBM4 mass production ahead of schedule (Wolfe conference, CFO Mark Murphy) | Stock +10% in a day. TD Cowen $600 PT (Street high) |
| Mar 18, 2026 | $461.73 | Q2 FY2026 blowout: $23.86B rev (+75% seq), 81% GM, EPS $12.20 vs $9.31 est | +8% after hours despite Dow -768pts. Q3 guide $33.5B |
| Mar 23, 2026 | $404.35 | Pullback from peak — profit-taking after 4x move in 3 months | -12% from peak, still +42% YTD |

The key inflection was December 2024 to January 2026. For a full year (Dec 2023 to Dec 2024), MU went nowhere — $84 to $84 — while SMH rose from $174 to $242. The market was pricing memory as a lagging AI play: [[NVIDIA]] had the direct revenue, memory would benefit eventually. When the DRAM pricing data confirmed the shortage in Q4 2025 (contract prices +45-50% QoQ), the re-rating was violent. MU went from $103 to $365 in thirteen months — the market pricing in three years of earnings growth in one move.

The December 2024 low ($103.47) marked the last time MU traded below the semiconductor complex on a price/earnings basis. Everything after was catch-up: the stock quadrupled while the earnings estimates quintupled, so the forward PE actually compressed from ~25x to ~11x during the rally. Cheap going up — the rarest kind of move.

## Relative price

### MU / SMH ratio

The MU/SMH ratio measures how the market prices Micron against the semiconductor complex. But raw price ratios are misleading without beta context. MU's beta to SMH has historically been ~1.3-1.5x (memory is higher-beta than the broad semi index). A raw ratio increase during a bull market partly reflects beta, not alpha. The beta-adjusted ratio strips that out.

| Date | MU | SMH | Raw MU/SMH | MU β (est) | Beta-adj ratio | Context |
|------|-----|------|-----------|------------|----------------|---------|
| Dec 2022 | $49.83 | $100.81 | 0.494 | ~1.4 | ~0.35 | Memory downturn — MU cheapest on risk-adj basis |
| Jun 2023 | $64.50 | $146.00 | 0.442 | ~1.4 | ~0.32 | Post-China ban — MU underperforms even adjusted for beta |
| Jun 2024 | $140.25 | $260.61 | 0.538 | ~1.3 | ~0.41 | First AI wave — MU gaining but beta explains most of it |
| Dec 2024 | $103.47 | $240.92 | 0.429 | ~1.3 | ~0.33 | **Trough** — worst risk-adjusted relative performance |
| Sep 2025 | $161.61 | $321.11 | 0.503 | ~1.3 | ~0.39 | Recovery starting — still underperforming on risk-adj basis |
| Dec 2025 | $225.43 | $338.19 | 0.667 | ~1.4 | ~0.48 | Breakout — MU closing the gap even beta-adjusted |
| Jan 2026 | $365.00 | $390.39 | 0.935 | ~1.5 | ~0.62 | Near parity — genuine alpha emerging |
| Feb 2026 | $410.34 | $414.78 | 0.989 | ~1.5 | ~0.66 | MU generating real excess returns beyond beta |
| Mar 2026 | $461.73 | $393.67 | 1.173 | ~1.5 | ~0.78 | **MU exceeds SMH** — unprecedented risk-adj outperformance |
| Mar 23 | $404.35 | $391.36 | 1.033 | ~1.5 | ~0.69 | Settled — still well above historical range |

The raw ratio went from 0.429 to 1.173 — a 2.7x move. But the beta-adjusted ratio went from ~0.33 to ~0.78 — a 2.4x move. The difference matters: roughly 10% of the raw outperformance was just beta (MU taking more risk in a rising market). The remaining 90% was genuine re-rating — the market repricing memory from "lagging AI beneficiary" to "the bottleneck."

The December 2024 trough at 0.33 beta-adjusted was the moment of maximum relative cheapness. The market was paying up for [[NVIDIA]], [[ASML]], [[TSMC]] — the "proven" AI plays — while treating Micron as a cyclical commodity name that would lag. The DRAM pricing data broke that framing.

### MU vs Korean peers

The relative pricing against [[SK Hynix]] and [[Samsung]] tracks a different dynamic: the US premium and HBM positioning.

In 2025, all three memory stocks rose broadly together (SK Hynix +11.5%, Samsung +16% YTD as of Jan 2026). But Micron dramatically outperformed: +239% in calendar 2025 vs peers in the teens. The divergence accelerated in late 2025 and early 2026 on:

1. **CHIPS Act / industrial policy** — $6.165B in grants + 25% ITC. Lutnick using Micron as the domestic champion, threatening Korean peers with 100% tariffs
2. **HBM share gains** — Micron overtook Samsung for #2 in HBM (21% share vs Samsung's 17%, Q2 2025 data). SK Hynix still dominant at 62%
3. **US listing premium** — Micron is the only pure-play US-listed large-cap memory name. Index inclusion, institutional allocation, and options liquidity drive a structural premium vs KRX-listed peers
4. **Korea discount** — SK Hynix and Samsung trade at lower multiples partly due to Korean governance structures, currency risk, and North Korea tail risk

The relative trade between MU and Korean peers also reflects HBM generation cycles. When SK Hynix was the sole qualified HBM3E supplier for NVIDIA (2024), it outperformed. When Micron qualified and announced HBM4 ahead of schedule (Feb 2026), MU outperformed. Each qualification win reshuffles the relative pricing.
