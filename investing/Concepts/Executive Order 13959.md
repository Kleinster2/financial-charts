**Executive Order 13959** — the November 12, 2020 Trump executive order barring US persons from transacting in publicly traded securities of designated "Communist Chinese Military Companies" (CCMC). The first US measure to use capital-markets access, rather than trade or technology, as a China-pressure lever. Its most visible casualties were the three Chinese telecom ADRs — [[China Mobile]] (CHL), [[China Unicom]] (CHU), and [[China Telecom]] (CHA) — delisted from the NYSE in early 2021. Later amended and renamed the NS-CMIC list under a June 2021 Biden order.
