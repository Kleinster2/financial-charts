#concept #housing #rates #macro

Mortgage rates — Key driver of housing affordability and activity. 30-year fixed at 6.51% as of May 21 2026, up from 6.36% the prior week and down from 6.86% a year earlier. The January 2026 easing path has been interrupted by the Iran-war Treasury selloff. Fed policy influences but does not directly set mortgage rates.

### May 2026 Treasury spillover

Reuters' May 24 Treasury-market analysis identified mortgages as one of the first household channels through which the Iran-war bond rout becomes politically visible. The 10-year Treasury touched 4.69% earlier in the week and last traded at 4.56%, versus a local FRED close of 4.67% on May 19. With PMMS at 6.51%, the 30-year mortgage spread over the 10-year close is roughly 184 bp, well inside the normal 150-250 bp range. That means the household-rate pain is being driven mainly by the Treasury benchmark rising, not by an unusual mortgage-specific spread blowout.

The affordability implication is direct: if the 10-year moves toward the 5% pain level discussed by Reuters' market sources, the mortgage-rate arithmetic points back toward the upper-6s / low-7s even without MBS-spread widening. That would re-intensify the lock-in effect and pressure purchase demand just as the administration is trying to contain gasoline-price and borrowing-cost anxiety before the midterms.

## What drives mortgage rates

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| 10-year Treasury yield | Primary benchmark (~4%+ currently) |
| Fed funds rate | Indirect — influences short end |
| MBS spreads | Risk premium over [[Treasuries]] |
| [[Inflation]] expectations | Higher → higher rates |
| Economic growth | Stronger → higher rates |
| Supply/demand | Fed QT reducing MBS demand |

Key relationship: Mortgage rates ≈ 10-year Treasury + ~150-250 bps spread.

## Fed policy link

| Fed action | Mortgage rate impact |
|------------|---------------------|
| Rate cuts | Tends to lower (but not 1:1) |
| QT (balance sheet) | Raises spreads |
| Forward guidance | Shapes expectations |

Dec 2025 Fed projections: Median expectation is one 0.25% cut in 2026.

Reality: "Fed doesn't set mortgage rates directly, but its policies and market expectations strongly influence them."

## Housing market impact

| Rate level | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Above 7% | Severe affordability stress |
| 6-7% | Elevated, lock-in effect |
| 5.5-6% | Modest relief |
| Below 5.5% | Refi wave triggers |

Lock-in effect: Homeowners with sub-4% mortgages reluctant to sell/move.
