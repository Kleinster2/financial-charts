# Housing market dynamics

The relationship between housing activity (existing home sales, new construction, mortgage rates) and downstream consumer spending categories. Existing home sales drive a cascade of purchases — furniture, appliances, mattresses, home improvement — making housing turnover a leading indicator for a broad swath of consumer discretionary. Mattress purchases correlate particularly strongly with existing home sales, as buyers typically replace mattresses when moving. The 2022-2025 housing freeze (driven by mortgage rates above 6.5% and limited inventory from the "lock-in effect" where homeowners with sub-4% mortgages refuse to sell) has been a primary demand headwind for mattress companies like [[Sleep Number]], [[Tempur-Sealy International]], and [[Purple Innovation]].

The lock-in effect created a structural supply shortage: existing home sales fell from 6.1M annualized (2021) to ~4.0M (2023-2024), near 30-year lows. This simultaneously suppressed move-related spending and inflated home prices (low supply), producing the unusual combination of unaffordable housing and weak housing-adjacent consumer demand. New construction partially offset the supply gap, benefiting homebuilders like [[D.R. Horton]] and [[Lennar]], but new home sales don't generate the same downstream spending multiplier as existing home turnover.
