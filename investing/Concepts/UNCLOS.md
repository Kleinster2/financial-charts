# UNCLOS

The United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (1982) — the international legal framework governing maritime rights, territorial waters, exclusive economic zones, and passage through international straits. Part III (Articles 37-44) establishes transit passage rights through straits used for international navigation, including the [[Strait of Hormuz]], [[Strait of Malacca]], [[Danish Straits]], and [[Turkish Straits]]. Article 38 provides that transit passage "shall not be impeded" and cannot be suspended. Notably, the US, [[Israel]], and [[Iran]] are all non-signatories.
