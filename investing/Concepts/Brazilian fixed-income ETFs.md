# Brazilian fixed-income ETFs

A small but growing family of B3-listed ETFs that hold Brazilian government fixed income — Tesouro Selic, prefixados, and inflation-linked (IPCA / IMA-B) paper. Their pitch is a liquid, low-cost, exchange-traded cash-and-duration sleeve: an alternative to a bank [[CDB]] or a renda-fixa fund for parking and managing the fixed-income allocation.

## Why they matter (the cash-sleeve use)

In a Carteira Aberta portfolio review, the guest described moving his "caixa" (cash) out of bank renda-fixa funds and into these B3 fixed-income ETFs — citing lower tax (the 15% long-term fixed-income bracket rather than a fund's fee drag) and daily liquidity. That is the core appeal: a tradeable, cheap, liquid proxy for Tesouro exposure used as the cash leg of a portfolio.

They are essentially uncorrelated with Brazilian equities (LFTS11 vs [[EWZ]] ~0.06) — by construction, since they track government rates, not the stock market. A cash/rate instrument, not equity beta — the same "not Brazil-equity beta" property the listed [[FII]] sleeve shows, for the same reason.

## The family

| Ticker | Tracks | Role |
|--------|--------|------|
| LFTS11 | Tesouro Selic (Investo Teva) | Cash / floating-rate — the closest to a money-market proxy (tracked, full history) |
| LFTB11 | Short-duration Treasury 7–60 day (Investo MarketVector) | Cash-like; very newly listed, only a stub price series in the data so far |
| IMAB11 | IMA-B (IPCA-linked Tesouro) | Inflation duration |
| IRFM11 / FIXA11 | IRF-M / prefixado | Fixed-rate duration |
| B5P211 / IB5M11 | IMA-B 5 / 5+ | IPCA, short vs long duration |

Tracked here: LFTS11 (full history) and LFTB11 (listed, stub series). The IPCA and prefixado duration ETFs are listed for reference and can be added on request.

## Watch-outs

- Still small and less liquid than the US fixed-income ETF market — bid/ask spreads and tracking error can be wider.
- The 15% tax rate applies only after the long-term holding period; short-term trades hit a higher bracket.
