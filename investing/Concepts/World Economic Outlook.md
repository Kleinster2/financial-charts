# World Economic Outlook

The World Economic Outlook (WEO) is the [[IMF]]'s flagship macro publication — the Fund's twice-yearly (April and October, with January and July updates) global growth and inflation forecast and the closest thing to a consensus baseline for the world economy. It is produced by the Research Department under the Economic Counsellor; [[Pierre-Olivier Gourinchas]] was the author of record 2022 through July 2026.

The WEO matters to the vault as a forecast track record, not just a number: the Fund's relative optimism on US growth after [[Liberation Day tariffs|Liberation Day]] — resisting the sharp 2025 downgrades other forecasters made — was vindicated when the US grew 2.1% in 2025 (vs 2.8% in 2024).
