Digital Advertising is the ~$700B global market for online advertising across search, social, display, video, and commerce formats, structurally dominated by [[Google]] and [[Meta]] but facing disruption from AI-driven search and retail media networks.

## Synopsis

The digital advertising market is the revenue engine behind the consumer internet. [[Google]] (search + YouTube) and [[Meta]] (Facebook + Instagram) together capture roughly 50% of global digital ad spend, with [[Amazon]] as the fast-growing third force via its retail media business. The market grew ~10-12% annually through 2024-2025, driven by the shift from linear TV to connected TV/streaming, the growth of retail media (where [[Amazon]], [[Walmart]], and [[Instacart]] sell ad placements against purchase-intent data), and the relentless optimization of programmatic buying through real-time bidding exchanges.

The structural question for investors is whether AI disrupts the search advertising model that underpins [[Google]]'s business. If AI assistants answer queries directly (via [[ChatGPT]], [[Claude]], [[Gemini]], or [[Perplexity]]), the traditional search results page — where Google extracts $175B+/yr in ad revenue — loses traffic. Google's counter is AI Overviews (embedding AI summaries with ads), but the click-through economics may be permanently worse. Meanwhile, [[Apple]]'s ATT privacy changes (2021) already demonstrated that platform policy can destroy ad targeting — [[Snap]] and [[Meta]] lost a combined $230B+ in market cap when ATT degraded their signal. The sector's investability depends on which companies can maintain or rebuild targeting precision in a privacy-constrained, AI-disrupted landscape: [[Meta]]'s Advantage+ (AI-automated ad creation and targeting) and [[Amazon]]'s first-party purchase data appear best positioned.
