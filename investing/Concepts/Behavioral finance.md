## Synthesis

Behavioral finance is the micro-foundation under the vault's market-dynamics concepts: the individual biases catalogued below (herding, recency, confirmation bias, anchoring) are the mechanisms by which a story propagates, over-extends, and reverses. Scaled up, they become [[Narrative lifecycle investing]] (conviction-phase trading), reflexivity ([[Soros Fund Management|Soros]]), and the [[Minsky cycle]] (confidence to fragility). Their automated form is [[Thesis lock-in]], where an LLM hard-codes confirmation bias into a research workflow. (Chart not applicable — this is a framework concept with no single price series.)

## Key biases

| Bias | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Loss aversion | Losses hurt 2x more than gains feel good |
| Anchoring | Over-reliance on first information |
| Confirmation bias | Seeking confirming evidence |
| Recency bias | Overweighting recent events |
| Herding | Following the crowd |
| Overconfidence | Overestimating own ability |

---

## Loss aversion

| Behavior | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Hold losers too long | "It'll come back" |
| Sell winners too early | Lock in gains |
| Risk-seeking when losing | Double down |
| Risk-averse when winning | Protect gains |

## Protecting yourself

| Bias | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Loss aversion | Pre-set stop losses |
| Confirmation | Seek disconfirming evidence |
| Overconfidence | Track decisions, humble sizing |
| Recency | Use long-term data |
| Herding | Have written investment plan |

## Narrative and reflexivity

Two traditions extend behavioral finance from individual bias to market-level dynamics:

- [[Robert Shiller]]'s narrative economics — contagious stories form "narrative constellations" that drive collective economic decisions; the academic basis for [[Narrative lifecycle investing]].
- [[Soros Fund Management|George Soros]]'s reflexivity — perception and fundamentals feed back on each other, so conviction drives price, which then moves the fundamentals it was supposed to measure (reflexivity is a vault stub candidate).

## AI-era biases

[[Thesis lock-in]] is confirmation bias automated: an LLM defends an encoded thesis by reinterpreting contrary evidence rather than updating. When many models share inputs and biases, a personal weakness becomes a systemic one — compressing and intensifying the [[Narrative lifecycle investing|narrative cycle]].
