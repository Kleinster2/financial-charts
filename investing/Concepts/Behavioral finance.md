**Behavioral finance** — Psychology of investing. Cognitive biases affect decisions. Kahneman, Thaler. Exploitable inefficiencies.

---

## Key biases

| Bias | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Loss aversion** | Losses hurt 2x more than gains feel good |
| **Anchoring** | Over-reliance on first information |
| **Confirmation bias** | Seeking confirming evidence |
| **Recency bias** | Overweighting recent events |
| **Herding** | Following the crowd |
| **Overconfidence** | Overestimating own ability |

---

## Loss aversion

| Behavior | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Hold losers too long | "It'll come back" |
| Sell winners too early | Lock in gains |
| Risk-seeking when losing | Double down |
| Risk-averse when winning | Protect gains |

---

## Market implications

| Bias | Market effect |
|------|---------------|
| Herding | Bubbles and crashes |
| Recency | Momentum, mean reversion |
| Anchoring | Sticky valuations |
| Overconfidence | Excessive trading |

---

## Exploiting biases

| Opportunity | Strategy |
|-------------|----------|
| Overreaction | Contrarian investing |
| Herding extremes | Fade consensus |
| Loss aversion | Buy capitulation |
| Recency | Mean reversion |

---

## Protecting yourself

| Bias | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Loss aversion | Pre-set stop losses |
| Confirmation | Seek disconfirming evidence |
| Overconfidence | Track decisions, humble sizing |
| Recency | Use long-term data |
| Herding | Have written investment plan |

---

## Prospect theory (Kahneman/Tversky)

| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Losses > gains | Asymmetric pain |
| Reference points | Framing matters |
| Probability weighting | Overweight rare events |
