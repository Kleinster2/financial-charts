# 2025 IPO market

The 2025 IPO market looked normal by deal count but still carried a post-bubble quality filter. Issuance recovered meaningfully from 2022-2024, larger deals returned, and [[SPAC]] supply revived. The market still did not deliver the kind of broad, frictionless growth-stock window that defined 2021.

## Synthesis

2025 was the bridge year between the post-2022 repair and the 2026 mega-IPO absorption test. Renaissance Capital's headline was constructive: 202 IPOs raised $44B, up 35% by count and 49% by proceeds from 2024. But the details were still selective. The number of $100M+ IPOs was below the 10-year average, median deal size stayed small, and the Renaissance IPO Index lagged the S&P 500.

That makes 2025 more useful as a filtration year than as a boom year. Public investors paid for obvious scarcity and first-day excitement, but they kept marking down weaker post-IPO stories. [[Figma]] and [[Circle]] showed that first-day heat could return. [[Chime]] showed that a major [[Fintech|fintech]] could still de-rate after listing. [[CoreWeave]], [[Klarna]], [[eToro]], and [[SailPoint]] made the backlog more visible, but the market had not yet been asked to digest the trophy supply now embedded in [[2026 IPO pipeline]].
