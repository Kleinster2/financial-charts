# Mario Draghi report

"The Future of European Competitiveness" — the September 9 2024 report by [[Mario Draghi]] for the [[European Commission]], which became the reference text for the EU's competitiveness debate. It diagnosed a widening productivity and growth gap between Europe and the United States (and a strategic dependence on China), and called for a step-change in investment, deeper capital markets, lower energy costs, and coordinated [[Industrial policy]].

## Why it matters here

The report is the policy embodiment of the European-decline narrative that [[Paul Krugman]] and [[Brad DeLong]] challenge on measurement grounds in the [[US-Europe productivity paradox]]. If the productivity-growth gap is methodology-dependent (constant-price shows decline, PPP shows none), then the report's core urgency premise is weaker than it appears — though its other arguments (fragmentation, energy costs, capital-market depth, strategic dependence) stand on separate ground. [[Luis Garicano]]'s work on EU competitiveness sits in the same agenda, and the [[Industrial policy]] note treats the report as the EU's doctrinal anchor for scale-matching US/China industrial policy. The underlying question — whether a producer-side productivity gap is a welfare gap — is the subject of [[Technological surplus capture]].
