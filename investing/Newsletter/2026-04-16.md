# 2026-04-16 — Jensen positions, Beijing escalates, Bolsonaro leads

## Jensen's position paper — three pillars, premium tokens, and the ASIC rounding error

Dwarkesh Patel's 103-minute interview with [[Jensen Huang]] (Apr 15) is the most comprehensive public articulation of the [[CUDA moat]] to date — richness/programmability, install base, everywhere — reinforcing each other such that an ASIC's 65% vs NVIDIA's 70% gross margin reads as a rounding error against full-stack value capture. [[Broadcom]]'s thesis gets a direct rebuttal; whether that holds is the live debate.

The interpretive shift is the [[Premium inference tokens]] frame. Jensen reframed the [[Groq]] deal as deliberate market segmentation inside CUDA — premium, low-latency, SRAM silicon at high ASP on one axis; volume, throughput-optimized, HBM GPUs at standard ASP on the other. That's not defensive hedging. It's the collapse of the "tokens are commodities" thesis, because latency is a pricing axis that doesn't commoditize.

The [[CoreWeave]] defense carried numbers for the first time — $2B real EBITDA, $6.3B cost-of-goods guarantee, $2B equity. Jensen reframes the circular-financing critique as anchor-tenant economics, but the structure depends on Microsoft demand holding. [[Anthropic]] is characterized as "a unique instance, not a trend" — the hedge against the TPU-displacement narrative, since Anthropic trains on NVIDIA + [[TPU]] + [[Trainium]] simultaneously.

Roadmap cadence confirmed through 2029: Blackwell → [[Rubin|Vera Rubin]] (2026, first samples back Feb) → Vera Rubin Ultra (2027) → Feynman on TSMC A16 1.6nm (2028) → unnamed (2029). [[TSMC]] gets the 30-year no-contract relationship story with FIFO fixed-price allocation. The tension: if Jensen's three-pillar read is right, ASIC share stays containable. If wrong, the 65%/70% math starts mattering.

## Panama: NDRC summons Maersk and MSC — the symmetric trap tightens

The FT (McMorrow + Leahy, Apr 15) confirmed [[NDRC]] summoned [[Maersk]] and [[MSC]] separately in early March demanding withdrawal from Balboa and Cristóbal. [[Vincent Clerc]] attended in person at NDRC on March 20; MSC's Diego Aponte communicates in writing. April 2026 added two new legal instruments — a supply-chain-security law with exit-ban threat for individuals deemed to "harm" China's supply chains, plus counter-extraterritorial jurisdiction rules applying directly to Panama.

The symmetric trap is the frame now across [[Maersk]], [[MSC]], and [[CK Hutchison]] notes: all three face two-sided pressure, just on different horizons. Can't refuse Panama (US leverage via CFIUS, docking rights); can't ignore NDRC (China leverage via port-call access, new exit-ban exposure for executives).

The pressure valve is the US-operator-backfill argument — if European groups exit, [[BlackRock]]'s consortium is the obvious bidder, which is worse for Beijing than the current arrangement. The European shippers are using exactly that argument with NDRC. Beijing's demand is constrained by its own worse alternative. Watching for whether that's enough to freeze the escalation or whether the new exit-ban rules raise the personal cost for Clerc and Aponte beyond what the backfill math can offset.

## Brazil: first Bolsonaro to lead Polymarket, and the BRL is already there

[[Austral Continental]] (Drew Crawford, Apr 16) flagged that the [[2026 Brazil presidential election]] Polymarket contract has flipped for the first time — Flávio 40 / Lula 39 / Field 21 on $50.2M cumulative volume since Sep 2025. Datafolha Apr 11 confirmed the move: 46-45 Flávio in the runoff, a four-point swing from March's 46-43 Lula lead.

The [[Brazilian real]] is already trading on fiscal-continuity optimism — USDBRL ~4.99, +26% BRL appreciation since the December 2024 low, Selic at 14.75% (down from the February 15.00% peak), [[Polymarket]] pricing 95% probability of another cut at the Apr 28-29 COPOM. The trailing real rate is ~10.9%, still among the highest in EM.

The tension: the rally is built on carry and on fiscal-continuity expectations anchored by the [[Arcabouço Fiscal]] (Haddad's 2023 replacement for the 2016 spending cap), yet debt/GDP is projected toward 95% by end-2026 and 2026 IPCA is tracking 4.71% — above the 4.50% ceiling for the first time this cycle. Flávio's market-liberal-vs-populist orientation is genuinely open (the Brazil vault carries the full biography). If the candidate-continuity trade continues without that resolving, the BRL is pricing a fiscal path the incumbent fiscal framework may not deliver.

## Norway holds, Denmark's academics walked — the two poles of foreign-institutional US stance

FT (Clarfelt + Duguid, Apr 15): [[Jens Stoltenberg]], now Norway's finance minister, said the [[Government Pension Fund Global|GPFG]] has "no plans to reduce our exposure in the US" — the fund crossed $2T with >50% US concentration and, per Stoltenberg, "there is no way to hide" a balance that size. Acknowledged worries: Middle East war impact, WWII-high government debt, persistent inflation, European growth, stock overvaluation. The position holds anyway.

Bracket that against [[AkademikerPension]], the Danish academics' fund that sold all US [[Treasuries]] in January 2026 on debt/fiscal-outlook grounds. GPFG right-tail, AkademikerPension left-tail. The interesting action for [[Dollar reserve status erosion]] isn't at the poles — it's where the median foreign institutional investor lands over the next two to three quarters, and whether the signal leaves the public-debate layer and shows up in Treasury auction rollover composition.
