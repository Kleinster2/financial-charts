# 2026-05-07 — A bifurcation tape, a space-intel divergence, and a $22B private benchmark

## The premium-suburban consumer cracked in four names — and the math says it isn't a basket

Four names sold off hard on Q1 prints today: [[Zoetis]] -21.1%, [[Vital Farms]] -23.8%, [[Whirlpool]] -13.9%, [[Tapestry]] -8.7%. The narrative writes itself — premium pet care, organic eggs, durable-goods discretionary, accessible luxury. All four name-checked the same demographic in commentary. ZTS attributed the miss to "increased price sensitivity, decline in veterinary visits, softer demand for premium innovative products." [[Whirlpool]] slashed FY26 GAAP EPS to $2.45-2.95 from a January guide of ~$6.25 — the largest discrete-period reset of [[Marc Bitzer]]'s eight-year tenure. [[Vital Farms]] cut FY26 revenue to $775-800M vs $909M consensus and announced a butter-business wind-down. [[Tapestry]] absorbed a 180bps tariff-and-duty operating-margin headwind despite a Q3 beat and raised guide.

[[McDonald's]] CEO [[Chris Kempczinski]] said it cleanest: "challenging environment, certainly not improving, may be getting a little bit worse." MCD's value playbook worked — SSS +3.8%, Q1 beat. At the other end of the income distribution the same playbook is breaking.

The math says don't trade it as a cohort. Cluster validation run on both ZTS and WHR falsified each as a tradable basket. ZTS-[[Henry Schein|HSIC]] correlation is 0.07 — effectively independent. WHR is a singleton inside an 11-name housing-cycle super-cluster ([[Masco]], [[Fortune Brands Innovations|FBIN]], [[Stanley Black & Decker|SWK]], [[D.R. Horton|DHI]], [[Lennar|LEN]], [[NVR]], [[PulteGroup|PHM]], [[Home Depot|HD]], [[Lowe's|LOW]], XHB, ITB) that aggregates without it. Every "should be a peer" name pulls into the housing-cycle factor; WHR doesn't. The implication: long-WHR / short-builders wouldn't hedge factor risk because the names don't co-move enough. The tape is four separate idiosyncratic stories that share a demographic, not a tradable consumer-cycle basket.

The mirror image was real and large — [[Datadog]] +31.3%, [[Fortinet]] +23.7%, [[Axon]] +11.9% on AI-revenue and cybersecurity beats, with sympathy bids in [[MongoDB]] +14.3%, [[Snowflake]] +8.8%, [[Zscaler]] +9.4%, [[Palo Alto Networks]] +7.2%. [[Mike Wilson]]'s May 4 frame — Q1 EPS +16%, $600B 2026 capex — is showing up in the print; [[Jared Bernstein]]'s May 1-7 consumer-pulse stress is showing up in the same tape from the other side. Both can be right through H1 2026; the H2 transition is the test.

## Two space-intel names traded opposite directions on the same day

[[HawkEye 360]] (NYSE: HAWK) IPO'd today, priced at $26 last night, closed +30% to $34.00 — a $3.15B initial market cap on $416M raised. Same day, [[BlackSky]] (BKSY) printed Q1 and closed -25.4%. Both are space-intelligence pure-plays. The market sharply differentiated — HAWK on RF-SIGINT (jammer detection, comms intercept, threat-emitter geolocation) plus multi-year IDIQ contract structure; BKSY on optical imagery plus annual government-commercial-imagery cycles weak through the Iran-war period.

HAWK's S-1 disclosed FY 2025 revenue $117.7M (+74% YoY), positive EBITDA $24.8M, positive net income $2.7M, funded backlog $302.7M vs $44.1M prior — a 7× increase. Net-income-positive on debut differentiates HAWK from the broader space-IPO cohort. The [[Innovative Signal Analysis]] acquisition (Dec 2025, $150M) added vertical-integration to the analytics stack ahead of listing. For the [[Iran conflict supply chain cascade|Iran-war intelligence cycle]] thread, the war's RF-spectrum demand is exactly the workflow HawkEye 360 monetizes; the +30% / -25% same-day reversal is the market expressing it.

The pairing also surfaced two Phase 0 misses in the existing mover screen — HAWK wasn't in the database (IPO-day blind spot); BKSY was a sub-σ borderline. Combined with [[Chime]]'s -7.5% on a beat-and-raise that sat below both the 2.5σ screen threshold and the 8% absolute fallback, today's tape drove three new screening scripts: enhanced `quick_movers.py` with three-trigger flagging, `audit_vault_tickers.py` for Phase-0-invisible names (1,129 gaps surfaced today), and `ipo_debut_tracker.py` with `--private-watch` mode for funding-round events.
