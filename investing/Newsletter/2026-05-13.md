# May 13, 2026 — Two things got repriced in 48 hours, and the vault learned to remember the curve

## The rate-cut prior for 2026 is gone

April [[CPI]] printed at 3.8% YoY / 0.6% MoM on May 12 — the highest year-over-year reading since May 2023. Energy drove more than 40% of the monthly rise, tying the consumer-side number directly to the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]]. Core CPI ran 2.8% YoY / 0.4% MoM, both ten basis points above consensus. Market-implied probability of any 2026 [[Federal Reserve|Fed]] cut collapsed to roughly 5%; implied probability of a 2026 hike rose to roughly 22%.

[[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)|ZQ]] strip repriced cleanly. The March 2026 baseline in [[Rate expectations]] had a downward-sloping curve, ~57bp of implied easing through Dec 2027, terminal near 3.07%, peak FOMC cut probability of 28% at Jul 29. The May 13 snapshot has a hump curve peaking at 3.845% in Jun-Jul 2027, terminal 3.695%, and no FOMC meeting prices any cut above 8%. The market has not just removed the easing — it is now pricing a small tightening through mid-2027 before partial easing back. Cumulative implied cuts across the entire meeting schedule: -9bp. Effectively zero.

![[zq-multi-contract-shift-history.png|Same three ZQ contracts, two-year window — what changes is the market's view of where the Fed will be]]

The shift-history chart is the right object to stare at: three [[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)|ZQ]] contracts (Dec 2026 / Jun 2027 / Dec 2027) over the past two years, each walking from ~4.2% in May 2024 down to ~2.85% in late September 2024 (when the first Fed cut landed and the curve briefly priced terminal sub-3% across the whole window), then back up to today's 3.70-3.85%. Roughly 150bp peak-to-trough per contract. The May 12 print is the rightmost vertical dashed line, contributing the last ~25bp of the latest leg.

Cross-asset signature was textbook for a hawkish-CPI session: rate-sensitive tech beta took the hit ([[Qualcomm]] -11.5% — its worst day since 2020 — [[AST SpaceMobile]] -11.6% on Q1 earnings, [[Monday.com]] -6.4%, [[CoreWeave]] -6.1%, [[HubSpot]] -6.1%, [[Intel]] -6.8%, [[Circle]] -6.2%); hard-asset / energy bid ([[Copper]] to $14,000/ton, [[Ero Copper]] +10.1%, [[Venture Global]] +14.2% on Q1 LNG beat, [[Qnity Electronics]] +9.9% on Q1, [[Humana]] +7.7% on MA strength). Yesterday's chip-AI rally that took QCOM up 60% in a month is the same trade running in reverse.

## Anthropic and OpenAI just severed the parallel private market

On May 11, [[Anthropic]] posted a public notice declaring all unauthorized secondary transfers of its stock void — not voidable — under Delaware corporate law. [[OpenAI]] followed May 12 with identical legal language. Both notices named specific platforms publicly: [[Forge Global]] and [[Hiive]] (specifically their "new offerings"), [[Sydecar]] for back-office administration, plus pure SPV operators [[Open Door Partners]], [[Unicorns Exchange]], [[Pachamama Capital]], [[Lionheart Ventures]], [[Upmarket]]. Tokenized [[OpenAI]] perp markets dropped 36% in 24 hours; [[Destiny Tech100]] (DXYZ) closed -25.05% on the [[NYSE]].

The void-versus-voidable distinction is the structural point. A voidable regime leaves downstream good-faith buyers with equitable defenses — they paid honest money, did not know the chain was unauthorized, get to keep the position. A void regime eliminates those defenses and treats the chain as having never existed; recourse runs against the seller, not against the underlying shares. Crypto lawyer Gabriel Shapiro flagged this as the most aggressive Delaware position an issuer can take. The economics of every retail-access vehicle whose structure depends on issuer tolerance of secondary plumbing just changed.

The operational trigger appears to have been the gap between primary-round and tokenized prices: [[Anthropic]]'s tokenized implied valuation reached $1.6T against the $380B February 2026 primary round — a 4.2x premium — while [[Anthropic]] was reportedly negotiating its next primary round at ~$900B. When the parallel market sets the headline number for your company, the cost of inaction crosses the cost of confrontation. The structural asymmetry to watch: [[SpaceX]] has not joined, so the issuer-side void posture currently runs across the two AI labs but stops at the spaceflight issuer. If [[SpaceX]] follows, every retail-access AI / spaceflight product is on notice simultaneously; if it does not, the gap becomes a tradable feature. Full dated-event treatment lives in [[Anthropic SPV void May 11 2026]].

## The vault learned to remember the curve

The day's two macro stories share an awkward shape: they are regime breaks visible only with persisted history. The ZQ curve repriced 50-70bp; the tokenized [[Anthropic]] valuation collapsed by an undisclosed amount from $1.6T. Both stories want a "where were we six months ago" view. Until today the vault could not give one — `fed_rate_expectations.py` was downloading [[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)|ZQ]] and [[SOFR futures (SR3)|SR3]] from yfinance live on every call, with nothing persisted. So today we built it.

New `rate_futures_daily` table, new `update_rate_futures.py` writer, 10,347 [[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)|ZQ]] rows backfilled across 21 contracts spanning May 2024 to today, wired into `update_market_data.py --assets ratefutures` for nightly refresh. New instrument notes for [[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)]] and [[SOFR futures (SR3)]] now sit between [[CME Group]] and [[Rate expectations]] — the rate-expectations concept used to read off two contracts with no notes of their own, which made [[Rate expectations]] the source of truth for instrument mechanics by default. The instrument notes carry that load now. [[SOFR futures (SR3)]] history is a known gap: yfinance and Stooq both return snapshot-only data for SR3 contracts, so the SR3 strip will accumulate from today forward — historical SR3 needs CME QuikStrike or a paid scrape, both out of scope.

The new multi-contract chart in [[Fed Funds futures (ZQ)]] is what the persistence actually unlocks. The implied rate for Dec 2026 walked 4.12% → 3.42% → 3.715% over two years. The May 12 CPI shock is responsible for ~25bp of the latest leg, the September 2024 first-cut anchor put in the trough. Next time the curve repositions, we will see the move in SQL not WebFetch.
