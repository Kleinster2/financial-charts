#thesis #energy #power #datacenter

**Power infrastructure bottleneck** — Grid constraints, not demand, limiting data center buildout. 5-12 year interconnection queues creating power scarcity premium.

---

## Core thesis

Data center demand is growing 22-23% annually, but grid interconnection delays now stretch 5-12 years. Power access — not land, not capital — is the binding constraint. Companies with existing generation capacity or grid access trade at a premium.

Gap: 85 GW new data center requests by 2030, but only ~100 GW total capacity (including surplus) available. [[PJM]]'s 60 GW shortfall warning (Feb 2026) is the most concrete quantification yet — equivalent to 60 nuclear reactors' worth of new capacity.

Sightline's 2026 pipeline data made the bottleneck more concrete. The schedule-setting constraint is no longer just an abstract interconnection queue. It is specific electrical equipment: [[Large power transformers]], switchgear, batteries, and substation work. Capital can fund the campus, but it cannot accelerate the box that energizes it.

## Why now

1. AI capex surge — Hyperscalers committing unprecedented capital
2. Queue congestion — Berkeley Lab shows "unprecedented backlogs"
3. Supply lag — Large turbine orders won't deliver until late 2020s
4. Regulatory friction — FERC reforms not final until April 2026

"Power crunch appears most severe through 2028-2029."

## IPP thesis

Independent power producers benefit from:
1. Existing capacity — No interconnection wait
2. Baseload reliability — Nuclear + gas for 24/7 AI workloads
3. Pricing power — Scarcity premium on available megawatts
4. Direct hyperscaler deals — Calpine signed 190 MW with CyrusOne

"Colocation behaves less like a real estate product and more like a power access product."
