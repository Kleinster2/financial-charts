# Strait of Malacca

**Strait of Malacca** is the 550-mile waterway between [[Malaysia]], [[Singapore]], and [[Indonesia]] connecting the Indian Ocean to the South China Sea — the world's largest oil chokepoint by volume at ~24M bbl/day (2024) and ~25% of global seaborne trade.

## May 2026 shadow-fleet and China-security read

The FT's May 24 visual story puts Malacca in two overlapping risk frames. For [[China]], Malacca remains a core energy and cargo corridor exposed to US and partner scrutiny. For sanctioned oil flows, the article highlights Iran-linked shadow-fleet vessels taking non-standard routes through [[Lombok Strait]] instead of the shorter Malacca path.

The extracted FT / [[UANI]] / [[MarineTraffic]] map shows the Iranian supertanker Huge near [[Sri Lanka]] on Apr 23, reappearing in [[Lombok Strait]] on May 3, and appearing by satellite in the South China Sea on May 13. [[Dimitris Ampatzidis]] at [[Kpler]] links that behavior to efforts to avoid detection around Malacca.
