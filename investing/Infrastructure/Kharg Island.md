# Kharg Island

Iran's primary crude oil export terminal, handling 90% of the country's oil exports. A 7-mile-long island in the northern [[Persian Gulf]], approximately 400 miles north of the [[Strait of Hormuz]], near [[Kuwait]] and close to mainland [[Iran]]. Population ~15,000.

## Strategic significance

What South Pars is to [[Iran]]'s domestic gas, Kharg Island is to its oil exports. The terminal handles 90% of Iranian crude exports, making it the single most important piece of Iran's economic infrastructure. There are very few alternatives — the Jask terminal (outside the strait) loaded for only the 5th time in 5 years during the March 2026 conflict, and takes ~10 days to load a VLCC vs 1-2 days at Kharg.

---

## March 2026 — military targets struck, oil infrastructure spared

On March 15, the US bombed military targets on Kharg Island (air defenses, naval base, airport) but [[Trump II|President Trump]] explicitly stated he "chose NOT to wipe out the oil infrastructure." He threatened to "immediately reconsider" if Iran interferes with Hormuz passage. Iran's ~90% crude export capacity remains intact. This preserves the Iran-to-[[China]] flow channel — destroying Kharg would have eliminated Iran's incentive to keep Hormuz partially open.

## 31st MEU seizure scenario (MEI panel, March 20 — Mulroy)

[[Mick Mulroy]] (fmr Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Middle East, ex-CIA officer, ex-Marine, [[Middle East Institute|MEI]] Distinguished Military Fellow) identified Kharg Island as one of two possible missions for the 31st MEU (Marine Expeditionary Unit), being redirected from the Indo-Pacific. The ground component is a battalion landing team — 600 Marines. If this was a planned mission, the MEU would already be there — its redeployment from the Far East indicates an added contingency plan. "You would not be pulling a national asset from the Far East."

Mulroy raised the key question: what if Iran doesn't care? "Great, you have our stuff, we're still keeping the straits closed." That's a potential tactical victory for Iran — seizing Kharg may not compel Iran to reopen Hormuz.

---

## Escalation risk — Connelly's warning

[[Colby Connelly]] ([[Middle East Institute|MEI]] Senior Fellow, energy expert, speaking from Dubai) posed the critical question: given how Iran has already responded to infrastructure attacks (retaliating against [[Ras Laffan]] and 6-7 Gulf facilities after the South Pars strike), the risk of escalation from any action against Kharg is "hard to overstate." Iran's retaliation against Gulf energy infrastructure could be even MORE forceful than the South Pars response.

Connelly emphasized that Iran has very few alternatives to Kharg for oil exports — meaning any action against the terminal would be viewed as a direct attack on Iran's economic survival. The escalation calculus is asymmetric: the US/Israel might seize the island with 600 Marines, but Iran's response could include deploying all 5,000 of its mines in the [[Strait of Hormuz]], escalating attacks on [[Qatar]]'s gas infrastructure, and activating [[Houthis]] to threaten Red Sea lanes.

Source: MEI panel, March 20 2026 — Mulroy/Connelly
