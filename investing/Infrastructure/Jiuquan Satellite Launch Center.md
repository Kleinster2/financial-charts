# Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center

Primary [[China|Chinese]] satellite launch facility, located in the Gobi Desert in northwestern China. Launch site for [[Shenzhou]] crewed missions to [[Tiangong]] and for [[TEE-01B]] (June 2024), the Chinese-built imaging satellite subsequently transferred to [[Iran]]'s [[IRGC]] Aerospace Force via the [[Earth Eye Co]] "in-orbit delivery" contract (see [[IRGC Chinese satellite targeting March 2026]]). Operates manned and unmanned missions including crewed spaceflight, commercial satellite launches, and test payloads.

## Shenzhou-23 lunar-readiness mission (May 2026)

[[Shenzhou]]-23 launched from Jiuquan on May 24, 2026 aboard a Long March 2F, carrying commander Zhu Yangzhu, pilot Zhang Yuanzhi / Zhang Zhiyuan, and payload specialist Li Jiaying / Lai Ka-ying. Reuters and AP framed the mission as part of the [[Chinese crewed lunar program]]: one crew member is expected to remain aboard [[Tiangong]] for a year while CMSA studies long-duration physiology and autonomous docking procedures relevant to the 2030 moon-landing target.
