# Turkish Straits

**Turkish Straits** — the Bosporus and Dardanelles — connect the Black Sea to the Mediterranean via the Sea of Marmara. Transit of ~2.5-3M bbl/day of crude, primarily Russian and Kazakh exports. The BTC pipeline (1M bpd) serves as a partial bypass.

## May 2026 country dependency read

The FT's May 24 visual story uses [[Ukraine]] to show how exposed countries can be to one or two sea gates. In the article's Verschuur-Lumma-Hall country table, [[Turkish Straits]] are Ukraine's largest maritime chokepoint: 42% of imports, 66% of exports, and 57% of total seaborne trade.

This reinforces why Black Sea access is not only a geopolitical issue. For markets, the Bosporus/Dardanelles system is an export-cash-flow gate for grain, metals, energy-linked cargoes, and sanctioned or war-affected trade.
