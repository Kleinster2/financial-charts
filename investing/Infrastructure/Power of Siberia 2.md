# Power of Siberia 2

Power of Siberia 2 -- planned Russia-to-China natural-gas pipeline via [[Mongolia]]. Strategically, it is Russia's post-Europe gas pivot and China's non-Hormuz pipeline hedge against [[LNG]] import disruption.

## Strategic read

For [[Russia]], Power of Siberia 2 is the most important physical outlet for gas volumes stranded by [[Europe]]'s post-2022 exit from Russian pipeline supply. It cannot fully replace the former European market on price or flexibility, but it gives Moscow a long-duration Asian demand sink and strengthens the [[Russia]]-[[China]] commodity axis.

For [[China]], the pipeline is a supply-security option rather than a pure demand bet. The [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]] exposed China's reliance on Middle East [[LNG]], especially [[QatarEnergy]] volumes routed through Hormuz. A pipeline from [[Russia]] does not eliminate LNG exposure, but it adds a non-maritime, non-Middle-East molecule source at scale.

The May 20, 2026 Bloomberg transcript described the Kremlin as saying the parameters of a deal had been agreed while "nuances" remained. AP's same-day Xi-Putin coverage confirmed the energy-trade framing around the Beijing meeting, while Caliber.Az reported that the main parameters included route and construction plans. Treat the project as directionally advanced, but not yet a de-risked FID-equivalent project.
