# Persian Gulf

The Persian Gulf is the enclosed body of water between Iran and the Arabian Peninsula that feeds directly into the [[Strait of Hormuz]]. It is one of the world's most important oil-export corridors and a core reference geography for crude, LNG, insurance, and naval-risk analysis.

## Why it matters

1. A large share of seaborne Gulf hydrocarbons originates inside or adjacent to the Persian Gulf basin.
2. Any disruption to traffic at the [[Strait of Hormuz]] immediately changes pricing, insurance, and routing for flows tied to the Persian Gulf.
3. The basin anchors the export economics of [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Iran]], [[Iraq]], [[Kuwait]], the [[UAE]], and [[Qatar]].
