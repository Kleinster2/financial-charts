**Diego Garcia** -- a US/UK joint military facility on a coral atoll in the central [[Indian Ocean]], part of the British Indian Ocean Territory (Chagos Archipelago). The largest atoll in the Chagos group, it hosts Naval Support Facility Diego Garcia, which serves as a forward logistics hub, B-52/B-2 bomber staging base, intelligence collection station, and maritime prepositioning site. The base sits approximately 1,600 km south of [[India]]'s southern tip, 3,700 km from [[Iran]]'s southern coast, and 2,500 km from the [[Strait of Hormuz]] -- deliberately positioned beyond the range of most regional ballistic missile threats, though the [[Diego Garcia attack 2026|March 2026 Iranian missile attack]] tested that assumption.

## Strategic role

Diego Garcia's value is its remoteness -- far enough from continental threats to be secure, central enough to project power across the [[Indian Ocean]], [[Persian Gulf]], and East Africa. It served as a bomber staging base for operations in [[Iraq]] (1991, 2003), [[Afghanistan]] (2001-2021), and the 2026 [[Operation Epic Fury|Iran campaign]]. The prepositioning ships carry enough equipment and supplies to sustain a Marine Expeditionary Brigade for 30 days without resupply.

The base's perceived invulnerability was challenged on March 21, 2026, when [[Iran]] fired two intermediate-range ballistic missiles at the facility. Neither reached the target -- one broke up mid-flight, the other was intercepted by an [[SM-3]]. See [[Diego Garcia attack 2026]].

## Sovereignty dispute

The [[United Kingdom]] detached the Chagos Archipelago from [[Mauritius]] in 1965 (three years before Mauritian independence) and expelled the indigenous Chagossian population (~1,500-2,000 people) between 1968-1973 to make way for the US military facility. [[Mauritius]] has never recognized UK sovereignty. In 2019, the [[International Court of Justice]] issued an advisory opinion that the UK's continued administration was unlawful. In 2024, the UK agreed to return sovereignty to [[Mauritius]] while retaining a 99-year lease on Diego Garcia for the military base. The transfer remains incomplete as of March 2026.
