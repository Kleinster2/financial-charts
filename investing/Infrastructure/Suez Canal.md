# Suez Canal

193km artificial waterway in [[Egypt]] connecting the Mediterranean to the Red Sea, the principal sea-route shortcut between Europe and Asia. Built 1859–1869, expanded 2015. Operated by the Suez Canal Authority. Carries roughly 12% of world trade and 30% of global container traffic in normal conditions; flows are highly sensitive to Red Sea security ([[Houthis]] / [[Bab el-Mandeb]]) and to crisis-driven rerouting in the Persian Gulf.

## Why Suez Canal matters for this vault

Suez is one of the three pivotal maritime chokepoints regularly tracked for trade-disruption risk, alongside the [[Strait of Hormuz]] and the [[Bab el-Mandeb]]. When [[Bab el-Mandeb]] is contested by the [[Houthis]], Suez throughput collapses because southbound vessels reroute around the Cape. When [[Strait of Hormuz]] is contested, Suez can become a transit beneficiary for rerouted Iranian / Gulf goods that go overland into [[Turkey]] and then load at [[Mersin]] for Suez transit to Asia. Egyptian transit revenue is itself a meaningful budget line for the [[Egypt]] sovereign.
