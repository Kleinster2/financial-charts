# Spaceport Nova Scotia

**Spaceport Nova Scotia** is a commercial orbital launch complex under development in Canso, Nova Scotia by [[Maritime Launch Services]]. It is relevant because launch-site capacity is becoming a bottleneck in the space industrial base: the constraint is no longer only rockets, but range processes, licensed launches, airspace, safety systems, and launch-site economics.

## Isar partnership (May 2026)

Isar Aerospace and Maritime Launch Services signed a May 26, 2026 letter of intent to advance orbital launch readiness from Spaceport Nova Scotia. The official release says the site is licensed, actively under development, and positioned for the orbit profiles used by Earth-observation and communications constellations.

The important point is not that the site has solved launch economics. It has not. The Commercial Space Federation / Rational Futures launch-capacity report explicitly argues that non-traditional spaceports can add resilience but are unlikely to be funded by market activity alone without government support. Spaceport Nova Scotia therefore belongs in the same bucket as other industrial-base assets that may be strategically useful before they are privately financeable on pure throughput.
