# Gulf of Oman

The Gulf of Oman is the body of water connecting the [[Strait of Hormuz]] (and therefore the Persian Gulf) to the Arabian Sea and the wider Indian Ocean. It is the first open water a tanker reaches after transiting out of the Strait, and its northern coastline hosts the [[UAE]] port of [[Fujairah]] — the world's third-largest bunkering hub and a critical transshipment point.

## Why it matters

1. **Hormuz bypass loading.** Oil loaded at Fujairah arrives via the [[ADCOP]] (Abu Dhabi Crude Oil Pipeline), which runs from Habshan inside the UAE directly to the Gulf of Oman coast, bypassing the Strait of Hormuz entirely. During the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]], ADCOP-delivered crude loaded at Fujairah became one of the main Gulf-origin supply routes not affected by the closure.
2. **TD34 emergency route.** In March 2026, the [[Baltic Exchange]] launched **TD34** (270,000mt Gulf of Oman → China [[VLCC]]) on public trial as an emergency alternative to the closed TD3C route. TD34 prices Gulf-of-Oman loadings that are physically transacting, in contrast to the hypothetical TD3C assessments continuing inside the closed strait. See [[Imaginary freight rate]].
3. **Shipping staging area.** Throughout the Hormuz closure, 150+ vessels anchored off the Gulf of Oman coast waiting for transit clearance or rerouting instructions.
