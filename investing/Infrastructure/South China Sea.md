# South China Sea

South China Sea — strategic maritime basin linking Northeast Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Indian Ocean trade system. For investing purposes it is a shipping, energy, and geopolitical-risk corridor rather than a country note.

## Kalshi Taiwan risk proxy (May 20, 2026)

[[Kalshi]]'s KXTAIWANLVL4 ladder is a useful regulated-market proxy for Taiwan escalation because direct war markets are not listed on Kalshi. The contract asks whether the [[State Department]] issues a Level 4 travel advisory for [[Taiwan]] before each deadline; it therefore captures a public US-government escalation signal rather than a military event itself.

| Level 4 advisory before | Last | Bid / ask | Read |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Jul. 1, 2026 | 3.2c | 2.3c / 3.2c | Near-term Taiwan travel shutdown is priced as a tail. |
| Jan. 1, 2027 | 16c | 16c / 17c | 2026 escalation risk is live but not central. |
| Jan. 1, 2028 | 32c | 29c / 33c | The market starts assigning meaningful risk into 2027. |
| Jan. 1, 2029 | 46c | 46c / 47c | Late-decade risk approaches coin-flip. |
| Jan. 1, 2030 | 49c | 50c / 55c | The long tail is high but not an assumed rupture. |

For the investing vault, this belongs in the same risk family as [[Taiwan concentration risk]] and [[US-China security]]: the market is not pricing imminent crisis, but it is pricing a persistent late-decade probability that the US formally tells civilians to avoid Taiwan. That is the kind of signal that can reprice semiconductors, shipping, insurance, and defense even without an invasion market.
