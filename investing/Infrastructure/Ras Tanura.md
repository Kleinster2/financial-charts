# Ras Tanura

Ras Tanura is the world's largest crude oil loading terminal, operated by [[Saudi Aramco]] on the eastern coast of [[Saudi Arabia]] at the Persian Gulf, inside the [[Strait of Hormuz]]. It is the primary export point for Saudi crude — Arab Light and other grades — and the standard loading port referenced by the [[Baltic Exchange]] **TD3C** (270,000mt Middle East Gulf → China [[VLCC]]) benchmark. Tanker rates quoted as "TD3C" are economically rates *loading at Ras Tanura*, and Ras Tanura therefore anchors the pricing of roughly 85% of Middle East crude exports to Asia.

## Why it matters

1. **TD3C reference port.** The benchmark VLCC index that settles [[BWET]] and the ICE TD3C futures contract prices voyages from Ras Tanura. During the [[2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis]], Baltic panellists continued assessing TD3C even after Ras Tanura loadings collapsed — see [[Imaginary freight rate]].
2. **Hormuz exposure.** Ras Tanura sits *inside* the [[Strait of Hormuz]], meaning every departing VLCC must transit the chokepoint. Unlike the [[Yanbu]] Red Sea export alternative (via the [[East-West Pipeline]]), Ras Tanura has no Hormuz bypass. During the March 2026 closure, Saudi Arabia diverted volumes via [[East-West Pipeline]] to Yanbu on the Red Sea.
3. **VLCC concentration.** The terminal handles the largest class of crude carriers; fleet availability at Ras Tanura directly drives the global VLCC time-charter market.
