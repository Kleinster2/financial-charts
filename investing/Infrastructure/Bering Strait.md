# Bering Strait

The Bering Strait is the narrow passage between [[Russia]] and the [[United States]] connecting the Pacific and Arctic oceans. It matters to the investing vault because it is the entry/exit constraint for some [[Northern Sea Route]] optionality.

## May 2026 chokepoint dependency read

The FT article notes that Arctic shipping can bypass several traditional chokepoints, but any practical Northern Sea Route still inherits a chokepoint at Bering. That is the redundancy paradox: a route can reduce dependence on one set of straits while creating dependence on another.
