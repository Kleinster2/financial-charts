## Foundry

### [[TSMC]]
- **Market share**: 72% in Q3 2025, up 6pp YoY
- **Foundry 2.0 era**: $84.8B industry revenue in Q3, +17% YoY
- **Pricing**: 4th consecutive year of price hikes for sub-5nm nodes (3-5% avg), effective Jan 2026
- **Japan pivot**: Kumamoto Fab 2 may skip 6nm and go straight to 2nm — shifting from auto clients to [[NVIDIA]]/[[AMD]] AI chips
- **N-2 rule**: Taiwan weighing stricter export rule — TSMC limited to tech 2 generations behind leading edge for overseas
- **VEU revoked**: U.S. withdrew expedited export licenses for China fabs, but TSMC says no supply chain disruption

### [[Samsung]]
- **Q4 2025 earnings beat**: Operating profit over KRW 20 trillion (~$14B), 30% above consensus (Korea Economic Daily)
- **Taylor fab**: 93.6% complete, targeting July 2026. Upgraded from 4nm to 2nm. Capacity doubled to 50K wafers/month
- **2nm yields**: 55-60% (up from 40%), approaching [[TSMC]] levels
- **Customer wins**:
  - [[AMD]] EPYC Venice deal decision expected Jan 2026
  - [[NVIDIA]] exploring Samsung as second foundry after Groq licensing deal
  - Google, Qualcomm also reportedly eyeing Taylor 2nm
- **Exynos 2600**: World's first 2nm mobile chip announced for Galaxy S26 — proof point for process viability
- Implication for [[Short TSMC long Korea]]: Multiple catalysts materializing

### [[Intel Foundry Services]]
- **18A milestone**: Completed "five nodes in four years" roadmap. Panther Lake laptops shipping
- **Yields**: Still working out kinks — next 2 years will be "yield wars"
- **NVIDIA passed**: Tested 18A but stopped moving forward. Intel says external adoption expected on 14A
- **18A variants**: 18A-P (performance) and 18A-PT (3D stacking with Foveros Direct)
- **Magdeburg cancelled**: €30B Germany fab suspended to focus on US operations

- Silicon Motion CEO: "Simultaneous shortages of HDDs, DRAM, HBM, and NAND" — once-in-a-decade
- HBM cannibalizes commodity DRAM at 3:1 ratio, worsening with each generation
- Only new capacity (Micron ID1) expected 2027
